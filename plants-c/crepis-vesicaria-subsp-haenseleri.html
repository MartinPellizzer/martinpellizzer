Crepis vesicaria subsp. haenseleri

Crepis vesicaria subsp.

haenseleri, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single, erect spike, typical of the genus Crepis. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local insect populations.
