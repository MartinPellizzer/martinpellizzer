Carex petitiana subsp. attenuata

Carex petitiana subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic wildlife.
