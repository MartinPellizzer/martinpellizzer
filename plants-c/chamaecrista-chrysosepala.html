Chamaecrista chrysosepala

Chamaecrista chrysosepala, commonly known as golden-sepaled burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow floral sepals that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
