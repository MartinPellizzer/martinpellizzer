Croton kleinii

Croton kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that vary in hue from green to red and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
