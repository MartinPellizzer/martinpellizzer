Calicotome villosa var. genuina

Calicotome villosa var. genuina, commonly known as the rough bristly broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving the plant its "villosa" designation. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
