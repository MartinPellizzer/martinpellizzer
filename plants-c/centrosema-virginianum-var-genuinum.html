Centrosema virginianum var. genuinum

Centrosema virginianum var. genuinum, commonly known as the Virginia serrate cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas. It has a climbing habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used traditionally in agricultural systems.
