Capparis acacioides

Capparis acacioides, commonly known as the African date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits resembling dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
