Cnicus bracteatus

Cnicus bracteatus, commonly known as the sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Cnicus bracteatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
