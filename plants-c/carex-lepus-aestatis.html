Carex lepus-aestatis

Carex lepus-aestatis, commonly known as the summer sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, often appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex lepus-aestatis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
