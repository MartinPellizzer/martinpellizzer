Crataegus × smithii

Crataegus × smithii, commonly known as the Smith hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is edible for birds and some mammals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
