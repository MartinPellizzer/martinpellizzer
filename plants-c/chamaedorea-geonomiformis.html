Chamaedorea geonomiformis

Chamaedorea geonomiformis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, fertile soil.

This ornamental plant is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
