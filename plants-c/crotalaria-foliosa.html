Crotalaria foliosa

Crotalaria foliosa, commonly known as the leafy crotalaria, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are usually divided into three to five leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure, typical of the genus Crotalaria. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
