Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. major

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. major, commonly known as tall reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its structure and appearance make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
