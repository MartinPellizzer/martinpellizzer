Cuphea montana

Cuphea montana, commonly known as the mountain cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often red or orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
