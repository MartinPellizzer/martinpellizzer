Cissus pauciflora

Cissus pauciflora, commonly known as the sand dollar plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that can climb or trail, often adorned with small, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers, which are typically followed by small, round, sand-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Cissus pauciflora is often used in rock gardens or as a hanging plant due to its compact growth habit.
