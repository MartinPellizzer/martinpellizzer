Celtis malagasica

Celtis malagasica, commonly known as the Madagascar hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and coastal areas, and is adapted to the tropical climate of Madagascar.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
