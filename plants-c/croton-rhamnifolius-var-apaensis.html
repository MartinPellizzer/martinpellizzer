Croton rhamnifolius var. apaensis

Croton rhamnifolius var. apaensis, commonly known as the Apaensis Croton, is a shrub native to parts of East Africa.

It features large, palmate leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
