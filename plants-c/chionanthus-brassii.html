Chionanthus brassii

Chionanthus brassii, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Chionanthus brassii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
