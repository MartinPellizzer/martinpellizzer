Calyptrogyne tutensis

Calyptrogyne tutensis, commonly known as the Tutu Islands sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Cook Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to thrive in saline and coastal environments. Calyptrogyne tutensis plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes in its native habitat.
