Colea nana var. catati

Colea nana var. catati, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils.

This hardy plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
