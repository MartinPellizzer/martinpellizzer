Crocus longiflorus

Crocus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey and Greece. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with six petals, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crocus longiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early spring blooms.
