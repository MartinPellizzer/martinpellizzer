Chrysophyllum bombycinum

Chrysophyllum bombycinum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow-leafed cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn yellow before falling. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
