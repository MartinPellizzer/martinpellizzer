Campsiandra angustifolia var. rosea

Campsiandra angustifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered campsiandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
