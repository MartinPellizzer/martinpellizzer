Commiphora africana var. africana

Commiphora africana var. africana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically has smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ecological role and potential pharmacological properties.
