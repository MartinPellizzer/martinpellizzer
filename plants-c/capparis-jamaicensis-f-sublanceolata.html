Capparis jamaicensis f. sublanceolata

Capparis jamaicensis f. sublanceolata, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and distinctive, often spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, giving it the specific epithet sublanceolata.

It thrives in coastal and limestone habitats, often found in the understory of tropical dry forests.
