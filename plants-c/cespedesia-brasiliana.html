Cespedesia brasiliana

Cespedesia brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian grass or *Cespedesia brasiliana*, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological significance in certain habitats.
