Convolvulus siamensis

Convolvulus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and twining habit. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
