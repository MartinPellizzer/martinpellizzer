Callitriche papuana

Callitriche papuana, commonly known as the Papuan water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds, streams, and wetlands, and has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which attract pollinators like insects. Its leaves are narrow and whorled, often floating on the water or submerged in the mud.

Callitriche papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
