Cinnamomum uninervium

Cinnamomum uninervium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or Philippine cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its essential oils and is also used in the production of spices and perfumes.
