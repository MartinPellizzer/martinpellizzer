Copaifera laevis

Copaifera laevis, commonly known as the white copaiba, is a species of tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant produces yellowish to reddish resin, which is harvested for its aromatic and medicinal properties. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin.

Copaifera laevis is also valued for its ecological role in tropical rainforests.
