Coreopsis cyclocarpa var. pinnatisecta

Coreopsis cyclocarpa var. pinnatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a hardy addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
