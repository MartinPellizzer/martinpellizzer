Cucurbita pepo var. nucina

Cucurbita pepo var. nucina, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a variety of winter squash characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruits.

The plant is an annual herbaceous vine that produces large, palmately lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. Its fruits typically mature to a dark green or orange color with a distinctive, knobby surface. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring a long growing season to develop fully.

It is valued for its nutty flavor and is often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
