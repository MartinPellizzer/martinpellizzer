Condalia sonorae

Condalia sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran condalia, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments with low rainfall. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.

Condalia sonorae is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
