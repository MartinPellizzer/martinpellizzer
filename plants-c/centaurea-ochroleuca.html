Centaurea ochroleuca

Centaurea ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow starthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive central spine. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Centaurea ochroleuca is considered an invasive species in many parts of the world due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
