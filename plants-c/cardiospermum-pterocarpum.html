Cardiospermum pterocarpum

Cardiospermum pterocarpum, commonly known as the horsegram or sickle bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces distinctive kidney-shaped pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
