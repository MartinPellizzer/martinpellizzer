Calea thysanolepis

Calea thysanolepis, commonly known as the "tongue of the night" or "moonflower," is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its large, showy, white, tubular flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which resembles a lantern. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Calea thysanolepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
