Cosmos nitidus

Cosmos nitidus, commonly known as the white cosmos, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, airy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cosmos nitidus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
