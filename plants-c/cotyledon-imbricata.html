Cotyledon imbricata

Cotyledon imbricata, commonly known as the "Beaded Window Plant," is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. Its leaves are capable of storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
