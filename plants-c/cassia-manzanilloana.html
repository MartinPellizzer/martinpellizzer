Cassia manzanilloana

Cassia manzanilloana, commonly known as the Mexican golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cassia manzanilloana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
