Campanula rapunculus var. subpyrenaica

Campanula rapunculus var. subpyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to purple in color, and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.
