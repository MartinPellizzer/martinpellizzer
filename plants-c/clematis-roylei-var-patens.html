Clematis roylei var. patens

Clematis roylei var. patens, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
