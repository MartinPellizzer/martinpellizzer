Croton pseudofragrans

Croton pseudofragrans, commonly known as the fragrant croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant foliage.
