Clusia venusta

Clusia venusta, commonly known as the shining glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are followed by orange-red berries. Clusia venusta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
