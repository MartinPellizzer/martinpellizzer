Crotalaria stipularia var. genuina

Crotalaria stipularia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf horsegram, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with stipules that are often mistaken for leaves, contributing to its common name. It is cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a protein-rich feed for livestock.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
