Cymbidium javanicum

Cymbidium javanicum, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Cymbidium javanicum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and clustered inflorescences.
