Carex umbrosa var. huetiana

Carex umbrosa var. huetiana, commonly known as the Huet's shadow sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
