Cytisus Biflorus Subsp. Elongatus

Cytisus biflorus subsp. elongatus is a rare and lesser-known member of the legume family. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of North Africa. This subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaves and delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
