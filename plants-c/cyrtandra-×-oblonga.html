Cyrtandra × oblonga

Cyrtandra × oblonga, commonly known as the hybrid blue ginger, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra pedata and Cyrtandra oblonga, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. It features striking blue and purple flowers that are often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a glossy texture, making it a visually appealing plant in shaded garden environments.
