Croton luetzelburgii

Croton luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
