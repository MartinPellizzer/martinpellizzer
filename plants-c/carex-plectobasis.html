Carex plectobasis

Carex plectobasis, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering structures, called spikes, consist of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex plectobasis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
