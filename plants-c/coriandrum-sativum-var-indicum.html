Coriandrum sativum var. indicum

Coriandrum sativum var. indicum, commonly known as Indian cumin, is a variant of the coriander plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is an annual herb characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, dry fruits known as seeds, which are widely used as a spice in various cuisines. These seeds are rich in essential oils, contributing to their distinctive flavor and aroma.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for cultivation in warm, arid climates.
