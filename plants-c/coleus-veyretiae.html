Coleus veyretiae

Coleus veyretiae, commonly known as the Veyret's coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are small and tubular, typically appearing in clusters at the leaf axils.
