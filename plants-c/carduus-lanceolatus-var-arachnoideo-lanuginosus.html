Carduus lanceolatus var. arachnoideo-lanuginosus

Carduus lanceolatus var. arachnoideo-lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear purple to pink flowers with ray florets. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering period typically occurring in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
