Cassida parvula

Cassida parvula, commonly known as the small leaf blister beetle, is a species of beetle rather than a plant, so it does not have botanical aspects in the traditional sense.

However, it is often associated with plants as a pest that feeds on the leaves of various plants, causing damage through its feeding habits. The beetle's life cycle involves laying eggs on plant surfaces, and the larvae feed on plant tissue, leading to characteristic blistering on the leaves. It is commonly found in agricultural and garden settings where its host plants are present.

Due to its impact on plant health, it is a subject of study in entomology and integrated pest management.
