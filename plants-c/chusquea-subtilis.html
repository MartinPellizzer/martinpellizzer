Chusquea subtilis

Chusquea subtilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.

Chusquea subtilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
