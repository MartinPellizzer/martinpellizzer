Calpidia gracilescens

Calpidia gracilescens, commonly known as the slender calpidia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it relies on pollinators like beetles and flies for reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Calpidia gracilescens is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
