Clematis calypso

Clematis calypso, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically slender and climbing. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis calypso is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
