Clerodendrum diepenhorstii

Clerodendrum diepenhorstii, commonly known as the Diepenhorst's clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, inflated, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
