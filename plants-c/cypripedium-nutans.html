Cypripedium nutans

Cypripedium nutans, commonly known as the nodding lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that curve downward, giving it a nodding appearance. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows, often growing in rich, organic soil.

Cypripedium nutans is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
