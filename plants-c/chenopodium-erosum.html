Chenopodium erosum

Chenopodium erosum, commonly known as rough goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is characterized by its rough, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dry, triangular seeds. Chenopodium erosum is part of the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae) and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.
