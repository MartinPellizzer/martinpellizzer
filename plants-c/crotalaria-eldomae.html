Crotalaria eldomae

Crotalaria eldomae, commonly known as the golden crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

Crotalaria eldomae is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
