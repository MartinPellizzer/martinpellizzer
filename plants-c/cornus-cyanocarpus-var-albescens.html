Cornus cyanocarpus var. albescens

Cornus cyanocarpus var. albescens, commonly known as the white-fruited dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, white to pale blue fruits that are typically eaten by birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes in native landscapes.
