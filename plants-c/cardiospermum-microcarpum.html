Cardiospermum microcarpum

Cardiospermum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited heartseed.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas, characterized by its heart-shaped seeds encased in a fibrous, red or orange outer layer. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
