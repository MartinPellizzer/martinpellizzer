Centaurea gymnocarpa var. plumosa

Centaurea gymnocarpa var. plumosa, commonly known as the feathery centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its airy, lace-like foliage and vibrant floral display.
