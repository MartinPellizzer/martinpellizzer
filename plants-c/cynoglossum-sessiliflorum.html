Cynoglossum sessiliflorum

Cynoglossum sessiliflorum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
