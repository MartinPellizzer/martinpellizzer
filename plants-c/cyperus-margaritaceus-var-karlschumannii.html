Cyperus margaritaceus var. karlschumannii

Cyperus margaritaceus var. karlschumannii, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
