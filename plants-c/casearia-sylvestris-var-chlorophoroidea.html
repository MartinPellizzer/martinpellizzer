Casearia sylvestris var. chlorophoroidea

Casearia sylvestris var. chlorophoroidea, commonly known as the green-leaved casearia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
