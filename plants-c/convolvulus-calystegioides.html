Convolvulus calystegioides

Convolvulus calystegioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
