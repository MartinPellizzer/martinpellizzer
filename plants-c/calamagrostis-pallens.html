Calamagrostis pallens

Calamagrostis pallens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
