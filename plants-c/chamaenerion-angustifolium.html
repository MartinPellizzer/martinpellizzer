Chamaenerion angustifolium

Chamaenerion angustifolium, commonly known as fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Fireweed is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in abandoned fields.

It is a hardy perennial that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
