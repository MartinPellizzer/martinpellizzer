Carex jankowskii

Carex jankowskii, commonly known as the Jankowski's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, often green or brownish flowers. Native to certain regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, typically in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
