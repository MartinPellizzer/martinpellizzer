Centaurea solstitialis var. pycnoclada

Centaurea solstitialis var. pycnoclada, commonly known as the denseleaf common knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, composite inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

It is considered an invasive species in many areas due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
