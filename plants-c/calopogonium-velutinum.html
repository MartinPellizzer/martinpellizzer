Calopogonium velutinum

Calopogonium velutinum, commonly known as velvetleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become an invasive species in some areas.
