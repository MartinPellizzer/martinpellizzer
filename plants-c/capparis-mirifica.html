Capparis mirifica

Capparis mirifica, commonly known as the "Kampot pepper" or "Mirifica flower," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers and pungent, aromatic fruits. The plant belongs to the Capparaceae family and is known for its unique chemical composition, which includes various bioactive compounds. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
