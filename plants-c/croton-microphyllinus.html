Croton microphyllinus

Croton microphyllinus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green hues. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
