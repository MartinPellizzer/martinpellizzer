Chrysoscias media

Chrysoscias media, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant's ability to retain its foliage through winter contributes to its common name, "everlasting.".
