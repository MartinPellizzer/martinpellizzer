Cocos pityrophylla

Cocos pityrophylla, commonly known as the yellow coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow fruits, which contain a white, fleshy aril surrounding the hard, brown shell. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and the aril is used in various culinary applications.

This species is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in warm, humid climates.
