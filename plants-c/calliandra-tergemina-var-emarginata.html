Calliandra tergemina var. emarginata

Calliandra tergemina var. emarginata, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red bract-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
