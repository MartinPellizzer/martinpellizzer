Crotalaria virgultalis

Crotalaria virgultalis, commonly known as the yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Crotalaria virgultalis is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.
