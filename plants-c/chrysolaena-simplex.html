Chrysolaena simplex

Chrysolaena simplex, commonly known as the whiteleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a compact inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
