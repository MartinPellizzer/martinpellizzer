Centaurea luisieri

Centaurea luisieri, commonly known as Luisi's centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Luisi's centaury is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
