Crocodilina

Crocodilina, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that resemble the scales of a crocodile, hence its common name. The plant produces striking, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many aroid species. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light.

Crocodilina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
