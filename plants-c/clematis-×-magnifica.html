Clematis × magnifica

Clematis × magnifica, commonly known as the Royal Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive violet center. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-brown hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × magnifica is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
