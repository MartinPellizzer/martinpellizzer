Crocus rhodensis

Crocus rhodensis, commonly known as the Rhodian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often emerging in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive purple or brown center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Crocus rhodensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
