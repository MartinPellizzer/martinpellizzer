Croton ramellae

Croton ramellae, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from green to red and purple, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
