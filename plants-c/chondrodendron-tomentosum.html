Chondrodendron tomentosum

Chondrodendron tomentosum, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking white flowers that open at night. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
