Cycladenia humilis var. tomentosa

Cycladenia humilis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bluebead, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of blue and purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense, woolly coating of fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
