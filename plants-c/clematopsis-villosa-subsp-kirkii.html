Clematopsis villosa subsp. kirkii

Clematopsis villosa subsp.

kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's hairy clematopsis, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in terminal panicles during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
