Celsia lyrata var. sinuata

Celsia lyrata var. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
