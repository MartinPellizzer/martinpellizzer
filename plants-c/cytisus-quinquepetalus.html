Cytisus quinquepetalus

Cytisus quinquepetalus, commonly known as the five-petaled broom, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its slender, branching stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
