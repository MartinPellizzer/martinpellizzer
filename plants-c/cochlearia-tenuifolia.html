Cochlearia tenuifolia

Cochlearia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved cochlearia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to its coastal or moist habitats.

Cochlearia tenuifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
