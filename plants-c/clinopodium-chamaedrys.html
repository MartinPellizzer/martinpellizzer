Clinopodium chamaedrys

Clinopodium chamaedrys, commonly known as caraway or wild chervil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Clinopodium chamaedrys is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
