Chaetaria ramosa

Chaetaria ramosa, commonly known as the branched hairgrass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems with dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its common name.

Chaetaria ramosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
