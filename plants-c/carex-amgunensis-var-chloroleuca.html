Carex amgunensis var. chloroleuca

Carex amgunensis var. chloroleuca, commonly known as the pale-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a pale green or yellowish hue, contributing to its common name.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
