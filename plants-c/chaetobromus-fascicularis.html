Chaetobromus fascicularis

Chaetobromus fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are blue or purple in color.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
