Clematis jonesii

Clematis jonesii, commonly known as Jones' Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its twining stems.

Clematis jonesii is relatively low maintenance but benefits from regular pruning to encourage flowering.
