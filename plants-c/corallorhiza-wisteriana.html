Corallorhiza wisteriana

Corallorhiza wisteriana, commonly known as the Wister's coralroot, is a flowering plant in the orchid family.

It is a non-photosynthetic plant that relies entirely on mycorrhizal fungi for its nutrients. The plant lacks chlorophyll and has a pale, root-like appearance with no leaves or stems. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

This species is often found in shaded, moist woodlands across the eastern United States.
