Calamus superciliatus

Calamus superciliatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tropical plant characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
