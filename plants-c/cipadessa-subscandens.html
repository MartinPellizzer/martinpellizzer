Cipadessa subscandens

Cipadessa subscandens, commonly known as the climbing cipadessa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Cipadessa subscandens is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it climbs along trees or shrubs.
