Carduus fussii

Carduus fussii, commonly known as the Fussi thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, serrated foliage that is often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.
