Cranfillia nigra

Cranfillia nigra, commonly known as the black cranfillia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's unique morphology includes elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. Its reproductive cycle involves the production of tiny, black seeds that are dispersed by wind and animals.

Due to habitat destruction, Cranfillia nigra is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
