Calamus croftii

Calamus croftii, commonly known as the yellow bamboo or Croft's calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus croftii thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.
