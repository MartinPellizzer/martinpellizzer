Chaetopappa asteroides

Chaetopappa asteroides, commonly known as the starflower or desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and waxy leaf surfaces to reduce water loss.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for desert wildlife.
