Carex stricta var. turfosa

Carex stricta var. turfosa, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense, upright tussocks, creating a distinctive clumped appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-brown flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
