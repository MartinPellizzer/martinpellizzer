Colchicum Neapolitanum Subsp. Haynaldii

Colchicum neapolitanum subsp. haynaldii is a rare and striking member of the Colchicaceae family native to southern Europe. This subspecies is distinguished by its pale pink to white flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts. Its beauty and ecological importance make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
