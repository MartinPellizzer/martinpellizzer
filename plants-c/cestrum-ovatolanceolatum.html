Cestrum ovatolanceolatum

Cestrum ovatolanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
