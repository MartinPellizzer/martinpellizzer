Crocus sativus var. vernus

Crocus sativus var. vernus, commonly known as the early crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with six petals and a central yellow stamen, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its early flowering habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and early bloom, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
