Chamelaucium schuermannii

Chamelaucium schuermannii, commonly known as the Schuermann's tea-tree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is well adapted to sandy soils and saline environments, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Chamelaucium schuermannii is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
