Campanula speciosa var. brevifolia

Campanula speciosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and short, giving it its varietal name, while the stems are usually hairy and upright. This species thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
