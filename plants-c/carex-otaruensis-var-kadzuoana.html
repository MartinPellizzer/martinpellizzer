Carex otaruensis var. kadzuoana

Carex otaruensis var. kadzuoana, commonly known as the Kadzuoana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
