Commelina speciosa

Commelina speciosa, commonly known as the showy spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically borne on slender, leafless stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, white to yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

Commelina speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
