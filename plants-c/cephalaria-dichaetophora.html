Cephalaria dichaetophora

Cephalaria dichaetophora, commonly known as the two-branched cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
