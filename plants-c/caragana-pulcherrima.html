Caragana pulcherrima

Caragana pulcherrima, commonly known as the beautiful caragana, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for harsh climates. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Caragana pulcherrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
