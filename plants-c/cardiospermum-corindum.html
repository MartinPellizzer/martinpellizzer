Cardiospermum corindum

Cardiospermum corindum, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
