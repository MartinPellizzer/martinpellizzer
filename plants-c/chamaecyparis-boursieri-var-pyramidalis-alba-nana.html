Chamaecyparis boursieri var. pyramidalis-alba-nana

Chamaecyparis boursieri var. pyramidalis-alba-nana, commonly known as the dwarf white cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a pyramidal shape.

It features scale-like leaves that are white to bluish-white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of South America and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
