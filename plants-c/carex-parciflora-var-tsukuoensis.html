Carex parciflora var. tsukuoensis

Carex parciflora var. tsukuoensis, commonly known as the Tsukuoensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or wetlands.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
