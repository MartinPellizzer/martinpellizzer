Cinna tenuiflora

Cinna tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, alternate, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals, and appear in early summer.

Cinna tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
