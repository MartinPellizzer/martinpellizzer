Cyphomandra divaricata

Cyphomandra divaricata, commonly known as the purple passionfruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It produces distinctive, fleshy, purple fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine growth.

The fruit's high acidity and unique flavor make it a popular choice for jams and beverages.
