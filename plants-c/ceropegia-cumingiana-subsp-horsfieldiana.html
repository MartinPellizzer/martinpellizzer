Ceropegia cumingiana subsp. horsfieldiana

Ceropegia cumingiana subsp.

horsfieldiana, commonly known as the Horsfield's ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features tubular, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers with a unique structure that resembles a small, closed bell. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
