Commiphora staphyleifolia

Commiphora staphyleifolia, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.
