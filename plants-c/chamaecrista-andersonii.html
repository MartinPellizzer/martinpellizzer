Chamaecrista andersonii

Chamaecrista andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Chamaecrista andersonii is often found in coastal dunes and other xeric habitats.
