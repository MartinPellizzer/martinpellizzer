Coccosperma subcapitatum

Coccosperma subcapitatum, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and ability to thrive in saline and tidal environments. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils, preventing erosion.

Coccosperma subcapitatum plays a vital role in the ecological balance of mangrove ecosystems.
