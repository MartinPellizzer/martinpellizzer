Chenopodium album f. subhastatum

Chenopodium album f. subhastatum, commonly known as lamb's-quarters, is a common weed found in various habitats across temperate regions.

It is an annual plant characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by dry, three-angled fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a wide range of environmental conditions.
