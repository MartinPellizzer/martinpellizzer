Cucumis sativus var. squamosus

Cucumis sativus var. squamosus, commonly known as the scaly cucumber, is a rare variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its distinctive scaly or warty surface on the fruit, which sets it apart from typical cucumbers. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and typically grows as a vine, producing elongated, green fruits. It is native to southern Europe and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and mild flavor.
