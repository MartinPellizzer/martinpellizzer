Convolvulus tricolor var. pseudotricolor

Convolvulus tricolor var. pseudotricolor, commonly known as the variable blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically display a combination of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and ease of care.
