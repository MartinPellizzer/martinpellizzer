Clinopodium suaveolens

Clinopodium suaveolens, commonly known as sweet catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and emit a pleasant minty fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in summer.

Clinopodium suaveolens is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.
