Crotalaria tetrasperma

Crotalaria tetrasperma, commonly known as the four-seeded cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, green pods containing four seeds each, which are used as a food source in some parts of the world. It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments, posing a challenge for ecological management.
