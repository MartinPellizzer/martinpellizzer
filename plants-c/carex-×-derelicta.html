Carex × derelicta

Carex × derelicta, commonly known as the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, three-lobed flowers, typically green to brown in color, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and disturbed soils.

Carex × derelicta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
