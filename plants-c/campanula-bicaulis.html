Campanula bicaulis

Campanula bicaulis, commonly known as the two-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems that often branch near the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula bicaulis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
