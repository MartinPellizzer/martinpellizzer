Cyperus zollingerianus

Cyperus zollingerianus, commonly known as the Zollinger flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along waterways, and has slender, grass-like leaves with a triangular cross-section. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikelets that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus zollingerianus is adapted to moist environments and can tolerate varying levels of salinity.
