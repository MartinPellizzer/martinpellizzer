Chrysosplenium alternifolium

Chrysosplenium alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate-leaf golden spleenwort, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive member of the saxifragaceae family with ecological and ornamental value.
