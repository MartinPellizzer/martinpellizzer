Canna crocea

Canna crocea, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a prominent white midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Canna crocea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
