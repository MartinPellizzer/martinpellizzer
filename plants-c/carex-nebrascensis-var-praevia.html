Carex nebrascensis var. praevia

Carex nebrascensis var. praevia, commonly known as the Prairie sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow-green flowers. It is often found in moist to wet soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable environments.
