Coridochloa semialata var. ambigua

Coridochloa semialata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and inconspicuous.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
