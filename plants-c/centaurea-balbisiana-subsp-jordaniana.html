Centaurea balbisiana subsp. jordaniana

Centaurea balbisiana subsp.

jordaniana, commonly known as Jordanian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright blue to purple composite flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
