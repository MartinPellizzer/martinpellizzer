Convolvulus garberi

Convolvulus garberi, commonly known as Garber's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its coiled stems and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or lavender, blooming in the early morning.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
