Centaurea pulchra

Centaurea pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple petals and yellow-orange center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open habitats like meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea pulchra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
