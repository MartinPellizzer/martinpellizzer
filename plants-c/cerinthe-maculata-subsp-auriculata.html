Cerinthe maculata subsp. auriculata

Cerinthe maculata subsp.

auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cerinthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It features striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
