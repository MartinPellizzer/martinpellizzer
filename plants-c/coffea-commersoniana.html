Coffea commersoniana

Coffea commersoniana, commonly known as the Colombian coffee plant, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a rough texture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for its aromatic and flavorful coffee beans.
