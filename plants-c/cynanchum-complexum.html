Cynanchum complexum

Cynanchum complexum, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its environment.
