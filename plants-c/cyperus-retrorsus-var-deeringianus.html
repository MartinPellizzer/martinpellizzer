Cyperus retrorsus var. deeringianus

Cyperus retrorsus var. deeringianus, commonly known as the Deering's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
