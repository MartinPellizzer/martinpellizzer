Chassalia angustifolia

Chassalia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, exhibiting a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achene fruits.

Chassalia angustifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
