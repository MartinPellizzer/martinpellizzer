Cogswellia simulans

Cogswellia simulans, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are usually small and spiny, providing additional protection against herbivores.

Cogswellia simulans is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
