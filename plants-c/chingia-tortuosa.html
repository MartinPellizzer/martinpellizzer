Chingia tortuosa

Chingia tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of certain host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, white or pale green berries that are often overlooked. The plant's unique twisting growth pattern gives it its common name and helps it anchor itself to its host. It is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
