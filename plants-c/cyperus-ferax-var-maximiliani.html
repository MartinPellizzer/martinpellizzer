Cyperus ferax var. maximiliani

Cyperus ferax var. maximiliani, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in compact clusters.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
