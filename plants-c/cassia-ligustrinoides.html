Cassia ligustrinoides

Cassia ligustrinoides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they have multiple pairs of small leaflets, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Cassia ligustrinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
