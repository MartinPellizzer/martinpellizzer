Campanula persicifolia var. parviflora

Campanula persicifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
