Cirsium eriophorum subsp. richterianum

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

richterianum, commonly known as the Richter's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are purple to pink, forming dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stem.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bristly leaves and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
