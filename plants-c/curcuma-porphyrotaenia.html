Curcuma porphyrotaenia

Curcuma porphyrotaenia, commonly known as the purple-veined ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, orange-yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture. Its rhizomes, which are underground stems, are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Curcuma porphyrotaenia make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Zingiberaceae family.
