Cerasus incisa var. bukosanensis

Cerasus incisa var. bukosanensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant colors in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
