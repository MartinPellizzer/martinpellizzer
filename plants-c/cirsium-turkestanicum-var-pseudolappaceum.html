Cirsium turkestanicum var. pseudolappaceum

Cirsium turkestanicum var. pseudolappaceum, commonly known as the Turkistan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with spiny leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces large, showy purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. Its leaves are typically grayish-green with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant is known for its hardiness in arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
