Calliandra sesquipedalis

Calliandra sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant calliandra, is a striking leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery inflorescences that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it a visually impressive species. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the elongated stalks. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Calliandra sesquipedalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
