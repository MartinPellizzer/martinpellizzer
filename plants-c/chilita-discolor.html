Chilita discolor

Chilita discolor, commonly known as the Spotted Chilita, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Chilita discolor plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
