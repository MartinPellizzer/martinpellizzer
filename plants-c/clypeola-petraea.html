Clypeola petraea

Clypeola petraea, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Clypeola petraea is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
