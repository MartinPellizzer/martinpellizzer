Campanula grammosepala var. spathiphylla

Campanula grammosepala var. spathiphylla, commonly known as the Spathiphylla bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
