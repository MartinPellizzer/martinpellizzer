Caesalpinia madagascariensis

Caesalpinia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral spikes that appear during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its compound fruit is a long, flat pod that splits open to release seeds.

It is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in savannas and rocky areas across its native habitat.
