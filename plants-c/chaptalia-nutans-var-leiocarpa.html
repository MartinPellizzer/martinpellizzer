Chaptalia nutans var. leiocarpa

Chaptalia nutans var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered chaptalia, is a variant of the chaptalia genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and bloom during the warmer months. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
