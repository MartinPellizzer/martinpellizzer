Clematis glycinoides var. dentata

Clematis glycinoides var. dentata, commonly known as the Japanese climbing hydrangea, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that range from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
