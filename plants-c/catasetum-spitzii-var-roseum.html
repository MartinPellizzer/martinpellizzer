Catasetum spitzii var. roseum

Catasetum spitzii var. roseum, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is distinguished by its vibrant pink to reddish-pink flowers, which are often adorned with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by specific insects, featuring unique structures that aid in the transfer of pollen.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
