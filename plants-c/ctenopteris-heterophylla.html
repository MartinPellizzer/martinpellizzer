Ctenopteris heterophylla

Ctenopteris heterophylla, commonly known as the variable water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that vary in shape depending on the environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and along riverbanks. Its leaves are divided into pinnae, which are often serrated or toothed.

Ctenopteris heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
