Cattleya violacea var. huebneri

Cattleya violacea var. huebneri, commonly known as the Huebner's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, violet-colored flowers with intricate patterns and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively ease of cultivation.
