Clematis wissmanniana

Clematis wissmanniana, commonly known as the Wissmann's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive, recurved appearance. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis wissmanniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
