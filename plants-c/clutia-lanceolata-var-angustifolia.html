Clutia lanceolata var. angustifolia

Clutia lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered with a fine, hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Clutia lanceolata var. angustifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
