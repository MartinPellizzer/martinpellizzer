Centaurea alba subsp. leucomalla

Centaurea alba subsp.

leucomalla, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
