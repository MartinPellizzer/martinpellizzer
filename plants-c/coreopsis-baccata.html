Coreopsis baccata

Coreopsis baccata, commonly known as the scarlet coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its upright growth habit and vibrant color make it a visually striking addition to native plant gardens.
