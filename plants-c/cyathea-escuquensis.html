Cyathea escuquensis

Cyathea escuquensis, commonly known as the Escuque hakea, is a species of tree fern native to South America, particularly found in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded soils.

Cyathea escuquensis plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
