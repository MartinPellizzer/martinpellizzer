Clematis uncinata var. okinawensis

Clematis uncinata var. okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawan Clematis, is a rare flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces white to pale blue flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its limited distribution and ornamental value.
