Castanea fastigiata

Castanea fastigiata, commonly known as the upright chestnut.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. The plant features dense, upright growth habit with dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green catkins, and it produces edible, spiny nuts enclosed in a prickly husk.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in landscaping.
