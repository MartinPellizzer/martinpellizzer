Cyathea pichinchae

Cyathea pichinchae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of the fronds above.

Cyathea pichinchae plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
