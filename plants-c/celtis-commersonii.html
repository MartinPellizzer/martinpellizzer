Celtis commersonii

Celtis commersonii, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, winged fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.

The plant plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
