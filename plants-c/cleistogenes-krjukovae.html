Cleistogenes krjukovae

Cleistogenes krjukovae, commonly known as the Krjukov's bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system.

Cleistogenes krjukovae plays an important role in stabilizing soil and maintaining grassland ecosystems.
