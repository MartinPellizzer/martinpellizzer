Cynanchum mannii

Cynanchum mannii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its stout, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing compounds such as cardenolides.

Cynanchum mannii is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
