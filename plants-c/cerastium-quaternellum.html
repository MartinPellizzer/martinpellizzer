Cerastium quaternellum

Cerastium quaternellum, commonly known as four-leafed mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
