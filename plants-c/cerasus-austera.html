Cerasus austera

Cerasus austera, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other cherry species. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cerasus austera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive spring blossoms and hardy nature.
