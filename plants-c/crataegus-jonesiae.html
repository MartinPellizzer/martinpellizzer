Crataegus jonesiae

Crataegus jonesiae, commonly known as the Jonesia hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Crataegus jonesiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
