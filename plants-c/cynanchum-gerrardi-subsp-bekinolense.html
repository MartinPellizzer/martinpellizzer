Cynanchum gerrardi subsp. bekinolense

Cynanchum gerrardi subsp.

bekinolense, commonly known as the Bektashi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
