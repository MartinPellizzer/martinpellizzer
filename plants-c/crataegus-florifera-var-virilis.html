Crataegus florifera var. virilis

Crataegus florifera var. virilis, commonly known as the male flowering hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to produce abundant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
