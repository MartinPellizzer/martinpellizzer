Cerasus verrucosa f. tadshikistanica

Cerasus verrucosa f. tadshikistanica, commonly known as the Tadzhikistan cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus species.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Tajikistan. The plant features small, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is typically smooth and grayish.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in local horticulture.
