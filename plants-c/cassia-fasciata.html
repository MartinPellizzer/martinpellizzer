Cassia fasciata

Cassia fasciata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

Cassia fasciata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
