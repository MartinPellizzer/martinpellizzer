Ceropegia yampwapwa

Ceropegia yampwapwa, commonly known as the African violet or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
