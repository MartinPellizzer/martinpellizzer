Cyrtochilum loesenerianum

Cyrtochilum loesenerianum, commonly known as the Loesener's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower structure known as a resupinate labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are relatively small but striking, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Cyrtochilum loesenerianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, exotic blooms.
