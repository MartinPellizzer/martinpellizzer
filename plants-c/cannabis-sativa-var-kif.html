Cannabis sativa var. kif

Cannabis sativa var. kif, commonly known as hashish plant, is a variety of the Cannabis sativa species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and broad, serrated leaves. The plant produces resinous glands called trichomes, which are rich in cannabinoids such as THC. It is primarily cultivated for its buds, which are used to produce hashish.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is subject to legal restrictions in many countries.
