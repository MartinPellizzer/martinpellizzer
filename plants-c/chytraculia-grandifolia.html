Chytraculia grandifolia

Chytraculia grandifolia, commonly known as the grandifoliate chytra, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chytraculaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
