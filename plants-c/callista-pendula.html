Callista pendula

Callista pendula, commonly known as the hanging callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the dry conditions of its natural habitat.

Callista pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
