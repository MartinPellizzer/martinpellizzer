Choisya × dewitteana

Choisya × dewitteana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry or Mexican holly.

It is a hybrid shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in the spring. It produces bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall, which are edible for birds but not typically for humans.

Choisya × dewitteana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
