Cheiranthus odoratus

Cheiranthus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are small and contained within slender pods that split open when mature.
