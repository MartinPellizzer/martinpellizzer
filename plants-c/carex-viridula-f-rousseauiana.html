Carex viridula f. rousseauiana

Carex viridula f. rousseauiana, commonly known as the Rousseau's sedge, is a variant of the green sedge species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its foliage is usually a deep green, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
