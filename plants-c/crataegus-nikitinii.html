Crataegus nikitinii

Crataegus nikitinii, commonly known as Nikitin's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
