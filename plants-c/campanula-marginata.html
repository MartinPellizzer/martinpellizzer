Campanula marginata

Campanula marginata, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a white or silvery margin, giving it its species name "marginata.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and wildflower meadows.
