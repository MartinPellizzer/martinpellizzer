Cyathea conjugata

Cyathea conjugata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

Cyathea conjugata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
