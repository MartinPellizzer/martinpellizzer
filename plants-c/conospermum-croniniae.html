Conospermum croniniae

Conospermum croniniae, commonly known as the Cronin's conospermum, is a shrub native to the southwest of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and appear in clusters, blooming during the cooler months.

Conospermum croniniae is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathland and coastal environments.
