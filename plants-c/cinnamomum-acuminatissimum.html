Cinnamomum acuminatissimum

Cinnamomum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by oval, reddish-brown fruits. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for flavoring, similar to other cinnamon species.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
