Clerodendrum glaberrimum

Clerodendrum glaberrimum, commonly known as the white clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
