Chaetochloa comosa

Chaetochloa comosa, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like structures along the leaf blades, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chaetochloa comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate, grass-like appearance.
