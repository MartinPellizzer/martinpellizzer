Chassalia bracteata

Chassalia bracteata, commonly known as the golden chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a central disk of tubular flowers surrounded by ray florets, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Chassalia bracteata is often cultivated for its vibrant color and is used in both garden settings and as a cut flower.
