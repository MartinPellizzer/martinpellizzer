Calathea leonae

Calathea leonae, commonly known as the leopard calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves with a mix of green, silver, and red hues, resembling a leopard's spotted pattern. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea leonae is relatively low maintenance but benefits from regular humidity and careful watering to maintain its vibrant appearance.
