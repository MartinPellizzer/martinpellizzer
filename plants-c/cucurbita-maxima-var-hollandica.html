Cucurbita maxima var. hollandica

Cucurbita maxima var. hollandica, commonly known as the Dutch White Frisket, is a cultivar of the pumpkin species characterized by its large, round, white to pale yellow fruits.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to North America. The plant produces trailing vines with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It is primarily grown for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, nutty flavor.

This variety is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications due to its unique appearance and versatility.
