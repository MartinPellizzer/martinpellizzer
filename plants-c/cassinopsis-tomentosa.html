Cassinopsis tomentosa

Cassinopsis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cassinopsis, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cassinopsis tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
