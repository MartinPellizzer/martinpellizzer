Cistus × gardianus var. tomentosus

Cistus × gardianus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage, which provides a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
