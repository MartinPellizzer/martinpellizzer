Calophyllum praetermissum

Calophyllum praetermissum, commonly known as the African sandpaper tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in various medicinal applications.

The tree is often grown for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
