Cinnamomum validinerve var. poilanei

Cinnamomum validinerve var. poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar cinnamon or Poilane's cinnamon, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by oval, dark brown berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species.
