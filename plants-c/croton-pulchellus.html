Croton pulchellus

Croton pulchellus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright red to deep orange and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
