Calanthe leuseri

Calanthe leuseri, commonly known as the Leuser calanthe, is a rare and elegant orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly beautiful, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers during the rainy season, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. Calanthe leuseri thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
