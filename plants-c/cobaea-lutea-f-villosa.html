Cobaea lutea f. villosa

Cobaea lutea f. villosa, commonly known as the woolly yellow cup, is a striking member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the common cup plant. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical structure includes a woody stem and bracteate inflorescences, making it a unique addition to gardens.
