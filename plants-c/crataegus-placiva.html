Crataegus placiva

Crataegus placiva, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Crataegus placiva is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
