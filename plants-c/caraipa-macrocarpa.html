Caraipa macrocarpa

Caraipa macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high rainfall.

Due to its rapid growth, it is often used for erosion control and as a source of sustainable bamboo products.
