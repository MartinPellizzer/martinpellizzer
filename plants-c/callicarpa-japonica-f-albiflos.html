Callicarpa japonica f. albiflos

Callicarpa japonica f. albiflos, commonly known as the whiteflower beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of white flowers that appear in late spring, contrasting with its dark purple berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for its floral display. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The whiteflower beautyberry is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
