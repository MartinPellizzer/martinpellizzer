Coleus entebbensis

Coleus entebbensis, commonly known as the African shieldleaf, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
