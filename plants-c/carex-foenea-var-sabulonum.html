Carex foenea var. sabulonum

Carex foenea var. sabulonum, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as dunes, prairies, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
