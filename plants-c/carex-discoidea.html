Carex discoidea

Carex discoidea, commonly known as the disk sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex discoidea is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
