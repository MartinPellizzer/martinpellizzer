Cordyline metallica

Cordyline metallica, commonly known as the silver cordyline, is a striking evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically during the spring or summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dramatic visual appeal.

Cordyline metallica thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
