Carex foliosa

Carex foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often borne on slender, branching stems. Carex foliosa is often found in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
