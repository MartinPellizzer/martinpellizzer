Croton lundianus var. gracilentus

Croton lundianus var. gracilentus, commonly known as the graceful croton, is a tropical shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that display a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
