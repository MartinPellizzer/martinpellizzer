Cirsium pseudopersonata subsp. kusnezowianum

Cirsium pseudopersonata subsp.

kusnezowianum, commonly known as the Kusnezow's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage is covered in dense, stiff hairs, which provide protection against herbivores and harsh weather conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
