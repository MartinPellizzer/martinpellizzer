Capparis Atamisquea

Atamisquea caper, scientifically known as Capparis atamisquea, is a rare and unique plant native to the high Andean regions of Peru. This shrub is characterized by its thorny stems and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Despite its rugged appearance, it plays a vital role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species. The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its healing benefits. Due to habitat loss and climate change, Capparis atamisquea faces increasing threats to its survival.
