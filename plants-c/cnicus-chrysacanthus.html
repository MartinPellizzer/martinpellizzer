Cnicus chrysacanthus

Cnicus chrysacanthus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
