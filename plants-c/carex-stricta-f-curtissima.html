Carex stricta f. curtissima

Carex stricta f. curtissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often submerged or floating in shallow water.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
