Cyperus tegetum var. protracta

Cyperus tegetum var. protracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Cyperus tegetum var. protracta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
