Carex atlantica var. incomperta

Carex atlantica var. incomperta, commonly known as the Atlantic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
