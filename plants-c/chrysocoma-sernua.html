Chrysocoma sernua

Chrysocoma sernua, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
