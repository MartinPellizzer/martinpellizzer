Cerastium morrisonense var. formosanum

Cerastium morrisonense var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.
