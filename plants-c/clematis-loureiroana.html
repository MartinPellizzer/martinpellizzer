Clematis loureiroana

Clematis loureiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive violet-blue hue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbers.

Clematis loureiroana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
