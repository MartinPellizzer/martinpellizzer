Cyathea × hybrida

Cyathea × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyathea cooperi and Cyathea mulleri.

It is a tall, trunked fern that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, featuring a fibrous, woody stem covered in a layer of dead fronds. The plant produces large, bi-lobed, bright green fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-drained, acidic soil.

Cyathea × hybrida is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
