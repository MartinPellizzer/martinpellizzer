Chamaecyparis pisifera f. filifera

Chamaecyparis pisifera f. filifera, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cypress.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer that features scale-like leaves arranged in flat, linear sprays. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and is native to the mountainous regions of central Japan. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its foliage has a silvery-blue hue, adding a striking visual contrast to landscapes.
