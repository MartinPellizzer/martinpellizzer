Cachrys involucrata

Cachrys involucrata, commonly known as the goldenrods, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cachrys involucrata plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
