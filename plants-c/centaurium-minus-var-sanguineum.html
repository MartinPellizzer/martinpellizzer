Centaurium minus var. sanguineum

Centaurium minus var. sanguineum, commonly known as the blood centaury, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often reddish in color.

This variety is distinguished by its vivid floral coloration, which sets it apart from other centaury species.
