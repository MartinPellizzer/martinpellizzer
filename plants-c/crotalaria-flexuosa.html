Crotalaria flexuosa

Crotalaria flexuosa, commonly known as the curlleaf cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems that often curve or twist. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
