Calanthe cardiochila

Calanthe cardiochila, commonly known as the heart-lung orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Calanthe cardiochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
