Carduus nervosus

Carduus nervosus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in dry, disturbed soils. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas where it can spread aggressively.
