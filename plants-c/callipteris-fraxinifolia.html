Callipteris fraxinifolia

Callipteris fraxinifolia, commonly known as the golden screwtree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The golden screwtree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
