Cyrtochilum aureum

Cyrtochilum aureum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive golden lip. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it thrives in areas with high humidity and moderate temperatures.

Cyrtochilum aureum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
