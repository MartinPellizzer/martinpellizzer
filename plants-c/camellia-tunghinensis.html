Camellia tunghinensis

Camellia tunghinensis, commonly known as the Tung-hin camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a waxy texture and a subtle fragrance.

Camellia tunghinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
