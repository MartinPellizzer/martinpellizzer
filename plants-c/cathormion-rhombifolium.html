Cathormion rhombifolium

Cathormion rhombifolium, commonly known as the diamond-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
