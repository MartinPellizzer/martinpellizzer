Ceropegia sagittata

Ceropegia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive shape that resembles a narrow tube. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia sagittata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
