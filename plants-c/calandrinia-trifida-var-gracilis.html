Calandrinia trifida var. gracilis

Calandrinia trifida var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient addition to xeriscapic gardens.
