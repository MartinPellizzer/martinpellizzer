Cereus pottsii

Cereus pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' candleplant, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and flowering habits make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
