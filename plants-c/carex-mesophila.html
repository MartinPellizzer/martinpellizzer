Carex mesophila

Carex mesophila, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and other damp environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Carex mesophila is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and improve waterlogged soils.
