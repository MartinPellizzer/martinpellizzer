Commiphora spathulifoliolata

Commiphora spathulifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and incense purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and traditional uses.
