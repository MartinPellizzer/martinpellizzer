Coleus splendidus

Coleus splendidus, commonly known as the brilliant coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and purple. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often arranged in clusters.

Coleus splendidus is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
