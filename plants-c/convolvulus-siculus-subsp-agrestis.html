Convolvulus siculus subsp. agrestis

Convolvulus siculus subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a trailing or climbing perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
