Caladium lividum

Caladium lividum, commonly known as the Indian shot or arrowhead plant, is a tropical perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped, or shield-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by red, spherical berries. Caladium lividum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its striking foliage makes it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
