Calamus acanthospathus

Calamus acanthospathus, commonly known as the spiny calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine spines, giving it its common name. It produces a striking inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Calamus acanthospathus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
