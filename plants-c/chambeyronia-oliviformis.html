Chambeyronia oliviformis

Chambeyronia oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped fern, is a unique species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved fronds that resemble the shape of an olive. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in humid climates.

Chambeyronia oliviformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
