Cincinalis tomentosa

Cincinalis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cincinalis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, white tomentum covering its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
