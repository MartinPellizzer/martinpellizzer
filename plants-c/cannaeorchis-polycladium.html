Cannaeorchis polycladium

Cannaeorchis polycladium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the rainy season.

The plant's rhizome system allows it to spread and form colonies, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
