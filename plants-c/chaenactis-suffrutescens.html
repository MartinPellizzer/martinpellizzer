Chaenactis suffrutescens

Chaenactis suffrutescens, commonly known as the desert paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, often grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Its succulent stems and leaves help it conserve water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
