Chrysophyllum lanatum

Chrysophyllum lanatum, commonly known as the Natal apple or wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single large seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
