Chamaemelum × valentinum

Chamaemelum × valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's Day flower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining species from the Chamaemelum genus.
