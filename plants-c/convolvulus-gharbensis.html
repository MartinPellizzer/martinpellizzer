Convolvulus gharbensis

Convolvulus gharbensis, commonly known as the Gharbensis morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant features trailing stems with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. It is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, often found growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
