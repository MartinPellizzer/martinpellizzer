Chionanthus sutepensis

Chionanthus sutepensis, commonly known as the Peruvian snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

Chionanthus sutepensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
