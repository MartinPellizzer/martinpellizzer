Cestrum falcatum var. soukupii

Cestrum falcatum var. soukupii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.
