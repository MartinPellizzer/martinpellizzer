Clethra ferruginea

Clethra ferruginea, commonly known as rusty clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Clethra ferruginea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
