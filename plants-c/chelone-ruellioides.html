Chelone ruellioides

Chelone ruellioides, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
