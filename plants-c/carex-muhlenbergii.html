Carex muhlenbergii

Carex muhlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
