Carex × neofilipendula

Carex × neofilipendula, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a species within the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex filifera and Carex pendula, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage with cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
