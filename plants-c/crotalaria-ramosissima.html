Crotalaria ramosissima

Crotalaria ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its branched stems and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in various environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
