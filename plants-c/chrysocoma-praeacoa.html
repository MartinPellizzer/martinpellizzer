Chrysocoma praeacoa

Chrysocoma praeacoa, commonly known as the Cape mountain aloes, is a succulent plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and conservation areas.
