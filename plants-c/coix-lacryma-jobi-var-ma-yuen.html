Coix lacryma-jobi var. ma-yuen

Coix lacryma-jobi var. ma-yuen, commonly known as Job's tears or Chinese pearl millet, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, teardrop-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering structure that produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in a tough, fibrous husk, which is removed during processing for culinary or medicinal use.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is cultivated in various parts of Asia for its nutritional and therapeutic properties.
