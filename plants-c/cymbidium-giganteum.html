Cymbidium giganteum

Cymbidium giganteum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and red.

Cymbidium giganteum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
