Cotyledon farinosa

Cotyledon farinosa, commonly known as the mealy cup or mealy cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a mealy appearance. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Cotyledon farinosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
