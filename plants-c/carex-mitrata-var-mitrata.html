Carex mitrata var. mitrata

Carex mitrata var. mitrata, commonly known as the mitred sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, triangular spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex mitrata var. mitrata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
