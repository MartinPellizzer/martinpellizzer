Centropogon solanifolius var. speciosus

Centropogon solanifolius var. speciosus, commonly known as the showy false nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in sandy soils with ample sunlight. Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, often with a bluish tint, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in local cuisines.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
