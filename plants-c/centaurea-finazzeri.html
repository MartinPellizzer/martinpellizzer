Centaurea finazzeri

Centaurea finazzeri, commonly known as the Finazzeri's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its vibrant purple to pinkish-blue flowers. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
