Chaenorhinum origanifolium f. glabratum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium f. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous honeysuckle.

This plant is a flowering species characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, resembling those of oregano, giving it its specific epithet.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
