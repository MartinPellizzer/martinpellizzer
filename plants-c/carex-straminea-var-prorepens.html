Carex straminea var. prorepens

Carex straminea var. prorepens, commonly known as the creeping sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are typical of the Carex genus. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is noted for its ability to form dense mats, which help in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
