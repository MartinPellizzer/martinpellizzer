Clematis recta subsp. kirilowii

Clematis recta subsp.

kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilowii Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It features slender, twining stems that can climb to considerable heights, often reaching several meters in length.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
