Cinnagrostis

Cinnagrostis, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, yellow or gold-colored inflorescences, which consist of many small florets arranged in a composite head. Cinnagrostis species are typically herbaceous perennials, often found in meadows, prairies, and along roadsides. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Cinnagrostis alpina and Cinnagrostis pauciflora, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
