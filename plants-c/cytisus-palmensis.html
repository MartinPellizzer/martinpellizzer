Cytisus palmensis

Cytisus palmensis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to coastal and rocky environments makes it a hardy species.

The plant's root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
