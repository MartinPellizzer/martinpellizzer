Capparis acutifolia subsp. obovata

Capparis acutifolia subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and obovate, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow berries that mature to a dark purple color.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
