Cyperus laetus var. parciflorus

Cyperus laetus var. parciflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various wetland habitats, contributing to its ecological significance.
