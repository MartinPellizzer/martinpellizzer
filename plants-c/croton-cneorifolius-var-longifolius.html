Croton cneorifolius var. longifolius

Croton cneorifolius var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
