Cereus grandiflorus var. uranos

Cereus grandiflorus var. uranos, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a unique hybrid plant that combines the vibrant colors of the cyanea species with the hardy structure of the cereus grandiflorus.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, large white or pink flowers that bloom at night, a feature typical of many cacti. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
