Chamaecrista anceps

Chamaecrista anceps, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Chamaecrista anceps is typically found in sandy soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is often used in native plant gardens.
