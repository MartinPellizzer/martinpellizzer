Carex xanthoathera

Carex xanthoathera, commonly known as the yellow-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex xanthoathera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
