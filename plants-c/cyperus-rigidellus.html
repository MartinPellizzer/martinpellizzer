Cyperus rigidellus

Cyperus rigidellus, commonly known as the rigid flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its slender, triangular stems are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, clustered flowers in summer.

Cyperus rigidellus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
