Carex pauciflora f. elatior

Carex pauciflora f. elatior, commonly known as the elevated carex, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
