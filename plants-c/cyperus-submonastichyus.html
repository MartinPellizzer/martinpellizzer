Cyperus submonastichyus

Cyperus submonastichyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or seasonal wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
