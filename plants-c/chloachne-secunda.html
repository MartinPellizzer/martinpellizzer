Chloachne secunda

Chloachne secunda, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are followed by narrow, spike-like seed heads.

Chloachne secunda plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
