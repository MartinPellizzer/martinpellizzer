Cnidium tenuifolium

Cnidium tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf cowkilling, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic compounds. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The roots and rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
