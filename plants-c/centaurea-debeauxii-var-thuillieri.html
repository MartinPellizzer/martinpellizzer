Centaurea debeauxii var. thuillieri

Centaurea debeauxii var. thuillieri, commonly known as the Thuillier's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern France.

It features striking blue to purple composite flowers with yellow centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
