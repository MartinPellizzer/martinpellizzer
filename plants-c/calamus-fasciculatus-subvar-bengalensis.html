Calamus fasciculatus subvar. bengalensis

Calamus fasciculatus subvar. bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South and Southeast Asia.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
