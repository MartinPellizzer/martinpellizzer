Cassia punctulata

Cassia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated seed pods that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cassia punctulata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
