Cissus segmentata

Cissus segmentata, commonly known as the rock fig or stonecress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub with woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
