Celastrus senegalensis

Celastrus senegalensis, commonly known as the African bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
