Caladenia hoffmanii subsp. graniticola

Caladenia hoffmanii subsp.

graniticola, commonly known as the granite rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid endemic to Western Australia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in granite outcrops and gravelly areas.

The plant produces a single, slender stem with a single flower that is usually pale pink to white in color. Its flowers have a distinctive labellum that is divided into two lobes, a characteristic feature of many caladenia species.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing the plant's resilience to harsh conditions.
