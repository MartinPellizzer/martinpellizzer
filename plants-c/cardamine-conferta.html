Cardamine conferta

Cardamine conferta, commonly known as the clustered watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it often forms dense clusters in wet soils.

Cardamine conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
