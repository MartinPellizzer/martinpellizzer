Carex gembolensis var. timorensis

Carex gembolensis var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

The Timor sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
