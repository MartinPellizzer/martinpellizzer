Chromolaena ivifolia

Chromolaena ivifolia, commonly known as the horseweed or crofton weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dry, winged fruits. It is often invasive in tropical and subtropical regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
