Castelnavia multipartita f. pendulosa

Castelnavia multipartita f. pendulosa, commonly known as the hanging castelnavia, is a unique variant of the castelnavia plant characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features elongated, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated edges and a fibrous root system.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in both horticultural displays and traditional medicine.
