Celastrus dependens

Celastrus dependens, commonly known as the hanging staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, orange-red berries containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
