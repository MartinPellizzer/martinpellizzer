Cordia salzmannii var. albiflora

Cordia salzmannii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
