Chenopodium anthelminticum var. subhirsutum

Chenopodium anthelminticum var. subhirsutum, commonly known as giant goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its succulent stems and leaves store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
