Ctenitis ochrorachis

Ctenitis ochrorachis, commonly known as the golden fan fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, golden hairs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are divided into numerous narrow segments, giving it a delicate and intricate appearance.

Ctenitis ochrorachis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
