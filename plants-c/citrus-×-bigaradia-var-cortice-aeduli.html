Citrus × bigaradia var. cortice-aeduli

Citrus × bigaradia var. cortice-aeduli, commonly known as the sweet orange.

This variety is a hybrid citrus species characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and juicy, sweet flesh. It is native to Southeast Asia and has been widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and commercial applications. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy green leaves and fragrant white flowers.

Its fruit develops from the flower and is harvested when fully mature, often in the fall.
