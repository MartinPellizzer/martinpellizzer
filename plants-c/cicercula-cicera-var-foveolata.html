Cicercula cicera var. foveolata

Cicercula cicera var. foveolata, commonly known as the foveolate cicer, is a variant of the genus Cicercula, which belongs to the legume family Fabaceae.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive foveolate (pitted) surface on its seed pods, giving it a unique appearance. It is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and adaptation to extreme environments.
