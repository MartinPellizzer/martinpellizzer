Clematis wenshanensis

Clematis wenshanensis, commonly known as the Wenshan Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, typically white or pale blue flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
