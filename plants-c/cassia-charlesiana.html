Cassia charlesiana

Cassia charlesiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Cassia charlesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
