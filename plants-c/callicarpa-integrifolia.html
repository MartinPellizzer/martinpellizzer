Callicarpa integrifolia

Callicarpa integrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are smooth along their edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
