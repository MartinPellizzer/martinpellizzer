Clematis albida

Clematis albida, commonly known as the white clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces feathery, branched stems that often climb or twine around supports. Its botanical structure includes showy, five-petaled flowers with numerous stamens, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Clematis albida prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
