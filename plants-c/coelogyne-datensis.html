Coelogyne datensis

Coelogyne datensis, commonly known as the Datoong orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of 3 to 5, often in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its pseudobulbs for storage of water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne datensis is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements and garden displays.
