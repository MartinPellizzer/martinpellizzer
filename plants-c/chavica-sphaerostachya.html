Chavica sphaerostachya

Chavica sphaerostachya, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Chavica sphaerostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
