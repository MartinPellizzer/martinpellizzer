Crotalaria florida var. florida

Crotalaria florida var. florida, commonly known as the Florida crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with erect, branching stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
