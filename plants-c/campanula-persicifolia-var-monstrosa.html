Campanula persicifolia var. monstrosa

Campanula persicifolia var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a contrasting white throat. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
