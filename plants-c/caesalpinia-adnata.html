Caesalpinia adnata

Caesalpinia adnata, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Caesalpinia adnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
