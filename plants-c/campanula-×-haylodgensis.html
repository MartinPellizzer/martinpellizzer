Campanula × haylodgensis

Campanula × haylodgensis, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula × haylodgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
