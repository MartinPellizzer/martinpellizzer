Chrysophyllum brasiliense var. minus

Chrysophyllum brasiliense var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sweet, orange-red berries that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and fruit cultivation.

Its compact size and aesthetic appeal make it suitable for both landscaping and conservation efforts in its natural habitat.
