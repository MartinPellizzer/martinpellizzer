Carex trispiculata

Carex trispiculata, commonly known as the three-bristled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
