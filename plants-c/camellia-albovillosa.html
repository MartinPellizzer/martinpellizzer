Camellia albovillosa

Camellia albovillosa, commonly known as the white-hairy camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a dense, hairy covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Camellia albovillosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
