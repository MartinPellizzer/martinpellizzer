Crusea subalata

Crusea subalata, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects its adaptation to high-altitude environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with rocky or alpine conditions.
