Centaurea rhaetica

Centaurea rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky and grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea rhaetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
