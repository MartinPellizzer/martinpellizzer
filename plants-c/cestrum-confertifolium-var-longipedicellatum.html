Cestrum confertifolium var. longipedicellatum

Cestrum confertifolium var. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces bright yellow or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and botanical collections.
