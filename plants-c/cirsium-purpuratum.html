Cirsium purpuratum

Cirsium purpuratum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium purpuratum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
