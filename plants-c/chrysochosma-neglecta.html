Chrysochosma neglecta

Chrysochosma neglecta, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a distinctive yellowish-green color and a mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, typically after rainfall. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Chrysochosma neglecta is known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
