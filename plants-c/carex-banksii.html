Carex banksii

Carex banksii, commonly known as the Banks' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps.

Carex banksii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
