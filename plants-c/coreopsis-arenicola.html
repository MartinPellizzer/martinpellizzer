Coreopsis arenicola

Coreopsis arenicola, commonly known as beach coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It is well adapted to sandy and salty environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in dune ecosystems. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve moisture.

Beach coreopsis is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
