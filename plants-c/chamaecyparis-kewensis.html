Chamaecyparis kewensis

Chamaecyparis kewensis, commonly known as the Kew's cypress, is a rare coniferous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are bright green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it flowers in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its ornamental value.

This species is also notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a unique addition to horticultural collections.
