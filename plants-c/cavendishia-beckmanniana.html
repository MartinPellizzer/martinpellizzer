Cavendishia beckmanniana

Cavendishia beckmanniana, commonly known as the Beckmann's cavendishia, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces spiny areoles and has a distinctive branching pattern as it matures. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
