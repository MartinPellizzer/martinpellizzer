Cassia densifolia

Cassia densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia densifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
