Cornus purpurea

Cornus purpurea, commonly known as red twig dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red or reddish-brown stems that remain visible throughout the winter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or pink berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy texture.

Cornus purpurea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
