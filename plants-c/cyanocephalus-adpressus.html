Cyanocephalus adpressus

Cyanocephalus adpressus, commonly known as the bluehead plant, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and distinctive blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits known as schizocarps, which split into two one-seeded parts upon maturity. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and compact growth habit.
