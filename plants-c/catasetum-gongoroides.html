Catasetum gongoroides

Catasetum gongoroides, commonly known as the orchid with a distinctive yellow and green coloration, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure, including a specialized column and pollinia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Catasetum gongoroides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
