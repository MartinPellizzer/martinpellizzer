Chrysobalanus guianensis

Chrysobalanus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese chrysobalanus, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalaneaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single large seed. It thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
