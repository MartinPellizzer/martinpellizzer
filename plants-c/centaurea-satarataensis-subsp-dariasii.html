Centaurea satarataensis subsp. dariasii

Centaurea satarataensis subsp.

dariasii, commonly known as the Darias' cornflower, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of Turkey. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid and nutrient-poor environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique floral morphology.
