Cynanchum morrenioides

Cynanchum morrenioides, commonly known as Morren's dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by follicles containing silky seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
