Conophyllum obtusipetalum

Conophyllum obtusipetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled conophyllum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Conophyllum obtusipetalum is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
