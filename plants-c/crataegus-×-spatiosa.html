Crataegus × spatiosa

Crataegus × spatiosa, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and they serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus × spatiosa is native to Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
