Cyperus mutisii var. asper

Cyperus mutisii var. asper, commonly known as rough flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
