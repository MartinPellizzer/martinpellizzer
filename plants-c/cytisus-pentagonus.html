Cytisus pentagonus

Cytisus pentagonus, commonly known as the five-angled broom, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, often thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive five-angled appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Cytisus pentagonus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
