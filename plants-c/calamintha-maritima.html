Calamintha maritima

Calamintha maritima, commonly known as coastal hyssop, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines, thriving in salty and wind-swept environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, rounded fruits known as nutlets.

Calamintha maritima is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
