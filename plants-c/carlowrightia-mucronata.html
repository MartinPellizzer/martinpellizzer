Carlowrightia mucronata

Carlowrightia mucronata, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
