Cissus adenocephala

Cissus adenocephala, commonly known as the bonebrake or creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or trees.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
