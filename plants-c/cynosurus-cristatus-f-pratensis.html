Cynosurus cristatus f. pratensis

Cynosurus cristatus f. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow hair grass, is a variant of the tufted hair grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, compact spikelet that is often adorned with a tuft of bristles at the tip.

This form is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local insect populations.
