Carica cestriflora

Carica cestriflora, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces small, fragrant flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. The plant's leaves are thick, waxy, and lance-shaped, with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside. The fruit is a small, yellow-orange berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
