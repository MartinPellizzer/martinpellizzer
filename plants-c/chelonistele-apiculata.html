Chelonistele apiculata

Chelonistele apiculata, commonly known as the spiny rock cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Chelonistele apiculata is often found growing on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
