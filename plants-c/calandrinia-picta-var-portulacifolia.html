Calandrinia picta var. portulacifolia

Calandrinia picta var. portulacifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its ability to store water in its leaves. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
