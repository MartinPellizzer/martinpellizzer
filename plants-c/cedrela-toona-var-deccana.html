Cedrela toona var. deccana

Cedrela toona var. deccana, commonly known as Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
