Crataegus monogyna subsp. heterophylla

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open scrublands.
