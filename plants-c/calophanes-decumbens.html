Calophanes decumbens

Calophanes decumbens, commonly known as the creeping calophane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it has a fibrous root system.

Calophanes decumbens is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
