Clitoria javitensis f. bracteosubtenda

Clitoria javitensis f. bracteosubtenda, commonly known as the subtended bract clitoria, is a variant of the winged bean plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers, which are clustered in racemes. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. Its foliage is trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaves that are often used in herbal remedies.

The species is valued for both ornamental and agricultural purposes due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
