Chrysolaena cristobaliana

Chrysolaena cristobaliana, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
