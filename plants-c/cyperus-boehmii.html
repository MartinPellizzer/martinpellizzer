Cyperus boehmii

Cyperus boehmii, commonly known as the Boehm's flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus boehmii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
