Calluna vulgaris f. albopurpurea

Calluna vulgaris f. albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a variant of the common heather.

It is characterized by its striking white and purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to acidic, well-drained soils in heathland environments. It has small, needle-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wildlife habitats.
