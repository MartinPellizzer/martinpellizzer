Cestrum macrophyllum

Cestrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, broad, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cestrum macrophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
