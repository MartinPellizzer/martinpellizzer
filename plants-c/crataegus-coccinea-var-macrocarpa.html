Crataegus coccinea var. macrocarpa

Crataegus coccinea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. Its fruit is a pome, typically eaten by birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
