Citrus aurea

Citrus aurea, commonly known as the golden orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces edible citrus fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Citrus aurea is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and its culinary uses.
