Crepis hookeriana

Crepis hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
