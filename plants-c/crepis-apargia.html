Crepis apargia

Crepis apargia, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Crepis apargia is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at high elevations.
