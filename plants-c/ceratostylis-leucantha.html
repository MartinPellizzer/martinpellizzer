Ceratostylis leucantha

Ceratostylis leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and have a delicate, fragrant appearance.

Ceratostylis leucantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
