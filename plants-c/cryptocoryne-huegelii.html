Cryptocoryne huegelii

Cryptocoryne huegelii, commonly known as the Java crypts, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a bluish-green coloration. This plant thrives in moderate to high light conditions and requires stable water parameters to maintain its health. It is often cultivated in freshwater aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Cryptocoryne huegelii is also known for its ability to help in maintaining water clarity by absorbing excess nutrients.
