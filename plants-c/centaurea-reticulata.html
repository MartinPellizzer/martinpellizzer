Centaurea reticulata

Centaurea reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, conical cluster. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Centaurea reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
