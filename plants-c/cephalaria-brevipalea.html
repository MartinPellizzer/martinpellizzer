Cephalaria brevipalea

Cephalaria brevipalea, commonly known as the short-paleate cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephalaria brevipalea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
