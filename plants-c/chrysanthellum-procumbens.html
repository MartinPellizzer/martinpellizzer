Chrysanthellum procumbens

Chrysanthellum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive, almost star-shaped appearance. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the cooler months, making it a unique addition to gardens in suitable climates.
