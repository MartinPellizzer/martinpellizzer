Crassula wrightiana

Crassula wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
