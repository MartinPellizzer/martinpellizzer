Citrus × aurantium var. voangkely

Citrus × aurantium var. voangkely, commonly known as Vietnamese bitter orange, is a citrus hybrid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, aromatic peel. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is primarily used for its peel, which contains essential oils and is valued in traditional medicine and perfumery.
