Commelina pubinervis

Commelina pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy nerve grass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Commelina pubinervis is notable for its ornamental value and its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.
