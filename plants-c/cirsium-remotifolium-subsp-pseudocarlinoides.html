Cirsium remotifolium subsp. pseudocarlinoides

Cirsium remotifolium subsp.

pseudocarlinoides, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence is a composite head surrounded by bracts, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is characterized by its relatively small size and distinct, recurved leaf morphology.
