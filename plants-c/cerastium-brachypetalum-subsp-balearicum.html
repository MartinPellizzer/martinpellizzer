Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. balearicum

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals that are shorter than the sepals, giving it the specific epithet *brachypetalum*. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or along cliffs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and delicate floral display.
