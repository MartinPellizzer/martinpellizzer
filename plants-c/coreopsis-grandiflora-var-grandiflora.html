Coreopsis grandiflora var. grandiflora

Coreopsis grandiflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy as well.

It is well adapted to well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
