Crataegus uncta

Crataegus uncta, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Crataegus uncta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in hedgerows and gardens.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, with its flowers and berries used in traditional herbal remedies.
