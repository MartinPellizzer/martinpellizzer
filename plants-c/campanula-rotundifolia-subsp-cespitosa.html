Campanula rotundifolia subsp. cespitosa

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

cespitosa, commonly known as the round-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a perennial species that spreads by rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
