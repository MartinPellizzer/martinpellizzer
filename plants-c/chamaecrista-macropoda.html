Chamaecrista macropoda

Chamaecrista macropoda, commonly known as the largeleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by distinctive, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in diverse ecological settings.
