Carex glauca var. laxiflora

Carex glauca var. laxiflora, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from blue-green to grayish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
