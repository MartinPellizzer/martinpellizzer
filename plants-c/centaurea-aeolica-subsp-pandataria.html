Centaurea aeolica subsp. pandataria

Centaurea aeolica subsp.

pandataria, commonly known as the Aeolian centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of tightly packed small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
