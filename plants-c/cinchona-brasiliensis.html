Cinchona brasiliensis

Cinchona brasiliensis, commonly known as the Peruvian bark, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is most notable for its bark, which contains alkaloids such as quinine, historically used to treat malaria. Its cultivation has played a significant role in the global pharmaceutical industry.
