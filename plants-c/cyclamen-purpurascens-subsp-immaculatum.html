Cyclamen purpurascens subsp. immaculatum

Cyclamen purpurascens subsp.

immaculatum, commonly known as the white cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with distinctive red stamens.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding a delicate ornamental value to gardens. Cyclamen purpurascens subsp.

immaculatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
