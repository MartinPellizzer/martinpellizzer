Calocedrus macrolepis

Calocedrus macrolepis, commonly known as the Japanese incense cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and aromatic wood. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its cones are small and woody.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and incense production.
