Centaurea cuneifolia

Centaurea cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.
