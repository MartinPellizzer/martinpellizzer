Carex atrata subsp. longistolonifera

Carex atrata subsp.

longistolonifera, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are dark green and somewhat glossy, while its inflorescence consists of spikes of tiny, three-ranked flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
