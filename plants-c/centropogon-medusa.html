Centropogon medusa

Centropogon medusa, commonly known as the Medusa flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky inflorescence that resembles the mythological figure of Medusa, giving it its common name. The plant features vibrant orange and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often surrounded by long, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
