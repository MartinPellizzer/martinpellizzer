Campanula fragilis var. barrelieri

Campanula fragilis var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
