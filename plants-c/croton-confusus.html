Croton confusus

Croton confusus, commonly known as the confusion croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
