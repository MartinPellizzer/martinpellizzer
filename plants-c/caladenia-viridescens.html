Caladenia viridescens

Caladenia viridescens, commonly known as the Emerald Spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the genus Caladenia, which is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive spider-like appearance, and it blooms from late autumn to early spring.

The species is known for its delicate and intricate floral structure, which is characteristic of many Caladenia species.
