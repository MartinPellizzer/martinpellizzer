Carex phankei

Carex phankei, commonly known as the Phankai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its propagation.

Carex phankei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
