Carduus hillii

Carduus hillii, commonly known as the Hill's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, flowering stalk that bears purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
