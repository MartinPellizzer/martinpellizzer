Celtis ferarum

Celtis ferarum, commonly known as the smallleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms and ashes, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The small, yellowish-green fruits are small drupes that mature in the fall and are often eaten by birds and small mammals.
