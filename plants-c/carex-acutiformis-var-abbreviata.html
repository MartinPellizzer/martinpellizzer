Carex acutiformis var. abbreviata

Carex acutiformis var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, with the lower ones being shorter and more abbreviated.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
