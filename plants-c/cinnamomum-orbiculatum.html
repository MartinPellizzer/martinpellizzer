Cinnamomum orbiculatum

Cinnamomum orbiculatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The tree produces small yellow flowers followed by red berries, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
