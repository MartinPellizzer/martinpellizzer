Cerastium nanum

Cerastium nanum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands, along stream banks, and in rocky areas.

Cerastium nanum is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
