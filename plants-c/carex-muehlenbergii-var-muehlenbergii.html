Carex muehlenbergii var. muehlenbergii

Carex muehlenbergii var. muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a waxy coating. Its flowering spikes are usually brown or reddish and appear in late summer to early fall.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
