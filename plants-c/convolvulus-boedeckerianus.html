Convolvulus boedeckerianus

Convolvulus boedeckerianus, commonly known as the Boedecker's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
