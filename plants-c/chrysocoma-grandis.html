Chrysocoma grandis

Chrysocoma grandis, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Chrysocoma grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
