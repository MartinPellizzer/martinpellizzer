Carex appressa var. diaphana

Carex appressa var. diaphana, commonly known as the glassy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet to moist soils in shaded to partially shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a striking bluish-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
