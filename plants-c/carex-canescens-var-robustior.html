Carex canescens var. robustior

Carex canescens var. robustior, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
