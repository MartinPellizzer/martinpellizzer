Christella cretacea

Christella cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous Christella, is a rare fossil plant species that dates back to the Cretaceous period.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, narrow leaves with a distinct venation pattern. The plant is primarily found in sedimentary rock formations in specific regions of North America. Its structure suggests it was a herbaceous plant adapted to warm, coastal environments.

Fossilized remains provide important insights into the flora of ancient ecosystems.
