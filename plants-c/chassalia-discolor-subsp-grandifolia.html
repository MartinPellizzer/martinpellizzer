Chassalia discolor subsp. grandifolia

Chassalia discolor subsp.

grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered chassalia, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features showy, yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, terminal head. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed habitats.
