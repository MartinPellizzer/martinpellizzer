Carex thibetica var. minor

Carex thibetica var. minor, commonly known as the Tibetan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is noted for its resilience to cold climates and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
