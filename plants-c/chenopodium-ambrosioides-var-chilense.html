Chenopodium ambrosioides var. chilense

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. chilense, commonly known as wild wormseed, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits called utricles, which contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally for its medicinal and pesticidal properties.
