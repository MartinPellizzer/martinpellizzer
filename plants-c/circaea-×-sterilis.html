Circaea × sterilis

Circaea × sterilis, commonly known as the sterile white bead-vine, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white, bead-like flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

While it is not known for producing viable seeds, it spreads through rhizomes, making it a persistent ground cover in suitable habitats.
