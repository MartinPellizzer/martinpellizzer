Chionanthus terniflorus var. acuminatus

Chionanthus terniflorus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its varietal name "acuminatus," which refers to the sharp, pointed tips. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable plant for gardens and landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
