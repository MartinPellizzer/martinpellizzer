Cuphea sucumbiensis

Cuphea sucumbiensis, commonly known as the weeping cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that resemble weeping strands of red or pink flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.

Cuphea sucumbiensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
