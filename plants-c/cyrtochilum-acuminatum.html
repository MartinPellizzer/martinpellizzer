Cyrtochilum acuminatum

Cyrtochilum acuminatum, commonly known as the acute-angled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Cyrtochilum acuminatum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
