Clianthus pictus

Clianthus pictus, commonly known as the New Zealand waratah, is a striking shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with a distinctive, almost papery texture. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable ornamental plant.

Clianthus pictus is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
