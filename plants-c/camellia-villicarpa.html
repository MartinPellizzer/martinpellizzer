Camellia villicarpa

Camellia villicarpa, commonly known as the silky camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red, blooming in late winter to early spring. Camellia villicarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile addition to subtropical gardens.
