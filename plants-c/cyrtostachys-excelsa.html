Cyrtostachys excelsa

Cyrtostachys excelsa, commonly known as the giant bamboo or tall bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Cyrtostachys excelsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
