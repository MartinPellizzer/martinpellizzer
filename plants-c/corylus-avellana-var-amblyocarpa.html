Corylus avellana var. amblyocarpa

Corylus avellana var. amblyocarpa, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm long and composed of 5–9 oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers, which develop into edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its fruit, known as a hazelnut, is an important food source for wildlife and humans alike.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of nuts in horticultural settings.
