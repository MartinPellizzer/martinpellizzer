Cistus halimifolius var. obtusifolius

Cistus halimifolius var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the sweet rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly rounded at the tips. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in full sun.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
