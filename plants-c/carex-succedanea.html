Carex succedanea

Carex succedanea, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex succedanea is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
