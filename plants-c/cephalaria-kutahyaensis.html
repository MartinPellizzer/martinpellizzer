Cephalaria kutahyaensis

Cephalaria kutahyaensis, commonly known as the Turkish cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
