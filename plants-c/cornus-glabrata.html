Cornus glabrata

Cornus glabrata, commonly known as smooth cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, followed by red, drupes that persist into winter. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Cornus glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
