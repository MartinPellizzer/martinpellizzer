Chamaerops depressa

Chamaerops depressa, commonly known as the dwarf palm or the flat-palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is a small, low-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features a slender, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often curved or drooping. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible, sweet fruits that are consumed by various animals.

Chamaerops depressa is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.
