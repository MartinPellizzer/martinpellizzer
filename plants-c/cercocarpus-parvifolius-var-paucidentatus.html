Cercocarpus parvifolius var. paucidentatus

Cercocarpus parvifolius var. paucidentatus, commonly known as the slenderleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact, multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
