Colchicum eghimocymbion

Colchicum eghimocymbion, commonly known as the Armenian autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils, often found in mountainous areas.

The species is closely related to other colchicums and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
