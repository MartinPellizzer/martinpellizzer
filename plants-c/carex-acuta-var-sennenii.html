Carex acuta var. sennenii

Carex acuta var. sennenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a glossy green surface, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers in the summer.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant form and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
