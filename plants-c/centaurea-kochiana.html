Centaurea kochiana

Centaurea kochiana, commonly known as Koch's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing showy composite flower heads.

Centaurea kochiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
