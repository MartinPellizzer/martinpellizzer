Cyclopogon maderoi

Cyclopogon maderoi, commonly known as the Mader's cyclopogon, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, high-altitude conditions of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Cyclopogon maderoi is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
