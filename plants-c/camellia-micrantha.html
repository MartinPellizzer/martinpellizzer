Camellia micrantha

Camellia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered camellia, is a species native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that are usually pink or red, with five to eight petals. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Camellia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
