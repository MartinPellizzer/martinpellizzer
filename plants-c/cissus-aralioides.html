Cissus aralioides

Cissus aralioides, commonly known as the Indian nettle or climbing fig, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus aralioides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
