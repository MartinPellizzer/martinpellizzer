Capparis hereroensis

Capparis hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but have a tangy, acidic taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Capparis hereroensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
