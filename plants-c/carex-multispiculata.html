Carex multispiculata

Carex multispiculata, commonly known as the many-spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

Carex multispiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
