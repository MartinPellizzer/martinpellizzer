Commelina zeylanica

Commelina zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers with three petals, often accompanied by two white or pale blue bracts that resemble petals. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Commelina zeylanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
