Carex inconspicua var. hieronymi

Carex inconspicua var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
