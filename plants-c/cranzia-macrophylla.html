Cranzia macrophylla

Cranzia macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf cranza, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
