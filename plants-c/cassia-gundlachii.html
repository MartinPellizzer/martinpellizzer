Cassia gundlachii

Cassia gundlachii, commonly known as desert cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia gundlachii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
