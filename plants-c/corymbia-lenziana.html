Corymbia lenziana

Corymbia lenziana, commonly known as the red ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, mottled bark that ranges from pale grey to reddish-brown. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Corymbia lenziana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
