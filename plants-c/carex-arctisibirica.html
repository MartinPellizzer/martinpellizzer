Carex arctisibirica

Carex arctisibirica, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in tundra or alpine environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in harsh climates.

Carex arctisibirica is an important component of the vegetation in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
