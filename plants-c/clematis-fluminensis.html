Clematis fluminensis

Clematis fluminensis, commonly known as the river clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Clematis fluminensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
