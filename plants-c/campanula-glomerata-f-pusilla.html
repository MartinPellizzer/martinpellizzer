Campanula glomerata f. pusilla

Campanula glomerata f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf clustered bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are densely arranged along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and easy maintenance in garden settings.
