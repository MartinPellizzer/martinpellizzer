Cissus acetosa

Cissus acetosa, commonly known as the sour fig or rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white tubercles. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting berries. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Cissus acetosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
