Commelina montigena

Commelina montigena, commonly known as the mountain commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it relies on insects for pollination.

Commelina montigena is often found in lowland and montane habitats, showcasing its adaptability to diverse ecological conditions.
