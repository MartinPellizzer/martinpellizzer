Cirsium pumilum f. fultius

Cirsium pumilum f. fultius, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, basal leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage is densely hairy, providing a distinctive texture, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is notable for its broader, more robust leaves compared to other forms of Cirsium pumilum.
