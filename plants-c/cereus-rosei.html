Cereus rosei

Cereus rosei, commonly known as the night bloomer or night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is a member of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its flowers typically bloom only once a year, making it a rare and striking sight.

Cereus rosei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
