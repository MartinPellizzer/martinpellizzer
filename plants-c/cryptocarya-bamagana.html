Cryptocarya bamagana

Cryptocarya bamagana, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in leaf axils, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

Cryptocarya bamagana is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
