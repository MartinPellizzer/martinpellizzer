Cola simiarum

Cola simiarum, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain stimulant-rich seeds. These seeds are the source of cola nut, which contains caffeine and other alkaloids.

The tree is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
