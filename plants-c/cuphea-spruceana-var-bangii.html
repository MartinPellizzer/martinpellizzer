Cuphea spruceana var. bangii

Cuphea spruceana var. bangii, commonly known as the Bangi cuphea, is a compact, herbaceous shrub native to Central America.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
