Carex gracilis f. amblylepis

Carex gracilis f. amblylepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical, leaf-like structures called culms. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
