Cistus glaucus

Cistus glaucus, commonly known as the whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure with silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, coastal areas.

Cistus glaucus is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
