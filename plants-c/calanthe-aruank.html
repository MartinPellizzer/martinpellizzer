Calanthe aruank

Calanthe aruank, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinct lip.

Calanthe aruank is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
