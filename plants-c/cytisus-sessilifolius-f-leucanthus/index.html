Cytisus Sessilifolius F. Leucanthus

Cytisus sessilifolius f. leucanthus is a rare and striking variant of the broom plant known for its unique white flowers. This form stands out among its green-flowered relatives with its pale blooms that catch the sunlight beautifully. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after addition to gardens and botanical collections. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively obscure plant in the world of horticulture.
