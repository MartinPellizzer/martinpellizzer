Cytisus austriacus var. microphyllus

Cytisus austriacus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the Austrian broom or dwarf broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
