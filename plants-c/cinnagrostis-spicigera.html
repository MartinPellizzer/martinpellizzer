Cinnagrostis spicigera

Cinnagrostis spicigera, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cinnagrostis spicigera plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
