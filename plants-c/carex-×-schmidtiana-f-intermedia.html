Carex × schmidtiana f. intermedia

Carex × schmidtiana f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and moist soil environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other wet areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
