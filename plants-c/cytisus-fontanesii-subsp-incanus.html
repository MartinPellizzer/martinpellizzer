Cytisus fontanesii subsp. incanus

Cytisus fontanesii subsp.

incanus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and slightly hairy. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
