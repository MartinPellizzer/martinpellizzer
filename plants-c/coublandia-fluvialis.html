Coublandia fluvialis

Coublandia fluvialis, commonly known as the riverbloom vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and capable of absorbing nutrients from waterlogged soils.

Coublandia fluvialis is often found along riverbanks and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
