Cousinia acanthodendron

Cousinia acanthodendron, commonly known as the spiny bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia acanthodendron is often found in rocky slopes and steppe habitats.
