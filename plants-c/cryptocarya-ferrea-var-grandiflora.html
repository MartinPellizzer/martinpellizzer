Cryptocarya ferrea var. grandiflora

Cryptocarya ferrea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Coryza grandiflora*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and construction.
