Clematis globosa

Clematis globosa, commonly known as the globe clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis globosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
