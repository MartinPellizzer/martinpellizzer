Crotalaria vexillata

Crotalaria vexillata, commonly known as the scarlet crown-bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
