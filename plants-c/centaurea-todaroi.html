Centaurea todaroi

Centaurea todaroi, commonly known as the Todaro's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to dry climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spiked bracts around its flower heads.

Centaurea todaroi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
