Chenopodium petiolare var. leptophylloides

Chenopodium petiolare var. leptophylloides, commonly known as slenderleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often used in traditional culinary practices. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
