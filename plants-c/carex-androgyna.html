Carex androgyna

Carex androgyna, commonly known as the male sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. Carex androgyna is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and forest floors.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
