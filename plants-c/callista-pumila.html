Callista pumila

Callista pumila, commonly known as the dwarf callista, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with hairy, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
