Cordia berteroi

Cordia berteroi, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Cordia berteroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
