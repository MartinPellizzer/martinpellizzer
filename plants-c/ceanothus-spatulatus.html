Ceanothus spatulatus

Ceanothus spatulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of thick, leathery leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces showy clusters of blue or purple flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Ceanothus spatulatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
