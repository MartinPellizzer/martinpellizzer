Croton variabilis

Croton variabilis, commonly known as the variable croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that varies in hue from green to red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cup-shaped structures called cyathia. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture, often with prominent veins.

Croton variabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant and varied leaf colors.
