Convolvulus subquinquelobus

Convolvulus subquinquelobus, commonly known as the five-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, round berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
