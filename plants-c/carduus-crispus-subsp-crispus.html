Carduus crispus subsp. crispus

Carduus crispus subsp.

crispus, commonly known as the curly thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
