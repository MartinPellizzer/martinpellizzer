Crocus hadriaticus f. parnassicus

Crocus hadriaticus f. parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassus crocus, is a rare and ornamental bulbous plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators.
