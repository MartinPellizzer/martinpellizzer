Cosmos tenellus

Cosmos tenellus, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and feathery leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a yellow center, and bloom from late summer into early autumn.

Cosmos tenellus is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
