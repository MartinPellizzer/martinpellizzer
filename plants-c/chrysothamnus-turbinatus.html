Chrysothamnus turbinatus

Chrysothamnus turbinatus, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and conical disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chrysothamnus turbinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
