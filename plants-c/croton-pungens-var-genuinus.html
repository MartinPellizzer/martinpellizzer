Croton pungens var. genuinus

Croton pungens var. genuinus, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of this plant make it both visually appealing and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
