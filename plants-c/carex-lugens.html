Carex lugens

Carex lugens, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to regions of North America, it typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
