Campanulastrum americanum

Campanulastrum americanum, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Campanulastrum americanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
