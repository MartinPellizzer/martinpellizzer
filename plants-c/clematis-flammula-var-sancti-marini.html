Clematis flammula var. sancti-marini

Clematis flammula var. sancti-marini, commonly known as the San Marino yellow clematis, is a compact, deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically covered with fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a climbing plant.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
