Carex niigatensis

Carex niigatensis, commonly known as the Niigata sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the Niigata Prefecture, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Carex niigatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
