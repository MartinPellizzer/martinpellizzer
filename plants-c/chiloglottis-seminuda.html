Chiloglottis seminuda

Chiloglottis seminuda, commonly known as the slender female orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its specific ecological requirements.
