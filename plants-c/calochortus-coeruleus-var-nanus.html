Calochortus coeruleus var. nanus

Calochortus coeruleus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf blue dicks, is a compact, flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, sky-blue to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
