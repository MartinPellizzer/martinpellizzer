Convolvulus undulifolius var. secundus

Convolvulus undulifolius var. secundus, commonly known as the undulating convolvulus, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and undulating leaves.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which bloom in clusters. Its stems are trailing or climbing, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
