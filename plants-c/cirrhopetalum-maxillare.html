Cirrhopetalum maxillare

Cirrhopetalum maxillare, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals and sepals that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by small insects.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
