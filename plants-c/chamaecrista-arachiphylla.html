Chamaecrista arachiphylla

Chamaecrista arachiphylla, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its small, oblong pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
