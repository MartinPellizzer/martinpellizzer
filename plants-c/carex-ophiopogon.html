Carex ophiopogon

Carex ophiopogon, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex ophiopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
