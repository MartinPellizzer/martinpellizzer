Calyptranthes yaraensis

Calyptranthes yaraensis, commonly known as the Yaraensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and inconspicuous white flowers. The plant relies on a network of roots to attach to host trees, from which it derives some of its nutrients. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for certain insect species and birds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in some conservation areas.
