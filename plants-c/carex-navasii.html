Carex navasii

Carex navasii, commonly known as Navas' sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the western United States. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Navas' sedge produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.
