Cussonia hamata

Cussonia hamata, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.
