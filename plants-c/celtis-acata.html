Celtis acata

Celtis acata, commonly known as the Chinese laurustinus, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

Celtis acata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
