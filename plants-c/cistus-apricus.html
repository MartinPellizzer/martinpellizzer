Cistus apricus

Cistus apricus, commonly known as the early rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing shrub form and grey-green foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow or orange flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Cistus apricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
