Calea tolimana

Calea tolimana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, white or pink petals that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming behavior. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea tolimana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
