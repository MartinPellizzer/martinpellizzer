Commicarpus australis

Commicarpus australis, commonly known as the Australian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Commicarpus australis is adapted to arid conditions and is an important plant in local ecosystems.
