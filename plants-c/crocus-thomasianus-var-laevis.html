Crocus thomasianus var. laevis

Crocus thomasianus var. laevis, commonly known as the Thomasian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
