Campanula carniolica

Campanula carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Campanula carniolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
