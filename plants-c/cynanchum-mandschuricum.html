Cynanchum mandschuricum

Cynanchum mandschuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its latex contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, thriving in temperate climates.
