Calamus subinermis

Calamus subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the genus Calamus. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Calamus subinermis is valued in traditional medicine and is also used in the production of rattan and other handicrafts.
