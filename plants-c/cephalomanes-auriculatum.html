Cephalomanes auriculatum

Cephalomanes auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate headman, is a unique species of sea anemone found in tropical and subtropical marine environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-like crown of tentacles that resembles a head, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in shallow coastal waters, often attached to rocky substrates or coral reefs. Its vibrant colors and specialized structure allow it to efficiently capture prey and defend against predators.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a significant role in maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems.
