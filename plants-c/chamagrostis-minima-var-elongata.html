Chamagrostis minima var. elongata

Chamagrostis minima var. elongata, commonly known as the slender dwarf reed, is a low-growing grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, erect leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of invertebrates and birds.
