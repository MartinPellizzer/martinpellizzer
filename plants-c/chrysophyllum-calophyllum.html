Chrysophyllum calophyllum

Chrysophyllum calophyllum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are yellow when ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
