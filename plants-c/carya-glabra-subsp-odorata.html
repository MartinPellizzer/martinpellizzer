Carya glabra subsp. odorata

Carya glabra subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the sweet chestnut or American chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins and female flowers, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like nuts.

It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is known for its ecological and historical significance.
