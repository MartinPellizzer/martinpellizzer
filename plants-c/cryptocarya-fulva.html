Cryptocarya fulva

Cryptocarya fulva, commonly known as the golden candlewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and mild climates. Its leaves are thick, glossy, and often have a distinctive yellow or reddish hue on the underside.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
