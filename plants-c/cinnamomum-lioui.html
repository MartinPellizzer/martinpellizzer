Cinnamomum lioui

Cinnamomum lioui, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum liui, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong cinnamon scent when crushed.

The bark is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cinnamon.
