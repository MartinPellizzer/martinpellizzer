Chesneya karatavica

Chesneya karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica chesneya, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing adaptations to its harsh environment. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for arid ecosystems and its potential for genetic research.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment.
