Croton flavoglandulosus

Croton flavoglandulosus, commonly known as the yellow gland croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish glandular structures on its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
