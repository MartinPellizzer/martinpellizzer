Coccoloba petenensis

Coccoloba petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a small, shrub-like plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fleshy berries. These berries are typically a vibrant red or purple color, which attracts various birds and insects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
