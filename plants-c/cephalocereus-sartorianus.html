Cephalocereus sartorianus

Cephalocereus sartorianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance. These flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
