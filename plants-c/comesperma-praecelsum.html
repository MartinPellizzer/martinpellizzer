Comesperma praecelsum

Comesperma praecelsum, commonly known as the magnificent comesperma, is a rare and striking shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
