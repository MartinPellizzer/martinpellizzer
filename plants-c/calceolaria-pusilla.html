Calceolaria pusilla

Calceolaria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf slipper orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
