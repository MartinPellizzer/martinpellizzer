Cardamine chilensis

Cardamine chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces, adapting to aquatic environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape typical of the family.

Cardamine chilensis is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
