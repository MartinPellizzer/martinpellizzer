Clematis tangutica var. pubescens

Clematis tangutica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Tibetan Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, pubescent foliage, which is a key feature of its varietal name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
