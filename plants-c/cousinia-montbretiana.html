Cousinia montbretiana

Cousinia montbretiana, commonly known as the mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tibet. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and produces yellow to orange flower heads in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky, and alpine environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Cousinia montbretiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
