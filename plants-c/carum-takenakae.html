Carum takenakae

Carum takenakae, commonly known as Persian cumin.

It is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are aromatic and used in traditional medicine. The seeds are often harvested for their culinary and medicinal properties.
