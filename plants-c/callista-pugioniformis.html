Callista pugioniformis

Callista pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens throughout temperate regions.
