Convolvulus tschimganicus

Convolvulus tschimganicus, commonly known as the Tschimgan convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with specific ecological requirements.
