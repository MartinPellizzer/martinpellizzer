Crataegus punctata f. intermedia

Crataegus punctata f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn species.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange berries and thorned branches, which are typical features of the Crataegus genus. The plant is native to parts of eastern North America and thrives in woodland environments. Its leaves are usually ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ecological benefits to wildlife.
