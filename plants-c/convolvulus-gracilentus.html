Convolvulus gracilentus

Convolvulus gracilentus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Native to southern Africa, it is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
