Cyathea alutacea

Cyathea alutacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense network, aiding in water absorption and soil stabilization. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea alutacea is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments due to its preference for moist, well-drained soils.
