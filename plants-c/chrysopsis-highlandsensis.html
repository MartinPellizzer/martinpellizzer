Chrysopsis highlandsensis

Chrysopsis highlandsensis, commonly known as the Highlands goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
