Carex acuta var. nigra

Carex acuta var. nigra, commonly known as black-tipped spike sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features dark green, narrow leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in wetland habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its darkly pigmented tips on the leaves, giving it its common name.
