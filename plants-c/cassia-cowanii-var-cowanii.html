Cassia cowanii var. cowanii

Cassia cowanii var. cowanii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
