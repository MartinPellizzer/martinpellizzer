Campanula perneglecta

Campanula perneglecta, commonly known as the neglected bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Campanula perneglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
