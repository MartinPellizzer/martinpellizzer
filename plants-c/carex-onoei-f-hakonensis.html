Carex onoei f. hakonensis

Carex onoei f. hakonensis, commonly known as Hakon's sedge, is a variant of the Carex onoei species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This subspecies is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
