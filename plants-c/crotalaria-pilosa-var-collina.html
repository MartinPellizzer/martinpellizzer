Crotalaria pilosa var. collina

Crotalaria pilosa var. collina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This species is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
