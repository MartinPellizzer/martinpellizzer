Crassula bullulata

Crassula bullulata, commonly known as the Woolly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula bullulata is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
