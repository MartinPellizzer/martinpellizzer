Centaurea friderici subsp. jabukensis

Centaurea friderici subsp.

jabukensis, commonly known as the Jabukenskaya Centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, often with a reddish tinge.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
