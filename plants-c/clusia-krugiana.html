Clusia krugiana

Clusia krugiana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. These flowers are followed by orange to red berries that contain seeds.

Clusia krugiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
