Clusia decussata

Clusia decussata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia decussata is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and poor soil conditions.
