Clinopodium macranthum

Clinopodium macranthum, commonly known as large-flowered wood betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and produces showy, two-lipped blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Clinopodium macranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
