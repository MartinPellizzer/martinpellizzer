Corylopsis glabrescens

Corylopsis glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth corylopsis, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching stems covered in small, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Corylopsis glabrescens is well-suited for woodland gardens and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
