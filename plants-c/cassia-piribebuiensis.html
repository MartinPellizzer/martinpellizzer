Cassia piribebuiensis

Cassia piribebuiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

Cassia piribebuiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
