Calyptranthes vexata

Calyptranthes vexata, commonly known as the velvetleaf calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Calyptranthes vexata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
