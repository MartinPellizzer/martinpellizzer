Calopetalon ringens

Calopetalon ringens, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. Calopetalon ringens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.
