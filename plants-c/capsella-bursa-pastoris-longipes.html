Capsella bursa-pastoris longipes

Capsella bursa-pastoris longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in temperate regions across the globe. It has basal leaves that are lobed and often hairy, with slender stems that can reach up to several feet in height.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, contributing to its role as a weed in agricultural settings.
