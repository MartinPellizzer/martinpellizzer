Clematis apiifolia var. argentilucida

Clematis apiifolia var. argentilucida, commonly known as the silverleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and the delicate blooms that appear along its climbing stems.
