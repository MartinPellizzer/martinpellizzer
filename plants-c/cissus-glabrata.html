Cissus glabrata

Cissus glabrata, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a trailing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus glabrata is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
