Crataegus rustica

Crataegus rustica, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus rustica is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along streambanks, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
