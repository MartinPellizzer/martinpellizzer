Crinitina tatarica

Crinitina tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white throat. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Crinitina tatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
