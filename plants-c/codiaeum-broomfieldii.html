Codiaeum broomfieldii

Codiaeum broomfieldii, commonly known as the Broomfield's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Codiaeum broomfieldii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.
