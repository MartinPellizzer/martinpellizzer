Candollea diuroides

Candollea diuroides, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
