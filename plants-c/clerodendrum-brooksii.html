Clerodendrum brooksii

Clerodendrum brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks' glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive tubular shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Clerodendrum brooksii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
