Clematis × pulcherrima

Clematis × pulcherrima, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Clematis × pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
