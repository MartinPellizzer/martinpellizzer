Carex giraldiana

Carex giraldiana, commonly known as the Giraldi's sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
