Cladium vaginale

Cladium vaginale, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often wrapped around the stems, forming a sheathing structure. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and swamps. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the muddy substrate.

Cladium vaginale plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
