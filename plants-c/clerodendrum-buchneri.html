Clerodendrum buchneri

Clerodendrum buchneri, commonly known as the Burchner's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
