Calamagrostis arundinacea var. varia

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. varia, commonly known as variable reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
