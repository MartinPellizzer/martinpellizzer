Carex middendorffii

Carex middendorffii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex middendorffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
