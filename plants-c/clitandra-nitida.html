Clitandra nitida

Clitandra nitida, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Clitandra nitida is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
