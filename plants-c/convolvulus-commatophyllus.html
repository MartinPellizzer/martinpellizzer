Convolvulus commatophyllus

Convolvulus commatophyllus, commonly known as the hairy leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Convolvulus commatophyllus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
