Chenopodium atripliciforme

Chenopodium atripliciforme, commonly known as smooth lambsquarters, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often concealed by bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium atripliciforme is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
