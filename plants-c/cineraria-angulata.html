Cineraria angulata

Cineraria angulata, commonly known as the angular cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive angular shape, giving it its common name.

Cineraria angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
