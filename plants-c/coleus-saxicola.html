Coleus saxicola

Coleus saxicola, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to purple. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
