Colubrina ruffa var. antillana

Colubrina ruffa var. antillana, commonly known as the Antillean beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy purple to violet berries that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
