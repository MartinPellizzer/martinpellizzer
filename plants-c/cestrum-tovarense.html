Cestrum tovarense

Cestrum tovarense, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum tovarense is also known for its fragrant flowers, which can emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.
