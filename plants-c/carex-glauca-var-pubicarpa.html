Carex glauca var. pubicarpa

Carex glauca var. pubicarpa, commonly known as the blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of sedges. Its leaves are usually narrow and stiff, with a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
