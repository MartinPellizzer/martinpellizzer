Campanula alliariifolia var. cordata

Campanula alliariifolia var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright stems bearing bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and hardy plants.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
