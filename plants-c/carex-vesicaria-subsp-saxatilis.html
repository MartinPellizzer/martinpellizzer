Carex vesicaria subsp. saxatilis

Carex vesicaria subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color. It is often found in mountainous areas and is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor and well-drained substrates.
