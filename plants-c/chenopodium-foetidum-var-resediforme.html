Chenopodium foetidum var. resediforme

Chenopodium foetidum var. resediforme, commonly known as skunkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is typically found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is an annual herb that thrives in moist to wet environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
