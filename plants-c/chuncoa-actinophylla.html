Chuncoa actinophylla

Chuncoa actinophylla, commonly known as the Chilean star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a short, thick stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Chuncoa actinophylla is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
