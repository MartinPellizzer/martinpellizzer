Carex fuscifructus

Carex fuscifructus, commonly known as the dark-fruited sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikes.

Carex fuscifructus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
