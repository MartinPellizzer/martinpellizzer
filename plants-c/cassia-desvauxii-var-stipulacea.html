Cassia desvauxii var. stipulacea

Cassia desvauxii var. stipulacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and small, yellowish inflorescences. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
