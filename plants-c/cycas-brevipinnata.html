Cycas brevipinnata

Cycas brevipinnata, commonly known as the short-pinnate cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the island of New Guinea and is characterized by its compact, tree-like structure with a thick, unbranched trunk. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation concern.
