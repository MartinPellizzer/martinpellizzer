Cryptandra globulosa

Cryptandra globulosa, commonly known as the globular cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spherical or globular-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and has small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water. It produces small white or pink flowers, which attract nectar-feeding insects.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
