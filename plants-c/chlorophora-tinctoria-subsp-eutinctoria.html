Chlorophora tinctoria subsp. eutinctoria

Chlorophora tinctoria subsp.

eutinctoria, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a species native to western Africa. It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters.

The plant has a straight trunk with a smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.
