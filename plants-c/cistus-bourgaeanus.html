Cistus bourgaeanus

Cistus bourgaeanus, commonly known as the golden rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its aromatic leaves and nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cistus bourgaeanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
