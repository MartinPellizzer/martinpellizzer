Cipoia

Cipoia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Cipoia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies.
