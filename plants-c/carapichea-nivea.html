Carapichea nivea

Carapichea nivea, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
