Cyperus kaessneri

Cyperus kaessneri, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, compact structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spikelet cluster, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
