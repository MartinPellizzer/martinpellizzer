Calamaria coromandelina

Calamaria coromandelina, commonly known as the Coromandel calamary, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Calamaria coromandelina prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
