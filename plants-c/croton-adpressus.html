Croton adpressus

Croton adpressus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Croton adpressus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
