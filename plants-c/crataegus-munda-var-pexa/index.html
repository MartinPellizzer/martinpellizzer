Crataegus Munda Var. Pexa

Crataegus munda var. pexa is a lesser-known species of hawthorn native to specific regions of southern Europe. This variant is distinguished by its unique floral and fruiting characteristics compared to its parent species. It thrives in rocky and dry environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this botanical gem for future generations.
