Carex vestita var. kennedyi

Carex vestita var. kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer, often with a pale green or brownish hue. Native to specific regions of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife.
