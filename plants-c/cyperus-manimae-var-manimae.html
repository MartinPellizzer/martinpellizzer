Cyperus manimae var. manimae

Cyperus manimae var. manimae, commonly known as the Manimae flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedge species characterized by their triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
