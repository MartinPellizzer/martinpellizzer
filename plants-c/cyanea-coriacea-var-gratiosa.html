Cyanea coriacea var. gratiosa

Cyanea coriacea var. gratiosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. It has cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in the region's flora.
