Canna recurvata

Canna recurvata, commonly known as the recurved cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Canna recurvata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for water gardens and tropical landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in diverse habitats.
