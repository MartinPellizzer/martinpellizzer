Corydalis cornupetala

Corydalis cornupetala, commonly known as the Chinese larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Corydalis cornupetala is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
