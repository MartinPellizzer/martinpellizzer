Caragana spinosissima

Caragana spinosissima, commonly known as the thorny shrub or goatweed, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its spiny branches and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by legume pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive drought conditions.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat in dry landscapes.
