Campanula ovata

Campanula ovata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

Campanula ovata is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
