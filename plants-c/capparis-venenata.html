Capparis venenata

Capparis venenata, commonly known as the poison gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with thorny stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a strong, sour taste. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Capparis venenata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
