Cuphea fulgens

Cuphea fulgens, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers. The plant produces vibrant, showy bracts that surround the actual flowers, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea fulgens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
