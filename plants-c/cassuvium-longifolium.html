Cassuvium longifolium

Cassuvium longifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
