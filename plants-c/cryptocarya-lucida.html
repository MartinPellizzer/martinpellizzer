Cryptocarya lucida

Cryptocarya lucida, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical, black berries that are edible for birds.

Cryptocarya lucida is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
