Crinum filifolium

Crinum filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaf crinum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is known for its strong, pleasant fragrance.

Crinum filifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
