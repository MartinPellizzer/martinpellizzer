Cirsium matritense

Cirsium matritense, commonly known as the Madrid thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, stiff hairs.

Cirsium matritense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
