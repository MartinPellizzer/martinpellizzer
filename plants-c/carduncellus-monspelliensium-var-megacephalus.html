Carduncellus monspelliensium var. megacephalus

Carduncellus monspelliensium var. megacephalus, commonly known as the giant cardoon, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, and lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and historical use in both culinary and medicinal applications make it a notable botanical specimen.
