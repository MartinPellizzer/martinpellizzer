Cucumis melo var. cossonianus

Cucumis melo var. cossonianus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a vine, producing yellow flowers and developing fruit that ripens to a golden or orange hue. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
