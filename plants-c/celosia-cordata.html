Celosia cordata

Celosia cordata, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, modified leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

Celosia cordata is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for warm climates.
