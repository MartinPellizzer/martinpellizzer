Cussonia chartacea

Cussonia chartacea, commonly known as the African arrowroot or paper plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Cussonia chartacea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can grow up to several meters in height.
