Croton meridionalis var. pseudolepidotus

Croton meridionalis var. pseudolepidotus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive yellow-green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
