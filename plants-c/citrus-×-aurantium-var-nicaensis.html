Citrus × aurantium var. nicaensis

Citrus × aurantium var. nicaensis, commonly known as the Nicaensis orange, is a citrus hybrid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic peel. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are white and fragrant, blooming in clusters.

The fruit is valued for its high essential oil content and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
