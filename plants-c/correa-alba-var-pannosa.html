Correa alba var. pannosa

Correa alba var. pannosa, commonly known as the silverleaf correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its pale green, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
