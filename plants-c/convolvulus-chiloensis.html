Convolvulus chiloensis

Convolvulus chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
