Cheiridopsis brevipes

Cheiridopsis brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental appeal contribute to its status as a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
