Carduus nutans var. chrysacanthoides

Carduus nutans var. chrysacanthoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting or golden groundsel, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange composite flower heads and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in a compact, globular shape. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
