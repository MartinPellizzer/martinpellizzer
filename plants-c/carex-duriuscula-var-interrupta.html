Carex duriuscula var. interrupta

Carex duriuscula var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
