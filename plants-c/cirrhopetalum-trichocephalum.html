Cirrhopetalum trichocephalum

Cirrhopetalum trichocephalum, commonly known as the hairy-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
