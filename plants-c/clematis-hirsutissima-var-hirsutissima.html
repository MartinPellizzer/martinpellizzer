Clematis hirsutissima var. hirsutissima

Clematis hirsutissima var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
