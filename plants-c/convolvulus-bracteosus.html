Convolvulus bracteosus

Convolvulus bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces slender, coiling stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Convolvulus bracteosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
