Crotalaria chiayiana

Crotalaria chiayiana, commonly known as the Chiayi crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, segmented pod, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
