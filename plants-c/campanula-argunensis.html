Campanula argunensis

Campanula argunensis, commonly known as the Argun bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features a sturdy stem that can grow up to 60 cm tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland borders.

Campanula argunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
