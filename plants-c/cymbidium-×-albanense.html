Cymbidium × albanense

Cymbidium × albanense, commonly known as the Albanian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It prefers cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
