Coleus scutellarioides var. laxus

Coleus scutellarioides var. laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often variegated in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorled inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
