Chlorophytum subpetiolatum var. pilosifolium

Chlorophytum subpetiolatum var. pilosifolium, commonly known as the curly snake plant, is a variant of the snake plant family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that are typically green with a white or silver stripe running along the central vein. The leaves are often curled or twisted, giving the plant its common name. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant native to southern Africa.

This variety is well adapted to indoor growing conditions and thrives in bright, indirect light.
