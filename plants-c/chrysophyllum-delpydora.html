Chrysophyllum delpydora

Chrysophyllum delpydora, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by yellow or orange berries containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
