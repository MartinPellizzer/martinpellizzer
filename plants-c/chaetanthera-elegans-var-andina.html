Chaetanthera elegans var. andina

Chaetanthera elegans var. andina, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
