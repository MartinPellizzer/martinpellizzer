Clintonia parviflora var. plicata

Clintonia parviflora var. plicata, commonly known as the pleated Clintonia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often pleated, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
