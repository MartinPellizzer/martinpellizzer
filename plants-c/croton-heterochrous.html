Croton heterochrous

Croton heterochrous, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in bright, indirect light. Its foliage is not only ornamental but also exhibits a unique form of chlorophyll variation.

Croton heterochrous is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
