Campanula filicaulis var. pseudoantiatlantica

Campanula filicaulis var. pseudoantiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand coastal conditions. This variety thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators in coastal gardens.
