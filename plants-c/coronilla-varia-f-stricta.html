Coronilla varia f. stricta

Coronilla varia f. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact form and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or as a cover crop.
