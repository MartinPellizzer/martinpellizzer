Chelone glabra var. chlorantha

Chelone glabra var. chlorantha, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
