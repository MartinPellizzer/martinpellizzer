Chenopodium × trigonocarpum

Chenopodium × trigonocarpum, commonly known as goosefoot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a goose's foot, often with a rough or hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are typically tiny and wind-dispersed. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Chenopodium × trigonocarpum is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
