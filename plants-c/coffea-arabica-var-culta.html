Coffea arabica var. culta

Coffea arabica var. culta, commonly known as the typical or standard variety of Arabica coffee, is a small evergreen shrub native to Ethiopia.

It produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing. The plant thrives in tropical highland regions with cool temperatures and high altitudes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The flowers are white and fragrant, typically blooming in spring, followed by the fruiting season.
