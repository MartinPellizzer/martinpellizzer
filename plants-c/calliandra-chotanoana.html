Calliandra chotanoana

Calliandra chotanoana, commonly known as the Mexican wisteria, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
