Cucumis melo var. sapidissimus

Cucumis melo var. sapidissimus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its large, sweet, and aromatic fruits.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant produces elongated, ribbed fruits with a pale green rind and a juicy, honey-sweet flesh. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches full size and exhibits a change in color, indicating maturity.
