Cardamine jamesonii

Cardamine jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky crevices. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a peppery aroma, similar to that of other watercress species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
