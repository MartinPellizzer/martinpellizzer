Campanula petrophila var. longiflora

Campanula petrophila var. longiflora, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are hairy and robust.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
