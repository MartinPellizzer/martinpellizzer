Calanthe africana

Calanthe africana, commonly known as the African calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Calanthe africana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
