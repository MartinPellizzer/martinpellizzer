Commelina nudiflora f. agraria

Commelina nudiflora f. agraria, commonly known as the agrarian commelina, is a variant of the common commelina, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.

The agrarian form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
