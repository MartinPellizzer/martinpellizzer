Cissus cucumerifolia

Cissus cucumerifolia, commonly known as the cucumber-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that resemble cucumber leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, cucumber-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cissus cucumerifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
