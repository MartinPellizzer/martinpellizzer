Cistanche senegalensis

Cistanche senegalensis, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on species of the genus Prosopis. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a fibrous, branching structure. It is native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East, where it thrives in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
