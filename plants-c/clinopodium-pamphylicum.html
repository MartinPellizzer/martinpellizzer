Clinopodium pamphylicum

Clinopodium pamphylicum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their aromatic properties.

Clinopodium pamphylicum is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
