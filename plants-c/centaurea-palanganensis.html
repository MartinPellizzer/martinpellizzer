Centaurea palanganensis

Centaurea palanganensis, commonly known as the Palangan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a rough texture, and it produces bright yellow to orange flowers.

Centaurea palanganensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
