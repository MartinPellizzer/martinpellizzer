Calceolaria ericoides subsp. ericoides

Calceolaria ericoides subsp.

ericoides, commonly known as the Ericoid Calceolaria, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small pouches, a feature typical of the Calceolaria genus.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
