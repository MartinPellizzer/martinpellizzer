Centaurea nevadensis

Centaurea nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea nevadensis is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
