Cirsium ligulare subsp. montenegrinum

Cirsium ligulare subsp.

montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and distinctive purple to pink flower heads.

The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, it thrives in rocky and dry habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience in harsh environments.
