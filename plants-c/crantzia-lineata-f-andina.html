Crantzia lineata f. andina

Crantzia lineata f. andina, commonly known as the Andean crantzia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to the high-altitude environment.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
