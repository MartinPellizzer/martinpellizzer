Chilochium

Chilochium, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid environments. The leaves are usually broad and strap-like, providing a lush green backdrop to the colorful blooms.

Chilochium species are prized for their ornamental value and are often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
