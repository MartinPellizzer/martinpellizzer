Collinsia rattanii

Collinsia rattanii, commonly known as the rattanii flowering plant, is a species native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is well adapted to dry conditions and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Collinsia rattanii is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
