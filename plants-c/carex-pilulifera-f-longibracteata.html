Carex pilulifera f. longibracteata

Carex pilulifera f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a variant of the sedge species Carex pilulifera.

It is characterized by its elongated bracts that resemble small, elongated leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
