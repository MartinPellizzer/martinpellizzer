Clinopodium yakusimense

Clinopodium yakusimense, commonly known as Yakusim mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue and bloom in late summer.

Clinopodium yakusimense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local herbal remedies.
