Crotalaria rathjensiana

Crotalaria rathjensiana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is an annual herb with a hairy stem and compound leaves, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
