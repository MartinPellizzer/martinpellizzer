Chrysopogon stipoides

Chrysopogon stipoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands, open woodlands, and along riverbanks, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Chrysopogon stipoides is also known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
