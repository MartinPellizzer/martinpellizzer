Commelina erecta var. greenei

Commelina erecta var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
