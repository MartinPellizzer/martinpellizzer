Capsella bursa-pastoris viridis

Capsella bursa-pastoris viridis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along pathways. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

The species is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in salads and traditional herbal remedies.
