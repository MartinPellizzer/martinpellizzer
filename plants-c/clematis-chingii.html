Clematis chingii

Clematis chingii, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
