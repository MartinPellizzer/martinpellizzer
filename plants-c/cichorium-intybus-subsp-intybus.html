Cichorium intybus subsp. intybus

Cichorium intybus subsp.

intybus, commonly known as French chicory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It features a rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often curly or fringed.

The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in salads or as a coffee substitute.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
