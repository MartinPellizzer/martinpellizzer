Costus quartus

Costus quartus, commonly known as the zigzag ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its slender, rhizomatous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a zigzag pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically in shades of yellow or orange.

Costus quartus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild aromatic properties.
