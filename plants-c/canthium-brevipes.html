Canthium brevipes

Canthium brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and often milky sap. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium brevipes is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
