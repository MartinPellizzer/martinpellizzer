Chamaedorea galeottiana

Chamaedorea galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like, compound leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
