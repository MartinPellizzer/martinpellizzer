Cristaria cyanea

Cristaria cyanea, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, although it is often mistaken for a member of the Cyperaceae family due to its similar appearance.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Cristaria cyanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
