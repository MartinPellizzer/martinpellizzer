Carex cephalantha

Carex cephalantha, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex cephalantha plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
