Calamagrostis macrolepis var. paczoskii

Calamagrostis macrolepis var. paczoskii, commonly known as the Pacific silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a temperate climate.
