Cousinia arachnoidea

Cousinia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-like flowering plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Cousinia arachnoidea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
