Carex arakamensis

Carex arakamensis, commonly known as the Arakamensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex arakamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
