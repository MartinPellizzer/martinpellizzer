Correa reflexa var. coriacea

Correa reflexa var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, rough texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
