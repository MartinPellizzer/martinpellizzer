Calea cabrerae

Calea cabrerae, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale blue flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral behavior. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calea cabrerae is often cultivated for its beauty and nocturnal blooming habit, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
