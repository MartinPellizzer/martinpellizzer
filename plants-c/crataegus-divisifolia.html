Crataegus divisifolia

Crataegus divisifolia, commonly known as the split-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, maple-like leaves that are often divided into three or more segments. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
