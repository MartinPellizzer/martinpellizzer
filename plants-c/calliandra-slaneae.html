Calliandra slaneae

Calliandra slaneae, commonly known as the Slane's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
