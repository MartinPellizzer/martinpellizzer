Campanula velenowskyi

Campanula velenowskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Campanula velenowskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
