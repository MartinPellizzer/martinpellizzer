Cymbidium pictum

Cymbidium pictum, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful floral bracts that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium pictum is prized in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
