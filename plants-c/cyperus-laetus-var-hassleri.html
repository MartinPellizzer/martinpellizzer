Cyperus laetus var. hassleri

Cyperus laetus var. hassleri, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Cyperus laetus var. hassleri is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
