Chrysocoryne incrassata

Chrysocoryne incrassata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are tubular and often adorned with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
