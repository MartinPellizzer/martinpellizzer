Cupressus lawsoniana gracilis-pendula

Cupressus lawsoniana gracilis-pendula, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a variety of the Lawson cypress that exhibits a weeping or pendulous growth habit.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar form and long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery-white underneath, giving it a striking visual appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its unique shape and year-round aesthetic appeal.
