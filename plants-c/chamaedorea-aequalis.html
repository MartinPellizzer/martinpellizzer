Chamaedorea aequalis

Chamaedorea aequalis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the Mexican feather plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular houseplant in tropical and subtropical regions.
