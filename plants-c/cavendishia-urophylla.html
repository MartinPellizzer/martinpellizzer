Cavendishia urophylla

Cavendishia urophylla, commonly known as the velvet leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
