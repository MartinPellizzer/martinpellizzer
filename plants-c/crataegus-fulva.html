Crataegus fulva

Crataegus fulva, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus fulva is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
