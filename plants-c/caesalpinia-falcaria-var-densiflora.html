Caesalpinia falcaria var. densiflora

Caesalpinia falcaria var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
