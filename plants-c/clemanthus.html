Clemanthus

Clemanthus, commonly known as the bluebell or bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. These plants are typically herbaceous, with opposite leaves and often grow in moist, shaded environments. Clemanthus species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
