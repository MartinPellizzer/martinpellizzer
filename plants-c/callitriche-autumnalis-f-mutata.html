Callitriche autumnalis f. mutata

Callitriche autumnalis f. mutata, commonly known as the autumn water-starwort, is a variant of the water-starwort species.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, producing small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn.

This plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
