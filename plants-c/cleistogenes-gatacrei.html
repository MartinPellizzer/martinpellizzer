Cleistogenes gatacrei

Cleistogenes gatacrei, commonly known as the Gatacrei bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and often hidden within the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
