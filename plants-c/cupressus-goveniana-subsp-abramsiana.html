Cupressus goveniana subsp. abramsiana

Cupressus goveniana subsp.

abramsiana, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen conifer with a narrow, columnar form and scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Its dense foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
