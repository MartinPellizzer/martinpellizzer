Cucumis melo var. macrocastanus

Cucumis melo var. macrocastanus, commonly known as the Afghan melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon plant characterized by its large, elongated fruit.

The fruit typically has a pale green rind with a rough texture and a sweet, aromatic flesh. It is native to Central Asia and has been historically grown for its edible qualities. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing botanical features with other cucurbits like cucumbers and watermelons.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
