Chaptalia pringlei

Chaptalia pringlei, commonly known as the Andean chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The flowers, which are usually yellow or white, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
