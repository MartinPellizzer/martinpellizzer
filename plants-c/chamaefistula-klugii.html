Chamaefistula klugii

Chamaefistula klugii, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Iranian plateau.

It is characterized by its dense, gnarled branches and thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by woody, cylindrical fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
