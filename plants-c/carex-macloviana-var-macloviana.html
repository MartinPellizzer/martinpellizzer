Carex macloviana var. macloviana

Carex macloviana var. macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in the summer months.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.
