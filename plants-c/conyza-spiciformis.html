Conyza spiciformis

Conyza spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Conyza spiciformis is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
