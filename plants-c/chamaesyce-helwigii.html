Chamaesyce helwigii

Chamaesyce helwigii, commonly known as the helwig's sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert landscaping.
