Conyza thyrsoidea

Conyza thyrsoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can thrive in a variety of climates. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Conyza thyrsoidea is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in ecological succession.
