Copiapoa longistaminea subsp. imperialis

Copiapoa longistaminea subsp.

imperialis, commonly known as the Imperial Copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense, white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water due to its arid habitat.

This subspecies is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
