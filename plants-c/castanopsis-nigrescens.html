Castanopsis nigrescens

Castanopsis nigrescens, commonly known as the black-barked tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a prickly husk. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests with high humidity.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
