Centaurea chrysolepis f. subcitrina

Centaurea chrysolepis f. subcitrina, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a variant of the golden centaury species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features spiny, bract-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical interest lies in its distinct coloration and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
