Cymbalaria microcalyx subsp. microcalyx

Cymbalaria microcalyx subsp.

microcalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and hairy, with a slightly succulent texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
