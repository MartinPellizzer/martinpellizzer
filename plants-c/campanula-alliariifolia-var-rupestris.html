Campanula alliariifolia var. rupestris

Campanula alliariifolia var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often resembling those of the garlic plant, giving it its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
