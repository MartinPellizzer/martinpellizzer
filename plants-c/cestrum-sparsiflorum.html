Cestrum sparsiflorum

Cestrum sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, yellowish seeds.
