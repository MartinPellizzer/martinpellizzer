Cerasus × eminens

Cerasus × eminens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–15 feet and has simple, ovate leaves that turn green in summer and yellow in autumn. Its flowers are produced in clusters and are an important part of its ornamental value.

Cerasus × eminens is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
