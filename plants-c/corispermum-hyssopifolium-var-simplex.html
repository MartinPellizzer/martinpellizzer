Corispermum hyssopifolium var. simplex

Corispermum hyssopifolium var. simplex, commonly known as the simple leafed glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats where it thrives in high-sodium soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is notable for its ability to accumulate salt in its tissues, making it an important component of salt-tolerant vegetation.
