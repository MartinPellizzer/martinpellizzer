Centrocarpha fulgida

Centrocarpha fulgida, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing a striking display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centrocarpha fulgida is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
