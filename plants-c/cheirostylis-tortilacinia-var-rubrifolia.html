Cheirostylis tortilacinia var. rubrifolia

Cheirostylis tortilacinia var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red-pink inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often featuring a unique coloration pattern with contrasting hues.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
