Cephalophora plantaginea var. spathulata

Cephalophora plantaginea var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed cephalophora, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
