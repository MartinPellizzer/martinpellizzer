Cordia sangrinaria

Cordia sangrinaria, commonly known as the blood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating skin conditions and as an antiseptic.

The plant's unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitats.
