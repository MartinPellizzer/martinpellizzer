Coleus gibbsiae

Coleus gibbsiae, commonly known as Gibbs' coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus gibbsiae is relatively easy to grow and is popular in tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
