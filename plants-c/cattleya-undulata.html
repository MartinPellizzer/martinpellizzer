Cattleya undulata

Cattleya undulata, commonly known as the wavy cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya undulata is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
