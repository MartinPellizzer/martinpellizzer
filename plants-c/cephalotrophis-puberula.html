Cephalotrophis puberula

Cephalotrophis puberula, commonly known as the hairy-headed water frog, is a species of aquatic frog found in the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy growth on the head and back, which provides camouflage in its moist environment. The plant-like appearance of its skin and the presence of small, leaf-like structures on its body are adaptations to its amphibious lifestyle. It thrives in freshwater habitats with dense vegetation, using its webbed toes for efficient movement through water.

The species plays a vital role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for larger predators.
