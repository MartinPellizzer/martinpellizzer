Chenopodium esculentum

Chenopodium esculentum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Lambsquarters is a common weed found in agricultural fields and disturbed soils.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
