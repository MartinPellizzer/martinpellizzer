Cereus brachiatus

Cereus brachiatus, commonly known as the hooked cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of long, hooked spines. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically fragrant. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
