Cnicus hilgendorfi

Cnicus hilgendorfi, commonly known as the Hilgendorf's sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
