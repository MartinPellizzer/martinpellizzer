Campanula rotundifolia f. cleistocodona

Campanula rotundifolia f. cleistocodona, commonly known as the rounded-leaf bellflower, is a botanical variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive rounded, heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
