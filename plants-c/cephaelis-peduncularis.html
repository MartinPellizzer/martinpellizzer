Cephaelis peduncularis

Cephaelis peduncularis, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its relatively short stature.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
