Claytonia virginica var. latifolia

Claytonia virginica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf springbeauty, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface, and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by showy, petal-like bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
