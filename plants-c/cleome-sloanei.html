Cleome sloanei

Cleome sloanei, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces numerous blooms throughout the growing season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Cleome sloanei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to full sun and well-drained soil.
