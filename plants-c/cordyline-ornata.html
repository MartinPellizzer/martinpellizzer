Cordyline ornata

Cordyline ornata, commonly known as the orange tree fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Cordyline ornata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
