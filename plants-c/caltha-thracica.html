Caltha thracica

Caltha thracica, commonly known as the Thrace buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmately divided leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
