Coccoloba cardiophylla

Coccoloba cardiophylla, commonly known as the heartleaf coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by ornamental, coral-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
