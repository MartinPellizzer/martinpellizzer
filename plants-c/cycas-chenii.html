Cycas chenii

Cycas chenii, commonly known as the Chen's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province. This plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. Cycas chenii is a dioecious species, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and it produces cones rather than flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
