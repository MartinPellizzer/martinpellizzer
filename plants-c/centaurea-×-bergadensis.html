Centaurea × bergadensis

Centaurea × bergadensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Centaurea × bergadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
