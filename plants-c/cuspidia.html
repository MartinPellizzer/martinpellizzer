Cuspidia

Cuspidia, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, often blooming in winter.

Cuspidia is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
