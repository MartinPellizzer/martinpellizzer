Camissonia claviformis var. aurantiaca

Camissonia claviformis var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden keyleaf, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky soils and sunny environments, often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a notable species within its native range.
