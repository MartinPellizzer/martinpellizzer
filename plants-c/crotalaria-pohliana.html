Crotalaria pohliana

Crotalaria pohliana, commonly known as the Pohli's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with two prominent lobes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria pohliana is also noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
