Calamintha graveolens subsp. purpurascens

Calamintha graveolens subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as purple honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intensely colored flowers and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the Calamintha genus.
