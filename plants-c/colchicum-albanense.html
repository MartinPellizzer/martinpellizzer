Colchicum albanense

Colchicum albanense, commonly known as the Albanian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces purple or reddish-brown flowers that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and studied for its potential pharmaceutical applications.
