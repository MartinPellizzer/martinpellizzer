Centaurea sonchifolia subsp. maritima

Centaurea sonchifolia subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant characterized by its spiky, blue to purple composite flower heads. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a central stem that bears the showy inflorescence. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in saline conditions.

The subspecies maritima is distinguished by its more compact growth and salt tolerance compared to other members of the species.
