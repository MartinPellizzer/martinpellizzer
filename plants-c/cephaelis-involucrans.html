Cephaelis involucrans

Cephaelis involucrans, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces bright orange to red flowers during the monsoon season. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, adding to its ornamental value.

Cephaelis involucrans is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
