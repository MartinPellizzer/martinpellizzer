Chloothamnus chilianthus

Chloothamnus chilianthus, commonly known as the Chilean goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chloothamnus chilianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
