Crepis karakuschensis

Crepis karakuschensis, commonly known as the Karakushan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
