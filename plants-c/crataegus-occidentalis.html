Crataegus occidentalis

Crataegus occidentalis, commonly known as the Western hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
