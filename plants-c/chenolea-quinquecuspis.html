Chenolea quinquecuspis

Chenolea quinquecuspis, commonly known as the five-awned Chenolea, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristle-like awns, giving it its common name. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Chenolea quinquecuspis is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for native insects.
