Cousinia perspolitana

Cousinia perspolitana, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Cousinia perspolitana is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.
