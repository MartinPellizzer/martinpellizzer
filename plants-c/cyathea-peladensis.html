Cyathea peladensis

Cyathea peladensis, commonly known as the Peladens tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking visual presence in its natural habitat.

Cyathea peladensis plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
