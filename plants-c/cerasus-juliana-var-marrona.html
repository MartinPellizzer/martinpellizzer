Cerasus juliana var. marrona

Cerasus juliana var. marrona, commonly known as the purple leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which transitions to red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf color and seasonal interest.

The species is relatively hardy and suitable for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
