Catasetum fergusonii

Catasetum fergusonii, commonly known as the Ferguson's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several showy flowers.

Catasetum fergusonii is noted for its unique pollination mechanism, involving specialized structures that facilitate the transfer of pollen to specific pollinators.
