Carex panicea

Carex panicea, commonly known as panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
