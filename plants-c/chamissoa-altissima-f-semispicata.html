Chamissoa altissima f. semispicata

Chamissoa altissima f. semispicata, commonly known as the silver chamaedorea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a silvery-blue hue on the underside of the leaves, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by blue-black berries, which are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
