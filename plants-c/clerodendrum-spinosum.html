Clerodendrum spinosum

Clerodendrum spinosum, commonly known as the spiked clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its spiny stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by ornamental berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
