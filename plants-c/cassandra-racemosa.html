Cassandra racemosa

Cassandra racemosa, commonly known as the clustered cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Cassandra racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
