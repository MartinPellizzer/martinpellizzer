Cordyline rubra f. violascens

Cordyline rubra f. violascens, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in summer, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and colorful foliage make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
