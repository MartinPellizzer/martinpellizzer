Cyclosorus subcaudipinnus

Cyclosorus subcaudipinnus, commonly known as the narrow-tailed water clover, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as streams and marshes. The plant has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that form a dense, floating mat on the water surface. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
