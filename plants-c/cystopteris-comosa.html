It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the rhizome often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Cystopteris comosa thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or soil in forested areas.
