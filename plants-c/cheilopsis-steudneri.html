Cheilopsis steudneri

Cheilopsis steudneri, commonly known as the Steudner's beard-tongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is a valuable species for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
