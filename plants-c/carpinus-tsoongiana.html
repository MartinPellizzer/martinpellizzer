Carpinus tsoongiana

Carpinus tsoongiana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
