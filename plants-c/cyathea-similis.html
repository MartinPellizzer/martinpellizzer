Cyathea similis

Cyathea similis, commonly known as the common tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea similis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique structure and growth habit make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
