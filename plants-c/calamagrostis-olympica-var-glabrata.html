Calamagrostis olympica var. glabrata

Calamagrostis olympica var. glabrata, commonly known as the Olympic grass, is a variant of the tall oatgrass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and upright growth habit, making it a striking ornamental grass. The plant produces feathery, golden-brown plumes in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for landscaping and native plant gardens.
