Cytisus sylvestris

Cytisus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild broom, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Cytisus sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
