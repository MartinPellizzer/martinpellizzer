Crataegus iracunda var. diffusa

Crataegus iracunda var. diffusa, commonly known as the spreading hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and stream banks. The plant features thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red-pink berry that ripens in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
