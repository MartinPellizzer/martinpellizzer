Camelina microcarpa

Camelina microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf camelina, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Camelina microcarpa is also noted for its potential as a source of oilseed for agricultural and industrial uses.
