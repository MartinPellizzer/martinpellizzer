Capparis emarginata

Capparis emarginata, commonly known as the coastal caper, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, waxy, purple or greenish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Capparis emarginata thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.
