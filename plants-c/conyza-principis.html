Conyza principis

Conyza principis, commonly known as the common ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Conyza principis is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause significant respiratory issues in humans.
