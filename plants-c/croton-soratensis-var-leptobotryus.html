Croton soratensis var. leptobotryus

Croton soratensis var. leptobotryus, commonly known as the slender-fruit croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for horticultural and ecological studies.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
