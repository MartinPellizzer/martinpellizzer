Corynopuntia marenae

Corynopuntia marenae, commonly known as the Maren's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny segments that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Corynopuntia marenae plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
