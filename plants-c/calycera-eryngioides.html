Calycera eryngioides

Calycera eryngioides, commonly known as the spiked calycera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiky, cylindrical flower heads that resemble those of thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Calycera eryngioides is native to southern Africa and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
