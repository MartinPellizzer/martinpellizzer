Cyperus flavescens subvar. capitatus

Cyperus flavescens subvar. capitatus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

The botanical features of this species make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
