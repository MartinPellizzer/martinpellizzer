Cissus allophylloides

Cissus allophylloides, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by green, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus allophylloides is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
