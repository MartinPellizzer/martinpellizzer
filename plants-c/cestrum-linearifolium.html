Cestrum linearifolium

Cestrum linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, often branched stems. The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, with a distinctive scent, while the flowers are tubular and fragrant, usually appearing in clusters. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
