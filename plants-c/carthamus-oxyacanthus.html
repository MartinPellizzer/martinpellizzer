Carthamus oxyacanthus

Carthamus oxyacanthus, commonly known as the Persian safflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bright red or orange seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable in various cultural contexts.
