Calamus eximius

Calamus eximius, commonly known as the noble canes or giant arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a tall, robust perennial plant that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, characterized by its stout, segmented rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fibrous, starchy fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties, often used in traditional practices.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important resource in its native ecosystems.
