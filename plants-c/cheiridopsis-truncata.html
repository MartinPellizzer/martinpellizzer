Cheiridopsis truncata

Cheiridopsis truncata, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its low water requirements and resilience to harsh conditions, it is well adapted to arid environments.
