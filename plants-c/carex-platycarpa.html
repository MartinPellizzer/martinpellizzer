Carex platycarpa

Carex platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a three-sided, dry capsule that contains multiple seeds.

Carex platycarpa is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
