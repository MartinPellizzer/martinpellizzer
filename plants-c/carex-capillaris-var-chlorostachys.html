Carex capillaris var. chlorostachys

Carex capillaris var. chlorostachys, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from blue-green to silver-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
