Colubrina americana

Colubrina americana, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Colubrina americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
