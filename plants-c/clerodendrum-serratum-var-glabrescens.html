Clerodendrum serratum var. glabrescens

Clerodendrum serratum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky clerodendrum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are usually smooth to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
