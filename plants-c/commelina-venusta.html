Commelina venusta

Commelina venusta, commonly known as the pretty bluebell, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and texture to their landscapes.
