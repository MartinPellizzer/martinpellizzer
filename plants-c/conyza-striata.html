Conyza striata

Conyza striata, commonly known as slenderleaf horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Conyza striata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
