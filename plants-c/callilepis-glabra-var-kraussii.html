Callilepis glabra var. kraussii

Callilepis glabra var. kraussii, commonly known as the Cape jasmine or white-flowered wild jasmine, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.
