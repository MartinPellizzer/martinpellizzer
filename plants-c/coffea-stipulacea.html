Coffea stipulacea

Coffea stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule coffee.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries containing seeds used for coffee production.

Due to its unique botanical features and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species within the Coffea genus.
