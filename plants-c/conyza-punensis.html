Conyza punensis

Conyza punensis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows from 30 to 100 centimeters tall, with hairy stems and lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Conyza punensis is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
