Cephaelis ferruginea

Cephaelis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty ceiba, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
