Crataegus premora

Crataegus premora, commonly known as the sand crataegus, is a species of hawthorn native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins.

Crataegus premora is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
