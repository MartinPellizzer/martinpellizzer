Cypripedium caudatum var. wallisii

Cypripedium caudatum var. wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to form clumps over time. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
