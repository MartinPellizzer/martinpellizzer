Callicarpa dentosa

Callicarpa dentosa, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting various birds and wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The berries are edible for humans but are often consumed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal.
