Crepidium atratum

Crepidium atratum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of white and green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountain slopes. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Crepidium atratum is a perennial species that blooms in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
