Centrosema pascuorum

Centrosema pascuorum, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Centrosema pascuorum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
