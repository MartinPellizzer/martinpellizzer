Clematis horripilata

Clematis horripilata, commonly known as the hairy clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis horripilata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
