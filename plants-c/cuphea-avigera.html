Cuphea avigera

Cuphea avigera, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, tubular red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cuphea avigera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
