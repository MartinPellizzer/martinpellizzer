Cyperus houghtonii

Cyperus houghtonii, commonly known as Houghton's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and riverbanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes.

Cyperus houghtonii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
