Carex indica var. laetebrunnea

Carex indica var. laetebrunnea, commonly known as the soft-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically soft and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
