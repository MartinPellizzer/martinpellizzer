Citrus × bigaradia var. canaliculata

Citrus × bigaradia var. canaliculata, commonly known as the canaliculated lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits.

The plant produces fruit with a thin, smooth peel and a high concentration of essential oils, giving it a strong citrus aroma. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge, typical of many citrus species. The canaliculated lemon is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and use in perfumery and culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
