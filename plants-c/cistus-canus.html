Cistus canus

Cistus canus, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas. It produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cistus canus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
