Casearia crassipes

Casearia crassipes, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture.

The berries, which are edible, are a key feature of the plant and attract various birds and small mammals.
