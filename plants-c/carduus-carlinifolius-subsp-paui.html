Carduus carlinifolius subsp. paui

Carduus carlinifolius subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is noted for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
