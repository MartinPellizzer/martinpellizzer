Cyperus brevifolius f. pumilus

Cyperus brevifolius f. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf flatsedge, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, characterized by their triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense clumps with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet or moist soils, making it a common species in wetlands and marshy areas.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
