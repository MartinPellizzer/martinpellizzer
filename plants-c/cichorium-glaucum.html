Cichorium glaucum

Cichorium glaucum, commonly known as blue chicory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Cichorium glaucum is also valued for its edible leaves, which can be used in salads or as a coffee substitute.
