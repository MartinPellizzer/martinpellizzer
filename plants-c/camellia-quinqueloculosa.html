Camellia quinqueloculosa

Camellia quinqueloculosa, commonly known as the five-chambered camellia, is a species native to China and is part of the Theaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Camellia quinqueloculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
