Convolvulus dorycnium var. turcomanicus

Convolvulus dorycnium var. turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
