Carex emmonsii var. australis

Carex emmonsii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
