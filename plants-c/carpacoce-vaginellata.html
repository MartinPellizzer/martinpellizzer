Carpacoce vaginellata

Carpacoce vaginellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-winged fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
