Carex albolutescens var. alata

Carex albolutescens var. alata, commonly known as the white-tipped sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical spikes of pale yellow-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system and spreads primarily through rhizomes, forming dense clumps.

Its foliage is often covered with a fine, white bloom, giving it its common name.
