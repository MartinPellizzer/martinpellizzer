Cyperus lucidus var. sanguineofuscus

Cyperus lucidus var. sanguineofuscus, commonly known as the red-bracted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically clustered at the base of the plant. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence features reddish-brown bracts that give it its common name.

Cyperus lucidus var. sanguineofuscus is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.
