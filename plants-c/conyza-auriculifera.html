Conyza auriculifera

Conyza auriculifera, commonly known as the auriculate conyza, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with hairy surfaces and distinctive auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its wind-pollinated flowers.
