Chilita sanluisensis

Chilita sanluisensis, commonly known as the San Luis mountain chile, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It produces small, round fruits that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, peppery flavor.
