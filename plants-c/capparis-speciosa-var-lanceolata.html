Capparis speciosa var. lanceolata

Capparis speciosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which are followed by small, fleshy, orange-red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
