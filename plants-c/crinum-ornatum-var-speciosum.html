Crinum ornatum var. speciosum

Crinum ornatum var. speciosum, commonly known as the showy crinum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The showy crinum is known for its strong fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
