Codonoprasum flexum

Codonoprasum flexum, commonly known as the flexible codonopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
