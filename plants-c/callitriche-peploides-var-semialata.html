Callitriche peploides var. semialata

Callitriche peploides var. semialata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, producing small, white or pale green blooms.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
