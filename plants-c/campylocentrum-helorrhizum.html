Campylocentrum helorrhizum

Campylocentrum helorrhizum, commonly known as the purple-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous root system, and its leaves are typically ovate with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Campylocentrum helorrhizum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
