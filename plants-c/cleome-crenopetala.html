Cleome crenopetala

Cleome crenopetala, commonly known as the fringed cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its stems are often hairy, and the flowers have five to seven petals with fringed edges, giving it its common name.

Cleome crenopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
