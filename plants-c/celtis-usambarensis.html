Celtis usambarensis

Celtis usambarensis, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, smooth fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Celtis usambarensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
