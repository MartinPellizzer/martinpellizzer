Cocculus Japonicus

Cocculus japonicus is a lesser-known but fascinating plant native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and unique, twisting vines. While not widely cultivated, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its rarity and ecological significance make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
