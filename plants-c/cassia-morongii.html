Cassia morongii

Cassia morongii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia morongii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
