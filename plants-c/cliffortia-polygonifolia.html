Cliffortia polygonifolia

Cliffortia polygonifolia, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the species is well adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

Cliffortia polygonifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
