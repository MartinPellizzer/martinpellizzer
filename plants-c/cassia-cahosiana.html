Cassia cahosiana

Cassia cahosiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and can be used in traditional medicine.
