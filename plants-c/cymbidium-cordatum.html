Cymbidium cordatum

Cymbidium cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with tall, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive heart-shaped lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Cymbidium cordatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in horticulture.
