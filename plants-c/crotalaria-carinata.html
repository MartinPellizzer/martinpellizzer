Crotalaria carinata

Crotalaria carinata, commonly known as the spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria carinata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
