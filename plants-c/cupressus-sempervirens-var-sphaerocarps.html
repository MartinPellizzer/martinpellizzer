Cupressus sempervirens var. sphaerocarps

Cupressus sempervirens var. sphaerocarps, commonly known as the globe cypress, is a variant of the Mediterranean cypress.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and scale-like leaves that give it a unique, compact appearance. This variety is native to the southern regions of Europe and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is relatively slow-growing and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
