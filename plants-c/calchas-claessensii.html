Calchas claessensii

Calchas claessensii, commonly known as the African moonviolet, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
