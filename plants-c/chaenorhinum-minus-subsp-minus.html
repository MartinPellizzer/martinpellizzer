Chaenorhinum minus subsp. minus

Chaenorhinum minus subsp.

minus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe. It features small, showy yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in racemes along the stems.

The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain regions.
