Cordia linnaei

Cordia linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean's cordia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale yellow, producing a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
