Cichorium caeruleum

Cichorium caeruleum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lobed. Its scientific name reflects its striking blue coloration, which is a key feature in its botanical description.

Cichorium caeruleum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
