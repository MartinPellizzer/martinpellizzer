Cousinia teraranica

Cousinia teraranica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Cousinia teraranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
