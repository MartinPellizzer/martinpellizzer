Crataegus × pseudoazarolus

Crataegus × pseudoazarolus, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to egg-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically appealing in gardens.
