Carex composita

Carex composita, commonly known as the composite sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex composita is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
