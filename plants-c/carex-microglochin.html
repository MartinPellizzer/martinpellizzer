Carex microglochin

Carex microglochin, commonly known as the small glochid carex, is a species of sedge found in wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Carex microglochin is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in disturbed wetlands.
