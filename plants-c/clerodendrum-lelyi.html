Clerodendrum lelyi

Clerodendrum lelyi, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent stamen. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Clerodendrum lelyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
