Calanthe pilosa

Calanthe pilosa, commonly known as the hairy calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with dark veins.

Calanthe pilosa thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
