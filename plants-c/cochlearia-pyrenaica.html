Cochlearia pyrenaica

Cochlearia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the genus Cochlearia. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Cochlearia pyrenaica is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh mountain conditions.
