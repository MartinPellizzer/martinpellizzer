Carex nutans

Carex nutans, commonly known as the drooping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that mature into seeds. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Carex nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
