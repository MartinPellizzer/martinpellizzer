Centaurea × noguerensis

Centaurea × noguerensis, commonly known as the Noguer's cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
