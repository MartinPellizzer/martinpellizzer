Cardamine hastulata

Cardamine hastulata, commonly known as the forked watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist woodland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, kidney-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette and produces clusters of tiny, white, four-petaled flowers. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

The plant is characterized by its preference for shady, damp environments and is often found in streamside areas and forest floors.
