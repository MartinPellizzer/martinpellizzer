Costus afer

Costus afer, commonly known as African costus, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

Costus afer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
