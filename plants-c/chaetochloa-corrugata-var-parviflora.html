Chaetochloa corrugata var. parviflora

Chaetochloa corrugata var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
