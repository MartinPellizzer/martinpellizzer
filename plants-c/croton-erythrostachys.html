Croton erythrostachys

Croton erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
