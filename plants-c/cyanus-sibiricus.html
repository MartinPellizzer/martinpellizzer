Cyanus sibiricus

Cyanus sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Cyanus sibiricus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
