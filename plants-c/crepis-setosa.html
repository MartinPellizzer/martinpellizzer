Crepis setosa

Crepis setosa, commonly known as the slender sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are surrounded by bracts. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Crepis setosa is an annual or biennial herb that is generally considered a weed in agricultural settings.
