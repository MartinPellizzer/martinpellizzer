Callilepis nepotiana

Callilepis nepotiana, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the eastern and southern regions of South Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
