Calendula arvensis subsp. hydruntina

Calendula arvensis subsp.

hydruntina, commonly known as the Sicilian marigold, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
