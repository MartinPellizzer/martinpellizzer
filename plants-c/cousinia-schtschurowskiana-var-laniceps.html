Cousinia schtschurowskiana var. laniceps

Cousinia schtschurowskiana var. laniceps, commonly known as the woolly cushion couchgrass, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of silver-gray, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important species in its native habitat.
