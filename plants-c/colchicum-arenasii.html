Colchicum arenasii

Colchicum arenasii, commonly known as the desert colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features a short, stocky rhizome and produces striking, waxy, violet-blue flowers with reflexed petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It blooms in late spring or early summer, often in rocky or sandy soils.

Colchicum arenasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
