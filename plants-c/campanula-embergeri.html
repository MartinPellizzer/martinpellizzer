Campanula embergeri

Campanula embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Campanula embergeri is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
