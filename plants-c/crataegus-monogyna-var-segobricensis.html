Crataegus monogyna var. segobricensis

Crataegus monogyna var. segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric wild hawthorn, is a variety of the common hawthorn native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and hedgerows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and greyish in younger trees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
