Castilleja sulphurea

Castilleja sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur cupleaf.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants to obtain some of its nutrients. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and distinctive yellow-green leaves. It is native to the western United States and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its environment.
