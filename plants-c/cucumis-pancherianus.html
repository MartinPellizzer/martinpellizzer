Cucumis pancherianus

Cucumis pancherianus, commonly known as the Indian cucumber or Pancherian cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in local cuisines for their mild flavor and crisp texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Cucumis pancherianus include trailing vines, palmate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom before the fruit develops.
