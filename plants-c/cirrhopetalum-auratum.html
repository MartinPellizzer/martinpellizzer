Cirrhopetalum auratum

Cirrhopetalum auratum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble small bells, which are often adorned with intricate, feathery patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Cirrhopetalum auratum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
