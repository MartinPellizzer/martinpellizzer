Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. ludens

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. ludens, commonly known as the sticky flower goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
