Carex viridula var. oedocarpa

Carex viridula var. oedocarpa, commonly known as the slender bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
