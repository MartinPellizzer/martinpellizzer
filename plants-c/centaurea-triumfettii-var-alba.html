Centaurea triumfettii var. alba

Centaurea triumfettii var. alba, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-gray hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
