Canna aureovittata

Canna aureovittata, commonly known as the golden stripe cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by vibrant, colorful bracts, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
