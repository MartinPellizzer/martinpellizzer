Clematis terniflora var. latisepala

Clematis terniflora var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
