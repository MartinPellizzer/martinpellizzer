Coriaria japonica subsp. intermedia

Coriaria japonica subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as Japanese coral tree, is a shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
