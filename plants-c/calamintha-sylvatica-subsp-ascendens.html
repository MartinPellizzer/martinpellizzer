Calamintha sylvatica subsp. ascendens

Calamintha sylvatica subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as mountain calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces aromatic, mint-scented leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
