Cupressus arizonica subsp. glabra

Cupressus arizonica subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the Arizona white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in flat sprays.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological stability in its native range.
