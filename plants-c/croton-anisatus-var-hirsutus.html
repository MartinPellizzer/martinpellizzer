Croton anisatus var. hirsutus

Croton anisatus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in some regions.
