Cassine paragua

Cassine paragua, commonly known as the Paraguay sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing three seeds. Cassine paragua is often found in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
