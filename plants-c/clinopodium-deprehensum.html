Clinopodium deprehensum

Clinopodium deprehensum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a minty scent, which is a common trait among members of its family.

Clinopodium deprehensum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
