Carex maximowiczii var. pallida

Carex maximowiczii var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or pale in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
