Crepis apula

Crepis apula, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, characterized by its rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Crepis apula is noted for its resilience and is a common species in mountainous areas of Europe.
