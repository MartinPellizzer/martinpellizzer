Coreopsis amplexicaulis

Coreopsis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and alternate leaves that often clasp the stem. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
