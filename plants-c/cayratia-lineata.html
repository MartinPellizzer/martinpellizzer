Cayratia lineata

Cayratia lineata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cayratia lineata is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
