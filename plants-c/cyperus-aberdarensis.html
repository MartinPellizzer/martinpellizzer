Cyperus aberdarensis

Cyperus aberdarensis, commonly known as the Aberdare flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering structures called spikes. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils and is often found in swampy areas.

Cyperus aberdarensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
