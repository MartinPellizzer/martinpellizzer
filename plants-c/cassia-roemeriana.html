Cassia roemeriana

Cassia roemeriana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia roemeriana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
