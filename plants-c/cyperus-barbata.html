Cyperus barbata

Cyperus barbata, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus barbata produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
