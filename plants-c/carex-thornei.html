Carex thornei

Carex thornei, commonly known as the Thorne's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in the eastern United States. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex thornei plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
