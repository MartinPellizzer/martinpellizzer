Casearia olivacea

Casearia olivacea, commonly known as the olive tree of the genus Casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers, which are followed by small, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Casearia olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
