Cyanospermum angolense

Cyanospermum angolense, commonly known as the Angolan blue-green alga, is a type of cyanobacterium that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure and the ability to perform photosynthesis. This organism is capable of nitrogen fixation, making it important in nutrient cycling within aquatic ecosystems. It often forms dense blooms in nutrient-rich waters, which can impact water quality.

Cyanospermum angolense is also studied for its potential applications in biotechnology and environmental remediation.
