Carex distenta

Carex distenta, commonly known as the spreading sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Carex distenta is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
