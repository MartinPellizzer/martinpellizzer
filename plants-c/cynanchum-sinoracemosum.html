Cynanchum sinoracemosum

Cynanchum sinoracemosum, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic, containing various alkaloids. It typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional Chinese medicine and ecological studies.
