Cuscuta diaphana

Cuscuta diaphana, commonly known as the slender dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrition.

The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it parasitizes a variety of herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, allowing it to spread across different habitats.
