Chaetochloa versicolor

Chaetochloa versicolor, commonly known as the variable hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and its ability to thrive in water-saturated soils. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that can vary depending on environmental conditions. Chaetochloa versicolor produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
