Carex goodenowii f. aquatiliformis

Carex goodenowii f. aquatiliformis, commonly known as the waterleaf sedge, is a variant of the Carex genus native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant thrives in moist to aquatic habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, and is often found in regions with high humidity. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
