Cerasus triloba var. plena

Cerasus triloba var. plena, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or yaezakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its profuse display of double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin, and it produces small, dark red fruits in the fall.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
