Cassuvium reniforme

Cassuvium reniforme, commonly known as the kidney-shaped leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive reniform (kidney-shaped) leaves.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often a deep green, with some varieties exhibiting variegated patterns of white or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by fruit that resembles a small, fleshy capsule.

Cassuvium reniforme is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ease of care.
