Cassia arborea

Cassia arborea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the appearance of a fiery crown. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces long, slender pods that contain flat, dark seeds.

Cassia arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
