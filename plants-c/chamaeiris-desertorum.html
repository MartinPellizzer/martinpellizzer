Chamaeiris desertorum

Chamaeiris desertorum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
