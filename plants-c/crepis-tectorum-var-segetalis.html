Crepis tectorum var. segetalis

Crepis tectorum var. segetalis, commonly known as the field mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. This variant is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

It is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for insects and birds.
