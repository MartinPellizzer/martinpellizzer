Campanula rotundifolia var. sabatia

Campanula rotundifolia var. sabatia, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are rounded and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
