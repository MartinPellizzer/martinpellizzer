Copiapoa cinerea subsp. cinerea

Copiapoa cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the pale copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing species that forms a dense clump over time.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
