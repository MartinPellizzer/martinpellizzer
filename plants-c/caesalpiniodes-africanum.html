Caesalpiniodes africanum

Caesalpiniodes africanum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light gray, while its roots are deep and extensive, helping it thrive in various soil types.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
