Cyathea riparia

Cyathea riparia, commonly known as the riparian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near rivers and in humid forests. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in soil while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Cyathea riparia plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
