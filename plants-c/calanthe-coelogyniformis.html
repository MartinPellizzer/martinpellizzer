Calanthe coelogyniformis

Calanthe coelogyniformis, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a dense spike of pale pink to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, with petals and sepals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
