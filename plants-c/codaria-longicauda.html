Codaria longicauda

Codaria longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed codaria, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. Its small, white flowers appear in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
