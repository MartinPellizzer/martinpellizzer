Cyathodes imbricata

Cyathodes imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Cyathodes imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
