Cymbidium nodosum

Cymbidium nodosum, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cymbidium nodosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
