Campanula cenisia

Campanula cenisia, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula cenisia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
