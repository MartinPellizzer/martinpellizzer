Cytisus supinus var. danubialis

Cytisus supinus var. danubialis, commonly known as the Danube dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Danube region of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, thornless stems and needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
