Caladenia septuosa

Caladenia septuosa, commonly known as the Western Shield Spider-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often in coastal or inland regions. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, and it produces a single, elongated seed pod that contains numerous minute seeds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
