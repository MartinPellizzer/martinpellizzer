Cinnamomum dulce var. ammannii

Cinnamomum dulce var. ammannii, commonly known as the sweet cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are often harvested for their essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its contribution to local biodiversity.
