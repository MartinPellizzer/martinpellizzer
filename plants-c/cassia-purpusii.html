Cassia purpusii

Cassia purpusii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. Cassia purpusii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
