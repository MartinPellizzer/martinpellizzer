Carduus acanthoides subsp. sintenisii

Carduus acanthoides subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other members of the Carduus genus.
