Cassia javanica var. pubiflora

Cassia javanica var. pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The hairy cassia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
