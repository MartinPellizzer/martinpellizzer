Clematis texensis f. major

Clematis texensis f. major, commonly known as the Texas Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
