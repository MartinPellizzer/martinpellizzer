Cyrtanthus clavatus

Cyrtanthus clavatus, commonly known as the knobbed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has strap-like leaves that are grass-like in appearance, and it produces distinctive, rounded tubers underground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Cyrtanthus clavatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
