Chrysocoma mozambicensis

Chrysocoma mozambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique goldenbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.
