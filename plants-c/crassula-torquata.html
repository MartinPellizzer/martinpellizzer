Crassula torquata

Crassula torquata, commonly known as the "Lace Plant" or "Bead Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the tips. The leaves are arranged in whorls around the stems, creating a visually striking appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.
