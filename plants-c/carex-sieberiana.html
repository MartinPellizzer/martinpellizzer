Carex sieberiana

Carex sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex sieberiana is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms highlights its ecological significance.
