Cedrus libanotica f. viridis

Cedrus libanotica f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved cedar of Lebanon, is a unique variant of the cedar of Lebanon.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage, which contrasts with the typical gray-green leaves of the species. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant is a coniferous evergreen with a conical shape and needle-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
