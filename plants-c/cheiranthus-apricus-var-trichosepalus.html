Cheiranthus apricus var. trichosepalus

Cheiranthus apricus var. trichosepalus, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The variety trichosepalus is distinguished by its hairy sepals, which give it a unique appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
