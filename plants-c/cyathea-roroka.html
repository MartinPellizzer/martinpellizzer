Cyathea roroka

Cyathea roroka, commonly known as the Roroka tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to its striking appearance.

Cyathea roroka plays a significant ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
