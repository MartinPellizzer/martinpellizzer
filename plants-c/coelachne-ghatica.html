Coelachne ghatica

Coelachne ghatica, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Coelachne ghatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
