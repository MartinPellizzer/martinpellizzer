Capurodendron nanophyllum

Capurodendron nanophyllum, commonly known as the tiny-leafed evergreen, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its extremely small, leathery leaves that are densely packed along its slender, woody stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by tiny, round fruits that contain a single seed. Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a critically endangered species.

Botanists study it to better understand its ecological role and potential for conservation in its fragile mountain ecosystem.
