Carum multiflorum

Carum multiflorum, commonly known as clustered caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clustered umbels. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and carminative properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
