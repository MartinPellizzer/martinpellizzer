Centaurea bethurica

Centaurea bethurica, commonly known as Bethuric centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with prominent central disc flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Centaurea bethurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
