Cytisus striatus subsp. welwitschii

Cytisus striatus subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschii broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features slender, often thorny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
