Crassula montana subsp. montana

Crassula montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
