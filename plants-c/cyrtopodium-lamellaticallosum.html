Cyrtopodium lamellaticallosum

Cyrtopodium lamellaticallosum, commonly known as the shell-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, elongated leaf that has a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces pendulous flowers with pale yellow petals and sepals, often with a contrasting red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Cyrtopodium lamellaticallosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
