Chloris mollis

Chloris mollis, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, pale blue or greenish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Chloris mollis is also an important forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
