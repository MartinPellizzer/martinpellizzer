Campomanesia triflora

Campomanesia triflora, commonly known as pitahaya or wolfberry, is a flowering plant native to Brazil and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
