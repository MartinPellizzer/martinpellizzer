Chenopodium parabolicum

Chenopodium parabolicum, commonly known as parabolic goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically oval to triangular in shape and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Chenopodium parabolicum is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
