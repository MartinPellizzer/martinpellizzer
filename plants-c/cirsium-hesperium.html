Cirsium hesperium

Cirsium hesperium, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Cirsium hesperium plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
