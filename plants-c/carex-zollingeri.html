Carex zollingeri

Carex zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Zollinger's sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
