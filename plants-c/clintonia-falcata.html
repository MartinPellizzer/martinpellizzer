Clintonia falcata

Clintonia falcata, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs.

Clintonia falcata thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
