Cakile alacranensis

Cakile alacranensis, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and fleshy, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
