Cuphea bustamanta

Cuphea bustamanta, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea bustamanta is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
