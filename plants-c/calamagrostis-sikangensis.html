Calamagrostis sikangensis

Calamagrostis sikangensis, commonly known as the Sichuan reed, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along streams. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that add ornamental value.

Calamagrostis sikangensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
