Crocus reticulatus lusus violaceus

Crocus reticulatus lusus violaceus, commonly known as the purple-crimson crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking violet and purple flowers. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that typically appear in early spring. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its vivid coloration and is a popular choice in rock gardens and alpine settings.
