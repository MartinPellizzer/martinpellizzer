Centranthus nevadensis subsp. maroccanus

Centranthus nevadensis subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan red campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
