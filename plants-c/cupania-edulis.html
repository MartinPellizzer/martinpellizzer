Cupania edulis

Cupania edulis, commonly known as the Brazilian grape or cupuassu, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces edible fruits with a unique, aromatic flavor. The plant's leaves are palmately lobed, and its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences. The fruit, which resembles a small grape, contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, fleshy pulp.

It is valued not only for its culinary use but also for its potential medicinal properties.
