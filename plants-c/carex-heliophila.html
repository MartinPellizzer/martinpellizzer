Carex heliophila

Carex heliophila, commonly known as the sun-loving sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in full sun to partial shade and thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and stiff, with a prominent central vein, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex heliophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
