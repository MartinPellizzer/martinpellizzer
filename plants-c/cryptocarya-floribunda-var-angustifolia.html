Cryptocarya floribunda var. angustifolia

Cryptocarya floribunda var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a woody drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
