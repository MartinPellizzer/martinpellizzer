Crotalaria retusa var. retusa

Crotalaria retusa var. retusa, commonly known as the smallflower cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, pod-like structures that split open when mature, releasing the seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage and ornamental purposes.
