Cynanchum californicum

Cynanchum californicum, commonly known as California milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their distinctive floral structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus.

Cynanchum californicum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
