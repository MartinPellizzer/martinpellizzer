Coleus degasparisianus

Coleus degasparisianus, commonly known as the Degasparisian coleus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically features broad, ovate leaves with striking variegation in shades of green, red, and white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly found in the wild outside its native range.
