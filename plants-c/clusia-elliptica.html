Clusia elliptica

Clusia elliptica, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green on the top and white on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. Clusia elliptica is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various soil types.
