Ceropegia articulata

Ceropegia articulata, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
