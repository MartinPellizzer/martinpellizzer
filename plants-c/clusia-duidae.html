Clusia duidae

Clusia duidae, commonly known as the Brazilian shield-leaf plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which is also known as the gutta-percha family. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often shield-shaped, providing a unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Clusia duidae is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
