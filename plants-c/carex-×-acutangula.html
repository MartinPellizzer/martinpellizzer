Carex × acutangula

Carex × acutangula, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid sedge species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

Carex × acutangula is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
