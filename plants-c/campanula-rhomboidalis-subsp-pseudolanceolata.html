Campanula rhomboidalis subsp. pseudolanceolata

Campanula rhomboidalis subsp.

pseudolanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to violet in color, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, clustered blooms.
