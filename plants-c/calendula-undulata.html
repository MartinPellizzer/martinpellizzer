Calendula undulata

Calendula undulata, commonly known as the wavy calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Calendula undulata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
