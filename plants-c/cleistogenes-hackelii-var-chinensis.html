Cleistogenes hackelii var. chinensis

Cleistogenes hackelii var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese false brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
