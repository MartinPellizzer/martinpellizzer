Cyperus cunninghamii

Cyperus cunninghamii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as swampy soils and marshes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided flowering spikes.

Cyperus cunninghamii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
