Chamaeiris monnieri

Chamaeiris monnieri, commonly known as the creeping mouse ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white down, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic value.
