Combretum lecananthum

Combretum lecananthum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that resemble flames, typically blooming in red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The bark is smooth and light-colored, with a distinctive flaky appearance that adds to its visual appeal.
