Chrysoma ericoides

Chrysoma ericoides, commonly known as the silverleaf ericoid, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that gives it a silvery appearance, especially when viewed in sunlight. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
