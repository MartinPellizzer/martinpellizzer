Coronilla emerus var. minor

Coronilla emerus var. minor, commonly known as lesser crown vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and erosion control.
