Caragana conferta

Caragana conferta, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Caragana conferta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
