Cytisus grandiflorus subsp. maurus

Cytisus grandiflorus subsp.

maurus, commonly known as the Mauretanian broom, is a shrub native to North Africa. It features slender, often spiny stems and is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to harsh, Mediterranean-type climates.
