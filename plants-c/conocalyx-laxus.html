Conocalyx laxus

Conocalyx laxus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or near water bodies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade.
