Cyathea catacampta

Cyathea catacampta, commonly known as the Catacampta tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
