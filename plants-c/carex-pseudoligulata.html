Carex pseudoligulata

Carex pseudoligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
