Combretum elgonense

Combretum elgonense, commonly known as Elgon combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
