Campanula pusilla var. descensa

Campanula pusilla var. descensa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
