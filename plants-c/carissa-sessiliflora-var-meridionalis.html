Carissa sessiliflora var. meridionalis

Carissa sessiliflora var. meridionalis, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is notable for its edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and fruit-bearing gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
