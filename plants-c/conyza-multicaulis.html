Conyza multicaulis

Conyza multicaulis, commonly known as the common cudweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a slender, branching stem that is often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza multicaulis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
