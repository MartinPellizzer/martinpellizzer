Conophytum depressum subsp. depressum

Conophytum depressum subsp.

depressum, commonly known as the "Bushman's hat," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
