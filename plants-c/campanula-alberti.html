Campanula alberti

Campanula alberti, commonly known as Albert's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula alberti is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
