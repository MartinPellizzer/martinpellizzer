Christannia salicifolia

Christannia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with slender, willow-like stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Christannia salicifolia is adapted to thrive in hot, dry conditions and is often found in rocky soils.
