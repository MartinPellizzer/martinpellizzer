Chamaedorea velutina

Chamaedorea velutina, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, gracefully curved stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a favored ornamental plant.
