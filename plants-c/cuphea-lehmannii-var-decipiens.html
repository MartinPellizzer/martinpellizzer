Cuphea lehmannii var. decipiens

Cuphea lehmannii var. decipiens, commonly known as the false flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble small flames, which are actually composed of fused petals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
