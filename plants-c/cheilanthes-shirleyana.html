Cheilanthes shirleyana

Cheilanthes shirleyana, commonly known as Shirley’s rockcap, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of thick, leathery fronds that are typically green to gray-green in color. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing on limestone or sandstone substrates. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with small, ovate pinnae that are arranged closely along the rachis.

Shirley’s rockcap is native to regions of Mexico and is valued for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
