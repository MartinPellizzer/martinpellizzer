Cassia acunae

Cassia acunae, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia acunae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for warm climates.
