Cocculus grandifolius

Cocculus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf devil's bit, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain a single, large, kidney-shaped seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
