Cirsium utahense

Cirsium utahense, commonly known as Utah thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its structure includes a thick, hairy stem and basal rosettes of leaves.
