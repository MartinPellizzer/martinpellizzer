Chasechloa humbertiana

Chasechloa humbertiana, commonly known as Humbert's feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feather-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its ornamental value lies in its elegant appearance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Chasechloa humbertiana is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
