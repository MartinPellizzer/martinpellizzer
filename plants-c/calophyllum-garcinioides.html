Calophyllum garcinioides

Calophyllum garcinioides, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also important in ecological contexts, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
