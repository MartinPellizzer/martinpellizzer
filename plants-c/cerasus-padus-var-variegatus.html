Cerasus padus var. variegatus

Cerasus padus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which often display a variegated pattern of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
