Carduus micropterus

Carduus micropterus, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a stout, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with coarse, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, consisting of many small florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, serrated margin and a hairy surface.

The plant is often found in meadows, wastelands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
