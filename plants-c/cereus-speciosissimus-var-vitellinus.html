Cereus speciosissimus var. vitellinus

Cereus speciosissimus var. vitellinus, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive golden-yellow coloration, particularly on its areoles. The plant features spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are large, white, and nocturnal, opening at night and emitting a sweet fragrance.

This variety is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
