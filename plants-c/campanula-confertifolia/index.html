Campanula Confertifolia

Campanula confertifolia, also known as the clustered bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its elegant appearance and ease of care have made it a favorite among gardeners and botanists alike. With its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value, Campanula confertifolia remains a beloved addition to many gardens.
