Carex glauca subsp. erythrostachys

Carex glauca subsp.

erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color, and its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
