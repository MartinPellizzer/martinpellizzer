Croton gracilipes var. paraguariensis

Croton gracilipes var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
