Callista calostoma

Callista calostoma, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that are oppositely arranged along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
