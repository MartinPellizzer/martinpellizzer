Centaurea lovricii

Centaurea lovricii, commonly known as the Lovric's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in composite flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands.

Centaurea lovricii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
