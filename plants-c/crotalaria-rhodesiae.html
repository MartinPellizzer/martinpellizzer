Crotalaria rhodesiae

Crotalaria rhodesiae, commonly known as Rhodes' cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria rhodesiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
