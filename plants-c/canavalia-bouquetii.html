Canavalia bouquetii

Canavalia bouquetii, commonly known as the pink bean or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, pod-like fruits and are often used in traditional medicine.

Canavalia bouquetii is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
