Crassula capitella subsp. nodulosa

Crassula capitella subsp.

nodulosa, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a compact, bushy rosette.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
