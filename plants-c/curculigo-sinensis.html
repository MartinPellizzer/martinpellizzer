Curculigo sinensis

Curculigo sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese snail orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The botanical interest in Curculigo sinensis lies in its unique floral structure and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
