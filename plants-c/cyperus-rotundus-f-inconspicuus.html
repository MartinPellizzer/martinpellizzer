Cyperus rotundus f. inconspicuus

Cyperus rotundus f. inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the common flatsedge.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a compact, upright growth habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced floral structures compared to the typical form.
