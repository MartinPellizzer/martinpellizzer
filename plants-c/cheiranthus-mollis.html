Cheiranthus mollis

Cheiranthus mollis, commonly known as the silky mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. It is an annual or biennial species, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The silky mustard plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
