Casparya hirta

Casparya hirta, commonly known as the hairy caspary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Casparya hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
