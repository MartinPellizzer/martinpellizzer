Carex umbrosa var. rostrata

Carex umbrosa var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
