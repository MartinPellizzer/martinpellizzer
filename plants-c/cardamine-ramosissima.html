Cardamine ramosissima

Cardamine ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland floors. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spherical inflorescence, producing a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
