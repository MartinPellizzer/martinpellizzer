Cassia frondosa var. crista

Cassia frondosa var. crista, commonly known as the crested cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, winged pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name "crista" refers to the unique, crest-like shape of its foliage.
