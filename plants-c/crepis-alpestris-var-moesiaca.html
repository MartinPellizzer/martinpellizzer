Crepis alpestris var. moesiaca

Crepis alpestris var. moesiaca, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude environments.
