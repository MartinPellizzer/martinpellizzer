Crataegus monogyna subsp. polyacantha

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

polyacantha, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
