Commiphora buruxa

Commiphora buruxa, commonly known as wild myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in the production of incense and perfumes.
