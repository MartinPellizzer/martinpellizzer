Calliandra houstoniana subsp. stylesii

Calliandra houstoniana subsp.

stylesii, commonly known as the Houston honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright orange to red flowers.

The plant is adapted to warm, dry climates and thrives in full sun. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
