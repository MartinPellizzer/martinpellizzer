Cissus puncticulosa

Cissus puncticulosa, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, diamond-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, raised dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus puncticulosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
