Cornus stolonifera f. californica

Cornus stolonifera f. californica, commonly known as the California dogwood, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, white or pink bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
