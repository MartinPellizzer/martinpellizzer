Cousinia pentacanthoides

Cousinia pentacanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia pentacanthoides is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
