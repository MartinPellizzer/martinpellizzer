Cynopaema cannabinum

Cynopaema cannabinum, commonly known as the cannabis plant, is a flowering herbaceous plant belonging to the Cannabaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its serrated leaves and greenish-white flowers. The plant contains various cannabinoids, including tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and cannabidiol (CBD), which are responsible for its psychoactive and therapeutic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its medicinal and recreational uses, it is subject to legal regulation in many countries.
