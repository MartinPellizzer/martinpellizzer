Carex oederi var. androgyna

Carex oederi var. androgyna, commonly known as the Androgyna sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
