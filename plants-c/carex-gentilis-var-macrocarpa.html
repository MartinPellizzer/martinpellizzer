Carex gentilis var. macrocarpa

Carex gentilis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, upright leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with distinctive, pale blue to greenish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
