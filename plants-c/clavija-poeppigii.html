Clavija poeppigii

Clavija poeppigii, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has small, narrow leaves and thrives in dry, sunny environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Clavija poeppigii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
