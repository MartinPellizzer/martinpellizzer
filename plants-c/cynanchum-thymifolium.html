Cynanchum thymifolium

Cynanchum thymifolium, commonly known as the thyme-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
