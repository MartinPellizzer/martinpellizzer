Claoxylon albicans

Claoxylon albicans, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Claoxylon albicans is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
