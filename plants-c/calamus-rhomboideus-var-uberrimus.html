Calamus rhomboideus var. uberrimus

Calamus rhomboideus var. uberrimus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

This variant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
