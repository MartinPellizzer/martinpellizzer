Combretum collinum subsp. dumetorum

Combretum collinum subsp.

dumetorum, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and savannas, and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
