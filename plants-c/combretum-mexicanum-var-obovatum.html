Combretum mexicanum var. obovatum

Combretum mexicanum var. obovatum, commonly known as Mexican flame tree or *Koelreuteria* species, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with obovate leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, woody, bead-like fruits that persist through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and showy foliage make it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical regions.
