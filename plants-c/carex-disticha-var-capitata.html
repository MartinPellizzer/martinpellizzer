Carex disticha var. capitata

Carex disticha var. capitata, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
