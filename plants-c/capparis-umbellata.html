Capparis umbellata

Capparis umbellata, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy, green berries that mature to a reddish color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Capparis umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
