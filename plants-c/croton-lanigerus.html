Croton lanigerus

Croton lanigerus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking foliage.
