Cola ndongensis

Cola ndongensis, commonly known as the African kola nut, is a small evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive two-lobed fruits that contain the aromatic kola nuts used in traditional ceremonies and as a stimulant. The tree thrives in tropical rainforest climates and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Its ecological and cultural significance makes it an important species in its native region.
