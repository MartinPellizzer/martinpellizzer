Campanula cervicaria subsp. transsilvanica

Campanula cervicaria subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lanceolate, hairy leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
