Clinopodium gracile var. sachalinense

Clinopodium gracile var. sachalinense, commonly known as Sachalin savory, is a perennial herb native to the island of Sakhalin.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its small, white to pink flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is distinguished by its delicate foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
