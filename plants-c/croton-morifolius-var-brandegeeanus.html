Croton morifolius var. brandegeeanus

Croton morifolius var. brandegeeanus, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow, especially during the growing season. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is cultivated primarily for its ornamental value.
