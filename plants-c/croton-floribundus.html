Croton floribundus

Croton floribundus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in clusters during the flowering season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is rough and fissured. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Croton floribundus is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
