Coleus pseudospeciosus

Coleus pseudospeciosus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from deep purples to bright reds and oranges. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and tubular, usually appearing in clusters at the ends of branches.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
