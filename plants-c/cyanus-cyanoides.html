Cyanus cyanoides

Cyanus cyanoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the top of the plant. The plant has slender, hairy stems and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Cyanus cyanoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
