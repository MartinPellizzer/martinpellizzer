Convolvulus longipilis

Convolvulus longipilis, commonly known as the long-haired morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with long, silky hairs. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

Convolvulus longipilis is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
