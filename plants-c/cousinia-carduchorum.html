Cousinia carduchorum

Cousinia carduchorum, commonly known as the Karakoram cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is known for its hairy, basal leaves and yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia carduchorum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
