Cyperus esculentus var. sativus

Cyperus esculentus var. sativus, commonly known as water chestnut, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in Asia and Africa.

It features erect, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its edible underground tubers are the primary part used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common aquatic or semi-aquatic species.
