Coreocarpus ixtapanus

Coreocarpus ixtapanus, commonly known as the Mexican rock fig, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin.

Coreocarpus ixtapanus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
