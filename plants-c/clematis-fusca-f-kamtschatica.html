Clematis fusca f. kamtschatica

Clematis fusca f. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatica Clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
