Chondrilla maracandica

Chondrilla maracandica, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Chondrilla maracandica is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
