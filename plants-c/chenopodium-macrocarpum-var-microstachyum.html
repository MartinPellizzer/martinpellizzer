Chenopodium macrocarpum var. microstachyum

Chenopodium macrocarpum var. microstachyum, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems and leaves.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Eurasia but has been naturalized in many regions, including North America, where it often thrives in disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistent presence in various habitats.

Chenopodium macrocarpum var. microstachyum is also known for its edible leaves, which are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and nutrients.
