Crocus vitellinus

Crocus vitellinus, commonly known as the yellow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers with six petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Crocus vitellinus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
