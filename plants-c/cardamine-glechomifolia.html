Cardamine glechomifolia

Cardamine glechomifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a peppery aroma, similar to other members of its family.

The small white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.
