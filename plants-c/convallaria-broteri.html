Convallaria broteri

Convallaria broteri, commonly known as the Bornean lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Convallaria broteri is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
