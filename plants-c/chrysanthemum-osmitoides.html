Chrysanthemum osmitoides

Chrysanthemum osmitoides, commonly known as the Osmitoides chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, often arranged in a radiate pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its botanical structure includes finely divided, feather-like leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.
