Carex stenophylla var. duriuscula

Carex stenophylla var. duriuscula, commonly known as the hard-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the sedge family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, wet environments.
