Cyperus flavescens f. paraensis

Cyperus flavescens f. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
