Callicarpa prolifera

Callicarpa prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
