Campanula conferta var. glabra

Campanula conferta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, smooth hairs, giving it its varietal name "glabra.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
