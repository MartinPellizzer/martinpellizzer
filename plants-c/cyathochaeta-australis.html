Cyathochaeta australis

Cyathochaeta australis, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyathochaeta australis is adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy conditions, making it a characteristic species of coastal ecosystems.
