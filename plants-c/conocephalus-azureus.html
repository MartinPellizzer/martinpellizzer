Conocephalus azureus

Conocephalus azureus, commonly known as the Azure-headed Grasshopper, is a species of grasshopper native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking azure-blue head and thorax, which serve as a form of aposematic coloration to warn potential predators. The plant-like appearance of its body, with elongated limbs and a segmented exoskeleton, gives it a resemblance to certain flowering plants. While not a plant itself, it is often found in habitats where specific flora thrive, such as grasslands and savannas.

Its life cycle and ecological role are closely tied to the vegetation in its native environment.
