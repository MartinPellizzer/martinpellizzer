Coriflora bigelovii

Coriflora bigelovii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Coriflora bigelovii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh desert climates.
