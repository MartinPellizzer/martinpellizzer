Chrysophyllum jamaicense

Chrysophyllum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
