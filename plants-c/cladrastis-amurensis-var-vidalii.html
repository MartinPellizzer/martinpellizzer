Cladrastis amurensis var. vidalii

Cladrastis amurensis var. vidalii, commonly known as the Vidalii Climbing Cucumber Tree, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Russia and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces showy, pendulous white flowers in early spring, followed by yellow, cucumber-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is often used as a climbing plant in trellises or as a ground cover.
