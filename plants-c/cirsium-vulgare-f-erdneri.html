Cirsium vulgare f. erdneri

Cirsium vulgare f. erdneri, commonly known as the Erdner's thistle, is a horticultural form of the common thistle.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, preferring full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, spiny margins.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
