Cyperus riedelianus

Cyperus riedelianus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with wet or seasonally flooded soils. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbed leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers.

Cyperus riedelianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
