Crocus vernus var. clavatus

Crocus vernus var. clavatus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or rockeries.

Its flowers are also a source of nectar for early pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
