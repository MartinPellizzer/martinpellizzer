Clematis viticella alba

Clematis viticella alba, commonly known as the white Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis viticella alba is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
