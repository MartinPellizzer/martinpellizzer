Cometes alternifolia

Cometes alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaved cometes, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of lance-shaped leaves along its slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical classification places it in the family Rutaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like citrus.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
