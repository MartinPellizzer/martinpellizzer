Cereus pentlandii var. miniatus

Cereus pentlandii var. miniatus, commonly known as the miniature night bloomer, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
