Carex blankinshipii

Carex blankinshipii, commonly known as the Blankinship sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex blankinshipii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
