Carex hostii

Carex hostii, commonly known as the Hosta-like sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly curled at the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-sided, spike-like fruits.

Carex hostii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
