Chionolaena isabellae

Chionolaena isabellae, commonly known as the Isabelline silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as cliffs and slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to withstand drought and intense sunlight.
