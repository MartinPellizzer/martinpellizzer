Cystopteris acuta

Cystopteris acuta, commonly known as the acute spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. Cystopteris acuta is often found growing on rocks or soil in moist, shaded environments.

It is a hardy plant that is commonly used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
