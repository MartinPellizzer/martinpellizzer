Catasetum fimbriatum var. ornithorrhynchum

Catasetum fimbriatum var. ornithorrhynchum, commonly known as the bird-beak orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved flowers that resemble a bird's beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that can range in color from yellow to orange.

The botanical features of this orchid include specialized structures for pollination, such as a modified lip that aids in attracting specific pollinators.
