Crataegus pinnatifida f. bracteata

Crataegus pinnatifida f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences, which are modified leaves that enclose the flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
