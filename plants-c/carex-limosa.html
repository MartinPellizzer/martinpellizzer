Carex limosa

Carex limosa, commonly known as silky flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Native to wetlands and marshy areas, it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
