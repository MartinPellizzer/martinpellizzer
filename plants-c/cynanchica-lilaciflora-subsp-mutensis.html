Cynanchica lilaciflora subsp. mutensis

Cynanchica lilaciflora subsp.

mutensis, commonly known as the Mutan lilacvine, is a perennial climbing plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
