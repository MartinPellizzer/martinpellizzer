Cryptochilus siamensis

Cryptochilus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces leaves that are usually lance-shaped and slightly fleshy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
