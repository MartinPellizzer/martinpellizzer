Crassula jasminea

Crassula jasminea, commonly known as the sweet-scented crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental appeal and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
