Celtis zizyphoides

Celtis zizyphoides, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Celtis zizyphoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in urban and natural landscapes.
