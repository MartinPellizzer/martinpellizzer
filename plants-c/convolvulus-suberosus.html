Convolvulus suberosus

Convolvulus suberosus, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and range in color from white to pink.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
