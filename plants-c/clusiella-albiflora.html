Clusiella albiflora

Clusiella albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered clusiella, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes species known for their showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding a delicate appearance to the plant.

Clusiella albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
