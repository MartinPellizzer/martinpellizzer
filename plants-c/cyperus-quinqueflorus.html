Cyperus quinqueflorus

Cyperus quinqueflorus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense mats that can stabilize soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a useful plant in ecological restoration.

Cyperus quinqueflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
