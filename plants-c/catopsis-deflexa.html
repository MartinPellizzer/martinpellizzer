Catopsis deflexa

Catopsis deflexa, commonly known as the flexed catopsis, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial bromeliad that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that form a central cup to collect water. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, emerging from the leaf axils.

Catopsis deflexa is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
