Cleomella macbrideana

Cleomella macbrideana, commonly known as the MacBride's cleome, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Cleomella macbrideana is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
