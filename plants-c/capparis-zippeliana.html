Capparis zippeliana

Capparis zippeliana, commonly known as the Zippel's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, green capsule that matures into a red, fleshy berry.

Capparis zippeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
