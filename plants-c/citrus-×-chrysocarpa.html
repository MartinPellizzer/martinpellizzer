Citrus × chrysocarpa

Citrus × chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden mandarin.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit resulting from the cross between the mandarin and the pummelo. The plant is characterized by its small, round, and orange-colored fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its sweet, juicy fruit.
