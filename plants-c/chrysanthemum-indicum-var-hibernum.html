Chrysanthemum indicum var. hibernum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. hibernum, commonly known as the Irish chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.

Its foliage consists of lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color.
