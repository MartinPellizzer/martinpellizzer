Chrysopsis linifolia

Chrysopsis linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow ray flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Chrysopsis linifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
