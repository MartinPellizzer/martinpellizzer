Crupina crupinastrum

Crupina crupinastrum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Crupina crupinastrum is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
