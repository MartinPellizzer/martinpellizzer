Chassalia leucocarpa

Chassalia leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and pale, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
