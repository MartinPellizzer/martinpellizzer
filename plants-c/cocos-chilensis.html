Cocos chilensis

Cocos chilensis, commonly known as the Peruvian chestnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisine.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
