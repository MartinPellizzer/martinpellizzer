Carduus trilobatus

Carduus trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's robust structure and adaptive features make it a resilient species in various environments.
