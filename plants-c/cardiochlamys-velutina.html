Cardiochlamys velutina

Cardiochlamys velutina, commonly known as the velvet heart orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of pink and white, with a velvety texture on the petals.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate species in conservation efforts.
