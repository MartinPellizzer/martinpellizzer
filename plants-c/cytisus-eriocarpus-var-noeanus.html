Cytisus eriocarpus var. noeanus

Cytisus eriocarpus var. noeanus, commonly known as the Noean Broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
