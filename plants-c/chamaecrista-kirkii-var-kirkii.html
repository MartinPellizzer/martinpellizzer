Chamaecrista kirkii var. kirkii

Chamaecrista kirkii var. kirkii, commonly known as Kirk's ticktree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a long flowering period during the rainy season.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
