Cuphea ixodes

Cuphea ixodes, commonly known as the stickyseed cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Cuphea ixodes is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
