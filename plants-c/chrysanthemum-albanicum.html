Chrysanthemum albanicum

Chrysanthemum albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers, which bloom in autumn, range in color from yellow to white, adding ornamental value to its natural setting.

Chrysanthemum albanicum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
