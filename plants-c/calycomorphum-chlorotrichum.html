Calycomorphum chlorotrichum

Calycomorphum chlorotrichum, commonly known as the golden star ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Calycomorphum chlorotrichum is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
