Clematis florida

Clematis florida, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its stems are woody and climbing. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis florida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
