Cirsium heldreichii subsp. euboicum

Cirsium heldreichii subsp.

euboicum, commonly known as the Euboean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Greek island of Euboea. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple-pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its distribution is limited to specific regions in southern Europe, making it a rare and localized species.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological preferences.
