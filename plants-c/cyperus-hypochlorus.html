Cyperus hypochlorus

Cyperus hypochlorus, commonly known as the yellow papyrus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedge plants, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered flowering heads. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikelets, contributing to its ornamental value in aquatic landscapes.

Cyperus hypochlorus is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist conditions.
