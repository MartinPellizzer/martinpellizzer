Clinopodium einseleanum

Clinopodium einseleanum, commonly known as the mountain basil or alpine thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds.

Clinopodium einseleanum is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
