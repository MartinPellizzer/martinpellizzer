Cicer punctulatum

Cicer punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-pink flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Cicer punctulatum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
