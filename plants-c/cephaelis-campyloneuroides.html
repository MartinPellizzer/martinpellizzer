Cephaelis campyloneuroides

Cephaelis campyloneuroides, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
