Coelogyne fluctuata

Coelogyne fluctuata, commonly known as the wandering orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal climates. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne fluctuata is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a cut flower.
