Cyperus iria

Cyperus iria, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Cyperus iria typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and water feature plantings.
