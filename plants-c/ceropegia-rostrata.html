Ceropegia rostrata

Ceropegia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, often tuberous stems and leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found growing in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions as a houseplant or garden specimen.
