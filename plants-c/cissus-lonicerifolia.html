Cissus lonicerifolia

Cissus lonicerifolia, commonly known as the climbing hedge or devil's backbone, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive, spiny stem that aids in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its rapid growth makes it both useful for erosion control and a potential invasive species in non-native environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its unique leaf venation and succulent stems.
