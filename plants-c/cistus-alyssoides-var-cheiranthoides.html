Cistus alyssoides var. cheiranthoides

Cistus alyssoides var. cheiranthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
