Cheiranthus × kewensis

Cheiranthus × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew's cheiranthus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cheiranthus cheiri and Cheiranthus hybridus.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its foliage consists of serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Cheiranthus × kewensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
