Centaurea × mutabilis

Centaurea × mutabilis, commonly known as the Blushing Coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, conical flower heads that change color from pink to red as the blooms mature. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Centaurea × mutabilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
