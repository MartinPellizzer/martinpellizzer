Cytisus dorycnioides

Cytisus dorycnioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often thorned stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal and rocky landscapes.
