Cardamomum cannicarpum

Cardamomum cannicarpum, commonly known as the black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces dark reddish-brown, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically cultivated at high altitudes.

The essential oils extracted from its seeds are valued for their distinctive spicy and sweet aroma.
