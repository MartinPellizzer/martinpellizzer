Cirsium vinaceum

Cirsium vinaceum, commonly known as the red thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium vinaceum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
