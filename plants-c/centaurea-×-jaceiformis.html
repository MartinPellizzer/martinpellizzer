Centaurea × jaceiformis

Centaurea × jaceiformis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Centaurea jacea and Centaurea cyanus, resulting in a plant with distinctive blue to purple flowers. The species is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
