Chorispora tenella var. arcuata

Chorispora tenella var. arcuata, commonly known as the hooked chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often curl into hooks, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are oval and hairy, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
