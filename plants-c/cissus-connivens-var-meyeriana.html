Cissus connivens var. meyeriana

Cissus connivens var. meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with a waxy texture, and its stems are covered in small, reddish-brown tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by round, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its hardiness and ability to purify indoor air.
