Campanula andrewsii var. pelia

Campanula andrewsii var. pelia, commonly known as the Sierra campanula, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 7,000 feet. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula andrewsii var. pelia is often found in open woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
