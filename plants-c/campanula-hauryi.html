Campanula hauryi

Campanula hauryi, commonly known as the Hauri bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
