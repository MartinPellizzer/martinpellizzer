Calophyllum spectabile var. ceramicum

Calophyllum spectabile var. ceramicum, commonly known as the Cerulean Calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
