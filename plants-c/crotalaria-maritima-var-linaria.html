Crotalaria maritima var. linaria

Crotalaria maritima var. linaria, commonly known as the sea cowpea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along shorelines. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like structures and are adapted to disperse in coastal environments.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
