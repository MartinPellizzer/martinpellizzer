Crotalaria hostmanii

Crotalaria hostmanii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and is known for its role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Crotalaria hostmanii is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
