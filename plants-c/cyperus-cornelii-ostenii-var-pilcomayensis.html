Cyperus cornelii-ostenii var. pilcomayensis

Cyperus cornelii-ostenii var. pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
