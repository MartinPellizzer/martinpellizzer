Chenopodina hostii

Chenopodina hostii, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Chenopodiaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive drought conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
