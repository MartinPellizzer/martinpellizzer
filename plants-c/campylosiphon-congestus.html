Campylosiphon congestus

Campylosiphon congestus, commonly known as the clustered siphon.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure and milky sap. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, while its flowers are clustered and showy, with five petals.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
