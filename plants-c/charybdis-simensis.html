Charybdis simensis

Charybdis simensis, commonly known as the giant red sea anemone, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the family Actiniidae.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, cylindrical body covered in long, venomous tentacles. The organism thrives in shallow tropical and subtropical waters, often found on coral reefs. Its vibrant red coloration serves as a warning to potential predators due to its potent toxins.

The plant-like appearance of the anemone is due to its sessile lifestyle and its symbiotic relationship with certain species of clownfish.
