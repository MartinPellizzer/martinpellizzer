Cyperus melanospermus var. hexalatus

Cyperus melanospermus var. hexalatus, commonly known as the blackseed sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Its fruiting structures, called achenes, are distinctive for their dark, hard seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
