Croton mazapensis

Croton mazapensis, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright yellow to deep red. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Croton mazapensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
