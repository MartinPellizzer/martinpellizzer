Cerastium campanulatum f. platyphyllum

Cerastium campanulatum f. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has rounded, fleshy leaves that are broader than the typical species, giving it its distinguishing feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies for its purported calming properties.
