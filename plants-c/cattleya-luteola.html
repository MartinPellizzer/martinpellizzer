Cattleya luteola

Cattleya luteola, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive slipper-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike with several blooms.

Cattleya luteola is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
