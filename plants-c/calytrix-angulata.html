Calytrix angulata

Calytrix angulata, commonly known as the angular calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive angular shape, giving it its common name.

Calytrix angulata is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in dunes and rocky habitats.
