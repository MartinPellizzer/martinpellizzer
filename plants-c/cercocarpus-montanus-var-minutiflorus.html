Cercocarpus montanus var. minutiflorus

Cercocarpus montanus var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the Mojave creosotebush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has aromatic, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

The Mojave creosotebush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
