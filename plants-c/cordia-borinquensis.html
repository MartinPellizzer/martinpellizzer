Cordia borinquensis

Cordia borinquensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican cotton tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sandy soils, making it an important component of Puerto Rico's coastal ecosystems.
