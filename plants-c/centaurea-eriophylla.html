Centaurea eriophylla

Centaurea eriophylla, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea eriophylla is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
