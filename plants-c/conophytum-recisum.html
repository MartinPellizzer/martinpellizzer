Conophytum recisum

Conophytum recisum, commonly known as the "Cape Rhabdodendron," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, basal rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves. The leaves are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge, especially when exposed to full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-drained soil.
