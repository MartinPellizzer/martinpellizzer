Cnidium dubium

Cnidium dubium, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Cnidium dubium is often found in moist, shaded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
