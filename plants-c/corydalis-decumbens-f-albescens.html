Corydalis decumbens f. albescens

Corydalis decumbens f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered corydalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its creeping stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are typically green, while the flowers are notable for their pale white to cream coloration. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.
