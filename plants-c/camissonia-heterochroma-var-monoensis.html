Camissonia heterochroma var. monoensis

Camissonia heterochroma var. monoensis, commonly known as the Mono Mountain larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and are often covered in fine hairs.
