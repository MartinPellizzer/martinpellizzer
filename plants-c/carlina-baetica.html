Carlina baetica

Carlina baetica, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Carlina baetica is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
