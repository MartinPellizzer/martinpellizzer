Chenopodium chilense var. incisa

Chenopodium chilense var. incisa, commonly known as the Chilean knotweed, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, terminal clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Chenopodium chilense var. incisa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
