Cousinia elymaitica

Cousinia elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymaitic cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Cousinia elymaitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
