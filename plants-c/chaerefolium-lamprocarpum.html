Chaerefolium lamprocarpum

Chaerefolium lamprocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume with compound leaves that have a distinctive silvery-gray appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
