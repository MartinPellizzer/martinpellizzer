Caladenia latifolia var. alba

Caladenia latifolia var. alba, commonly known as the White-veined Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, showy flowers with a white to pale pink coloration. The petals and sepals are often adorned with distinctive veining, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens due to its unique and elegant appearance.
