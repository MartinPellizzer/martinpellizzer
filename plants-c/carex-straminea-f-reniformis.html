Carex straminea f. reniformis

Carex straminea f. reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, flat, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows.

This variety is notable for its distinctive leaf shape and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
