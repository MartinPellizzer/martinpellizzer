Campnosperma gummiferum

Campnosperma gummiferum, commonly known as the gum tree or black wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its dark, flaky bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in forested areas.

Campnosperma gummiferum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
