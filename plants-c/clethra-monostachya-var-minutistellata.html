Clethra monostachya var. minutistellata

Clethra monostachya var. minutistellata, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pepperbush, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and fragrant blooms, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
