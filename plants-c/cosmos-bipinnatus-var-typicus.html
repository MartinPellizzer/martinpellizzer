Cosmos bipinnatus var. typicus

Cosmos bipinnatus var. typicus, commonly known as the Chinese aster, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. It is typically grown as an annual, blooming from summer to fall, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in garden ecosystems.
