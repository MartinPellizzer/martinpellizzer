Cyathea paleolata var. villosa

Cyathea paleolata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant's fronds are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
