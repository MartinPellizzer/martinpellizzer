Cyathea hewittii

Cyathea hewittii, commonly known as the Hewitt's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea hewittii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
