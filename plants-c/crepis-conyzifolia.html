Crepis conyzifolia

Crepis conyzifolia, commonly known as the woolly goatgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. Its leaves are covered in dense, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis conyzifolia is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.
