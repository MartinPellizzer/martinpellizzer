Cirsium quercetorum var. walkerianum

Cirsium quercetorum var. walkerianum, commonly known as the Walker's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts that form a distinctive involucrum.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
