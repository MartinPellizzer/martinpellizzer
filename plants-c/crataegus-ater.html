Crataegus ater

Crataegus ater, commonly known as the black hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and wildlife. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters. Crataegus ater is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
