Cousinia outichaschensis f. commixta

Cousinia outichaschensis f. commixta, commonly known as the mixed-flowered cushion cinquefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique floral arrangement.

This perennial plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. It features dense, low-growing cushions of foliage with tightly packed, small, composite flowers. The inflorescence displays a mix of yellow and white florets, contributing to its ornamental value.

Its adaptations to harsh climates make it a resilient and hardy species within its native range.
