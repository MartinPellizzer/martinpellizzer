Carex qualicumensis

Carex qualicumensis, commonly known as the Qualicum sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, greenish-white flowers.

Carex qualicumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
