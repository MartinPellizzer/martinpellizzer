Calandrinia gayana var. hirtella

Calandrinia gayana var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in the morning and close in the heat of the day.
