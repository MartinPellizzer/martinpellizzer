Cytisus glaber

Cytisus glaber, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus glaber is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
