Centaurea × sanchisiana var. pectiniformis

Centaurea × sanchisiana var. pectiniformis, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescence that resembles a tuft of wool, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is native to the Mediterranean region.
