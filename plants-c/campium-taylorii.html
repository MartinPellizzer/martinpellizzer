Campium taylorii

Campium taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's campion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Campium taylorii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
