Carex excisa

Carex excisa, commonly known as the cut-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are sharply cut and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex excisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
