Copaifera pubiflora

Copaifera pubiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate. Copaifera pubiflora is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife in its native habitat.
