Centaurea boissieri subsp. boissieri

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces cylindrical, bristly seed heads that are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
