Carex scirpoides var. capillacea

Carex scirpoides var. capillacea, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
