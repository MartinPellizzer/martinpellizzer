Combesia muscosa var. purpusii

Combesia muscosa var. purpusii, commonly known as the mossy combesia, is a variant of the combesia genus characterized by its dense, moss-like growth habit.

It typically forms low, compact mats that thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has small, overlapping leaves that are usually green to reddish in color, depending on environmental conditions. It is a non-vascular plant, meaning it lacks true roots, stems, and leaves in the traditional sense.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of high humidity and undisturbed habitats.
