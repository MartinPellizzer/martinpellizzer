Campanula rotundifolia f. parviflora

Campanula rotundifolia f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, often hairy leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. This cultivar is a compact form of the species, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
