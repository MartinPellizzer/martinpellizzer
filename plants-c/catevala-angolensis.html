Catevala angolensis

Catevala angolensis, commonly known as the Angola cat's claw, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hooked thorns and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by leguminous pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
