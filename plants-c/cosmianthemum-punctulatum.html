Cosmianthemum punctulatum

Cosmianthemum punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted cosmos, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, bright pink or red flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The spotted cosmos is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
