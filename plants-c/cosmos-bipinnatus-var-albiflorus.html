Cosmos bipinnatus var. albiflorus

Cosmos bipinnatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and easy-to-grow plant for both novice and experienced gardeners.
