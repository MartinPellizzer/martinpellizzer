Callista bifalcis

Callista bifalcis, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with intricate, colorful petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
