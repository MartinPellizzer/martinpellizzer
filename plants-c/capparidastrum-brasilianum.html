Capparidastrum brasilianum

Capparidastrum brasilianum, commonly known as Brazilian caper or Brazilian caper bush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

The fruit is small, capsule-like, and contains several seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
