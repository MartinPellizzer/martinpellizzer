Cardamine pseudopratensis

Cardamine pseudopratensis, commonly known as the false spring beauty, is a delicate herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.
