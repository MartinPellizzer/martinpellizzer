Crocanthus luteolus

Crocanthus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow crocanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
