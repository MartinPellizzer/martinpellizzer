Campuloclinium hirsutum

Campuloclinium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy campuloclinium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
