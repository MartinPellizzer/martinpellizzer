Carex vulgaris var. turfosa

Carex vulgaris var. turfosa, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
