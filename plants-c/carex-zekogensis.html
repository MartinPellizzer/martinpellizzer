Carex zekogensis

Carex zekogensis, commonly known as the Hainan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex zekogensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
