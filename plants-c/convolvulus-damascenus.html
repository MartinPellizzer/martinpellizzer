Convolvulus damascenus

Convolvulus damascenus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
