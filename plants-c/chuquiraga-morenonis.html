Chuquiraga morenonis

Chuquiraga morenonis, commonly known as the Andean burr bush, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces distinctive burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by clinging to animals. Its leaves are typically hairy and adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment.

Chuquiraga morenonis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
