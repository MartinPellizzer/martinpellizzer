Cardamine caledonica

Cardamine caledonica, commonly known as the Scottish water-cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams, in woodlands, and in damp grasslands. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and cress species.
