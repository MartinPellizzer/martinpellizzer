Croton colombianus

Croton colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton colombianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
