Crataegus porteri var. caerulescens

Crataegus porteri var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purplish fruit, which matures in the fall, and its thorny branches that provide structural support. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the distinctive blue berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting birds and supporting local biodiversity.
