Cyperus vestitus

Cyperus vestitus, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus vestitus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
