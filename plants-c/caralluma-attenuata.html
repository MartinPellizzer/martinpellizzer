Caralluma attenuata

Caralluma attenuata, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are segmented and often adorned with spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange during the holiday season, typically between November and January. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Caralluma attenuata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
