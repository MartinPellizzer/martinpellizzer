Chenopodium melanocarpum

Chenopodium melanocarpum, commonly known as blackstem goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Chenopodium melanocarpum is an important species for wildlife, providing food for birds and small mammals.
