Ceropegia intermedia var. wightii

Ceropegia intermedia var. wightii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, trailing stems with small, waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, often with a slight violet hue. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
