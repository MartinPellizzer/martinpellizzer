Coussapoa asperifolia subsp. rhamnoides

Coussapoa asperifolia subsp.

rhamnoides, commonly known as the rough-leaved coussapoa, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, often glandular, leaves.

The leaves are typically elliptical to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

This plant is adapted to humid, tropical conditions and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
