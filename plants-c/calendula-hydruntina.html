Calendula hydruntina

Calendula hydruntina, commonly known as the sea calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are hairy and serrated.

Calendula hydruntina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
