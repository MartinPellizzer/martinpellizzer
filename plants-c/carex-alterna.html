Carex alterna

Carex alterna, commonly known as the alternate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Native to parts of Asia, Carex alterna thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
