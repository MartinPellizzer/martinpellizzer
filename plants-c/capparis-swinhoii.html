Capparis swinhoii

Capparis swinhoii, commonly known as the Chinese Caper or Swinho's Caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, green berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Capparis swinhoii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
