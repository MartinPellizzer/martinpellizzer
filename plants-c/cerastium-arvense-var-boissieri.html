Cerastium arvense var. boissieri

Cerastium arvense var. boissieri, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread via runners make it a common weed in cultivated areas.
