Ceropegia mutabilis

Ceropegia mutabilis, commonly known as the variable ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, often pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a somewhat fleshy texture, adapted to store water.

The species is noted for its ability to produce flowers that change color as they mature, adding to its ornamental appeal.
