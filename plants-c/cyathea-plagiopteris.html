Cyathea plagiopteris

Cyathea plagiopteris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a distinctive, slightly curved shape. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
