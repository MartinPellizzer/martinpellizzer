Calamintha chinensis var. souliei

Calamintha chinensis var. souliei, commonly known as Chinese calamint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
