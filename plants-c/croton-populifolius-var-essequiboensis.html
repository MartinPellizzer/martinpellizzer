Croton populifolius var. essequiboensis

Croton populifolius var. essequiboensis, commonly known as the Essequibo croton, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of the poplar tree, giving it its species name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
