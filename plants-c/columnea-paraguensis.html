Columnea paraguensis

Columnea paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or trailing vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its nectar-rich blooms.
