Centaurea napulifera

Centaurea napulifera, commonly known as the Naples thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive blue or purple hue on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Centaurea napulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
