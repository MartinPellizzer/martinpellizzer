Clematopsis costata

Clematopsis costata, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, fragrant, tubular flower that is typically yellow or orange. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Clematopsis costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
