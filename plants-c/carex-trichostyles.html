Carex trichostyles

Carex trichostyles, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Carex trichostyles plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
