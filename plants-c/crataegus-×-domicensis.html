Crataegus × domicensis

Crataegus × domicensis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its small, thorned stems and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and some mammals. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Crataegus × domicensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
