Cyperus cyperoides var. subcompositus

Cyperus cyperoides var. subcompositus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture, and it is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
