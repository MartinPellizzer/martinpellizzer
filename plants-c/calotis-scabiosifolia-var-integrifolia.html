Calotis scabiosifolia var. integrifolia

Calotis scabiosifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf calotis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have smooth margins, distinguishing it from other varieties of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
