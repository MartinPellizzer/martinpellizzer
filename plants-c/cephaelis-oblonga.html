Cephaelis oblonga

Cephaelis oblonga, commonly known as the purple-flowered celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy purple or pink flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Cephaelis oblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
