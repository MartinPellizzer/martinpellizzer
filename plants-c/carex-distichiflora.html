Carex distichiflora

Carex distichiflora, commonly known as the slender flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
