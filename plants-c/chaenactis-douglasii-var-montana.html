Chaenactis douglasii var. montana

Chaenactis douglasii var. montana, commonly known as mountain paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small brush-like structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a silvery or grayish hue, and its flowers bloom in shades of pink to purple.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
