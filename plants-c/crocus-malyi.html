Crocus malyi

Crocus malyi, commonly known as the Malyi crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring season. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Crocus malyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
