Caltha populago

Caltha populago, commonly known as the marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white down.

Caltha populago is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
