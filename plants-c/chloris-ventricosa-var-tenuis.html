Chloris ventricosa var. tenuis

Chloris ventricosa var. tenuis, commonly known as slender hill barley, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
