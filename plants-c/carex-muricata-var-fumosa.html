Carex muricata var. fumosa

Carex muricata var. fumosa, commonly known as the smoky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive purplish or smoky hue along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex muricata var. fumosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
