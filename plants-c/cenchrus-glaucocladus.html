Cenchrus glaucocladus

Cenchrus glaucocladus, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cenchrus glaucocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
