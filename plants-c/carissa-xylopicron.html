Carissa xylopicron

Carissa xylopicron, commonly known as the Natal plume or Natal kaross, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and attractive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Carissa xylopicron is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
