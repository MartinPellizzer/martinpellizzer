Coreopsis pickeringii

Coreopsis pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to moist to wet conditions.

Coreopsis pickeringii is a valuable plant for wetland restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
