Ceropegia mairei var. tenella

Ceropegia mairei var. tenella, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
