Carduus platypus var. maurus

Carduus platypus var. maurus, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
