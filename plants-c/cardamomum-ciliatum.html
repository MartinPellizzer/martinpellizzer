Cardamomum ciliatum

Cardamomum ciliatum, commonly known as the mountain cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are dark green, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its fragrant seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
