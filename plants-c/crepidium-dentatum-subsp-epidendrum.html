Crepidium dentatum subsp. epidendrum

Crepidium dentatum subsp.

epidendrum, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its erect, wiry stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
