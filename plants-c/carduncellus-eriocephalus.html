Carduncellus eriocephalus

Carduncellus eriocephalus, commonly known as the woolly cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem and large, woolly leaves that are often used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisines. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, forming a dense, rounded head that attracts pollinators.

The plant is valued both for its edible parts and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
