Calamus ocreatus

Calamus ocreatus, commonly known as the hooded canescent, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in wet, shady environments such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Calamus ocreatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
