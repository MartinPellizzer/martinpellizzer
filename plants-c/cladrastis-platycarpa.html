Cladrastis platycarpa

Cladrastis platycarpa, commonly known as the sweetcliff tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with ovate to elliptic leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in racemes during the spring, which are followed by smooth, spherical fruits that mature to a dark blue or black color. Its botanical features make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological studies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
