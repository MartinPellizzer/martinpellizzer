Cytisus leucanthus var. bizanthynus

Cytisus leucanthus var. bizanthynus, commonly known as the Greek yellow broom, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. The plant has slender, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in gardens and natural landscapes.
