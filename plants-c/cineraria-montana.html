Cineraria montana

Cineraria montana, commonly known as the mountain cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers.

Cineraria montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
