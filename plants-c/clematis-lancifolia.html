Clematis lancifolia

Clematis lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in summer. Its botanical characteristics include a complex inflorescence with bracts and a distinctive, five-lobed corolla.

Clematis lancifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
