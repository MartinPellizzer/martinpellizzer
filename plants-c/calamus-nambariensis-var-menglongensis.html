Calamus nambariensis var. menglongensis

Calamus nambariensis var. menglongensis, commonly known as Menglong bamboo, is a unique variant of the calamus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic materials. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its cultural and practical significance in local communities.
