Canthium monoflorum

Canthium monoflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, which is a characteristic feature of the spurge family.

Canthium monoflorum produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds.
