Centaurea alba subsp. deusta

Centaurea alba subsp.

deusta, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
