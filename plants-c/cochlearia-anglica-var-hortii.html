Cochlearia anglica var. hortii

Cochlearia anglica var. hortii, commonly known as the English cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single umbel. The plant is native to the British Isles and thrives in coastal and calcareous soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it tolerates salt spray and poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
