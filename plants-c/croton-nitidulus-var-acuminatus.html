Croton nitidulus var. acuminatus

Croton nitidulus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
