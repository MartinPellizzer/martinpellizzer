Chusquea cylindrica

Chusquea cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Chusquea cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a landscape plant.
