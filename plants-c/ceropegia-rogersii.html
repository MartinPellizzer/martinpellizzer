Ceropegia rogersii

Ceropegia rogersii, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, serpentine stems that curl and twist, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia rogersii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
