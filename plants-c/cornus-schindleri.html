Cornus schindleri

Cornus schindleri, commonly known as the Schindler's dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typical of the Cornaceae family. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Cornus schindleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
