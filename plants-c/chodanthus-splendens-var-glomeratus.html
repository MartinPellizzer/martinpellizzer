Chodanthus splendens var. glomeratus

Chodanthus splendens var. glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered splendor cactus, is a succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with short, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-water environments.
