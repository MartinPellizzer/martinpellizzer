Chenopodium buchananii

Chenopodium buchananii, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Chenopodium buchananii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
