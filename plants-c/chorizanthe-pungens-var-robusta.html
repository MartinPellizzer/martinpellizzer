Chorizanthe pungens var. robusta

Chorizanthe pungens var. robusta, commonly known as the robust choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bristly, spiny stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
