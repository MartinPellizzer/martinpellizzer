Carduus pinnatifidus

Carduus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides. It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to survive in arid environments.
