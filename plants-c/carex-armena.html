Carex armena

Carex armena, commonly known as Armenian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Carex armena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
