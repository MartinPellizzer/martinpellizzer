Cerastium quaternellum subsp. octandrum

Cerastium quaternellum subsp.

octandrum, commonly known as the eight-stamen chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, white flowers with eight stamens, giving it its specific epithet "octandrum.".

The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges or rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
