Callitriche

Callitriche, commonly known as water starwort, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants belonging to the family Callitrichaceae.

These plants typically grow in shallow water, such as ponds, streams, and wetlands, and are characterized by their small, floating or submerged leaves. They often have tiny, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink, which help in attracting pollinators. Callitriche species are known for their rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.

Some species are used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
