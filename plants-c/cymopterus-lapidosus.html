Cymopterus lapidosus

Cymopterus lapidosus, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its root system is deep and adapted to extract moisture from dry conditions.

The plant is also known for its aromatic scent and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
