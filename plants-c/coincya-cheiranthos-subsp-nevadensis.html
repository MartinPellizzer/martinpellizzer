Coincya cheiranthos subsp. nevadensis

Coincya cheiranthos subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is of interest in ecological studies of alpine flora.
