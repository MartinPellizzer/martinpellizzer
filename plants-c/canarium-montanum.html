Canarium montanum

Canarium montanum, commonly known as mountain candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree is also valued for its timber.

Canarium montanum thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.
