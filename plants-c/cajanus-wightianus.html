Cajanus wightianus

Cajanus wightianus, commonly known as the Wight's pigeonpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are used for food and fodder. Cajanus wightianus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated in warm climates.
