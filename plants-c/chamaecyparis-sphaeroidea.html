Chamaecyparis sphaeroidea

Chamaecyparis sphaeroidea, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or kuril cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan and the Kuril Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and dense, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, rounded cones and is often cultivated as a ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological applications.
