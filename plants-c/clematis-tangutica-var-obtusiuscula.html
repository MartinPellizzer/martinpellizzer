Clematis tangutica var. obtusiuscula

Clematis tangutica var. obtusiuscula, commonly known as the Tangut Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its form.
