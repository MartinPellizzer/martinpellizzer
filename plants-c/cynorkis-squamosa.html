Cynorkis squamosa

Cynorkis squamosa, commonly known as the scaly orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, scaly appearance on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
