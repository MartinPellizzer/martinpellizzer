Centaurea scabiosa subsp. integra

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

integra, commonly known as the common knapweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its striking purple to pink composite flowers and spiny, bristly foliage.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract a variety of pollinators.

The subspecies integra is distinguished by its more robust growth and fully developed leaves compared to other subspecies.
