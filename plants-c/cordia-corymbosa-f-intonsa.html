Cordia corymbosa f. intonsa

Cordia corymbosa f. intonsa, commonly known as the white-flowered cordia, is a variant of the Cordia corymbosa species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its white, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, fleshy seeds.
