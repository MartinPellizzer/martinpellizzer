Commelina debilis

Commelina debilis, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina debilis is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
