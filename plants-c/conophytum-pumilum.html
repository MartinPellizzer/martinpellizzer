Conophytum pumilum

Conophytum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf conophytum, is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Conophytum pumilum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
