Carex laevivaginata

Carex laevivaginata, commonly known as the smooth sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex laevivaginata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
