Chloranthus obtusifolius

Chloranthus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaved chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are often subtended by leaf-like bracts. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest in botanical studies due to its primitive characteristics.
