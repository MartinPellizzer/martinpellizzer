Cinnamomum thunbergii

Cinnamomum thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Thunberg's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
