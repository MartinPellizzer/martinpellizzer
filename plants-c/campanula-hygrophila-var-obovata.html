Campanula hygrophila var. obovata

Campanula hygrophila var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
