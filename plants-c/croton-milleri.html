Croton milleri

Croton milleri, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a houseplant or in gardens.
