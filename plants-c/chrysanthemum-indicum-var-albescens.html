Chrysanthemum indicum var. albescens

Chrysanthemum indicum var. albescens, commonly known as the white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its pale to white petals and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

This variety is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
