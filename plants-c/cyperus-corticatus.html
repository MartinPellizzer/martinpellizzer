Cyperus corticatus

Cyperus corticatus, commonly known as the paper reed or slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a flowering structure called a spikelet. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, preferring shallow water or moist soil. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Cyperus corticatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
