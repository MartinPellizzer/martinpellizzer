Callicarpa tectonifolia

Callicarpa tectonifolia, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
