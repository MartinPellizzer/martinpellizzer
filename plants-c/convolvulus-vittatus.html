Convolvulus vittatus

Convolvulus vittatus, commonly known as the spotted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
