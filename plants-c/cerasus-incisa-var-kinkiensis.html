Cerasus incisa var. kinkiensis

Cerasus incisa var. kinkiensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or kinkan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young.
