Clematis brachyura

Clematis brachyura, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Clematis brachyura thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its climbing habit makes it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
