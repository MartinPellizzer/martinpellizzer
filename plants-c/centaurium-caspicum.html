Centaurium caspicum

Centaurium caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian centaury, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are typically pale blue or purple in color.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.
