Crotalaria uguenensis

Crotalaria uguenensis, commonly known as the Uguen's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria uguenensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
