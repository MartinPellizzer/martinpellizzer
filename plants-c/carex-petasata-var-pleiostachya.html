Carex petasata var. pleiostachya

Carex petasata var. pleiostachya, commonly known as the many-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is notable for its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist to wet environments. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
