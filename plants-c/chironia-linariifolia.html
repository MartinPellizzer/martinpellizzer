Chironia linariifolia

Chironia linariifolia, commonly known as the linaria-leaved chironia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other small insects, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

Chironia linariifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
