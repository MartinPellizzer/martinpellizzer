Chenopodium gigantospermum var. typicum

Chenopodium gigantospermum var. typicum, commonly known as giant goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny shell. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its succulent leaves and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying desert flora.
