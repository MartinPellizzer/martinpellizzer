Chenopodium bushianum var. cinerascens

Chenopodium bushianum var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization in dryland ecosystems.
