Cacalia tanalensis

Cacalia tanalensis, commonly known as the Tanal's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root systems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, featuring delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Cacalia tanalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
