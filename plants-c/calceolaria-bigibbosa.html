Calceolaria bigibbosa

Calceolaria bigibbosa, commonly known as the two-lipped calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria bigibbosa thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
