Chrysojasminum leptophyllum

Chrysojasminum leptophyllum, commonly known as the golden jasmine or white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually simple, opposite, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
