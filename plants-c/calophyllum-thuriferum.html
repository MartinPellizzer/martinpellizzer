Calophyllum thuriferum

Calophyllum thuriferum, commonly known as the sacred sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrant properties and is used in religious ceremonies and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
