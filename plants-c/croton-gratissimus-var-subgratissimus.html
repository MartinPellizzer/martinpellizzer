Croton gratissimus var. subgratissimus

Croton gratissimus var. subgratissimus, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
