Centaurea thrinciifolia

Centaurea thrinciifolia, commonly known as the Thrace clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically surrounded by red or purple bracts. The plant grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a slender, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Centaurea thrinciifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
