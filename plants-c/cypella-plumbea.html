Cypella plumbea

Cypella plumbea, commonly known as the blue cypella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Cypella plumbea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
