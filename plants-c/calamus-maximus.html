Calamus maximus

Calamus maximus, commonly known as the giant reed or arrowroot plant, is a tall, rhizomatous perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, commonly known as the palm family, though it is not a true palm. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Calamus maximus thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
