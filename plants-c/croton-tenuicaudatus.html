Croton tenuicaudatus

Croton tenuicaudatus, commonly known as the flame tree or torch tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
