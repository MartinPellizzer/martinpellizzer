Capparis panamensis

Capparis panamensis, commonly known as the Panama caper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for traditional uses.
