Crassula ramuliflora var. typica

Crassula ramuliflora var. typica, commonly known as the Rosary Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, shrub-like growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
