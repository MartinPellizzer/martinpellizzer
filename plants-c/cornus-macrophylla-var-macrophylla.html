Cornus macrophylla var. macrophylla

Cornus macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
