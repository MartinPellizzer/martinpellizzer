Calceolaria serrata

Calceolaria serrata, commonly known as the serrated hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its common name, and it produces delicate, colorful blooms in shades of pink, purple, and white.

Calceolaria serrata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
