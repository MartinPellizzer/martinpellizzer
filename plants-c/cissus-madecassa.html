Cissus madecassa

Cissus madecassa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves that have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Cissus madecassa is also valued for its ability to climb and its use in traditional medicine.
