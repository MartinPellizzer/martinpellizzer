Corydalis fungosa

Corydalis fungosa, commonly known as the fungus corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant produces small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. Corydalis fungosa thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a cut flower.
