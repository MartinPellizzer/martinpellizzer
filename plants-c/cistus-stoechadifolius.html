Cistus stoechadifolius

Cistus stoechadifolius, commonly known as the Scottish heath or narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Scotland and northern England.

It is characterized by its grey-green, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathland and coastal habitats. It is a hardy species that can tolerate salt spray and is adapted to harsh, windy environments.

Cistus stoechadifolius plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
