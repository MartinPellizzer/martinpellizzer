Cleisostoma furcatum

Cleisostoma furcatum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has long, slender stems that often hang from trees, and its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Cleisostoma furcatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, aromatic blooms.
