Climacoptera susamyrica

Climacoptera susamyrica, commonly known as the cloud mountain fern, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and delicate, feathery fronds that resemble the structure of a fern. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in cloud forests. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
