Cynanchum annulare

Cynanchum annulare, commonly known as the annual milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and prairies. The plant features slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, with a hairy texture.

Cynanchum annulare is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.
