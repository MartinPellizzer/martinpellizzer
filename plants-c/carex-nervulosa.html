Carex nervulosa

Carex nervulosa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

Carex nervulosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
