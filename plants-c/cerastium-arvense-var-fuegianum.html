Cerastium arvense var. fuegianum

Cerastium arvense var. fuegianum, commonly known as the Fuegian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern Andes and Tierra del Fuego.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit with hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
