Coelogyne edentula

Coelogyne edentula, commonly known as the toothless orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant orchid blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal.

Coelogyne edentula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and greenhouses.
