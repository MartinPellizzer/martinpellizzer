Cyperus bulamensis

Cyperus bulamensis, commonly known as the Bulamensis flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its morphology features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus bulamensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
