Chaenomeles cathayensis

Chaenomeles cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to red flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and attractive, peeling bark.
