Cirrhopetalum aureum

Cirrhopetalum aureum, commonly known as the golden petaled orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small but highly ornamental, with a complex structure that includes a spurred lip.

Cirrhopetalum aureum is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
