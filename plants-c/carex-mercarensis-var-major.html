Carex mercarensis var. major

Carex mercarensis var. major, commonly known as the large bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing numerous tiny brown flowers.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
