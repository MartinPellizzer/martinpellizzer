Chaetochloa tenax

Chaetochloa tenax, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and wetland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often covered in silky hairs.

Chaetochloa tenax is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
