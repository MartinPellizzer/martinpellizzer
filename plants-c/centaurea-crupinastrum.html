Centaurea crupinastrum

Centaurea crupinastrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces achenes as its fruit type. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Centaurea crupinastrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
