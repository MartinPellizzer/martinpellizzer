Chenopodium candolleanum

Chenopodium candolleanum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible berries with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Chenopodium candolleanum is valued for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
