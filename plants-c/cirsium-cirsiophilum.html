Cirsium cirsiophilum

Cirsium cirsiophilum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
