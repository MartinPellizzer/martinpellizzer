Chrysophyllum granatense

Chrysophyllum granatense, commonly known as the granate star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications due to its unique flavor.
