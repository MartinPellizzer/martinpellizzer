Croton magniglandulifer

Croton magniglandulifer, commonly known as the large glandular croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent veins and numerous glandular structures on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
