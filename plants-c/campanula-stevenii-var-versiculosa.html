Campanula stevenii var. versiculosa

Campanula stevenii var. versiculosa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
