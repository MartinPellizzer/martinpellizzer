Carex consimilis

Carex consimilis, commonly known as the similar sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many Cyperaceae species.

Carex consimilis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
