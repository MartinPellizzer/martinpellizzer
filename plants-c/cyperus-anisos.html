Cyperus anisos

Cyperus anisos, commonly known as the broadleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Cyperus anisos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to tolerate wet conditions.
