Cobaea scandens var. foliis-variegatis

Cobaea scandens var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a striking flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or pink markings. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, which bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and elegant blossoms in gardens and as a climbing plant.
