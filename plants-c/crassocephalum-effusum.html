Crassocephalum effusum

Crassocephalum effusum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African violet.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
