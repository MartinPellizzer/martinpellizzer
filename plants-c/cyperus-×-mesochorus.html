Cyperus × mesochorus

Cyperus × mesochorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyperus ligularis and Cyperus difformis. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth makes it an effective plant for erosion control and wetland restoration projects.
