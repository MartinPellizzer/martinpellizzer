Couepia caryophylloides

Couepia caryophylloides, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often fragrant and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that mature into woody capsules containing seeds.

Couepia caryophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
