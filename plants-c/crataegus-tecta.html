Crataegus tecta

Crataegus tecta, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Crataegus tecta is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
