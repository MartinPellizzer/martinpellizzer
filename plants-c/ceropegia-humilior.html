Ceropegia humilior

Ceropegia humilior, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia humilior is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
