Calceolaria zamorana

Calceolaria zamorana, commonly known as the Zamora's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to purple, are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Calceolaria zamorana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
