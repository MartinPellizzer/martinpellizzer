Cirsium monspessulanum subsp. monspessulanum

Cirsium monspessulanum subsp.

monspessulanum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features spiny, upright stems and large, rough, gray-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its showy purple flowers appear in late summer, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
