Carex paradoxa f. brachystachya

Carex paradoxa f. brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stalked bog sedge, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
