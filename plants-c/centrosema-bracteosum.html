Centrosema bracteosum

Centrosema bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate centrosema, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a climbing habit and can be used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
