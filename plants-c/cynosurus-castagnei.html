Cynosurus castagnei

Cynosurus castagnei, commonly known as the Castagnei's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
