Carex vaginata var. altocaulis

Carex vaginata var. altocaulis, commonly known as the tall-sheath sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
