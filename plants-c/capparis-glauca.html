Capparis glauca

Capparis glauca, commonly known as the blue caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline and well-drained conditions. The plant features small, waxy, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
