Capparis aurantioides

Capparis aurantioides, commonly known as the orange caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces vibrant yellow flowers. The plant has thorned stems and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, edible seeds.

Capparis aurantioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
