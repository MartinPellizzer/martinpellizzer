Carex × stygia

Carex × stygia, commonly known as the black sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
