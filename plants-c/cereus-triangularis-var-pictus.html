Cereus triangularis var. pictus

Cereus triangularis var. pictus, commonly known as the Variegated Cactus, is a unique species native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its variegated leaves, which have a distinctive mottled appearance, make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
