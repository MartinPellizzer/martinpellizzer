Carex laxiflora var. michauxii

Carex laxiflora var. michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and it serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
