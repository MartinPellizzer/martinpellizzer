Cusparia officinalis

Cusparia officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its historical use in traditional medicine highlights its significance in herbal remedies.
