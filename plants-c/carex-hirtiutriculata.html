Carex hirtiutriculata

Carex hirtiutriculata, commonly known as the hairy-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate climates.

Carex hirtiutriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
