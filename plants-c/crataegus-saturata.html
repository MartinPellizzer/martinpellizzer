Crataegus saturata

Crataegus saturata, commonly known as the red chokecherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by edible red berries in late summer. These berries are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
