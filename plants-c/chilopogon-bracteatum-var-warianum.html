Chilopogon bracteatum var. warianum

Chilopogon bracteatum var. warianum, commonly known as the Warianum daisy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of white ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
