Cacalia arborescens var. lessingiana

Cacalia arborescens var. lessingiana, commonly known as the Lessing's tree dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features a woody base with soft, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
