Cattleya quadricolor

Cattleya quadricolor, commonly known as the four-color orchid, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, multicolored flowers.

It originates from the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves and produces showy blooms with four distinct color zones on each petal. It is typically grown as a sympodial orchid, requiring well-draining potting media and bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated for floral arrangements.
