Cirsium clavatum var. americanum

Cirsium clavatum var. americanum, commonly known as the American thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
