Capparis longifolia

Capparis longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that mature into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, waxy texture.

Capparis longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
