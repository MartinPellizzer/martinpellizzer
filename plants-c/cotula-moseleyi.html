Cotula moseleyi

Cotula moseleyi, commonly known as the yellow starbursh, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cotula moseleyi typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact size and vibrant blooms.
