Corynella gracilis

Corynella gracilis, commonly known as the slender corymbia, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and paperbark trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Corynella gracilis is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with salt spray tolerance.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
