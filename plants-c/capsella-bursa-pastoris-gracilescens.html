Capsella bursa-pastoris gracilescens

Capsella bursa-pastoris gracilescens, commonly known as the common chickweed or lesser mouse-ear.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small white or pink flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of hairy, bladder-like structures called silicles that contain seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is widespread across temperate regions.
