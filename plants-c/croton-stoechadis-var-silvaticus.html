Croton stoechadis var. silvaticus

Croton stoechadis var. silvaticus, commonly known as the forest croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or dense woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
