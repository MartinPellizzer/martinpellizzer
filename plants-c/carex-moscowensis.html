Carex moscowensis

Carex moscowensis, commonly known as Moscow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
