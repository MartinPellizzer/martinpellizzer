Calamagrostis montevidensis var. ampliflora

Calamagrostis montevidensis var. ampliflora, commonly known as the Montevideo grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicle-like inflorescences that produce abundant, fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
