Coreopsis linearifolia

Coreopsis linearifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
