Chrysocoma pinifolia

Chrysocoma pinifolia, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
