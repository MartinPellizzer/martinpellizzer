Cassia bonariensis

Cassia bonariensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia bonariensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
