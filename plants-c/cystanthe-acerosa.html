Cystanthe acerosa

Cystanthe acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its stiff, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Cystanthe acerosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
