Convolvulus hermanniae

Convolvulus hermanniae, commonly known as Hermann's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that help it climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Convolvulaceae family.
