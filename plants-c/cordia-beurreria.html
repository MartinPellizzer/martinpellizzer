Cordia beurreria

Cordia beurreria, commonly known as the buttercup tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cordia beurreria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
