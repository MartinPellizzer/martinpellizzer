Carex oedipostyla

Carex oedipostyla, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetlands and moist habitats in North America, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spike of tiny, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
