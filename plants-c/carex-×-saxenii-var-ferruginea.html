Carex × saxenii var. ferruginea

Carex × saxenii var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, usually brown or reddish flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive reddish or rust-colored hue, especially along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its texture and color.
