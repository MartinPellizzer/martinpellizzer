Cirsium × freyerianum

Cirsium × freyerianum, commonly known as the Freyer's thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, showy purple or pink flower heads. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive tendencies in some regions, it is sometimes cultivated but requires careful management.
