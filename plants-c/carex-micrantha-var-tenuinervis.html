Carex micrantha var. tenuinervis

Carex micrantha var. tenuinervis, commonly known as narrow-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
