Centaurium texense

Centaurium texense, commonly known as Texas mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, particularly Texas. The plant features small, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurium texense is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a rare species within its range.
