Convolvulus mesatlanticus

Convolvulus mesatlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
