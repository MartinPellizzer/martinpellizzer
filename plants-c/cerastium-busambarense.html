Cerastium busambarense

Cerastium busambarense, commonly known as the mountain chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cerastium busambarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
