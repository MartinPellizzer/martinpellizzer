Cyperus balfourii var. tsiafajavonensis

Cyperus balfourii var. tsiafajavonensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas with saturated soil.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations in tropical ecosystems.
