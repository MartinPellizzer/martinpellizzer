Cryptocarya concinna

Cryptocarya concinna, commonly known as the Australian nut or slender nut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain one or two edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Indigenous practices. Cryptocarya concinna thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
