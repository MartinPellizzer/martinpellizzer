Cineraria longifolia var. macrochaeta

Cineraria longifolia var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the longleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
