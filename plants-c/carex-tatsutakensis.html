Carex tatsutakensis

Carex tatsutakensis, commonly known as the Tatsutake sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
