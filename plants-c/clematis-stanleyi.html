Clematis stanleyi

Clematis stanleyi, commonly known as Stanley's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, showy form. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a support structure for climbing.

Clematis stanleyi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various climates.
