Cymbopogon figarianus

Cymbopogon figarianus, commonly known as the African lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cymbopogon figarianus is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
