Calanthe madagascariensis

Calanthe madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinct, intricate lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
