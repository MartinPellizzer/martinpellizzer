Cucumis melo var. carthagenens

Cucumis melo var. carthagenens, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, striped fruit with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and palmate leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
