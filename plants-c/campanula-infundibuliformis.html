Campanula infundibuliformis

Campanula infundibuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine displays.
