Cinnamomum chloroxylon

Cinnamomum chloroxylon, commonly known as wild cinnamon or Indian cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by reddish-brown, capsule-like fruits. The tree is valued for its aromatic bark, which contains essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, smooth bark, and a dense canopy of foliage.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
