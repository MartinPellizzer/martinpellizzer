Cavendishia tarapotana var. gilgiana

Cavendishia tarapotana var. gilgiana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
