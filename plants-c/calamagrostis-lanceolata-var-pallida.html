Calamagrostis lanceolata var. pallida

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. pallida, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, oat-like seed heads that add ornamental value in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
