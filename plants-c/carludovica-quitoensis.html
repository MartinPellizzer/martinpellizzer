Carludovica quitoensis

Carludovica quitoensis, commonly known as the Quito poinsettia, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers, which bloom during the holiday season. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its succulent, paddle-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant, especially during Christmas and New Year celebrations.
