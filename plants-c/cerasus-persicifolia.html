Cerasus persicifolia

Cerasus persicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Cerasus persicifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
