Canthium dyscriton

Canthium dyscriton, commonly known as the variable spurred spurge, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments in tropical regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy texture.

Canthium dyscriton produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in cyathium clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
