Convolvulus laciniatus

Convolvulus laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. It is a climbing vine that thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
