Cyrtomium macrophyllum var. acuminatum

Cyrtomium macrophyllum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Cyrtomium.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with pinnae that are acutely pointed, giving it its specific epithet.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens due to its attractive foliage.
