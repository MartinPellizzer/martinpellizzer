Carduus macrolepis

Carduus macrolepis, commonly known as the large-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
