Carex helferi

Carex helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Carex helferi is also known for its ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
