Crotalaria criniramea

Crotalaria criniramea, commonly known as the silky crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, yellowish-white flower clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its unique floral display.
