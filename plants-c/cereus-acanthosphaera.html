Cereus acanthosphaera

Cereus acanthosphaera, commonly known as the white star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinct appearance, featuring white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, rounded fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
