Clematis baominiana

Clematis baominiana, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a flowering vine native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its long, climbing stems and showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clematis baominiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
