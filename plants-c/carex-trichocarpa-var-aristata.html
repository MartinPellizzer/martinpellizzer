Carex trichocarpa var. aristata

Carex trichocarpa var. aristata, commonly known as the arrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
