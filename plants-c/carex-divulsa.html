Carex divulsa

Carex divulsa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
