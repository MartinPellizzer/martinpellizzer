Cadia multifoliolata

Cadia multifoliolata, commonly known as the multi-leaved cadia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
