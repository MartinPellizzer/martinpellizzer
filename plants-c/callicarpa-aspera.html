Callicarpa aspera

Callicarpa aspera, commonly known as rough beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
