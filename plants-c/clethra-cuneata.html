Clethra cuneata

Clethra cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet fern, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Clethra cuneata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
