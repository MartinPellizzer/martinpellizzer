Cavendishia urichiana

Cavendishia urichiana, commonly known as the Urichiana Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions with its succulent leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
