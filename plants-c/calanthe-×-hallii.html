Calanthe × hallii

Calanthe × hallii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a species resulting from the cross between Calanthe triplicata and Calanthe formosana.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Calanthe × hallii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
