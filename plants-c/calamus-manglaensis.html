Calamus manglaensis

It is native to the wetlands and marshy areas of northern India, particularly in the state of Himachal Pradesh. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense stands, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss.
