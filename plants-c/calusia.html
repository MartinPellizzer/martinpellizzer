Calusia

Calusia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Calusia is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
