Cachrys acaulis

Cachrys acaulis, commonly known as the cushion cushionflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, hairy stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
