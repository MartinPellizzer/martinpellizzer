Cirsium ugoense

Cirsium ugoense, commonly known as the Ugo's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium ugoense is known for its hardy nature and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
