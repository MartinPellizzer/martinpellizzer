Capsella cordata

Capsella cordata, commonly known as heartleaf or heart-leaf.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually cordate, with a broad base and a pointed tip, while its flowers are arranged in clusters.

Capsella cordata is an annual plant that produces small, dry fruits called silicles, which contain several seeds.
