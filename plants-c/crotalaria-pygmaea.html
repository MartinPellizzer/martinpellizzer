Crotalaria pygmaea

Crotalaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open when mature.

Crotalaria pygmaea is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
