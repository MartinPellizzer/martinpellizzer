Crepis polymorpha var. virens

Crepis polymorpha var. virens, commonly known as the green-headed mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves, and the plant is characterized by its compact growth habit.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
