Cassia × homophylla

Cassia × homophylla, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or Vietnamese cinnamon, is a hybrid shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Cassia × homophylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
