Coleus glabratus

Coleus glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a potted plant.
