Campanula thaliana var. monostachya

Campanula thaliana var. monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a hairy texture. This perennial species thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
