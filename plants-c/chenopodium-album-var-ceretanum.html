Chenopodium album var. ceretanum

Chenopodium album var. ceretanum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with reddish stems that become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields.
