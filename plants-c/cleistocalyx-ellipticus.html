Cleistocalyx ellipticus

Cleistocalyx ellipticus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a velvety texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cleistocalyx ellipticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
