Coleus efoliatus

Coleus efoliatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for containers and decorative arrangements.
