Caragana sinica

Caragana sinica, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Caragana sinica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
