Canarium lagunense

Canarium lagunense, commonly known as the Guinean candlenut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed. Canarium lagunense is often cultivated for its aromatic resin, which has traditional uses in incense and medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
