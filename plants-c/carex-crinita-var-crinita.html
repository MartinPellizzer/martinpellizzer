Carex crinita var. crinita

Carex crinita var. crinita, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
