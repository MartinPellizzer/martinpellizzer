Cyperus strigosus var. compositus

Cyperus strigosus var. compositus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in a panicle, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
