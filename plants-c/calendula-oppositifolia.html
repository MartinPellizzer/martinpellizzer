Calendula oppositifolia

Calendula oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has opposite leaves that are oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it grows up to 30 centimeters in height. Calendula oppositifolia is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
