Carex davidi

Carex davidi, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland gardens or stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer.

Carex davidi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
