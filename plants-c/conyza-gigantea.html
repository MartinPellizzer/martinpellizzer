Conyza gigantea

Conyza gigantea, commonly known as giant cudweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Conyza gigantea is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
