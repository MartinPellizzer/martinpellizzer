Carex digitata subsp. pallens

Carex digitata subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
