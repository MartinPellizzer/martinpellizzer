Cistus fasciculatus

Cistus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Cistus fasciculatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
