Corymbia × paractia

Corymbia × paractia, commonly known as the red gum or red blood gum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Corymbia ficifolia and Corymbia tessellaris.

It is a tall, spreading tree with a rough, fissured bark that often displays a reddish hue. The leaves are lanceolate, glossy, and dark green, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

The tree is well-suited to coastal and subcoastal regions, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
