Commelina spirata

Commelina spirata, commonly known as spiral arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with striking floral structures. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the arum family.

Commelina spirata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
