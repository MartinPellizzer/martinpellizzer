Calliandra vespertina

Calliandra vespertina, commonly known as evening flame, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
