Crotalaria mucronata var. acutifolia

Crotalaria mucronata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
