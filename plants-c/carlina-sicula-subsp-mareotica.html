Carlina sicula subsp. mareotica

Carlina sicula subsp.

mareotica, commonly known as the Mareotic carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite heads. Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering period from summer to autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
