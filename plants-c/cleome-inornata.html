Cleome inornata

Cleome inornata, commonly known as the common cleome, is an annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Cleome inornata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
