Cheilanthes ternifolia var. lanuginosa

Cheilanthes ternifolia var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly cliff brake, is a perennial fern native to arid and rocky environments in North America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and hairy, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on cliff faces or in crevices. Its fronds are typically dark green and develop spore-producing structures on their undersides.

This variety is notable for its adaptation to harsh, semi-arid conditions.
