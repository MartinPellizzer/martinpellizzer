Caperonia cordata

Caperonia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf caper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that often grows close to the ground.

Caperonia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
