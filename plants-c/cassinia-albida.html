Cassinia albida

Cassinia albida, commonly known as the white cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Cassinia albida is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
