Cistus viscosus

Cistus viscosus, commonly known as rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, sticky leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Cistus viscosus plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
