Cyphostemma kirkianum subsp. trifoliolatum

Cyphostemma kirkianum subsp.

trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaf moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and attractive fruit make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
