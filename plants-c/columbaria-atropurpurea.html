Columbaria atropurpurea

Columbaria atropurpurea, commonly known as purple smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, panicle-like inflorescences that range from pale purple to deep crimson, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Columbaria atropurpurea is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
