Cyanocephalus caprariifolius

Cyanocephalus caprariifolius, commonly known as the blue-headed caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often woody and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
