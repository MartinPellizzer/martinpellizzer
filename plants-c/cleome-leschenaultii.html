Cleome leschenaultii

Cleome leschenaultii, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like flower clusters. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Cleome leschenaultii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
