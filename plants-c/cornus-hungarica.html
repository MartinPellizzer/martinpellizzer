Cornus hungarica

Cornus hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

Cornus hungarica is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
