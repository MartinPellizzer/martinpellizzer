Cinnamomum galeottianum

Cinnamomum galeottianum, commonly known as the Madagascar cinnamon or Cinnamomum galeottianum, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are valued for their aromatic properties and traditional uses.

It is often cultivated for its unique scent and ornamental value in tropical gardens.
