Capparis surinamensis

Capparis surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible, cucumber-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Capparis surinamensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
