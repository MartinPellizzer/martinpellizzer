Cacalia mexicana

Cacalia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open woodland areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical seed heads that mature into fluffy, white pappus structures.

Cacalia mexicana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
