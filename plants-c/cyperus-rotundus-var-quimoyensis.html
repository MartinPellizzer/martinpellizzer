Cyperus rotundus var. quimoyensis

Cyperus rotundus var. quimoyensis, commonly known as the Quimoyensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, triangular stems that often root at the nodes, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
