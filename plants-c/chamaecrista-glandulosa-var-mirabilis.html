Chamaecrista glandulosa var. mirabilis

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. mirabilis, commonly known as the yellow coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.
