Calamagrostis balkharica

Calamagrostis balkharica, commonly known as the Balkh reed, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically a striking silver-green color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often adorned with silky hairs.

Calamagrostis balkharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
