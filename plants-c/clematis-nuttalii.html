Clematis nuttalii

Clematis nuttalii, commonly known as Nuttall's clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
