Cruciata coronata

Cruciata coronata, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
