Carex pseudobiwensis

Carex pseudobiwensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to East Asia, particularly Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, typically green or brown, and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and landscape design.
