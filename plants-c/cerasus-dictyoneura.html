Cerasus dictyoneura

Cerasus dictyoneura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits known as cherries, which are edible and widely consumed. Its ornamental value is highly appreciated, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a deep green color.
