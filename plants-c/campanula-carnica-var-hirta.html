Campanula carnica var. hirta

Campanula carnica var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
