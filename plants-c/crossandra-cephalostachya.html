Crossandra cephalostachya

Crossandra cephalostachya, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Crossandra cephalostachya is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
