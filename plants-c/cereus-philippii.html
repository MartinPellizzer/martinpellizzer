Cereus philippii

Cereus philippii, commonly known as the Christmas Cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that resemble a tree. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cereus philippii is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
