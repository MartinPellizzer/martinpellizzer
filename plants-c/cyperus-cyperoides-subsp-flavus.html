Cyperus cyperoides subsp. flavus

Cyperus cyperoides subsp.

flavus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its yellowish coloration in the inflorescence compared to other Cyperus species.
