Cytisus scoparius f. bicolor

Cytisus scoparius f. bicolor, commonly known as the variegated broom, is a hybrid form of the common broom.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display a mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It is a shrub native to the British Isles and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
