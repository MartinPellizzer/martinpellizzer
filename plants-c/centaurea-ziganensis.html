Centaurea ziganensis

Centaurea ziganensis, commonly known as the Serbian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers.

Centaurea ziganensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
