Chironia ecklonii

Chironia ecklonii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
