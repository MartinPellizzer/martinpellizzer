Croton flavens

Croton flavens, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
