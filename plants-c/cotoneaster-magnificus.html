Cotoneaster magnificus

Cotoneaster magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster magnificus is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and small mammals.
