Cinnamomum nominale

Cinnamomum nominale, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
