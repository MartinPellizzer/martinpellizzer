Clinhymenia

Clinhymenia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum clinhymenium*, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
