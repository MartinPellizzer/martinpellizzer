Calathea elegans

Calathea elegans, commonly known as the White Butterfly plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, dark green leaves with intricate white and silver veins that resemble the pattern of a butterfly's wings. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Calathea elegans is known for its ability to purify the air and thrive in humid environments.
