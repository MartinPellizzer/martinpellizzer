Cuphea ownbeyi

Cuphea ownbeyi, commonly known as the desert cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its unique flower structure attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Cuphea ownbeyi is valued in xeriscaping for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
