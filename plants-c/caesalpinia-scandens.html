Caesalpinia scandens

Caesalpinia scandens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery inflorescences that appear in late spring, giving it its striking visual appeal. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Caesalpinia scandens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
