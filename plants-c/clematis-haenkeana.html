Clematis haenkeana

Clematis haenkeana, commonly known as the New Zealand clematis, is a climbing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five to seven petals. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

Clematis haenkeana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
