Critoniopsis floribunda var. sevillana

Critoniopsis floribunda var. sevillana, commonly known as the Seville bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
