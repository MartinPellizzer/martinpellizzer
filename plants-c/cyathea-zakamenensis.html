Cyathea zakamenensis

Cyathea zakamenensis, commonly known as the Zakamen's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique combination of primitive and advanced features, including a well-developed root system and a robust vascular structure. Its fronds are typically green and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea zakamenensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
