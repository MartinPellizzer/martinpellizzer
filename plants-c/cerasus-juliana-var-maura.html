Cerasus juliana var. maura

Cerasus juliana var. maura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and cultural significance.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
