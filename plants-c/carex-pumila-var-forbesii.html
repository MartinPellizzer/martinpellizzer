Carex pumila var. forbesii

Carex pumila var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
