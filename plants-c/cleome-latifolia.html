Cleome latifolia

Cleome latifolia, commonly known as spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow upright, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with toothed margins, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Cleome latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
