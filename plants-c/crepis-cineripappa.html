Crepis cineripappa

Crepis cineripappa, commonly known as the ash-gray hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow with pale green ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Crepis cineripappa typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.
