Centaurea × subdiffusa

Centaurea × subdiffusa, commonly known as the hybrid everlasting or hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers, which resemble those of its parent species, Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea micrantha. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
