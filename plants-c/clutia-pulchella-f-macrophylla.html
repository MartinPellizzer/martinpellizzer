Clutia pulchella f. macrophylla

Clutia pulchella f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved clutia, is a variant of the clutia plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
