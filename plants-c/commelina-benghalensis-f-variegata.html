Commelina benghalensis f. variegata

Commelina benghalensis f. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking foliage with variegated leaf patterns, often displaying white, yellow, and green hues. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
