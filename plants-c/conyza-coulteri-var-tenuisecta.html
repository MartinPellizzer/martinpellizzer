Conyza coulteri var. tenuisecta

Conyza coulteri var. tenuisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient weed in various habitats.
