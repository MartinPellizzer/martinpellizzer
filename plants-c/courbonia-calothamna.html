Courbonia calothamna

Courbonia calothamna, commonly known as the narrow-leafed courbonia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
