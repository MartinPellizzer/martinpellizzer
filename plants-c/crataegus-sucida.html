Crataegus sucida

Crataegus sucida, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often sour in taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have serrated edges.
