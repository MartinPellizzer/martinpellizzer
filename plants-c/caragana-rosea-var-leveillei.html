Caragana rosea var. leveillei

Caragana rosea var. leveillei, commonly known as the purple-flowered shrub or the Levellie's caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in challenging climates.
