Crinum acaule

Crinum acaule, commonly known as the naked stem lily, is a bulbous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features a short, thick stem that supports its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically has strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in summer.

Crinum acaule is known for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
