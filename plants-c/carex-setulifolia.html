Carex setulifolia

Carex setulifolia, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a fine, bristly texture along the edges.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated.
