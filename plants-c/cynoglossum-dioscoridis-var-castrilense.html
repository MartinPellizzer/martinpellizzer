Cynoglossum dioscoridis var. castrilense

Cynoglossum dioscoridis var. castrilense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five lobes that resemble a tongue. The leaves are lanceolate, often with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
