Calceolaria appendiculata

Calceolaria appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate slipperwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Calceolaria appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
