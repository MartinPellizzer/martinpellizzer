Cirsium polycephalum

Cirsium polycephalum, commonly known as the many-headed thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a large number of small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cirsium polycephalum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
