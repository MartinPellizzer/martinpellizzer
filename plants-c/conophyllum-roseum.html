Conophyllum roseum

Conophyllum roseum, commonly known as the rose spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Conophyllum roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
