Comarostaphylis pyrifolia

Comarostaphylis pyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the lucky bamboo, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its upright, bamboo-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and air-purifying properties.

Comarostaphylis pyrifolia thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
