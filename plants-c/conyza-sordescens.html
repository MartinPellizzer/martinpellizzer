Conyza sordescens

Conyza sordescens, commonly known as the brownweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers produce tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Conyza sordescens is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments.
