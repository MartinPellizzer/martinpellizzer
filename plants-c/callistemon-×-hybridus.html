Callistemon × hybridus

Callistemon × hybridus, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a hybrid species within the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, brush-like inflorescences that range in color from red to pink or yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value.
