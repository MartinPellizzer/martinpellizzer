Coursetia guaranitica var. albiflora

Coursetia guaranitica var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower coursetia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.
