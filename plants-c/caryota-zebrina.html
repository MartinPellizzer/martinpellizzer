Caryota zebrina

Caryota zebrina, commonly known as the zebra palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, striped leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with a fibrous, spongy texture. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and often have a silvery or white midrib.

Caryota zebrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
