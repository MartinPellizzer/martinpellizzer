Carex monadelpha

Carex monadelpha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
