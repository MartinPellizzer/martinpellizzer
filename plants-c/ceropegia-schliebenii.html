Ceropegia schliebenii

Ceropegia schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's whiskery vine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, cordate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
