Crataegus hyrcana

Crataegus hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges, shrublands, and rocky areas.

Crataegus hyrcana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitats.
