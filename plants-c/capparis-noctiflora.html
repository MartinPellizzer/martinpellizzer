Capparis noctiflora

Capparis noctiflora, commonly known as the night-flowering caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom predominantly at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually a deep violet or purple color, with five petals that open in the evening.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
