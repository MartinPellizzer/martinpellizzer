Cyperus crinitus

Cyperus crinitus, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Cyperus crinitus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscape design for its unique foliage.
