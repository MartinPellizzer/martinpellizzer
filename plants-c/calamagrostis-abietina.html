Calamagrostis abietina

Calamagrostis abietina, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive silver-gray foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer, typically in shades of green to brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
