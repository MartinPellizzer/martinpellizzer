Centrosolenia pusilla

Centrosolenia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf centrosolenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and bloom in clusters.

Centrosolenia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural collections for its unique appearance.
