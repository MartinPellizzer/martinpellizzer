Crataegus pinnatifida f. geholensis

Crataegus pinnatifida f. geholensis, commonly known as the Geholensian hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its finely lobed leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in native habitats.
