Calliandra coriacea

Calliandra coriacea, commonly known as the rough calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences of pink or red flowers, which are arranged in cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental and agroforestry purposes.

Calliandra coriacea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
