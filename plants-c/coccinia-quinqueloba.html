Coccinia quinqueloba

Coccinia quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed cucumber, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small cucumbers. The fruits have a mild, sweet flavor and are often consumed raw or cooked.

It is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
