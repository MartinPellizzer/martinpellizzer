Crocanthemum chihuahuense

Crocanthemum chihuahuense, commonly known as the Chihuahua aster, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Crocanthemum chihuahuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
