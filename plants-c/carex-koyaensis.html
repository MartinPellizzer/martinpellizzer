Carex koyaensis

Carex koyaensis, commonly known as the Koya sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex koyaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
