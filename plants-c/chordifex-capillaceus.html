Chordifex capillaceus

Chordifex capillaceus, commonly known as the silky feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Chordifex capillaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
