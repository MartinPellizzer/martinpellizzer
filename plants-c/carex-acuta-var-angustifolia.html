Carex acuta var. angustifolia

Carex acuta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize wetland areas.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
