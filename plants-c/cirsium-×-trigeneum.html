Cirsium × trigeneum

Cirsium × trigeneum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of North America. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
