Convolvulus fischerianus

Convolvulus fischerianus, commonly known as Fischer's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
