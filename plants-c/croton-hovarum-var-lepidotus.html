Croton hovarum var. lepidotus

Croton hovarum var. lepidotus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
