Chromatotriccum brevilabium

Chromatotriccum brevilabium, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive narrow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
