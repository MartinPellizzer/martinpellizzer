Cuphea pterosperma var. brasiliensis

Cuphea pterosperma var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cuphea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
