Chaetocyperus bonariensis var. fluitans

Chaetocyperus bonariensis var. fluitans, commonly known as the floating flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and floating leaves that allow it to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its buoyant leaves help it remain afloat on water surfaces, aiding in photosynthesis.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers.
