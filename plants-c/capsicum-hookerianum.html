Capsicum hookerianum

Capsicum hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's chili or the Andean yellow pepper, is a species native to South America, particularly the Andean regions.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow, and it produces yellow or orange fruits that are usually small and pointed.

This species is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional Andean cuisines.
