Chimarrhis cymosa var. microcarpa

Chimarrhis cymosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in suitable climates.
