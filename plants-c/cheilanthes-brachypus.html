Cheilanthes brachypus

Cheilanthes brachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a species of fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, lance-shaped fronds. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is often found in crevices or on limestone formations. It is drought-tolerant and possesses a well-developed root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry environments.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round coverage and contributing to the ecological stability of its habitat.
