Conoclinium ianthinum

Conoclinium ianthinum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Conoclinium ianthinum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
