Canarium salomonense

Canarium salomonense, commonly known as Solomon's candlenut, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Samoa and Tonga.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a hard, oily seed that is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species.
