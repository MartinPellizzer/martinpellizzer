Calinea

Calinea, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Calinea has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and North America.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
