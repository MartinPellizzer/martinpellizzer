Crassula perforata subsp. perforata

Crassula perforata subsp.

perforata, commonly known as the elephant's foot or crassula jelly plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal watering, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
