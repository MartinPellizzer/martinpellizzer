Centaurea castellana var. baetica

Centaurea castellana var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetica centaurea, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts, which are typical features of the Centaurea genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
