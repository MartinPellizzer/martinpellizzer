Cuphea thesioides

Cuphea thesioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea thesioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
