Cruckshanksia peduncularis

Cruckshanksia peduncularis, commonly known as the silverleaf cruckshanksia, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected conservation areas.
