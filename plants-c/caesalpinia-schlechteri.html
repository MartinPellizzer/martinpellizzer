Caesalpinia schlechteri

Caesalpinia schlechteri, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically bisexual, featuring five petals. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Caesalpinia schlechteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
