Cinnamomum leptopus

Cinnamomum leptopus, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic bark and leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
