Campanula malacitana

Campanula malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula malacitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
