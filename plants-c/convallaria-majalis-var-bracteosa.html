Convallaria majalis var. bracteosa

Convallaria majalis var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate lily of the valley, is a variant of the familiar lily of the valley plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracts, which are modified leaves that enclose the flower buds. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a rhizomatous root system.

Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland settings.
