Caragana acanthophylla

Caragana acanthophylla, commonly known as the sandthorn or goat's thorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It has deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Caragana acanthophylla plays a role in preventing soil erosion and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
