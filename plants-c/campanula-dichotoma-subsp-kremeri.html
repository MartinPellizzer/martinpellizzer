Campanula dichotoma subsp. kremeri

Campanula dichotoma subsp.

kremeri, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
