Calyptranthes elegans

Calyptranthes elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Calyptranthes elegans is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
