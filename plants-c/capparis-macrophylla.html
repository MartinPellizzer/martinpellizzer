Capparis macrophylla

Capparis macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, greenish-black fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
