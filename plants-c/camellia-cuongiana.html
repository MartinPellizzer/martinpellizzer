Camellia cuongiana

Camellia cuongiana, commonly known as the Cuong mountain camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its large, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red blossoms that bloom in late winter to early spring. Camellia cuongiana prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical climates.

Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide year-round interest in gardens.
