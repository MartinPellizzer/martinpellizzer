Carduus hamulosus var. spinigerus

Carduus hamulosus var. spinigerus, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its rough texture.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
