Cirrhopetalum cyclosepalon

Cirrhopetalum cyclosepalon, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds and small, clustered spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive golden hue that becomes more pronounced as they mature.

The species is notable for its unique reproductive structures and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
