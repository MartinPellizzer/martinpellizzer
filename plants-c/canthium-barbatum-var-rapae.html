Canthium barbatum var. rapae

Canthium barbatum var. rapae, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
