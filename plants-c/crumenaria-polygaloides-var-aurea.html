Crumenaria polygaloides var. aurea

Crumenaria polygaloides var. aurea, commonly known as the golden crumenaria, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
