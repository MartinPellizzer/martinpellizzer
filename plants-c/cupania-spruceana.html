Cupania spruceana

Cupania spruceana, commonly known as the spruceleaf cupania, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of many members of its family. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Cupania spruceana is often found in moist, woodlands and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
