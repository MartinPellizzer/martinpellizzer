Calamus rumphii

Calamus rumphii, commonly known as the Rumphius' canes, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring a robust trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by clustered fruits containing edible seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for crafting purposes.

Calamus rumphii is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitats.
