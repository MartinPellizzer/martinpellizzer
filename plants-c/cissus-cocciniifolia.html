Cissus cocciniifolia

Cissus cocciniifolia, commonly known as the Crimson Leaf Cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from dark green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus cocciniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to trail or climb in gardens and indoor spaces.
