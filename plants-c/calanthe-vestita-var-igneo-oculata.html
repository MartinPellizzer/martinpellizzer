Calanthe vestita var. igneo-oculata

Calanthe vestita var. igneo-oculata, commonly known as the flame-eyed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a bright orange or red eye-like marking on the lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized for its unique floral patterns and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
