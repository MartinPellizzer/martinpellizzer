Caladenia verrucosa

Caladenia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and a single, strikingly colored spur that resembles a spider's leg. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, elongated labellum.

Caladenia verrucosa is a terrestrial orchid that plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
