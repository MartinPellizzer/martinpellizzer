Centaurea bulgarica

Centaurea bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads that bloom from June to September. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Centaurea bulgarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
