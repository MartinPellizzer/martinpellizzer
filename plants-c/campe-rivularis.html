Campe rivularis

Campe rivularis, commonly known as the water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It has small, delicate leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and bloom in early summer.

Campe rivularis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
