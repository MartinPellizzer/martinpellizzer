Chrysophyllum dumetorum

Chrysophyllum dumetorum, commonly known as the African cherry or wild mango, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are similar in appearance to mangoes but are typically smaller. The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is valued for its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
