Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp. sylvatica

Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, arching culms with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that are typically golden to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
