Cassia longicoma

Cassia longicoma, commonly known as the longpod senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Cassia longicoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in tropical gardens and reforestation projects.
