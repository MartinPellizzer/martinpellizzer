Carex helingeeriensis

Carex helingeeriensis, commonly known as the Hingeeri sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex helingeeriensis is often found in rocky or gravelly environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
