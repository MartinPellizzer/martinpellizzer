Carelia sessilifolia

Carelia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf carelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

Carelia sessilifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
