Carex complanata

Carex complanata, commonly known as flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are flat, grass-like, and often form dense clumps, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Carex complanata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
