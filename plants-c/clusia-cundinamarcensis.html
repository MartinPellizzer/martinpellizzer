Clusia cundinamarcensis

Clusia cundinamarcensis, commonly known as the Cundinamarca clusia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Clusiaceae.

It is native to Colombia, specifically the Cundinamarca region, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the dry season. It is often found in lowland tropical forests and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
