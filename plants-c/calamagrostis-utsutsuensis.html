Calamagrostis utsutsuensis

Calamagrostis utsutsuensis, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tan plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and dramatic seed heads.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
