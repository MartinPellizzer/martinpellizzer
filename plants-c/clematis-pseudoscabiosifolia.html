Clematis pseudoscabiosifolia

Clematis pseudoscabiosifolia, commonly known as the pink clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features include a climbing habit and a long flowering period, contributing to its ornamental value.
