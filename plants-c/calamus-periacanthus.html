Calamus periacanthus

Calamus periacanthus, commonly known as the red-flowered calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences, which are highly valued in horticulture. Calamus periacanthus thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
