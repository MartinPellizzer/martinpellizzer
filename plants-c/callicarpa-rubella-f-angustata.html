Callicarpa rubella f. angustata

Callicarpa rubella f. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and clusters of small, dark purple berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
