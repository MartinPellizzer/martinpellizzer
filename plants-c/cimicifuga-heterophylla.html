Cimicifuga heterophylla

Cimicifuga heterophylla, commonly known as the white bugbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that bear large, white, fluffy flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often arranged alternately along the stem.

Cimicifuga heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
