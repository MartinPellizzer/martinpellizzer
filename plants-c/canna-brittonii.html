Canna brittonii

Canna brittonii, commonly known as the Britton canna, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a deep green color. The plant produces striking, spadix-like flowers surrounded by vibrant, colorful bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Canna brittonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
