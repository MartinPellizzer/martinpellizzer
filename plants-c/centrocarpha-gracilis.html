Centrocarpha gracilis

Centrocarpha gracilis, commonly known as slenderleaf coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open prairies and dry, rocky soils, preferring full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.

Centrocarpha gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
