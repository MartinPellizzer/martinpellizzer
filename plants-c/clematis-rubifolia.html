Clematis rubifolia

Clematis rubifolia, commonly known as the red-fruited clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are red and edible, mature in the fall and serve as a food source for birds.

Clematis rubifolia thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
