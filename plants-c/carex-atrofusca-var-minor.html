Carex atrofusca var. minor

Carex atrofusca var. minor, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often dark brown in color.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
