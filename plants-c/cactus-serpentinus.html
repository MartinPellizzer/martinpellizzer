Cactus serpentinus

Cactus serpentinus, commonly known as the red-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The cactus is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
