Chenopodium striatum f. purpureogriseum

Chenopodium striatum f. purpureogriseum, commonly known as the purple-gray goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple and grayish foliage, which gives it a unique aesthetic appeal. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
