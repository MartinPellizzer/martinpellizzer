Capparis mollicella

Capparis mollicella, commonly known as the velvet caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and oval, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Capparis mollicella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
