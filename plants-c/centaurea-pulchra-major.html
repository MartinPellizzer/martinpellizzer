Centaurea pulchra-major

Centaurea pulchra-major, commonly known as the showy cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments like grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue.

Centaurea pulchra-major is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is part of the Asteraceae family.
