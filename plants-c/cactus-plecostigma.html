Cactus plecostigma

Cactus plecostigma, commonly known as the plecostigma cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Native to South America, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
