Calamagrostis breviligulata

Calamagrostis breviligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with silver. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelets that are often purple or pink in color.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
