Cousinia arbelensis

Cousinia arbelensis, commonly known as the Arbel's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cousinia arbelensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
