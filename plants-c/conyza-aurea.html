Conyza aurea

Conyza aurea, commonly known as golden conyza, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, with tubular corollas that open into ray florets, attracting various pollinators.

Conyza aurea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.
