Capparis gibbosa

Capparis gibbosa, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, characterized by its thorny stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in terminal racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments.

Capparis gibbosa is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
