Crocus biflorus subsp. atrospermus

Crocus biflorus subsp.

atrospermus, commonly known as the black bulb crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It produces distinctive, showy flowers with dark purple to nearly black petals, often with a yellow or orange center.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or open habitats. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
