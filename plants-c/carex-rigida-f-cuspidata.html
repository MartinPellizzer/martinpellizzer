Carex rigida f. cuspidata

Carex rigida f. cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its sharp, pointed leaf tips, which give it its specific epithet "cuspidata.".
