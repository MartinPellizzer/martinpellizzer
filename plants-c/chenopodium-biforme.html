Chenopodium biforme

Chenopodium biforme, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs and have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Lambsquarters is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural fields.

It is also known for its nutritional value, as it contains high levels of protein and minerals.
