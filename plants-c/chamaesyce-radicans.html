Chamaesyce radicans

Chamaesyce radicans, commonly known as creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, clustered yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
