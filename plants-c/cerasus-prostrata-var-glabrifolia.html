Cerasus prostrata var. glabrifolia

Cerasus prostrata var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell or glabrous cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale blossoms. This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
