Calamagrostis purpurea subsp. pseudopurpurea

Calamagrostis purpurea subsp.

pseudopurpurea, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland restoration projects.
