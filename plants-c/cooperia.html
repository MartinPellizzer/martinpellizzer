Cooperia

Cooperia, commonly known as the grass genus, is a group of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in temperate regions and are characterized by their slender, grass-like appearance. Cooperia species often grow in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. They are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The genus includes several species that are important for forage and soil stabilization.
