Colubrina cubensis var. cubensis

Colubrina cubensis var. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its attractive, clustered, violet-blue berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
