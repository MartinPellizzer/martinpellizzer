Cornus alba var. flaviramea

Cornus alba var. flaviramea, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more vibrant in the winter months. The plant produces small white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
