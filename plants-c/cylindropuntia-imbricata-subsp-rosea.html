Cylindropuntia imbricata subsp. rosea

Cylindropuntia imbricata subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a distinctive appearance with cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically pink to reddish in color, especially when mature.

The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and bats. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
