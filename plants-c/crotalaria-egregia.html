Crotalaria egregia

Crotalaria egregia, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically appealing in botanical gardens.
