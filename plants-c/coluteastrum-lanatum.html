Coluteastrum lanatum

Coluteastrum lanatum, commonly known as hairy colutea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume-like pods that contain several seeds. Native to regions of Africa and the Middle East, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
