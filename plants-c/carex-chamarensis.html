Carex chamarensis

Carex chamarensis, commonly known as the Chamarensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex chamarensis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
