Chionanthus tetrandrus

Chionanthus tetrandrus, commonly known as the four-threaded fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

Chionanthus tetrandrus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
