Croton macrobuxus var. glandulifer

Croton macrobuxus var. glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
