Crocus scharojanii subsp. lazicus

Crocus scharojanii subsp.

lazicus, commonly known as the Lazik crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It produces striking violet to blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Crocus genus.
