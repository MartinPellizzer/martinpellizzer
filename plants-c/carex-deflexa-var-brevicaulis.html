Carex deflexa var. brevicaulis

Carex deflexa var. brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. This sedge typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
