Cardamine intermedia

Cardamine intermedia, commonly known as intermediate watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist environments in North America.

It typically grows in shaded, wet habitats such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, similar to other members of the Brassicaceae family. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
