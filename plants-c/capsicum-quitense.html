Capsicum quitense

Capsicum quitense, commonly known as the Peruvian chili or Andean chili, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional Andean cuisine for their mild to medium heat level and distinct flavor.

Capsicum quitense is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
