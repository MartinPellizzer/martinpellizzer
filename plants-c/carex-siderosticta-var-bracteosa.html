Carex siderosticta var. bracteosa

Carex siderosticta var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
