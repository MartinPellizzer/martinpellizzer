Chionanthus luteus

Chionanthus luteus, commonly known as the yellow fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Chionanthus luteus thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
