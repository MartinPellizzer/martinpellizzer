Centaurea perrottettii

Centaurea perrottettii, commonly known as the Perrott's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and bright yellow to red flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Centaurea perrottettii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy growth habits.
