Chamaecrista conferta var. virgata

Chamaecrista conferta var. virgata, commonly known as the Virginia sensitiveplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland areas and along stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its sensitive stems that curl when touched, a trait shared with other members of the genus.

The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to humid, subtropical climates.
