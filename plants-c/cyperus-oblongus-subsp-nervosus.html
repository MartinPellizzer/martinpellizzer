Cyperus oblongus subsp. nervosus

Cyperus oblongus subsp.

nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-veined flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves with prominent veins.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
