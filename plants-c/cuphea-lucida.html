Cuphea lucida

Cuphea lucida, commonly known as the shining cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cuphea lucida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.
