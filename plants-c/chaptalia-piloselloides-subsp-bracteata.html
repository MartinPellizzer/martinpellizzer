Chaptalia piloselloides subsp. bracteata

Chaptalia piloselloides subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate goatweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks. It features small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is often found in the Andean regions of Argentina and Chile.
