Claytonia trigona

Claytonia trigona, commonly known as the mountain chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
