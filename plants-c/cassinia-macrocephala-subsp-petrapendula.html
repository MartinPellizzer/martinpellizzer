Cassinia macrocephala subsp. petrapendula

Cassinia macrocephala subsp.

petrapendula, commonly known as the rock cassinia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
