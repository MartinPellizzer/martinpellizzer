Cestrum diasae

Cestrum diasae, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
