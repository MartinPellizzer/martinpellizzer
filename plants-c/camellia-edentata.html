Camellia edentata

Camellia edentata, commonly known as the toothless camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and glossy, with a deep green hue.

Camellia edentata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
