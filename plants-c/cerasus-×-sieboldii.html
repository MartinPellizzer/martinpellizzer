Cerasus × sieboldii

Cerasus × sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus sieboldii, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall.

Cerasus × sieboldii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic flowers and historical significance in Japanese culture.
