Cineraria gigantea

Cineraria gigantea, commonly known as the giant cineraria, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it is considered rare in the wild.
