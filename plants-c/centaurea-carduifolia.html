Centaurea carduifolia

Centaurea carduifolia, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea carduifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
