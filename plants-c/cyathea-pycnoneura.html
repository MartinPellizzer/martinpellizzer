Cyathea pycnoneura

Cyathea pycnoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive narrow, lance-shaped appearance.

Cyathea pycnoneura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
