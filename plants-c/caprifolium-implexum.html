Caprifolium implexum

Caprifolium implexum, commonly known as the tangled honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in the fall.

Caprifolium implexum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks.
