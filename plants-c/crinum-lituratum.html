Crinum lituratum

Crinum lituratum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily or the silver lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a silvery or bluish sheen, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Crinum lituratum is known for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
