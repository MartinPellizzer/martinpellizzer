Cistus canus var. lusitanicus

Cistus canus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian whitethorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, tubular pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Cistus canus var. lusitanicus is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as maquis and scrublands.
