Cuphea serpyllifolia

Cuphea serpyllifolia, commonly known as the carpetweed cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields.

Cuphea serpyllifolia is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
