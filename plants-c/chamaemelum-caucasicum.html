Chamaemelum caucasicum

Chamaemelum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and slender, aromatic stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a soft, silvery down, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
