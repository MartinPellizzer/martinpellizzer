Campylotropis muehleana

Campylotropis muehleana, commonly known as the Muehleana false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
