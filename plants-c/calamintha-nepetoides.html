Calamintha nepetoides

Calamintha nepetoides, commonly known as caraway mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its strong, mint-like scent and is often found in moist, shady environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Calamintha nepetoides is sometimes used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
