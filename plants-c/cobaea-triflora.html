Cobaea triflora

Cobaea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that resembles a small, round capsule. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cobaea triflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
