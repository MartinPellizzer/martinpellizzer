Claytonia australasica var. racemosa

Claytonia australasica var. racemosa, commonly known as the New Zealand spring beauty, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features rosettes of broad, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine and subalpine regions.
