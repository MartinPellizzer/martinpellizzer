Cypripedium × laurebellum

Cypripedium × laurebellum, commonly known as the laurel orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single, large, white or pinkish flower that has a distinctive pouch-like structure called a lip. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This species thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in rich, acidic soils.
