Clerodendrum glandulosum

Clerodendrum glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it its common name. It grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat.

The bark and leaves contain various chemical compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and botanical research.
