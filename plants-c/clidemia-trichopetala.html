Clidemia trichopetala

Clidemia trichopetala, commonly known as the hairy leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. The plant lacks true roots and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides sugars through photosynthesis. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

The species is often found growing on trees in moist, shaded environments.
