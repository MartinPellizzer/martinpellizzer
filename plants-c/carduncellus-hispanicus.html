Carduncellus hispanicus

Carduncellus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its thick, ridged stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in cooking and medicine.

Its robust growth makes it adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
