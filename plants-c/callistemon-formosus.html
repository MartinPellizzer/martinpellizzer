Callistemon formosus

Callistemon formosus, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright pink cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
