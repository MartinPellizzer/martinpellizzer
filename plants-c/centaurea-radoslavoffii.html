Centaurea radoslavoffii

Centaurea radoslavoffii, commonly known as Radoslavov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Bulgaria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spiky flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
