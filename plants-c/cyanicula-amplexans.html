Cyanicula amplexans

Cyanicula amplexans, commonly known as the clinging bluebells, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant blue-violet flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to the temperate regions of southern Africa. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stems, giving it its common name. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer.

Cyanicula amplexans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
