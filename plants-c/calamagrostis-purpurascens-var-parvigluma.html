Calamagrostis purpurascens var. parvigluma

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. parvigluma, commonly known as the purple moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and bogs of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and inflorescence that consists of dense, spike-like clusters of purple to reddish-brown flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peatlands and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
