Chloroxylon faho

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense canopy of elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
