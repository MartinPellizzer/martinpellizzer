Cyperus congensis

Cyperus congensis, commonly known as the Congo flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus congensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
