Cuphea egleri

Cuphea egleri, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea egleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to landscapes throughout the growing season.
