Chrysophyllum steyermarkii

Chrysophyllum steyermarkii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
