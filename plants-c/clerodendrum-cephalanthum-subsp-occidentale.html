Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp. occidentale

Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the Western flame bush, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
