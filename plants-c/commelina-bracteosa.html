Commelina bracteosa

Commelina bracteosa, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a white or pale blue bract beneath them. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional medicine.

Commelina bracteosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
