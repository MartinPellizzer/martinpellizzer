Crinum crispum

Crinum crispum, commonly known as the curly-leaved lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features broad, strap-like leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. Crinum crispum is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
