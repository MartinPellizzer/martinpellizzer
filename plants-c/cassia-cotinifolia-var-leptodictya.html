Cassia cotinifolia var. leptodictya

Cassia cotinifolia var. leptodictya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
