Cynanchica borbasiana

Cynanchica borbasiana, commonly known as the Borbasian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes many species of milkweeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive structure that includes a crown of hooked filaments.

It produces toxic latex, which can be harmful if ingested, and is often found in dry, open habitats.
