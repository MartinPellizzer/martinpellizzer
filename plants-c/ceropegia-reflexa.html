Ceropegia reflexa

Ceropegia reflexa, commonly known as the silver leaf or spider plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces delicate, zigzagging stems that often trail or climb. The leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It blooms with small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellow, attracting specific pollinators like moths.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
