Carlina echinus

Carlina echinus, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by a dense layer of bracts. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Carlina echinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
