Chenopodium carnosulum f. robustum

Chenopodium carnosulum f. robustum, commonly known as the robust goosefoot, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of whitish powder. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
