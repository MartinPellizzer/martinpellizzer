Callista lawsonia

Callista lawsonia, commonly known as the Lawson's callista, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
