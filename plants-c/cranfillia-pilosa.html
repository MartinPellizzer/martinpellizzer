Cranfillia pilosa

Cranfillia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cranfillia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
