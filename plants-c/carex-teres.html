Carex teres

Carex teres, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex teres plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
