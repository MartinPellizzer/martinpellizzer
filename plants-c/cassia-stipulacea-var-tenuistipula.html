Cassia stipulacea var. tenuistipula

Cassia stipulacea var. tenuistipula, commonly known as narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with prominent stipules. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia stipulacea var. tenuistipula is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
