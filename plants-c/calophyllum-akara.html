Calophyllum akara

Calophyllum akara, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits that contain hard, woody seeds. Its wood is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Calophyllum akara thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
