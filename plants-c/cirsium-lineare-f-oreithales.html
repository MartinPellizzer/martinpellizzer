Cirsium lineare f. oreithales

Cirsium lineare f. oreithales, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a distinctive variant of the common plume thistle.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces tall, slender stems with purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
