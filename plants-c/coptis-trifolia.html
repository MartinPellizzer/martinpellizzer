Coptis trifolia

Coptis trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, including berberine, which have medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
