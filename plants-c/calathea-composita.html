Calathea composita

Calathea composita, commonly known as the Variegated Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are broad and have intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink, which change subtly in response to light conditions. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect sunlight and requires consistent moisture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
