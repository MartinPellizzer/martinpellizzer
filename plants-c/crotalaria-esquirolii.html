Crotalaria esquirolii

Crotalaria esquirolii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Crotalaria esquirolii is also known for its role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
