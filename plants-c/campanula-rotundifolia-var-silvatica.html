Campanula rotundifolia var. silvatica

Campanula rotundifolia var. silvatica, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves and bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
