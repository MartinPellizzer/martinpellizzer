Chloranthus ceylanicus

Chloranthus ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and aromatic oils. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its primitive floral morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
