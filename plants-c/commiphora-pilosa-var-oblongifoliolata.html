Commiphora pilosa var. oblongifoliolata

Commiphora pilosa var. oblongifoliolata, commonly known as the African gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves that are oblong to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish, aromatic resinous exudates. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and traditional uses in local communities.
