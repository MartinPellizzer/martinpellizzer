Carex mexicana

Carex mexicana, commonly known as Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often grouped into clusters.

Carex mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
