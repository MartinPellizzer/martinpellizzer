Crantzia lineata var. subulata

Crantzia lineata var. subulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crantzia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
