Campanula bellidifolia subsp. aucheri

Campanula bellidifolia subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and beautiful floral display.
