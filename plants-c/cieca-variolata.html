Cieca variolata

Cieca variolata, commonly known as the variegated cieca, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking, multicolored foliage.

It belongs to the family Ciecaceae and is native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features elongated, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are typically small and inconspicuous, contrasting with its vibrant leaves.

Cieca variolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
