Carex kurtziana

Carex kurtziana, commonly known as Kurtz's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex kurtziana is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
