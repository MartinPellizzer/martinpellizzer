Carex cryptostachys

Carex cryptostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex cryptostachys plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
