Calea divaricata

Calea divaricata, commonly known as the nightshade or sacred plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces violet or blue flowers that emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea divaricata is also noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
