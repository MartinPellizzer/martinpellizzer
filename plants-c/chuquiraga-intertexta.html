Chuquiraga intertexta

Chuquiraga intertexta, commonly known as the Andean burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a type of fruit called a cypsela, which is typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
