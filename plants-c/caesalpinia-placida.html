Caesalpinia placida

Caesalpinia placida, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
