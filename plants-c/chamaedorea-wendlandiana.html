Chamaedorea wendlandiana

Chamaedorea wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features slender, upright stems that resemble bamboo, with long, narrow, dark green leaves arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and moderate humidity. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for both residential and commercial indoor spaces.
