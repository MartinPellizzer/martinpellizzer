Cereus jubatus

Cereus jubatus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that are often adorned with spines. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
