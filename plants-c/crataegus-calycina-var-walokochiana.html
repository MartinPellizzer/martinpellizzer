Crataegus calycina var. walokochiana

Crataegus calycina var. walokochiana, commonly known as the Walokochian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, often white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with serrated edges, and it has a generally compact growth habit.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
