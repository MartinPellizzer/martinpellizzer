Clausena longipes

Clausena longipes, commonly known as the longipes mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to true mangosteen, though they are not closely related. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
