Convolvulus illecebrosus

Convolvulus illecebrosus, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet or white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

The species is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
