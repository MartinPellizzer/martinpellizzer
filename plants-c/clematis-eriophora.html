Clematis eriophora

Clematis eriophora, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a perennial vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
