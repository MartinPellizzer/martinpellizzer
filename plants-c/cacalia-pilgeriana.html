Cacalia pilgeriana

Cacalia pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia pilgeriana is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
