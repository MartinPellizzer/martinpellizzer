Chevaliera castanea

Chevaliera castanea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, red berries that contain the seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of high-quality timber.
