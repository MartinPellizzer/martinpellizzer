Capparis angustifolia

Capparis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Capparis angustifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
