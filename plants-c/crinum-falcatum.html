Crinum falcatum

Crinum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Crinum falcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
