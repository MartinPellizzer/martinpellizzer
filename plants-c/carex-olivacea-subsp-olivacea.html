Carex olivacea subsp.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or greenish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. Carex olivacea subsp.

olivacea is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
