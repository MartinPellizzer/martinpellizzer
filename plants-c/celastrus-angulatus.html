Celastrus angulatus

Celastrus angulatus, commonly known as the angular staffvetch, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinct angular shape, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in axillary inflorescences. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of understory vegetation.
