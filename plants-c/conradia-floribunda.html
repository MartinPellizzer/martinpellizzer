Conradia floribunda

Conradia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Conradia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
