Cymbilabia

Cymbilabia, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a compact, fleshy stem with small, clustered leaves that can curl into a tight rosette when dry. The plant produces delicate, pink or white flowers during the rainy season, which bloom briefly before withering. Cymbilabia is known for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a state of dormancy.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for studying plant resilience in harsh environments.
