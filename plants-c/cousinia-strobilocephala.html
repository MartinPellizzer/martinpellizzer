Cousinia strobilocephala

Cousinia strobilocephala, commonly known as the clustered goa or goa flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Cousinia strobilocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
