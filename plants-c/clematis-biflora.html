Clematis biflora

Clematis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
