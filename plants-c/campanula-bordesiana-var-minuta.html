Campanula bordesiana var. minuta

Campanula bordesiana var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a hardy ground cover.
