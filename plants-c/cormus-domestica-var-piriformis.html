Cormus domestica var. piriformis

Cormus domestica var. piriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped cormus, is a unique variant of the cormus plant characterized by its distinctive pear-shaped corms.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The corms are typically harvested for their edible, starchy tubers, which have a mild, nutty flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique shape and culinary uses, it is a sought-after plant in both traditional and modern agriculture.
