Carex lushanensis

Carex lushanensis, commonly known as the Lushan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Lushan region, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in wetlands, stream sides, and forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
