Carex chishimana

Carex chishimana, commonly known as the Chishimana sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to East Africa, particularly Tanzania, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Carex chishimana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
