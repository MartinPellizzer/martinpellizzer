Callianthemum sachalinense

Callianthemum sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin crownflower, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of pink to white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and it produces small, showy flowers in early spring.

Callianthemum sachalinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms.
