Calendula maroccana subsp. maroccana

Calendula maroccana subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan marigold, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its flowers bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
