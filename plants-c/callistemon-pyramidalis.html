Callistemon pyramidalis

Callistemon pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or crimson bottlebrush flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a dense, upright fashion, giving it a pyramidal shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
