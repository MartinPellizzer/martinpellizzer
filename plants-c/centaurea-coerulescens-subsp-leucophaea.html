Centaurea coerulescens subsp. leucophaea

Centaurea coerulescens subsp.

leucophaea, commonly known as the white-centred knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive white center, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
