Caesalpinia mimosifolia

Caesalpinia mimosifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like, bipinnate leaves that turn a vibrant red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
