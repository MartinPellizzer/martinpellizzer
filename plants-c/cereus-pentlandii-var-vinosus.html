Cereus pentlandii var. vinosus

Cereus pentlandii var. vinosus, commonly known as the wine cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its reddish-brown, ribbed stems and sharp, spiny areoles. It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its unique beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The varietal name "vinosus" refers to its wine-red coloration, particularly in the stems and flowers.
