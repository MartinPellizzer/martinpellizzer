Chenopodium citriodorum

Chenopodium citriodorum, commonly known as lemon chenopod, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are aromatic, with a citrus-like scent, contributing to its common name.

Chenopodium citriodorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
