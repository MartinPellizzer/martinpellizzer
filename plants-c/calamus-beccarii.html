Calamus beccarii

Calamus beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a rhizomatous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wet, swampy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.
