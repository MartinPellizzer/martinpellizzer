Crepidium inexspectatum

Crepidium inexspectatum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with faintly colored lips, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Crepidium inexspectatum is relatively rare and has specific ecological requirements, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
