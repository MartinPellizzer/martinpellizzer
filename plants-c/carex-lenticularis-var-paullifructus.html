Carex lenticularis var. paullifructus

Carex lenticularis var. paullifructus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variant is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
