Cistus × chantalis

Cistus × chantalis, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes various species of evergreen shrubs characterized by their aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink or white flowers in late summer, which are often used in perfumery due to their strong scent. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and covered in fine, white hairs, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.

Cistus × chantalis is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardens and natural landscapes.
