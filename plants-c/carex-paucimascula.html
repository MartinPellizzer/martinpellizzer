Carex paucimascula

Carex paucimascula, commonly known as the few-male sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex paucimascula plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
