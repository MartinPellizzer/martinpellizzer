Campanula sibirica

Campanula sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cooler regions of Asia and Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula sibirica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
