Cymboglossum maculosum

Cymboglossum maculosum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Cymboglossum maculosum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
