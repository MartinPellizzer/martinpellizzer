Carex tangulashanensis

Carex tangulashanensis, commonly known as the Tangulashan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like inflorescences. Carex tangulashanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in studies of plant diversity and wetland ecosystems.
