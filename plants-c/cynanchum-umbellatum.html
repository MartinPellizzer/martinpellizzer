Cynanchum umbellatum

Cynanchum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features a branching stem covered in fine hairs and produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in umbellate clusters. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a slender, twisted follicle that contains several seeds.
