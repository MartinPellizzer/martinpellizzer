Carex montana f. pallescens

Carex montana f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or boggy soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along stream banks. Its pale green foliage and delicate appearance make it an attractive addition to water gardens and natural landscapes.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its habitat.
