Cyperus tenuis var. brevior

Cyperus tenuis var. brevior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with a short, rhizomatous stem.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
