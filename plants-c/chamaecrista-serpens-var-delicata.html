Chamaecrista serpens var. delicata

Chamaecrista serpens var. delicata, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the species is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
