Calathea matudae

Calathea matudae, commonly known as the Matudae Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often displaying shades of green, silver, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
