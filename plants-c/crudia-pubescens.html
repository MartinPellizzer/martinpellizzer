Crudia pubescens

Crudia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Crudia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
