Carex disticha var. compactior

Carex disticha var. compactior, commonly known as the compact tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its dense, compact growth habit, forming low, mat-like clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
