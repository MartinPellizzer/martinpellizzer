Caltha palustris var. parnassifolia

Caltha palustris var. parnassifolia, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
