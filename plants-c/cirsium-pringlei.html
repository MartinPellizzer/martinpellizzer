Cirsium pringlei

Cirsium pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium pringlei is often found in areas with limited soil moisture, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
