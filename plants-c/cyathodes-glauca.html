Cyathodes glauca

Cyathodes glauca, commonly known as the silverleaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences called cyathodes. These flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Cyathodes glauca is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
