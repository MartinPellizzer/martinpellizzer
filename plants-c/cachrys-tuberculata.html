Cachrys tuberculata

Cachrys tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
