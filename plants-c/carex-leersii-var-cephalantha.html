Carex leersii var. cephalantha

Carex leersii var. cephalantha, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its dense, clustered growth habit and its flowering spikes that emerge from the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
