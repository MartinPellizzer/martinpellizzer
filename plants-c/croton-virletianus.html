Croton virletianus

Croton virletianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically palmate, with lobes that can range from green to red or purple, depending on the variety. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
