Cerasus × niko-montana

Cerasus × niko-montana, commonly known as the Niko-Montana cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It blooms with clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
