Cephalaria subindivisa

Cephalaria subindivisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
