Crataegus integriloba

Crataegus integriloba, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
