Carpesium divaricatum var. matsuei

Carpesium divaricatum var. matsuei, commonly known as the Japanese thornapple, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are covered in bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens due to its distinctive appearance and seasonal blooms.
