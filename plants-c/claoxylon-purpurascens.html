Claoxylon purpurascens

Claoxylon purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Claoxylon purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
