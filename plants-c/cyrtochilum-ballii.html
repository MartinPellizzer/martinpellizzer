Cyrtochilum ballii

Cyrtochilum ballii, commonly known as the Ball's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives on decomposing organic matter. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it is known for its long-lasting blooms that can persist for several weeks.

Cyrtochilum ballii is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
