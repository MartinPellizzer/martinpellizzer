Camaridium

Camaridium, commonly known as the sea hare, is a type of marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its soft, often colorful body and the presence of a muscular foot used for crawling along the ocean floor. Camaridium species are typically found in shallow coastal waters and are known for their ability to secrete a mucus-like substance. They play an important role in marine ecosystems by feeding on algae and other organic matter.

Some species are also notable for their ability to change color as a form of camouflage or communication.
