Cousinia chamaepeucides

Cousinia chamaepeucides, commonly known as the cushion cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette structure, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple during the summer months. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cousinia chamaepeucides is well adapted to rocky soils and high altitudes, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
