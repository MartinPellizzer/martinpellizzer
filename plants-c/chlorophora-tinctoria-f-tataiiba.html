Chlorophora tinctoria f. tataiiba

Chlorophora tinctoria f. tataiiba, commonly known as the red-stemmed sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
