Croton emporiorum

Croton emporiorum, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring rough, often hairy stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive yellow or greenish coloration. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
