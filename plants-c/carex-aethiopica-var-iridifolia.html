Carex aethiopica var. iridifolia

Carex aethiopica var. iridifolia, commonly known as the Blue-flowered Rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.
