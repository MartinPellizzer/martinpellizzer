Cordylanthus maritimus subsp. palustris

Cordylanthus maritimus subsp.

palustris, commonly known as salt marsh buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America. It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in salt marshes and tidal areas.

The plant has small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, glandular hairs. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates. Cordylanthus maritimus subsp.

palustris is adapted to withstand periodic flooding and high salinity levels.
