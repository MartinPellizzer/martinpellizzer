Cereus mixtecensis

Cereus mixtecensis, commonly known as the Mixtec cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with thick stems and reduced leaves.

Cereus mixtecensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
