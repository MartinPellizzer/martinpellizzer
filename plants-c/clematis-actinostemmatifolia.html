Clematis actinostemmatifolia

Clematis actinostemmatifolia, commonly known as the star-stemmed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, arranged in a palmate pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be trained to climb trellises or walls.
