Chalepoa

Chalepoa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Chalepoa is often found in moist, shaded environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
