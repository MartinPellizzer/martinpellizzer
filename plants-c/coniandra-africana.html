Coniandra africana

Coniandra africana, commonly known as the African coniandra, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, globose capsules that split open to release seeds.

Coniandra africana is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
