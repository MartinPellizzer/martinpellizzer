Chascolytrum rhomboideum

Chascolytrum rhomboideum, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. It is often found growing in rocky crevices and along mountain slopes.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
