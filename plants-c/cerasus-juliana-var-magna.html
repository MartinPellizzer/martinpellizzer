Cerasus juliana var. magna

Cerasus juliana var. magna, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yuzu cherry, is a variety of the Prunus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces white or pink flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its aromatic leaves and its role in traditional Japanese culture.
