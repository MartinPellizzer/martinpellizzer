Calliandra revoluta

Calliandra revoluta, commonly known as the curled calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often curled or twisted. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
