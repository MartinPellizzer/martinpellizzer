Centaurea scabiosa subsp. menteyerica

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

menteyerica, commonly known as the Alpine sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces small, bract-like leaves and has a compact, bushy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in cooler climates.
