Crotalaria glabrescens

Crotalaria glabrescens, commonly known as the white crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, South America, and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base of each petal.

Crotalaria glabrescens is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a useful plant in agricultural systems.
