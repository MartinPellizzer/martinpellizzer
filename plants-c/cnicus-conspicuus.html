Cnicus conspicuus

Cnicus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

The leaves are generally hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
