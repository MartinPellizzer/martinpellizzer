Carex drucei

Carex drucei, commonly known as Druce's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or grassland habitats. It has triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex drucei is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.
