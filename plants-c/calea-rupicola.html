Calea rupicola

Calea rupicola, commonly known as the rock calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may have a hairy texture.

Calea rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
