Clematis aethusifolia var. latisecta

Clematis aethusifolia var. latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leaved clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers with a sweet fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or walls.
