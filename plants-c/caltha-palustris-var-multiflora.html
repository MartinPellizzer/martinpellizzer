Caltha palustris var. multiflora

Caltha palustris var. multiflora, commonly known as the marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in northern regions.

It features bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and flies, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
