Cordiglottis multicolor

Cordiglottis multicolor, commonly known as the multicolored heartleaf, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from deep greens to bright reds and purples, often resembling heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
