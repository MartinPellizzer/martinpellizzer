Clerodendrum catalpifolium

Clerodendrum catalpifolium, commonly known as the catalpa-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to red flowers, which bloom in the summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, resembling the shape of catalpa leaves, hence its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
