Coelogyne calcarata

Coelogyne calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, leathery leaves and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with a distinctive spurred lip.

Coelogyne calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
