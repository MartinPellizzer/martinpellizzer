Calligonum josephii

Calligonum josephii, commonly known as Joseph's tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing coastal and desert landscapes.

Its resilience to drought and extreme temperatures makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
