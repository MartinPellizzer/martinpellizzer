Clerodendrum seretii

Clerodendrum seretii, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in summer. Its flowers are followed by distinctive, woody capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
