Callilepis lancifolia

Callilepis lancifolia, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
