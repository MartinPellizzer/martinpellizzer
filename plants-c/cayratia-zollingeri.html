Cayratia zollingeri

Cayratia zollingeri, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Cayratia zollingeri is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
