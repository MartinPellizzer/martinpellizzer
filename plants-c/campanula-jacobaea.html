Campanula jacobaea

Campanula jacobaea, commonly known as the Jacob's tea or ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula jacobaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
