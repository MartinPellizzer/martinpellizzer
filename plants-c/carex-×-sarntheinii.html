Carex × sarntheinii

Carex × sarntheinii, commonly known as the Sarnthein sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species is native to parts of Europe and is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant typically grows to a height of 60 to 120 centimeters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
