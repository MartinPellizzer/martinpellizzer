Campanula rotundifolia var. delitschiana

Campanula rotundifolia var. delitschiana, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and broad, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
