Conophytum roodiae subsp. sanguineum

Conophytum roodiae subsp.

sanguineum, commonly known as the blood-red conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially when exposed to sunlight.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
