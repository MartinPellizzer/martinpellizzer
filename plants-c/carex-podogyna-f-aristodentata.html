Carex podogyna f. aristodentata

Carex podogyna f. aristodentata, commonly known as the aristate sedge, is a variant of the sedge species Carex podogyna.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaf tips that form a sharp, aristate (arrow-shaped) appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This ornamental plant is valued for its upright growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
