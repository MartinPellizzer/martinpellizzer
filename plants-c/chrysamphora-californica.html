Chrysamphora californica

Chrysamphora californica, commonly known as the California flamebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often used in native plant landscaping.
