Crataegus intermedia var. digyna

Crataegus intermedia var. digyna, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and small mammals but considered toxic to humans.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting wildlife.
