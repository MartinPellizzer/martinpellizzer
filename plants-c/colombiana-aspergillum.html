Colombiana aspergillum

Colombiana aspergillum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions. Its fruit, which resembles a small, hard pod, contains edible seeds used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
