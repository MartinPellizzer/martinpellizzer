Corylus avellana subsp. memorabilis

Corylus avellana subsp.

memorabilis, commonly known as the edible hazelnut, is a subspecies of the hazel tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellowish-green male catkins.

The female flowers develop into oval, edible nuts encased in a spiky husk. The subspecies is notable for its high oil content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and industrial uses.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in hedgerows or as an ornamental plant.
