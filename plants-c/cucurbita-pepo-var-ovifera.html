Cucurbita pepo var. ovifera

Cucurbita pepo var. ovifera, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits with a rough, ridged surface.

The plant is an annual vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. Its fruits are typically orange to yellow in color and can weigh several pounds, making them suitable for both culinary and ornamental use. The plant's leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it has a robust root system that helps it establish quickly in well-drained soil.

It is commonly cultivated for its edible seeds and flesh, which are rich in nutrients.
