Caprifolium glehnii

Caprifolium glehnii, commonly known as the Glehn's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in early autumn and is an important food source for birds and small mammals.
