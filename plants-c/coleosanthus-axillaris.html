Coleosanthus axillaris

Coleosanthus axillaris, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic species, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its environment.

The flowers are usually brightly colored, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects and birds.
