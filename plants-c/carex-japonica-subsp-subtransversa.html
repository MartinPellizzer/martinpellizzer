Carex japonica subsp. subtransversa

Carex japonica subsp.

subtransversa, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
