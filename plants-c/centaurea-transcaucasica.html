Centaurea transcaucasica

Centaurea transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea transcaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
