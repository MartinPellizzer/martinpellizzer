Cynanchum glaucum

Cynanchum glaucum, commonly known as blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cynanchum glaucum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
