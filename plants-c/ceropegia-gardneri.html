Ceropegia gardneri

Ceropegia gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia gardneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
