Capparis zeylanica

Capparis zeylanica, commonly known as the Sri Lankan caper, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with thorny branches and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
