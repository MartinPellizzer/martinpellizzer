Cyrtopodium holstii

Cyrtopodium holstii, commonly known as the Holst's corydalis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cyrtopodium holstii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
