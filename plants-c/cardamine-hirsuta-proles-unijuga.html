Cardamine hirsuta proles unijuga

Cardamine hirsuta proles unijuga, commonly known as the single-seeded watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a siliqua, containing a single seed, which is a distinguishing characteristic of the subspecies.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its delicate flavor in culinary uses.
