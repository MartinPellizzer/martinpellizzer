Cyperus giolii var. nogalensis

Cyperus giolii var. nogalensis, commonly known as the Mexican flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. This variant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The plant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
