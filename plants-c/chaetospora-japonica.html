Chaetospora japonica

Chaetospora japonica, commonly known as Japanese haircap moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and the presence of specialized structures called rhizoids that anchor it to substrates. The leaves are usually elongated and have a distinct midrib, with a smooth or serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rocky surfaces.

This moss plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
