Calyptrocoryne cochinchinensis

Calyptrocoryne cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperales and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit that resembles true star anise. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit develops in clusters and is used in traditional medicine for its purported antispasmodic and digestive properties.

The botanical features of this plant make it distinct within its family and contribute to its ecological and medicinal significance.
