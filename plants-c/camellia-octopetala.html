Camellia octopetala

Camellia octopetala, commonly known as the eight-petaled camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have eight petals, which give it its common name. The plant is an evergreen shrub or small tree, often growing up to 3 meters in height. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

Camellia octopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
