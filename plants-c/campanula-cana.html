Campanula cana

Campanula cana, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it thrives in well-drained soil in partial to full shade.

Campanula cana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
