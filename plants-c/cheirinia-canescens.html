Cheirinia canescens

Cheirinia canescens, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Cheirinia canescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
