Celtis micrantha

Celtis micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. Celtis micrantha is often found in dry, open woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
