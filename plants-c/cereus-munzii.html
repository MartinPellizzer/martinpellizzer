Cereus munzii

Cereus munzii, commonly known as the desert rose, is a cacti species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of large, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are typically white or pink and emit a subtle fragrance.

Cereus munzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
