Camellia polygama

Camellia polygama, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Camellia polygama is also valued for its ability to produce edible seeds, which can be pressed into oil.
