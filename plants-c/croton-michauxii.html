Croton michauxii

Croton michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Croton michauxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
