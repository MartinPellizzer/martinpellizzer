Carex podocarpa f. pallidior

Carex podocarpa f. pallidior, commonly known as the pale-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex podocarpa species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is native to parts of North America. Its leaves are generally narrower and lighter in color compared to the typical form, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
