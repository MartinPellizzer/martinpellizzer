Colletia pungens

Colletia pungens, commonly known as the spiny candelabra cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp spines, which help to deter herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its stems to survive in dry environments.

Colletia pungens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique structure and vibrant blooms.
