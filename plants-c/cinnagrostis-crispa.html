Cinnagrostis crispa

Cinnagrostis crispa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers with five petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cinnagrostis crispa is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinator populations.
