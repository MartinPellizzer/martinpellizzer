Carduus × woodwardii

Carduus × woodwardii, commonly known as the western thistle.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

It is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
