Calea clematidifolia

Calea clematidifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
