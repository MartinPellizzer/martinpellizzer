Cirsium ferox

Cirsium ferox, commonly known as the bristly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant is notable for its dense, spiny foliage and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. Cirsium ferox thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its hardy nature and ecological role in supporting pollinators make it an important species in its native habitats.
