Calamintha officinalis f. brevifrons

Calamintha officinalis f. brevifrons, commonly known as narrow-leaved calamint, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in terminal spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
