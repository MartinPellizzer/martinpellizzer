Carex gracilispica

Carex gracilispica, commonly known as slender-spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces three-nucleate seeds.

Carex gracilispica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
