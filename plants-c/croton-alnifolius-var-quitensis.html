Croton alnifolius var. quitensis

Croton alnifolius var. quitensis, commonly known as the Andean croton, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by distinctive, often colorful fruits. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
