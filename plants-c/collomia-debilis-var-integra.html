Collomia debilis var. integra

Collomia debilis var. integra, commonly known as the desert phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
