Carex scita var. riishirensis

Carex scita var. riishirensis, commonly known as the Shikoku sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
