Centropogon isabellinus

Centropogon isabellinus, commonly known as the Isabella's fireplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

Centropogon isabellinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
