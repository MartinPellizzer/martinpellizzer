Chelone angustifolia

Chelone angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in soft, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
