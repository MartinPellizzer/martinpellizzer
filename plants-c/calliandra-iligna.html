Calliandra iligna

Calliandra iligna, commonly known as the everblooming coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery clusters of bright red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom continuously throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Calliandra iligna is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
