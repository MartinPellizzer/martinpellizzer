Cyathea gracillima

Cyathea gracillima, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside.
