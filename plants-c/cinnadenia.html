Cinnadenia

Cinnadenia, commonly known as the "Cinnamomum of the Andes," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. These plants are shrubs or small trees, characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are often leathery and have a distinct, spicy scent, similar to that of cinnamon.

Cinnadenia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their unique foliage and fragrance.
