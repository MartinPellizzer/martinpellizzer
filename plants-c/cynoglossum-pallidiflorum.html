Cynoglossum pallidiflorum

Cynoglossum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Cynoglossum pallidiflorum is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
