Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cayennense

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cayennense, commonly known as cinnamon bark or cassia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its bark is harvested for its essential oils and tannins, which contribute to its commercial value.

This variety is often cultivated for its economic importance in the spice trade.
