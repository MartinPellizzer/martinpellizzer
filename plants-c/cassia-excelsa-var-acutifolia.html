Cassia excelsa var. acutifolia

Cassia excelsa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves with acuminate leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are followed by long, slender pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
