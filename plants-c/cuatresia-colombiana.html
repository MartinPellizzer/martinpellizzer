Cuatresia colombiana

Cuatresia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian quatrilla, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.
