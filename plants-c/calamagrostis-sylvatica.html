Calamagrostis sylvatica

Calamagrostis sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces panicle-shaped inflorescences with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Calamagrostis sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
