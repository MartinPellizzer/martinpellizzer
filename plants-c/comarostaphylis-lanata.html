Comarostaphylis lanata

Comarostaphylis lanata, commonly known as the woolly phyllirea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
