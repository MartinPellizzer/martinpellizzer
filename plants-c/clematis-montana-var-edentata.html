Clematis montana var. edentata

Clematis montana var. edentata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 20 feet, with palmately compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
