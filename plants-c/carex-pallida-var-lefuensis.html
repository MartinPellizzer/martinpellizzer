Carex pallida var. lefuensis

Carex pallida var. lefuensis, commonly known as the Lefu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
