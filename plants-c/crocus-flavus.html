Crocus flavus

Crocus flavus, commonly known as the yellow crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Iridaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like irises and gladiolas.

The yellow crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
