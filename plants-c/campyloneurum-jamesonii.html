Campyloneurum jamesonii

Campyloneurum jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's rock fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in shaded, humid environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
