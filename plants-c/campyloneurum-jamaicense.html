Campyloneurum jamaicense

Campyloneurum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces sporophytes that develop into cones, which release spores for reproduction. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
