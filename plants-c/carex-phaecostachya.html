Carex phaecostachya

Carex phaecostachya, commonly known as black-stalked sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex phaecostachya plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
