Cistus imbricatus

Cistus imbricatus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
