Cerophyllum douglasii

Cerophyllum douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's water shield, is a semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or wetland environments, with its leaves floating on the water surface. The plant has broad, shield-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps repel water. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Cerophyllum douglasii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
