Cerastium vulgatum var. kajanense

Cerastium vulgatum var. kajanense, commonly known as the Kajanense mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
