Clematis cirrhosa var. obtusifolia

Clematis cirrhosa var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems with soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with rounded lobes, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its cascading growth habit.
