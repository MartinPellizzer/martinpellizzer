Calea solidaginea

Calea solidaginea, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves. Calea solidaginea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
