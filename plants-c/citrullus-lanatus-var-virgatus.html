Citrullus lanatus var. virgatus

Citrullus lanatus var. virgatus, commonly known as the horned melon, is a unique variant of the watermelon species.

It is characterized by its thick, spiky rind that resembles a horned helmet, giving it its common name. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, arid climates. Its fruit is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature.

The flesh inside is usually a pale yellow or white, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor.
