Carduus nitidus

Carduus nitidus, commonly known as the slender carducum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, rounded cluster.

Carduus nitidus is often found in meadows and along roadsides, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
