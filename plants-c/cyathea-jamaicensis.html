Cyathea jamaicensis

Cyathea jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in soil while absorbing nutrients.

Cyathea jamaicensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
