Colocasia antiquorum var. esculenta

Colocasia antiquorum var. esculenta, commonly known as elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are also used in traditional cuisines, particularly in Southeast Asian countries.

The plant belongs to the Araceae family and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden beneath the leaf structures.
