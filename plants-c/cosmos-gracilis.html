Cosmos gracilis

Cosmos gracilis, commonly known as slender cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with yellow centers, and bloom from summer to fall.

Cosmos gracilis is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
