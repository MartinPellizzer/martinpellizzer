Cucumis melo var. durans

Cucumis melo var. durans, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its long, slender shape and smooth, thin skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant typically grows on a vine with lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. The fruit is usually harvested when it is still green, though it can be left to ripen slightly for a sweeter flavor.

It is valued for its crisp texture and mild, sweet taste, making it a popular choice in various cuisines.
