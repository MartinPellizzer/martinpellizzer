Calamagrostis stricta var. brevior

Calamagrostis stricta var. brevior, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
