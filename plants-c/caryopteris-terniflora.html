Caryopteris terniflora

Caryopteris terniflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Caryopteris terniflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
