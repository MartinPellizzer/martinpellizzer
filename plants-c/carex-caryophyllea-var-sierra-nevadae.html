Carex caryophyllea var. sierra-nevadae

Carex caryophyllea var. sierra-nevadae, commonly known as Sierra Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
