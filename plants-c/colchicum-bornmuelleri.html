Colchicum bornmuelleri

Colchicum bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's colchicum, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking, showy flowers with bright purple petals and a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

The flowering period occurs in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural environment.
