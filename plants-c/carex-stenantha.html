Carex stenantha

Carex stenantha, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and streambanks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
