Carduus kirghisicus

Carduus kirghisicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a tall, spiny stem and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
