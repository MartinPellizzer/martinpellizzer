Carex pallescens

Carex pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex pallescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
