Capparis bhamoensis

Capparis bhamoensis, commonly known as the Bhamo glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Myanmar.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes species known for their showy, often yellow or red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Capparis bhamoensis is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 1,500 meters.
