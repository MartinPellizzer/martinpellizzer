Carduus meonanthus subsp. valentinus

Carduus meonanthus subsp.

valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the spring.

Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and a tall, unbranched stem that supports its composite flowers.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often ecologically important species in its native habitat.
