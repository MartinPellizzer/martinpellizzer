Cimicifuga cimicifuga var. intermedia

Cimicifuga cimicifuga var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are compound, with feather-like divisions, and it produces toxic compounds that deter herbivores.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
