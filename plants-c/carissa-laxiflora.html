Carissa laxiflora

Carissa laxiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that ripens to a reddish hue.

Carissa laxiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
