Cirsium × reyi

Cirsium × reyi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × reyi is often considered an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
