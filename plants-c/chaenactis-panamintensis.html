Chaenactis panamintensis

Chaenactis panamintensis, commonly known as the Panamint sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. This plant is well adapted to arid environments, with hairy leaves that reduce water loss and deep root systems for accessing groundwater. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme desert conditions.
