Calotropis hamiltonii

Calotropis hamiltonii, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its hairy stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and has been used in traditional medicine.

Calotropis hamiltonii plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
