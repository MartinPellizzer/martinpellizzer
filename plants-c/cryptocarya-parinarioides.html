Cryptocarya parinarioides

Cryptocarya parinarioides, commonly known as the Australian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are yellowish-green in color, followed by oval-shaped berries that are dark blue when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
