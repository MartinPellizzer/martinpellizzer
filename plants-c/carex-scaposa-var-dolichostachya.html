Carex scaposa var. dolichostachya

Carex scaposa var. dolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
