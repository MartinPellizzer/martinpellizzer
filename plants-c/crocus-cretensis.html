Crocus cretensis

Crocus cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the island of Crete.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.

The Cretan crocus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
