Cephaelis tetrandra

Cephaelis tetrandra, commonly known as the four-angled corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cephaelis tetrandra is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
