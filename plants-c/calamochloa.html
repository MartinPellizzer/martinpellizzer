Calamochloa

Calamochloa, commonly known as the swamp canegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plants typically grow in wet or swampy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions.
