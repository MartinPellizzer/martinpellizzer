Cissus adenocaulis var. eglandulosa

Cissus adenocaulis var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cissus, is a climbing vine native to parts of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with prominent veins and small, glandular structures on the leaf surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by three-winged fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
