Calamus trigynus

Calamus trigynus, commonly known as the three-styled calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with three stigmas, giving it its specific epithet "trigynus.". It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Calamus trigynus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
