Cytisus supinus var. microphyllus

Cytisus supinus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It features small, narrow leaves and produces yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal environments.
