Carlina vulgaris subsp. acanthophylla

Carlina vulgaris subsp.

acanthophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite heads. Its foliage is characterized by rough, serrated margins and glandular dots.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
