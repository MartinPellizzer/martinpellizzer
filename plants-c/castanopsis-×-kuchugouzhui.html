Castanopsis × kuchugouzhui

Castanopsis × kuchugouzhui, commonly known as the hybrid chinquapin, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Castanopsis sieboldii and Castanopsis tibetana.

It is a deciduous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and western China. The plant features dense, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.

The hybrid exhibits characteristics of both parent species, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
