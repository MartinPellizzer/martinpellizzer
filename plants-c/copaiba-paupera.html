Copaiba paupera

Copaiba paupera, commonly known as white copaiba, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its resin, known as copal, is widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Copaiba paupera plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
