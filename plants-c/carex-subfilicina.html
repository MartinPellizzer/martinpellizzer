Carex subfilicina

Carex subfilicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex subfilicina plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
