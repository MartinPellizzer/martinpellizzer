Carex brizoides var. remota

Carex brizoides var. remota, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
