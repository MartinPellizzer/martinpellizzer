Chamaesyce celastroides var. saxicola

Chamaesyce celastroides var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
