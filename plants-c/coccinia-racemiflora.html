Coccinia racemiflora

Coccinia racemiflora, commonly known as the clustered cucumber or jungle cucumber, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and gourds. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and climbing.

Coccinia racemiflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.
