Callista terminalis

Callista terminalis, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

The flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in island ecosystems.
