Carex praecox f. nana

Carex praecox f. nana, commonly known as dwarf early sedge, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to moist, temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistently moist soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in wetland gardens and as a ground cover in shaded areas.
