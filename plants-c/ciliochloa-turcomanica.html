Ciliochloa turcomanica

Ciliochloa turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting a low-growing, clump-forming habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue sheen, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense, tuft-like clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and reproductive structures, which are typical of the Poaceae family.
