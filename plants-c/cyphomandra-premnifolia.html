Cyphomandra premnifolia

Cyphomandra premnifolia, commonly known as the purple potato or wild potato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are its edible parts. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, pubescent layer.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and unique tuberous structure.
