Calceolaria tetragona subsp. endopogon

Calceolaria tetragona subsp.

endopogon, commonly known as the purple calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking, pouch-like flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching. Calceolaria tetragona subsp.

endopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
