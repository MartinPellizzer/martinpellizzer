Colchicum atticum

Colchicum atticum, commonly known as the Greek autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, erect stems, and they are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

Colchicum atticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
