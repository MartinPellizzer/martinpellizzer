Convolvulus turpethum

Convolvulus turpethum, commonly known as the turpethum bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Convolvulus turpethum is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and arid conditions.
