Capparis tonduzii

Capparis tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduz's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Capparis tonduzii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
