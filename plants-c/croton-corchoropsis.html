Croton corchoropsis

Croton corchoropsis, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
