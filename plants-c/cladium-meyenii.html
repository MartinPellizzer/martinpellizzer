Cladium meyenii

Cladium meyenii, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and have a grass-like appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and swamps. Its rhizomes form dense stands, contributing to the structural complexity of wetland ecosystems.

Cladium meyenii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
