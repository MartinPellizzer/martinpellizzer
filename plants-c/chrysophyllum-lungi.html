Chrysophyllum lungi

Chrysophyllum lungi, commonly known as the African cherry or lungi cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
