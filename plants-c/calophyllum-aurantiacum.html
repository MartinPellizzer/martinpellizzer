Calophyllum aurantiacum

Calophyllum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a dense canopy of glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and dye-making.
