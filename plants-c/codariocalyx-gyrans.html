Codariocalyx gyrans

Codariocalyx gyrans, commonly known as the spiral calyx vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped calyx that wraps around its stem as it grows. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Codariocalyx gyrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
