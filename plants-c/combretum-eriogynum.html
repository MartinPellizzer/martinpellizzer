Combretum eriogynum

Combretum eriogynum, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
