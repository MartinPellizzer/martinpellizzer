Ceanothus triflorus

Ceanothus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus triflorus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
