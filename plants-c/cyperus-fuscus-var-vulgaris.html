Cyperus fuscus var. vulgaris

Cyperus fuscus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways. Its reproductive structures are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus fuscus var. vulgaris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
