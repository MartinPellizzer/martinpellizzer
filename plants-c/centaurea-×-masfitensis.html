Centaurea × masfitensis

Centaurea × masfitensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a cross between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea solstitialis, resulting in a species with distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Centaurea × masfitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
