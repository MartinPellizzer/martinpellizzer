Cyperus bellus var. tanganyicanus

Cyperus bellus var. tanganyicanus, commonly known as the African sedges, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps near water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
