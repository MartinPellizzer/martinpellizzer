Colchicum heldreichii

Colchicum heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has a bulbous corm, which serves as its storage organ and allows it to survive harsh winter conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are typically broad and lance-shaped.

Colchicum heldreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
