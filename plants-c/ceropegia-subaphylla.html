Ceropegia subaphylla

Ceropegia subaphylla, commonly known as the white-flowered African violet, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, somewhat hairy stems and leaves that are typically rounded or ovate. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is popular as a houseplant and is relatively easy to care for.
