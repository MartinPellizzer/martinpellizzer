Chirocalyx abyssinicus

Chirocalyx abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian chirocalyx, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx that remains attached to the fruit even after maturity, a feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
