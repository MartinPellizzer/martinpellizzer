Centaurea × romanica

Centaurea × romanica, commonly known as the Roman cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by ray florets and a central disc of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in herbal medicine.
