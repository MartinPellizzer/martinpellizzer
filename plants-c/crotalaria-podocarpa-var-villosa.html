Crotalaria podocarpa var. villosa

Crotalaria podocarpa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its distinctive seed pods that are inflated and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
