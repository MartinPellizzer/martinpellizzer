Cacalia lucida

Cacalia lucida, commonly known as the shining cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features delicate, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Cacalia lucida is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
