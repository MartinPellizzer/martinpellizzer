Calceolaria crenata var. floribunda

Calceolaria crenata var. floribunda, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble small cups, often with a golden or orange hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces numerous blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors contribute to its ornamental value.
