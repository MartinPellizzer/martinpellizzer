Chrysophyllum nitidissimum

Chrysophyllum nitidissimum, commonly known as the glossy apple or African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, sweet, and juicy fruits with a white, soft pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture-making.
