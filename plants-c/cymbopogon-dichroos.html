Cymbopogon dichroos

Cymbopogon dichroos, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Silvergrass is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
