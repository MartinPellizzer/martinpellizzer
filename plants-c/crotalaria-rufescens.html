Crotalaria rufescens

Crotalaria rufescens, commonly known as the red root cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.

Crotalaria rufescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
