Centaurea dubia

Centaurea dubia, commonly known as the doubtful bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Centaurea dubia is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants.
