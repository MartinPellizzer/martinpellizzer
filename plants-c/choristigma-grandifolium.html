Choristigma grandifolium

Choristigma grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved choristigma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
