Canarium hirtipetalum

Canarium hirtipetalum, commonly known as the hairy-petaled canarium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its fragrant blooms. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Canarium hirtipetalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
