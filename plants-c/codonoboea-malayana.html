Codonoboea malayana

Codonoboea malayana, commonly known as the Malay mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
