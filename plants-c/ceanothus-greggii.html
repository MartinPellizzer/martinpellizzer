Ceanothus greggii

Ceanothus greggii, commonly known as the Gregg's ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Ceanothus greggii is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
