Cestrum poasanum

Cestrum poasanum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its fruits are berry-like and toxic.

Cestrum poasanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
