Calendula tragus

Calendula tragus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
