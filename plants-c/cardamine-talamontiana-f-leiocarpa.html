Cardamine talamontiana f. leiocarpa

Cardamine talamontiana f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of ovate, smooth, and slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its morphology and habitat make it a notable species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
