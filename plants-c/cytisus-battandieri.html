Cytisus battandieri

Cytisus battandieri, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus battandieri is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
