Ceratostylis longifolia

Ceratostylis longifolia, commonly known as the long-styled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers have a distinctive, elongated spur that is a notable feature in its morphology.

Ceratostylis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
