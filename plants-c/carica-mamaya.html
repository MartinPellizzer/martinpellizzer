Carica mamaya

Carica mamaya, commonly known as papaya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flesh-filled fruits. The plant produces a single, large fruit per flowering stalk, which ripens quickly and is rich in enzymes like papain. Its botanical features include a short, fibrous stem and a distinctive, sweet-smelling flower.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for its nutritious fruit.
