Calophyllum mesoamericanum

Calophyllum mesoamericanum, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, orange-colored fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
