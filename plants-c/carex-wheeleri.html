Carex wheeleri

Carex wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex wheeleri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
