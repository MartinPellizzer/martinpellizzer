Camellia exigua

Camellia exigua, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep red, bloom in winter and early spring.

Camellia exigua prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
