Cleisostoma fratrum

Cleisostoma fratrum, commonly known as the "brother's orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces a single, showy bloom that emerges from a leafless stem, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a striking contrast to the flowers.

Cleisostoma fratrum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
