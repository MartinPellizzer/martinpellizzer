Cyrtorchis arcuata subsp. arcuata

Cyrtorchis arcuata subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the curved-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne above the leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is often found in the Southeastern region of the United States.
