Coryphantha chlorantha

Coryphantha chlorantha, commonly known as the blue ghost cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale blue to greenish-gray stems, which are cylindrical and often covered in a whitish coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise muted appearance. Coryphantha chlorantha is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it popular among succulent enthusiasts.

Its unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a striking addition to arid landscapes and rock gardens.
