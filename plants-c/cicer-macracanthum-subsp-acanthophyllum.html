Cicer macracanthum subsp. acanthophyllum

Cicer macracanthum subsp.

acanthophyllum, commonly known as the thorny vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant.
