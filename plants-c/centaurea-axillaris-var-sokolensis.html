Centaurea axillaris var. sokolensis

Centaurea axillaris var. sokolensis, commonly known as the Sokolov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
