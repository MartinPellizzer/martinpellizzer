Carduus nutans subsp. macrocephalus

Carduus nutans subsp.

macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, spiny, purple flower heads that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter.

The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
