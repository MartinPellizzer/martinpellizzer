Cissus jaegeri

Cissus jaegeri, commonly known as the silver arrow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardens and trellises.
