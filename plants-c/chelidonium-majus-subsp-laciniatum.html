Chelidonium majus subsp. laciniatum

Chelidonium majus subsp.

laciniatum, commonly known as the shredded celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from May to June.

The plant has distinctive, lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its latex-rich sap contains alkaloids, which can be toxic to humans and animals.
