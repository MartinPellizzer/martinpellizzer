Cerastium arvense var. hirsutum

Cerastium arvense var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and meadows.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats.
