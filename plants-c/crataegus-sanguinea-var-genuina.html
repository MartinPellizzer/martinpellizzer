Crataegus sanguinea var. genuina

Crataegus sanguinea var. genuina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
