Chusquea scabra

Chusquea scabra, commonly known as the rough chusquea, is a species of bamboo native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, wiry culms and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow, pointed, and covered with fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Chusquea scabra is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, upright clumps.
