Chupalon splendens

Chupalon splendens, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Chupalon splendens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
