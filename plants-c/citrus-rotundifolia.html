Citrus rotundifolia

Citrus rotundifolia, commonly known as the calamondin orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, round, orange fruits that are typically less than an inch in diameter. The fruit has a thick, bitter rind and a segmented, juicy pulp that is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in subtropical regions.
