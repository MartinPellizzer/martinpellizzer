Claoxylon paucinerve

Claoxylon paucinerve, commonly known as the slender tamanu tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves with a distinct venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
