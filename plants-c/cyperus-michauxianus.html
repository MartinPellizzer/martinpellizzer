Cyperus michauxianus

Cyperus michauxianus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Cyperus michauxianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
