Cirsium spurium

Cirsium spurium, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with rough, serrated edges and a woolly texture, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Cirsium spurium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
