Campanula stricta var. muricata

Campanula stricta var. muricata, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of short, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
