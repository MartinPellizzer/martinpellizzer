Cladium crinitum

Cladium crinitum, commonly known as the silky sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, making it an important component of marsh ecosystems. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize the soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cladium crinitum plays a vital role in wetland vegetation, contributing to water filtration and nutrient cycling.
