Crepis pannonica subsp. pannonica

Crepis pannonica subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in dry grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with a hairy stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal head of small yellow florets surrounded by white bracts.

The species is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
