Calceolaria integrifolia subvar. rugosa

Calceolaria integrifolia subvar. rugosa, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are often purple or violet in color. The plant features hairy, rugose (wrinkled) leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
