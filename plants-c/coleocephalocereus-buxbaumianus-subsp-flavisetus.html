Coleocephalocereus buxbaumianus subsp. flavisetus

Coleocephalocereus buxbaumianus subsp.

flavisetus, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically yellow to green in color.

The plant produces white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
