Cucumis melo var. vard

Cucumis melo var. vard, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its thick, smooth rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruits are usually oblong or round, with a pale green or yellowish exterior.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
