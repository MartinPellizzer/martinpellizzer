Croton perdicipes var. latifolius

Croton perdicipes var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
