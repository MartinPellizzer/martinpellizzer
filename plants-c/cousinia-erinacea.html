Cousinia erinacea

Cousinia erinacea, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its distinctive, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine habitats.

Cousinia erinacea is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
