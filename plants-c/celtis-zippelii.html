Celtis zippelii

Celtis zippelii, commonly known as the Zippel's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. Celtis zippelii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 feet.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
