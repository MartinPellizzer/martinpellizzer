Circaea alpina f. simplicissima

Circaea alpina f. simplicissima, commonly known as the alpine woodruff, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
