Carex laxa

Carex laxa, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
