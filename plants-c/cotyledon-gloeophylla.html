Cotyledon gloeophylla

Cotyledon gloeophylla, commonly known as the blue cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, usually in spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Cotyledon gloeophylla is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
