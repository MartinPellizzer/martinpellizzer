Carex elliottii

Carex elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its cylindrical, grass-like leaves are usually dark green, and it produces spike-like flower clusters in the summer.

Carex elliottii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
