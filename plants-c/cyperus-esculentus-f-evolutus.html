Cyperus esculentus f. evolutus

Cyperus esculentus f. evolutus, commonly known as the tuberous sedge, is a variant of the tuberous sedge characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant is notable for its swollen rhizomes, which serve as storage organs and are edible in some cultures. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color.

The leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its adaptability in various moist environments.
