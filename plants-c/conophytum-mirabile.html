Conophytum mirabile

Conophytum mirabile, commonly known as the "Cape Rainplant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and having a shallow root system.

Conophytum mirabile is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
