Crepidium witkowskianum

Crepidium witkowskianum, commonly known as the Witkowsky's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of white and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
