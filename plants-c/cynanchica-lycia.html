Cynanchica lycia

Cynanchica lycia, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe ecosystems.

Cynanchica lycia plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
