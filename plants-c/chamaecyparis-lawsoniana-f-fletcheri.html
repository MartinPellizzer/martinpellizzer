Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. fletcheri

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. fletcheri, commonly known as the Fletcher's Lawson cypress, is a unique form of the Lawson cypress native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are a rich, dark green. This variety exhibits a slow growth rate, making it ideal for small garden spaces or as a specimen plant. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes flaky with age, adding visual interest.

The plant is evergreen, providing year-round foliage and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
