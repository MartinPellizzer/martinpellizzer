Conicosia

Conicosia, commonly known as the "Everlasting" plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Conicosia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Its name derives from the Greek words "konis" meaning dust and "ios" meaning resembling, referring to the fine, powdery texture of its flower heads.
