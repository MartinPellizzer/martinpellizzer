Cyperus pseudosurinamensis

Cyperus pseudosurinamensis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, fibrous seeds.

Cyperus pseudosurinamensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
