Chenopodium petiolare var. sinuatum

Chenopodium petiolare var. sinuatum, commonly known as garden spinach, is a cultivated variant of the Chenopodium genus.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically harvested before they fully mature. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable. It has a shallow root system and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its leaves are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable addition to many diets.
