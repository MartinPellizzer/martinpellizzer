Cattleya patinii var. alba

Cattleya patinii var. alba, commonly known as the white cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pink or greenish tint at the base. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
