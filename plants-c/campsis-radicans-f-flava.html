Campsis radicans f. flava

Campsis radicans f. flava, commonly known as the yellow trumpetvine, is a cultivar of the native trumpetvine.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which contrast sharply with its bright green foliage. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 15 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
