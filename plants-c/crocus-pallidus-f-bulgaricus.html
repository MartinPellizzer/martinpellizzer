Crocus pallidus f. bulgaricus

Crocus pallidus f. bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian crocus, is a botanical variant of the pale crocus.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly Bulgaria. The plant produces delicate, nodding flowers with pale yellow to cream petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its unique floral characteristics and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
