Chamaemelum incrassatum

Chamaemelum incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and dark green, thick leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
