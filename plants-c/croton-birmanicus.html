Croton birmanicus

Croton birmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Croton birmanicus is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
