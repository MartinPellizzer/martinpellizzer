Carduus candicans

Carduus candicans, commonly known as the showy cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and striking yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, somewhat woody stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has historical uses in herbal medicine and as a source of vegetable matter.
