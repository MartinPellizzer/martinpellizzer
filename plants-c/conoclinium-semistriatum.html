Conoclinium semistriatum

Conoclinium semistriatum, commonly known as the Mexican honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
