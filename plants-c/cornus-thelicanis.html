Cornus thelicanis

Cornus thelicanis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cornel.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Cornaceae, characterized by its slender leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, woodland environments. Its fruits are small, red drupes that mature in the fall, providing food for various wildlife.

Cornus thelicanis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
