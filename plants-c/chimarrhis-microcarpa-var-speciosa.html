Chimarrhis microcarpa var. speciosa

Chimarrhis microcarpa var. speciosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The Christmas bell is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
