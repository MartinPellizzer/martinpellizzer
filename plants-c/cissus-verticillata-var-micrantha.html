Cissus verticillata var. micrantha

Cissus verticillata var. micrantha, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a climbing or trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from red to black as they mature. It is adapted to thrive in rocky, arid environments and is often found growing on cliffs or in rocky crevices.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
