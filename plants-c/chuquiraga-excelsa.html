Chuquiraga excelsa

Chuquiraga excelsa, commonly known as the Andean burr or chuchuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spherical, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments, typically between 2,000 and 4,000 meters above sea level.

Chuquiraga excelsa is adapted to harsh, cold climates and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.
