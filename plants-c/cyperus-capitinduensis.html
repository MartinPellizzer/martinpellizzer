Cyperus capitinduensis

Cyperus capitinduensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

Cyperus capitinduensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
