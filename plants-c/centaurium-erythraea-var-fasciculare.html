Centaurium erythraea var. fasciculare

Centaurium erythraea var. fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered bloodwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, bright red flowers clustered in dense whorls. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its striking floral display and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
