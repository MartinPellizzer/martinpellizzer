Cistus kahiricus

Cistus kahiricus, commonly known as the Sudanese rock rose, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in spring. The plant has gray-green, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in sandy soils, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Cistus kahiricus is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
