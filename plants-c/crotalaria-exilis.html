Crotalaria exilis

Crotalaria exilis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and erect stems. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria exilis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
