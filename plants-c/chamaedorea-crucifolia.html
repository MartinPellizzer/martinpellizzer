Chamaedorea crucifolia

Chamaedorea crucifolia, commonly known as the crossleaf bamboo, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features narrow, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a cross-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers bright, indirect light with high humidity. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, making it an attractive addition to indoor spaces.

Chamaedorea crucifolia is well-suited for container gardening and requires well-draining soil to thrive.
