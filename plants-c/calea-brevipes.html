Calea brevipes

Calea brevipes, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. Its leaves are broad and oval, often covered in fine hairs.

Calea brevipes is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
