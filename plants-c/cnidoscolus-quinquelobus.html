Cnidoscolus quinquelobus

Cnidoscolus quinquelobus, commonly known as the "arrow leaf" or "chaya," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five lobes, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spiny, egg-shaped fruits containing edible seeds. Its stems and leaves contain compounds that can cause irritation, requiring careful handling.

Despite its potential hazards, it is valued for its nutritional content and medicinal properties.
