Crataegus pinnatifida f. betulifolia

Crataegus pinnatifida f. betulifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, willow-like leaves that are narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red to orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
