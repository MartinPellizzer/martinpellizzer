Calamintha alba

Calamintha alba, commonly known as white hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a minty fragrance.

Calamintha alba is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
