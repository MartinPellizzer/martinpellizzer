Carex aquatilis f. laxa

Carex aquatilis f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf water sedge, is a variant of the water sedge species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, often forming a mat-like structure on the surface of water or moist soil. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. It thrives in shallow water or damp terrestrial habitats, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
