Cebatha pendula

Cebatha pendula, commonly known as the hanging cebatha, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, while its flowers emit a subtle fragrance.

Cebatha pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and private collections.
