Camellia assamica var. polyneura

Camellia assamica var. polyneura, commonly known as the Assam camellia, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas and northeastern India.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is evergreen and typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive central yellow stripe, and are highly valued for their ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
