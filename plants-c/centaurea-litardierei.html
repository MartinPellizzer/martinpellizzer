Centaurea litardierei

Centaurea litardierei, commonly known as the Litardiere's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. It is a perennial species that produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae.
