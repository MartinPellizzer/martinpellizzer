Costus spiralis var. jacquinii

Costus spiralis var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's spiral costus, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to orange flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden and horticultural collections.
