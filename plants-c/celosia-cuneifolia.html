Celosia cuneifolia

Celosia cuneifolia, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Celosia cuneifolia is known for its long-lasting blooms and is popular in gardens and floral arrangements.
