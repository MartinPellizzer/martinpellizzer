Cephalaria plumosa

Cephalaria plumosa, commonly known as the woolly cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, fluffy appearance, with grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Cephalaria plumosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
