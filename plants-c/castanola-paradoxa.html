Castanola paradoxa

Castanola paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical castanola, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, elongated fruit capsules. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Castanola paradoxa is considered a species of conservation concern.
