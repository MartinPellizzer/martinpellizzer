Crataegus laevigata f. ellipticifolia

Crataegus laevigata f. ellipticifolia, commonly known as the ellipticifolia hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves, which are typically broader and more elongated than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
