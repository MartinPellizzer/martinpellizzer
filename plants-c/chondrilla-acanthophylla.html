Chondrilla acanthophylla

Chondrilla acanthophylla, commonly known as the spiny leaf chondrilla, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Chondrilla acanthophylla is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
