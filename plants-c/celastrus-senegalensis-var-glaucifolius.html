Celastrus senegalensis var. glaucifolius

Celastrus senegalensis var. glaucifolius, commonly known as the African bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, simple, and often glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
