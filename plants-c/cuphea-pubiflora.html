Cuphea pubiflora

Cuphea pubiflora, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea pubiflora is relatively drought-tolerant once established, requiring minimal maintenance.
