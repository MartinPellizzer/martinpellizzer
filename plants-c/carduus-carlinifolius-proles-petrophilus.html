Carduus carlinifolius proles petrophilus

Carduus carlinifolius proles petrophilus, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial plant characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, woolly leaves.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
