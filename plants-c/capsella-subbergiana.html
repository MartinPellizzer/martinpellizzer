Capsella subbergiana

Capsella subbergiana, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
