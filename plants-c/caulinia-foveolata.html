Caulinia foveolata

Caulinia foveolata, commonly known as the horned violets, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like appendages on its leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Caulinia foveolata is valued for its unique morphology and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
