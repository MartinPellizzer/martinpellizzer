Chenopodium succosum

Chenopodium succosum, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Chenopodium succosum is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
