Ceropegia manoharii

Ceropegia manoharii, commonly known as the Manohari's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
