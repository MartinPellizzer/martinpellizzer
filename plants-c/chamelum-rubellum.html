Chamelum rubellum

Chamelum rubellum, commonly known as the red velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elongated, pinnate leaves that turn a striking red or orange color during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
