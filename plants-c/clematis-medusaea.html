Clematis medusaea

Clematis medusaea, commonly known as the ghostly clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and delicate blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
