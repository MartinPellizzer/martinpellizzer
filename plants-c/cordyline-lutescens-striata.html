Cordyline lutescens-striata

Cordyline lutescens-striata, commonly known as the golden stripe cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are yellow-green with distinct white or cream stripes. The leaves are glossy and often have a waxy texture, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and ability to add a tropical feel to gardens.
