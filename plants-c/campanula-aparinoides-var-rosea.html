Campanula aparinoides var. rosea

Campanula aparinoides var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pink to rose-colored flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in a fine layer of white down. This variety thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
