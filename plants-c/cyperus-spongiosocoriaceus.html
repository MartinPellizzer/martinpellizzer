Cyperus spongiosocoriaceus

Cyperus spongiosocoriaceus, commonly known as the sponge-like sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features dense, fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in three rows. Cyperus spongiosocoriaceus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its unique sponge-like structure in the roots helps it absorb and retain water efficiently.
