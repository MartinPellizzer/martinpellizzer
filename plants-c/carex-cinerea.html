Carex cinerea

Carex cinerea, commonly known as gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex cinerea is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
