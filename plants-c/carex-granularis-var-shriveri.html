Carex granularis var. shriveri

Carex granularis var. shriveri, commonly known as Shriver's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive texture and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.

This variety is also noted for its ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
