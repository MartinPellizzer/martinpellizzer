Carissa hirsuta

Carissa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Carissa hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
