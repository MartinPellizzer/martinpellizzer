Carex setifolia

Carex setifolia, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. Native to wet or moist habitats, it thrives in environments with good drainage and partial to full sunlight. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
