Carex rhizantha

Carex rhizantha, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Carex rhizantha produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
