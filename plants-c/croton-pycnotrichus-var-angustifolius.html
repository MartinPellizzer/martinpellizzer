Croton pycnotrichus var. angustifolius

Croton pycnotrichus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
