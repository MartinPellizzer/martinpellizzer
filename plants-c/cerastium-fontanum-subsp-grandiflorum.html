Cerastium fontanum subsp. grandiflorum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with dense pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.
