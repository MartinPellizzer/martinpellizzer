Calathea erythrolepis

Calathea erythrolepis, commonly known as the Crimson-veined Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with deep red veins contrasting against its green leaves. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for those seeking vibrant, low-maintenance houseplants.
