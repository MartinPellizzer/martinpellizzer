Cheiropsis semitriloba

Cheiropsis semitriloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Cheiropsis semitriloba is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
