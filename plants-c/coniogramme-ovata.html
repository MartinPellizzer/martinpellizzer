Coniogramme ovata

Coniogramme ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases known as sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Coniogramme ovata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
