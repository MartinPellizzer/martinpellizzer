Columnea coronocrypta

Columnea coronocrypta, commonly known as the Crowned Crypta Columnea, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its foliage consists of elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Columnea coronocrypta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
