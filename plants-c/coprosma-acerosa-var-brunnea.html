Coprosma acerosa var. brunnea

Coprosma acerosa var. brunnea, commonly known as the bronzewoods or bronze coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that exhibits a striking bronze to reddish hue, especially in full sun. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth texture and a waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
