Coffea dactylifera

Coffea dactylifera, commonly known as the Arabian coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, thriving in tropical climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ornamental and economic significance.
