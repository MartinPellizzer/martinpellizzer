Chamaecytisus litwinowii

Chamaecytisus litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinow's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has aromatic, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
