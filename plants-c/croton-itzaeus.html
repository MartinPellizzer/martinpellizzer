Croton itzaeus

Croton itzaeus, commonly known as the Itza croton, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
