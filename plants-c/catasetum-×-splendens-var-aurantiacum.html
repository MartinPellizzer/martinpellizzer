Catasetum × splendens var. aurantiacum

Catasetum × splendens var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex flowering structure typical of the Catasetum genus.

This variety is prized for its striking color and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates.
