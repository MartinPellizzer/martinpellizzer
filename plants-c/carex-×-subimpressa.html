Carex × subimpressa

Carex × subimpressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. This plant is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Carex × subimpressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
