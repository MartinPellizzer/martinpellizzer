Carex hypoleucos

Carex hypoleucos, commonly known as the white-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically light green to white in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex hypoleucos plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
