Cosmos roseus

Cosmos roseus, commonly known as the profumata or the sweet cosmos, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers have a subtle, sweet fragrance, which adds to its ornamental appeal.

Cosmos roseus is often cultivated as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
