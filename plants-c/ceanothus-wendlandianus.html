Ceanothus wendlandianus

Ceanothus wendlandianus, commonly known as the Wendland's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ceanothus wendlandianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
