Chamissoa muricata

Chamissoa muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf chamissoa, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dark green, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Chamissoa muricata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
