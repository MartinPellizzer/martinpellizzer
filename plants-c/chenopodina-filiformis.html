Chenopodina filiformis

Chenopodina filiformis, commonly known as the slender chenopod, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Chenopodina filiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
