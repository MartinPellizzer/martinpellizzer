Ceropegia burchardii

Ceropegia burchardii, commonly known as the Burchard's arrowhead, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
