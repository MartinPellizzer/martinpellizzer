Croton cooperianus

Croton cooperianus, commonly known as the red-cork croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in the spring. Its leaves are thick and waxy, with a rough texture, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

Croton cooperianus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
