Cucumis melo var. prescottii

Cucumis melo var. prescottii, commonly known as the Prescott melon, is a rare and ancient variety of melon native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, round fruit with a thick, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a herbaceous vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, arid climates. It is closely related to other melon species but is distinguished by its unique morphology and genetic makeup.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists for its potential in breeding programs and conservation efforts.
