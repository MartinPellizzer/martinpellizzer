Calamintha suaveolens var. acinoides

Calamintha suaveolens var. acinoides, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves with a mint-like scent, and its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
