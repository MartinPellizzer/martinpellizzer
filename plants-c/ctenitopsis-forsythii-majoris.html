Ctenitopsis forsythii-majoris

Ctenitopsis forsythii-majoris, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
