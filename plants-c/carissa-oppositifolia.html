Carissa oppositifolia

Carissa oppositifolia, commonly known as the Natal plum or sweet evergreen, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Carissa oppositifolia is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
