Cissampelos ebracteata

Cissampelos ebracteata, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruits are elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its unique climbing habit and medicinal properties.
