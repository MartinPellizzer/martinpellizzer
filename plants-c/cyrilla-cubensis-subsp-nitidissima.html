Cyrilla cubensis subsp. nitidissima

Cyrilla cubensis subsp.

nitidissima, commonly known as the Cuban cyrilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy in appearance.

The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
