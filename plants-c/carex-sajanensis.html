Carex sajanensis

Carex sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sajanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
