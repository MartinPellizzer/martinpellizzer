Cyathea recommutata

Cyathea recommutata, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the structural support of the plant.

Cyathea recommutata plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests, providing habitat and shelter for various species.
