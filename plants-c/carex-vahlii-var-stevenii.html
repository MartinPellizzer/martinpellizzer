Carex vahlii var. stevenii

Carex vahlii var. stevenii, commonly known as Steven's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-parted flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex vahlii var. stevenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
