Cerasus × syodoi

Cerasus × syodoi, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the fruit, when present, is a small, sweet cherry.

Cerasus × syodoi is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
