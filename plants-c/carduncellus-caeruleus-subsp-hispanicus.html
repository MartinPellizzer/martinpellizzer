Carduncellus caeruleus subsp. hispanicus

Carduncellus caeruleus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

It is often found in scrublands and along coastal areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
