Capparis foetida

Capparis foetida, commonly known as the stinking cucumber or sour cabbage, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pungent, often foul-smelling flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when ripe, containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable in its flowers and fruits.
