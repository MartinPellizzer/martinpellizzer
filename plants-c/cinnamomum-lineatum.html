Cinnamomum lineatum

Cinnamomum lineatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
