Chrysophyllum bumelioides

Chrysophyllum bumelioides, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or wild cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
