Campanula sibthorpiana var. filicaulis

Campanula sibthorpiana var. filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.

The variety filicaulis is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Campanula sibthorpiana.
