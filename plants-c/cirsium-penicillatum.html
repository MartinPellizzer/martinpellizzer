Cirsium penicillatum

Cirsium penicillatum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cirsium penicillatum is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
