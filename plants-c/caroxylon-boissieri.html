Caroxylon boissieri

Caroxylon boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's carob tree, is a species of leguminous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of elongated pods.

Caroxylon boissieri is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
