Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. asperulum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. asperulum, commonly known as the roughleaf white marguerite, is a cultivar of the common marguerite plant.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The leaves are notably rough to the touch, giving the plant its specific epithet "asperulum.". It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in garden borders and rock gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
