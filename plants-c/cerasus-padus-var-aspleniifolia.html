Cerasus padus var. aspleniifolia

Cerasus padus var. aspleniifolia, commonly known as the aspleniifoliate cherry, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small, sweet, edible drupe that ripens in summer.
