Calamintha officinalis subsp. glandulosa

Calamintha officinalis subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as glandular calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong mint-like fragrance.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
