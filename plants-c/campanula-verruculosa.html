Campanula verruculosa

Campanula verruculosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula verruculosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
