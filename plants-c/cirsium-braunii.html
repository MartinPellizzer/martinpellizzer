Cirsium braunii

Cirsium braunii, commonly known as Braun's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its stems are often covered in stiff hairs, providing additional protection against herbivores.

Cirsium braunii is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
