Caulophyllum thalictroides subsp. robustum

Caulophyllum thalictroides subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust bishop's cap, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow.

The plant features a short, thick rhizome and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
