Cistus monspeliensis subsp. ledon

Cistus monspeliensis subsp.

ledon, commonly known as the Ledon rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native environment.
