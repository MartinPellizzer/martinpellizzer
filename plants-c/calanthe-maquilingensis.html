Calanthe maquilingensis

Calanthe maquilingensis, commonly known as the Maquiling orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and striking floral display. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
