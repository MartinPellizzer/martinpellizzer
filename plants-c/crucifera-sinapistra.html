Crucifera sinapistra

Crucifera sinapistra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions across Europe and Asia. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees.

It is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in agricultural systems as a cover crop.
