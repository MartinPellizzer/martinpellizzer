Crepis laevigata

Crepis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth hoary cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, often hairy, and have a distinctive lobed or divided appearance.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
