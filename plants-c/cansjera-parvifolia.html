Cansjera parvifolia

Cansjera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved candelabra, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
