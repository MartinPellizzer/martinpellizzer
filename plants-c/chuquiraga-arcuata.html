Chuquiraga arcuata

Chuquiraga arcuata, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers are often surrounded by velvety, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Chuquiraga arcuata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Andean medicine.
