Clematis ochotensis var. ternata

Clematis ochotensis var. ternata, commonly known as the Far Eastern clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often hairy beneath.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
