Carex montana var. procera

Carex montana var. procera, commonly known as tall mountain sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces spiky, cylindrical inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and stream banks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a bluish-green color.

Carex montana var. procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
