Carex zeyheri

Carex zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
