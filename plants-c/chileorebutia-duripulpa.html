Chileorebutia duripulpa

Chileorebutia duripulpa, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with a dense covering of woolly, white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Chileorebutia duripulpa is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
