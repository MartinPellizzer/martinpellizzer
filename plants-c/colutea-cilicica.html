Colutea cilicica

Colutea cilicica, commonly known as the Armenian colutea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Colutea cilicica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
