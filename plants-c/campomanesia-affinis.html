Campomanesia affinis

Campomanesia affinis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that develop into edible, bright red berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

Its ornamental value and nutritional benefits make it both a popular garden plant and a valuable fruit crop.
