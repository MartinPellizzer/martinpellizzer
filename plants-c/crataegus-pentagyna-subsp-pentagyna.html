Crataegus pentagyna subsp. pentagyna

Crataegus pentagyna subsp.

pentagyna, commonly known as the five-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically five-lobed, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
