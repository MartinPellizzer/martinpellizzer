Chascolytrum bulbosum

Chascolytrum bulbosum, commonly known as the mountain onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Andes.

It features a thick, fleshy bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in high-altitude, arid environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand frost, making it a resilient species in mountainous regions.

Its bulbs are sometimes used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
