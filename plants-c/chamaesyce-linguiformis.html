Chamaesyce linguiformis

Chamaesyce linguiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and succulent-like leaves.
