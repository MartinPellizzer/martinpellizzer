Chlorophytum rhizopendulum

Chlorophytum rhizopendulum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or white stripe along the midrib. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

The plant is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
