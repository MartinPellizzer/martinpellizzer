Canna buekii

Canna buekii, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, tropical-looking flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Cannaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms, which can persist from summer through early autumn.

Canna buekii is also valued for its resilience and adaptability to various growing conditions.
