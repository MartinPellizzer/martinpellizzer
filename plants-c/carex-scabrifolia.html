Carex scabrifolia

Carex scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous greenish flowers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex scabrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
