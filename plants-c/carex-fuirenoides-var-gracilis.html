Carex fuirenoides var. gracilis

Carex fuirenoides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
