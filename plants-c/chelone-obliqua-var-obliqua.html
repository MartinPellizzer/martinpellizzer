Chelone obliqua var. obliqua

Chelone obliqua var. obliqua, commonly known as the zigzagged bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive zigzagging stem, which gives it its common name, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
