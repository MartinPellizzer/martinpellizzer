Chionodoxa × allenii

Chionodoxa × allenii, commonly known as the Persian glory flower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of cup-shaped, nodding flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Chionodoxa × allenii is valued for its early bloom and delicate appearance, adding a touch of elegance to spring gardens.
