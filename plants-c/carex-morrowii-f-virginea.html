Carex morrowii f. virginea

Carex morrowii f. virginea, commonly known as the Morrow's sedge, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

Its foliage remains green throughout the growing season, providing visual interest in shaded or semi-shaded garden settings.
