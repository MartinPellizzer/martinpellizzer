Croton petraeus

Croton petraeus, commonly known as the rock croton, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Croton petraeus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
