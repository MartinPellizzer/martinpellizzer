Commiphora bruceae

Commiphora bruceae, commonly known as Bruce's myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown of dark green leaves. It produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and incense purposes.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
