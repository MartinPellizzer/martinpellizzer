Centaurea eliasii

Centaurea eliasii, commonly known as Elias's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has spiny leaves that help deter herbivores. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea eliasii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
