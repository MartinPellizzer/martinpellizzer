Ceropegia pygmaea

Ceropegia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia pygmaea is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
