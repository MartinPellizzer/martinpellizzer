Cestrum corymbosum var. parvifolium

Cestrum corymbosum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and nocturnal blooming habit.
