Chrysoma parishii

Chrysoma parishii, commonly known as the Cape goldflax, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to the Western Cape region. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Chrysoma parishii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
