Conyza laevigata

Conyza laevigata, commonly known as whiteleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features a branching stem covered with fine, white hairs, and its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly lobed margin. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and open fields, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza laevigata is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
