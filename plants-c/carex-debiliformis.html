Carex debiliformis

Carex debiliformis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex debiliformis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
