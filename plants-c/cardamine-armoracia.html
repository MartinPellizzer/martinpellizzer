Cardamine armoracia

Cardamine armoracia, commonly known as broad-leaved watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other cruciferous plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.
