Calea brachiata

Calea brachiata, commonly known as the "sensitive plant of the night," is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open at night. The plant has velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment to flourish.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
