Carex melanostachya

Carex melanostachya, commonly known as black-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green with a bluish tinge. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex melanostachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
