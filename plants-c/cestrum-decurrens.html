Cestrum decurrens

Cestrum decurrens, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum decurrens thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant night-time fragrance.
