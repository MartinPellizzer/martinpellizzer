Canthium macrocalyx

Canthium macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited spurge, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its fruits are fleshy, three-lobed capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Canthium macrocalyx is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadside areas and forest edges in tropical regions.
