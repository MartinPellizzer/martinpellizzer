Chamaecrista lineata var. pinoi

Chamaecrista lineata var. pinoi, commonly known as the Pino's burr legume, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant color to its native habitat.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental features.
