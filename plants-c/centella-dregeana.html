Centella dregeana

Centella dregeana, commonly known as the African pennywort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny white flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Centella dregeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
