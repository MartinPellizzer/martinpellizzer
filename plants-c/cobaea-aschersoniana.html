Cobaea aschersoniana

Cobaea aschersoniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or cup-of-sun, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it can grow up to several feet tall.

Its unique appearance and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
