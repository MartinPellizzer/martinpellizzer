Cymbalaria pluttula

Cymbalaria pluttula, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial species that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, prostrate or ascending stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pinkish, and arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Cymbalaria pluttula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
