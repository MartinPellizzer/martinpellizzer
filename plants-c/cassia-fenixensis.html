Cassia fenixensis

Cassia fenixensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Cassia fenixensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
