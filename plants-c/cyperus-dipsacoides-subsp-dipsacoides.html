Cyperus dipsacoides subsp. dipsacoides

Cyperus dipsacoides subsp.

dipsacoides, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, dry fruits.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
