Cerastium caespitosum f. serpentini

Cerastium caespitosum f. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's hair cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodland or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
