Cordia suaveolens

Cordia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a fragrant scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
