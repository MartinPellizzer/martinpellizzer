Calamagrostis varia subsp. corsica

Calamagrostis varia subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to golden flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique beauty and adaptability, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens and naturalized areas.
