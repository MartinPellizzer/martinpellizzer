Cyclotrichium origanifolium

Cyclotrichium origanifolium, commonly known as the lemon yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat, circular head, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has aromatic, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that resemble those of oregano, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cyclotrichium origanifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
