Centaurea atriplicifolia

Centaurea atriplicifolia, commonly known as the leafy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Centaurea atriplicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
