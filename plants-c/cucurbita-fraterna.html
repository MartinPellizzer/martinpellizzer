Cucurbita fraterna

Cucurbita fraterna, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers and has a climbing habit, often requiring support structures for growth. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it is used in various traditional cuisines across Central America.
