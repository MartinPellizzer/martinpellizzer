Capsella bursa-pastoris mediterranea

Capsella bursa-pastoris mediterranea, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white pubescence. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.
