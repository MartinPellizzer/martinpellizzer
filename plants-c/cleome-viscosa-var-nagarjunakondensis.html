Cleome viscosa var. nagarjunakondensis

Cleome viscosa var. nagarjunakondensis, commonly known as the NagarjunaKonda cleome, is a variant of the common cleome species native to specific regions of India.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are borne in terminal clusters.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
