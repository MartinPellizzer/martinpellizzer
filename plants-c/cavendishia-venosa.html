Cavendishia venosa

Cavendishia venosa, commonly known as the Veinscale Cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its unique, spiny stems and striking, colorful flowers. The plant features a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers, which can be red, yellow, or orange, bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Cavendishia venosa is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
