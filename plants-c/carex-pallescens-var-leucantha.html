Carex pallescens var. leucantha

Carex pallescens var. leucantha, commonly known as white-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green to white in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
