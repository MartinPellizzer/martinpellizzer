Cissus scarlatina

Cissus scarlatina, commonly known as the red-leafed devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, reddish leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by heart-shaped, red berries. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus scarlatina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
