Capsicum annuum var. chinense

Capsicum annuum var. chinense, commonly known as the chili pepper or bird's eye chili, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, fiery fruits that range in color from green to red, yellow, or orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by the characteristic pepper fruits.

This variety is prized for its high capsaicin content, which gives it its spicy flavor and medicinal properties.
