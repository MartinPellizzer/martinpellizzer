Citrullus aquosus

Citrullus aquosus, commonly known as watermelon, is a vine species belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, lobed leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces yellowish male flowers and female flowers that develop into the familiar red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is highly hydrated, containing a high percentage of water and is rich in nutrients such as vitamin C and lycopene.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for warm climates and full sun exposure.
