Campylospermum descoingsii

Campylospermum descoingsii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate drought make it a resilient species in harsh environments.
