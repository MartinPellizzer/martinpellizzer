Chloris subaequigluma

Chloris subaequigluma, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, finger-like arrangements, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Chloris subaequigluma is often found in grasslands and open habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
