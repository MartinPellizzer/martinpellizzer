Convolvulus owariensis

Convolvulus owariensis, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in certain regions.
