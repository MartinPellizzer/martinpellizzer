Croton lacteus

Croton lacteus, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that display a range of hues including green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
