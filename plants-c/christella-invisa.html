Christella invisa

Christella invisa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
