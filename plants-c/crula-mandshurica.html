Crula mandshurica

Crula mandshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer.

Crula mandshurica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
