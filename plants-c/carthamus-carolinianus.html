Carthamus carolinianus

Carthamus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina poppy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, branched stems and feathery leaves. Its flowers consist of five petals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.
