Carduus crispus var. longifolius

Carduus crispus var. longifolius, commonly known as the curled thistle or long-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in water retention.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
