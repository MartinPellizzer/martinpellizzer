Crataegus crus-galli var. salicifolia

Crataegus crus-galli var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elongated and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It typically grows in moist, woodland areas and is often found in hedgerows or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
