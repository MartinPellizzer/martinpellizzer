Copiapoa hypogaea subsp. laui

Copiapoa hypogaea subsp.

laui, commonly known as the Laui cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that help it survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring. It has a thick, water-storing stem that allows it to endure long periods of drought.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
