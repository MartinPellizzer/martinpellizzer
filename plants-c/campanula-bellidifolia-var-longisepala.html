Campanula bellidifolia var. longisepala

Campanula bellidifolia var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique floral structure and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
