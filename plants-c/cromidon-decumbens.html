Cromidon decumbens

Cromidon decumbens, commonly known as the creeping cromidon, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, covered in small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or damp meadows.

Its botanical structure is adapted for survival in these specific habitats, with a dense root system that helps anchor it in loose soil.
