Crepidium epidendrum

Crepidium epidendrum, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with purple veins on pale pink or white petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Crepidium epidendrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
