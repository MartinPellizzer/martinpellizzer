Chloris foliosa

Chloris foliosa, commonly known as the featherleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chloris foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
