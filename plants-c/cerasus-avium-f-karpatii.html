Cerasus avium f. karpatii

Cerasus avium f. karpatii, commonly known as the Karpatian cherry, is a botanical variant of the wild cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features, which set it apart from the typical Cerasus avium. The plant exhibits specific morphological traits, such as unique leaf shapes and flower structures, that are of interest to botanists. It is native to certain regions of the Carpathian Mountains, where it thrives in specific ecological conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential horticultural applications.
