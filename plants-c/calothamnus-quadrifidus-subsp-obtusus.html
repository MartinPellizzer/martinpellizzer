Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp. obtusus

Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp.

obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

The subspecies obtusus is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other subspecies of Calothamnus quadrifidus.
