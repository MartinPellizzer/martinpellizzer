Crepidium productum

Crepidium productum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in loose, well-drained substrates.

Crepidium productum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
