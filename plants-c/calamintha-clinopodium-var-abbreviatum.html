Calamintha clinopodium var. abbreviatum

Calamintha clinopodium var. abbreviatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory ailments.
