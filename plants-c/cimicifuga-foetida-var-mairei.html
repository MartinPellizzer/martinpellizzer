Cimicifuga foetida var. mairei

Cimicifuga foetida var. mairei, commonly known as Chinese angelica or Maire's bugbane, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, compound leaves with a serrated margin and produces showy, white to pale pink, clustered flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its root system is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
