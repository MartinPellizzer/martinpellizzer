Calopsis membranacea

Calopsis membranacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for gardens.

Calopsis membranacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
