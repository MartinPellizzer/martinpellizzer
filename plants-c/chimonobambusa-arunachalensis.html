Chimonobambusa arunachalensis

Chimonobambusa arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal bamboo, is a species of grass-like flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in moist, mountainous regions. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
