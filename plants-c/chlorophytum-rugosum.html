Chlorophytum rugosum

Chlorophytum rugosum, commonly known as the tiger tooth plant, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features linear, grass-like leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant.
