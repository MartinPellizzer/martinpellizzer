Croton acutus

Croton acutus, commonly known as the acute croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with serrated edges and vibrant, often colorful foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
