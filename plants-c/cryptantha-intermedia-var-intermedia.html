Cryptantha intermedia var. intermedia

Cryptantha intermedia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Sierra bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
