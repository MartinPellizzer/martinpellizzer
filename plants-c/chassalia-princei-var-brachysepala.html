Chassalia princei var. brachysepala

Chassalia princei var. brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled chassalia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent, short sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
