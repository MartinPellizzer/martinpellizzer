Carex buxbaumii var. macrostachya

Carex buxbaumii var. macrostachya, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its larger spikelets compared to other Carex species.
