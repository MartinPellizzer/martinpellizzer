Centaurea spicata

Centaurea spicata, commonly known as spiked centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, erect stems bear clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Centaurea spicata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
