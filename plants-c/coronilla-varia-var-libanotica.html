Coronilla varia var. libanotica

Coronilla varia var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese Coronilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
