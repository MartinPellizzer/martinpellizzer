Capsicum grandiflorum

Capsicum grandiflorum, commonly known as the sweet pepper or bell pepper, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant produces fruits that can range in color from green to red, orange, or yellow, depending on maturity. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
