Chenopodium ficifolium f. subcymosum

Chenopodium ficifolium f. subcymosum, commonly known as the subcymose goosefoot, is a variant of the species Chenopodium ficifolium.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters called cymes. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America.

Its botanical features make it notable for its adaptability and ecological significance in its native habitat.
