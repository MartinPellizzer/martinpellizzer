Cornus × horseyi

Cornus × horseyi, commonly known as the dwarf cornel.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, white to pink bracts that appear in spring. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Cornus × horseyi is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
