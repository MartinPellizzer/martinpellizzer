Cerasus duracina var. macrophylla

Cerasus duracina var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf cherry, is a botanical variant of the cherry species characterized by its notably large, ovate leaves.

This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. Its flowers are typically pink to white, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. The fruit, a small cherry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
