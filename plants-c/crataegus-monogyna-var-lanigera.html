Crataegus monogyna var. lanigera

Crataegus monogyna var. lanigera, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish down. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
