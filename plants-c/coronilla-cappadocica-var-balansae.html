Coronilla cappadocica var. balansae

Coronilla cappadocica var. balansae, commonly known as the Cappadocian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
