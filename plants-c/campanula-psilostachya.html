Campanula psilostachya

Campanula psilostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula psilostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
