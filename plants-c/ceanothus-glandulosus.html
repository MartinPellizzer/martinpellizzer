Ceanothus glandulosus

Ceanothus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage that secretes a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ceanothus glandulosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
