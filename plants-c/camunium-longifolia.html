Camunium longifolia

Camunium longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf camunium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Camunium longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
