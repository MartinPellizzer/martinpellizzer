Cirsium balcanicum

Cirsium balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with coarse teeth and a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium balcanicum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
