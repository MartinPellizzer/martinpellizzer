Carex humpatensis

Carex humpatensis, commonly known as the Humpatensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland ecosystems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it forms dense clumps with slender, grass-like leaves. Carex humpatensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
