Calophyllum acidus

Calophyllum acidus, commonly known as the sour tamarind tree, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
