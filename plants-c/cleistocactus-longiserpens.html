Cleistocactus longiserpens

Cleistocactus longiserpens, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to northern South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
