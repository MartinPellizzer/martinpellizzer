Cremanthodium palmatum subsp. rhodocephalum

Cremanthodium palmatum subsp.

rhodocephalum, commonly known as the red-headed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh, dry climates.
