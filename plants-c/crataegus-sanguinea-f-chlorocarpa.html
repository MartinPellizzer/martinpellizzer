Crataegus sanguinea f. chlorocarpa

Crataegus sanguinea f. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruited hawthorn, is a horticultural form of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow berries that remain on the plant well into winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is not typically eaten by humans but is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
