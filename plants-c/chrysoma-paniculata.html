Chrysoma paniculata

Chrysoma paniculata, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
