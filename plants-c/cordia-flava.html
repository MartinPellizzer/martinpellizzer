Cordia flava

Cordia flava, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Cordia flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
