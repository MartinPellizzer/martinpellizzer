Cinnamomum alternifolium

Cinnamomum alternifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its essential oils and timber.
