Carex semicrinita

Carex semicrinita, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex semicrinita is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
