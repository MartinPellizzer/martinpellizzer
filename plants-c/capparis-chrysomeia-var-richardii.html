Capparis chrysomeia var. richardii

Capparis chrysomeia var. richardii, commonly known as the richardii caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright yellow, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
