Cassinopsis tinifolia

Cassinopsis tinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf cassinopsis, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Cassinopsis tinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
