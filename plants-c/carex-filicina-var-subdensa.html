Carex filicina var. subdensa

Carex filicina var. subdensa, commonly known as the dense filicin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often densely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
