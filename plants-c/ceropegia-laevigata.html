Ceropegia laevigata

Ceropegia laevigata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring trailing stems and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to arid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Ceropegia laevigata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
