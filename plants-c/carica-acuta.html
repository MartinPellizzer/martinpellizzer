Carica acuta

Carica acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and fruit characteristics, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
