Clematis songorica var. boissieriana

Clematis songorica var. boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
