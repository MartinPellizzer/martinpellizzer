Cymbidium trifidum

Cymbidium trifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Cymbidium trifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both its beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
