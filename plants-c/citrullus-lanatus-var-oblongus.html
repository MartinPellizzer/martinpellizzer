Citrullus lanatus var. oblongus

Citrullus lanatus var. oblongus, commonly known as the oblong watermelon, is a variant of the watermelon species characterized by its elongated, oblong fruit shape.

This variety typically produces large, green rind with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a juicy, sweet red or pink flesh. The plant is a vigorous vine with trailing stems and palmate leaves, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is commonly cultivated for its high water content and mild, refreshing flavor.
