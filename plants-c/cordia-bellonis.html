Cordia bellonis

Cordia bellonis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Cordia bellonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
