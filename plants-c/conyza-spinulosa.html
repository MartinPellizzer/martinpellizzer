Conyza spinulosa

Conyza spinulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza spinulosa is known for its ability to thrive in dry, open habitats.
