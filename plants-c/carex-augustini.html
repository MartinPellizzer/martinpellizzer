Carex augustini

Carex augustini, commonly known as the Augustini sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex augustini plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
