Cyathea glabrescens

Cyathea glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to the plant's ornamental value. Cyathea glabrescens prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
