Cassia katangensis var. nuda

Cassia katangensis var. nuda, commonly known as the naked cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters and features compound leaves with 10-20 pairs of lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
