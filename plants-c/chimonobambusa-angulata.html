Chimonobambusa angulata

Chimonobambusa angulata, commonly known as the angular bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
