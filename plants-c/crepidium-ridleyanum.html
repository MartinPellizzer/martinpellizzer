Crepidium ridleyanum

Crepidium ridleyanum, commonly known as the Ridley's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a short rhizome system.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
