Cirrhaea silvana

Cirrhaea silvana, commonly known as the silverleaf cirrhaea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add both beauty and biodiversity to their landscapes.
