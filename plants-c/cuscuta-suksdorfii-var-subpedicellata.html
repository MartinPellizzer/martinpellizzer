Cuscuta suksdorfii var. subpedicellata

Cuscuta suksdorfii var. subpedicellata, commonly known as the dwarf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around host plants. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to crops and ornamental plants.
