Canna coccinea var. bicolor

Canna coccinea var. bicolor, commonly known as the red and yellow canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that range from deep red to bright yellow, often with a contrasting edge. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a broad, colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display in gardens and landscapes.
