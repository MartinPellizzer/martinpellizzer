Campanula lactiflora

Campanula lactiflora, commonly known as the milk bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula lactiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
