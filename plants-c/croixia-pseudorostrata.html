Croixia pseudorostrata

Croixia pseudorostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Croixia pseudorostrata is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
