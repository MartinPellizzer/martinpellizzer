Clusia garcibarrigae

Clusia garcibarrigae, commonly known as the golden glory or yellow glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a yellow or white underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Clusia garcibarrigae is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
