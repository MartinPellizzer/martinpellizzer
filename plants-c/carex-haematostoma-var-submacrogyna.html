Carex haematostoma var. submacrogyna

Carex haematostoma var. submacrogyna, commonly known as the blood-stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the stems, which gives it its common name.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers, typical of many sedges.
