Cissus teysmannii

Cissus teysmannii, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Cissus teysmannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
