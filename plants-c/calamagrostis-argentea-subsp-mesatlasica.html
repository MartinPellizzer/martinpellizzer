Calamagrostis argentea subsp. mesatlasica

Calamagrostis argentea subsp.

mesatlasica, commonly known as the silver reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often adorned with silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
