Cousinia xanthina

Cousinia xanthina, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. Cousinia xanthina thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environments.
