Carduus defloratus var. rhaeticus

Carduus defloratus var. rhaeticus, commonly known as the Rhaetic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of both yellow disk flowers and pale blue to white ray flowers.

It is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
