Cymbopogon excavatus

Cymbopogon excavatus, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps and long, narrow leaves that often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Cymbopogon excavatus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
