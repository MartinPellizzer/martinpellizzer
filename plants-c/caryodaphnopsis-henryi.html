Caryodaphnopsis henryi

Caryodaphnopsis henryi, commonly known as the Chinese stone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and scaly bark. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
