Carissa mitis

Carissa mitis, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow cactus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange or red berries. Its fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart, slightly bitter flavor.

Carissa mitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
