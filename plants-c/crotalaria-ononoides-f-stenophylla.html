Crotalaria ononoides f. stenophylla

Crotalaria ononoides f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false morning glory, is a variant of the Crotalaria genus characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants, and it produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent violet markings. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive floral display.
