Chlorophora tinctoria var. ovata

Chlorophora tinctoria var. ovata, commonly known as the orange sterculia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, orange-pink fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
