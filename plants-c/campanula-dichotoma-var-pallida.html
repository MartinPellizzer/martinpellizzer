Campanula dichotoma var. pallida

Campanula dichotoma var. pallida, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
