Centropogon curvatus var. pearcei

Centropogon curvatus var. pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's zigzag grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamp edges, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering structure consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
