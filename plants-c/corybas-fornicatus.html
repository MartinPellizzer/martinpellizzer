Corybas fornicatus

Corybas fornicatus, commonly known as the hooded lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, hooded flower spike. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive hood-shaped lip that is often brightly colored. It is native to New Zealand and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Corybas fornicatus is known for its unique morphology and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
