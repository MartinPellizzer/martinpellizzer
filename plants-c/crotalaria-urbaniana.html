Crotalaria urbaniana

Crotalaria urbaniana, commonly known as the urban candleplant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble candles, often with a contrasting dark center. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and urban environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria urbaniana is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.
