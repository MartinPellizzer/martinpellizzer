Cymbopogon quinhonensis

Cymbopogon quinhonensis, commonly known as the Quinhonensis citron-grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils, similar to those of lemongrass, have been studied for their potential aromatic and therapeutic properties.
