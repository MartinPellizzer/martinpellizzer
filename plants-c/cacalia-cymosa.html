Cacalia cymosa

Cacalia cymosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia cymosa thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
