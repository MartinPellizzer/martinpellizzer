Clematis sulcata

Clematis sulcata, commonly known as the wavyleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, wavy-edged leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis sulcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
