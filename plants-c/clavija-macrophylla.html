Clavija macrophylla

Clavija macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf clavija, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
