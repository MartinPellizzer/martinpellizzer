Cuphea goldmanii

Cuphea goldmanii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea goldmanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
