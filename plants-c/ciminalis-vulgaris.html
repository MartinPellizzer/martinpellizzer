Ciminalis vulgaris

Ciminalis vulgaris, commonly known as common thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties. Its leaves contain thymol, a compound known for its antimicrobial effects.

Ciminalis vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies.
