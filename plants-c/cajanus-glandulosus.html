Cajanus glandulosus

Cajanus glandulosus, commonly known as hairy groundnut, is an annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.

Cajanus glandulosus is also used for forage and as a source of oil and protein in some parts of the world.
