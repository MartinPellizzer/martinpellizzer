Cayratia geniculata

Cayratia geniculata, commonly known as the climbing beautyberry, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often considered unpalatable. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Cayratia geniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
