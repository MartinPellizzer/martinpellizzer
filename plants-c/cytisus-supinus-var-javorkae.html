Cytisus supinus var. javorkae

Cytisus supinus var. javorkae, commonly known as the dwarf yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to rocky landscapes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and compound leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in difficult soil conditions.
