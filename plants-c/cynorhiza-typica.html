Cynorhiza typica

Cynorhiza typica, commonly known as the typical root plant, is a species characterized by its unique root system.

It belongs to the family Cynorhizaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant is notable for its thick, fibrous roots that serve as a storage organ for nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Cynorhiza typica is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
