Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. pinifolius

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

pinifolius, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and needle-like leaves, which give it a resemblance to pine trees.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh, dry climates.
