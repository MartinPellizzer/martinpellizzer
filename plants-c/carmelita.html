Carmelita

Carmelita, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage that ranges from green to purple and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by orange or red tuberous roots. Carmelita thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
