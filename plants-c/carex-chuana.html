Carex chuana

Carex chuana, commonly known as Chuana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Carex chuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
