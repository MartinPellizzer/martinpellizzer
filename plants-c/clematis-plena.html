Clematis plena

Clematis plena, commonly known as the fully double clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its densely packed, double blooms that resemble peonies, with numerous petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens. Native to Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, and is prized for its showy, long-lasting flowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, ornamental blooms.
