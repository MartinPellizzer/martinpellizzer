Cassia fikifiki

Cassia fikifiki, commonly known as the Tahitian cassia, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia fikifiki is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular plant in horticulture.
