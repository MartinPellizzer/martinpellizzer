Chenopodium eremaeum

Chenopodium eremaeum, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or dry soils.

Chenopodium eremaeum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
