Cymbidium siamense

Cymbidium siamense, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, each featuring a three-lobed lip and a distinctive coloration pattern. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Cymbidium siamense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional floral arrangements.
