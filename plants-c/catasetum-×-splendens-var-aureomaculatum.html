Catasetum × splendens var. aureomaculatum

Catasetum × splendens var. aureomaculatum, commonly known as the golden-spotted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant yellow or orange coloration with distinctive golden spots. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers, often with a strong, sweet fragrance.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
