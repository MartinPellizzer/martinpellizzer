Cyperus erectus var. pleiocarpus

Cyperus erectus var. pleiocarpus, commonly known as the many-seeded flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

The variety pleiocarpus is distinguished by its numerous fruits, which contribute to its common name.
