Carex brunnescens subsp. brunnescens

Carex brunnescens subsp.

brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are usually a deep green to brownish color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.
