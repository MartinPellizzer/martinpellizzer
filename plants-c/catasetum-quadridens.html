Catasetum quadridens

Catasetum quadridens, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column that aids in pollination by specific wasp species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the bark of trees in its natural habitat.

Catasetum quadridens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in pollinator interactions.
