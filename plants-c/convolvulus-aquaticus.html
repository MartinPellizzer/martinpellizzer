Convolvulus aquaticus

Convolvulus aquaticus, commonly known as water bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist soil and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its vines can spread aggressively, forming dense mats that help prevent erosion.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to climb using its tendrils.
