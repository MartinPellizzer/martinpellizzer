Calamagrostis montana var. balcanica

Calamagrostis montana var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often densely arranged. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
