Coronilla glauca var. pentaphylloides

Coronilla glauca var. pentaphylloides, commonly known as the blue crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth habit.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
