Coryphantha missouriensis var. caespitosa

Coryphantha missouriensis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the Missouri prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, globular stems and produces yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are often followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals.

This variety is typically found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed soils.
