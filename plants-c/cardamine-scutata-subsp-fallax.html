Cardamine scutata subsp. fallax

Cardamine scutata subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax watercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in salads or as a garnish.
