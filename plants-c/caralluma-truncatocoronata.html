Caralluma truncatocoronata

Caralluma truncatocoronata, commonly known as the Hooded Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
