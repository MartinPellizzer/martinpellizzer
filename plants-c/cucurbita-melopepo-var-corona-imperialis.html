Cucurbita melopepo var. corona-imperialis

Cucurbita melopepo var. corona-imperialis, commonly known as the Mexican scalloped gourd, is a cultivar of the genus Cucurbita native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and scalloped-shaped fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is a herbaceous vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Mexican cuisine.
