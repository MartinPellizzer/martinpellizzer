Carex oederi subsp. subglobosa

Carex oederi subsp.

subglobosa, commonly known as the globular sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
