Corycium crispum

Corycium crispum, commonly known as the curly corycium, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, wavy-edged leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for cloud forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, Corycium crispum is considered endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
