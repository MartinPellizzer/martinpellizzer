Calamus cambojensis

Calamus cambojensis, commonly known as the golden cane reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender, and often yellowish-green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus cambojensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland environments.
