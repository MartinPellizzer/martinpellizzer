Cistrum napifolium

Cistrum napifolium, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cistrum napifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and botanical collections.
