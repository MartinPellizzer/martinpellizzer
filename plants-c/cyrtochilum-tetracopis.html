Cyrtochilum tetracopis

Cyrtochilum tetracopis, commonly known as the four-winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense spike. Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption in its rocky, high-altitude habitat.

Cyrtochilum tetracopis is known for its unique morphology and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
