Chisocheton formicarum

Chisocheton formicarum, commonly known as the ant tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
