Centaurea × vulpina

Centaurea × vulpina, commonly known as the foxglove centaury, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and conical flower heads that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Centaurea × vulpina is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas where it thrives in full sun.
