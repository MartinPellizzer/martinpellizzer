Cerinthe

Cerinthe, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, with two lips, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Cerinthe is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
