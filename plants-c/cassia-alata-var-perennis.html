Cassia alata var. perennis

Cassia alata var. perennis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are often hairy. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
