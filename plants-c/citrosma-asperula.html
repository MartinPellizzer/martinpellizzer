Citrosma asperula

Citrosma asperula, commonly known as the rough citrosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and pharmacological research.
