Clematis antunesii

Clematis antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes' Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the Canary Islands.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple, with five petals and a distinctive, showy structure. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
