Cestrum contrerasianum

Cestrum contrerasianum, commonly known as the night-blooming coffee plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its nocturnal blooming habit and pleasant fragrance.
