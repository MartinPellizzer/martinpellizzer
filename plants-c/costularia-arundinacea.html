Costularia arundinacea

Costularia arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo grass or reed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense colonies. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Costularia arundinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
