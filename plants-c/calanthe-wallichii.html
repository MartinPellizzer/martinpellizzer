Calanthe wallichii

Calanthe wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
