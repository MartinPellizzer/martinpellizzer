Croton cladotrichus

Croton cladotrichus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
