Capsicum annuum var. oblongoconicum

Capsicum annuum var. oblongoconicum, commonly known as the long pepper or bird pepper, is a variant of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its elongated, conical fruit shape and typically has a mild to medium heat level. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves, thriving in warm climates. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit develops from the ovary of the flower and is used both as a spice and in culinary applications.
