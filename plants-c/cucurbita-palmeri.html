Cucurbita palmeri

Cucurbita palmeri, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and melons. The plant is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny pads, which help it retain water in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible fruits known as tunas.

The plant has both ornamental and medicinal uses, and it plays a role in traditional Native American cultures.
