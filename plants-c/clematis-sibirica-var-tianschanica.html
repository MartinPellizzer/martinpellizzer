Clematis sibirica var. tianschanica

Clematis sibirica var. tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Central Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, palmately compound leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
