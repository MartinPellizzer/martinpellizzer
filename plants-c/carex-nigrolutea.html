Carex nigrolutea

Carex nigrolutea, commonly known as the yellowish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a yellowish tinge on the underside. Its spike-like inflorescence consists of small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

Carex nigrolutea plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
