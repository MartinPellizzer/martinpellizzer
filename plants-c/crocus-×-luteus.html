Crocus × luteus

Crocus × luteus, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant flower with six petals and three sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically appearing after the flowers.

This species is native to the Mediterranean region and is cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in saffron production.
