Calamus timorensis

Calamus timorensis, commonly known as Timor calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the island of Timor in Indonesia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers. Calamus timorensis is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
