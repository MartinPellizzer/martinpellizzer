Cnicus reglensis

Cnicus reglensis, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant with hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a spherical, hairy structure that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cnicus reglensis is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sandy environments.
