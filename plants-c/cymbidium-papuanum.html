Cymbidium papuanum

Cymbidium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred lip. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Cymbidium papuanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
