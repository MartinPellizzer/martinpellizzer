Coleonema album var. gracile

Coleonema album var. gracile, commonly known as silverleaf woolly bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or pale red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
