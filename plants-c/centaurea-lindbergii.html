Centaurea lindbergii

Centaurea lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Centaurea lindbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
