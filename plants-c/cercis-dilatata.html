Cercis dilatata

Cercis dilatata, commonly known as the Chinese redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
