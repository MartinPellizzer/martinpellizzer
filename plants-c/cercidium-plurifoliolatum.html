Cercidium plurifoliolatum

Cercidium plurifoliolatum, commonly known as the desert shrub or creosote bush, is a drought-tolerant plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are typically small and often have multiple leaflets, which help reduce water loss through transpiration.

Cercidium plurifoliolatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
