Cassia sophera var. ligustrinoides

Cassia sophera var. ligustrinoides, commonly known as the Chinese cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
