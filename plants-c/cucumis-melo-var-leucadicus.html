Cucumis melo var. leucadicus

Cucumis melo var. leucadicus, commonly known as the white muskmelon, is a variant of the muskmelon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine with lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit develops from a single pistillate flower and is typically harvested when fully mature.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated in warm climates for its culinary and medicinal uses.
