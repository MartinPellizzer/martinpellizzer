Conyza stipulata

Conyza stipulata, commonly known as the slenderleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Conyza stipulata is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
