Calamus javensis var. intermedius

Calamus javensis var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
