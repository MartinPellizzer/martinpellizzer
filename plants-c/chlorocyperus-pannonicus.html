Chlorocyperus pannonicus

Chlorocyperus pannonicus, commonly known as the Panonic flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to yellowish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chlorocyperus pannonicus plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
