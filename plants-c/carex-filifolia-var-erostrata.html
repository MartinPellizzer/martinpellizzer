Carex filifolia var. erostrata

Carex filifolia var. erostrata, commonly known as the eroded thread sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish flowers in spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
