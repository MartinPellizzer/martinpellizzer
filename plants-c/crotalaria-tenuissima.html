Crotalaria tenuissima

Crotalaria tenuissima, commonly known as narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant produces seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Crotalaria tenuissima is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
