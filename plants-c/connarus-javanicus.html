Connarus javanicus

Connarus javanicus, commonly known as the Java connarus, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Connarus javanicus is typically found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
