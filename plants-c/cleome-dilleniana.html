Cleome dilleniana

Cleome dilleniana, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, fern-like leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall, with branching stems that often have a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome dilleniana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
