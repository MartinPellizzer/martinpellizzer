Carex lanuginosa var. oriens

Carex lanuginosa var. oriens, commonly known as the oriental cotton grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive, cotton-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and forest floors. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique visual appeal.
