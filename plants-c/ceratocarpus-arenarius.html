Ceratocarpus arenarius

Ceratocarpus arenarius, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
