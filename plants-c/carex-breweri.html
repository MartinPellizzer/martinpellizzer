Carex breweri

Carex breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is native to North America, particularly in the western United States and parts of Canada.

Carex breweri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
