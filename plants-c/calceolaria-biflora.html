Calceolaria biflora

Calceolaria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered slipper orchid, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique bilaterally symmetrical flowers, which resemble tiny slippers, and its vibrant colors, often ranging from purple to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, thriving in rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Calceolaria biflora is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
