Cerastium lapponicum

Cerastium lapponicum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Cerastium lapponicum is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across the northern hemisphere.
