Cyperus bulbocaulis var. atrosanguineus

Cyperus bulbocaulis var. atrosanguineus, commonly known as black blood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, often with a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the midribs.

It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.
