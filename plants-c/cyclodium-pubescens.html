Cyclodium pubescens

Cyclodium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cyclodium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. Cyclodium pubescens is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
