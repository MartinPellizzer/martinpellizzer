Clematis orientalis var. vulgaris

Clematis orientalis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful floral displays.
