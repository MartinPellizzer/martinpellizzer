Cumbia niedenzuana

Cumbia niedenzuana, commonly known as the Mexican cumbia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
