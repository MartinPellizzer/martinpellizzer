Crepis ugandensis

Crepis ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda thistle, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
