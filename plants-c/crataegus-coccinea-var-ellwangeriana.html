Crataegus coccinea var. ellwangeriana

Crataegus coccinea var. ellwangeriana, commonly known as the Ellwangeriana Hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value, particularly during the autumn when its foliage turns a striking red or orange.
