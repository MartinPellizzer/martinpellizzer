Clinopodium filiforme

Clinopodium filiforme, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance.

Clinopodium filiforme is typically found in woodland areas and moist meadows, where it thrives in partial shade.
