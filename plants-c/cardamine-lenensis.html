Cardamine lenensis

Cardamine lenensis, commonly known as the Siberian watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme.

Cardamine lenensis is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
