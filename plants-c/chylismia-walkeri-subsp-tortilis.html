Chylismia walkeri subsp. tortilis

Chylismia walkeri subsp.

tortilis, commonly known as the twisted chylismia, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its compact, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
