Clusia cambessedesii

Clusia cambessedesii, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Clusiaceae family, also known as the guttiferae family, and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Clusia cambessedesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral displays.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
