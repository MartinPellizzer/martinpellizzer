Carex oxyandra

Carex oxyandra, commonly known as the sharp-angled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex oxyandra is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
