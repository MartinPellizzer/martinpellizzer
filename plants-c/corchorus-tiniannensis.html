Corchorus tiniannensis

Corchorus tiniannensis, commonly known as the Sudanese dock, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive leaves with a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisines.
