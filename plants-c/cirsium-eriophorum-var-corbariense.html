Cirsium eriophorum var. corbariense

Cirsium eriophorum var. corbariense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its rough texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
