Catasetum × freitasii

Catasetum × freitasii, commonly known as the Freitas's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insect pollinators.

Catasetum × freitasii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
