Cheirostylis parvifolia

Cheirostylis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cheirostylis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a slight fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
