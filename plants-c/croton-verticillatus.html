Croton verticillatus

Croton verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique growth habit.

Croton verticillatus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
