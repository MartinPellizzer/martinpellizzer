Calosmon acstivale

Calosmon acstivale, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are erect and branching. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Calosmon acstivale is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
