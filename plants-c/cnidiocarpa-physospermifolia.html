Cnidiocarpa physospermifolia

Cnidiocarpa physospermifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its extensive root system. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
