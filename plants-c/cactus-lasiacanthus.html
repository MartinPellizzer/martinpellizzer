Cactus lasiacanthus

Cactus lasiacanthus, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It features spiny stems and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers bloom during the day, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
