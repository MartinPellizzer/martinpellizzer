Carum appuanum subsp. appuanum

Carum appuanum subsp.

appuanum, commonly known as the Appuan caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, linear leaves and small white or yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits called mericarps, which contain the essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
