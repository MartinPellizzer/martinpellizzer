Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. erecta

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. erecta, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress, characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit.

It is a coniferous evergreen with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its elegant form and hardy nature.
