Croton maieuticus

Croton maieuticus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a distinctive yellow or red underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
