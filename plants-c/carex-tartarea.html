Carex tartarea

Carex tartarea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, which are often followed by dark, spherical berries.

Carex tartarea is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
