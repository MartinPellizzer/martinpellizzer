Carex atherodes var. glabra

Carex atherodes var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth soft sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and smooth, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
