Chimonocalamus baviensis

Chimonocalamus baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, where it thrives in moist, tropical environments. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
