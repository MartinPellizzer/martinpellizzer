Carduus defloratus var. carlinoides

Carduus defloratus var. carlinoides, commonly known as the Carlin thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a solitary, purple-blue flower head that blooms in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowering period makes it a notable species for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
