Cyperus hylaeus

Cyperus hylaeus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its slender, grass-like stems are often found in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Cyperus hylaeus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
