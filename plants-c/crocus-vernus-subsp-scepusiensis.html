Crocus vernus subsp. scepusiensis

Crocus vernus subsp.

scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian crocus, is a botanical variant of the common crocus. It is native to parts of southern Europe, particularly in regions such as Italy and the Balkans.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a white center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and prefers partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early flowering habit.
