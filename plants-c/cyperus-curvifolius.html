Cyperus curvifolius

Cyperus curvifolius, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers.

Cyperus curvifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in aquatic landscaping.
