Cassinia tegulata

Cassinia tegulata, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits called cypselae.

Cassinia tegulata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
