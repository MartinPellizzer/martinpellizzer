Celtis pumila

Celtis pumila, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. The bark is thick, scaly, and dark gray, providing a rugged appearance. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Celtis pumila plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and small mammals.
