Cinnamomum elongatum

Cinnamomum elongatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped, dark purple fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a pleasant, spicy aroma.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its valuable essential oils and bark.
