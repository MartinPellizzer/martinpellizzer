Canarium decumanum

Canarium decumanum, commonly known as the Indian cheese tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits resembling small, round oranges.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
