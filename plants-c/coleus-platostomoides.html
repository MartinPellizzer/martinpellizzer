Coleus platostomoides

Coleus platostomoides, commonly known as the broadleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors, often mistaken for flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
