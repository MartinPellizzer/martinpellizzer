Crassula swaziensis f. guruensis

Crassula swaziensis f. guruensis, commonly known as the Guru's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky outcrops and well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying a reddish tinge under direct sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
