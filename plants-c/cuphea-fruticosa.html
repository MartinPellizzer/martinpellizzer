Cuphea fruticosa

Cuphea fruticosa, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has upright, woody stems and small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea fruticosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
