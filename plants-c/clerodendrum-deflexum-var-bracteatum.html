Clerodendrum deflexum var. bracteatum

Clerodendrum deflexum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate cleerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. These flowers are followed by distinctive bracteate fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal properties in traditional practices.
