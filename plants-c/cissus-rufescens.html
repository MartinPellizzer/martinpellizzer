Cissus rufescens

Cissus rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often red-colored stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.
