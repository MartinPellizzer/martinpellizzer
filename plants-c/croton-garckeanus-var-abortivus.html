Croton garckeanus var. abortivus

Croton garckeanus var. abortivus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique leaf structure.
