Cydonia japonica var. wilsonii

Cydonia japonica var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Japanese quince.

It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces yellow or orange berries that ripen in late summer, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
