Campanula eriantha

Campanula eriantha, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula eriantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
