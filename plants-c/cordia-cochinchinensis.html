Cordia cochinchinensis

Cordia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese red cedar, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and woodworking.
