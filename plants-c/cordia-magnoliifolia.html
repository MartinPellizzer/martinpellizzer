Cordia magnoliifolia

Cordia magnoliifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered cordia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, smooth seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
