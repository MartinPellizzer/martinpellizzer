Cordia oaxacana

Cordia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Cordia oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
