Camellia luteonerva

Camellia luteonerva, commonly known as the yellow-veined camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent yellow veins that stand out against the dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle yellow hue. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Camellia luteonerva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
