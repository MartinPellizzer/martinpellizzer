Ciminalis pseudoaquatica

Ciminalis pseudoaquatica, commonly known as the ghost water plant, is a unique aquatic species characterized by its translucent, leaf-like structures that resemble delicate, floating ribbons.

It thrives in shallow, still waters with low light conditions, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. The plant's foliage is typically pale green to white, allowing it to blend seamlessly with its surroundings, providing camouflage from herbivores. It reproduces through vegetative propagation, with new shoots developing from the base of existing plants.

Despite its delicate appearance, it plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and stabilizing sediment.
