Calliandra trinervia var. trinervia

Calliandra trinervia var. trinervia, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to red bracteate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters, with a branching structure that supports its ornamental display. Its flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
