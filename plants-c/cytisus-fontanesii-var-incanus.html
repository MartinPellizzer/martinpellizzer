Cytisus fontanesii var. incanus

Cytisus fontanesii var. incanus, commonly known as the silver buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
