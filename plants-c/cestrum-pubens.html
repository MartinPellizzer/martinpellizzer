Cestrum pubens

Cestrum pubens, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular white flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants.

Cestrum pubens is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and pleasant scent.
