Carum brachycarpum

Carum brachycarpum, commonly known as dwarf caraway, is a small flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are finely divided and aromatic. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its compact size and fragrant foliage.

Carum brachycarpum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
