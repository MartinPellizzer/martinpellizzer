Cardiospermum corindum f. elongatum

Cardiospermum corindum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale pink to white flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces showy, five-petaled blooms. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
