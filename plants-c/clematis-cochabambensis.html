Clematis cochabambensis

Clematis cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba Clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and can reach heights of several meters.

Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
