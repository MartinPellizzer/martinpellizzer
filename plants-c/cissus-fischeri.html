Cissus fischeri

Cissus fischeri, commonly known as the fisher's grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in tiny, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or trained on trellises.

Cissus fischeri is valued for its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
