Clitoria mariana var. mariana

Clitoria mariana var. mariana, commonly known as the common butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as natural food coloring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
