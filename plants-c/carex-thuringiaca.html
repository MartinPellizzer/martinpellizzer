Carex thuringiaca

Carex thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes in the summer.

Carex thuringiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
