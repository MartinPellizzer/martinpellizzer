Carex mannii

Carex mannii, commonly known as the African sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Carex mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
