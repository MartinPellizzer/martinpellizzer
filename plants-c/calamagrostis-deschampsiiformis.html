Calamagrostis deschampsiiformis

Calamagrostis deschampsiiformis, commonly known as the silvergrass or Deschampsiiform silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. It produces tall, slender spikes of pale green to golden yellow flowers in late summer.

Calamagrostis deschampsiiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
