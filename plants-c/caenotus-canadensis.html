Caenotus canadensis

Caenotus canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Caenotus canadensis is often found in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.
