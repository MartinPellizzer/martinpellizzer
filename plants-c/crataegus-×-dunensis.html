Crataegus × dunensis

Crataegus × dunensis, commonly known as the Dunedin hawthorn, is a hybrid species native to New Zealand.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 2–6 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
