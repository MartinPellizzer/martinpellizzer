Charpentiera tomentosa

Charpentiera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly charpentiera, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying shades of white or pale pink.

Charpentiera tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
