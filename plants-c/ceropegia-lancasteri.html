Ceropegia lancasteri

Ceropegia lancasteri, commonly known as the laceleaf or white-flowered ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, waxy, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia lancasteri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
