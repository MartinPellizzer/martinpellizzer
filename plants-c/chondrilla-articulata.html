Chondrilla articulata

Chondrilla articulata, commonly known as the jointed burrweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some areas but also has potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
