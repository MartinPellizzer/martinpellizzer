Cyathea macgillivrayi

Cyathea macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in structure and appearance.

Cyathea macgillivrayi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
