Carex pendula f. pallida

Carex pendula f. pallida, commonly known as the pale form of hanging sedge.

This plant is a variant of Carex pendula, characterized by its pale, almost white inflorescence. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence hanging in a loose, drooping cluster.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
