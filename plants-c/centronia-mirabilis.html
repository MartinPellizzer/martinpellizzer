Centronia mirabilis

Centronia mirabilis, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, ovate leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
