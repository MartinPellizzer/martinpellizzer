Carex curvula var. tripartita

Carex curvula var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-part sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
