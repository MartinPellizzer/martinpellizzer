Celtis scabra

Celtis scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible, dark blue berries. Celtis scabra is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
