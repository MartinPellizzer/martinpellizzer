Cotyledon singularis

Cotyledon singularis, commonly known as the singular cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with striking patterns and colors. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. Its growth habit is typically compact, forming a rosette shape that helps protect the plant from harsh environmental conditions.

Cotyledon singularis is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
