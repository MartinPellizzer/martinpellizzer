Caryota furfuracea var. furcata

Caryota furfuracea var. furcata, commonly known as the split-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a short trunk and broad, fan-shaped leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
