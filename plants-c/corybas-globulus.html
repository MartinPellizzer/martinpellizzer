Corybas globulus

Corybas globulus, commonly known as the globular rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, globular-shaped flower spike, which is typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, fleshy flowers that are often clustered at the base of the spike. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.

Corybas globulus is a low-growing plant with a short, creeping stem and is adapted to survive in arid or semi-arid environments.
