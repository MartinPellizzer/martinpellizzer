Casearia bourdillonii

Casearia bourdillonii, commonly known as the golden casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Casearia bourdillonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
