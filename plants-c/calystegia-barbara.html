Calystegia barbara

Calystegia barbara, commonly known as the common blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.
