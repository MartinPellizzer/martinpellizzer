Chrysophyllum parvulum

Chrysophyllum parvulum, commonly known as the small-leaf chrysophyllum, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, citrus-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
