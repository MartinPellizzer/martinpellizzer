Commelina benghalensis

Commelina benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Commelina benghalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
