Crataegus vivida

Crataegus vivida, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, open woods or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
