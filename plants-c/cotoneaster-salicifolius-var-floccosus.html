Cotoneaster salicifolius var. floccosus

Cotoneaster salicifolius var. floccosus, commonly known as the silvery cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
