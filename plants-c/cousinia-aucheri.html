Cousinia aucheri

Cousinia aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Cousinia aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
