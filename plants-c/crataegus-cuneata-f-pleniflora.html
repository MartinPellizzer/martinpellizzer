Crataegus cuneata f. pleniflora

Crataegus cuneata f. pleniflora, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. Native to China, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.
