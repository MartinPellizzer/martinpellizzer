Carex papuana

Carex papuana, commonly known as the Papuan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikelets that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
