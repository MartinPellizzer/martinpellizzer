Croton kingianus

Croton kingianus, commonly known as the King's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with vibrant green veins and colorful, waxy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
