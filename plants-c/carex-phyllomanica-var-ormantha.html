Carex phyllomanica var. ormantha

Carex phyllomanica var. ormantha, commonly known as the Ormantha sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wetlands. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
