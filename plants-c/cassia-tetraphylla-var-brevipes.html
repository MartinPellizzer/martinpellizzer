Cassia tetraphylla var. brevipes

Cassia tetraphylla var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with lanceolate leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its short, stout stems and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.
