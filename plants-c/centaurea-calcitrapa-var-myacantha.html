Centaurea calcitrapa var. myacantha

Centaurea calcitrapa var. myacantha, commonly known as the rock centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces vibrant blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
