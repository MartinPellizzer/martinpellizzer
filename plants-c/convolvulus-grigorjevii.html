Convolvulus grigorjevii

Convolvulus grigorjevii, commonly known as the Armenian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or disturbed areas, and it is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in its native habitat.
