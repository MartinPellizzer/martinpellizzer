Cerastium lanigerum f. dollineri

Cerastium lanigerum f. dollineri, commonly known as the Dolliner's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the genus Cerastium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
