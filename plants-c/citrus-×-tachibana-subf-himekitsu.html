Citrus × tachibana subf. himekitsu

Citrus × tachibana subf.

himekitsu, commonly known as the Japanese tachibana, is a citrus hybrid native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, fragrant citrus fruits that are typically harvested when green.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the citrus family.
