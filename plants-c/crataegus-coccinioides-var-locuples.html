Crataegus coccinioides var. locuples

Crataegus coccinioides var. locuples, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along streambanks.
