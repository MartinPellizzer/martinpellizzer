Calandrinia paniculata

Calandrinia paniculata, commonly known as the clustered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant red or orange flowers, often in clusters. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Calandrinia paniculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
