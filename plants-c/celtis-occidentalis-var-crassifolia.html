Celtis occidentalis var. crassifolia

Celtis occidentalis var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf boxelder, is a variant of the boxelder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broader and more robust than those of the typical boxelder. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, disturbed areas.

This variant is valued for its adaptability and ornamental qualities in landscaping.
