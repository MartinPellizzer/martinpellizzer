Chrysaspis rytidosemia

Chrysaspis rytidosemia, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a striking golden hue, especially under direct sunlight. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

The spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature among ferns.
