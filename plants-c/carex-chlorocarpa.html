Carex chlorocarpa

Carex chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided berries.

Carex chlorocarpa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
