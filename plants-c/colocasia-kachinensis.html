Colocasia kachinensis

Colocasia kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking spatulate inflorescences with a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
