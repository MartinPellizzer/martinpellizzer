Chrysanthemum shahrudense

Chrysanthemum shahrudense, commonly known as the Shahrud chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow to orange petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
