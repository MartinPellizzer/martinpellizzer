Centaurea cana f. fulgens

Centaurea cana f. fulgens, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bracts that are often more showy than the actual flowers. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
