Cyathea brevipinna

Cyathea brevipinna, commonly known as the short-crested tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea brevipinna plays a significant role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
