Cereus speciosissimus var. guillardieri

Cereus speciosissimus var. guillardieri, commonly known as the Christmas Star Cactus, is a cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of star-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has short, cylindrical stems covered in areoles, which bear spines and flowers. Its foliage consists of thick, fleshy leaves that are often lost as the plant matures.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
