Chondrilla kossinskyi

Chondrilla kossinskyi, commonly known as the Kossinsky's burrweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or human clothing. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
