Calea multiplinervia var. angustifolia

Calea multiplinervia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calea, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
