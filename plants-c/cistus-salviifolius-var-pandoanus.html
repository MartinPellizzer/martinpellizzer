Cistus salviifolius var. pandoanus

Cistus salviifolius var. pandoanus, commonly known as the broadleaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
