Cordia candida

Cordia candida, commonly known as white cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Cordia candida is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
