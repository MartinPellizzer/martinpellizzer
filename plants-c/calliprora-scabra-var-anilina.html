Calliprora scabra var. anilina

Calliprora scabra var. anilina, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rugged stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
