Crassula dodii

Crassula dodii, commonly known as the "Beaded Window" or "Pillow Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, often adorned with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
