Calylophus hartwegii var. toumeyi

Calylophus hartwegii var. toumeyi, commonly known as the toumey's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has linear, grass-like leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.
