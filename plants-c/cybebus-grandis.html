Cybebus grandis

Cybebus grandis, commonly known as the grand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct yellowish-green color. Its flowers are yellow and produce seeds that are dispersed by wind, similar to other members of the dandelion family.

Cybebus grandis is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
