Cocos eriospatha

Cocos eriospatha, commonly known as the hairy coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces coconuts, which are encased in a fibrous husk and contain a sweet, white liquid known as coconut water. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the leaves.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
