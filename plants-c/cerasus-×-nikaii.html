Cerasus × nikaii

Cerasus × nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × yedoensis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus serrulata.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 8 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, dark red drupe.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
