Cucumis melo var. striatus

Cucumis melo var. striatus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species characterized by its distinct striped rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central and Southwest Asia. The plant produces large, oblong fruits with a thick, warty skin that varies in color from pale green to yellowish-orange. The fruit's interior is typically pale yellow to orange, with a crisp, juicy texture and a mild, honey-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and use in culinary applications.
