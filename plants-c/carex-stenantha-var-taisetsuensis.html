Carex stenantha var. taisetsuensis

Carex stenantha var. taisetsuensis, commonly known as the Taisetsu Carex, is a sedge species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
