Calea zacatechichi var. xanthina

Calea zacatechichi var. xanthina, commonly known as the yellow calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
