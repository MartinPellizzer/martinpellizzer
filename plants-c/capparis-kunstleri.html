Capparis kunstleri

Capparis kunstleri, commonly known as the desert capers, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to withstand hot and dry conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its leaves are usually small and fleshy, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Capparis kunstleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
