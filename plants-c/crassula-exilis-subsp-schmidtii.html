Crassula exilis subsp. schmidtii

Crassula exilis subsp.

schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidth's baby chain, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa. It features dense clusters of small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that form a rosette pattern.

The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
