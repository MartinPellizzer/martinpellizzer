Campanula glaucophylla

Campanula glaucophylla, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to violet. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine, powdery white coating, giving them a bluish-gray appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
