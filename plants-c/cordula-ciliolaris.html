Cordula ciliolaris

Cordula ciliolaris, commonly known as the silky cordula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cordula ciliolaris prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
