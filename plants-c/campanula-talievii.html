Campanula talievii

Campanula talievii, commonly known as the Taliev's bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Campanula talievii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
