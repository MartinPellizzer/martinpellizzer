Corydalis fumariifolia f. azurea

Corydalis fumariifolia f. azurea, commonly known as the Azure Corydalis, is a variant of the species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its role in the broader Corydalis genus.
