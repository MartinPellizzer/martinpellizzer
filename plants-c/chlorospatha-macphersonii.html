Chlorospatha macphersonii

Chlorospatha macphersonii, commonly known as the MacPherson's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green or white leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique yellowish-green hue, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
