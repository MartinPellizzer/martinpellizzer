Carex gibertii

Carex gibertii, commonly known as the Gibert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to certain regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, spikelet-like fruits.

Carex gibertii is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
