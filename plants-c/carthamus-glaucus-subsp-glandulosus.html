Carthamus glaucus subsp. glandulosus

Carthamus glaucus subsp.

glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and striking yellow ray flowers.

The plant produces spiny, globular heads that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
