Cornus purshii

Cornus purshii, commonly known as Pursh's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy white bracts that surround small yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Cornus purshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
