Cheiranthus oblanceolatus

Cheiranthus oblanceolatus, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are oblanceolate, meaning they are broadly ovate with a gradual narrowing towards the base. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in rocky or disturbed soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
