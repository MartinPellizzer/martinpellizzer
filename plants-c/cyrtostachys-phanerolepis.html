Cyrtostachys phanerolepis

Cyrtostachys phanerolepis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often golden-yellow in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.

Cyrtostachys phanerolepis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
