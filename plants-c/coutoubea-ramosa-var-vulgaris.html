Coutoubea ramosa var. vulgaris

Coutoubea ramosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The bark is smooth and light-colored, becoming fissured with age.
