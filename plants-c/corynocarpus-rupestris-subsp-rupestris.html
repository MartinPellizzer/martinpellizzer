Corynocarpus rupestris subsp. rupestris

Corynocarpus rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock kōwhai, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive orange or red berries. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing on cliffs or in dry, sunny areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use by Māori for its wood and medicinal properties.
