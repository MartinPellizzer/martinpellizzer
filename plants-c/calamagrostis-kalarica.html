Calamagrostis kalarica

Calamagrostis kalarica, commonly known as the Kalarica reed, is a perennial grass native to the wetlands of Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often pinkish or reddish in color, adding ornamental value to wetland habitats.
