Coleus idukkianus

Coleus idukkianus, commonly known as the Idukki coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorled clusters.

Coleus idukkianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
