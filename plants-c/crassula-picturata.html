Crassula picturata

Crassula picturata, commonly known as the "Painted Crassula," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems with a distinctive pattern of green and red markings, resembling a painted canvas. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
