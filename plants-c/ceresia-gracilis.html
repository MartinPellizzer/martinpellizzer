Ceresia gracilis

Ceresia gracilis, commonly known as the slender ceresia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves contribute to its elegant appearance.

Ceresia gracilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
