Cinnamomum stracheyi

Cinnamomum stracheyi, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed.

The bark, which is the primary source of its aromatic compounds, contains essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
