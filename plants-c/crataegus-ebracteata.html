Crataegus ebracteata

Crataegus ebracteata, commonly known as the naked-stalk hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often thornless stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Crataegus ebracteata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
