Cimicifuga acerina var. macrophylla

Cimicifuga acerina var. macrophylla, commonly known as the red-veined bugbane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white to pale pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are palmately compound, with elongated, reddish veins that give it its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
