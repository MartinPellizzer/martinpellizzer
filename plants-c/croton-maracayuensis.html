Croton maracayuensis

Croton maracayuensis, commonly known as the Maracayu croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, it is not widely cultivated due to its specific ecological requirements and limited commercial use.
