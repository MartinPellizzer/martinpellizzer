Ceraia gynoglottis

Ceraia gynoglottis, commonly known as the African orchid or the Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are known for their intricate, delicate structure.

Ceraia gynoglottis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
