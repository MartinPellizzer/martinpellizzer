Carex pichinchensis

Carex pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex pichinchensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
