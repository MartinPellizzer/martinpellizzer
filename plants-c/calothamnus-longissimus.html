Calothamnus longissimus

Calothamnus longissimus, commonly known as the long-stemmed candlebush, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters, typically during the autumn season. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Calothamnus longissimus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
