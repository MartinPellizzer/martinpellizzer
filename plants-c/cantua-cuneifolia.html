Cantua cuneifolia

Cantua cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cantua, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.

Cantua cuneifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
