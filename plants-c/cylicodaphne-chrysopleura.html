Cylicodaphne chrysopleura

Cylicodaphne chrysopleura, commonly known as the golden sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, though it requires well-drained soil and a warm climate to thrive.

The plant's botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
