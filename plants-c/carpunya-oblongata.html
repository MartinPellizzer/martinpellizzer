Carpunya oblongata

Carpunya oblongata, commonly known as the oblong leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with intricate patterns resembling veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
