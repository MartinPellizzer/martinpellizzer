Cicerbita × favratii

Cicerbita × favratii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow rue, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, palmately compound leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
