Cordia micayensis

Cordia micayensis, commonly known as the Micay's cottonwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Isle of Pines.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in coastal and moist forest environments, often growing in sandy or limestone soils.
