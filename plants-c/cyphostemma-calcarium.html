Cyphostemma calcarium

Cyphostemma calcarium, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
