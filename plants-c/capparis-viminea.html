Capparis viminea

Capparis viminea, commonly known as the creeping cucumber or sweet cucumber, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, fleshy fruits resembling cucumbers. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorn-like structures and a unique ability to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
