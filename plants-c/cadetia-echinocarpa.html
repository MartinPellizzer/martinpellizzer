Cadetia echinocarpa

Cadetia echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny fruit gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its fruit is a capsule that becomes spiny when mature, aiding in seed dispersal.

Cadetia echinocarpa is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
