Chaetopappa bellioides var. hirticaulis

Chaetopappa bellioides var. hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairystem golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its hairy stems and leaves are adaptations to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
