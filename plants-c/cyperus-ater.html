Cyperus ater

Cyperus ater, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands in wetlands or along water edges. Its leaves are grass-like, with a dark green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but has some ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
