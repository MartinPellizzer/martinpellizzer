Ceropegia parvipuncta

Ceropegia parvipuncta, commonly known as the small-punctured ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
