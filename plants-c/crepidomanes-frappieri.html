Crepidomanes frappieri

Crepidomanes frappieri, commonly known as the frappier's rock cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in racemes. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
