Caesalpinia exostemma subsp. tampicoana

Caesalpinia exostemma subsp.

tampicoana, commonly known as the Tampico coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling coral in appearance. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
