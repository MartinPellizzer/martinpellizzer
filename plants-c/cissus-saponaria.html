Cissus saponaria

Cissus saponaria, commonly known as the soap plant or devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features distinctive, waxy, shield-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
