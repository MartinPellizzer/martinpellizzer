Cyathea ferrugineoides

Cyathea ferrugineoides, commonly known as the rusty tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green with a leathery texture and can reach lengths of up to three meters.

Cyathea ferrugineoides plays an important role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
