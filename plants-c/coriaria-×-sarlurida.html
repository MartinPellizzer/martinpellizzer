Coriaria × sarlurida

Coriaria × sarlurida, commonly known as the hybrid coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by the showy red berries.

The berries are toxic to humans and livestock, making it important to handle with care.
