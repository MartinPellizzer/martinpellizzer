Cucurbita radicans

Cucurbita radicans, commonly known as the spreading gourd, is a vine-like plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers that are either male or female, with the female flowers developing into orange to yellow fruits. Its fruits are typically oval or round and have a thick, rugged rind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits in various regions.
