Carex brevior var. molesta

Carex brevior var. molesta, commonly known as the molested sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and bogs. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are borne on short, compact spikes.
