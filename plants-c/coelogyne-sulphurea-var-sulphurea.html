Coelogyne sulphurea var. sulphurea

Coelogyne sulphurea var. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. Its blooms, which appear in late winter to early spring, are highly prized for their striking color and ornamental value.

Coelogyne sulphurea var. sulphurea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
