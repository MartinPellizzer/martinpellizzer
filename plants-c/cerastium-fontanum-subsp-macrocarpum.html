Cerastium fontanum subsp. macrocarpum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of tiny white flowers in early spring.

The plant's fruits are distinctive, being larger and more elongated compared to other members of the species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
