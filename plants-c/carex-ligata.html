Carex ligata

Carex ligata, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often green or reddish in color. Carex ligata is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to form dense colonies.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
