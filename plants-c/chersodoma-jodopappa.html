Chersodoma jodopappa

Chersodoma jodopappa, commonly known as the Jodopappa lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive red or purple stripe along the central vein. These flowers bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.

The leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to the plant's elegant appearance.
