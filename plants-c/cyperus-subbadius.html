Cyperus subbadius

Cyperus subbadius, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering structures consist of small, three-lobed flowers that are often arranged in dense clusters.

Cyperus subbadius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
