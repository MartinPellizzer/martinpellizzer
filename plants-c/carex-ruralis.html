Carex ruralis

Carex ruralis, commonly known as the field sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, three-lobed flowers. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
