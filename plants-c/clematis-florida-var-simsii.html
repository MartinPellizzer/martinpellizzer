Clematis florida var. simsii

Clematis florida var. simsii, commonly known as the Japanese vine or white clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
