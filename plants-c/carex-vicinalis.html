Carex vicinalis

Carex vicinalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Carex vicinalis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
