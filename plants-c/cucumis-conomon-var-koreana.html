Cucumis conomon var. koreana

Cucumis conomon var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean cucumber, is a cultivated variety of the cucumber plant native to Korea.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits with a smooth, pale green skin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil conditions. It is typically grown in temperate climates and requires a warm, sunny environment for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its crisp texture and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption.
