Crinitaria pontica

Crinitaria pontica, commonly known as the Pontic sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Black Sea region.

It is a perennial herb that grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense mats.

Crinitaria pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
