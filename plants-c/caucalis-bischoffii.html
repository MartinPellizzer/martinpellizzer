Caucalis bischoffii

Caucalis bischoffii, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
