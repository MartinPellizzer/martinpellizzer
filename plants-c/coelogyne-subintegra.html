Coelogyne subintegra

Coelogyne subintegra, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers with a distinctive white and yellow coloration. The plant produces fragrant blooms that often appear in winter or early spring. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Coelogyne subintegra is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
