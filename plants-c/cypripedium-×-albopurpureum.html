Cypripedium × albopurpureum

Cypripedium × albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-tailed lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a large, pouch-like lip that is often purple or pink in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and meadows, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
