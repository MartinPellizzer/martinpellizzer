Crotalaria magnifolia

Crotalaria magnifolia, commonly known as the showy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent black veins, which are borne in racemes along the stem. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Crotalaria magnifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
