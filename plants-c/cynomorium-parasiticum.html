Cynomorium parasiticum

Cynomorium parasiticum, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the roots of other plants to obtain nutrients.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. Its fruit is a elongated, fleshy structure that contains several seeds.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists and herbalists.
