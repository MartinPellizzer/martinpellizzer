Climacoptera lanata

Climacoptera lanata, commonly known as the hairy climacoptera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in scrublands or arid regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying adaptive plant structures in harsh climates.
