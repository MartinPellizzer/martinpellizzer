Convolvulus crenatifolius var. argentinicus

Convolvulus crenatifolius var. argentinicus, commonly known as the Argentine convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
