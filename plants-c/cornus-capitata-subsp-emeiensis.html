Cornus capitata subsp. emeiensis

Cornus capitata subsp.

emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei mountain cornel, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
