Cuphea acinos

Cuphea acinos, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cuphea acinos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
