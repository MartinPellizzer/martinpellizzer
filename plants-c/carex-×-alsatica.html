Carex × alsatica

Carex × alsatica, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and is often found in moist or wetland environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Carex × alsatica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
