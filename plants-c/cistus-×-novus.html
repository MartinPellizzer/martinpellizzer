Cistus × novus

Cistus × novus, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a cross between Cistus ladaniferus and Cistus monspeliensis, resulting in a plant with distinctive floral and foliar characteristics. The species is known for its aromatic foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cistus × novus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
