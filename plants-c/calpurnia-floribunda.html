Calpurnia floribunda

Calpurnia floribunda, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Calpurnia floribunda is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
