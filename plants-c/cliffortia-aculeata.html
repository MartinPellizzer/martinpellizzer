Cliffortia aculeata

Cliffortia aculeata, commonly known as the sandstone corymbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found on coastal cliffs and slopes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
