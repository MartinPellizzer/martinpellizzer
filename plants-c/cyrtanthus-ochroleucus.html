Cyrtanthus ochroleucus

Cyrtanthus ochroleucus, commonly known as the white-eyed iris, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically white with a yellow center, blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cyrtanthus ochroleucus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
