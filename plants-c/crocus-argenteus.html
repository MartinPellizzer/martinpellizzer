Crocus argenteus

Crocus argenteus, commonly known as the silver crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces striking, showy flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late autumn to early winter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The bulbs are toxic if ingested, so care should be taken when handling the plant.
