Caesia corymbosa var. versicolor

Caesia corymbosa var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowering stems are erect and bear clusters of showy, pea-like blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
