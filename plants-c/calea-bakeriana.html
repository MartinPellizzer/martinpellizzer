Calea bakeriana

Calea bakeriana, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Calea bakeriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique flowering behavior.
