Croton subjucundus

Croton subjucundus, commonly known as the silver croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and silver. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Croton subjucundus typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
