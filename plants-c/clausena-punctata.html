Clausena punctata

Clausena punctata, commonly known as the sour plum or wild mango, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sour taste and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
