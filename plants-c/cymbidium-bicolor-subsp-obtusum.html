Cymbidium bicolor subsp. obtusum

Cymbidium bicolor subsp.

obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than those of other Cymbidium species.

The plant produces striking inflorescences with flowers that range in color from white to pink and sometimes red, depending on the variety. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
