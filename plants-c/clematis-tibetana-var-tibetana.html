Clematis tibetana var. tibetana

Clematis tibetana var. tibetana, commonly known as the Tibetan Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It features woody stems that climb by means of small tendrils, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, actinomorphic flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a desirable addition to specialized botanical collections.
