Campanula rhodii proles songeonii

Campanula rhodii proles songeonii, commonly known as the Songeonii bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
