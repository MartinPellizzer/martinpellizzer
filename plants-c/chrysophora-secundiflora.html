Chrysophora secundiflora

Chrysophora secundiflora, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Chrysophora secundiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
