Corallodendron velutinum

Corallodendron velutinum, commonly known as the velvet corallodendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
