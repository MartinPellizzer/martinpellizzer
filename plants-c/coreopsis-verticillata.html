Coreopsis verticillata

Coreopsis verticillata, commonly known as the whorled coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Coreopsis verticillata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
