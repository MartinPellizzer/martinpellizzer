Centaurea corcubionensis

Centaurea corcubionensis, commonly known as the Corsican thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or limestone soils. The plant features striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by spiny bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea corcubionensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
