Croton roxanae

Croton roxanae, commonly known as the Kashmir croton, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that range from yellow to red and orange. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Croton. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking leaf colors.

Croton roxanae is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
