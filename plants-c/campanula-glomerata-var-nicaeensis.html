Campanula glomerata var. nicaeensis

Campanula glomerata var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
