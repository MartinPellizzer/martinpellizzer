Croton argyrophyllus var. genuinus

Croton argyrophyllus var. genuinus, commonly known as the silver-leaf croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with silver and green tones. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
