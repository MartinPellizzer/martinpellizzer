Carex graeffeana

Carex graeffeana, commonly known as the Graceful Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers.

Carex graeffeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
