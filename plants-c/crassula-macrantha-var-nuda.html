Crassula macrantha var. nuda

Crassula macrantha var. nuda, commonly known as the naked crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the warmer months, usually in spring or summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
