Centaurea scabiosa subsp. badensis

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

badensis, commonly known as the Baden centaury, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It features upright, spindly stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces striking, spiky flower heads composed of blue to purple bracts and yellow disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
