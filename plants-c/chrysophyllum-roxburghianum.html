Chrysophyllum roxburghianum

Chrysophyllum roxburghianum, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or carambola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star-shaped fruit. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit turns from green to yellow-orange when ripe.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a tangy, sweet flavor.
