Carlina hispanica subsp. hispanica

Carlina hispanica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish cudweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched stems with yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
