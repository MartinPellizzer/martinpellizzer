Convallaria orientalis

Convallaria orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Lily of the Valley, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
