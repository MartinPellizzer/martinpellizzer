Crataegus wilkinsonii

Crataegus wilkinsonii, commonly known as the Wilkinson hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Alabama and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, and it serves as a food source for various birds and small mammals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
