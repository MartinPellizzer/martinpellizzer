Carex coriogyne

Carex coriogyne, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
