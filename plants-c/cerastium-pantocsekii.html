Cerastium pantocsekii

Cerastium pantocsekii, commonly known as the Hungarian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
