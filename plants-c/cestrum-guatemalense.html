Cestrum guatemalense

Cestrum guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cestrum guatemalense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
