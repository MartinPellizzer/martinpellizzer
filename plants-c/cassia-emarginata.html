Cassia emarginata

Cassia emarginata, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Cassia emarginata is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a useful plant in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
