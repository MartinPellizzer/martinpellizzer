Cyperus sanguinolentus var. teysmannii

Cyperus sanguinolentus var. teysmannii, commonly known as the red-flowered flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish in color.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
