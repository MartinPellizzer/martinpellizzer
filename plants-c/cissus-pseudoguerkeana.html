Cissus pseudoguerkeana

Cissus pseudoguerkeana, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
