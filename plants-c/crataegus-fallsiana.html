Crataegus fallsiana

Crataegus fallsiana, commonly known as the Falls Mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
