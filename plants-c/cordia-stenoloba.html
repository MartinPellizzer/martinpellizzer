Cordia stenoloba

Cordia stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange or red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in dry forest or scrubland habitats.

Cordia stenoloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
