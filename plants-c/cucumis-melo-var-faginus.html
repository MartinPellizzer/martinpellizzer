Cucumis melo var. faginus

Cucumis melo var. faginus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon family characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit.

The plant produces large, orange to yellow melons with a sweet, aromatic flesh. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The leaves are broad and lobed, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is valued for its flavor and versatility in culinary uses.
