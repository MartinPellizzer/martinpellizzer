Ceropegia batesii

Ceropegia batesii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive, waxy appearance. Its leaves are usually opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it a striking appearance.

Ceropegia batesii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
