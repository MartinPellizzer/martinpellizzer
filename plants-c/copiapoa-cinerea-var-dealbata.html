Copiapoa cinerea var. dealbata

Copiapoa cinerea var. dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or pale gray spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
