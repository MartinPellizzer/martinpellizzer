Cyathea hildegardis

Cyathea hildegardis, commonly known as the Hildegard's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyathea hildegardis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to forest structure and providing habitat for various organisms.
