Carex divisa

Carex divisa, commonly known as the divided sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often divided or lobed, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex divisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
