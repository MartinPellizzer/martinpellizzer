Cynoctonum patagonicum

Cynoctonum patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Cynoctonum patagonicum is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in local folk medicine.
