Crocus imperati var. suaveolens

Crocus imperati var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which are often fragrant. Its corms are used in traditional medicine and as a source of saffron-like pigments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
