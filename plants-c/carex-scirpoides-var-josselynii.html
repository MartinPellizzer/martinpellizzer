Carex scirpoides var. josselynii

Carex scirpoides var. josselynii, commonly known as Josselyn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
