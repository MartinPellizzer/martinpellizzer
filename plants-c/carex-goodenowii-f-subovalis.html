Carex goodenowii f. subovalis

Carex goodenowii f. subovalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense vegetation in its habitat.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
