Crinum leucophyllum

Crinum leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-flowered crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, trumpet-shaped blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and unique appearance.
