Cyperus mundulus

Cyperus mundulus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus mundulus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
