Commelina erecta f. hamipila

Commelina erecta f. hamipila, commonly known as the erect commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue-violet flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
