Calceolaria cunninghamii

Calceolaria cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often violet or purple, with a distinctive hood-like structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Calceolaria cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
