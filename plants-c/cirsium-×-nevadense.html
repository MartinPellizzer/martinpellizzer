Cirsium × nevadense

Cirsium × nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra knapweed, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a short, creeping stem and produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits variability in morphology and is often found in regions with diverse environmental conditions.
