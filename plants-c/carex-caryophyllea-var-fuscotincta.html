Carex caryophyllea var. fuscotincta

Carex caryophyllea var. fuscotincta, commonly known as the purple tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that often display a purplish hue, especially along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. It is a valuable plant for wetland restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
