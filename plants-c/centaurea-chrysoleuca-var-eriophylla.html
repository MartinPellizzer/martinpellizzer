Centaurea chrysoleuca var. eriophylla

Centaurea chrysoleuca var. eriophylla, commonly known as the goldenblue centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its showy inflorescences.
