Catananche caespitosa

Catananche caespitosa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a sprawling growth habit with a dense, mat-like structure formed by stolons. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Catananche caespitosa is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
