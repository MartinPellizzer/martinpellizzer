Colletia serratifolia

Colletia serratifolia, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Colletia serratifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
