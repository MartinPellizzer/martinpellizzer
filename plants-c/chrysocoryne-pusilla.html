Chrysocoryne pusilla

Chrysocoryne pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf golden crown.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a container plant.
