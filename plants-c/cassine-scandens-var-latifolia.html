Cassine scandens var. latifolia

Cassine scandens var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf cassine, is a woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by orange to red berries. Its growth habit is climbing, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees or structures.

It thrives in humid environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.
