Carrichtera annua

Carrichtera annua, commonly known as the annual carrichtera, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Carrichtera annua is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
