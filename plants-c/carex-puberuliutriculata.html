Carex puberuliutriculata

Carex puberuliutriculata, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with soft, pubescent leaves that give it a silky texture. Native to wet or moist environments, this plant typically grows in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in a loose panicle, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
