Cyphostemma sessilifolium

Cyphostemma sessilifolium, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and opposite, sessile leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
