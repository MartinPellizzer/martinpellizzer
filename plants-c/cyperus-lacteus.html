Cyperus lacteus

Cyperus lacteus, commonly known as the milky sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often white or pale yellow. Cyperus lacteus is characterized by its milky sap, which is released when the stems are broken.

It thrives in moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.
