Carex melanostylis

Carex melanostylis, commonly known as black-styled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its dark purple to blackish flower bracts are a distinctive feature, giving it its common name.

Carex melanostylis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
