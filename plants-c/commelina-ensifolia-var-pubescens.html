Commelina ensifolia var. pubescens

Commelina ensifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy swordleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the genus Commelina. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
