Chaenocephalus arboreus

Chaenocephalus arboreus, commonly known as the tree heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
