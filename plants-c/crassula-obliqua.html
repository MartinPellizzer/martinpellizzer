Crassula obliqua

Crassula obliqua, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Crassula obliqua is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
