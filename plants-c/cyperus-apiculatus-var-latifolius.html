Cyperus apiculatus var. latifolius

Cyperus apiculatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of moist soil conditions.
