Carthamus involucratus

Carthamus involucratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Carthamus involucratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
