Calamintha grandiflora f. rhombifolia

Calamintha grandiflora f. rhombifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf calamint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically rhombic or ovate in shape, contributing to its distinct appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for herbal gardens and traditional medicine.
