Calais laciniata

Calais laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its delicate, frilly fronds.

Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. The plant features compound leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Calais laciniata requires consistent moisture and indirect light to flourish.
