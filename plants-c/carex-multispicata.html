Carex multispicata

Carex multispicata, commonly known as the many-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Carex multispicata thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
