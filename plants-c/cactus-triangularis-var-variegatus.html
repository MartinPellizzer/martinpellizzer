Cactus triangularis var. variegatus

Cactus triangularis var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated triangle cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular stems that are often adorned with a striking variegation of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
