Carex limprichtii

Carex limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to specific regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
