Cephaelis affinis

Cephaelis affinis, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to lilac-colored flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cephaelis affinis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
