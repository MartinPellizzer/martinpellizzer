Celtis amblyphylla

Celtis amblyphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cannaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round berries that turn dark purple when mature. Celtis amblyphylla typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.
