Caprifolium flavescens

Caprifolium flavescens, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Caprifolium flavescens is typically found in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
