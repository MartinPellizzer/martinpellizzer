Campanula striata

Campanula striata, commonly known as the stripe bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, forming bell-shaped blossoms on slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Campanula striata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
