Crocus biflorus subsp. crewei

Crocus biflorus subsp.

crewei, commonly known as the Crewe's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, often fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
