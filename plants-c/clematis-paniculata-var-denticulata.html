Clematis paniculata var. denticulata

Clematis paniculata var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows along forest edges and in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
