Campanula semisecta

Campanula semisecta, commonly known as the half-split bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Campanula semisecta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
