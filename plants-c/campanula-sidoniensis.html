Campanula sidoniensis

Campanula sidoniensis, commonly known as the Sidon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Campanula sidoniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
