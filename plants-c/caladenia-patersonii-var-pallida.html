Caladenia patersonii var. pallida

Caladenia patersonii var. pallida, commonly known as the pale spider orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and its distinctive, pale pink to cream-colored flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heaths. It produces a single, showy flower per stem, which blooms during the autumn season.

This orchid is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is often found in areas with specific soil and climate conditions.
