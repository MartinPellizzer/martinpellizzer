Chamissoa altissima var. laxiflora

Chamissoa altissima var. laxiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf chamissoa, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
