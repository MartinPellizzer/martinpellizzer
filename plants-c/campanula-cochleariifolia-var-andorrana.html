Campanula cochleariifolia var. andorrana

Campanula cochleariifolia var. andorrana, commonly known as the Andorrana bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-flowering blooms.
