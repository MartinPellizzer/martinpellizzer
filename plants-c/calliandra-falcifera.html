Calliandra falcifera

Calliandra falcifera, commonly known as the sicklepod calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Calliandra falcifera is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
