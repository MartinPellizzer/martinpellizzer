Coussarea nodosa var. umbellaris

Coussarea nodosa var. umbellaris, commonly known as the umbellate coussarea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in umbrella-like inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
