Carex krausei

Carex krausei, commonly known as the Krause's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex krausei is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
