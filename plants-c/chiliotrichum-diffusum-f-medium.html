Chiliotrichum diffusum f. medium

Chiliotrichum diffusum f. medium, commonly known as the medium form of the hairy bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a hairy texture. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, white trichomes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to rock gardens and alpine displays.
