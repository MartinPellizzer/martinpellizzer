Campanula incanescens var. exappendiculata

Campanula incanescens var. exappendiculata, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
