Cerastium caesareosylvaticum

Cerastium caesareosylvaticum, commonly known as the blue snowflake, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of white pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
