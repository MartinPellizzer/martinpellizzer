Clerodendrum zambesiacum

Clerodendrum zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesi flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value.
