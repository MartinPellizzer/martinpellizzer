Chenopodium album var. novopokrovskianum

Chenopodium album var. novopokrovskianum, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.
