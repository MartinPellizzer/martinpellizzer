Cronquistianthus chamaedrifolius

Cronquistianthus chamaedrifolius, commonly known as the creeping evergreen shrub, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing form with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.
