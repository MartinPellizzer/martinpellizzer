Coreopsis leavenworthii

Coreopsis leavenworthii, commonly known as the painted daisy, is a native flowering plant of the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange petals and a central disk of darker tones. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis leavenworthii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
