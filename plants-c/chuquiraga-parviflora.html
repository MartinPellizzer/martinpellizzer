Chuquiraga parviflora

Chuquiraga parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. It features small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Chuquiraga parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
