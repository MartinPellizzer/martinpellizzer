Cirrhopetalum longiscapum

Cirrhopetalum longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers, which can range in color from white to pale yellow, are notable for their intricate structure and are often pollinated by specific insect species.
