Crossandra longipes

Crossandra longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Crossandra longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
