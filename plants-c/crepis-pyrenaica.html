Crepis pyrenaica

Crepis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,000 meters. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis pyrenaica is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
