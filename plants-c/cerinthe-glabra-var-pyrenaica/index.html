Cerinthe Glabra Var. Pyrenaica

Cerinthe glabra var. pyrenaica is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its striking violet-blue flowers. This variant of Cerinthe glabra is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth habit. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical gem.
