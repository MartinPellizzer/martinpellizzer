Caryopteris incana var. szechuanensis

Caryopteris incana var. szechuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan bluemouth, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in the summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This variety is particularly valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.
