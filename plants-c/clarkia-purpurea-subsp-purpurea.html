Clarkia purpurea subsp. purpurea

Clarkia purpurea subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
