Calopogonium sericeum

Calopogonium sericeum, commonly known as silky calopogonium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and pastures.

Silky calopogonium is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
