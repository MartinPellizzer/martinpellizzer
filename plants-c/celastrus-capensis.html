Celastrus capensis

Celastrus capensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that produces small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers, which are followed by bright orange, lantern-like fruits. The plant has smooth, often thornless stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
