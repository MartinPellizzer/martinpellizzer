Cacalia bolleana

Cacalia bolleana, commonly known as the Bolleana bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia bolleana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
