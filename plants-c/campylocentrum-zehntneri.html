Campylocentrum zehntneri

Campylocentrum zehntneri, commonly known as the Zehntner's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange with intricate patterns, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
