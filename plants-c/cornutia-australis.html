Cornutia australis

Cornutia australis, commonly known as the Australian horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its flowers are usually red or orange, with five petals and a long, slender style.

Cornutia australis is an important plant in local ecosystems, providing food for various insects and birds.
