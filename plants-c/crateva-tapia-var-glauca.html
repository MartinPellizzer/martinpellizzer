Crateva tapia var. glauca

Crateva tapia var. glauca, commonly known as the blue star or blue balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is a variant of Crateva tapia, distinguished by its more intense blue pigmentation in the flowers.
