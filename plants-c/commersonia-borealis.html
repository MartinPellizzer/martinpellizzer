Commersonia borealis

Commersonia borealis, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Antarctica.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that thrives in the extreme cold and nutrient-poor soils of its environment. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of the Southern Hemisphere. Its leaves are short and dense, helping to reduce water loss in the dry, windy conditions.

Commersonia borealis plays a minor but ecologically significant role in the sparse vegetation of Antarctica's coastal areas.
