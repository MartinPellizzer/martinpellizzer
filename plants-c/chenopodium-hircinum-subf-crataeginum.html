Chenopodium hircinum subf. crataeginum

Chenopodium hircinum subf.

crataeginum, commonly known as the woolly goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
