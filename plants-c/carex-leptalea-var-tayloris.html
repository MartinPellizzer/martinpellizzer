Carex leptalea var. tayloris

Carex leptalea var. tayloris, commonly known as Taylor's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often borne on short, erect stems.
