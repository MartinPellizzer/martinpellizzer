Cirsium × tryphilinum

Cirsium × tryphilinum, commonly known as the Tryphillan thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium horridulum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe. It has a robust root system and can form dense stands, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
