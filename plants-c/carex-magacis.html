Carex magacis

Carex magacis, commonly known as the Magacis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex magacis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
