Cissus andongensis

Cissus andongensis, commonly known as the Andong devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Cissus andongensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
