Coleosanthus secundiflorus

Coleosanthus secundiflorus, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, resembling those of the true dandelion.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
