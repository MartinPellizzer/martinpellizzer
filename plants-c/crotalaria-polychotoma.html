Crotalaria polychotoma

Crotalaria polychotoma, commonly known as the many-seed crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seed pods are elongated, dehiscent capsules that release numerous small, flat seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
