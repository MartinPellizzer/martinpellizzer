Ceropegia arabica var. superba

Ceropegia arabica var. superba, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The leaves are typically thick, waxy, and have a silvery or grayish hue, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from hanging baskets or shelves.
