Ceropegia clavicorona

Ceropegia clavicorona, commonly known as the hooded milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has thin, trailing stems and produces small, white to yellowish leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a warm, sunny environment.

Ceropegia clavicorona is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique floral structure and adaptability to various growing conditions.
