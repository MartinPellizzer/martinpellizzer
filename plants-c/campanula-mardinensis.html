Campanula mardinensis

Campanula mardinensis, commonly known as the Mardin bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Campanula mardinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
