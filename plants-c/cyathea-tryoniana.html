Cyathea tryoniana

Cyathea tryoniana, commonly known as the Tryon's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can span several meters in width.

Cyathea tryoniana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
