Carludovica coronata

Carludovica coronata, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a succulent plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small and may fall off during periods of drought.

Carludovica coronata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in arid environments.
