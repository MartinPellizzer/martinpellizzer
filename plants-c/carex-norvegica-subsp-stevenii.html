Carex norvegica subsp. stevenii

Carex norvegica subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
