Codiaeum cornutum

Codiaeum cornutum, commonly known as the horned croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features distinctive, elongated, horn-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically broad and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Codiaeum cornutum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
