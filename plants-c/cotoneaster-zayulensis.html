Cotoneaster zayulensis

Cotoneaster zayulensis, commonly known as the Yarlung Tsangpo cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of Tibet and Nepal.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
