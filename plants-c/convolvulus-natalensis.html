Convolvulus natalensis

Convolvulus natalensis, commonly known as Natal morning glory, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky areas or along riverbanks. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
