Centaurea × olweri

Centaurea × olweri, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit variability in flower color and plant structure.
