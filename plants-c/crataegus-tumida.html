Crataegus tumida

Crataegus tumida, commonly known as the swollen hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
