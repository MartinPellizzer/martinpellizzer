Calamintha fenzlii

Calamintha fenzlii, commonly known as Fenzli's germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Calamintha fenzlii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.
