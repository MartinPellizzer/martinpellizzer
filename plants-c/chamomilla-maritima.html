Chamomilla maritima

Chamomilla maritima, commonly known as sea chamomile, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers with white petals. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.

Chamomilla maritima is also valued for its role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
