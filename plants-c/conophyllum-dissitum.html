Conophyllum dissitum

Conophyllum dissitum, commonly known as the hairy conophyllum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Conophyllum dissitum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
