Callicarpa hexandra

Callicarpa hexandra, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters and are followed by vibrant red berries. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are edible and often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
