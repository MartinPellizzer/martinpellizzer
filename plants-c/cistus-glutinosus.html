Cistus glutinosus

Cistus glutinosus, commonly known as sticky rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that exude a sticky resin when crushed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
