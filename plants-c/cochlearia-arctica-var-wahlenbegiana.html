Cochlearia arctica var. wahlenbegiana

Cochlearia arctica var. wahlenbegiana, commonly known as the Arctic cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette, helping to conserve heat and moisture.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across the Northern Hemisphere.
