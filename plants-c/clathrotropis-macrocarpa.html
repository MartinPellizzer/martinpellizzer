Clathrotropis macrocarpa

Clathrotropis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant clathrotropis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying a striking combination of colors. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
