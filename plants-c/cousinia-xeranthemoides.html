Cousinia xeranthemoides

Cousinia xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, woolly leaves, and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring a deep root system and thick, fleshy leaves to conserve water. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia xeranthemoides is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
