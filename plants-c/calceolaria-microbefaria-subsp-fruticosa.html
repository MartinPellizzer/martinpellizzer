Calceolaria microbefaria subsp. fruticosa

Calceolaria microbefaria subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub calceolaria, is a perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces striking, hooded flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a tendency to curl at the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
