Carex ferax

Carex ferax, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex ferax plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
