Crataegus pinetorum

Crataegus pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
