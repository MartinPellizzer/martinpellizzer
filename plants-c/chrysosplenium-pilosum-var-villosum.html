Chrysosplenium pilosum var. villosum

Chrysosplenium pilosum var. villosum, commonly known as silky sanded-spleenwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
