Cnidoscolus rostratus subsp. hintonii

Cnidoscolus rostratus subsp.

hintonii, commonly known as the hinton's spurge, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often pubescent stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and may have a waxy coating.

The plant is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats.
