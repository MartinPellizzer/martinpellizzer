Carlina lobeliana

Carlina lobeliana, commonly known as the Lobel's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower structure. Carlina lobeliana typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
