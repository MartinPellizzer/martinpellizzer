Carex laricina

Carex laricina, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually reddish or purplish, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Carex laricina plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
