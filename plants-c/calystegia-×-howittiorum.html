Calystegia × howittiorum

Calystegia × howittiorum, commonly known as the hybrid morning glory, is a perennial vine native to Australia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
