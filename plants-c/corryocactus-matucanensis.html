Corryocactus matucanensis

Corryocactus matucanensis, commonly known as the Matucanensis Cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, segmented stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
