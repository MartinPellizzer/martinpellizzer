Cycloptychis polygaloides

Cycloptychis polygaloides, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds.

Cycloptychis polygaloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
