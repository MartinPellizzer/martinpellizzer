Calea platylepis var. reticulata

Calea platylepis var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined calea, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, which gives it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
