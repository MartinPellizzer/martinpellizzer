Caesalpinia tampicoana

Caesalpinia tampicoana, commonly known as the Tampico flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its seed pods are long, slender, and covered in hooked spines. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The Tampico flame tree is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
