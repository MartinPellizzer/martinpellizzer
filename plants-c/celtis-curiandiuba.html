Celtis curiandiuba

Celtis curiandiuba, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by oval, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Celtis curiandiuba is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
