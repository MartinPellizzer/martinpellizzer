Chaetocarpus globosus f. puberula

Chaetocarpus globosus f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy globe sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spherical, densely packed flower heads that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand saline conditions.
