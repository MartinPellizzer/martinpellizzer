Crocus versicolor var. floribundus

Crocus versicolor var. floribundus, commonly known as the multicolored crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting markings. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers fade.

The bulbs are adapted to well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
