Cyperus sphaerostachys

Cyperus sphaerostachys, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in swamps, ponds, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus sphaerostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
