Campanula takesimana

Campanula takesimana, commonly known as the Takeyama bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Campanula takesimana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
