Cynoglossum rugulosum

Cynoglossum rugulosum, commonly known as the rough hound's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its scientific name reflects its tongue-like appearance, with "cynoglossum" meaning dog's tongue in Greek.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
