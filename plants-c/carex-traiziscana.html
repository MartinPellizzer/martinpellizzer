Carex traiziscana

Carex traiziscana, commonly known as the Andean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex traiziscana is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native habitat.
