Carex festiva var. stricta

Carex festiva var. stricta, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
