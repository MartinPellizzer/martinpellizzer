Centratherum scariosum

Centratherum scariosum, commonly known as the scarlet cup plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, cup-shaped bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Centratherum scariosum is valued in gardens for its vibrant fall color and ability to thrive in woodland conditions.
