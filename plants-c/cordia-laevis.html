Cordia laevis

Cordia laevis, commonly known as the smooth cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Cordia laevis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
