Cyperus siamensis

Cyperus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Cyperus siamensis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and establish dense vegetation in aquatic habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
