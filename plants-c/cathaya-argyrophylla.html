Cathaya argyrophylla

Cathaya argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, large, fan-shaped leaves that have a silvery-white underside. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being more rounded and woody. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is known for its slow growth rate.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Cathaya argyrophylla is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
