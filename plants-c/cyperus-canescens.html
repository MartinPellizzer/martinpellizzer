Cyperus canescens

Cyperus canescens, commonly known as the gray flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually covered in a fine layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus canescens is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist conditions.
