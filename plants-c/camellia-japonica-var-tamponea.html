Camellia japonica var. tamponea

Camellia japonica var. tamponea, commonly known as the Tamponea camellia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that range from red to pink. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in humid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its winter flowering display.
