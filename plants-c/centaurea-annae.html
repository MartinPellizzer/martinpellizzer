Centaurea annae

Centaurea annae, commonly known as the Annae's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to violet, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea annae is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
