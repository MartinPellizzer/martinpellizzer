Camellia crispula

Camellia crispula, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, double flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces red or orange seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Camellia crispula thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to bloom in winter, adding color to the landscape during colder months.
