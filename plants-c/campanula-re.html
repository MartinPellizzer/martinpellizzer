Campanula re

Campanula re, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to various regions across Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in meadows and woodland edges. Campanula re has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
