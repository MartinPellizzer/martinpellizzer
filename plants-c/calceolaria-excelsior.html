Calceolaria excelsior

Calceolaria excelsior, commonly known as the mountain slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like structure that resembles a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Calceolaria excelsior is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
