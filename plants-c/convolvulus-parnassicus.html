Convolvulus parnassicus

Convolvulus parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Mediterranean and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically clustered in loose racemes, and the plant is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils.

Convolvulus parnassicus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
