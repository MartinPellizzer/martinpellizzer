Croton fruticulosus

Croton fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Croton fruticulosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
