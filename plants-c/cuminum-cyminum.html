Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, brown seeds that are widely used as a spice in various cuisines around the world. Cumin thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, and it is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal applications.
