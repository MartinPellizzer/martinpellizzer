Cistus crassifolius

Cistus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, thick, and leathery leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal areas.

Cistus crassifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
