Cyperus platycaulis var. recedens

Cyperus platycaulis var. recedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and aquatic areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various waterlogged habitats.
