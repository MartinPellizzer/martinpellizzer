Carex aboriginum

Carex aboriginum, commonly known as the Aboriginal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, swampy areas, and along watercourses. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Carex aboriginum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
