Calluna vulgaris subf. subplena

Calluna vulgaris subf.

subplena, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a subspecies of the common heather. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically produces clusters of bright pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
