Clinopodium gracile var. latifolium

Clinopodium gracile var. latifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. The plant features slender, aromatic stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are clustered in whorls and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
