Centaurea eugenii

Centaurea eugenii, commonly known as the Moldavian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Centaurea eugenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
