Cyperus lucidulus

Cyperus lucidulus, commonly known as the glossy grass or water flag, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a shiny appearance.

Cyperus lucidulus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often submerged or floating on the water's surface.
