Carex incurva f. erecta

Carex incurva f. erecta, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex incurva.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
