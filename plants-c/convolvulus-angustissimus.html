Convolvulus angustissimus

Convolvulus angustissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has slender, climbing stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
