Chenopodium × unarii

Chenopodium × unarii, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid species native to New Zealand.

It is a leafy vegetable with a mild, spinach-like flavor, often cultivated for its nutritional value. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters.
