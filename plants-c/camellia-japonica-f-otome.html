Camellia japonica f. otome

Camellia japonica f. otome, commonly known as the Japanese camellia variegated form, is a decorative shrub characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically variegated with white, yellow, and green patterns, providing visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during late winter to early spring. It is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade with well-drained, acidic soil.

This cultivar is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
