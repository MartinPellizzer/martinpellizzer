Cucubalus multifidus

Cucubalus multifidus, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild starfruit, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruit resembling a star, hence its common name. The fruit is typically edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
