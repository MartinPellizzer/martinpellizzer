Carex exsiccata var. globosa

Carex exsiccata var. globosa, commonly known as the globular sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive three-parted structure typical of sedges.
