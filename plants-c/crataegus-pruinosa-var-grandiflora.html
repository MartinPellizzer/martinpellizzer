Crataegus pruinosa var. grandiflora

Crataegus pruinosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and persistent fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
