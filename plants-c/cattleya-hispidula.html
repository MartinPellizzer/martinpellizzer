Cattleya hispidula

Cattleya hispidula, commonly known as the rough cattleya, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features broad, dark green leaves and has a complex root system adapted to its moist, shaded habitat. Cattleya hispidula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.

It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
