Cirsium ferox var. lobelii

Cirsium ferox var. lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
