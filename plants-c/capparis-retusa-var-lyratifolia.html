Capparis retusa var. lyratifolia

Capparis retusa var. lyratifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often fragrant and have a lyre-shaped arrangement, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy capsules that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
