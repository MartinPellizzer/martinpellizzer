Cousinia scariosa

Cousinia scariosa, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its showy yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia scariosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
