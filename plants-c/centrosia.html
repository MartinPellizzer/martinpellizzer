Centrosia

Centrosia, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Musaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, star-shaped fruit that turns yellow when ripe. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

Centrosia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
