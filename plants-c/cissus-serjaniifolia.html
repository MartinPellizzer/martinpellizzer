Cissus serjaniifolia

Cissus serjaniifolia, commonly known as the Indian snow vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is popular as a houseplant and for ornamental purposes.
