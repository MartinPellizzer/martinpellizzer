Cineraria lewisii

Cineraria lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cineraria lewisii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
