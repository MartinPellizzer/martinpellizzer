Coptosperma kibuwae

Coptosperma kibuwae, commonly known as the Kibuye swamp fig, is a tropical plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered, small greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked.

It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
