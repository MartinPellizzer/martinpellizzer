Callista × fitzgeraldii

Callista × fitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's callista, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

Callista × fitzgeraldii is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for coastal and inland gardens.
