Crotalaria triflora

Crotalaria triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

Crotalaria triflora is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
