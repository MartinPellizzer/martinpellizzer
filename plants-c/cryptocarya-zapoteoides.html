Cryptocarya zapoteoides

Cryptocarya zapoteoides, commonly known as the Mexican tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
