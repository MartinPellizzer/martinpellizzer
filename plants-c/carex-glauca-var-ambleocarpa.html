Carex glauca var. ambleocarpa

Carex glauca var. ambleocarpa, commonly known as the blue sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from blue-green to silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters along the upper stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
