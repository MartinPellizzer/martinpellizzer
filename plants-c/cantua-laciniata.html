Cantua laciniata

Cantua laciniata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Cantua laciniata is often found in high-altitude habitats, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
