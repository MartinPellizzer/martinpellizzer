Cucurbita pepo subsp. fraterna

Cucurbita pepo subsp.

fraterna, commonly known as the wild pumpkin or Mexican bear's paw, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a herbaceous vine that produces small, usually hairy, yellow flowers.

The plant is native to the Americas and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and lobed leaves. Its fruit is typically small, round, and can vary in color from green to orange.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and is also of interest in botanical research due to its genetic diversity.
