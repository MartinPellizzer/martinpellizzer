Cineraria sarmentosa

Cineraria sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy blue or violet flowers. The plant features hairy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria sarmentosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
