Croton polytrichus subsp. brachystachyus

Croton polytrichus subsp.

brachystachyus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
