Cinnamomum pilosum

Cinnamomum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, slightly oily texture.

Cinnamomum pilosum is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its bark and leaves for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
