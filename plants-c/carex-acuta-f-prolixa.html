Carex acuta f. prolixa

Carex acuta f. prolixa, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes, pond edges, and damp woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
