Cynara tournefortii

Cynara tournefortii, commonly known as the French artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, hairy, and lobed leaves. The plant produces spiny, purple bracts that enclose a cluster of edible, fleshy flower buds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its edible parts, which are used in various culinary traditions.
