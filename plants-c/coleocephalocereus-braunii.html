Coleocephalocereus braunii

Coleocephalocereus braunii, commonly known as the Braun's night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
