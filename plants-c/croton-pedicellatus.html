Croton pedicellatus

Croton pedicellatus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that range from green to red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
