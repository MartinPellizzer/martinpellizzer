Crepis Amplexifolia

Crepis amplexifolia is a unique flowering plant known for its distinctive rosette of basal leaves. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are tightly wrapped around the stem, giving it a compact and unusual appearance. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
