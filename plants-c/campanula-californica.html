Campanula californica

Campanula californica, commonly known as the California bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
