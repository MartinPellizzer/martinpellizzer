Canarium multifidum

Canarium multifidum, commonly known as the broadleaf candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing oily seeds. The tree is valued for its timber and the oil extracted from its seeds, which has various industrial applications.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
