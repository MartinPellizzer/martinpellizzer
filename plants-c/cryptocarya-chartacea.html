Cryptocarya chartacea

Cryptocarya chartacea, commonly known as the New Zealand mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of other trees, forming a symbiotic relationship with its host. Its fruit is a small, black drupe that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

Cryptocarya chartacea plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and supporting various wildlife species.
