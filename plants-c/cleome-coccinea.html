Cleome coccinea

Cleome coccinea, commonly known as the bloody cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cleome coccinea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
