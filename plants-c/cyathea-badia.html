Cyathea badia

Cyathea badia, commonly known as the black tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a layer of dead leaves. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea badia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
