Corylus avellana var. sphaerocarpa

Corylus avellana var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the spherical hazelnut, is a variety of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its round, dense nuts that are typically enclosed in a thick, woody husk. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include alternate, simple leaves and catkin-like male flowers.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nut production.
