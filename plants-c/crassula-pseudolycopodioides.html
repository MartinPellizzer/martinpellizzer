Crassula pseudolycopodioides

Crassula pseudolycopodioides, commonly known as the rock rose or false lycopod, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
