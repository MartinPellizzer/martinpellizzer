Cerastium caespitosum var. ianthes

Cerastium caespitosum var. ianthes, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common ornamental and ground-cover plant in horticulture.
