Convolvulus capituliferus

Convolvulus capituliferus, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, twining stems that produce clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
