Croton calcicola

Croton calcicola, commonly known as the limestone croton, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is adapted to rocky and calcareous soils, making it a hardy species.

Croton calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife in its ecosystem.
