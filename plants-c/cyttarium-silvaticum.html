Cyttarium silvaticum

Cyttarium silvaticum, commonly known as the forest cyttarium, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble glass, allowing light to pass through them. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom during the rainy season. Cyttarium silvaticum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on the trunks of large trees.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
