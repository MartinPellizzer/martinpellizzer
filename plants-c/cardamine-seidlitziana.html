Cardamine seidlitziana

Cardamine seidlitziana, commonly known as the Seidlitz's watercress, is a delicate annual herb native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet soils, often found near streams or in damp woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist conditions.
