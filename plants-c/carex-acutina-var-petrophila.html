Carex acutina var. petrophila

Carex acutina var. petrophila, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cold, moist environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
