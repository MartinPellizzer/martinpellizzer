Crataegus stricta

Crataegus stricta, commonly known as the thornless hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its dense, thornless branches and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.

Crataegus stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
