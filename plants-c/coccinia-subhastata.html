Coccinia subhastata

Coccinia subhastata, commonly known as the winged cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble cucumbers. Its tendrils help it climb and spread across vegetation.

Coccinia subhastata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
