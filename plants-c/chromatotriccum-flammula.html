Chromatotriccum flammula

Chromatotriccum flammula, commonly known as the flame moss, is a small, delicate moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with bright orange to reddish filaments that give it its common name. The plant forms dense, cushion-like mats and is often found on decaying wood or in rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a protonemata stage followed by the development of leafy shoots.

Chromatotriccum flammula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in damp habitats.
