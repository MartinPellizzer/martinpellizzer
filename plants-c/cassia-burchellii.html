Cassia burchellii

Cassia burchellii, commonly known as yellow flame, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.

Cassia burchellii also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
