Camaridium robustum

Camaridium robustum, commonly known as the robust camaridium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a tall, erect stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers in clusters at the leaf axils, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Camaridium robustum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
