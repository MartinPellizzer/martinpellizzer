Capsella biformis

Capsella biformis, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of four-petaled white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It produces small, round, pale yellow pods that contain several seeds.

Capsella biformis is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas where it can thrive in sunny conditions.
