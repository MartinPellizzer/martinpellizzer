Coleus equisetiformis

Coleus equisetiformis, commonly known as the horse-tail coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its unique, branched stems that resemble those of horsetail plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a striking coloration, often displaying shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in whorls along the stems.

Coleus equisetiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
