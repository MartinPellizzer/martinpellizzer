Carex durifolia

Carex durifolia, commonly known as hardleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along streams. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex durifolia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
