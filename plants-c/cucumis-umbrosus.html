Cucumis umbrosus

Cucumis umbrosus, commonly known as the shadow cucumber, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, elongated fruits that often have a slightly wrinkled texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued in some traditional medicinal practices for its purported health benefits.
