Cucumis acutangulus

Cucumis acutangulus, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, bitter-tasting fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is often grown as a climbing vine and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional properties.
