Centaurea tenuifolia subsp. pinae

Centaurea tenuifolia subsp.

pinae, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive blue or purple florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry grasslands. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
