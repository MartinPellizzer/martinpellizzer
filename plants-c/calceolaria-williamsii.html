Calceolaria williamsii

Calceolaria williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Calceolaria williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
