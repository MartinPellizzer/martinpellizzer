Chrysophyllum pomiforme

Chrysophyllum pomiforme, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a star, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a pear. The plant thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
