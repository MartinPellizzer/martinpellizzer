Celosia cristata var. castrensis

Celosia cristata var. castrensis, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both container and in-ground planting.

Its showy blooms come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and pink, adding a striking visual element to landscapes.
