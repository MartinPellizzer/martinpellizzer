Carex trichodes

Carex trichodes, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the top of the plant.

Carex trichodes is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
