Cyathea tomentosissima

Cyathea tomentosissima, commonly known as the woolly tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive silvery-white coating on its young fronds, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyathea tomentosissima plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
