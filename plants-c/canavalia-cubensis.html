Canavalia cubensis

Canavalia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its potential in agroforestry and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
