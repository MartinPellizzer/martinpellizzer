Clusia intermedia

Clusia intermedia, commonly known as the waxleaf shrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its ability to retain its foliage year-round makes it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
