Cyathea salvinii

Cyathea salvinii, commonly known as the Salvin's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the development of its woody trunk, while its fronds are typically dark green and leathery.

Cyathea salvinii is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and swamps.
