Clerodendrum standleyi

Clerodendrum standleyi, commonly known as the red-flowered clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat branched as the plant matures.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
