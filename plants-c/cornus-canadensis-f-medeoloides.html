Cornus canadensis f. medeoloides

Cornus canadensis f. medeoloides, commonly known as the Canadian dogwood or dwarf dogwood, is a rare and delicate plant belonging to the Cornaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender, often hairy stems that form a dense mat. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rich, forested areas.
