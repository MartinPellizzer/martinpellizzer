Cyathea longipes

Cyathea longipes, commonly known as the longiped fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Cyathea longipes has a distinctive root system that helps it anchor in rocky or clay soils.

Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in tropical forests.
