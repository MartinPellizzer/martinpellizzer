Cyperus diffusus subsp. bancanus

Cyperus diffusus subsp.

bancanus, commonly known as swamp arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels. Cyperus diffusus subsp.

bancanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
