Chenopodium × hajongardense

Chenopodium × hajongardense, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a species that results from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berlandieri.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its broad, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically appearing in late summer. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
