Chrysopogon nutans var. linnaeanus

Chrysopogon nutans var. linnaeanus, commonly known as the Linnaean grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to golden flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
