Clusia rosea var. colombiana

Clusia rosea var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
