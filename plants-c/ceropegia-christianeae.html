Ceropegia christianeae

Ceropegia christianeae, commonly known as the "Blue Ghost" or "Ghost Flower," is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, tubular stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, which are pollinated by specific species of flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
