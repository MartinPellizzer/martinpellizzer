Chenopodium opulifolium var. olukondae

Chenopodium opulifolium var. olukondae, commonly known as the Olukonda goosefoot, is a species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used traditionally in local cuisines.
