Comanthera imbricata

Comanthera imbricata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline, thriving in salty, windy environments. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Comanthera imbricata is often found in dune systems and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
