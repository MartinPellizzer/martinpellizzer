Costus argyrophyllus

Costus argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf costus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Costus argyrophyllus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
