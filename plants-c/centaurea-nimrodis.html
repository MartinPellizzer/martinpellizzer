Centaurea nimrodis

Centaurea nimrodis, commonly known as the Sinai centaury, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its bright blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea nimrodis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
