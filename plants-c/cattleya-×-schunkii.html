Cattleya × schunkii

Cattleya × schunkii, commonly known as the Schunk's cattleya, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossiae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple, with a distinctive lip that is often a different color. It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

Cattleya × schunkii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
