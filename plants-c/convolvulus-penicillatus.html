Convolvulus penicillatus

Convolvulus penicillatus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing herbaceous plant that produces small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
