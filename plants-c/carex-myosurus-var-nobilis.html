Carex myosurus var. nobilis

Carex myosurus var. nobilis, commonly known as the noble sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
