Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. glareosus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

glareosus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. It has a bushy growth habit with multiple branches and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness.
