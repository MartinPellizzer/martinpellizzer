Camelina alyssum subsp. integerrima

Camelina alyssum subsp.

integerrima, commonly known as the alpine camelina, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry climates. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
