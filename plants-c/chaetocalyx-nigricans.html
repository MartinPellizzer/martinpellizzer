Chaetocalyx nigricans

Chaetocalyx nigricans, commonly known as the black-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often adorned with white or pale pink markings. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Chaetocalyx nigricans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
