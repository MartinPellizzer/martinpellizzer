Carex humilis var. scirrobasis

Carex humilis var. scirrobasis, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting various wildlife species.
