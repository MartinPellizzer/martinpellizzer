Clinopodium × bellantianum

Clinopodium × bellantianum, commonly known as the hybrid mint or hybrid wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Clinopodium vulgare and Clinopodium polytrichum, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Clinopodium × bellantianum is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
