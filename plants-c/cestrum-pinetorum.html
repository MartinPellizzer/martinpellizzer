Cestrum pinetorum

Cestrum pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also noted for its fragrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
