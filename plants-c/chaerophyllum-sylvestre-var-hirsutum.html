Chaerophyllum sylvestre var. hirsutum

Chaerophyllum sylvestre var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy hedge parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae (carrot) family.
