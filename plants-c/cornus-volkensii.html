Cornus volkensii

Cornus volkensii, commonly known as the yellow dogwood, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Cornus volkensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
