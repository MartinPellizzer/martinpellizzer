Celosia virgata var. paniculata

Celosia virgata var. paniculata, commonly known as the flame flower or crested flame, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by these colorful, elongated structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its unique appearance and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity in horticultural settings.
