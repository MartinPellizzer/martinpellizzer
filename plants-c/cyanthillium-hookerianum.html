Cyanthillium hookerianum

Cyanthillium hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Cyanthillium hookerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
