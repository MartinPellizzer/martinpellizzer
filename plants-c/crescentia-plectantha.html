Crescentia plectantha

Crescentia plectantha, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Crescentia plectantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
