Ceanothus prostratus var. pinetorum

Ceanothus prostratus var. pinetorum, commonly known as the creeping ceanothus, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
