Carex koidzumiana

Carex koidzumiana, commonly known as the Koidzumi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which are followed by triangular, spike-like fruits.

Carex koidzumiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
