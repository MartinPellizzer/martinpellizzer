Cyperus texensis

Cyperus texensis, commonly known as Texas flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus texensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
