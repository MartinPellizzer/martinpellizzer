Chrysocoma cernua

Chrysocoma cernua, commonly known as the hanging chrysocoma, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chrysocoma cernua is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
