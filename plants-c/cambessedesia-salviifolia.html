Cambessedesia salviifolia

Cambessedesia salviifolia, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fruits. Its roots are known to produce a milky sap that has been used in traditional medicine.

Cambessedesia salviifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its shade-providing capabilities.
