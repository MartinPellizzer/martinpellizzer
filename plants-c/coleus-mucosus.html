Coleus mucosus

Coleus mucosus, commonly known as the sticky coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus mucosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
