Colutea vesicaria

Colutea vesicaria, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces small, inflated pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Colutea vesicaria is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
