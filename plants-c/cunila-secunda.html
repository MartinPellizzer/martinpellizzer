Cunila secunda

Cunila secunda, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, carrots, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a licorice-like flavor to dishes.

Cunila secunda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
