Cinnamomum floribundum

Cinnamomum floribundum, commonly known as the fragrant cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive, spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries containing winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic bark and ornamental value.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and bark are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
