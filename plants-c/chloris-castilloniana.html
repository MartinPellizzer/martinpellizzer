Chloris castilloniana

Chloris castilloniana, commonly known as Castillon's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
