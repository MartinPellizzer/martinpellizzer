Carex sempervirens f. semipallescens

Carex sempervirens f. semipallescens, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive silvery-blue hue due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded forest floors.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in shady, damp areas.
