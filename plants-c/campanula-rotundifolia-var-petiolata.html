Campanula rotundifolia var. petiolata

Campanula rotundifolia var. petiolata, commonly known as the petioled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of rounded, hairy leaves with long petioles, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, hardy plants.
