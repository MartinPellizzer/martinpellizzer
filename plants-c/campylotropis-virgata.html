Campylotropis virgata

Campylotropis virgata, commonly known as the Virginia cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for forage or ornamental purposes.

Campylotropis virgata plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
