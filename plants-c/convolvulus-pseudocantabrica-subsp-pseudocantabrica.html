Convolvulus pseudocantabrica subsp. pseudocantabrica

Convolvulus pseudocantabrica subsp.

pseudocantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or coastal environments and is often found in sunny, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
