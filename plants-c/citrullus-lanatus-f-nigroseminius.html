Citrullus lanatus f. nigroseminius

Citrullus lanatus f. nigroseminius, commonly known as the black-seeded watermelon, is a botanical variant of the watermelon species characterized by its dark-colored seeds.

This cultivar is prized for its large, sweet, and juicy fruits with a vibrant red flesh. The black seeds are not only visually distinctive but also contribute to the plant's genetic diversity. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's vines are vigorous, and it is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
