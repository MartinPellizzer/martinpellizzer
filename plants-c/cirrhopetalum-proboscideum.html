Cirrhopetalum proboscideum

Cirrhopetalum proboscideum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, featuring intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant has long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees and other insects, which are attracted by the plant's strong fragrance.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Cirrhopetalum proboscideum is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
