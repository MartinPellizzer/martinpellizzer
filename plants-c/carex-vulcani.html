Carex vulcani

Carex vulcani, commonly known as the volcanic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to volcanic regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with high mineral content. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex vulcani is often found in rocky or volcanic soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.
