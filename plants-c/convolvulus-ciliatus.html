Convolvulus ciliatus

Convolvulus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
