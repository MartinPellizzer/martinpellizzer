Carex multiculmis

Carex multiculmis, commonly known as the many-branched sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets.

Carex multiculmis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
