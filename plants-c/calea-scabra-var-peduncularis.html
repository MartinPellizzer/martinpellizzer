Calea scabra var. peduncularis

Calea scabra var. peduncularis, commonly known as the red velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
