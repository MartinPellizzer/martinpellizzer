Cyananthus petiolatus

Cyananthus petiolatus, commonly known as the blue-flowered petiole plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, petioled leaves and clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other pollinators attracted to its vibrant color.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
