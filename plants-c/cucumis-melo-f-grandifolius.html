Cucumis melo f. grandifolius

Cucumis melo f. grandifolius, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variant of the melon plant characterized by its large, broad leaves.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant produces elongated, edible fruits that are typically harvested when still immature and tender. Its botanical features include a vigorous vine growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and texture, making it a popular choice in specialty gardening and culinary applications.
