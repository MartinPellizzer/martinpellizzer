Centaurea tadshicorum

Centaurea tadshicorum, commonly known as the Tadshik Centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, hairy leaves. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and arid environments. It produces small, bracteate flower heads that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
