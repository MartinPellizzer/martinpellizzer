Centropogon aristi var. subterpilis

Centropogon aristi var. subterpilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a distinctive glabrous surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
