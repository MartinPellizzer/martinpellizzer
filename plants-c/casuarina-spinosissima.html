Casuarina spinosissima

Casuarina spinosissima, commonly known as the spinous casuarina, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, cylindrical flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

Casuarina spinosissima is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often used in coastal stabilization efforts.
