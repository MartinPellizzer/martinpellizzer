Carex reticulosa subsp. stricta

Carex reticulosa subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
