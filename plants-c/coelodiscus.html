Coelodiscus

Coelodiscus, commonly known as the African orchid or the spiral orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat. Coelodiscus species are known for their intricate floral structures and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.
