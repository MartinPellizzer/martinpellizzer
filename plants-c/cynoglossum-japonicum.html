Cynoglossum japonicum

Cynoglossum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese mouse ear, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
