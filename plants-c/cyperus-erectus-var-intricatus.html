Cyperus erectus var. intricatus

Cyperus erectus var. intricatus, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
