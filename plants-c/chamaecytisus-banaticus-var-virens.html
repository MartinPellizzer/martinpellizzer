Chamaecytisus banaticus var. virens

Chamaecytisus banaticus var. virens, commonly known as the green broom, is a shrub native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and evergreen foliage. The leaves are small, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
