Catapodium rigidum var. majus

Catapodium rigidum var. majus, commonly known as the rigid grass or catapodium, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
