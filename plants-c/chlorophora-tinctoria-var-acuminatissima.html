Chlorophora tinctoria var. acuminatissima

Chlorophora tinctoria var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the acuminata sycamore, is a variant of the sycamore tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive, rough texture. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
