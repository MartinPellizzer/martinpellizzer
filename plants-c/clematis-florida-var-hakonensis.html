Clematis florida var. hakonensis

Clematis florida var. hakonensis, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features showy, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
