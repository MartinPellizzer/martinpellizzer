Cladium occidentale

Cladium occidentale, commonly known as the western sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland ecosystems in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that form dense stands in shallow water or swampy areas. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, clustered flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Cladium occidentale plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetlands by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.
