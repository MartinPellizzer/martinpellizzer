Coryphantha valida

Coryphantha valida, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical or cylindrical shape and is covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Coryphantha valida is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
