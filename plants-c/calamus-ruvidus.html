Calamus ruvidus

Calamus ruvidus, commonly known as the rough calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Calamus ruvidus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
