Cyperus muelleri

Cyperus muelleri, commonly known as the mueller sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, three-parted flowers in spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
