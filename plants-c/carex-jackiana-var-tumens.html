Carex jackiana var. tumens

Carex jackiana var. tumens, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, woolly-haired inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
