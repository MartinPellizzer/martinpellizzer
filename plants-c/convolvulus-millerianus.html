Convolvulus millerianus

Convolvulus millerianus, commonly known as the Miller's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, sunny environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
