Capparis bariensis

Capparis bariensis, commonly known as the Bariensis Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, cap-like fruits. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Capparis bariensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
