Cucurbita pepo var. texana

Cucurbita pepo var. texana, commonly known as the Texas gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces elongated, often ribbed fruits with a waxy coating. The plant typically grows on trellises or supports due to its climbing habit. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruits can vary in size and color depending on the variety.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some traditional culinary applications.
