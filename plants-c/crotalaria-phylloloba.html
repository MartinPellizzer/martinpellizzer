Crotalaria phylloloba

Crotalaria phylloloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria phylloloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
