Cyperus tonduzianus

Cyperus tonduzianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Cyperus tonduzianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
