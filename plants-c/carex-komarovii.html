Carex komarovii

Carex komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green to reddish in color.

Carex komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its upright, textured appearance.
