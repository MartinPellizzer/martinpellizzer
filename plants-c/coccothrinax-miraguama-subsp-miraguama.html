Coccothrinax miraguama subsp. miraguama

Coccothrinax miraguama subsp.

miraguama, commonly known as the Miraguama palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Caribbean. It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry to moderately humid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
