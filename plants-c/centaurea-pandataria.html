Centaurea pandataria

Centaurea pandataria, commonly known as the western coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, pink to reddish-purple flower heads. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive conical shape formed by its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Centaurea pandataria is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
