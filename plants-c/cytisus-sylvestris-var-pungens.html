Cytisus sylvestris var. pungens

Cytisus sylvestris var. pungens, commonly known as the bristly broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its rough, bristly texture, which gives it its common name.
