Carex subglabra

Carex subglabra, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex subglabra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
