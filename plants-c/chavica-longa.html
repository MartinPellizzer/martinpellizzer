Chavica longa

Chavica longa, commonly known as the longpod senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant can grow up to several meters in height.

Chavica longa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
