Carex moenchiana

Carex moenchiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex moenchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
