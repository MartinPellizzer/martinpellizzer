Crossopetalum integrifolium

Crossopetalum integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaf crossopetalum, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
