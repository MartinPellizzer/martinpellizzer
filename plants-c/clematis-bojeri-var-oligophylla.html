Clematis bojeri var. oligophylla

Clematis bojeri var. oligophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 6 meters in length, supported by small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The flowers are typically blue to violet, with five petals and a prominent central pistil, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
