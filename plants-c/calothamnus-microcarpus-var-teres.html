Calothamnus microcarpus var. teres

Calothamnus microcarpus var. teres, commonly known as the narrow-leafed emu bush, is a small, erect shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to dry conditions and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in low-water garden landscapes.
