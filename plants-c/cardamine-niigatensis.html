Cardamine niigatensis

Cardamine niigatensis, commonly known as the Niigata watercress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a short growing season and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
