Cyrtomidictyum

Cyrtomidictyum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Cyrtomidictyum is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
