Cassia mensicola

Cassia mensicola, commonly known as moon's cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia mensicola thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
