Croton ivohibensis var. macrocalyx

Croton ivohibensis var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and often variegated leaves, which can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.

However, it is considered toxic to humans and animals, requiring careful handling and placement.
