Carex familiaris

Carex familiaris, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex familiaris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in diverse wetland conditions.
