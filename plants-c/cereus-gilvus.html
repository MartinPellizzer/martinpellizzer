Cereus gilvus

Cereus gilvus, commonly known as the yellow cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or white, which open at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Cereus gilvus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
