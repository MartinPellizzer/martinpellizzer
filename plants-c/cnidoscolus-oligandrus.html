Cnidoscolus oligandrus

Cnidoscolus oligandrus, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and climbing habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by spiny, edible fruits. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and the fruit is consumed raw or cooked.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
