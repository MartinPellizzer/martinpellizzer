Coreopsis drummondii

Coreopsis drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Drummond's tickseed is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
