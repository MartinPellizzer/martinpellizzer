Commelina neurophylla

Commelina neurophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in a spike-like cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
