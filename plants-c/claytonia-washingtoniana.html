Claytonia washingtoniana

Claytonia washingtoniana, commonly known as the Washington's springbeauty, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant produces small, star-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by heart-shaped leaves. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Claytonia washingtoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
