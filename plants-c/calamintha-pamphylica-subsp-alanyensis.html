Calamintha pamphylica subsp. alanyensis

Calamintha pamphylica subsp.

alanyensis, commonly known as Alanyan germander, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
