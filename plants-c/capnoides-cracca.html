Capnoides cracca

Capnoides cracca, commonly known as the red-flowered capnoides, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Capnoides cracca is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
