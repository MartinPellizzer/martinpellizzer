Chromanthus

Chromanthus, commonly known as the rainbow flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that often resemble petals, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plants typically have opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Chromanthus species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They thrive in warm, humid environments and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
