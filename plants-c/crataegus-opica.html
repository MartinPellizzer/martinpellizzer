Crataegus opica

Crataegus opica, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits, known as haws, are red or orange berries that ripen in autumn.

Crataegus opica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
