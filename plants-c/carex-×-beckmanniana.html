Carex × beckmanniana

Carex × beckmanniana, commonly known as the Beckmann’s sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are primarily pollinated by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
