Chamaecrista pauciflora

Chamaecrista pauciflora, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is deep, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.
