Chimarrhis venezuelensis

Chimarrhis venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling coral-like structures. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
