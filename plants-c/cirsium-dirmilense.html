Cirsium dirmilense

Cirsium dirmilense, commonly known as the Dirmil thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to specific regions in Europe, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and full sun. Its morphology includes a robust stem with bracts that protect the flowering heads.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in grasslands and wastelands.
