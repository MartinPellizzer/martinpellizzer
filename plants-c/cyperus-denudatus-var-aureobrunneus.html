Cyperus denudatus var. aureobrunneus

Cyperus denudatus var. aureobrunneus, commonly known as the golden brown flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with golden or brown hues, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers.

This variety is valued for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
