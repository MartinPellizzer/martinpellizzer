Cereus pentlandii var. lilacinus-superbus

Cereus pentlandii var. lilacinus-superbus, commonly known as the Superbus night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, fragrant flowers at night. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, open only during the night and close by dawn.

This unique blooming habit makes it a popular choice for nocturnal gardeners and collectors.
