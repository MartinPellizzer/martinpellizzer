Copaifera mildbraedii

Copaifera mildbraedii, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellowish flowers. The tree produces a valuable resin, often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
