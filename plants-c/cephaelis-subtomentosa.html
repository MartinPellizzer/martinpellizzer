Cephaelis subtomentosa

Cephaelis subtomentosa, commonly known as the hairy cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence.

Cephaelis subtomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
