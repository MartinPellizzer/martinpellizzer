Carlina biebersteinii subsp. biebersteinii

Carlina biebersteinii subsp.

biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, such as cliffs and slopes, where it thrives in full sun.

The plant features a tall, sparsely leafed stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its distinctive purple to blue flowers, which bloom in summer, are arranged in a conical shape, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
