Calamintha nepeta subsp. heterotricha

Calamintha nepeta subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a minty, slightly camphor-like scent. Calamintha nepeta subsp.

heterotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
