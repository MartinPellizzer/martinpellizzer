Carex approximata f. ramosa

Carex approximata f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, which are often green to reddish in color.
