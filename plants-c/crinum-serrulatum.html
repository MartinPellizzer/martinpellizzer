Crinum serrulatum

Crinum serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spidery appearance. The plant is part of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes many ornamental and sometimes toxic plants. Crinum serrulatum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
