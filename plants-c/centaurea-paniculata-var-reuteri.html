Centaurea paniculata var. reuteri

Centaurea paniculata var. reuteri, commonly known as the Reuter's thistle, is a variant of the panicled centaury.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and pale blue to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in marginal habitats and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
