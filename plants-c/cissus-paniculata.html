Cissus paniculata

Cissus paniculata, commonly known as the rock fig or creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and erosion control.
