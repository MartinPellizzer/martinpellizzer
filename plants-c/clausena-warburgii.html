Clausena warburgii

Clausena warburgii, commonly known as the African yellow-wood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and fragrant, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, yellow-orange fruits that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes planted in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
