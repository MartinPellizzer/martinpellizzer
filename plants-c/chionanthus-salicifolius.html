Chionanthus salicifolius

Chionanthus salicifolius, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, fragrant flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has long, narrow, willow-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways.

The silky willow is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
