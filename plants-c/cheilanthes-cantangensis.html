Cheilanthes cantangensis

Cheilanthes cantangensis, commonly known as the Cantung fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth and rocky crevices.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a compact, clustered formation.
