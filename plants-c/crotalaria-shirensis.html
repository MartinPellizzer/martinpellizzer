Crotalaria shirensis

Crotalaria shirensis, commonly known as the Shire cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a robust root system that aids in soil stabilization.

Crotalaria shirensis is also valued for its role in agricultural systems due to its ability to improve soil fertility.
