Campanula suanetica f. appendiculata

Campanula suanetica f. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It features clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to specialized horticultural collections.
