Ceropegia arachnoidea var. breviloba

Ceropegia arachnoidea var. breviloba, commonly known as the spider plant or woolly ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, trailing stems covered in fine, hair-like structures that give it a distinctive, almost spider-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, which are pollinated by specific insects. Its leaves are fleshy and oval, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
