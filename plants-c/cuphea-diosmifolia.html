Cuphea diosmifolia

Cuphea diosmifolia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea diosmifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its eye-catching blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
