Clitoria ternatea f. fasciculata

Clitoria ternatea f. fasciculata, commonly known as the dwarf butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a variant of the common butterfly pea, characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
