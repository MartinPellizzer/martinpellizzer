Chloris polydactyla

Chloris polydactyla, commonly known as the many-fingered grass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chloris polydactyla is an important plant in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
