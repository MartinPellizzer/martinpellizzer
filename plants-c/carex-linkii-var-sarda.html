Carex linkii var. sarda

Carex linkii var. sarda, commonly known as the Sardinian sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
