Chamaeangis divitiflora

Chamaeangis divitiflora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Chamaeangis divitiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
