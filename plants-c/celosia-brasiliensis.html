Celosia brasiliensis

Celosia brasiliensis, commonly known as the flame flower or Brazilian cockscomb, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with simple, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements contribute to its ornamental value.
