Cattleya tenuis

Cattleya tenuis, commonly known as the slender cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are relatively small compared to other cattleya species, and it blooms during the warmer months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
