Cardiospermum hirsutum

Cardiospermum hirsutum, commonly known as the "velvetleaf" or "sicklepod," is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into inflated, membranous pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

Cardiospermum hirsutum is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
