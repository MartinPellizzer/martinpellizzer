Cayaponia americana var. subintegrifolia

Cayaponia americana var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
