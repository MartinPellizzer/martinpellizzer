Corydalis curviflora subsp. pseudosmithii

Corydalis curviflora subsp.

pseudosmithii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Corydalis curviflora subsp.

pseudosmithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
