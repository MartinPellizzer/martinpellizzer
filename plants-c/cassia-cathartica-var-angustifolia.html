Cassia cathartica var. angustifolia

Cassia cathartica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties.
