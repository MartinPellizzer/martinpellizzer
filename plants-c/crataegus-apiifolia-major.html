Crataegus apiifolia major

Crataegus apiifolia major, commonly known as the large-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
