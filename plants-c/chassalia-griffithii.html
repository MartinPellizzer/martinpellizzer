Chassalia griffithii

Chassalia griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan chassalia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
