Carex trappistarum var. obtegens

Carex trappistarum var. obtegens, commonly known as the Trappist sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its flowering spikes produce small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
