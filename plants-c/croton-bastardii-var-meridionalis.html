Croton bastardii var. meridionalis

Croton bastardii var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern croton, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
