Corybas naviculisepalus

Corybas naviculisepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that protects the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understoreys. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct, spurred lip.

Corybas naviculisepalus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
