Cymbopogon iwarancusa

Cymbopogon iwarancusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often overlooked due to its more prominent foliage. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic oils, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional and modern botanical studies.
