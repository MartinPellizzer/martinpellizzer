Campanula monteverdensis

Campanula monteverdensis, commonly known as the Monteverde bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Campanula monteverdensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
