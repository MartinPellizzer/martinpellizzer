Carex percostata

Carex percostata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to wetland and marsh environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, three-rowed berries.

Carex percostata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
