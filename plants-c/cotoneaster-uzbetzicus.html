Cotoneaster uzbetzicus

Cotoneaster uzbetzicus, commonly known as the Uzbetik cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Cotoneaster uzbetzicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
