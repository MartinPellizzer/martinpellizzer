Chrysosplenium pilosum var. fulvum

Chrysosplenium pilosum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden mossy saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, moss-like appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded areas.
