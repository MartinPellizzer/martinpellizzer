Convolvulus althaeoides var. dissectus

Convolvulus althaeoides var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
