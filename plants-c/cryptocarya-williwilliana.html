Cryptocarya williwilliana

Cryptocarya williwilliana, commonly known as the Williwilli nut, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often appearing in clusters, and it produces edible, nut-like fruits that are an important food source for native wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
