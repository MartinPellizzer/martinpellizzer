Cistus × vinyalsii

Cistus × vinyalsii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
