Carex atrata subsp. caucasica

Carex atrata subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows in forested areas and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
