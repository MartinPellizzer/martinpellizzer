Carex goodenowii var. dolia

Carex goodenowii var. dolia, commonly known as the Dolia sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
