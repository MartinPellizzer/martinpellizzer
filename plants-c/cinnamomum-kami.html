Cinnamomum kami

Cinnamomum kami, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the樟科 ( Lauraceae ) family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are glossy and have a strong, spicy scent, while the bark contains essential oils used in traditional medicine.

Cinnamomum kami is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
