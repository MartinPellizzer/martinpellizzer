Cytisus podolicus

Cytisus podolicus, commonly known as the Podolic broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
