Cleome limoneolens

Cleome limoneolens, commonly known as lemon-scented cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that emit a faint lemony aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and easy cultivation, it is often used in mixed borders and as a cut flower.
