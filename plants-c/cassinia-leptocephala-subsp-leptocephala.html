Cassinia leptocephala subsp. leptocephala

Cassinia leptocephala subsp.

leptocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodland edges. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender, elongated flower heads and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
