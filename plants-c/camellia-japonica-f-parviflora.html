Camellia japonica f. parviflora

Camellia japonica f. parviflora, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with small flowers, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elliptical, red or orange seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia japonica f. parviflora is valued for its winter blooming habit and ornamental appeal.
