Campanula isaurica

Campanula isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula isaurica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
