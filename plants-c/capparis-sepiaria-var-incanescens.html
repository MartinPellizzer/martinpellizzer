Capparis sepiaria var. incanescens

Capparis sepiaria var. incanescens, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale green, spiny stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, spherical buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
