Commiphora sennii

Commiphora sennii, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant produces a resinous sap that is harvested for its aromatic properties. This sap is used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in perfumery.

Its botanical characteristics include simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.
