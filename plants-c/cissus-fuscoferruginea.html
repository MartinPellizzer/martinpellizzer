Cissus fuscoferruginea

Cissus fuscoferruginea, commonly known as the rusty shield ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are typically a mix of green, brown, and rust-colored hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by green, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Cissus fuscoferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
