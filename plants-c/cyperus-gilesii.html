Cyperus gilesii

Cyperus gilesii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus gilesii thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
