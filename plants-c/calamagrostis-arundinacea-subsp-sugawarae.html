Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp. sugawarae

Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp.

sugawarae, commonly known as the Japanese sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. It produces fluffy, white to pale brown panicles that bloom in late summer, attracting birds and insects.

Due to its graceful appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
