Chaetogastra sulphurea

Chaetogastra sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur tailed orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes. The plant produces striking yellow and orange flowers with a sulfur-yellow hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
