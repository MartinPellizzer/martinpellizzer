Caryota propinqua

Caryota propinqua, commonly known as the slender palm or bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber in certain regions.
