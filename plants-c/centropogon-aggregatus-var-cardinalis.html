Centropogon aggregatus var. cardinalis

Centropogon aggregatus var. cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal firecracker, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds, playing a role in local ecosystems.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
