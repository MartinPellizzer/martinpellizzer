Cirrhopetalum putidum

Cirrhopetalum putidum, commonly known as the stinking orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant or foul-smelling blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate petal structures that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments with high biodiversity.
