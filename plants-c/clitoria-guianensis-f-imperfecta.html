Clitoria guianensis f. imperfecta

Clitoria guianensis f. imperfecta, commonly known as the Guianan butterfly pea, is a variant of the butterfly pea plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically butterfly-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, sharing characteristics with other legumes.
