Centaurea yildizii

Centaurea yildizii, commonly known as Yildiz's cornflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae.

Centaurea yildizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
