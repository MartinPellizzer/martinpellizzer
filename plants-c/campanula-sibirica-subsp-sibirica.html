Campanula sibirica subsp. sibirica

Campanula sibirica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the colder regions of Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
