Chenopodium jenissejense

Chenopodium jenissejense, commonly known as the Jenisejense goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the cold regions of Siberia and is adapted to grow in harsh, arctic or alpine environments. The plant typically has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as utricles.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates.
