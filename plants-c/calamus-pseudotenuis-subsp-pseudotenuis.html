Calamus pseudotenuis subsp. pseudotenuis

Calamus pseudotenuis subsp.

pseudotenuis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Araceae family. It is a climbing or creeping plant characterized by its slender, jointed stems and narrow, elongated leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
