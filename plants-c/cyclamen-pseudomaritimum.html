Cyclamen pseudomaritimum

Cyclamen pseudomaritimum, commonly known as the coastal cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Cyclamen pseudomaritimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom during the cooler months.
