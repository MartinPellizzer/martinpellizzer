Carex rosea var. arkansana

Carex rosea var. arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, leaf-like bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
