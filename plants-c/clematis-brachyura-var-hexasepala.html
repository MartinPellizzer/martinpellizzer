Clematis brachyura var. hexasepala

Clematis brachyura var. hexasepala, commonly known as the six-lobed clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy strands. It produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
