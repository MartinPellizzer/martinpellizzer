Cnidium officinale

Cnidium officinale, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its distinctive, often aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, containing various bioactive compounds.

Cnidium officinale is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though it can be toxic if not prepared properly.
