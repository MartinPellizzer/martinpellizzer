Combretum mechowianum subsp. taborense

Combretum mechowianum subsp.

taborense, commonly known as the Tabore Combretum, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
