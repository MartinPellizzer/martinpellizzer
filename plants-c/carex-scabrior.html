Carex scabrior

Carex scabrior, commonly known as rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically rough to the touch, hence its common name. The plant produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex scabrior plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
