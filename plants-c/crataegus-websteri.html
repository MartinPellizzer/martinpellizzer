Crataegus websteri

Crataegus websteri, commonly known as Webster's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
