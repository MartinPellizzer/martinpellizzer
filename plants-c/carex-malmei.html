Carex malmei

Carex malmei, commonly known as the Malme's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, three-ranked spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by triangular, grass-like leaves.

Carex malmei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
