Cathedra grandiflora

Cathedra grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora cathedra, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features a woody stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and vibrant blooms, it is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
