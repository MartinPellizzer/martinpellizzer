Cedrela toona var. pilistaminea

Cedrela toona var. pilistaminea, commonly known as the Philippine tamanu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
