Conophytum reconditum subsp. buysianum

Conophytum reconditum subsp.

buysianum, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps it store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
