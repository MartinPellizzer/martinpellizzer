Cephalophyllum breviflorum

Cephalophyllum breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered headplant, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted green foliage. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
