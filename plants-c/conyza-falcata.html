Conyza falcata

Conyza falcata, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
