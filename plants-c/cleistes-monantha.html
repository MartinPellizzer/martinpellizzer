Cleistes monantha

Cleistes monantha, commonly known as the white orchid or the white fairy orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, fragrant, white flowers with a unique, lantern-like shape. The plant has a short rhizome and fibrous roots, and its leaves are linear and grass-like. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.
