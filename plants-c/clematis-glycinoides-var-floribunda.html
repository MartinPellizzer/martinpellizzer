Clematis glycinoides var. floribunda

Clematis glycinoides var. floribunda, commonly known as the Japanese climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or fences. Its rapid growth habit requires regular pruning to maintain shape and encourage flowering.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
