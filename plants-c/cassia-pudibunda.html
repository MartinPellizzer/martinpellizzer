Cassia pudibunda

Cassia pudibunda, commonly known as the shy cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits and are dispersed by wind, aiding in the plant's propagation.
