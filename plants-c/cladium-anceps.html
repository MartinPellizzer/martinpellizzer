Cladium anceps

Cladium anceps, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland ecosystems in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-like leaves that are often covered in small, sharp teeth along the edges. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms. Cladium anceps plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and filtering water.

Its rhizomes contribute to the formation of peat in certain wetland environments.
