Calobota cuspidosa

Calobota cuspidosa, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Calobota cuspidosa is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant in suitable climates.
