Cleome laburnifolia

Cleome laburnifolia, commonly known as the golden cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features delicate, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, resembling a cascade of small bells. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome laburnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
