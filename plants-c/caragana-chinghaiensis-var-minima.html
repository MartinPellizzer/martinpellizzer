Caragana chinghaiensis var. minima

Caragana chinghaiensis var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese pea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Qinghai region of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, branching stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow or cream, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant for landscaping in dry regions.
