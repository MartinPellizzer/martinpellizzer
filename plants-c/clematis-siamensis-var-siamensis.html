Clematis siamensis var. siamensis

Clematis siamensis var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are covered with fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to climb trellises or other supports.
