Cytisus purpureus var. villosus

Cytisus purpureus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
