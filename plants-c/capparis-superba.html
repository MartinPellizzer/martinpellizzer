Capparis superba

Capparis superba, commonly known as the giant caper, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, round, green fruits. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a unique ability to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.

The plant is also known for its aromatic and slightly spicy flavor, making it both ornamental and culinary.
