Campanula rotundifolia subsp. albanica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
