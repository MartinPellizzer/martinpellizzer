Chenopodium ficifolium var. humiliforme

Chenopodium ficifolium var. humiliforme, commonly known as the dwarf goosefoot, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are ovate to broadly elliptic, with a distinctive waxy coating and a bluish-green color. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters.

This plant is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
