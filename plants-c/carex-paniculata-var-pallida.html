Carex paniculata var. pallida

Carex paniculata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces slender, drooping spikes of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. It has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
