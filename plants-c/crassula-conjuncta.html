Crassula conjuncta

Crassula conjuncta, commonly known as the jointed crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the warmer months.

Crassula conjuncta is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
