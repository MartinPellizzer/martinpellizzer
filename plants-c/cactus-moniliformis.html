Cactus moniliformis

Cactus moniliformis, commonly known as the chain cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions due to its succulent stems that store water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
