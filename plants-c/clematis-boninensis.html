Clematis boninensis

Clematis boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin clematis, is a species native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its fruits are follicles that release seeds through a spiral mechanism.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
