Commelina madagascarica

Commelina madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture.
