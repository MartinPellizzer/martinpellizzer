Cicerbita macrorhiza var. saxatilis

Cicerbita macrorhiza var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
