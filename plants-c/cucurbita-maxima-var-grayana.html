Cucurbita maxima var. grayana

Cucurbita maxima var. grayana, commonly known as the Gray Giant pumpkin, is a variant of the common squash species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can weigh over 100 pounds, with a distinctive gray-green skin and a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its impressive size and is popular in agricultural fairs and competitions.
