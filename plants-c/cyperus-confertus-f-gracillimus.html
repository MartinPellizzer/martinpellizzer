Cyperus confertus f. gracillimus

Cyperus confertus f. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soil conditions, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens or as a ground cover in humid environments.
