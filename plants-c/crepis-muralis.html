Crepis muralis

Crepis muralis, commonly known as the wall mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or disturbed soils, often found growing on walls, slopes, and in cracks of stone. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Crepis muralis is known for its resilience and ability to colonize barren or neglected areas.
