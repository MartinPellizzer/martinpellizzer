Coelogyne darlacensis

Coelogyne darlacensis, commonly known as the Darlac orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
