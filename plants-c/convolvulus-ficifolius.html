Convolvulus ficifolius

Convolvulus ficifolius, commonly known as the fig-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
