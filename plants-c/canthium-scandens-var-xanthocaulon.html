Canthium scandens var. xanthocaulon

Canthium scandens var. xanthocaulon, commonly known as yellow-stemmed canthium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to moist, sunny environments and is often found in savannas and forest edges.
