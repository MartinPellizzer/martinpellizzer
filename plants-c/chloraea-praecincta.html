Chloraea praecincta

Chloraea praecincta, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Chloraea praecincta is well adapted to the diverse climates of Australia, often found in woodland and heathland environments.
