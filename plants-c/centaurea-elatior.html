Centaurea elatior

Centaurea elatior, commonly known as the greater centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces spiny, bract-covered seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Centaurea elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
