Celtis aspera

Celtis aspera, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Celtis aspera is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
