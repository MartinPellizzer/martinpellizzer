Capparis verticillaris

Capparis verticillaris, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
