Cirsium quercetorum

Cirsium quercetorum, commonly known as the oak thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a thick, woody stem and a composite flower head that consists of both ray and disk florets.

Cirsium quercetorum is adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
