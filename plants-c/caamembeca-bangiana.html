Caamembeca bangiana

Caamembeca bangiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
