Cerinthe major var. oranensis

Cerinthe major var. oranensis, commonly known as the Algerian toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, with a prominent dark spot at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
