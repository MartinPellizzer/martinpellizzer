Crudia gabonensis

Crudia gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon wood or African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elongated seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The tree is often found in moist forested areas and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
