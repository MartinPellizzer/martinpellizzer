Chenopodium atriplicifolium

Chenopodium atriplicifolium, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are edible and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dry, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable due to its mild flavor and nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
