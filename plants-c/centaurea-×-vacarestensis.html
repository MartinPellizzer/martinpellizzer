Centaurea × vacarestensis

Centaurea × vacarestensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × vacarestensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
