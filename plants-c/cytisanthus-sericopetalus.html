Cytisanthus sericopetalus

Cytisanthus sericopetalus, commonly known as the silver-petaled flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fine, silky coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
