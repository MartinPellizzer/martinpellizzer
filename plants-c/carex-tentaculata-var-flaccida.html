Carex tentaculata var. flaccida

Carex tentaculata var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid sedges, is a perennial sedge native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. It is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator of hydrological conditions in its environment.
