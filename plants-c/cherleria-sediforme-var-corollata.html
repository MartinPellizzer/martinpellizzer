Cherleria sediforme var. corollata

Cherleria sediforme var. corollata, commonly known as the coral bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver or gray-green in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
