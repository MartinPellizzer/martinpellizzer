Cirrhopetalum aemulum

Cirrhopetalum aemulum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces vivid yellow petals with intricate patterns, making it a visually captivating species. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and requires specific humidity levels to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
