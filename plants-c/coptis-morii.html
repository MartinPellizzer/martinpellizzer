Coptis morii

Coptis morii, commonly known as the Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright yellow rhizomes that are rich in alkaloids, making it notable in traditional medicine. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Coptis morii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
