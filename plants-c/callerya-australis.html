Callerya australis

Callerya australis, commonly known as the Australian callerya, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Callerya australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
