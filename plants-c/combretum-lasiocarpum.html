Combretum lasiocarpum

Combretum lasiocarpum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping due to its striking visual appeal.
