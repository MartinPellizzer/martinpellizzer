Cordylanthus eremicus subsp. kernensis

Cordylanthus eremicus subsp.

kernensis, commonly known as the Kern County flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The species produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
