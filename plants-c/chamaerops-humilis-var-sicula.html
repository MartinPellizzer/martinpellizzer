Chamaerops humilis var. sicula

Chamaerops humilis var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian palm, is a compact, slow-growing palm native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a short, sturdy trunk with a crown of feather-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by black, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in urban and coastal landscapes.
