Cassia arlindo-andradei

Cassia arlindo-andradei, commonly known as the Arlindo Andrade's Cassia, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests.

The species is named in honor of the botanist Arlindo Andrade, reflecting its significance in botanical research.
