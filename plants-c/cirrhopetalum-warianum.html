Cirrhopetalum warianum

Cirrhopetalum warianum, commonly known as the Wariangan orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, long, and slender petals that resemble flowing hair, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
