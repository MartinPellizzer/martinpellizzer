Cordyline baueri

Cordyline baueri, commonly known as the orange tree fern, is a striking evergreen plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a crown of large, glossy, waxy leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a reddish or orange tinge on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and ability to add a tropical feel to indoor or outdoor spaces.
