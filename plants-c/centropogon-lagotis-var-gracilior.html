Centropogon lagotis var. gracilior

Centropogon lagotis var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to white. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which contribute to its graceful appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
