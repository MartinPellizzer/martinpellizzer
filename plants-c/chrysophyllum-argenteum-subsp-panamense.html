Chrysophyllum argenteum subsp. panamense

Chrysophyllum argenteum subsp.

panamense, commonly known as the Panama silverleaf, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for local consumption.
