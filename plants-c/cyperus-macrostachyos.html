Cyperus macrostachyos

Cyperus macrostachyos, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands.

Cyperus macrostachyos is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for waterlogged conditions.
