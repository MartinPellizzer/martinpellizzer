Cynanchum mevei

Cynanchum mevei, commonly known as the Meve's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
