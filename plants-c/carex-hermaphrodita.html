Carex hermaphrodita

Carex hermaphrodita, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex hermaphrodita is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
