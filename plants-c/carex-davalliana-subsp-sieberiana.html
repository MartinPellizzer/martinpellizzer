Carex davalliana subsp. sieberiana

Carex davalliana subsp.

sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This species is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
