Carex lasiocarpa var. lanuginosa

Carex lasiocarpa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

The downy sedge is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
