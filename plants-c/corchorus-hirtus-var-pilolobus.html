Corchorus hirtus var. pilolobus

Corchorus hirtus var. pilolobus, commonly known as the woolly or hairy dock, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are broadly ovate to triangular in shape, with a rough texture and a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by green bracts.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
