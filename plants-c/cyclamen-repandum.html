Cyclamen repandum

Cyclamen repandum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus.

It features heart-shaped, velvety foliage and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its tubers are adapted for survival during dry periods, allowing it to regrow in the spring.

Cyclamen repandum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
