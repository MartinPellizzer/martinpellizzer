Cuphea bustamanta var. reipublicae

Cuphea bustamanta var. reipublicae, commonly known as the "Red Hot Poker" or "Cuphea with Red Flowers," is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense spikes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its unique floral structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators, enhancing its ecological value.
