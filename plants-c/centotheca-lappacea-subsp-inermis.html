Centotheca lappacea subsp. inermis

Centotheca lappacea subsp.

inermis, commonly known as the smooth centotheca, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically features slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by the absence of the bristly margins found in other members of the species.
