Crataegus hertiflora

Crataegus hertiflora, commonly known as the Hertfordshire hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and small mammals. It typically grows in woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe.

Crataegus hertiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
