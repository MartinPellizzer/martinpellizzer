Cirrhopetalum antenniferum

Cirrhopetalum antenniferum, commonly known as the Antenniferum bellflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant features elongated, slender stems that support clusters of pale blue to violet flowers with distinctive, wavy petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
