Cyanus canus

Cyanus canus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Cyanus canus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
