Clinopodium raimondoi

Clinopodium raimondoi, commonly known as Raimond's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, oval berries.

The leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
