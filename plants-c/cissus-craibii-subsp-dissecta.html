Cissus craibii subsp. dissecta

Cissus craibii subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the dissected devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its ability to climb and its low maintenance requirements make it popular in both indoor and outdoor settings.
