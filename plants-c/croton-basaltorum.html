Croton basaltorum

Croton basaltorum, commonly known as the basalt croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to regions with volcanic soil.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by distinctive seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
