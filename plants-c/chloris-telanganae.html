Chloris telanganae

Chloris telanganae, commonly known as the Telangana grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Chloris telanganae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
