Chionanthus parviflorus

Chionanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the smallflower snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
