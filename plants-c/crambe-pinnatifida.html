Crambe pinnatifida

Crambe pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crambe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features slender, pinnately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Crambe pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its potential in biofuel production.
