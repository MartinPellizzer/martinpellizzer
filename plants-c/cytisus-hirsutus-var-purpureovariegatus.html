Cytisus hirsutus var. purpureovariegatus

Cytisus hirsutus var. purpureovariegatus, commonly known as the purple variegated broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, arching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive variegated pattern of purple and green. Its fragrant, yellow flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
