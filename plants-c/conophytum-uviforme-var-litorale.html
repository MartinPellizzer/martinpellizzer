Conophytum uviforme var. litorale

Conophytum uviforme var. litorale, commonly known as the coastal stone plant, is a succulent native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
