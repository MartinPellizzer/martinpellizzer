Crataegus chrysocarpa f. rubescens

Crataegus chrysocarpa f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus characterized by its distinctive red berries.

This cultivar is native to parts of eastern North America and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features thorny branches, deciduous leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which mature to a bright red color, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
