Carex orizabae

Carex orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex orizabae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
