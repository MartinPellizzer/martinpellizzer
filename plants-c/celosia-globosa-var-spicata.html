Celosia globosa var. spicata

Celosia globosa var. spicata, commonly known as the flame flower or crested grass.

It is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, papery bracts, which are the main ornamental feature. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and container planting.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
