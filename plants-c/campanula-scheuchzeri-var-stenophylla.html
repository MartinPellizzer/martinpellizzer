Campanula scheuchzeri var. stenophylla

Campanula scheuchzeri var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
