Calonema microchilum

Calonema microchilum, commonly known as the small-lipped calonema, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Madagascar, characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and a deep green color. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and borne in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink.

Calonema microchilum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
