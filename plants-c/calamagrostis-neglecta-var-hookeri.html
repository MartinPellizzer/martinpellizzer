Calamagrostis neglecta var. hookeri

Calamagrostis neglecta var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with spikelets that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
