Caltha fistulosa

Caltha fistulosa, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are typically arrow-shaped. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
