Coleus blancoi

Coleus blancoi, commonly known as the white coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that vary in color from green to red, purple, and white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
