Cyperus nashii

Cyperus nashii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including South Africa, and is often found in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or reddish in color. Cyperus nashii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and can serve as habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
