Cercocarpus montanus var. paucidentatus

Cercocarpus montanus var. paucidentatus, commonly known as the Sierra cliff-foxtail, is a shrub native to California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a slow growth rate.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
