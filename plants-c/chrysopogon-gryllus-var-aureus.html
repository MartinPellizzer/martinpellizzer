Chrysopogon gryllus var. aureus

Chrysopogon gryllus var. aureus, commonly known as golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small, delicate flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.

The plant's unique coloration and resilience contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
