Ceanothus cyaneus

Ceanothus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue curl buckeye, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus cyaneus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
