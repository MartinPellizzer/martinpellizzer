Carex digitata var. rhizina

Carex digitata var. rhizina, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
