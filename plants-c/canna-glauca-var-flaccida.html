Canna glauca var. flaccida

Canna glauca var. flaccida, commonly known as the pale canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a pale green to bluish-gray in color. The plant produces showy, orange-red flower spikes that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
