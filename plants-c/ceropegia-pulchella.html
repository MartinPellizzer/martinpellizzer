Ceropegia pulchella

Ceropegia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet leaf or silver vine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for decorative purposes.
