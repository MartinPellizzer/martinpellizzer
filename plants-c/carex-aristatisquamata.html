Carex aristatisquamata

Carex aristatisquamata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex aristatisquamata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
