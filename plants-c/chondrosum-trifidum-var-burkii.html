Chondrosum trifidum var. burkii

Chondrosum trifidum var. burkii, commonly known as the Burki's thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
