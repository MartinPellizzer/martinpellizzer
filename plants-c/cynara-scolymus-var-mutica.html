Cynara scolymus var. mutica

Cynara scolymus var. mutica, commonly known as the fuzzy artichoke, is a cultivar of the globe artichoke.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact, globe-shaped flower heads. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically broader and more robust compared to the standard artichoke.

The fuzzy artichoke is valued for its unique texture and flavor in culinary applications.
