Cissus flavicans

Cissus flavicans, commonly known as the yellow-cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in tropical gardens.
