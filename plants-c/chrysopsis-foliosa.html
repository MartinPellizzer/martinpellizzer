Chrysopsis foliosa

Chrysopsis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Chrysopsis foliosa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
