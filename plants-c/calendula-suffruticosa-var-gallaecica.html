Calendula suffruticosa var. gallaecica

Calendula suffruticosa var. gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician calendula, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
