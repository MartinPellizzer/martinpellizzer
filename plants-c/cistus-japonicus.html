Cistus japonicus

Cistus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese rockrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with leathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cistus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and fragrance.
