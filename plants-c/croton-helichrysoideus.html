Croton helichrysoideus

Croton helichrysoideus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
