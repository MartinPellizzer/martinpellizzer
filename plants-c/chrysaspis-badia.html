Chrysaspis badia

Chrysaspis badia, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-green foliage and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its root system is specialized for nutrient absorption from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
