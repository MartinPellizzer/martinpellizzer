Cotyledon mucizonia subsp. abylaea

Cotyledon mucizonia subsp.

abylaea, commonly known as the Orange-veined Cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with prominent orange or red veins.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
