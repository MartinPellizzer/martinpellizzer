Chamaecrista hatschbachii

Chamaecrista hatschbachii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and desert flora.
