Carex atrata var. typica

Carex atrata var. typica, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in consistently moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically brown or reddish in color.

Carex atrata var. typica plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
