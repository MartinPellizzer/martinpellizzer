Campanula alpini

Campanula alpini, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula alpini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
