Cassia xanthocoma

Cassia xanthocoma, commonly known as golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
