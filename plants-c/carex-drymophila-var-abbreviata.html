Carex drymophila var. abbreviata

Carex drymophila var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. This variety typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
