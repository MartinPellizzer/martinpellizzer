Coelogyne loricata

Coelogyne loricata, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with greenish veins, and they can last for several weeks.

Coelogyne loricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in horticultural circles for its beauty and resilience.
