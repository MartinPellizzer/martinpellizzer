Cattleya × vedastii

Cattleya × vedastii, commonly known as the Venda orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a wide range of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex, fragrant floral structure that attracts pollinators.

This orchid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
