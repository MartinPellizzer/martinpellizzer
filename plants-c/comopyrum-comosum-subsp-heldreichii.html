Comopyrum comosum subsp. heldreichii

Comopyrum comosum subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that are often pink to purplish in color.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
