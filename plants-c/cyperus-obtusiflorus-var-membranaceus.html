Cyperus obtusiflorus var. membranaceus

Cyperus obtusiflorus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, membranous layer, giving it its varietal name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
