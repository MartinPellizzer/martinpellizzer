Cestrum corcovadense

Cestrum corcovadense, commonly known as the night bloomer or yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming at night, which attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
