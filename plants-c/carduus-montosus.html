Carduus montosus

Carduus montosus, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, silver-gray leaves with rough textures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Carduus montosus is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
