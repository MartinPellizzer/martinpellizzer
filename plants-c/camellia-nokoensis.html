Camellia nokoensis

Camellia nokoensis, commonly known as the Noko camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern Japan.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring, come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Camellia nokoensis prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in temperate climates.
