Chasmatophyllum rouxii

Chasmatophyllum rouxii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a rare and striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces striking violet-blue berries that contain toxic compounds, making it a cautionary example of botanical defense mechanisms. Its unique floral morphology and nocturnal blooming habits make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
