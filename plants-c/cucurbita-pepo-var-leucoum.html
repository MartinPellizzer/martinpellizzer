Cucurbita pepo var. leucoum

Cucurbita pepo var. leucoum, commonly known as the white-fruited pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature. The plant is a vigorous annual vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous, hollow stem and a system of tendrils for climbing.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a food source in some traditional cuisines.
