Carex brunnea

Carex brunnea, commonly known as brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green to reddish-brown in color, depending on the season. It produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets, which are usually green or brown.

Carex brunnea is often found in moist soils, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
