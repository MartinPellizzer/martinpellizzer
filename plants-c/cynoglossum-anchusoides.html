Cynoglossum anchusoides

Cynoglossum anchusoides, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Cynoglossum anchusoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
