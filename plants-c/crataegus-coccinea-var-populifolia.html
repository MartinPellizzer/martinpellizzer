Crataegus coccinea var. populifolia

Crataegus coccinea var. populifolia, commonly known as the red thornapple or red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
