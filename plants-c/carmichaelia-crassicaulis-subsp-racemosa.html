Carmichaelia crassicaulis subsp. racemosa

Carmichaelia crassicaulis subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
