Calamagrostis epigejos var. glaucescens

Calamagrostis epigejos var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green blades that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
