Clerodendrum buchholzii

Clerodendrum buchholzii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Clerodendrum buchholzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
