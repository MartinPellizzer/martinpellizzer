Clematis trullifera

Clematis trullifera, commonly known as the clustered Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis trullifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
