Caladenia williamsiae

Caladenia williamsiae, commonly known as the Williams' spider orchid, is an endemic species native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with a single, slender flower. The plant typically grows in sandy soils within heathlands and woodlands, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale pink to cream in color, with a distinctive, elongated labellum that resembles a spider's leg.

Caladenia williamsiae is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its natural habitat.
