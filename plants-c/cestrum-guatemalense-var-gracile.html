Cestrum guatemalense var. gracile

Cestrum guatemalense var. gracile, commonly known as the slender cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.
