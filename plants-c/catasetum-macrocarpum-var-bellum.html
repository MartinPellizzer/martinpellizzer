Catasetum macrocarpum var. bellum

Catasetum macrocarpum var. bellum, commonly known as the bell-shaped catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, pendulous flowers with a distinctive bell-shaped lip that is often brightly colored. The plant produces a unique structure called a "catasetum," which is a specialized pollination mechanism. It thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological adaptations for pollination by specific insect species.
