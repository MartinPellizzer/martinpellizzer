Chamelaucium uncinatum

Chamelaucium uncinatum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Chamelaucium uncinatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
