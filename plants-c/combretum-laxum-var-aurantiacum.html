Combretum laxum var. aurantiacum

Combretum laxum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, orange-red leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, often appearing after the leaves have dropped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and drought tolerance.
