Campanula glomerata subsp. glabriflora

Campanula glomerata subsp.

glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous flower petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
