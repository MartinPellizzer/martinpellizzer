Carex cyrtostachya

Carex cyrtostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of spikelets. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
