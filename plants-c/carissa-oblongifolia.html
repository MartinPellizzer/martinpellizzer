Carissa oblongifolia

Carissa oblongifolia, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell.

It is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Carissa oblongifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, tropical climates.
