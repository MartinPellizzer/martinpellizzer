Cyperus esculentus f. princeps

Cyperus esculentus f. princeps, commonly known as water chestnut, is a variant of the water chestnut plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in wetland environments, characterized by its tuberous roots that are edible. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its tubers are harvested for consumption, offering a crunchy texture and slightly sweet flavor.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary use and its role in wetland ecosystems.
