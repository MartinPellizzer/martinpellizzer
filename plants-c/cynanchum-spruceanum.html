Cynanchum spruceanum

Cynanchum spruceanum, commonly known as the spruce honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

Cynanchum spruceanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
