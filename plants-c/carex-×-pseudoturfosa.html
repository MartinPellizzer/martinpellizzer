Carex × pseudoturfosa

Carex × pseudoturfosa, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water edges. It has rhizomes that help in spreading and forming dense mats, which can stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland management.
