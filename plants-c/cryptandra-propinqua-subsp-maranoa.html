Cryptandra propinqua subsp. maranoa

Cryptandra propinqua subsp.

maranoa, commonly known as the Maranoa cryptandra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Queensland and New South Wales in Australia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has woody stems covered in fine, white hairs.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often recurved, with a greyish-green coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It is adapted to low rainfall and can survive in sandy or gravelly soils.
