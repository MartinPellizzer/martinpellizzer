Cyperus marginatus var. blandus

Cyperus marginatus var. blandus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
