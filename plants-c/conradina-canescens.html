Conradina canescens

Conradina canescens, commonly known as sandhill coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance.
