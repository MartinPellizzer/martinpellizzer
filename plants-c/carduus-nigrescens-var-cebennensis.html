Carduus nigrescens var. cebennensis

Carduus nigrescens var. cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebenna thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branching stem that produces purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
