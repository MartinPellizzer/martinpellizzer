Cyperus tenuispiculatus

Cyperus tenuispiculatus, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its root system is fibrous, and it can form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus tenuispiculatus is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated soils.
