Cinnamomum longipetiolatum

Cinnamomum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and reddish-brown bark, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are broad and have a distinctive, fragrant scent, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

This species is also appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its attractive foliage and growth habit.
