Cyperus burkartii

Cyperus burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in wetlands or along water bodies. Its slender, triangular stems are usually unbranched, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Cyperus burkartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
