Cissus vanmeelii

Cissus vanmeelii, commonly known as the Van Meel's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often translucent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus vanmeelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
