Clusia riojensis

Clusia riojensis, commonly known as the Rioja Clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes plants known for their ornamental and medicinal uses. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often glossy and can vary in shape from ovate to elliptical. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by bright red or orange berries.

Clusia riojensis is often cultivated for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments and its potential use in traditional medicine.
