Chloranthus multistachys

Chloranthus multistachys, commonly known as the many-stamen chloranthus, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the base of the leaves. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
