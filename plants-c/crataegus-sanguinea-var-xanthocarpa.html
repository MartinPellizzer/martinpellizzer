Crataegus sanguinea var. xanthocarpa

Crataegus sanguinea var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-red fruits, which mature in the fall and are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thorny branches and lobed leaves. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in spring before the leaves, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of this variety make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
