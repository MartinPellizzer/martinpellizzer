Cotoneaster hurusawaianus

Cotoneaster hurusawaianus, commonly known as the Hawaiian cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that develop from clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas within mesic forests, where it thrives in the unique climate of Hawaii. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

This species plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
