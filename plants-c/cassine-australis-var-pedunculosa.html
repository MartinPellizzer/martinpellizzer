Cassine australis var. pedunculosa

Cassine australis var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coast, where it thrives in full sun. Its woody stems and evergreen foliage make it a hardy, ornamental species.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
