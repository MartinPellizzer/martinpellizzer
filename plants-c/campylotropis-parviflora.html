Campylotropis parviflora

Campylotropis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It features slender, zigzagging stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Campylotropis parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
