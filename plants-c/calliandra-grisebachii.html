Calliandra grisebachii

Calliandra grisebachii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological settings.
