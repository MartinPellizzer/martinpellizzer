Cytisus australis

Cytisus australis, commonly known as the Australian broom, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, often spiny stems and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun.

Cytisus australis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
