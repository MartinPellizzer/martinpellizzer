Crataegus monogyna laciniata

Crataegus monogyna laciniata, commonly known as the common hawthorn or simply hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along roadsides.
