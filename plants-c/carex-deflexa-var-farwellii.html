Carex deflexa var. farwellii

Carex deflexa var. farwellii, commonly known as the Farwell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of brown flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
