Carex balansae

Carex balansae, commonly known as the Balansae sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
