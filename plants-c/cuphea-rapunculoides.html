Cuphea rapunculoides

Cuphea rapunculoides, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, spiky flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost woolly appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea rapunculoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
