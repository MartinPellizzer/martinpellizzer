Chrysoglossum chapaense

Chrysoglossum chapaense, commonly known as the Chapa's chrysoglossum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil conditions, preferring full sun exposure.

Chrysoglossum chapaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
