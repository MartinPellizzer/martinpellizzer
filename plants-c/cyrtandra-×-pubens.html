Cyrtandra × pubens

Cyrtandra × pubens, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeard ginger, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra caribea and Cyrtandra tenuifolia, both native to the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its striking white and pink flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a textured appearance.

Cyrtandra × pubens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens due to its vibrant floral display.
