Crataegus × carrierei

Crataegus × carrierei, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus mollis and Crataegus rhipidophylla, exhibiting a mix of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–10 meters, featuring thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland habitats.
