Coptis teeta

Coptis teeta, commonly known as the Himalayan goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for containing berberine, an alkaloid with medicinal properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ecological significance and traditional uses, it is also of interest in conservation efforts.
