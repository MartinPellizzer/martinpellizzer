Caryophyllus malaccensis

Caryophyllus malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca carnation, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, herbaceous structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Caryophyllus malaccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
