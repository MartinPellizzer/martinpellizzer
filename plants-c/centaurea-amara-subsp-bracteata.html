Centaurea amara subsp. bracteata

Centaurea amara subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as bitter centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, bracteate involucres that surround its florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
