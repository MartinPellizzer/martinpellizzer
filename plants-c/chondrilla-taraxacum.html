Chondrilla taraxacum

Chondrilla taraxacum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head, known as a pappus, which consists of fine, hair-like structures that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Chondrilla taraxacum is known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential indicator plant for soil contamination.
