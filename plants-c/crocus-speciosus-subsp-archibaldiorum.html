Crocus speciosus subsp. archibaldiorum

Crocus speciosus subsp.

archibaldiorum, commonly known as the Archibald's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, early spring blooms featuring large, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout rhizome that supports its growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
