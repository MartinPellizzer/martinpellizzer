Carota sativa

Carota sativa, commonly known as the common carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its taproot, which is typically orange in color but can vary in hue depending on the variety. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves and small, white umbellate flowers that appear in the second year of growth. The root is the primary edible part, rich in beta-carotene and nutrients.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and use in cooking.
