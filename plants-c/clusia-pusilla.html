Clusia pusilla

Clusia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf clusia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Clusia pusilla thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
