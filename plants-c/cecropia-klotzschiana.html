Cecropia klotzschiana

Cecropia klotzschiana, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and a distinctive crown of long, drooping leaves. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its seeds are enclosed in woody capsules and dispersed by animals.

Cecropia klotzschiana plays an important role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various species.
