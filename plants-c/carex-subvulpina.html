Carex subvulpina

Carex subvulpina, commonly known as the foxglove sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish to brownish flowers. Native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
