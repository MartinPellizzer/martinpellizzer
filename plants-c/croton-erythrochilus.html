Croton erythrochilus

Croton erythrochilus, commonly known as the red-lip croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, red, and yellow, often with a distinctive red margin. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique, branched inflorescence that bears small, clustered flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in garden settings.
