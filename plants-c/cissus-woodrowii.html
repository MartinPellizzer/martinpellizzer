Cissus woodrowii

Cissus woodrowii, commonly known as the rock fig or creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and erosion control.
