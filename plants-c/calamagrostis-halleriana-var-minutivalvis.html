Calamagrostis halleriana var. minutivalvis

Calamagrostis halleriana var. minutivalvis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, blue-green blades that are typically less than 2 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland restoration and riparian areas.

Its fine texture and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
