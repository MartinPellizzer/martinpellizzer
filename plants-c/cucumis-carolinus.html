Cucumis carolinus

Cucumis carolinus, commonly known as the Carolina cucumber, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vine that produces small, edible fruits with a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its fruits are typically less than 10 centimeters in length and are often used in local cuisines.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is well adapted to wetland environments.
