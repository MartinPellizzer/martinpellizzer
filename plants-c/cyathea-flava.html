Cyathea flava

Cyathea flava, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

Cyathea flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
