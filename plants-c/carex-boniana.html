Carex boniana

Carex boniana, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex boniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
