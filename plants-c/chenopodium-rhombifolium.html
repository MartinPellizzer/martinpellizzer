Chenopodium rhombifolium

Chenopodium rhombifolium, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly rhombic leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Chenopodium rhombifolium is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tart flavor and are used in traditional culinary practices.
