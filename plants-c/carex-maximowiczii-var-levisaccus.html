Carex maximowiczii var. levisaccus

Carex maximowiczii var. levisaccus, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
