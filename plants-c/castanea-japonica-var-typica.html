Castanea japonica var. typica

Castanea japonica var. typica, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its palmately compound leaves and spiny fruit capsules. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often growing in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
