Cissus rubescens

Cissus rubescens, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that often have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. Cissus rubescens thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
