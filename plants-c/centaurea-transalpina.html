Centaurea transalpina

Centaurea transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea transalpina is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
