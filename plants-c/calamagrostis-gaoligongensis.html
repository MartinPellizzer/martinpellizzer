Calamagrostis gaoligongensis

Calamagrostis gaoligongensis, commonly known as the Gaoligong mountain reed, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in high-altitude grasslands and forest edges. Its flowering stems bear panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to brown in color.

Calamagrostis gaoligongensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
