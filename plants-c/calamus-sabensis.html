Calamus sabensis

Calamus sabensis, commonly known as the Sabah calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and arrow-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Calamus sabensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family plants.

Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and commercial applications.
