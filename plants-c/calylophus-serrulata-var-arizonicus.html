Calylophus serrulata var. arizonicus

Calylophus serrulata var. arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a bluish-green hue. Its showy flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive central tuft of filaments.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies.
