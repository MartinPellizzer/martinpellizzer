Clinogyne congensis

Clinogyne congensis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Clinogyne congensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
