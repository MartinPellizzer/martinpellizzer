Chamaecyparis obtusa f. tetragona

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. tetragona, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or four-cornered cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is a deep, glossy green. The plant features four-sided branches, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its longevity in cultivation.
