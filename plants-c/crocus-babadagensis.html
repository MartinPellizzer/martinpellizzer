Crocus babadagensis

Crocus babadagensis, commonly known as the Babadag crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of three outer petals and three inner tepals, forming a cup-like shape.

Crocus babadagensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
