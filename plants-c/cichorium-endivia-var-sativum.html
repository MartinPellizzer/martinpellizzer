Cichorium endivia var. sativum

Cichorium endivia var. sativum, commonly known as Belgian endive, is a leafy vegetable belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale yellow to white leaves that are crisp and slightly bitter. The plant is typically grown in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are often harvested before the plant bolts, ensuring a tender texture.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is commonly used in salads and as a garnish.
