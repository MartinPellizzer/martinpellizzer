Crocus yataganensis

Crocus yataganensis, commonly known as the Anatolian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are typically borne on short stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous corm.

Crocus yataganensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
