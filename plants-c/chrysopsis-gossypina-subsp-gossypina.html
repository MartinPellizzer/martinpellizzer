Chrysopsis gossypina subsp. gossypina

Chrysopsis gossypina subsp.

gossypina, commonly known as the cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a common weed in dry landscapes.
