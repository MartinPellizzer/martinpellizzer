Callicarpa japonica f. parvifolia

Callicarpa japonica f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically no larger than 2 cm in length, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of vibrant purple berries in the fall, which are a striking feature and attract birds and other wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
