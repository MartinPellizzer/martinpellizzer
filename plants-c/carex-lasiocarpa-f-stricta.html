Carex lasiocarpa f. stricta

Carex lasiocarpa f. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
