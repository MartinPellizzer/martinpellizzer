Capparis vientianensis

Capparis vientianensis, commonly known as the Vientiane caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a reddish color, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
