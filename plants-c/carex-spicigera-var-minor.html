Carex spicigera var. minor

Carex spicigera var. minor, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that form dense, cylindrical clusters. This variety is typically found in wetland or moist soil environments, such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
