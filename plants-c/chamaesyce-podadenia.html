Chamaesyce podadenia

Chamaesyce podadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Chamaesyce podadenia is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
