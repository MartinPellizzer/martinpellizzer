Cynanchum clavidens

Cynanchum clavidens, commonly known as the spiny milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweed species. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green and clustered in umbels, producing a milky sap that contains toxic compounds.

Cynanchum clavidens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting specific pollinators.
