Cystorchis aberrans

Cystorchis aberrans, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, showy red or pink flower. The plant has a short lifespan, typically flowering and setting seed within a few months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid forest floor.

Cystorchis aberrans is often found in shaded, moist environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
