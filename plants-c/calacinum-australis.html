Calacinum australis

Calacinum australis, commonly known as the southern calacin, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Calacinum australis thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in coastal and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
