Callitris macrostachya

Callitris macrostachya, commonly known as the Queensland cypress, is a species of conifer native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, scale-like foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The Queensland cypress is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
