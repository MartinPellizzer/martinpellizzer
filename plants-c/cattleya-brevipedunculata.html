Cattleya brevipedunculata

Cattleya brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces large, showy flowers with vibrant colors, often in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features fleshy, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a short peduncle bearing a few to several flowers, which bloom during the dry season.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
