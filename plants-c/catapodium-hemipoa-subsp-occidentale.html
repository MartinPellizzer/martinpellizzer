Catapodium hemipoa subsp. occidentale

Catapodium hemipoa subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western catapodium, is a grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
