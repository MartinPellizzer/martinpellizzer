Chamaeraphis italica var. aparine

Chamaeraphis italica var. aparine, commonly known as Italian sandspurry, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, hooked hairs, which aid in seed dispersal. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
