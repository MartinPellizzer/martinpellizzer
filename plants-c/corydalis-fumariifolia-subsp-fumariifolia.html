Corydalis fumariifolia subsp. fumariifolia

Corydalis fumariifolia subsp.

fumariifolia, commonly known as the flame-colored lousewort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade. Corydalis fumariifolia subsp.

fumariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in rockeries.
