Calamintha clinopodium var. chinensis

Calamintha clinopodium var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese calamint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are aromatic, slightly hairy, and have a mint-like scent, which is characteristic of the genus Calamintha.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
