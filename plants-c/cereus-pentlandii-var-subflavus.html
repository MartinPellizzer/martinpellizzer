Cereus pentlandii var. subflavus

Cereus pentlandii var. subflavus, commonly known as the golden night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, white flowers that open at night and emit a fragrant scent. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is particularly noted for its rapid growth and ability to bloom prolifically under suitable conditions.
