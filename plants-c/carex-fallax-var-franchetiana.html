Carex fallax var. franchetiana

Carex fallax var. franchetiana, commonly known as the Franchet's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
