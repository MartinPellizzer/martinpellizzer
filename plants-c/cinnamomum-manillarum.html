Cinnamomum manillarum

Cinnamomum manillarum, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
