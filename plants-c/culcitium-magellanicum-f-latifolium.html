Culcitium magellanicum f. latifolium

Culcitium magellanicum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf form of the Magellanic culcitium, is a variant of a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique foliage and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
