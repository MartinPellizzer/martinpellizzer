Carpobrotus dulcis

Carpobrotus dulcis, commonly known as the sweet fig or ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small figs. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
