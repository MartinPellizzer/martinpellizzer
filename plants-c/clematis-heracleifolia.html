Clematis heracleifolia

Clematis heracleifolia, commonly known as the giant clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces distinctive, woody, spirally twisted stems. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
