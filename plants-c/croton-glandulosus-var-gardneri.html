Croton glandulosus var. gardneri

Croton glandulosus var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
