Curtopogon gracilis

Curtopogon gracilis, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the tips.

Curtopogon gracilis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
