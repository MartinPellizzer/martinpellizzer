Callista palpebrae

Callista palpebrae, commonly known as the eyelid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Callista palpebrae is a perennial species that is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
