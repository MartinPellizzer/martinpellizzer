Cochleanthes guianensis

Cochleanthes guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan cochleanthes, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Cochleanthes guianensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
