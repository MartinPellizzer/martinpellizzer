Caladium pictum

Caladium pictum, commonly known as the painted leaf caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that displays shades of red, pink, and white in intricate patterns. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, making them visually appealing. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
