Crepis froelichiana

Crepis froelichiana, commonly known as the Froelich's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow flowers arranged in a single head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has small, linear leaves and produces seeds in a schizocarp, which split into two parts upon maturity.

Crepis froelichiana is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
