Campanula trachelium var. monstrosa

Campanula trachelium var. monstrosa, commonly known as the Giant Bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or pink throat. The plant features a sturdy stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, supporting clusters of showy blooms. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
