Cirrhopetalum curtisii var. purpureum

Cirrhopetalum curtisii var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered cirrhopetalum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are known for their delicate, intricate structure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
