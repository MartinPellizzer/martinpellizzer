Cyperus pumilus f. patens

Cyperus pumilus f. patens, commonly known as the dwarf sedges, is a compact, low-growing plant with a rosette-like structure.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is often used in garden landscapes for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
