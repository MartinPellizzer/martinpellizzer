Chisocheton longistipitatus

Chisocheton longistipitatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed chisocheton, is a tropical plant belonging to the Clusiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
