Commiphora palmatifoliolata

Commiphora palmatifoliolata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly the savannas of Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble a hand, giving it its scientific name. It produces a fragrant resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.
