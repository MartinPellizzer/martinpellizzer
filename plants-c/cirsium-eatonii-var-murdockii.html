Cirsium eatonii var. murdockii

Cirsium eatonii var. murdockii, commonly known as the Murdock's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
