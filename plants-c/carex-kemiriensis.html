Carex kemiriensis

Carex kemiriensis, commonly known as the Java sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

Carex kemiriensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
