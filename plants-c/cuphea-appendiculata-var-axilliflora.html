Cuphea appendiculata var. axilliflora

Cuphea appendiculata var. axilliflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
