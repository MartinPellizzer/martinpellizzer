Campylocentrum tenellum

Campylocentrum tenellum, commonly known as the dwarf starfruit or small starfruit, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit and foliage.
