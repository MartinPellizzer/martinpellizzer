Carex reichenbachii

Carex reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a valuable groundcover in wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
