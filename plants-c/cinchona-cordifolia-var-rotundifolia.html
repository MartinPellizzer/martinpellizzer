Cinchona cordifolia var. rotundifolia

Cinchona cordifolia var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is cultivated for its bark, which contains quinine, a compound used in medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the production of antimalarial drugs.
