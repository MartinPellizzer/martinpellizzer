Campylocentrum iglesiasii

Campylocentrum iglesiasii, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and thrives in the specific microclimates of its native habitat. It has small, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, pendulous inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
