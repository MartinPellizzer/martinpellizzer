Carex uruguensis var. pseudoechinata

Carex uruguensis var. pseudoechinata, commonly known as the Uruguayan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
