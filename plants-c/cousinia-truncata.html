Cousinia truncata

Cousinia truncata, commonly known as the Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia truncata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
