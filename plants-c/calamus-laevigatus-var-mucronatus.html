Calamus laevigatus var. mucronatus

Calamus laevigatus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the blunt-tipped calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
