Cleome angustifolia var. namaquensis

Cleome angustifolia var. namaquensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
