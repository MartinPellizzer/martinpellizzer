Convolvulus petiolaris

Convolvulus petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to regions in Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
