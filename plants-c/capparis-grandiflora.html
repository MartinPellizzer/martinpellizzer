Capparis grandiflora

Capparis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange center. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Capparis grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
