Cryptocarya guillauminii

Cryptocarya guillauminii, commonly known as the Guillaumin's cryptomeria, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically features simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

Cryptocarya guillauminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
