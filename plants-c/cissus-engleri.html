Cissus engleri

Cissus engleri, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cissus engleri is also valued for its ability to purify air and its ornamental appeal.
