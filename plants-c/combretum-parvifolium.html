Combretum parvifolium

Combretum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are small and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
