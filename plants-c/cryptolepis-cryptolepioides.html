Cryptolepis cryptolepioides

Cryptolepis cryptolepioides, commonly known as African snake root, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
