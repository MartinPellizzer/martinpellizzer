Carex cirrhulosa

Carex cirrhulosa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, usually green or brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and rhizomes, which help it spread and establish in suitable environments.

Carex cirrhulosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
