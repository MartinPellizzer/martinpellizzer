Coussarea auriculata

Coussarea auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces woody, pendulous fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
