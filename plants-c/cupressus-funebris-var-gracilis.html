Cupressus funebris var. gracilis

Cupressus funebris var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender mourning cypress, is a variant of the mourning cypress native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, columnar form and scale-like leaves. The plant has dark, fissured bark and produces small, spherical cones. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species suitable for specific horticultural applications.
