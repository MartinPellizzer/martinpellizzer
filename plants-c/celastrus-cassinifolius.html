Celastrus cassinifolius

Celastrus cassinifolius, commonly known as the Chinese staff vine, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
