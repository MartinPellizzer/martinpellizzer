Cattleya × ruschii

Cattleya × ruschii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya moschata.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant features broad, dark green leaves and produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. It is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Cattleya × ruschii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.
