Capparis viburnifolia

Capparis viburnifolia, commonly known as the sweet thorn or honey thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis viburnifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
