Cyanus ragusinus

Cyanus ragusinus, commonly known as the Tuscan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Cyanus ragusinus is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought-resistant properties.
