Calothamnus homalophyllus var. angustifolius

Calothamnus homalophyllus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed emu bush, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in spikes, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
