Carthamus tinctorius

Carthamus tinctorius, commonly known as safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant produces seeds that are used for oil extraction and as a natural dye. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Safflower is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.
