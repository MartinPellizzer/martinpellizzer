Causonis tenuifolia

Causonis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf causonis, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies such as lakes and rivers. Its botanical features make it an important component of the local wetland ecosystems.

Causonis tenuifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
