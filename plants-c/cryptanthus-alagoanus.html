Cryptanthus alagoanus

Cryptanthus alagoanus, commonly known as the Alagoas carnation or Alagoas bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
