Cydonia maulei var. alpina

Cydonia maulei var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine quince, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruit that is similar in appearance to a quince but has a distinct flavor. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit in local cuisines.
