Conyza kotschyi

Conyza kotschyi, commonly known as desert composite, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant is herbaceous, with slender stems and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, possessing deep taproots and hairy foliage to reduce water loss.

Conyza kotschyi is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some areas.
