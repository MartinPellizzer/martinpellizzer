Campanula clisophylla

Campanula clisophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
