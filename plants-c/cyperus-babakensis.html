Cyperus babakensis

Cyperus babakensis, commonly known as the Babakken sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Cyperus babakensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
