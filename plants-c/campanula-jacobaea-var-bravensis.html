Campanula jacobaea var. bravensis

Campanula jacobaea var. bravensis, commonly known as the Jacob's tea or mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its historical use in traditional medicine has also contributed to its botanical significance.
