Citrus hystrix var. southwickii

Citrus hystrix var. southwickii, commonly known as the sour orange.

This variant is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, while the plant has a shrub-like growth habit. It is notable for its high acidity and is often used in the production of marmalade and other preserved foods.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a hedge or ornamental plant.
