Convolvulus radicans

Convolvulus radicans, commonly known as the trailing bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and disturbed areas.

Convolvulus radicans is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not managed properly.
