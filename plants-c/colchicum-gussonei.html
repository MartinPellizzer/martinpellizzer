Colchicum gussonei

Colchicum gussonei, commonly known as the Sicilian autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It features striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm, which serves as its primary storage organ. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive spurred petal, typical of the Colchicaceae family.

It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
