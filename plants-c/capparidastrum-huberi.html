Capparidastrum huberi

Capparidastrum huberi, commonly known as the African caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
