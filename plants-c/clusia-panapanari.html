Clusia panapanari

Clusia panapanari, commonly known as the Panamanian glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Clusia panapanari is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
