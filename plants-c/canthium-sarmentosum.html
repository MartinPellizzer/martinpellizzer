Canthium sarmentosum

Canthium sarmentosum, commonly known as the climbing canthium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems that twine around supports, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Canthium sarmentosum is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.
