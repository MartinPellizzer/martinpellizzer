Cerastium eriophorum

Cerastium eriophorum, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit, with small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cerastium eriophorum is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is an important component of early successional plant communities.
