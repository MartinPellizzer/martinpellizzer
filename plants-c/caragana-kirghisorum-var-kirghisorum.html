Caragana kirghisorum var. kirghisorum

Caragana kirghisorum var. kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh climates.
