Coleus gallaensis

Coleus gallaensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian coleus, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorled clusters.

Coleus gallaensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.
