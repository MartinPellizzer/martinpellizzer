Clematis chlorantha

Clematis chlorantha, commonly known as the green-flowered clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a vine, with trifoliate leaves and long, slender stems that can climb or trail. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Clematis chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
