Cirsium flaccidum

Cirsium flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium flaccidum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
