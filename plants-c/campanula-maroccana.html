Campanula maroccana

Campanula maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
