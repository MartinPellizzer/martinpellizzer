Cotoneaster allochrous

Cotoneaster allochrous, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster allochrous is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
