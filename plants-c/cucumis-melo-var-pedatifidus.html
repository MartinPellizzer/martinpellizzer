Cucumis melo var. pedatifidus

Cucumis melo var. pedatifidus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its elongated, slender fruit.

The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and typically features lobed leaves that are distinct from the more common cucumbers. It produces a thin, crisp, and slightly sweet fruit with a mild aroma. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and texture, making it a popular choice in specialty markets.
