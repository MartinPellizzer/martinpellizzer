Conforata ageratum

Conforata ageratum, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has feathery, aromatic leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
