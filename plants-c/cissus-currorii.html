Cissus currorii

Cissus currorii, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus currorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
