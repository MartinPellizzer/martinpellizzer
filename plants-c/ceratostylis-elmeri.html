Ceratostylis elmeri

Ceratostylis elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.
