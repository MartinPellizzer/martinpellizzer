Cissus sylvatica

Cissus sylvatica, commonly known as the rock fig or Indian nettle, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus sylvatica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.
