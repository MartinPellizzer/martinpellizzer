Chrysobalanus retusus

Chrysobalanus retusus, commonly known as the roughcheek or yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and medicinal properties. The tree is also noted for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural compounds.
