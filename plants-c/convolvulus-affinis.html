Convolvulus affinis

Convolvulus affinis, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It has a vigorous growth habit and can become invasive in certain environments.

Its climbing or trailing stems allow it to spread along the ground or up structures, making it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
