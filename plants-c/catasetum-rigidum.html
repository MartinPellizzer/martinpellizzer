Catasetum rigidum

Catasetum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its inflorescence is usually a raceme of showy, spurred flowers, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Catasetum rigidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
