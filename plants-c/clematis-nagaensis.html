Clematis nagaensis

Clematis nagaensis, commonly known as the Naga clematis, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a heart-shaped base.

Clematis nagaensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
