Chironia lutea

Chironia lutea, commonly known as the yellow chironia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chironia lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
