Chrysocoma opposita

Chrysocoma opposita, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has small, oval leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
