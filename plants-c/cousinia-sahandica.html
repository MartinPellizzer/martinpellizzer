Cousinia sahandica

Cousinia sahandica, commonly known as the Sahand mountain carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia sahandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 2,000 meters.
