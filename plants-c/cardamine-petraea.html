Cardamine petraea

Cardamine petraea, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a small perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky slopes and forested areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette and produces yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, and the plant is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cardamine petraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
