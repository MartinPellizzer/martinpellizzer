Cotoneaster himalayensis

Cotoneaster himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically white or pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cotoneaster himalayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
