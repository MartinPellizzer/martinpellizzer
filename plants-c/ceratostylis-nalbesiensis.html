Ceratostylis nalbesiensis

Ceratostylis nalbesiensis, commonly known as the Nalbese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.
