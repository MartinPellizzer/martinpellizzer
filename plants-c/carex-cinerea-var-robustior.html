Carex cinerea var. robustior

Carex cinerea var. robustior, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
