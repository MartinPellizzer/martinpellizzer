Conyza incana

Conyza incana, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while its fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Conyza incana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
