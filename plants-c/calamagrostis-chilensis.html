Calamagrostis chilensis

Calamagrostis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and coastal areas.

Calamagrostis chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
