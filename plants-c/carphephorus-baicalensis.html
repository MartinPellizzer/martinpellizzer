Carphephorus baicalensis

Carphephorus baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical sedge, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves, typically growing in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It is often found in the temperate regions of China, Korea, and Russia.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
