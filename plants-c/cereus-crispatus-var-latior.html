Cereus crispatus var. latior

Cereus crispatus var. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night, a common trait among many cacti. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, which helps it conserve moisture.

Overall, it is a hardy, low-maintenance plant well-suited for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
