Cyperus blepharoleptos

Cyperus blepharoleptos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. This plant is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. Cyperus blepharoleptos has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in various aquatic environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
