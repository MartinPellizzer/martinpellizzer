Cactus nivosus

Cactus nivosus, commonly known as the snow cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Overall, Cactus nivosus exemplifies the adaptations of succulents to extreme climates.
