Conopodium bolanderi

Conopodium bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive bluish-green color.
