Cereus aragonii var. palmatus

Cereus aragonii var. palmatus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a unique hybrid plant that combines the vibrant red color of the Gymnocalycium mihantha with the green, leaf-like structure of the Cereus aragonii.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking white flowers at night, a trait typical of many cacti adapted to desert conditions. Its slow growth rate and distinctive appearance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it requires minimal care, making it an ideal plant for beginners.
