Cirsium congestum subsp. afghanicum

Cirsium congestum subsp.

afghanicum, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical heads.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
