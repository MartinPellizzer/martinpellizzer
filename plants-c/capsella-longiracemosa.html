Capsella longiracemosa

Capsella longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, elongated inflorescences composed of small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a minor role in ecological systems as a source of nectar for pollinators.
