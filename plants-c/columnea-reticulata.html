Columnea reticulata

Columnea reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined flame flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green, with prominent veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Columnea reticulata is a member of the Gesneriaceae family, known for its diverse and showy flowering plants.
