Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. iridis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

iridis, commonly known as the iridescent goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow to golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with silver-green foliage that reflects light, giving it a shimmering appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
