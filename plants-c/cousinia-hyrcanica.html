Cousinia hyrcanica

Cousinia hyrcanica, commonly known as the Hyrcanian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia hyrcanica is adapted to arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.
