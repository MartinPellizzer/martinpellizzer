Cuphea ligustrina var. chamaedendrum

Cuphea ligustrina var. chamaedendrum, commonly known as the red flame cuphea, is a compact, bushy shrub native to Mexico.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a nice contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce continuous blooms from summer through fall.
