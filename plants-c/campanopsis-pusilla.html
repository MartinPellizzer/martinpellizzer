Campanopsis pusilla

Campanopsis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Campanopsis pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
