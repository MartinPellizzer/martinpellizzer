Cynanchum cabulicum

Cynanchum cabulicum, commonly known as Afghan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans.

Cynanchum cabulicum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
