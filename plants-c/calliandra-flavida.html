Calliandra flavida

Calliandra flavida, commonly known as the yellow calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Calliandra flavida is drought-tolerant and suitable for use in xeriscaping and erosion control.
