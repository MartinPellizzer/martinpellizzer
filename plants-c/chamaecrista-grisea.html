Chamaecrista grisea

Chamaecrista grisea, commonly known as the gray cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of swamps. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Chamaecrista grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
