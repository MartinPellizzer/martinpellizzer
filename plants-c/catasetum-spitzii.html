Catasetum spitzii

Catasetum spitzii, commonly known as the Spitz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a unique coloration pattern, typically with a contrasting lip. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, such as bees and wasps, due to their specialized structure.

The botanical features of Catasetum spitzii make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
