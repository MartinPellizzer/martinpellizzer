Centaurea pullata var. excelsior

Centaurea pullata var. excelsior, commonly known as the Alpine centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found at high altitudes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or purple flower heads that are composed of numerous small florets. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.

Centaurea pullata var. excelsior is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in traditional remedies.
