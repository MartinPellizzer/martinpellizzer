Cnicus montanus

Cnicus montanus, commonly known as mountain sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a wild edible plant.
