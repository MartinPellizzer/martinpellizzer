Cyathea alpicola

Cyathea alpicola, commonly known as the alpine tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in montane and subalpine regions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its growth in rocky or forested areas.

Cyathea alpicola plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various invertebrates and birds.
