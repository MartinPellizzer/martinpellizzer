Carduus acanthoides subsp. acanthoides

Carduus acanthoides subsp.

acanthoides, commonly known as the acanthoid thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, though it is generally considered a weed in agricultural settings.
