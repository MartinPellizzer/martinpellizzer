Cerasus mollis

Cerasus mollis, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or Prunus serrulata, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of pink or white. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cerasus mollis is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
