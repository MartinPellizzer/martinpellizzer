Claytonia parviflora var. utahensis

Claytonia parviflora var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical structure is adapted to survive in high-altitude environments with cold, short growing seasons.
