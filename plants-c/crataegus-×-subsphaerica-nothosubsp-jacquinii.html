Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp.

jacquinii, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the genus Crataegus, which includes various species of hawthorns characterized by their thorny branches and showy flowers.

The plant typically features white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.
