Chaetogastra origanoides

Chaetogastra origanoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, tubular flowers that are violet to blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
