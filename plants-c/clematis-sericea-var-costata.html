Clematis sericea var. costata

Clematis sericea var. costata, commonly known as the silky clematis or the wavy-leaved clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and heart-shaped, wavy-edged leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
