Carex fontanesiana

Carex fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex fontanesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
