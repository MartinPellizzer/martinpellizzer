Carex vulpinoidea var. xanthocarpa

Carex vulpinoidea var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish to reddish-brown berries, which are typically wind-dispersed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
