Cyathea decrescens

Cyathea decrescens, commonly known as the decrescens tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizome system supports the development of new shoots, contributing to its ability to form dense stands in humid, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the growing season.
