Calophyllum pulcherrimum var. obtusum

Calophyllum pulcherrimum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf calophyllum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense canopy of elliptical leaves. The leaves are typically glossy and have a slightly waxy texture, with a broad, rounded tip. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its potential in traditional medicine.
