Chlamysperma arenaroides

Chlamysperma arenaroides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments with its thick leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or desert areas.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species well-suited to extreme climates.
