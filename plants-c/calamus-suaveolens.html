Calamus suaveolens

Calamus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet-scented arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing herb characterized by its long, flexible stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
