Crepidium lilacinum

Crepidium lilacinum, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Crepidium lilacinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
