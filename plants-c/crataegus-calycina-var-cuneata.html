Crataegus calycina var. cuneata

Crataegus calycina var. cuneata, commonly known as the cuneate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorned branches and oval to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its leaves are often described as having a cuneate base, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
