Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. rhodopeus

Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope rosehip bush, is a shrub native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and bright orange to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used traditionally in herbal remedies. Its foliage is evergreen, with leathery, dark green leaves that provide year-round visual interest.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
