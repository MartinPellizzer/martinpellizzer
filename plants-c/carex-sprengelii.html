Carex sprengelii

Carex sprengelii, commonly known as Sprengel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in early summer. Carex sprengelii prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
