Ceanothus oblanceolatus

Ceanothus oblanceolatus, commonly known as the coast ceanothus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to coastal climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
