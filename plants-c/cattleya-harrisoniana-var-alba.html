Cattleya harrisoniana var. alba

Cattleya harrisoniana var. alba, commonly known as the white harrison cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a subtle pinkish or greenish tinge. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its elegance and ease of cultivation.
