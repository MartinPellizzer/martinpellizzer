Cornus ignorata

Cornus ignorata, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more prominent in winter. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Cornus ignorata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
