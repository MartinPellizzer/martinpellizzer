Cyperus reflexus f. anterna

Cyperus reflexus f. anterna, commonly known as the silver rush, is a variant of the silvergrass plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
