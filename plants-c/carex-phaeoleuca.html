Carex phaeoleuca

Carex phaeoleuca, commonly known as the pale-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are usually pale green to bluish-gray, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex phaeoleuca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
