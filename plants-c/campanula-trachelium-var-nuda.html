Campanula trachelium var. nuda

Campanula trachelium var. nuda, commonly known as the naked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
