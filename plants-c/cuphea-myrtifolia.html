Cuphea myrtifolia

Cuphea myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea myrtifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
