Crataegus × pausiaca

Crataegus × pausiaca, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities. Crataegus × pausiaca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
