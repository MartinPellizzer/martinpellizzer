Chamaecrista dalbergiifolia

Chamaecrista dalbergiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
