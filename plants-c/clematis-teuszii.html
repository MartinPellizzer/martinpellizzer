Clematis teuszii

Clematis teuszii, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis teuszii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
