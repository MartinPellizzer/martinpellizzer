Chenopodium ficifolium

Chenopodium ficifolium, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are often reddish beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright orange, fleshy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Chenopodium ficifolium is known for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
