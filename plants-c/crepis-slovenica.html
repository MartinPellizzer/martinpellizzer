Crepis slovenica

Crepis slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, dandelion-like yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Crepis slovenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
