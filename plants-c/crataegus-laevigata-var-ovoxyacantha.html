Crataegus laevigata var. ovoxyacantha

Crataegus laevigata var. ovoxyacantha, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy on top and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting diverse insect life.
