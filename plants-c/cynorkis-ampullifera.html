Cynorkis ampullifera

Cynorkis ampullifera, commonly known as the bottle-shaped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
