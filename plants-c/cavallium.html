Cavallium

Cavallium, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Cavallium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
