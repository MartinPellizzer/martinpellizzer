Coprosma divaricata

Coprosma divaricata, commonly known as the variable coprosma, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a glossy appearance. The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, and they can vary in color from dark green to reddish-brown depending on the variety. It produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
