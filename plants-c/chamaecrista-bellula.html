Chamaecrista bellula

Chamaecrista bellula, commonly known as the small cowpea or dwarf poinsettia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive light green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
