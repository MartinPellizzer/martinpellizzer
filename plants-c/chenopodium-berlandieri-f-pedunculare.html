Chenopodium berlandieri f. pedunculare

Chenopodium berlandieri f. pedunculare, commonly known as prairie goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
