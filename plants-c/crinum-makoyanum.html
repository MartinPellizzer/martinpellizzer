Crinum makoyanum

Crinum makoyanum, commonly known as the Makoy's crinum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its bulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Crinum makoyanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
