Cyanus pichleri subsp. extrarosularis

Cyanus pichleri subsp.

extrarosularis, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, blue to purple composite flowers and hairy, glandular leaves adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
