Cheirodendron gaudichaudii

Cheirodendron gaudichaudii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood in some regions.
