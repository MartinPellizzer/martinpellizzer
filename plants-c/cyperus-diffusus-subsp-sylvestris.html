Cyperus diffusus subsp. sylvestris

Cyperus diffusus subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense clusters. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated seeds. Cyperus diffusus subsp.

sylvestris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
