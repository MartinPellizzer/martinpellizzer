Cyperus oliganthus

Cyperus oliganthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in three rows, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus oliganthus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
