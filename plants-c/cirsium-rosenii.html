Cirsium rosenii

Cirsium rosenii, commonly known as the Rosen's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Cirsium rosenii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to thrive in cold, high-altitude climates.
