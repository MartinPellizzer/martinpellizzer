Cerasus × hisauchiana

Cerasus × hisauchiana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamazakura, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly for its early spring blossoms that appear before the leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals, and are followed by small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer.

Cerasus × hisauchiana is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
