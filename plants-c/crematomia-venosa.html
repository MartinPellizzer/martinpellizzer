Crematomia venosa

Crematomia venosa, commonly known as the veiny cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and ribbed stems that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing both protection and reducing transpiration.

Crematomia venosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.
