Cirsium baytopae

Cirsium baytopae, commonly known as the Baytop's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium baytopae is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
