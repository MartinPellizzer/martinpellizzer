Chassalia subochreata

Chassalia subochreata, commonly known as the golden chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chassalia subochreata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
