Castanea crenata var. elongata

Castanea crenata var. elongata, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut.

This variant is a subspecies of the chestnut tree native to Japan, characterized by its elongated nuts and hairy foliage. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate climates, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its nuts and its aesthetic appeal.
