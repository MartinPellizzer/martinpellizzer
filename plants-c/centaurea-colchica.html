Centaurea colchica

Centaurea colchica, commonly known as the Colchic Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and bristly leaves, which are adapted to thrive in rocky, arid environments. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Centaurea colchica is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its use in herbal remedies.

Its ecological significance is further highlighted by its ability to stabilize soils in its native habitat.
