Calophyllum blumutense

Calophyllum blumutense, commonly known as the Blumutense Mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
