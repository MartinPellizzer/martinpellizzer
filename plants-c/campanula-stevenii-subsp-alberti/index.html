Campanula Stevenii Subsp. Alberti

Campanula stevenii subsp. alberti is a rare and striking plant native to the alpine regions of the Alps. Known for its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. This subspecies is often found in rocky crevices and mountain meadows, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its delicate appearance makes it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike. Due to its limited habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring careful conservation efforts.
