Canarium rugosum

Canarium rugosum, commonly known as the rough-leafed candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. The oil extracted from its seeds is used in traditional medicine and as a source of biodiesel.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
