Carex wallichiana f. glabrescens

Carex wallichiana f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a variant of the common sedge species native to the Himalayas and parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream sides. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
