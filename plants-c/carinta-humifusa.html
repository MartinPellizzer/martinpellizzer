Carinta humifusa

Carinta humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woods, fields, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carinta humifusa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
