Cupania dissitiflora

Cupania dissitiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cupania, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Cupania dissitiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowers.
