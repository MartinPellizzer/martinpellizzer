Cnicus arizonicus

Cnicus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cnicus arizonicus is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
