Chamaecrista cruziana

Chamaecrista cruziana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, dark seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
