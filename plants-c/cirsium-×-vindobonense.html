Cirsium × vindobonense

Cirsium × vindobonense, commonly known as the Vienna thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. This perennial plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its hybrid origin results from crosses between different species of thistles, contributing to its adaptability.

The plant is often considered invasive in certain regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
