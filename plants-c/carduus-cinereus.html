Carduus cinereus

Carduus cinereus, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-green, spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its latex-like sap contains compounds that can cause skin irritation.

The gray thistle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
