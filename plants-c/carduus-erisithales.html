Carduus erisithales

Carduus erisithales, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Botanically, it is notable for its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
