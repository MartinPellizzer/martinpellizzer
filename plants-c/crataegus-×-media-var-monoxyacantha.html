Crataegus × media var. monoxyacantha

Crataegus × media var. monoxyacantha, commonly known as the single-seed hawthorn, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically one-seeded, distinguishing it from other hawthorn varieties. It is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
