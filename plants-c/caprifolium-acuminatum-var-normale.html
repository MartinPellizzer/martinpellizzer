Caprifolium acuminatum var. normale

Caprifolium acuminatum var. normale, commonly known as the common honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The species is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
