Celosia pleiogyna

Celosia pleiogyna, commonly known as the flame flower or red dwarf celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia pleiogyna is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
