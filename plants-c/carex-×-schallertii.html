Carex × schallertii

Carex × schallertii, commonly known as the Schallert's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist to wet conditions, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens and bog landscapes.

Carex × schallertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
