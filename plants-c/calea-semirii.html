Calea semirii

Calea semirii, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive shape, while the stem is often branched and upright.

Calea semirii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
