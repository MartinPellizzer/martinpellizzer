Campanula bellardii var. hoppeana

Campanula bellardii var. hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
