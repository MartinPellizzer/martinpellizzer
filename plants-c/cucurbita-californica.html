Cucurbita californica

Cucurbita californica, commonly known as the California pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, with the fruit typically being a round or oval pumpkin. Its vines can grow up to 10 feet long, and the fruit can weigh several pounds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pumpkin varieties.
