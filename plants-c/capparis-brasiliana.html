Capparis brasiliana

Capparis brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian caper or Brazilian caper bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, green berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and dry forests.

Capparis brasiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
