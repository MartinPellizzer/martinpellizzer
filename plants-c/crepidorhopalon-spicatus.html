Crepidorhopalon spicatus

Crepidorhopalon spicatus, commonly known as the spiky leaf fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and often hairy leaf surfaces, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually elongated and can form dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is noted for its specialized reproductive structures and adaptability to humid conditions.
