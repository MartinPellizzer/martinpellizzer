Cassia frondosa var. pubescens

Cassia frondosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a soft, hairy texture on both sides. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
