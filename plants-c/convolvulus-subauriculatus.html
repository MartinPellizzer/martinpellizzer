Convolvulus subauriculatus

Convolvulus subauriculatus, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Convolvulus subauriculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, long-lasting blooms.
