Coleus affinis

Coleus affinis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with compact foliage that often features striking color patterns. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Coleus affinis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
