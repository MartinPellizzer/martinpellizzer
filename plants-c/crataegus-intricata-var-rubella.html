Crataegus intricata var. rubella

Crataegus intricata var. rubella, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and paler underneath. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that persist into winter. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
