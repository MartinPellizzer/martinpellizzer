Cinna pendula var. bolanderi

Cinna pendula var. bolanderi, commonly known as the Bolander's weeping reed, is a rare variant of the weeping reed native to California.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, cascading appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often tinged with silver or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique form and is often cultivated in moist, sunny gardens.
