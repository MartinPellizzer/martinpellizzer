Canavalia puberula

Canavalia puberula, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

Canavalia puberula is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
