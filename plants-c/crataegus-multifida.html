Crataegus multifida

Crataegus multifida, commonly known as the five-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a hand with five fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Crataegus multifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
