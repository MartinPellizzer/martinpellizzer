Crepis achyrophoroides

Crepis achyrophoroides, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, compact rosette of leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces numerous small achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
