Casearia fasciculata

Casearia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
