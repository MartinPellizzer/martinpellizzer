Connarus celatus

Connarus celatus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Connarus celatus is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
