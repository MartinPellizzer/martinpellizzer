Commelina alpestris

Commelina alpestris, commonly known as the alpine spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Commelina alpestris is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
