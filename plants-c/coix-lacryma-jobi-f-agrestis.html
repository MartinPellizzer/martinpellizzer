Coix lacryma-jobi f. agrestis

Coix lacryma-jobi f. agrestis, commonly known as the wild millet or Chinese sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
