Clarionea fonki

Clarionea fonki, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Clarionea fonki is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
