Chloranthus denticulatus

Chloranthus denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have finely toothed margins.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
