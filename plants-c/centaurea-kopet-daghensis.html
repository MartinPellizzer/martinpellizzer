Centaurea kopet-daghensis

Centaurea kopet-daghensis, commonly known as Kopet-Dag centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Kopet-Dag mountain range in Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces vibrant blue to purple flowers.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
