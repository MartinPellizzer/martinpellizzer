Centaurea calcitrapa subsp. angusticeps

Centaurea calcitrapa subsp.

angusticeps, commonly known as the narrow-headed centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, with five petals and prominent central stamens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
