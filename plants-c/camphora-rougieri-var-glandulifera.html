Camphora rougieri var. glandulifera

Camphora rougieri var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular camphor tree, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.
