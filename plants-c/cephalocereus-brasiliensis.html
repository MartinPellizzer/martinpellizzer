Cephalocereus brasiliensis

Cephalocereus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brasiliensis cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and indoor environments.
