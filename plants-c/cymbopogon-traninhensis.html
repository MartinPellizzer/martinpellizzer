Cymbopogon traninhensis

Cymbopogon traninhensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese citron grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic leaves. The plant is known for its essential oils, which are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes spreading through rhizomes, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Cymbopogon traninhensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in tropical climates.
