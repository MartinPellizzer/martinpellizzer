Cestrum anagyris

Cestrum anagyris, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are followed by small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Cestrum anagyris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its fragrant blooms.
