Cassia rigidifolia var. veadeirana

Cassia rigidifolia var. veadeirana, commonly known as the Veadeirana cassia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing flat, oblong seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in warmer climates.
