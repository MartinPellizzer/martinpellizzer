Carex transversa

Carex transversa, commonly known as the transverse sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like stems and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Carex transversa is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
