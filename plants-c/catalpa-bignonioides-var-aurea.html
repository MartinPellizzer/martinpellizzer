Catalpa bignonioides var. aurea

Catalpa bignonioides var. aurea, commonly known as the golden catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces long, drooping clusters of white, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
