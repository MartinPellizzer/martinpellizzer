Caroxylon divaricatum

Caroxylon divaricatum, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging branches that give it a unique appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stems. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds.

Caroxylon divaricatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
