Carduus pycnocephalus-alatus

Carduus pycnocephalus-alatus, commonly known as the woolly cardue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, purple-tinged composite flower heads. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name, and its leaves are typically lobed with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its strong, bitter taste and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
