Crotalaria recta var. katangensis

Crotalaria recta var. katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base of the petals, which is a distinguishing feature. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
