Centaurea arguta

Centaurea arguta, commonly known as the silky centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow central disks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Centaurea arguta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
