Corydalis pseudocava

Corydalis pseudocava, commonly known as the narrowleaf Corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Corydalis pseudocava is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
