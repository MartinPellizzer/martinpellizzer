Callopisma cordifolium

Callopisma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved callopisma, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
