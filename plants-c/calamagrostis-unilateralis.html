Calamagrostis unilateralis

Calamagrostis unilateralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet to moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Calamagrostis unilateralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
