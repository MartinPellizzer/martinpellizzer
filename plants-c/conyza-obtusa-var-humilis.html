Conyza obtusa var. humilis

Conyza obtusa var. humilis, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza obtusa var. humilis is known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds, which are easily dispersed by wind.
