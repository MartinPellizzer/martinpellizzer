Conandron ramondioides f. pilosus

Conandron ramondioides f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved conandron, is a variant of the conandron plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Ericaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
