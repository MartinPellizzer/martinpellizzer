Cestrum colombianum

Cestrum colombianum, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
