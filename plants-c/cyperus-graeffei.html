Cyperus graeffei

Cyperus graeffei, commonly known as the grasspaddles, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Cyperus graeffei is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
