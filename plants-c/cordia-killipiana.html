Cordia killipiana

Cordia killipiana, commonly known as the Florida rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cordia killipiana is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
