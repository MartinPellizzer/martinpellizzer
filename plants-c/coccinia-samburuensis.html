Coccinia samburuensis

Coccinia samburuensis, commonly known as the Samburu cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils.

It produces small, green, cucumber-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional diets. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and typically unisexual.

Coccinia samburuensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in some African communities.
