Caladium bicolor var. curwadlii

Caladium bicolor var. curwadlii, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it popular for ornamental gardening.

It is typically grown as an annual in cooler regions but can be perennial in USDA hardiness zones 8-10.
