Chrysopogon glabratus

Chrysopogon glabratus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Chrysopogon glabratus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
