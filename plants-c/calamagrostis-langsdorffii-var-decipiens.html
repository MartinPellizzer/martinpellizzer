Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. decipiens

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive reed, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage, contributing to its deceptive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
