Carex dueensis var. rostrata

Carex dueensis var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
