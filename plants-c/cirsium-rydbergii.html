Cirsium rydbergii

Cirsium rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are lance-shaped with stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a compact, spherical cluster.

Cirsium rydbergii is often found in open areas with well-drained soil and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
