Cleome laevigata

Cleome laevigata, commonly known as the smooth cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal racemes, blooming from late summer to early fall. Its flowers are typically hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

Cleome laevigata is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
