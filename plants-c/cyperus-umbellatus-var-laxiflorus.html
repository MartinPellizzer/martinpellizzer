Cyperus umbellatus var. laxiflorus

Cyperus umbellatus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, consisting of numerous small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
