Connellia nutans

Connellia nutans, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of bell-shaped, violet or blue-purple flowers that hang downward.

Connellia nutans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
