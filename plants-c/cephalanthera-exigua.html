Cephalanthera exigua

Cephalanthera exigua, commonly known as the small-headed lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a dense, spike-like inflorescence. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a faintly fragrant scent. It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and requires specific soil conditions for successful cultivation.

This species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
