Clerodendrum caeruleum

Clerodendrum caeruleum, commonly known as blue clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic and medicinal plants.
