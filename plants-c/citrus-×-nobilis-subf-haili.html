Citrus × nobilis subf. haili

Citrus × nobilis subf.

haili, commonly known as the Hainan lemon, is a citrus hybrid native to southern China. It is characterized by its small, yellow, and slightly aromatic fruits that are typically used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit has a high acidity level and is often used to make beverages and medicinal tonics. This subsp.

is valued for its unique flavor profile and adaptability to subtropical climates.
