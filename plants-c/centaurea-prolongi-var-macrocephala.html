Centaurea prolongi var. macrocephala

Centaurea prolongi var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, showy inflorescences composed of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight bluish-gray hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
