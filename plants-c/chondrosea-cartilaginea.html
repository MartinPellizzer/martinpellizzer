Chondrosea cartilaginea

Chondrosea cartilaginea, commonly known as the glassy seaweed, is a species of red algae that thrives in marine environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent thallus that often appears glass-like due to its high water content. This plant typically grows in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches to rocks or other substrates. Its structure consists of branching filaments that can form dense mats on the seabed.

Chondrosea cartilaginea plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
