Calpidia ovalifolia

Calpidia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf calpidia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. Calpidia ovalifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
