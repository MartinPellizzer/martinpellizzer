Clinopodium fauriei var. japonicum

Clinopodium fauriei var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese hedgenettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and emit a mint-like fragrance when crushed.

The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to pale purple.
