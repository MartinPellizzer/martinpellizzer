Calliandra cruziana

Calliandra cruziana, commonly known as the pink coral tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
