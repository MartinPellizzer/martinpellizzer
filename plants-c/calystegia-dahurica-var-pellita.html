Calystegia dahurica var. pellita

Calystegia dahurica var. pellita, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of East Asia.

It is a trailing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
