Cyrtochilum mandibulare

Cyrtochilum mandibulare, commonly known as the mandible orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble a jaw or mandible, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Cyrtochilum mandibulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
