Clausena anisum-olens subsp. mollis

Clausena anisum-olens subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a woody structure typical of the Rutaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
