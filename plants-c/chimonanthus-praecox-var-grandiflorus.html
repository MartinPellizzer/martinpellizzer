Chimonanthus praecox var. grandiflorus

Chimonanthus praecox var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
