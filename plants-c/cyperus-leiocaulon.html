Cyperus leiocaulon

Cyperus leiocaulon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface. Cyperus leiocaulon produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
