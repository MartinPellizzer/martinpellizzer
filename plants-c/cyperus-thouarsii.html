Cyperus thouarsii

Cyperus thouarsii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus thouarsii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
