Carex glauca f. algerica

Carex glauca f. algerica, commonly known as Algerian blue sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to northern Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that gives them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
