Carya ovalis var. megacarpa

Carya ovalis var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant hickory, is a variant of the hickory tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, elongated nuts, which can grow up to 5 inches in length. The plant produces alternate, pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. Its flowers are dioecious, with separate male and female plants, and it relies on insect pollination.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in woodworking and construction.
