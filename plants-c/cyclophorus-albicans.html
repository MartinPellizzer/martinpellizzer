Cyclophorus albicans

Cyclophorus albicans, commonly known as the white cyclophorus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
