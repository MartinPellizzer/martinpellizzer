Cardamomum linguiforme

Cardamomum linguiforme, commonly known as the tongue-shaped cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, have a strong, spicy aroma and are often utilized in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
