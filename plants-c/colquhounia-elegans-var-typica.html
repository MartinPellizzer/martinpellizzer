Colquhounia elegans var. typica

Colquhounia elegans var. typica, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features finely divided, silvery-white leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, often used in horticultural displays. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
