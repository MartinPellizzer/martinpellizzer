Carex xanthophysa var. minor

Carex xanthophysa var. minor, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish inflorescences that are typically arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
