Crepidium rheedei subsp. rheedei

Crepidium rheedei subsp.

rheedei, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, rosette-like growth habit, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
