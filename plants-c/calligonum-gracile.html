Calligonum gracile

Calligonum gracile, commonly known as the slender calligonum, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions. Its growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and habitat restoration in degraded landscapes.

Calligonum gracile is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
