Cassia chrysocarpa var. psilocarpa

Cassia chrysocarpa var. psilocarpa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of this species are encased in a pod and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
