Crocus almehensis

Crocus almehensis, commonly known as the Almería crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in arid environments, and it blooms in early spring.

The Almería crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
