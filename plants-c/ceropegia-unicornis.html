Ceropegia unicornis

Ceropegia unicornis, commonly known as the Whale's Tail Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features long, trailing stems with tubular, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, intricate structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is popular as a houseplant and in rock gardens.
