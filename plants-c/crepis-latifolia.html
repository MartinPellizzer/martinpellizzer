Crepis latifolia

Crepis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has basal leaves that are broad and spoon-shaped. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster, and it produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Crepis latifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
