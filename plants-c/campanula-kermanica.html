Campanula kermanica

Campanula kermanica, commonly known as the Kerman bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula kermanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
