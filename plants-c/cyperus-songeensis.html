Cyperus songeensis

Cyperus songeensis, commonly known as the Songe grass, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or purplish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus songeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
