Cyperus megapotamicus var. jaeggii

Cyperus megapotamicus var. jaeggii, commonly known as the Jaegg's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in marshy areas and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
