Combretum albidum var. cooperi

Combretum albidum var. cooperi, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to withstand drought conditions.
