Clidemia paraguayensis

Clidemia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay waxplant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests and along stream banks.
