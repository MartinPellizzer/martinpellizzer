Cyclanthera eremocarpa var. subintegrifolia

Cyclanthera eremocarpa var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical structure includes a robust stem with tendrils for climbing support.

The varietal name subintegrifolia refers to its nearly entire leaf margins, which are slightly toothed.
