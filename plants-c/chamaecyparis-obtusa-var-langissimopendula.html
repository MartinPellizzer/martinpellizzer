Chamaecyparis obtusa var. langissimopendula

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. langissimopendula, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Hinoki cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cypress characterized by its long, pendulous branches.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The foliage is dense and dark green, with a fine, scale-like texture that gives it a soft appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is known for its graceful form and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
