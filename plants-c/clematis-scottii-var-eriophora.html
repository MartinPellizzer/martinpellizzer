Clematis scottii var. eriophora

Clematis scottii var. eriophora, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability in garden settings.
