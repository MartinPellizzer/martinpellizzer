Craspedia incana

Craspedia incana, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Craspedia incana is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various health purposes.
