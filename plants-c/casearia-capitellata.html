Casearia capitellata

Casearia capitellata, commonly known as the clustered casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
