Cornus intermedia

Cornus intermedia, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become prominent in winter. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

Cornus intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
