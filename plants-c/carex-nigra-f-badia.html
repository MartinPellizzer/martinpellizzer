Carex nigra f. badia

Carex nigra f. badia, commonly known as the black sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America. The plant features dark green, narrow leaves that are often tinged with purple, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other areas with consistent moisture.

The botanical characteristics of Carex nigra f. badia make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
