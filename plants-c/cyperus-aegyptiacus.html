Cyperus aegyptiacus

Cyperus aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread easily and form dense colonies.

Cyperus aegyptiacus is often used in ornamental gardening and can also serve as a natural water filter in aquatic ecosystems.
