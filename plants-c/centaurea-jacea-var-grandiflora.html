Centaurea jacea var. grandiflora

Centaurea jacea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are gray-green in color, and its flower heads are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a conical shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
