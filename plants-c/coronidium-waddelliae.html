Coronidium waddelliae

Coronidium waddelliae, commonly known as the Waddell's crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called cypsela. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ecological resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
