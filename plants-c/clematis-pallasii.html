Clematis pallasii

Clematis pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its climbing habit requires support structures, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Clematis pallasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
