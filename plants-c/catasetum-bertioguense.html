Catasetum bertioguense

Catasetum bertioguense, commonly known as the Bertioga orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is prized by horticulturists for its beauty and unique botanical features.
