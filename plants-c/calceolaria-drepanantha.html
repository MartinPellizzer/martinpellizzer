Calceolaria drepanantha

Calceolaria drepanantha, commonly known as the hookleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Calceolaria drepanantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
