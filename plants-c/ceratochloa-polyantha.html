Ceratochloa polyantha

Ceratochloa polyantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become invasive in certain environments.
