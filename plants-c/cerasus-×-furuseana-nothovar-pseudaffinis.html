Cerasus × furuseana nothovar. pseudaffinis

Cerasus × furuseana nothovar. pseudaffinis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface. Its fruit is a small, yellowish plum that ripens in summer, though it is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This hybrid exhibits variability in flower color and leaf shape, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
