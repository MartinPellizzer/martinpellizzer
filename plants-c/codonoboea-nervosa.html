Codonoboea nervosa

Codonoboea nervosa, commonly known as the veined codonopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
