Convolvulus hystrix subsp. hystrix

Convolvulus hystrix subsp.

hystrix, commonly known as the prickly bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually covered with fine, stiff hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with five petals and a prominent, spurred corolla.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
