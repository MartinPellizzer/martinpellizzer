Carex onusta

Carex onusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Carex onusta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
