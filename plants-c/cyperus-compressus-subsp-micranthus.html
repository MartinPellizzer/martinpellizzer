Cyperus compressus subsp. micranthus

Cyperus compressus subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
