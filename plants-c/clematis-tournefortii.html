Clematis tournefortii

Clematis tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil. It is known for its vigorous growth and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters.

Clematis tournefortii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
