Calamintha menthifolia var. baetica

Calamintha menthifolia var. baetica, commonly known as Spanish mint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in herbal remedies for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
