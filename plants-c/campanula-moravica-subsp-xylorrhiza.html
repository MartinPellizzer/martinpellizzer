Campanula moravica subsp. xylorrhiza

Campanula moravica subsp.

xylorrhiza, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to violet in color, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
