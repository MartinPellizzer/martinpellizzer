Cacalia maximowicziana

Cacalia maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cacalia maximowicziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
