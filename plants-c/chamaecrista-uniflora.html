Chamaecrista uniflora

Chamaecrista uniflora, commonly known as the one-flower coral bean, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, raceme-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
