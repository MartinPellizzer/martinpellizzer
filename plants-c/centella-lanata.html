Centella lanata

Centella lanata, commonly known as hairy centella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its distinctive foliage.
