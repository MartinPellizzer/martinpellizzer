Croton laui

Croton laui, commonly known as the Hawaiian croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored foliage, which can range from green to red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, also known as the spurge family, and is known for its milky sap. Croton laui typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its native habitat.
