Clerodendrum tomentosum var. typicum

Clerodendrum tomentosum var. typicum, commonly known as the velvetleaf cleerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or in tropical gardens.
