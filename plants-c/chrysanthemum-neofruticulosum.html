Chrysanthemum neofruticulosum

Chrysanthemum neofruticulosum, commonly known as the fruticose chrysanthemum, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, during the late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
