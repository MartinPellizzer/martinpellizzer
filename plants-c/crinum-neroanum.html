Crinum neroanum

Crinum neroanum, commonly known as the black night lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a dark central stripe. The plant produces strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its tropical appearance. Crinum neroanum is typically grown in USDA hardiness zones 8 through 11, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking dramatic, exotic foliage and blooms.
