Chassalia coriacea var. coriacea

Chassalia coriacea var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough chassalia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems covered in dense, rough, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique texture and vibrant floral display make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
