Crepis intermedia var. gracilis

Crepis intermedia var. gracilis, commonly known as slender intermediate hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
