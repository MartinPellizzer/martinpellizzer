Canarium hirsutum var. majus

Canarium hirsutum var. majus, commonly known as the hairy candlenut.

This variant is characterized by its hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. It is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and fruits that develop in clusters, often used in traditional practices and local economies.
