Crataegus oxyacantha var. hirsuta

Crataegus oxyacantha var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
