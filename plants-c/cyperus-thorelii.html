Cyperus thorelii

Cyperus thorelii, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale yellow to brown flowers, which are borne on slender, spike-like inflorescences. Cyperus thorelii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet or moist conditions.

It is also used in water features and as a ground cover in landscaping.
