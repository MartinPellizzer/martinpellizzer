Casuarina pumila var. hirtella

Casuarina pumila var. hirtella, commonly known as the silky casuarina, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

Casuarina pumila var. hirtella is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
