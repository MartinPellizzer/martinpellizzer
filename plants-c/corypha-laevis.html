Corypha laevis

Corypha laevis, commonly known as the giant panda palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, ornamental palm that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the plant, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Corypha laevis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
