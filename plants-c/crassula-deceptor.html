Crassula deceptor

Crassula deceptor, commonly known as the "Deceiver Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in a compact, rosette form, with leaves that are often colored in shades of green, red, or purple. Its small, star-shaped flowers bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its appearance.

Crassula deceptor is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
