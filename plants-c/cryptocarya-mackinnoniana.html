Cryptocarya mackinnoniana

Cryptocarya mackinnoniana, commonly known as the Mackinnon's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forests, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
