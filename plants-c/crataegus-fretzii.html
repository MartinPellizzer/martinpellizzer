Crataegus fretzii

Crataegus fretzii, commonly known as Fretz's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
