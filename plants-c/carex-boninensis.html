Carex boninensis

Carex boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan and the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex boninensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
