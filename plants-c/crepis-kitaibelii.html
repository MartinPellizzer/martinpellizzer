Crepis kitaibelii

Crepis kitaibelii, commonly known as the Kitaibel's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Crepis kitaibelii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
