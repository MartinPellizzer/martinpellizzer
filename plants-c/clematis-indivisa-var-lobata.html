Clematis indivisa var. lobata

Clematis indivisa var. lobata, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five lobes, often with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often used in landscaping for covering trellises or walls.
