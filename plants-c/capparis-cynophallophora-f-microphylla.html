Capparis cynophallophora f. microphylla

Capparis cynophallophora f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
