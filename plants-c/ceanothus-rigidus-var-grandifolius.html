Ceanothus rigidus var. grandifolius

Ceanothus rigidus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the grandleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicinal practices by indigenous peoples.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
