Cynanchum kaschgaricum

Cynanchum kaschgaricum, commonly known as the Karakorum milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
