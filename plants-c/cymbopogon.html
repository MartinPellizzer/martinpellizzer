Cymbopogon

Cymbopogon, commonly known as citron grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. They are characterized by their tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Many species within this genus are cultivated for their aromatic essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Cymbopogon species also play a role in traditional medicine and are sometimes used as ornamental plants.
