Cereus pugionifer var. quadrangulispinus

Cereus pugionifer var. quadrangulispinus, commonly known as the four-angled cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a distinctive four-angled cross-section. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
