Cyanopis madagascariensis

Cyanopis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan blueberry, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its fruits are edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
