Chaenactis kyhosii

Chaenactis kyhosii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada composite, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in high-altitude regions. It has a low-growing habit with woolly, silver-green leaves that help reduce water loss.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
