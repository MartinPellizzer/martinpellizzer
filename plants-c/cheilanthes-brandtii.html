Cheilanthes brandtii

Cheilanthes brandtii, commonly known as the Brandt's rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows on rocky outcrops and in arid environments. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with finely divided, linear leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. It is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a hardy species.

The fronds are usually spore-bearing and contribute to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.
