Camellia indochinensis var. indochinensis

Camellia indochinensis var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
