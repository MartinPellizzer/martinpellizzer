Carex helferi subsp. mapaniifolia

Carex helferi subsp.

mapaniifolia, commonly known as the Mapaniifolia sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
