Carex saxatilis subsp. grahamii

Carex saxatilis subsp.

grahamii, commonly known as Graham's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
