Canavalia wurdackii

Canavalia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Canavalia wurdackii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
