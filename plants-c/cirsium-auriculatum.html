Cirsium auriculatum

Cirsium auriculatum, commonly known as the golden star thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, violet to blue-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are typically yellow or white.

Cirsium auriculatum is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
