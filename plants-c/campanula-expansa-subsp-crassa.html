Campanula expansa subsp. crassa

Campanula expansa subsp.

crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
