Chusquea parviflora

Chusquea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered dwarf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a clump-forming plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features slender, erect culms with small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall bamboo-like appearance.

Chusquea parviflora is valued for its ornamental potential and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic and ecological benefits.
