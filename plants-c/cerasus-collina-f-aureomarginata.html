Cerasus collina f. aureomarginata

Cerasus collina f. aureomarginata, commonly known as the golden-margin cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus species.

It is characterized by its striking pink to white flowers with a distinctive golden margin on the petal edges. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a dark green color, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal.
