Ceratostylis ficinioides

Ceratostylis ficinioides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms that bloom throughout the year.

Ceratostylis ficinioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
