Cyperus ubertus

Cyperus ubertus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and flowering structures.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in its native habitat.

Cyperus ubertus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
