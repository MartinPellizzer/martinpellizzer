Camellia theifera

Camellia theifera, commonly known as the thief plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and well-drained soil. It produces small, red berries that contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

Camellia theifera is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
