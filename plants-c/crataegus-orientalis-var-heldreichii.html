Crataegus orientalis var. heldreichii

Crataegus orientalis var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, rocky slopes, and scrublands.
