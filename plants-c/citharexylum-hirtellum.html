Citharexylum hirtellum

Citharexylum hirtellum, commonly known as the woolly cithara or hairy cithara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
