Calceolaria ipecacuanha

Calceolaria ipecacuanha, commonly known as the "Indian snakeroot," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers come in shades of purple and white.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties.
