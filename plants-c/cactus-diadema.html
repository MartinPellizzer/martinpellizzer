Cactus diadema

Cactus diadema, commonly known as the star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture from light rains.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xerophytic landscapes.
