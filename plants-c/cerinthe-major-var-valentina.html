Cerinthe major var. valentina

Cerinthe major var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically a vibrant shade of blue or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its blooming period extends from late spring to early autumn, adding a long-lasting display of color.
