Commiphora kua

Commiphora kua, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The tree produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in traditional medicine.
