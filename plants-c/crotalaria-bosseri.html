Crotalaria bosseri

Crotalaria bosseri, commonly known as desert crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Crotalaria genus. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, featuring small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Crotalaria bosseri is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
