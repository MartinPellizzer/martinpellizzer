Carex queretarensis

Carex queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to brown in color.

Carex queretarensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
