Ceratopetalum apetalum f. montanum

Ceratopetalum apetalum f. montanum, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and unique floral structures that are typically reduced in size. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
