Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. albiforme

Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. albiforme, commonly known as the white-veined goosefoot, is a variant of the Chenopodium genus characterized by its distinctive white venation on the leaves.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green or grayish hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
