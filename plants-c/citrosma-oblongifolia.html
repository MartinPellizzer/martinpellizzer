Citrosma oblongifolia

Citrosma oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
