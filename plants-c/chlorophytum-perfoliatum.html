Chlorophytum perfoliatum

Chlorophytum perfoliatum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with green and white stripes. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by dark blue berries.

This hardy plant is also noted for its air-purifying properties, enhancing its appeal as both an ornamental and functional plant.
