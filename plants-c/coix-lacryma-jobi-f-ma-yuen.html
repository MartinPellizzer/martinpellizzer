Coix lacryma-jobi f. ma-yuen

Coix lacryma-jobi f. ma-yuen, commonly known as Chinese Job's tears or sweet Job's tears, is a cultivated variant of the Coix genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering spike that bears small, clustered flowers. The seeds are rich in starch and contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its medicinal value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often grown in wetlands or paddy fields.
