Cattleya luteola var. fastuosa

Cattleya luteola var. fastuosa, commonly known as the Yellow Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
