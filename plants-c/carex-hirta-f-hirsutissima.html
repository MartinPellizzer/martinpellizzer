Carex hirta f. hirsutissima

Carex hirta f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Carex.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive, textured appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
