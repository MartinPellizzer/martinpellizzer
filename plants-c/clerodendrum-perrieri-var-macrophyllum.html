Clerodendrum perrieri var. macrophyllum

Clerodendrum perrieri var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
