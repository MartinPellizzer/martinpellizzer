Chromatotriccum riparium

Chromatotriccum riparium, commonly known as the riverbank moss, is a small, creeping moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and stream edges.

It has a dense, turfy growth habit and forms dark green to reddish-brown mats depending on environmental conditions. The plant's leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophytes.

Chromatotriccum riparium plays an important role in stabilizing soil and filtering water in aquatic ecosystems.
