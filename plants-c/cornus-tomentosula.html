Cornus tomentosula

Cornus tomentosula, commonly known as the woolly dogwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cornaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in areas of California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a dark red drupe, which is edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Cornus tomentosula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.
