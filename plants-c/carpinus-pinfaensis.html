Carpinus pinfaensis

Carpinus pinfaensis, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
