Calophyllum wallichianum var. tahanense

Calophyllum wallichianum var. tahanense, commonly known as the Tahanense White Ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a broad, spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries containing seeds.

This species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
