Cyathea costaricensis

Cyathea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the upright structure of the plant.

Cyathea costaricensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
