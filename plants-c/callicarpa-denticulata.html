Callicarpa denticulata

Callicarpa denticulata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are not typically edible for humans but are a food source for birds and other wildlife.
