Caprifolium balearicum

Caprifolium balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Balearic Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
