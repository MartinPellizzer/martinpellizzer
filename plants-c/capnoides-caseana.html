Capnoides caseana

Capnoides caseana, commonly known as the Indian orchid or the white orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the forests of India and is characterized by its delicate, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it prefers humid, shaded environments.

Capnoides caseana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
