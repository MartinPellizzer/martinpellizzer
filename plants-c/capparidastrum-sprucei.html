Capparidastrum sprucei

Capparidastrum sprucei, commonly known as the African caper, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
