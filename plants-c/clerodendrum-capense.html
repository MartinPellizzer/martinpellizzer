Clerodendrum capense

Clerodendrum capense, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
