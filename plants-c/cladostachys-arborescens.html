Cladostachys arborescens

Cladostachys arborescens, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

Cladostachys arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
