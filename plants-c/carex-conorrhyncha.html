Carex conorrhyncha

Carex conorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-necked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex conorrhyncha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
