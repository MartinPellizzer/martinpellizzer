Capparis leucophylla var. parviflora

Capparis leucophylla var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
