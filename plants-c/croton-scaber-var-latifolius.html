Croton scaber var. latifolius

Croton scaber var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive texture.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green with prominent veins, and the plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to adapt to indoor conditions.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
