Carex ferruginea subsp. tristis

Carex ferruginea subsp.

tristis, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish or rusty hues, particularly along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually brownish in color.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
