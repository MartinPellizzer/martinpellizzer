Cucumis longipes

Cucumis longipes, commonly known as the longipes cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and hairy stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow or orange, indicating maturity.
