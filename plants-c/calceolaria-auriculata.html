Calceolaria auriculata

Calceolaria auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, auricle-like pouch at the base of its petals, which gives it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a garden ornamental.
