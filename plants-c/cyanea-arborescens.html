Cyanea arborescens

Cyanea arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the year. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Cyanea arborescens is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Hawaii.
