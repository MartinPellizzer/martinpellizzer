Coleobotrys raphidophora

Coleobotrys raphidophora, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Its unique floral structure and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
