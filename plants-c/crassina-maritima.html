Crassina maritima

Crassina maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to maritime environments. It is often found growing along dunes and rocky shores.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
