Ctenitis sieberiana

Ctenitis sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The fronds are large, feather-like, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the trunk. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
