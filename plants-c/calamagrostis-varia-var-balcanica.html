Calamagrostis varia var. balcanica

Calamagrostis varia var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its striking blue-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is appreciated in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
