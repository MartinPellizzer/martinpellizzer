Catasetum fimbriatum var. viridulum

Catasetum fimbriatum var. viridulum, commonly known as the emerald-streaked orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-blue flowers with distinctive white streaks along the petals. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of several showy, fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators.

The varietal name *viridulum* refers to its vivid green coloration, which is a notable feature in both its flowers and foliage.
