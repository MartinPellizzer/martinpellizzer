Corryocactus spiniflorus

Corryocactus spiniflorus, commonly known as the spiny flower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system to access groundwater.

Corryocactus spiniflorus is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of its ecosystem.
