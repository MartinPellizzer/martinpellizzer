Coffea arabica var. angustifolia

Coffea arabica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Arabica, is a variant of the coffee plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. This variety is prized for its aromatic and flavorful beans, which are often used in specialty coffee blends. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, humid climates and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique traits, it is relatively rare and cultivated in limited areas.
