Calamagrostis fuscata

Calamagrostis fuscata, commonly known as the dark reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has slender, dark green blades that are often tinged with purple at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are usually brown or purplish in color.

Calamagrostis fuscata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
