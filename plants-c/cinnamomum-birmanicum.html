Cinnamomum birmanicum

Cinnamomum birmanicum, commonly known as Burmese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a fragrant scent when crushed.

The bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.
