Clinopodium neglectum var. neglectum

Clinopodium neglectum var. neglectum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, fragrant leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Clinopodium neglectum var. neglectum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
