Crotalaria uliginosa

Crotalaria uliginosa, commonly known as swamp crotalaria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria. The plant typically grows in wet, muddy habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base.

Crotalaria uliginosa is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects.
