Crataegus komarovii

Crataegus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
