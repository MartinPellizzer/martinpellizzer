Cirsium eatonii var. hesperium

Cirsium eatonii var. hesperium, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its botanical features include a stout, hairy stem and composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
