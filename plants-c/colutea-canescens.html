Colutea canescens

Colutea canescens, commonly known as the hoary bupleurum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Colutea canescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
