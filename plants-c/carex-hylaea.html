Carex hylaea

Carex hylaea, commonly known as the yellow sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often bright green.

Carex hylaea produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
