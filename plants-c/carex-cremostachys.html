Carex cremostachys

Carex cremostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

Carex cremostachys is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
