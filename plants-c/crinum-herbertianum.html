Crinum herbertianum

Crinum herbertianum, commonly known as Herbert's crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Crinum herbertianum is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes.
