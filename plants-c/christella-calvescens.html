Christella calvescens

Christella calvescens, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
