Campanula lyrata subsp. lyrata

Campanula lyrata subsp.

lyrata, commonly known as the lyrata bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
