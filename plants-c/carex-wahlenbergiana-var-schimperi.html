Carex wahlenbergiana var. schimperi

Carex wahlenbergiana var. schimperi, commonly known as the Schimperi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
