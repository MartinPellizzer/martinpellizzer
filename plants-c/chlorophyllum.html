Chlorophyllum

Chlorophyllum, commonly known as the common green algae, is a genus of freshwater green algae belonging to the order Chlorophyrales.

These organisms are characterized by their simple, unicellular structure and the presence of chloroplasts containing chlorophyll a and b, which are essential for photosynthesis. They typically grow in aquatic environments such as ponds, lakes, and damp soil, where they play a vital role in the ecosystem by producing oxygen and serving as a food source for various aquatic organisms.

Chlorophyllum species are often found in clusters, forming dense mats on surfaces like rocks and submerged vegetation.
