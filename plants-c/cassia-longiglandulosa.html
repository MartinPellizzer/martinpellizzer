Cassia longiglandulosa

Cassia longiglandulosa, commonly known as the long-haired cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, glandular hairs that give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia longiglandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal applications in herbal remedies.
