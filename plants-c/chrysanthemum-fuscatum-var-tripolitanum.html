Chrysanthemum fuscatum var. tripolitanum

Chrysanthemum fuscatum var. tripolitanum, commonly known as the Tripolitanum chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens across southern Europe.
