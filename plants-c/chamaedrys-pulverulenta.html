Chamaedrys pulverulenta

Chamaedrys pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant for coastal and arid environments.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
