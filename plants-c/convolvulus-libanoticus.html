Convolvulus libanoticus

Convolvulus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or spread along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
