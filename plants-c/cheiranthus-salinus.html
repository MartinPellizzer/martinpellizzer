Cheiranthus salinus

Cheiranthus salinus, commonly known as the saltbush or desert mustards, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and is often found growing in saline soils. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought and high salt concentrations. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, which helps in reducing water loss.

Cheiranthus salinus plays a role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
