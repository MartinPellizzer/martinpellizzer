Callicarpa ruptofoliata

Callicarpa ruptofoliata, commonly known as the red-berried beauty, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that appear in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and wildlife benefits.
