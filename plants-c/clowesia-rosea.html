Clowesia rosea

Clowesia rosea, commonly known as the rose-like daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy, pink to lavender-colored ray flowers surrounding a central disk of yellow florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Clowesia rosea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
