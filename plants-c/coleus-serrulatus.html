Coleus serrulatus

Coleus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
