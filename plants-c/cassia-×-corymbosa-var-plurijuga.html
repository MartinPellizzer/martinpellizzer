Cassia × corymbosa var. plurijuga

Cassia × corymbosa var. plurijuga, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a hybrid species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, showy, orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, giving the plant a feathery appearance. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
