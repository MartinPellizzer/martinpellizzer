Chenopodium graveolens f. pumilum

Chenopodium graveolens f. pumilum, commonly known as dwarf lambsquarters, is a short-lived annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields. It is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
