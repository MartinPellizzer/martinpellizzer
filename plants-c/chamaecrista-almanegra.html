Chamaecrista almanegra

Chamaecrista almanegra, commonly known as black bean or almanegra, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by narrow, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Chamaecrista almanegra is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
