Carex linkii subsp. ambigua

Carex linkii subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
