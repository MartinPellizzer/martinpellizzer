Ceropegia surrecta

Ceropegia surrecta, commonly known as the Rainbow Flower or Ghost Plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its leaves are small and opposite, and it thrives in well-draining soil with indirect sunlight.

Ceropegia surrecta is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
