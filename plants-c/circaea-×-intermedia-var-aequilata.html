Circaea × intermedia var. aequilata

Circaea × intermedia var. aequilata, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeard, is a hybrid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
