Cereus speciosissimus var. kiardii

Cereus speciosissimus var. kiardii, commonly known as the kiardii cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features dense clusters of white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its stems are ribbed and can store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
