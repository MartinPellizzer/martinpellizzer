Croton calocephalus

Croton calocephalus, commonly known as the "Sickle-leaved Croton," is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive yellow or greenish coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance, it is popular in horticulture and landscape design.
