Chamaecrista granulata

Chamaecrista granulata, commonly known as the rough ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland edges and sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers. It is known for its distinctive, hairy stems and is often found in moist to mesic habitats.

Chamaecrista granulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
