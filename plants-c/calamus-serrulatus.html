Calamus serrulatus

Calamus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender herbaceous plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus serrulatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.
