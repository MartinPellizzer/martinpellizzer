Cerastium schizopetalum var. schizopetalum

Cerastium schizopetalum var. schizopetalum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems and hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Its leaves are often lobed or toothed, and the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
