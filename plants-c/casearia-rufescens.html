Casearia rufescens

Casearia rufescens, commonly known as red-leaf casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits. Its leaves are often reddish when mature, giving it its common name, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Casearia rufescens is adapted to moist, tropical environments and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
