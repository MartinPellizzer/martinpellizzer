Clematis mauritiana var. coriacea

Clematis mauritiana var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
