Centaurea boissieri subsp. transmalvana

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

transmalvana, commonly known as the Transalpine centaury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, bright blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
