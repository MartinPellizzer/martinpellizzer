Corema conradii

Corema conradii, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

Corema conradii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as swamps and hammocks.
