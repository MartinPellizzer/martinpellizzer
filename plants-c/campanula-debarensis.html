Campanula debarensis

Campanula debarensis, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Campanula debarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
