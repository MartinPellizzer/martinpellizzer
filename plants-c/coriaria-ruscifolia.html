Coriaria ruscifolia

Coriaria ruscifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Coriariaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant is often found in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its berries are toxic to humans and livestock, containing compounds that can cause severe illness.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
