Carex scirpoidea subsp. pseudoscirpoidea

Carex scirpoidea subsp.

pseudoscirpoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
