Cirsium italicum

Cirsium italicum, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flowers and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium italicum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
