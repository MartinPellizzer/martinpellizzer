Caesalpinia coriacea

Caesalpinia coriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking, flame-colored bracteate flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Caesalpinia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
