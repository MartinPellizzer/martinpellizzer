Carex blanda var. varians

Carex blanda var. varians, commonly known as the smooth bluejoint grass, is a perennial sedge native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

Carex blanda var. varians is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
