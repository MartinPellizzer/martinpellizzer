Cleome psoraleifolia

Cleome psoraleifolia, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow, pink, or white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.

Cleome psoraleifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
