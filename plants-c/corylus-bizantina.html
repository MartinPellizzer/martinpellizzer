Corylus bizantina

Corylus bizantina, commonly known as the Turkish hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the hazel genus (Corylus) in the birch family (Betulaceae). The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The nuts are small, edible, and encased in a thick, spiky husk.
