Cheirinia longisiliqua

Cheirinia longisiliqua, commonly known as the long-stalked bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its distinctive long, slender flower stalks contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

Cheirinia longisiliqua is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
