Cochlearia mexicana

Cochlearia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs. Cochlearia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
