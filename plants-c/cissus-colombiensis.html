Cissus colombiensis

Cissus colombiensis, commonly known as the Colombian devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant has distinctive, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.
