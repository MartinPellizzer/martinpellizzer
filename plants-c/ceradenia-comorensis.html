Ceradenia comorensis

Ceradenia comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro star orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Comoros archipelago.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that display vivid colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ceradenia comorensis is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
