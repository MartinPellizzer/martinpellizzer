Chascanum adenostachyum

Chascanum adenostachyum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and a prominent central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chascanum adenostachyum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
