Callista rhodocentra

Callista rhodocentra, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
