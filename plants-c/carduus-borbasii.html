Carduus borbasii

Carduus borbasii, commonly known as the Borbas's thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
