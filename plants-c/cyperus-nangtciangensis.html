Cyperus nangtciangensis

Cyperus nangtciangensis, commonly known as the Nangtciang flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
