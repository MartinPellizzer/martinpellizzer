Connarus planchonianus

Connarus planchonianus, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
