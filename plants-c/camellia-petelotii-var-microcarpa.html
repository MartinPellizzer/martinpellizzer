Camellia petelotii var. microcarpa

Camellia petelotii var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant. The flowers are typically small, with a deep red or pink color, and are borne in clusters. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in humid, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
