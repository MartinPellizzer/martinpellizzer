Carex × larminatii

Carex × larminatii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Carex, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. This particular species is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex × larminatii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
