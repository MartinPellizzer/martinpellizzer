Crepidium megalanthum

Crepidium megalanthum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features long, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. Its flowers are striking, with large, showy petals that can reach several inches in length, and they are usually brightly colored, ranging from white to pink or purple.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
