Cousinia umbilicata

Cousinia umbilicata, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Cousinia umbilicata is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
