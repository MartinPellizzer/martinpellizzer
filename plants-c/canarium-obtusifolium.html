Canarium obtusifolium

Canarium obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
