Cinnamosma fragrans var. perrieri

Cinnamosma fragrans var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong, spicy scent when broken. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing flat, aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

This species is valued for its essential oils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
