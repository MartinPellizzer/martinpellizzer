Cirsium hypopsilum

Cirsium hypopsilum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
