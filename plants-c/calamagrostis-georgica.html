Calamagrostis georgica

Calamagrostis georgica, commonly known as the Georgica grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis georgica is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
