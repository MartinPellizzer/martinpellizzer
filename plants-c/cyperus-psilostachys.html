Cyperus psilostachys

Cyperus psilostachys, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or moist environments. Cyperus psilostachys is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
