Ceropegia dichotoma subsp. dichotoma

Ceropegia dichotoma subsp.

dichotoma, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and its ability to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple or white flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
