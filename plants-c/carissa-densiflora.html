Carissa densiflora

Carissa densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches, which provide structural support. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in late summer.

Carissa densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
