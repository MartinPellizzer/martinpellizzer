Crataegus geniculata

Crataegus geniculata, commonly known as the nodding hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has small, oval leaves with serrated edges and a dense, often thorny structure. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus geniculata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
