Camara aculeata var. subinermis

Camara aculeata var. subinermis, commonly known as the smoothleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant in the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other varieties of Camara aculeata. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is native to parts of South America and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
