Clintonia podanisia var. fascicularis

Clintonia podanisia var. fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered bluebead, is a rare and striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter, providing a unique visual feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
