Crinum taitense var. queenslandicum

Crinum taitense var. queenslandicum, commonly known as the Queensland crinum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of fragrant white or pale pink blossoms. It is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
