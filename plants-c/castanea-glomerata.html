Castanea glomerata

Castanea glomerata, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces small, spiny capsules that contain one to three edible nuts. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Castanea glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional horticulture.
