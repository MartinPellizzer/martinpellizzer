Canarium clementis

Canarium clementis, commonly known as the Clementine candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its seeds are valued for their oil content and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
