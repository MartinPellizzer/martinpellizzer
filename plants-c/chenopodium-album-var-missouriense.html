Chenopodium album var. missouriense

Chenopodium album var. missouriense, commonly known as Missouri goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind and can persist in the soil for several years.
