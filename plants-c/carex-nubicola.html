Carex nubicola

Carex nubicola, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and other mountainous areas.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Carex nubicola plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
