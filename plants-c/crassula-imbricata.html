Crassula imbricata

Crassula imbricata, commonly known as the Ice Plant or Frost Plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Crassula imbricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
