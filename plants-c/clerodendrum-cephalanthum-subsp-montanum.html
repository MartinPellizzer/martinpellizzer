Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp. montanum

Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain clerodendrum, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
