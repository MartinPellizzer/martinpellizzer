Cassia brevipila

Cassia brevipila, commonly known as the dwarf cassia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. Its small, oblong pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia brevipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
