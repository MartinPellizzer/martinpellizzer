Cupressus chilensis

Cupressus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and dense, feathery foliage that forms a pyramidal shape. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, and it produces small, spherical cones. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cupressus chilensis is also valued for its durability and resistance to pests and diseases.
