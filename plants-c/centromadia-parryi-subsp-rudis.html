Centromadia parryi subsp. rudis

Centromadia parryi subsp.

rudis, commonly known as the rough centromadia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow to brown, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The subspecies rudis is distinguished by its rough, textured leaves and robust stems, which help it withstand harsh desert conditions.
