Cocculus limacia

Cocculus limacia, commonly known as the devil's apple, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant features a vine-like structure with palmate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated fruits. The fruit contains a single, large, white, kidney-shaped seed.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
