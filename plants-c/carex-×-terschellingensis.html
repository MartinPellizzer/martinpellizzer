Carex × terschellingensis

Carex × terschellingensis, commonly known as the Terschelling sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its hardy nature and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable soil and water conditions.
