Chaetocyperus rugulosus

Chaetocyperus rugulosus, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in short, bristly hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
