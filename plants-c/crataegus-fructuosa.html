Crataegus fructuosa

Crataegus fructuosa, commonly known as the fruiting hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
