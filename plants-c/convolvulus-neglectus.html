Convolvulus neglectus

Convolvulus neglectus, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to many other regions.

Convolvulus neglectus is often considered a weed due to its aggressive spreading nature.
