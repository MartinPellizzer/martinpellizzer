Cyperus edulis

Cyperus edulis, commonly known as nutsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, spongy stems and small, clustered flower spikes. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, disturbed soils. Its rhizomes allow it to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many areas.

Nutsedge is often found in agricultural fields and wetlands, where it can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
