Crataegus asperifolia

Crataegus asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along streambanks.
