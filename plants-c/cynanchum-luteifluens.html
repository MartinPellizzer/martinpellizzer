Cynanchum luteifluens

Cynanchum luteifluens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers with distinctive hooded structures. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas along stream banks.

Cynanchum luteifluens is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
