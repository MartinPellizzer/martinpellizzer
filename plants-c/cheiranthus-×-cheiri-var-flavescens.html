Cheiranthus × cheiri var. flavescens

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically four-petaled and borne in clusters. The plant is native to southern Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden settings.

Its foliage is basal, with linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
