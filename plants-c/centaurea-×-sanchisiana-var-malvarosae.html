Centaurea × sanchisiana var. malvarosae

Centaurea × sanchisiana var. malvarosae, commonly known as the Malvarosa thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often found in botanical gardens and natural reserves.
