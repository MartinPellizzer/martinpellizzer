Cymbopappus hilliardiae

Cymbopappus hilliardiae, commonly known as the Hilliard's rigid bush, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, often spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in winter. The plant has small, pappus-equipped seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems and waxy leaf surfaces.

Cymbopappus hilliardiae is often found in sand dunes and rocky outcrops, contributing to the ecological stability of these environments.
