Crataegus flava var. lobata

Crataegus flava var. lobata, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its yellowish flowers and red berries. The leaves are typically lobed, giving it the specific epithet *lobata*. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
