Cyperus brevifolius subsp. luridus

Cyperus brevifolius subsp.

luridus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically yellow or reddish in color.

This subspecies is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in wet or seasonally flooded habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of moist environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
