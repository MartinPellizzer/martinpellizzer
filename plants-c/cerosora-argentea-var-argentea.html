Cerosora argentea var. argentea

Cerosora argentea var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinct silver underside. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
