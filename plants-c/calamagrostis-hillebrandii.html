Calamagrostis hillebrandii

Calamagrostis hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often golden or brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
