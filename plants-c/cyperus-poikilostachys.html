Cyperus poikilostachys

Cyperus poikilostachys, commonly known as the variable flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Cyperus poikilostachys is known for its adaptability to various water conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
