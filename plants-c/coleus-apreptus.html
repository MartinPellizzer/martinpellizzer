Coleus apreptus

Coleus apreptus, commonly known as the red-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can display shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
