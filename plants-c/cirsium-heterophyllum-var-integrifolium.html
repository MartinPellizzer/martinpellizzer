Cirsium heterophyllum var. integrifolium

Cirsium heterophyllum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
