Cyathea paleacea

Cyathea paleacea, commonly known as the paleaceas fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can remain on the plant for several years.

Cyathea paleacea is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
