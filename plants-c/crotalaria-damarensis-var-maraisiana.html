Crotalaria damarensis var. maraisiana

Crotalaria damarensis var. maraisiana, commonly known as the Marais false cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
