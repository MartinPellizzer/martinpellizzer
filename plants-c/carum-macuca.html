Carum macuca

Carum macuca, commonly known as wild chervil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic foliage has been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.

Carum macuca is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and enhancing biodiversity in natural habitats.
