Capparis sciaphila

Capparis sciaphila, commonly known as the shade caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features woody stems and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Capparis sciaphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
