Coccinia quercifolia

Coccinia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Coccinia quercifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
