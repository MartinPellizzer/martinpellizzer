Carex brunnea subsp. brunnea

Carex brunnea subsp.

brunnea, commonly known as the brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its stiff, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, usually brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
