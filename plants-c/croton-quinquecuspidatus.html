Croton quinquecuspidatus

Croton quinquecuspidatus, commonly known as the five-pointed croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically lobed and can have a velvety texture, adding to its visual appeal.

Croton quinquecuspidatus is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
