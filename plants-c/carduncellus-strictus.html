Carduncellus strictus

Carduncellus strictus, commonly known as the rigid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing showy, composite flower heads.

Carduncellus strictus is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
