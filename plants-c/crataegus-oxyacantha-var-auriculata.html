Crataegus oxyacantha var. auriculata

Crataegus oxyacantha var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaf bases and clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that matures in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
