Ceradenia maackii

Ceradenia maackii, commonly known as the Maack's ceradenia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient photosynthesis.

Ceradenia maackii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
