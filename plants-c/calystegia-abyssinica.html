Calystegia abyssinica

Calystegia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
