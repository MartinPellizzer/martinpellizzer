Chamaecyparis obtusa aurea-gracilis

Chamaecyparis obtusa aurea-gracilis, commonly known as the golden thread cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen with a narrow, pyramidal shape and finely textured foliage. The leaves are scale-like, bright golden in color, and emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and ability to maintain its color throughout the year.
