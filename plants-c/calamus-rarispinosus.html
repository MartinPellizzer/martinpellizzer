Calamus rarispinosus

Calamus rarispinosus, commonly known as the rare spiny calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented rhizomes and tall, arching inflorescences that bear small, clustered spikes of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, spiny fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by water or animals. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Calamus rarispinosus is considered vulnerable in its natural range.
