Connarus coriaceus

Connarus coriaceus, commonly known as the African leatherwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Connarus coriaceus is valued for its durable wood, which has been traditionally used for making tools and furniture.
