Crataegus hybrida

Crataegus hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and some mammals.

Crataegus hybrida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
