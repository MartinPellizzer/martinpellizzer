Campanula latifolia var. intermedia

Campanula latifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive flowers and foliage, which contribute to its use in both wild and cultivated settings.
