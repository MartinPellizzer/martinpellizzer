Catharanthus ovalis var. tomentellus

Catharanthus ovalis var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly Madagascar vine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed.
