Centaurea aemulans

Centaurea aemulans, commonly known as the mimic centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that resemble those of other centaurea species.
