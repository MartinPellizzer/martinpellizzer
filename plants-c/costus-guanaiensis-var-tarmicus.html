Costus guanaiensis var. tarmicus

Costus guanaiensis var. tarmicus, commonly known as the Tarmic costus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Costaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
