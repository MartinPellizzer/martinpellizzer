Cephalophyllum inaequale

Cephalophyllum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal-headed cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth habit. The plant features short, cylindrical stems with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the top of the stems in a striking display.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
