Cucumis melo var. microspermus

Cucumis melo var. microspermus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a botanical variety of the melon genus characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits.

It is native to the Middle East and has been cultivated for centuries for its edible, seedless fruit. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is usually round or oval, with a thin, pale green rind and a juicy, honey-sweet flesh.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique flavor and ornamental appeal.
