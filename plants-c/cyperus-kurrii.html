Cyperus kurrii

Cyperus kurrii, commonly known as the Kurri grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Cyperus kurrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
