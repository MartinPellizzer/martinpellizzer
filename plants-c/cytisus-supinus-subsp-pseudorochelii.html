Cytisus supinus subsp. pseudorochelii

Cytisus supinus subsp.

pseudorochelii, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
