Calophyllum warburgii

Calophyllum warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's candlewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces yellowish-orange flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
