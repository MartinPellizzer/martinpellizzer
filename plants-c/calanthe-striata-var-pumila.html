Calanthe striata var. pumila

Calanthe striata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
