Corydalis remota var. rotundiloba

Corydalis remota var. rotundiloba, commonly known as the round-leaf Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically ovate to round in shape, which gives the variety its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and bloom.
