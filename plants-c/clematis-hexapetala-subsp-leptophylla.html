Clematis hexapetala subsp. leptophylla

Clematis hexapetala subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, often with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white to pale blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
