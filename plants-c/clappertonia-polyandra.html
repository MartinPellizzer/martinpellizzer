Clappertonia polyandra

Clappertonia polyandra, commonly known as the golden wax tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a golden underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clappertonia polyandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
