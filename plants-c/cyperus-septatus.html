Cyperus septatus

Cyperus septatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. Cyperus septatus thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its adaptability to moist conditions makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in wetland habitats.
