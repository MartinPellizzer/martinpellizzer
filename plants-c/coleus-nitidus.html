Coleus nitidus

Coleus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from deep red to vibrant purple and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor settings. Its leaves are often hairy and have a glossy texture, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
