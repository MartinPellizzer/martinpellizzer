Clinopodium bolivianum subsp. diffusum

Clinopodium bolivianum subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as Bolivian woodruff, is a perennial herb native to South America. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, pleasant fragrance when crushed.

It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.
