Clerodendrum peekelii

Clerodendrum peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or orange calyx. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clerodendrum peekelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
