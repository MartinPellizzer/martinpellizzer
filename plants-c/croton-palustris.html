Croton palustris

Croton palustris, commonly known as swamp croton, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender peduncles. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, swampy environments.

Croton palustris is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
