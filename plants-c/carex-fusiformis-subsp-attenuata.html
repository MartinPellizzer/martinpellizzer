Carex fusiformis subsp. attenuata

Carex fusiformis subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
