Cytisus hirsutus subsp. leucotrichus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-stemmed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. This subspecies is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
