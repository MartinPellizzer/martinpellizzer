Catasetum roseoalbum

Catasetum roseoalbum, commonly known as the pink-white orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insects. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
