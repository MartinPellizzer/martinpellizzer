Calamus macracanthus

Calamus macracanthus, commonly known as the broad-armed calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant with long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus macracanthus thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
