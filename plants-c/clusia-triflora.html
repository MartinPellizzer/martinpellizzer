Clusia triflora

Clusia triflora, commonly known as the waxleaf croton, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and often variegated with yellow or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Clusia triflora is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is often used in horticulture for indoor and outdoor decoration.
