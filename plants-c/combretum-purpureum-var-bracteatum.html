Combretum purpureum var. bracteatum

Combretum purpureum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the purple flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to red flowers that appear in dense clusters during the winter months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
