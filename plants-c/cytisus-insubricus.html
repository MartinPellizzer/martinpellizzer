Cytisus insubricus

Cytisus insubricus, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus insubricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
