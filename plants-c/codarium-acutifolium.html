Codarium acutifolium

Codarium acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved codariopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Codariaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually greenish or yellowish, and it produces small, dry fruits.
