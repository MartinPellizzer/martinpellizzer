Citrus × aurantium var. mellarosa

Citrus × aurantium var. mellarosa, commonly known as the blood orange, is a citrus hybrid that originates from the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive deep red or purplish pigmentation in the flesh of its fruit, which is due to the presence of anthocyanins. The plant is a small evergreen tree with thick, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is not only valued for its unique flavor but also for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
