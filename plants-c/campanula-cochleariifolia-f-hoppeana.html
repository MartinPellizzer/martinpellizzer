Campanula cochleariifolia f. hoppeana

Campanula cochleariifolia f. hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
