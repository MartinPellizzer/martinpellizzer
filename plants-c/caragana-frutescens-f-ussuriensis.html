Caragana frutescens f. ussuriensis

Caragana frutescens f. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri shrub locust, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern regions of Russia and parts of China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves that are typically 5 to 11 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as steppes and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in landscaping.
