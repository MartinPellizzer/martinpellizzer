Cereus spegazzinii var. hassleri

Cereus spegazzinii var. hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler's night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

It is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
