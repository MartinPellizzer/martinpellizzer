Caopia mandurr

Caopia mandurr, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by round, segmented fruits. The fruit's outer layer, or pericarp, is thick and leathery, while the inner segments contain the edible, white, juicy flesh.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
