Calandrinia disperma

Calandrinia disperma, commonly known as the scattered-flowered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a short-lived perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, bright red or orange flowers. Its flowers are typically borne on erect stems and bloom throughout the growing season.

Calandrinia disperma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
