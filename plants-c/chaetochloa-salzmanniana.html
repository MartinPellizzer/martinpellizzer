Chaetochloa salzmanniana

Chaetochloa salzmanniana, commonly known as Salzmann's hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
