Combretum boehmii

Combretum boehmii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The bark is smooth and often reddish-brown, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
