Cytisus striatus var. eriocarpus

Cytisus striatus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, often hairy stems. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy layer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
