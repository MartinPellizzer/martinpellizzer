Chrysanthemum eginense

Chrysanthemum eginense, commonly known as the Eginese chrysanthemum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, making it a valuable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, which contributes to its adaptability in diverse environmental conditions.
