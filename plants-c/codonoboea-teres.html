Codonoboea teres

Codonoboea teres, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a reddish tint. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
