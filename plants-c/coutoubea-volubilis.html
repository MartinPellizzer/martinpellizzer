Coutoubea volubilis

Coutoubea volubilis, commonly known as the climbing trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous, twining growth habit and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, with bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Coutoubea volubilis is often used in gardens as a climbing ornamental due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
