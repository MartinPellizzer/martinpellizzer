Cymbidium viride

Cymbidium viride, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers with yellow and green markings, which bloom during the winter months. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Cymbidium viride is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, well-drained conditions.
