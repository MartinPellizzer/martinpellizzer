Campanula rotundifolia var. velutina

Campanula rotundifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
