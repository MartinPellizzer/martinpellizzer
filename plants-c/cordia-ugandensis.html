Cordia ugandensis

Cordia ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda cordia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cordia ugandensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
