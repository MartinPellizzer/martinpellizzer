Ceanothus velutinus var. velutinus

Ceanothus velutinus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to California, USA.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral habitats. It produces clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers in the spring.

The leaves are evergreen, and the shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
