Cinnamomum thomsonii

Cinnamomum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
