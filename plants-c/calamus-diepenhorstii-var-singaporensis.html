Calamus diepenhorstii var. singaporensis

Calamus diepenhorstii var. singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araucariaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Singapore, and thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is often found in conservation areas.
