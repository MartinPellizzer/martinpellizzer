Cliftonia

Cliftonia, commonly known as the redroot or redroot dogwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cornaceae.

It consists of two species, Cliftonia feayi and Cliftonia sanguinea, both native to the southeastern United States. These plants are characterized by their showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

Cliftonia species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
