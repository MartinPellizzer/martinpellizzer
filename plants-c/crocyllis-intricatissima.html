Crocyllis intricatissima

Crocyllis intricatissima, commonly known as the intricate crocus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its complex, intricately patterned flowers that display a wide range of colors, often with a distinctive central eye. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
