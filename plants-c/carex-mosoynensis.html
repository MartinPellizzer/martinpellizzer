Carex mosoynensis

Carex mosoynensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
