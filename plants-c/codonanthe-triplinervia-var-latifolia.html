Codonanthe triplinervia var. latifolia

Codonanthe triplinervia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed codonanthe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
