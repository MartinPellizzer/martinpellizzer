Cynanchica cristata

Cynanchica cristata, commonly known as the crownvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive crown-like appearance due to its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Cynanchica cristata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and is also used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth and deep root system.
