Caesalpinia crista var. parvistipula

Caesalpinia crista var. parvistipula, commonly known as the crown thorn or parvistipula crown thorn, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a distinctive crown-like appearance due to its dense, branching structure. The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.
