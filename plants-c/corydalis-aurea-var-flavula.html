Corydalis aurea var. flavula

Corydalis aurea var. flavula, commonly known as the golden corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with lance-shaped lobes. Its flowers are characterized by two upper petals that are larger and more showy than the lower ones.

Corydalis aurea var. flavula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
