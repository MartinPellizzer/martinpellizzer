Centaurea drabifolia subsp. austro-occidentalis

Centaurea drabifolia subsp.

austro-occidentalis, commonly known as the western dwarf coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky yellow to orange flower heads. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower structure.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
