Calamus nambariensis var. hoollongapariensis

Calamus nambariensis var. hoollongapariensis, commonly known as the Hoollongaparai bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family palms.

It is native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly in the states of Assam and Meghalaya, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates. This variant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often found in dense forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
