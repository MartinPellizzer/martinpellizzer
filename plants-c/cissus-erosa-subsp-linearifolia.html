Cissus erosa subsp. linearifolia

Cissus erosa subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and prominent veins.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises and vertical gardens.
