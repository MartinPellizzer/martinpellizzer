Conospermum patens

Conospermum patens, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in heathland and coastal scrub habitats. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
