Celtis amboinensis

Celtis amboinensis, commonly known as the white-fruited hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. Celtis amboinensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
