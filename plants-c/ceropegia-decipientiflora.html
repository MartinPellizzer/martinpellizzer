Ceropegia decipientiflora

Ceropegia decipientiflora, commonly known as the deceptive flower or false ceropegia, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing or trailing plant with thin, succulent stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of yellow or orange. These flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as flies and beetles.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky areas or along riverbanks.
