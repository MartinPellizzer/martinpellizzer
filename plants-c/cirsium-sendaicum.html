Cirsium sendaicum

Cirsium sendaicum, commonly known as the Sendai thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
