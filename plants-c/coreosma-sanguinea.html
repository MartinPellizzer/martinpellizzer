Coreosma sanguinea

Coreosma sanguinea, commonly known as the scarlet coreosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
