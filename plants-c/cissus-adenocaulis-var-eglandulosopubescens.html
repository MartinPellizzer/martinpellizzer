Cissus adenocaulis var. eglandulosopubescens

Cissus adenocaulis var. eglandulosopubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by green, edible berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
