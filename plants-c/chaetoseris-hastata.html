Chaetoseris hastata

Chaetoseris hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces small yellow flowers that develop into elongated seed heads.

The species is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand various environmental conditions.
