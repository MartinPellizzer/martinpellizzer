Crotalaria pseudokipandensis

Crotalaria pseudokipandensis, commonly known as the Kipandeni crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
