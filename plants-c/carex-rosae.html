Carex rosae

Carex rosae, commonly known as the rose sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex rosae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
