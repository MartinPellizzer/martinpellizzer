Cassia hypnotica

Cassia hypnotica, commonly known as the sleepy tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain numerous flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Cassia hypnotica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species within the legume family.
