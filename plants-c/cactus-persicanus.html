Cactus persicanus

Cactus persicanus, commonly known as the Persian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a spiny, shrub-like plant with thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Cactus persicanus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
