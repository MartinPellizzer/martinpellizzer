Ceropegia perlata

Ceropegia perlata, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Ceropegia perlata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
