Cnicus diacantha

Cnicus diacantha, commonly known as the two-lobed sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Cnicus diacantha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
