Conyza amplexicaulis

Conyza amplexicaulis, commonly known as the "velvetleaf," is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that often encircle the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Conyza amplexicaulis is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds.
