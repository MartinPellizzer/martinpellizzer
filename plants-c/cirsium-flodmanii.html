Cirsium flodmanii

Cirsium flodmanii, commonly known as Flodman's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistles and flowering plants. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium flodmanii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.
