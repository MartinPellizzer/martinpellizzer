Crataegus kellermanii

Crataegus kellermanii, commonly known as Kellerman's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. This shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
