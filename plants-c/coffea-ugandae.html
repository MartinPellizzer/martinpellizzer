Coffea ugandae

Coffea ugandae, commonly known as the Uganda coffee or East African coffee, is a species native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces red, fleshy fruits containing seeds used for coffee production. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high rainfall and well-drained soil. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and dark green color.

Coffea ugandae is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often grown in small-scale agricultural settings.
