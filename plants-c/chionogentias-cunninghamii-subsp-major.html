Chionogentias cunninghamii subsp. major

Chionogentias cunninghamii subsp.

major, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
