Carex lepidocarpa var. nevadensis

Carex lepidocarpa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a slightly waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
