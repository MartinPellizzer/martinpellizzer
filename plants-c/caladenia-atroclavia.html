Caladenia atroclavia

Caladenia atroclavia, commonly known as the Black-veined Spider-orchid, is an Australian orchid species characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance.

It features a slender, hairy stem with a single, erect flower that has a dark purple or blackish hue. The orchid's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. It is typically found in heathlands and sandy soils across southeastern Australia.

This species is known for its unique coloration and is a subject of interest in both botanical studies and conservation efforts.
