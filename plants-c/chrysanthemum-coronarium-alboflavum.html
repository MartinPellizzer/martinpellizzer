Chrysanthemum coronarium alboflavum

Chrysanthemum coronarium alboflavum, commonly known as the white-flowered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow to white flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
