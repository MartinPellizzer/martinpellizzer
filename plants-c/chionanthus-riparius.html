Chionanthus riparius

Chionanthus riparius, commonly known as the silver leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and silvery-white underneath, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Chionanthus riparius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
