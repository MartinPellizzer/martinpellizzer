Cayratia grandifolia

Cayratia grandifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
