Cyperus platystachyus

Cyperus platystachyus, commonly known as the broad-stemmed flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus platystachyus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate waterlogged conditions.
