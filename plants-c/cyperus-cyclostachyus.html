Cyperus cyclostachyus

Cyperus cyclostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically slightly rolled. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Cyperus cyclostachyus is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
