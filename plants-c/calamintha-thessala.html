Calamintha thessala

Calamintha thessala, commonly known as Greek catnip, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
