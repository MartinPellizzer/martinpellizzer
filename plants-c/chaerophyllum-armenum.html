Chaerophyllum armenum

Chaerophyllum armenum, commonly known as Armenian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Chaerophyllum armenum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
