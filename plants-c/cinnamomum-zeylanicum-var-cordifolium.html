Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cordifolium

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cordifolium, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf or Ceylon cinnamon, is a species native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is closely related to other cinnamon species. Its leaves contain essential oils rich in cinnamaldehyde, which contributes to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

This variant is distinguished by its heart-shaped foliage and is valued for both its aromatic and therapeutic uses.
