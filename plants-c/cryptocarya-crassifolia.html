Cryptocarya crassifolia

Cryptocarya crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cryptocarya, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple berries.

Cryptocarya crassifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
