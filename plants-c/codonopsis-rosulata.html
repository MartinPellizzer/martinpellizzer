Codonopsis rosulata

Codonopsis rosulata, commonly known as the rose-like bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental value to its habitat.
