Correa ventricosa

Correa ventricosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year.

Correa ventricosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in mild coastal climates.
