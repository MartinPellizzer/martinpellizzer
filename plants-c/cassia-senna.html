Cassia senna

Cassia senna, commonly known as sicklepod, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, and its yellow flowers that resemble those of the legume family. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Cassia senna is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
