Croton cascarilla var. linearis

Croton cascarilla var. linearis, commonly known as the linear croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
