Cardamine komarovii f. macrophylla

Cardamine komarovii f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on elongated stems. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in water gardens.
