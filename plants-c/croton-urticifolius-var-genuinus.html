Croton urticifolius var. genuinus

Croton urticifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the stinging croton, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a potentially hazardous plant for both humans and animals.
