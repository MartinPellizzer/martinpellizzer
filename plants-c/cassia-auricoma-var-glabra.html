Cassia auricoma var. glabra

Cassia auricoma var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are encased in elongated, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
