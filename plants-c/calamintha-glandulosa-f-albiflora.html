Calamintha glandulosa f. albiflora

Calamintha glandulosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, mint-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
