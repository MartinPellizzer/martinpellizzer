Carex grandiligulata

Carex grandiligulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex grandiligulata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
