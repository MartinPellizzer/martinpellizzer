Correa glabra var. glabra

Correa glabra var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
