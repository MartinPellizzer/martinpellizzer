Cineraria tomentolanata

Cineraria tomentolanata, commonly known as the woolly cineraria, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
