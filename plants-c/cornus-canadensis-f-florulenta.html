Cornus canadensis f. florulenta

Cornus canadensis f. florulenta, commonly known as the Canadian dogwood or dwarf dogwood, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically forms dense mats and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
