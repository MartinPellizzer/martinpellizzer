Clinopodium arkansanum

Clinopodium arkansanum, commonly known as Arkansas mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and streambanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong mint-like aroma. Its flowers are small, white to pale blue, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Clinopodium arkansanum is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
