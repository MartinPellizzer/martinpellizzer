Carduus drummondii

Carduus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a purple center.

Drummond's thistle is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
