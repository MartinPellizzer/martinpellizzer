Colchicum lockmannii

Colchicum lockmannii, commonly known as the Lockmann's autumn crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are borne on tall, slender stems, often above a cluster of basal leaves. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
