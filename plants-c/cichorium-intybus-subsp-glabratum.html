Cichorium intybus subsp. glabratum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with dark green, lobed leaves that are often hairy but can become smooth in certain conditions.

The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its leaves and roots are used in culinary and medicinal applications, including the production of chicory coffee and herbal remedies.
