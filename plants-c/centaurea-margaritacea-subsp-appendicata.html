Centaurea margaritacea subsp. appendicata

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

appendicata, commonly known as the Armenian star-thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, bract-like structures at the base of the flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
