Chaetanthera humilis

Chaetanthera humilis, commonly known as the small hairy honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
