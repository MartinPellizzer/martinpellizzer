Carex goodenowii var. nigra

Carex goodenowii var. nigra, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features dark green, strap-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
