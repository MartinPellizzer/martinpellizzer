Cardamine violifolia

Cardamine violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and arranged in a raceme.

The plant is known for its peppery, aromatic foliage, which is often used in salads and as a garnish.
