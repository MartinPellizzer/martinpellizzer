Carex anceps var. striatula

Carex anceps var. striatula, commonly known as the striped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The varietal name "striatula" refers to the distinctive striped pattern on its leaves, which adds to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
