Clematis columbiana var. dissecta

Clematis columbiana var. dissecta, commonly known as the Pacific silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a deciduous vine that produces delicate, fragrant, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant features compound leaves with finely dissected foliage, giving it a lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native environment.
