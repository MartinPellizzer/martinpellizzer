Corchorus sidoides subsp. vermicularis

Corchorus sidoides subsp.

vermicularis, commonly known as the African mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
