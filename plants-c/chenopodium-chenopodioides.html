Chenopodium chenopodioides

Chenopodium chenopodioides, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like structures that resemble leaves but are actually modified stems. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
