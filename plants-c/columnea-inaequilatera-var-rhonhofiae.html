Columnea inaequilatera var. rhonhofiae

Columnea inaequilatera var. rhonhofiae, commonly known as the Rhonhof's flame vine, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. It is a trailing or climbing plant that can be grown in hanging baskets or trained on supports.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological value, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and conservation purposes.
