Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. junceus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

junceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage, which are typical features of the Asteraceae family.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
