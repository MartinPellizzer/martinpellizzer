Cieca cavanillesii

Cieca cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' cieca, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along riverbanks. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and the challenges it presents for cultivation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts critical for its survival.
