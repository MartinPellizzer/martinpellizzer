Carthamus × battandieri

Carthamus × battandieri, commonly known as the Turkish poppy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid orange to red flowers with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a bold visual impact to gardens.
