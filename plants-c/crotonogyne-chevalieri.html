Crotonogyne chevalieri

Crotonogyne chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Crotonogyne chevalieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking leaf patterns and resilience in various growing conditions.
