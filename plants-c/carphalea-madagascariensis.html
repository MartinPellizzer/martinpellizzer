Carphalea madagascariensis

Carphalea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar balm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, balm-like resin that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
