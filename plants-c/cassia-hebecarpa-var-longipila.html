Cassia hebecarpa var. longipila

Cassia hebecarpa var. longipila, commonly known as the longpod cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in panicles. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
