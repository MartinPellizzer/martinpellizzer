Crataegus baxteri

Crataegus baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
