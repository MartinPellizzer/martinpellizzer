Cordia erythrococca

Cordia erythrococca, commonly known as the red cocoon tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into distinctive red, fleshy capsules. The fruit, known as a "cocoon," contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.
