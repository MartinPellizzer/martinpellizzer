Calamus oblongus subsp. irregularis

Calamus oblongus subsp.

irregularis, commonly known as the irregular calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia. It is a tall, slender bamboo-like plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences.

The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern with irregularly spaced nodes and a distinctive, twisted stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
