Calathea propinqua

Calathea propinqua, commonly known as the laceleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, intricate foliage with a marbled pattern. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, often displaying shades of green, silver, and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
