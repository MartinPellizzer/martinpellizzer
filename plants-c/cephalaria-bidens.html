Cephalaria bidens

Cephalaria bidens, commonly known as the two-toothed cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Cephalaria bidens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
