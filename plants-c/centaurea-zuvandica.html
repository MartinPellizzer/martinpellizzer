Centaurea zuvandica

Centaurea zuvandica, commonly known as the Zuvand thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Centaurea zuvandica is considered a rare and endemic species.
