Carenidium

Carenidium, commonly known as the golden cup or golden cupflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that resemble small cups, hence its common name. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped or ovate. They are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in dry, open habitats.

Carenidium species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
