Chlorocyperus glomeratus

Chlorocyperus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or bluish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Chlorocyperus glomeratus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
