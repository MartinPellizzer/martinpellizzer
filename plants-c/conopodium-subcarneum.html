Conopodium subcarneum

Conopodium subcarneum, commonly known as the swamp bladdernut, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic compounds. The plant has smooth, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically alternate and lanceolate in shape. Its fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.

Conopodium subcarneum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
