Camara lilacina

Camara lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Camara lilacina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
