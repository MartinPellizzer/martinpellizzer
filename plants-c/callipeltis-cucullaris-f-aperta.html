Callipeltis cucullaris f. aperta

Callipeltis cucullaris f. aperta, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a unique variant of the orchid species Callipeltis cucullaris.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard form. This fern-like orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
