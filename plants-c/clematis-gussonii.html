Clematis gussonii

Clematis gussonii, commonly known as the rock clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and trellis-covered walls.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures and regular pruning to maintain its shape.
