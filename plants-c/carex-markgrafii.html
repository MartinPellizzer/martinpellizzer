Carex markgrafii

Carex markgrafii, commonly known as the Markgraf's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex markgrafii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
