Capparis didymobotrys

Capparis didymobotrys, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is part of the Capparaceae family, which is known for its distinctive floral structures and often spiny stems.
