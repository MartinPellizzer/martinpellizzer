Camphora officinarum var. hippocratei

Camphora officinarum var. hippocratei, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves, which contain high concentrations of camphor. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and dark green, glossy foliage. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its essential oils and timber.
