Clinopodium pilosum

Clinopodium pilosum, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Clinopodium pilosum is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and damp soils, and it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
