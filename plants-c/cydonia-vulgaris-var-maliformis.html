Cydonia vulgaris var. maliformis

Cydonia vulgaris var. maliformis, commonly known as the Persian apple or mulberry tree, is a variant of the common quince.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit, which is typically orange to yellow in color, has a sweet and aromatic flavor. This plant is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
