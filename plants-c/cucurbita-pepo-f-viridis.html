Cucurbita pepo f. viridis

Cucurbita pepo f. viridis, commonly known as the green gourd, is a variant of the common squash species.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy, and typically green skin, which can vary in color depending on the variety. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible qualities.
