Centranthus calcitrapae var. trichocarpus

Centranthus calcitrapae var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the Trichocarpus centranthus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
