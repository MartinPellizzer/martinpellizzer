Centropogon mojandensis

Centropogon mojandensis, commonly known as the Mojanda grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
