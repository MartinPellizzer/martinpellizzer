Cistanche flava subsp. bicolor

Cistanche flava subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the yellow parasitic herb, is a flowering plant in the Orobanchaceae family. It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis.

Native to arid regions of China, it thrives in sandy soils and is often found in desert environments. The plant has distinctive yellow to orange-red flowers and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique botanical features make it an important subject in studies of parasitic plant ecology and medicinal botany.
