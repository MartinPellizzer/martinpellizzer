Chloris ruahensis

Chloris ruahensis, commonly known as the Ruahene grass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
