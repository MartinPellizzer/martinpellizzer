Ceradenia fendleri

Ceradenia fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive pattern, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Ceradenia fendleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
