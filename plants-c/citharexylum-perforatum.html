Citharexylum perforatum

Citharexylum perforatum, commonly known as the Australian honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Australia and New Zealand.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often perforated, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy areas.

Citharexylum perforatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its fragrance and visual interest.
