Cnicus siculus

Cnicus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cnicus siculus is known for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
