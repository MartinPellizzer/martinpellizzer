Coffea pervilleana

Coffea pervilleana, commonly known as the Pervillea coffee plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinct, aromatic scent.

While not widely cultivated for commercial coffee production, it holds significance in local botanical studies and traditional uses.
