Calamagrostis guatemalensis

Calamagrostis guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan reed, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are adorned with small, feathery flowers.

Calamagrostis guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
