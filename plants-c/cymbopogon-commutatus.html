Cymbopogon commutatus

Cymbopogon commutatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic and contain essential oils. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and thrives in well-drained soils. It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Its botanical features make it valuable in both horticultural and commercial applications.
