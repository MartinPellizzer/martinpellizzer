Chenopodium pseudomicrophyllum

Chenopodium pseudomicrophyllum, commonly known as the tiny-leaf goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States.

Chenopodium pseudomicrophyllum is often found growing in sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
