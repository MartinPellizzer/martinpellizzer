Cocculus laevis

Cocculus laevis, commonly known as the smooth cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody vines and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright orange or red berries. Its roots and stems contain various alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
