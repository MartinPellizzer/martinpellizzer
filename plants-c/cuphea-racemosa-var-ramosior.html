Cuphea racemosa var. ramosior

Cuphea racemosa var. ramosior, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually pink to red in color and are arranged in racemes, which are elongated clusters of flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
