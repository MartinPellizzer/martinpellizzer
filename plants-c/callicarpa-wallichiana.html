Callicarpa wallichiana

Callicarpa wallichiana, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly the striking color of its fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa wallichiana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
