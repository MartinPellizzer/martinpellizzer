Copiapoa decorticans

Copiapoa decorticans, commonly known as the "Cocopops" or "Copiapoa cactus," is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick trunk and produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It has a unique ability to store water in its stem, allowing it to survive in the arid environment.

Copiapoa decorticans is also noted for its slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
