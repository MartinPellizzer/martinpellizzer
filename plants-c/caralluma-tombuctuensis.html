Caralluma tombuctuensis

Caralluma tombuctuensis, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It produces small, yellowish-green berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is often studied for its potential in arid land restoration and medicinal applications.
