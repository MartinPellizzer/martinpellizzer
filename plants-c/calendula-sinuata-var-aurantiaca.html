Calendula sinuata var. aurantiaca

Calendula sinuata var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden wedge calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, wedge-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
