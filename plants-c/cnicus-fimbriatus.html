Cnicus fimbriatus

Cnicus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
