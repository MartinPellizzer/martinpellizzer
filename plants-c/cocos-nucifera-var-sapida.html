Cocos nucifera var. sapida

Cocos nucifera var. sapida, commonly known as the sweet coconut or sapid coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its high sugar content in the coconut water.

This variety is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The fruit produces a larger, sweeter liquid compared to the typical coconut, making it popular in certain culinary traditions. It is often cultivated for its edible water and flesh, which are used in food and beverages.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable crop in coastal and island communities.
