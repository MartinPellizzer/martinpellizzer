Cornus alba var. elata

Cornus alba var. elata, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more vivid in winter, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
