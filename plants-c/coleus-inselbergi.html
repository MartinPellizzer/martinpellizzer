Coleus inselbergi

Coleus inselbergi, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a striking plant native to the rocky outcrops of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
