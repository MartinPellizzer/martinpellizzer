Crataegus peruviana

Crataegus peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Crataegus peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
