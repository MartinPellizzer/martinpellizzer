Clematis hirsuta var. glabrescens

Clematis hirsuta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth Clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, though the variety glabrescens is less hairy than the typical hirsuta. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
