Cystopora lotiflora

Cystopora lotiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered bladderwax, is a member of the Cistaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its glandular, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
