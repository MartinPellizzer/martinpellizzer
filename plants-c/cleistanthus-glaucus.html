Cleistanthus glaucus

Cleistanthus glaucus, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that provide dense shade. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cleistanthus glaucus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides.
