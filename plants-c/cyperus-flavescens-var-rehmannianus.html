Cyperus flavescens var. rehmannianus

Cyperus flavescens var. rehmannianus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator plant for wetland environments.
