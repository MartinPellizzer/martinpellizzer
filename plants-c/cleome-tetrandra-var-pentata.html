Cleome tetrandra var. pentata

Cleome tetrandra var. pentata, commonly known as the five-petaled cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while the stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
