Chamaeanthus carinatus

Chamaeanthus carinatus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Chamaeanthus carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
