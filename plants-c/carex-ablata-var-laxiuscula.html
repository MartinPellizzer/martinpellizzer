Carex ablata var. laxiuscula

Carex ablata var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This plant is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
