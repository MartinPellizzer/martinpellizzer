Carex tokioensis

Carex tokioensis, commonly known as Tokyo sedge, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Tokyo sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
