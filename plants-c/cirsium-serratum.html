Cirsium serratum

Cirsium serratum, commonly known as the serrated thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Cirsium serratum typically grows in dry meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
