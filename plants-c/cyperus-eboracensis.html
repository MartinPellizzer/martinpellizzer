Cyperus eboracensis

Cyperus eboracensis, commonly known as the Eboracensis flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes.

Cyperus eboracensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
