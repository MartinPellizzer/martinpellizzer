Crinum × grandiflorum

Crinum × grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant crinum or hybrid crinum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often evergreen, and its bulbs are used for propagation.

Crinum × grandiflorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
