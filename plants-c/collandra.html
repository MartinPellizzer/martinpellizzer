Collandra

Collandra, commonly known as the blueberry plant, is a shrub native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins, particularly vitamin C. Collandra thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
