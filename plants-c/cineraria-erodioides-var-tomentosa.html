Cineraria erodioides var. tomentosa

Cineraria erodioides var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
