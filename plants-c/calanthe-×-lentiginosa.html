Calanthe × lentiginosa

Calanthe × lentiginosa, commonly known as the speckled calanthe, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with dark purple spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
