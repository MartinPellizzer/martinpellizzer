Cestrum luteoflorum

Cestrum luteoflorum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds, and the plant is known for its fragrant, night-blooming flowers.

Cestrum luteoflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
