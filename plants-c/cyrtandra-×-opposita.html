Cyrtandra × opposita

Cyrtandra × opposita, commonly known as the hybrid beautyflower, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra uniflora and Cyrtandra tetragona. This species is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
