Cubeba crassipes

Cubeba crassipes, commonly known as wild cubeba, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaves, and a distinctive scent due to the presence of essential oils.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
