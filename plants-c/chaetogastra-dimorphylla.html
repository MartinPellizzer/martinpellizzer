Chaetogastra dimorphylla

Chaetogastra dimorphylla, commonly known as the two-form grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive two-form leaves, which can appear different depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
