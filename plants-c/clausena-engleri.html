Clausena engleri

Clausena engleri, commonly known as the Malabar cowrie or wild mango, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when mature.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy aril, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
