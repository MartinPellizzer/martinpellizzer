Calamus mollis

Calamus mollis, commonly known as the soft reed or sweet reed, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams. The plant features long, slender, grass-like leaves that can reach several feet in length. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Aroid family.

Calamus mollis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
