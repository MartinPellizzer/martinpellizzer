Cereus coerulescens f. landbeckii

Cereus coerulescens f. landbeckii, commonly known as the Blue Cereus, is a unique cactus species characterized by its striking blue-gray spines and pale green stems.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its growth habit is typically columnar, with a slow growth rate and a tendency to form clumps over time.

The f. landbeckii form is distinguished by its more elongated stems and reduced spination compared to the typical species.
