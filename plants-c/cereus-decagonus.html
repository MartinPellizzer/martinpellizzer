Cereus decagonus

Cereus decagonus, commonly known as the decagon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure that gives it its name. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its spines are short and often covered in fine, white hairs.
