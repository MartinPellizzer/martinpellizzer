Cyperus unioloides f. reductus

Cyperus unioloides f. reductus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Cyperus.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its botanical features include reduced leaf blades and a distinct, narrow stem structure.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
