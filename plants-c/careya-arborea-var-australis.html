Careya arborea var. australis

Careya arborea var. australis, commonly known as the Australian careya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
