Cordia uncinulata

Cordia uncinulata, commonly known as the hooked cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and berries.
