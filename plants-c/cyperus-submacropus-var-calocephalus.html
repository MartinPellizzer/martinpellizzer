Cyperus submacropus var. calocephalus

Cyperus submacropus var. calocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common species in marshy areas and along waterways. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly recurved at the edges.

The flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are adapted for wind pollination.
