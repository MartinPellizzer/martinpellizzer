Caruelina pohliana

Caruelina pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
