Campanula carpatica riverslea

Campanula carpatica riverslea, commonly known as the Canterbury bells, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It features bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy leaves that are oval and slightly pointed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
