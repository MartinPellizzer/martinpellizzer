Croton rhamnifolius var. heliotropiifolius

Croton rhamnifolius var. heliotropiifolius, commonly known as the sunflower croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of the sunflower, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
