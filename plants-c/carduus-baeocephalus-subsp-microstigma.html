Carduus baeocephalus subsp. microstigma

Carduus baeocephalus subsp.

microstigma, commonly known as the narrow-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a spiny, upright stem with a grayish-green color and is covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical head, typical of the Compositae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a rough, serrated texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
