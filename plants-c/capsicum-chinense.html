Capsicum chinense

Capsicum chinense, commonly known as the chili pepper or habanero, is a species native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant colors, ranging from yellow to red. The plant produces small, often fiery fruits that are rich in capsaicin, the compound responsible for their heat. Capsicum chinense thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include lobed leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.
