Cistus albidus var. parvifolius

Cistus albidus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, silver-green foliage with glandular hairs that release a distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife gardens.
