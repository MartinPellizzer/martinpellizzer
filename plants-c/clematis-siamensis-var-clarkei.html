Clematis siamensis var. clarkei

Clematis siamensis var. clarkei, commonly known as the Siam Clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
