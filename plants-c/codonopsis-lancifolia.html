Codonopsis lancifolia

Codonopsis lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginseng, is a perennial herb native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Codonopsis lancifolia is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
