Carex scopulorum var. scopulorum

Carex scopulorum var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in stream banks, wet meadows, and forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
