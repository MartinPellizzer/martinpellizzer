Croton andreeus

Croton andreeus, commonly known as the Andree's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Croton andreeus typically grows in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it is toxic if ingested.
