Cynoglossum borbonicum var. adscendens

Cynoglossum borbonicum var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
