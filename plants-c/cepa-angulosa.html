Cepa angulosa

Cepa angulosa, commonly known as the angular onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clustered, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cepa angulosa is known for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
