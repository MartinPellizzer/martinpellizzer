Cardamine flexuosa var. kamtschatica

Cardamine flexuosa var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschata mountain water-cress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive wavy margin, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
