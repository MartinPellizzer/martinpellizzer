Caladenia × exoleta

Caladenia × exoleta, commonly known as the hybrid spider orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Australia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Caladenia flava and Caladenia latifolia, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically features a single, slender stem with a single or two flowers, each resembling a spider in appearance due to their elongated, narrow petals. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with red or brown markings, and it blooms during the autumn season.

Caladenia × exoleta is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
