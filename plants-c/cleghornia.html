Cleghornia

Cleghornia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, wooded areas. Cleghornia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical name reflects its historical significance in herbal medicine.
