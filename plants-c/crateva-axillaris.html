Crateva axillaris

Crateva axillaris, commonly known as the Indian butter bean or the wild bean plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
