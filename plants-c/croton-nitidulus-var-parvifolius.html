Croton nitidulus var. parvifolius

Croton nitidulus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are small and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
