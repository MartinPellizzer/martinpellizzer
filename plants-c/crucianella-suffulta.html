Crucianella suffulta

Crucianella suffulta, commonly known as the bladder nut, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, inflated, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
