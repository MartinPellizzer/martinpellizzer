Carlina onopordifolia

Carlina onopordifolia, commonly known as the lesser hawk's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing a showy display of yellow petals surrounding a central disk.

Carlina onopordifolia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
