Cardamomum sceptrum

Cardamomum sceptrum, commonly known as the sacred cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are elongated and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
