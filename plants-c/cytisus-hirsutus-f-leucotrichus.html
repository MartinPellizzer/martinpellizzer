Cytisus hirsutus f. leucotrichus

Cytisus hirsutus f. leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-hairy broom, is a variant of the common broom characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers.

This shrub is native to parts of Europe and is known for its dense, thorny growth habit. The leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its "hirsutus" designation. The 'leucotrichus' form specifically exhibits a more pronounced white or silver appearance on its foliage and flower bracts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or disturbed areas.
