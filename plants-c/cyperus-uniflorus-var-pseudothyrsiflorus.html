Cyperus uniflorus var. pseudothyrsiflorus

Cyperus uniflorus var. pseudothyrsiflorus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often mistaken for grasses, and its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
