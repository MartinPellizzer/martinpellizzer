Cyperus dietrichiae var. fractuosus

Cyperus dietrichiae var. fractuosus, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
