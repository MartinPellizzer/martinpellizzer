Caladenia doutchiae

Caladenia doutchiae, commonly known as the Drouth's Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Western Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a dark purple or brownish hue. The flower's intricate structure includes a labellum that resembles a spider, giving it its common name. It typically blooms during the cooler months, from June to August.

This plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
