Carex erythrovaginata

Carex erythrovaginata, commonly known as the red-vagina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex erythrovaginata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
