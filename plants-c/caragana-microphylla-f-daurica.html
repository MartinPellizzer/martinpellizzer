Caragana microphylla f. daurica

Caragana microphylla f. daurica, commonly known as the Daurian shrub or dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy structure. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible but tart.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rocky or dry soils.
