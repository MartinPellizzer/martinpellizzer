Cistus albanicus

Cistus albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian rockrose, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
