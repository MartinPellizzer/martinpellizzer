Convolvulus hermannioides

Convolvulus hermannioides, commonly known as Hermann's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
