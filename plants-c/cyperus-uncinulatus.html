Cyperus uncinulatus

Cyperus uncinulatus, commonly known as the hooked flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus uncinulatus is characterized by its distinctive hooked awns on the seeds, which aid in seed dispersal.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
