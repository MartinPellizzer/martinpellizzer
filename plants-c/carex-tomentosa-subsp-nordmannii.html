Carex tomentosa subsp. nordmannii

Carex tomentosa subsp.

nordmannii, commonly known as the Nordmann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.

The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
