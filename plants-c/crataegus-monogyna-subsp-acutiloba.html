Crataegus monogyna subsp. acutiloba

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

acutiloba, commonly known as the sharplobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.
