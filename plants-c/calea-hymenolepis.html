Calea hymenolepis

Calea hymenolepis, commonly known as the sacred flower or angel's trumpet, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish flowers that resemble trumpets, which open at night. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Calea hymenolepis is also appreciated for its cultural significance in traditional practices.
