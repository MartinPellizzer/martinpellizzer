Cassia enneaphylla

Cassia enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with nine leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia enneaphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
