Clerodendrum griffithianum

Clerodendrum griffithianum, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.
