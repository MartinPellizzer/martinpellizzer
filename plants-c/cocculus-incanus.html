Cocculus incanus

Cocculus incanus, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and trumpet-shaped, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces smooth, spherical berries that turn from green to yellowish as they mature. Its stems are often twining and can reach lengths of several meters, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
