Carex subspathacea f. stricta

Carex subspathacea f. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.

The plant is relatively easy to cultivate and serves as an important food source for various wildlife species.
