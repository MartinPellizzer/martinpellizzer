Cynanchum tetrapterum

Cynanchum tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive milkweed-like appearance and milky sap. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish, arranged in clusters, and are followed by follicles that contain the seeds.

Cynanchum tetrapterum is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
