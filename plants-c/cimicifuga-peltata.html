Cimicifuga peltata

Cimicifuga peltata, commonly known as white bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, spathiform flowers. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are often described as fern-like in appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cimicifuga peltata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
