Citrus × pumila var. sinensis

Citrus × pumila var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mandarin.

It is a small citrus tree native to southern China, characterized by its small, fragrant fruits. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves and produces yellow-orange fruit with a thin, loose rind. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp.

It is often used in culinary applications and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
