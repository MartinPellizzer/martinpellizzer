Cyclosorus grandissimus

Cyclosorus grandissimus, commonly known as the giant cyclosorus, is a rare and distinctive member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern with a creeping rhizome that produces both fertile and sterile fronds. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
