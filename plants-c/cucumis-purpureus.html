Cucumis purpureus

Cucumis purpureus, commonly known as the purple cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, purple-fruited gourds that are typically harvested when immature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.
