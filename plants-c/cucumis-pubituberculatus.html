Cucumis pubituberculatus

Cucumis pubituberculatus, commonly known as the hairy warty cucumber, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and warty, spiny fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are generally small, with a thick, bitter rind and a juicy, edible interior.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
