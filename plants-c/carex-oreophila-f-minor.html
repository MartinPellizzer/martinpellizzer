Carex oreophila f. minor

Carex oreophila f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf mountain sedge, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its delicate form and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
