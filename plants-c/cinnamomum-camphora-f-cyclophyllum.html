Cinnamomum camphora f. cyclophyllum

Cinnamomum camphora f. cyclophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a unique botanical variant of the camphor laurel.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and distinctive elliptical leaves with a rough, textured upper surface. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in humid, subtropical climates. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.
