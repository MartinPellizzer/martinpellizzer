Chuquiraga hystrix

Chuquiraga hystrix, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globular stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Chuquiraga hystrix is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
