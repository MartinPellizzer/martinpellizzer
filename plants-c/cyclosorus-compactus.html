Cyclosorus compactus

Cyclosorus compactus, commonly known as the small water fern, is a small aquatic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and streams. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with finely divided, feather-like fronds. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Cyclosorus compactus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality through its biological activity.
