Capurodendron oblongifolium

Capurodendron oblongifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood.

It is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil, characterized by its woody structure and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in musical instrument making, it has been subject to conservation concerns.
