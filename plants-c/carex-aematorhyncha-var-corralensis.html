Carex aematorhyncha var. corralensis

Carex aematorhyncha var. corralensis, commonly known as the Corralensis sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
