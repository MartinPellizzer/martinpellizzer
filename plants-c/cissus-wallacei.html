Cissus wallacei

Cissus wallacei, commonly known as the rock fig or stonecrop, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically succulent and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces.

Its ability to climb and its unique foliage make it a distinctive ornamental plant.
