Calytrix billardierei

Calytrix billardierei, commonly known as the pink mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
