Calea ternifolia

Calea ternifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the evening," is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral structure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Calea ternifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its purported healing benefits.
