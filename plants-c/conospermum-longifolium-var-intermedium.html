Conospermum longifolium var. intermedium

Conospermum longifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to pink, which appear during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
