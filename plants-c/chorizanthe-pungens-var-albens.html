Chorizanthe pungens var. albens

Chorizanthe pungens var. albens, commonly known as silverleaf jointvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Amaranthaceae family.
