Cardamine bonariensis var. minima

Cardamine bonariensis var. minima, commonly known as the miniature watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated, slender fruits.

This variant is noted for its compact size and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in aquatic gardens.
