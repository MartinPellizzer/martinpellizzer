Chamaesyce capillaris

Chamaesyce capillaris, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
