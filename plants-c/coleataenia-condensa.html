Coleataenia condensa

Coleataenia condensa, commonly known as the African water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in shallow water bodies.

Coleataenia condensa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
