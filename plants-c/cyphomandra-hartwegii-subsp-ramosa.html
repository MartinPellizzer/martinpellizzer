Cyphomandra hartwegii subsp. ramosa

Cyphomandra hartwegii subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the hairy potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, glandular stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

While not cultivated for food, it is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
