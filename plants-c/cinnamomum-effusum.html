Cinnamomum effusum

Cinnamomum effusum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum micranthum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark reddish-brown berries, which contain the seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
