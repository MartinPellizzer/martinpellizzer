Chrysanthemum purpureiflorum

Chrysanthemum purpureiflorum, commonly known as the purpleflower chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features finely divided, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom in late summer and early autumn.
