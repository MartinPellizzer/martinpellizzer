Carex inconspicua f. indecora

Carex inconspicua f. indecora, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage with distinct variegation patterns. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often marked with white or yellow stripes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
