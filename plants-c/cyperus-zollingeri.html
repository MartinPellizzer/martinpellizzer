Cyperus zollingeri

Cyperus zollingeri, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Cyperus zollingeri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
