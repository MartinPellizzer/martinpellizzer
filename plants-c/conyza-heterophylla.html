Conyza heterophylla

Conyza heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from basal rosettes to alternate leaves depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
