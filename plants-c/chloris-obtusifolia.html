Chloris obtusifolia

Chloris obtusifolia, commonly known as broadleaf grass or broad-leaved chloris, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for low-maintenance gardens.
