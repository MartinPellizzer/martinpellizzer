Calamus pertenuis

Calamus pertenuis, commonly known as slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and tall, arching leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Its ornamental value is limited, but it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
