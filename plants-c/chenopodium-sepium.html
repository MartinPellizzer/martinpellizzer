Chenopodium sepium

Chenopodium sepium, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as fields, roadsides, and gardens. It is a cosmopolitan species, widely distributed across temperate regions of the world.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
