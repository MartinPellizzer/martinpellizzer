Coriflora reticulata

Coriflora reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reticulate pattern on the underside, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
