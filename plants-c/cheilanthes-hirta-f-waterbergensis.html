Cheilanthes hirta f. waterbergensis

Cheilanthes hirta f. waterbergensis, commonly known as the Waterberg rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is a perennial species that produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
