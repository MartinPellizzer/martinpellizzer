Clinopodium sachalinense

Clinopodium sachalinense, commonly known as Sakhalin mint, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, including regions of Russia, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance, while its small blue to purple flowers bloom in summer.

Clinopodium sachalinense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
