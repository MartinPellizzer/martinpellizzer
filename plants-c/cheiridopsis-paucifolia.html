Cheiridopsis paucifolia

Cheiridopsis paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded stems covered in small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Cheiridopsis paucifolia is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
