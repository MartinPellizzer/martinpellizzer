Centaurea sergii

Centaurea sergii, commonly known as the Crimean centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Crimea.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Centaurea sergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
