Cassia gaudichaudii

Cassia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia gaudichaudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
