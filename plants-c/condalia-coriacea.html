Condalia coriacea

Condalia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
