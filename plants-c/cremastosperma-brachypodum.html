Cremastosperma brachypodum

Cremastosperma brachypodum, commonly known as the short-footed burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
