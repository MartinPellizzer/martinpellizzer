Cousinia popovii

Cousinia popovii, commonly known as the Armenian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia popovii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
