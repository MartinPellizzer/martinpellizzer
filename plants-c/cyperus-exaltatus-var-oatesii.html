Cyperus exaltatus var. oatesii

Cyperus exaltatus var. oatesii, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

This variety is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
