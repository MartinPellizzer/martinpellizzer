Calamagrostis montevidensis var. linearis

Calamagrostis montevidensis var. linearis, commonly known as the slender marsh reed, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its flowering stems bear panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
