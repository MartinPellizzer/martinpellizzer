Croton hancei var. tsoi

Croton hancei var. tsoi, commonly known as the Tsoi's croton, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring a thick, woody stem with a distinctive reddish-brown bark. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are usually bright green with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
