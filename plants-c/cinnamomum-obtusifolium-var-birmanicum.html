Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. birmanicum

Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. birmanicum, commonly known as Burmese cinnamon or Burmese camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and growth habit.
