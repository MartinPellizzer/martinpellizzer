Crassula volkensii subsp. coleae

Crassula volkensii subsp.

coleae, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, rounded stems that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
