Cuphea potamophila

Cuphea potamophila, commonly known as the river cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often velvety and dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Cuphea potamophila is valued in gardens for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
