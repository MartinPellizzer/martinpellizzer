Camissoniopsis guadalupensis

Camissoniopsis guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Texas and New Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh conditions. Camissoniopsis guadalupensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
