Cotoneaster acuminatus

Cotoneaster acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Cotoneaster acuminatus is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
