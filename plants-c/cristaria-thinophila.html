Cristaria thinophila

Cristaria thinophila, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its stems help support the floating foliage.

Cristaria thinophila plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.
