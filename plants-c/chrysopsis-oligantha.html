Chrysopsis oligantha

Chrysopsis oligantha, commonly known as the slender chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and it is known for its compact growth habit.

Chrysopsis oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
