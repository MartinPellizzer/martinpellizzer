Crataegus tkatschenkoi

Crataegus tkatschenkoi, commonly known as the Katschenko hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not cultivated for human consumption. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
