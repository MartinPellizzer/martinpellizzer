Calanthe caudatilabella

Calanthe caudatilabella, commonly known as the long-labellum calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated labellum that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Calanthe caudatilabella is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
