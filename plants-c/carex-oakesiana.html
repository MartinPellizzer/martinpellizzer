Carex oakesiana

Carex oakesiana, commonly known as Oakes' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are pollinated by wind.
