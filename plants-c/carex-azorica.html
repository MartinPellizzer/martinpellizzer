Carex azorica

Carex azorica, commonly known as the Azorean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex azorica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
