Cerinthe major f. pseudosemipurpurea

Cerinthe major f. pseudosemipurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered horned poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to red, with a distinctive horn-like projection at the base. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
