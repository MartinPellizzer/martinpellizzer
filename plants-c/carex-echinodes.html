Carex echinodes

Carex echinodes, commonly known as the spiky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex echinodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
