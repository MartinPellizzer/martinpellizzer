Centaurea × budaiana

Centaurea × budaiana, commonly known as the Buda hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
