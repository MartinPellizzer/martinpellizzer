Crepis tonkinensis

Crepis tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
