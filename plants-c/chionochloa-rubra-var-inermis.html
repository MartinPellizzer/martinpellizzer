Chionochloa rubra var. inermis

Chionochloa rubra var. inermis, commonly known as the silver tussock grass, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which helps it thrive in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant forms tussocks that are well adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, rocky soils and is an important component of New Zealand's native grassland ecosystems.
