Carex tuckermanii

Carex tuckermanii, commonly known as Tuckerman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex tuckermanii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
