Canarium findens

Canarium findens, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its most notable botanical feature is the hard, aromatic seed enclosed within the fruit, which is used to produce nutmeg and mace.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its economic importance in the spice trade.
