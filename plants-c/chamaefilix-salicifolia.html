Chamaefilix salicifolia

Chamaefilix salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Chamaefilix salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
