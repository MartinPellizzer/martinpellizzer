Cyperus nervosus

Cyperus nervosus, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along water edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense clusters, which are often submerged or floating.

Cyperus nervosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
