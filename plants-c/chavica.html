Chavica

Chavica, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of toothed, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a bright yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed ball with feathery seeds. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has naturalized in many parts of the world.

Chavica is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
