Cladium melanocarpum

Cladium melanocarpum, commonly known as black fruit sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into dark, berry-like fruits, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in saturated soils and plays a crucial role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Cladium melanocarpum is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
