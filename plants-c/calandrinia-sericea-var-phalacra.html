Calandrinia sericea var. phalacra

Calandrinia sericea var. phalacra, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
