Calla ovatifolia

Calla ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calla, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces striking white or pale yellow flowers.

Calla ovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
