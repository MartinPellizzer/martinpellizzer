Ceiba mythica

Ceiba mythica, commonly known as the ceiba tree, is a towering tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bombacaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The tree produces showy, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, fibrous pods containing seeds. Its bark is thick and corky, providing resistance to drought and pests.

The ceiba tree plays a significant role in the ecosystems of tropical rainforests, offering habitat and sustenance to various wildlife species.
