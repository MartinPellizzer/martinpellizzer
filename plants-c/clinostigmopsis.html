Clinostigmopsis

Clinostigmopsis, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a genus of flowering plants native to the southeastern United States.

It consists of two species, Clinostigmopsis calyculata and Clinostigmopsis corymbosa, both characterized by their evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plants typically grow in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially sunny environments. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
