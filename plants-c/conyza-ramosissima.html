Conyza ramosissima

Conyza ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy weed or sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb characterized by its branched stems and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments, thriving in open areas with full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Conyza ramosissima is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
