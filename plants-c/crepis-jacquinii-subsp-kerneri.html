Crepis jacquinii subsp. kerneri

Crepis jacquinii subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as Kern's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the summer months.

The plant has basal leaves that are woolly and heart-shaped, with a compact growth habit. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous terrain.
