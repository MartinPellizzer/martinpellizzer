Crepis biennis var. scabra

Crepis biennis var. scabra, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
