Carex remotispicula

Carex remotispicula, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence.

Carex remotispicula plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
