Clematis heracleifolia var. speciosa

Clematis heracleifolia var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or walls.
