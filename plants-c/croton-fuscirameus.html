Croton fuscirameus

Croton fuscirameus, commonly known as the black-veined croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves with dark green veins contrasting against a lighter background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
