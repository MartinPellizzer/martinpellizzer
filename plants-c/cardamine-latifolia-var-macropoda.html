Cardamine latifolia var. macropoda

Cardamine latifolia var. macropoda, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist woodland areas.

It features small, trifoliate leaves that are broad and slightly heart-shaped, with a peppery aroma. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and is often found in streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
