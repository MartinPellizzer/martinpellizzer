Cogswellia utriculata var. papillata

Cogswellia utriculata var. papillata, commonly known as the Papillate Cogswellia, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in small, rounded tubercles, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
