Carex flava var. viridula

Carex flava var. viridula, commonly known as the green-flowered yellow sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or yellow in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
