Cavendishia smithii

Cavendishia smithii, commonly known as the Smith's Cavendish, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers are often large and come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Cavendishia smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
