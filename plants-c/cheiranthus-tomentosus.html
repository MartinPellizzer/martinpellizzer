Cheiranthus tomentosus

Cheiranthus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-blue leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and dry landscapes.
