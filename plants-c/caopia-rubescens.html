Caopia rubescens

Caopia rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant features vibrant red petals with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice for horticultural displays. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Caopia rubescens is noted for its fragrant blooms, which typically appear during the spring and summer months.
