Crotalaria subdecurrens

Crotalaria subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and elongated. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
