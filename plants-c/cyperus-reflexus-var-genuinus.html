Cyperus reflexus var. genuinus

Cyperus reflexus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes and swamps. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often pale green or brown, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments.

Cyperus reflexus var. genuinus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing wetland soils.
