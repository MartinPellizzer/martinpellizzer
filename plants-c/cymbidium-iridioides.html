Cymbidium iridioides

Cymbidium iridioides, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with tall, erect stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium iridioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
