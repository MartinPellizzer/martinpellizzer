Cristonia biloba subsp. pubescens

Cristonia biloba subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cristonia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its subspecific name "pubescens.". It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
