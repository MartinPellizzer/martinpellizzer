Calceolaria benthamii

Calceolaria benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

Calceolaria benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
