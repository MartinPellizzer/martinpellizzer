Crepis salicifolia

Crepis salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and small, yellowish flower heads that appear in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in dry, sandy soils. It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and blooms from late spring to early summer.

Crepis salicifolia is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gravelly areas.
