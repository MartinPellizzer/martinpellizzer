Copernicia torreana

Copernicia torreana, commonly known as the Mexican palm or the Mexican coconut palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, each containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Copernicia torreana is also valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
