Cryptocarya glaucescens var. camphorata

Cryptocarya glaucescens var. camphorata, commonly known as the camphorwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, camphor-scented leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The wood of this plant is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
