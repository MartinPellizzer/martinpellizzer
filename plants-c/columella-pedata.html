Columella pedata

Columella pedata, commonly known as the spurred columbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and is known for its showy, violet-colored flowers. Columella pedata has a taproot system and produces a cluster of small, green, oval-shaped fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
