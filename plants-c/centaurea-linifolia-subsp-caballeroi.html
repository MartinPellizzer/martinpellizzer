Centaurea linifolia subsp. caballeroi

Centaurea linifolia subsp.

caballeroi, commonly known as the Spanish fennel or sea centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, bright blue or purple flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a grass-like appearance, and it is often found growing in coastal or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
