Cousinia dasylepis

Cousinia dasylepis, commonly known as the Kashmiri fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically pale yellow to white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cousinia dasylepis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
