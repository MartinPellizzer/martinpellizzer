Cirsium monspessulanum var. hispanica

Cirsium monspessulanum var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
