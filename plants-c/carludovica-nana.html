Carludovica nana

Carludovica nana, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf cactus, is a small, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a compact, rounded body with short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its slow growth rate makes it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a container plant.
