Cobana

Cobana, commonly known as the Mexican elderberry, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the genus *Sambucus* and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when fully ripe. Cobana is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
