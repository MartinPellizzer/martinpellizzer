Calceolaria meyeniana subsp. cheiranthoides

Calceolaria meyeniana subsp.

cheiranthoides, commonly known as the "Mexican slipper orchid," is a member of the Campanulaceae family. It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
