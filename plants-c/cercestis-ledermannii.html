Cercestis ledermannii

Cercestis ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in a spike, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Cercestis ledermannii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
