Chorigyne pendula

Chorigyne pendula, commonly known as the hanging chorigyne, is a unique plant species characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features elongated, drooping stems that support clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its delicate structure, it requires careful handling and specific growing conditions to flourish.
