Crataegus erubescens

Crataegus erubescens, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Crataegus erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
