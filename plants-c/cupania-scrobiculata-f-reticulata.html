Cupania scrobiculata f. reticulata

Cupania scrobiculata f. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated cupania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive reticulate venation on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
