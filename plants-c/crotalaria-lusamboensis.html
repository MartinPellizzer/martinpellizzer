Crotalaria lusamboensis

Crotalaria lusamboensis, commonly known as the Lusambo cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiny pod, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria lusamboensis is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
