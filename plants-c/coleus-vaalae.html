Coleus vaalae

Coleus vaalae, commonly known as the Vaal's coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from red to purple and green. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with square stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and tubular, appearing in clusters, and are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Coleus vaalae thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
