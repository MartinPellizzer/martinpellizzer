Cyperus pseuderemicus

Cyperus pseuderemicus, commonly known as the false papyrus, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus pseuderemicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
