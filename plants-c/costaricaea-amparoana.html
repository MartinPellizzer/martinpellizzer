Costaricaea amparoana

Costaricaea amparoana, commonly known as the Amparo woodstar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the humid lowland forests of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark green berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
