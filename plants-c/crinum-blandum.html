Crinum blandum

Crinum blandum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spider lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features strap-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a strong, spicy scent, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Crinum blandum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
