Cupressus lawsoniana f. stewartii

Cupressus lawsoniana f. stewartii, commonly known as the Stewart's lacey cypress, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its finely textured, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and typically develops a narrow, columnar form. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

This cultivar is valued for its unique aesthetic and is often used in horticultural displays.
