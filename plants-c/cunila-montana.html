Cunila montana

Cunila montana, commonly known as mountain caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oblong fruits called mericarps, which are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cunila montana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
