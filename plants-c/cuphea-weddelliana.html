Cuphea weddelliana

Cuphea weddelliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in warm climates.
