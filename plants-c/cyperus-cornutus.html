Cyperus cornutus

Cyperus cornutus, commonly known as spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along pond edges. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually green and arranged in two rows around the stem.

Cyperus cornutus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
