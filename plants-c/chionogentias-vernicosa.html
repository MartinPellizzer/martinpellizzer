Chionogentias vernicosa

Chionogentias vernicosa, commonly known as the silverleaf snowbush, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Chionogentias vernicosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
