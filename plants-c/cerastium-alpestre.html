Cerastium alpestre

Cerastium alpestre, commonly known as alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are often succulent, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Cerastium alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
