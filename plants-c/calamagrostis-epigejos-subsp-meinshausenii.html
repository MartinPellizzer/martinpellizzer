Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. meinshausenii

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp.

meinshausenii, commonly known as the meinhausean reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

This subspecies is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to wetland gardens.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
