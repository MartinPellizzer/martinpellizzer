Cerasus incana var. latifolia

Cerasus incana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
