Centaurea polypodiifolia

Centaurea polypodiifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, fern-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in summer. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are typically arranged in narrow clusters and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea polypodiifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
