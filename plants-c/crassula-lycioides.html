Crassula lycioides

Crassula lycioides, commonly known as the stone rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Crassula lycioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
