Cucurbita pepo convar. clypeata

Cucurbita pepo convar. clypeata, commonly known as the scallop gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive warty, ridged skin.

The plant is a trailing or climbing vine that produces small, typically yellow or orange flowers. Its fruits are usually oblong or oval, with a thick, ribbed rind and a sweet, edible flesh. It is native to the Americas and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
