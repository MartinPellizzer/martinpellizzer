Cirsium squarrosum

Cirsium squarrosum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Cirsium squarrosum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
