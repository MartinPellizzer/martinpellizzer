Cotula montevidensis

Cotula montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces tiny yellow flowers that are often arranged in clusters, and it is known for its prolonged blooming period.

Cotula montevidensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
