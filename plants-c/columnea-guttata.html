Columnea guttata

Columnea guttata, commonly known as the spotted flame or red-flowered columnea, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and its stems are often hairy. Columnea guttata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses due to its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
