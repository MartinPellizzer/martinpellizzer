Coleosanthus verbenaceus

Coleosanthus verbenaceus, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family, which includes plants like verbena and ligustrum. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Coleosanthus verbenaceus is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
