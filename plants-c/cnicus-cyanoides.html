Cnicus cyanoides

Cnicus cyanoides, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cnicus cyanoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
