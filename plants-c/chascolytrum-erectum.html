Chascolytrum erectum

Chascolytrum erectum, commonly known as the Chilean onion, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
