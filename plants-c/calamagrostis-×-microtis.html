Calamagrostis × microtis

Calamagrostis × microtis, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces panicles of fluffy, white to pale blue florets that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis × microtis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
