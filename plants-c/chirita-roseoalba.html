Chirita roseoalba

Chirita roseoalba, commonly known as the white-flowered chirita, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred corolla. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
