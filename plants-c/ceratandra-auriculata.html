Ceratandra auriculata

Ceratandra auriculata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their fragrant scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
