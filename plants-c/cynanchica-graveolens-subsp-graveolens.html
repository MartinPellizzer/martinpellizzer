Cynanchica graveolens subsp. graveolens

Cynanchica graveolens subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the sweet vernal grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in spikes, blooming in early spring.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
