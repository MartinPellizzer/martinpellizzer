Cleome gaudichaudii

Cleome gaudichaudii, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like blooms that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, are borne on long, slender pedicels.

Cleome gaudichaudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
