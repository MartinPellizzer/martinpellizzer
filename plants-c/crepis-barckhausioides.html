Crepis barckhausioides

Crepis barckhausioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that develop into achene fruits. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It is a perennial species with a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
