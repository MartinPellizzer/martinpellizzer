Centaurea paui

Centaurea paui, commonly known as the Pau's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often hairy. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Centaurea paui is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
