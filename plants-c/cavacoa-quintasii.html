Cavacoa quintasii

Cavacoa quintasii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance.

Cavacoa quintasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant color and elegant growth habit.
