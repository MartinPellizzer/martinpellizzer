Cyperus lipocarphioides

Cyperus lipocarphioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

Cyperus lipocarphioides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
