Cayratia calcicola

Cayratia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone vine, is a climbing plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Cayratia calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky and degraded landscapes.
