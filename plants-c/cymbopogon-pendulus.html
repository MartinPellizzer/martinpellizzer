Cymbopogon pendulus

Cymbopogon pendulus, commonly known as the hanging grass or pendulous lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping inflorescences and aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like scent. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
