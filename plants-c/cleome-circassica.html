Cleome circassica

Cleome circassica, commonly known as the spider flower or cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, preferring warm climates.

Cleome circassica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
