Cornus hemsleyi var. longistyla

Cornus hemsleyi var. longistyla, commonly known as the longistyla dogwood, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
