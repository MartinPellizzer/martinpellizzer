Campanula radicosa

Campanula radicosa, commonly known as the root bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Campanula radicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
