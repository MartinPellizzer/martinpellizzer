Clematis chrysocarpa var. poggei

Clematis chrysocarpa var. poggei, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range from white to pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
