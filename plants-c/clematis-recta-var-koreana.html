Clematis recta var. koreana

Clematis recta var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
