Clematis angustifolia

Clematis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a narrow, lance-shaped appearance.

Clematis angustifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
