Chondrorhyncha amabilis

Chondrorhyncha amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant blossoms that bloom in the early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
