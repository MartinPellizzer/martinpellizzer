Cordemoya subpeltata

Cordemoya subpeltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cordemoyá, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically white or pale green, with a distinct scent.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
