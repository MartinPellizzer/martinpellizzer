Campylotropis henryi

Campylotropis henryi, commonly known as Henry's bentweed, is a perennial leguminous plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Campylotropis henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
