Cuphea thymoides var. saturejoides

Cuphea thymoides var. saturejoides, commonly known as the flame wheel plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular red flowers arranged in whorls around the stems, creating a unique and eye-catching display. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens due to its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
