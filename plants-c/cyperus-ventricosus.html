Cyperus ventricosus

Cyperus ventricosus, commonly known as the globe thistle or swamp bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, spiky flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its stems can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Cyperus ventricosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
