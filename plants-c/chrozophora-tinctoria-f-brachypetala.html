Chrozophora tinctoria f. brachypetala

Chrozophora tinctoria f. brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petioled dyer's weed, is a variant of the common dyer's weed.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. It is characterized by its shorter petioles compared to the typical form, which is a distinguishing feature of this foliage.

This plant is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
