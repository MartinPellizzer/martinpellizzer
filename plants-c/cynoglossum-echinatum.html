Cynoglossum echinatum

Cynoglossum echinatum, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that resemble small bells, typically ranging in color from purple to blue. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Cynoglossum echinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
