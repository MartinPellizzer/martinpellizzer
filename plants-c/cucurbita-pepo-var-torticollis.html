Cucurbita pepo var. torticollis

Cucurbita pepo var. torticollis, commonly known as the twisted gourd, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted growth pattern and thick, warty skin. The plant typically produces small, elongated fruits with a rough, textured surface. It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
