Chenopodium klinggraeffii

Chenopodium klinggraeffii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used as a food source by both wildlife and humans.
