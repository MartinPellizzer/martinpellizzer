Cachrys macrocarpa

Cachrys macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited silverleaf, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Cachrys macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
