Carex gynandra var. porteri

Carex gynandra var. porteri, commonly known as African water plantain, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It has rhizomatous growth and can form dense mats, making it an effective colonizer of disturbed wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.
