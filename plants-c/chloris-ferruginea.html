Chloris ferruginea

Chloris ferruginea, commonly known as red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown seed heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Chloris ferruginea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its visual appeal.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
