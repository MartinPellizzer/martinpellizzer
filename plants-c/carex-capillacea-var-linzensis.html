Carex capillacea var. linzensis

Carex capillacea var. linzensis, commonly known as the Linzenis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
