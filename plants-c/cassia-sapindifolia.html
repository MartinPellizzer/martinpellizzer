Cassia sapindifolia

Cassia sapindifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia sapindifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
