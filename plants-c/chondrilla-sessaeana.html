Chondrilla sessaeana

Chondrilla sessaeana, commonly known as the Socotra candelabra, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching structure that resembles a candle, with long, thin stems and clustered leaves. The plant is adapted to the arid and harsh conditions of its native habitat, showcasing remarkable resilience. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is notable for its striking appearance.

Due to its ecological significance and rarity, it is considered a conservation priority in its native region.
