Conyza bakeri

Conyza bakeri, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters tall, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Conyza bakeri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
