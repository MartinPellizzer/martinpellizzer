Cyathea vaupelii

Cyathea vaupelii, commonly known as the Vaupel's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. The plant has large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea vaupelii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
