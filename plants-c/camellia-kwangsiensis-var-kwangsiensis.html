Camellia kwangsiensis var. kwangsiensis

Camellia kwangsiensis var. kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi camellia, is a species native to southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils under shaded conditions. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
