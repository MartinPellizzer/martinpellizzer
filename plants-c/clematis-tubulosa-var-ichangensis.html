Clematis tubulosa var. ichangensis

Clematis tubulosa var. ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers with five petals, typically in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical study.
