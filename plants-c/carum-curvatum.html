Carum curvatum

Carum curvatum, commonly known as curled caraway, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. Its seeds, which are oval and aromatic, are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The leaves are finely divided and have a mild, carroty scent when crushed.
