Carex grisea var. rigida

Carex grisea var. rigida, commonly known as rigid gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
