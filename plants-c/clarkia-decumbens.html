Clarkia decumbens

Clarkia decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and grasslands.

Clarkia decumbens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
