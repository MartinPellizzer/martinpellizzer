Centaurea amara var. lanuginosa

Centaurea amara var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the lanuginous cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance, and its flowers are usually pink or purple.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
