Cassebeeria beccariana

Cassebeeria beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian's cassia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
