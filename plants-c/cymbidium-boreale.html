Cymbidium boreale

Cymbidium boreale, commonly known as the northern orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Cymbidium boreale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in traditional Chinese horticulture.
