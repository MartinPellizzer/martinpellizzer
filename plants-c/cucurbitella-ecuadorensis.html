Cucurbitella ecuadorensis

Cucurbitella ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian horned melon, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces distinctive, spiky, green fruits that resemble a horned melon, with a thick, waxy skin. Its flowers are typically yellow with five lobed petals, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is edible, though its taste and texture vary, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
