Callicarpa elegans

Callicarpa elegans, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance, turning red in the autumn. It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in moist environments.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant flowers and ability to attract wildlife, particularly birds.
