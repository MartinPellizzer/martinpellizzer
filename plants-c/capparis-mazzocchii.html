Capparis mazzocchii

Capparis mazzocchii, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a species of flowering plant in the Capparaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, and thrives in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by green, edible capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are succulent and can be used in cooking, similar to other caper species.

Capparis mazzocchii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
