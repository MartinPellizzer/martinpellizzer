Choerospondias axillaris var. pubinervis

Choerospondias axillaris var. pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined Chinese gall tree, is a variant of the Chinese gall tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with compound leaves that have a distinctive hairy texture on the veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red fruits that are edible for some bird species. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental purposes in tropical and subtropical regions.

The variant is noted for its unique foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value.
