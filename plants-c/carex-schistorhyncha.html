Carex schistorhyncha

Carex schistorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks, it thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
