Centaurea paniculata subsp. ochrolopha

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

ochrolopha, commonly known as the redcentaurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often growing in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
