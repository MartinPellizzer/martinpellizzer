Cleome tenuis

Cleome tenuis, commonly known as slender cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in loose, terminal inflorescences. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Cleome tenuis is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
