Chrysopogon fulvus var. serrulatus

Chrysopogon fulvus var. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated false grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
