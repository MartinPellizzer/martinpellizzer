Cirsium legrandii

Cirsium legrandii, commonly known as the LeGrand's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cirsium legrandii is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
