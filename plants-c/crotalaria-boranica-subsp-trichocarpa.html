Crotalaria boranica subsp. trichocarpa

Crotalaria boranica subsp.

trichocarpa, commonly known as the African spurred orchid, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that resemble orchids, with vibrant colors ranging from yellow to orange.

The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
