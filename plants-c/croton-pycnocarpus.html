Croton pycnocarpus

Croton pycnocarpus, commonly known as the dense-fruited croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking, colorful floral bracts that range from red to orange. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically orange or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, exotic foliage.
