Campanula persicifolia subsp. persicifolia

Campanula persicifolia subsp.

persicifolia, commonly known as the peach-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive peach-like scent.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and attractive foliage.
