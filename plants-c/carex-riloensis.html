Carex riloensis

Carex riloensis, commonly known as the Riloensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in the Balkans.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles.

Carex riloensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
