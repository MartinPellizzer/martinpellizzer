Carlina corymbosa var. corymbosa

Carlina corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in waste areas. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture and are arranged in a basal rosette.
