Crossopetalum ternifolium subsp. ternifolium

Crossopetalum ternifolium subsp.

ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved crossopetalum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval to elliptical leaflets arranged in a whorl at the ends of the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
