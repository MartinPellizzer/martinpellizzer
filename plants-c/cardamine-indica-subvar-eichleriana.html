Cardamine indica subvar. eichleriana

Cardamine indica subvar. eichleriana, commonly known as the Eichler's watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a peppery, aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
