Crataegus lalandei

Crataegus lalandei, commonly known as the Laland's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological importance.

Crataegus lalandei is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
