Calophyllum pustulatum

Calophyllum pustulatum, commonly known as the pustular calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
