Cyperus scoparius

Cyperus scoparius, commonly known as spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, triangular stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus scoparius is adapted to moist or aquatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
