Crossoglossa longissima

Crossoglossa longissima, commonly known as the long-stalked cross-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
