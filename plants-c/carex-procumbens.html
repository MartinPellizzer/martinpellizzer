Carex procumbens

Carex procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-parted flowers.

Carex procumbens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
