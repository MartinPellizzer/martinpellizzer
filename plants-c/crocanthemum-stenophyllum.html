Crocanthemum stenophyllum

Crocanthemum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or violet. It is often found in coastal or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
