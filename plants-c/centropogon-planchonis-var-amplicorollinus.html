Centropogon planchonis var. amplicorollinus

Centropogon planchonis var. amplicorollinus, commonly known as the broad-leaf centropogon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, attract various pollinators including bees and hummingbirds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
