Corylus avellana var. sylvestris

Corylus avellana var. sylvestris, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features alternate, simple, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
