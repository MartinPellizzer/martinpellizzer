Carex racemosa

Carex racemosa, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces erect, spiky inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or damp environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life further highlights its ecological significance.
