Centaurium erythraea subsp. apertum

Centaurium erythraea subsp.

apertum, commonly known as the open-centred centaury, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant grows in grasslands, rocky slopes, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
