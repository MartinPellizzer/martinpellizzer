Crepis mollis

Crepis mollis, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features small, pale green leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Crepis mollis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
