Cytisus rhodopeus var. heterophyllus

Cytisus rhodopeus var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the Rhodope shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, exhibiting a compact growth form. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
