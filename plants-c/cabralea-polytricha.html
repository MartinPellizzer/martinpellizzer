Cabralea polytricha

Cabralea polytricha, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or cabralea.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to Brazil, particularly in the Atlantic Forest region. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. Its wood is highly valued for its density and rich color, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now listed as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
