Crataegus × oxyacanthoides

Crataegus × oxyacanthoides, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacanthoides, characterized by its thorny branches and showy pink to white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
