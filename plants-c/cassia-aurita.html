Cassia aurita

Cassia aurita, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Cassia aurita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
