Crotalaria argenteotomentosa subsp. argenteotomentosa

Crotalaria argenteotomentosa subsp.

argenteotomentosa, commonly known as silver-tomentose cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray pubescent leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop make it of interest in horticulture and agriculture.
