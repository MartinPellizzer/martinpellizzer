Carriella

Carriella, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often adorned with bright, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter, producing vibrant pink, red, or orange flowers that add a festive touch to indoor spaces. Carriella thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a favored choice for both ornamental and decorative purposes.
