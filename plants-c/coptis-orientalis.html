Coptis orientalis

Coptis orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its yellow rhizomes, which contain the alkaloid berberine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically important.
