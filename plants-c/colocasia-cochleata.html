Colocasia cochleata

Colocasia cochleata, commonly known as the elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Its foliage is often used in landscaping for its dramatic visual impact.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
