Crataegus pyracantha var. crenulata

Crataegus pyracantha var. crenulata, commonly known as the Crenate Firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright red berries that persist into winter. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely serrated margins that give it its specific epithet "crenulata.". It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and winter interest.
