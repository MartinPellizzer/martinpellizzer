Camissonia lewisii

Camissonia lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval and covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Camissonia lewisii is often found in coastal and inland regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
