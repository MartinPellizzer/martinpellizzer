Carduus baeocephalus subsp. baeocephalus

Carduus baeocephalus subsp.

baeocephalus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a dense, purple to pink flower head composed of many small florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

The species is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
