Cattleya wittigiana var. longifolia

Cattleya wittigiana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered Cattleya, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with a distinctive red lip. The plant is an epiphyte, often found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its striking blooms and resilience in cultivation.
