Crucifera coerulea

Crucifera coerulea, commonly known as the blue mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to wet environments.
