Crepis monrealensis

Crepis monrealensis, commonly known as the Monreal's mouse-ear, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It is a short-lived perennial, often reaching heights of only a few inches.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
