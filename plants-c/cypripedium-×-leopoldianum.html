Cypripedium × leopoldianum

Cypripedium × leopoldianum, commonly known as the Leopold's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with a contrasting yellow or green base.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
