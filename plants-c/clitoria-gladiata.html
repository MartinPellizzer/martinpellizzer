Clitoria gladiata

Clitoria gladiata, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which are arranged in racemes. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are rich in anthocyanins, giving them their vibrant color and making them popular in herbal teas.

Clitoria gladiata is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
