Chamaecrista leonardae

Chamaecrista leonardae, commonly known as Leonard's ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
