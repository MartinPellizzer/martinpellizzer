Carex hyperborea f. pumila

Carex hyperborea f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Arctic sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
