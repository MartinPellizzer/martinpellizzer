Cephaelis tomentosa

Cephaelis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly celandine, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cephaelis tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
