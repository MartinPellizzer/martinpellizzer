Carlina corymbosa subsp. hispanica

Carlina corymbosa subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple or blue flower heads.

The plant produces a dense cluster of small yellow flowers surrounded by bracts that form a distinctive crown-like structure. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The leaves are typically gray-green with a velvety texture and are covered in fine hairs.
