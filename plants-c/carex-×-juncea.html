Carex × juncea

Carex × juncea, commonly known as jointed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its distinctive jointed stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex × juncea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
