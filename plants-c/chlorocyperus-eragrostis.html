Chlorocyperus eragrostis

Chlorocyperus eragrostis, commonly known as yellow flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to yellow-green in color. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
