Carex contigua f. pseudoguestphalica

Carex contigua f. pseudoguestphalica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a sedge species characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and establish dense colonies. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
