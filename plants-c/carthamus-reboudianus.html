Carthamus reboudianus

Carthamus reboudianus, commonly known as the Persian safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in coarse hairs and produces vibrant yellow or red flowers with five petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in dyeing and medicine.
