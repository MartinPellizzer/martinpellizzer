Cirsium campylon

Cirsium campylon, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, prickly edges, and its flowers are purple to pink, forming dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Cirsium campylon plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in habitats with well-drained soils.
