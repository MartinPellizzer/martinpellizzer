Cryptocarya obliqua

Cryptocarya obliqua, commonly known as the oblique cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

Cryptocarya obliqua is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
