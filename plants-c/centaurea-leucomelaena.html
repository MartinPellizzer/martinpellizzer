Centaurea leucomelaena

Centaurea leucomelaena, commonly known as the white-veined centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are pale blue to white, with prominent yellow central disks, and it produces bristly, spiny seed heads.
