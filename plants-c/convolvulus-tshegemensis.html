Convolvulus tshegemensis

Convolvulus tshegemensis, commonly known as the Tshege Mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
