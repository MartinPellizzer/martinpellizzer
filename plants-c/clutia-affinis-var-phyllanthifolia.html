Clutia affinis var. phyllanthifolia

Clutia affinis var. phyllanthifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
