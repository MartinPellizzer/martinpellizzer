Capparis leucophylla

Capparis leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible but have a sharp, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Capparis leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
