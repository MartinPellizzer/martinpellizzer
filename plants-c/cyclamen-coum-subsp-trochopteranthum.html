Cyclamen coum subsp. trochopteranthum

Cyclamen coum subsp.

trochopteranthum, commonly known as the Turkish cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique flower shape, which is more elongated compared to other cyclamen species.
