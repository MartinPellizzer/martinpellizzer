Cissus cuneifolia

Cissus cuneifolia, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, translucent dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus cuneifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
