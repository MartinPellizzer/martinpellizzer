Calcearia minuta

Calcearia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf snowball vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a trailing or climbing plant with small, white, spherical flowers that resemble miniature snowballs. The plant features delicate, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its botanical appeal lies in its unique floral display and compact growth habit.
