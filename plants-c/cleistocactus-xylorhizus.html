Cleistocactus xylorhizus

Cleistocactus xylorhizus, commonly known as the red-ribbed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pink spines. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening. It is well adapted to desert conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
