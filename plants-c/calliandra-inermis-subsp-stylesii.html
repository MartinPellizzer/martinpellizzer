Calliandra inermis subsp. stylesii

Calliandra inermis subsp.

stylesii, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers.

The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

This plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in ecological settings.
