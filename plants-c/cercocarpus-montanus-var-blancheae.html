Cercocarpus montanus var. blancheae

Cercocarpus montanus var. blancheae, commonly known as Blanche's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, twisted leaves and produces small, red or brown seeds enclosed in woody pods. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The species is also recognized for its role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
