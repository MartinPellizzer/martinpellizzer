Clypea

Clypea, commonly known as the shield fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that give it its common name. Clypea species typically have a creeping rhizome and produce small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
