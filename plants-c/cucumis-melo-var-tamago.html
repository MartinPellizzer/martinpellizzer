Cucumis melo var. tamago

Cucumis melo var. tamago, commonly known as the Japanese melon, is a variety of melon native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green rind and sweet, juicy, pale yellow flesh. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by the edible fruit.

This variety is prized for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
