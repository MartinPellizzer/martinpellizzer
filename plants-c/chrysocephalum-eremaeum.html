Chrysocephalum eremaeum

Chrysocephalum eremaeum, commonly known as the sturt's desert-rose, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces striking yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a compact growth form that reduces water loss. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Chrysocephalum eremaeum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils in degraded areas.
