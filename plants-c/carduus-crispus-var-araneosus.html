Carduus crispus var. araneosus

Carduus crispus var. araneosus, commonly known as the spider-thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and a distinctive, spiky flower head that resembles a spider's web. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas where it can thrive in full sun.
