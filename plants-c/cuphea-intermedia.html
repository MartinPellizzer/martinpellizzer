Cuphea intermedia

Cuphea intermedia, commonly known as the flame flower or red hot catwhiskers, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble cat whiskers, which are highly attractive to hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces showy inflorescences that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Cuphea intermedia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
