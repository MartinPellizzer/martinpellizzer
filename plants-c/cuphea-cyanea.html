Cuphea cyanea

Cuphea cyanea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a mist-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.

Cuphea cyanea is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
