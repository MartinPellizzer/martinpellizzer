Colymbada toletana var. argecillensis

Colymbada toletana var. argecillensis, commonly known as the Toletan marsh bellflower, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific wetland habitats in southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the wet conditions of its environment.
