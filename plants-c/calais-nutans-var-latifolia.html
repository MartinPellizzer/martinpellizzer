Calais nutans var. latifolia

Calais nutans var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved calais nut, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
