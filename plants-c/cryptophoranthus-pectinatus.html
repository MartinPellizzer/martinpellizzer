Cryptophoranthus pectinatus

Cryptophoranthus pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly cryptophoranthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
