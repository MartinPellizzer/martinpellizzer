Coreopsis mutica var. carnosifolia

Coreopsis mutica var. carnosifolia, commonly known as the woolly coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant landscapes.
