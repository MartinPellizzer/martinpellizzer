Chelone obliqua subsp. typica

Chelone obliqua subsp.

typica, commonly known as the zigzagged hollyhock, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
