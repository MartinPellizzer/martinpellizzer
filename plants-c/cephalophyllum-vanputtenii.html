Cephalophyllum vanputtenii

Cephalophyllum vanputtenii, commonly known as the Vanputten's headplant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Cephalophyllum vanputtenii is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
