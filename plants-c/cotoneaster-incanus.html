Cotoneaster incanus

Cotoneaster incanus, commonly known as the silver cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Cotoneaster incanus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to different climates.
