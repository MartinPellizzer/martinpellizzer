Croton scouleri var. macraei

Croton scouleri var. macraei, commonly known as the Oregon croton, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
