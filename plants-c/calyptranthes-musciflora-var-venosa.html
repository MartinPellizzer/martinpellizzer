Calyptranthes musciflora var. venosa

Calyptranthes musciflora var. venosa, commonly known as the hairy calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a distinctive woolly or hairy texture on its leaves and stems. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a velvety feel, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
