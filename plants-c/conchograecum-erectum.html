Conchograecum erectum

Conchograecum erectum, commonly known as the upright conchograecum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, moist soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
