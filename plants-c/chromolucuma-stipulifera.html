Chromolucuma stipulifera

Chromolucuma stipulifera, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are fragrant and yellowish-white, producing small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
