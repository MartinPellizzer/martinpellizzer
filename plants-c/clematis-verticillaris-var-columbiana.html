Clematis verticillaris var. columbiana

Clematis verticillaris var. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of three to five. The plant has slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its attractive flowers and vigorous climbing habit.
