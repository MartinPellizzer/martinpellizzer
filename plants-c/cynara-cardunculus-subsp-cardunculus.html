Cynara cardunculus subsp. cardunculus

Cynara cardunculus subsp.

cardunculus, commonly known as the common cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been historically used for its edible leaves and stems.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
