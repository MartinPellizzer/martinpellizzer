Cynomorium coccineum

Cynomorium coccineum, commonly known as the red hemlock, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, reddish stems and waxy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
