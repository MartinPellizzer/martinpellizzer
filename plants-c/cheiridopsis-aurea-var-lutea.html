Cheiridopsis aurea var. lutea

Cheiridopsis aurea var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the winter months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
