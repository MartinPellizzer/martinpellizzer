Cleomella serrulata

Cleomella serrulata, commonly known as the serrated cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender stems and narrow, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, making it an important plant in ecological settings.

Cleomella serrulata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
