Cyperus ascotrigonus

Cyperus ascotrigonus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with small, spike-like inflorescences.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to form dense clusters near the water's surface.
