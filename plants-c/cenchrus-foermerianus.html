Cenchrus foermerianus

Cenchrus foermerianus, commonly known as the desert grass or foermerianus grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Cenchrus foermerianus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
