Clerodendrum rubellum

Clerodendrum rubellum, commonly known as the red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.

Clerodendrum rubellum is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic and medicinal plants.
