Cyperus distichophyllus

Cyperus distichophyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus distichophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
