Celastrus procumbens

Celastrus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that typically grows along the ground or on low structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are usually clustered in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, elliptical seeds.
