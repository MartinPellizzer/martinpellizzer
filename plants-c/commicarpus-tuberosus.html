Commicarpus tuberosus

Commicarpus tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous commicarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
