Cryptocarya glauca

Cryptocarya glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf cryptocarya, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, often bluish-tinted leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that matures in the summer months.

Cryptocarya glauca is typically found in coastal forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
