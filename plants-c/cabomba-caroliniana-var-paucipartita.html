Cabomba caroliniana var. paucipartita

Cabomba caroliniana var. paucipartita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often emerging from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and light penetration.
