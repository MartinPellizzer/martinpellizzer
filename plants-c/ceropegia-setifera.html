Ceropegia setifera

Ceropegia setifera, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
