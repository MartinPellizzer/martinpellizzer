Calamus spectabilis var. sumatranus

Calamus spectabilis var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by dark blue-black fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in certain indigenous communities.
