Cyrtostylis reniformis

Cyrtostylis reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed torch orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved flowers that resemble a torch, with a distinctive red or orange coloration. The plant typically grows in wet tropical or subtropical regions, often in rainforest or swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a waxy texture, and it produces a single flower spike that can bear multiple blooms.

Cyrtostylis reniformis is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
