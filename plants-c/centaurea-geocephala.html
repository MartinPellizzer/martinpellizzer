Centaurea geocephala

Centaurea geocephala, commonly known as the mountain centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea geocephala is often found in alpine and coastal habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
