Chrysophyllum oliviforme var. typicum

Chrysophyllum oliviforme var. typicum, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a species of small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and on limestone soils.

The Natal plum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
