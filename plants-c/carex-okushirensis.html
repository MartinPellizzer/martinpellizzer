Carex okushirensis

Carex okushirensis, commonly known as the Okushire sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex okushirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
