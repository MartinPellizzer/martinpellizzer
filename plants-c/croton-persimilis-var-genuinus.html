Croton persimilis var. genuinus

Croton persimilis var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
