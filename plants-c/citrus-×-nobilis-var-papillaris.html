Citrus × nobilis var. papillaris

Citrus × nobilis var. papillaris, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, segmented rind that is edible. The fruit contains a sweet, segmented pulp enclosed within a thin, edible membrane. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
