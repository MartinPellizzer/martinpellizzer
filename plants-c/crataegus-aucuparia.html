Crataegus aucuparia

Crataegus aucuparia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus aucuparia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
