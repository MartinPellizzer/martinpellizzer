Carlina vulgaris f. humilliana

Carlina vulgaris f. humilliana, commonly known as the dwarf garden carline, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
