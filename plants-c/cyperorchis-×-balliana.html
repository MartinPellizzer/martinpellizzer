Cyperorchis × balliana

Cyperorchis × balliana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a fascinating plant that combines the characteristics of its parent species, Cyperorchis appendiculatus and Cyperorchis pendulus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptation to moist environments. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of pink and white. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are adorned with small, intricate flowers.

Cyperorchis × balliana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
