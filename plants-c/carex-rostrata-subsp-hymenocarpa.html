Carex rostrata subsp. hymenocarpa

Carex rostrata subsp.

hymenocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and stream banks. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow leaves and specific floral structures that set it apart from other Carex species.
