Crataegus quinebaugensis

Crataegus quinebaugensis, commonly known as the Quinebaug hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
