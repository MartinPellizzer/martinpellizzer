Cracca talpa

Cracca talpa, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has yellow disc flowers surrounded by purple to brown ray flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cracca talpa is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
