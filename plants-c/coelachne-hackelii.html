Coelachne hackelii

Coelachne hackelii, commonly known as the hackel's fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Coelachne hackelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
