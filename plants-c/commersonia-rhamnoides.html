Commersonia rhamnoides

Commersonia rhamnoides, commonly known as the Chilean buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Commersonia rhamnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and adaptability.
