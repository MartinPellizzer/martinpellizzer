Cichlanthus atropurpureus

Cichlanthus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple cichloris, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its vibrant purple and red foliage, which becomes more intense during the cooler months. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and hardy nature.
