Crabbea acaulis

Crabbea acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems. The plant produces showy, brightly colored flowers that are typically pink or violet in hue. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Crabbea acaulis is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
