Caryopteris incana var. brachyodonta

Caryopteris incana var. brachyodonta, commonly known as compact blue mist spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
