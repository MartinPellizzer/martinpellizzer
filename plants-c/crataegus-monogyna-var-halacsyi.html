Crataegus monogyna var. halacsyi

Crataegus monogyna var. halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and dense foliage. The plant produces white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
