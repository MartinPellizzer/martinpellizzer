Carponema

Carponema, commonly known as the slime mold, is a unique organism that exhibits characteristics of both fungi and protists.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on decaying wood or leaf litter. The plant-like structure of Carponema consists of a gelatinous mass that spreads across surfaces, forming a network of filaments called a mycelium. During reproduction, it produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which release spores into the air.

Despite its appearance, Carponema plays an important ecological role in breaking down organic matter and recycling nutrients in the ecosystem.
