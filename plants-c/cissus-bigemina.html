Cissus bigemina

Cissus bigemina, commonly known as the creeping fig or Indian ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, glandular dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, red or orange berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus bigemina is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
