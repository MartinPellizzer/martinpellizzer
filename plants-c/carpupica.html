Carpupica

Carpupica, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant scent. Carpupica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
