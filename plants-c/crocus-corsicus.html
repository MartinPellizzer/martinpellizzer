Crocus corsicus

Crocus corsicus, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Crocus corsicus is a member of the Iridaceae family, which includes other well-known flowering plants.
