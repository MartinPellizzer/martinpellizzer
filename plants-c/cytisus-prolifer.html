Cytisus prolifer

Cytisus prolifer, commonly known as the common broom, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers and slender, thorny stems. The plant has small, pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and wastelands.

Cytisus prolifer is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
