Carex sinaica

Carex sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East, particularly the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex sinaica is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat, contributing to the ecological diversity of desert environments.
