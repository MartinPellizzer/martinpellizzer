Covilhamia ovata

Covilhamia ovata, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
