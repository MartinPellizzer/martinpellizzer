Carex longii var. meridionalis

Carex longii var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
