Corchorus caryophylloides

Corchorus caryophylloides, commonly known as the red-flowered dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsules containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.
