Cytisus pendulus

Cytisus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus pendulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique form and flowering display.
