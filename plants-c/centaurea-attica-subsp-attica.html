Centaurea attica subsp. attica

Centaurea attica subsp.

attica, commonly known as the Greek knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features upright, branched stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright red or orange, forming dense, spherical clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
