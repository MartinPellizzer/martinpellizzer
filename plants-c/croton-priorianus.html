Croton priorianus

Croton priorianus, commonly known as the red croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are typically lobed and can vary in color from green to red or purple, depending on the variety.

Croton priorianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
