Carex glauca var. brachylepis

Carex glauca var. brachylepis, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
