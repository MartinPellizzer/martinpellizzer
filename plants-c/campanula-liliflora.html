Campanula liliflora

Campanula liliflora, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
