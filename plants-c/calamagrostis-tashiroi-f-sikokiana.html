Calamagrostis tashiroi f. sikokiana

Calamagrostis tashiroi f. sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a ornamental grass native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-white plumes in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, providing a soft texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal display.
