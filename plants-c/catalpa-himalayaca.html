Catalpa himalayaca

Catalpa himalayaca, commonly known as the Himalayan catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region of Asia.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces long, bean-like pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Catalpa himalayaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of temperate climates.
