Coelogyne chrysotropis

Coelogyne chrysotropis, commonly known as the golden tropic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and elongated, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Coelogyne chrysotropis is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique flowering pattern.
