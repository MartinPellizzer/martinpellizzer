Carex thouarsii

Carex thouarsii, commonly known as the Tahitian sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific islands, including Tahiti and other French Polynesia islands.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Carex thouarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
