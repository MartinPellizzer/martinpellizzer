Cymbopogon acutispathaceus

Cymbopogon acutispathaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved citron grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its essential oils, particularly citral, have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
