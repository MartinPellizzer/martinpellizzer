Crocus cancellatus var. damascenus

Crocus cancellatus var. damascenus, commonly known as the Damascus saffron crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, saffron-colored flowers with a distinctive, deeply veined pattern. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge, making it a notable early-flowering species. Its bulbs are prized for their ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal care once established.
