Coleus mirabilis var. mecjowianus

Coleus mirabilis var. mecjowianus, commonly known as the Mecjowianus coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its bushy growth habit and compact size. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a textured surface, adding visual interest to gardens or indoor spaces.

This variety is popular for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation in warm climates.
