Convolvulus obtusifolius

Convolvulus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. It produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
