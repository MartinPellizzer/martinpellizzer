Calamagrostis arundinacea var. macrotricha

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. macrotricha, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, grass-like perennial plant native to Eurasia.

It features long, narrow, bluish-green leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of fluffy, silver-gray flower heads that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable wetland plant and a potential invasive species.
