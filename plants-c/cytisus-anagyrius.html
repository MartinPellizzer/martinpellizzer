Cytisus anagyrius

Cytisus anagyrius, commonly known as the Cyprus broom, is a shrub native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
