Cuscuta reflexa var. grandiflora

Cuscuta reflexa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to North America and is often found growing on various herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, and human activity, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and natural vegetation.
