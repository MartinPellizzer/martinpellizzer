Cissus tiliiformis

Cissus tiliiformis, commonly known as the sandalwood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by pale yellow fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
