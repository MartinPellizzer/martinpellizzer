Cuphea ligustrina var. commersoniana

Cuphea ligustrina var. commersoniana, commonly known as the wax plant or Brazilian waxflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, wax-like flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract hummingbirds make it a valuable addition to ornamental landscapes.
