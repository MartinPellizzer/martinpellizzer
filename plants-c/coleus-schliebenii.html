Coleus schliebenii

Coleus schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with intricate patterns of red, purple, and green. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
