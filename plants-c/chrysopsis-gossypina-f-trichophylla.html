Chrysopsis gossypina f. trichophylla

Chrysopsis gossypina f. trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it its common name and provides protection against desiccation. Its flowering stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
