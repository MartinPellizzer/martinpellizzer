Caryocar brasiliense var. planifolium

Caryocar brasiliense var. planifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are typically large and woody. The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and have been used by indigenous communities for centuries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

However, it is currently threatened by deforestation and habitat loss.
