Coleus stachyoides

Coleus stachyoides, commonly known as the silverleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.
