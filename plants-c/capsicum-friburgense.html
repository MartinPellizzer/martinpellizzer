Capsicum friburgense

Capsicum friburgense, commonly known as the Friburgense chili, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. This plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are yellow, and the fruits are usually small, measuring around 3 to 5 centimeters in length.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has a mild to spicy flavor, making it suitable for culinary use in some regions.
