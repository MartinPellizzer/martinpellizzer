Calamus dianbaiensis

Calamus dianbaiensis, commonly known as the Dianbai bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers. Calamus dianbaiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a distinctive member of the bamboo family.
