Croton seputubensis

Croton seputubensis, commonly known as the Malay croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange. Croton seputubensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
