Cyathea estelae

Cyathea estelae, commonly known as the Estela's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea estelae plays an important ecological role in its habitat by offering shelter and food to various wildlife species.
