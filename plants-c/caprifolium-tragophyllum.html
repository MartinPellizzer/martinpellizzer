Caprifolium tragophyllum

Caprifolium tragophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

Caprifolium tragophyllum is often found in moist woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
