Cerradicola decumbens

Cerradicola decumbens, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like structure that aids in seed dispersal.

Cerradicola decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
