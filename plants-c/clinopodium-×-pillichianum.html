Clinopodium × pillichianum

Clinopodium × pillichianum, commonly known as the hybrid mint or pillichian hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Clinopodium vulgare and Clinopodium polytrichum, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their menthol-like scent and potential medicinal properties.

Clinopodium × pillichianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
