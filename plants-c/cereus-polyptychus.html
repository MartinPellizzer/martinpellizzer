Cereus polyptychus

Cereus polyptychus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Cereus polyptychus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
