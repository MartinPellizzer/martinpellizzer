Cotoneaster majoricensis

Cotoneaster majoricensis, commonly known as the Island cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Majorica, part of the Balearic Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval in shape, with a serrated margin.

Cotoneaster majoricensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
