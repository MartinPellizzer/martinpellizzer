Coronaria sibirica

Coronaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive crown-like structure that gives it its common name.

Coronaria sibirica is known for its hardiness and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
