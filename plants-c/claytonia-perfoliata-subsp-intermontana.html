Claytonia perfoliata subsp. intermontana

Claytonia perfoliata subsp.

intermontana, commonly known as the mountain stalked springbeauty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of ovate, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct morphology and geographic distribution compared to other Claytonia species.
