Campylocentrum serranum

Campylocentrum serranum, commonly known as the serrated corymbia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

The fruit, which is a small, woody capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind.
