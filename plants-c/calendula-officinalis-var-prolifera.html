Calendula officinalis var. prolifera

Calendula officinalis var. prolifera, commonly known as the common marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces numerous small, daisy-like blooms that are often used in herbal medicine and as a natural dye. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features include alternate leaves and a taproot system, which contribute to its hardiness and adaptability.
