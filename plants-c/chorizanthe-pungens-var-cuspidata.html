Chorizanthe pungens var. cuspidata

Chorizanthe pungens var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bursera, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water. It produces small, dry fruits called follicles that contain seeds.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is part of the Amaranthaceae family.
