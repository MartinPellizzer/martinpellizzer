Cochlioda densiflora f. aurea

Cochlioda densiflora f. aurea, commonly known as the golden-flowered cochlioda, is a unique variant of the species Cochlioda densiflora.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking golden-yellow flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. Its botanical features include small, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, clustered flowers.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and compact form.
