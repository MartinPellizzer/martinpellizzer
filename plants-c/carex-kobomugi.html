Carex kobomugi

Carex kobomugi, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it a valuable species for erosion control.

The flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with the plant relying on wind pollination for reproduction.
