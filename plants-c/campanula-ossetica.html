Campanula ossetica

Campanula ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula ossetica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
