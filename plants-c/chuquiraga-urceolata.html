Chuquiraga urceolata

Chuquiraga urceolata, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Chuquiraga urceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
