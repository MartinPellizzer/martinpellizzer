Cydonia lagenaria

Cydonia lagenaria, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear or Persian apple, is a deciduous tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit that turns yellow-orange when mature. The plant produces a pungent, aromatic fruit with a gritty texture and a sweet, juicy interior. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and nutritional properties.
