Caletia ericodes

Caletia ericodes, commonly known as the Ericaceous caletia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Caletia ericodes is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to flourish in challenging soil conditions.
