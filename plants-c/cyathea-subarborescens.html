Cyathea subarborescens

Cyathea subarborescens, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen plant that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a stout, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, overlapping leaf bases. The plant has a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of over 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
