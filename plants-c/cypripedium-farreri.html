Cypripedium farreri

Cypripedium farreri, commonly known as the Farrer's lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Cypripedium farreri is a rare and protected species, valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
