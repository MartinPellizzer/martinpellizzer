Carex tenuistachya

Carex tenuistachya, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex tenuistachya is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
