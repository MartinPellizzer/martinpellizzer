Chiloterus

Chiloterus, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. Chiloterus thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
