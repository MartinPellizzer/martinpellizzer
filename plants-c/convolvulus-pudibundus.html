Convolvulus pudibundus

Convolvulus pudibundus, commonly known as the shy convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale yellow or white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms throughout the warmer months and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
