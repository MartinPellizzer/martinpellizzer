Croton rivinifolius var. scouleri

Croton rivinifolius var. scouleri, commonly known as the Scouler's croton, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
