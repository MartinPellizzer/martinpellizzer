Celastrus pentagynus

Celastrus pentagynus, commonly known as the five-styles creeper, is a woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
