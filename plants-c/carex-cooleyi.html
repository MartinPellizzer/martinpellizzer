Carex cooleyi

Carex cooleyi, commonly known as the Cooley's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its foliage is often a deep green, and it serves as a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
