Croton oppositifolius

Croton oppositifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
