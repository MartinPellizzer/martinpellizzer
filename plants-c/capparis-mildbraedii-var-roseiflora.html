Capparis mildbraedii var. roseiflora

Capparis mildbraedii var. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit, resembling a small, green berry, is edible and has a tangy flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
