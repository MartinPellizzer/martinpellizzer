Ceropegia elliptica

Ceropegia elliptica, commonly known as the African violet or silver leaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Ceropegia elliptica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
