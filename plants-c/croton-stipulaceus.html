Croton stipulaceus

Croton stipulaceus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a soft texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
