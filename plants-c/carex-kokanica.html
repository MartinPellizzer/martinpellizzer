Carex kokanica

Carex kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or grassy habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green in color.

Carex kokanica produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
