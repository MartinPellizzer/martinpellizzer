Campanula pulla var. ramosa

Campanula pulla var. ramosa, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has a compact growth habit with branching stems that produce numerous small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
