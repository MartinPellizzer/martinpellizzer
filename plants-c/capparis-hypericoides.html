Capparis hypericoides

Capparis hypericoides, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly succulent texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to withstand harsh, arid conditions.
