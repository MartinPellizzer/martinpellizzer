Carduncellus rhaponticoides

Carduncellus rhaponticoides, commonly known as the rhubarb root, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its stout, reddish roots and triangular, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
