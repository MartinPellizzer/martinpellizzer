Chamaemelum balsamita

Chamaemelum balsamita, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and bushy growth habit, often found in meadows and dry grasslands. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a mild, balsamic scent, contributing to its medicinal uses.

Chamaemelum balsamita is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
