Carex buxbaumii subsp. hartmaniorum

Carex buxbaumii subsp.

hartmaniorum, commonly known as Hartman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hartman's sedge is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.
