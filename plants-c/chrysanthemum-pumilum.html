Chrysanthemum pumilum

Chrysanthemum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf chrysanthemum, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a decorative ground cover.
