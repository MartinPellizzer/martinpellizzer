Campylanthus antonii

Campylanthus antonii, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Campylanthus antonii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.
