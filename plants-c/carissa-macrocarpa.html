Carissa macrocarpa

Carissa macrocarpa, commonly known as the yellow bell bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a bright orange, fleshy berry that contains seeds.

Carissa macrocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
