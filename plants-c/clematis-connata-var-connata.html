Clematis connata var. connata

Clematis connata var. connata, commonly known as the rock clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features woody stems that can climb and twine around supports, reaching lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

This species is well-suited to rocky or well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
