Chloranthus pernyanus

Chloranthus pernyanus, commonly known as the Perny's chloranthus, is a member of the Chloranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
