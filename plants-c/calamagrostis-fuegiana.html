Calamagrostis fuegiana

Calamagrostis fuegiana, commonly known as the Fuegian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as grasslands and riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated.

Calamagrostis fuegiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
