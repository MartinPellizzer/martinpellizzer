Cordyline gloriosa

Cordyline gloriosa, commonly known as the red ginger or red cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or purple color. The plant produces fragrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Cordyline gloriosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
