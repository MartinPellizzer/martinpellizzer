Cannabis americana

Cannabis americana, commonly known as hemp, is a species of the Cannabaceae family native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous stalks and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant contains lower concentrations of tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) compared to its cousin, Cannabis sativa. It is cultivated for its fibers, seeds, and oils, which have various industrial and nutritional applications.

The botanical features of Cannabis americana make it well-suited for agricultural and commercial use.
