Carludovica imperialis

Carludovica imperialis, commonly known as the Imperial nightshade, is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like deadly nightshade.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
