Calamus javensis

Calamus javensis, commonly known as the Java calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and tall, arching leaves. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.

Calamus javensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small organisms.
