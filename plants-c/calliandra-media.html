Calliandra media

Calliandra media, commonly known as the yellow calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with small, delicate leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra media is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
