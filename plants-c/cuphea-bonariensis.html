Cuphea bonariensis

Cuphea bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or cuphea flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea bonariensis is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
