Cardamomum pilosum

Cardamomum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are broad and have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Cardamomum pilosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its unique scent and potential health benefits.
