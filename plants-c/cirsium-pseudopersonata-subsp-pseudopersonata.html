Cirsium pseudopersonata subsp. pseudopersonata

Cirsium pseudopersonata subsp.

pseudopersonata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery-gray underside, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
