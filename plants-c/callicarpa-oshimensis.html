Callicarpa oshimensis

Callicarpa oshimensis, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking purple to red berries that ripen in the fall, attracting birds and adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Callicarpa oshimensis is valued for its vibrant foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
