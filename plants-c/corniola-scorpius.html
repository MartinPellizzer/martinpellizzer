Corniola scorpius

Corniola scorpius, commonly known as the scorpion grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
