Commiphora resiniflua

Commiphora resiniflua, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, milky sap, which is often tapped for its resinous properties. The plant typically grows in dry, tropical, and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The resin produced by Commiphora resiniflua has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
