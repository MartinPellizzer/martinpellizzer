Cirsium andrewsii

Cirsium andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, bristly surfaces, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium andrewsii is often found in regions with moderate to high elevations, thriving in well-drained soils.
