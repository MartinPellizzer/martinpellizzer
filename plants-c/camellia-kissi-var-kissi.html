Camellia kissi var. kissi

Camellia kissi var. kissi, commonly known as the African camellia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its flowers are known for their delicate, waxy petals and subtle fragrance.

Camellia kissi var. kissi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
