Chomelia myrtifolia

Chomelia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf chomelia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a distinct myrtle-like scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
