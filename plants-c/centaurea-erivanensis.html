Centaurea erivanensis

Centaurea erivanensis, commonly known as the Erivan centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
