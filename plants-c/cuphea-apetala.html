Cuphea apetala

Cuphea apetala, commonly known as the naked flower cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that lack petals, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, vividly colored bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea apetala is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
