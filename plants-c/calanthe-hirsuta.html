Calanthe hirsuta

Calanthe hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
