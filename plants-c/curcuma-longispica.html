Curcuma longispica

Curcuma longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its striking, elongated floral spurs. The botanical features of Curcuma longispica make it a valuable ornamental and medicinal plant in tropical regions.
