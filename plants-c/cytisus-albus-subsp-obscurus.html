Cytisus albus subsp. obscurus

Cytisus albus subsp.

obscurus, commonly known as the dark scurfy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and grayish-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more scurfy appearance compared to the typical form of Cytisus albus.
