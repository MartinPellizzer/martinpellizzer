Cornus hongkongensis subsp. tonkinensis

Cornus hongkongensis subsp.

tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin cornel, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in subtropical regions.
