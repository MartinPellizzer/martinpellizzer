Crucifera ruderalis

Crucifera ruderalis, commonly known as the wild radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It is a hardy, weedy plant often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-100 cm, with hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Crucifera ruderalis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its rapid growth cycle.
