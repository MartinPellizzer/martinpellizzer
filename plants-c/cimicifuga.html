Cimicifuga

Cimicifuga, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in late summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
