Campanula staintonii

Campanula staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula staintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
