Convolvulus pungens

Convolvulus pungens, commonly known as the prickly bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.

Convolvulus pungens is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
