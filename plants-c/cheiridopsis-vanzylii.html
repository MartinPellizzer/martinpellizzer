Cheiridopsis vanzylii

Cheiridopsis vanzylii, commonly known as the Van Zyl's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
