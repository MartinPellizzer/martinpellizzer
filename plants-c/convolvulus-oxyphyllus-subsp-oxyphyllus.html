Convolvulus oxyphyllus subsp. oxyphyllus

Convolvulus oxyphyllus subsp.

oxyphyllus, commonly known as the rock convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
