Crataegus limnophila

Crataegus limnophila, commonly known as the water hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus limnophila thrives in moist soils and is often found along the edges of ponds, lakes, and streams.

Its adaptability to aquatic environments makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
