Cipura paradisiaca

Cipura paradisiaca, commonly known as the paradise cipura, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native regions.
