Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp. lucidum

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp.

lucidum, commonly known as the glossy chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscape design.
