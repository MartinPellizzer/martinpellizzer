Calophyllum decipiens

Calophyllum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive candlewood, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in humid, warm climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
