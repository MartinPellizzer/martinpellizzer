Crataegus alnorum

Crataegus alnorum, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus alnorum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
