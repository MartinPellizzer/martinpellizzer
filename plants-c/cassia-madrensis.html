Cassia madrensis

Cassia madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican golden trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
