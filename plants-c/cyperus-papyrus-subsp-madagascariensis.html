Cyperus papyrus subsp. madagascariensis

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar papyrus, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that has historically been used by indigenous communities.

This subspecies is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
