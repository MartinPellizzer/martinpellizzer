Chrysanthemum coronopifolium subsp. ceratophylloides

Chrysanthemum coronopifolium subsp.

ceratophylloides, commonly known as the horned-leaved chrysanthemum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed, and sometimes horn-like leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
