Crepis taraxacifolia var. haenseleri

Crepis taraxacifolia var. haenseleri, commonly known as the Alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, showy yellow flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are followed by a distinctive, fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
