Cephalophyllum ceresianum

Cephalophyllum ceresianum, commonly known as the Cape Flats daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cephalophyllum ceresianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
