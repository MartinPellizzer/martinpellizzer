Campanula alpini var. polyadenia

Campanula alpini var. polyadenia, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine landscapes.
