Clematis multiflora

Clematis multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis multiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
