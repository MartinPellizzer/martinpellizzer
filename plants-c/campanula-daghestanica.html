Campanula daghestanica

Campanula daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula daghestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
