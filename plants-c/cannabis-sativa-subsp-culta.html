Cannabis sativa subsp. culta

Cannabis sativa subsp.

culta, commonly known as hemp, is a variety of the cannabis plant characterized by its low tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) content and high cannabidiol (CBD) levels. It is typically cultivated for industrial purposes such as fiber production, seed oil, and nutritional supplements.

The plant has narrow leaves and can grow up to several meters tall, with a fibrous stem that is harvested for its strong, durable fibers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its legal status in many regions, its cultivation is often regulated for specific agricultural and commercial uses.
