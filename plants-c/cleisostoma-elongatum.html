Cleisostoma elongatum

Cleisostoma elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Cleisostoma elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
