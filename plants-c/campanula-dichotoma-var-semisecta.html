Campanula dichotoma var. semisecta

Campanula dichotoma var. semisecta, commonly known as the divided bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine beds.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
