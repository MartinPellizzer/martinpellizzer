Camellia cuspidata

Camellia cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Camellia cuspidata typically blooms in winter, adding vibrant color to gardens during the colder months.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is well-suited to shaded, moist environments.
