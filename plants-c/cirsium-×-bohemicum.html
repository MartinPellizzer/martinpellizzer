Cirsium × bohemicum

Cirsium × bohemicum, commonly known as the Bohemian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Cirsium × bohemicum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
