Canarium maluense subsp. borneense

Canarium maluense subsp.

borneense, commonly known as Bornean candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood and small, elliptical leaves.

The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The plant is valued for its timber, traditional uses, and ecological importance in Bornean ecosystems.
