Caesalpinia rubicunda f. subobtusata

Caesalpinia rubicunda f. subobtusata, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Acacia, is a variant of the Caesalpinia rubicunda, a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in dense, fluffy clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, elliptical leaflets arranged in feathery patterns. This cultivar is prized for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to a range of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often used in gardens and landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny environments.
