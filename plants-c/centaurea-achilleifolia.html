Centaurea achilleifolia

Centaurea achilleifolia, commonly known as the Achilles' heel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea achilleifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
