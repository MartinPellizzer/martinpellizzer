Crataegus sanguinea f. microphylla

Crataegus sanguinea f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf scarlet hawthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and showy pink to red flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and small landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, flowering shrub with seasonal interest.
