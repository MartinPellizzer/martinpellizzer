Conyza arbutifolia

Conyza arbutifolia, commonly known as the "false holly," is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Conyza arbutifolia produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
