Campanula sibirica subsp. charkeviczii

Campanula sibirica subsp.

charkeviczii, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
