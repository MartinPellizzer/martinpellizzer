Cerastium fischerianum var. ciliatipetalum

Cerastium fischerianum var. ciliatipetalum, commonly known as the hairy-petaled mouse-ear, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves with hairy margins, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rock crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
