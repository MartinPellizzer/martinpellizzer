Crataegus monogyna var. csatoi

Crataegus monogyna var. csatoi, commonly known as the Hungarian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and open areas.
