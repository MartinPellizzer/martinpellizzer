Cissus dimidiata

Cissus dimidiata, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus dimidiata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
