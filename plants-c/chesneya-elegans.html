Chesneya elegans

Chesneya elegans, commonly known as the elegant chesneya, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant features tall, strap-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
