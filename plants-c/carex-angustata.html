Carex angustata

Carex angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often hidden among the leaves, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
