Cedrota

Cedrota, commonly known as the Mexican cedron or *Cedrela odorata*, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and long, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Cedrota is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ornamental appeal.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various indigenous cultures.
