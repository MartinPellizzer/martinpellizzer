Cephalocarpus rigidus var. mucronatus

Cephalocarpus rigidus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved headcup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rigid, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a distinctive, cup-shaped structure that aids in seed dispersal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
