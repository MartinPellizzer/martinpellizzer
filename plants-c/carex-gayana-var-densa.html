Carex gayana var. densa

Carex gayana var. densa, commonly known as the dense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This variety is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower spikes during the growing season. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
