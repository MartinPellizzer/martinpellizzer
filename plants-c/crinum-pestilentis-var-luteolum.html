Crinum pestilentis var. luteolum

Crinum pestilentis var. luteolum, commonly known as the yellow crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It is a bulbous plant that produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The yellow crinum is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
