Commelina opulens

Commelina opulens, commonly known as the showy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with three petals and a distinctive three-lobed spathe, giving it a unique appearance.

Commelina opulens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
