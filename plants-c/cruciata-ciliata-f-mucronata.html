Cruciata ciliata f. mucronata

Cruciata ciliata f. mucronata, commonly known as the mucronate cruciata, is a variant of the cruciata ciliata, a species native to certain regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
