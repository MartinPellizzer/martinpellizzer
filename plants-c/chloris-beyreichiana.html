Chloris beyreichiana

Chloris beyreichiana, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
