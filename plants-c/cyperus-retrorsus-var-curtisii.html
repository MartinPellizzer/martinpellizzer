Cyperus retrorsus var. curtisii

Cyperus retrorsus var. curtisii, commonly known as the curly flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved or twisted, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus retrorsus var. curtisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions.
