Croton xalapensis

Croton xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from green to red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton xalapensis is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other plants with similar botanical features.
