Cintia

Cintia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Cintia is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important role in supporting pollinators during the early growing season.
