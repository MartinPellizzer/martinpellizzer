Cephalophora setigera

Cephalophora setigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish-brown underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often overlooked due to its dense foliage. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in some areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
