Centaurea boissieri var. straminea

Centaurea boissieri var. straminea, commonly known as the pale centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a grayish or whitish underside.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
