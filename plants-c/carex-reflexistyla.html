Carex reflexistyla

Carex reflexistyla, commonly known as the reflexed sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, particularly in Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex reflexistyla plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
