Crotalaria oocarpa

Crotalaria oocarpa, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Crotalaria oocarpa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
