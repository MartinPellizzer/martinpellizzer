Cordia myxa

Cordia myxa, commonly known as the sticky gum tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its rough, peeling bark and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are fleshy, yellow berries that contain numerous seeds.

Cordia myxa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
