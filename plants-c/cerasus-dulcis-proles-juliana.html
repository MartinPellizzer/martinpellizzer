Cerasus dulcis proles juliana

Cerasus dulcis proles juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and is renowned for its early spring blossoms, which appear before the leaves. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, sweet, and tart berries that ripen in late summer.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
