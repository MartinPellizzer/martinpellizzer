Cyathea pendula

Cyathea pendula, commonly known as the hanging tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and remain evergreen throughout the year.

Cyathea pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
