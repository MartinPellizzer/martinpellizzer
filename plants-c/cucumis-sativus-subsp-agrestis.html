Cucumis sativus subsp. agrestis

Cucumis sativus subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as wild cucumber, is a perennial vine native to warm temperate and tropical regions. It produces small, often bitter fruits that are typically less cultivated than its domesticated cousin, Cucumis sativus.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, and it thrives in well-drained soil. Its fruits are used in some traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
