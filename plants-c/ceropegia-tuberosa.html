Ceropegia tuberosa

Ceropegia tuberosa, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent that features thick, fleshy stems and leaves, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia tuberosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
