Clethra longispicata

Clethra longispicata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and have a smooth texture. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Clethra longispicata is often found in moist, acidic soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
