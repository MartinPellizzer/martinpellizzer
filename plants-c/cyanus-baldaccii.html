Cyanus baldaccii

Cyanus baldaccii, commonly known as the Italian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cyanus baldaccii typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and wastelands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native environment.
