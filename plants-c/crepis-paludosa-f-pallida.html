Crepis paludosa f. pallida

Crepis paludosa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale meadow crabgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and meadows. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish or whitish hue due to the presence of fine pubescence.

This subspecies is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
