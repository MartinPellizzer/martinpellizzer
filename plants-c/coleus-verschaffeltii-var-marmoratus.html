Coleus verschaffeltii var. marmoratus

Coleus verschaffeltii var. marmoratus, commonly known as the Marbleleaf Coleus, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and purple hues, creating a marbled effect. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for containers and garden borders.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its visual appeal and texture.
