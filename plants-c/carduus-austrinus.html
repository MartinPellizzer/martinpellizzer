Carduus austrinus

Carduus austrinus, commonly known as the Australian thistle, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Its robust structure and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
