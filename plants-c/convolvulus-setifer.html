Convolvulus setifer

Convolvulus setifer, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink or white, with five lobes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
