Clerodendrum comans

Clerodendrum comans, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Clerodendrum comans thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant, colorful foliage.
