Calamus urdanetanus

Calamus urdanetanus, commonly known as the Urdaneta cane, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark, fibrous fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus urdanetanus is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
