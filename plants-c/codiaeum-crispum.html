Codiaeum crispum

Codiaeum crispum, commonly known as the croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique, lobed foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
