Crassocephalum guineense

Crassocephalum guineense, commonly known as African blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
