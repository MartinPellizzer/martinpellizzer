Chorizanthe jonesiana

Chorizanthe jonesiana, commonly known as Jones' jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its drought tolerance makes it an adaptation to arid environments.
