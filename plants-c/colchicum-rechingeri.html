Colchicum rechingeri

Colchicum rechingeri, commonly known as the Eastern Persian meadow saffron, is a species of flowering plant in the family Colchicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran, Afghanistan, and Pakistan. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, and its bulbs contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
