Clusia pseudomangle

Clusia pseudomangle, commonly known as false mangle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Clusia pseudomangle is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
