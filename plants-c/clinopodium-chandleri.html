Clinopodium chandleri

Clinopodium chandleri, commonly known as Chandler's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant has small, fragrant leaves and produces clusters of purple or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
