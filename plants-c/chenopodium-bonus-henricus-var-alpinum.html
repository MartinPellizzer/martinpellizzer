Chenopodium bonus-henricus var. alpinum

Chenopodium bonus-henricus var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture, and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive at high altitudes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
