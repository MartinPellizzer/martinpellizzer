Calamintha baetica

Calamintha baetica, commonly known as Spanish germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Calamintha baetica is valued for its fragrant leaves and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
