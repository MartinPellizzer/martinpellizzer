Carex × mainensis

Carex × mainensis, commonly known as the mainensis sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in tight, cylindrical clusters.

Carex × mainensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
