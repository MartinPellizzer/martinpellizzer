Calliandra colombiana

Calliandra colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or red bracteate inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
