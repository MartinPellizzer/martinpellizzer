Camaridium luteobrunneum

Camaridium luteobrunneum, commonly known as the yellow-brown camaridium, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, yellowish-brown flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its low-growing nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its unique coloration and resilience.
