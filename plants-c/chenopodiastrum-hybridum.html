Chenopodiastrum hybridum

Chenopodiastrum hybridum, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with pale green to reddish stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often succulent and adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species capable of thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

Chenopodiastrum hybridum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
