Cerasus Yunnanensis Var. Polybotrys

Cerasus yunnanensis var. polybotrys is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan province in China. Known for its vibrant pink and white flowers, it is often referred to as the "pink peach" due to its resemblance to the common peach tree. This variant of the Cerasus genus is prized for its ornamental value and unique floral display. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Due to its limited natural range and increasing demand, it is becoming a sought-after species among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
