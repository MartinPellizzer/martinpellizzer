Casearia atlantica

Casearia atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic casearia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
