Cyperus melanostachyus var. capitatus

Cyperus melanostachyus var. capitatus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems and produces dense clusters of purple to brown spikelets at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking color and texture.
