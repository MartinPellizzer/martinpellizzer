Catalpa bignonioides var. purpurea

Catalpa bignonioides var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color, appearing in late spring. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
