Canna rotundifolia

Canna rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Canna rotundifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
