Corysanthes vinosa

Corysanthes vinosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Corysanthes vinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some Indigenous communities.
