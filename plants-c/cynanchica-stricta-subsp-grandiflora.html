Cynanchica stricta subsp. grandiflora

Cynanchica stricta subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
