Chamaemelum cladanthus

Chamaemelum cladanthus, commonly known as the clustered chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom in the summer months. Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Chamaemelum cladanthus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
