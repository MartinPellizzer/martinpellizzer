Crocus imperatonianus var. princeps

Crocus imperatonianus var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces corms as its underground storage organs. Native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
