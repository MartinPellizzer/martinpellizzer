Cleome giorgii

Cleome giorgii, commonly known as the Mexican Marvel or spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, colorful flowers that range from pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive, spiky center. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming from summer to early fall.

Cleome giorgii is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
