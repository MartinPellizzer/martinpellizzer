Carex alboviridis

Carex alboviridis, commonly known as the white-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. Carex alboviridis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
