Commidendrum spurium

Commidendrum spurium, commonly known as the white-stemmed devil's ivy, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and variegated, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to climb or trail makes it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
