Calamagrostis areantha

Calamagrostis areantha, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

Calamagrostis areantha is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
