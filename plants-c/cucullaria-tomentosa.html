Cucullaria tomentosa

Cucullaria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cucullaria tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
