Cnicus jorullensis

Cnicus jorullensis, commonly known as the Jorullense pricklyweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native region.
