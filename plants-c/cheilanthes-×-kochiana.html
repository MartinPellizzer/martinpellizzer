Cheilanthes × kochiana

Cheilanthes × kochiana, commonly known as the hybrid rock fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cheilanthes lanata and Cheilanthes tomentosa.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and leathery, evergreen fronds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The fronds are typically gray-green in color and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.
