Centaurea saxatilis subsp. gaudinii

Centaurea saxatilis subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudini's cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a grayish-green hue, and it produces small, bracteate flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
