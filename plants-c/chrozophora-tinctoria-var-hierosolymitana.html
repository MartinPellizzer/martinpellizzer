Chrozophora tinctoria var. hierosolymitana

Chrozophora tinctoria var. hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its edible tubers, which are rich in inulin and have a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
