Campanula veneris

Campanula veneris, commonly known as the Venus's bell, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Campanula veneris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
