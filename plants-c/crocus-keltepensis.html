Crocus keltepensis

Crocus keltepensis, commonly known as the Keltepe crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, early spring blooming habit. The plant produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
