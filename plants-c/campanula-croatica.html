Campanula croatica

Campanula croatica, commonly known as the Croatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula croatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
