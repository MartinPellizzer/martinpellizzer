Campanula lactiflora var. glabra

Campanula lactiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, smooth texture, distinguishing it from other varieties of Campanula. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
