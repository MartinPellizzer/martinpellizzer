Cobaea biaurita

Cobaea biaurita, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive two-lobed shape. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Cobaea biaurita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
