Cleome muricata

Cleome muricata, commonly known as the spiked cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cleome muricata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
