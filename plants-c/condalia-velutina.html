Condalia velutina

Condalia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
