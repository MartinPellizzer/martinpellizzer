Cassia leschenaultiana

Cassia leschenaultiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cassia leschenaultiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
