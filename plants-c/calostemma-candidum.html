Calostemma candidum

Calostemma candidum, commonly known as the white lily of the Nile, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of grasses or sedges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade.

Calostemma candidum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
