Clematis japonica f. villosula

Clematis japonica f. villosula, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis with a velvety texture, is a deciduous vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can climb trellises or walls with ease.

Its foliage provides a lush green backdrop, complementing the vibrant blooms throughout the growing season.
