Crataegus ancisa

Crataegus ancisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny branches and clusters of small, oval leaves.
