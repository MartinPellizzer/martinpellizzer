Ciconium cocciniflorum

Ciconium cocciniflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered ciconium, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
