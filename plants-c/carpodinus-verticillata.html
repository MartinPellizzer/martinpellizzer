Carpodinus verticillata

Carpodinus verticillata, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive spirally arranged leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, aiding in its propagation.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.
