Cassia crassisepala

Cassia crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Cassia crassisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
