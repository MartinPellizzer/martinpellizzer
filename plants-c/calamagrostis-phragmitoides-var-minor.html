Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. minor

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. minor, commonly known as the slender reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, releasing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.
