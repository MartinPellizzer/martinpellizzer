Crataegus subinermis

Crataegus subinermis, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, drupes that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
