Cuphea origanifolia

Cuphea origanifolia, commonly known as the orange cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea origanifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add color to garden spaces.
