Crataegus oxyacantha var. heterophylla

Crataegus oxyacantha var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from ovate to lanceolate, and its thorny branches. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
