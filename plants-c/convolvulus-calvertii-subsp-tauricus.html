Convolvulus calvertii subsp. tauricus

Convolvulus calvertii subsp.

tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Tauric Chersonese region of Crimea. It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, trailing stems that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
