Chamaecytisus proliferus

Chamaecytisus proliferus, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.
