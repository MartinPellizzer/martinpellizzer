Cassia sophera

Cassia sophera, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous, hairy coating. Cassia sophera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
