Carex leavenworthii

Carex leavenworthii, commonly known as Leavenworth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex leavenworthii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
