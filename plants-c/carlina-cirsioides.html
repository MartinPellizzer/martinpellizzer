Carlina cirsioides

Carlina cirsioides, commonly known as rough carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, dark green leaves and purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina cirsioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
