Calea rotundifolia

Calea rotundifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-colored flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Calea rotundifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
