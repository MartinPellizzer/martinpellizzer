Callicarpa micrantha

Callicarpa micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruits. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, often turning red in the autumn.

Callicarpa micrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds.
