Caesalpinia erlangeri

Caesalpinia erlangeri, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and bright orange to red floral spikes. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry woodland habitats.

Caesalpinia erlangeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
