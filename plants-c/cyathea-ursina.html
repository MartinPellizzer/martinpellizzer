Cyathea ursina

Cyathea ursina, commonly known as the bear's paw fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its robust, trunk-like stem and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to three meters and are adorned with a distinctive, hairy texture on the underside.

Cyathea ursina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological structure of its native habitat.
