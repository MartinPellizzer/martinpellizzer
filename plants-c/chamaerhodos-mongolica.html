Chamaerhodos mongolica

Chamaerhodos mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Mongolia and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chamaerhodos mongolica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
