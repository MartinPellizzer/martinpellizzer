Chytroglossa aurata

Chytroglossa aurata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
