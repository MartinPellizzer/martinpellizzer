Celtis tournefortii subsp. aetnensis

Celtis tournefortii subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna hackberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to Mediterranean climates and its role in local ecosystems.
