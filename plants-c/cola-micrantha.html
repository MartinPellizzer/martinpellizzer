Cola micrantha

Cola micrantha, commonly known as African cola or small-fruited cola, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant produces elliptical fruits that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cola micrantha is valued for its caffeine content and has cultural significance in various African communities.
