Cinnamomum maruba

Cinnamomum maruba, commonly known as the dwarf cinnamon or mountain cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark purple berries. The plant produces a fragrant essential oil, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
