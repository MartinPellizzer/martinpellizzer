Cytisus holopetalus

Cytisus holopetalus, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, often thorny stems and small, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus holopetalus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
