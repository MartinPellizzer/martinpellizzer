Carex capillaris var. paludosa

Carex capillaris var. paludosa, commonly known as bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist to saturated soils, often found in bogs, fens, and marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
