Coleus verticillatus

Coleus verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or purple. Its vibrant leaves make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
