Carex cespitosa var. gracilis

Carex cespitosa var. gracilis, commonly known as slender tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges, and its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
