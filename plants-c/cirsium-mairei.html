Cirsium mairei

Cirsium mairei, commonly known as the Maire's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and striking purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, upright growth habit. Cirsium mairei thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens that feature drought-tolerant and hardy plants.
