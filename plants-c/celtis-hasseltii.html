Celtis hasseltii

Celtis hasseltii, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Celtis hasseltii is adapted to various soil types and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
