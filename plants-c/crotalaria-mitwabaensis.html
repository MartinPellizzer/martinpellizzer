Crotalaria mitwabaensis

Crotalaria mitwabaensis, commonly known as the Mitwaba crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It has a bushy growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
