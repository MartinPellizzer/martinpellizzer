Convolvulus pitardii var. leucochnous

Convolvulus pitardii var. leucochnous, commonly known as the white-flowered convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the stems are hairy and somewhat succulent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
