Cymbopogon phoenix

Cymbopogon phoenix, commonly known as the golden grass or phoenix grass, is a species of grass native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow to golden-green foliage that adds a striking visual appeal to landscapes. The plant typically grows in warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils, which can be used in aromatherapy and fragrance industries.
