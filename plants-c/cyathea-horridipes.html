Cyathea horridipes

Cyathea horridipes, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer that gives it its common name. The plant features a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. Cyathea horridipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
