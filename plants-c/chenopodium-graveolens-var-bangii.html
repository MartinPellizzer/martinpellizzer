Chenopodium graveolens var. bangii

Chenopodium graveolens var. bangii, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Americas and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct aroma. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is edible, with young leaves used in cooking, though they can be somewhat bitter.
