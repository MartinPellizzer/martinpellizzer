Centaurea gussonei

Centaurea gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower heads.

Centaurea gussonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
