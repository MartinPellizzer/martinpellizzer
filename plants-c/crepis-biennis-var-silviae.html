Crepis biennis var. silviae

Crepis biennis var. silviae, commonly known as the Silvia's annual hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
