Cuphea watsoniana

Cuphea watsoniana, commonly known as the flame bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea watsoniana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
