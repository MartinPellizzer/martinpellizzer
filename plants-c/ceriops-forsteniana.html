Ceriops forsteniana

Ceriops forsteniana, commonly known as the mangrove buttonwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, allowing it to thrive in brackish and saline environments. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated by wind or insects.

Ceriops forsteniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
