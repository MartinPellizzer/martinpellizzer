Cestrum tillettii

Cestrum tillettii, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum tillettii is also known for its nocturnal fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as moths and bats.
