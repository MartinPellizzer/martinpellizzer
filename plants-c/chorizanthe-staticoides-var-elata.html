Chorizanthe staticoides var. elata

Chorizanthe staticoides var. elata, commonly known as the tall sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and scrubland habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.
