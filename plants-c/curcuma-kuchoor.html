Curcuma kuchoor

Curcuma kuchoor, commonly known as the purple ginger or purple turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant produces thick, fleshy rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive, spicy aroma.

Curcuma kuchoor thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
