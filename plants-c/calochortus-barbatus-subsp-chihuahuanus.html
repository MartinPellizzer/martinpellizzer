Calochortus barbatus subsp. chihuahuanus

Calochortus barbatus subsp.

chihuahuanus, commonly known as the Chihuahuan mariposa lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America. It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its vibrant blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
