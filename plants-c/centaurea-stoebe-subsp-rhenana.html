Centaurea stoebe subsp. rhenana

Centaurea stoebe subsp.

rhenana, commonly known as the Rhenish Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is a perennial herb with a robust root system and erect, spiny stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in dense, spherical heads, which are surrounded by stiff, bristly leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological characteristics.
