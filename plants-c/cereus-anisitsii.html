Cereus anisitsii

Cereus anisitsii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens. Its flowers, which can be white or pink, are typically short-lived but highly showy.

Cereus anisitsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
