Cymbidium insigne

Cymbidium insigne, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. It is a perennial evergreen orchid that thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Cymbidium insigne is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
