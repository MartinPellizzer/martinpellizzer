Chrysanthemum santa-maria

Chrysanthemum santa-maria, commonly known as the Santa Maria chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.
