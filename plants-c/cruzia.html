Cruzia

Cruzia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy taste. Cruzia has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the branches.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
