Cordia sebestena

Cordia sebestena, commonly known as the red cedar or wild fig, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Cordia sebestena is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical environments.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and sustenance for birds and insects.
