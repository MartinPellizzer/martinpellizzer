Cynanchum japonicum var. albiflorum

Cynanchum japonicum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, ribbed fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been studied for potential therapeutic applications.
