Chamaecrista neesiana var. laxiracemosa

Chamaecrista neesiana var. laxiracemosa, commonly known as the laxiracemous burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers clustered in loose racemes, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
