Centaurea axillaris var. occitanica

Centaurea axillaris var. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern France and parts of Spain.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers with a distinctive purple-blue center, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
