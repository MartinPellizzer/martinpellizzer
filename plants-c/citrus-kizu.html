Citrus kizu

Citrus kizu, commonly known as Japanese mandarin orange, is a small citrus fruit native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species like the common mandarin and pomelo. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel that is easy to peel. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is typically harvested in late winter to early spring and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor.
