Cousinia atropatana

Cousinia atropatana, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Cousinia atropatana is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
