Centaurea ossaea

Centaurea ossaea, commonly known as the Armenian crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea ossaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
