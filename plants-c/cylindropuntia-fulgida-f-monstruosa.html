Cylindropuntia fulgida f. monstruosa

Cylindropuntia fulgida f. monstruosa, commonly known as the monstrous cholla, is a striking cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a dense, spiny clump of cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its stems are covered in small, tubercles with clusters of spines, giving it a distinctive and rugged appearance.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid, well-drained soils.
