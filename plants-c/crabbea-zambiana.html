Crabbea zambiana

Crabbea zambiana, commonly known as the Zambian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
