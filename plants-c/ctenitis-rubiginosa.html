Ctenitis rubiginosa

Ctenitis rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a slightly rough texture on the underside.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shaded, humid environments.
