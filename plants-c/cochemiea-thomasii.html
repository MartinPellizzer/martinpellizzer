Cochemiea thomasii

Cochemiea thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas candelabra cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive branching structure that resembles a candlestick. The plant features spiny stems and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water.

Cochemiea thomasii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
