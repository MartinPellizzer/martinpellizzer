Cryptocarya griffithiana

Cryptocarya griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian laurel or Griffith's cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Cryptocarya griffithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
