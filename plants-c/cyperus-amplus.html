Cyperus amplus

Cyperus amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a broad, flat appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus amplus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its distinctive foliage.
