Crotalaria erecta

Crotalaria erecta, commonly known as the upright false cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its pods contain toxic seeds that can be harmful to livestock.

Crotalaria erecta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
