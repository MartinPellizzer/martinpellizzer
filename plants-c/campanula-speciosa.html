Campanula speciosa

Campanula speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
