Callicarpa longipes var. laui

Callicarpa longipes var. laui, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin and turn red in the autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
