Crataegus flabellata

Crataegus flabellata, commonly known as the fanleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open areas.

Crataegus flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
