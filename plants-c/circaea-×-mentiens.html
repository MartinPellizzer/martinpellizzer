Circaea × mentiens

Circaea × mentiens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10–30 cm. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
