Codiaeum lacteum

Codiaeum lacteum, commonly known as the White-veined Cudweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
