Carex foenea var. perplexa

Carex foenea var. perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
