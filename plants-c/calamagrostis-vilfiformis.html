Calamagrostis vilfiformis

Calamagrostis vilfiformis, commonly known as the slender reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, silver-gray flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis vilfiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
