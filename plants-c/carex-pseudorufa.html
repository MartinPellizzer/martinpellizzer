Carex pseudorufa

Carex pseudorufa, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Carex pseudorufa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
