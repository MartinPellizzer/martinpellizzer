Croton segoviarus

Croton segoviarus, commonly known as the Segovia croton, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
