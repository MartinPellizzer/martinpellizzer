Clematis carrizoensis

Clematis carrizoensis, commonly known as Carrizo Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a vigorous vine that can grow up to 15 feet in length, featuring compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis carrizoensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
