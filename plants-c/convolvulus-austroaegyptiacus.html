Convolvulus austroaegyptiacus

Convolvulus austroaegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
