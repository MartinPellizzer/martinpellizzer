Cheirostylis grandiflora

Cheirostylis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cheirostylis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a slender, erect stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their intricate, spurred structure typical of orchids.

Cheirostylis grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
