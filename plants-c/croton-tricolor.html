Croton tricolor

Croton tricolor, commonly known as the three-color croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated foliage, with leaves displaying shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.

Croton tricolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf colors and adaptability to various growing conditions.
