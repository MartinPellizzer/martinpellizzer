Ceratolobus forgetiana

Ceratolobus forgetiana, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
