Cyrtopera obstipa

Cyrtopera obstipa, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. Cyrtopera obstipa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
