Chloris bahiensis

Chloris bahiensis, commonly known as Bahian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in panicles, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern. It is often found in open areas, such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
