Croton spicatus

Croton spicatus, commonly known as the spiky croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by woody, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

Croton spicatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
