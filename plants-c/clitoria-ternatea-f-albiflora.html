Clitoria ternatea f. albiflora

Clitoria ternatea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered winged bean, is a variant of the winged bean plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or pink hues of the species. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and slightly hairy.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional value, making it a popular choice in both gardens and agricultural settings.
