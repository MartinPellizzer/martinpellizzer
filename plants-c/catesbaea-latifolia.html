Catesbaea latifolia

Catesbaea latifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pataua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
