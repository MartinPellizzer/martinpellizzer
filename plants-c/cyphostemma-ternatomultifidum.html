Cyphostemma ternatomultifidum

Cyphostemma ternatomultifidum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves that are often deeply lobed or trifoliate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
