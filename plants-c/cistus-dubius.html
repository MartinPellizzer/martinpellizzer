Cistus dubius

Cistus dubius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cistus dubius is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
