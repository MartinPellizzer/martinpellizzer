Claytonia asarifolia

Claytonia asarifolia, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Claytonia asarifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
