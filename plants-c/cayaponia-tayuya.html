Cayaponia tayuya

Cayaponia tayuya, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Cayaponia tayuya is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local medicine.
