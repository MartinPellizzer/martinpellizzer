Cineraria pentactina

Cineraria pentactina, commonly known as the five-lobed cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue or violet petals that are often tinged with white, creating a dramatic visual effect. Its leaves are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cineraria pentactina thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
