Chondrorhyncha andreae

Chondrorhyncha andreae, commonly known as the Andean glassy fern, is a unique species of fern native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble glass, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are arranged in clusters along the fronds.

Due to its specialized habitat, Chondrorhyncha andreae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
