Cerastium azoricum

Cerastium azoricum, commonly known as the Azorean chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Azores.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves and produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Cerastium azoricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
