Cuscuta epithymum var. decipiens

Cuscuta epithymum var. decipiens, commonly known as the yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is often found growing on grasses, shrubs, and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through attachment to clothing and vehicles.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
