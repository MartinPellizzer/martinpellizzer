Calandrinia pumila

Calandrinia pumila, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy, branching stems and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

Calandrinia pumila is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.
