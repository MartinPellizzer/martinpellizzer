Campanula celsii subsp. spathulifolia

Campanula celsii subsp.

spathulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lanceolate to spatulate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Campanulaceae family and is known for its hardy nature in cooler climates.
