Campanula collina subsp. fondervisii

Campanula collina subsp.

fondervisii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
