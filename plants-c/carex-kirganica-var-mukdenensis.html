Carex kirganica var. mukdenensis

Carex kirganica var. mukdenensis, commonly known as Mukden sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Mukden sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
