Crataegus pectinata

Crataegus pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
