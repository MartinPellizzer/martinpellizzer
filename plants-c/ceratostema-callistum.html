Ceratostema callistum

Ceratostema callistum, commonly known as the silver bell vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to China.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems that can twine around supports, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and as a decorative vine.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its attractive flowers and unique foliage.
