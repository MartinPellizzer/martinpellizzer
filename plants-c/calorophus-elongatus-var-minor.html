Calorophus elongatus var. minor

Calorophus elongatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser candleplant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, candle-like stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
