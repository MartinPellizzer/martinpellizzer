Carex cryptandra

Carex cryptandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as swamps, marshes, and along waterways. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Carex cryptandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in aquatic habitats.
