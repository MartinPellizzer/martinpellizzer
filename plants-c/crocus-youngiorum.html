Crocus youngiorum

Crocus youngiorum, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which are borne on short, sturdy stems. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, well-drained soils, and it thrives in temperate climates with cool winters.

The Persian crocus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
