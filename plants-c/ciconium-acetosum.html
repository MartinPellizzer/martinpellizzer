Ciconium acetosum

Ciconium acetosum, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces violet flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
