Cissus hamaderohensis

Cissus hamaderohensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rock fig, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
