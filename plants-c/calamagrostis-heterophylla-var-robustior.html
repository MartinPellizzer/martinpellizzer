Calamagrostis heterophylla var. robustior

Calamagrostis heterophylla var. robustior, commonly known as the robust reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland restoration projects.
