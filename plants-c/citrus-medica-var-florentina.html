Citrus medica var. florentina

Citrus medica var. florentina, commonly known as the pomegranate orange, is a botanical variant of the common citron.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and segmented, orange-colored fruit. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its fruit is typically smaller than standard citrus fruits and has a unique, slightly tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
