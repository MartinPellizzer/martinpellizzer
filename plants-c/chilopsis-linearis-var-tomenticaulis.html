Chilopsis linearis var. tomenticaulis

Chilopsis linearis var. tomenticaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf trumpet creeper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, red-tinged stems and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low water requirements make it an attractive and drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
