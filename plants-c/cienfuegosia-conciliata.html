Cienfuegosia conciliata

Cienfuegosia conciliata, commonly known as the Cuban flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and light gray in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cienfuegosia conciliata is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
