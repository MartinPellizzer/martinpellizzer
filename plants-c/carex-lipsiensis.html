Carex lipsiensis

Carex lipsiensis, commonly known as the Lipscomb sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often brown or reddish in color.

Carex lipsiensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
