Convolvulus trichotosus

Convolvulus trichotosus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually ranging in color from white to pink. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, open environments.
