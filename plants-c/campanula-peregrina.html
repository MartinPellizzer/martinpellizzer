Campanula peregrina

Campanula peregrina, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula peregrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
