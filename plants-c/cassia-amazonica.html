Cassia amazonica

Cassia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is known for its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia amazonica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
