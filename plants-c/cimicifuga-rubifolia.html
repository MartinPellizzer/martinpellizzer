Cimicifuga rubifolia

Cimicifuga rubifolia, commonly known as red-root, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in late summer. Its root system is notable for its reddish color and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga rubifolia thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States.
