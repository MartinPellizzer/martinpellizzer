Cathartocarpus trinitatis

Cathartocarpus trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, hair-like florets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and wind.
