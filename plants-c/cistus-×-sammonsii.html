Cistus × sammonsii

Cistus × sammonsii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its aromatic leaves and resilience to drought make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is a cross between Cistus ladaniferus and Cistus monspeliensis, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.
