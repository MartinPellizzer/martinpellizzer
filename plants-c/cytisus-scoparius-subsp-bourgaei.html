Cytisus scoparius subsp. bourgaei

Cytisus scoparius subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a shrub native to western Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, wiry stems.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, demonstrating tolerance to salt spray and wind. Its leaves are small, compound, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
