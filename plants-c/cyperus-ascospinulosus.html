Cyperus ascospinulosus

Cyperus ascospinulosus, commonly known as the spinous flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus ascospinulosus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
