Callitriche verna var. emarginata

Callitriche verna var. emarginata, commonly known as the spring water-star, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has small, floating or submerged leaves that are often oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are tiny, with four petals, and appear in clusters near the water surface.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
