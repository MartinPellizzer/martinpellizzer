Cissus chloroleuca

Cissus chloroleuca, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinct white veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in local cuisines. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions make it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Cissus chloroleuca is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in various African communities.
