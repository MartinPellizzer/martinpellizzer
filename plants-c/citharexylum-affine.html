Citharexylum affine

Citharexylum affine, commonly known as the silverleaf cithara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
