Commelina saxicola

Commelina saxicola, commonly known as the rock commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Commelina saxicola is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
