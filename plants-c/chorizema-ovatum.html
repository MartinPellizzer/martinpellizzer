Chorizema ovatum

Chorizema ovatum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with thick, fleshy stems and waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, hairy fruits that are dispersed by the wind. Its adaptations to desert conditions include deep root systems and the ability to store water in its tissues.

Chorizema ovatum plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
