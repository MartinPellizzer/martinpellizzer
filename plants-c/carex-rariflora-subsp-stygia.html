Carex rariflora subsp. stygia

Carex rariflora subsp.

stygia, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This subspecies typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, particularly in North America and Eurasia.
