Campanula rapuncula

Campanula rapuncula, commonly known as the bellflower or the mouse ear.

This perennial herb is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine down.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
