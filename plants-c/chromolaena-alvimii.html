Chromolaena alvimii

Chromolaena alvimii, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and clearings.

Chromolaena alvimii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
