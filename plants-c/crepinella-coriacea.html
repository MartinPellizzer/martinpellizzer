Crepinella coriacea

Crepinella coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved crepinella, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
