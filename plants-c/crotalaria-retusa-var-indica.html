Crotalaria retusa var. indica

Crotalaria retusa var. indica, commonly known as the Indian crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are often accompanied by distinctive, spurred petals. It thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful species in certain agricultural contexts.
