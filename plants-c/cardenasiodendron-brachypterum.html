Cardenasiodendron brachypterum

Cardenasiodendron brachypterum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental species.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban and tropical landscapes.
