Cereus ochracanthus

Cereus ochracanthus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive reddish coloration on its spines, which gives it its common name. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Cereus ochracanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
