Cyclamen peloponnesiacum f. albiflorum

Cyclamen peloponnesiacum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered cyclamen, is a rare and striking hybrid plant native to the Peloponnese region of Greece.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and its unique white, almost porcelain-like flowers that contrast sharply with its deep green foliage. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a prized addition to rock gardens and shady borders. Its flowers are usually borne on short, sturdy stems and are often described as having a delicate, almost ethereal appearance.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
