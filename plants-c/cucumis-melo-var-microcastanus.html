Cucumis melo var. microcastanus

Cucumis melo var. microcastanus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a compact, bushy plant that produces small, elongated fruits.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and South Asia. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is usually harvested when it is still green and tender, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption.
