Chaetocyperus arenicola

Chaetocyperus arenicola, commonly known as the sand flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and saline environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small organisms.
