Croton longipedicellatus subsp. longipedicellatus

Croton longipedicellatus subsp.

longipedicellatus, commonly known as the longipedicellate croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red or purple.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
