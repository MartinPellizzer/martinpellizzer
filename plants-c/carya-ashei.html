Carya ashei

Carya ashei, commonly known as the chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit, which consists of a spiny husk enclosing a single edible nut. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and clusters of yellow-green male catkins.

The chinquapin is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
