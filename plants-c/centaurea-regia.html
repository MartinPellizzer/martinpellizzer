Centaurea regia

Centaurea regia, commonly known as the royal centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and bristly foliage, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Centaurea regia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
