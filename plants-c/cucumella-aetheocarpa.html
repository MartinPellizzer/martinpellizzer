Cucumella aetheocarpa

Cucumella aetheocarpa, commonly known as the golden cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant features trailing stems with palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, elongated, yellowish-orange gourd that matures in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
