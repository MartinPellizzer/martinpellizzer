Cybele villosa

Cybele villosa, commonly known as the hairy cybele, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, hairy stems that are often covered in fine white or grayish hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a similar hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cybele villosa produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, often blooming in early to mid-summer.
