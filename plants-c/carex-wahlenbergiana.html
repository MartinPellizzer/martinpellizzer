Carex wahlenbergiana

Carex wahlenbergiana, commonly known as Wahlenberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tundra and alpine environments. Its grass-like leaves are arranged in dense, grass-like clusters, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Wahlenberg's sedge is an important component of the vegetation in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
