Cinchona microphylla

Cinchona microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruits are red berries containing seeds, and it is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of quinine in its bark.

This species plays an important role in both traditional medicine and the production of antimalarial drugs.
