Clematis flabellata

Clematis flabellata, commonly known as the broad-leaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, with a flaring, bell-shaped form. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis flabellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
