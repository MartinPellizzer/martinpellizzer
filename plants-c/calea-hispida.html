Calea hispida

Calea hispida, commonly known as the "Mexican hairless plant," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its unique, hairy leaves that are covered in fine, soft trichomes, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow to orange in color and bloom in the spring.

Calea hispida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
