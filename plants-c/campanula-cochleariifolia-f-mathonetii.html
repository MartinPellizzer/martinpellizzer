Campanula cochleariifolia f. mathonetii

Campanula cochleariifolia f. mathonetii, commonly known as the Mathonet's bellflower, is a rare and striking perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, with a distinctive shape that resembles a small bell.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its rarity and unique appearance.
