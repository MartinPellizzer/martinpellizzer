Campuloclinium surinamense

Campuloclinium surinamense, commonly known as Surinam sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Suriname and Guyana.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
