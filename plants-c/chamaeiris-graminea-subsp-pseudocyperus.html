Chamaeiris graminea subsp. pseudocyperus

Chamaeiris graminea subsp.

pseudocyperus, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its growth habit is rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form dense vegetation.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
