Cissampelos caapeba

Cissampelos caapeba, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
