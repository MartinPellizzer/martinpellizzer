Carex orbicularis var. bulungensis

Carex orbicularis var. bulungensis, commonly known as the Bulungensis sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are generally broad and rounded, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
