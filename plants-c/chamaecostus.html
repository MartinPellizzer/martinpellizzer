Chamaecostus

Chamaecostus, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Chamaecostus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
