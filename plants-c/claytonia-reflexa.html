Claytonia reflexa

Claytonia reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed springbeauty, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptical, hairy leaves and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals that reflex outward, giving the species its common name.

Claytonia reflexa is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
