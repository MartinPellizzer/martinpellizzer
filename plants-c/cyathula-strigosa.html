Cyathula strigosa

Cyathula strigosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and often milky sap. The plant features a low-growing, herbaceous structure with simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Cyathula strigosa is adapted to grow in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
