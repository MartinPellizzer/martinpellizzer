Ceanothus gloriosus var. exaltatus

Ceanothus gloriosus var. exaltatus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada ceanothus, is a shrub native to California's coastal and mountain regions.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, bluish-green leaves that provide year-round structure. The plant produces showy clusters of sky-blue to white flowers in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife make it a valued species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
