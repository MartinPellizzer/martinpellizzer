Chaenorhinum minus f. pumilum

Chaenorhinum minus f. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf common toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, slender leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is often found in temperate climates and is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
