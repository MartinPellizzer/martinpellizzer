Cassia chrysocarpa var. macrocarpa

Cassia chrysocarpa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as golden cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-rich pods that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical regions.
