Chamaecrista cavalcantina

Chamaecrista cavalcantina, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that enclose its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
