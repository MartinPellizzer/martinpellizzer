Commelina nervosa

Commelina nervosa, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and it produces small, three-lobed bracts that surround the flower.

Commelina nervosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
