Cyperus tuckerianus

Cyperus tuckerianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus tuckerianus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
