Cecchia

Cecchia, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cecchia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
