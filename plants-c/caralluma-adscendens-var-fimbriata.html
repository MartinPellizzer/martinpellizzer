Caralluma adscendens var. fimbriata

Caralluma adscendens var. fimbriata, commonly known as the Fimbriated Hooded Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-draining soil and full sun.
