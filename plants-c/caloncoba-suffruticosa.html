Caloncoba suffruticosa

Caloncoba suffruticosa, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, white flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has woody stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
