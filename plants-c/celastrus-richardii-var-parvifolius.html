Celastrus richardii var. parvifolius

Celastrus richardii var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in short inflorescences, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
