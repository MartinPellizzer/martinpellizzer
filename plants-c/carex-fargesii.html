Carex fargesii

Carex fargesii, commonly known as the Farges sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Carex fargesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
