Chrysophyllum africanum var. multinervatum

Chrysophyllum africanum var. multinervatum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish fruits with a sweet, acidic pulp. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
