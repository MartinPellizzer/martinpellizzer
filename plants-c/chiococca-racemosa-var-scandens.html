Chiococca racemosa var. scandens

Chiococca racemosa var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing chiococca, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and ecological interactions.
