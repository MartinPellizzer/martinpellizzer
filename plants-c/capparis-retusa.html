Capparis retusa

Capparis retusa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Capparis retusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
