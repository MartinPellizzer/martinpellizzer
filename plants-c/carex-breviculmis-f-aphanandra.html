Carex breviculmis f. aphanandra

Carex breviculmis f. aphanandra, commonly known as the slender-bracted sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
