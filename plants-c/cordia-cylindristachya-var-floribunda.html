Cordia cylindristachya var. floribunda

Cordia cylindristachya var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
