Carpinus macrostachya

Carpinus macrostachya, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and small, elongated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fruit is a small, winged nut enclosed in a spiky husk.

The tree is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental qualities.
