Cyathea divergens var. sipaliwiniana

Cyathea divergens var. sipaliwiniana, commonly known as the Sipaliwinian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Guyana and Suriname.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
