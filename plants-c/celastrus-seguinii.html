Celastrus seguinii

Celastrus seguinii, commonly known as the Mexican twiner, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
