Calamagrostis pringlei

Calamagrostis pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's reed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue-green hues. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open areas such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage.

Calamagrostis pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
