Cistus × hybridus f. secallianus

Cistus × hybridus f. secallianus, commonly known as the Sicilian rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and dry environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs that secrete essential oils, contributing to its distinctive scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
