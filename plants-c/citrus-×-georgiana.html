Citrus × georgiana

Citrus × georgiana, commonly known as the Meyer lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originated in the early 20th century through the crossbreeding of the lemon and the citron. This plant is characterized by its small, round, and often fragrant yellow fruit, which is typically harvested when it reaches a mature size. The leaves are glossy and dark green, while the tree has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening.

It is valued for its high vitamin C content and aromatic zest, commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
