Codiaeum variegatum f. latifolium

Codiaeum variegatum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored, and often variegated leaves that come in shades of green, yellow, red, and orange. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
