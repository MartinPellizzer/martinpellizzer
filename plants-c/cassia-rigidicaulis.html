Cassia rigidicaulis

Cassia rigidicaulis, commonly known as the rigid candlewood, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, rigid stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing small, flat seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Cassia rigidicaulis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
