Ceratandra

Ceratandra, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plants are characterized by their unique, helmet-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. Ceratandra species typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrial plants, often found in humid, shaded environments.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
