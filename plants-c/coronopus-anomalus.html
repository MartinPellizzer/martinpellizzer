Coronopus anomalus

Coronopus anomalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
