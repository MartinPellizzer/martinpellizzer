Canthium glaucum

Canthium glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange cyathia, which are specialized flower structures. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Its latex is milky and can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.
