Clusia guayanae

Clusia guayanae, commonly known as the Guayana glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

Clusia guayanae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
