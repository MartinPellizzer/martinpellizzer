Cleome roridula

Cleome roridula, commonly known as the desert willow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and ability to store water. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cleome roridula is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands.
