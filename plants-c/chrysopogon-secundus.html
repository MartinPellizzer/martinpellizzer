Chrysopogon secundus

Chrysopogon secundus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. This plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Chrysopogon secundus is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
