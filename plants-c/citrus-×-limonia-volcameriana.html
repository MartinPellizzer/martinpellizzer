Citrus × limonia volcameriana

Citrus × limonia volcameriana, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel, which is rich in vitamin C. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The fruit's unique flavor and nutritional profile make it a popular choice in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
