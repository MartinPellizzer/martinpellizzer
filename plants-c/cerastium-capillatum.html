Cerastium capillatum

Cerastium capillatum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds.

Cerastium capillatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate foliage and early blooming habit.
