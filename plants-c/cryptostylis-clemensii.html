Cryptostylis clemensii

Cryptostylis clemensii, commonly known as the Clemens' spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, narrow leaves and clustered, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in understorey vegetation. It has a short flowering period, usually in late winter to early spring, and relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
