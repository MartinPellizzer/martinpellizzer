Convolvuloides palmata

Convolvuloides palmata, commonly known as the palmate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas within tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
