Crambe palmatifida

Crambe palmatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved crambe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Crambe palmatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its potential in biofuel production.
