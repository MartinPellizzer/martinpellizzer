Clethra stolonifera

Clethra stolonifera, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Clethra stolonifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
