Cupressus devoniana

Cupressus devoniana, commonly known as the Devonian cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it has aromatic, fibrous bark that peels in thin layers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
