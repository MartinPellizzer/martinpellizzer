Coudenbergia angelicifolia

Coudenbergia angelicifolia, commonly known as the angel's leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
