Conyza ternatensis

Conyza ternatensis, commonly known as the slenderleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as roadsides and disturbed soils, and is often found in regions with warm climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Conyza ternatensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
