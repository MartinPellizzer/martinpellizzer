Cyperus biglumis

Cyperus biglumis, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Cyperus biglumis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in aquatic conditions.
