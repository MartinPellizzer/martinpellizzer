Ceropegia kalmbacheriana

Ceropegia kalmbacheriana, commonly known as the Kalmbacher's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Ceropegia kalmbacheriana is also known for its ability to form aerial roots, which it uses to climb and support itself.
