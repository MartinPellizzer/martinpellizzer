Cissampelos torulosa

Cissampelos torulosa, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its stems are woody and can climb by means of tendrils, allowing it to grow along trees or shrubs.
