Crataegus incisa

Crataegus incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Crataegus incisa is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

It is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
