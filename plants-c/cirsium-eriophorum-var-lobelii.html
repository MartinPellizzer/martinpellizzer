Cirsium eriophorum var. lobelii

Cirsium eriophorum var. lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and is adapted to arid conditions, with hairy stems that help reduce water loss.

The flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family, and attract various pollinators.
