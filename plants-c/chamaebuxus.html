Chamaebuxus

Chamaebuxus, commonly known as boxwood, is a genus of evergreen shrubs belonging to the family Buxaceae.

These plants are characterized by their dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. They are native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and are often cultivated as ornamental plants. Chamaebuxus species typically have small, rounded leaves that are arranged in clusters, and they produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

The wood of these plants is dense and durable, historically used for making intricate carvings and furniture.
