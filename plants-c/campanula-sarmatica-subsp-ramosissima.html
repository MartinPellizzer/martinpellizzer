Campanula sarmatica subsp. ramosissima

Campanula sarmatica subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it often forms dense colonies in rocky or grassy habitats. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
