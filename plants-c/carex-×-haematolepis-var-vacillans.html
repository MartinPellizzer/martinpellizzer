Carex × haematolepis var. vacillans

Carex × haematolepis var. vacillans, commonly known as the redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, usually pale to brownish flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
