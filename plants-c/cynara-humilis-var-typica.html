Cynara humilis var. typica

Cynara humilis var. typica, commonly known as the common artichoke, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of spiny, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a compact, spiky flower head that develops into a edible, thistle-like vegetable. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

The plant is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive characteristics.
