Campanula tricuspidata

Campanula tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula tricuspidata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
