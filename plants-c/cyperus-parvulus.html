Cyperus parvulus

Cyperus parvulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, preferring shallow water or moist soil. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts.

Cyperus parvulus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery foliage.
