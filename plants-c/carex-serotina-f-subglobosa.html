Carex serotina f. subglobosa

Carex serotina f. subglobosa, commonly known as the late-blooming sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
