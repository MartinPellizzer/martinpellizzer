Conospermum taxifolium var. typicum

Conospermum taxifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the slender-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences and are often visited by birds and insects.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
