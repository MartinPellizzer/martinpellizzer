Carpinus caroliniana var. virginiana

Carpinus caroliniana var. virginiana, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that mature in the fall. Its dense, compact growth habit makes it suitable for urban landscapes and small garden spaces.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
