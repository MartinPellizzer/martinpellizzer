Calamagrostis huebneriana var. pubescens

Calamagrostis huebneriana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
