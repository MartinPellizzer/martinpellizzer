Centaurea toletana subsp. tentudaica

Centaurea toletana subsp.

tentudaica, commonly known as the Tenthudaic Bladder Campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers in summer. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, composite flowers surrounded by bracts.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
