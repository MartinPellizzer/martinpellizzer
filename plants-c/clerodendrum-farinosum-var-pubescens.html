Clerodendrum farinosum var. pubescens

Clerodendrum farinosum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spiderflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name 'pubescens' refers to the soft, hairy texture of its foliage.
