Cousinia bornmuelleriana

Cousinia bornmuelleriana, commonly known as the Armenian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy, basal leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Cousinia bornmuelleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
