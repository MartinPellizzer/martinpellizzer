Ceropegia distincta subsp. verruculosa

Ceropegia distincta subsp.

verruculosa, commonly known as the African violet or velvet leaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, intricate structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
