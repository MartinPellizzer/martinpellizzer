Crioceras dipladeniiflorus

Crioceras dipladeniiflorus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
