Cordia oblongifolia

Cordia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Cordia oblongifolia thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
