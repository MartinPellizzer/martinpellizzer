Clerodendrum uncinatum

Clerodendrum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
