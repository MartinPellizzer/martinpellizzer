Cassia parahyba

Cassia parahyba, commonly known as flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in landscaping for its striking floral display.

Cassia parahyba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in urban environments.
