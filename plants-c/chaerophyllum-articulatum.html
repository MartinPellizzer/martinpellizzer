Chaerophyllum articulatum

Chaerophyllum articulatum, commonly known as the jointed hogweed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like leaves and umbellate inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions. The plant features a distinctive jointed stem and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The plant is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
