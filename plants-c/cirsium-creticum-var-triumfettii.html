Cirsium creticum var. triumfettii

Cirsium creticum var. triumfettii, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect with bracts.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
