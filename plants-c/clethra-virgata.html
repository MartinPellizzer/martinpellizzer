Clethra virgata

Clethra virgata, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Clethra virgata is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
