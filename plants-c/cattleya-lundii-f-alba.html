Cattleya lundii f. alba

Cattleya lundii f. alba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid variety renowned for its striking white petals and vibrant yellow lip.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its elegant, fragrant blooms that typically appear during the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a popular ornamental species in horticulture and its contribution to the genetic diversity of orchid hybrids.
