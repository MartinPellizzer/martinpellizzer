Cirsium monspessulanum var. discolor

Cirsium monspessulanum var. discolor, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The varietal name *discolor* refers to the variation in flower coloration observed in different populations.
