Chrysocoma cymosa

Chrysocoma cymosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

Chrysocoma cymosa is also valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
