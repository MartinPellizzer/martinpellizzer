Chionogentias saxosa

Chionogentias saxosa, commonly known as the snow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in alpine environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Chionogentias saxosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
