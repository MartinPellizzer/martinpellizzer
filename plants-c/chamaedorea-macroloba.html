Chamaedorea macroloba

Chamaedorea macroloba, commonly known as the giant feather palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light conditions. Its leaves are composed of numerous elongated leaflets, which give it a feathery appearance.

The plant is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
