Ceropegia columnaris

Ceropegia columnaris, commonly known as the columnar devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has succulent stems that are often branched and covered in small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
