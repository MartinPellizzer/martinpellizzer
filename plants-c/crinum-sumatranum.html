Crinum sumatranum

Crinum sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran crinum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Crinum sumatranum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
