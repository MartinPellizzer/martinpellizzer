Cordia ulei var. ucayaliensis

Cordia ulei var. ucayaliensis, commonly known as the Amazonian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and as a source of timber. It also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine, with various parts of the plant being used to treat ailments.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for numerous wildlife species in the region.
