Carex microdonta var. controversa

Carex microdonta var. controversa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
