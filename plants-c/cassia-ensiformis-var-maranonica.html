Cassia ensiformis var. maranonica

Cassia ensiformis var. maranonica, commonly known as the Maranon cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in riverine forests and disturbed areas.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
