Cyperus purpureovaginatus

Cyperus purpureovaginatus, commonly known as the purple-veined flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a purple or reddish hue along the veins, which gives it its common name.

Cyperus purpureovaginatus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
