Ceratostylis heleocharis

Ceratostylis heleocharis, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch, and its unique, spiral-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. It has a short, stocky rhizome and produces small, lance-shaped leaves.

The orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators.
