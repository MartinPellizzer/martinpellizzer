Cymbochasma borysthenica

Cymbochasma borysthenica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of southern Ukraine and southern Russia.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, ascending stems. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun conditions.
