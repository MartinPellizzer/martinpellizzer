Campanula pseudovaldensis

Campanula pseudovaldensis, commonly known as the false bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula pseudovaldensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
