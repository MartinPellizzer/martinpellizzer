Capsicum hispidum var. glabriusculum

Capsicum hispidum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Mexican chili or habanero pepper, is a wild species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub-like plant with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that are followed by bright red, bell-shaped fruits. The fruits are typically small to medium in size and have a high capsaicin content, contributing to their spicy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional cuisines.
