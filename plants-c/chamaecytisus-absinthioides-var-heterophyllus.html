Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. heterophyllus

Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, thorny stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically small, alternate, and have a distinctive silvery-green color due to their dense pubescence. It produces yellowish-pink flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well adapted to poor, dry soils and is often used in coastal or rocky garden settings.
