Crotalaria linifolia var. angustifolia

Crotalaria linifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
