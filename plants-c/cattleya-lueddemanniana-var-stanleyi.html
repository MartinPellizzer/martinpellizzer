Cattleya lueddemanniana var. stanleyi

Cattleya lueddemanniana var. stanleyi, commonly known as the Stanley's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, and showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange with red or purple markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for both indoor and greenhouse cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to orchid collections and a favorite among horticultural enthusiasts.
