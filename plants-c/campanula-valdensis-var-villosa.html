Campanula valdensis var. villosa

Campanula valdensis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Campanula valdensis var. villosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
