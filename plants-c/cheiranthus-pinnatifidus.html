Cheiranthus pinnatifidus

Cheiranthus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
