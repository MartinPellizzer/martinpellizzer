Cinnamomum sericeum

Cinnamomum sericeum, commonly known as silver flame or silver cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue-black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
