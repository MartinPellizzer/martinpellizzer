Carex fulleri

Carex fulleri, commonly known as the Vancouver sedge, is a perennial plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shady conditions.
