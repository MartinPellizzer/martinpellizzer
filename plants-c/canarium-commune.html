Canarium commune

Canarium commune, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, oval fruits containing a single seed. The oil extracted from its seeds is used in cooking and traditional medicine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also valued for its timber.
