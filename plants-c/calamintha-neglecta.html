Calamintha neglecta

Calamintha neglecta, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic properties are due to the presence of essential oils, particularly thymol and carvacrol.

Calamintha neglecta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
