Corymbia disjuncta

Corymbia disjuncta, commonly known as the Red River gum, is a species of flowering tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and long, pendulous clusters of flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish hue on their upper surface. This tree thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
