Cuscuta epithymum subsp. approximata

Cuscuta epithymum subsp.

approximata, commonly known as the common dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients. It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around host plants.

The plant is widespread in various habitats, including fields, gardens, and disturbed areas. It is often considered a weed due to its ability to infest a wide range of host species.

Its life cycle involves seed dispersal through the wind, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.
