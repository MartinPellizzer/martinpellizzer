Clusia magnifolia

Clusia magnifolia, commonly known as the shining glory or silverleaf glory, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It is well adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Clusia magnifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience to urban environments.
