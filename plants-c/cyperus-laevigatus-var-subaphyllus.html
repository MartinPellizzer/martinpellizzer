Cyperus laevigatus var. subaphyllus

Cyperus laevigatus var. subaphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
