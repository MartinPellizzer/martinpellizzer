Campanula americana

Campanula americana, commonly known as American bellflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
