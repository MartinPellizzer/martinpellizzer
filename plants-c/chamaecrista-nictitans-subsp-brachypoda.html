Chamaecrista nictitans subsp. brachypoda

Chamaecrista nictitans subsp.

brachypoda, commonly known as the shortpod cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a legume characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
