Capsicum axi

Capsicum axi, commonly known as the wild chili pepper, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by the development of its fleshy, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
