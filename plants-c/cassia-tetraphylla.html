Cassia tetraphylla

Cassia tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Cassia tetraphylla is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
