Codonanthemum parviflorum

Codonanthemum parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved codonanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
