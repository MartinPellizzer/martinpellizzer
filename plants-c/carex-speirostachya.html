Carex speirostachya

Carex speirostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, typical of many sedges. Carex speirostachya thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
