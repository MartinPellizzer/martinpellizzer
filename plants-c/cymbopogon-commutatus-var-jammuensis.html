Cymbopogon commutatus var. jammuensis

Cymbopogon commutatus var. jammuensis, commonly known as the Jammu grass, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale-green florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
