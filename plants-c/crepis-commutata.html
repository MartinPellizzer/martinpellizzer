Crepis commutata

Crepis commutata, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is typically herbaceous, with a compact growth habit.

Crepis commutata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
