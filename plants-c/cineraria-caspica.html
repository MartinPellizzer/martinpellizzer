Cineraria caspica

Cineraria caspica, commonly known as the Caspian cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry climates.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
