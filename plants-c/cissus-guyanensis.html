Cissus guyanensis

Cissus guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its leaves are typically glossy and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Cissus guyanensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb.
