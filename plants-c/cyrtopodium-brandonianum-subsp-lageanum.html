Cyrtopodium brandonianum subsp. lageanum

Cyrtopodium brandonianum subsp.

lageanum, commonly known as the Brazilian hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with reddish-brown markings. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
