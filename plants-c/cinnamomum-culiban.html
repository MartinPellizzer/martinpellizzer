Cinnamomum culiban

Cinnamomum culiban, commonly known as the Malayan cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
