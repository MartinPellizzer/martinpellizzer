Ceropegia nanior

Ceropegia nanior, commonly known as the small-leaved ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a unique corona structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia nanior is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
