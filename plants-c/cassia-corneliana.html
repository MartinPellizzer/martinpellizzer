Cassia corneliana

Cassia corneliana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Cassia corneliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its showy floral display.
