Cassia borbonioides

Cassia borbonioides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Cassia borbonioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
