Centaurea formanekii

Centaurea formanekii, commonly known as the Formanek's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea formanekii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
