Chrysothamnus linifolius

Chrysothamnus linifolius, commonly known as silverleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact growth habit. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
