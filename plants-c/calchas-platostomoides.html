Calchas platostomoides

Calchas platostomoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering stems are often unbranched and bear a single spike of flowers.

Calchas platostomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
