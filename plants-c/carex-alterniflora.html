Carex alterniflora

Carex alterniflora, commonly known as the alternate-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to wetland and marsh environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces three-sided, cylindrical spikes of flowers, typically in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
