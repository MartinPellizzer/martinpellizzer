Centaurea × frayana

Centaurea × frayana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea solstitialis (yellow starthistle). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers that range from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden cultivation and ecological restoration projects.
