Cordia leucophlyctis

Cordia leucophlyctis, commonly known as the white-leafed cordia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically white or pale green on the underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, tropical lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
