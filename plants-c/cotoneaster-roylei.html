Cotoneaster roylei

Cotoneaster roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its showy flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Cotoneaster roylei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its winter interest and structural form.
