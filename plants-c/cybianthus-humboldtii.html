Cybianthus humboldtii

Cybianthus humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastlines of Chile and Peru. It is known for its resilience to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Cybianthus humboldtii is often found in maritime environments where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
