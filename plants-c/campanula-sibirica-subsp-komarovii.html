Campanula sibirica subsp. komarovii

Campanula sibirica subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features erect stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, bearing clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are lanceolate and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
