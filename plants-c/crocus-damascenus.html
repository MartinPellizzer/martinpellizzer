Crocus damascenus

Crocus damascenus, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features showy, early spring blooms with bright yellow petals and a distinctive, fragrant, violet-colored center. The plant produces a starchy, edible corm that is often used in traditional cuisine. Its flowers are among the first to appear in spring, making it a symbol of the season's renewal.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
