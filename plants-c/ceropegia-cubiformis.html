Ceropegia cubiformis

Ceropegia cubiformis, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia cubiformis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
