Centaurea longifolia

Centaurea longifolia, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Centaurea longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
