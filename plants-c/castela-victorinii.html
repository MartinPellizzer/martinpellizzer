Castela victorinii

Castela victorinii, commonly known as the Madagascar starflower, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Castela victorinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
