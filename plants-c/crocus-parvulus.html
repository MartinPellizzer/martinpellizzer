Crocus parvulus

Crocus parvulus, commonly known as the small crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, early spring blooms with bright yellow or orange petals. Its bulbs are relatively small and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Crocus parvulus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to bloom before many other spring flowers.
