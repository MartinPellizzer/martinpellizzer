Capparis breynia f. parviflora

Capparis breynia f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered caper, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually small and fleshy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
