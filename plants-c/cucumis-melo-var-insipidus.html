Cucumis melo var. insipidus

Cucumis melo var. insipidus, commonly known as the edible wild cucumber, is a variant of the melon genus native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, less sweet flavor compared to cultivated melons. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, often climbing or spreading along the ground. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, arid environments.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional cuisines.
