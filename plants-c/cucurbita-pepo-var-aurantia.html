Cucurbita pepo var. aurantia

Cucurbita pepo var. aurantia, commonly known as the orange pumpkin, is a cultivated variety of the common gourd.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow-orange skin and sweet, edible flesh. The plant is a vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, which are followed by the characteristic pumpkin fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely used in culinary applications and also has ornamental value in gardens.
