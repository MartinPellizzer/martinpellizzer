Campylostemon laurentii

Campylostemon laurentii, commonly known as the laurentian campylostemon, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
