Campanula velutina var. malacitana

Campanula velutina var. malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
