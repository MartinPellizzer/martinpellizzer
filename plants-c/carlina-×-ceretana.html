Carlina × ceretana

Carlina × ceretana, commonly known as the hybrid garden carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features robust, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its showy purple-blue flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina × ceretana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
