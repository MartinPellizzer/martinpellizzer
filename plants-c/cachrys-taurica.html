Cachrys taurica

Cachrys taurica, commonly known as the Tauric catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing herb with fleshy, often hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the region's flora.
