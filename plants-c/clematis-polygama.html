Clematis polygama

Clematis polygama, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody vines and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires support due to its climbing habit.

Clematis polygama is also valued for its fragrant blossoms and adaptability to various garden settings.
