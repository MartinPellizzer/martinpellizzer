Cestrum parqui var. glabriusculum

Cestrum parqui var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically at night. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
