Campanula patula var. dasycarpa

Campanula patula var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
