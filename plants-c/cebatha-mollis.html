Cebatha mollis

Cebatha mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Cebatha mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
