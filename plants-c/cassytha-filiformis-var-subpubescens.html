Cassytha filiformis var. subpubescens

Cassytha filiformis var. subpubescens, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a parasitic vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, thread-like stems that can grow along other plants, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white flowers and red berries, which are adapted for dispersal by birds. Its leaves are opposite and usually covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a silky appearance.

It is often found in humid environments, where it thrives by drawing nutrients from its host plants.
