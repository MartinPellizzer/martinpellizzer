Cytisus × versicolor

Cytisus × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid broom, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It is a hybrid between Cytisus scoparius (common broom) and Cytisus purpureus (purple broom). The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and disturbed areas.
