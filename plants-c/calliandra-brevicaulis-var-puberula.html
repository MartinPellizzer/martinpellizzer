Calliandra brevicaulis var. puberula

Calliandra brevicaulis var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The hairy calliandra is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in soil enrichment.
