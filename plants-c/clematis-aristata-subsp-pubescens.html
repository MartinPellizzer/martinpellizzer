Clematis aristata subsp. pubescens

Clematis aristata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, with five petals and a distinctive, recurved shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
