Campylospermum calophyllum

Campylospermum calophyllum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
