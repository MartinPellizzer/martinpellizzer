Centaurea noeana

Centaurea noeana, commonly known as the Greek star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across various habitats.

Centaurea noeana is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
