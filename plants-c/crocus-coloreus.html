Crocus coloreus

Crocus coloreus, commonly known as the red crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with six petals, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. Crocus coloreus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit, adding color to gardens when few other plants are in flower.
