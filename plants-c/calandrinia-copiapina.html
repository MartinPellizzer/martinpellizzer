Calandrinia copiapina

Calandrinia copiapina, commonly known as the Copiapo ice plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, bright pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Calandrinia copiapina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
