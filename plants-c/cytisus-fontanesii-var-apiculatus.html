Cytisus fontanesii var. apiculatus

Cytisus fontanesii var. apiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, often branched stems that are covered in fine, yellowish hairs. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It produces small, bright yellow flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months.

This variety is adapted to rocky and sunny environments, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
