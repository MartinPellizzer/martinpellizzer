Clinopodium acutifolium

Clinopodium acutifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Clinopodium acutifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
