Cardamine hirsuta proles flexuosa

Cardamine hirsuta proles flexuosa, commonly known as the curly watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive wavy, curled margin, which is a key botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it a common sight in stream banks and woodland areas.
