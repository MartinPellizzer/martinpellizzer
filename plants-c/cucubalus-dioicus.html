Cucubalus dioicus

Cucubalus dioicus, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers, and is characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and oval, serrated leaves. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines across the continent.
