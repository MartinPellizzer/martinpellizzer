Campomanesia heterophylla

Campomanesia heterophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or soursop.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that vary in shape along the stem. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by yellow, fleshy berries. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
