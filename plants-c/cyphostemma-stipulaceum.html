Cyphostemma stipulaceum

Cyphostemma stipulaceum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dark blue to black when mature. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Cyphostemma stipulaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
