Carex pallescens var. chalcodeta

Carex pallescens var. chalcodeta, commonly known as the gray sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually a bluish-gray color, which helps in distinguishing it from other sedges.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.
