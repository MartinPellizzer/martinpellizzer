Commiphora guerichiania

Commiphora guerichiania, commonly known as the Guerichian myrrh tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitats.
