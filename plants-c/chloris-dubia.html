Chloris dubia

Chloris dubia, commonly known as the slender grass or slender couch, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its durability and low maintenance requirements.
