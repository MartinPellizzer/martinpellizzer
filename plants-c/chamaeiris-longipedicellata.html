Chamaeiris longipedicellata

Chamaeiris longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-peduncled sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal spikes, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Chamaeiris longipedicellata is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
