Convolvulus benehoavensis

Convolvulus benehoavensis, commonly known as the Benhoavensis bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. It is native to arid regions and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to harsh environments.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
