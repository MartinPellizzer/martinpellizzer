Celosia chenopodiifolia

Celosia chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the flame flower or fire plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Celosia chenopodiifolia is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
