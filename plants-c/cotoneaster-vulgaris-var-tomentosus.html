Cotoneaster vulgaris var. tomentosus

Cotoneaster vulgaris var. tomentosus, commonly known as the downy cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and small mammals.
