Carex sempervirens f. laxiflora

Carex sempervirens f. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, dark green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish flowers in spike-like clusters, which appear in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This ornamental plant is valued for its durability and ability to thrive in shady, wet conditions.
