Cassia latistipula var. glauca

Cassia latistipula var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is distinguished by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a bluish-gray appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
