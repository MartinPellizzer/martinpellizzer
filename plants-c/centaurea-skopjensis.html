Centaurea skopjensis

Centaurea skopjensis, commonly known as the Skopje centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea skopjensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
