Crocus biflorus subsp. adami

Crocus biflorus subsp.

adami, commonly known as the Adam's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and habitat preferences compared to other crocus species.
