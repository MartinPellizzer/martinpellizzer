Catasetum scurra

Catasetum scurra, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which resemble a lady's slipper in shape. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers have a unique structure with a specialized labellum that plays a role in pollination by specific bee species.

Catasetum scurra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
