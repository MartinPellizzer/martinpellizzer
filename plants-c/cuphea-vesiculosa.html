Cuphea vesiculosa

Cuphea vesiculosa, commonly known as the bubblecup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round seed pods that resemble bubbles, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cuphea vesiculosa is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
