Colocasia heterochroma

Colocasia heterochroma, commonly known as the variegated elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a unique coloration pattern, often featuring white, cream, or yellow markings against a deep green background. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its distinctive inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Colocasia heterochroma thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
