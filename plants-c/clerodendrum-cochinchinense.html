Clerodendrum cochinchinense

Clerodendrum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The small, tubular flowers are typically pale yellow or green, providing nectar for pollinators.
