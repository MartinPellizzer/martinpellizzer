Caragana qingheensis

Caragana qingheensis, commonly known as the Qingheensis shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to northern China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by legume pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.

Its resilience and hardy nature make it suitable for reforestation projects in harsh climates.
