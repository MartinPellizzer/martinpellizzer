Callitris schwarzii

Callitris schwarzii, commonly known as the sand pine, is a species of conifer native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bark is often fissured and scaly, providing a rugged appearance.

Callitris schwarzii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
