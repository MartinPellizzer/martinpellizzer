Centaurea heratensis

Centaurea heratensis, commonly known as Herat's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea heratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
