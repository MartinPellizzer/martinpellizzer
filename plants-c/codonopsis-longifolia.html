Codonopsis longifolia

Codonopsis longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or the threadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its roots are valued in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Codonopsis longifolia make it an important species in both horticulture and medicinal botany.
