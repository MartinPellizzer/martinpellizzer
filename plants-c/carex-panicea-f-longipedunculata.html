Carex panicea f. longipedunculata

Carex panicea f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled panicled sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spikelets that form a panicle inflorescence. The leaves are typically grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette. This subspecies is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
