Catasetum × splendens var. lucianii

Catasetum × splendens var. lucianii, commonly known as the Lucian's Catasetum, is a hybrid orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that often display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces specialized pollinia that are transferred to the pollinators by a unique mechanism involving a sticky appendage. It typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in orchid hybridization and its ornamental value in cultivation.
