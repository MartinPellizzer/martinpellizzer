Carex acuta var. turfosa

Carex acuta var. turfosa, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
