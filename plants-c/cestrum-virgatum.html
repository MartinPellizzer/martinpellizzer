Cestrum virgatum

Cestrum virgatum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal beauty.
