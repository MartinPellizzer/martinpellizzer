Cercouratea fieldingiana

Cercouratea fieldingiana, commonly known as the Fielding's cypress, is a rare and ancient coniferous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and conical shape, typical of many cypress species. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary history, offering insights into the development of coniferous flora.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
