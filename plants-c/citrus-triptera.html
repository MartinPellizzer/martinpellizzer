Citrus triptera

Citrus triptera, commonly known as the three-lobed citrus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow. Its botanical features include a thick, aromatic peel and a juicy, segmented pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
