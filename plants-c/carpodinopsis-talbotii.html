Carpodinopsis talbotii

Carpodinopsis talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its succulent leaves allow it to store water, making it drought-tolerant and well-adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
