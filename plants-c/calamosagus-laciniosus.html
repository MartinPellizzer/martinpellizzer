Calamosagus laciniosus

Calamosagus laciniosus, commonly known as the slender swamp reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to thrive in waterlogged environments.

Calamosagus laciniosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic species.
