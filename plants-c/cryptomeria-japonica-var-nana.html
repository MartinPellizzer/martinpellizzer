Cryptomeria japonica var. nana

Cryptomeria japonica var. nana, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features a rounded, dense foliage with scale-like leaves that are a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative specimen plant.

Its dwarf form makes it ideal for small spaces, offering a unique ornamental value in horticulture.
