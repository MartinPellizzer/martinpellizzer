Crepis foetida var. hispida

Crepis foetida var. hispida, commonly known as the stinking hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by a series of bracts, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
