Carex qinghaiensis

Carex qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Qinghai region of China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, high-altitude environments such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Carex qinghaiensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
