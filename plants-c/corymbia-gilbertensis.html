Corymbia gilbertensis

Corymbia gilbertensis, commonly known as Gilbert's bloodwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia, particularly in Queensland and the Northern Territory. The tree is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
