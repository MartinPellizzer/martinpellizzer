Combretum octagonum

Combretum octagonum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often reddish, exfoliating bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Combretum octagonum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
