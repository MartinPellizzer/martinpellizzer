Cheirodendron trigynum

Cheirodendron trigynum, commonly known as the three-flowered cheirodendron, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited economic use.
