Cavendishia rhynchophylla

Cavendishia rhynchophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
