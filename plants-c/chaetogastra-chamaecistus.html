Chaetogastra chamaecistus

Chaetogastra chamaecistus, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.
