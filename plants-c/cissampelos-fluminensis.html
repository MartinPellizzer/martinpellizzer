Cissampelos fluminensis

Cissampelos fluminensis, commonly known as the river cissampelos, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.
