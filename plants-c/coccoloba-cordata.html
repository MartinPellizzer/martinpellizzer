Coccoloba cordata

Coccoloba cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, heart-shaped appearance. Its showy, yellow flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.
