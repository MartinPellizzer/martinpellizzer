Cattleya chlorantha

Cattleya chlorantha, commonly known as the Green Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to greenish-white, often with a yellowish or pinkish tint. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Cattleya chlorantha is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
