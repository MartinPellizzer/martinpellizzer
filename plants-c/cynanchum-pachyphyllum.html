Cynanchum pachyphyllum

Cynanchum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and produces a milky sap that can be toxic. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
