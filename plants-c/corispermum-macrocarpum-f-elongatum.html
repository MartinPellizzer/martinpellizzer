Corispermum macrocarpum f. elongatum

Corispermum macrocarpum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated bladderwort, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid or coastal conditions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines and salt marshes.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to harsh, saline habitats.
