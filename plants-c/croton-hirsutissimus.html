Croton hirsutissimus

Croton hirsutissimus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
