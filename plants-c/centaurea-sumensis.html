Centaurea sumensis

Centaurea sumensis, commonly known as the Sumian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea sumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
