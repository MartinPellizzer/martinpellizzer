Cucurbita pepo var. xanthoum

Cucurbita pepo var. xanthoum, commonly known as the yellow squash, is a variant of the common squash species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, elongated fruits that typically measure between 6 to 12 inches in length. The plant is an annual vine with palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are commonly used in culinary applications.
