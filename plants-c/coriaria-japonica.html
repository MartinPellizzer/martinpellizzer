Coriaria japonica

Coriaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Coriaria japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
