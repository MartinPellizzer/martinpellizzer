Cassia mimosoides subsp. leschenaultiana

Cassia mimosoides subsp.

leschenaultiana, commonly known as the Leschenault's cassia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the mimosa plant, hence its common name.

The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that are typical of the legume family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. Cassia mimosoides subsp.

leschenaultiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
