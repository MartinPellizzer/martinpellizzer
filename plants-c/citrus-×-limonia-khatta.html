Citrus × limonia khatta

Citrus × limonia khatta, commonly known as the khatta lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between the citron and the pomelo.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruit with a thick, bumpy rind and a high concentration of citric acid. The plant is typically evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its tart flavor and medicinal properties.
