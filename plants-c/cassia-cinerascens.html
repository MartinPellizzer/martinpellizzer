Cassia cinerascens

Cassia cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive ash-gray coloration. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

Cassia cinerascens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
