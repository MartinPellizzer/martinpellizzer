Cordia scabra

Cordia scabra, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest environments.

Cordia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
