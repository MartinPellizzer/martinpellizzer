Cassia stillifera

Cassia stillifera, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its wood is hard and durable.

Cassia stillifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
