Coursetia seleri

Coursetia seleri, commonly known as the Seler's coursetia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
