Cyrtacanthus corymbosus

Cyrtacanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. Cyrtacanthus corymbosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
