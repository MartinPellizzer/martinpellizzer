Cucumis microspermus var. koreanus

Cucumis microspermus var. koreanus, commonly known as the Korean cucumber, is a variant of the species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces small, elongated fruits with a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant has hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional Korean cuisine and as a decorative plant.
