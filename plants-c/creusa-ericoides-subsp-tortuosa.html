Creusa ericoides subsp. tortuosa

Creusa ericoides subsp.

tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted heath, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in acidic conditions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its twisted, spiraling growth pattern, which gives it its common name.
