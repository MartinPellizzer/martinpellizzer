Cyperus violifolius

Cyperus violifolius, commonly known as the violetleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with violet, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus violifolius produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among its foliage, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
