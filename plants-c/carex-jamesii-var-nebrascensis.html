Carex jamesii var. nebrascensis

Carex jamesii var. nebrascensis, commonly known as the Nebraska sedge, is a perennial plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
