Chamaecrista conferta var. simulans

Chamaecrista conferta var. simulans, commonly known as the purple crown wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with small, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
