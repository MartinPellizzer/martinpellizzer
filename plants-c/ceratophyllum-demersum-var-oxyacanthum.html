Ceratophyllum demersum var. oxyacanthum

Ceratophyllum demersum var. oxyacanthum, commonly known as the spiked water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of mucus. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and canals.

This variety is notable for its ability to form dense mats, which can provide habitat for aquatic organisms but may also become invasive in certain ecosystems.
