Cephalaria dirmilensis

Cephalaria dirmilensis, commonly known as the Dirmil's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
