Colymbada amblensis var. tentudaica

Colymbada amblensis var. tentudaica, commonly known as the Tented Marsh Balm, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in temperate regions.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in aquatic environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
