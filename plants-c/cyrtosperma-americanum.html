Cyrtosperma americanum

Cyrtosperma americanum, commonly known as the American arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a stout, fleshy rhizome that stores starch. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is native to the Americas. Its flowers are clustered in an inflorescence called a spathe, which is often green or reddish in color.

The plant is valued for its starchy roots, which are used as a food source in some regions.
