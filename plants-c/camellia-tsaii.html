Camellia tsaii

Camellia tsaii, commonly known as the Taiwan camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China and Taiwan.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The flowers are typically red or pink and bloom in late winter to early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Camellia tsaii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
