Coleus ternatus

Coleus ternatus, commonly known as the three-leafed coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Coleus ternatus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
