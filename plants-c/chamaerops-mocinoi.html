Chamaerops mocinoi

Chamaerops mocinoi, commonly known as the Mocino's fan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the region's mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

However, it is considered rare and is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
