Caesalpinia glandulosopedicellata

Caesalpinia glandulosopedicellata, commonly known as the hairy pod senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its stems are covered in glandular hairs. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
