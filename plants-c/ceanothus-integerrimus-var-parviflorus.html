Ceanothus integerrimus var. parviflorus

Ceanothus integerrimus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
