Chamaecrista tabascoensis

Chamaecrista tabascoensis, commonly known as the Tabasco burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
