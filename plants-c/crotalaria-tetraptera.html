Crotalaria tetraptera

Crotalaria tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive four-winged pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The seeds are edible and can be processed into a protein-rich flour.
