Cassia fasciculata var. depressa

Cassia fasciculata var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, twisted seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many parts of the world.
