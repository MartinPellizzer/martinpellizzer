Cytisus creticus

Cytisus creticus, commonly known as Cretan broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus creticus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
