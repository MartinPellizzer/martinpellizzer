Cirsium simplex

Cirsium simplex, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a single, upright head.

Cirsium simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
