Crocus imperatonianus var. montanus

Crocus imperatonianus var. montanus, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold climates and short growing seasons.
