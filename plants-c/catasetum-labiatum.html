Catasetum labiatum

Catasetum labiatum, commonly known as the lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lip-like petals that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with several showy blooms, and its roots are adapted to grow in epiphytic conditions. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight and requires well-draining potting media.

Catasetum labiatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
