Corynephorus fascicularis

Corynephorus fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and erosion control.
