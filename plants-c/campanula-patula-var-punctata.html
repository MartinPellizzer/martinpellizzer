Campanula patula var. punctata

Campanula patula var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow spot at the base. The plant produces showy spikes of flowers during the summer months, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
