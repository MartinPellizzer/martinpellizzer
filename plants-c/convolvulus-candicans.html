Convolvulus candicans

Convolvulus candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep pink, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hanging baskets.

Convolvulus candicans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
