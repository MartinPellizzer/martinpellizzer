Chailletia macrophylla

Chailletia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a showy white flower that blooms during the day, followed by a red-brown fruit that splits open to release its seeds. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, while its leaves float on the water's surface.

Chailletia macrophylla plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
