Caesalpinia melanosticta var. greggii

Caesalpinia melanosticta var. greggii, commonly known as Gregg's ticktree, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
