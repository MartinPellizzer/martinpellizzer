Chloranthus holostegius var. trichoneurus

Chloranthus holostegius var. trichoneurus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed boxwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and lack of true wood in the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the stems. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
