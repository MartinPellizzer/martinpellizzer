Crocus thessalus

Crocus thessalus, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, early spring blooms with vivid yellow petals and a distinctive, three-lobed tepal structure. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears one to three flowers, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a spherical bulb.

Crocus thessalus is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens.
