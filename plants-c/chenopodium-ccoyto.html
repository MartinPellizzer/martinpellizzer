Chenopodium ccoyto

Chenopodium ccoyto, commonly known as quinoa, is an annual plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its bushy growth habit and small, green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich seeds. Quinoa is adapted to thrive in arid and high-altitude environments, making it a resilient crop.

Its seeds are rich in protein, fiber, and essential amino acids, contributing to its global popularity as a pseudocereal.
