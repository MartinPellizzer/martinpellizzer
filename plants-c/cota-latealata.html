Cota latealata

Cota latealata, commonly known as the late yellow cota, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with white tips, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
