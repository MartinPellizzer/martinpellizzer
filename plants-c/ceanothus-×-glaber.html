Ceanothus × glaber

Ceanothus × glaber, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal climates. Its leaves are typically leathery and aromatic, with a distinctive, sometimes spicy scent.

Ceanothus × glaber is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
