Carex camptotropa

Carex camptotropa, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on a single, terminal spike.

Carex camptotropa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
