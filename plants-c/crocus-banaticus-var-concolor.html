Crocus banaticus var. concolor

Crocus banaticus var. concolor, commonly known as the concolor crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
