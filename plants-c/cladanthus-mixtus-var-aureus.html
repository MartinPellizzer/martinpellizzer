Cladanthus mixtus var. aureus

Cladanthus mixtus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive golden throat, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
