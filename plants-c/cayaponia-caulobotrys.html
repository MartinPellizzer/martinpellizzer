Cayaponia caulobotrys

Cayaponia caulobotrys, commonly known as the stilt-rooted morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
