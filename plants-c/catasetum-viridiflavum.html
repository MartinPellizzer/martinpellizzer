Catasetum viridiflavum

Catasetum viridiflavum, commonly known as the green-yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and have a unique structure with specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum viridiflavum is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
