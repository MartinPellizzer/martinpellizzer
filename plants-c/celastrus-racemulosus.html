Celastrus racemulosus

Celastrus racemulosus, commonly known as the racemose bindweed, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, and elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its spread.

Botanically, it is part of the Celastraceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
