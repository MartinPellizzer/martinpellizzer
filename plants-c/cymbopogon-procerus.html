Cymbopogon procerus

Cymbopogon procerus, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cymbopogon procerus is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
