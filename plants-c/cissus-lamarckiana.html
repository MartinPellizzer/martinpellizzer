Cissus lamarckiana

Cissus lamarckiana, commonly known as the creeping oxalis, is a tropical climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus lamarckiana thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
