Carex melananthoides

Carex melananthoides, commonly known as black sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Carex melananthoides is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
