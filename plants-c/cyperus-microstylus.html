Cyperus microstylus

Cyperus microstylus, commonly known as the narrow-styled flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually pale green or brown. It is characterized by its narrow, short-styled inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other species in the genus.

Cyperus microstylus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
