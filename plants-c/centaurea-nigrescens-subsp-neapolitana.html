Centaurea nigrescens subsp. neapolitana

Centaurea nigrescens subsp.

neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, spiny stems and purple to blue flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the plant produces small, bristly seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
