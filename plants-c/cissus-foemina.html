Cissus foemina

Cissus foemina, commonly known as the female fig or lady's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus foemina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
