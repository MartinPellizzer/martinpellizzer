Centaurea boissieri subsp. rouyi

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

rouyi, commonly known as the Caucasian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
