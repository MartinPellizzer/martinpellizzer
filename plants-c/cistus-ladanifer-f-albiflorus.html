Cistus ladanifer f. albiflorus

Cistus ladanifer f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rockrose, is a subspecies of the rockrose plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
