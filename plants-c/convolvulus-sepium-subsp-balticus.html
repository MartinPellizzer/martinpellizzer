Convolvulus sepium subsp. balticus

Convolvulus sepium subsp.

balticus, commonly known as Baltic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant produces vibrant purple or pink blooms that typically appear in the early summer. It has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in waste areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other convolvulus species.
