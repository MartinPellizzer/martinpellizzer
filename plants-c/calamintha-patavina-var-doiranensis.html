Calamintha patavina var. doiranensis

Calamintha patavina var. doiranensis, commonly known as the Doiran germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
