Calathea clivorum

Calathea clivorum, commonly known as the cliff calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns and variegation. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
