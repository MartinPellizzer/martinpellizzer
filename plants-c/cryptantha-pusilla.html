Cryptantha pusilla

Cryptantha pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf flaxvetch, is a small annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It has small, linear leaves and produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cryptantha pusilla thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
