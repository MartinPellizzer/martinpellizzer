Cerbera dichotoma

Cerbera dichotoma, commonly known as the poison shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its branched, shrub-like structure and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds such as cerberin. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cerbera dichotoma is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or disturbed soils.
