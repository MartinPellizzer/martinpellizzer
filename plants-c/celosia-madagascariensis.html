Celosia madagascariensis

Celosia madagascariensis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Celosia madagascariensis is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
