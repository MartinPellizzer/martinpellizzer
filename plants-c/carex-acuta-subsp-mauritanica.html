Carex acuta subsp. mauritanica

Carex acuta subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It is often found in coastal dunes and saline environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora in its native habitat.
