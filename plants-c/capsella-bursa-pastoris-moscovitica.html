Capsella bursa-pastoris moscovitica

Capsella bursa-pastoris moscovitica, commonly known as the common weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is widely distributed across temperate regions. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
