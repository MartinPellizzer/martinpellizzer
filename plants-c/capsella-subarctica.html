Capsella subarctica

Capsella subarctica, commonly known as the arctic knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold climates.

Capsella subarctica thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or barren habitats.
