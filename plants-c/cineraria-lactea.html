Cineraria lactea

Cineraria lactea, commonly known as the milky cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy substance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Cineraria lactea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
