Corylus chinensis

Corylus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
