Carex pendula

Carex pendula, commonly known as hanging sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features long, drooping inflorescences that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
