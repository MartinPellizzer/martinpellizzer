Cephalostachyum griffithii

Cephalostachyum griffithii, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an important component of alpine vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
