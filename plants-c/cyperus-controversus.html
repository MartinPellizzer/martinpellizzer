Cyperus controversus

Cyperus controversus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus controversus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
