Caragana dasyphylla

Caragana dasyphylla, commonly known as the hardy peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a feathery pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow or green, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Caragana dasyphylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its role in soil stabilization.
