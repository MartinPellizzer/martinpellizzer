Coriandrum cicuta

Coriandrum cicuta, commonly known as water hemlock, is a highly toxic plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. The plant contains toxic compounds such as cicutoxin, which can cause severe poisoning in humans and animals. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found near streams and wetlands.

Due to its extreme toxicity, it is considered one of the most dangerous plants in North America.
