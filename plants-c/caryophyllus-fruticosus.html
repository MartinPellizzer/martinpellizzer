Caryophyllus fruticosus

Caryophyllus fruticosus, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid climates. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Caryophyllus fruticosus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
