Cyathea xantholepis

Cyathea xantholepis, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

Cyathea xantholepis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forests.
