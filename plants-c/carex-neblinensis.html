Carex neblinensis

Carex neblinensis, commonly known as the Neblin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
