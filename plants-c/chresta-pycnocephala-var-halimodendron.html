Chresta pycnocephala var. halimodendron

Chresta pycnocephala var. halimodendron, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate regions.
