Cyperus chaetophyllus

Cyperus chaetophyllus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Cyperus chaetophyllus thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
