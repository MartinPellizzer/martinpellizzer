Crassula rubescens var. laxa

Crassula rubescens var. laxa, commonly known as the Woolly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially when exposed to bright light. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal.
