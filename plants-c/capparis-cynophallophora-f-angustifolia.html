Capparis cynophallophora f. angustifolia

Capparis cynophallophora f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, acidic berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
