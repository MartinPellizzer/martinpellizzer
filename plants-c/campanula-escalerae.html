Campanula escalerae

Campanula escalerae, commonly known as the Escalera bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Campanula escalerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.
