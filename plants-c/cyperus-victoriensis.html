Cyperus victoriensis

Cyperus victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its cylindrical, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus victoriensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive, upright growth habit and adaptability to various water conditions.
