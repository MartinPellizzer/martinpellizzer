Copiapoa grandiflora subsp. ritteri

Copiapoa grandiflora subsp.

ritteri, commonly known as the Ritter's giant cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, covered in dense, white spines.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in arid conditions with minimal rainfall and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
