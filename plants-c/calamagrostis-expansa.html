Calamagrostis expansa

Calamagrostis expansa, commonly known as the silvergrass or giant reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of long, drooping panicles that produce small, spikelet-like flowers.

Calamagrostis expansa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
