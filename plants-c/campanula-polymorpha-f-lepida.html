Campanula polymorpha f. lepida

Campanula polymorpha f. lepida, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its attractive blooms and ease of care.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
