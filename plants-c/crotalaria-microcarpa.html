Crotalaria microcarpa

Crotalaria microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bean-like pods. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
