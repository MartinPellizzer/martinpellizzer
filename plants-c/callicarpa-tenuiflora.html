Callicarpa tenuiflora

Callicarpa tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces vibrant purple berries in the fall, which are a key feature for both ornamental and ecological purposes. These berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
