Capparis longgangensis

Capparis longgangensis, commonly known as the Longgang Caper, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a sharp, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in dry woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
