Cuphea expansa

Cuphea expansa, commonly known as the waxcup plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or pink bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea expansa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
