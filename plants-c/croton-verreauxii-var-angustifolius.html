Croton verreauxii var. angustifolius

Croton verreauxii var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and hue depending on the season and light conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
