Carex decipiens

Carex decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex decipiens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
