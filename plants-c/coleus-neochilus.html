Coleus neochilus

Coleus neochilus, commonly known as the purple-leafed coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and exhibit a range of hues, including purple, red, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its striking leaf coloration and is used in gardens and as a houseplant.
