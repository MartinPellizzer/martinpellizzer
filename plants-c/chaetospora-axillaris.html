Chaetospora axillaris

Chaetospora axillaris, commonly known as the hairy root orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, root-like structures that aid in attachment to host trees and moisture retention. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
