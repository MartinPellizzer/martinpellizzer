Cordia lima var. subinflata

Cordia lima var. subinflata, commonly known as the swamp cordia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Florida and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
