Cephalanthera erecta var. subaphylla

Cephalanthera erecta var. subaphylla, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
