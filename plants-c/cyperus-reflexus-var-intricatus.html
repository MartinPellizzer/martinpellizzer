Cyperus reflexus var. intricatus

Cyperus reflexus var. intricatus, commonly known as the intricately branched flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its intricate branching pattern and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
