Chamaecrista virginis

Chamaecrista virginis, commonly known as Virginia burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. Its flowers are usually bright yellow and appear in clusters, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Chamaecrista virginis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
