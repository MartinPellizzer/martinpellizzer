Carex × xanthocarpa

Carex × xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow flowering sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and prefers full to partial sun. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex × xanthocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
