Comolia purpurea

Comolia purpurea, commonly known as the purple comolia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its visual appeal.
