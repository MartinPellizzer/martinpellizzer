Calea septuplinervia

Calea septuplinervia, commonly known as the seven-veined calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Calea septuplinervia is also noted for its fragrant scent, which attracts various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
