Chrysopogon argutus

Chrysopogon argutus, commonly known as the sharp grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Chrysopogon argutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
