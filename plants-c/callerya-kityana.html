Callerya kityana

Callerya kityana, commonly known as the Kityana orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, come in shades of white, pink, and lavender, making it a striking ornamental plant.
