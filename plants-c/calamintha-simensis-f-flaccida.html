Calamintha simensis f. flaccida

Calamintha simensis f. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid mountain hyssop, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, fragrant leaves with a mint-like scent. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical traits, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
