Cymbopogon calciphilus

Cymbopogon calciphilus, commonly known as the limestone grass, is a perennial grass species native to regions with limestone soils.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This plant thrives in alkaline conditions and is often found in rocky or calcareous environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in panicles, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
