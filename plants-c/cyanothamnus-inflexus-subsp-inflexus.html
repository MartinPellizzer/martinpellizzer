Cyanothamnus inflexus subsp. inflexus

Cyanothamnus inflexus subsp.

inflexus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebells, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight blue-green hue. This species is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in open woodlands and heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
