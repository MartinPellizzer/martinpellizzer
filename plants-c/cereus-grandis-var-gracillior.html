Cereus grandis var. gracillior

Cereus grandis var. gracillior, commonly known as the slender night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The flowers are typically short-lived but highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
