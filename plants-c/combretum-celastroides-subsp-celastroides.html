Combretum celastroides subsp. celastroides

Combretum celastroides subsp.

celastroides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting birds and insects.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural spread.
