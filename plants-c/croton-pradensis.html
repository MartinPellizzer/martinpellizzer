Croton pradensis

Croton pradensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.
