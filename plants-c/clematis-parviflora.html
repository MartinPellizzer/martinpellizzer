Clematis parviflora

Clematis parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
