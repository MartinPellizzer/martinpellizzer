Cassia sophera var. schinifolia

Cassia sophera var. schinifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
