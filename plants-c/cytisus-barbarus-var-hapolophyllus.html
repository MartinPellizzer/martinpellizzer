Cytisus barbarus var. hapolophyllus

Cytisus barbarus var. hapolophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, thorny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is closely related to other cytisus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
