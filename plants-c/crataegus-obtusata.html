Crataegus obtusata

Crataegus obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth and have a rounded or obtuse tip. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus obtusata is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
