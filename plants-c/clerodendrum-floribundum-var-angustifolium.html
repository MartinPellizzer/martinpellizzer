Clerodendrum floribundum var. angustifolium

Clerodendrum floribundum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. Its growth habit is often bushy, with multiple branches arising from the base.

The leaves are usually aromatic, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
