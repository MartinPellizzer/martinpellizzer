Campanula garganica subsp. lepida

Campanula garganica subsp.

lepida, commonly known as the Gargano bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans and parts of southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural habitat.
