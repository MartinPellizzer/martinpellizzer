Cucumis sativus var. flavus

Cucumis sativus var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow cucumber, is a variant of the cucumber plant characterized by its distinct yellow or pale green fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is cultivated for its edible, typically smaller fruits. The plant produces tendrils and heart-shaped leaves, typical of the cucumber genus. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its unique color and flavor, often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
