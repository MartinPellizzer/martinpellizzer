Centaurea grisebachii subsp. paucijuga

Centaurea grisebachii subsp.

paucijuga, commonly known as the narrowleaf centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flower heads.

The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
