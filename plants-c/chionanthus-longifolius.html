Chionanthus longifolius

Chionanthus longifolius, commonly known as the whiteflower viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
