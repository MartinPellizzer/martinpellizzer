Critonia morifolia

Critonia morifolia, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or gold in color. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
