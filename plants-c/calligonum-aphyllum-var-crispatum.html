Calligonum aphyllum var. crispatum

Calligonum aphyllum var. crispatum, commonly known as the curly sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss in harsh environments.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize dunes.
