Canna × nigricans

Canna × nigricans, commonly known as black canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Canna × nigricans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
