Caladenia thysanochila

Caladenia thysanochila, commonly known as the feather-lip orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia.

This plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and its distinctive, showy flowers that typically feature a single, elongated lip. The orchid's flowers are usually pink or purple, with a prominent, feathery appendage that gives it its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Caladenia thysanochila is a perennial plant that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
