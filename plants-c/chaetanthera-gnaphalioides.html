Chaetanthera gnaphalioides

Chaetanthera gnaphalioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
