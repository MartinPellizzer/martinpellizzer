Cerasus caproniana var. persiciflora

Cerasus caproniana var. persiciflora, commonly known as the Persian cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of white or pink. The leaves are ovate and serrated, turning yellow in autumn. The fruit is a small, edible drupe with a sweet, tart flavor.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
