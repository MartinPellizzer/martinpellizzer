Colocasia montana

Colocasia montana, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking purple or white spadices, which are the actual flower structures surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Colocasia montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
