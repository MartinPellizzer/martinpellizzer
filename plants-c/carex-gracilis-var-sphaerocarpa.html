Carex gracilis var. sphaerocarpa

Carex gracilis var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the spherical sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often borne on short, upright stems. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
