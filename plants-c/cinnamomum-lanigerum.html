Cinnamomum lanigerum

Cinnamomum lanigerum, commonly known as the Indian cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark that is harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.
