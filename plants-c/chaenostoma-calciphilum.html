Chaenostoma calciphilum

Chaenostoma calciphilum, commonly known as the limestone waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to calcareous wetlands and stream margins.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike during the summer months. It thrives in alkaline soils and requires a consistently moist environment.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species for aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
