Condalia celata

Condalia celata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery or grayish underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
