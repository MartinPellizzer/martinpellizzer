Calliandra mollis

Calliandra mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod, and the plant has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Calliandra mollis also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
