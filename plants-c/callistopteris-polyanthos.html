Callistopteris polyanthos

Callistopteris polyanthos, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically golden to bronze in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Callistopteris polyanthos plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small organisms.
