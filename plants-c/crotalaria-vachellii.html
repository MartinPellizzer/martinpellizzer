Crotalaria vachellii

Crotalaria vachellii, commonly known as the Vachell's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and palmate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred petal that attracts pollinators.

Crotalaria vachellii is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
