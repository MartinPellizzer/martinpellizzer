Cydonia vulgaris var. macrocarpa

Cydonia vulgaris var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces large, fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by orange to reddish fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a thick, aromatic skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp when fully mature. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
