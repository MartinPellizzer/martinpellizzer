Callostylis bambusifolia

Callostylis bambusifolia, commonly known as the bambooleaf callostylis, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that resemble bamboo, with a smooth, bluish-green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and horticultural collections.
