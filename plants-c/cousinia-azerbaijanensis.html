Cousinia azerbaijanensis

Cousinia azerbaijanensis, commonly known as the Azerbaijani cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Cousinia azerbaijanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
