Cyclamen rhodium subsp. peloponnesiacum

Cyclamen rhodium subsp.

peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnese cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Greece. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive silver or white mottling.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and containers.
