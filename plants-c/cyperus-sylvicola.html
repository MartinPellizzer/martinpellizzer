Cyperus sylvicola

Cyperus sylvicola, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus sylvicola is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
