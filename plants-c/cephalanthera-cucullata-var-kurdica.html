Cephalanthera cucullata var. kurdica

Cephalanthera cucullata var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant possesses a short, rhizomatous stem and produces a single, showy flower per stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This variety is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
