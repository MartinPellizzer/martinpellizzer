Calamus pilosissimus

Calamus pilosissimus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
