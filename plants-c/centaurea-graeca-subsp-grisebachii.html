Centaurea graeca subsp. grisebachii

Centaurea graeca subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the Greek knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a composite head, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust stems and showy inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
