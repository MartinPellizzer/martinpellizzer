Chamaepeuce obtusa

Chamaepeuce obtusa, commonly known as the bluntleaf chamaepeuce, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a blunt tip, which are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters during the spring.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
