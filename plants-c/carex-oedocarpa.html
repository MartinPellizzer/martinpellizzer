Carex oedocarpa

Carex oedocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex oedocarpa is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
