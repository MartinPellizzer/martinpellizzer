Callicarpa longiloba

Callicarpa longiloba, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, aromatic scent. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
