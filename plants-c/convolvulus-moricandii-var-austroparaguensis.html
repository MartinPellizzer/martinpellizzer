Convolvulus moricandii var. austroparaguensis

Convolvulus moricandii var. austroparaguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the morning. It is native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina, and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cover ground with its rapid growth.
