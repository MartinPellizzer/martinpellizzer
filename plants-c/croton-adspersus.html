Croton adspersus

Croton adspersus, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that often feature a mottled pattern resembling a leopard's spots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton adspersus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
