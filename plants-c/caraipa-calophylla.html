Caraipa calophylla

Caraipa calophylla, commonly known as the Caraipa rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Colombia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, aromatic bark that emits a strong scent. Caraipa calophylla produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries.

It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
