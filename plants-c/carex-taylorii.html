Carex taylorii

Carex taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Carex taylorii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
