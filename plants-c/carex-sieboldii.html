Carex sieboldii

Carex sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex sieboldii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
