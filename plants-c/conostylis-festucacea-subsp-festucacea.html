Conostylis festucacea subsp. festucacea

Conostylis festucacea subsp.

festucacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf conostylis, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale blue to purple flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
