Cabreriella oppositicordia

Cabreriella oppositicordia, commonly known as the Mexican red maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its opposite, cordate (heart-shaped) leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
