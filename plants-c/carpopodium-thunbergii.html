Carpopodium thunbergii

Carpopodium thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is a floating plant with delicate, feathery leaves that resemble the leaves of the water clover. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Carpopodium thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
