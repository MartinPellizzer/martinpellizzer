Capparis cumingii

Capparis cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodland or scrubland.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
