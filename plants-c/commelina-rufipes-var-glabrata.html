Commelina rufipes var. glabrata

Commelina rufipes var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowering stems are usually upright, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the tips.

The variety glabrata is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy foliage compared to other varieties of Commelina rufipes.
