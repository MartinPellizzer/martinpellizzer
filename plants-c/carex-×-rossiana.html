Carex × rossiana

Carex × rossiana, commonly known as the Rosser's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, characterized by its clumping growth habit and dense foliage.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a bluish-green in color, with a distinctive appearance that makes it popular in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments. Carex × rossiana is adaptable to a range of soil types but prefers well-drained, consistently moist conditions.

It is a hardy perennial that is often used in water gardens and as a ground cover in shaded areas.
