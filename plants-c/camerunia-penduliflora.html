Camerunia penduliflora

Camerunia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which bear small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Camerunia penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
