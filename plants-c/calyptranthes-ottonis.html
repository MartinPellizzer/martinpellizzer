Calyptranthes ottonis

Calyptranthes ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often accompanied by small, glandular hairs.

Calyptranthes ottonis is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in maritime climates.
