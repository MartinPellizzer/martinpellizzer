Copernicia nana

Copernicia nana, commonly known as the dwarf copernicia palm, is a small, compact palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, slender trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is a popular choice for container gardening and small spaces.
