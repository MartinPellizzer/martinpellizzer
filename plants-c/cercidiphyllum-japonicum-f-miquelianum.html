Cercidiphyllum japonicum f. miquelianum

Cercidiphyllum japonicum f. miquelianum, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnately compound leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by slender, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
