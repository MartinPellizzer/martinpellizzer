Crassocephalum luteum

Crassocephalum luteum, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it spreads readily through its creeping growth habit.

The plant's bright flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
