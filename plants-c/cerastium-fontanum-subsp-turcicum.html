Cerastium fontanum subsp. turcicum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

turcicum, commonly known as Turkish mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans. It features prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and densely hairy.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Cerastium fontanum subsp.

turcicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
