Cirsium portoricense

Cirsium portoricense, commonly known as the Portuguese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role.

Cirsium portoricense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
