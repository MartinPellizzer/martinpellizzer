Chascanum obovatum

Chascanum obovatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple petals that surround a central, cone-shaped cluster of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
