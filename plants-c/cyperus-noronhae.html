Cyperus noronhae

Cyperus noronhae, commonly known as the Brazilian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus noronhae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
