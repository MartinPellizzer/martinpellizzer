Chrysocoma crassa

Chrysocoma crassa, commonly known as the roughleaf chrysocoma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
