Cassia polytricha

Cassia polytricha, commonly known as the Chinese cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Cassia polytricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
