Campanula rotundifolia subsp. guiraoi

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

guiraoi, commonly known as the Guirao's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its rounded, heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The species is typically found in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
