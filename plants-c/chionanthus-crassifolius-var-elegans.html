Chionanthus crassifolius var. elegans

Chionanthus crassifolius var. elegans, commonly known as the silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Chionanthus crassifolius var. elegans is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
