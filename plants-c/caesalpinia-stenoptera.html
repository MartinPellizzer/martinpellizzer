Caesalpinia stenoptera

Caesalpinia stenoptera, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange floral clusters that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
