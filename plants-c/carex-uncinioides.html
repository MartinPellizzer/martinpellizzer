Carex uncinioides

Carex uncinioides, commonly known as the hooked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
