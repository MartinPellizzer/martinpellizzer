Cecropia albicans

Cecropia albicans, commonly known as the white cecropia, is a tropical tree belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, lowland forests. Its rapid growth rate and ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants make it ecologically significant.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
