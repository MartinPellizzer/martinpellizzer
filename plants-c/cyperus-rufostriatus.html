Cyperus rufostriatus

Cyperus rufostriatus, commonly known as the red-striped flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus rufostriatus thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of water quality.
