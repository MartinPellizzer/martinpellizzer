Carex schiedeana var. perstricta

Carex schiedeana var. perstricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish brown, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
