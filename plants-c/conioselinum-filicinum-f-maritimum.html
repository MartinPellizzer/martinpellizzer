Conioselinum filicinum f. maritimum

Conioselinum filicinum f. maritimum, commonly known as the sea parsley.

This plant is a coastal variant of the species, adapted to grow in sandy or rocky coastal environments. It features slender, aromatic leaves with a fern-like appearance, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is often found in maritime regions where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of coastal flora.
