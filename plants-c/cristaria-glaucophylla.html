Cristaria glaucophylla

Cristaria glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed crista, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
