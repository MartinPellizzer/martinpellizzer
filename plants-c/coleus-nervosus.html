Coleus nervosus

Coleus nervosus, commonly known as the veined coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display intricate patterns of red, purple, and green. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with prominent veins that give the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
