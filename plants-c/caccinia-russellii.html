Caccinia russellii

Caccinia russellii, commonly known as Russell's caccinia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that reflects sunlight.

Caccinia russellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
