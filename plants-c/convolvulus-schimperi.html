Convolvulus schimperi

Convolvulus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

The flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
