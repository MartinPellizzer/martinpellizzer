Citrus × aurantium var. crispa

Citrus × aurantium var. crispa, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, thick-skinned fruit that turns orange when mature. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed whole, including the peel. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is also notable, with fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.
