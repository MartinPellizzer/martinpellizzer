Callipteris chocoensis

Callipteris chocoensis, commonly known as the Choco palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near rivers or in dense forest understories. Its leaves are long and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.

The Choco palm is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
