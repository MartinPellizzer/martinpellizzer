Coronilla gibraltarica

Coronilla gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar Coronilla, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and grayish in appearance.

Coronilla gibraltarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
