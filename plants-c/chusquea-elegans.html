Chusquea elegans

Chusquea elegans, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a clump-forming plant that produces slender, hollow stems with a smooth, often bluish-green surface. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its graceful appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscapes.
