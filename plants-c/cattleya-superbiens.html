Cattleya superbiens

Cattleya superbiens, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a vibrant pink or red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya superbiens is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
