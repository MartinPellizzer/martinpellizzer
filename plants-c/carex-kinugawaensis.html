Carex kinugawaensis

Carex kinugawaensis, commonly known as the Kinugawa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex kinugawaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
