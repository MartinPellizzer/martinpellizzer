Calamintha marifolia

Calamintha marifolia, commonly known as mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other mint species, and it is known for its strong mint-like fragrance. Calamintha marifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

It is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens.
