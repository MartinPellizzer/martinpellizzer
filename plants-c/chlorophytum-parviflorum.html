Chlorophytum parviflorum

Chlorophytum parviflorum, commonly known as the spider plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features long, arching leaves that are typically variegated with white and green stripes, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions and its ease of care, making it a popular houseplant. It also has the ability to propagate through offsets, which are small plantlets that grow from the base of the mother plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
