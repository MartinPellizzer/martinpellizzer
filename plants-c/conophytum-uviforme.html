Conophytum uviforme

Conophytum uviforme, commonly known as the "moonstone plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are small and yellowish, often clustered in a raceme.
