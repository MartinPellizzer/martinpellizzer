Crepis albanica

Crepis albanica, commonly known as the Albanian cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in Albania, Kosovo, and parts of Greece. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, and its leaves are usually alternate and lobed.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience.
