Capparidastrum coimbranum

Capparidastrum coimbranum, commonly known as the Coimbra's caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in humid, lowland areas.

The plant has limited commercial use but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and distribution.
