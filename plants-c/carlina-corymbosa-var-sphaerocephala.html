Carlina corymbosa var. sphaerocephala

Carlina corymbosa var. sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that form a dense basal cluster. The plant produces a spherical, spiky flower head that is typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
