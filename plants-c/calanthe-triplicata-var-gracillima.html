Calanthe triplicata var. gracillima

Calanthe triplicata var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in humid climates.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
