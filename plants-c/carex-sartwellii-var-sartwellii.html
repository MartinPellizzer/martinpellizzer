Carex sartwellii var. sartwellii

Carex sartwellii var. sartwellii, commonly known as Sartwell’s sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sartwell’s sedge is often found in shaded environments and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
