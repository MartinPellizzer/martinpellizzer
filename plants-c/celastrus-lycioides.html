Celastrus lycioides

Celastrus lycioides, commonly known as the false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Celastrus lycioides belongs to the family Celastraceae and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
