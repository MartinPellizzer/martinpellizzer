Cestrum lasianthum

Cestrum lasianthum, commonly known as the white jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
