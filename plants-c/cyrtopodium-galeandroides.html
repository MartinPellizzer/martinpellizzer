Cyrtopodium galeandroides

Cyrtopodium galeandroides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Cyrtopodium galeandroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
