Crataegus berberifolia var. edita

Crataegus berberifolia var. edita, commonly known as the yellowleaf hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage, which sets it apart from other hawthorn species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
