Coleus buchananii

Coleus buchananii, commonly known as the red coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and orange. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on growing conditions.

Coleus buchananii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking visual appeal in gardens and indoor settings.
