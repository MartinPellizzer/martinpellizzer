Campylotropis macrocarpa subsp. hengduanshanensis

Campylotropis macrocarpa subsp.

hengduanshanensis, commonly known as the Hengduan Mountains campylotropis, is a shrub native to the Hengduan Mountains region of China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes during the spring season. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous forest habitats.
