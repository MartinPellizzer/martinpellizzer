Clerodendrum goossensii

Clerodendrum goossensii, commonly known as the African balm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
