Cunninghamia selaginoides

Cunninghamia selaginoides, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
