Crepis moesiaca

Crepis moesiaca, commonly known as the Moesia thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish flower heads that develop into bristly seed heads.

Crepis moesiaca is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
