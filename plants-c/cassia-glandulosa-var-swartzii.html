Cassia glandulosa var. swartzii

Cassia glandulosa var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's cassia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with glandular structures, which are small, raised, and often yellowish in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its small, oblong pods contain numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
