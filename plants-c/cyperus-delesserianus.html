Cyperus delesserianus

Cyperus delesserianus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded environments, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often subtended by leaf-like bracts. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on wind pollination.

Cyperus delesserianus plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
