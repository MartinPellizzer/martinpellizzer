Callicarpa vestita

Callicarpa vestita, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
