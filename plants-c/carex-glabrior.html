Carex glabrior

Carex glabrior, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex glabrior plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
