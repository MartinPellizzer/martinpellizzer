Cupressus amoena

Cupressus amoena, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
