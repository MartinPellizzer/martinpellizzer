Chusquea fimbriligulata subsp. peruviana

Chusquea fimbriligulata subsp.

peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian weeping bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that form dense clumps, often giving it a weeping appearance.

The plant produces feathery, branched inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Native to the Andean regions of South America, it thrives in humid, temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
