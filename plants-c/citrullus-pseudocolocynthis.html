Citrullus pseudocolocynthis

Citrullus pseudocolocynthis, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It produces distinctive, spiny, horn-like fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and bears small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines and has a slightly bitter, cucumber-like flavor.
