Cymbopogon thwaitesii

Cymbopogon thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' citron grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Cymbopogon thwaitesii is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for its essential oils.
