Calendula marginata

Calendula marginata, commonly known as the scarlet globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are typically borne on tall, spiny stems. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. Calendula marginata thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and wildflower habitats.
