Cerasus juliana var. meridionalis

Cerasus juliana var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern Japanese cherry, is a variant of the Prunus juliana species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, producing clusters of small, pinkish-white blossoms in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese garden design.
