Camaridium longum

Camaridium longum, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and showy, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom sequentially throughout the growing season.

Camaridium longum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
