Camellia chunii var. pentaphylax

Camellia chunii var. pentaphylax, commonly known as the five-leaf camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically five-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during late winter to early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
