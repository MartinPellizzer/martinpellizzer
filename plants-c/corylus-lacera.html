Corylus lacera

Corylus lacera, commonly known as the filbert or hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are harvested in the fall. Corylus lacera thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in both horticulture and wildlife habitats.
