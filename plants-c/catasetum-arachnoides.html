Catasetum arachnoides

Catasetum arachnoides, commonly known as the silky catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate, spurred petals and a unique lip structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
