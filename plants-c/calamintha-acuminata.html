Calamintha acuminata

Calamintha acuminata, commonly known as narrow-leaved calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, often with a strong mint-like fragrance.

Calamintha acuminata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
