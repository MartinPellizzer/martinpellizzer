Cereus variabilis var. salm-dyckianus

Cereus variabilis var. salm-dyckianus, commonly known as the Salm-Dyck's night bloomer, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collectors.
