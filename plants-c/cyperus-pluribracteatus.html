Cyperus pluribracteatus

Cyperus pluribracteatus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus pluribracteatus is known for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it a common plant in coastal areas.
