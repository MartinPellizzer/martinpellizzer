Cytisus philippinensis

Cytisus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine broom, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
