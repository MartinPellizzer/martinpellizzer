Camellia pentaphylacoides

Camellia pentaphylacoides, commonly known as the five-leaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically five-lobed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

Camellia pentaphylacoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
