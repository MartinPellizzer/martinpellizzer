Chasmanthera palmata

Chasmanthera palmata, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Chasmanthera palmata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
