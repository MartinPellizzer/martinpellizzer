Corymbia × pedimontana

Corymbia × pedimontana, commonly known as the red blood gum, is a hybrid species native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, fissured bark that ranges from grey to red-brown in color. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Corymbia × pedimontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
