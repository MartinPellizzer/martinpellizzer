Cyphia erecta var. ufipana

Cyphia erecta var. ufipana, commonly known as the red-flowered cyphia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows in tropical or subtropical environments, often in shaded, moist areas. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
