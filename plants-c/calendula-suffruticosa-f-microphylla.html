Calendula suffruticosa f. microphylla

Calendula suffruticosa f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf calendula, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has woody stems and leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for perennial gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
