Cryptotaenia japonica f. pinnatisecta

Cryptotaenia japonica f. pinnatisecta, commonly known as Japanese cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features finely divided, pinnatisected leaves that give it a feathery appearance, often resembling ferns. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its delicate foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
