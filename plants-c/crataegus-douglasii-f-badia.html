Crataegus douglasii f. badia

Crataegus douglasii f. badia, commonly known as the black thorn or black hawthorn, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, sharp thorns. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands or rocky areas.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
