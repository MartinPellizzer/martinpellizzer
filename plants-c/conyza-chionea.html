Conyza chionea

Conyza chionea, commonly known as "white dandelion," is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features small, white composite flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, often with a serrated edge.

Conyza chionea is known for its wind-dispersed seeds, which are encased in a fluffy pappus, aiding in its spread.
