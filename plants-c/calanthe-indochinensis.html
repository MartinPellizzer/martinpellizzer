Calanthe indochinensis

Calanthe indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in shaded, moist forest environments and requires well-drained soil.

Calanthe indochinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
