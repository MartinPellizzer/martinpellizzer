Carex flava var. argillacea

Carex flava var. argillacea, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other sedge species. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
