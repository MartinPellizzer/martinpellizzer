Cuphea lobophora var. elongata

Cuphea lobophora var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed wax plant, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, red or orange flowers that resemble wax candles, which are borne in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a striking addition to any garden setting.
