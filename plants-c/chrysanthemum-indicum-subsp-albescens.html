Chrysanthemum indicum subsp. albescens

Chrysanthemum indicum subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a cluster called a corymb.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and fragrance.
