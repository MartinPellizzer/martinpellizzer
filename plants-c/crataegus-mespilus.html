Crataegus mespilus

Crataegus mespilus, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and distinctive red or yellow fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruits, known as medlars, have a complex, aromatic flavor and were historically used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
