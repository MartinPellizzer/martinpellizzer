Cestrum impressum

Cestrum impressum, commonly known as the rough cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. Its flowers are fragrant, tubular, and usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

The plant is known for its nocturnal fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
