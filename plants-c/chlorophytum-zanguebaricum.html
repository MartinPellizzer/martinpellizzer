Chlorophytum zanguebaricum

Chlorophytum zanguebaricum, commonly known as the Tiger's Tail or Tiger's Head, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow stripes, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, bell-shaped white or greenish flowers. It is a succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant in many regions.
