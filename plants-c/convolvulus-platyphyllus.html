Convolvulus platyphyllus

Convolvulus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant is typically found in open areas, such as grasslands and rocky soils, and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
