Cornus canadensis f. semivirescens

Cornus canadensis f. semivirescens, commonly known as the red-twigged dogwood, is a variant of the Canadian dogwood.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant features slender stems that turn a striking red or reddish-brown in the fall, adding ornamental value. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red berries.

The foliage is usually a deep green, providing a contrast to the colorful stems.
