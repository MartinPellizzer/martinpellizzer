Carex riparia var. claviformis

Carex riparia var. claviformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
