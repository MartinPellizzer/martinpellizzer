Clitoria leptostachya var. fruticosa

Clitoria leptostachya var. fruticosa, commonly known as the purple orchid bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, bright purple flowers that are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy and twining. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds are typically enclosed in hairy pods and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
