Crotalaria genistifolia

Crotalaria genistifolia, commonly known as the flame bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Crotalaria genistifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
