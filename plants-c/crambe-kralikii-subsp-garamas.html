Crambe kralikii subsp. garamas

Crambe kralikii subsp.

garamas, commonly known as the Garamas crambe, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, pod-like structure, and the plant is known for its high oil content, making it a potential candidate for biodiesel production.
