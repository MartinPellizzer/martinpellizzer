Carex nipponica

Carex nipponica, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
