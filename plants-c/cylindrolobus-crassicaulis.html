Cylindrolobus crassicaulis

Cylindrolobus crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

Cylindrolobus crassicaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
