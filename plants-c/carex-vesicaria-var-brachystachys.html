Carex vesicaria var. brachystachys

Carex vesicaria var. brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green to brown in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its ability to tolerate wet conditions makes it a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
