Calceolaria latifolia var. longipetiolata

Calceolaria latifolia var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the broadleaf slipper orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, broad leaves and striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its unique morphology and adaptability to shaded garden settings.
