Calamagrostis stolizkae

Calamagrostis stolizkae, commonly known as the Stolizka's reed, is a grass species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Calamagrostis stolizkae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife in its native habitat.
