Citrus × vulgaris var. fuctu-maxima

Citrus × vulgaris var. fuctu-maxima, commonly known as the Mexican lime, is a hybrid citrus species originating from the intercrossing of the lime (Citrus aurantifolia) and the lemon (Citrus limon).

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruit with a thick, yellowish rind and a high acidity level. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and is valued for its juice, which is widely used in culinary and beverage applications.

The fruit is also rich in vitamin C and has various medicinal uses.
