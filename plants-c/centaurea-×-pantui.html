Centaurea × pantui

Centaurea × pantui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × pantui is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
