Cineraria leucanthema

Cineraria leucanthema, commonly known as the white-flowered cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-blue foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its showy white or pale yellow flowers bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
