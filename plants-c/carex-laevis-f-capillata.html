Carex laevis f. capillata

Carex laevis f. capillata, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with smooth, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
