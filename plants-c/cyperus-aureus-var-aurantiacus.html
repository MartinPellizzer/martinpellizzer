Cyperus aureus var. aurantiacus

Cyperus aureus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as golden flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that are often bright yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, making it common in marshy areas and along waterways. Its flowers are typically wind-pollinated, and the plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant color.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including periodic flooding.
