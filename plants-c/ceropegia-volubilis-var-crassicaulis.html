Ceropegia volubilis var. crassicaulis

Ceropegia volubilis var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lion's mouth, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often pink or red in color. Its leaves are typically oval and succulent, with a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
