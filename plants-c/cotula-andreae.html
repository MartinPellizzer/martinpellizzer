Cotula andreae

Cotula andreae, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Cotula andreae has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small florets in a composite flower head.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
