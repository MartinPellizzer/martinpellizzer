Cynorkis bobyi

Cynorkis bobyi, commonly known as the Bobyi's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. It blooms during the wet season, relying on seasonal rainfall for its growth and reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
