Canavalia bicarinata

Canavalia bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ribbed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with two prominent leaflets and a distinctive, hairy stem. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible beans and has nitrogen-fixing properties that benefit soil fertility.

Canavalia bicarinata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
