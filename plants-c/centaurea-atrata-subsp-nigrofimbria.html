Centaurea atrata subsp. nigrofimbria

Centaurea atrata subsp.

nigrofimbria, commonly known as the black-fringed centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its dark purple to black flowers are surrounded by narrow, fringed bracts, giving it its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
