Cyclamen europaeum subsp. ponticum

Cyclamen europaeum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Turkey. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking, nodding flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in harsh conditions, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. Cyclamen europaeum subsp.

ponticum is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the winter and early spring.
