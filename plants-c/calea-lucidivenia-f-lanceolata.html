Calea lucidivenia f. lanceolata

Calea lucidivenia f. lanceolata, commonly known as the silverleaf calea, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and elegant flowers make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
