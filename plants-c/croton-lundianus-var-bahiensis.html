Croton lundianus var. bahiensis

Croton lundianus var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically alternate and simple, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in warm climates.
