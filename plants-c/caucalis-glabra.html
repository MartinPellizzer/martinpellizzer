Caucalis glabra

Caucalis glabra, commonly known as the smooth bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant is known for its ability to capture small aquatic organisms using specialized bladder-like structures called bladders. These bladders function as traps to immobilize prey, which the plant then digests for nutrients.

Caucalis glabra typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches.
