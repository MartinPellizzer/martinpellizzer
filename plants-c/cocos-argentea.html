Cocos argentea

Cocos argentea, commonly known as the silver coconut or silver palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-lobed fruits, each containing a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
