Colocasia humilis

Colocasia humilis, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like philodendrons and calla lilies. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden beneath the leaves.

Colocasia humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
