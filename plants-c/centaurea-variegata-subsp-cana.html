Centaurea variegata subsp. cana

Centaurea variegata subsp.

cana, commonly known as the variable centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, which often display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or red.

The plant produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowers.
