Cardamomum grana-paradisi

Cardamomum grana-paradisi, commonly known as the paradise granadilla, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant leaves and striking red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its rhizomes contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is cultivated both for ornamental purposes and for its potential medicinal benefits.
