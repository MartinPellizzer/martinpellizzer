Connarus juglandifolius

Connarus juglandifolius, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are alternate and often resemble those of hickory trees. The plant produces small, green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

The tree plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
