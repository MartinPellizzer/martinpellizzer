Cordia fasciculata

Cordia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
