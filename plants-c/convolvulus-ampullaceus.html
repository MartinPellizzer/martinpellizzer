Convolvulus ampullaceus

Convolvulus ampullaceus, commonly known as the bladder vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy, sprawling stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
