Campanula versicolor f. mrkvickana

Campanula versicolor f. mrkvickana, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a horticultural form of the species Campanula versicolor.

It is characterized by its variable flower colors, ranging from blue to violet and white, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and easy care requirements.

This form is prized for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
