Cereus pecten-aboriginum

Cereus pecten-aboriginum, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit, producing large, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive crown-like shape and indigenous origins.
