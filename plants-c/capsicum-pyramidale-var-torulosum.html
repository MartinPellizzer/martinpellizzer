Capsicum pyramidale var. torulosum

Capsicum pyramidale var. torulosum, commonly known as the golden bell pepper.

This variety is characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped fruits that range in color from yellow to orange and red. It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves.

It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruit development.
