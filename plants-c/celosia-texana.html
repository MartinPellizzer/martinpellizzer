Celosia texana

Celosia texana, commonly known as the Texas celosia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Celosia texana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
