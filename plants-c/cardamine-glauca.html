Cardamine glauca

Cardamine glauca, commonly known as blue water crowfoot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wooded areas. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply divided into slender, thread-like segments. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a dense, round head.

Cardamine glauca is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
