Clematis dasyoneura

Clematis dasyoneura, commonly known as the rough-stemmed clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in rough, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
