Costus laxus

Costus laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf costus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typical of the genus Costus.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
