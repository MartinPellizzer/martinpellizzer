Capparis acutifolia subsp. sabiifolia

Capparis acutifolia subsp.

sabiifolia, commonly known as the Persian candytuft, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters along the stems.

The plant has woody stems with thorny branches, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system to access water.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
