Citrullus lanatus subsp. mucosospermus

Citrullus lanatus subsp.

mucosospermus, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits with a high water content and a sweet, red or yellow flesh.

The plant typically grows in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The subspecies is notable for its edible, juicy seeds and is cultivated for both its fruit and seed production.
