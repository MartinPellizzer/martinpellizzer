Caprifolium hispidulum var. californicum

Caprifolium hispidulum var. californicum, commonly known as the California thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible berries that turn red when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive thimble-like shape, giving it its common name.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
