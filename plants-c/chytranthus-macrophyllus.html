Chytranthus macrophyllus

Chytranthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf chytranthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are large, ovate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
