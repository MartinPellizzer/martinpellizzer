Cuphea brachyantha

Cuphea brachyantha, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, cone-like flower clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cuphea brachyantha is also known for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
