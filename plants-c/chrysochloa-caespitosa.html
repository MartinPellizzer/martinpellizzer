Chrysochloa caespitosa

Chrysochloa caespitosa, commonly known as the golden speargrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, bright green leaves that are often tinged with yellow at the base. The plant produces small, pale yellow to golden flowers in summer, which are arranged in loose, open panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
