Chionanthus gigantifolius

Chionanthus gigantifolius, commonly known as the giant-leaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, ovate to elliptical leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant white to cream-colored flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with smooth, gray bark.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
