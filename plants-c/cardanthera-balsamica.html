Cardanthera balsamica

Cardanthera balsamica, commonly known as the balsam poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
