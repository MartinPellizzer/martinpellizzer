Coreosma odorata

Coreosma odorata, commonly known as the sweet pepper vine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Coreosma odorata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy blooms.
