Carduus americanus var. perplexans

Carduus americanus var. perplexans, commonly known as the American thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including disturbed or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

It is often considered an invasive species in agricultural areas due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
