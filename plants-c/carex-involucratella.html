Carex involucratella

Carex involucratella, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex involucratella plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
