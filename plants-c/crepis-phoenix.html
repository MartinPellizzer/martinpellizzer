Crepis phoenix

Crepis phoenix, commonly known as the golden hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that resemble a cluster of small sunflowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Crepis phoenix is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
