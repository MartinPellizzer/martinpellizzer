Capparis mariana

Capparis mariana, commonly known as the Hawaiian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Capparis mariana thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii.
