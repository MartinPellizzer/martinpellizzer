Calamagrostis epigejos f. laeviculmis

Calamagrostis epigejos f. laeviculmis, commonly known as the velvet false panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit.

It features narrow, flat leaves with a distinctive silver-green coloration on the underside, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of pale green to golden flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows or along stream banks.

This ornamental grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
