Castanopsis purpurea

Castanopsis purpurea, commonly known as the purple conebush, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting in suitable climates.
