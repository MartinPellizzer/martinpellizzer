Cucumis melo var. tauricus

Cucumis melo var. tauricus, commonly known as the Caspian cucumber, is a botanical variety of melon native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, crisp flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
