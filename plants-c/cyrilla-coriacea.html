Cyrilla coriacea

Cyrilla coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf cyrilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain several seeds. Cyrilla coriacea is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
