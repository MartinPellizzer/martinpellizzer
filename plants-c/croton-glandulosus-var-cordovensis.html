Croton glandulosus var. cordovensis

Croton glandulosus var. cordovensis, commonly known as the Cordovensian croton, is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
