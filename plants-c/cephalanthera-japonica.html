Cephalanthera japonica

Cephalanthera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese twayblade, is a perennial orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces a single, helmet-shaped flower spike with two small, hood-like petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions.

Cephalanthera japonica is a mycoheterotrophic plant, relying on fungal associations for most of its nutrients.
