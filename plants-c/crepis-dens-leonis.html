Crepis dens-leonis

Crepis dens-leonis, commonly known as the dense-leaved sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Crepis dens-leonis is an annual or biennial herb that can be invasive in certain regions.
