Cochliospermum cavanillesii

Cochliospermum cavanillesii, commonly known as the red leadtree, is a small deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that split open to release its seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces showy yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
