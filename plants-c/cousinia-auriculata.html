Cousinia auriculata

Cousinia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Cousinia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
