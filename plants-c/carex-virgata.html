Carex virgata

Carex virgata, commonly known as slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
