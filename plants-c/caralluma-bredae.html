Caralluma bredae

Caralluma bredae, commonly known as the Desert Caper, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss through transpiration.

Caralluma bredae is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
