Centaurea affinis var. peloponesiaca

Centaurea affinis var. peloponesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads that are typical of the Centaurea genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
