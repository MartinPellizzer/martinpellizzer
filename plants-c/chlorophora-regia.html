Chlorophora regia

Chlorophora regia, commonly known as the royal chlorophora, is a tropical tree native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
