Chamaecrista glandulosa var. andreana

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. andreana, commonly known as the Andean burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant or used in ecological restoration projects.
