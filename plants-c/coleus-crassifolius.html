Coleus crassifolius

Coleus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, ovate leaves that come in a variety of colors, including red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Coleus crassifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
