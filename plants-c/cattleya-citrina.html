Cattleya citrina

Cattleya citrina, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are known for their fragrant, sweet scent.

Cattleya citrina is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and ornamental value.
