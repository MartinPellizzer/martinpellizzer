Cyrtandropsis sphaerocalyx

Cyrtandropsis sphaerocalyx, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
