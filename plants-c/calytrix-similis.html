Calytrix similis

Calytrix similis, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Calytrix similis is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
