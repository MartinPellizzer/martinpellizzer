Cucumis melo var. chate

Cucumis melo var. chate, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variety of the melon family.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and pale green fruit with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows on a trailing vine with hairy stems and palmate leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often harvested when still immature for its crisp texture and refreshing taste.
