Carex pensylvanica var. glumabunda

Carex pensylvanica var. glumabunda, commonly known as the Pennsylvania sedge, is a perennial sedge native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
