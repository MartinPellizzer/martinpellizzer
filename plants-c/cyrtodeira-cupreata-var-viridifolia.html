Cyrtodeira cupreata var. viridifolia

Cyrtodeira cupreata var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed cycad, is a variant of the cycad species Cyrtodeira cupreata.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often bright green leaves that contrast with the more typical bronze or copper-colored foliage of the parent species. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
