Circaea caulescens var. robusta

Circaea caulescens var. robusta, commonly known as the robust trailing cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features a prostrate or decumbent growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
