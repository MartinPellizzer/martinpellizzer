Carex paradoxa var. gracilis

Carex paradoxa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow, marshy areas and is adapted to saturated soil conditions. It is often found in association with other wetland plants such as sedges and rushes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
