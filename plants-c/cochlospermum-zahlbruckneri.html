Cochlospermum zahlbruckneri

Cochlospermum zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces round, fleshy fruits with a sweet, edible pulp containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
