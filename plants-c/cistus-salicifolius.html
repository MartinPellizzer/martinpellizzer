Cistus salicifolius

Cistus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Cistus salicifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
