Cuviera semseii

Cuviera semseii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red-orange ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name honors the Spanish botanist José Cuviéra, who contributed significantly to the study of Mexican flora.
