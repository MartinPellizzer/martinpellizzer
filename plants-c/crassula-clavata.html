Crassula clavata

Crassula clavata, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Crassula clavata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
