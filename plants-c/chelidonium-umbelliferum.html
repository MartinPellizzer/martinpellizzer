Chelidonium umbelliferum

Chelidonium umbelliferum, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, rhizomatous root system and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are arranged in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Chelidonium umbelliferum is known for its toxic sap, which contains alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
