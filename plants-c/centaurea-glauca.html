Centaurea glauca

Centaurea glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and produces small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea glauca is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wastelands.
