Cistus × banaresii f. symphytoides

Cistus × banaresii f. symphytoides, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The hybrid form exhibits traits from its parent species, Cistus banaresii and Cistus symphytoides, resulting in a unique botanical profile.
