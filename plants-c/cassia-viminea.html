Cassia viminea

Cassia viminea, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia viminea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
