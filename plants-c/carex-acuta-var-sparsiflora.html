Carex acuta var. sparsiflora

Carex acuta var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
