Chlorocyperus globosus

Chlorocyperus globosus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow-green in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
