Cissus atacorensis

Cissus atacorensis, commonly known as the Brazilian devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to thrive in semi-arid conditions.

Cissus atacorensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing environments.
