Carex pensylvanica var. amblyolepis

Carex pensylvanica var. amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
