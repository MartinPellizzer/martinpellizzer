Catasetum × sodiroi

Catasetum × sodiroi, commonly known as the orchid with the unique pollination mechanism, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that often display a mix of colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a distinctive, spurred labellum that plays a key role in its specialized pollination process.

Catasetum × sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique biological traits.
