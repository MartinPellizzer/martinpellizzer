Cupressus thurifera

Cupressus thurifera, commonly known as the Canary Island cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is adapted to the arid and coastal climates of its native habitat. Cupressus thurifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native environment.
