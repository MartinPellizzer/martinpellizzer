Carex scoparia var. fulva

Carex scoparia var. fulva, commonly known as yellow sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered brown flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its bright yellowish-green foliage, which gives it its common name.
