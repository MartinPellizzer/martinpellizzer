Calycogonium acunanum

Calycogonium acunanum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
