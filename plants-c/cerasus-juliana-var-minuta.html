Cerasus juliana var. minuta

Cerasus juliana var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
