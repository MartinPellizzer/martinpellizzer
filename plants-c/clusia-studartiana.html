Clusia studartiana

Clusia studartiana, commonly known as the Brazilian shield tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Clusiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Clusia studartiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
