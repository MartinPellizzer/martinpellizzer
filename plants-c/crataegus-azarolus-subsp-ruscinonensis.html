Crataegus azarolus subsp. ruscinonensis

Crataegus azarolus subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Caucasian hawthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange fruits in the autumn.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces thorned stems, typical of many hawthorn species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
