Cephalaria linearifolia var. serrata

Cephalaria linearifolia var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated linearleaf cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are linear with serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
