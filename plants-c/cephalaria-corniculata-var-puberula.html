Cephalaria corniculata var. puberula

Cephalaria corniculata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
