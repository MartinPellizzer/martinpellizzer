Ceanothus verrucosus var. crassifolius

Ceanothus verrucosus var. crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
