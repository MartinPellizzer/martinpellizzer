Catalpa punctata var. pubescens

Catalpa punctata var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical pod that splits open to release its seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
