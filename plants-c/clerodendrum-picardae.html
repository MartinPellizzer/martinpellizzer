Clerodendrum picardae

Clerodendrum picardae, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals.

Clerodendrum picardae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
