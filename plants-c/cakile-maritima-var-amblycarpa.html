Cakile maritima var. amblycarpa

Cakile maritima var. amblycarpa, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a variant of the bladderwrack plant that thrives in coastal environments.

It is a halophyte, meaning it is adapted to grow in salty, marine conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with fleshy, lobed leaves that help it retain water in harsh coastal climates. It often forms dense mats on rocky shores, contributing to coastal stabilization.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by wind and water, ensuring its survival in dynamic marine ecosystems.
