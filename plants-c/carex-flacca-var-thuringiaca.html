Carex flacca var. thuringiaca

Carex flacca var. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges.

This variety is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
