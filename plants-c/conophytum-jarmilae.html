Conophytum jarmilae

Conophytum jarmilae, commonly known as the "Jarmilae's rockrose," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and conservationists.
