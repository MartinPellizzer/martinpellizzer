Chuquiraga acanthophylla

Chuquiraga acanthophylla, commonly known as the Chilean thornbush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chuquiraga acanthophylla plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
