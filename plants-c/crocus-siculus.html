Crocus siculus

Crocus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms with bright yellow petals and a distinctive, three-lobed tepal structure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in perfumery and medicine.

The plant is also notable for its ability to bloom before many other spring flowers, making it a symbol of early spring in its native habitat.
