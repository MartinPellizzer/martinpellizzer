Carex scouleri

Carex scouleri, commonly known as the redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, three-winged seeds.

Carex scouleri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
