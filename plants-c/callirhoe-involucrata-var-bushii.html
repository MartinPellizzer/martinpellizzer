Callirhoe involucrata var. bushii

Callirhoe involucrata var. bushii, commonly known as the Bush's blanketflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
