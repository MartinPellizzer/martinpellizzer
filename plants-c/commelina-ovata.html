Commelina ovata

Commelina ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers with a white throat. The plant is characterized by its erect stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
