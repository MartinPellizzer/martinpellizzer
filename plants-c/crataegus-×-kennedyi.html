Crataegus × kennedyi

Crataegus × kennedyi, commonly known as the Kennedy hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–10 meters, featuring thorned branches and oval to elliptical leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by small, red berries that ripen in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
