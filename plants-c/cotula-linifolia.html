Cotula linifolia

Cotula linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, silver-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Cotula linifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
