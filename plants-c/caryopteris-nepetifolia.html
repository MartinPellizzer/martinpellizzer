Caryopteris nepetifolia

Caryopteris nepetifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-blue down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Caryopteris nepetifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
