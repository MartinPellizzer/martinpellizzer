Carex hudsonii lusus leucorhyncha

Carex hudsonii lusus leucorhyncha, commonly known as the white-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are white and fibrous, contributing to its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
