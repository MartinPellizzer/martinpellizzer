Clematis occidentalis var. albiflora

Clematis occidentalis var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower western clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with a distinctive yellow center. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that ripen to red, containing seeds dispersed by birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis occidentalis var. albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
