Campanula exul

Campanula exul, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula exul is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
