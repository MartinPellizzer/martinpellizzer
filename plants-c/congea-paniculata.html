Congea paniculata

Congea paniculata, commonly known as the Indian balsam or wild balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste places.

Congea paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
