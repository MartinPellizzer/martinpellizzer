Cotula quinqueloba

Cotula quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Cotula quinqueloba is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
