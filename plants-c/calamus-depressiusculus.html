Calamus depressiusculus

Calamus depressiusculus, commonly known as the flat-stemmed sweetgrass, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional uses.

Calamus depressiusculus thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and forest understories.
