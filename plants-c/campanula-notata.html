Campanula notata

Campanula notata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula notata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
