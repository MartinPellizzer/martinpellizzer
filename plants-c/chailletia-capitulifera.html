Chailletia capitulifera

Chailletia capitulifera, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small cups. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
