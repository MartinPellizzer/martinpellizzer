Carex graciliformis

Carex graciliformis, commonly known as slender form carex, is a species of sedge native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to partially shaded environments.

Carex graciliformis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
