Chamaecyparis pisifera f. filifera-aureovariegata

Chamaecyparis pisifera f. filifera-aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated Japanese cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that exhibit a striking golden-yellow variegation. The plant is evergreen, with a conical shape and a slow growth rate, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

The foliage provides year-round color, adding ornamental value to landscapes.
