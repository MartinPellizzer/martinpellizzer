Cupressus thyoides var. variegata

Cupressus thyoides var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated incense cedar, is a cultivar of the incense cedar tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and yellow markings on its needle-like leaves. The plant has a compact, dense growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
