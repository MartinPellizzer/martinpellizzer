Cyperus pallidus

Cyperus pallidus, commonly known as the pale flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or yellow.

Cyperus pallidus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
