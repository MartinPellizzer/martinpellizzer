Calophanes radicans

Calophanes radicans, commonly known as the creeping calophane, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Calophanes radicans is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to cascade from hanging baskets.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
