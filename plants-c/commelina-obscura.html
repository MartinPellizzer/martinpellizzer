Commelina obscura

Commelina obscura, commonly known as the dark commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes a variety of flowering plants often referred to as the spiderwort family. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are borne in clusters and are accompanied by bracts that are often more showy than the actual blooms.

Commelina obscura is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
