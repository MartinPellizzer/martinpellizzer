Carex crinita var. paleacea

Carex crinita var. paleacea, commonly known as the pale-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its pale, bluish-green foliage distinguishes it from other Carex species, and it produces small, clustered brownish flowers in late summer.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
