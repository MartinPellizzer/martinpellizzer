Clinopodium nepalense

Clinopodium nepalense, commonly known as Himalayan oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potentially therapeutic properties.

Clinopodium nepalense is valued not only for its ecological role but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in local communities.
