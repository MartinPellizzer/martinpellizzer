Cleistanthus patulus

Cleistanthus patulus, commonly known as the white-flowered cleistanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Cleistanthus patulus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
