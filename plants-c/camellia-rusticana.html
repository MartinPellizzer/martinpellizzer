Camellia rusticana

Camellia rusticana, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Camellia rusticana prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
