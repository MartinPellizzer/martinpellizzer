Comes borealis

Comes borealis, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Comes borealis plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native regions by providing nectar for pollinators.
