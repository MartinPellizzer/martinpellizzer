Cassia longiracemosa

Cassia longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in long, drooping racemes. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Cassia longiracemosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
