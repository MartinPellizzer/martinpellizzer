Cistus cistifolius

Cistus cistifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grey-green, narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Cistus cistifolius is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
