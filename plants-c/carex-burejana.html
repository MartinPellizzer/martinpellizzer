Carex burejana

Carex burejana, commonly known as the Burejana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

The flowering spikes are usually clustered at the top of the plant, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
