Cattleya guttata var. caerulea

Cattleya guttata var. caerulea, commonly known as the Blue Lady, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

This hybrid variety is characterized by its vibrant blue-violet petals and a distinctive white lip, which set it apart from other Cattleya species. It typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its unique coloration and rarity make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
