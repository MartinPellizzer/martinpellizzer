Crocus speciosus

Crocus speciosus, commonly known as the showy crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of saffron-like pigments.
