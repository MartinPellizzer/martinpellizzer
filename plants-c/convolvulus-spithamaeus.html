Convolvulus spithamaeus

Convolvulus spithamaeus, commonly known as the Egyptian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports or other vegetation. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
