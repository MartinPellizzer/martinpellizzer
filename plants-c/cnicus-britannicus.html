Cnicus britannicus

Cnicus britannicus, commonly known as the British gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along coastal areas.

Cnicus britannicus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
