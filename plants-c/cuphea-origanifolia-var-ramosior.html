Cuphea origanifolia var. ramosior

Cuphea origanifolia var. ramosior, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and pollinator habitats.
