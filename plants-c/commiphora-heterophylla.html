Commiphora heterophylla

Commiphora heterophylla, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Its leaves are alternate and vary in shape, often being elliptical or ovate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits.
