Cynoglossum tenerum

Cynoglossum tenerum, commonly known as the velvetleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynoglossum tenerum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
