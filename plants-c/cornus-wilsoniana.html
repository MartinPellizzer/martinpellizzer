Cornus wilsoniana

Cornus wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Cornus wilsoniana is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
