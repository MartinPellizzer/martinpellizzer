Calathea tarrazuensis

Calathea tarrazuensis, commonly known as the Tarrazu calathea, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaf patterns that change with the time of day, a phenomenon known as nyctochromism. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with intricate veining and vibrant hues of green, red, and purple.

Calathea tarrazuensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
