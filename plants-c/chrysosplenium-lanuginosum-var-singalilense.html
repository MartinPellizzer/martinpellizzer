Chrysosplenium lanuginosum var. singalilense

Chrysosplenium lanuginosum var. singalilense, commonly known as the silky chrysosplenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically white or pale green, which are followed by tiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
