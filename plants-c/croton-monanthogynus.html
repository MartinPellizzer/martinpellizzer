Croton monanthogynus

Croton monanthogynus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
