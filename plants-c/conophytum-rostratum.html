Conophytum rostratum

Conophytum rostratum, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Conophytum rostratum is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
