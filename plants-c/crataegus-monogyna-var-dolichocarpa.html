Crataegus monogyna var. dolichocarpa

Crataegus monogyna var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to lanceolate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, red to orange berry that ripens in late summer, often persisting into winter. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
