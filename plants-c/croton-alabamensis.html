Croton alabamensis

Croton alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama croton, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from yellow to red and orange hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
