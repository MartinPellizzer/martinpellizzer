Cyclanthus indivisus

Cyclanthus indivisus, commonly known as the false arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyclanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Cyclanthus indivisus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
