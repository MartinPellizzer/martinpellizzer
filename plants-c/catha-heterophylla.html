Catha heterophylla

Catha heterophylla, commonly known as Khat, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and East Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its leaves contain stimulant compounds such as cathinone and cathine, which contribute to its traditional use.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is cultivated and used in various cultural and social contexts across its native regions.
