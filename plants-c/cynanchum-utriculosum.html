Cynanchum utriculosum

Cynanchum utriculosum, commonly known as the bladderleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Cynanchum utriculosum is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
