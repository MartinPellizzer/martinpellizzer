Carissa edulis var. lucubea

Carissa edulis var. lucubea, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Natal plum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
