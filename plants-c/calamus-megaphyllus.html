Calamus megaphyllus

Calamus megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach lengths of up to three meters, making it one of the largest species within the genus Calamus. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and for making rattan products.

The botanical features of Calamus megaphyllus make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
