Campanula cochleariifolia var. vagans

Campanula cochleariifolia var. vagans, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is noted for its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
