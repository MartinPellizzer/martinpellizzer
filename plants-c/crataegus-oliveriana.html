Crataegus oliveriana

Crataegus oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, small leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a red or orange berry that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
