Convolvulus nevisiensis

Convolvulus nevisiensis, commonly known as the Nevis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the island of Nevis in the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers and fibrous roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
