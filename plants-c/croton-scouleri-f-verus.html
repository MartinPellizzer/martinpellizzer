Croton scouleri f. verus

Croton scouleri f. verus, commonly known as the purple-leafed croton, is a variant of the Croton scouleri species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of green, yellow, and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique leaf patterns.
