Calypso oppositifolia

Calypso oppositifolia, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
