Colpodium thomsonii

Colpodium thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its delicate, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface.

Colpodium thomsonii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
