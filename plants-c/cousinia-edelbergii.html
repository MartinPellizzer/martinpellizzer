Cousinia edelbergii

Cousinia edelbergii, commonly known as the Edelberg's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Cousinia edelbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
