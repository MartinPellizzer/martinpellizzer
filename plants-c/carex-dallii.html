Carex dallii

Carex dallii, commonly known as Dall's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streams. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Carex dallii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
