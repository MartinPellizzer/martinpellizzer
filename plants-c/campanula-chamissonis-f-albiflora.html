Campanula chamissonis f. albiflora

Campanula chamissonis f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower campanula, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
