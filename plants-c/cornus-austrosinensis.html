Cornus austrosinensis

Cornus austrosinensis, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Cornus austrosinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
