Carex melanocystis var. misera

Carex melanocystis var. misera, commonly known as the black-cysted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Its foliage is often dark green, with a slightly rough texture, and it is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
