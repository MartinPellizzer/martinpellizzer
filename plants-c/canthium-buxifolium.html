Canthium buxifolium

Canthium buxifolium, commonly known as the boxleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant has simple, opposite, and often glossy leaves that resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium buxifolium is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain areas.
