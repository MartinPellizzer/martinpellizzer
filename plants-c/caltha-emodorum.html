Caltha emodorum

Caltha emodorum, commonly known as the purple marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with five to eight petals, and it produces small, dark berries in the fall.

Caltha emodorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
