Cuphea cyanea var. hirtella

Cuphea cyanea var. hirtella, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea cyanea var. hirtella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
