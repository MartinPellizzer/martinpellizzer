Conostegia petiolata

Conostegia petiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued in musical instrument crafting. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing small, winged seeds.

Conostegia petiolata is often found in moist, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.
