Cereus speciosissimus var. unduliflorus

Cereus speciosissimus var. unduliflorus, commonly known as the undulating-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. Its flowers have a distinctive undulating pattern on the petals, giving it its common name.

It is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
