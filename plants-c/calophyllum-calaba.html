Calophyllum calaba

Calophyllum calaba, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its roots and bark are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

The tree is also valued for its durable, fragrant wood, which is used in the production of incense and furniture.
