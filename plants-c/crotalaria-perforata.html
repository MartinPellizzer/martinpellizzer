Crotalaria perforata

Crotalaria perforata, commonly known as the perforate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Crotalaria perforata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
