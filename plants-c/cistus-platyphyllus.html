Cistus platyphyllus

Cistus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems, dark green, leathery leaves, and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
