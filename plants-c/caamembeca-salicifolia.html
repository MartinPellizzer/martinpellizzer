Caamembeca salicifolia

Caamembeca salicifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways.

Caamembeca salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
