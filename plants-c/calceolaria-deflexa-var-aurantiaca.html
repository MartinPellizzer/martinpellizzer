Calceolaria deflexa var. aurantiaca

Calceolaria deflexa var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden cup flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, pouch-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria deflexa var. aurantiaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
