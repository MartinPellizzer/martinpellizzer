Canna densifolia

Canna densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are densely packed and often have a dark green color with a metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna densifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
