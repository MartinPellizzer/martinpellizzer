Cestrum auriculatum

Cestrum auriculatum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Cestrum auriculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
