Cyathella mucronata

Cyathella mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Cyathella, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyathaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences known as cyathia. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called phyllaries. Cyathella mucronata typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
