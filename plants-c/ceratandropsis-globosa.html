Ceratandropsis globosa

Ceratandropsis globosa, commonly known as the globe senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with elliptical leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.
