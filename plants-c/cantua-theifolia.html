Cantua theifolia

Cantua theifolia, commonly known as the thief's flower, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Cantua theifolia is considered a delicate species with conservation concerns.
