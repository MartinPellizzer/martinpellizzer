Crataegus collina var. sordida

Crataegus collina var. sordida, commonly known as the sordid hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with 5 to 9 oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its bark is often fissured and has a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to wildlife.
