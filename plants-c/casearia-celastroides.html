Casearia celastroides

Casearia celastroides, commonly known as the red sandalwood or white sandalwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often bird-dispersed, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
