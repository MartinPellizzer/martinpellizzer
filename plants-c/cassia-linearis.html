Cassia linearis

Cassia linearis, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Cassia linearis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
