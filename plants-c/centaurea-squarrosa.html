Centaurea squarrosa

Centaurea squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea squarrosa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
