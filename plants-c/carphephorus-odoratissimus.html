Carphephorus odoratissimus

Carphephorus odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented grass or the fragrant grass, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth and aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, greenish flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique scent and its use in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant.
