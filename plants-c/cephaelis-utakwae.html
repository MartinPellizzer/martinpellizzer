Cephaelis utakwae

Cephaelis utakwae, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Cephaelis utakwae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
