Cliffortia tricuspidata

Cliffortia tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-toothed rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cliffortia tricuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
