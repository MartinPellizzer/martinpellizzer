Chloris barbata

Chloris barbata, commonly known as the hairy-footed grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Chloris barbata is commonly found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
