Centaurea ceratophylla subsp. danielae

Centaurea ceratophylla subsp.

danielae, commonly known as Daniel's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
