Carex flava var. rectirostrata

Carex flava var. rectirostrata, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a yellowish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
