Caltha serotina

Caltha serotina, commonly known as late marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Caltha serotina is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
