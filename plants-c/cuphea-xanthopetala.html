Cuphea xanthopetala

Cuphea xanthopetala, commonly known as the yellow whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea xanthopetala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display.
