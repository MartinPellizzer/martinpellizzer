Carex aperta

Carex aperta, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along streambanks. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, with a smooth surface, and it often forms dense clumps.

Carex aperta is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
