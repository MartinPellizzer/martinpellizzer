Cyperus sesquiflorus f. dunensis

Cyperus sesquiflorus f. dunensis, commonly known as the Dunedin flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in coastal areas or lowland wetlands. It has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
