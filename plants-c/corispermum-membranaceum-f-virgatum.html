Corispermum membranaceum f. virgatum

Corispermum membranaceum f. virgatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential uses in phytoremediation.
