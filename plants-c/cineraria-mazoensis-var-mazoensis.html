Cineraria mazoensis var. mazoensis

Cineraria mazoensis var. mazoensis, commonly known as the Mazo cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
