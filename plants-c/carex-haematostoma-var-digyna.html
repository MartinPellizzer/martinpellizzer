Carex haematostoma var. digyna

Carex haematostoma var. digyna, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It has fibrous roots and is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
