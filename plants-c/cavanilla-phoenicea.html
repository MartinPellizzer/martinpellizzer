Cavanilla phoenicea

Cavanilla phoenicea, commonly known as the red-flowered cavanilla, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Cavanilla phoenicea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
