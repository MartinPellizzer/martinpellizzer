Calophyllum cordato-oblongum

Calophyllum cordato-oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
