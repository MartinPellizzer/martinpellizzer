Curio articulatus var. globosus

Curio articulatus var. globosus, commonly known as the globe-shaped umbrella plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, rounded, umbrella-like clusters of leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant features glossy, dark green foliage with a somewhat waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate watering.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
