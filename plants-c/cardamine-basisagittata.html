Cardamine basisagittata

Cardamine basisagittata, commonly known as the lanceleaf watercress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. It has a delicate, peppery flavor and is sometimes used in salads or as a garnish.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
