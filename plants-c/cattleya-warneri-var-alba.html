Cattleya warneri var. alba

Cattleya warneri var. alba, commonly known as the White Warner Cattleya, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white flowers with a velvety texture and a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette shape.

This variety is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
