Costus brasiliensis

Costus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian costus, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its ornamental leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Costus brasiliensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
