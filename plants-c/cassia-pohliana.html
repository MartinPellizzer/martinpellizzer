Cassia pohliana

Cassia pohliana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, papery flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia pohliana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
