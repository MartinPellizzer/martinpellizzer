Cistus garganicus

Cistus garganicus, commonly known as the Garganica rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Cistus garganicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
