Croton excurrens

Croton excurrens, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is popular in horticulture but requires well-drained soil and protection from frost.
