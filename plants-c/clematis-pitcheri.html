Clematis pitcheri

Clematis pitcheri, commonly known as the western clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and showy, usually white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets and produces small, follicle-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
