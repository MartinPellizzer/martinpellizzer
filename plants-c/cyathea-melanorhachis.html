Cyathea melanorhachis

Cyathea melanorhachis, commonly known as the black-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea melanorhachis plays a significant role in its ecosystem by offering habitat and shelter to various organisms.
