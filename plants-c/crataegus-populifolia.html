Crataegus populifolia

Crataegus populifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, maple-like leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
