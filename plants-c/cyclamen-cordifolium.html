Cyclamen cordifolium

Cyclamen cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Cyclamen cordifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also valued for its ability to bloom during the winter months, adding color to gardens in colder seasons.
