Canella axillaris

Canella axillaris, commonly known as the mountain pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and dark, glossy, elliptical leaves. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Canella axillaris is valued for its essential oils, which have been used for their antiseptic and stimulant properties.
