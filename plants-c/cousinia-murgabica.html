Cousinia murgabica

Cousinia murgabica, commonly known as the Murgab cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features tightly packed, herbaceous leaves that help it withstand harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its arid habitat.

Cousinia murgabica is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in semi-arid regions.
