Cosmia

Cosmia, commonly known as the cosmos, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions of the Americas and are known for their showy, daisy-like flowers. Cosmia species typically have ray florets that are either white, pink, or red, and they bloom in summer. The plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sunlight, making them easy to grow in a variety of climates.
