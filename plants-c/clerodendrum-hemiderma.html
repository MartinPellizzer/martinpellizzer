Clerodendrum hemiderma

Clerodendrum hemiderma, commonly known as the Indian flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
