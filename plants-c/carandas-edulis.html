Carandas edulis

Carandas edulis, commonly known as the edible caranda, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor. The fruit's pulp is often consumed fresh, while the seeds can be dried and used as a spice.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its nutritional and culinary uses.
