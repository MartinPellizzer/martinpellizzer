Cristaria viridiluteola

Cristaria viridiluteola, commonly known as the greenish bluish water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Cristaria viridiluteola plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
