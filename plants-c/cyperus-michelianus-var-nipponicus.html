Cyperus michelianus var. nipponicus

Cyperus michelianus var. nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as ponds and rice fields. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on wind for pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
