Capsicum malacophyllum

Capsicum malacophyllum, commonly known as the Malacophyllum pepper, is a rare and exotic species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated fruit that turns a vibrant red when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a glossy surface that reflects sunlight.

Despite its botanical interest, it is rarely cultivated due to its specific growing requirements and limited availability.
