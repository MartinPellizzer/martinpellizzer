Centaurea erdneri

Centaurea erdneri, commonly known as the Erdner's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces a compact, upright growth habit.

Centaurea erdneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
