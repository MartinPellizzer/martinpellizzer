Caltha major

Caltha major, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Caltha major is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
