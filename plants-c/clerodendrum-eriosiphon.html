Clerodendrum eriosiphon

Clerodendrum eriosiphon, commonly known as the velvetleaf clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
