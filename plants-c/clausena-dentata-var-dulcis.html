Clausena dentata var. dulcis

Clausena dentata var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet chekka or sweet kumara, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
