Cyperus submacropus

Cyperus submacropus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cyperus submacropus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
