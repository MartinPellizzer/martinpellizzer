Catalpa himalayensis

Catalpa himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender seed pods that contain numerous winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The Himalayan catalpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
