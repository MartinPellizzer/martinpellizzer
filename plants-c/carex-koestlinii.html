Carex koestlinii

Carex koestlinii, commonly known as the Koestlin's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like structures, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its propagation.

Carex koestlinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
