Carex bonatiana

Carex bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex bonatiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
