Campanula mollis

Campanula mollis, commonly known as the soft bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanula mollis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
