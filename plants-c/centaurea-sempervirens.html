Centaurea sempervirens

Centaurea sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers with distinctive red or orange centers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Centaurea sempervirens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
