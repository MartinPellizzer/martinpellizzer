Carex halleriana

Carex halleriana, commonly known as the Haller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex halleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
