Camelina sativa var. hirsuta

Camelina sativa var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy camelina, is a variant of the oilseed plant Camelina sativa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hairy camelina is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.
