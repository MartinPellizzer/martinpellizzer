Calliandra renvoizeana

Calliandra renvoizeana, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Calliandra renvoizeana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
