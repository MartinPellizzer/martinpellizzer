Crassula fusca

Crassula fusca, commonly known as the pale crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
