Crystallopollen latifolium

Crystallopollen latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf crystal pollen plant, is a unique species characterized by its broad, flat leaves that resemble pollen grains in appearance.

It is native to arid regions and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which contribute to its ornamental value. Its leaves are coated with a waxy layer, helping to reduce water loss in dry environments.

This plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
