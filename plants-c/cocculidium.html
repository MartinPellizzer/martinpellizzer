Cocculidium

Cocculidium, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders, giving the plant its common name. Cocculidium species are generally herbaceous and can be annual or perennial, depending on the species.
