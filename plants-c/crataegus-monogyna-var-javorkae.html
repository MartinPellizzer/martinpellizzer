Crataegus monogyna var. javorkae

Crataegus monogyna var. javorkae, commonly known as the Javorka hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
