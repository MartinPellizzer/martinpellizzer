Chamaepeuce stellata

Chamaepeuce stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, yellow petals that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain the seeds.

Chamaepeuce stellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
