Calceolaria witasekiana

Calceolaria witasekiana, commonly known as the Witasek's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces delicate, colorful blooms in shades of pink, red, and purple.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
