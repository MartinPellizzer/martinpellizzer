Conradia sloanei

Conradia sloanei, commonly known as the San Pedro cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in rocky areas and is a symbol of the desert landscape.
