Carduncellus caeruleus var. launaeifolia

Carduncellus caeruleus var. launaeifolia, commonly known as the blue thistle or blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
