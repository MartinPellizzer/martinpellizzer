Castanea × pulchella

Castanea × pulchella, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to China and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its nut production.

Castanea × pulchella is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to certain diseases.
