Convolvulus siculus subsp. flexuosus

Convolvulus siculus subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as the Sicilian morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
