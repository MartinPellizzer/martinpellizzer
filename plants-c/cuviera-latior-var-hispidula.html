Cuviera latior var. hispidula

Cuviera latior var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf cuviera, is a variant of the cuviera plant characterized by its broader leaves and hairy texture.

It is native to certain regions of Central America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy surface that provides a unique tactile quality. This plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the genus Cuviera, valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
