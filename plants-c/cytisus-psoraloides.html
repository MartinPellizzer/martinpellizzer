Cytisus psoraloides

Cytisus psoraloides, commonly known as the red broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, yellow pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its foliage is typically dark green and aromatic, with leaves that are trifoliate in structure.
