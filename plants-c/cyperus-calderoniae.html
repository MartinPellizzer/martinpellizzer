Cyperus calderoniae

Cyperus calderoniae, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of wetland environments, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
