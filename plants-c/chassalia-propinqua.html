Chassalia propinqua

Chassalia propinqua, commonly known as the slender chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers make it a distinctive addition to the landscape.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ease of care.
