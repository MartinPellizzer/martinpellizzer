Claytonia trifida

Claytonia trifida, commonly known as the "peacock plant," is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are often green with red or purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
