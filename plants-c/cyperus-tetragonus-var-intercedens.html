Cyperus tetragonus var. intercedens

Cyperus tetragonus var. intercedens, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a common indicator of waterlogged soils.
