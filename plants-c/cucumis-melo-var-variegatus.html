Cucumis melo var. variegatus

Cucumis melo var. variegatus, commonly known as the striped muskmelon, is a cultivated variant of the muskmelon species.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green, yellow, and white patterns. The plant produces large, aromatic fruits with a rough, netted rind and a sweet, juicy interior. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for both its ornamental value and edible fruit.
