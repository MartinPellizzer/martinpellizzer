Centaurea hadacii

Centaurea hadacii, commonly known as the Hadacii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers range in color from purple to pink.

Centaurea hadacii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
