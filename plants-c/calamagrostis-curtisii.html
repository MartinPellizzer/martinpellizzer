Calamagrostis curtisii

Calamagrostis curtisii, commonly known as Curtis' reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are usually pale green to golden in color.

Curtis' reedgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
