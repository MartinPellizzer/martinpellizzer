Cordia ulmifolia var. lineata

Cordia ulmifolia var. lineata, commonly known as the lined cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale midrib, giving it its varietal name "lineata.". The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
