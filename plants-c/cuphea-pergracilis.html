Cuphea pergracilis

Cuphea pergracilis, commonly known as the slender cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea pergracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
