Croton rimbachii

Croton rimbachii, commonly known as the Rimbach's croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, or orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough or hairy texture.

Croton rimbachii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
