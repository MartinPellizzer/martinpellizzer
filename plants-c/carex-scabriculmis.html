Carex scabriculmis

Carex scabriculmis, commonly known as the rough-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex scabriculmis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
