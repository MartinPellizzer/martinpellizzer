Chloranthus monostachyus

Chloranthus monostachyus, commonly known as the single-stemmed chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
