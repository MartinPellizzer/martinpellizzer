Cananga caribaea

Cananga caribaea, commonly known as the West Indian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

The wood of Cananga caribaea is prized for its fine grain and is used in furniture and cabinet-making.
