Cephalomanes acrosorum

Cephalomanes acrosorum, commonly known as the acrosorus shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are released during the dry season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
