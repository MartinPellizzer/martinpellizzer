Calytrix longiflora

Calytrix longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Calytrix longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
