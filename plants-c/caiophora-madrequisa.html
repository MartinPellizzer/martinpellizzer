Caiophora madrequisa

Caiophora madrequisa, commonly known as the Madrequisa orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid habitat.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
