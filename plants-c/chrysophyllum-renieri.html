Chrysophyllum renieri

Chrysophyllum renieri, commonly known as the African cherry or West African cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
