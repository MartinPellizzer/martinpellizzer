Chenopodium carinatum var. holopterum

Chenopodium carinatum var. holopterum, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in clusters. The plant produces a dry, capsule-like fruit containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many regions.
