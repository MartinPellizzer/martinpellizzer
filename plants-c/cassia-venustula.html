Cassia venustula

Cassia venustula, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
