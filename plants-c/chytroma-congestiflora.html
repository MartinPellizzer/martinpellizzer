Chytroma congestiflora

Chytroma congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered chytroma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
