Cyathea impolita

Cyathea impolita, commonly known as the pale tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can reach significant lengths, supporting its tall structure.

The fronds are usually a deep green, with a leathery texture and a distinct, elegant appearance.
