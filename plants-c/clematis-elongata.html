Clematis elongata

Clematis elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis elongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
