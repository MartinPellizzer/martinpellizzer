Centaurea deusta var. brunnea

Centaurea deusta var. brunnea, commonly known as the dark-centred centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are often covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive dark center, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
