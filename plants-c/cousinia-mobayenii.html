Cousinia mobayenii

Cousinia mobayenii, commonly known as the Persian feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, feathery inflorescences, which are characteristic of the genus Cousinia. It is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes in regions with similar climatic conditions.
