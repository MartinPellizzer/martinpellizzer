Cercocarpus mojadensis

Cercocarpus mojadensis, commonly known as Mojave curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils.

Cercocarpus mojadensis plays a vital role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and preventing soil erosion.
