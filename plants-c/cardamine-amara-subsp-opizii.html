Cardamine amara subsp. opizii

Cardamine amara subsp.

opizii, commonly known as the Opiz's bitter celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
