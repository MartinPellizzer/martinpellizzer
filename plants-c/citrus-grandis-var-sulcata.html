Citrus grandis var. sulcata

Citrus grandis var. sulcata, commonly known as the rough lemon.

This variety is characterized by its thick, ridged rind and small, sour fruit. It is a hybrid citrus plant, originating from a cross between the citron and the mandarin. The leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly serrated margin.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a rootstock for other citrus plants.
