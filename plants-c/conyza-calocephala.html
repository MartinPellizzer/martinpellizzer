Conyza calocephala

Conyza calocephala, commonly known as the common white daisy, is an annual flowering plant native to Africa, Australia, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural areas. It produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
