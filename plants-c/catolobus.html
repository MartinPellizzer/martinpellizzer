Catolobus

Catolobus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and prefer indirect sunlight. Catolobus species are popular as houseplants due to their ease of care and aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, adding to their ornamental value.
