Callicarpa remotiserrulata

Callicarpa remotiserrulata, commonly known as the red-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa remotiserrulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens for its striking autumn foliage and fruit.
