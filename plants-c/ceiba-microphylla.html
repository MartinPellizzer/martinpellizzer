Ceiba microphylla

Ceiba microphylla, commonly known as the little-leaf ceiba, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and a crown of small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is smooth and often has a light gray or brown color.

Ceiba microphylla plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
