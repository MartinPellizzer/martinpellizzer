Chionanthus picrophloius

Chionanthus picrophloius, commonly known as the yellow fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Chionanthus picrophloius is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.
