Campanula colorata var. anomala

Campanula colorata var. anomala, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft pubescence. This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in alpine conditions.
