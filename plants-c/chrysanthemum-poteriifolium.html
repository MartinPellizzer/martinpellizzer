Chrysanthemum poteriifolium

Chrysanthemum poteriifolium, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
