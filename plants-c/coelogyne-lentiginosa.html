Coelogyne lentiginosa

Coelogyne lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Coelogyne lentiginosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral display.
