Calamus thysanolepis var. polylepis

Calamus thysanolepis var. polylepis, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a variant of the genus Calamus, which belongs to the family Arecaceae.

This plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and arched, pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, followed by elongated, winged fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
