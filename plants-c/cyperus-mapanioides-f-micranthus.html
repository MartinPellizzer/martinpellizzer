Cyperus mapanioides f. micranthus

Cyperus mapanioides f. micranthus, commonly known as the dwarf flatsedge, is a compact, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
