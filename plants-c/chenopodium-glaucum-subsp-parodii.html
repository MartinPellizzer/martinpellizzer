Chenopodium glaucum subsp. parodii

Chenopodium glaucum subsp.

parodii, commonly known as wild spinach or pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clustered inflorescences, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
