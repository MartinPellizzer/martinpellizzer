Centaurea lavrenkoana

Centaurea lavrenkoana, commonly known as the Lavrenko's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea lavrenkoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
