Centroglossa peruviana

Centroglossa peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vivid colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient photosynthesis.

Centroglossa peruviana is often found in humid, shaded areas, preferring well-drained soil and cool temperatures.
