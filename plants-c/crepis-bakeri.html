Crepis bakeri

Crepis bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, achene-like seeds.

Crepis bakeri is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
