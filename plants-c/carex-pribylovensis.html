Carex pribylovensis

Carex pribylovensis, commonly known as the Pribylovsky sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to eastern Europe and parts of western Siberia, typically found in wetlands and moist soils. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
