Carex petricosa var. edwardsii

Carex petricosa var. edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards' sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
