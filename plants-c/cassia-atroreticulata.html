Cassia atroreticulata

Cassia atroreticulata, commonly known as black-veined cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with dark, reticulate veins. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia atroreticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
