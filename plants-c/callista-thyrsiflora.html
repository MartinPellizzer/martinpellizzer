Callista thyrsiflora

Callista thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
