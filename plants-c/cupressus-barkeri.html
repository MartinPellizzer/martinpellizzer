Cupressus barkeri

Cupressus barkeri, commonly known as the Barker's cypress, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and its ability to form compact, shrub-like growth forms. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cupressus barkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
