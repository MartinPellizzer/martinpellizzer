Conyza triloba

Conyza triloba, commonly known as the common ironweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Conyza triloba produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
