Comanthera circinnata

Comanthera circinnata, commonly known as the spiral-shaped orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat requirements, Comanthera circinnata is considered a delicate and endangered species.
