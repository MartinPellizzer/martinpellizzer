Clerodendrum penduliflorum

Clerodendrum penduliflorum, commonly known as the hangingflower or pendulous clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
