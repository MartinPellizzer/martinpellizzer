Carex remota var. brizopyrum

Carex remota var. brizopyrum, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it serves as a habitat and food source for various invertebrates and birds.
