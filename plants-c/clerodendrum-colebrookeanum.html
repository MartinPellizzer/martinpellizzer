Clerodendrum colebrookeanum

Clerodendrum colebrookeanum, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
