Cyathea scandens

Cyathea scandens, commonly known as the climbing tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
