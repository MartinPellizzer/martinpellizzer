Cnicus hillii

Cnicus hillii, commonly known as the Hill's prickly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy leaves and thrives in dry, rocky soils, making it well adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Cnicus hillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
