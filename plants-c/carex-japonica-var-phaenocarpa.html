Carex japonica var. phaenocarpa

Carex japonica var. phaenocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex japonica var. phaenocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
