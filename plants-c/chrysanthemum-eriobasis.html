Chrysanthemum eriobasis

Chrysanthemum eriobasis, commonly known as the woolly chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Chrysanthemum eriobasis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
