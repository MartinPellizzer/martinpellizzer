Centaurea aemilii

Centaurea aemilii, commonly known as the "blueweed" or "Italian centaury," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea aemilii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
