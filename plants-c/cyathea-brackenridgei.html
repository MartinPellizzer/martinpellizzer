Cyathea brackenridgei

Cyathea brackenridgei, commonly known as the Brackenridge's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea brackenridgei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
