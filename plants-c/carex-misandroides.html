Carex misandroides

Carex misandroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flower clusters.

Carex misandroides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
