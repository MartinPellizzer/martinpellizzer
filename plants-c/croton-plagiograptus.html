Croton plagiograptus

Croton plagiograptus, commonly known as the zigzag croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with alternating shades of green and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
