Caraguata multiflora

Caraguata multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Caraguata multiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
