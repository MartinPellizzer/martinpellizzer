Chauliodon deflexicalcaratum

Chauliodon deflexicalcaratum, commonly known as the Moonlight Fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It features intricate, feather-like fronds that shimmer with a silvery sheen under moonlight, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity and well-draining soil. Its reproductive system relies on wind-pollinated spores, which are released from specialized sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its unique adaptations and limited habitat, it is considered a conservation concern.
