Clematis orientalis var. longifoliola

Clematis orientalis var. longifoliola, commonly known as the long-leaved oriental clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are usually 5 to 7 inches in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its climbing habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
