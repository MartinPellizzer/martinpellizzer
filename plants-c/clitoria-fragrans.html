Clitoria fragrans

Clitoria fragrans, commonly known as the sacred bean or butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Clitoria fragrans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
