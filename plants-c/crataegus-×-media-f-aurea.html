Crataegus × media f. aurea

Crataegus × media f. aurea, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a vibrant yellow color during the growing season. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and seasonal interest.
