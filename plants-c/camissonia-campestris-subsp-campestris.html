Camissonia campestris subsp. campestris

Camissonia campestris subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field camissonia, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface, and it is often found in coastal and inland regions of the western United States.
