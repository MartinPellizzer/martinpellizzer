Cremosperma jucundum

Cremosperma jucundum, commonly known as the sweet-scented jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, reddish-brown fruits resembling small dates. The fruits are known for their sweet, honey-like flavor and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
