Carex × dematranea

Carex × dematranea, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an effective pioneer species in wetland restoration.

Carex × dematranea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
