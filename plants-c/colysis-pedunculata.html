Colysis pedunculata

Colysis pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
