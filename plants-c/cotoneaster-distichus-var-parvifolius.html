Cotoneaster distichus var. parvifolius

Cotoneaster distichus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf cotoneaster, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
