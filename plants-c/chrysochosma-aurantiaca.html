Chrysochosma aurantiaca

Chrysochosma aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The golden cupplant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
