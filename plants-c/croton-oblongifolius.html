Croton oblongifolius

Croton oblongifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to Africa.

It features alternate, elliptical to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive greenish or yellowish hue. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional use.
