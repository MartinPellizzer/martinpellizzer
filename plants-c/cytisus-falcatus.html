Cytisus falcatus

Cytisus falcatus, commonly known as the sickle broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a slight curl, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

The plant is also known for its fragrant blossoms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
