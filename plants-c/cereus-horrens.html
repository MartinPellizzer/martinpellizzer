Cereus horrens

Cereus horrens, commonly known as the devil's trumpet, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of yellow or white. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Cereus horrens is often cultivated for its striking appearance and nocturnal flowering habit.
