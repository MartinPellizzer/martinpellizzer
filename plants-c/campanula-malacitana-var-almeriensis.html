Campanula malacitana var. almeriensis

Campanula malacitana var. almeriensis, commonly known as the Almerian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
