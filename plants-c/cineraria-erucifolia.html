Cineraria erucifolia

Cineraria erucifolia, commonly known as the woolly cineraria, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
