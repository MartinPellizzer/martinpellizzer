Cinnamomum molle

Cinnamomum molle, commonly known as sweet cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their antimicrobial properties.

Cinnamomum molle thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
