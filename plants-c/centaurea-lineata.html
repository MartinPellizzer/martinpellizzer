Centaurea lineata

Centaurea lineata, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescence.

Centaurea lineata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
