Clusia articulata

Clusia articulata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic value.

Clusia articulata is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
