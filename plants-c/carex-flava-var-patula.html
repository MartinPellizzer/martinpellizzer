Carex flava var. patula

Carex flava var. patula, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
