Cereus peruvianus var. reclinatus

Cereus peruvianus var. reclinatus, commonly known as the leaning candle cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often lean or droop, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking form and relatively easy care requirements.
