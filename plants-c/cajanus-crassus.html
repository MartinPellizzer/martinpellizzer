Cajanus crassus

Cajanus crassus, commonly known as the rough legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in arid environments, making it a resilient species. Cajanus crassus is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices in various cultures.
