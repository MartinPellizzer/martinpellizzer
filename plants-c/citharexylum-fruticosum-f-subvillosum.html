Citharexylum fruticosum f. subvillosum

Citharexylum fruticosum f. subvillosum, commonly known as the silky citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, softly hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
