Carex × goyozanensis

Carex × goyozanensis, commonly known as the Goyozan sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, native to Japan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Carex × goyozanensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
