Cardamine hirsuta f. multicaulis

Cardamine hirsuta f. multicaulis, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the common watercress species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in woodland areas. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to wet, nutrient-rich soils.
