Cola lepidota

Cola lepidota, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a rough, leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in various medicinal preparations.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
