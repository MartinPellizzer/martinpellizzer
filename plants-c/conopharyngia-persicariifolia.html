Conopharyngia persicariifolia

Conopharyngia persicariifolia, commonly known as the Persian leaf plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
