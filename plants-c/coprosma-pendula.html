Coprosma pendula

Coprosma pendula, commonly known as the weeping coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that bear clusters of small, bright green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Coprosma pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
