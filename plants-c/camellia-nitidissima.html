Camellia nitidissima

Camellia nitidissima, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species native to China and is prized for its ornamental value.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Camellia nitidissima is also valued for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
