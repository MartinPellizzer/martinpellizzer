Cissus sicyoides f. paraguayensis

Cissus sicyoides f. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay grape ivy, is a trailing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it popular in both indoor and outdoor gardening.
