Carex pachystylis

Carex pachystylis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are usually yellowish-green.

Carex pachystylis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
