Castanopsis neocavaleriei

Castanopsis neocavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage. The tree produces small, cup-shaped fruits that enclose one or two seeds, typical of the Fagaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil types make it a popular choice for landscaping.
