Convolvulus wallichianus

Convolvulus wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
