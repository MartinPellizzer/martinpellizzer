Cardamine richardsonii

Cardamine richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually heart-shaped.

The flowers consist of numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil, typical of the buttercup genus.
