Citrus × intermedia

Citrus × intermedia, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originated from the cross between the sweet orange and the pomelo. The plant is characterized by its thick, peeling rind and small, acidic fruits. It is often used as a rootstock in citrus cultivation due to its hardiness and resistance to diseases.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, with a strong aromatic scent.
