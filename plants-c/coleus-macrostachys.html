Coleus macrostachys

Coleus macrostachys, commonly known as the giant flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can be red, orange, or yellow, often with contrasting green veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of its branches. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
