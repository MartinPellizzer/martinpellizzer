Cornus mascula var. aurea-elegantissima

Cornus mascula var. aurea-elegantissima, commonly known as the golden elderberry, is a striking shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange inflorescences that appear in early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
