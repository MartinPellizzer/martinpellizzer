Crassula cephalophora var. tayloriae

Crassula cephalophora var. tayloriae, commonly known as the Taylor's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
