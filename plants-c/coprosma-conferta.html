Coprosma conferta

Coprosma conferta, commonly known as the silver coprosma, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are usually a glossy green or silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forest margins and rocky areas, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.

Coprosma conferta is also known for its adaptability to different soil types and its use in ecological restoration projects.
