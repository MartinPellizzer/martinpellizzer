Celtis alba

Celtis alba, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. Celtis alba is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
