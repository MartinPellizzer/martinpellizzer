Cassia disadena

Cassia disadena, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is often smooth and grayish in color.

Cassia disadena is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
