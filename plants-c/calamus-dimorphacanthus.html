Calamus dimorphacanthus

Calamus dimorphacanthus, commonly known as the two-awned calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus dimorphacanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
