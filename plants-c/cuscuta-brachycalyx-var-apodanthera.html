Cuscuta brachycalyx var. apodanthera

Cuscuta brachycalyx var. apodanthera, commonly known as the short calyx dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure with thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to North America and is often found growing on grasses, shrubs, and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed through the wind, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host and is considered a potential agricultural weed.
