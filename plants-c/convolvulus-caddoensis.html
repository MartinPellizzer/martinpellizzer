Convolvulus caddoensis

Convolvulus caddoensis, commonly known as Caddo morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Caddo morning glory is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and along stream banks.
