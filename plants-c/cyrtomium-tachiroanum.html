Cyrtomium tachiroanum

Cyrtomium tachiroanum, commonly known as the Tachiroanum holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds with a distinctive, narrow, and upright growth habit. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in clusters called sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
