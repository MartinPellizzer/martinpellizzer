Campanopsis emirnensis

Campanopsis emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
