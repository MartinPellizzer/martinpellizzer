Campanula erucifolia

Campanula erucifolia, commonly known as the horned violets, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula erucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
