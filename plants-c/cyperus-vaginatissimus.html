Cyperus vaginatissimus

Cyperus vaginatissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps. Its flowering structures, called spikelets, are small and clustered, producing tiny seeds.

Cyperus vaginatissimus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
