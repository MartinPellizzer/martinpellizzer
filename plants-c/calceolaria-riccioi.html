Calceolaria riccioi

Calceolaria riccioi, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are hairy and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Calceolaria riccioi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
