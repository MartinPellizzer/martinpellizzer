Claoxylon biciliatum

Claoxylon biciliatum, commonly known as the two-ribbed sandpaper tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
