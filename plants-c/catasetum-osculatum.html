Catasetum osculatum

Catasetum osculatum, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble the bodies of orchid bees. The plant produces a unique structure called a gynostemium, which is a fused column of reproductive organs. Its flowers are pollinated by orchid bees, which are attracted by the scent and appearance of the blooms.

Catasetum osculatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting pollinator species.
