Chlorocyperus longus

Chlorocyperus longus, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

Chlorocyperus longus is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
