Cassia apoucouita var. floribunda

Cassia apoucouita var. floribunda, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often covering the entire plant. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
