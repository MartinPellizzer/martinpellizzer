Campanula trachelium subsp. athoa

Campanula trachelium subsp.

athoa, commonly known as the Scottish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
