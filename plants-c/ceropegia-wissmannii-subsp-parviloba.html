Ceropegia wissmannii subsp. parviloba

Ceropegia wissmannii subsp.

parviloba, commonly known as the string of pearls plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa. It features trailing stems with tubular, fleshy leaves that resemble small pearls, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
