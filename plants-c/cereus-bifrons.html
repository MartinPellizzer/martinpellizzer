Cereus bifrons

Cereus bifrons, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. It produces striking white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract pollinators.

The flowers are short-lived, typically lasting only a few hours, making the plant a rare and beautiful sight.
