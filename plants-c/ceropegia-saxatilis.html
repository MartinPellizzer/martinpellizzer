Ceropegia saxatilis

Ceropegia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
