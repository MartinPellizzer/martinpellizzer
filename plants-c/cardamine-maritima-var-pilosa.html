Cardamine maritima var. pilosa

Cardamine maritima var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy water cress, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near the shorelines and is often found in brackish or freshwater environments.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a valued plant in certain culinary traditions.
