Carex obliquitruncata

Carex obliquitruncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex obliquitruncata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
