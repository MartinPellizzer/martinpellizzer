Clematis fulvicoma

Clematis fulvicoma, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis fulvicoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
