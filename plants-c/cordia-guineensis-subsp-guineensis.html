Cordia guineensis subsp. guineensis

Cordia guineensis subsp.

guineensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually gland-dotted and have a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of white, fleshy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
