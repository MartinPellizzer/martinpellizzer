Couthovia astyla

Couthovia astyla, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered in dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh alpine conditions.
