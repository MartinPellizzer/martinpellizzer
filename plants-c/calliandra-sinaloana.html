Calliandra sinaloana

Calliandra sinaloana, commonly known as the Sinaloa calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra sinaloana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
