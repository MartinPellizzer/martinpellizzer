Camellia simplicifolia

Camellia simplicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the winter and early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Camellia simplicifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
