Carex praeclara

Carex praeclara, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex praeclara is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
