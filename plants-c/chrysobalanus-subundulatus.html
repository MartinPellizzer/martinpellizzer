Chrysobalanus subundulatus

Chrysobalanus subundulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chrysobalanus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by ellipsoid fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
