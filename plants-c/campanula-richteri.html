Campanula richteri

Campanula richteri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula richteri is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting flowers.
