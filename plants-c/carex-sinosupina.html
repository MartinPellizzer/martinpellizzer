Carex sinosupina

Carex sinosupina, commonly known as the Sino-supina sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex sinosupina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
