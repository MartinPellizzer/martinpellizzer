Chaenostoma campanulatum

Chaenostoma campanulatum, commonly known as the bellflower of the genus Chaenostoma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive blue or violet hue. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
