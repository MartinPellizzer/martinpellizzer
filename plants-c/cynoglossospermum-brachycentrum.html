Cynoglossospermum brachycentrum

Cynoglossospermum brachycentrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
