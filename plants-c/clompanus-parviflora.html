Clompanus parviflora

Clompanus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered clompanus, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clompanus parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
