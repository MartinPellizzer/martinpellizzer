Cieca montevidensis

Cieca montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.
