Cirsium helleri

Cirsium helleri, commonly known as Heller's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Heller's thistle plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
