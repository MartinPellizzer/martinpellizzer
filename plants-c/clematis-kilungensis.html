Clematis kilungensis

Clematis kilungensis, commonly known as the Kilung clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, often with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
