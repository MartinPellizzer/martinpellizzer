Carthamus syriacus

Carthamus syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers with five petals. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and serrated, while its flowering period typically occurs in late summer. The seeds are enclosed in a spiky, hairy capsule and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
