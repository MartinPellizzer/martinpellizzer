Croton claussenianus var. tomentellus

Croton claussenianus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
