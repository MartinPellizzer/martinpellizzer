Ctenitis lindenii

Ctenitis lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, giving the plant a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Ctenitis lindenii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
