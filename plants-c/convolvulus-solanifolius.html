Convolvulus solanifolius

Convolvulus solanifolius, commonly known as the Solanum-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces bright yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
