Cyrtochilum turpe

Cyrtochilum turpe, commonly known as the Turpentine Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features a short, wiry stem and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, organic-rich soil.

Cyrtochilum turpe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
