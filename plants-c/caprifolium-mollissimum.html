Caprifolium mollissimum

Caprifolium mollissimum, commonly known as the silky honeysuckle, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in forested or rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
