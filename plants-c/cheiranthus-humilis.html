Cheiranthus humilis

Cheiranthus humilis, commonly known as the low honesty, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its low-growing habit and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a typical trait of the Brassicaceae family.

Cheiranthus humilis is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
