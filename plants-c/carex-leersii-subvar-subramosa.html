Carex leersii subvar. subramosa

Carex leersii subvar. subramosa, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green coloration on the underside.

The flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
