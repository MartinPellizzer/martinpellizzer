Charianthus fadyenii

Charianthus fadyenii, commonly known as the Fadyenii's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
