Cerasus acida var. rhexii

Cerasus acida var. rhexii, commonly known as the sour cherry variety rhexii, is a botanical variant of the sour cherry species.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are typically used in cooking and preserves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It produces white flowers in the spring, followed by its distinctive red or black berries in late summer.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
