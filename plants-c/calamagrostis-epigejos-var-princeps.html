Calamagrostis epigejos var. princeps

Calamagrostis epigejos var. princeps, commonly known as Prince's panicled grass, is a perennial grass native to the northeastern United States.

It features tall, slender culms with drooping panicles of silver-green to golden florets, creating a striking visual display in late summer and autumn. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland restoration and rain gardens. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds texture to landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and improving soil stability.
