Carduncellus monspelliensium subsp. matritensis

Carduncellus monspelliensium subsp.

matritensis, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
