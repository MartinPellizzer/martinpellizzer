Cattleya luteola var. lepida

Cattleya luteola var. lepida, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble a lady's slipper, giving it its common name. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
