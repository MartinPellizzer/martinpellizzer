Carum proliferum

Carum proliferum, commonly known as the clustered caraway, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called mericarps, which contain the aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Carum proliferum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
