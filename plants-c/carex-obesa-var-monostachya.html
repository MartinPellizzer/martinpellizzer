Carex obesa var. monostachya

Carex obesa var. monostachya, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
