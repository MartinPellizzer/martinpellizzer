Cassia kotschyana

Cassia kotschyana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant produces long, narrow, greenish-yellow pods that contain several seeds. Cassia kotschyana thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
