Cereus rhombeus

Cereus rhombeus, commonly known as the rhombic cactus, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a columnar cactus with a smooth, green stem that often develops a reddish tinge under intense sunlight. The plant produces white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its simple structure and striking appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
