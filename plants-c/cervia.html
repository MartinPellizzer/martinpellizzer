Cervia

Cervia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, spiny leaves. The plant is typically found in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cervia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
