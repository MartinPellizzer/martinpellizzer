Cordyline jacquinii

Cordyline jacquinii, commonly known as the red-veined dracaena, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with prominent red veins. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant coloration and air-purifying qualities.

Cordyline jacquinii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
