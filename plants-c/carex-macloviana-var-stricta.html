Carex macloviana var. stricta

Carex macloviana var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically found in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Carex macloviana var. stricta is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
