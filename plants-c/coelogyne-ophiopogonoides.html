Coelogyne ophiopogonoides

Coelogyne ophiopogonoides, commonly known as the Ophiopogon-like orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, with showy, lanceolate petals.

Coelogyne ophiopogonoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
