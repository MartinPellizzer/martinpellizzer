Cupressus sempervirens subvar. pyramidalis

Cupressus sempervirens subvar. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Italian cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches, giving it a uniform appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant form and adaptability to various soil types.
