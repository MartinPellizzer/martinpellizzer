Citharexylum myrianthum

Citharexylum myrianthum, commonly known as the velvetleaf citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
