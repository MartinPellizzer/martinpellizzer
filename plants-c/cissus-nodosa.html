Cissus nodosa

Cissus nodosa, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a vining plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Cissus nodosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant.
