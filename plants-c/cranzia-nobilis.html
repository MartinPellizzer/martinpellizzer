Cranzia nobilis

Cranzia nobilis, commonly known as the noble cranza, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Cranzia nobilis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
