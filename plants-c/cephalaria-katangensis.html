Cephalaria katangensis

Cephalaria katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
