Cyperus nyassicus

Cyperus nyassicus, commonly known as the African sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cyperus nyassicus is also sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to wet conditions.
