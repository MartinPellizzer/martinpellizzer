Carlina texedae

Carlina texedae, commonly known as the Texas aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Carlina texedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
