Carpinus intermedia

Carpinus intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mixed hardwood forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
