Cylicodaphne oblonga

Cylicodaphne oblonga, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like shape formed by its dense clusters of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 feet in ideal conditions. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Cylicodaphne oblonga is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
