Cephalocereus arrabidae

Cephalocereus arrabidae, commonly known as the Portuguese torch cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
