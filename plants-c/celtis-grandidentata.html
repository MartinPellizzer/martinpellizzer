Celtis grandidentata

Celtis grandidentata, commonly known as the velvetleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Celtis grandidentata thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability and ornamental features make it a common sight in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
