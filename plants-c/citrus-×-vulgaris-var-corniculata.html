Citrus × vulgaris var. corniculata

Citrus × vulgaris var. corniculata, commonly known as the citron, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers and has a dense, evergreen foliage. The fruit is often used in cooking, traditional medicine, and as a source of essential oils.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other citrus varieties, particularly in its size and aroma.
