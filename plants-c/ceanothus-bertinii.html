Ceanothus bertinii

Ceanothus bertinii, commonly known as the California lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Ceanothus bertinii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife in its native habitat.
