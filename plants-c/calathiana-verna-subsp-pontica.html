Calathiana verna subsp. pontica

Calathiana verna subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
