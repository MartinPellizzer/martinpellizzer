Campanula rhomboidalis subvar. hispida

Campanula rhomboidalis subvar. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
