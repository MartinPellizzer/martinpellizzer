Convolvulus ruber

Convolvulus ruber, commonly known as red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it both a popular garden plant and a weed in agricultural settings.
