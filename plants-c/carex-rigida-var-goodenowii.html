Carex rigida var. goodenowii

Carex rigida var. goodenowii, commonly known as Goodenow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and densely packed. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle ornamental quality to its appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
