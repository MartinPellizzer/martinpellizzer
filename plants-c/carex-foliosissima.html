Carex foliosissima

Carex foliosissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
