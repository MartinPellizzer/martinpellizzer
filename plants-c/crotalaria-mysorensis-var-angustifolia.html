Crotalaria mysorensis var. angustifolia

Crotalaria mysorensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are typically narrow and ovate, with a distinctive pinnate structure. This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
