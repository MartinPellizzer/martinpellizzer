Chamaerops humilis var. macrocarpa

Chamaerops humilis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant date palm, is a variety of the dwarf date palm native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter. The plant produces edible dates, though it is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
