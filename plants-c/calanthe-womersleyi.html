Calanthe womersleyi

Calanthe womersleyi, commonly known as the Womersley's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with intricate patterns, and it prefers shady, moist environments in the undergrowth of forests.

Calanthe womersleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
