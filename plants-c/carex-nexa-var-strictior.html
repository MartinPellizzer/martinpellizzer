Carex nexa var. strictior

Carex nexa var. strictior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and streambanks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

The botanical features of Carex nexa var. strictior make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
