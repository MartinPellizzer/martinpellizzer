Cyperus ovatus

Cyperus ovatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps near water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus ovatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
