Crassula sarcocaulis subsp. rupicola

Crassula sarcocaulis subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette formation.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
