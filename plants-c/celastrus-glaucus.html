Celastrus glaucus

Celastrus glaucus, commonly known as the white bittersweet, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.
