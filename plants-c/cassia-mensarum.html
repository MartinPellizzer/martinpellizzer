Cassia mensarum

Cassia mensarum, commonly known as the mountain cassia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, rectangular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cassia mensarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
