Caenopteris fabiana

Caenopteris fabiana, commonly known as the Fabiana huperzia, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Caenopteris fabiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
