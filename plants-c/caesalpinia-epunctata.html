Caesalpinia epunctata

Caesalpinia epunctata, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered red flowers. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for use in gardens and landscapes where its vibrant floral display is desired.
