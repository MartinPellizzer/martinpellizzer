Chaetoseris roborowskii

Chaetoseris roborowskii, commonly known as the Roborowskii's hairyhead, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
