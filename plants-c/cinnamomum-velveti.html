Cinnamomum velveti

Cinnamomum velveti, commonly known as the velvet cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety bark, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
