Centaurea × andresiana

Centaurea × andresiana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk, which is typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea × andresiana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including gardens and wildflower meadows.
