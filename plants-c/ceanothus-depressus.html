Ceanothus depressus

Ceanothus depressus, commonly known as the low ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal and inland scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Ceanothus depressus is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
