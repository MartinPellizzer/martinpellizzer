Chaetogastra asperifolia

Chaetogastra asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved chaetogastra, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctively rugged surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in specialized habitats within its native range.
