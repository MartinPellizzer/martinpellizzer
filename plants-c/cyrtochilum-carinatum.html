Cyrtochilum carinatum

Cyrtochilum carinatum, commonly known as the ridge-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It features a compact, low-growing plant with a single, fleshy pseudobulb that supports a pair of narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive ridge-like structure on the lip. It thrives in shady, moist environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral characteristics.
