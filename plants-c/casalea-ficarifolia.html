Casalea ficarifolia

Casalea ficarifolia, commonly known as the "crown of thorns," is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
