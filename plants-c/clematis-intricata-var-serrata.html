Clematis intricata var. serrata

Clematis intricata var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

Clematis intricata var. serrata is relatively hardy but may require protection in colder climates.
