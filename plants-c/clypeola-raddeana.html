Clypeola raddeana

Clypeola raddeana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, with five spreading lobes that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clypeola raddeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its resilience and beauty.
