Carex odontostoma var. variegata

Carex odontostoma var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated saw-sedge, is a perennial sedge native to parts of Asia.

It features grass-like leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern, often displaying white or yellow stripes along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
