Chenopodium acutifolium

Chenopodium acutifolium, commonly known as sharpleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and coastal dunes.

Chenopodium acutifolium is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
