Crataegus monogyna subsp. jacquinii

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

jacquinii, commonly known as the common hawthorn or Jacquin's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
