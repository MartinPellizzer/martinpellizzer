Cynara cyrenaica

Cynara cyrenaica, commonly known as the artichoke thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, and the plant produces a edible heart that is harvested before the flowers open.

Cynara cyrenaica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
