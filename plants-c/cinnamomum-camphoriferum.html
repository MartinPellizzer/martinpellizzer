Cinnamomum camphoriferum

Cinnamomum camphoriferum, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, camphor-containing wood, which has been historically valued for its medicinal and commercial uses. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
