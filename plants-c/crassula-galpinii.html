Crassula galpinii

Crassula galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
