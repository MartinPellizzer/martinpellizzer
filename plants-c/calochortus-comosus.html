Calochortus comosus

Calochortus comosus, commonly known as the starry glory-of-the-snow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Calochortus.

It is native to California and Baja California, where it grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically blooms in early spring.

Calochortus comosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
