Callicarpa villosa

Callicarpa villosa, commonly known as the hairy beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.
