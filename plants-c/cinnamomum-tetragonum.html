Cinnamomum tetragonum

Cinnamomum tetragonum, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum bark, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is the primary source of its commercial value. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a fragrant, slightly medicinal scent. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries.

It is often cultivated for its essential oils and traditional medicinal uses.
