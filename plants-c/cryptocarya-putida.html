Cryptocarya putida

Cryptocarya putida, commonly known as the New Zealand honeysuckle, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a dark blue, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Cryptocarya putida is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
