Cotoneaster melanocarpus subsp. moravicus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus subsp.

moravicus, commonly known as the Moravian black-fruited cotoneaster, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn reddish in autumn, adding ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its winter interest and adaptability to various garden conditions.
