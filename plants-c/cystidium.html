Cystidium

Cystidium, commonly known as the "cystidium plant," is a unique type of plant structure found in certain species of algae and fungi.

It refers to a specialized cell or group of cells that form a protective covering or a specialized structure. These cystidia are often involved in defense mechanisms, such as trapping prey or deterring herbivores. They can be found in various forms, including elongated or branched structures.

The presence and morphology of cystidia are important characteristics used in the classification of certain plant-like organisms.
