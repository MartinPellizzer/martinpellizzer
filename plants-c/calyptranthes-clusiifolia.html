Calyptranthes clusiifolia

Calyptranthes clusiifolia, commonly known as the glossy leaf tree, is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often glossy and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
