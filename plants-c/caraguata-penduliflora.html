Caraguata penduliflora

Caraguata penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets arranged in an alternate pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Caraguata penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
