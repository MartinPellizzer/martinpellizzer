Choananthus cyrtanthiflorus

Choananthus cyrtanthiflorus, commonly known as the mountain glory-of-the-snow, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
