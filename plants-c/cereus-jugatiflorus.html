Cereus jugatiflorus

Cereus jugatiflorus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours.

Cereus jugatiflorus is often cultivated for its striking floral display and unique blooming habit.
