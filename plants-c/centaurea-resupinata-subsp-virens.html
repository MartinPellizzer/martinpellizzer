Centaurea resupinata subsp. virens

Centaurea resupinata subsp.

virens, commonly known as the greenleaf sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features erect, branched stems and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its greenish hue in the foliage, distinguishing it from other centaurea species.
