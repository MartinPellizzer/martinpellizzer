Colutea crocea

Colutea crocea, commonly known as the crimson balm or red clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Colutea crocea is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
