Centaurea virgata subsp. squarrosa

Centaurea virgata subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a prominent central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
