Carex lutensis

Carex lutensis, commonly known as the Lutens sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist or waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex lutensis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
