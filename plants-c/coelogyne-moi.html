Coelogyne moi

Coelogyne moi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Coelogyne moi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
