Cirsium shimae

Cirsium shimae, commonly known as the Shimane thistle, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Cirsium shimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
