Cephalanthus occidentalis f. angustifolia

Cephalanthus occidentalis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepper bush, is a variant of the common sweet pepper bush.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
