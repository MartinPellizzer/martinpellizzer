Cyperus tenuispiculatus var. pseudatterimus

Cyperus tenuispiculatus var. pseudatterimus, commonly known as the narrow-bracted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedge plants characterized by their triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense colonies in shallow water or moist soil. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

The species is notable for its slender, spicate appearance and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
