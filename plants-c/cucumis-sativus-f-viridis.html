Cucumis sativus f. viridis

Cucumis sativus f. viridis, commonly known as the green cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruits that are typically green in color and have a mild, crisp texture. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces small yellow flowers and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

This variety is valued for its use in salads and as a decorative element in gardens.
