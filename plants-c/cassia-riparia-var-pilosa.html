Cassia riparia var. pilosa

Cassia riparia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and long, drooping panicles of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pods containing flat, rectangular seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
