Costus crispiflorus

Costus crispiflorus, commonly known as the curlyflower costus, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy, white to pale yellow bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Costus crispiflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
