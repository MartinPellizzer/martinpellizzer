Chamaedorea andreana

Chamaedorea andreana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil and indirect light. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Chamaedorea andreana is also valued for its ability to thrive indoors, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
