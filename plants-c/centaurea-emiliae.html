Centaurea emiliae

Centaurea emiliae, commonly known as Emilia's centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Centaurea emiliae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
