Crataegus simulans

Crataegus simulans, commonly known as the mock thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy spring blooms and autumn foliage.
