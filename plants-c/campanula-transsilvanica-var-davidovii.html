Campanula transsilvanica var. davidovii

Campanula transsilvanica var. davidovii, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and meadows, where it contributes to the floral diversity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
