Cuphea roseana

Cuphea roseana, commonly known as the roseate whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant rose-pink to red flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea roseana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
