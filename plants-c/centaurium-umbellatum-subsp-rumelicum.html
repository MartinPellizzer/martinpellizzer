Centaurium umbellatum subsp. rumelicum

Centaurium umbellatum subsp.

rumelicum, commonly known as the alpine eyebright, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in a single umbel.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
