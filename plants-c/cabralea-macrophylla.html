Cabralea macrophylla

Cabralea macrophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or cabralea, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and is known for its slow growth rate. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
