Chironia baccifera var. grandiflora

Chironia baccifera var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-fruited chironia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
