Convolvulus leucochnous

Convolvulus leucochnous, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, often growing along rocky soils or in disturbed areas. It produces small, round berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals or through wind.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
