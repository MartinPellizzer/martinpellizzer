Cereus speciosissimus var. maurantianus

Cereus speciosissimus var. maurantianus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
