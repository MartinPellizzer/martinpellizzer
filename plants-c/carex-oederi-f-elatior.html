Carex oederi f. elatior

Carex oederi f. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it blooms in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This ornamental plant is valued for its upright growth habit and ability to provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
