Cyperus atropurpureus

Cyperus atropurpureus, commonly known as purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically green or purple in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking purple foliage, making it a popular choice for water gardens and landscape design.
