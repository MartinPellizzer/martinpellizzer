Convolvulus bufalinus

Convolvulus bufalinus, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
