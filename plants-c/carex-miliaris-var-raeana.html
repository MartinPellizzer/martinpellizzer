Carex miliaris var. raeana

Carex miliaris var. raeana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
