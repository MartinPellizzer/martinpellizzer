Centaurea penicillata

Centaurea penicillata, commonly known as the needlestem globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
