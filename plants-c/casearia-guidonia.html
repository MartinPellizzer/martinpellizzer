Casearia guidonia

Casearia guidonia, commonly known as the red-leaf casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant during the cooler months. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
