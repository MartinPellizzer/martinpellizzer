Carex linnaeana

Carex linnaeana, commonly known as Linnaean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Carex linnaeana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
