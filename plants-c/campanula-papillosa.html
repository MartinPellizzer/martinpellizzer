Campanula papillosa

Campanula papillosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are usually hairy and serrated, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
