Chassalia caudifolia

Chassalia caudifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chassalia caudifolia is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
