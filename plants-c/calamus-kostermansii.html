Calamus kostermansii

Calamus kostermansii, commonly known as the Kostermans' canes, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, followed by dark blue berries. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Calamus kostermansii thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland forests.
