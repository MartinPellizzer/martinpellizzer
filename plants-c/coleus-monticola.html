Coleus monticola

Coleus monticola, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus monticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
