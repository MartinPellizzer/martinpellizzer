Caladium smaragdinum

Caladium smaragdinum, commonly known as the Emerald Queen, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a yellow margin. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and indoor spaces.

Caladium smaragdinum is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 2 feet tall, making it a popular choice for adding color and texture to landscapes.
