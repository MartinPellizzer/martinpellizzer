Calamagrostis reverdattoi

Calamagrostis reverdattoi, commonly known as the Italian reedgrass, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. The plant features narrow, blade-like leaves and produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its ornamental value in gardens.

Calamagrostis reverdattoi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance of waterlogged soils.
