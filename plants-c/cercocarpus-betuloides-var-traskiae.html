Cercocarpus betuloides var. traskiae

Cercocarpus betuloides var. traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, curling leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native plant communities.
