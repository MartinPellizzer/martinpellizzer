Cassia machaeriifolia

Cassia machaeriifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. The leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
