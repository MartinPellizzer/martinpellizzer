Crataegus monopyrena

Crataegus monopyrena, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally not consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

The Pyrenean hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
