Cephalophyllum vandermerwei

Cephalophyllum vandermerwei, commonly known as the "cup-shaped flower" or "cup plant," is a rare and unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette of fleshy, green leaves that form a cup-like structure at the center. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
