Convolvulus oxysepalus

Convolvulus oxysepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Africa. The plant features slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils, and its flowers are small, funnel-shaped, and usually pink or purple in color. The sepals are narrow and persistent, giving the plant its specific epithet.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
