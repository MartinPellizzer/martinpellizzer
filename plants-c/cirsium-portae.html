Cirsium portae

Cirsium portae, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple or pink, arranged in a dense cluster.

Cirsium portae is known for its hardy nature and is often found in coastal and arid environments.
