Cleistes vinosa

Cleistes vinosa, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, hooded shape. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Cleistes vinosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
