Ceropegia fimbriata

Ceropegia fimbriata, commonly known as the laceleaf string of hearts, is a tropical climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy edge, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
