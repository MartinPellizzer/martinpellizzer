Clusia angustifolia

Clusia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

Clusia angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
