Campanula cochleariifolia f. hauryi

Campanula cochleariifolia f. hauryi, commonly known as the Hauri's bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It features clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in gardens with well-drained soil.
