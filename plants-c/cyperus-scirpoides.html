Cyperus scirpoides

Cyperus scirpoides, commonly known as swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stems, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus scirpoides is known for its ability to tolerate standing water and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
