Carpinus viminea var. chiukiangensis

Carpinus viminea var. chiukiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spreading crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with doubly serrated margins, and turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to urban environments.
