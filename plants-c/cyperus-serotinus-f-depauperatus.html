Cyperus serotinus f. depauperatus

Cyperus serotinus f. depauperatus, commonly known as the sparse cyperus, is a variant of the common flatsedge.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its reduced foliage and slender, grass-like stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
