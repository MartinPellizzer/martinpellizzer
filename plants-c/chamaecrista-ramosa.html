Chamaecrista ramosa

Chamaecrista ramosa, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chamaecrista ramosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil health.
