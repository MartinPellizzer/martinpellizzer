Cichorium intybus

Cichorium intybus, commonly known as chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and its leaves, which can be either curly or smooth. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and the Mediterranean region. Chicory is known for its bitter taste and is often used in salads or as a coffee substitute.

It also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
