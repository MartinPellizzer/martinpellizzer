Calicotome intermedia

Calicotome intermedia, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorny branches.

Calicotome intermedia is often used for soil stabilization due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.
