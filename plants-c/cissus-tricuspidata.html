Cissus tricuspidata

Cissus tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed fig-leaf gooseberry, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a ground cover.

The leaves are also valued for their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their air-purifying qualities.
