Centaurea lyratifolia

Centaurea lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyreleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and its unique, lyre-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea lyratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
