Calea divergens

Calea divergens, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, nocturnal blooms that open at dusk. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a purple or violet hue. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calea divergens is often cultivated for its unique beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
