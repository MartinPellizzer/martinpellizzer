Cucumis naudinianus

Cucumis naudinianus, commonly known as the Naudin's cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruits are small, green cucumbers that can be harvested when immature for culinary use.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in horticultural cultivation.
