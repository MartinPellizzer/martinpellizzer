Calamagrostis nyingchiensis

Calamagrostis nyingchiensis, commonly known as the Nyingchi reed, is a perennial grass species native to the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces in China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern.

Calamagrostis nyingchiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
