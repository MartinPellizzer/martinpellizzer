Cinnamomum burmanni f. heyneanum

Cinnamomum burmanni f. heyneanum, commonly known as Burmann's cinnamon, is a botanical variant of the cinnamon tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is prized for its culinary and medicinal uses. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, with a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
