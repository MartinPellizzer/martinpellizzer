Chionanthus incurvifolius

Chionanthus incurvifolius, commonly known as the curlleaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, curling leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
