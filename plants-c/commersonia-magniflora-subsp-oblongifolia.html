Commersonia magniflora subsp. oblongifolia

Commersonia magniflora subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed commersonia, is a species native to southern South America. It is a shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
