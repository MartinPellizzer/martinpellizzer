Cousinia rawanduzensis

Cousinia rawanduzensis, commonly known as the Rawanduz thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features robust, hairy stems and narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that help it adapt to arid environments. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators.

Cousinia rawanduzensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
