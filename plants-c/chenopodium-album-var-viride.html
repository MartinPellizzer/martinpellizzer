Chenopodium album var. viride

Chenopodium album var. viride, commonly known as green lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional diets and herbal remedies.
