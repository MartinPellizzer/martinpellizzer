Chenopodium osbornianum

Chenopodium osbornianum, commonly known as Osborn's goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in dry, rocky areas. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture, and its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences. It produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Chenopodium osbornianum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
