Cryptanthus undulatus var. ruber

Cryptanthus undulatus var. ruber, commonly known as the red-stemmed winterbloom, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or orange, which bloom during the cooler months. Its compact size and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate light to flourish.
