Cocos paraguayensis

Cocos paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay coconut, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
