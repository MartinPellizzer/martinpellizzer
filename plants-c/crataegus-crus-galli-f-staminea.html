Crataegus crus-galli f. staminea

Crataegus crus-galli f. staminea, commonly known as the stamen-bearing hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which includes showy stamens that are often more prominent than the pistils. This form is typically found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ecological benefits.
