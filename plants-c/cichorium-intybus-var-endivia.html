Cichorium intybus var. endivia

Cichorium intybus var. endivia, commonly known as Belgian endive, is a leafy vegetable cultivated for its crisp, pale green leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, slightly bitter leaves that are often wrapped around a white, tender heart. The plant is typically grown in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are harvested before they become too fibrous, ensuring a tender texture.

This variety is prized for its unique flavor and is often used in salads, soups, and as a garnish.
