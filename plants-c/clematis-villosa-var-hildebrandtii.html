Clematis villosa var. hildebrandtii

Clematis villosa var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white to pale blue. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or trellises.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
