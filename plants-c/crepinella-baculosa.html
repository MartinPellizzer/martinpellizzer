Crepinella baculosa

Crepinella baculosa, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a sprawling habit, featuring small, fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its succulent foliage allows it to store water, adapting it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
