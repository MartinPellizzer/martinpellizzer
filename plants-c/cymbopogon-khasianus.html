Cymbopogon khasianus

Cymbopogon khasianus, commonly known as Himalayan lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or pale yellow. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
