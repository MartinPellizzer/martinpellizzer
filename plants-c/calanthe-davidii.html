Calanthe davidii

Calanthe davidii, commonly known as the David's calanthe, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a compact, rhizomatous growth habit with broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a raceme along the stem. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native regions.
