Callistemon shiressii

Callistemon shiressii, commonly known as the Crimson Bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red floral spikes that resemble bottle brushes, which are actually clusters of stamens. The plant produces small, white, egg-shaped flowers that develop into woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value throughout the year.
