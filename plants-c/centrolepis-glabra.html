Centrolepis glabra

Centrolepis glabra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centrolepis, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure and fleshy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
