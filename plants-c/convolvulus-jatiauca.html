Convolvulus jatiauca

Convolvulus jatiauca, commonly known as the Jatiauca morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is native to regions in South America, particularly Brazil. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a garden plant.
