Carex satsumensis var. nakaii

Carex satsumensis var. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai’s sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Nakai’s sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
