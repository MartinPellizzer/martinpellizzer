Copiapoa griseoviolacea

Copiapoa griseoviolacea, commonly known as the gray violet cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its pale gray to greenish stem and clusters of violet or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a dense, spiny exterior that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb water from infrequent rains.

Copiapoa griseoviolacea is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
