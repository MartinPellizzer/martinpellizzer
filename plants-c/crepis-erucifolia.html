Crepis erucifolia

Crepis erucifolia, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits, and has bracts that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Crepis erucifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
