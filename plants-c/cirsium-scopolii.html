Cirsium scopolii

Cirsium scopolii, commonly known as the Scopolii thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene fruits.

Cirsium scopolii is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas where it contributes to local biodiversity.
