Canavalia taiwaniana

Canavalia taiwaniana, commonly known as Taiwan bean or wild soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to various soil types.

Canavalia taiwaniana plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
