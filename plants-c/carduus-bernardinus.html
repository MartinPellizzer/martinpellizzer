Carduus bernardinus

Carduus bernardinus, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive member of the thistle genus.
