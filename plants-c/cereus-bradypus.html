Cereus bradypus

Cereus bradypus, commonly known as the slow-growing cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, a trait typical of many cacti adapted to desert environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Cereus bradypus is known for its slow growth rate and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
