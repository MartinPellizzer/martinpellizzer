Coutarea campanilla

Coutarea campanilla, commonly known as the bell-shaped coutarea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
