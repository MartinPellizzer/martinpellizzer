Callicarpa japonica f. taquetii

Callicarpa japonica f. taquetii, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
