Cereus kerberi

Cereus kerberi, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract pollinators. Its growth habit is typically solitary, with a tall, branching structure.

Cereus kerberi is well adapted to arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.
