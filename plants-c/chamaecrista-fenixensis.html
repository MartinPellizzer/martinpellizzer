Chamaecrista fenixensis

Chamaecrista fenixensis, commonly known as the Phoenix Crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive crown-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Chamaecrista fenixensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
