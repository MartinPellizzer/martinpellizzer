Carex atrata var. magellanica

Carex atrata var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
