Chenopodium album f. opuliforme

Chenopodium album f. opuliforme, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is a variant of the common lambsquarter.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and stout, branched stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its small greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

This plant is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
