Chamaecrypta diasciifolia

Chamaecrypta diasciifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaved cryptantha, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species with silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
