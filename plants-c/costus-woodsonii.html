Costus woodsonii

Costus woodsonii, commonly known as the Florida costus, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
