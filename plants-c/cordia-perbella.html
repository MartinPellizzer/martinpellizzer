Cordia perbella

Cordia perbella, commonly known as the evergreen cordia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
