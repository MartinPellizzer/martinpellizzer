Canarium trifoliolatum

Canarium trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaf canarium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard shell.

Canarium trifoliolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
