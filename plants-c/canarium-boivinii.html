Canarium boivinii

Canarium boivinii, commonly known as the Bornean candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its latex is milky and has been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Canarium boivinii is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
