Capsicum meianthum

Capsicum meianthum, commonly known as the white bell pepper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to Mexico and Central America. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.
