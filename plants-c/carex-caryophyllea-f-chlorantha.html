Carex caryophyllea f. chlorantha

Carex caryophyllea f. chlorantha, commonly known as the chloranthus sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or wet meadows. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are generally a bright green color.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.
