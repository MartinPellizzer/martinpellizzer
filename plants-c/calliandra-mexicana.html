Calliandra mexicana

Calliandra mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
