Calliandra hirsuta

Calliandra hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, bright red or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets that give it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its showy blooms.

Additionally, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
