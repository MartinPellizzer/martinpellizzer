Crassula intricata

Crassula intricata, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "Coral Necklace," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish hue, often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
