Carex pairae var. javanica

Carex pairae var. javanica, commonly known as the Java sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as they remain consistently moist.
