Centaurea cadmea subsp. pontica

Centaurea cadmea subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic centaurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and open woodlands.

The plant features upright, branched stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright red or purple, arranged in dense, cylindrical heads, which attract various pollinators.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display.
