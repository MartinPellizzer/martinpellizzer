Cousinia stenolepis

Cousinia stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its narrow, linear leaves are a distinguishing feature, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.

Cousinia stenolepis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in dry gardens.
