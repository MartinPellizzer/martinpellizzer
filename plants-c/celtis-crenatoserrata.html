Celtis crenatoserrata

Celtis crenatoserrata, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with irregularly serrated margins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.

Celtis crenatoserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
