Cnicus hookerianus

Cnicus hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features a robust, spiny stem with dark green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cnicus hookerianus is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
