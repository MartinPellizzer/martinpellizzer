Crataegus amelanchier

Crataegus amelanchier, commonly known as the serviceberry or Juneberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, often turning red or purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in younger trees.

Crataegus amelanchier is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
