Citrus × aurantium var. sinensis

Citrus × aurantium var. sinensis, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the Rutaceae family, characterized by its small, rounded, and typically easy-to-peel fruit. The fruit has a thin, loose rind that separates easily from the segmented pulp, which is juicy and sweet. It contains a high concentration of vitamin C and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant flowers and ornamental appeal.
