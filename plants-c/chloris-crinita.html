Chloris crinita

Chloris crinita, commonly known as silky grass or silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky-textured leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland margins.

Chloris crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its visual texture and low maintenance requirements.
