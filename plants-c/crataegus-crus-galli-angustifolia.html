Crataegus crus-galli angustifolia

Crataegus crus-galli angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, and small white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
