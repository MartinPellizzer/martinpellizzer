Calathea eburnea

Calathea eburnea, commonly known as the White Vein Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves with intricate white veins that create a shimmering effect. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea eburnea is known for its sensitivity to temperature and humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
