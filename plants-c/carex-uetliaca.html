Carex uetliaca

Carex uetliaca, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex uetliaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
