Chamaesyce peplis

Chamaesyce peplis, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, pale green to blue-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, blooming in spring.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
