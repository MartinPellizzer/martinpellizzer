Centaurea castellana var. asperrima

Centaurea castellana var. asperrima, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
