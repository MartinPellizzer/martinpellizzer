Cestrum hassleri

Cestrum hassleri, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum hassleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
