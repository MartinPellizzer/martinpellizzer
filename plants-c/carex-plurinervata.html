Carex plurinervata

Carex plurinervata, commonly known as the many-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers usually separated on different spikes.

Carex plurinervata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
