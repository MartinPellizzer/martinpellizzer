Cyperus subbulbosus

Cyperus subbulbosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Cyperus subbulbosus is often found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
