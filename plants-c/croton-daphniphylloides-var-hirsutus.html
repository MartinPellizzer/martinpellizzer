Croton daphniphylloides var. hirsutus

Croton daphniphylloides var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves.

The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically hairy, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
