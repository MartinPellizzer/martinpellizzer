Carex obtusata f. bracteata

Carex obtusata f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a glossy appearance, and the inflorescence consists of male and female flowers arranged in separate spikes.

This variety is distinguished by its prominent bracts that enclose the female flowers, adding to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
