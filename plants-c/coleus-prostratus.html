Coleus prostratus

Coleus prostratus, commonly known as creeping coleus, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features vibrant, showy foliage with leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Coleus prostratus is often used in ground covers or as a border plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
