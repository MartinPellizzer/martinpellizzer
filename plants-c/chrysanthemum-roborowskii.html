Chrysanthemum roborowskii

Chrysanthemum roborowskii, commonly known as the Siberian chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
