Cyclopeltis Cumingiana

Cuming's cyclopeltis, scientifically known as Cyclopeltis cumingiana, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. This delicate plant is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped fronds that distinguish it from other ferns in the family. Despite its striking appearance, Cuming's cyclopeltis remains poorly understood due to its limited distribution and elusive habitat. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this vulnerable species from threats such as habitat loss and climate change. Its study offers valuable insights into the biodiversity and ecological dynamics of its native environment.
