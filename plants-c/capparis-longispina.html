Capparis longispina

Capparis longispina, commonly known as the long-spined caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, greenish fruits. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, white pubescence, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Capparis longispina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
