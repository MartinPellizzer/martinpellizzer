Centropogon macrophyllus var. congestus

Centropogon macrophyllus var. congestus, commonly known as the clustered centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or swampy habitats.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by small insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
