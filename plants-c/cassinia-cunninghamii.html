Cassinia cunninghamii

Cassinia cunninghamii, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spherical, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.
