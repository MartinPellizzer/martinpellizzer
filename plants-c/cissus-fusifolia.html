Cissus fusifolia

Cissus fusifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily.

Cissus fusifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or walls.
