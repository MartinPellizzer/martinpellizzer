Cephalocereus catingicola

Cephalocereus catingicola, commonly known as the "Cerro de la Muerte" or "Death Mountain cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a dense, spiny exterior that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This cactus is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
