Calea chodatii var. intermedia

Calea chodatii var. intermedia, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence composed of dense, white to pale blue ray florets. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable ornamental and traditional plant in its native habitat.
