Cassia browniana

Cassia browniana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia browniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
