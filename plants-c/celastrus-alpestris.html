Celastrus alpestris

Celastrus alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine staffviolet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Celastrus alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
