Clematis urticifolia f. albiflora

Clematis urticifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflowered stinging clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its stems and leaves contain irritants, such as calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause skin irritation.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
