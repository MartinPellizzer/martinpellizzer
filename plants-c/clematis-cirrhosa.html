Clematis cirrhosa

Clematis cirrhosa, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis cirrhosa is also valued for its ability to climb trellises and structures, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
