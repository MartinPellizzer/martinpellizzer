Cirsium tioganum var. coloradense

Cirsium tioganum var. coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
