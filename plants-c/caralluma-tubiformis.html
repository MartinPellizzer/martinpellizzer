Caralluma tubiformis

Caralluma tubiformis, commonly known as the horned devil or Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems that resemble horns, which help it store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
