Cedrus libani var. pendula-sargentii

Cedrus libani var. pendula-sargentii, commonly known as the Lebanese cedar weeping form, is a unique variant of the Lebanon cedar, characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a narrow, pyramidal shape when mature. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and dense, providing a lush appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This cultivar is prized for its distinctive form and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
