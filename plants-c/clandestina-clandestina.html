Clandestina clandestina

Clandestina clandestina, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying solely on its roots for sustenance. The plant produces delicate, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in specific, moist, acidic pine rockland habitats, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is listed as endangered in several states.
