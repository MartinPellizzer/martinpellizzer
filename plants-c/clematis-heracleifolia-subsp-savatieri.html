Clematis heracleifolia subsp. savatieri

Clematis heracleifolia subsp.

savatieri, commonly known as the Persian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or trellises. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

However, it requires careful pruning to maintain its shape and encourage flowering.
