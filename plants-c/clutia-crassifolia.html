Clutia crassifolia

Clutia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved clutia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clutia crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
