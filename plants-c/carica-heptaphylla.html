Carica heptaphylla

Carica heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with seven prominent veins. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to other papaya species but are less commonly cultivated. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is edible and contains enzymes that aid in digestion, similar to other papaya varieties.
