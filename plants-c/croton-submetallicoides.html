Croton submetallicoides

Croton submetallicoides, commonly known as the metallic croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, metallic-green leaves that shimmer in sunlight. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique coloration and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
