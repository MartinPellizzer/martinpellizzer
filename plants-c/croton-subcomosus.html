Croton subcomosus

Croton subcomosus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of yellow, red, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton subcomosus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
