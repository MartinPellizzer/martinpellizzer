Cousinia odontolepis subsp. kurdica

Cousinia odontolepis subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish toothbrush, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
