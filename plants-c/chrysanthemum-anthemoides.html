Chrysanthemum anthemoides

Chrysanthemum anthemoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a compact growth habit.
