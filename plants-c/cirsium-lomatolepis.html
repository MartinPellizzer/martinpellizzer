Cirsium lomatolepis

Cirsium lomatolepis, commonly known as the crested thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
