Crucifera edentula

Crucifera edentula, commonly known as the toothless mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

This plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated edge, though the species name "edentula" refers to the absence of teeth-like structures on the leaf margins. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator species for certain soil conditions.
