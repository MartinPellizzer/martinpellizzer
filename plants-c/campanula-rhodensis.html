Campanula rhodensis

Campanula rhodensis, commonly known as the Rhodian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its charming, bell-like blooms.
