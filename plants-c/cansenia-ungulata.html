Cansenia ungulata

Cansenia ungulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed canescent, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Cansenia ungulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
