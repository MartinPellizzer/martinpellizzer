Cerastium arvense subsp. arvense

Cerastium arvense subsp.

arvense, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically forms dense mats of small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.
