Callipteris rivalis

Callipteris rivalis, commonly known as the Florida windmill palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, windmill-like appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy soils along coastal areas and is well adapted to salt spray and wind. It has a single trunk that can reach heights of up to 15 feet.

Callipteris rivalis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
