Crossandra gabonica

Crossandra gabonica, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Crossandra gabonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
