Cirsium darwasicum

Cirsium darwasicum, commonly known as the Darwassian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, prickly hairs, while its flowers are usually purple or pink in color.

Cirsium darwasicum plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
