Cassia venulosa

Cassia venulosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. Cassia venulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
