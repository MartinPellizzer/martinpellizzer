Cucumella cinerea

Cucumella cinerea, commonly known as the gray cucumber.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its vine-like growth habit and distinctive grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.
