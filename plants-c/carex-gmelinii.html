Carex gmelinii

Carex gmelinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex gmelinii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
