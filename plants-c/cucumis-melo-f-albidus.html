Cucumis melo f. albidus

Cucumis melo f. albidus, commonly known as the white muskmelon, is a botanical variant of the muskmelon species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, with lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and flavor, making it a popular choice in specialty gardening and markets.
