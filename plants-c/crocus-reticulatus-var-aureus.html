Crocus reticulatus var. aureus

Crocus reticulatus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
