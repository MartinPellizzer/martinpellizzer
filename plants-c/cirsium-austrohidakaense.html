Cirsium austrohidakaense

Cirsium austrohidakaense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It has a rosette of basal leaves and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cirsium austrohidakaense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
