Clerodendrum wallichii

Clerodendrum wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan flame of the woods, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
