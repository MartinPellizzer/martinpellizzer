Cereus grandiflorus var. maximiliani

Cereus grandiflorus var. maximiliani, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering cactus characterized by its segmented, branching stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically red, orange, or yellow, with five petals that open in the evening.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.
