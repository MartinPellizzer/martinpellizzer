Chrysosplenium komarovii

Chrysosplenium komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's golden spleenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly in China, Korea, and Japan. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
