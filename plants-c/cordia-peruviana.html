Cordia peruviana

Cordia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian allspice or red cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Cordia peruviana is also valued for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
