Cleome tenuicaulis

Cleome tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Cleome tenuicaulis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
