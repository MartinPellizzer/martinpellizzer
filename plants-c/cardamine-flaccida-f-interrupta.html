Cardamine flaccida f. interrupta

Cardamine flaccida f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted watercress, is a botanical variant of the watercress species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, alternate leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by tiny, elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils along streams, ponds, and wetlands.

This fern-like plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
