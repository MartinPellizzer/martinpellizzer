Caustis flexuosa

Caustis flexuosa, commonly known as the spiral aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The spiral aloe is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
