Carex cruciata var. argocarpus

Carex cruciata var. argocarpus, commonly known as the redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, dark green leaves that have a distinctive reddish hue along the midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
