Crotalaria serengetiana

Crotalaria serengetiana, commonly known as the Serengeti cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can be toxic to livestock if consumed in large quantities.
