Chamaelirium viridiflorum

Chamaelirium viridiflorum, commonly known as greenflower larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include showy, spurred petals and a distinctive seed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
