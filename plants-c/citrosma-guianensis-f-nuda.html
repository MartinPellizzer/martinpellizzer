Citrosma guianensis f. nuda

Citrosma guianensis f. nuda, commonly known as the naked citrosma, is a variant of the citrosma genus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fruit that resembles a capsule. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
