Cyperus kipasensis

Cyperus kipasensis, commonly known as the Kipasensis flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus kipasensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
