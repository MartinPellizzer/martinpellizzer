Ceropegia floribundior var. mlimakito

Ceropegia floribundior var. mlimakito, commonly known as the Mlimakito moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often variegated with silver and green hues. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with intricate patterns resembling lace. It thrives in well-draining soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation or as a trailing plant.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
