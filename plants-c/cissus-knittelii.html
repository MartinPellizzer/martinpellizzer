Cissus knittelii

Cissus knittelii, commonly known as the Knittel's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are often covered with fine, white pubescence, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Cissus knittelii is adapted to thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
