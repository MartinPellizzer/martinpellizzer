Coccoloba barbadensis var. mexicana

Coccoloba barbadensis var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and berries.
