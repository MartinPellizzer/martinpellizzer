Coccinia sessilifolia var. variifolia

Coccinia sessilifolia var. variifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf cucumber gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, elongated fruits with a mild, sweet flavor. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a system of fasciculate roots.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its unique appearance and versatile use.
