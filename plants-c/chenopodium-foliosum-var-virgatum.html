Chenopodium foliosum var. virgatum

Chenopodium foliosum var. virgatum, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The leaves are typically bluish-green in color, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
