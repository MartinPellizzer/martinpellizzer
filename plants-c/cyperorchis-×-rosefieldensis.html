Cyperorchis × rosefieldensis

Cyperorchis × rosefieldensis, commonly known as the Rosefield marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas with rich, moist soil.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
