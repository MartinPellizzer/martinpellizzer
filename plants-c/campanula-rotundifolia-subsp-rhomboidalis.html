Campanula rotundifolia subsp. rhomboidalis

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

rhomboidalis, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rhomboidal-shaped leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology compared to other members of the Campanula genus.
