Crataegus lettermanii

Crataegus lettermanii, commonly known as the Letterman hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
