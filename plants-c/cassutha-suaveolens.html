Cassutha suaveolens

Cassutha suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cassutha suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
