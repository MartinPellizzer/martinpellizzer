Chamaefistula lanata

Chamaefistula lanata, commonly known as the woolly apple tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its flowers are usually pale green or white and appear in clusters.

Chamaefistula lanata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and seasonal interest.
