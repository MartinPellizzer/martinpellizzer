Canarium patentinervium

Canarium patentinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined canarium, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves with broad, parallel venation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
