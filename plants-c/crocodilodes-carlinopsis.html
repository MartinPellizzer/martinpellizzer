Crocodilodes carlinopsis

Crocodilodes carlinopsis, commonly known as the Carlin's crocodile orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble the scales of a crocodile, giving it its distinctive common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized roots that form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
