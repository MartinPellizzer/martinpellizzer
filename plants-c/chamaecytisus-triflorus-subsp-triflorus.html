Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp. triflorus

Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp.

triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters of three.

The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
