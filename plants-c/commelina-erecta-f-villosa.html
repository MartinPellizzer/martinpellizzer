Commelina erecta f. villosa

Commelina erecta f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its specific epithet "villosa.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
