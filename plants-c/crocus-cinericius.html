Crocus cinericius

Crocus cinericius, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. The plant blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies, though its medicinal properties remain under study.
