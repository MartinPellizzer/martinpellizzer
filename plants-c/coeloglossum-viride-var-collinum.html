Coeloglossum viride var. collinum

Coeloglossum viride var. collinum, commonly known as the green hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a dense cluster of disk florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are upright and branching.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
