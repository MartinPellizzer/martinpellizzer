Campium subcordatum

Campium subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive heart-shaped base, and they are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Campium subcordatum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
