Centaurea montaltensis

Centaurea montaltensis, commonly known as the Montalt's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Centaurea montaltensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
