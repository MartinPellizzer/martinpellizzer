Celosia persicaria

Celosia persicaria, commonly known as the flame flower or cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky inflorescences that resemble combs or flames. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, pink, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Celosia persicaria thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is often cultivated for its decorative value in gardens and floral arrangements.
