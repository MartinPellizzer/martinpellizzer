Calochortus barbatus var. chihuahuanus

Calochortus barbatus var. chihuahuanus, commonly known as the Chihuahuan rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, often blooming after rainfall. Its bulbs are an essential part of its life cycle, storing nutrients for periods of drought.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
