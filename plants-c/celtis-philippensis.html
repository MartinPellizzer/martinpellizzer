Celtis philippensis

Celtis philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine boxwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cannaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn black when mature.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
