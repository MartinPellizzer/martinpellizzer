Cinnamomum hammelianum

Cinnamomum hammelianum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used to make the spice cinnamon. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, appearing in clusters, and it produces oval, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
