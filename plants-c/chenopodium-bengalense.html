Chenopodium bengalense

Chenopodium bengalense, commonly known as Indian goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and alternate, broadly ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-lobed capsules. Chenopodium bengalense is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some regions.
