Calamagrostis lanceolata var. armena

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. armena, commonly known as Armenian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender, arching blades that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces feathery plumes in late summer, which give it a distinctive ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
