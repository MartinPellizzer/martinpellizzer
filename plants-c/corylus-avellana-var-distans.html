Corylus avellana var. distans

Corylus avellana var. distans, commonly known as the European hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm long and composed of 5–9 oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish male flowers and female flowers that develop into edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its nuts, which are used in food, cosmetics, and traditional medicine.
