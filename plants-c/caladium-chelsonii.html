Caladium chelsonii

Caladium chelsonii, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against bright green or white backgrounds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are not typically noticeable.

Caladium chelsonii is often cultivated for its vibrant leaf coloration and is used in landscape design to add visual interest to gardens and indoor spaces.
