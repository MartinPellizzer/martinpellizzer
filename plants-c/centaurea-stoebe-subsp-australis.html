Centaurea stoebe subsp. australis

Centaurea stoebe subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered with stiff, hairy leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in composite flower heads, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and extensive.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
