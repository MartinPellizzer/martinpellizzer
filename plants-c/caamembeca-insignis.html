Caamembeca insignis

Caamembeca insignis, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits and hardy nature.
