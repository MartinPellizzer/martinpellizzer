Capsicum baccatum var. praetermissum

Capsicum baccatum var. praetermissum, commonly known as the "Peruvian purple" or "Purple Beauty" pepper, is a wild variety native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, conical fruits that range in color from deep purple to red when mature. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it produces small, yellow-green fruits that turn vibrant purple as they ripen.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional Andean cuisine and herbal remedies.
