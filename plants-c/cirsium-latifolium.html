Cirsium latifolium

Cirsium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a robust stem with spiny leaves that are broad and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish-blue flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by a series of bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and a waxy coating.

Cirsium latifolium is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
