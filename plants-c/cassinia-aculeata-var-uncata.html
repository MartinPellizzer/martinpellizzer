Cassinia aculeata var. uncata

Cassinia aculeata var. uncata, commonly known as the rough corymbia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often glandular, and it produces small, woody fruits called follicles. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some horticultural settings.
