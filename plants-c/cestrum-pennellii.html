Cestrum pennellii

Cestrum pennellii, commonly known as night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters along the stems.
