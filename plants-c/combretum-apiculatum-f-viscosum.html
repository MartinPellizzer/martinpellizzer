Combretum apiculatum f. viscosum

Combretum apiculatum f. viscosum, commonly known as the sticky combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
