Croton digitatus

Croton digitatus, commonly known as the fingerleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Croton digitatus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
