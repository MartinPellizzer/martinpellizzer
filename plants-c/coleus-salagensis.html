Coleus salagensis

Coleus salagensis, commonly known as the Salagon Island coleus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Salagon in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.
