Cyperus consanguineus var. usterii

Cyperus consanguineus var. usterii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
