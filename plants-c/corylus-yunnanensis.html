Corylus yunnanensis

Corylus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan hazelnut, is a species of hazelnut native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions. The plant produces oval, woody nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its edible nuts and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
