Crocus vernus var. pallens

Crocus vernus var. pallens, commonly known as the pale crocus, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas and gardens. It blooms in early spring, usually around March or April, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The pale crocus is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.
