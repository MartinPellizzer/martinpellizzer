Citronella ilicifolia

Citronella ilicifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal teas and aromatherapy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its essential oils are also used in natural insect repellents due to their strong citrus aroma.
