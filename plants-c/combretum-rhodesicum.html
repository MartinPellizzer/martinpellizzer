Combretum rhodesicum

Combretum rhodesicum, commonly known as Rhodes' combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that split open to release seeds. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
