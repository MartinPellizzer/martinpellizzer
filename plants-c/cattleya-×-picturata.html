Cattleya × picturata

Cattleya × picturata, commonly known as the picturata orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns resembling brushstrokes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers bloom in spring and are prized for their beauty and fragrance in horticultural settings.
