Chaetocyperus bonariensis

Chaetocyperus bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
