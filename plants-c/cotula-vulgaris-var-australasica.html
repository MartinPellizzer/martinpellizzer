Cotula vulgaris var. australasica

Cotula vulgaris var. australasica, commonly known as the Australian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
