Calystegia macrostegia subsp. cyclostegia

Calystegia macrostegia subsp.

cyclostegia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bindweed, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure at the base. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
