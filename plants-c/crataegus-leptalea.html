Crataegus leptalea

Crataegus leptalea, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus leptalea thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
