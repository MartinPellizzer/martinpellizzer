Carthamus mareoticus

Carthamus mareoticus, commonly known as the Egyptian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Valley.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces large, oblong seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its growth habit is annual, and it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Carthamus mareoticus make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
