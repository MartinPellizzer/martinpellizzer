Callicarpa longivillosa

Callicarpa longivillosa, commonly known as the velvet beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-purple berries that turn red when ripe. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa longivillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
