Crambe arborea var. indivisa

Crambe arborea var. indivisa, commonly known as the tree crambe, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often lobed or divided. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, seed-filled pods. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in phytoremediation, it has attracted interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
