Carex brizoides f. brunnescens

Carex brizoides f. brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brownish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
