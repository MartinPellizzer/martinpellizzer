Cordia wallichii

Cordia wallichii, commonly known as the Indian snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Cordia wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
