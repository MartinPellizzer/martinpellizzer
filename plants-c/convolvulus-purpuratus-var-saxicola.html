Convolvulus purpuratus var. saxicola

Convolvulus purpuratus var. saxicola, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
