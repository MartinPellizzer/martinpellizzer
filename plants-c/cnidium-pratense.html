Cnidium pratense

Cnidium pratense, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Cnidium pratense can grow up to 2 meters tall and is known for its distinctive, often irritating sap.
