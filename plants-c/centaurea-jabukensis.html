Centaurea jabukensis

Centaurea jabukensis, commonly known as the Jabukovac thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Centaurea jabukensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
