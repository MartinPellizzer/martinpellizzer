Clematis patens var. revoluta

Clematis patens var. revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vigorous climbing habit.
