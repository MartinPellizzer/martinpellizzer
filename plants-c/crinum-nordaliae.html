Crinum nordaliae

Crinum nordaliae, commonly known as the Northern Crinum, is a bulbous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are often evergreen, providing a lush foliage base. Crinum nordaliae typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
