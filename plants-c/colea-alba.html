Colea alba

Colea alba, commonly known as white coleus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Colea alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
