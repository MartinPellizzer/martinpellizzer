Clematis triloba

Clematis triloba, commonly known as the fragrant clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its climbing habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
