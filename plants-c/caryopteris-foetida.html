Caryopteris foetida

Caryopteris foetida, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Caryopteris foetida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
