Cleome denticulata

Cleome denticulata, commonly known as the cutleaf cleome, is an annual flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome denticulata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
