Cistus × stenophyllus f. guttatus

Cistus × stenophyllus f. guttatus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often with a resinous scent.

This species is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
