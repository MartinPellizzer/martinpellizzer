Clematis parviloba subsp. pierotii

Clematis parviloba subsp.

pierotii, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States. It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
