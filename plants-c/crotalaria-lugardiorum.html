Crotalaria lugardiorum

Crotalaria lugardiorum, commonly known as the African spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
