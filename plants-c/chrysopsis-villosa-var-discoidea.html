Chrysopsis villosa var. discoidea

Chrysopsis villosa var. discoidea, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
