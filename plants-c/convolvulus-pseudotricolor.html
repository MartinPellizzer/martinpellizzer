Convolvulus pseudotricolor

Convolvulus pseudotricolor, commonly known as the false blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is native to regions in the Mediterranean and parts of Europe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and attractive blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in some environments.
