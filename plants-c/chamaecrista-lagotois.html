Chamaecrista lagotois

Chamaecrista lagotois, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. It typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
