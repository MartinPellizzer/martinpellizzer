Cnemidostachys ditassoides

Cnemidostachys ditassoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
