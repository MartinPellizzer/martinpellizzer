Cordylanthus filifolius

Cordylanthus filifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf filifolius, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Cordylanthus filifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
