Campanula rotundifolia proles ruscinonensis

Campanula rotundifolia proles ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its round, heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The subspecies proles ruscinonensis is distinguished by specific morphological traits, including flower size and leaf shape, which set it apart from other campanula species.
