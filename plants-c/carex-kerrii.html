Carex kerrii

Carex kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex kerrii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
