Clematis triloba var. guaranitica

Clematis triloba var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, often with a hairy texture, and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
