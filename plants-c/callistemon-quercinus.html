Callistemon quercinus

Callistemon quercinus, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds.

The tree is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
