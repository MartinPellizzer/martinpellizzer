Couepia caryophylloides subsp. caryophylloides

Couepia caryophylloides subsp.

caryophylloides, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is a climbing vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
