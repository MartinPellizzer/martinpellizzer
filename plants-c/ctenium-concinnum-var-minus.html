Ctenium concinnum var. minus

Ctenium concinnum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser ctenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically features slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

The foliage is often used in traditional herbal practices for its mild aromatic properties.
