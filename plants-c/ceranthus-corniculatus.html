Ceranthus corniculatus

Ceranthus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with prominent, horn-like structures on the petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Ceranthus corniculatus is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides, where it adds vibrant color to the landscape.
