Carduus ellenbeckii

Carduus ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's thistle, is a perennial plant native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
