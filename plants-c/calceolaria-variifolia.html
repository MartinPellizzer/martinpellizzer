Calceolaria variifolia

Calceolaria variifolia, commonly known as the variable slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and vary in color, contributing to its ornamental value.

Calceolaria variifolia is appreciated for its delicate appearance and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
