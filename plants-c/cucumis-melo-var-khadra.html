Cucumis melo var. khadra

Cucumis melo var. khadra, commonly known as Persian cucumber, is a botanically distinct variety of melon characterized by its elongated, slender shape and smooth, thin skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squashes. The plant typically produces small, pale green fruits with a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its crisp texture and use in fresh consumption or as a decorative element in gardens.
