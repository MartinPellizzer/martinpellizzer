Calamus solitarius

Calamus solitarius, commonly known as the solitary calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that typically grows in wet, marshy environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and basket-making. Its flowering spikes are small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, greenish-white flowers.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
