Covellia guttata

Covellia guttata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-filled capsules for reproduction.

Covellia guttata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
