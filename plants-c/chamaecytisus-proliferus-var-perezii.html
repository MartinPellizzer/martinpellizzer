Chamaecytisus proliferus var. perezii

Chamaecytisus proliferus var. perezii, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
