Casearia thonneri

Casearia thonneri, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are edible for some animals but are generally not consumed by humans due to their bitter taste.
