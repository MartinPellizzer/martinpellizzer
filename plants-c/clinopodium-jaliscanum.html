Clinopodium jaliscanum

Clinopodium jaliscanum, commonly known as Jalisco mint, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium jaliscanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
