Cymbidium tigrinum

Cymbidium tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that feature a mix of yellow and orange hues with dark red or purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Cymbidium tigrinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
