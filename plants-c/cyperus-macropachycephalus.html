Cyperus macropachycephalus

Cyperus macropachycephalus, commonly known as the broad-headed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus macropachycephalus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
