Castanea vesca var. vesubiana

Castanea vesca var. vesubiana, commonly known as the Italian sweet chestnut.

This variant is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and the fruit matures in the fall.
