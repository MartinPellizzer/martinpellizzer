Cissus pseudonivea

Cissus pseudonivea, commonly known as the silver arrow or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus pseudonivea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
