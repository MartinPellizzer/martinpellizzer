Calligonum aciferum

Calligonum aciferum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
