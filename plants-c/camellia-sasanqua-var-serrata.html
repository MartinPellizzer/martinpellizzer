Camellia sasanqua var. serrata

Camellia sasanqua var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and botanical collections.
