Centropogon ferrugineus var. tomentellus

Centropogon ferrugineus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly bluish grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The variety tomentellus is distinguished by its densely hairy leaves and stems, giving it a woolly appearance.
