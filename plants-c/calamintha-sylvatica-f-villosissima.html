Calamintha sylvatica f. villosissima

Calamintha sylvatica f. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly wood mint, is a botanical variant of the wood mint species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villosissima, foliage that gives it a distinctive, textured appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic leaves and visual appeal.
