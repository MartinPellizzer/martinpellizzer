Carex densipilosa

Carex densipilosa, commonly known as dense-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

The plant's dense, hairy appearance helps it retain moisture and withstand wet conditions.
