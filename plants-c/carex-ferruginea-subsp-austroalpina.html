Carex ferruginea subsp. austroalpina

Carex ferruginea subsp.

austroalpina, commonly known as the Alpine brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its cylindrical, dark brown to reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine vegetation.
