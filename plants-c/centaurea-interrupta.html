Centaurea interrupta

Centaurea interrupta, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea interrupta is a hardy species that thrives in arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
