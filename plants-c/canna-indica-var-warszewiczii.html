Canna indica var. warszewiczii

Canna indica var. warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, colorful leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a distinctive red or purple midrib. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by broad, colorful bracts, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
