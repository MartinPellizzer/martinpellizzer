Cereus beneckei

Cereus beneckei, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. It produces large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance.

The flowers typically bloom only once a year, making the plant a striking and rare sight in the botanical world.
