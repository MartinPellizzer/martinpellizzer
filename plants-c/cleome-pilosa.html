Cleome pilosa

Cleome pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Cleome pilosa is an annual plant that blooms throughout the summer months.
