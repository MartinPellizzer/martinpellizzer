Cleome erucago

Cleome erucago, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome erucago thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its distinctive floral display.
