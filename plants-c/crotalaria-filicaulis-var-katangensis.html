Crotalaria filicaulis var. katangensis

Crotalaria filicaulis var. katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and yellow floral clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

This species is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
