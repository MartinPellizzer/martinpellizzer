Callianthe pachecoana

Callianthe pachecoana, commonly known as the Pacheco's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
