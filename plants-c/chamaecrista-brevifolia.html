Chamaecrista brevifolia

Chamaecrista brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf burr legume, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and often covered in short, stiff hairs, which aid in seed dispersal.

Chamaecrista brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
