Crocus rujanensis

Crocus rujanensis, commonly known as the Rujan crocus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
