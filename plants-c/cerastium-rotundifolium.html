Cerastium rotundifolium

Cerastium rotundifolium, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium rotundifolium is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its delicate flowers and creeping growth habit.
