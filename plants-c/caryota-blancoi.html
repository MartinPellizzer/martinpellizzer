Caryota blancoi

Caryota blancoi, commonly known as the white-veined palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a tree with a short trunk and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as decorative elements due to their aesthetic value.

Caryota blancoi thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
