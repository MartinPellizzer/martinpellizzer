Calea pellucidinerva

Calea pellucidinerva, commonly known as the "glassy calea," is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, translucent white bracts that resemble glass. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
