Cryptolepis eburnea

Cryptolepis eburnea, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
