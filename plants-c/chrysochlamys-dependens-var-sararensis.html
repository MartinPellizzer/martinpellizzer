Chrysochlamys dependens var. sararensis

Chrysochlamys dependens var. sararensis, commonly known as the Sararensis variety of the golden chrysochlamys, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and evergreen foliage, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings for its unique appearance.
