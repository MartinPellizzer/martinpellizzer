Croton yerbalium var. lanatus

Croton yerbalium var. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage and resilience to adverse conditions.
