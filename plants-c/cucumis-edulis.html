Cucumis edulis

Cucumis edulis, commonly known as the cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to South Asia.

It is a trailing vine that produces edible, elongated fruits with a high water content and a mild, slightly sweet flavor. The plant has palmate leaves and yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Cucumis edulis is cultivated worldwide for its fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
