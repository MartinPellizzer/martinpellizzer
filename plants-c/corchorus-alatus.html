Corchorus alatus

Corchorus alatus, commonly known as the "sicklepod" or "bottlebrush plant," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for making textiles and paper.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
