Cavendishia talamancensis

Cavendishia talamancensis, commonly known as the Talamancan orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica and Panama.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that help it thrive in its natural habitat.

Cavendishia talamancensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
