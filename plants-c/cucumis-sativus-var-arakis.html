Cucumis sativus var. arakis

Cucumis sativus var. arakis, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit, which typically measures around 15 to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit, similar to other cucumber species. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is prized for its crisp texture and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in culinary applications.
