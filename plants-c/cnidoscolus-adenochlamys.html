Cnidoscolus adenochlamys

Cnidoscolus adenochlamys, commonly known as the giant arrowhead or arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round, red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it both a decorative and potentially useful species in certain ecological contexts.
