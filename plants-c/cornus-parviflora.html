Cornus parviflora

Cornus parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its small, white flowers and red or orange berries. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall foliage, which ranges from yellow to red.

Cornus parviflora is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
