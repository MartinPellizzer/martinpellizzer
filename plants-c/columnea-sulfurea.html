Columnea sulfurea

Columnea sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur-flowered flame plant, is a strikingly colorful member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which are tubular and often adorned with red or orange markings. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Columnea sulfurea is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
