Carex acrolepis

Carex acrolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering structures, known as spikes, contain small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex acrolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
