Campanula raineri

Campanula raineri, commonly known as the Rainer bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows to a height of about 20 to 30 inches. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Campanula raineri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
