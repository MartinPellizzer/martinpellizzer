Chondrodendron nemophilum

Chondrodendron nemophilum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence that resembles a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a ghost.

Due to its habitat specificity and rarity, Chondrodendron nemophilum is considered an endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
