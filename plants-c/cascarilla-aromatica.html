Cascarilla aromatica

Cascarilla aromatica, commonly known as aromatic cascarilla, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant is notable for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its bark and roots contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
