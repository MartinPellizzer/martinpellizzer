Clematis cirrhosa var. triloba

Clematis cirrhosa var. triloba, commonly known as the trailing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers with five to seven petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its cascading floral display.
