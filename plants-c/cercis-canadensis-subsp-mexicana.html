Cercis canadensis subsp. mexicana

Cercis canadensis subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its ornamental heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant produces small, flat, brown pods that contain several seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The Mexican redbud is valued for its compact size and seasonal color display.
