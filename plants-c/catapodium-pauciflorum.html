Catapodium pauciflorum

Catapodium pauciflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in spike-like clusters. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Catapodium pauciflorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
