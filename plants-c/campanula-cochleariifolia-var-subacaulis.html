Campanula cochleariifolia var. subacaulis

Campanula cochleariifolia var. subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, cylindrical fruits known as capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
