Colicodendron pulchellum

Colicodendron pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful colicodendron, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
