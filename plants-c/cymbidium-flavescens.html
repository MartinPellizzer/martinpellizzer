Cymbidium flavescens

Cymbidium flavescens, commonly known as the yellow cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, arranged in a fan shape around the stem.

Cymbidium flavescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
