Citharexylum lycioides

Citharexylum lycioides, commonly known as the silky pear tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, pear-shaped fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in both landscape design and horticultural cultivation.
