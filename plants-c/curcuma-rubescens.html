Curcuma rubescens

Curcuma rubescens, commonly known as red turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its ornamental and medicinal value. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers and has aromatic, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a compact growth habit.

Curcuma rubescens is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and potential health benefits.
