Cinnamomum rougierii

Cinnamomum rougierii, commonly known as the Guangxi cinnamon or Guangxi cassia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark brown fruits containing single seeds. Its foliage is dense and glossy, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical gardens.

Cinnamomum rougierii thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.
