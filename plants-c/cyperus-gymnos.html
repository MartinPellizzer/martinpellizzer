Cyperus gymnos

Cyperus gymnos, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically at the base of the stems. Cyperus gymnos is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
