Carduncellus caeruleus

Carduncellus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue carduncel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
