Calamus ornatus var. celebicus

Calamus ornatus var. celebicus, commonly known as the Celebes calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
