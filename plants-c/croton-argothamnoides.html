Croton argothamnoides

Croton argothamnoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
