Cryptocarya thouvenotii

Cryptocarya thouvenotii, commonly known as the Tuamotu laurifolium, is a small evergreen tree native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal forests and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinct, spicy aroma, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
