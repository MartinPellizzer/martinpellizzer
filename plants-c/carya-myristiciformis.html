Carya myristiciformis

Carya myristiciformis, commonly known as the sweet gum or Franklin tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive, star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny capsules that contain smooth, glossy seeds. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
