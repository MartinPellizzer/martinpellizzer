Cerasus reticulata

Cerasus reticulata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yuzen, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value is highly prized in gardens and parks, particularly for its showy blossoms.

The species is also cultivated for its historical and cultural significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
