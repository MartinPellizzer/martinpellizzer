Cousinia spryginii

Cousinia spryginii, commonly known as the Kashmir copper grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia spryginii is often used in alpine gardens for its hardiness and unique appearance.
