Cyperorchis tigrina

Cyperorchis tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower spikes. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. Native to regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
