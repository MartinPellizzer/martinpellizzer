Campomanesia warmingiana

Campomanesia warmingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and commonly used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
