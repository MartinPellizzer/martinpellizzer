Cordia aspera subsp. aspera

Cordia aspera subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or seasonally wet habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
