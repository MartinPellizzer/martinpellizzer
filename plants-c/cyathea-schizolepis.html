Cyathea schizolepis

Cyathea schizolepis, commonly known as the Schizolepis tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in tropical landscapes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
