Cineraria filifolia

Cineraria filifolia, commonly known as the hairleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cineraria filifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
