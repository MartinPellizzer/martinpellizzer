Calocephalus gnaphalioides

Calocephalus gnaphalioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that appear in late summer. It is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
