Crambe sinuatodentata

Crambe sinuatodentata, commonly known as the sinuate-cleft crambe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply sinuate and dentate, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months.

Crambe sinuatodentata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal environments.
