Celastrus novoguineensis

Celastrus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea staff vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
