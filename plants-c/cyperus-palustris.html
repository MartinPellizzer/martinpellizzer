Cyperus palustris

Cyperus palustris, commonly known as marsh flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus palustris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
