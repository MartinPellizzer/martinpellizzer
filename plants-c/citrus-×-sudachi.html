Citrus × sudachi

Citrus × sudachi, commonly known as Japanese sudachi, is a small, round citrus fruit native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thin, peely skin and tart, aromatic pulp. The plant is typically cultivated in subtropical climates and is valued for its use in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are evergreen and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The fruit is often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and as a flavoring agent in various food products.
