Crepis lomonosovae

Crepis lomonosovae, commonly known as the Lomonosov's mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Crepis lomonosovae is adapted to survive in extreme climates, making it a resilient member of polar ecosystems.
