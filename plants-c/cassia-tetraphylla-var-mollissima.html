Cassia tetraphylla var. mollissima

Cassia tetraphylla var. mollissima, commonly known as silky cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its fruit is a flat, elongated pod that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
