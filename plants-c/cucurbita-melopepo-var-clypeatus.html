Cucurbita melopepo var. clypeatus

Cucurbita melopepo var. clypeatus, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, warty, and often ribbed fruit, which can vary in color from green to orange or red when mature. The plant is a vine that produces large, palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically used in culinary applications, though it is known for its bitter taste.
