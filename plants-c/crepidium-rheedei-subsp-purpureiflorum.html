Crepidium rheedei subsp. purpureiflorum

Crepidium rheedei subsp.

purpureiflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
