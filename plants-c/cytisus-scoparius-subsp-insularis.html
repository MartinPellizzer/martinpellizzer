Cytisus scoparius subsp. insularis

Cytisus scoparius subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or rocky habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a hardy ornamental plant.
