Crotalaria pseudonatalitia

Crotalaria pseudonatalitia, commonly known as the Natal spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria pseudonatalitia is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
