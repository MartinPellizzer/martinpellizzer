Carex turgida

Carex turgida, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Carex turgida plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
