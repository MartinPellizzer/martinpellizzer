Convolvulus kleinii

Convolvulus kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Convolvulus kleinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
