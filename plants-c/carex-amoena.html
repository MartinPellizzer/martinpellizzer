Carex amoena

Carex amoena, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, cylindrical stems and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
