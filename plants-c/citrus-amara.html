Citrus amara

Citrus amara, commonly known as bitter orange, is a citrus species native to Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and bitter, seedy fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

The tree is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
