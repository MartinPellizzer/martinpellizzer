Cousinia layardii

Cousinia layardii, commonly known as the Layard's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

Cousinia layardii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
