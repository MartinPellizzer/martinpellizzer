Centaurea viciosoi

Centaurea viciosoi, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea viciosoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
