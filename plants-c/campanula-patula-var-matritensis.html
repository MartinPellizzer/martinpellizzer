Campanula patula var. matritensis

Campanula patula var. matritensis, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
