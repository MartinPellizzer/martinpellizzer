Cinnamomum halmaheirae

Cinnamomum halmaheirae, commonly known as the Madagascar cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark brown, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
