Caladium bicolor var. mirabile

Caladium bicolor var. mirabile, commonly known as the "Rainbow" caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, heart-shaped leaves that come in shades of red, white, pink, and green. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Caladium bicolor var. mirabile is typically grown as an annual in temperate regions due to its sensitivity to frost.
