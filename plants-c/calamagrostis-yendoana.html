Calamagrostis yendoana

Calamagrostis yendoana, commonly known as the Yendoan reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value.

Calamagrostis yendoana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
