Carex alpina f. inferalpina

Carex alpina f. inferalpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in alpine and subalpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas. Its botanical features include narrow, erect stems and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects in high-altitude environments.
