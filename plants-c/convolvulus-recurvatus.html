Convolvulus recurvatus

Convolvulus recurvatus, commonly known as the bentleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often curled or folded. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Convolvulus recurvatus is a perennial herb that can spread through seeds and rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
