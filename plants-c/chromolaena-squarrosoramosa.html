Chromolaena squarrosoramosa

Chromolaena squarrosoramosa, commonly known as the roughleaf false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
