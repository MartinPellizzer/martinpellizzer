Cyperus corymbosus

Cyperus corymbosus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale yellow flowers, which appear at the ends of its stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water edges.

Cyperus corymbosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
