Cullumia ciliaris subsp. ciliaris

Cullumia ciliaris subsp.

ciliaris, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used by Indigenous peoples for fiber and basket-making.
