Caenopteris myriophylla

Caenopteris myriophylla, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its fronds are composed of multiple layers of tightly packed leaflets, giving it a dense, lush appearance.

Caenopteris myriophylla plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
