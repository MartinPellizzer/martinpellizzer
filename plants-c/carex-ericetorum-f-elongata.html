Carex ericetorum f. elongata

Carex ericetorum f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated sedge, is a variant of the Ericetorum sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a cylindrical cluster. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments.
