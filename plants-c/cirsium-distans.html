Cirsium distans

Cirsium distans, commonly known as the distant thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its robust growth and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
