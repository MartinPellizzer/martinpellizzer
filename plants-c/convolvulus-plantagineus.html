Convolvulus plantagineus

Convolvulus plantagineus, commonly known as the silky blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or purple flowers with a velvety appearance. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers open in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
