Cylindrolobus gretcheniae

Cylindrolobus gretcheniae, commonly known as the Schomburgkia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
