Chaerophyllum tainturieri var. dasycarpum

Chaerophyllum tainturieri var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the rough-seeded chervil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of small, hairy, dry capsules that mature in late summer.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
