Chonemorpha malabarica

Chonemorpha malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, star-shaped berry that turns from green to orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
