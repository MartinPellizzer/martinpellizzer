Cestrum roseum

Cestrum roseum, commonly known as the pink trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a woody shrub or climber, often growing up to 10 feet tall, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Cestrum roseum is also noted for its fragrant flowers and is often used in ornamental gardening.
