Carduus chevallieri

Carduus chevallieri, commonly known as the French thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant features silver-gray, woolly leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its showy purple to blue composite flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carduus chevallieri is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in disturbed environments.
