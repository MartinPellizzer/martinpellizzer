Cupressus horizontalis var. pendula

Cupressus horizontalis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a cultivar of the western red cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

This variety is often used as a specimen tree or in hedges due to its elegant form and adaptability.
