Caladenia paradoxa

Caladenia paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with long, thin petals and a distinctive, recurved labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn season, producing strikingly colored flowers in shades of yellow and pink.

Caladenia paradoxa is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
