Cephalantheropsis gracilis

Cephalantheropsis gracilis, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and a tall, slender flowering stem. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cephalantheropsis gracilis is known for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
