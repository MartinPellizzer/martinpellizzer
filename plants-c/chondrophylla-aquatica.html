Chondrophylla aquatica

Chondrophylla aquatica, commonly known as water chondrophylla, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that float on the water's surface.

It thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments, often found in ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the water. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and bloom in late spring.

Chondrophylla aquatica plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.
