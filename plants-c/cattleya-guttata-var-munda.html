Cattleya guttata var. munda

Cattleya guttata var. munda, commonly known as the spotted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration and prominent dark markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting broad, leathery leaves.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral display.
