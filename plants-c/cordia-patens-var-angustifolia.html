Cordia patens var. angustifolia

Cordia patens var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cordia, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are usually narrow and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
