Centaurea nigra var. transalpina

Centaurea nigra var. transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine dark centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spiny bracts around its flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Centaurea nigra var. transalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
