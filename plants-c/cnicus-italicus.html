Cnicus italicus

Cnicus italicus, commonly known as the Italian sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to September. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild laxative properties.

Cnicus italicus thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry meadows.
