Corethrogyne californica var. incana

Corethrogyne californica var. incana, commonly known as the woolly corethrogyne, is a perennial shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a valued ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
