Campanula elliptica

Campanula elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The leaves are elliptical and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This perennial plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
