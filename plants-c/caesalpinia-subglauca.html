Caesalpinia subglauca

Caesalpinia subglauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, glaucous-colored leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several elliptical seeds.

Caesalpinia subglauca is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.
