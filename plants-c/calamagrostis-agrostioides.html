Calamagrostis agrostioides

Calamagrostis agrostioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purple in hue, adding visual interest in the late summer and autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
