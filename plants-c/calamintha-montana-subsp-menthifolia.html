Calamintha montana subsp. menthifolia

Calamintha montana subsp.

menthifolia, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a mint-like scent and making it useful in herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
