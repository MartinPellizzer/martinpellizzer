Colutea melanocalyx subsp. davisiana

Colutea melanocalyx subsp.

davisiana, commonly known as the Davis's cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairy stems and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
