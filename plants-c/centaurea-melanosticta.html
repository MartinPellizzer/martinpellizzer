Centaurea melanosticta

Centaurea melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough, hairy texture.

Centaurea melanosticta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
