Corethrostylis microphylla

Corethrostylis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mist orchid, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and often clustered at the base of the plant.

The species is known for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its specific ecological requirements.
