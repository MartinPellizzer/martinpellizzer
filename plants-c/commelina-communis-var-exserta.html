Commelina communis var. exserta

Commelina communis var. exserta, commonly known as the slender dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals and prominent white sepals, typical of the Commelina genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
