Cleistogenes serotina var. nakaii

Cleistogenes serotina var. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that remain closed (cleistogamous) for much of the growing season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
