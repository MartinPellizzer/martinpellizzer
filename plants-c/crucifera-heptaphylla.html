Crucifera heptaphylla

Crucifera heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leafed crucifer, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its distinctive arrangement of seven leaves on its stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
