Cerbera linearifolia

Cerbera linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cerbera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

Cerbera linearifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
