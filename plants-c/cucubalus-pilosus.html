Cucubalus pilosus

Cucubalus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy sponge gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is typically a sponge-like structure with a pale green exterior and a fibrous interior.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary traditions.
