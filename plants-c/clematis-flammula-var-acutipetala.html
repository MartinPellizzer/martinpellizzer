Clematis flammula var. acutipetala

Clematis flammula var. acutipetala, commonly known as the sharp-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 feet long, supporting its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is characterized by ovate, serrated leaves with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
