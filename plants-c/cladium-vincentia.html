Cladium vincentia

Cladium vincentia, commonly known as the Vincent's sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater wetlands, often in association with other aquatic vegetation. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marshy areas.

Cladium vincentia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
