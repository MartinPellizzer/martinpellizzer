Cyathea rugosula

Cyathea rugosula, commonly known as the rough-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a rugged texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea rugosula plays a significant ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
