Caesalpinia sepiaria var. pubescens

Caesalpinia sepiaria var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
