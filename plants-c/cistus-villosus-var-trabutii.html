Cistus villosus var. trabutii

Cistus villosus var. trabutii, commonly known as the Trabut's hairy rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to rocky and arid environments. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
