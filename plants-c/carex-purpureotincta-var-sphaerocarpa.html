Carex purpureotincta var. sphaerocarpa

Carex purpureotincta var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the purple-tinctured sphaerocarpa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with dark green leaves that often have a purplish tinge. The plant produces compact, spherical clusters of small, spike-like flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
