Chirita tamiana

Chirita tamiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Chirita tamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
