Commelina deficiens

Commelina deficiens, commonly known as the slender spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender, upright stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Commelina deficiens is a member of the Commelinaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological significance in wetland habitats.
