Carlina sicula var. longibracteata

Carlina sicula var. longibracteata, commonly known as the Sicilian carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features a robust, upright stem with rough, grayish-green leaves that have a distinctive lobed shape. Its inflorescence consists of purple-blue ray flowers surrounding yellow disk flowers, typical of the genus Carlina.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience and ornamental value in gardens.
