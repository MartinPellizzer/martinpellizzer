Ctenitis chiapasensis

Ctenitis chiapasensis, commonly known as the Chiapas shield fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, shield-shaped fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
