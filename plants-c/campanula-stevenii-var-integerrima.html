Campanula stevenii var. integerrima

Campanula stevenii var. integerrima, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.

Campanula stevenii var. integerrima is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
