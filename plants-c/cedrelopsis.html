Cedrelopsis

Cedrelopsis, commonly known as the African cedar, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

It consists of a single species, Cedrelopsis saxicola, which is native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Cedrelopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
