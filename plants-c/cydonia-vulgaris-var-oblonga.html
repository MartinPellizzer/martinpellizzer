Cydonia vulgaris var. oblonga

Cydonia vulgaris var. oblonga, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits known as quinces. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive lobed shape. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
