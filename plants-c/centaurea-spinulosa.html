Centaurea spinulosa

Centaurea spinulosa, commonly known as the thistleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-covered flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea spinulosa is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.
