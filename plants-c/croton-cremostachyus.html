Croton cremostachyus

Croton cremostachyus, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
