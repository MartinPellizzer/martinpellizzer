Ceropegia stapeliiformis subsp. stapeliiformis

Ceropegia stapeliiformis subsp.

stapeliiformis, commonly known as the stapeliiform ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, waxy leaves that help it store water.

The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellow, adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
