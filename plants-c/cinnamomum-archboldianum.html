Cinnamomum archboldianum

Cinnamomum archboldianum, commonly known as the Archbold's cinnamon.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines, characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland forests.
