Crotalaria lanceolata

Crotalaria lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often accompanied by a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Crotalaria lanceolata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
