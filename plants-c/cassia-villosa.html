Cassia villosa

Cassia villosa, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some applications in herbal remedies.
