Crinum subcernuum

Crinum subcernuum, commonly known as the African lily or blue crinum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crinum subcernuum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
