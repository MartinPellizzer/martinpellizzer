Cyperus straminicolor var. betsileensis

Cyperus straminicolor var. betsileensis, commonly known as the Betsilean flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-ranked flower clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its unique coloration and growth form, making it a notable species within its genus.
