Cerasus incana var. viridis

Cerasus incana var. viridis, commonly known as the green cherry tree, is a variant of the Prunus incana species.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round cherries that are typically green when mature, though they may turn red or black depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
