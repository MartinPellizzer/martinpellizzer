Carex siderosticta f. stenophylla

Carex siderosticta f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a variant of the slender sedges species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver or white midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets during the flowering season.

This form is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
