Chirita medica

Chirita medica, commonly known as the medicinal verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Chirita medica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.
