Crocus stridii

Crocus stridii, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blooms that are often used in traditional medicine. Crocus stridii thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of cold dormancy to trigger flowering.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
