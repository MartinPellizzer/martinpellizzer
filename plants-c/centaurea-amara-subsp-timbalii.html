Centaurea amara subsp. timbalii

Centaurea amara subsp.

timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalus centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bracteate flower heads. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
