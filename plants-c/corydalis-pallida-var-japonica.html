Corydalis pallida var. japonica

Corydalis pallida var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese white corydalis, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
