Cardamine ovata subsp. lehmannii

Cardamine ovata subsp.

lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's watercress, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and edible leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the typical form of Cardamine ovata.
