Chaetostoma glaziovii var. pallidum

Chaetostoma glaziovii var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale chaetostoma, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light conditions. Its unique leaf structure allows it to float on the water's surface while maintaining strong root systems.

This variety is prized in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
