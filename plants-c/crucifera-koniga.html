Crucifera koniga

Crucifera koniga, commonly known as the golden cross, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
