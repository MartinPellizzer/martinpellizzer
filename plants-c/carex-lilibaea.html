Carex lilibaea

Carex lilibaea, commonly known as the Sierra Valley sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streams. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex lilibaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
