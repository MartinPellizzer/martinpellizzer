Cyperus lomatophyllus

Cyperus lomatophyllus, commonly known as umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

Cyperus lomatophyllus is also valued for its role in erosion control and wetland restoration projects.
