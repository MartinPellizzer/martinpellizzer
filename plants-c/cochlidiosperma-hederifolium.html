Cochlidiosperma hederifolium

Cochlidiosperma hederifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of ivy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes many leguminous plants.
