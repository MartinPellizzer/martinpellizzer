Callicarpa villosissima

Callicarpa villosissima, commonly known as the hairy beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
