Carex acuta var. macrocarpa

Carex acuta var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
