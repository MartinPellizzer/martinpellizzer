Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp. origanifolium

Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp.

origanifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed common toadflax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, branched stems and opposite, narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with four petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its rapid growth and short life cycle.
