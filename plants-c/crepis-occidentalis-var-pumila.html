Crepis occidentalis var. pumila

Crepis occidentalis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf western cockleburs, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically features small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.

This variety is notable for its resilience in arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
