Clinopodium pallidum

Clinopodium pallidum, commonly known as pale wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It has small, pale green to grayish leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Clinopodium pallidum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
