Chailletia oleifolia

Chailletia oleifolia, commonly known as the oilleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are rich in oils, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round berries. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The oil extracted from its leaves is valued for its unique properties in both culinary and industrial applications.
