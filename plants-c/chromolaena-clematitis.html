Chromolaena clematitis

Chromolaena clematitis, commonly known as little leaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields due to its adaptability and rapid growth. It can become invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
