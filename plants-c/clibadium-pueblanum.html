Clibadium pueblanum

Clibadium pueblanum, commonly known as the Pueblan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to other prickly pear cacti. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and traditional contexts.
