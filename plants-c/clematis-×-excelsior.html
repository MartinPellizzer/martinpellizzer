Clematis × excelsior

Clematis × excelsior, commonly known as the Royal Clematis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Clematis viticella and Clematis alpina.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is deciduous, with heart-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its floral display.

Clematis × excelsior is well-suited for trellises, arbors, and other support structures in gardens.
