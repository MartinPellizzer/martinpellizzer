Cerasus erecta

Cerasus erecta, commonly known as the upright cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Cerasus erecta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
