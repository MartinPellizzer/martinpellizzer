Carex retrorsa var. bradleyi

Carex retrorsa var. bradleyi, commonly known as Bradley's hairgrass, is a sedge species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled at the tips. It produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are often reddish-brown in color.

Bradley's hairgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
