Campanula pusilla f. hirciana

Campanula pusilla f. hirciana, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower or the sheep's bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
