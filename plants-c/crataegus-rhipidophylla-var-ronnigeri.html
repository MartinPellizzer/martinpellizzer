Crataegus rhipidophylla var. ronnigeri

Crataegus rhipidophylla var. ronnigeri, commonly known as the Ronniger's hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
