Carex neofilipes

Carex neofilipes, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
