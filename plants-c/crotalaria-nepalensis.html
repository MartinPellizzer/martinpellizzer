Crotalaria nepalensis

Crotalaria nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal spurred guinea flower, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and a hairy stem.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain toxic alkaloids, making it both a potential weed and a plant of interest in pharmacological research.
