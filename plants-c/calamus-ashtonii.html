Calamus ashtonii

Calamus ashtonii, commonly known as Ashton's sweet flag, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
