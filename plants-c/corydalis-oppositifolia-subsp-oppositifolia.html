Corydalis oppositifolia subsp.

oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite in arrangement, giving it its common name, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Corydalis oppositifolia subsp.

oppositifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in garden settings.
