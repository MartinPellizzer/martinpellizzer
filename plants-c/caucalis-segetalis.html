Caucalis segetalis

Caucalis segetalis, commonly known as the field caucalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Caucalis segetalis is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
