Convolvulus crenatifolius var. montevidensis

Convolvulus crenatifolius var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
