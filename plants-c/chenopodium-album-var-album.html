Chenopodium album var. album

Chenopodium album var. album, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, succulent leaves that are usually alternate and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are tiny, dry, and often wind-dispersed. Lambsquarters is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has become naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.
