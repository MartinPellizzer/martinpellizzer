Campanula tomentosa var. ephesia

Campanula tomentosa var. ephesia, commonly known as the Ephesian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both garden landscapes and horticultural collections.
