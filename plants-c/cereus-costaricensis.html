Cereus costaricensis

Cereus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican torch cactus, is a species native to Central America.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, unbranched stem that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, a characteristic feature of many cacti in the night-blooming family. It has a spiny exterior with areoles that bear both spines and flowers.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
