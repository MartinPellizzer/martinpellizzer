Chrysanthemum segetum var. integrifolium

Chrysanthemum segetum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the field chrysanthemum, is a species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, entire leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand various environmental conditions.
