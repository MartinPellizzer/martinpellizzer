Chamaecrista ciliolata var. pulchella

Chamaecrista ciliolata var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and its distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
