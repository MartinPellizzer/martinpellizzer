Collomia biflora var. soehrensii

Collomia biflora var. soehrensii, commonly known as the Soehrens' twinflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is part of the tribe Polemonieae and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant communities.
