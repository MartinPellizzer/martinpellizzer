Cirsium appendiculatum

Cirsium appendiculatum, commonly known as the hooked thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its robust, upright growth habit.

Cirsium appendiculatum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
