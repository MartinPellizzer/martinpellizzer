Carex megalepis

Carex megalepis, commonly known as the large-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex megalepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
