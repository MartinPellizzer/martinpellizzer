Chocho

Chocho, commonly known as the Andean mountain potato, is a tuberous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has thick, waxy leaves that help it survive in cold, dry conditions. Its tubers, which are the edible part, come in various colors and have a nutty, earthy flavor.

Chocho is an important crop for indigenous communities in South America, valued both for its nutritional content and cultural significance.
