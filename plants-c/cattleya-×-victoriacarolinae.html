Cattleya × victoriacarolinae

Cattleya × victoriacarolinae, commonly known as the Victoria Carolina orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, well-lit environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with periodic dry spells.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral display.
