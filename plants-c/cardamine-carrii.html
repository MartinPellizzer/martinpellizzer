Cardamine carrii

Cardamine carrii, commonly known as Carpathian watercress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a peppery scent, similar to other members of its family.

The small white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
