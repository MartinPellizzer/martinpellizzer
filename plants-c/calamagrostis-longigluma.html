Calamagrostis longigluma

Calamagrostis longigluma, commonly known as the tall bluegrass or bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 to 4 meters in height. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically blue-green in color, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue, and they are arranged alternately along the stem.

Calamagrostis longigluma is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions.
