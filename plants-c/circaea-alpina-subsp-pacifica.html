Circaea alpina subsp. pacifica

Circaea alpina subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils in coastal and alpine environments.

The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine, woolly coating.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy or gravelly soils in its habitat.
