Cenchrus hillebrandianus

Cenchrus hillebrandianus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
