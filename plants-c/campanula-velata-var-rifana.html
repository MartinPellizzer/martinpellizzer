Campanula velata var. rifana

Campanula velata var. rifana, commonly known as the Rifan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa and the Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
