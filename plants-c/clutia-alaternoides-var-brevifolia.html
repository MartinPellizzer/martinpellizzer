Clutia alaternoides var. brevifolia

Clutia alaternoides var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a short petiole. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
