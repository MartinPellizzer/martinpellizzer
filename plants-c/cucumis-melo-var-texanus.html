Cucumis melo var. texanus

Cucumis melo var. texanus, commonly known as the Texas watermelon, is a cultivar of the watermelon species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick, green rind and juicy, sweet red flesh. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of cucurbit species. Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system adapted to arid conditions.
