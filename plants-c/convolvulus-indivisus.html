Convolvulus indivisus

Convolvulus indivisus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving the plant its common name.
