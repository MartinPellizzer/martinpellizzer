Carex curvula proles rodnensis

Carex curvula proles rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnian sedge, is a perennial plant native to specific regions in Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
