Corema album

Corema album, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers with prominent fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark berries that are eaten by birds.

Corema album is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
