Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp. euambrosioides

Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp.

euambrosioides, commonly known as wormseed, is an annual herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its small, yellow-green flowers and produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant has succulent leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments. It is often found in dry meadows, along roadsides, and in areas with poor soil conditions.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health purposes.
