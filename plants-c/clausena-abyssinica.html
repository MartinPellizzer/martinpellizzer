Clausena abyssinica

Clausena abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian lemon or African citrus, is a small evergreen tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange fruits that resemble citrus. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white flowers that are followed by the edible, acidic fruits. The fruit's peel contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine for its antifungal and antibacterial properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
