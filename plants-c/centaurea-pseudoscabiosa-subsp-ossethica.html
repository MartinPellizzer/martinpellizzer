Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp. ossethica

Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp.

ossethica, commonly known as the Ossetian knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity.
