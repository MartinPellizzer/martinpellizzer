Coelogyne trifida

Coelogyne trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of three to five. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and indirect light.

Coelogyne trifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
