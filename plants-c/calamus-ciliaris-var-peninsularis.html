Calamus ciliaris var. peninsularis

Calamus ciliaris var. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers. It is native to the coastal regions of the Arabian Peninsula and is adapted to saline and brackish water conditions.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
