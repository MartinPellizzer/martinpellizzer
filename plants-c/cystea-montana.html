Cystea montana

Cystea montana, commonly known as the mountain cystea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Cystea montana produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.
