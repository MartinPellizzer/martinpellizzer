Caroxylon subaphyllum

Caroxylon subaphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf desert shrub, is a small, hardy plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Caroxylon subaphyllum plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
