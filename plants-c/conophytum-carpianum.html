Conophytum carpianum

Conophytum carpianum, commonly known as the "Cape Flats stonecrop," is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paired leaves that form a rosette, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
