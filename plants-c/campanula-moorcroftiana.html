Campanula moorcroftiana

Campanula moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula moorcroftiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
