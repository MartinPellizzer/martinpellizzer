Carex × sabauda

Carex × sabauda, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × sabauda is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
