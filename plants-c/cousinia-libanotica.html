Cousinia libanotica

Cousinia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon goatherb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a woolly texture, and it produces small yellow or pink flowers in clusters.

Cousinia libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
