Crocus veluchensis var. micranthus

Crocus veluchensis var. micranthus, commonly known as the Alpian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, early spring blooms in shades of white to pale yellow. The flowers are typically borne on short stems and have six petals, with a distinctive, rounded shape. This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
