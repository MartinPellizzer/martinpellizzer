Capsicum annuum var. weinmannii

Capsicum annuum var. weinmannii, commonly known as the Trinidad Poblano, is a variant of the common chili pepper native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow shape and typically ranges from light green to orange when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fruits which can be harvested when green or allowed to ripen to a vibrant red. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its mild to medium heat level and is often used in culinary applications across Latin American cuisines.
