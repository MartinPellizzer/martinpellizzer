Cornella unalaschkensis

Cornella unalaschkensis, commonly known as the Alaska cornel, is a deciduous shrub native to the western regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae, which includes other well-known plants like the dogwood. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Cornella unalaschkensis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, and it is adapted to cold climates.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive red or purple coloration in the fall.
