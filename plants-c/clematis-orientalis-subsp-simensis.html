Clematis orientalis subsp. simensis

Clematis orientalis subsp.

simensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian clematis, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia. It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
