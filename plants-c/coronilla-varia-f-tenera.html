Coronilla varia f. tenera

Coronilla varia f. tenera, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant in crop rotation systems.
