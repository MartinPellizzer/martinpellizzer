Cissus stipulacea var. hochstetteri

Cissus stipulacea var. hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive dark green color and prominent veins. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
