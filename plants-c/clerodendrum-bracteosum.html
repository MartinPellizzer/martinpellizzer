Clerodendrum bracteosum

Clerodendrum bracteosum, commonly known as the Chinese flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with tubular, red or orange flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
