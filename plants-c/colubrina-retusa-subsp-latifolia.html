Colubrina retusa subsp. latifolia

Colubrina retusa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than they are long, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
