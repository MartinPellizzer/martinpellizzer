Caryocar glabrum var. pilosum

Caryocar glabrum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy araçá, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. The seeds are encased in a hard, smooth shell and are often dispersed by animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
