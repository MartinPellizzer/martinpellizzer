Casearia amplectens

Casearia amplectens, commonly known as the climbing casearia, is a shrub or vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its growth habit involves climbing or trailing, often using its tendrils to attach to supports.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
