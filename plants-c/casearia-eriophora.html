Casearia eriophora

Casearia eriophora, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, violet-blue berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
