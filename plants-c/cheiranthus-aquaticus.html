Cheiranthus aquaticus

Cheiranthus aquaticus, commonly known as water cheeseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, ditches, and riverbanks. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or deeply divided, and its flowers are bright yellow with four petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cheiranthus aquaticus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
