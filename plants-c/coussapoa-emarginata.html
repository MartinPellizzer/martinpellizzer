Coussapoa emarginata

Coussapoa emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
