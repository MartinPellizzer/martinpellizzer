Calliandra seemannii

Calliandra seemannii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red bract-like flowers that resemble plumes, which bloom throughout the year. The plant has bipinnate leaves, composed of small, feather-like leaflets, and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators.

Additionally, it is used in some regions for its nitrogen-fixing properties, benefiting soil fertility.
