Chlorocyperus prolixus

Chlorocyperus prolixus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are often mistaken for true grasses, but it has distinct sedge characteristics.

Chlorocyperus prolixus is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
