Camellia achrysantha

Camellia achrysantha, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is a member of the Theaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia achrysantha is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
