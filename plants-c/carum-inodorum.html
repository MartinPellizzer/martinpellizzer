Carum inodorum

Carum inodorum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that form umbels. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its aromatic seeds are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its hollow stems and pungent, anise-like scent.
