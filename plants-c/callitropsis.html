Callitropsis

Callitropsis, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a genus of coniferous evergreen shrubs native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

These plants are characterized by their compact growth habit, with dense foliage and needle-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. They typically grow in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes, demonstrating a high degree of cold tolerance. The cones of Callitropsis are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to their unique ecological adaptations, these plants are of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
