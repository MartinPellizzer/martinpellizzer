Cascabela plumeriifolia

Cascabela plumeriifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered cascabela, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Cascabela plumeriifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
