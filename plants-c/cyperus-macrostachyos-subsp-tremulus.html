Cyperus macrostachyos subsp. tremulus

Cyperus macrostachyos subsp.

tremulus, commonly known as the trembling flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and three-sided stems.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
