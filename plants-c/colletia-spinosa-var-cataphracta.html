Colletia spinosa var. cataphracta

Colletia spinosa var. cataphracta, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, white or purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
