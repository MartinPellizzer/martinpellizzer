Clerodendrum ferrugineum

Clerodendrum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-leaved clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in tropical and subtropical regions.
