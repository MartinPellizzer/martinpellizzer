Centaurea nigrescens subsp. nigrescens

Centaurea nigrescens subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as black-centred coneflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features erect, branching stems with narrow, grey-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, conical heads, with dark purple to black petals that darken as they age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
