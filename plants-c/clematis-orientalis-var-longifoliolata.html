Clematis orientalis var. longifoliolata

Clematis orientalis var. longifoliolata, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
