Cissus humbertii

Cissus humbertii, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus humbertii is also valued for its ability to climb and trail along surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
