Cnicus bulgaricus

Cnicus bulgaricus, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and mountain slopes. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
