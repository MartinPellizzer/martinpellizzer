Cistus creticus var. tenorei

Cistus creticus var. tenorei, commonly known as the Cretan rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky, coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
