Colchicum imperatoris-friderici

Colchicum imperatoris-friderici, commonly known as the Emperor's Colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces violet-blue to purple flowers with prominent stamens and a distinctive, spurred corolla. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often withering before flowering.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
