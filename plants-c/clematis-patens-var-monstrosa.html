Clematis patens var. monstrosa

Clematis patens var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant white clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
