Cardamine californica proles integrifolia

Cardamine californica proles integrifolia, commonly known as the California watercress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from a rosette of basal leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, with a distinctive lobed or divided shape, and are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
