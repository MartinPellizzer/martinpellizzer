Cirsium congestissimum

Cirsium congestissimum, commonly known as the dense thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands. Its botanical features include a stout, woody stem and a compact growth habit.

Cirsium congestissimum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle species.
