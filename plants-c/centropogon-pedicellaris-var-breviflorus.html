Centropogon pedicellaris var. breviflorus

Centropogon pedicellaris var. breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often bright red or purple, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
