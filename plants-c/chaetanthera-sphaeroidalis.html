Chaetanthera sphaeroidalis

Chaetanthera sphaeroidalis, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
