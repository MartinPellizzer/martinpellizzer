Carex hyalina

Carex hyalina, commonly known as the translucent sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to translucent, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic plants.

Carex hyalina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
