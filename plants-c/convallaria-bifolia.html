Convallaria bifolia

Convallaria bifolia, commonly known as two-leaved lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Liliaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in woodland areas with moist, well-drained soil. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with a distinct bell shape. Its berries are red and contain toxic seeds, making it a plant to handle with care.

Convallaria bifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
