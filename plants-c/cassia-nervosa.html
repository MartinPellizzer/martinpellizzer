Cassia nervosa

Cassia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and yellow, clustered flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pods that contain small, dark seeds. Cassia nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and tree-like forms depending on the environment.
