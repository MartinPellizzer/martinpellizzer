Carex augustinowiczii subsp. soyaeensis

Carex augustinowiczii subsp.

soyaeensis, commonly known as the Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are usually green or brown in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
