Carex atrofusca var. fusca

Carex atrofusca var. fusca, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense stands.

The flowers are usually brown to black in color, which gives the plant its common name.
