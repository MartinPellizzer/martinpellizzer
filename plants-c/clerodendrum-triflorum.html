Clerodendrum triflorum

Clerodendrum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically appear in clusters of three, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clerodendrum triflorum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland or streamside habitats.
