Claoxylon ellipticum

Claoxylon ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
