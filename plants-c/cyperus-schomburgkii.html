Cyperus schomburgkii

Cyperus schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus schomburgkii is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
