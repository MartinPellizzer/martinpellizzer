Clusia ramosa

Clusia ramosa, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Clusia ramosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
