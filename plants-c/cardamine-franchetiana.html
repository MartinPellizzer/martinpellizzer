Cardamine franchetiana

Cardamine franchetiana, commonly known as the Franchet's watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soils.

Cardamine franchetiana is often found in temperate regions of Asia, particularly in China and Japan.
