Cleisostoma chrysochilum

Cleisostoma chrysochilum, commonly known as the golden-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a lip in shape. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
