Cotoneaster sternianus

Cotoneaster sternianus, commonly known as the Sierra cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and has small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Cotoneaster sternianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
