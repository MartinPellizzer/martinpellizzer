Callianthemum berardii

Callianthemum berardii, commonly known as the Berard's pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in spring. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
