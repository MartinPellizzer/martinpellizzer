Cigdemia cinerea

Cigdemia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
