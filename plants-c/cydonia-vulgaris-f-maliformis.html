Cydonia vulgaris f. maliformis

Cydonia vulgaris f. maliformis, commonly known as the Persian apple or sand apple, is a botanical variant of the quince.

It is characterized by its unique, often irregular fruit shape and a thick, rough skin that ranges from yellow to brown. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. Its flowers are typically pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by the distinctive fruit that ripens in late summer.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
