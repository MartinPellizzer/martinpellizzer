Carica crassipetala

Carica crassipetala, commonly known as the thick-leaved papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery in texture. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Its flowering stems are often covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps protect the plant from excessive sunlight.

Carica crassipetala is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
