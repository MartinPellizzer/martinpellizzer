Carex cheesemanii

Carex cheesemanii, commonly known as Cheeseman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided seed capsules.

Carex cheesemanii is often found in shaded, cool environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
