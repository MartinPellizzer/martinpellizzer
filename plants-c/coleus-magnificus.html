Coleus magnificus

Coleus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent coleus, is a vibrant tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from deep reds and purples to bright yellows and oranges. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a velvety texture and prominent veins.

Coleus magnificus thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
