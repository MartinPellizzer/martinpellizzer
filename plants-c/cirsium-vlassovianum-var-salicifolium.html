Cirsium vlassovianum var. salicifolium

Cirsium vlassovianum var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, silvery-green, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are typically surrounded by a dense layer of spiny bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
