Campanula latifolia f. alba

Campanula latifolia f. alba, commonly known as the White Bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its white flowers bloom in summer, adding a delicate beauty to gardens and natural landscapes.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
