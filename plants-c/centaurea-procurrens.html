Centaurea procurrens

Centaurea procurrens, commonly known as the creeping cornflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers with red or purple centers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and disturbed areas.

Centaurea procurrens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid conditions.
