Centaurea stapfiana

Centaurea stapfiana, commonly known as the Stapf's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its striking blue to purple flowers are often accompanied by bristly, spiny bracts.

Centaurea stapfiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
