Cymbidium humile

Cymbidium humile, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species native to East Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a terrestrial orchid with slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, green, and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing several flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

Cymbidium humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
