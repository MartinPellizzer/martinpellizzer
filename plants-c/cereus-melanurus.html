Cereus melanurus

Cereus melanurus, commonly known as the black spines cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, open at night and emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

Cereus melanurus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
