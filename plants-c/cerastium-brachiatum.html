Cerastium brachiatum

Cerastium brachiatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium brachiatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
