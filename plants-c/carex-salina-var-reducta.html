Carex salina var. reducta

Carex salina var. reducta, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal areas with saline soils. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
