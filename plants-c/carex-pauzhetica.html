Carex pauzhetica

Carex pauzhetica, commonly known as the Pauzhetica sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex pauzhetica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
