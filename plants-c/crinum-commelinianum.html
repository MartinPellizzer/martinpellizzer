Crinum commelinianum

Crinum commelinianum, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid environments with rich, organic soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a prominent central spike, and bloom during the warmer months.

Crinum commelinianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
