Convolvulus arvensis var. sagittifolius

Convolvulus arvensis var. sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is a trailing or climbing vine with hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its spread.

This species is considered an invasive weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
