Clusiella isthmensis

Clusiella isthmensis, commonly known as the Isthmian clusiella, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes species known for their showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its humid, tropical habitat.
