Carex glareosa var. ursina

Carex glareosa var. ursina, commonly known as the Urshina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late summer, adding subtle floral interest.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
