Chamaecyparis obtusa f. filicoides

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. filicoides, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress, is a unique variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like foliage that resembles ferns, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant is evergreen and typically grows in a columnar or narrowly conical shape. Its foliage is dense and dark green, with a soft texture that contrasts with its rigid structure.

This cultivar is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
