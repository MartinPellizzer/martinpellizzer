Catasetum × tapiriceps

Catasetum × tapiriceps, commonly known as the tapir's head orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy floral structure. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers with a distinctive, often red or purple, spurred lip that resembles the head of a tapir. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Catasetum × tapiriceps is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
