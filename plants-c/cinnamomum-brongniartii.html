Cinnamomum brongniartii

Cinnamomum brongniartii, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, and is characterized by its aromatic, bark-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Cinnamomum brongniartii is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
