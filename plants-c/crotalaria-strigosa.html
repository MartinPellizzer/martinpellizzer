Crotalaria strigosa

Crotalaria strigosa, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Crotalaria strigosa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
