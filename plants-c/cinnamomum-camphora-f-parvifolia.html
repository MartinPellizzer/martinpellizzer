Cinnamomum camphora f. parvifolia

Cinnamomum camphora f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved camphor tree, is a variant of the camphor laurel native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
