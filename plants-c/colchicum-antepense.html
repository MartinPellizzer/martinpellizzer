Colchicum antepense

Colchicum antepense, commonly known as the Armenian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a short, fibrous rootstock and produces basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, with six reflexed petals and prominent stamens.

Colchicum antepense is notable for containing colchicine, a compound with medicinal and toxic properties.
