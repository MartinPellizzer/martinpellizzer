Cleome procumbens subsp. obtusa

Cleome procumbens subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf cleome, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground, often forming a mat-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
