Cuphea racemosa f. corymbifera

Cuphea racemosa f. corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered cuphea, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, arranged in dense, branching inflorescences. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its unique floral structure also makes it an attractive species for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
