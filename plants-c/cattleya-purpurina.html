Cattleya purpurina

Cattleya purpurina, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red, with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya purpurina is prized in horticulture for its beauty and fragrance, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
