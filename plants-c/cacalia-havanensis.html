Cacalia havanensis

Cacalia havanensis, commonly known as the Havana bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats.

Its botanical structure includes alternate leaves and a taproot system, making it adapted to its native environment.
