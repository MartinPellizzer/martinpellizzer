Crataegus flava trilobata

Crataegus flava trilobata, commonly known as the yellow three-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves, which are typically three-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Crataegus flava trilobata is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
