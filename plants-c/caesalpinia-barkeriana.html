Caesalpinia barkeriana

Caesalpinia barkeriana, commonly known as the Barker's coral tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, flame-colored inflorescences. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Caesalpinia barkeriana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
