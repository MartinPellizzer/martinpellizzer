Corymbia trachyphloia

Corymbia trachyphloia, commonly known as the rough-barked apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Victoria. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
