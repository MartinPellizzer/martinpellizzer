Cucurbita pyxidaris var. flava-major

Cucurbita pyxidaris var. flava-major, commonly known as the yellow crookneck squash, is a variant of the common squash species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, tapering fruit that typically turns from a bright yellow to an orange-red hue as it matures. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its fruits are often harvested when young and tender for culinary use.

This variety is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and versatility in cooking.
