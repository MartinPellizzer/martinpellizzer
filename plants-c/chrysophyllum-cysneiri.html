Chrysophyllum cysneiri

Chrysophyllum cysneiri, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single seed, similar to cherries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
