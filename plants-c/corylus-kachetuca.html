Corylus kachetuca

Corylus kachetuca, commonly known as the Kachetuca hazelnut, is a species of hazel native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high elevations. The plant produces small, round nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which mature in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Corylus kachetuca plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
