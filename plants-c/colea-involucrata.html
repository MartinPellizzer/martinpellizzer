Colea involucrata

Colea involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in composite flower heads. Native to South America, it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its structure and appearance make it a popular ornamental plant in certain regions.
