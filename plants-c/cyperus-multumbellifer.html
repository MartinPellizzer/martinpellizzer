Cyperus multumbellifer

Cyperus multumbellifer, commonly known as the many-umbel sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a flowering structure that consists of multiple umbels. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist soil conditions. Its inflorescence features small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often arranged in multiple umbels, giving it its common name.

Cyperus multumbellifer plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
