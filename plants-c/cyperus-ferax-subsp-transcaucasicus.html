Cyperus ferax subsp. transcaucasicus

Cyperus ferax subsp.

transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Transcaucasian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
