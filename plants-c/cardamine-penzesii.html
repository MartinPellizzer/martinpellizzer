Cardamine penzesii

Cardamine penzesii, commonly known as the Penzes' watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly toothed margin.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.
