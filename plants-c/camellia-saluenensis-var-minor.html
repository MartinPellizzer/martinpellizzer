Camellia saluenensis var. minor

Camellia saluenensis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is typically found in shaded, moist habitats such as mountain forests. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is noted for its resilience to cold temperatures compared to other camellia varieties.
