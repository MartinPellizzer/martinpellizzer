Coelogyne pumila var. recurva

Coelogyne pumila var. recurva, commonly known as the dwarf coelogyne, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest undergrowth. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces small, clustered pseudobulbs that support its foliage. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red hues.

This variety is prized for its compact size and delicate floral display, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
