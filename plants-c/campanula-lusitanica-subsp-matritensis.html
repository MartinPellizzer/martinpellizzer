Campanula lusitanica subsp. matritensis

Campanula lusitanica subsp.

matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
