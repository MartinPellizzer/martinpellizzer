Capparis chrysomeia f. meridionalis

Capparis chrysomeia f. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern yellow caper, is a variant of the yellow caper plant native to southern regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny growth. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
