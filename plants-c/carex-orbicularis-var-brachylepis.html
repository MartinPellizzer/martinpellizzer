Carex orbicularis var. brachylepis

Carex orbicularis var. brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
