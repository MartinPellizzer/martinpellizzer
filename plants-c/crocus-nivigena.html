Crocus nivigena

Crocus nivigena, commonly known as the snow crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold climates. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with six petals, often appearing before the leaves emerge in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and the plant is known for its early blooming habit.

Crocus nivigena plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by providing early nectar sources for pollinators.
