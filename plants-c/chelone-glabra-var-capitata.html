Chelone glabra var. capitata

Chelone glabra var. capitata, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features lance-shaped, smooth, waxy leaves that are typically light green to white in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects its smooth (glabra) leaves and the clustered (capitata) arrangement of its flowers.
