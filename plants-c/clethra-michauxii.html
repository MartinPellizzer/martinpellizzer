Clethra michauxii

Clethra michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its aromatic, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial shade, often found in forest understories. Its showy, clustered flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
