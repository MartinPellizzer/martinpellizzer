Cereus peruvianus var. brasiliensis

Cereus peruvianus var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian night bloomer, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features areoles, which are small clusters of spines, and it produces large, showy flowers that open at night. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a sweet fragrance.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
