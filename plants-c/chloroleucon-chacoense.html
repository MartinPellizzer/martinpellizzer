Chloroleucon chacoense

Chloroleucon chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay, Argentina, and Bolivia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
