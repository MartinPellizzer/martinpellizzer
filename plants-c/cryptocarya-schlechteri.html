Cryptocarya schlechteri

Cryptocarya schlechteri, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in forested areas. Its leaves are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Cryptocarya schlechteri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
