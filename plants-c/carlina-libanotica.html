Carlina libanotica

Carlina libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple-blue flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant produces both ray and disk flowers, with the outer rays being sterile and the inner ones fertile. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Carlina libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
