Cardamine innovans

Cardamine innovans, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in cold, nutrient-poor environments.
