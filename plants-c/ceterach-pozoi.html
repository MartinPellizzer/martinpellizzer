Ceterach pozoi

Ceterach pozoi, commonly known as the mountain cabbage, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Ceterach pozoi plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
