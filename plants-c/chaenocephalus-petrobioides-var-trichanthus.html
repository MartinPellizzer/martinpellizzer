Chaenocephalus petrobioides var. trichanthus

Chaenocephalus petrobioides var. trichanthus, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a variant of the bladderwrack species found in rocky intertidal zones.

It is a brown algae that thrives in shallow, saline waters along coastlines. The plant has a distinctive, flattened thallus with a leathery texture and a bladdery structure that helps it retain water. It plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Its reproductive structures are typically found at the base of the plant, contributing to its propagation in tidal environments.
