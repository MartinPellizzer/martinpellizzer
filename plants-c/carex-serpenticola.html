Carex serpenticola

Carex serpenticola, commonly known as the serpent's tail sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex serpenticola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
