Clematis papuligera

Clematis papuligera, commonly known as the rough clematis, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb supports easily.

Clematis papuligera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
