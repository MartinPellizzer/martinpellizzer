Canna variegata

Canna variegata, commonly known as the variegated canna, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It features large, broad leaves that are often brightly colored with variegation of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna variegata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
