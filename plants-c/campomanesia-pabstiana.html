Campomanesia pabstiana

Campomanesia pabstiana, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing a single seed. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
