Coryphantha Elephantidens Subsp. Bumamma

Coryphantha elephantidens subsp. bumamma is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. Known for its unique, elongated tubercles and vibrant yellow flowers, this plant is a favorite among succulent enthusiasts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for desert gardens. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked due to its subtle appearance and limited distribution. This subspecies is a testament to the adaptability and resilience of desert flora.
