Clusia duartei

Clusia duartei, commonly known as the Duarte's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, also known as the guttiferae, and is characterized by its evergreen nature and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated.

Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
