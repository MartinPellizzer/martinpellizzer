Carex wahuensis subsp. wahuensis

Carex wahuensis subsp.

wahuensis, commonly known as the Wahu sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

This perennial plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its grass-like foliage consists of narrow, sword-shaped leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early to mid-summer.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
