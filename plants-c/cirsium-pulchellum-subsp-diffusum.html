Cirsium pulchellum subsp. diffusum

Cirsium pulchellum subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and riparian areas.

The plant features a robust root system and has spiny, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are purple to pink, appearing in late summer, and are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
