Calceolaria ascendens var. chiloensis

Calceolaria ascendens var. chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that are adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
