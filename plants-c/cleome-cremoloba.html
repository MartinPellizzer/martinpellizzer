Cleome cremoloba

Cleome cremoloba, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant, blue-violet flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall. Cleome cremoloba thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
