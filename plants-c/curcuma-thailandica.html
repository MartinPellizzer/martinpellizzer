Curcuma thailandica

Curcuma thailandica, commonly known as Thai turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and broad, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, fragrant blooms.

The rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to their aromatic properties.
