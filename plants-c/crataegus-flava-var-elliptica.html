Crataegus flava var. elliptica

Crataegus flava var. elliptica, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers and elliptical leaves, which distinguish it from other hawthorn species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the autumn, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
