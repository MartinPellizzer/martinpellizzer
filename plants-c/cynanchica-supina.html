Cynanchica supina

Cynanchica supina, commonly known as the creeping trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that can spread over long distances. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a heart-shaped base.

Cynanchica supina is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
