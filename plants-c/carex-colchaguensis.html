Carex colchaguensis

Carex colchaguensis, commonly known as the Andes sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as mountain meadows and forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex colchaguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
