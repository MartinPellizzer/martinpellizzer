Cardamine chilensis var. nana

Cardamine chilensis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf water crowfoot, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
