Carex ornithopoda var. hausmannii

Carex ornithopoda var. hausmannii, commonly known as the Hausmann's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
