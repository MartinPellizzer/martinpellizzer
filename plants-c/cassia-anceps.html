Cassia anceps

Cassia anceps, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are pinnately compound, with multiple pairs of lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
