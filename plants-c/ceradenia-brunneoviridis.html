Ceradenia brunneoviridis

Ceradenia brunneoviridis, commonly known as the green-brown orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique combination of green and brown hues in its foliage and flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinct coloration pattern that aids in pollination.

Ceradenia brunneoviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
