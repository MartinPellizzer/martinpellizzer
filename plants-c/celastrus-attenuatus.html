Celastrus attenuatus

Celastrus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Celastrus attenuatus is known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a common sight in forest understories.
