Crocus biflorus subsp. ionopharynx

Crocus biflorus subsp.

ionopharynx, commonly known as the Ionian crocus, is a rare and striking member of the Iridaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in Greece and Albania.

This subspecies is characterized by its large, pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, often darker throat. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.
