Crataegus granatensis

Crataegus granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Crataegus granatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
