Croton sarcopetalus var. longipetiolatus

Croton sarcopetalus var. longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage and unique floral structures.

The plant features thick, waxy leaves with a striking coloration, often displaying shades of green, red, and yellow. Its inflorescences are complex, with small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The leaves are typically elongated with long petioles, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its decorative value in tropical gardens.
