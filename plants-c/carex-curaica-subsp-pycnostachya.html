Carex curaica subsp. pycnostachya

Carex curaica subsp.

pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
