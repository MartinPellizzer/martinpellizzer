Callicarpa cuspidata

Callicarpa cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and emits a mild, sweet scent. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are used in traditional medicine.
