Croton lamdongensis

Croton lamdongensis, commonly known as the Lamdong croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often economically important species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton lamdongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
