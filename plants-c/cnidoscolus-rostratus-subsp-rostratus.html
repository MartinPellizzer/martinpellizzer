Cnidoscolus rostratus subsp. rostratus

Cnidoscolus rostratus subsp.

rostratus, commonly known as the spiny leaf huito, is a shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent spines along the margins.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by edible, spiky fruits containing small, starchy seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both a distinctive ornamental plant and a source of traditional food in some regions.
