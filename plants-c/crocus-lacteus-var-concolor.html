Crocus lacteus var. concolor

Crocus lacteus var. concolor, commonly known as the pale crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded corms for storage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
