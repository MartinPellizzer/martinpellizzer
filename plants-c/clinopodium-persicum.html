Clinopodium persicum

Clinopodium persicum, commonly known as Persian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pink or purple flowers are clustered in whorls, typical of the mint family.

Clinopodium persicum is valued for its fragrant foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing and antiseptic properties.
