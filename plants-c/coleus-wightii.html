Coleus wightii

Coleus wightii, commonly known as the wightii coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually clustered in spikes, adding to its visual appeal.
