Centropogon semperflorens

Centropogon semperflorens, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a species native to South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to retain its color and form for an extended period, hence its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
