Cynanchum miserum

Cynanchum miserum, commonly known as the miserable milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, with a distinctive structure that includes a corona surrounding the reproductive parts.

Cynanchum miserum is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
