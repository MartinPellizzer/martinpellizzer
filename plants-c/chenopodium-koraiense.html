Chenopodium koraiense

Chenopodium koraiense, commonly known as Korean goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, dry fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Chenopodium koraiense is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
