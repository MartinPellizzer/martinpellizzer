Carex microglochin subsp. fuegina

Carex microglochin subsp.

fuegina, commonly known as the Falkland Island sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern tip of South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, often saline soils, commonly found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.

The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
