Canarium mahassan

Canarium mahassan, commonly known as the Mahassan nut tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single large seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell. The seeds are valued for their nutritional content and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

This plant thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
