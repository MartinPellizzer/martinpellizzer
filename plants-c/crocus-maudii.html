Crocus maudii

Crocus maudii, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Persia.

It produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge, making it a striking early-flowering species. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Crocus maudii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
