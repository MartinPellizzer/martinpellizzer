Cocos capanemae

Cocos capanemae, commonly known as the Socotra coconut, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra.

This plant belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and large, fibrous fruit. It thrives in the arid and semi-arid environments of its native habitat, adapting to the harsh climate with deep root systems.

The plant's botanical features make it an important part of the local ecosystem and a subject of scientific interest due to its evolutionary adaptations.
