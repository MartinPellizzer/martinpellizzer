Campanula persicifolia var. angustifolia

Campanula persicifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
