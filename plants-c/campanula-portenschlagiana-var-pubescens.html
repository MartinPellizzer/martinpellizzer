Campanula portenschlagiana var. pubescens

Campanula portenschlagiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
