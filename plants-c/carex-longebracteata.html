Carex longebracteata

Carex longebracteata, commonly known as the long bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Carex longebracteata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
