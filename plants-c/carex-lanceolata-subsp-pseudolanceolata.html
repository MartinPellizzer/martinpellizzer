Carex lanceolata subsp. pseudolanceolata

Carex lanceolata subsp.

pseudolanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green and slightly glossy.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
