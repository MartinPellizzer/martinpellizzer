Carex kinabaluensis

Carex kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Borneo, particularly found in the vicinity of Mount Kinabalu in Malaysia. This plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Carex kinabaluensis plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
