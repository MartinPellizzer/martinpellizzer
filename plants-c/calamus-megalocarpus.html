Calamus megalocarpus

Calamus megalocarpus, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its rhizomes are thick and starchy, making it a valuable source of carbohydrates.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible rhizomes, which have a mild, sweet flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
