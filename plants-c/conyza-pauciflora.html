Conyza pauciflora

Conyza pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces pale yellow to white flowers.

Conyza pauciflora is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
