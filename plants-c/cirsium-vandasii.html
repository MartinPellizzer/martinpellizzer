Cirsium vandasii

Cirsium vandasii, commonly known as the Vanda's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, blue to purple flowering heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium vandasii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
