Cyathea alleniae

Cyathea alleniae, commonly known as the Allen's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Cyathea alleniae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
