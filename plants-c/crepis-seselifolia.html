Crepis seselifolia

Crepis seselifolia, commonly known as the slender sow-thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in a single head. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Crepis seselifolia is an annual or biennial herb that is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
