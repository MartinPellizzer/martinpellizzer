Carlina × vayredae nothosubsp. szaferi

Carlina × vayredae nothosubsp.

szaferi, commonly known as the Szafer's carline thistle, is a hybrid plant native to the Pyrenees region. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy composite flower heads.

The plant features silver-white, woolly foliage that is often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow in the center with pink or purple ray florets, creating a striking visual display.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
