Chenopodium petiolare f. reniforme

Chenopodium petiolare f. reniforme, commonly known as the broad-leaf goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant characterized by its distinctive broad, rounded leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The leaves are typically succulent, fleshy, and have a slightly tangy flavor, making it a subject of both horticultural and culinary interest. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
