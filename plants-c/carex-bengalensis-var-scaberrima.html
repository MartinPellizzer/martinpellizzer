Carex bengalensis var. scaberrima

Carex bengalensis var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough carex, is a perennial sedge native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
