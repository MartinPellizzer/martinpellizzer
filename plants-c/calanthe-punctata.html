Calanthe punctata

Calanthe punctata, commonly known as the spotted calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, lanceolate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Calanthe punctata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in shaded garden settings.
