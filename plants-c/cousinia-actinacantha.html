Cousinia actinacantha

Cousinia actinacantha, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces tall, branched stems with clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Cousinia actinacantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
