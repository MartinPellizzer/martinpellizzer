Cleome bahiana

Cleome bahiana, commonly known as Brazilian spider flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse display of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleome bahiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
