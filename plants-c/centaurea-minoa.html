Centaurea minoa

Centaurea minoa, commonly known as the Crete thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and its seed heads are typically bristly and prickly. Centaurea minoa is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
