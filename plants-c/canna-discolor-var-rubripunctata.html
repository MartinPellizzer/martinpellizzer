Canna discolor var. rubripunctata

Canna discolor var. rubripunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge along the veins. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by brightly colored, modified leaves called bracts, which are typically red or orange with contrasting white or yellow spots. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its vibrant foliage and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
