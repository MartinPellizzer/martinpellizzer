Catevala bilineata

Catevala bilineata, commonly known as the two-lined caterpillar fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant features bipinnate fronds with a unique two-lined appearance on the underside, which is a key identifying trait. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered and covered by a protective indusium.

Catevala bilineata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
