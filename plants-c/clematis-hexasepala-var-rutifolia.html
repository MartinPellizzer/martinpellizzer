Clematis hexasepala var. rutifolia

Clematis hexasepala var. rutifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, usually blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with stems that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are actinomorphic, featuring six sepals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
