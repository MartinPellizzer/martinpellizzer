Ceratostylis senilis

Ceratostylis senilis, commonly known as the old man's beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a unique, pendulous inflorescence.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
