Chosenia bracteosa f. adenantha

Chosenia bracteosa f. adenantha, commonly known as the Adenantha reed, is a grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
