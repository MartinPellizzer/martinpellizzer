Centaurea hypericifolia

Centaurea hypericifolia, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or honey flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a perennial herb with a bushy growth habit, often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.

Centaurea hypericifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
