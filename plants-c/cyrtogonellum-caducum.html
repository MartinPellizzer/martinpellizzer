Cyrtogonellum caducum

Cyrtogonellum caducum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
