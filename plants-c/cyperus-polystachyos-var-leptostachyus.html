Cyperus polystachyos var. leptostachyus

Cyperus polystachyos var. leptostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stalked flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
