Carex × bolina

Carex × bolina, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Carex × bolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
