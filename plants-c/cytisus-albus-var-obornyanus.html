Cytisus albus var. obornyanus

Cytisus albus var. obornyanus, commonly known as the white broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
