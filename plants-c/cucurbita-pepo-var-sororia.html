Cucurbita pepo var. sororia

Cucurbita pepo var. sororia, commonly known as the miniature pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often vibrantly colored fruits, which range from yellow to orange and red. This plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It typically grows on trailing vines and produces both male and female flowers.

The fruit is used in culinary applications and as ornamental decoration due to its unique appearance.
