Cissus nodiglandulosa

Cissus nodiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and small, glandular structures on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are often covered in tiny, sticky glands, which give it its scientific name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
