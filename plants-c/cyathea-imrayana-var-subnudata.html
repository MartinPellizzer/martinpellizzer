Cyathea imrayana var. subnudata

Cyathea imrayana var. subnudata, commonly known as the subnudate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, tropical rainforest environments, where it thrives in shaded, moist conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the fronds are sometimes utilized in local crafts.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced stipe and less hairy fronds compared to other varieties of Cyathea imrayana.
