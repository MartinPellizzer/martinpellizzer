Crataegus relicta

Crataegus relicta, commonly known as the relic hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
