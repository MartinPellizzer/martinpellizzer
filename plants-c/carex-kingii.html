Carex kingii

Carex kingii, commonly known as the King's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Carex kingii is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
