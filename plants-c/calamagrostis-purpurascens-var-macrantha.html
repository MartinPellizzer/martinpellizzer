Calamagrostis purpurascens var. macrantha

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. macrantha, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of purple or pinkish flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced in the autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
