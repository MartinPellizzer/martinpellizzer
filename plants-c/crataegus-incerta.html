Crataegus incerta

Crataegus incerta, commonly known as the uncertain hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Crataegus incerta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
