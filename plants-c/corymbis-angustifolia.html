Corymbis angustifolia

Corymbis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf corymbis, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Corymbis angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
