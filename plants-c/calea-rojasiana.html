Calea rojasiana

Calea rojasiana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea rojasiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
