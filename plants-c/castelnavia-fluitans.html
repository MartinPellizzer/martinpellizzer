Castelnavia fluitans

Castelnavia fluitans, commonly known as the floating castle vine, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its trailing, vine-like growth habit.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often floating just above the water's surface. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and float on the water, aiding in photosynthesis.

Castelnavia fluitans is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens for its visual appeal.
