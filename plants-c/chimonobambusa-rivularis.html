Chimonobambusa rivularis

Chimonobambusa rivularis, commonly known as the river bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its culms, or stems, are hollow and exhibit a smooth, pale surface.

Chimonobambusa rivularis is often found in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and in mountainous regions.
