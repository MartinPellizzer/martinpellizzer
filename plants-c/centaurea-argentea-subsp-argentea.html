Centaurea argentea subsp. argentea

Centaurea argentea subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive purple or red center, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
