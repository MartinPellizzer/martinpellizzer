Cattleya grahamii

Cattleya grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It blooms once a year, typically during the summer months.

Cattleya grahamii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouse environments.
