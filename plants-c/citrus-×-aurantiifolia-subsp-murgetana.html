Citrus × aurantiifolia subsp. murgetana

Citrus × aurantiifolia subsp.

murgetana, commonly known as the Mexican lime or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It produces small, green, fragrant fruits with a high acidity and a distinctive aromatic zest.

The leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, often with a serrated edge, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates. It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking, beverages, and traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and culinary traditions.
