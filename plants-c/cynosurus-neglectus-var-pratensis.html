Cynosurus neglectus var. pratensis

Cynosurus neglectus var. pratensis, commonly known as meadow hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.
