Coris monspeliensis subsp. monspeliensis

Coris monspeliensis subsp.

monspeliensis, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and oval-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
