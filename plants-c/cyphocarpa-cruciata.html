Cyphocarpa cruciata

Cyphocarpa cruciata, commonly known as the crossberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the humid regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, contributing to its ornamental value.
