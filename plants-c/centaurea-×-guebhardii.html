Centaurea × guebhardii

Centaurea × guebhardii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
