Caryodaphne browniana var. ferruginea

Caryodaphne browniana var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leafed caryodaphne, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically reddish-brown to rusty in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in hue, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.
