Conyza purpurascens

Conyza purpurascens, commonly known as the purple weed or purple coneflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are typically arranged in a composite head.

Conyza purpurascens is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
