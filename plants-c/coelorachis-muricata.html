Coelorachis muricata

Coelorachis muricata, commonly known as the spiny bamboo or hairy bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive, hairy nodes along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, textured surface.

Coelorachis muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
