Caladenia iridescens

Caladenia iridescens, commonly known as the Rainbow Orchid, is an orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, iris-like flowers that display a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white, often with a metallic sheen. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it blooms during the cooler months of spring and autumn.

The species is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral displays.
