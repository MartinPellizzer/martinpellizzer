Costus guianicus

Costus guianicus, commonly known as the Guianan costus, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Costus guianicus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.
