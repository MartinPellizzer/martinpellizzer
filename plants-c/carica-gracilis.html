Carica gracilis

Carica gracilis, commonly known as the slender papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated fruit that resembles a small papaya. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The fruit is edible, containing a soft, sweet flesh surrounded by a fibrous core.
