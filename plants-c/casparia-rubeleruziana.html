Casparia rubeleruziana

Casparia rubeleruziana, commonly known as the Crimson Spine Fern, is a rare and unique species of fern characterized by its striking, deeply lobed fronds.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a distinctive reddish hue on its young fronds, which fades to a deep green as it matures. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the undersides of its leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
