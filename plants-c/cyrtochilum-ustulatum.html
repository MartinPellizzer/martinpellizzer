Cyrtochilum ustulatum

Cyrtochilum ustulatum, commonly known as the scorched orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Cyrtochilum ustulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
