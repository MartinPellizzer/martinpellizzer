Chrysopsis camporum

Chrysopsis camporum, commonly known as the Camp's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its yellow flowers are small and daisy-like, often appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Chrysopsis camporum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
