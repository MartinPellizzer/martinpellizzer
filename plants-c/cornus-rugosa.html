Cornus rugosa

Cornus rugosa, commonly known as rough fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough, sandpapery texture on the upper surface.

Cornus rugosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
