Cerastium arvense var. alpicola

Cerastium arvense var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
