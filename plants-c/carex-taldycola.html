Carex taldycola

Carex taldycola, commonly known as the Taldykol sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Carex taldycola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
