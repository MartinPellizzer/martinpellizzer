Canavalia cuspidigera

Canavalia cuspidigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and pointed, and its distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, spiny husk. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Canavalia cuspidigera is also valued for its potential use in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
