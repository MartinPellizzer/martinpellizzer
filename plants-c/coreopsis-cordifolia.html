Coreopsis cordifolia

Coreopsis cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules after flowering.

Coreopsis cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
