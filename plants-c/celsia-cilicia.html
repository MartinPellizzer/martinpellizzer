Celsia cilicia

Celsia cilicia, commonly known as the Cilician bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features slender stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Celsia cilicia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
