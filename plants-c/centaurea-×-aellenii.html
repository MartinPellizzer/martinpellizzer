Centaurea × aellenii

Centaurea × aellenii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × aellenii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
