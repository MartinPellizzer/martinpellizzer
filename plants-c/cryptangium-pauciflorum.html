Cryptangium pauciflorum

Cryptangium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered cryptangium, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia, typically found in heathlands and dry forests. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in dense clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, a characteristic feature of the genus.
