Carduus olympicus

Carduus olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine environments. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a single, showy head.

The plant is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
