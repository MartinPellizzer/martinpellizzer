Carex jamesonii var. chordalis

Carex jamesonii var. chordalis, commonly known as the chordate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are typical of the sedge family.
