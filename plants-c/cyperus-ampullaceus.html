Cyperus ampullaceus

Cyperus ampullaceus, commonly known as the bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus ampullaceus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, and is often found in areas with standing water.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
