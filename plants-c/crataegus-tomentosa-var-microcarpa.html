Crataegus tomentosa var. microcarpa

Crataegus tomentosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.

The variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
