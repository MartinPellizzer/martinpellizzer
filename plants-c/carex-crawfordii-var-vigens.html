Carex crawfordii var. vigens

Carex crawfordii var. vigens, commonly known as the vigorous sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower spikes. This variety is known for its dense, grassy foliage and ability to thrive in moist to wet soils. It often grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
