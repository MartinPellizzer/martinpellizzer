Crinum firmifolium var. geophilum

Crinum firmifolium var. geophilum, commonly known as the rock crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels at the top of tall, narrow stems. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
