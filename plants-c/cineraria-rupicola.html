Cineraria rupicola

Cineraria rupicola, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces vibrant blue or violet flowers.

Cineraria rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
