Convolvulus hewittaceus

Convolvulus hewittaceus, commonly known as the Hewitt's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals or through wind.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
