Crataegus intricata var. horseyi

Crataegus intricata var. horseyi, commonly known as the Horsey's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
