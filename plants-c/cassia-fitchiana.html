Cassia fitchiana

Cassia fitchiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia fitchiana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged along a central stem.
