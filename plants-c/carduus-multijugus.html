Carduus multijugus

Carduus multijugus, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a stout, branching stem covered in soft, woolly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a loose, branched cluster, producing small, oval-shaped fruits known as achene.
