Coccoloba incrassata

Coccoloba incrassata, commonly known as the broadleaf spinach or red mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a reddish or purplish hue. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline environments, making it a halophyte. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in green to red bracts.

Coccoloba incrassata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
