Cynanchum subulatum

Cynanchum subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. The plant belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is known for its milky sap. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.
