Cattleya sincorana

Cattleya sincorana, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded environment. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

Cattleya sincorana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beautiful, fragrant blooms.
