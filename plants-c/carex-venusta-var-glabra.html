Carex venusta var. glabra

Carex venusta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with smooth, cylindrical stems that often grow in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and slightly pointed, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Carex venusta var. glabra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
