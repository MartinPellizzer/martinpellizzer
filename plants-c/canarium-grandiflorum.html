Canarium grandiflorum

Canarium grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and large, showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a hard, oily seed known as candlenut, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and economic importance in local communities.
