Carduus chrysacanthus var. leucanthus

Carduus chrysacanthus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, globe-shaped flower heads composed of yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense layer of white or pale yellow disk florets. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
