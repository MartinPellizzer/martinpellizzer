Cassia xanthadena

Cassia xanthadena, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cassia xanthadena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
