Corydalis sibirica subsp. elata

Corydalis sibirica subsp.

elata, commonly known as the tall Siberian corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-purple flowers with two upper petals that are often reflexed. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
