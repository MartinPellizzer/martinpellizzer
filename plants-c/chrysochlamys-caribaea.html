Chrysochlamys caribaea

Chrysochlamys caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean goldenbush, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
