Cistus atriplicifolius subsp. macrocalycinus

Cistus atriplicifolius subsp.

macrocalycinus, commonly known as the large-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
