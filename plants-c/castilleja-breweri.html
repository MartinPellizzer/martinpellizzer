Castilleja breweri

Castilleja breweri, commonly known as the Brewster's paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients through its roots. The plant produces showy, red-orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often obscured by the dense inflorescence.

Castilleja breweri thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
