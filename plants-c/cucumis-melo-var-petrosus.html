Cucumis melo var. petrosus

Cucumis melo var. petrosus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruits with a smooth, pale green skin. The plant typically grows as a vine, producing both male and female flowers on separate stems. The fruit is usually harvested when it reaches maturity, which is indicated by a change in color and texture.

It is cultivated for its crisp, edible flesh, which has a mild, refreshing flavor.
