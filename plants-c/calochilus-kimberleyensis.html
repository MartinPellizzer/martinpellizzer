Calochilus kimberleyensis

Calochilus kimberleyensis, commonly known as the Kimberley spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like floral structure with long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow column. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the region's seasonal rainfall patterns. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a contrasting red or orange lip.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
