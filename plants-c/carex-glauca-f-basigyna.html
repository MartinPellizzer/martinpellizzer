Carex glauca f. basigyna

Carex glauca f. basigyna, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
