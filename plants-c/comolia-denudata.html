Comolia denudata

Comolia denudata, commonly known as the naked comolia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
