Cyperus fresenii

Cyperus fresenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus fresenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
