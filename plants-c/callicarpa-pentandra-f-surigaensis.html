Callicarpa pentandra f. surigaensis

Callicarpa pentandra f. surigaensis, commonly known as the Surigaon mango or Surigaon callicarpa, is a variant of the Chinese lantern plant.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Philippines, particularly in the province of Surigao del Sur. The plant is characterized by its clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped fruits that turn orange when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
