Cattleya velutina var. punctata

Cattleya velutina var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted velvet orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and striking, fragrant flowers with a unique spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically produces large, showy blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ornamental value.
