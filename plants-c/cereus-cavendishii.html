Cereus cavendishii

Cereus cavendishii, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit, producing large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they emit a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

Due to its unique flowering pattern, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare plants.
