Calamus salicifolius

Calamus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow, willow-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus salicifolius is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
