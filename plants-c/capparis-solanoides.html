Capparis solanoides

Capparis solanoides, commonly known as the common caper, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, round, yellowish fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Capparis solanoides thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
