Cytisus balansae

Cytisus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine, hairy textures.

Cytisus balansae thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the mild, maritime climate of its native region.
