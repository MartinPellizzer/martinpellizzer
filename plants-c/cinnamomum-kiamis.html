Cinnamomum kiamis

Cinnamomum kiamis, commonly known as Kiamis cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval-shaped berries containing a single seed. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Cinnamomum kiamis is considered a threatened species.
