Chamaecrista incurvata var. zanclodes

Chamaecrista incurvata var. zanclodes, commonly known as the Zanclodes burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the legume family, Fabaceae.
