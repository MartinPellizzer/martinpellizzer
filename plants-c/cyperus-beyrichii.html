Cyperus beyrichii

Cyperus beyrichii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Cyperus beyrichii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
