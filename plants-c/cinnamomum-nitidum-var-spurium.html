Cinnamomum nitidum var. spurium

Cinnamomum nitidum var. spurium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
