Cucumis sativus var. donii

Cucumis sativus var. donii, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of cucumber native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender shape and thin, delicate skin. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is usually harvested when it is still immature, before it fully matures and turns yellow.
