Carex inclusa

Carex inclusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Native to wetland habitats in North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex inclusa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
