Chenopodium × subhastatum

Chenopodium × subhastatum, commonly known as buffalo spinach, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor and are used in various culinary traditions.
