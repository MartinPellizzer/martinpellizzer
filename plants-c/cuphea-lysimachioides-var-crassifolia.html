Cuphea lysimachioides var. crassifolia

Cuphea lysimachioides var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, often growing in moist, well-drained soils in partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract hummingbirds.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its role in native plant landscaping.
