Crataegus oakesiana

Crataegus oakesiana, commonly known as the Oakes' hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems.
