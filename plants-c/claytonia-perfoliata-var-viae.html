Claytonia perfoliata var. viae

Claytonia perfoliata var. viae, commonly known as the "Snow-in-Summer," is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
