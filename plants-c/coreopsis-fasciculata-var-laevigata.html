Coreopsis fasciculata var. laevigata

Coreopsis fasciculata var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving the plant its varietal name.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens and wildflower landscapes.
