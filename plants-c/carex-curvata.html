Carex curvata

Carex curvata, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that curve upward, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex curvata produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.
