Coelogyne coccinea

Coelogyne coccinea, commonly known as the crimson lady orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces aerial roots for attachment to trees. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments with indirect sunlight.

Coelogyne coccinea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
