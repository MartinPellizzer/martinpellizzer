Clinopodium tomentosum

Clinopodium tomentosum, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are densely covered with white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also covered in a soft, fuzzy coating. It produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers during the summer months.

This plant is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
