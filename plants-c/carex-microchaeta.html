Carex microchaeta

Carex microchaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a fibrous root system. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces distinctive three-sided, triangular seeds.

Carex microchaeta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
