Chrysopsis ericoides

Chrysopsis ericoides, commonly known as the heath goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage consists of narrow, evergreen leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Chrysopsis ericoides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
