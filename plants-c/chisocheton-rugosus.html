Chisocheton rugosus

Chisocheton rugosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved chisocheton, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its rugged, textured bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland rainforests.
