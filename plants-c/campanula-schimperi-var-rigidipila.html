Campanula schimperi var. rigidipila

Campanula schimperi var. rigidipila, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highland regions of Ethiopia.

It features upright, rigid stems that are often hairy, supporting clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
