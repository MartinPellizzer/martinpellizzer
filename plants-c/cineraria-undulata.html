Cineraria undulata

Cineraria undulata, commonly known as the wavy cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cineraria undulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
