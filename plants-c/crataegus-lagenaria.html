Crataegus lagenaria

Crataegus lagenaria, commonly known as the balloon bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, orange-red fruits that resemble small balloons. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its showy flowers appear in spring, followed by the unique fruiting bodies that attract birds and other wildlife.

The balloon bush is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
