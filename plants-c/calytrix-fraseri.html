Calytrix fraseri

Calytrix fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is known for its dense, cylindrical flower clusters. Calytrix fraseri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
