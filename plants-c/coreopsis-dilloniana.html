Coreopsis dilloniana

Coreopsis dilloniana, commonly known as the Dillon's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in open woods and along roadsides. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis dilloniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
