Cymbopogon mandalaiaensis

Cymbopogon mandalaiaensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its tall, cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique fragrance and potential medicinal properties, it has attracted interest for cultivation and research.
