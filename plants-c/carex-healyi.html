Carex healyi

Carex healyi, commonly known as Healy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and triangular stems, typical of many sedges. Native to specific regions of North America, particularly the western United States, it thrives in moist to wet soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
