Croton sclerocalyx var. pubescens

Croton sclerocalyx var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cyathium, a typical structure for the genus Croton. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its succulent nature, it is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
