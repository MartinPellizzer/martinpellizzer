Cerasus avium proles juliana

Cerasus avium proles juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or purple berries in summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
