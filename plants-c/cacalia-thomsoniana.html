Cacalia thomsoniana

Cacalia thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical seed heads after blooming.

Thomson's bellflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
