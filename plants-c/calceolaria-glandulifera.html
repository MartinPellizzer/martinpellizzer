Calceolaria glandulifera

Calceolaria glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular calceolaria, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in montane regions. Its leaves are usually glandular and hairy, contributing to its unique appearance.

The flowers, which are bilaterally symmetrical, come in shades of red, pink, and white, attracting various pollinators.
