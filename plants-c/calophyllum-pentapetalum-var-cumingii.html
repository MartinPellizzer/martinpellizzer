Calophyllum pentapetalum var. cumingii

Calophyllum pentapetalum var. cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish-orange fruits with a thick, fibrous rind. The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
