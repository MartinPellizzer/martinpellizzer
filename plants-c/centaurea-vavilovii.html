Centaurea vavilovii

Centaurea vavilovii, commonly known as Vavilov's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, with the outer rays being the most prominent.

Centaurea vavilovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
