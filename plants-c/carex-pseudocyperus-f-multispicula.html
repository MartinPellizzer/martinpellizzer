Carex pseudocyperus f. multispicula

Carex pseudocyperus f. multispicula, commonly known as the many-spiked sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Carex, characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread and survival in wetland habitats.

This form is distinguished by its multiple spicules, which contribute to its specific taxonomic classification.
