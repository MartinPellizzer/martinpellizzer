Celtis sinensis f. rotundata

Celtis sinensis f. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaf Chinese laurustin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical to round leaves that are typically smooth on both sides. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round drupes that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
