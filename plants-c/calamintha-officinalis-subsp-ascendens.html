Calamintha officinalis subsp. ascendens

Calamintha officinalis subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as ascending caraway, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruits, known as seeds, are small, ribbed, and have a distinctive caraway-like aroma.

The subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
