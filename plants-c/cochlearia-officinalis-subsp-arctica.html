Cochlearia officinalis subsp. arctica

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic kidneyweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It typically features rosettes of silver-white, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in cold, dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary strategies for survival in extreme northern habitats.
