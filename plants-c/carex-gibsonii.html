Carex gibsonii

Carex gibsonii, commonly known as Gibson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

Carex gibsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
