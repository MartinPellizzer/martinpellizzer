Cedrus libanotica var. glauca

Cedrus libanotica var. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the Lebanon cedar.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and striking blue-gray foliage. The leaves are short, stiff, and scale-like, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
