Cerasus laurocerasus var. caucasica

Cerasus laurocerasus var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its flowers are typically white or pink, blooming in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
