Chenopodium caudatum

Chenopodium caudatum, commonly known as wild spinach or lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dry, three-angled seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Chenopodium caudatum is valued for its edible leaves and has been used traditionally in various cuisines.
