Celeri graveolens

Celeri graveolens, commonly known as celery root or celeriac, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, knobby, white or yellowish underground stem, which is the primary edible part. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically harvested in the first year before the root matures. It thrives in cool climates and requires a long growing season to develop its distinctive root.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various culinary applications.
