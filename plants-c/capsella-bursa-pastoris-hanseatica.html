Capsella bursa-pastoris hanseatica

Capsella bursa-pastoris hanseatica, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with smooth, hairy, or glandular stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its fruits are distinctive spherical capsules, often referred to as "purses" due to their shape, containing numerous tiny seeds.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
