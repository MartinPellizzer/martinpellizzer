Crotalaria guianensis

Crotalaria guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese crown flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a crown-like structure. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces pods that contain elliptical seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its ornamental value.

Crotalaria guianensis also has a history of use in traditional medicine.
