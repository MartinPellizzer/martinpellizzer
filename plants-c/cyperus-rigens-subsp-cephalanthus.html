Cyperus rigens subsp. cephalanthus

Cyperus rigens subsp.

cephalanthus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, typically with purple or brown flowers. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along waterways.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
