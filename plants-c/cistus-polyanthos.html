Cistus polyanthos

Cistus polyanthos, commonly known as the sweet rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Cistus polyanthos is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
