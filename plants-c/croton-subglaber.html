Croton subglaber

Croton subglaber, commonly known as the smooth croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage.
