Conyza fruticulosa

Conyza fruticulosa, commonly known as the hairy weed or false dandelion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, which are easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Conyza fruticulosa is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
