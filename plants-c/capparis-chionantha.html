Capparis chionantha

Capparis chionantha, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale blue or white flowers and its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis chionantha thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
