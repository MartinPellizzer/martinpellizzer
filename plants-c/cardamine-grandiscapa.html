Cardamine grandiscapa

Cardamine grandiscapa, commonly known as the large-flowered water-cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forested areas. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and often have a peppery aroma. Its small, white or greenish flowers are borne on slender, branching stems and are arranged in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional herbal medicine.
