Cactus reticulatus

Cactus reticulatus, commonly known as the netted cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that are often covered in a dense, net-like pattern of tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its adaptations include thick, waxy skin to reduce water loss and the ability to store water in its stems.

The netted cactus is often used in traditional medicine and is a popular plant for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
