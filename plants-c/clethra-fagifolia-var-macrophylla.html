Clethra fagifolia var. macrophylla

Clethra fagifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are large, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with serrated edges. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland restoration projects.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
