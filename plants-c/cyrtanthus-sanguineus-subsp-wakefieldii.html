Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp. wakefieldii

Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp.

wakefieldii, commonly known as the Wakefield's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red and orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Iris family, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
