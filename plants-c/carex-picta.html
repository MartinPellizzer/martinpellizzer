Carex picta

Carex picta, commonly known as the painted sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dark green leaves with a distinctive white or silver stripe running down the midrib. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
