Cuphea micrantha var. longeciliata

Cuphea micrantha var. longeciliata, commonly known as the long-stalked cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The unique floral structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
