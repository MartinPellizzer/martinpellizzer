Corybas umbonatus

Corybas umbonatus, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family with a distinctive, helmet-shaped flower.

It is native to Australia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, showy flower that is often pink or purple in color. Its specialized structure includes a labellum that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

The species is adapted to specific ecological niches, making it a rare and ecologically significant plant.
