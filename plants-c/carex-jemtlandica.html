Carex jemtlandica

Carex jemtlandica, commonly known as Jemtland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Carex jemtlandica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
