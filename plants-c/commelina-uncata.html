Commelina uncata

Commelina uncata, commonly known as the slender spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Commelina uncata is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
