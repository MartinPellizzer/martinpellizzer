Cuphea divaricata

Cuphea divaricata, commonly known as the flame bean or fire bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and has a sprawling growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
