Crinum anomalum

Crinum anomalum, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is part of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and economically important species. Crinum anomalum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
