Commiphora reghinii

Commiphora reghinii, commonly known as the Somali myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for incense and medicinal purposes. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in coastal regions.
