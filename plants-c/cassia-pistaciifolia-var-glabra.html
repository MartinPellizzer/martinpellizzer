Cassia pistaciifolia var. glabra

Cassia pistaciifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, yellowish inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia pistaciifolia var. glabra prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
