Clematis texensis var. major

Clematis texensis var. major, commonly known as the Texas Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
