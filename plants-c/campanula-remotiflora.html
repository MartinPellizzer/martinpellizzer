Campanula remotiflora

Campanula remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces elongated stems that bear its characteristic flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula remotiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
