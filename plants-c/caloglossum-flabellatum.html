Caloglossum flabellatum

It is characterized by its translucent, fan-like structure composed of delicate, feathery tentacles that surround a central mouth. The organism lacks a true skeleton and instead relies on a flexible mesogloea for structural support. It is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches itself to rocky substrates.
