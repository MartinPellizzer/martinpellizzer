Clompanus villosa

Clompanus villosa, commonly known as the hairy clompanus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clompanus villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
