Carex polyrrhiza f. brevifolia

Carex polyrrhiza f. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved basket sedge, is a variant of the basket sedge species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in freshwater habitats. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its survival in waterlogged conditions.

The inflorescence consists of several spikelets, which are often green or brown in color, depending on the stage of development.
