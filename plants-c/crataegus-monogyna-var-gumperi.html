Crataegus monogyna var. gumperi

Crataegus monogyna var. gumperi, commonly known as the Gumper's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
