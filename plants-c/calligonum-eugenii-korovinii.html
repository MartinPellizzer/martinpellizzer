Calligonum eugenii-korovinii

Calligonum eugenii-korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Its resilience makes it an important species for stabilizing desert ecosystems.
