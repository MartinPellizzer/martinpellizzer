Crataegus oxyacantha var. elegans

Crataegus oxyacantha var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting.
