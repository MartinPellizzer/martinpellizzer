Calea purpusii

Calea purpusii, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calea purpusii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
