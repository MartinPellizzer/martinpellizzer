Cyathea borbonica var. laevigata

Cyathea borbonica var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically smooth and glossy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
