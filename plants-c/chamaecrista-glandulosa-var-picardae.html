Chamaecrista glandulosa var. picardae

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. picardae, commonly known as the picard's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along waterways, thriving in moist, sandy soils.

Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
