Clymenia polyandra

Clymenia polyandra, commonly known as the evergreen boxwood, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Clymenia polyandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture for its ability to form compact, bushy shapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.
