Christiana mennegae

Christiana mennegae, commonly known as the Texas bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern regions of Texas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The Texas bluebonnet is an important symbol of Texas and is the state flower.
