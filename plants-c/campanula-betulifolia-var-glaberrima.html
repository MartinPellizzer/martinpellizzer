Campanula betulifolia var. glaberrima

Campanula betulifolia var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
