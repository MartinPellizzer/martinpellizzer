Crataegus curvisepala subsp. lindmanii

Crataegus curvisepala subsp.

lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure.
