Carex cespitosa var. waisbeckeri

Carex cespitosa var. waisbeckeri, commonly known as Waisbecker's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
