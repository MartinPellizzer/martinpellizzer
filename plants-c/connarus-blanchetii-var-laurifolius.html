Connarus blanchetii var. laurifolius

Connarus blanchetii var. laurifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf connarus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
