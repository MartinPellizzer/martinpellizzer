Carex leersii var. angustifolia

Carex leersii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
