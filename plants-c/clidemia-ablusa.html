Clidemia ablusa

Clidemia ablusa, commonly known as silverleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Clidemia ablusa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
