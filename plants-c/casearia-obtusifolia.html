Casearia obtusifolia

Casearia obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
