Cigdemia pisidica

Cigdemia pisidica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Crimea region of Ukraine.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cigdemia pisidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
