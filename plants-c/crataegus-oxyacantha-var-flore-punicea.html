Crataegus oxyacantha var. flore-punicea

Crataegus oxyacantha var. flore-punicea, commonly known as the red flowered hawthorn, is a variety of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of ovate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The fruit is a red, juicy pome that ripens in the fall and is often used in jams and culinary applications.
