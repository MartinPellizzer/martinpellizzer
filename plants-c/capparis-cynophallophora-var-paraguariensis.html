Capparis cynophallophora var. paraguariensis

Capparis cynophallophora var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan sour apple, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and fleshy, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in local cuisines. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

The fruit contains a single seed and is known for its tart, acidic flavor.
