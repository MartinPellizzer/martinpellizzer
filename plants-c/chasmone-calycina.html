Chasmone calycina

Chasmone calycina, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage is lance-shaped and glossy, adding ornamental value to its floral display.

Chasmone calycina is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
