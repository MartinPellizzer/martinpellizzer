Clusia diguensis

Clusia diguensis, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, often blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Clusia diguensis is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to drought.
