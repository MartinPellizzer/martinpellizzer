Cuphea aspera

Cuphea aspera, commonly known as the rough cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers come in shades of red, pink, and purple, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
