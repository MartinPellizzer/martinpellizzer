Cleome arabica var. lutea

Cleome arabica var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spiders due to their elongated petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and branching.

Cleome arabica var. lutea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
