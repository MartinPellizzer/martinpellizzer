Chrysogonum leandrii

Chrysogonum leandrii, commonly known as the goldenstar, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
