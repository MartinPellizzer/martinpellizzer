Conringia alpina

Conringia alpina, commonly known as the alpine bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often modified into bladder-like structures that can trap small aquatic organisms.

Conringia alpina is an important part of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and serving as a food source for various invertebrates.
