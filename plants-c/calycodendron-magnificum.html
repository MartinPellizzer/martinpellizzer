Calycodendron magnificum

Calycodendron magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent calycodendron, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a woody structure with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
