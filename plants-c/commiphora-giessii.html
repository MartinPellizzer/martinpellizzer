Commiphora giessii

Commiphora giessii, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color, and are followed by small, woody fruits.
