Cissus spinosopilosa

Cissus spinosopilosa, commonly known as the spiny leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface, and they often have small spines along the edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
