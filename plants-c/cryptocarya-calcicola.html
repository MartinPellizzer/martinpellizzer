Cryptocarya calcicola

Cryptocarya calcicola, commonly known as the limestone cryptocarya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. This plant is adapted to grow in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or coastal environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
