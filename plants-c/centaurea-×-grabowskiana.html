Centaurea × grabowskiana

Centaurea × grabowskiana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to red flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into early autumn.
