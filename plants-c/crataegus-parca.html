Crataegus parca

Crataegus parca, commonly known as the sparse thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Crataegus parca is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
