Combretum mellifluum var. cuspidatum

Combretum mellifluum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the dry season. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive prolonged droughts.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
