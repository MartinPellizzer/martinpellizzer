Cymbidium flavum

Cymbidium flavum, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate patterns and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cymbidium flavum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
