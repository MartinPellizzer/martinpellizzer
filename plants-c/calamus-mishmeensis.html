Calamus mishmeensis

Calamus mishmeensis, commonly known as the Mishme reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Indian states of Assam and Manipur. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus mishmeensis plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
