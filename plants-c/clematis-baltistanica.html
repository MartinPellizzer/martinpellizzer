Clematis baltistanica

Clematis baltistanica, commonly known as the Baltistan clematis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with slender, elongated leaflets, and it has a climbing habit due to its twining stems.

Clematis baltistanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
