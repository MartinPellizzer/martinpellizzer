Carex oligocarpa var. sartwelliana

Carex oligocarpa var. sartwelliana, commonly known as Sartwell's sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

This variety is often found in the northeastern United States and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
