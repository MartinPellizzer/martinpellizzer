Coppensia

Coppensia, commonly known as the bluebell or bluebell flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Coppensia has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant blooms during the spring season.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
