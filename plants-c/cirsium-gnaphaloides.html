Cirsium gnaphaloides

Cirsium gnaphaloides, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

The plant has a robust root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
