Cucumis trifoliatus

Cucumis trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaf cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets, and produces small, yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. Its fruit is a small, elongated cucumber-like fruit that is usually green and edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
