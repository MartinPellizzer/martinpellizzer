Cirsium × jaubertianum

Cirsium × jaubertianum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
