Cucumis dissectus

Cucumis dissectus, commonly known as the dissected cucumber, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely lobed leaves and small, pale green fruits that resemble miniature cucumbers. Native to the arid regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in hot, dry climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a trailing growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticultural researchers.
