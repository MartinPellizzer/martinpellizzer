Chassalia subcordatifolia

Chassalia subcordatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.
