Carroa diffusa

Carroa diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in humid environments. Its stems are typically erect and can grow up to several feet tall.

Carroa diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
