Crossopetalum ilicifolium

Crossopetalum ilicifolium, commonly known as the fragrant leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that resemble those of the holly plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
