Commelina filiformis

Commelina filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically blue or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
