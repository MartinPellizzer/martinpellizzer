Chenopodium berlandieri f. integrum

Chenopodium berlandieri f. integrum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, in fields, and near riverbanks. Its seeds are small and dry, often dispersed by wind, and it is considered an annual or biennial species.

It has been used historically by Native American tribes for food and medicinal purposes.
