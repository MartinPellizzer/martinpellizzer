Cyrtochilum decolorans

Cyrtochilum decolorans, commonly known as the decoloring orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, though they may fade over time. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Cyrtochilum decolorans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
