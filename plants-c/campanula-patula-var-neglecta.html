Campanula patula var. neglecta

Campanula patula var. neglecta, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Campanula patula var. neglecta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
