Chrysophae

Chrysophae, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. Chrysophae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
