Corydalis longicalcarata var. nonsaccata

Corydalis longicalcarata var. nonsaccata, commonly known as the long-beaked corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
