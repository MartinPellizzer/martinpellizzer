Cheiropsis balearica

Cheiropsis balearica, commonly known as the Balearic rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the Balearic Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. It is often found growing on coastal cliffs and limestone outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and botanical study.
