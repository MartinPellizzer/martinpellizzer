Calceolaria saxatilis

Calceolaria saxatilis, commonly known as the rock calceolaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique bilabiate flowers, which resemble small cups or pouches, and are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Calceolaria saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
