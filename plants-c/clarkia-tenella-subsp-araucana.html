Clarkia tenella subsp. araucana

Clarkia tenella subsp.

araucana, commonly known as the Araucana Clarkia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and striking, bicolor flowers that range from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
