Cicer microphyllum

Cicer microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaf vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Cicer microphyllum is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
