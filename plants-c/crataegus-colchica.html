Crataegus colchica

Crataegus colchica, commonly known as the Colchian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, containing several seeds. This plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, often found in mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
