Clinopodium baeticum

Clinopodium baeticum, commonly known as Spanish lavender or mountain lavender, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Clinopodium baeticum thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
