Cestrum miersianum

Cestrum miersianum, commonly known as the Miers' nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cestrum miersianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
