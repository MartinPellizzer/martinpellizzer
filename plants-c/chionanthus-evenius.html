Chionanthus evenius

Chionanthus evenius, commonly known as the whiteflower ash or Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Chionanthus evenius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant floral display.
