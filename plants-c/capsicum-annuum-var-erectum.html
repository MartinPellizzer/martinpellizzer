Capsicum annuum var. erectum

Capsicum annuum var. erectum, commonly known as the bird pepper or erect capsicum, is a variety of chili pepper characterized by its upright growth habit.

It produces small, often yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. The plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its mild to moderate heat. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include simple leaves and a compact, bushy structure that makes it suitable for container gardening.
