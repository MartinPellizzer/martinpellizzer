Cassebeeria axillaris

Cassebeeria axillaris, commonly known as the axillary cassia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods that split open when mature.

Cassebeeria axillaris is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
