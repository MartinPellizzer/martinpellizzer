Cochlearia officinalis

Cochlearia officinalis, commonly known as the common cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in coastal areas and rocky soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
