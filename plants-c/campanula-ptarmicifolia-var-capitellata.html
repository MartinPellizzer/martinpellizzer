Campanula ptarmicifolia var. capitellata

Campanula ptarmicifolia var. capitellata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
