Cytisus insularis

Cytisus insularis, commonly known as the Socotra caper, is a flowering shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Cytisus insularis is notable for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native environment.
