Centaurea carduiformis var. thrinciifolia

Centaurea carduiformis var. thrinciifolia, commonly known as the Thrincian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles, with bracts that are often tinged with red. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
