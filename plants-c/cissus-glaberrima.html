Cissus glaberrima

Cissus glaberrima, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Cissus glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
