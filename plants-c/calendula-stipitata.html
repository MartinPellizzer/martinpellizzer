Calendula stipitata

Calendula stipitata, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red petals and yellow stamens, which form a showy, globe-like flower head. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Calendula stipitata is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
