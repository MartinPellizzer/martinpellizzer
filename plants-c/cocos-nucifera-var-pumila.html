Cocos nucifera var. pumila

Cocos nucifera var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf coconut palm, is a compact variant of the coconut tree.

It is characterized by its shorter stature, typically reaching heights of around 5 to 8 meters. The plant produces small, round coconuts that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
