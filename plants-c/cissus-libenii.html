Cissus libenii

Cissus libenii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
