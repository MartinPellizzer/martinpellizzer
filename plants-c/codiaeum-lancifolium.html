Codiaeum lancifolium

Codiaeum lancifolium, commonly known as the flame leaf or croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and velvety leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, which gives it its scientific name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Codiaeum lancifolium thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
