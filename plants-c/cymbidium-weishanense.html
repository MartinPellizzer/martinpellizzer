Cymbidium weishanense

Cymbidium weishanense, commonly known as the Weishan orchid, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Weishan Mountains.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, producing fragrant, lance-shaped petals in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Cymbidium weishanense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
