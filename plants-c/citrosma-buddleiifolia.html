Citrosma buddleiifolia

Citrosma buddleiifolia, commonly known as the ghost tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, yellow-orange fruits. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a distinctive species within the Rutaceae family.

The tree is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
