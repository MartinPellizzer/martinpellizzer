Calea divaricata var. diffusa

Calea divaricata var. diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique, exotic plants.
