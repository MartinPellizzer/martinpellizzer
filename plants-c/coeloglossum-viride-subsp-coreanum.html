Coeloglossum viride subsp. coreanum

Coeloglossum viride subsp.

coreanum, commonly known as the Korean green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which is a distinctive feature of the species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
