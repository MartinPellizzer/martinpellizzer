Casuarina nobilis

Casuarina nobilis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with feathery, needle-like foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are grouped into cylindrical clusters. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dunes and along the shoreline.

Casuarina nobilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
