Coleocephalocereus aureus subsp. elongatus

Coleocephalocereus aureus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the elongated golden cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

This subspecies is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
