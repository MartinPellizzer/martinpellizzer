Cerasus × fukudana

Cerasus × fukudana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with simple, ovate leaves that turn green in summer. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are borne in clusters and are highly fragrant.

Cerasus × fukudana is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a symbol of renewal and transience.
