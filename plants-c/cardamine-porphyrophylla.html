Cardamine porphyrophylla

Cardamine porphyrophylla, commonly known as the purple-stemmed watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with a slightly succulent texture. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
