Carduus defloratus var. entraunensis

Carduus defloratus var. entraunensis, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, yellowish-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces dense clusters of yellow flowers, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
