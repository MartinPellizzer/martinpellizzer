Causonis japonica var. pseudotrifolia

Causonis japonica var. pseudotrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese false trifoliate, is a variant of the genus Causonis native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
