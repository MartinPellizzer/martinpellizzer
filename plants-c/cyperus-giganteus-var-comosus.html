Cyperus giganteus var. comosus

Cyperus giganteus var. comosus, commonly known as the giant umbrella sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, forming a dense, grass-like clump. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a smooth, waxy texture that enhances its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in water gardens or as a landscape accent.
