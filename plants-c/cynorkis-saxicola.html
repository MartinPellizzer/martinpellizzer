Cynorkis saxicola

Cynorkis saxicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in crevices. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in well-drained, mineral-rich soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
