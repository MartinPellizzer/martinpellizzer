Cecropia schreberiana subsp. schreberiana

Cecropia schreberiana subsp.

schreberiana, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its massive, buttressed trunk and large, palmate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
