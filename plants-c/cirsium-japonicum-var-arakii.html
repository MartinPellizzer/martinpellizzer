Cirsium japonicum var. arakii

Cirsium japonicum var. arakii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassy areas. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
