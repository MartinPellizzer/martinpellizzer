Calliandra antioquiae

Calliandra antioquiae, commonly known as the Antioquian calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance.

Calliandra antioquiae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
