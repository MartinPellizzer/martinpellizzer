Carex lucorum var. austrolucorum

Carex lucorum var. austrolucorum, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
