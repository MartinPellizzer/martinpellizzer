Campanula spatulata

Campanula spatulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula spatulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
