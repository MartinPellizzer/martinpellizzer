Cirsium pseudolappaceum

Cirsium pseudolappaceum, commonly known as the false lappage thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are spiny and deeply lobed, often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing purple to pinkish hues that attract various pollinators.
