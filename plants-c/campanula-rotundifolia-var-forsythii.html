Campanula rotundifolia var. forsythii

Campanula rotundifolia var. forsythii, commonly known as the Yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
