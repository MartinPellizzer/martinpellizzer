Croton antisyphiliticus var. echiifolius

Croton antisyphiliticus var. echiifolius, commonly known as the Echiifolius croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
