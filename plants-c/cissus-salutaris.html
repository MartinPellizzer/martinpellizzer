Cissus salutaris

Cissus salutaris, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its rapid growth rate, making it a popular houseplant. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's adaptability and resilience contribute to its widespread cultivation and use.
