Cassia lactea

Cassia lactea, commonly known as the white cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and long, slender leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Cassia lactea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
