Cercocarpus parvifolius var. glaber

Cercocarpus parvifolius var. glaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and compact, multi-branched growth habit. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid and semi-arid regions.
