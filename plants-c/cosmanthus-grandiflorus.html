Cosmanthus grandiflorus

Cosmanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered cosmanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
