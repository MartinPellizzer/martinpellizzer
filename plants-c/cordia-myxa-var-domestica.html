Cordia myxa var. domestica

Cordia myxa var. domestica, commonly known as the common wax tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
