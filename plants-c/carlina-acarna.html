Carlina acarna

Carlina acarna, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the showy, violet to purple flower head, which consists of both ray and disk flowers.

Carlina acarna is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils in full sun.
