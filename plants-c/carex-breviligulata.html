Carex breviligulata

Carex breviligulata, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the seeds.

Carex breviligulata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
