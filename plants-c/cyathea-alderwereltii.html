Cyathea alderwereltii

Cyathea alderwereltii, commonly known as the Alderwereltii tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in some cultures.
