Commelina japonica

Commelina japonica, commonly known as Japanese spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows up to 30 cm tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, making it an important plant in ecological and ornamental horticulture.
