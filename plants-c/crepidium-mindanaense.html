Crepidium mindanaense

Crepidium mindanaense, commonly known as the Mindanao orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
