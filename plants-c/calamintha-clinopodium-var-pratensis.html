Calamintha clinopodium var. pratensis

Calamintha clinopodium var. pratensis, commonly known as meadow calamint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to violet, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

The leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild mint-like scent and potential medicinal properties.
