Coleus laurentii

Coleus laurentii, commonly known as the spotted dieffenbachia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and purple. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in tropical gardens.
