Ceratostigma abyssinicum

Ceratostigma abyssinicum, commonly known as the African rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to Ethiopia.

It features small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has woody stems and produces red or orange berries that persist into winter. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and long-lasting berries.
