Circaea lutetiana proles intermedia

Circaea lutetiana proles intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
