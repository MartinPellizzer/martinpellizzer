Cyperus flavus var. peduncularis

Cyperus flavus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its growth habit and flowering structure are typical of many Cyperus species, making it adaptable to a variety of wetland habitats.
