Chytroma collina

Chytroma collina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Chytroma collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
