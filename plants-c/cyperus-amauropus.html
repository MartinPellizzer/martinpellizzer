Cyperus amauropus

Cyperus amauropus, commonly known as the black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Cyperus amauropus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
