Cirsium chanroenicum

Cirsium chanroenicum, commonly known as the Chanroen's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to specific regions in Europe, this plant thrives in dry, calcareous soils and open habitats. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
