Carex aphyllopus var. aphyllopus

Carex aphyllopus var. aphyllopus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
