Convolvulus batatilla

Convolvulus batatilla, commonly known as the lesser woodbine, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus batatilla is typically found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in sunny to partially shaded environments.
