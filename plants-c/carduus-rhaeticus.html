Carduus rhaeticus

Carduus rhaeticus, commonly known as the Rhaetic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, followed by distinctive spherical, bristly seed heads.

Carduus rhaeticus is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry meadows, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
