Crinum stracheyi

Crinum stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's lily, is a striking tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette at the base.

Crinum stracheyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
