Callicarpa teneriflora

Callicarpa teneriflora, commonly known as the Canary Island beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to pink as they mature. These flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.

Callicarpa teneriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
