Colchicum eichleri

Colchicum eichleri, commonly known as the Eichler's autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive purple-pink stripe along the tepals, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Colchicum eichleri is part of the Colchicaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after addition to alpine and rock garden collections.
