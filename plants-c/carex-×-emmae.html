Carex × emmae

Carex × emmae, commonly known as the Emma Reed sedge.

It is a hybrid species within the Carex genus, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. This plant typically forms clumps of narrow, linear leaves that can vary in color from green to silver or bluish-gray. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex × emmae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
