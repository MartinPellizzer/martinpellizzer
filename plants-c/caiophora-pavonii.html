Caiophora pavonii

Caiophora pavonii, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a strikingly colorful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, such as bees and butterflies.

Caiophora pavonii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
