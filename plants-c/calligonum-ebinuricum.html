Calligonum ebinuricum

Calligonum ebinuricum, commonly known as the Ebinurian saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Calligonum ebinuricum plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
