Calamus pachyrostris

Calamus pachyrostris, commonly known as the broad-beaked arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

Calamus pachyrostris thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
