Centaurea × sanctae-barbarae

Centaurea × sanctae-barbarae, commonly known as the Holy Barbara's star, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads with bright yellow centers, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with Saint Barbara, a Christian martyr.
