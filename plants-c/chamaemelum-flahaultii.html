Chamaemelum flahaultii

Chamaemelum flahaultii, commonly known as Flahault's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, narrow leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties, similar to other chamomile varieties.
