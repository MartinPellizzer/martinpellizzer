Centaurea verbascifolia

Centaurea verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Centaurea verbascifolia is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
