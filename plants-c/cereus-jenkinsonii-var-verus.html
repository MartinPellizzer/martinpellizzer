Cereus jenkinsonii var. verus

Cereus jenkinsonii var. verus, commonly known as the yellow night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or yellow. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique blooming pattern, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
