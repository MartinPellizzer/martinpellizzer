Coleus ciliatus

Coleus ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Coleus ciliatus thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.
