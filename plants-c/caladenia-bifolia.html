Caladenia bifolia

Caladenia bifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and its unique, spider-like floral structure. The plant produces two distinct sepals and petals, with the labellum being a prominent, intricate feature. It typically blooms in spring, producing flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white.

Caladenia bifolia is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in sandy or loamy soils, often found in open forest or heathland environments.
