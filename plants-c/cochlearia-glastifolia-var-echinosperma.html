Cochlearia glastifolia var. echinosperma

Cochlearia glastifolia var. echinosperma, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and are visited by various pollinators.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.
