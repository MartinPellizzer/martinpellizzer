Cryptocarya lawsonii

Cryptocarya lawsonii, commonly known as the Lawson's tallowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and has a dense, rounded canopy. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also noted for its ornamental appeal.
