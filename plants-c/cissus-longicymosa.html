Cissus longicymosa

Cissus longicymosa, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Cissus longicymosa is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
