Chaetogastra hassleri

Chaetogastra hassleri, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered, small yellow flowers. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water in arid environments. Its leaves are often modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
