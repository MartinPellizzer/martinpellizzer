Cracca astragaloides

Cracca astragaloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Cracca astragaloides is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
