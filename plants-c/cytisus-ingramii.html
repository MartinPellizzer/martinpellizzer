Cytisus ingramii

Cytisus ingramii, commonly known as the Ingraham's broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical legume-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, oval, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cytisus ingramii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
