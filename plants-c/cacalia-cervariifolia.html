Cacalia cervariifolia

Cacalia cervariifolia, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cacalia cervariifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
