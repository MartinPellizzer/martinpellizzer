Chlorophora tinctoria var. chlorocarpa

Chlorophora tinctoria var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the greenheart tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a dark green to reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has a straight grain and is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and dark green color.
