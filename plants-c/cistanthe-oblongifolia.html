Cistanthe oblongifolia

Cistanthe oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cistanthe, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, oblong leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Cistanthe oblongifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
