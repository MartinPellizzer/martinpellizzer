Chamaesyce revoluta

Chamaesyce revoluta, commonly known as the curled sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, succulent stems and small, alternate leaves that are often curled or rolled. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
