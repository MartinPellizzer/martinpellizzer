Cereus speciosissimus var. lateritius

Cereus speciosissimus var. lateritius, commonly known as the "Red Cereus," is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive red or pink coloration on its stems, which is more pronounced in the varietal form lateritius. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
