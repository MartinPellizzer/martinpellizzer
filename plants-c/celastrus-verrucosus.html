Celastrus verrucosus

Celastrus verrucosus, commonly known as the roughleaf staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland or forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually opposite, with a rough, textured surface, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Celastrus verrucosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
