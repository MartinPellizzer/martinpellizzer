Calamus spinosus

Calamus spinosus, commonly known as spiny calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark brown, fibrous fruits. It thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its durable, fibrous materials used in traditional crafts and basket-making.
