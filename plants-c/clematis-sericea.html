Clematis sericea

Clematis sericea, commonly known as silver vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, ornamental climber.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators, and its rapid growth makes it ideal for covering trellises or arbors.
