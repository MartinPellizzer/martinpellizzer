Croton seminudus

Croton seminudus, commonly known as the naked croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a striking, colorful appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage.
