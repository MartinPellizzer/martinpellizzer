Cestrum calycinum

Cestrum calycinum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers with a sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths. It has dark green, glossy leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Cestrum calycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
