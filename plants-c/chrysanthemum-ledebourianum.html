Chrysanthemum ledebourianum

Chrysanthemum ledebourianum, commonly known as the desert chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
