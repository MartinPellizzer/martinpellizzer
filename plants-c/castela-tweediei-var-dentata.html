Castela tweediei var. dentata

Castela tweediei var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated castela, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with serrated leaf margins. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both horticultural and ecological settings.
