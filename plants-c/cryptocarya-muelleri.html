Cryptocarya muelleri

Cryptocarya muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and humid climates. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Cryptocarya muelleri is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
