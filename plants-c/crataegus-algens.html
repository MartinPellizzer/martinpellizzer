Crataegus algens

Crataegus algens, commonly known as the alpine hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Crataegus algens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
