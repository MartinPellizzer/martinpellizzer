Carex suksdorfii var. ovalis

Carex suksdorfii var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
