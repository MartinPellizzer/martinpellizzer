Chamaecrista compitalis

Chamaecrista compitalis, commonly known as the roadside crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered an invasive species in some regions.
