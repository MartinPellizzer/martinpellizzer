Connarus pentagynus

Connarus pentagynus, commonly known as the five-gynostemon tree, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation areas.
