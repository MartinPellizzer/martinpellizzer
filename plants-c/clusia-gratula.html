Clusia gratula

Clusia gratula, commonly known as the silver bush or Brazilian shield tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Clusia gratula is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
