Carex ericetorum f. nigra

Carex ericetorum f. nigra, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland edges.

Its dark green foliage and subtle floral structure make it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.
