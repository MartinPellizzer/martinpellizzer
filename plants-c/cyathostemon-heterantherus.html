Cyathostemon heterantherus

Cyathostemon heterantherus, commonly known as the variable anther plant, is a unique member of the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a cup-shaped hypanthium that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant exhibits a high degree of morphological variation, particularly in the arrangement and number of stamens. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, across parts of Southeast Asia.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model species for studying floral evolution and pollination mechanisms.
