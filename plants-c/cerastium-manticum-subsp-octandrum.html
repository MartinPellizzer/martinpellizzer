Cerastium manticum subsp. octandrum

Cerastium manticum subsp.

octandrum, commonly known as the eight-stamen cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It produces numerous stamens, which is a distinguishing feature of the subspecies.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
