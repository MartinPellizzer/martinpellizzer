Ceropegia ericamccoyae

Ceropegia ericamccoyae, commonly known as the Ericamccoy's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
