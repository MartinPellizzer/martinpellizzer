Cytisus malyei

Cytisus malyei, commonly known as the Malayan broom, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Cytisus malyei is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
