Campanula pusilla var. reniformis

Campanula pusilla var. reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, dainty blossoms.
