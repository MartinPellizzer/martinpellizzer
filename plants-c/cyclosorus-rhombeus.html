Cyclosorus rhombeus

Cyclosorus rhombeus, commonly known as the diamond fern, is a small, delicate fern species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks. Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
