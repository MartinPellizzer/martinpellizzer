Clinopodium intermedium

Clinopodium intermedium, commonly known as middle hill mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Clinopodium intermedium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
