Colchicum macedonicum

Colchicum macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
