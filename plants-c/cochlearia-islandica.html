Cochlearia islandica

Cochlearia islandica, commonly known as the Iceland cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, often in coastal areas. Its flowers are small and white or pink, arranged in a raceme, and it produces distinctive, elongated seed pods.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.
