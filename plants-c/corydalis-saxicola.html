Corydalis saxicola

Corydalis saxicola, commonly known as the rock corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained environments. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, often with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, delicate, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

Corydalis saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
