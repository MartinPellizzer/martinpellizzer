Chrysanthemum zahlbruckneri

Chrysanthemum zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahner's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to bloom in late summer and early autumn.
