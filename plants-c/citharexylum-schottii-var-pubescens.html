Citharexylum schottii var. pubescens

Citharexylum schottii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a dense covering of fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its varietal name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and visual interest.
