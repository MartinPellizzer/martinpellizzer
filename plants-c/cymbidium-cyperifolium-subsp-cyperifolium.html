Cymbidium cyperifolium subsp. cyperifolium

Cymbidium cyperifolium subsp.

cyperifolium, commonly known as the Cyperifolium Cymbidium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces pendulous spikes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
