Carex virgata var. abbreviata

Carex virgata var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-bracted sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curved, with a bluish-green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
