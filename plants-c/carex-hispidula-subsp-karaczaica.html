Carex hispidula subsp. karaczaica

Carex hispidula subsp.

karaczaica, commonly known as the Karacza sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is often found in regions with temperate to subarctic climates.
