Combesia phyturus

Combesia phyturus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale pink or white with a distinctive lip petal.

Combesia phyturus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
