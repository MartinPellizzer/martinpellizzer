Carex subspathacea f. curvata

Carex subspathacea f. curvata, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves that often exhibit a distinctive curling pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
