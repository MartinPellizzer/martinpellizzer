Commiphora gowlello

Commiphora gowlello, commonly known as the Somali myrrh tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fissured bark and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow or green, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.
