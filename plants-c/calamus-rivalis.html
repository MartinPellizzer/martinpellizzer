Calamus rivalis

Calamus rivalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

It is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its rhizomes are harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes, though they are less commonly used than those of other ginger species.

Calamus rivalis thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
