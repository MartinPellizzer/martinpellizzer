Calliandra matudae

Calliandra matudae, commonly known as the Matudae calliandra, is a species of leguminous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are usually pink or red, attracting various pollinators.

Calliandra matudae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in soil stabilization.
