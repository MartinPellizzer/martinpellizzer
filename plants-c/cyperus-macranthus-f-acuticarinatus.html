Cyperus macranthus f. acuticarinatus

Cyperus macranthus f. acuticarinatus, commonly known as the sharp-edged flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered flowering heads. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
