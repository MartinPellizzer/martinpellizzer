Calathea vinosa

Calathea vinosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns of deep green, silver, and burgundy. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
