Cinnagrostis cabrerae var. maxima

Cinnagrostis cabrerae var. maxima, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its showy flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
