Calamagrostis sylvatica var. iberica

Calamagrostis sylvatica var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian wood reed, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Calamagrostis sylvatica var. iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
