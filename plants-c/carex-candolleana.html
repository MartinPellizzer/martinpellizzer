Carex candolleana

Carex candolleana, commonly known as the Candolle's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower heads that are inconspicuous but important for pollination.

Carex candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
