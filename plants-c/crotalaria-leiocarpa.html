Crotalaria leiocarpa

Crotalaria leiocarpa, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and cylindrical seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria leiocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
