Crotalaria spartioides

Crotalaria spartioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Crotalaria spartioides is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.

However, some species within the genus are known to be toxic to livestock, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
