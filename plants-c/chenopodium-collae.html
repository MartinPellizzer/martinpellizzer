Chenopodium collae

Chenopodium collae, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its edible, succulent leaves that are rich in nutrients. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish, and it produces small, dry fruits.

It is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable due to its mild flavor and high nutritional value.
