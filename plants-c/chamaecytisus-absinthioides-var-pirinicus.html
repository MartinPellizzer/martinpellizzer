Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. pirinicus

Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. pirinicus, commonly known as the Pirin shrub cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
