Chenopodium secundiflorum

Chenopodium secundiflorum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its reddish stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings but also has historical use in traditional medicine.
