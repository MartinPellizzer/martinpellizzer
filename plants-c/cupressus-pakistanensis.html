Cupressus pakistanensis

Cupressus pakistanensis, commonly known as the Pakistan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in traditional medicine.
