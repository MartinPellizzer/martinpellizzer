Crinum piliferum

Crinum piliferum, commonly known as the hairy crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive periods of drought.

Crinum piliferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long bloom period during the summer months.
