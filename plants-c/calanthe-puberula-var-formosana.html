Calanthe puberula var. formosana

Calanthe puberula var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
