Capparis sola

Capparis sola, commonly known as the Indian fig or caper bush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure, with thorny stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces yellowish fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Capparis sola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
