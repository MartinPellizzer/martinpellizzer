Cicerbita atropurpurea

Cicerbita atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive, dark purple to reddish leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive crown-like pattern. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cicerbita atropurpurea is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
