Combretum celastroides subsp. orientale

Combretum celastroides subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters during the rainy season.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and crafts.
