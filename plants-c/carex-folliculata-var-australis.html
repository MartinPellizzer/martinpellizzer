Carex folliculata var. australis

Carex folliculata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the sedge family.

The species is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
