Chionanthus leprocarpus var. courtallensis

Chionanthus leprocarpus var. courtallensis, commonly known as the Malayan whiteflower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
