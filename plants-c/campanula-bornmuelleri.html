Campanula bornmuelleri

Campanula bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Campanula bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
