Campanula decumbens subsp. baetica

Campanula decumbens subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Iberian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It features slender stems that lie close to the ground, often forming a dense mat of foliage.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a soft, hairy texture.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and limestone outcrops.
