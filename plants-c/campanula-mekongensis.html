Campanula mekongensis

Campanula mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Campanula mekongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
