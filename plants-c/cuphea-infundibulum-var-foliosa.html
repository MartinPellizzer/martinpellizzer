Cuphea infundibulum var. foliosa

Cuphea infundibulum var. foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
