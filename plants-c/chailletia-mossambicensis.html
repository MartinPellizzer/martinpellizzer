Chailletia mossambicensis

Chailletia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique chailletia, is a rare and specialized moss species native to southern Africa.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in forest understories or on rocky outcrops. The plant has a delicate, cushion-like growth form, with small, overlapping leaves that are often densely packed. Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a unique example of moss adaptation to tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
