Carex flava var. laevigata

Carex flava var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brownish flowers, which are typical of the Carex genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
