Cochlospermum tinctorium

Cochlospermum tinctorium, commonly known as the crimson glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with alternating leaflets and produces woody capsules as fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
