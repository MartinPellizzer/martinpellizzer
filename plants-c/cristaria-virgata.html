Cristaria virgata

Cristaria virgata, commonly known as the featherleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or saline environments.

Cristaria virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
