Clerodendrum incisum var. macrosiphon

Clerodendrum incisum var. macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-siphon clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that range in color from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, surrounded by the vibrant bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
