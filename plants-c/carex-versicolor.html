Carex versicolor

Carex versicolor, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant features striking variegated foliage with green and white or yellow markings, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex versicolor is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
