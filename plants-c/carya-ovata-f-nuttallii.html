Carya ovata f. nuttallii

Carya ovata f. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's hickory, is a botanical variant of the shellbark hickory.

It is characterized by its large, thick-shelled nuts and elongated, ridged leaves. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with well-drained soils. It produces yellowish-green catkins and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also an important food source for wildlife.
