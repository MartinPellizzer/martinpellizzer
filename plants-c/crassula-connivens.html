Crassula connivens

Crassula connivens, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Crassula connivens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
