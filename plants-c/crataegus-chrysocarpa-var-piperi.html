Crataegus chrysocarpa var. piperi

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. piperi, commonly known as the yellow-fruited hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange fruits, which are typically ornamental and not edible. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
