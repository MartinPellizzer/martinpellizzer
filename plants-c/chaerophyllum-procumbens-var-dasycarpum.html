Chaerophyllum procumbens var. dasycarpum

Chaerophyllum procumbens var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited hogweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves and stems contain various bioactive compounds, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
