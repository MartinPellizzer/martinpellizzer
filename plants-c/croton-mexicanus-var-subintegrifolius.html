Croton mexicanus var. subintegrifolius

Croton mexicanus var. subintegrifolius, commonly known as Mexican croton, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
