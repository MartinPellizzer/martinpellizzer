Caladenia longiclavata var. rhomboidiformis

Caladenia longiclavata var. rhomboidiformis, commonly known as the rhomboid orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, lance-shaped or rhomboid-shaped flower that is typically pink or purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, fragrant bloom during the autumn season. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats.

This variety is noted for its unique floral morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
