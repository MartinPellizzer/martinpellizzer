Calonemorchis christineae

Calonemorchis christineae, commonly known as the Christine's calonemorchis, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its botanical significance lies in its morphological traits and ecological niche, contributing to the diversity of orchid flora.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
