Chasmopodium purpurascens

Chasmopodium purpurascens, commonly known as the purplegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Chasmopodium purpurascens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
