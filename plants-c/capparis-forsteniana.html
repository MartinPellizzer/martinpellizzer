Capparis forsteniana

Capparis forsteniana, commonly known as the Forsten's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
