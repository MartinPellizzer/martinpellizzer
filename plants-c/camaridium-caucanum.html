Camaridium caucanum

Camaridium caucanum, commonly known as the Caucanum plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
