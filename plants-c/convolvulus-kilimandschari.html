Convolvulus kilimandschari

Convolvulus kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions.

Convolvulus kilimandschari is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
