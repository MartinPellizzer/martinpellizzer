Clematis serratifolia

Clematis serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is vigorous, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous roots and hairy stems.
