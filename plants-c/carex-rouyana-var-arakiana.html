Carex rouyana var. arakiana

Carex rouyana var. arakiana, commonly known as the Arakian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries, which are a food source for various wildlife.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland gardens.
