Crataegus monogyna subsp. silicensis

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

silicensis, commonly known as the Silician hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
