Cycas siamensis subsp. balansae

Cycas siamensis subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Siam cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
