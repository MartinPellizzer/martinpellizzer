Centaurea scabiosa var. integrifolia

Centaurea scabiosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, spiky flower heads composed of blue to purple bracts and yellow disk flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
