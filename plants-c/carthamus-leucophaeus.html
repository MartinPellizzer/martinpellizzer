Carthamus leucophaeus

Carthamus leucophaeus, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules containing numerous small, dark seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
