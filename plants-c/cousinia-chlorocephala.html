Cousinia chlorocephala

Cousinia chlorocephala, commonly known as the bluehead cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its striking blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, adding visual interest to its harsh environment.

Cousinia chlorocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
