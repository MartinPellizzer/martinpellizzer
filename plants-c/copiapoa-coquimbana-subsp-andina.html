Copiapoa coquimbana subsp. andina

Copiapoa coquimbana subsp.

andina, commonly known as the Andean copiapoa, is a cactus native to the coastal regions of northern Chile. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed stem and produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring.

The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, with a deep root system to access moisture. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is relatively slow-growing, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in the Cactaceae family.
