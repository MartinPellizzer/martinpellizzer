Croton morifolius var. genuinus

Croton morifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically broad, elliptical, and display a mix of yellow, green, and red hues, making it highly ornamental. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
