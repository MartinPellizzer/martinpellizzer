Caralluma speciosa

Caralluma speciosa, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are fleshy and thick, adapted to store water, while its stems are branched and often spiny.

Caralluma speciosa is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
