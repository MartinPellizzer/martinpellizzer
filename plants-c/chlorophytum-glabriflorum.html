Chlorophytum glabriflorum

Chlorophytum glabriflorum, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
