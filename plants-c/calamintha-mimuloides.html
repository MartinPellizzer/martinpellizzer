Calamintha mimuloides

Calamintha mimuloides, commonly known as the mimuloides germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Calamintha mimuloides thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
