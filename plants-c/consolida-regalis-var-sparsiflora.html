Consolida regalis var. sparsiflora

Consolida regalis var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, blue to violet pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces long, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
