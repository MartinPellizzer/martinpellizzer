Centaurea grbavacensis

Centaurea grbavacensis, commonly known as the Grbavac thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces a compact, spherical flower head.

Centaurea grbavacensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
