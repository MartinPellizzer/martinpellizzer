Crupina intermedia

Crupina intermedia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble holly berries. The plant has lance-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in salt tolerance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

Crupina intermedia is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
