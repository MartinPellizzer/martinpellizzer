Cucurbita lagenaria

Cucurbita lagenaria, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a vine that produces large, hollow, bottle-shaped fruits with a smooth, pale green exterior. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in cooking and has been cultivated for thousands of years for its versatility and nutritional value.
