Cucurbita digitata

Cucurbita digitata, commonly known as the horse pumpkin or coral gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing vine that produces elongated, ribbed fruits with a distinctive warty texture. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in various cuisines.
