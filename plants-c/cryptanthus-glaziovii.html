Cryptanthus glaziovii

Cryptanthus glaziovii, commonly known as the "Rainbow Plant" or "Flor de la Lluvia," is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, often colorful leaves that range from red to purple and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, emerging from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant leaf colors.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
