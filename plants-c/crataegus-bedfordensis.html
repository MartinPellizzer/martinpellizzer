Crataegus bedfordensis

Crataegus bedfordensis, commonly known as Bedford hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Crataegus bedfordensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
