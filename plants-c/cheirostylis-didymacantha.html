Cheirostylis didymacantha

Cheirostylis didymacantha, commonly known as the twin-lobed orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
