Carex traversii

Carex traversii, commonly known as the Traverse's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex traversii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
