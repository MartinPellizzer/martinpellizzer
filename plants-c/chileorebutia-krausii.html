Chileorebutia krausii

Chileorebutia krausii, commonly known as the Candelabra Cactus, is a compact, branching cactus native to South America.

It features cylindrical stems that grow in a tree-like formation, often with a reddish-brown color and prominent areoles. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

Chileorebutia krausii is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
