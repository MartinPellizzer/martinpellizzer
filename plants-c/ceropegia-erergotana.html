Ceropegia erergotana

Ceropegia erergotana, commonly known as the African violet, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are often pale purple or white. The plant has heart-shaped or oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
