Cereus purpureopilosus

Cereus purpureopilosus, commonly known as the purple-hair cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of white, nocturnal flowers. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant's unique appearance and ability to bloom at night make it a striking addition to succulent collections.
