Carex oederi var. nevadensis

Carex oederi var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
