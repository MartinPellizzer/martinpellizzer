Colobanthus masonae

Colobanthus masonae, commonly known as Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Antarctica and the sub-Antarctic regions.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from December to February. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme cold and strong winds.

Colobanthus masonae plays a crucial role in the polar ecosystem, providing food and habitat for local fauna.
