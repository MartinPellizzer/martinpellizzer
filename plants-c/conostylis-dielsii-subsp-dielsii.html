Conostylis dielsii subsp. dielsii

Conostylis dielsii subsp.

dielsii, commonly known as the coastal conostylis, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or heathlands.

This species is adapted to saline and wind-prone environments, making it a hardy component of coastal vegetation.
