Cuscuta argentinana var. parviflora

Cuscuta argentinana var. parviflora, commonly known as the Argentinian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to South America and is often found growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in ecological interactions within its habitat.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can sometimes cause damage to agricultural crops.
