Campanula nemorosa

Campanula nemorosa, commonly known as the wood bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in woodland and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
