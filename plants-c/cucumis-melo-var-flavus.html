Cucumis melo var. flavus

Cucumis melo var. flavus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
