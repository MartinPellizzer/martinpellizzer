Chondrodendron microphyllum

Chondrodendron microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf chondrodendron, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
