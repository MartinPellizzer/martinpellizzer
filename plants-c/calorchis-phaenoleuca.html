Calorchis phaenoleuca

Calorchis phaenoleuca, commonly known as the white-lipped calorchis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged along the stem, and the flower features a distinctive white lip with a contrasting greenish hue. Calorchis phaenoleuca prefers shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements.
