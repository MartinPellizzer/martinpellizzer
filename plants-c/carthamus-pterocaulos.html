Carthamus pterocaulos

Carthamus pterocaulos, commonly known as the mountain safflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
