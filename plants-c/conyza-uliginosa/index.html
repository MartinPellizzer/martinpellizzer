Conyza Uliginosa

Conyza uliginosa is a flowering plant commonly found in wetland environments across the northern hemisphere. It is known for its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often seen along the edges of marshes and ponds. It plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds. Despite its modest appearance, Conyza uliginosa is a resilient species that contributes to the biodiversity of wetland habitats.
