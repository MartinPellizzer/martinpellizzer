Cedrelopsis trivalvis

Cedrelopsis trivalvis, commonly known as the African cedar or African sandalwood, is a member of the Cupressaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa and Namibia. The plant has a distinctive, gnarled trunk and produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional and modern applications.
