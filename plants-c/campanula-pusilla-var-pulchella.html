Campanula pusilla var. pulchella

Campanula pusilla var. pulchella, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
