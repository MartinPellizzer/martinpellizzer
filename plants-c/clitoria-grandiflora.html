Clitoria grandiflora

Clitoria grandiflora, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue or purple butterfly-shaped flowers, which are clustered in racemes. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in gardens and along roadsides.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye source.
