Cephalaria taurica

Cephalaria taurica, commonly known as Taurian cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Cephalaria taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
