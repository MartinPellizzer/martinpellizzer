Crataegus truncata

Crataegus truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that are deeply notched, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Crataegus truncata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
