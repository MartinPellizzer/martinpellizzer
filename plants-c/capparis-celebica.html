Capparis celebica

Capparis celebica, commonly known as the Moluccan fig or Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches, which help it survive in its tropical habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by fruits that resemble small, greenish-brown berries. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a rough texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

Capparis celebica is also noted for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
