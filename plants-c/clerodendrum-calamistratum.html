Clerodendrum calamistratum

Clerodendrum calamistratum, commonly known as the "Ceylon flame bush," is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
