Cornulaca alaschanica

Cornulaca alaschanica, commonly known as desert barley, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and has fibrous, cylindrical stems that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and medicinal purposes.

Cornulaca alaschanica is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
