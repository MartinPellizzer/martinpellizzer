Croton wallichii

Croton wallichii, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often brightly colored leaves that come in a variety of shades, including red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton wallichii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
