Capsella bursa-pastoris subarctica

Capsella bursa-pastoris subarctica, commonly known as the Arctic shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. Its leaves are often lobed and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in extreme environments.
