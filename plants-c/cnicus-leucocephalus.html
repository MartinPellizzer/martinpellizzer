Cnicus leucocephalus

Cnicus leucocephalus, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with a white center. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is characterized by its distinctive seed structure, which includes a pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
