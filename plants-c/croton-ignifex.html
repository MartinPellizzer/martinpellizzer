Croton ignifex

Croton ignifex, commonly known as the fire croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, flame-like foliage.

Native to Southeast Asia, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that can range in color from deep red to bright orange, depending on light exposure. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Croton.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
