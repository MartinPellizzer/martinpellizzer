Carex sphacelata

Carex sphacelata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex sphacelata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
