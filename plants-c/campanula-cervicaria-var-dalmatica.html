Campanula cervicaria var. dalmatica

Campanula cervicaria var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
