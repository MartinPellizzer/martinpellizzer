Carex longipes var. dissitiflora

Carex longipes var. dissitiflora, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of pale green to brownish flowers, which are often clustered in dense, tufted arrangements.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
