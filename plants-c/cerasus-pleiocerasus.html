Cerasus pleiocerasus

Cerasus pleiocerasus, commonly known as the Chinese dwarf cherry, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small, sweet cherry-like drupe that ripens in late summer.
