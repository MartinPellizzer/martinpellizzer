Cydonia cydonia var. maliformis

Cydonia cydonia var. maliformis, commonly known as the Persian apple or sand apple, is a variant of the quince plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy skin and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid climates. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, resulting in a sweet, aromatic flavor with a texture similar to a pear. This variety is valued for its resilience to drought and its historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
