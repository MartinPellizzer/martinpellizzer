Cleome refracta

Cleome refracta, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems and clusters of small, often pink or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome refracta is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
