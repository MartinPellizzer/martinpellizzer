Cirrhopetalum gamosepalum var. angustum

Cirrhopetalum gamosepalum var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cirrhopetalum, is a rare and specialized member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers exhibit a unique structure with fused petals and sepals, which is a key feature of the gamosepalum variety.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
