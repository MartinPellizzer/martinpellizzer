Chrysophyllum lanceolatum var. papuanum

Chrysophyllum lanceolatum var. papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan star apple, is a tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and dark green, often with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a round, yellow or orange berry with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines.
