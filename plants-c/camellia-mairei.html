Camellia mairei

Camellia mairei, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, double or single pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Camellia mairei thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in winter or early spring, adding visual interest to gardens during the colder months.
