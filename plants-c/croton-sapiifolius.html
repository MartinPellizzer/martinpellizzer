Croton sapiifolius

Croton sapiifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking foliage.
