Cirsium neomexicanum subsp. bakeri

Cirsium neomexicanum subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as Baker's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
