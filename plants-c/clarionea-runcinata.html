Clarionea runcinata

Clarionea runcinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Clarionea runcinata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

It is also noted for its rapid growth and potential as an invasive species in certain regions.
