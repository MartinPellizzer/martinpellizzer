Calceolaria inflexa

Calceolaria inflexa, commonly known as the "tiger's head" or "Mexican hat," is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, hood-like flowers that resemble a tiger's head, with a bilobed lower lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Calceolaria inflexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
