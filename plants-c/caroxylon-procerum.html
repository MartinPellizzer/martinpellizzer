Caroxylon procerum

Caroxylon procerum, commonly known as the tall cycad, is a species of cycad native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Caroxylon procerum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting slow growth and long lifespan.

It is considered a relict species, with a limited distribution and facing conservation challenges.
