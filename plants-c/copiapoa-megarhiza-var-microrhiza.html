Copiapoa megarhiza var. microrhiza

Copiapoa megarhiza var. microrhiza, commonly known as the miniature copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a compact, globular plant with a short, thick stem and dense, pale green to blue-gray spines. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. It has a shallow root system adapted to the arid environment, allowing it to absorb moisture from the surface.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
