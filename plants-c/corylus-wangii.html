Corylus wangii

Corylus wangii, commonly known as the Chinese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like inflorescences. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Corylus wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in nut production.
