Cyathea panamensis

Cyathea panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in maintaining forest biodiversity.

The plant's structure includes a central trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of compound leaves.
