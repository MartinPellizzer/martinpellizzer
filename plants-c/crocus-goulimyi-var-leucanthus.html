Crocus goulimyi var. leucanthus

Crocus goulimyi var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.

The variety is distinguished by its lighter flower color compared to the typical purple or yellow crocus species.
