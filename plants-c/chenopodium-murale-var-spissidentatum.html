Chenopodium murale var. spissidentatum

Chenopodium murale var. spissidentatum, commonly known as rough goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant has alternate, rough, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential nutritional and pharmacological properties.
