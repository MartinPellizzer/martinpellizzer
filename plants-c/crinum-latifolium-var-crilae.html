Crinum latifolium var. crilae

Crinum latifolium var. crilae, commonly known as the broadleaf crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and is often used in traditional medicine.
