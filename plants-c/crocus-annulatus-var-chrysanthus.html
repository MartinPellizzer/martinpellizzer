Crocus annulatus var. chrysanthus

Crocus annulatus var. chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and produces small, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
