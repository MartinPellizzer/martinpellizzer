Cytisus leucanthus var. frivaldszkyanus

Cytisus leucanthus var. frivaldszkyanus, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Carpathian Basin.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
