Cassia hecatophylla

Cassia hecatophylla, commonly known as the Christmas cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the winter, which are followed by elongated, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Cassia hecatophylla is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for local wildlife.
