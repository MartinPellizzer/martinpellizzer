Cryptocarya hypoglauca var. attenuata

Cryptocarya hypoglauca var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, blue-black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
