Celtis choseniana

Celtis choseniana, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.

Celtis choseniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
