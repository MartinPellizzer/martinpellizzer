Carex lamprocarpa

Carex lamprocarpa, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in shaded areas.

Carex lamprocarpa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
