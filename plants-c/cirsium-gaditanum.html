Cirsium gaditanum

Cirsium gaditanum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Cirsium gaditanum is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
