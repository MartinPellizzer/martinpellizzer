Cyathea henryi

Cyathea henryi, commonly known as the Henry's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea henryi plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
