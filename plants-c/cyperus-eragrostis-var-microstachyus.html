Cyperus eragrostis var. microstachyus

Cyperus eragrostis var. microstachyus, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
