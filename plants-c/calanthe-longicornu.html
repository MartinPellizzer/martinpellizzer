Calanthe longicornu

Calanthe longicornu, commonly known as the long-horned calanthe, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, India, and the Malay Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. Its flowers are often pink or white with a distinctive yellow lip, and it prefers shaded, moist environments.

Calanthe longicornu is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
