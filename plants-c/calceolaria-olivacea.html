Calceolaria olivacea

Calceolaria olivacea, commonly known as the olive cup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bilaterally symmetrical, pouch-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces vibrant, colorful blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and branching.

Calceolaria olivacea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
