Cattleya pfisteri

Cattleya pfisteri, commonly known as the Pfister's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and often feature a distinctive yellow lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

Cattleya pfisteri is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beautiful, fragrant blooms.
