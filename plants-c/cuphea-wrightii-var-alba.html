Cuphea wrightii var. alba

Cuphea wrightii var. alba, commonly known as white flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
