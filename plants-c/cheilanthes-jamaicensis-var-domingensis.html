Cheilanthes jamaicensis var. domingensis

Cheilanthes jamaicensis var. domingensis, commonly known as the Jamaican rock fern, is a perennial fern species native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in dry, sunny habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
