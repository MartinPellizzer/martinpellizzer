Clematis brasiliana var. minor

Clematis brasiliana var. minor, commonly known as the Brazilian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to South America.

It features climbing stems with thorns and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody vine structure and a preference for well-drained soil and full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
