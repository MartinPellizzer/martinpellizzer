Crocus vernus var. tulipaceus

Crocus vernus var. tulipaceus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from purple to white, often with a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering bulbs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging after the flowers have faded.

The bulbs are adapted to well-drained soil and can be cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value.
