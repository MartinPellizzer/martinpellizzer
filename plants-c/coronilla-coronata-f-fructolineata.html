Coronilla coronata f. fructolineata

Coronilla coronata f. fructolineata, commonly known as the fruit-lined crown vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that are lined with small, visible ridges, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
