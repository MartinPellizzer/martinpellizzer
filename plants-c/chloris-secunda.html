Chloris secunda

Chloris secunda, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescence. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its ornamental value comes from its unique coloration and airy appearance.

Chloris secunda is also valued for its low maintenance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
