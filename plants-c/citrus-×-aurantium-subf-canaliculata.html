Citrus × aurantium subf. canaliculata

Citrus × aurantium subf.

canaliculata, commonly known as the canary bell orange, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its unique fruit morphology. The fruit is typically small, with a thin, yellow-orange peel and a segmented, juicy pulp.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the citrus family, valued for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
