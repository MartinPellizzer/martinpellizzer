Cyperus lundellii

Cyperus lundellii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky, cylindrical cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus lundellii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
