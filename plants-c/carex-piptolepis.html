Carex piptolepis

Carex piptolepis, commonly known as the slender spike sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer.

Carex piptolepis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
