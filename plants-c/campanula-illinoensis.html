Campanula illinoensis

Campanula illinoensis, commonly known as Illinois bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
