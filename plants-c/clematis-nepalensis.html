Clematis nepalensis

Clematis nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Clematis nepalensis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
