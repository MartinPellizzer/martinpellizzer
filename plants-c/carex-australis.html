Carex australis

Carex australis, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex australis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
