Corymbia × opacula

Corymbia × opacula, commonly known as the red gum or red blood gum, is a hybrid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown to pinkish bark, which peels in thin, papery layers. The plant typically grows as a tall tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Corymbia × opacula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its striking appearance.
