Corysanthes intuta

Corysanthes intuta, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
