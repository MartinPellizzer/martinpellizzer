Cyperus meyenianus var. patentissimus

Cyperus meyenianus var. patentissimus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and pond edges, and can form dense stands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it reproduces through seeds and rhizomes.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator of poor water quality in some environments.
