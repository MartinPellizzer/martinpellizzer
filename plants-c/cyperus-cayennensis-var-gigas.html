Cyperus cayennensis var. gigas

Cyperus cayennensis var. gigas, commonly known as the giant umbrella sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, which can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense, upright clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in wetland gardens or as a pond margin plant.

Due to its unique appearance, it is popular in horticulture for its visual impact and adaptability to various aquatic environments.
