Caprifolium leschenaultii

Caprifolium leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Caprifolium leschenaultii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
