Cleome cordata

Cleome cordata, commonly known as heartleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often described as having a heart-shaped appearance, giving it its common name.

Cleome cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
