Crataegus uticaensis

Crataegus uticaensis, commonly known as the Utica hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
