Chrysophyllum macoucou

Chrysophyllum macoucou, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a yellow or orange skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.
