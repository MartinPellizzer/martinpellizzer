Callista radians

Callista radians, commonly known as the radiant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
