Clematis stans var. monoica

Clematis stans var. monoica, commonly known as the single-flowered clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, single flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinct, hairy style. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, reddish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
