Cousinia odontolepis var. leiolepis

Cousinia odontolepis var. leiolepis, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
