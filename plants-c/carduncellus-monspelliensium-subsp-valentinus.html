Carduncellus monspelliensium subsp. valentinus

Carduncellus monspelliensium subsp.

valentinus, commonly known as the Valentin's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
