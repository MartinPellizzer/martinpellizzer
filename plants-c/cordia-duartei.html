Cordia duartei

Cordia duartei, commonly known as the Duarte's cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cordia duartei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
