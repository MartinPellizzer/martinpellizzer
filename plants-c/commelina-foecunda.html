Commelina foecunda

Commelina foecunda, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers with a white throat. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Commelina foecunda is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
