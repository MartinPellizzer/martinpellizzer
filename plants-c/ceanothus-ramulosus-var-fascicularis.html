Ceanothus ramulosus var. fascicularis

Ceanothus ramulosus var. fascicularis, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its coastal habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to coastal climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
