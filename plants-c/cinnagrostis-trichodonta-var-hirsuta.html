Cinnagrostis trichodonta var. hirsuta

Cinnagrostis trichodonta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy filifolium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely hairy, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in late spring and are clustered in panicles.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
