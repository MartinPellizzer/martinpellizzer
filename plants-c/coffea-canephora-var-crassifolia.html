Coffea canephora var. crassifolia

Coffea canephora var. crassifolia, commonly known as the Congo coffee or West African coffee, is a variant of the robusta coffee species.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and is native to central and western Africa. This plant typically grows in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils. It produces oval, red berries that contain two seeds used for coffee production.

The plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
