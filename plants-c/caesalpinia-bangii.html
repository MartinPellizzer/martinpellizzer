Caesalpinia bangii

Caesalpinia bangii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Caesalpinia bangii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
