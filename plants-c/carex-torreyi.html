Carex torreyi

Carex torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Torrey's sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
