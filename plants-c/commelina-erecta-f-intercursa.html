Commelina erecta f. intercursa

Commelina erecta f. intercursa, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are prostrate or semi-erect.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
