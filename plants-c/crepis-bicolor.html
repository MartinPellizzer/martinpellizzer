Crepis bicolor

Crepis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Crepis bicolor is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
