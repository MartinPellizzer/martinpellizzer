Carex livida var. rufiniformis

Carex livida var. rufiniformis, commonly known as the red-veined sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. Native to moist environments, it thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its unique reddish veins on the leaves make it a distinctive and ornamental species in wetland gardens.
