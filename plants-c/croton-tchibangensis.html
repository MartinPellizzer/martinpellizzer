Croton tchibangensis

Croton tchibangensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed and vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
