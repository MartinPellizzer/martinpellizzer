Centaurea bofilliana

Centaurea bofilliana, commonly known as the Boillia's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean regions. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Centaurea bofilliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
