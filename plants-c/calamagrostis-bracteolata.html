Calamagrostis bracteolata

Calamagrostis bracteolata, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis bracteolata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
