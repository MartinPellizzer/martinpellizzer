Carex gentilis var. tonnerrei

Carex gentilis var. tonnerrei, commonly known as the Tonnerre sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
