Carex macrochaeta var. macrochlaena

Carex macrochaeta var. macrochlaena, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus, which belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed wetland areas.
