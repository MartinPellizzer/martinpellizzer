Cerastium pedunculare

Cerastium pedunculare, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, ovate leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are white, star-shaped, and arranged in clusters, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
