Cerastium formosanum

Cerastium formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its frost-resistant nature.

Cerastium formosanum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.
