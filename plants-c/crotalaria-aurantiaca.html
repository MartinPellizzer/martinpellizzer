Crotalaria aurantiaca

Crotalaria aurantiaca, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and palmately divided leaves. Its seed pods are pod-like and contain several elliptical seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria aurantiaca is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
