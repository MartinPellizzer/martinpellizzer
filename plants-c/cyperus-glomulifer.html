Cyperus glomulifer

Cyperus glomulifer, commonly known as the globular flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Cyperus glomulifer thrives in wetlands, marshes, and areas with moist soil, often forming dense vegetation.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
