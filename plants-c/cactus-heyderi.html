Cactus heyderi

Cactus heyderi, commonly known as the Heyder's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and botanical collections.
