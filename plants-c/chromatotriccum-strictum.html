Chromatotriccum strictum

Chromatotriccum strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to help clarify water by absorbing nutrients.

Chromatotriccum strictum is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and tolerate a range of water temperatures.
