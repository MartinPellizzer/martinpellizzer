Chiococca brachiata var. acutifolia

Chiococca brachiata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable regions.
