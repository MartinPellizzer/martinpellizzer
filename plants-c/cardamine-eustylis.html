Cardamine eustylis

Cardamine eustylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slender, upright stem, and its flowers are small, white, and arranged in racemes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
