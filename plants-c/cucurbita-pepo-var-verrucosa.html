Cucurbita pepo var. verrucosa

Cucurbita pepo var. verrucosa, commonly known as the hairy gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, ridged, and hairy stem and leaves. The fruit is typically elongated, with a green, warty surface that turns yellow or orange when mature. It produces small, yellow flowers and is primarily cultivated for its edible, fibrous fruit.

This variety is native to the Americas and is valued for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
