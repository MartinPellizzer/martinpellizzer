Cerastium lucorum subsp. macrocarpum

Cerastium lucorum subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are oval to heart-shaped, often covered in fine pubescence. Its fruit is a small, hairy capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
