Cardamine hirsuta proles rigida

Cardamine hirsuta proles rigida, commonly known as the rigid hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow, petal-less flowers that are clustered in a raceme, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its robust growth and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
