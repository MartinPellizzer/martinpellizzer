Cryptocarya subcorymbosa

Cryptocarya subcorymbosa, commonly known as the broadleaf sassafras, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often aromatic leaves, which are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in corymbs, and it produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Cryptocarya subcorymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
