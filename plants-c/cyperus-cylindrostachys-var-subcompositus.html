Cyperus cylindrostachys var. subcompositus

Cyperus cylindrostachys var. subcompositus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This variety is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its use in ornamental landscaping.
