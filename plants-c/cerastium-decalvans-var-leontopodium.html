Cerastium decalvans var. leontopodium

Cerastium decalvans var. leontopodium, commonly known as the snow pearlweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, frosty appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
