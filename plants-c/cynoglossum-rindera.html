Cynoglossum rindera

Cynoglossum rindera, commonly known as the velvetleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often with a velvety texture. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cynoglossum rindera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can also serve as a nectar source for pollinators.
