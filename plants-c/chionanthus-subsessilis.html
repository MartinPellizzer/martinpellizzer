Chionanthus subsessilis

Chionanthus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and graceful form.
