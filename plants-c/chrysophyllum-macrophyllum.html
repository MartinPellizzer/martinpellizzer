Chrysophyllum macrophyllum

Chrysophyllum macrophyllum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers, followed by edible, orange-red berries. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
