Centaurea affinis subsp. lacerata

Centaurea affinis subsp.

lacerata, commonly known as the fringed cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features striking blue to purple flowers with distinctive fringed petals, which give it its common name.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
