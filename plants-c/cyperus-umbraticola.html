Cyperus umbraticola

Cyperus umbraticola, commonly known as the shade cyperus, is a species of sedge that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate low light conditions and waterlogged soils.

Cyperus umbraticola plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
