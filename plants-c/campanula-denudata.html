Campanula denudata

Campanula denudata, commonly known as the naked bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. The plant features slender, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Campanula denudata thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats.
