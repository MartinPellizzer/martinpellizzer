Crassula namaquensis subsp. comptonii

Crassula namaquensis subsp.

comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
