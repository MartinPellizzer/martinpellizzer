Crepis pinnatifida

Crepis pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Crepis pinnatifida is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
