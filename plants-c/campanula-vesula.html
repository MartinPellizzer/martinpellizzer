Campanula vesula

Campanula vesula, commonly known as the vesicular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought resistance.

Campanula vesula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
