Capnoides flavula

Capnoides flavula, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Capnoides flavula thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
