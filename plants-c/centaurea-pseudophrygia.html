Centaurea pseudophrygia

Centaurea pseudophrygia, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with yellow centers, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea pseudophrygia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
