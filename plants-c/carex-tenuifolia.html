Carex tenuifolia

Carex tenuifolia, commonly known as slender thread sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands, wetlands, and stream banks. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the summer.

Carex tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
