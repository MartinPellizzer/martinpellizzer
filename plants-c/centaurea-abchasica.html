Centaurea abchasica

Centaurea abchasica, commonly known as Abkhazian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely haired stems and small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Centaurea abchasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
