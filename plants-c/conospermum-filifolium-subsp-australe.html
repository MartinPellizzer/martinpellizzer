Conospermum filifolium subsp. australe

Conospermum filifolium subsp.

australe, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves.

The plant produces striking, red or orange flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
