Celsia rhiphaea

Celsia rhiphaea, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant features small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and serrated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
