Chaenactis filifolia

Chaenactis filifolia, commonly known as the filifoliate skyrocket, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its slender, branching stems and is often found in open habitats across California and Nevada.
