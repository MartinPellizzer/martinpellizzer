Campanula scheuchzeri subsp. marcenoi

Campanula scheuchzeri subsp.

marcenoi, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
