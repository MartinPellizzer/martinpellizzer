Camellia wardii

Camellia wardii, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, while the flowers are usually red or pink with a yellow center.

Camellia wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
