Carum cylindricum

Carum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical caraway, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The seeds of Carum cylindricum are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
