Calamus myrianthus

Calamus myrianthus, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
