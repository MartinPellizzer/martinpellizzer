Cyanocephalus digitatus

Cyanocephalus digitatus, commonly known as the blue-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and blue-gray foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
