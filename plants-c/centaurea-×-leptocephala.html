Centaurea × leptocephala

Centaurea × leptocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, spindly stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

Centaurea × leptocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
