Centaurea crupina

Centaurea crupina, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features erect, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Centaurea crupina is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
