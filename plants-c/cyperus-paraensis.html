Cyperus paraensis

Cyperus paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and flooded grasslands, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Cyperus paraensis has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering spikes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
