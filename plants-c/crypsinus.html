Crypsinus

Crypsinus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has long, thin roots and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Crypsinus is considered endangered in many regions.
