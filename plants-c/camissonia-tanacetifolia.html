Camissonia tanacetifolia

Camissonia tanacetifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
