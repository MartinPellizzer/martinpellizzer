Clematis brevicaudata var. lissocarpa

Clematis brevicaudata var. lissocarpa, commonly known as the silky-haired clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and attractive foliage.
