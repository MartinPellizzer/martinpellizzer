Campanula alpina var. albiflora

Campanula alpina var. albiflora, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in harsh, mountainous environments.
