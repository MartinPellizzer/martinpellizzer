Cerasus collina f. flore-plena

Cerasus collina f. flore-plena, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry or Japanese double flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its dense, double blossoms that resemble peonies, with multiple layers of petals. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy spring blooms and its use in traditional Japanese gardens.
