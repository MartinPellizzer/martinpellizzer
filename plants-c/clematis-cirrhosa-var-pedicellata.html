Clematis cirrhosa var. pedicellata

Clematis cirrhosa var. pedicellata, commonly known as the long-hair clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
