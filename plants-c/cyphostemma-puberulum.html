Cyphostemma puberulum

Cyphostemma puberulum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
