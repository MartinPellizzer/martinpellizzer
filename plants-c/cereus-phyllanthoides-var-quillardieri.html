Cereus phyllanthoides var. quillardieri

Cereus phyllanthoides var. quillardieri, commonly known as the Quillardier's cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a ribbed stem with white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

It requires minimal water and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low humidity.
