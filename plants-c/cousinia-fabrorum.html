Cousinia fabrorum

Cousinia fabrorum, commonly known as the flame grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive at high altitudes.

Cousinia fabrorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
