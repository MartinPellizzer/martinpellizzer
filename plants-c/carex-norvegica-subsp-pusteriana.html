Carex norvegica subsp. pusteriana

Carex norvegica subsp.

pusteriana, commonly known as the Puster Valley sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountain meadows and stream banks.
