Campomanesia apiculata

Campomanesia apiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or erva-cidra, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red, edible fruits. The plant produces clusters of fragrant white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in landscaping, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
