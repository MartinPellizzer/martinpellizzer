Coffea foveolata

Coffea foveolata, commonly known as the Java coffee tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are white and fragrant, and it bears red or orange berries containing the seeds used for coffee.

This species is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often cultivated in tropical regions for specialty coffee production.
