Chamaesyce missurica var. petaloidea

Chamaesyce missurica var. petaloidea, commonly known as the pinkleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the Midwest.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy, alternate leaves that are often pink or reddish in color, contributing to its ornamental value. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
