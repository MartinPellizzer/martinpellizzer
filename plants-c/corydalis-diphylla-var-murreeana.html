Corydalis diphylla var. murreeana

Corydalis diphylla var. murreeana, commonly known as the Kashmiri corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features slender stems and clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
