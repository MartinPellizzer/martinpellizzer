Corispermum hyssopifolium var. emarginatum

Corispermum hyssopifolium var. emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved corispermum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Chenopodiaceae family, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in saline soils and is considered a halophyte, capable of thriving in high-salt environments.
