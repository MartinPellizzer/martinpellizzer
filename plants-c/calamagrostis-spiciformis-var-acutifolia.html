Calamagrostis spiciformis var. acutifolia

Calamagrostis spiciformis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences that bear spikelets, which are the flowering structures typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscape design in suitable climates.
