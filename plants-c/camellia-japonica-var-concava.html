Camellia japonica var. concava

Camellia japonica var. concava, commonly known as the concave camellia, is a species native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, reflexed petals that curve backward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, adding to its ornamental value.
