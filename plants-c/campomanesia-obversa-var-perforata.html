Campomanesia obversa var. perforata

Campomanesia obversa var. perforata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or pitanga-preta, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's flora and a subject of ecological study.
