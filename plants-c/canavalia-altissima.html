Canavalia altissima

Canavalia altissima, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Canavalia altissima is also valued as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional content.
