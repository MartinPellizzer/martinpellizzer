Coleus bernieri

Coleus bernieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from deep red to purple and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
