Coccinia engleri

Coccinia engleri, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, orange-red fruits resembling tiny pumpkins. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and hairy.

The fruit is edible when ripe and has a mildly sweet, nutty flavor.
