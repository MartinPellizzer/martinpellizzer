Cissampelos sympodialis var. grandifolia

Cissampelos sympodialis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cissampelos, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often deeply lobed, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure, typical of the Menispermaceae family. Its fruit is a fleshy, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
