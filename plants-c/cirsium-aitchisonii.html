Cirsium aitchisonii

Cirsium aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Cirsium aitchisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
