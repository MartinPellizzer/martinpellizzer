Cordylanthus pilosus var. trifidus

Cordylanthus pilosus var. trifidus, commonly known as the trifid bush lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a nectar source for pollinators.
