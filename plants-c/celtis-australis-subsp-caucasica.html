Celtis australis subsp. caucasica

Celtis australis subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, pale green fruits that turn red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
