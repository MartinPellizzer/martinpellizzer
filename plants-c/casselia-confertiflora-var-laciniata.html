Casselia confertiflora var. laciniata

Casselia confertiflora var. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated casselia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
