Clausena brevistyla var. papuana

Clausena brevistyla var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan olive, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Guinea.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits resembling olives. Its fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
