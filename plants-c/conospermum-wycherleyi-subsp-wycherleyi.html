Conospermum wycherleyi subsp. wycherleyi

Conospermum wycherleyi subsp.

wycherleyi, commonly known as the Wycherley's conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
