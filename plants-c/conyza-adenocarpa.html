Conyza adenocarpa

Conyza adenocarpa, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish composite flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Conyza adenocarpa is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which contributes to its invasive potential in some areas.
