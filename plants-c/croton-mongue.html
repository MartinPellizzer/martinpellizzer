Croton mongue

Croton mongue, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Croton mongue is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
