Catasetum × canaense

Catasetum × canaense, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature specialized structures called resupination, which aid in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are notable for their unique shape and the presence of a column that facilitates the transfer of pollen.

Catasetum × canaense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
