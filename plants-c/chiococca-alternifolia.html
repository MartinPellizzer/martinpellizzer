Chiococca alternifolia

Chiococca alternifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or wild coffee, is a climbing shrub native to tropical South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Chiococca alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
