Conospermum caeruleum subsp. spathulatum

Conospermum caeruleum subsp.

spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to red, which appear in spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral displays.
