Cornus stolonifera f. azurea

Cornus stolonifera f. azurea, commonly known as the Azure Cornus or Azure Dogwood, is a cultivar of the Cornus stolonifera species, which is native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which turns a vibrant red in the fall, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and produces small white flowers in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, and is relatively low maintenance once established.

This cultivar is valued for its unique coloration and adaptability to various garden settings.
