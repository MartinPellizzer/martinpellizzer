Capparis laurina

Capparis laurina, commonly known as the laurel caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in terminal umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, white seeds.

Capparis laurina is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
