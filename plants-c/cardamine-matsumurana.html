Cardamine matsumurana

Cardamine matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese water shield, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a dense cluster, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

Cardamine matsumurana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
