Campanula fransinea

Campanula fransinea, commonly known as the French bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula fransinea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
