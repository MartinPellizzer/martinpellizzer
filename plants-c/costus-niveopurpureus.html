Costus niveopurpureus

Costus niveopurpureus, commonly known as the white-purple costus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white or purple hues. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
