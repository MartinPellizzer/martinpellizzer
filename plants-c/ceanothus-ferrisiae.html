Ceanothus ferrisiae

Ceanothus ferrisiae, commonly known as the Sierra foothill ceanothus, is a shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of concern in some regions.
