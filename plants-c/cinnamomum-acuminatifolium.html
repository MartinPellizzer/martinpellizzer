Cinnamomum acuminatifolium

Cinnamomum acuminatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

The tree typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
