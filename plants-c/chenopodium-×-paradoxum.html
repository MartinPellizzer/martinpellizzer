Chenopodium × paradoxum

Chenopodium × paradoxum, commonly known as garden goosefoot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Chenopodium × paradoxum is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
