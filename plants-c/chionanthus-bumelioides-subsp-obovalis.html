Chionanthus bumelioides subsp. obovalis

Chionanthus bumelioides subsp.

obovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf whiteflower plant, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are oval-shaped, typically with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn yellow in the fall. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
