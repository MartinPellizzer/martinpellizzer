Crataegus macracantha var. succulenta

Crataegus macracantha var. succulenta, commonly known as the succulent hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and berries, which are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter.

The succulent leaves and berries make it a distinctive and resilient plant in its native habitat.
