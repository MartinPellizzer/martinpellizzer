Coronilla extensa

Coronilla extensa, commonly known as Spanish clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Coronilla extensa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
