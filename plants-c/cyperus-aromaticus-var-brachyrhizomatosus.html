Cyperus aromaticus var. brachyrhizomatosus

Cyperus aromaticus var. brachyrhizomatosus, commonly known as the narrow-rooted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and floodplains, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its rhizomes are short and branched, contributing to its ability to spread vegetatively.

The species is often found in disturbed or aquatic habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
