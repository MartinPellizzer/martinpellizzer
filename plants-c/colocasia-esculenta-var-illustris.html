Colocasia esculenta var. illustris

Colocasia esculenta var. illustris, commonly known as the Blue Spire Elephant Ear, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a distinctive blue or purple sheen. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage rather than its modest flowers. Its leaves can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, making it a focal point in tropical gardens.

It is a variant of the common taro plant, prized for its unique coloration and aesthetic appeal.
