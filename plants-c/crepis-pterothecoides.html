Crepis pterothecoides

Crepis pterothecoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters.

Crepis pterothecoides is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
