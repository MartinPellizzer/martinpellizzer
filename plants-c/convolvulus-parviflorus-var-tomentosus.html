Convolvulus parviflorus var. tomentosus

Convolvulus parviflorus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
