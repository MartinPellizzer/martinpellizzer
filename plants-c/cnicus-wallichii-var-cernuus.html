Cnicus wallichii var. cernuus

Cnicus wallichii var. cernuus, commonly known as the creeping sowthistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically pale green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single umbel, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
