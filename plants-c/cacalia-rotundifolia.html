Cacalia rotundifolia

Cacalia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf cacalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, rounded leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Cacalia rotundifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
