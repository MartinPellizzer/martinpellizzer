Chromolaena pharcidodes

Chromolaena pharcidodes, commonly known as the medicinal weed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, opposite leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing effects. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in agricultural areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it both a useful and problematic species in its native range.
