Cephalaria speciosa

Cephalaria speciosa, commonly known as the showy cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and produces striking purple to pink ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
