Ceropegia nepalensis

Ceropegia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
