Crepis modocensis subsp. typica

Crepis modocensis subsp.

typica, commonly known as Modoc thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct basal rosette structure.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.
