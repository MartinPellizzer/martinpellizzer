Cyperus amabilis var. subacaulis

Cyperus amabilis var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along waterways. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often arranged in spikelets that are wind-pollinated.
