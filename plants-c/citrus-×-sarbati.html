Citrus × sarbati

Citrus × sarbati, commonly known as the kaffir lime.

It is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its rough, green, and bumpy rind. The fruit is typically small, with a thick peel that contains aromatic oils. The leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, and the plant is often grown for its fragrant zest and peel used in cooking.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
