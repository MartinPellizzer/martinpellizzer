Calamagrostis cabrerae var. aristulata

Calamagrostis cabrerae var. aristulata, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, silver-blue leaves that turn coppery-red in the autumn, creating a dramatic seasonal display. The plant produces tall, feathery panicles of pale pink to white flowers in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for moist or wetland environments.

Its elegant appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for water gardens and pond edges.
