Carex saximontana

Carex saximontana, commonly known as the saximon tussilaginous sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Carex saximontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
