Caperonia capiibariensis

Caperonia capiibariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically display a range of colors including white, yellow, and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees and other plants without being parasitic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
