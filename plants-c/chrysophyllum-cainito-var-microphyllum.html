Chrysophyllum cainito var. microphyllum

Chrysophyllum cainito var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, mildly acidic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
