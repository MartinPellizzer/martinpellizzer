Carex digitalis

Carex digitalis, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

Carex digitalis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
