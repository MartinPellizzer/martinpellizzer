Callicarpa takakumensis

Callicarpa takakumensis, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
