Commelina wightii

Commelina wightii, commonly known as the Wight's spiderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinales order.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers that are often surrounded by white bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
