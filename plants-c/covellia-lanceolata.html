Covellia lanceolata

Covellia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and can be cultivated as a legume crop.

Covellia lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
