Corylus colurna var. glandulifera

Corylus colurna var. glandulifera, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a variant of the common hazelnut native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval, glandular leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers that develop into edible nuts enclosed in a woody husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its nuts, which are used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
