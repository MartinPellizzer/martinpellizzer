Calothamnus formosus

Calothamnus formosus, commonly known as the pink mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Calothamnus formosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
