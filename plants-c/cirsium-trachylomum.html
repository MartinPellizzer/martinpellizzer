Cirsium trachylomum

Cirsium trachylomum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically grayish-green with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine, prickly hairs.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and along trails.
