Carex remota f. doelliana

Carex remota f. doelliana, commonly known as the Doell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The foliage is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
