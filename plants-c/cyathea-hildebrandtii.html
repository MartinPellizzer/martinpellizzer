Cyathea hildebrandtii

Cyathea hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can persist for several years, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea hildebrandtii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to humid, tropical environments.
