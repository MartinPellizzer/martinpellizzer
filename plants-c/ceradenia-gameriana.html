Ceradenia gameriana

Ceradenia gameriana, commonly known as the Giant Ceradenia, is a striking member of the orchid family, native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, helping it survive in its challenging environment.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
