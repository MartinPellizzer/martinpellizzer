Cladochaeta velutina

Cladochaeta velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky weed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a creeping growth habit and often forms dense mats, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds.
