Cirsium laciniatum

Cirsium laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.

Cirsium laciniatum is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
