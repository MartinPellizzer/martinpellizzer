Corryocactus chachapoyensis

Corryocactus chachapoyensis, commonly known as the Chachapoya cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
