Cyathea mitrata

Cyathea mitrata, commonly known as the helmet fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea mitrata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
