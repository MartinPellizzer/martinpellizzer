Caragana zahlbruckneri var. pilosa

Caragana zahlbruckneri var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy peashrub, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soils in arid regions.
