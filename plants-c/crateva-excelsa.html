Crateva excelsa

Crateva excelsa, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or velvet bean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that enclose edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both agricultural and medicinal contexts.

Crateva excelsa is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural systems.
