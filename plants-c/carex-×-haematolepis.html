Carex × haematolepis

Carex × haematolepis, commonly known as the blood-stained sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown stems and leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Carex × haematolepis is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique coloration and ecological benefits.
