Carex buchananii

Carex buchananii, commonly known as Buchanan's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Buchanan's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
