Cuphea utriculosa

Cuphea utriculosa, commonly known as the bladdercup, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders, which give the plant its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea utriculosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
