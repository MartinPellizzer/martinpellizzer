Crotalaria pisiformis

Crotalaria pisiformis, commonly known as the kidney bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped seed pods and erect, branching stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual appeal to gardens. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Crotalaria pisiformis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
