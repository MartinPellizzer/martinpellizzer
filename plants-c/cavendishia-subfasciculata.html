Cavendishia subfasciculata

Cavendishia subfasciculata, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often display shades of red and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, clustered flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
