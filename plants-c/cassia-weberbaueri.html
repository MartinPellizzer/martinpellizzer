Cassia weberbaueri

Cassia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its aromatic properties. Cassia weberbaueri typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
