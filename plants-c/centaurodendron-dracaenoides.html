Centaurodendron dracaenoides

Centaurodendron dracaenoides, commonly known as the dragon tree of Madagascar, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicle clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and exotic species in horticulture.
