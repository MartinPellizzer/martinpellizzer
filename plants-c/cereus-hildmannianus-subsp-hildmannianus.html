Cereus hildmannianus subsp. hildmannianus

Cereus hildmannianus subsp.

hildmannianus, commonly known as the Hildmann's cactus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with sharp, reddish-brown spines. It produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers at night, which attract nocturnal pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry conditions.
