Callicarpa gracilis

Callicarpa gracilis, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that transition into bright red, fleshy berries in the fall. These berries are edible for humans and birds, and they contain numerous small seeds.

Callicarpa gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
