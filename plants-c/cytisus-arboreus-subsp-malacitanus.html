Cytisus arboreus subsp. malacitanus

Cytisus arboreus subsp.

malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has slender, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
