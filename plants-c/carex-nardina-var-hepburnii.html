Carex nardina var. hepburnii

Carex nardina var. hepburnii, commonly known as Hepburn's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. Native to regions of Asia, including Japan and China, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded woodland areas and can be cultivated in gardens with similar conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
