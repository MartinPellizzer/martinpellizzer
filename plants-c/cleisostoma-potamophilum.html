Cleisostoma potamophilum

Cleisostoma potamophilum, commonly known as the river stream orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments near streams and rivers. The plant features a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil along waterways.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
