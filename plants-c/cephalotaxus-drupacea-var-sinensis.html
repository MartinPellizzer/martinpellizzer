Cephalotaxus drupacea var. sinensis

Cephalotaxus drupacea var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to China.

It features a compact, shrub-like form with dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
