Cactus echinocactoides

Cactus echinocactoides, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This cactus is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and unique aesthetic.
