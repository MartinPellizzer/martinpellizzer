Chionachne barbata

Chionachne barbata, commonly known as the hairy snowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Chionachne barbata plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments.
