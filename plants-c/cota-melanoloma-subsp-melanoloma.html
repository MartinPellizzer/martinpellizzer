Cota melanoloma subsp. melanoloma

Cota melanoloma subsp.

melanoloma, commonly known as the black-leaf cota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and covered in fine, dark-colored pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens. Its leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a distinctive dark green color with a silvery underside.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
