Coelogyne sulphurea var. crassifolia

Coelogyne sulphurea var. crassifolia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
