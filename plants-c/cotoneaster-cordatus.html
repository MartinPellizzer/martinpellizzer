Cotoneaster cordatus

Cotoneaster cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster cordatus is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and other small animals.
