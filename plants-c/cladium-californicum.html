Cladium californicum

Cladium californicum, commonly known as the California sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of California.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of riparian and marsh ecosystems.

Cladium californicum plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
