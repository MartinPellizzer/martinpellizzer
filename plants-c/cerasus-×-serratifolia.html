Cerasus × serratifolia

Cerasus × serratifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruit. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blossoms and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
