Cupressus obtusa subsp. breviramea

Cupressus obtusa subsp.

breviramea, commonly known as the narrow-needle Japanese cypress, is a subspecies native to Japan. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves.

The foliage is typically dark green and arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
