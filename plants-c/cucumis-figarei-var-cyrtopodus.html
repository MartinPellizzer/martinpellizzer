Cucumis figarei var. cyrtopodus

Cucumis figarei var. cyrtopodus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a unique variant of the cucumber genus characterized by its distinctive, horn-like projections on the fruit.

This plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its stems are hairy and trailing. The fruit develops in clusters, often turning from green to yellow as it matures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
