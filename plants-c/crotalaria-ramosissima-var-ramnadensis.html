Crotalaria ramosissima var. ramnadensis

Crotalaria ramosissima var. ramnadensis, commonly known as the Ramnad spurred crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its branched stems and showy yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, often with a hairy texture.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
