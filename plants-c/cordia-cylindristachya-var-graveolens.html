Cordia cylindristachya var. graveolens

Cordia cylindristachya var. graveolens, commonly known as the fragrant cordia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, particularly noticeable in the evening.

Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
