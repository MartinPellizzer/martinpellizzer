Celtis membranacea

Celtis membranacea, commonly known as the paperleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas, stream banks, and disturbed sites.
