Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. gnaphalodes

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

gnaphalodes, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
