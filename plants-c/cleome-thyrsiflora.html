Cleome thyrsiflora

Cleome thyrsiflora, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome thyrsiflora thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
