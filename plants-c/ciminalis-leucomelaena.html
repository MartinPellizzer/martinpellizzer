Ciminalis leucomelaena

Ciminalis leucomelaena, commonly known as the white-veined ciminalis, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a pale green coloration and prominent white veins that create an elegant contrast. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
