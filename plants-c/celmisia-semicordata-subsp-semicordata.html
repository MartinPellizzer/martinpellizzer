Celmisia semicordata subsp. semicordata

Celmisia semicordata subsp.

semicordata, commonly known as the mountain celmisia, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often on rocky or scree slopes.

The plant has a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is an important component of New Zealand's alpine flora.
