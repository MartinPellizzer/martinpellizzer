Cryptanthus crassifolius

Cryptanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf cryptanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native arid environments.

Cryptanthus crassifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique flowering display.
