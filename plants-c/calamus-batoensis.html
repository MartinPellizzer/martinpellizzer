Calamus batoensis

Calamus batoensis, commonly known as the Batoensis canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus batoensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of materials for handicrafts.
