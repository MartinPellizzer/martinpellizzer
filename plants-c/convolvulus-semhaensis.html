Convolvulus semhaensis

Convolvulus semhaensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its vines can climb or trail, and it produces small, oblong fruits that contain seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
