Croton neoholstiifolius

Croton neoholstiifolius, commonly known as the Neoholstiifolius croton, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Croton.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Croton neoholstiifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
