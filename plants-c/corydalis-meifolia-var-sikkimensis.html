Corydalis meifolia var. sikkimensis

Corydalis meifolia var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim meadow corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Corydalis meifolia var. sikkimensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
