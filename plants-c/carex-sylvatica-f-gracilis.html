Carex sylvatica f. gracilis

Carex sylvatica f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a variant of the wood sedge species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The foliage is often dark green and densely arranged, contributing to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
