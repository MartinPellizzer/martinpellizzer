Cyperus braunii

Cyperus braunii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus braunii produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife species.
