Cyathea willdenowiana var. armigera

Cyathea willdenowiana var. armigera, commonly known as the armiger fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to form dense clumps over time.
