Clematis ranunculoides var. ranunculoides

Clematis ranunculoides var. ranunculoides, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length, often trailing or scrambling through vegetation. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is well adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
