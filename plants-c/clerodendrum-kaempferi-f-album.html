Clerodendrum kaempferi f. album

Clerodendrum kaempferi f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered glorybower, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3-5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of white or pale pink flowers, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles and bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual effect to gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
