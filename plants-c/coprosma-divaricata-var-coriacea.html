Coprosma divaricata var. coriacea

Coprosma divaricata var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
