Chaetogastra durangensis

Chaetogastra durangensis, commonly known as the Durango thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are covered in dense, spiny hairs, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
