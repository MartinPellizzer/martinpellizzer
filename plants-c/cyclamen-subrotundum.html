Cyclamen subrotundum

Cyclamen subrotundum, commonly known as the woolly cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a tuberous root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

Cyclamen subrotundum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
