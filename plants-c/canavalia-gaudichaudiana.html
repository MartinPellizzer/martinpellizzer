Canavalia gaudichaudiana

Canavalia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the white bean or gaudichaudia, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and can thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Canavalia gaudichaudiana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
