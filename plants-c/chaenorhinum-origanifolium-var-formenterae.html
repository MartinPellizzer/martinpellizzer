Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. formenterae

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. formenterae, commonly known as the Formentera dwarf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balearic Islands.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow and resemble those of oregano, giving it its species name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
