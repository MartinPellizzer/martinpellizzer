Campanula sibirica subsp. hohenackeri

Campanula sibirica subsp.

hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming blooms.
