Cyphostemma kirkianum subsp. kirkianum

Cyphostemma kirkianum subsp.

kirkianum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by long, narrow fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
