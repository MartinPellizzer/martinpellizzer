Corynephorus articulatus var. intermedius

Corynephorus articulatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate false brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
