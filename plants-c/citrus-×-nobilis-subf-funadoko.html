Citrus × nobilis subf. funadoko

Citrus × nobilis subf.

funadoko, commonly known as the Funadoko mandarin, is a lesser-known citrus hybrid originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It is adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subvariety is prized for its unique flavor and is often used in specialty citrus cultivation.
