Conyza hirtella

Conyza hirtella, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its yellow flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Conyza hirtella is often found in dry to moist soils and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds.
