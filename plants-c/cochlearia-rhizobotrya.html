Cochlearia rhizobotrya

Cochlearia rhizobotrya, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically hairy and lobed. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and coastal areas.

Cochlearia rhizobotrya is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
