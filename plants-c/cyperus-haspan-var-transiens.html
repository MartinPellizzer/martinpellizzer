Cyperus haspan var. transiens

Cyperus haspan var. transiens, commonly known as the wandering grass or transient flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
