Cunila tomentosa

Cunila tomentosa, commonly known as fuzzy cunila, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fuzzy, hairy leaves are a distinguishing characteristic, giving it a unique texture.

Cunila tomentosa is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
