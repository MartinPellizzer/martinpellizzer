Cassia tapahuascana

Cassia tapahuascana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia tapahuascana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
