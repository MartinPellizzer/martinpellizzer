Crataegus azarolus var. chrysoclada

Crataegus azarolus var. chrysoclada, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
