Celsia parvifolia

Celsia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved celsia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, ovate leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of alpine flora.
