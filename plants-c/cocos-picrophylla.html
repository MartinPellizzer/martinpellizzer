Cocos picrophylla

Cocos picrophylla, commonly known as the dwarf coconut palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is used for propagation. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
