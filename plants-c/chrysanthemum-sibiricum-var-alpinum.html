Chrysanthemum sibiricum var. alpinum

Chrysanthemum sibiricum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine chrysanthemum, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
