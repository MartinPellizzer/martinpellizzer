Caragana andassaica

Caragana andassaica, commonly known as the Armenian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by pea-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and semi-arid environments.

Caragana andassaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or degraded landscapes.
