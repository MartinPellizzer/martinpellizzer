Coelogyne bracteosa

Coelogyne bracteosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple fragrant blooms that appear during the rainy season.

Coelogyne bracteosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
