Calytrix insperata

Calytrix insperata, commonly known as the pink-flowered everlasting plant, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Western Australia, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Calytrix insperata is known for its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color and shape even when dried.
