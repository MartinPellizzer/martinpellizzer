Cucumis flexuosus var. curvatoalbus

Cucumis flexuosus var. curvatoalbus, commonly known as the white-curved cucumber, is a unique variant of the species native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, slightly curved fruits that mature to a white or pale yellow color. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with slender stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, crisp flavor, making it suitable for both decorative and culinary use.
