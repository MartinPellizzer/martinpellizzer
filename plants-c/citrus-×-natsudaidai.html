Citrus × natsudaidai

Citrus × natsudaidai, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or natsudaidai, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with a thin, smooth peel. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its high vitamin C content.

The botanical classification of Citrus × natsudaidai places it within the Rutaceae family, highlighting its relationship to other citrus species.
