Chamaesyce triligulata

Chamaesyce triligulata, commonly known as the three-lobed sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and dunes.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
