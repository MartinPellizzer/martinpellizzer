Cirsium horridulum subsp. chrismarii

Cirsium horridulum subsp.

chrismarii, commonly known as the prickly thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a rosette growth habit with a thick, fleshy stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust foliage and slightly different flower structure compared to other members of the Cirsium genus.
