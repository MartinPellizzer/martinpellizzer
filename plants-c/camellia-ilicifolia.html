Camellia ilicifolia

Camellia ilicifolia, commonly known as the holly-leaved camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, holly-like leaves and produces showy, camellia-type flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is evergreen and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia ilicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
