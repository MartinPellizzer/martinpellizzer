Centaurea limbata f. exigua

Centaurea limbata f. exigua, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and wastelands. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
