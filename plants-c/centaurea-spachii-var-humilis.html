Centaurea spachii var. humilis

Centaurea spachii var. humilis, commonly known as the Algerian knapweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
