Calamus kunstleri

Calamus kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of hard, spiky fruits. Calamus kunstleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their starch content, similar to other members of the genus.
