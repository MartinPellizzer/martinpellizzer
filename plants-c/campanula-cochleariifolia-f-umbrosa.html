Campanula cochleariifolia f. umbrosa

Campanula cochleariifolia f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a variant of the Campanula cochleariifolia species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is often used in woodland gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
