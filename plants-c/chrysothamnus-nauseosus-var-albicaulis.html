Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. albicaulis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. albicaulis, commonly known as whiteleaf goldenbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and yellow flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
