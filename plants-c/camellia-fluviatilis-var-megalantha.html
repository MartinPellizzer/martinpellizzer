Camellia fluviatilis var. megalantha

Camellia fluviatilis var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant river camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, with a deep red to pink coloration. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is adapted to humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
