Cacalia zigzag var. cuneata

Cacalia zigzag var. cuneata, commonly known as the zigzag bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This perennial plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
