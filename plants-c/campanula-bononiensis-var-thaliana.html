Campanula bononiensis var. thaliana

Campanula bononiensis var. thaliana, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be found in moist meadows and rocky slopes in its natural habitat.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
