Chrysophyllum gonavense

Chrysophyllum gonavense, commonly known as the Gonave cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
