Campanula rapunculus subvar. santae

Campanula rapunculus subvar. santae, commonly known as the Santa Campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
