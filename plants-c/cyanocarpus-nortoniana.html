Cyanocarpus nortoniana

Cyanocarpus nortoniana, commonly known as the New Zealand nutmeg tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas of New Zealand. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

The tree is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
