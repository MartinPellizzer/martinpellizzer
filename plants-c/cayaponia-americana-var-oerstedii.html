Cayaponia americana var. oerstedii

Cayaponia americana var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's cayaponia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
