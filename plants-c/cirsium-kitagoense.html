Cirsium kitagoense

Cirsium kitagoense, commonly known as the Kitago thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Cirsium kitagoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
