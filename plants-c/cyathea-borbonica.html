Cyathea borbonica

Cyathea borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Cyathea borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
