Chirita minutiserrulata

Chirita minutiserrulata, commonly known as the narrow-toothed chirita, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
