Clematis ochroleuca var. ovata

Clematis ochroleuca var. ovata, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
