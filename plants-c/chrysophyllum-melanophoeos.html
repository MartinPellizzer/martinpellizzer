Chrysophyllum melanophoeos

Chrysophyllum melanophoeos, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a thick-skinned, egg-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe, containing a creamy, sweet pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
