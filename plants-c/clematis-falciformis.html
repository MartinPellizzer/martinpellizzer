Clematis falciformis

Clematis falciformis, commonly known as the sickleleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are usually clustered in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis falciformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
