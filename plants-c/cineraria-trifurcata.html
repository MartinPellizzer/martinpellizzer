Cineraria trifurcata

Cineraria trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal environments. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Cineraria trifurcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
