Ceratochloa cathartica

Ceratochloa cathartica, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems.
