Crotalaria pauciflora

Crotalaria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bean-like pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with palmately compound leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are often borne on short pedicels, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria pauciflora is also noted for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
