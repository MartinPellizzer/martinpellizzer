Convolvulus tricolor subsp. cupanianus

Convolvulus tricolor subsp.

cupanianus, commonly known as the three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along roadsides or in disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
