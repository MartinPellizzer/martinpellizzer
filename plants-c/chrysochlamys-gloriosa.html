Chrysochlamys gloriosa

Chrysochlamys gloriosa, commonly known as the golden chrysochlamys, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for seaside landscapes.
