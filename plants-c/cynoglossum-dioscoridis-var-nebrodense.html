Cynoglossum dioscoridis var. nebrodense

Cynoglossum dioscoridis var. nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebroden Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Balkans.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, kidney-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
