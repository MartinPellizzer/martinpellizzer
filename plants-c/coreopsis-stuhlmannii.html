Coreopsis stuhlmannii

Coreopsis stuhlmannii, commonly known as Stuhlmann's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Coreopsis stuhlmannii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
