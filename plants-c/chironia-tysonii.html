Chironia tysonii

Chironia tysonii, commonly known as the Tysons' chironia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color, blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
