Calanthe angraeciflora

Calanthe angraeciflora, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to Madagascar and belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its elegant, upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers with white markings, which are borne on tall, slender stems. Calanthe angraeciflora thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in its native region.
