Cyrtochilum rolfeanum

Cyrtochilum rolfeanum, commonly known as the Rolfe's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow blooms with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Cyrtochilum rolfeanum prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
