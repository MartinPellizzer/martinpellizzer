Clusia valerii

Clusia valerii, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Clusia valerii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade.
