Cyperus squalidus

Cyperus squalidus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Cyperus squalidus is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in various aquatic habitats.
