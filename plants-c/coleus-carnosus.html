Coleus carnosus

Coleus carnosus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and come in a variety of colors, including green, purple, and red.

The flowers, which are usually pink or violet, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
