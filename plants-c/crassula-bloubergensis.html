Crassula bloubergensis

Crassula bloubergensis, commonly known as the Blouberg Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
