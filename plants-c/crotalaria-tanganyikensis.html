Crotalaria tanganyikensis

Crotalaria tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyika crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
