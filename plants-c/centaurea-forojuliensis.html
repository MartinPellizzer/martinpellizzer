Centaurea forojuliensis

Centaurea forojuliensis, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its bright blue or purple flowers. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native region.
