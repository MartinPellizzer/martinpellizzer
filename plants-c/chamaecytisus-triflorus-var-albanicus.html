Chamaecytisus triflorus var. albanicus

Chamaecytisus triflorus var. albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian flowering bush, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that remain on the stems throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
