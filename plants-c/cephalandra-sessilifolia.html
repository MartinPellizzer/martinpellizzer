Cephalandra sessilifolia

Cephalandra sessilifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
