Cassia houstoniana

Cassia houstoniana, commonly known as the Houstonia cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a woody structure with thorn-like stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for use in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
