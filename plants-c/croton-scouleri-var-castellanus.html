Croton scouleri var. castellanus

Croton scouleri var. castellanus, commonly known as the Castellanus croton, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with a deep green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes variegated underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
