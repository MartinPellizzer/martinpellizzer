Carex vulgaris var. antucensis

Carex vulgaris var. antucensis, commonly known as the Antuco sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
