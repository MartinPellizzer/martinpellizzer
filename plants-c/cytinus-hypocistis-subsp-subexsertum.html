Cytinus hypocistis subsp. subexsertum

Cytinus hypocistis subsp.

subexsertum, commonly known as the naked bladderpod, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structure that encloses the flower. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its morphology is adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves and a compact growth form.

The subspecies is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and ecological role in its native habitat.
