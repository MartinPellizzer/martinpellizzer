Cassia blanchetii

Cassia blanchetii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Cassia blanchetii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
