Cucumis reticulatus

Cucumis reticulatus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a climbing vine native to southern Asia.

It produces elongated, thin fruits with a mild, crisp flavor and a slightly sweet aroma. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is valued in traditional cuisines.
