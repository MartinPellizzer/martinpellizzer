Calytrix glabra

Calytrix glabra, commonly known as the smooth calytrix, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Calytrix glabra is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
