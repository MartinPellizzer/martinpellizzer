Carex × halophila subsp. affinis

Carex × halophila subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to high-salinity environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.
