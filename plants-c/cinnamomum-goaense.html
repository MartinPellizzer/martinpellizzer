Cinnamomum goaense

Cinnamomum goaense, commonly known as Goa cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale pink. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that contain one to three seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
