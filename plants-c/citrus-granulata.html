Citrus granulata

Citrus granulata, commonly known as the granulate citrus or granulated lemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pebbled bark and fragrant, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to spherical, greenish-yellow fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp with a mild citrus flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
