Cirsium ellenbergii

Cirsium ellenbergii, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flower heads. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in its native habitat.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
