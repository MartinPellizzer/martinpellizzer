Camelina foetida

Camelina foetida, commonly known as stinkcup, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is particularly noticeable when the fruits are mature.
