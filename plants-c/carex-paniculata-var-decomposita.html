Carex paniculata var. decomposita

Carex paniculata var. decomposita, commonly known as the decomposed sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale brown spikelets that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
