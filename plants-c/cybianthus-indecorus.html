Cybianthus indecorus

Cybianthus indecorus, commonly known as the elegant beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cybianthus indecorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
