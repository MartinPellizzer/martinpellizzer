Calophyllum brassii

Calophyllum brassii, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a broad, spreading canopy. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its seeds, which are used in the production of shea butter.
