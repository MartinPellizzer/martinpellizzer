Cassia aldabrensis

Cassia aldabrensis, commonly known as the Socotra cassia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is a small shrub characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. Cassia aldabrensis is notable for its unique morphology, including its thick, fleshy leaves and its role in the local ecosystem.
