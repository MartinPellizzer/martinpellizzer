Croton guianensis

Croton guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan croton, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five to nine lobes. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically yellow or greenish in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often covered in a sticky resin.
