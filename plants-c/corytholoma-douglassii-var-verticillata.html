Corytholoma douglassii var. verticillata

Corytholoma douglassii var. verticillata, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
