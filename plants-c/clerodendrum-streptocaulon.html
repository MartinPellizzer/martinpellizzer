Clerodendrum streptocaulon

Clerodendrum streptocaulon, commonly known as the red-flowered clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
