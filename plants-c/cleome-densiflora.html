Cleome densiflora

Cleome densiflora, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Cleome densiflora is an annual herb that blooms from summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
