Cordyline terminalis var. petiolaris

Cordyline terminalis var. petiolaris, commonly known as the broad-leafed cabbage tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Oceania.

It features tall, upright stems with large, lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in terminal spikes, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both gardens and indoor spaces.
