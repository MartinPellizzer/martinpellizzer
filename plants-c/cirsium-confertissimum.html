Cirsium confertissimum

Cirsium confertissimum, commonly known as the dense thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is distinguished by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
