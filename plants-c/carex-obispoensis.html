Carex obispoensis

Carex obispoensis, commonly known as the Obispo sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typical of many sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.

Carex obispoensis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
