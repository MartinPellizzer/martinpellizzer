Clematis cirrhosa var. angustifolia

Clematis cirrhosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with narrow, elongated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
