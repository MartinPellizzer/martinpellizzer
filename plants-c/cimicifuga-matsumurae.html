Cimicifuga matsumurae

Cimicifuga matsumurae, commonly known as Japanese bugbane, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear large, white, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces berries that are red when mature.

Cimicifuga matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
