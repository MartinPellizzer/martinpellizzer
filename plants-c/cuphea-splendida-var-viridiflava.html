Cuphea splendida var. viridiflava

Cuphea splendida var. viridiflava, commonly known as the golden cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a nice contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
