Crataegus henryi

Crataegus henryi, commonly known as Henry's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
