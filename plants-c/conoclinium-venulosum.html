Conoclinium venulosum

Conoclinium venulosum, commonly known as the fragrant mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Conoclinium venulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
