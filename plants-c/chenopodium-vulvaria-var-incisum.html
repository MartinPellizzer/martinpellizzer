Chenopodium vulvaria var. incisum

Chenopodium vulvaria var. incisum, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, though they are typically consumed young.
