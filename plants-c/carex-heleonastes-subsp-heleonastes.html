Carex heleonastes subsp. heleonastes

Carex heleonastes subsp.

heleonastes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, three-rowed berries.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
