Centranthus nevadensis subsp. atlanticus

Centranthus nevadensis subsp.

atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic redstem, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the northeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the Atlantic coast.

The plant produces vibrant red flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is adapted to salty air and coastal conditions, making it a hardy species for maritime environments.
