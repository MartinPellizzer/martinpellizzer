Clusia silvicola

Clusia silvicola, commonly known as the forest clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and often milky sap. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries.

Clusia silvicola is often found in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
