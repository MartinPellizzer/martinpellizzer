Crepidium bancanum

Crepidium bancanum, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
