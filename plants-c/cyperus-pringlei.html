Cyperus pringlei

Cyperus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus pringlei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
