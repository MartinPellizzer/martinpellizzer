Cordyline dennisonii

Cordyline dennisonii, commonly known as the New Zealand cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and rosette of large, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant can grow up to 15 meters in height, with leaves that can reach lengths of over 2 meters. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters at the top of the plant, followed by red berries.

Cordyline dennisonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
