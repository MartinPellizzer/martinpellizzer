Chenopodium nutans

Chenopodium nutans, commonly known as the nodding goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Americas and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often giving it a fluffy appearance. Its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Chenopodium nutans is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
