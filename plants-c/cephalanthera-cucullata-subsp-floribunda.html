Cephalanthera cucullata subsp. floribunda

Cephalanthera cucullata subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the showy helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces a single, helmet-shaped flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
