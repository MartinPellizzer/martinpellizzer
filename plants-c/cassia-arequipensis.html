Cassia arequipensis

Cassia arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia arequipensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
