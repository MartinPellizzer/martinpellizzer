Calanthe aquamarina

Calanthe aquamarina, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Calanthe aquamarina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
