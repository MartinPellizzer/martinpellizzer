Cacalia insignis

Cacalia insignis, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange ray flowers that surround a central disk of smaller florets. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Cacalia insignis is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to extreme conditions.
