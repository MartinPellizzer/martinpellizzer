Codonopsis ussuriensis var. minima

Codonopsis ussuriensis var. minima, commonly known as the small-flowered Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
