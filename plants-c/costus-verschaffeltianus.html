Costus verschaffeltianus

Costus verschaffeltianus, commonly known as the African costus, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces striking, showy inflorescences with orange-red bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
