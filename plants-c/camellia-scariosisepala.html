Camellia scariosisepala

Camellia scariosisepala, commonly known as the scarious-sepaled camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and often reddish-brown petal bases, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with showy, overlapping petals that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia scariosisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
