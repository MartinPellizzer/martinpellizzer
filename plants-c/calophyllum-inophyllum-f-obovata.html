Calophyllum inophyllum f. obovata

Calophyllum inophyllum f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed white cedar, is a variant of the native tropical tree Calophyllum inophyllum.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant is part of the Clusiaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal and humid environments.
