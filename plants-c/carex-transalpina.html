Carex transalpina

Carex transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.

Carex transalpina is generally hardy and adaptable, making it a resilient addition to gardens and natural habitats.
