Clematis grewiiflora

Clematis grewiiflora, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features climbing stems with thorns and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and trellises.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
