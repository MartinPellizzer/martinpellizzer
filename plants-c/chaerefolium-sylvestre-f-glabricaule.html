Chaerefolium sylvestre f. glabricaule

Chaerefolium sylvestre f. glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed woodruff, is a variant of the woodruff plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to Europe. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

This subspecies is often found in woodlands and along forest edges, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
