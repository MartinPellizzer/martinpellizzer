Carissa ovata

Carissa ovata, commonly known as the yellow bell bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, woody stems and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a small, oval berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

Carissa ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
