Cocculus lycioides

Cocculus lycioides, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. Its roots and stems contain various alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
