Chamarea capensis

Chamarea capensis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chamarea capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
