Coxia

Coxia, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rough, prickly leaves and spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Coxia typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a robust, branched stem.

Its flowers are small and clustered in heads, contributing to its role as a weed in agricultural settings.
