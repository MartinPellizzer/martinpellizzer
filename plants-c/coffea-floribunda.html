Coffea floribunda

Coffea floribunda, commonly known as the "flowering coffee," is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its ornamental white and pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries containing seeds that are used for coffee production. It prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate.

While not as widely cultivated as other coffee species, it holds significance in both horticulture and specialty coffee markets.
