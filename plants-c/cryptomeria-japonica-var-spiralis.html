Cryptomeria japonica var. spiralis

Cryptomeria japonica var. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral cedar, is a unique variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of branches.

This morphological feature gives the tree a striking, almost sculptural appearance, with branches curling in a helical pattern around the trunk. The plant is native to Japan and is typically found in mountainous regions where it thrives in cool, moist climates. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

The spiral cedar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
