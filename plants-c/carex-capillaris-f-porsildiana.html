Carex capillaris f. porsildiana

Carex capillaris f. porsildiana, commonly known as the Porsild's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wet meadows or along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include small, wind-pollinated flowers and the ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
