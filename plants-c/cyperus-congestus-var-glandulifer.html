Cyperus congestus var. glandulifer

Cyperus congestus var. glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. Its roots are often rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The species is characterized by the presence of glandular structures on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique botanical identity.
