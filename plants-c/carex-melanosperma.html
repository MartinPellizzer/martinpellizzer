Carex melanosperma

Carex melanosperma, commonly known as black-spermed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive dark green color and a rough texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of its flowering stems.
