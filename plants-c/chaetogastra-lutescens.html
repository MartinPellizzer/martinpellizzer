Chaetogastra lutescens

Chaetogastra lutescens, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, golden-colored hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in clusters.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance as a low-growing, ornamental plant.
