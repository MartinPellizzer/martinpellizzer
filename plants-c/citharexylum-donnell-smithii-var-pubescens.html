Citharexylum donnell-smithii var. pubescens

Citharexylum donnell-smithii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered with fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
