Crataegus roribacca

Crataegus roribacca, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Crataegus roribacca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
