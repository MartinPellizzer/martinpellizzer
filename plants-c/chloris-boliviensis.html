Chloris boliviensis

Chloris boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Chloris boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
