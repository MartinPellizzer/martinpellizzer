Crataegus lindmanii var. extrasepala

Crataegus lindmanii var. extrasepala, commonly known as the western hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, often attracting wildlife. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a rough texture.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing food for birds and small mammals.
