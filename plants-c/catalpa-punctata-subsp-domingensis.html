Catalpa punctata subsp. domingensis

Catalpa punctata subsp.

domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico. It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white to cream-colored flowers.

The plant produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
