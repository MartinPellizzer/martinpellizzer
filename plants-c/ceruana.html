Ceruana

Ceruana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to various regions in North America and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Ceruana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which have a mildly sour taste.
