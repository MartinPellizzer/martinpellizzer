Convolvulus sericeus

Convolvulus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender, hairy stems that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or pink flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
