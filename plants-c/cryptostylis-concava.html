Cryptostylis concava

Cryptostylis concava, commonly known as the hollow-stemmed spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, often hollow stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short flowering period during late winter to early spring.

Cryptostylis concava is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral morphology and ecological significance.
