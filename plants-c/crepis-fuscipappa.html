Crepis fuscipappa

Crepis fuscipappa, commonly known as the dark-leaved mouse-ear, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

Crepis fuscipappa thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
