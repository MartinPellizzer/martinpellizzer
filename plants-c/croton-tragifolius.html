Croton tragifolius

Croton tragifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from green to red, yellow, and orange, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant leaf colors.
