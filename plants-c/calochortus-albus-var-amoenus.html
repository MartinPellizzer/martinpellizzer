Calochortus albus var. amoenus

Calochortus albus var. amoenus, commonly known as the White Ghost Lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to California.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
