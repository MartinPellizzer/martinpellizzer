Centaurea jacea var. duboisii

Centaurea jacea var. duboisii, commonly known as the Dubois' cornflower, is a variant of the common centaurea.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly France and Spain. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
