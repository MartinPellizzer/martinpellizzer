Cyperus albosanguineus

Cyperus albosanguineus, commonly known as the white blood rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, pale pink to white flowers in dense clusters. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in areas with poor drainage. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Cyperus albosanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in water management systems.
