Cambessedesia hilariana var. vulgaris

Cambessedesia hilariana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the red-flowered pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking red-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
