Carex × taraspensis

Carex × taraspensis, commonly known as the Taraspensian sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and typically forms clumps of narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often borne on slender, leaf-like bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex × taraspensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
