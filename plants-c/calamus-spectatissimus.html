Calamus spectatissimus

Calamus spectatissimus, commonly known as the spectacled calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental leaves that feature a distinct yellowish or greenish banding pattern, resembling spectacles. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, a common feature among Aroids.
