Canella winterana

Canella winterana, commonly known as the winterbloom canella, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and cinnamon plants. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

Canella winterana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive winter blooming habit.
