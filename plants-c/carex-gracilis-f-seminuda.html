Carex gracilis f. seminuda

Carex gracilis f. seminuda, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced inflorescence and is often found in shaded, damp habitats.
