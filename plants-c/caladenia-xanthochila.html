Caladenia xanthochila

Caladenia xanthochila, commonly known as the yellow-stained orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive yellowish-tipped labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open forests and woodlands. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn season, and it is known for its striking coloration and intricate floral structure.

Caladenia xanthochila is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
