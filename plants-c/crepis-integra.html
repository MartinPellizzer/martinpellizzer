Crepis integra

Crepis integra, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes as its fruit.

Crepis integra is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
