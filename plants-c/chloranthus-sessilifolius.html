Chloranthus sessilifolius

Chloranthus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaved chloranthus, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, sessile leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive greenish-yellow color, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
