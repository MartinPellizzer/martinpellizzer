Catalpa purpurea f. denticulata

Catalpa purpurea f. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate catalpa, is a cultivar of the catalpa tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are lance-shaped with finely serrated edges, giving the plant its name. It produces showy, pendulous clusters of white to pale purple flowers in the spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
