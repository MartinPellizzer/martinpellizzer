Cochlearia borzaeana

Cochlearia borzaeana, commonly known as the Borzaeana horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its distinctive feature is the presence of horn-like projections on the seed pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

Cochlearia borzaeana is a rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
