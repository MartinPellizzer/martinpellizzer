Coelodiscus eriocarpus

Coelodiscus eriocarpus, commonly known as the velvet pod orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, velvety pods that enclose its seeds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Coelodiscus eriocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
