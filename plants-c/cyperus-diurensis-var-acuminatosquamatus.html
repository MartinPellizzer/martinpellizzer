Cyperus diurensis var. acuminatosquamatus

Cyperus diurensis var. acuminatosquamatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely arranged around the stem. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
