Chamaerops humilis var. inermis

Chamaerops humilis var. inermis, commonly known as the slender date palm, is a compact, cold-hardy palm native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces fan-shaped leaves that are deeply divided and covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant is characterized by its lack of spines, which distinguishes it from other chamaerops varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and container cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its elegant foliage and ability to add a tropical feel to landscapes in cooler climates.
