Cissus sicyoides f. lobata

Cissus sicyoides f. lobata, commonly known as the lobed cutleaf gooseberry, is a variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often deeply cut, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to attach to supports. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, tart berries.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
