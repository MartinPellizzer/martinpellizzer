Calea pachensis

Calea pachensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral morphology. Calea pachensis thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
