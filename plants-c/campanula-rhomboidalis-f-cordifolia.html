Campanula rhomboidalis f. cordifolia

Campanula rhomboidalis f. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial that produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens or borders. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
