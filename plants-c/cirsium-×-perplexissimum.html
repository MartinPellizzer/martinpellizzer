Cirsium × perplexissimum

Cirsium × perplexissimum, commonly known as the perplexing thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy structure and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia. Its hybrid nature results from crosses between different species of thistles, leading to a wide range of morphological variations.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its potential invasiveness.
