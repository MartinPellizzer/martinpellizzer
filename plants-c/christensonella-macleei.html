Christensonella macleei

Christensonella macleei, commonly known as the Macleay's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with pale greenish-white flowers that resemble translucent petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are small and delicate, typically blooming in spring, and it is known for its limited distribution and endangered status.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
