Combretum macrocarpum

Combretum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
