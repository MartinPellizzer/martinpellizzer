Campanula rotundifolia f. luxurians

Campanula rotundifolia f. luxurians, commonly known as the Woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety 'luxurians' is distinguished by its more densely packed foliage and showier floral display compared to the typical form.
