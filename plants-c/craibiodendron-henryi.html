Craibiodendron henryi

Craibiodendron henryi, commonly known as the Henry's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.

Craibiodendron henryi is also noted for its ability to produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring and summer months.
