Carex sumatrensis

Carex sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex sumatrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
