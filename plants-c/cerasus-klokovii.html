Cerasus klokovii

Cerasus klokovii, commonly known as the Siberian cherry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or forested areas. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Cerasus klokovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
