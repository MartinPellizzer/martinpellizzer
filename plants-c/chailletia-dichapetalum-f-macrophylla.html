Chailletia dichapetalum f. macrophylla

Chailletia dichapetalum f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved chailletia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
