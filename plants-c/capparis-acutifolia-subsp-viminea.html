Capparis acutifolia subsp. viminea

Capparis acutifolia subsp.

viminea, commonly known as the slender sour fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with five petals, which are typically followed by small, edible fruits resembling figs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
