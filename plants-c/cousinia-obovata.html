Cousinia obovata

Cousinia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

Cousinia obovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its potential ecological benefits.
