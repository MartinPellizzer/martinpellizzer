Cyathea baroumba

Cyathea baroumba, commonly known as the Barumbe tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the rainforests of New Guinea and northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is a perennial, evergreen species that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a slow growth rate.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities, and it plays an important role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
