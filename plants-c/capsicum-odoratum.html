Capsicum odoratum

Capsicum odoratum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper or wild chili, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique fragrance.
