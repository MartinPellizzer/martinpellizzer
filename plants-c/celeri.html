Celeri

Celeri, commonly known as celery, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. Celery is cultivated for its crisp, aromatic stalks and leaves, which are used in various cuisines worldwide.

It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
