Crataegus oxyacantha f. petermannii

Crataegus oxyacantha f. petermannii, commonly known as the Petermann's hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant produces red berries in the fall. This form is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and ecological studies.
