Crotalaria lamelliformis

Crotalaria lamelliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
