Chrysocoma peduncularis

Chrysocoma peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered chrysocoma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chrysocoma peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
