Cineraria cuneata

Cineraria cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria cuneata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
