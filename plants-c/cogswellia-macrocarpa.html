Cogswellia macrocarpa

Cogswellia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant red hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
