Chrysanthemum setaceum

Chrysanthemum setaceum, commonly known as the sickleleaf chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that resemble sickles, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance make it a favored plant among horticulturists.
