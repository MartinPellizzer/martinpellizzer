Carex nigra subsp. tornata

Carex nigra subsp.

tornata, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex nigra subsp.

tornata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
