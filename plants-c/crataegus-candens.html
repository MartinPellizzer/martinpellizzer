Crataegus candens

Crataegus candens, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Crataegus candens typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and persistent berries, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
