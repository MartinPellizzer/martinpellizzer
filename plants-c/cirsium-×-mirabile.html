Cirsium × mirabile

Cirsium × mirabile, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
