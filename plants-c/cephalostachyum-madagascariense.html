Cephalostachyum madagascariense

Cephalostachyum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or velvet grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
