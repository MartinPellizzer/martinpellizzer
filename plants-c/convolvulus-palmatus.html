Convolvulus palmatus

Convolvulus palmatus, commonly known as the palmate bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is a perennial vine that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.

Convolvulus palmatus is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it challenging to control.
