Carlina vulgaris subsp. stricta

Carlina vulgaris subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the rigid carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and spiny, grayish-green leaves.

The plant produces showy purple or pink flower heads that are typically surrounded by a ring of bracts. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats. Carlina vulgaris subsp.

stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
