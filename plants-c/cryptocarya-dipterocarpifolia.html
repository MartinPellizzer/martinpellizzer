Cryptocarya dipterocarpifolia

Cryptocarya dipterocarpifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
