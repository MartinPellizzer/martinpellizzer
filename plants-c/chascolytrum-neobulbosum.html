Chascolytrum neobulbosum

Chascolytrum neobulbosum, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Chascolytrum neobulbosum is an important species for local ecosystems, providing food for native pollinators and birds.
