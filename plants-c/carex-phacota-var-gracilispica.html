Carex phacota var. gracilispica

Carex phacota var. gracilispica, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in freshwater habitats.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
