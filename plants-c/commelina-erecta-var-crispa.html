Commelina erecta var. crispa

Commelina erecta var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue-violet flowers with white markings, which are borne on slender, erect stems. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly crinkled texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
