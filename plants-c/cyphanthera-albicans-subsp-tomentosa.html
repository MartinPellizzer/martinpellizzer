Cyphanthera albicans subsp. tomentosa

Cyphanthera albicans subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The subspecies tomentosa is distinguished by its particularly thick, felt-like covering on the leaves and stems. It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
