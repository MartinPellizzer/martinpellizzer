Cupressus × wangii

Cupressus × wangii, commonly known as the Chinese lacebark cypress, is a hybrid coniferous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive exfoliating bark that peels in thin, papery layers, revealing a mottled pattern of green and brown. The plant has scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is well-suited to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
