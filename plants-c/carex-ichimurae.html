Carex ichimurae

Carex ichimurae, commonly known as Ichimura's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Carex ichimurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
