Chrysanthemum roopianum

Chrysanthemum roopianum, commonly known as the Himalayan chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Chrysanthemum roopianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
