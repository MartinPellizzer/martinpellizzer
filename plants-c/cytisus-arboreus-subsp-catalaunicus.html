Cytisus arboreus subsp. catalaunicus

Cytisus arboreus subsp.

catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with a distinctive aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
