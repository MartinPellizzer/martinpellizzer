Coreopsis scabra

Coreopsis scabra, commonly known as rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with ray florets that are often tinged with red or orange, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.

Coreopsis scabra is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
