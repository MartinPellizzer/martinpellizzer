Carduus sanctae-balmae

Carduus sanctae-balmae, commonly known as the Holy Balma thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The name "Holy Balma" is believed to reference its association with religious or sacred contexts in some cultures.
