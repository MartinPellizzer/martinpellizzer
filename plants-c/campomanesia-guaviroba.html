Campomanesia guaviroba

Campomanesia guaviroba, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
