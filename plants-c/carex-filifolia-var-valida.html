Carex filifolia var. valida

Carex filifolia var. valida, commonly known as the slender filifolium sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. This variety is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
