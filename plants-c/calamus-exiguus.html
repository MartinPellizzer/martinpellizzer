Calamus exiguus

Calamus exiguus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family members. It thrives in wet, shady environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
