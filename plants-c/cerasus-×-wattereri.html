Cerasus × wattereri

Cerasus × wattereri, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × wattereri, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, reddish bark and oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes rather than for fruit production.
