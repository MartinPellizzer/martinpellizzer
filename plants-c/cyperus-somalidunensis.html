Cyperus somalidunensis

Cyperus somalidunensis, commonly known as the Somali flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Horn of Africa, particularly Somalia and Ethiopia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikelets that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low-water conditions.

Cyperus somalidunensis plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
