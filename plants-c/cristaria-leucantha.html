Cristaria leucantha

Cristaria leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Cristaria leucantha plays a role in water purification and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
