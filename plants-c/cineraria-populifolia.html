Cineraria populifolia

Cineraria populifolia, commonly known as the Poplar-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cineraria populifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
