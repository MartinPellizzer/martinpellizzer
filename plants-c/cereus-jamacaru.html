Cereus jamacaru

Cereus jamacaru, commonly known as the giant night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its growth is slow, making it a long-lived plant in suitable conditions.
