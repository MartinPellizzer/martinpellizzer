Calathea leucostachys

Calathea leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stalked calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with deep green veins and cream or white markings. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, often featuring a velvety texture. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
