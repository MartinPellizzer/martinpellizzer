Crataegus monogyna f. nordica

Crataegus monogyna f. nordica, commonly known as the northern hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact growth habit compared to the typical form. The leaves are usually narrower and the flowers are smaller and more clustered. It thrives in colder climates and is often found in northern Europe.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
