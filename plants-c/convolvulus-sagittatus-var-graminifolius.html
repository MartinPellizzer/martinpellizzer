Convolvulus sagittatus var. graminifolius

Convolvulus sagittatus var. graminifolius, commonly known as the slender arrowhead morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to support itself on nearby structures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
