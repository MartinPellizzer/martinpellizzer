Canscora macrocalyx

Canscora macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of pinnately lobed leaves and produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers with four petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
