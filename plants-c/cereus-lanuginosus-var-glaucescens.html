Cereus lanuginosus var. glaucescens

Cereus lanuginosus var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-tipped cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in soft, white spines and has a distinctive blue-green coloration. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
