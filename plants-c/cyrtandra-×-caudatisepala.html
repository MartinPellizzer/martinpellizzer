Cyrtandra × caudatisepala

Cyrtandra × caudatisepala, commonly known as the long-lobed Cyrtandra, is a hybrid plant species within the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated sepals that give it a unique appearance, often resembling a flower-like structure. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
