Cynanchum filiforme

Cynanchum filiforme, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous roots and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
