Cistus × corbariensis var. grandiflorus

Cistus × corbariensis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
