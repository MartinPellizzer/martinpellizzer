Crassa

Crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period.

Crassa species are also valued for their role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
