Cryptoceras pulchellum

Cryptoceras pulchellum, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of fine, golden-hued hairs. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires careful cultivation to ensure its survival.
