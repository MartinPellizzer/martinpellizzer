Castilleja raupii subsp. ursina

Castilleja raupii subsp.

ursina, commonly known as the Pacific red paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic flowering plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

The plant has slender, branching stems and produces showy, red or orange-red flowers in the spring. Its leaves are small and opposite, and it relies on other plants for some of its water and nutrients.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
