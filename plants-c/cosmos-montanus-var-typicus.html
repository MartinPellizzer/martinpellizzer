Cosmos montanus var. typicus

Cosmos montanus var. typicus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with bright orange petals and a central disk, blooming from summer to fall. The plant has feathery, slender leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and variety, with "montanus" referring to its mountainous habitat.
