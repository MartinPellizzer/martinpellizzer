Caraguata virescens

Caraguata virescens, commonly known as the green caraguata, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ecological role, it is an important component of the rainforest's biodiversity.
