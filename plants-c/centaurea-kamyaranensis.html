Centaurea kamyaranensis

Centaurea kamyaranensis, commonly known as Kamyaran thistle, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its structure includes a robust stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
