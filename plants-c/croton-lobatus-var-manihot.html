Croton lobatus var. manihot

Croton lobatus var. manihot, commonly known as Manihot croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking foliage.
