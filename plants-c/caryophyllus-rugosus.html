Caryophyllus rugosus

Caryophyllus rugosus, commonly known as the rough caryophyll, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant features hairy, upright stems and produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of pubescence, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Caryophyllus rugosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.
