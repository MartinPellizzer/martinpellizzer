Crataegus colorata

Crataegus colorata, commonly known as the Colorado hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
