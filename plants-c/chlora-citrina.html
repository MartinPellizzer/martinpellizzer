Chlora citrina

Chlora citrina, commonly known as the golden chloris, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-green foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in sunny environments.

Chlora citrina is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
