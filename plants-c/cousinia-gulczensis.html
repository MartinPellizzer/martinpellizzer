Cousinia gulczensis

Cousinia gulczensis, commonly known as the Karakoram fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Cousinia gulczensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
