Clematis fusca var. kamtschatica

Clematis fusca var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatica Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its vigorous growth and attractive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
