Crassula aristata

Crassula aristata, commonly known as the "Lace Flower" or "Coral Bells," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Crassula aristata is popular as a houseplant and in rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
