Cineraria petasitis

Cineraria petasitis, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-gray, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Cineraria petasitis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and hardiness.
