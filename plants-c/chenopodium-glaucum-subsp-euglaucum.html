Chenopodium glaucum subsp. euglaucum

Chenopodium glaucum subsp.

euglaucum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray, pubescent leaves that are typically oval to rhombic in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-angled capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
