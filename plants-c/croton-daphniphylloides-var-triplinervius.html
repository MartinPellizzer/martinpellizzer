Croton daphniphylloides var. triplinervius

Croton daphniphylloides var. triplinervius, commonly known as the three-veined croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant shades of green, red, and yellow. The leaves have three prominent veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
