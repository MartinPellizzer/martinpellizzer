Chamaecrista nictitans var. ramosa

Chamaecrista nictitans var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
