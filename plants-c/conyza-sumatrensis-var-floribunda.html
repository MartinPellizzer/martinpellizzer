Conyza sumatrensis var. floribunda

Conyza sumatrensis var. floribunda, commonly known as the Sumatran ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces numerous slender, hairy stems and has finely divided, serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
