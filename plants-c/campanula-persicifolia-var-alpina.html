Campanula persicifolia var. alpina

Campanula persicifolia var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. This variety is often found in rocky soils and meadows at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
