Carex fimbriata

Carex fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Carex fimbriata is characterized by its distinctive fringed edges on the leaf blades, which give it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
