Canavalia beniensis

Canavalia beniensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or benien bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 1-2 meters in height, featuring compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant produces legume pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source in some regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Canavalia beniensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in sustainable agriculture.
