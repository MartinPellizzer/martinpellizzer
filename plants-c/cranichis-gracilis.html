Cranichis gracilis

Cranichis gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with three sepals and three petals, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Cranichis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
