Caesalpinia watsonii

Caesalpinia watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in long, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Caesalpinia watsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
