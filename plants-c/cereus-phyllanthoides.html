Cereus phyllanthoides

Cereus phyllanthoides, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a single night.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
