Centaurea heterophylla

Centaurea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and variable leaf shapes, which contribute to its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea heterophylla is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
