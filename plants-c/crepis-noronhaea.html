Crepis noronhaea

Crepis noronhaea, commonly known as the Noronha's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate. It has small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
