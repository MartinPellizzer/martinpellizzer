Celtis bungeana

Celtis bungeana, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible berries that are clustered in small groups. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Celtis bungeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
