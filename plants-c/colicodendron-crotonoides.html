Colicodendron crotonoides

Colicodendron crotonoides, commonly known as the red crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny pods that contain toxic seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation in areas where children or livestock are present.
