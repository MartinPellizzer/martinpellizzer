Crepis sancta subsp. azerbaijanica

Crepis sancta subsp.

azerbaijanica, commonly known as the Azerbaijani mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters. Its leaves are typically hairy and oval-shaped, with a distinctive basal rosette.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas.
