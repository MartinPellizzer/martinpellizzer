Campanula corbariensis

Campanula corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbari bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Campanula corbariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
