Campomanesia vaccinioides

Campomanesia vaccinioides, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in diverse environments.
