Crinum canalifolium

Crinum canalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are often narrow and pointed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Crinum canalifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
