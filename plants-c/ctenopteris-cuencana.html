Ctenopteris cuencana

Ctenopteris cuencana, commonly known as the Cuencan fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, branching structure that gives it a delicate appearance.

Ctenopteris cuencana plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
