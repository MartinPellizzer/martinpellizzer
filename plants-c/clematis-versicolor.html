Clematis versicolor

Clematis versicolor, commonly known as the variable clematis, is a flowering vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, often bi-colored flowers that range from white to pink and blue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces long, climbing stems. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
