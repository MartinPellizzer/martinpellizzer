Cylindrolobus Rhodobracteus

Cylindrolobus rhodobracteus is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Australia. This plant is known for its vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and dunes. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Despite its beauty, it requires well-drained conditions to flourish.
