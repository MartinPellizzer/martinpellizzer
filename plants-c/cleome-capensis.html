Cleome capensis

Cleome capensis, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Cleome capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
