Citrus crassa

Citrus crassa, commonly known as the rough lemon, is a species of citrus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, and often pebbled rind, which distinguishes it from other citrus fruits. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically sour and not commonly consumed by humans. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a rootstock for other citrus plants due to its hardiness.

The leaves are evergreen, broad, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
