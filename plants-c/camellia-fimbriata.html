Camellia fimbriata

Camellia fimbriata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, double flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Camellia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
