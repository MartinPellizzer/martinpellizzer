Campylotropis macrocarpa f. longepedunculata

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate climbing clematis, is a variant of the species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender peduncles that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub or vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
