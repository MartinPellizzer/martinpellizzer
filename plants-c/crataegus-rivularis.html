Crataegus rivularis

Crataegus rivularis, commonly known as the river hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments. The plant features thorned branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it produces small, hard fruits that are edible but often bitter.

Crataegus rivularis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various birds and insects.
