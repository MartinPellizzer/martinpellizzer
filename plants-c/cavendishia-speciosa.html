Cavendishia speciosa

Cavendishia speciosa, commonly known as the showy cavendishia, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with red or purple markings. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thick, ribbed stems and spiny stems. Its leaves are usually modified into spines, and it produces fleshy, segmented fruits.

Cavendishia speciosa is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
