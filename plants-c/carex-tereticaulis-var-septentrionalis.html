Carex tereticaulis var. septentrionalis

Carex tereticaulis var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
