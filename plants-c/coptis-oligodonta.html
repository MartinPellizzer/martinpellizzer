Coptis oligodonta

Coptis oligodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldthread, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Coptis oligodonta is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
