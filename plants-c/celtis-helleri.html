Celtis helleri

Celtis helleri, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, white, fleshy fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks.

Celtis helleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different climates.
