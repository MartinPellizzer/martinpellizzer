Calamus pogonotium

Calamus pogonotium, commonly known as the bearded calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the arum family.

Calamus pogonotium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
