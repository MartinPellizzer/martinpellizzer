Cousinia korowiakowi

Cousinia korowiakowi, commonly known as the Korowiakow's cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia korowiakowi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
