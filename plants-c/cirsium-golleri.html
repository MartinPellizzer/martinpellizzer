Cirsium golleri

Cirsium golleri, commonly known as the Golleri thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are covered in dense, stiff hairs, providing protection against herbivores and harsh weather conditions.

Cirsium golleri is often found in alpine meadows and is an important species in its native ecosystem.
