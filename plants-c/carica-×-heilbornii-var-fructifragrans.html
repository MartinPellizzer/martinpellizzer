Carica × heilbornii var. fructifragrans

Carica × heilbornii var. fructifragrans, commonly known as the Natal pineapple.

This hybrid plant is a cross between *Carica papaya* and *Carica pubescens*, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It is characterized by its large, fleshy, yellow-orange fruits that are typically segmented and have a sweet, tropical flavor. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features include broad, heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant in tropical regions.
