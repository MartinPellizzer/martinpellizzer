Crataegus americana

Crataegus americana, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 20 feet and features thorned branches, simple oval leaves, and white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pome fruits and distinctive floral structure.
