Clematis connata var. pseudoconnata

Clematis connata var. pseudoconnata, commonly known as the western clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, white hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
