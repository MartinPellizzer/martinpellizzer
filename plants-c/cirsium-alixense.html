Cirsium alixense

Cirsium alixense, commonly known as the Alpes alpine thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium alixense is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
