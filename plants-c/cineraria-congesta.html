Cineraria congesta

Cineraria congesta, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cineraria congesta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
