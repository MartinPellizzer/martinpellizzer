Cardamine impatiens var. pilosa

Cardamine impatiens var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and often have a basal rosette structure, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
