Claoxylon brachyphyllum

Claoxylon brachyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamanu tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
