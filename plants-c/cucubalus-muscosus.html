Cucubalus muscosus

Cucubalus muscosus, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and serrated leaves that are covered in fine, venomous hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils across temperate regions. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environments, including fields, woodland edges, and gardens.

Despite its irritating properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
