Chamaecytisus wulfii

Chamaecytisus wulfii, commonly known as the Wulf's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, arching stems that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are evergreen, narrow, and slightly leathery, providing a dense, low-growing foliage.

Chamaecytisus wulfii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
