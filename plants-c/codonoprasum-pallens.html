Codonoprasum pallens

Codonoprasum pallens, commonly known as the pale codonopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its pale blue or white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
