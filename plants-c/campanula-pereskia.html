Campanula pereskia

Campanula pereskia, commonly known as the Pereskia campanula, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often with a woody base and succulent-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Campanula pereskia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
