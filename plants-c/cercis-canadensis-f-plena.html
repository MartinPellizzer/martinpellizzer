Cercis canadensis f. plena

Cercis canadensis f. plena, commonly known as the double red maple, is a cultivar of the red maple tree known for its striking floral display.

It features dense, double blooms that are pink to red in color, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant is native to North America and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and turns vibrant red in the fall.

This ornamental tree is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
