Cestrum endresii

Cestrum endresii, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often mistaken for ornamental features. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum endresii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nocturnal beauty.
