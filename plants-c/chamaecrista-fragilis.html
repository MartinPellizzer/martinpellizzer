Chamaecrista fragilis

Chamaecrista fragilis, commonly known as the fragile crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
