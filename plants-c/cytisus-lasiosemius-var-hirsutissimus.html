Cytisus lasiosemius var. hirsutissimus

Cytisus lasiosemius var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are small, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
