Cydonia vulgaris f. lusitanica

Cydonia vulgaris f. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian quince, is a botanical variant of the common quince.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula. The plant produces large, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late autumn. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and a strong scent.

The fruit is used in traditional culinary practices and has historical significance in Mediterranean agriculture.
