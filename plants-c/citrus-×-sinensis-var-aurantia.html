Citrus × sinensis var. aurantia

Citrus × sinensis var. aurantia, commonly known as the blood orange.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive deep red or crimson-colored flesh, which is rich in anthocyanins. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. The fruit has a sweet and tart flavor profile, with a complex aroma due to its high content of volatile oils.

Blood oranges are typically harvested in late winter to early spring and are prized for both their culinary and ornamental value.
