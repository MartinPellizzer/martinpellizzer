Cassia hintonii

Cassia hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Cassia hintonii is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
