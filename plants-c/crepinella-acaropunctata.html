Crepinella acaropunctata

Crepinella acaropunctata, commonly known as the spiny sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in small, spiny structures. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
