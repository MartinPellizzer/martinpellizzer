Cassia isidorea

Cassia isidorea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments, and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia isidorea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
