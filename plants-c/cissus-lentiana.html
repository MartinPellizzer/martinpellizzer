Cissus lentiana

Cissus lentiana, commonly known as the climbing fig or heartleaf cissus, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are glossy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus lentiana is also valued for its ability to climb and its potential medicinal properties.
