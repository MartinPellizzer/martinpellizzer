Commiphora discolor

Commiphora discolor, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces a resin that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
