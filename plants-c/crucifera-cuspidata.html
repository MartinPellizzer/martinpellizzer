Crucifera cuspidata

Crucifera cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This species is native to temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
