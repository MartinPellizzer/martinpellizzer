Centaurea paniculata subsp. brunnescens

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

brunnescens, commonly known as the brown centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. This subspecies is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
