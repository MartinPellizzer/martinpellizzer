Chamaemelum microcephalum

Chamaemelum microcephalum, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has small, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
