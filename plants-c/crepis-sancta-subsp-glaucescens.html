Crepis sancta subsp. glaucescens

Crepis sancta subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in a whitish, powdery coating known as glaucous.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and the plant produces small, oblong achenes as its fruit.
