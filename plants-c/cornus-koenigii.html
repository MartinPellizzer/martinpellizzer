Cornus koenigii

Cornus koenigii, commonly known as Indian cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, reddish bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Cornus koenigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
