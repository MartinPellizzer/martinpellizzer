Cyperus planiculmis

Cyperus planiculmis, commonly known as the flat-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Cyperus planiculmis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
