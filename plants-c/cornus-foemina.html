Cornus foemina

Cornus foemina, commonly known as white cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

Cornus foemina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
