Cedrela toona var. grandiflora

Cedrela toona var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant toona, is a tropical tree belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
