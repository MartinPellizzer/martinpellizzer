Cola monponensis

Cola monponensis, commonly known as the West African cola nut, is a species of the Sterculiaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, followed by capsules containing aromatic seeds.

The seeds, known as cola nuts, are rich in caffeine and have been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.
