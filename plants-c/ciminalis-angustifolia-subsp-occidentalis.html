Ciminalis angustifolia subsp. occidentalis

Ciminalis angustifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, aromatic leaves with a distinctive narrow shape and a strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in complex, umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
