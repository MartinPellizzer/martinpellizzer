Crotalaria farcta

Crotalaria farcta, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Australia, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit and produces small, three-lobed leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.

Crotalaria farcta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
