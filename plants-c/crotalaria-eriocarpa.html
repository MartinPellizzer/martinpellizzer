Crotalaria eriocarpa

Crotalaria eriocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant is known for its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a velvety appearance. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds.

Crotalaria eriocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
