Calcitrapa maroccana

Calcitrapa maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan calcitrapa, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and succulent leaves that store water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
