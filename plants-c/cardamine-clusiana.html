Cardamine clusiana

Cardamine clusiana, commonly known as the clustered watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its basal leaves that are divided into several lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. It produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in a raceme.

The seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family.
