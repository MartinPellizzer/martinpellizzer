Carex bathiei

Carex bathiei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that can reach several feet in height.

Carex bathiei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
