Cyperus nieuwlandii

Cyperus nieuwlandii, commonly known as the Newland's flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in poorly drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grassy, with a smooth texture.

Cyperus nieuwlandii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
