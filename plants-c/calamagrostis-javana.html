Calamagrostis javana

Calamagrostis javana, commonly known as Java reed, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Calamagrostis javana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetlands.
