Coleus newtonii

Coleus newtonii, commonly known as the Newton's coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and purple. Its small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus newtonii is primarily grown as an ornamental plant for its striking leaves rather than its flowers.
