Carthamus tenuis subsp. gracillimus

Carthamus tenuis subsp.

gracillimus, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with ray florets.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
