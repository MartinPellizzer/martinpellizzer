Corypha thebaica

Corypha thebaica, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a species of palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by clustered fruits that contain a single seed. Corypha thebaica is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
