Cereus baxaniensis f. pellucidus

Cereus baxaniensis f. pellucidus, commonly known as the transparent cereus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in a thin, waxy cuticle, giving it a translucent appearance under certain lighting conditions. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its growth habit is typically solitary, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
