Cerastium armeniacum

Cerastium armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Cerastium armeniacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
