Chrysopsis villosa var. sessiliflora

Chrysopsis villosa var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its structure and appearance make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical identification.
