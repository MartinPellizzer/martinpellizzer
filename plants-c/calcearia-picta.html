Calcearia picta

Calcearia picta, commonly known as the painted daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces numerous small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a flat head. Calcearia picta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and unique foliage structure.
