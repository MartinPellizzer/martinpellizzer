Calandrinia calocephala

Calandrinia calocephala, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has hairy, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with toothed edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Calandrinia calocephala is also known for its ability to reseed itself readily, contributing to its widespread distribution.
