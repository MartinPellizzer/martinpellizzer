Cliffortia serpyllifolia var. chamissonis

Cliffortia serpyllifolia var. chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's penny-wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
