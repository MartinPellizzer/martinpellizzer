Croton craspedotrichus var. scaberrimus

Croton craspedotrichus var. scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.
