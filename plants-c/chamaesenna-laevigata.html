Chamaesenna laevigata

Chamaesenna laevigata, commonly known as the silky glabrous shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
