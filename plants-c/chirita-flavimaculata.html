Chirita flavimaculata

Chirita flavimaculata, commonly known as the yellow-spotted wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellow or orange spot near the base, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Chirita. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical name reflects its unique leaf markings and its classification within the Gesneriaceae family.
