Cyperus oryzetorum

Cyperus oryzetorum, commonly known as the rice flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, often growing in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Cyperus oryzetorum is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
