Conophytum circumpunctatum

Conophytum circumpunctatum, commonly known as the "spotted moonstone," is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
