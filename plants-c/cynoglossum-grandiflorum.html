Cynoglossum grandiflorum

Cynoglossum grandiflorum, commonly known as the great blue eye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cynoglossum grandiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
