Calamagrostis turkestanica

Calamagrostis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan reed, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, riverbanks, and lake shores. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis turkestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils.
