Carex macloviana var. subfusca

Carex macloviana var. subfusca, commonly known as the dusky sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges. Its leaves are usually narrow, dark green, and have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are typically wind-pollinated.
