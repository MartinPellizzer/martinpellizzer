Coffea × borelorum

Coffea × borelorum, commonly known as the Bornean coffee.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora, native to the island of Borneo. This plant is characterized by its evergreen shrub form and dark green, glossy leaves. The flowers are white and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

Its fruits, known as cherries, contain two seeds that are used to produce coffee beans, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial coffee production.
