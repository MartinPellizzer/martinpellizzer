Cissampelos pareira subvar. usambarensis

Cissampelos pareira subvar. usambarensis, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The subvariety is distinguished by its slightly different leaf and fruit morphology compared to the typical form of Cissampelos pareira.
