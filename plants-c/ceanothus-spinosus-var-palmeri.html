Ceanothus spinosus var. palmeri

Ceanothus spinosus var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its showy floral display.

This variety is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
