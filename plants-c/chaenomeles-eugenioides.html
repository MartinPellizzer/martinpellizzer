Chaenomeles eugenioides

Chaenomeles eugenioides, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically inedible but can be used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.
