Croton lobatus var. digitatus

Croton lobatus var. digitatus, commonly known as the fingered croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically lobed, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its bold and eye-catching foliage.
