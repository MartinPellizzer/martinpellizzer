Crassula sarcocaulis

Crassula sarcocaulis, commonly known as the rock rose or stone plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Crassula sarcocaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
