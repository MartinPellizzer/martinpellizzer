Chaenactis tenuifolia var. orcuttiana

Chaenactis tenuifolia var. orcuttiana, commonly known as the Orcutt's leafy paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
