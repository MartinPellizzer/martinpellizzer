Chrysanthemum brevilobum

Chrysanthemum brevilobum, commonly known as the slenderleaf chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
