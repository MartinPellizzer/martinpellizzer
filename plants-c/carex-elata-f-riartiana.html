Carex elata f. riartiana

Carex elata f. riartiana, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a variant of the tall sedge species native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often dense and forms a clump, with cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.
