Campylocentrum lutheri

Campylocentrum lutheri, commonly known as the Luther's starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are simple and alternate. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential for cultivation in subtropical regions.
