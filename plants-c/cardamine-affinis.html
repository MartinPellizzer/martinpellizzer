Cardamine affinis

Cardamine affinis, commonly known as lesser watercress, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is widespread across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
