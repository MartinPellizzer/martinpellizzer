Caprifolium alpinum

Caprifolium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Caprifolium alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
