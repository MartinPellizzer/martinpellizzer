Curcuma elata

Curcuma elata, commonly known as the golden ginger or yellow turmeric, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which are rich in curcuminoids. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Curcuma elata is also used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.
