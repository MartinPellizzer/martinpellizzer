Crataegus × calycina

Crataegus × calycina, commonly known as the common hawthorn or midland hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacantha, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.
