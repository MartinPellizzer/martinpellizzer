Calamus meghalayensis

Calamus meghalayensis, commonly known as the Meghalaya calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the northeastern region of India, particularly found in the states of Meghalaya and Assam. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local communities.
