Campanula pulvinaris

Campanula pulvinaris, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula pulvinaris is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
