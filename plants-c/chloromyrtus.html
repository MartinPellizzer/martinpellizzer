Chloromyrtus

Chloromyrtus, commonly known as the honey myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, while the flowers are usually white or pink and clustered in dense inflorescences. Chloromyrtus species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and are also valued for their essential oils.

They thrive in well-drained soils and require full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
