Carpodinus schlechteri

Carpodinus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's carpetweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Overall, it is a low-growing plant with a compact habit, adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
