Cocos nucifera var. javanica

Cocos nucifera var. javanica, commonly known as the Javan coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces coconuts that are typically smaller and have a higher oil content compared to the common coconut. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of coconut products.
