Cajanus cajan var. flavus

Cajanus cajan var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used as a food source. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
