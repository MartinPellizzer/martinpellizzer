Cyperus capitatus

Cyperus capitatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. Cyperus capitatus typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
