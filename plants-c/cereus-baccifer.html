Cereus baccifer

Cereus baccifer, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, producing bright red, orange, or yellow flowers. Its leaves are modified into short, rounded stems, and it thrives in well-draining soil with indirect sunlight.

Cereus baccifer is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
