Clematis finetiana var. pedata

Clematis finetiana var. pedata, commonly known as the broadleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vigorous growth and floral display.
