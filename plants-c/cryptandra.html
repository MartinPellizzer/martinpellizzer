Cryptandra

Cryptandra, commonly known as the mountain devil, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their woody stems and often have a shrub-like growth habit. They typically produce small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Cryptandra species are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing various drought-resistant features.

The genus includes over 100 species, many of which are found in the southern parts of Australia.
