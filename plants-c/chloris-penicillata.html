Chloris penicillata

Chloris penicillata, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
