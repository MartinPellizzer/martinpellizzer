Crepis laciniata

Crepis laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
