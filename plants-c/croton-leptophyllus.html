Croton leptophyllus

Croton leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in tropical and subtropical regions.
