Cacalia maxima

Cacalia maxima, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, spiny leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy purple or pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Cacalia maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
