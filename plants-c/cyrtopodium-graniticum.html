Cyrtopodium graniticum

Cyrtopodium graniticum, commonly known as the granite orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained, rocky soils, often growing on cliffs or in crevices. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, and the plant is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Cyrtopodium graniticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
