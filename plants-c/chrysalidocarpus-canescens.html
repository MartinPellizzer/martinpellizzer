Chrysalidocarpus canescens

Chrysalidocarpus canescens, commonly known as the African palm, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
