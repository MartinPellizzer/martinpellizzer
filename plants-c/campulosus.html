Campulosus

Campulosus, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are typically berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Campulosus is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often growing in disturbed or rocky soils.
