Centaurea × maritima var. nasturtiifolia

Centaurea × maritima var. nasturtiifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Centaurea maritima and C. nasturtiifolia.

It is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean, adapted to salty and windy environments. The plant features striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in salads.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky coastal areas.
