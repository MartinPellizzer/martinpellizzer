Cynanchum abyssinicum

Cynanchum abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
