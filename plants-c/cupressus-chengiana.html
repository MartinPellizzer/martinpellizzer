Cupressus chengiana

Cupressus chengiana, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
