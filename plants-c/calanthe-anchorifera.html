Calanthe anchorifera

Calanthe anchorifera, commonly known as the anchor orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or clay soils.

Calanthe anchorifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in shady gardens for its elegant blooms.
