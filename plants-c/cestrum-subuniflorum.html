Cestrum subuniflorum

Cestrum subuniflorum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
