Crepis zacinthia

Crepis zacinthia, commonly known as the Zante thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces showy, purple or pink flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

Crepis zacinthia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
