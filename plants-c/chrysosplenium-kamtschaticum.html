Chrysosplenium kamtschaticum

Chrysosplenium kamtschaticum, commonly known as Kamtschatic mountain saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Chrysosplenium kamtschaticum is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
