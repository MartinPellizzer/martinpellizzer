Cirsium bichroophyllum

Cirsium bichroophyllum, commonly known as the two-colored thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant's stems are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves have a distinctive bluish-green color.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
