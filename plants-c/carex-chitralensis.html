Carex chitralensis

Carex chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the sedge family.

Carex chitralensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
