Curupira

Curupira, commonly known as the "guardian of the forest," is a mythical plant associated with the Amazon rainforest.

It is believed to have a human-like appearance, often depicted as a small, hairy figure with a red face. The plant is said to possess protective properties, warding off intruders and harmful forces. Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent.

While not a real plant, it holds deep cultural and spiritual significance among indigenous communities.
