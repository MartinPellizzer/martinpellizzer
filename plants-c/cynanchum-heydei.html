Cynanchum heydei

Cynanchum heydei, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial climbing plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are follicles that contain toxic seeds, which can be harmful if ingested.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
