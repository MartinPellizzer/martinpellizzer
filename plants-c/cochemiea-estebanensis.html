Cochemiea estebanensis

Cochemiea estebanensis, commonly known as the Esteban's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, which are often followed by small, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cochemiea estebanensis is a slow-growing plant that is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
