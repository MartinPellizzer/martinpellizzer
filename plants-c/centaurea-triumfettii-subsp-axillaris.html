Centaurea triumfettii subsp. axillaris

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and reduced leaflets along the stem. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
