Coronanthera pedunculosa

Coronanthera pedunculosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, which is why it is also called the Christmas bell. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
