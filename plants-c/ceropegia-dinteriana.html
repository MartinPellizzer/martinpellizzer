Ceropegia dinteriana

Ceropegia dinteriana, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that are highly specialized, attracting specific pollinators like certain moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
