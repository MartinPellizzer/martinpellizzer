Citrus medicolimonum

Citrus medicolimonum, commonly known as the desert lime or Christ's lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of southern Asia.

It produces small, round, green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe and acidic. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with succulent leaves and a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil. Its fruit contains high levels of vitamin C and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's resilience makes it an important species for reforestation and sustainable agriculture in dry climates.
