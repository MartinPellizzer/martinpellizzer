Crataegus talyschensis

Crataegus talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native environment.
