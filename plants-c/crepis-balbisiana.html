Crepis balbisiana

Crepis balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis balbisiana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
