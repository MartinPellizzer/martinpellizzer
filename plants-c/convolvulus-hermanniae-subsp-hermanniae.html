Convolvulus hermanniae subsp. hermanniae

Convolvulus hermanniae subsp.

hermanniae, commonly known as Hermann's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and grows along the ground or as a trailing vine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
