Calamintha clinopodium var. urticifolia

Calamintha clinopodium var. urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which can cause a stinging sensation upon contact. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

This variety is often grown as a ornamental and medicinal plant, valued for its fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.
