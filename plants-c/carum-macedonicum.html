Carum macedonicum

Carum macedonicum, commonly known as Macedonian caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Carum macedonicum is valued for its distinctive scent and potential medicinal properties.
