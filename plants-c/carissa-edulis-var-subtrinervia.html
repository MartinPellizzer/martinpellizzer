Carissa edulis var. subtrinervia

Carissa edulis var. subtrinervia, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with a distinct three-veined structure, which is characteristic of its subspecies. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits resembling plums. The fruit is edible, with a tart, juicy flesh that is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
