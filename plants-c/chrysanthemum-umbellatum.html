Chrysanthemum umbellatum

Chrysanthemum umbellatum, commonly known as the clustered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in umbellate clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
