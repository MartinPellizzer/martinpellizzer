Capparis maroniensis

Capparis maroniensis, commonly known as the Maroni prickly pear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and fleshy, edible fruits that are often consumed locally. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Capparis maroniensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
