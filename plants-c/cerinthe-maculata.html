Cerinthe maculata

Cerinthe maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive yellow or white spot on the lip. The plant grows from a tuberous root system and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Cerinthe maculata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
