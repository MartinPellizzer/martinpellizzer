Combretum bracteatum

Combretum bracteatum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannahs and woodland areas.

The bark is rough and fissured, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
