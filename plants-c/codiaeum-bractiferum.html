Codiaeum bractiferum

Codiaeum bractiferum, commonly known as the flame tree or croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from red to orange and yellow, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative element in landscaping.
