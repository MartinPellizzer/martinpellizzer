Chamomilla macrotis

Chamomilla macrotis, commonly known as the large-flowered chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Chamomilla macrotis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
