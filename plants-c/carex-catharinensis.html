Carex catharinensis

Carex catharinensis, commonly known as the Catawba sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex catharinensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
