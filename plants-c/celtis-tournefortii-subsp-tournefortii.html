Celtis tournefortii subsp. tournefortii

Celtis tournefortii subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
