Cocos

Cocos, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a unique fruit called the coconut, which contains both a liquid endosperm and a hard, fibrous husk. The coconut palm is valued for its versatile uses, including food, fiber, and oil production.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is a member of the genus Cocos.
