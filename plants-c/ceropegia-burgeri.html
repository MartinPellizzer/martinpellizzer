Ceropegia burgeri

Ceropegia burgeri, commonly known as the dwarf chain plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, trailing stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is popular as a houseplant and is relatively easy to care for.
