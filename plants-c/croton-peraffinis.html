Croton peraffinis

Croton peraffinis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
