Clusia criuva subsp. cambessedesii

Clusia criuva subsp.

cambessedesii, commonly known as the golden glory bush, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it has a reputation for being relatively drought-tolerant.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
