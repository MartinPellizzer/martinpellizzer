Cypripedium × claudii

Cypripedium × claudii, commonly known as the Claudii lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flower with a deep pink or purple coloration and a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Cypripedium × claudii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
