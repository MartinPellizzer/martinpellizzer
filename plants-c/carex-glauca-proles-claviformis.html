Carex glauca proles claviformis

Carex glauca proles claviformis, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
