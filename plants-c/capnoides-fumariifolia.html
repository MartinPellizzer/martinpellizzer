Capnoides fumariifolia

Capnoides fumariifolia, commonly known as the flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support as it grows vigorously.

Capnoides fumariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
