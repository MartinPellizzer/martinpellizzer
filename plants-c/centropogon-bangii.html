Centropogon bangii

Centropogon bangii, commonly known as the Bangi grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white florets. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or near water bodies.

Centropogon bangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
