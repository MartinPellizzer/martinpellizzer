Coreopsis leavenworthii var. lewtonii

Coreopsis leavenworthii var. lewtonii, commonly known as the Lewton's tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
