Cherleria laricifolia

Cherleria laricifolia, commonly known as the larchleaf cherleria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which resembles that of a larch tree, with needle-like leaves arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
