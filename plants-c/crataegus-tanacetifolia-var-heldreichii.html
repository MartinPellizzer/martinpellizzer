Crataegus tanacetifolia var. heldreichii

Crataegus tanacetifolia var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lanceolate leaves that resemble those of yarrow, hence its common name. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
