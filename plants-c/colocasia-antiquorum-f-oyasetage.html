Colocasia antiquorum f. oyasetage

Colocasia antiquorum f. oyasetage, commonly known as the giant taro or giant elephant ear, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green to purple-tinged coloration. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for tropical and subtropical gardens. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, though it produces small, pale yellow flowers in the summer.

This cultivar is valued for its dramatic visual impact and is often used in water gardens or as a focal point in shaded landscapes.
