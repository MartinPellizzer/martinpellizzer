Croton virbalae

Croton virbalae, commonly known as the Virbalas croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton virbalae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant foliage.
