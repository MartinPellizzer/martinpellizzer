Crucifera fontana

Crucifera fontana, commonly known as the watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately divided leaves and small, white or yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its seeds are small and contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive peppery flavor.

Crucifera fontana is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.
