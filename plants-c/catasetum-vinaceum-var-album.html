Catasetum vinaceum var. album

Catasetum vinaceum var. album, commonly known as the white orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flower spikes that bear showy, white blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and fragrant scent, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
