Calophaca soongorica

Calophaca soongorica, commonly known as the Soongorica dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disk florets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Calophaca soongorica is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
