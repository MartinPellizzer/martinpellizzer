Capsicum milleri

Capsicum milleri, commonly known as the desert chili or Miller's chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically used in local cuisines. It has thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments.

Capsicum milleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh conditions.
