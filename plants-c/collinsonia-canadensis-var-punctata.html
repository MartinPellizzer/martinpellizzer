Collinsonia canadensis var. punctata

Collinsonia canadensis var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or pale green pattern, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
