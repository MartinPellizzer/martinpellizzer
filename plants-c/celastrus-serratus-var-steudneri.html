Celastrus serratus var. steudneri

Celastrus serratus var. steudneri, commonly known as the Steudner's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
