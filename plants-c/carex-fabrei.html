Carex fabrei

Carex fabrei, commonly known as the Fabre's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Carex fabrei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
