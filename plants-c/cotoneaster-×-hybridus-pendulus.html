Cotoneaster × hybridus-pendulus

Cotoneaster × hybridus-pendulus, commonly known as the weeping cotoneaster, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from *Cotoneaster hybridus* and *Cotoneaster pendulus*.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping branches that bear small, reddish-orange flowers in the spring. The plant produces berries that range in color from red to black, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is often used in garden settings for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
