Clematis nutans var. pseudoconnata

Clematis nutans var. pseudoconnata, commonly known as the nodding clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive nodding habit. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
