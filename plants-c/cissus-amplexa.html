Cissus amplexa

Cissus amplexa, commonly known as the climbing fig or heartleaf cissus, is a trailing, woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
