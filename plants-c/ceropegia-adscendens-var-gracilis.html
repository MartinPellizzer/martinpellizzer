Ceropegia adscendens var. gracilis

Ceropegia adscendens var. gracilis, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often trailing or climbing over supports. The leaves are small, oval, and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species is well-suited for hanging baskets or trained on trellises, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
