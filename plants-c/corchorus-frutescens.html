Corchorus frutescens

Corchorus frutescens, commonly known as jute mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
