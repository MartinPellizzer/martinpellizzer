Calamagrostis neesii

Calamagrostis neesii, commonly known as the Nees' reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually golden or brown in hue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence on the underside.

Calamagrostis neesii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
