Choisya arizonica var. amplophylla

Choisya arizonica var. amplophylla, commonly known as Arizona holly.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant features elliptical leaves with waxy surfaces and produces blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
