Capparis sabiifolia

Capparis sabiifolia, commonly known as the African crossberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to survive in challenging environmental conditions.
