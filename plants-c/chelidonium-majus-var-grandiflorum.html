Chelidonium majus var. grandiflorum

Chelidonium majus var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow, saucer-shaped flowers and broad, lobed leaves. The plant contains alkaloids, which make it toxic and potentially harmful if ingested. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features include a hairy stem and a basal rosette of leaves.
