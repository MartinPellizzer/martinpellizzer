Chrysophyllum sanguinolentum subsp. sanguinolentum

Chrysophyllum sanguinolentum subsp.

sanguinolentum, commonly known as the crimson starfruit, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant red, star-shaped fruits that resemble small, glowing lanterns.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are small and fragrant, often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
