Carissa lanceolata

Carissa lanceolata, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow star.

It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular garden plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
