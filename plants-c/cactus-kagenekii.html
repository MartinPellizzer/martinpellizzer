Cactus kagenekii

Cactus kagenekii, commonly known as the Japanese cactus or the dragon's head cactus, is a unique species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant features a distinctive crown of spines and produces small, white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and enhancing its drought tolerance.

Cactus kagenekii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
