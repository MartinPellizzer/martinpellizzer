Curcuma kayahensis

Curcuma kayahensis, commonly known as Kayah turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, orange-yellow rhizomes. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are large, lanceolate, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, similar to other curcuma species.
