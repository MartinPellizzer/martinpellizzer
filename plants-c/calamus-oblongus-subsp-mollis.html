Calamus oblongus subsp. mollis

Calamus oblongus subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales. It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that are often covered with a soft, smooth texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically near the base of the stems. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
