Cytisus orientalis

Cytisus orientalis, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
