Commelina congestipantha

Commelina congestipantha, commonly known as the clustered commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae, which includes a variety of grass-like and flowering plants. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically blue or violet in color, with three petals and three sepals.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
