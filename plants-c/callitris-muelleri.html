Callitris muelleri

Callitris muelleri, commonly known as the mountain cypress, is a species of conifer native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, round cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This hardy species is well adapted to withstand drought and is commonly used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
