Cyperus malaccensis subsp. malaccensis

Cyperus malaccensis subsp.

malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family.

The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
