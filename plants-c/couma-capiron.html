Couma capiron

Couma capiron, commonly known as capiron or capiron tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemose inflorescences. The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
