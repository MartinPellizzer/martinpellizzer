Corysanthes maculata

Corysanthes maculata, commonly known as the spotted flame tree, is a tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers, which are clustered in panicles and bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Corysanthes maculata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
