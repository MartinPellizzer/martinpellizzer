Coleus berkeleyi

Coleus berkeleyi, commonly known as the Berkeley coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Coleus berkeleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
