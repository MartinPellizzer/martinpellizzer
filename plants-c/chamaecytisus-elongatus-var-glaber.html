Chamaecytisus elongatus var. glaber

Chamaecytisus elongatus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in Mediterranean climates.
