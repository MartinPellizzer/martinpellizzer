Cleome spinosa var. subinermis

Cleome spinosa var. subinermis, commonly known as the woolly cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is typically found in moist to wet soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
