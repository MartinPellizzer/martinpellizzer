Coccoloba ovata var. minor

Coccoloba ovata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf coral tree, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
