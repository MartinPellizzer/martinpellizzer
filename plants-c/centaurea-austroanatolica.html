Centaurea austroanatolica

Centaurea austroanatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive blue to purple petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its spiny leaves and showy inflorescences. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

Centaurea austroanatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
