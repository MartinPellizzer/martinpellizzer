Cota tinctoria subsp. parnassica

Cota tinctoria subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian cow-wheat, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple to pink flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
