Cinnamomum glanduliferum

Cinnamomum glanduliferum, commonly known as cinnamon bark tree or Chinese cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is rich in essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy scent.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
