Calophyllum cumingii

Calophyllum cumingii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright yellow, edible fruits. Its fruits are notable for their high vitamin C content and are used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
