Carex aquatilis f. cuspidata

Carex aquatilis f. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog sedge, is a variant of the broad-leaved bog sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pointed at the tips. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland habitats.
