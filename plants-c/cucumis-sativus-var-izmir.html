Cucumis sativus var. izmir

Cucumis sativus var. izmir, commonly known as the Izmir cucumber, is a cultivar of the cucumber plant native to the Aegean region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its elongated, smooth, and slightly ribbed fruit, which is typically harvested when immature for optimal flavor and texture. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a sprawling growth habit, requiring support for vertical growth in some conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This variety is prized for its crisp, refreshing taste and is widely used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
