Cerasus cerasoides var. rubea

Cerasus cerasoides var. rubea, commonly known as the red cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant features glossy, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. It produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
