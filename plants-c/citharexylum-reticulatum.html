Citharexylum reticulatum

Citharexylum reticulatum, commonly known as the honeycomb tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a rough, hairy texture. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
