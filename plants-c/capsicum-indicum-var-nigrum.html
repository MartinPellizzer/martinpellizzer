Capsicum indicum var. nigrum

Capsicum indicum var. nigrum, commonly known as black pepper.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to the Western Ghats of India. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark red to black berries when mature. The berries are harvested and dried to create the spice known as black pepper.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves.
