Cissus odontadenia

Cissus odontadenia, commonly known as the bone leaf or rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often lobed leaves that are typically green with a pale underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are yellow when mature. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is popular as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
