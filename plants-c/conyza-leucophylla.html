Conyza leucophylla

Conyza leucophylla, commonly known as whiteleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Conyza leucophylla is adapted to drought conditions, with a short growing season and rapid life cycle.
