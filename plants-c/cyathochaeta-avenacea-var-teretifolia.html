Cyathochaeta avenacea var. teretifolia

Cyathochaeta avenacea var. teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in dense, grass-like clusters with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which is characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.
