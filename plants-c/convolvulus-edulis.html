Convolvulus edulis

Convolvulus edulis, commonly known as the edible bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. Its roots are edible and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor, making it a traditional food source in some regions.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments, requiring careful management.
