Cynanchica aristata subsp. thessala

Cynanchica aristata subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
