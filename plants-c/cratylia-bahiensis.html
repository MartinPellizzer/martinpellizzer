Cratylia bahiensis

Cratylia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in reforestation projects.
