Coronilla montserratii

Coronilla montserratii, commonly known as the Catalan vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
