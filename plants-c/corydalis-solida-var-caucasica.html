Corydalis solida var. caucasica

Corydalis solida var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian Corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its early spring blooming habit and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
