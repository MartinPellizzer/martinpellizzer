Carex pseudochinensis

Carex pseudochinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
