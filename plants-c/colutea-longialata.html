Colutea longialata

Colutea longialata, commonly known as the longleaf cowpea, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
