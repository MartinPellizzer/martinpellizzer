Cymbidium formosanum

Cymbidium formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan orchid, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It is a perennial herb with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in various environmental conditions. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Cymbidium formosanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
