Cleome semitetrandra

Cleome semitetrandra, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant is typically herbaceous, growing as an annual or biennial, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a branched stem that produces numerous flowering shoots, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
