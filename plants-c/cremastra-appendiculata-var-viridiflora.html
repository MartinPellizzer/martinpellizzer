Cremastra appendiculata var. viridiflora

Cremastra appendiculata var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves and roots contain toxic compounds, making it a plant to handle with care.
