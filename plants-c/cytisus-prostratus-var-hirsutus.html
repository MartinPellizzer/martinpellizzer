Cytisus prostratus var. hirsutus

Cytisus prostratus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
