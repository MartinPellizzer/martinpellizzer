Centaurea paniculata subsp. hanryi

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

hanryi, commonly known as the Hanryi knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces dense, branched inflorescences of purple to pink flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
