Comparettia luerae

Comparettia luerae, commonly known as the Luera's slipper orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like floral structure that resembles a slipper, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich substrates for optimal growth.

Comparettia luerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
