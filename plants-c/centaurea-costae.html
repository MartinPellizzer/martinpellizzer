Centaurea costae

Centaurea costae, commonly known as the Costate Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
