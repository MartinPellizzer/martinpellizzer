Crataegus × intermixta

Crataegus × intermixta, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata, exhibiting a mix of characteristics from both parents. The plant typically has thorned stems, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red or orange berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
