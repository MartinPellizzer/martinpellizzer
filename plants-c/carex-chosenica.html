Carex chosenica

Carex chosenica, commonly known as the Chosen marsh sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America, typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a narrow, strap-like shape. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex chosenica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
