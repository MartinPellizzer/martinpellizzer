Carex vesicaria f. fluitans

Carex vesicaria f. fluitans, commonly known as the floating sedge, is a variant of the common sedge that exhibits unique aquatic adaptations.

This plant is characterized by its floating leaves, which are often held above the water surface, allowing for efficient photosynthesis. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, stable aquatic ecosystems.
