Chaetocalyx tomentosus

Chaetocalyx tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grey foliage and showy, clustered orange-red flowers that bloom in the dry season. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are often fragrant. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in full sun with well-drained soil.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.
