Caesalpinia glabra

Caesalpinia glabra, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Caesalpinia glabra is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
