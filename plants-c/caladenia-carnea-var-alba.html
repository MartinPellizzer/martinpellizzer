Caladenia carnea var. alba

Caladenia carnea var. alba, commonly known as the White Spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, white flowers. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, producing small, spider-like flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
