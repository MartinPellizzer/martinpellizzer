Campanula lusitanica f. verruculosa

Campanula lusitanica f. verruculosa, commonly known as the wrinkled bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright stems with ovate, hairy leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage and charming flowers make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.
