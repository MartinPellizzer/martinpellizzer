Callicarpa brevistyla

Callicarpa brevistyla, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking violet to purple berries that ripen in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and a glossy texture.

Callicarpa brevistyla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
