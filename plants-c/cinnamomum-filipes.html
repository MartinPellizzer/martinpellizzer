Cinnamomum filipes

Cinnamomum filipes, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, oily texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that contain aromatic seeds.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
