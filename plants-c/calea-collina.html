Calea collina

Calea collina, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that open at night and close in the morning, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are velvety and often have a distinctive pattern of dark veins.

Calea collina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
