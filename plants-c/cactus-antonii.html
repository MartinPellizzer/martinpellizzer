Cactus antonii

Cactus antonii, commonly known as the red-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a cylindrical shape with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Cactus antonii is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
