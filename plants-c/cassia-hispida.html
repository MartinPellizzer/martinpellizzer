Cassia hispida

Cassia hispida, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine. Cassia hispida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.
