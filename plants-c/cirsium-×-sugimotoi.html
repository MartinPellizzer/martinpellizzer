Cirsium × sugimotoi

Cirsium × sugimotoi, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
