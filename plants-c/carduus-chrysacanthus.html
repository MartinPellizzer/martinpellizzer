Carduus chrysacanthus

Carduus chrysacanthus, commonly known as the golden cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
