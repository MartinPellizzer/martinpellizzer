Clusia major

Clusia major, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia major is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
