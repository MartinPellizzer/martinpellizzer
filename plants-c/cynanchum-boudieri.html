Cynanchum boudieri

Cynanchum boudieri, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
