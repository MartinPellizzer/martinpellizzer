Cirsium benacense

Cirsium benacense, commonly known as the Benaco thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Cirsium benacense is also considered a potential invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
