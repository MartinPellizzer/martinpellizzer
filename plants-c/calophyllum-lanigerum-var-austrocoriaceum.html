Calophyllum lanigerum var. austrocoriaceum

Calophyllum lanigerum var. austrocoriaceum, commonly known as the Australian white ironwood, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale to dark grey bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional crafts and construction.
