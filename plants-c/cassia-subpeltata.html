Cassia subpeltata

Cassia subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cassia subpeltata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
