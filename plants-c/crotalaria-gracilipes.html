Crotalaria gracilipes

Crotalaria gracilipes, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
