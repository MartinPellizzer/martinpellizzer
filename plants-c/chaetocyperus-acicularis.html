Chaetocyperus acicularis

Chaetocyperus acicularis, commonly known as the needlegrass sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showing adaptability to waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
