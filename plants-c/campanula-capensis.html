Campanula capensis

Campanula capensis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
