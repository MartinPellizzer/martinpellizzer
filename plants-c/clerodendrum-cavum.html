Clerodendrum cavum

Clerodendrum cavum, commonly known as the hollow clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by distinctive, inflated, greenish-brown seed pods. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Clerodendrum cavum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
