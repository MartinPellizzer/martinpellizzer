Campomanesia reitziana

Campomanesia reitziana, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple or carambola, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that resemble a star when sliced. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces white flowers with a strong, aromatic scent. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
