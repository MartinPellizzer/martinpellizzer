Castanopsis tcheponensis

Castanopsis tcheponensis, commonly known as the Tchepon chestnut, is a species of evergreen tree native to western Africa, particularly found in the humid rainforests of Cameroon and Gabon.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Castanopsis tcheponensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in local crafts and construction.
