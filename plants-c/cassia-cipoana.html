Cassia cipoana

Cassia cipoana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia cipoana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
