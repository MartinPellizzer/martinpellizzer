Carex × subviridula

Carex × subviridula, commonly known as the green sedges, is a hybrid plant within the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clumping growth habit.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green, with the inflorescence consisting of spikelets that are usually pale green to brown. This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Carex × subviridula is generally low-maintenance, requiring minimal pruning and fertilization.
