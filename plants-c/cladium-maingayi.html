Cladium maingayi

Cladium maingayi, commonly known as the mangrove sedges, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is found in coastal areas, particularly in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Cladium maingayi plays a vital role in the ecological balance of tidal wetlands.
