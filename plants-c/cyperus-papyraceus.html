Cyperus papyraceus

Cyperus papyraceus, commonly known as paper rush, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cyperus papyraceus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ability to thrive in wet or moist conditions.
