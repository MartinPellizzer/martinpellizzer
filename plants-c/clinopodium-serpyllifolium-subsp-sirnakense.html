Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp. sirnakense

Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp.

sirnakense, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and have a distinctive purple or pink color.

This subspecies is valued for its strong, pungent aroma and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
