Cremneria pruinosa

Cremneria pruinosa, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost waxy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white or pale pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Cremneria pruinosa is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
