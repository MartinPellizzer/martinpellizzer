Crataegus oxyacantha var. laciniata

Crataegus oxyacantha var. laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lanceolate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
