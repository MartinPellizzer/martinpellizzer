Centaurea × casureperta

Centaurea × casureperta, commonly known as the hybrid garden centaury, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to Europe, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
