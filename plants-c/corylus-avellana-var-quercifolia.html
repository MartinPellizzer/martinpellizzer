Corylus avellana var. quercifolia

Corylus avellana var. quercifolia, commonly known as the Turkeynut hickory, is a variant of the hazelnut tree characterized by its distinctive, oak-like leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in temperate climates. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a unique appearance compared to other hazelnut species.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
