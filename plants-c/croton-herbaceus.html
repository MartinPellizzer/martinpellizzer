Croton herbaceus

Croton herbaceus, commonly known as the creeping croton, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a ground cover in shaded garden areas.
