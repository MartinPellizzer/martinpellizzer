Chaerophyllum taiwanianum

Chaerophyllum taiwanianum, commonly known as the Taiwan cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is native to Taiwan and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its small white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
