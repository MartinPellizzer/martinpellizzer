Cardamine pratensis var. occidentalis

Cardamine pratensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as western watercress, is a flowering plant native to moist, calcareous habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in stream banks, wet meadows, and other waterlogged areas.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
