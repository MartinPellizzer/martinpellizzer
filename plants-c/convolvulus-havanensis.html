Convolvulus havanensis

Convolvulus havanensis, commonly known as the Havana morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens, where it can spread aggressively.
