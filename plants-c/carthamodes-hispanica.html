Carthamodes hispanica

Carthamodes hispanica, commonly known as Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
