Chucoa

Chucoa, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with thick, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chucoa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
