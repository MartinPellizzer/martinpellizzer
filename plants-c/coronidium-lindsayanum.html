Coronidium lindsayanum

Coronidium lindsayanum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces distinctive bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native environment.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and vibrant seasonal display.
