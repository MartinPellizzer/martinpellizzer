Comparettia mirthae

Comparettia mirthae, commonly known as the Mirtha's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescence and its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Comparettia mirthae is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
