Cardamine cordata proles calbucana

Cardamine cordata proles calbucana, commonly known as the Calbucan watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
