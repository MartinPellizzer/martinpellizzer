Carex × muelleriana

Carex × muelleriana, commonly known as the mueller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex × muelleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
