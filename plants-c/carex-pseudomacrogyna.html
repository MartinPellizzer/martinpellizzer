Carex pseudomacrogyna

Carex pseudomacrogyna, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color, and it plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Carex pseudomacrogyna is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
