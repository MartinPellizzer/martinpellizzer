Cereus spachianus

Cereus spachianus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique symbiotic relationship with a specific species of fungus, which helps it absorb nutrients from the soil. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

It thrives in full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
