Cinnamomum tenuifolium

Cinnamomum tenuifolium, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Japanese camphor laurel, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and twigs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The tree's ability to produce camphor has made it historically significant in traditional medicine and industry.
