Centaurea finitima

Centaurea finitima, commonly known as the Iberian centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as steppes and scrublands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive blue or purple central disc. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
