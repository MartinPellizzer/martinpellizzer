Cenchrus elymoides

Cenchrus elymoides, commonly known as sand couchgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly drought-tolerant.

Due to its resilience, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but also plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
