Clusia cartilaginea

Clusia cartilaginea, commonly known as the rubber plant or swamp hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

Clusia cartilaginea is also noted for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it a common species in swampy or boggy habitats.
