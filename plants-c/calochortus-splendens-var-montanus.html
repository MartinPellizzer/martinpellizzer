Calochortus splendens var. montanus

Calochortus splendens var. montanus, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the genus Calochortus, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mariposa lilies. This particular variety is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
