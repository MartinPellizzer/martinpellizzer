Chrysopsis villosa

Chrysopsis villosa, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit, making it well adapted to arid conditions.

Chrysopsis villosa is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
