Campanumoea pilosula

Campanumoea pilosula, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
