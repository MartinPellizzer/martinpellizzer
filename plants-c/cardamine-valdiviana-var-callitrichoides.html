Cardamine valdiviana var. callitrichoides

Cardamine valdiviana var. callitrichoides, commonly known as the Valdivian water cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. Native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or stream-side habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and leaves that are usually alternate and pinnately divided.
