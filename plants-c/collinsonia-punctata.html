Collinsonia punctata

Collinsonia punctata, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, pinkish-purple flowers with distinctive black spots, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along its square, branched stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Collinsonia punctata is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
