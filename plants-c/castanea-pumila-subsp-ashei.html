Castanea pumila subsp. ashei

Castanea pumila subsp.

ashei, commonly known as the Allegheny chinkapin, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the chestnut family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and spiny, cup-like fruits that contain one or two edible nuts. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
