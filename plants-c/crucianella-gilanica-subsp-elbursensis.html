Crucianella gilanica subsp. elbursensis

Crucianella gilanica subsp.

elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburs stonecress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Elburs Mountains in Iran. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
