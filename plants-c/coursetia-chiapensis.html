Coursetia chiapensis

Coursetia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan coursetia, is a shrub native to the Mexican states of Chiapas and Oaxaca.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants, and it produces small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical forest environments and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
