Cestrum mutisii

Cestrum mutisii, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum mutisii is also known for its mild toxicity, with some parts of the plant containing toxic alkaloids.
