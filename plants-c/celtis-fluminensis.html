Celtis fluminensis

Celtis fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hackberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
