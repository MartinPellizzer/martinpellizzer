Crinum brasilense

Crinum brasilense, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Crinum brasilense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its strong fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
