Chrysopsis pumila

Chrysopsis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf goldenweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact stature and yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, daisy-like blooms appear in the summer months, adding a subtle yet vibrant touch to its habitat.

Chrysopsis pumila is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.
