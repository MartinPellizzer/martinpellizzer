Commelina ciliata

Commelina ciliata, commonly known as the spurred morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, blue or violet flowers with a distinctive spur at the base. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Commelina ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
