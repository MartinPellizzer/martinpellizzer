Cynanchum macrophyllum

Cynanchum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
