Campanula simplex var. dasycarpa

Campanula simplex var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the Woolly Bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
