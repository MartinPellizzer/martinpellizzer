Cyanotis lanata var. schweinfurthii

Cyanotis lanata var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's botanical features include a bulbous base, strap-like leaves, and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.
