Cyperus umbellatus var. laxatus

Cyperus umbellatus var. laxatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along water edges or in shallow water. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
