Cepa amboinensis

Cepa amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina onion, is a bulbous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy bulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The leaves and bulbs are edible, though they are not as commonly consumed as other onion varieties.
