Crepis senecioides

Crepis senecioides, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces numerous small achenes, which are dispersed by the wind.

It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
