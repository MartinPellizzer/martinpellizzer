Crocus adami

Crocus adami, commonly known as the Adam's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive purple or brown stripe. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, allowing it to bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
