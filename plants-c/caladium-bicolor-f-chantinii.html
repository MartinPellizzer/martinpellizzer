Caladium bicolor f. chantinii

Caladium bicolor f. chantinii, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" caladium, is a cultivar of the arrowhead plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a wide range of colors, including shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This cultivar is often used in container gardening and as a decorative ground cover in shaded areas.
