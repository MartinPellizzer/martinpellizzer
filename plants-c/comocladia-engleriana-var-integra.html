Comocladia engleriana var. integra

Comocladia engleriana var. integra, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.
