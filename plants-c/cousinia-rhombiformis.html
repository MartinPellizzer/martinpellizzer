Cousinia rhombiformis

Cousinia rhombiformis, commonly known as the rhomboid cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense cylindrical heads, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Cousinia rhombiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
