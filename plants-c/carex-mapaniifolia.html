Carex mapaniifolia

Carex mapaniifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
