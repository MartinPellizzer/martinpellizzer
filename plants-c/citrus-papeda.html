Citrus papeda

Citrus papeda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is typically harvested when it turns orange, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
