Callicarpa japonica var. luxurians

Callicarpa japonica var. luxurians, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, aromatic bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental berries and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
