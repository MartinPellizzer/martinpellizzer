Chrysanthemum winteri

Chrysanthemum winteri, commonly known as the alpine chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and prefers well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that aid in its propagation.

Chrysanthemum winteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
