Calliandra rubescens

Calliandra rubescens, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their vibrant color and long-lasting display.

Additionally, the plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
