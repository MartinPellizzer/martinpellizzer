Calamagrostis sylvatica var. colorata

Calamagrostis sylvatica var. colorata, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pinkish inflorescence, which contrasts with its silver-green foliage. This plant typically grows in wet or boggy habitats, such as marshes and heathlands, and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in moist environments.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, where it adds visual interest with its unique coloration.
