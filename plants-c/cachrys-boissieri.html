Cachrys boissieri

Cachrys boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cachrys boissieri is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
