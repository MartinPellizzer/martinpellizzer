Cinnamomum salicifolium

Cinnamomum salicifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Sichuan cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its species name *salicifolium*. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and red berries, which are used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are valued for their aromatic compounds, including cinnamaldehyde.

This species is often cultivated for its essential oils and has cultural significance in Chinese herbal practices.
