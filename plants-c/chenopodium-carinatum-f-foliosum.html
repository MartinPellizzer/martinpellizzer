Chenopodium carinatum f. foliosum

Chenopodium carinatum f. foliosum, commonly known as the curlyleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, and often lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are typically succulent and can be used in culinary applications, though it is more commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental species.
