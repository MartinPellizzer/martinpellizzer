Chamaecrista fasciculata var. robusta

Chamaecrista fasciculata var. robusta, commonly known as partridge pea, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by both indigenous peoples and livestock.
