Carex remotiformis

Carex remotiformis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, linear, and often have a distinct midrib, while its flower heads are small and clustered.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
