Corispermum marschallii var. angustifolium

Corispermum marschallii var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on erect, branched inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
