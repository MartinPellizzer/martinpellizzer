Campanula lactiflora var. olympica

Campanula lactiflora var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it adapts to cool, moist conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
