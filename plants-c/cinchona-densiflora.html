Cinchona densiflora

Cinchona densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark red to black berries, which contain the seeds. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other cinchona species known for their medicinal properties.

Cinchona densiflora is valued for its historical role in the production of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.
