Cardiandra alternifolia var. oppositifolia

Cardiandra alternifolia var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves, which are usually bright green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink, during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
