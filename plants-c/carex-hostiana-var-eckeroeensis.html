Carex hostiana var. eckeroeensis

Carex hostiana var. eckeroeensis, commonly known as the Eckeroe sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
