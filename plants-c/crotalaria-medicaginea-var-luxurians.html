Crotalaria medicaginea var. luxurians

Crotalaria medicaginea var. luxurians, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a perennial legume native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments, posing challenges in agricultural settings.
