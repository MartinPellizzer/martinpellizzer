Ceratostylis lancipetala

Ceratostylis lancipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a spike of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in soil, while its flowers are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
