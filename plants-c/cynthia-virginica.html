Cynthia virginica

Cynthia virginica, commonly known as the Virginia waterleaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, typically arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The Virginia waterleaf is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
