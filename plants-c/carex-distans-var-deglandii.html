Carex distans var. deglandii

Carex distans var. deglandii, commonly known as the Degland's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
