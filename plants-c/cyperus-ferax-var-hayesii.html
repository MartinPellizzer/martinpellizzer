Cyperus ferax var. hayesii

Cyperus ferax var. hayesii, commonly known as the Hayes' flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with small, clustered flower spikes.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
