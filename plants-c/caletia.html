Caletia

Caletia, commonly known as the bluebell of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a bulbous underground stem for storage of nutrients. Caletia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
