Calceolaria ascendens subsp. ascendens

Calceolaria ascendens subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as the ascending slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats, and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
