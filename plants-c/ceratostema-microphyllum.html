Ceratostema microphyllum

Ceratostema microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved horned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant features distinctive horn-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
