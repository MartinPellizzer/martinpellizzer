Chalepophyllum pungens

Chalepophyllum pungens, commonly known as the spiny false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in gardens and is more commonly found in natural habitats.
