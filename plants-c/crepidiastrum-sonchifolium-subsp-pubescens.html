Crepidiastrum sonchifolium subsp. pubescens

Crepidiastrum sonchifolium subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent leaf sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves that give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
