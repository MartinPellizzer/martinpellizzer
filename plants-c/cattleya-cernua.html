Cattleya cernua

Cattleya cernua, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant produces a single spike with multiple flowers, each having three petals and three sepals, with the lateral petals often curving downward. Cattleya cernua thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
