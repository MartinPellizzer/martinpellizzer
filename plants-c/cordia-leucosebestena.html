Cordia leucosebestena

Cordia leucosebestena, commonly known as the white-flowered cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by yellowish-orange fruits that contain three to four seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
