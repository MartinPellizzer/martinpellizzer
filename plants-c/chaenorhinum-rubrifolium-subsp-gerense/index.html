Chaenorhinum Rubrifolium Subsp. Gerense

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp. gerense is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is known for its vibrant red foliage and delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful conservation efforts. Its unique appearance makes it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
