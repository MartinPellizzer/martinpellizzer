Chamaecrista polymorpha

Chamaecrista polymorpha, commonly known as the cowpea or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It also has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
