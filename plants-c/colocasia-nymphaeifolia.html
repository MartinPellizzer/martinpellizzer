Colocasia nymphaeifolia

Colocasia nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink, with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Colocasia nymphaeifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and aquatic landscapes.
