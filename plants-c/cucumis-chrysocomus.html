Cucumis chrysocomus

Cucumis chrysocomus, commonly known as the golden cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it thrives in warm, dry climates.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
