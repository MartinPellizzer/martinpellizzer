Cassia gonoclada

Cassia gonoclada, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are yellow, clustered in racemes. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, seed-filled segments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cassia gonoclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a landscape plant.
