Caltha palustris var. integerrima

Caltha palustris var. integerrima, commonly known as the yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with wet conditions.
