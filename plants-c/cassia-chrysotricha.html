Cassia chrysotricha

Cassia chrysotricha, commonly known as golden cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in panicles and attract various pollinators. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine. Cassia chrysotricha thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
