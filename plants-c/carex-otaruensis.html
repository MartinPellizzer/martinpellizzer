Carex otaruensis

Carex otaruensis, commonly known as the Otaru sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
