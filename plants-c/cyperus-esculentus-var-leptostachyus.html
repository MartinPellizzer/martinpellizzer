Cyperus esculentus var. leptostachyus

Cyperus esculentus var. leptostachyus, commonly known as water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers. The plant has a bulbous, edible tuber that is harvested for its crunchy, nutty flavor. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown.

It is valued both for its culinary use and its ability to stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
