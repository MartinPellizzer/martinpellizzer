Cyanthillium cinereum var. linifolium

Cyanthillium cinereum var. linifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
