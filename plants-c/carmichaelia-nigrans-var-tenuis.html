Carmichaelia nigrans var. tenuis

Carmichaelia nigrans var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Carmichaelia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and may have a slight pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.
