Capsella scoliocaspica

Capsella scoliocaspica, commonly known as the narrowleaf weed or narrowleaf shepherd's purse.

It is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and Asia. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Brassicaceae family.

The plant's botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
