Clematis villosa var. teuszii

Clematis villosa var. teuszii, commonly known as the Teuszii Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and the plant has a soft, hairy texture on its stems and leaves.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
