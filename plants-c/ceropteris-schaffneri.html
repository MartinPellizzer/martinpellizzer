Ceropteris schaffneri

Ceropteris schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's water fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically green and have a finely divided structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are relatively short and often grow close to the soil surface.

Ceropteris schaffneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
