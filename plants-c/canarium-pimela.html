Canarium pimela

Canarium pimela, commonly known as the Pimela candlenut, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree's resin has also been utilized in various applications.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local fauna in its native environment.
