Casearia sericea

Casearia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, spherical berries that are edible.

Casearia sericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
