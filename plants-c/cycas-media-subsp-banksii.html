Cycas media subsp. banksii

Cycas media subsp.

banksii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cycad, is a species native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is a slow-growing, long-lived plant that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
