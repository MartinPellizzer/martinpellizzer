Coleostachys spicata

Coleostachys spicata, commonly known as the spike-leaved cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Coleostachys spicata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and resilience.
