Cissus hypoleuca

Cissus hypoleuca, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by pale yellow berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus hypoleuca is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
