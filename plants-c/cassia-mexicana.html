Cassia mexicana

Cassia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican senna, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem with a rough texture.

Cassia mexicana is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
