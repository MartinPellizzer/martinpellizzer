Cardamine caledonica var. brevistyla

Cardamine caledonica var. brevistyla, commonly known as the Scottish watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small white flowers are arranged in a raceme and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its culinary uses and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique habitat preferences.
