Codonopsis limprichtii var. pinifolia

Codonopsis limprichtii var. pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf codonopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble pine needles. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural and traditional uses.
