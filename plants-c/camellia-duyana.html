Camellia duyana

Camellia duyana, commonly known as the Duyana camellia, is a rare evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, subtropical climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

Camellia duyana is also valued for its potential in horticultural research due to its unique floral characteristics and limited distribution.
