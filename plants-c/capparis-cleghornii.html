Capparis cleghornii

Capparis cleghornii, commonly known as the cleghornii caper, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, greenish fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions with similar climates.

Capparis cleghornii is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand high temperatures.
