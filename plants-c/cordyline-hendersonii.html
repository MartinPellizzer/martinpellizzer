Cordyline hendersonii

Cordyline hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's cordyline, is a striking evergreen plant native to the island of Henderson in the Pitcairn Islands.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple. The plant is characterized by its rosette growth habit and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light conditions.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
