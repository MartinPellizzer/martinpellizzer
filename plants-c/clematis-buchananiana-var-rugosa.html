Clematis buchananiana var. rugosa

Clematis buchananiana var. rugosa, commonly known as the rugose clematis, is a climbing perennial shrub native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant has stout, woody stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, and its leaves are palmately compound with three to five leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
