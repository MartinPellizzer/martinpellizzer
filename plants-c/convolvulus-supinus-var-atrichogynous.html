Convolvulus supinus var. atrichogynous

Convolvulus supinus var. atrichogynous, commonly known as the hairyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

The species is known for its ability to climb using its twining stems, often found along walls or in rocky areas.
