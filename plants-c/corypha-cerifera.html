Corypha cerifera

Corypha cerifera, commonly known as the wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences that are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule containing a single seed.

Corypha cerifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in its native habitats.
