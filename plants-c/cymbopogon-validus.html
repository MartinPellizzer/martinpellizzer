Cymbopogon validus

Cymbopogon validus, commonly known as wild lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.

Cymbopogon validus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
