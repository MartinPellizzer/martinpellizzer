Caryopteris divaricata

Caryopteris divaricata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
