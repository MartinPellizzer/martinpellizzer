Cyperus graminifolius

Cyperus graminifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can dominate aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Cyperus graminifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
