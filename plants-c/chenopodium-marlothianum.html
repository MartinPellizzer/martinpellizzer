Chenopodium marlothianum

Chenopodium marlothianum, commonly known as the marloth's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically in summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Chenopodium marlothianum is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
