Carex brehmeri

Carex brehmeri, commonly known as Brehmer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex brehmeri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
