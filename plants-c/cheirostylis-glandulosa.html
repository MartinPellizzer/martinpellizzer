Cheirostylis glandulosa

Cheirostylis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cheirostylis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow blooms with a distinctive, glandular structure on its petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
