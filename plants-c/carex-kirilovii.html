Carex kirilovii

Carex kirilovii, commonly known as Kirilov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-stamened flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex kirilovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
