Colubrina pubescens

Colubrina pubescens, commonly known as the hairy colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The hairy colubrina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
