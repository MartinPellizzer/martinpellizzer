Caperonia angustissima

Caperonia angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf caperonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy habitats. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Caperonia angustissima is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
