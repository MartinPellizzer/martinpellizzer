Carex × arakanei

Carex × arakanei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Carex × arakanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
