Cylbanida

Cylbanida, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Cylbanida thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
