Cornus paucinervis

Cornus paucinervis, commonly known as the slender dogwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cornaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Cornus paucinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
