Campanula rotundifolia subf. grandiflora

Campanula rotundifolia subf.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
