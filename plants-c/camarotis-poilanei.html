Camarotis poilanei

Camarotis poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy, strap-like leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring.

Camarotis poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized collections due to its rarity and beauty.
