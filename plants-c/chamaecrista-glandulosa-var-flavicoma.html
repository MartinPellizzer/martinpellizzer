Chamaecrista glandulosa var. flavicoma

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. flavicoma, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, glandular hairs that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, branched clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is upright, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, making it suitable for garden borders or naturalized areas.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
