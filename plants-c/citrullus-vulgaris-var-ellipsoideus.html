Citrullus vulgaris var. ellipsoideus

Citrullus vulgaris var. ellipsoideus, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that produces elongated, elliptical fruits with a thin, edible rind. The plant is characterized by its lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers, which are typically monoecious. The fruits are often harvested when immature for their crisp, refreshing taste and mild flavor.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
