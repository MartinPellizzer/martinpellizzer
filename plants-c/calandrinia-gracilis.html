Calandrinia gracilis

Calandrinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calandrinia gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
