Croton eichleri

Croton eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri croton, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru, where it grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or purple, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping in warm climates.
