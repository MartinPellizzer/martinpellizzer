Clinopodium speciosum

Clinopodium speciosum, commonly known as showy mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Showy mountain mint is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
