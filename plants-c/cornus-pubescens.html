Cornus pubescens

Cornus pubescens, commonly known as the downy cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by bright red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Cornus pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
