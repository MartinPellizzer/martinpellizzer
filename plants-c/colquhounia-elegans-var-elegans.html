Colquhounia elegans var. elegans

Colquhounia elegans var. elegans, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
