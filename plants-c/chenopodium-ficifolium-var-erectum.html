Chenopodium ficifolium var. erectum

Chenopodium ficifolium var. erectum, commonly known as the upright goosefoot, is a species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, beak-like bracts. Its growth habit is upright, making it distinguishable from other varieties of Chenopodium.

It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.
