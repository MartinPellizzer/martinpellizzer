Croton trichocephalus var. leptophylloides

Croton trichocephalus var. leptophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and adaptability to various soil types.
