Celtis japonica f. rotundata

Celtis japonica f. rotundata, commonly known as the Japanese boxleaf elm, is a variant of the Japanese elm characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, typically growing in temperate climates. The leaves are thick, leathery, and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth and resistance to urban conditions, making it suitable for landscaping in gardens and parks.
