Calligonum rubescens

Calligonum rubescens, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish-brown with age, and its small, fleshy, pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its succulent leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky landscapes.

Calligonum rubescens plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
