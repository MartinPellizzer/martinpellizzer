Carex × sardloqensis

Carex × sardloqensis, commonly known as the Sardloqensis sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This species is typically found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
