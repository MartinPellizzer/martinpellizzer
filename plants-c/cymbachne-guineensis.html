Cymbachne guineensis

Cymbachne guineensis, commonly known as African grass or Guinea grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of grass species. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed soils.

Cymbachne guineensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used for forage or soil stabilization.
