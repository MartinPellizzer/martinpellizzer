Cosmos sulphureus var. exaristatus

Cosmos sulphureus var. exaristatus, commonly known as the sulphur cosmos, is a striking annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and also covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

The flowers are tubular at the base and open into a flat, radiate shape, making it a popular choice for garden displays.
