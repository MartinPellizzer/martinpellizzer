Cistus berthelotianus var. pilosus

Cistus berthelotianus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and traditional herbal uses.
