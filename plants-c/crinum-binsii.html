Crinum binsii

Crinum binsii, commonly known as the African lily or the red crinum, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often emerge from a bulbous base.

Crinum binsii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
