Cousinia glaphyrocephala

Cousinia glaphyrocephala, commonly known as the white-headed cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features grayish-blue leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a rounded inflorescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils at high elevations.

Cousinia glaphyrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
