Crotalaria globifera

Crotalaria globifera, commonly known as the globe flower or African globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive globular flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique structure that resembles small globes.

Crotalaria globifera is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
