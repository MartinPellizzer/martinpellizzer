Chamaesyce viminea

Chamaesyce viminea, commonly known as creeping sandweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It typically forms dense mats of prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly across disturbed soils. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

This plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or along roadsides.
