Chlorospatha bullata

Chlorospatha bullata, commonly known as the bullate leaf fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright green, leathery fronds that are often covered in small, rounded bumps, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients efficiently.

The bullate leaf fern is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to help stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
