Centaurea phrygia subsp. abnormis

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

abnormis, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
