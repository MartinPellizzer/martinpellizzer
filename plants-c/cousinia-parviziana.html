Cousinia parviziana

Cousinia parviziana, commonly known as the Persian spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cousinia parviziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add color to rocky landscapes.
