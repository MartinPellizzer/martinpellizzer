Camellia japonica var. hortensis

Camellia japonica var. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The flowers are typically pink, red, or white, with a distinctive double petal structure. This variety is well-suited for garden cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and landscapes.
