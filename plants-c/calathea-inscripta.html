Calathea inscripta

Calathea inscripta, commonly known as the variegated calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves with intricate patterns of green, silver, and white. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea inscripta is known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for interior gardening.
