Cyclosorus magnificus

Cyclosorus magnificus, commonly known as the golden spiraled fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral-shaped fronds that display a vibrant mix of green, gold, and copper hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients efficiently.

The cyclosorus magnificus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
