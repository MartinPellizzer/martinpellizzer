Cassia confinis

Cassia confinis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous racemes of yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important resource for pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cassia confinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
