Carex crawfordii

Carex crawfordii, commonly known as Crawford's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
