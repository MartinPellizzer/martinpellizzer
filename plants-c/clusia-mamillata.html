Clusia mamillata

Clusia mamillata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia mamillata is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution.
