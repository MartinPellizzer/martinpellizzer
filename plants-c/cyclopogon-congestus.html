Cyclopogon congestus

Cyclopogon congestus, commonly known as the clustered cyclopogon, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural research.
