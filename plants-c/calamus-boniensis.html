Calamus boniensis

Calamus boniensis, commonly known as the Bornean calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus boniensis is native to the rainforests of Borneo and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
