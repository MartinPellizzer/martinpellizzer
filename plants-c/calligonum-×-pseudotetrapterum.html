Calligonum × pseudotetrapterum

Calligonum × pseudotetrapterum, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a natural hybrid between Calligonum glaucum and Calligonum trichotomum, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Calligonum × pseudotetrapterum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
