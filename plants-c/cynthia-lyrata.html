Cynthia lyrata

Cynthia lyrata, commonly known as the lyrata cypress, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that are blue-green in color and its distinctive, scale-like bark. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

Cynthia lyrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
