Calamus hosensis

Calamus hosensis, commonly known as the Hosen's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Calamus hosensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family plants.

Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
