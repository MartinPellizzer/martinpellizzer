Cinnamomum rosselianum

Cinnamomum rosselianum, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
