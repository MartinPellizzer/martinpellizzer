Caesalpinia ignota

Caesalpinia ignota, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Caesalpinia ignota produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
