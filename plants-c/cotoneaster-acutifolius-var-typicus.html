Cotoneaster acutifolius var. typicus

Cotoneaster acutifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil types.

This species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in rocky or arid environments.
