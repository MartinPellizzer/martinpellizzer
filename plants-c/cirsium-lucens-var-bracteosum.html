Cirsium lucens var. bracteosum

Cirsium lucens var. bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteated thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched stem that bears purple to pink flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into prickly, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and meadows.
