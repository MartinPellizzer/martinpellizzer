Cynanchum decipiens

Cynanchum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds known for their specialized floral structures. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release silky, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cynanchum decipiens is adapted to moist, disturbed habitats and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
