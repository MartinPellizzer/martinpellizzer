Cyperus minutiflorus

Cyperus minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically occur in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in two rows, giving it a distinct appearance. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus minutiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic creatures.
