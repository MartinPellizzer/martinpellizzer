Chloranthus quadrifolius

Chloranthus quadrifolius, commonly known as the four-leaved angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
