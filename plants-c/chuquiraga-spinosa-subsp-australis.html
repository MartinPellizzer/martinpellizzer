Chuquiraga spinosa subsp. australis

Chuquiraga spinosa subsp.

australis, commonly known as the southern chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant has spiny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to tolerate coastal and arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
