Croton bigbendensis

Croton bigbendensis, commonly known as Big Bend croton, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Texas and New Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces vibrant, palmate leaves with a distinctive yellow-green coloration, and its flowers are small, clustered, and usually greenish-white in hue. Croton bigbendensis is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in xeriscaped gardens due to its low water requirements.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
