Chaetanthera tenuifolia

Chaetanthera tenuifolia, commonly known as slender false mignonette, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
