Centaurea limbata var. candidans

Centaurea limbata var. candidans, commonly known as the white-centred star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, spiny stems and produces showy, radial flowers with white to pale blue petals and a prominent yellow center. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems.
