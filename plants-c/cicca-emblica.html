Cicca emblica

Cicca emblica, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a small, sour berry that ripens to a golden orange color, containing high levels of vitamin C. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and medicinal properties.
