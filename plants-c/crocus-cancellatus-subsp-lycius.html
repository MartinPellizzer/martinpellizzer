Crocus cancellatus subsp. lycius

Crocus cancellatus subsp.

lycius, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive veined pattern. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
