Cryptocarya amygdalina

Cryptocarya amygdalina, commonly known as the bitter ash or sweet ash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cryptocarya amygdalina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
