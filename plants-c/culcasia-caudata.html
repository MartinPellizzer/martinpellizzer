Culcasia caudata

Culcasia caudata, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a glossy, deep green surface. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with red veins, which appear in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its size and growth habits, it is best suited for tropical or subtropical climates.
