Colchicum neapolitanum var. corsicum

Colchicum neapolitanum var. corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a tall, leafless stem. It is adapted to rocky and calcareous soils, often growing in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
