Campomanesia transalpina

Campomanesia transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Campomanesia transalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and winter blooms.
