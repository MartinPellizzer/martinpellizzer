Cotoneaster racemiflorus var. hissaricus

Cotoneaster racemiflorus var. hissaricus, commonly known as the Hissar cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in autumn, and it produces clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
