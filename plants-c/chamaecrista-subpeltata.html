Chamaecrista subpeltata

Chamaecrista subpeltata, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
