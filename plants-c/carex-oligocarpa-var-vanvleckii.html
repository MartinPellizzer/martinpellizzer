Carex oligocarpa var. vanvleckii

Carex oligocarpa var. vanvleckii, commonly known as the VanVleck sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
