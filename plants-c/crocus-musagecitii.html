Crocus musagecitii

Crocus musagecitii, commonly known as the Musa's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its early blooming habit, which can signal the arrival of spring in colder climates.
