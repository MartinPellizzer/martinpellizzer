Cytisus sauzeanus

Cytisus sauzeanus, commonly known as the Alaskan broom, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Cytisus sauzeanus is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
