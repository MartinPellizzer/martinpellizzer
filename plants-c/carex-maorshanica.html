Carex maorshanica

Carex maorshanica, commonly known as the New Zealand flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as stream banks and swampy areas. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, dark green leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
