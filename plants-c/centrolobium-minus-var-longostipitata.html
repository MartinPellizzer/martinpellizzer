Centrolobium minus var. longostipitata

Centrolobium minus var. longostipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, slender stipules that give it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
