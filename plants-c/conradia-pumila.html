Conradia pumila

Conradia pumila, commonly known as the smooth rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Conradia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
