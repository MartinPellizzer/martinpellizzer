Conyza perennis

Conyza perennis, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza perennis is known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution.
