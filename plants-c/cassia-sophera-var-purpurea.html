Cassia sophera var. purpurea

Cassia sophera var. purpurea, commonly known as purple flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, yellow, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
