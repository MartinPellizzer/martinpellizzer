Cordia obscura var. tomentosa

Cordia obscura var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough texture.

This species is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
