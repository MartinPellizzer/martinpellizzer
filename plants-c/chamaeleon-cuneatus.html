Chamaeleon cuneatus

Chamaeleon cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamaeleon, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
