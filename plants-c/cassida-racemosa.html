Cassida racemosa

Cassida racemosa, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive floral structures. Its foliage is usually hairy, and it can be invasive in certain regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
