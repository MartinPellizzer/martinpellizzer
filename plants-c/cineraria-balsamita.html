Cineraria balsamita

Cineraria balsamita, commonly known as sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Cineraria balsamita is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
