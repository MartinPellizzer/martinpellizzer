Carex filiformis var. aematorhyncha

Carex filiformis var. aematorhyncha, commonly known as the golden thread sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues, especially at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
