Carlina bracteata

Carlina bracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces showy yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Carlina bracteata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
