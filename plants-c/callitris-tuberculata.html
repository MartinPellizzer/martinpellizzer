Callitris tuberculata

Callitris tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-barked apple or mountain apple, is a species of coniferous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Callitris tuberculata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and sustenance to native fauna.
