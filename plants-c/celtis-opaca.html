Celtis opaca

Celtis opaca, commonly known as the sour gum or sugarberry, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, greenish fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color.

Celtis opaca is often found in moist soils along stream banks and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
