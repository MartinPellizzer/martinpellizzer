Cynanchum chiloense

Cynanchum chiloense, commonly known as Chilean milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its stems are covered in fine hairs and often have a milky latex when broken.

Cynanchum chiloense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with limited rainfall.
