Calea oxylepis

Calea oxylepis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flower heads that open at night. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea oxylepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nighttime blooming habit.
