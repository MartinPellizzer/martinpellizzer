Carduus leiophyllus

Carduus leiophyllus, commonly known as the silky thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
