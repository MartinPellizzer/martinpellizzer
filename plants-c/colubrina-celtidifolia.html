Colubrina celtidifolia

Colubrina celtidifolia, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or limestone soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to coastal environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
