Coccocypselum tontanea

Coccocypselum tontanea, commonly known as the silky rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
