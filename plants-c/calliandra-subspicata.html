Calliandra subspicata

Calliandra subspicata, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra subspicata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
