Clematis trifida

Clematis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three distinct leaflets, each typically lobed or notched. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white, blue, or purple. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis trifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant.
