Clerodendrum floribundum var. coriaceum

Clerodendrum floribundum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the thick-leaved clerodendrum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are thick and leathery, often with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its flowering display attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
