Chimonocalamus dumosus var. pygmaeus

Chimonocalamus dumosus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a small, clump-forming grass-like plant that typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely clustered at the base. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
