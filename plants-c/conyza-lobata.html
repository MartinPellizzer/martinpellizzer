Conyza lobata

Conyza lobata, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and opposite, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in loose arrays, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Conyza lobata is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
