Coronilla valentina subsp. glauca

Coronilla valentina subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in improving soil fertility.
