Conyza macrostachya

Conyza macrostachya, commonly known as the Mexican horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing small white or yellowish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
