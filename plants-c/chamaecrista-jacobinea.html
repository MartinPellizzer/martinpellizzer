Chamaecrista jacobinea

Chamaecrista jacobinea, commonly known as yellow cassia, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it useful for soil enrichment and erosion control.
