Cephalanthus naucleoides

Cephalanthus naucleoides, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, often dispersed by wind.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
