Cephalotaxus sinensis f. globosa

Cephalotaxus sinensis f. globosa, commonly known as the globe yew, is a unique cultivar of the yew plant characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

It features scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for birds. It is evergreen, maintaining its foliage throughout the year, and thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and compact form, making it suitable for small garden spaces.
