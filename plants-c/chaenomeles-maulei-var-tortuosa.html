Chaenomeles maulei var. tortuosa

Chaenomeles maulei var. tortuosa, commonly known as the Maule cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Chile, characterized by its ornamental value and unique foliage. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
