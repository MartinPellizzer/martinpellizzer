Callicarpa giraldiana

Callicarpa giraldiana, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
