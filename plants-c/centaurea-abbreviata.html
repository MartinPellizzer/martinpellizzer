Centaurea abbreviata

Centaurea abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Centaurea abbreviata is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
