Cercis canadensis var. alba

Cercis canadensis var. alba, commonly known as the white Canadian red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has a rounded crown and produces small, red samaras that mature in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate with coarsely toothed margins and turn vibrant red in the autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
