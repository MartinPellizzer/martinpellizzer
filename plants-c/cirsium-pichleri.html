Cirsium pichleri

Cirsium pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in Austria and the Czech Republic. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

Cirsium pichleri is a perennial plant that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
