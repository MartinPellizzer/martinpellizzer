Campanula trautvetteri

Campanula trautvetteri, commonly known as the Trautvetter's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, thriving in rocky or alpine environments.

Campanula trautvetteri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
