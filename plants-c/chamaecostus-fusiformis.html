Chamaecostus fusiformis

Chamaecostus fusiformis, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as a bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Chamaecostus fusiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
