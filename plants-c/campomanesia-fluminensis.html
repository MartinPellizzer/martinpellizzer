Campomanesia fluminensis

Campomanesia fluminensis, commonly known as pitanga-preta or black pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries, which are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
