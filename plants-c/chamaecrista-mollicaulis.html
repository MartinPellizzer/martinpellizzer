Chamaecrista mollicaulis

Chamaecrista mollicaulis, commonly known as the silky crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or subshrub with slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. The leaves are compound, consisting of pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
