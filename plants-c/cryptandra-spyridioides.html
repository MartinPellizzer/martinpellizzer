Cryptandra spyridioides

Cryptandra spyridioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cryptandra, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
