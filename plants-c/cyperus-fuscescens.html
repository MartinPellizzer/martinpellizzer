Cyperus fuscescens

Cyperus fuscescens, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Cyperus fuscescens produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
