Charidion cordatum

Charidion cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually appealing, low-maintenance plant.
