Crusea coccinea var. breviloba

Crusea coccinea var. breviloba, commonly known as the scarlet crusea, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of purple and yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
