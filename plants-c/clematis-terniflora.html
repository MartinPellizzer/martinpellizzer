Clematis terniflora

Clematis terniflora, commonly known as sweet autumn clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Clematis terniflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be propagated through cuttings or seeds.
