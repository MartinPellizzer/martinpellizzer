Carduus meonanthus subsp. meonanthus

Carduus meonanthus subsp.

meonanthus, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves with rough textures, and its root system is fibrous and extensive. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
