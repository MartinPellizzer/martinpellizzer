Cerasus × mohacsyana

Cerasus × mohacsyana, commonly known as the Mohács cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitana and Prunus avium.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender branches and dense foliage. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, sweet-tart red fruits. It is native to Hungary and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
