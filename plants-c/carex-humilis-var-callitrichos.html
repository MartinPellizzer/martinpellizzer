Carex humilis var. callitrichos

Carex humilis var. callitrichos, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
