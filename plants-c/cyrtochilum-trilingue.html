Cyrtochilum trilingue

Cyrtochilum trilingue, commonly known as the Three-lobed Slipper Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns and colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, blooming in clusters during the warmer months.

Cyrtochilum trilingue is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
