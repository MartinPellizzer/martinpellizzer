Commiphora laxiflora

Commiphora laxiflora, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in scrublands or woodlands. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and has leathery, oval-shaped leaves.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
