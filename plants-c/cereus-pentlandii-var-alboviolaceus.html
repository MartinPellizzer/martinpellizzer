Cereus pentlandii var. alboviolaceus

Cereus pentlandii var. alboviolaceus, commonly known as the white-violet cereus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white or pale violet spines. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of unusual succulents.
