Correa × rubra var. pulchella

Correa × rubra var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink correa, is a hybrid shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features striking, tubular pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white tomentum. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
