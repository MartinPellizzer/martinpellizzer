Crotalaria mongaensis

Crotalaria mongaensis, commonly known as the Mongaensis cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crotalaria mongaensis is also valued for its potential use in agroforestry and as a forage crop.
