Collinsia bicolor var. austromontana

Collinsia bicolor var. austromontana, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, lance-shaped foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
