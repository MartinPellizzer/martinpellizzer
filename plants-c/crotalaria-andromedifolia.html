Crotalaria andromedifolia

Crotalaria andromedifolia, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grassland habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria andromedifolia is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its role in soil enrichment and its value as a nectar source for pollinators.
