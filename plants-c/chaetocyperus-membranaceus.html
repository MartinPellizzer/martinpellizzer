Chaetocyperus membranaceus

Chaetocyperus membranaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive membranous texture, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
