Cyperus roxburghii

Cyperus roxburghii, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, purple or pinkish flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond margins. The plant has rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a common ornamental plant in horticulture.
