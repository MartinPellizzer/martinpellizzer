Croton ivohibensis var. lepidotus

Croton ivohibensis var. lepidotus, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
