Croton greveanus var. micraster

Croton greveanus var. micraster, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, colorful foliage that often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
