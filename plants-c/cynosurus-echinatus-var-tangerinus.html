Cynosurus echinatus var. tangerinus

Cynosurus echinatus var. tangerinus, commonly known as the Tangerine Sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and poorly drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish or purplish hue, especially in younger growth.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
