Carpobrotus quadrifidus f. rosea

Carpobrotus quadrifidus f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, prostrate stems and thick, waxy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden settings.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
