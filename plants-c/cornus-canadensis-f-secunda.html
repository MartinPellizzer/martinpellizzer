Cornus canadensis f. secunda

Cornus canadensis f. secunda, commonly known as the dwarf dogwood, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
