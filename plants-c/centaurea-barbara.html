Centaurea barbara

Centaurea barbara, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the central disk. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often reddish in color.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
