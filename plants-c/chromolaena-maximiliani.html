Chromolaena maximiliani

Chromolaena maximiliani, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by purple bracts, giving it a striking appearance. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields due to its adaptability.

Chromolaena maximiliani is also known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in certain regions.
