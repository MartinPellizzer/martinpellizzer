Cucurbita pepo var. valesiaca

Cucurbita pepo var. valesiaca, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant is an annual vine with lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
