Cereus pierre-braunianus

Cereus pierre-braunianus, commonly known as the Pierre-Braunianus Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are typically large, white or pale yellow, and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

It is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
