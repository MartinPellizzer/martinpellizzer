Cyperus remotiflorus var. schweickerdtii

Cyperus remotiflorus var. schweickerdtii, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella sedge, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water bodies. Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to help stabilize soil and filter water in aquatic environments.
