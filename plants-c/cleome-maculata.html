Cleome maculata

Cleome maculata, commonly known as the spotted cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cleome maculata is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.
