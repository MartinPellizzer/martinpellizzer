Centaurea × belgradensis

Centaurea × belgradensis, commonly known as the Belgrade centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southeastern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
