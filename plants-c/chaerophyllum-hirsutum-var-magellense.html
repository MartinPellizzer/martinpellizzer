Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. magellense

Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. magellense, commonly known as the Magellan cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hirsute, lobed leaves and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.
