Clinopodium axillare

Clinopodium axillare, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Clinopodium axillare is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
