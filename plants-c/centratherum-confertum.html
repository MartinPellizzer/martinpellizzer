Centratherum confertum

Centratherum confertum, commonly known as the clustered centipede grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

Centratherum confertum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ability to thrive in poor soils and its low maintenance requirements.
