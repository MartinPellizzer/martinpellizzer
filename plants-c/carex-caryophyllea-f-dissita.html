Carex caryophyllea f. dissita

Carex caryophyllea f. dissita, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

The foliage of Carex caryophyllea f. dissita is often silvery or bluish-green, giving it its common name.
