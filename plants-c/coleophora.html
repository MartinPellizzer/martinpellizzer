Coleophora

Coleophora, commonly known as case-bearers, are a genus of moths in the family Coleophoridae.

These small moths are characterized by their larvae, which construct protective cases made from plant material and silk. The botanical aspects of Coleophora include their host plants, which vary widely and often include grasses, sedges, and other herbaceous plants. The larvae's feeding habits can influence the growth and development of these plants.

Studying Coleophora provides insights into plant-insect interactions and ecological relationships in various habitats.
