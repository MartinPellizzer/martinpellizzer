Clinopodium caricum

Clinopodium caricum, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and have a distinctive purple or pink coloration.

Clinopodium caricum is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
