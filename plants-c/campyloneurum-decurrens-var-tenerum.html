Campyloneurum decurrens var. tenerum

Campyloneurum decurrens var. tenerum, commonly known as the soft-tipped shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically arching or pendulous. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its fertile leaves, which are usually arranged in a single row. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and growth habits make it a distinctive species within its genus.
