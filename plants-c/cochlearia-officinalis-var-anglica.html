Cochlearia officinalis var. anglica

Cochlearia officinalis var. anglica, commonly known as the English cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal cluster. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to maritime climates.

The species is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of medicinal compounds.
