Celosia virgata

Celosia virgata, commonly known as the flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually red, orange, or yellow, and they bloom in dense, cone-shaped clusters.

Celosia virgata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
