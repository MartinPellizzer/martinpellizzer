Cnicus argemone

Cnicus argemone, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features a hairy, branching stem with yellow flowers that have a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glandular surface.

Cnicus argemone is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed.
