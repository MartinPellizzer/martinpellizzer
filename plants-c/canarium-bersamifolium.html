Canarium bersamifolium

Canarium bersamifolium, commonly known as the Malayan canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are yellow-green in color, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
