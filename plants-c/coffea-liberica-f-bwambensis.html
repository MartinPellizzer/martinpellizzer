Coffea liberica f. bwambensis

Coffea liberica f. bwambensis, commonly known as the West African coffee.

This plant is a wild variety of Liberica coffee, native to the tropical regions of West Africa. It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and can grow up to 15 meters tall in its natural habitat. The flowers are white and fragrant, and the fruit produces large, oblong seeds used for coffee production.

It is less commonly cultivated than other coffee species but is valued for its unique flavor profile.
