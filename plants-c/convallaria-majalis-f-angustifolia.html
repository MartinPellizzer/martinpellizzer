Convallaria majalis f. angustifolia

Convallaria majalis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lily of the valley, is a cultivar of the lily of the valley plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. It is a perennial herb native to woodland areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
