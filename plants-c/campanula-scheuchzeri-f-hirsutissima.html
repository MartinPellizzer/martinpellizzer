Campanula scheuchzeri f. hirsutissima

Campanula scheuchzeri f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hirsute bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its hairy, robust stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its hirsute appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
