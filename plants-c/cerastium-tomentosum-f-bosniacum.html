Cerastium tomentosum f. bosniacum

Cerastium tomentosum f. bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian mouse-ear, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb native to the Balkans.

It features small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that are rounded and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
