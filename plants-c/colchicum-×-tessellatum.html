Colchicum × tessellatum

Colchicum × tessellatum, commonly known as the spotted autumn crocus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with contrasting white and purple markings. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its foliage consists of broad, arrow-shaped leaves that emerge in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
