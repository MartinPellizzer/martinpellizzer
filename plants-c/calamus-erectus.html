Calamus erectus

Calamus erectus, commonly known as the upright calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by ornamental fruits. Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in herbal medicine.

Calamus erectus is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
