Calophyllum auriculatum

Calophyllum auriculatum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
