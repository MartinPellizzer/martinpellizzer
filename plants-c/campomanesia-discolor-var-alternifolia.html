Campomanesia discolor var. alternifolia

Campomanesia discolor var. alternifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, red drupes that are visually similar to cherries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other well-known plants like the poison ivy and cashew.
