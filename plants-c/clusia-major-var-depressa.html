Clusia major var. depressa

Clusia major var. depressa, commonly known as the broadleaf poinsettia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
