Cyperus conglomeratus subsp. conglomeratus

Cyperus conglomeratus subsp.

conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
