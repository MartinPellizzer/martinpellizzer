Crepis chanetii

Crepis chanetii, commonly known as the Chanet's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are basal and linear, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis chanetii is often found in disturbed areas and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
