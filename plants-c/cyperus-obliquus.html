Cyperus obliquus

Cyperus obliquus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus obliquus is known for its adaptability to various water conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
