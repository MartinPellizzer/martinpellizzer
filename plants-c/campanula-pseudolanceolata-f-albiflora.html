Campanula pseudolanceolata f. albiflora

Campanula pseudolanceolata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white blooms.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, elegant appearance.
