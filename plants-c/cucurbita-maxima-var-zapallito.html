Cucurbita maxima var. zapallito

Cucurbita maxima var. zapallito, commonly known as the Zapallito squash, is a variety native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a type of winter squash characterized by its small, elongated, and often striped fruits. The plant produces large, palmately lobed leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to mature.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, offering a sweet and nutty flavor.
