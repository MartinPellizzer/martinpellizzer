Cleisostoma brevipes

Cleisostoma brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed hooded orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, terrestrial or epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive spur at the base of the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cleisostoma brevipes is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
