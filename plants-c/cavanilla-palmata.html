Cavanilla palmata

Cavanilla palmata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of narrow, arching leaves. Its flowers are small and grouped in clusters, often producing bright red fruits that are attractive to birds.

Cavanilla palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.
