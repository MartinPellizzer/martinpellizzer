Commelina nudiflora f. pilosissima

Commelina nudiflora f. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its hairy texture is a distinguishing feature, particularly on the stems and leaves.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
