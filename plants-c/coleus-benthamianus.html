Coleus benthamianus

Coleus benthamianus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Its flowers are small and tubular, typically appearing in clusters at the leaf axils.

Coleus benthamianus is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and easy care requirements.
