Catasetum christyanum var. chlorops

Catasetum christyanum var. chlorops, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are unique among the Catasetum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous small, fragrant blooms.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
