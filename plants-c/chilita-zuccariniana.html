Chilita zuccariniana

Chilita zuccariniana, commonly known as the Zucchini Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in small, spiny protrusions. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Chilita zuccariniana is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
