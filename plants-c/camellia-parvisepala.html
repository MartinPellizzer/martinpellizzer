Camellia parvisepala

Camellia parvisepala, commonly known as the small-sepal camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture. Its flowers are striking, with deep pink petals and prominent yellow stamens.

Camellia parvisepala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
