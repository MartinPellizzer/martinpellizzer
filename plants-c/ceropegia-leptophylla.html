Ceropegia leptophylla

Ceropegia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces unique flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape and a specialized structure called a syrphid lure, which attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia leptophylla is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and interesting floral features.
