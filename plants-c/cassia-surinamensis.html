Cassia surinamensis

Cassia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain numerous flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Cassia surinamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
