Cirrhopetalum lineatum

Cirrhopetalum lineatum, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
