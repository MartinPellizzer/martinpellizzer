Crataegus sanguinea var. altaica

Crataegus sanguinea var. altaica, commonly known as the Altai hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.
