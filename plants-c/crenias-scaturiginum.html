Crenias scaturiginum

Crenias scaturiginum, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a subtle golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical charm.

Crenias scaturiginum is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
