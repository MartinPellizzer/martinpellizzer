Coreopsis liebmannii

Coreopsis liebmannii, commonly known as the prairie coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Coreopsis liebmannii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
