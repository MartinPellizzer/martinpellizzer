Carex praecox f. petermannii

Carex praecox f. petermannii, commonly known as the early sedge, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often slightly hairy, and its flowers are small and clustered.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
