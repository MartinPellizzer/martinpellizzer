Cassia borneensis

Cassia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods.

Cassia borneensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
