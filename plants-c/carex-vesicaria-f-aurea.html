Carex vesicaria f. aurea

Carex vesicaria f. aurea, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to golden green in color, distinguishing it from its typical green form. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually reddish-brown in color.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in wetland gardens.
