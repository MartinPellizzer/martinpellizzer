Cylindropuntia alamosensis

Cylindropuntia alamosensis, commonly known as the Alamos prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems, which are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and contains a sweet, juicy pulp.

This resilient plant is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
