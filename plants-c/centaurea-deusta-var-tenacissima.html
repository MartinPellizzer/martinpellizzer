Centaurea deusta var. tenacissima

Centaurea deusta var. tenacissima, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, bristly leaves. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
