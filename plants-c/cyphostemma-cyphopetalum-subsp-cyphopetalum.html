Cyphostemma cyphopetalum subsp. cyphopetalum

Cyphostemma cyphopetalum subsp.

cyphopetalum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
