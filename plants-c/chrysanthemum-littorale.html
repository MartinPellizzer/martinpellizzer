Chrysanthemum littorale

Chrysanthemum littorale, commonly known as the sea chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It is well adapted to withstand wind and salt spray, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
