Cephalaria leucantha subsp. boetica

Cephalaria leucantha subsp.

boetica, commonly known as the whitehead cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
