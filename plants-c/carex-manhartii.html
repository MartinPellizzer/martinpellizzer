Carex manhartii

Carex manhartii, commonly known as the Manhart's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are generally dark green in color.

Carex manhartii produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated.
