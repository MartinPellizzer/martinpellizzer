Chrysophyllum lucentifolium subsp. lucentifolium

Chrysophyllum lucentifolium subsp.

lucentifolium, commonly known as the shining-leaf chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
