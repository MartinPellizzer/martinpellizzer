Crataegus audens

Crataegus audens, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, attracting various wildlife. Crataegus audens thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
