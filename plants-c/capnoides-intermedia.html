Capnoides intermedia

Capnoides intermedia, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Capnoides intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
