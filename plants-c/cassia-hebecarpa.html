Cassia hebecarpa

Cassia hebecarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically large and deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod that splits open to release seeds.

Cassia hebecarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
