Campanula glomerata var. cervicaria

Campanula glomerata var. cervicaria, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its hardy nature in temperate climates.
