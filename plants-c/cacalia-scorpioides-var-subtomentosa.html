Cacalia scorpioides var. subtomentosa

Cacalia scorpioides var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in open areas such as meadows and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered with fine, soft hairs, which give it the common name "hairy false dandelion.".

This variety is distinguished by its dense pubescence and is often found in the Pacific Northwest region.
