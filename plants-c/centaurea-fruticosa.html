Centaurea fruticosa

Centaurea fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, prickly hairs, and its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue. Its flowers are bright blue or purple, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea fruticosa is known for its hardy nature and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
