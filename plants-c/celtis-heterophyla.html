Celtis heterophyla

Celtis heterophyla, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by edible, round berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals.

Celtis heterophyla is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
