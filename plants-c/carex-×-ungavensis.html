Carex × ungavensis

Carex × ungavensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that typically bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
