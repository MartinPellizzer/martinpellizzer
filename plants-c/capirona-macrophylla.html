Capirona macrophylla

Capirona macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its enormous, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The leaves are supported by long, sturdy petioles that keep them floating on the water's surface. Its flowers are large, showy, and typically white or pink, blooming at night.

This plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
