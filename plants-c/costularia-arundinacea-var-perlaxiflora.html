Costularia arundinacea var. perlaxiflora

Costularia arundinacea var. perlaxiflora, commonly known as the golden thread plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
