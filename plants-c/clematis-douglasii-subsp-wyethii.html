Clematis douglasii subsp. wyethii

Clematis douglasii subsp.

wyethii, commonly known as the Wyeth's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States. It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of twining stems and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
