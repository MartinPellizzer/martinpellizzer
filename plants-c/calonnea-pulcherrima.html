Calonnea pulcherrima

Calonnea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its often arid or semi-arid habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
