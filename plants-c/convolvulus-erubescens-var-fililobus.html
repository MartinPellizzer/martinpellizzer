Convolvulus erubescens var. fililobus

Convolvulus erubescens var. fililobus, commonly known as the redstem morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is native to parts of North America and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
