Crataegus × pseudoalemanniensis

Crataegus × pseudoalemanniensis, commonly known as the false alemanian hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its ornamental value and adaptability.

It typically features thorned branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that persist into winter. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal in gardens. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The fruit of this species is edible but often used more for ornamental purposes than for consumption.
