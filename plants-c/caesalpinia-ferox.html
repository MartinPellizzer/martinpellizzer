Caesalpinia ferox

Caesalpinia ferox, commonly known as the spiked coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Caesalpinia ferox is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
