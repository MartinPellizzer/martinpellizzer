Celastrus hamelii

Celastrus hamelii, commonly known as the Hamel's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the understory flora in its native habitat.
