Connarus monocarpos subsp. monocarpos

Connarus monocarpos subsp.

monocarpos, commonly known as the single-fruited connarus, is a member of the Connaraceae family. It is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by a single, fleshy, berry-like fruit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
