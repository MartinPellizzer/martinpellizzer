Calamagrostis clarionis

Calamagrostis clarionis, commonly known as the Clarion grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Calamagrostis clarionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and stabilizing wetland soils.
