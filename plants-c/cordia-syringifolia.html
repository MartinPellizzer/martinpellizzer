Cordia syringifolia

Cordia syringifolia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Cordia syringifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
