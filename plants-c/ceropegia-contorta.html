Ceropegia contorta

Ceropegia contorta, commonly known as the "string of turtles," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, fleshy, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia contorta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
