Campanula losae

Campanula losae, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Campanula losae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
