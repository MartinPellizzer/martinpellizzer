Carex parallela

Carex parallela, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with linear, often glaucous leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex parallela is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
