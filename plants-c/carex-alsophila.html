Carex alsophila

Carex alsophila, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex alsophila plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
