Crepidium purpureiflorum

Crepidium purpureiflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
