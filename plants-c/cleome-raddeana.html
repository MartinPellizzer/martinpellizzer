Cleome raddeana

Cleome raddeana, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like inflorescences that consist of small white or pink flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome raddeana is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ease of care.
