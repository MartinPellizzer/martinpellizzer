Caesalpiniodes macracanthum

Caesalpiniodes macracanthum, commonly known as the large-armed acanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by velvety bracts. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
