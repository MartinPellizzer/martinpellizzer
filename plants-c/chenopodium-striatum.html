Chenopodium striatum

Chenopodium striatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its fruits are dry, three-angled capsules containing tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.
