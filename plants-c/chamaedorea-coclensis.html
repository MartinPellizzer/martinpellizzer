Chamaedorea coclensis

Chamaedorea coclensis, commonly known as the Mexican feather plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its elegant, feather-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant is a compact shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Chamaedorea coclensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
