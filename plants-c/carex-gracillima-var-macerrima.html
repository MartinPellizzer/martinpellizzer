Carex gracillima var. macerrima

Carex gracillima var. macerrima, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
