Cirsium dipsacolepis var. calcicola

Cirsium dipsacolepis var. calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a woody base that becomes more pronounced with age.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
