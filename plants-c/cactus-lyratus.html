Cactus lyratus

Cactus lyratus, commonly known as the singing cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cactus lyratus is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
