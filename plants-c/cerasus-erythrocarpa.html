Cerasus erythrocarpa

Cerasus erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Cerasus erythrocarpa is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
