Castalia flavovirens

Castalia flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green castalia, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its bright yellow and green variegated leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking coloration.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
