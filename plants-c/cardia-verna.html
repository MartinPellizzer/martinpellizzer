Cardia verna

Cardia verna, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cardia verna is part of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
