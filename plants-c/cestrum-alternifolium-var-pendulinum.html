Cestrum alternifolium var. pendulinum

Cestrum alternifolium var. pendulinum, commonly known as the weeping yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive, bell-shaped flowers and cascading growth habit.
