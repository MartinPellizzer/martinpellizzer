Cirsium barnebyi

Cirsium barnebyi, commonly known as the Barneby's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Cirsium barnebyi is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
