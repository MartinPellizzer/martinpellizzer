Capsella × abortiva

Capsella × abortiva, commonly known as the common weed or mouse-ear.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Capsella bursa-pastoris and Capsella rubella. This plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in various regions.
