Campanopsis nudicaulis

Campanopsis nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairless, giving it the common name "naked-stem.".

Campanopsis nudicaulis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
