Cassia peralteana

Cassia peralteana, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically three to five lobes, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Cassia peralteana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
