Cucumis melo var. mehanawy

Cucumis melo var. mehanawy, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and sweet, juicy flesh. The plant is a herbaceous vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It requires well-drained soil and regular watering for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
