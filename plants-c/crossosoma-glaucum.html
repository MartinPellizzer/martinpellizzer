Crossosoma glaucum

Crossosoma glaucum, commonly known as the blue crossosoma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy, bluish-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
