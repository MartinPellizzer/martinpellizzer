Cassinia aculeata subsp. aculeata

Cassinia aculeata subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the rough corymbia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in corymbs, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
