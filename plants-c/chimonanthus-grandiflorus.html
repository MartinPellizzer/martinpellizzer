Chimonanthus grandiflorus

Chimonanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are fragrant and have a distinctive, almost citrus-like scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
