Cucumis sativus var. chiar

Cucumis sativus var. chiar, commonly known as the chiar cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its distinct, often pale or variegated skin that sets it apart from standard cucumber varieties. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing small, elongated fruits that are usually harvested before full maturity. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, lobed leaves, and flowers that are typically yellow with five petals.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in specialty gardening and culinary applications.
