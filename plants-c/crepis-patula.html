Crepis patula

Crepis patula, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Crepis patula is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through self-seeding.
