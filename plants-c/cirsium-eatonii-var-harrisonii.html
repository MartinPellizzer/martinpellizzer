Cirsium eatonii var. harrisonii

Cirsium eatonii var. harrisonii, commonly known as the Harrison's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces. The plant produces pale purple to blue composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and flowering characteristics compared to other thistle species.
