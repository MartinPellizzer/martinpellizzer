Calathea villosa var. pardina

Calathea villosa var. pardina, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns of green, white, and silver. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
