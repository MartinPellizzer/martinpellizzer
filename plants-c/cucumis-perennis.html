Cucumis perennis

Cucumis perennis, commonly known as the perennial cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, edible fruits similar to cucumbers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and agricultural settings.
