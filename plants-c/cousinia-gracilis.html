Cousinia gracilis

Cousinia gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. It produces slender, erect stems with narrow leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
