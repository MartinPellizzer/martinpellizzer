Calea triantha

Calea triantha, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea triantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its ethereal beauty.
