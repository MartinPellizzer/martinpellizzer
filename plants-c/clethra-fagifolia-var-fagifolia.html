Clethra fagifolia var. fagifolia

Clethra fagifolia var. fagifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. These flowers are rich in nectar and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The shrub typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
