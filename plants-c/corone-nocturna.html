Corone nocturna

Corone nocturna, commonly known as the night crown, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, nocturnal blooms that open at dusk and close at dawn. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique blooming pattern, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
