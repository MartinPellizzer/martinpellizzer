Ceropegia nitida

Ceropegia nitida, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thin, fleshy, and often hairy stems that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a silvery or grayish coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Ceropegia nitida thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
