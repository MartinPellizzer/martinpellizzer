Cassia florida

Cassia florida, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
