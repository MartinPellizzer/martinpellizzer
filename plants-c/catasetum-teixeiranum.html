Catasetum teixeiranum

Catasetum teixeiranum, commonly known as the Teixeira's Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with distinctive, modified petals that aid in pollination by specific wasp species.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
