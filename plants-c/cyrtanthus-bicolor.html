Cyrtanthus bicolor

Cyrtanthus bicolor, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking white or pale pink flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is part of the Amaryllidaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Cyrtanthus bicolor is also valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
