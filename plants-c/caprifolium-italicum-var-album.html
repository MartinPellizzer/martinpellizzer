Caprifolium italicum var. album

Caprifolium italicum var. album, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant fragrance.
