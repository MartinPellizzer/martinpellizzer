Chamaecrista falcinella

Chamaecrista falcinella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Chamaecrista falcinella is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and fix nitrogen.
