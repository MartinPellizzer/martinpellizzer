Chenopodium bryoniifolium

Chenopodium bryoniifolium, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, prickly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Native to the western United States, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.
