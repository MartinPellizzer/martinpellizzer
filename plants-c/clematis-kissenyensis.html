Clematis kissenyensis

Clematis kissenyensis, commonly known as the Kisenyensi Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, typically during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires support for its climbing habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
