Calamagrostis neglecta var. obscura

Calamagrostis neglecta var. obscura, commonly known as the dark reed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green to almost black foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
