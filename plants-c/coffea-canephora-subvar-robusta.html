Coffea canephora subvar. robusta

Coffea canephora subvar. robusta, commonly known as Robusta coffee, is a species native to Central and Western Africa.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves and produces dark, glossy berries containing two seeds each. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and requires well-drained soil. It is known for its high caffeine content and strong, bitter flavor profile.

Robusta is primarily used in blends and for producing instant coffee due to its robustness and yield.
