Clusia cruciata

Clusia cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix shrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a cross-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Clusia cruciata is known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
