Cleome × ecuadorica

Cleome × ecuadorica, commonly known as the tropical spider flower, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Cleome hassleriana and Cleome gynandra.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble spider webs, blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleome × ecuadorica is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
