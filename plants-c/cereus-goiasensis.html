Cereus goiasensis

Cereus goiasensis, commonly known as the Goiania Cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers, which are typically white or pale pink, bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Cereus goiasensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
