Calandrinia sericea var. longipes

Calandrinia sericea var. longipes, commonly known as the silky calandrinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-covered stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
