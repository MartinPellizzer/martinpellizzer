Cotula filicula

Cotula filicula, commonly known as the slender groundsel, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. It has small, narrow leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Cotula filicula is generally drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
