Cyperus aterrimus var. agglomeratus

Cyperus aterrimus var. agglomeratus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
