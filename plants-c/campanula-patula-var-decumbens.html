Campanula patula var. decumbens

Campanula patula var. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features spreading stems that often lie close to the ground, producing clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and oval, with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
