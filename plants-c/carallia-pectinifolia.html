Carallia pectinifolia

Carallia pectinifolia, commonly known as the hairy pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bright red fruits that resemble pomegranates but are not related botanically. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange and are followed by the characteristic fruiting bodies.
