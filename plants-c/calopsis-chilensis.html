Calopsis chilensis

Calopsis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean calopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to arid conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calopsis chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
