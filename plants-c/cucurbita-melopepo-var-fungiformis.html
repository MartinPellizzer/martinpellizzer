Cucurbita melopepo var. fungiformis

Cucurbita melopepo var. fungiformis, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a variant of the species Cucurbita melopepo.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits that can weigh several hundred pounds. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and gourds. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces yellow flowers that transition to orange or red as the fruit matures.

This variety is often cultivated for its impressive size and is used in both culinary and decorative contexts.
