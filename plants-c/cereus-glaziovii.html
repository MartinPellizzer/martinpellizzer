Cereus glaziovii

Cereus glaziovii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, large white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Cereus glaziovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique blooming habit.
