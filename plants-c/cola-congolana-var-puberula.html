Cola congolana var. puberula

Cola congolana var. puberula, commonly known as the Congo cola or African cola, is a species of the Sterculiaceae family native to Central and West Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical leaves that are pubescent on the underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. The seeds are used in traditional medicine and for making cola beverages.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
