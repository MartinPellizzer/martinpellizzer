Coleus spectabilis

Coleus spectabilis, commonly known as the colorful coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and purple. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are usually dark green with contrasting veins. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil.

While it is primarily grown for its ornamental value, it is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
