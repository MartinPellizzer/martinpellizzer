Cactus speciosus var. grandiflorus

Cactus speciosus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous ornamental cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
