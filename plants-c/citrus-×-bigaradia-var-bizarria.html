Citrus × bigaradia var. bizarria

Citrus × bigaradia var. bizarria, commonly known as the bittersweet orange.

This hybrid citrus plant is a cross between the bitter orange (Citrus aurantium) and the sweet orange (Citrus sinensis). It is characterized by its thick, peely rind and segmented, juicy flesh that ranges from tart to sweet. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique flavor profile in culinary and medicinal uses.
