Cardamine amara f. monochlamydea

Cardamine amara f. monochlamydea, commonly known as the slender water crowfoot, is a variant of the water crowfoot genus Cardamine.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four sepals and no petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.
