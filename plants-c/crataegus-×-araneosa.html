Crataegus × araneosa

Crataegus × araneosa, commonly known as the webbace crabapple, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, branching pattern that resembles a spiderweb, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated margins, and it produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in spring.

The fruit is a small, red or yellow pome that ripens in late summer, often used in culinary or medicinal applications.
