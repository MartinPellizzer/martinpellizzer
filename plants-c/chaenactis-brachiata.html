Chaenactis brachiata

Chaenactis brachiata, commonly known as the branched rayless goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are often branched.
