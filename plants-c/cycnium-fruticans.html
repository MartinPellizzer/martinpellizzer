Cycnium fruticans

Cycnium fruticans, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, sunny habitats. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Cycnium fruticans is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
