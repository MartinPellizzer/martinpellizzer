Crataegus azarolus var. piriformis

Crataegus azarolus var. piriformis, commonly known as the Persian flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
