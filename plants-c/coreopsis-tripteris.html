Coreopsis tripteris

Coreopsis tripteris, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
