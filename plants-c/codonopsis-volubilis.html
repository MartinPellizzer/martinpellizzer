Codonopsis volubilis

Codonopsis volubilis, commonly known as the climbing monk's head or Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Codonopsis volubilis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
