Cicer arietinum subsp. eurasiaticum

Cicer arietinum subsp.

eurasiaticum, commonly known as the Eurasian chickpea, is a subspecies of the chickpea plant native to the temperate regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several angular seeds, which are the edible part. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a moderate climate with sufficient sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and agricultural purposes.
