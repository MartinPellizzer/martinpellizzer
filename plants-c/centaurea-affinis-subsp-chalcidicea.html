Centaurea affinis subsp. chalcidicea

Centaurea affinis subsp.

chalcidicea, commonly known as the Chalcidice centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
