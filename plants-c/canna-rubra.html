Canna rubra

Canna rubra, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or bronze tint. The plant produces showy, tropical-looking flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Canna rubra is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a bold addition to water gardens or landscape beds.
