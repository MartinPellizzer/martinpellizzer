Clematis heracleifolia var. maximowicziana

Clematis heracleifolia var. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
