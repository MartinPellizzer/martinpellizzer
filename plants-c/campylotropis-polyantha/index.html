Campylotropis Polyantha

Campylotropis polyantha is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is known for its unique clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered in many areas. Its beauty and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
