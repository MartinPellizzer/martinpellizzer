Carex pruinosa subsp. maximowiczii

Carex pruinosa subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that often has a silvery or grayish sheen due to the presence of fine, powdery hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
