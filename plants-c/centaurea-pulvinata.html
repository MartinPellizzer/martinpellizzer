Centaurea pulvinata

Centaurea pulvinata, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are upright with a tendency to branch towards the top.

Centaurea pulvinata is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, making it a notable presence in its native environments.
