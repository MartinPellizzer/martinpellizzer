Commiphora seineri

Commiphora seineri, commonly known as the African myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and aromatic resinous sap. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its resin, known as myrrh, has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense.

Commiphora seineri thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
