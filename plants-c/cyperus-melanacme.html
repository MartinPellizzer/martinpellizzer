Cyperus melanacme

Cyperus melanacme, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shoreline areas. Its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous small, greenish flowers.

Cyperus melanacme plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
