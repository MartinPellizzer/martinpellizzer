Crocus vernus var. propinquus

Crocus vernus var. propinquus, commonly known as the early crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit and ornamental value.

The variety is closely related to other crocus species and is sometimes used in horticultural breeding programs.
