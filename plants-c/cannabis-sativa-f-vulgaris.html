Cannabis sativa f. vulgaris

Cannabis sativa f. vulgaris, commonly known as hemp, is a variety of the cannabis plant characterized by its low tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) content and high cannabidiol (CBD) levels.

It is typically cultivated for industrial purposes such as fiber production, seed oil, and nutritional supplements. The plant features tall, slender stalks with broad, serrated leaves that are often used in textiles and paper manufacturing. Its flowering buds, while less potent than those of marijuana, are still utilized in some herbal remedies.

The botanical structure of Cannabis sativa f. vulgaris supports its versatility across agricultural and medicinal applications.
