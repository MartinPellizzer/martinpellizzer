Campanula munzurensis

Campanula munzurensis, commonly known as the Munzur bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
