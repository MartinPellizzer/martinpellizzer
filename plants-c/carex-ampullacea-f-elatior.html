Carex ampullacea f. elatior

Carex ampullacea f. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a distinctive plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
