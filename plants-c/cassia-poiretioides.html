Cassia poiretioides

Cassia poiretioides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are pinnately compound with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. Cassia poiretioides thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
