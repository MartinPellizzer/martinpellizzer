Carduus lobulatus

Carduus lobulatus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
