Curanga

Curanga, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of South and Central America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Curanga is also valued for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
