Clusia micrantha

Clusia micrantha, commonly known as the Mexican beauty leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a striking red or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia micrantha is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
