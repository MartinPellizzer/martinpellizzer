Cirsium daghestanicum

Cirsium daghestanicum, commonly known as the Daghestan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
