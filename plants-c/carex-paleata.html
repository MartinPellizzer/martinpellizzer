Carex paleata

Carex paleata, commonly known as pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pale green color.

Carex paleata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
