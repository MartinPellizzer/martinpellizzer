Croton bracteatus subsp. populifolius

Croton bracteatus subsp.

populifolius, commonly known as the Paper Mulberry or Poplar-leaved Croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the poplar tree, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
