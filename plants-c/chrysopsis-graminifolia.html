Chrysopsis graminifolia

Chrysopsis graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Chrysopsis graminifolia is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
