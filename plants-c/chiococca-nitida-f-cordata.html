Chiococca nitida f. cordata

Chiococca nitida f. cordata, commonly known as the cordate chiococca, is a variant of the chiococca species native to South America.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
