Cymbidium erythrostylum f. luteoalbum

Cymbidium erythrostylum f. luteoalbum, commonly known as the yellow-white cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its distinctive floral coloration.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery sheen. The flowers are notable for their unique combination of yellow and white hues, often with a red or pink throat. This orchid is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
