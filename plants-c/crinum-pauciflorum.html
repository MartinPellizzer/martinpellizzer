Crinum pauciflorum

Crinum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces its flowers during the summer months, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Crinum pauciflorum is also noted for its resilience and ability to naturalize over time.
