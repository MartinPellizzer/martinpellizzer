Cassia paposana

Cassia paposana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often a striking yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

Cassia paposana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
