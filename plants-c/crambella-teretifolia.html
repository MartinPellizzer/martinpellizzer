Crambella teretifolia

Crambella teretifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf crambella, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged alternately along its stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences at the tips of the branches.

Crambella teretifolia has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
