Croton paulinus

Croton paulinus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that range from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Croton paulinus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
