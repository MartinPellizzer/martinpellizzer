Cassiope tetragona var. saximontana

Cassiope tetragona var. saximontana, commonly known as the alpine Cassiope, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions.
