Ceradenia kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination. The plant has a short, stocky stem and produces few, large leaves that are thick and fleshy to store water in its arid habitat. Ceradenia kalbreyeri is known for its delicate beauty and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.
