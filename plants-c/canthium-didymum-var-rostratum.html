Canthium didymum var. rostratum

Canthium didymum var. rostratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowrie bean, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, smooth capsule, and the plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
