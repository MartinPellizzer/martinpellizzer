Campanula grandiflora

Campanula grandiflora, commonly known as the great bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of large, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
