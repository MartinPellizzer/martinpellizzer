Calothamnus pallidifolius

Calothamnus pallidifolius, commonly known as the pale-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, pale bloom. The plant produces striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Calothamnus pallidifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
