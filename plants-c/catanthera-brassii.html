Catanthera brassii

Catanthera brassii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Catanthera brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
