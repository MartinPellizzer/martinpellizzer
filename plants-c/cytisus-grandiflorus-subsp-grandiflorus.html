Cytisus grandiflorus subsp. grandiflorus

Cytisus grandiflorus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
