Cipura

Cipura, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Cipura is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
