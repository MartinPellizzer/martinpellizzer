Clitoria rotundifolia

Clitoria rotundifolia, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or purple petals, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant produces small, round green pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Clitoria rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
