Clematis mashanensis

Clematis mashanensis, commonly known as the Shandong Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of blue to violet petals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are usually serrated.

Clematis mashanensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
