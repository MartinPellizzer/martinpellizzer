Carex pluriflora

Carex pluriflora, commonly known as the many-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pluriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
