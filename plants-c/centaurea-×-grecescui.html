Centaurea × grecescui

Centaurea × grecescui, commonly known as the Greek cornflower, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea solstitialis.

It is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae, characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring full sun and well-drained soils. It is native to the Mediterranean region but has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.

Centaurea × grecescui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
