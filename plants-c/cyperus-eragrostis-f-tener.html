Cyperus eragrostis f. tener

Cyperus eragrostis f. tener, commonly known as the softleaf flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
