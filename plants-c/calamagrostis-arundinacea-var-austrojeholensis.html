Calamagrostis arundinacea var. austrojeholensis

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. austrojeholensis, commonly known as the Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that produce fluffy, silver-white plumes in late summer, giving it a distinctive ornamental appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
