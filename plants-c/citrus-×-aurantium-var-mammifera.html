Citrus × aurantium var. mammifera

Citrus × aurantium var. mammifera, commonly known as the grapefruit.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between pomelo and sweet orange. It is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and segmented, juicy flesh that ranges from pale yellow to deep red. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
