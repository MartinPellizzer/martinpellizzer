Chassalia boryana

Chassalia boryana, commonly known as the Boryan's chassalia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Chassalia boryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
