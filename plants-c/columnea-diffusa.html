Columnea diffusa

Columnea diffusa, commonly known as the trailing columnea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, often red or orange flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, velvety texture.

Columnea diffusa is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its attractive foliage and blooming habit.
