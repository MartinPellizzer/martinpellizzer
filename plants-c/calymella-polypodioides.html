Calymella polypodioides

Calymella polypodioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf water clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae, which includes various ferns and their allies. The plant is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in wetland environments.

Calymella polypodioides thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
