Convolvulus spectabilis

Convolvulus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a trailing perennial vine that produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit, often covering surfaces such as fences or trellises. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
