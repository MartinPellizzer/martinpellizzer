Carex cryptantha

Carex cryptantha, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in the summer, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex cryptantha is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
