Cyathocalyx magnifructus

Cyathocalyx magnifructus, commonly known as the grand fruit-bearing plant, is a striking species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cup-like structure, known as a cyathium. The plant produces clusters of small, clustered flowers that develop into fleshy, edible fruits. Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in humid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, nutrient-rich fruits.
