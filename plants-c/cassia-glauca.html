Cassia glauca

Cassia glauca, commonly known as the blue cassia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Cassia glauca is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
