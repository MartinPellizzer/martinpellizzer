Cacalia suaveolens

Cacalia suaveolens, commonly known as sweetweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, aromatic seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cacalia suaveolens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
