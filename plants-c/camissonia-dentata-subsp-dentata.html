Camissonia dentata subsp. dentata

Camissonia dentata subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the coastal beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and in open habitats. It has hairy leaves and produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to coastal environments with strong winds and salt spray.
