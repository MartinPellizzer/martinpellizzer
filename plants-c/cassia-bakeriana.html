Cassia bakeriana

Cassia bakeriana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Cassia bakeriana is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
