Curcuma stahlianthoides

Curcuma stahlianthoides, commonly known as the Stahli's turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and produces large, showy flowers with vivid yellow-orange petals. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture and a distinct, spicy aroma. It is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
