Croton chichenensis

Croton chichenensis, commonly known as the Chichenito or Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored leaves that display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
