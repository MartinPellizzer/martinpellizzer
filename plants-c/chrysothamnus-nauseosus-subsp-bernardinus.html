Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. bernardinus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

bernardinus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The subspecies bernardinus is distinguished by its more compact growth form and slightly different leaf structure compared to other subspecies.
