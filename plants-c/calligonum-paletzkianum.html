Calligonum paletzkianum

Calligonum paletzkianum, commonly known as the palezkian thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Calligonum paletzkianum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
