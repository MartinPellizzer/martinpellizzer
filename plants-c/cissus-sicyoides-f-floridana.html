Cissus sicyoides f. floridana

Cissus sicyoides f. floridana, commonly known as the Florida grape ivy, is a fast-growing, trailing vine native to Florida and parts of the Caribbean.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor spaces.
