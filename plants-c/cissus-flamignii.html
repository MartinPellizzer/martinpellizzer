Cissus flamignii

Cissus flamignii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of red, fleshy fruits. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Cissus flamignii is also known for its ability to climb using adventitious roots that form along its stems.
