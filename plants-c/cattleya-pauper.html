Cattleya pauper

Cattleya pauper, commonly known as the poor man's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its blooms are usually clustered in racemes and can last for several weeks.

Cattleya pauper is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
