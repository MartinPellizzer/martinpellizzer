Clusia coremandra

Clusia coremandra, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Clusia coremandra is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and moderate drought conditions.
