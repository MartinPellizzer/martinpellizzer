Corchorus depressus

Corchorus depressus, commonly known as the dwarf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Corchorus depressus is known for its fibrous roots, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fiber.
