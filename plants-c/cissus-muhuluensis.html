Cissus muhuluensis

Cissus muhuluensis, commonly known as the Muhulu Cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries that are often consumed by local communities. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
