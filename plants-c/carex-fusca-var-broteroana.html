Carex fusca var. broteroana

Carex fusca var. broteroana, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
