Chloraea rypaloglossa

Chloraea rypaloglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenhood, is an orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a terrestrial orchid, often found in the southern regions of Australia.

The species is known for its unique floral structure, which is adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.
