Chysis guimaraensis

Chysis guimaraensis, commonly known as the Guimaraes spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
