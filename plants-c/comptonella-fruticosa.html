Comptonella fruticosa

Comptonella fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub or the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Comptonella fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
