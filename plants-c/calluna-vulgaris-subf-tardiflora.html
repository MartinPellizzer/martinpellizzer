Calluna vulgaris subf. tardiflora

Calluna vulgaris subf.

tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering heather, is a subspecies of the common heather. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the British Isles.

This plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom later in the growing season. The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round foliage.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.
