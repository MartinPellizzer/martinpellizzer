Calysaccion longifolium

Calysaccion longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved calysaccion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
