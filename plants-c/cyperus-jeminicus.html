Cyperus jeminicus

Cyperus jeminicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

Cyperus jeminicus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
