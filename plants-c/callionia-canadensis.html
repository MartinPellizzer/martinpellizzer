Callionia canadensis

Callionia canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Callionia canadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
