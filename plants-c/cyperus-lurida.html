Cyperus lurida

Cyperus lurida, commonly known as the pale flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often reddish or purplish in color. Cyperus lurida is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
