Cyperus tacnensis var. weberbaueri

Cyperus tacnensis var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
