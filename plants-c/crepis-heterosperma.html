Crepis heterosperma

Crepis heterosperma, commonly known as the heterosperma mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It produces small, elongated achenes that are adapted for wind dispersal.
