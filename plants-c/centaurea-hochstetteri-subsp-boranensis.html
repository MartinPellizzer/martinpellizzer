Centaurea hochstetteri subsp. boranensis

Centaurea hochstetteri subsp.

boranensis, commonly known as the Boranense Scotch thistle, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and striking purple to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
