Cuphea setifera

Cuphea setifera, commonly known as the flame flower or whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea setifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
