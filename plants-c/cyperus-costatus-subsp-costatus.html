Cyperus costatus subsp. costatus

Cyperus costatus subsp.

costatus, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-ranked flowers. Cyperus costatus subsp.

costatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
