Carex karlongensis subsp. handelii

Carex karlongensis subsp.

handelii, commonly known as the Karlong sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
