Campanula umbrosa

Campanula umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, downy coating.

Campanula umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
