Chamaebuxus venenosa subvar. elliptica

Chamaebuxus venenosa subvar. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain toxic seeds.

The plant's unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
