Calamintha grandiflora f. oblongifolia

Calamintha grandiflora f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered calamint, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically oblong in shape, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and its aromatic properties.
