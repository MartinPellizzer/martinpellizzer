Cytisus lasiosemius f. sericeus

Cytisus lasiosemius f. sericeus, commonly known as the silky broom, is a botanical variant of the broom plant characterized by its finely hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, branched stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
