Camellia japonica var. sporrorthiana

Camellia japonica var. sporrorthiana, commonly known as the Sporrorthian camellia, is a botanical variant of the Japanese camellia characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This cultivar is noted for its large, showy flowers that typically exhibit a deep red to crimson hue with a contrasting white or pale pink base. The plant is an evergreen shrub native to Japan, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained, acidic soil. It produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

The Sporrorthian camellia is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
