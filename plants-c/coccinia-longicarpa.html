Coccinia longicarpa

Coccinia longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited ivy gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, orange to red fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture, while the fruit contains small, edible seeds.

Coccinia longicarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
