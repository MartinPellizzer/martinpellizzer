Conopodium marianum

Conopodium marianum, commonly known as the Marian's bladder, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its unique seed capsules.

Conopodium marianum is often found in shaded environments and is considered a member of the Apiaceae family.
