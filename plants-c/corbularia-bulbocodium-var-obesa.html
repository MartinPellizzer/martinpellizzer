Corbularia bulbocodium var. obesa

Corbularia bulbocodium var. obesa, commonly known as the fat bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous stem base and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
