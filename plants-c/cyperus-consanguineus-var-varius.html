Cyperus consanguineus var. varius

Cyperus consanguineus var. varius, commonly known as the variable flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage, often growing in wet or marshy environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The variety 'varius' is distinguished by its variable leaf morphology and flowering structures, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
