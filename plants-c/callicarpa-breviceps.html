Callicarpa breviceps

Callicarpa breviceps, commonly known as the short-headed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in catkins and are usually greenish-white in color.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
