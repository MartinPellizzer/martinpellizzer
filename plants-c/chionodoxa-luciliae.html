Chionodoxa luciliae

Chionodoxa luciliae, commonly known as the Persian glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It produces clusters of delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its early blooming habit makes it a valuable addition to gardens seeking to extend the spring flowering season.
