Ceropegia lopanthera

Ceropegia lopanthera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, pale greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia lopanthera is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
