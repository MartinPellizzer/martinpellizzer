Calea kunhardtii

Calea kunhardtii, commonly known as the "flower of the evening," is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn, a unique circadian behavior. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming properties.

Calea kunhardtii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
