Copaifera malmei

Copaifera malmei, commonly known as the yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, aromatic capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in medicine and perfumery.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife in its native habitats.
