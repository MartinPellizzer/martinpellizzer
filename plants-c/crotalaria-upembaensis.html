Crotalaria upembaensis

Crotalaria upembaensis, commonly known as the Upemba crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its ecological persistence.

Crotalaria upembaensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil ecosystem.
