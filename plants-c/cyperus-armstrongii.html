Cyperus armstrongii

Cyperus armstrongii, commonly known as the Armstrong's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas or along water bodies. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in three rows, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Cyperus armstrongii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
