Cassia lenitiva

Cassia lenitiva, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia lenitiva is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
