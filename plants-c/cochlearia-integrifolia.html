Cochlearia integrifolia

Cochlearia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it its species name "integrifolia.". It produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and limestone habitats.
