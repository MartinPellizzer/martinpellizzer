Cervaria angustifolia

Cervaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cervaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-yellow and appear in late spring.

Cervaria angustifolia is noted for its ecological value, providing habitat and nectar for various insect species.
