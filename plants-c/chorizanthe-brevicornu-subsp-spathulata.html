Chorizanthe brevicornu subsp. spathulata

Chorizanthe brevicornu subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate to spatulate leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
