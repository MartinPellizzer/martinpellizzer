Cyrtopodium falcilobum

Cyrtopodium falcilobum, commonly known as the sickle-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Cyrtopodium falcilobum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
