Calea scabrifolia

Calea scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Calea scabrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
