Cassia biflora var. rostrata

Cassia biflora var. rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in dry woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
