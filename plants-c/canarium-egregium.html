Canarium egregium

Canarium egregium, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits resembling apples. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a sticky, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of latex.
