Cyperus michoacanensis

Cyperus michoacanensis, commonly known as the Michoacan flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
