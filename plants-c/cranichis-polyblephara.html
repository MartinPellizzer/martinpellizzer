Cranichis polyblephara

Cranichis polyblephara, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
