Chaloupkaea chrysantha subsp. chrysantha

Chaloupkaea chrysantha subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden chaloupka, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along its stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
