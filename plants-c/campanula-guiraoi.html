Campanula guiraoi

Campanula guiraoi, commonly known as the Guirao's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula guiraoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
