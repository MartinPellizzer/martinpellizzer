Coleosanthus albicaulis

Coleosanthus albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes. Its flowers are typically visited by insects, contributing to pollination.

The species is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic inundation, making it a hardy coastal plant.
