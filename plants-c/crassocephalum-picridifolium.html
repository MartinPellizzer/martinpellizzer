Crassocephalum picridifolium

Crassocephalum picridifolium, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with a distinctive purple center. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
