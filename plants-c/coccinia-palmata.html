Coccinia palmata

Coccinia palmata, commonly known as the palmate false cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a palmate venation pattern, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a round, green berry that turns orange when mature, often used in traditional medicine.
