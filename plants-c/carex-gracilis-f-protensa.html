Carex gracilis f. protensa

Carex gracilis f. protensa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This sedges are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
