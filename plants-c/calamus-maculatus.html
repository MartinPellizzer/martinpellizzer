Calamus maculatus

Calamus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus maculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
