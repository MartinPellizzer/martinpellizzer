Callitriche minima

Callitriche minima, commonly known as lesser waterstar, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds, streams, and ditches, where it can float or root in the substrate. The plant has tiny, star-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in diameter, arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

Callitriche minima is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and aquatic organisms.
