Coronilla iberica

Coronilla iberica, commonly known as Spanish Crownvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Coronilla iberica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
