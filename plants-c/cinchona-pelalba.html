Cinchona pelalba

Cinchona pelalba, commonly known as white quinine tree, is a species of the Rubiaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that contains alkaloids, including quinine, which has historical significance in medicine. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, white flowers followed by orange-red berries.

The plant's ecological importance and medicinal properties make it a notable species in tropical regions.
