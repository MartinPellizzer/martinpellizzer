Cedrus libanitica subsp. stenocoma

Cedrus libanitica subsp.

stenocoma, commonly known as the narrowcone cedar, is a subspecies of the cedar tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and conical shape, with needle-like leaves that are shorter and more clustered compared to other cedar species.

The cones of this subspecies are typically smaller and more elongated, with a distinctive narrow, pointed shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with a temperate climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscaping.
