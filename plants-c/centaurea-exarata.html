Centaurea exarata

Centaurea exarata, commonly known as the purple globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in dense, globe-like clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Centaurea exarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
