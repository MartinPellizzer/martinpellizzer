Cassia ochnacea var. mollis

Cassia ochnacea var. mollis, commonly known as silky cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, ovate leaves that give it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
