Cyathea andaquiensis

Cyathea andaquiensis, commonly known as the Andean tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the plant's structure and helps in nutrient absorption from the soil.

Cyathea andaquiensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
