Chamaecrista portoricensis

Chamaecrista portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dry, open habitats.

Chamaecrista portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
