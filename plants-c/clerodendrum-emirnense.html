Clerodendrum emirnense

Clerodendrum emirnense, commonly known as the Persian glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Iranian medicine.
