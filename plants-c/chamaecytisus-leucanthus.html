Chamaecytisus leucanthus

Chamaecytisus leucanthus, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
