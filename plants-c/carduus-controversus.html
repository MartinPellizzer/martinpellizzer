Carduus controversus

Carduus controversus, commonly known as the thistleweed or the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Carduus controversus typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
