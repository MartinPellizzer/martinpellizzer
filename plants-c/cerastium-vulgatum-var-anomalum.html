Cerastium vulgatum var. anomalum

Cerastium vulgatum var. anomalum, commonly known as the variable mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin, and are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
