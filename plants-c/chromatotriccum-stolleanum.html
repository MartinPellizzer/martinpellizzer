Chromatotriccum stolleanum

Chromatotriccum stolleanum, commonly known as the creeping chlorophyllous alga, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure and the ability to produce chlorophyll, allowing it to photosynthesize efficiently. The plant forms dense mats on the bottom of lakes and ponds, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments. Its reproductive structures are specialized, enabling it to propagate through both sexual and asexual means.

Due to its ecological role, it plays a vital part in maintaining the balance of aquatic ecosystems.
