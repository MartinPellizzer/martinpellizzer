Cucumis melo var. cultus

Cucumis melo var. cultus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variant of the cucumber plant characterized by its elongated, thin fruit with a smooth, pale green skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central and South Asia. The plant typically grows on a trailing or climbing vine, producing small, yellow flowers that give way to the edible fruit.

Its botanical features include a fibrous, crisp texture and a mild, slightly sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption and culinary use.
