Cirsium × ispolatovii

Cirsium × ispolatovii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its morphology combines features of its parent species, Cirsium arvense and Cirsium younghusbandii, resulting in a unique appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
