Crescentia linearifolia

Crescentia linearifolia, commonly known as the Caribbean apple or mamey sapote, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit with a sweet, aromatic pulp and a single large seed. Crescentia linearifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Bombacaceae family.
