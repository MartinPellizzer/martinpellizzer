Convolvulus circinnatus

Convolvulus circinnatus, commonly known as the spiral bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has slender, climbing stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and hairy. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it has adapted to thrive in arid environments.
