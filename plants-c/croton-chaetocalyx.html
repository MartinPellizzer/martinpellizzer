Croton chaetocalyx

Croton chaetocalyx, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
