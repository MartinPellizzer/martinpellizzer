Cyathea caduca

Cyathea caduca, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a long, fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Cyathea caduca plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
