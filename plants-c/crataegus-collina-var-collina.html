Crataegus collina var. collina

Crataegus collina var. collina, commonly known as the sand hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with serrated edges, and the plant produces thorned stems, which are characteristic of many hawthorn species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
