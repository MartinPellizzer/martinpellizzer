Carex paupera

Carex paupera, commonly known as slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters in the summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex paupera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
