Chenopodium acutifolium var. dentatum

Chenopodium acutifolium var. dentatum, commonly known as sharpleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a dry, membranous fruit. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
