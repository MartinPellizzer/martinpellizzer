Campanula galushkoi

Campanula galushkoi, commonly known as the Galushko's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
