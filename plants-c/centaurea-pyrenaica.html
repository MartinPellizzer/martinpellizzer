Centaurea pyrenaica

Centaurea pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea pyrenaica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
