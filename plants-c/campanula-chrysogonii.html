Campanula chrysogonii

Campanula chrysogonii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are typically pale yellow to golden in color. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
