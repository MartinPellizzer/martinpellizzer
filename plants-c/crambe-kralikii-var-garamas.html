Crambe kralikii var. garamas

Crambe kralikii var. garamas, commonly known as the Garamas crambe, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
