Colubrina glomerata

Colubrina glomerata, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by narrow, elongated fruits that contain several seeds.

Colubrina glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
