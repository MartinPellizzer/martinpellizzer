Clematis integrifolia var. normalis

Clematis integrifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the common clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval, serrated leaflets that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or walls.
