Carex eminens

Carex eminens, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

Carex eminens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland environments.
