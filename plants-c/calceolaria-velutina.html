Calceolaria velutina

Calceolaria velutina, commonly known as the velvet slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often violet or purple in color. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that are adapted for specific pollinators, such as bees and flies. Calceolaria velutina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.
