Chrysophyllum beardii

Chrysophyllum beardii, commonly known as the African cherry or red cherry tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, edible pulp containing a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
