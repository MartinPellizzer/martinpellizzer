Codiaeum grande

Codiaeum grande, commonly known as the flame plant or croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated leaves that display shades of red, orange, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Codiaeum grande is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
