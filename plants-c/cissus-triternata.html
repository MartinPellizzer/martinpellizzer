Cissus triternata

Cissus triternata, commonly known as the three-parted devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. Cissus triternata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and unique morphology make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
