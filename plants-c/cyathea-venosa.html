Cyathea venosa

Cyathea venosa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety spores, giving the plant its common name. Cyathea venosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
