Colubrina retusa

Colubrina retusa, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink to reddish in color and bloom in the spring.

Colubrina retusa is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in coastal areas.
