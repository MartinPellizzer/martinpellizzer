Cordyline regina

Cordyline regina, commonly known as the Queen's candle, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The leaves are typically a vibrant blue-green, with a waxy texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the plant, followed by dark blue berries.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
