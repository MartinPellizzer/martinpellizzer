Calopogon pallidus f. albiflorus

Calopogon pallidus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bog orchid, is a rare and striking variant of the pale pink bog orchid.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers, which stand out against its grass-like foliage. This plant is typically found in wetland habitats, such as bogs and marshes, where it thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of a single spike of flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
