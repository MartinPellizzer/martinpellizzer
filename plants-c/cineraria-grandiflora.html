Cineraria grandiflora

Cineraria grandiflora, commonly known as the giant cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces large, showy blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
