Campanula rupestris subsp. anchusiflora

Campanula rupestris subsp.

anchusiflora, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for alpine and Mediterranean gardens.
