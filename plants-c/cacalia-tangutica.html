Cacalia tangutica

Cacalia tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets that give it a bell-shaped appearance.

Cacalia tangutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
