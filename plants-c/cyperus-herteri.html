Cyperus herteri

Cyperus herteri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus herteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
