Cracca dregeana

Cracca dregeana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. Cracca dregeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and flowers that range from white to purple in color.
