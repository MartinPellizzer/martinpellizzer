Corydalis blanda subsp. olympica

Corydalis blanda subsp.

olympica, commonly known as Olympic corydalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It produces delicate, pinkish-purple flowers with two upper petals and two lower, spurred petals, typical of the Fumariaceae family.

The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
