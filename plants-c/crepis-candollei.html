Crepis candollei

Crepis candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Crepis candollei is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
