Connarus subinaequifolius var. sericeus

Connarus subinaequifolius var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky connarus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The leaves are usually opposite, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within lowland forests.
