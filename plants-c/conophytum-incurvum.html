Conophytum incurvum

Conophytum incurvum, commonly known as the "Succulent Stone Plant," is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum incurvum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
