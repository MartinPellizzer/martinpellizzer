Chromolaena geranifolia

Chromolaena geranifolia, commonly known as false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic and have a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields due to its adaptability.

Chromolaena geranifolia is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
