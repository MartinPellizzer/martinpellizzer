Cerastium decalvans var. orbelicum

Cerastium decalvans var. orbelicum, commonly known as the Orbelicum mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
