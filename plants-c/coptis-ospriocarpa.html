Coptis ospriocarpa

Coptis ospriocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of alkaloids in its roots.
