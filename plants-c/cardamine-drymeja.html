Cardamine drymeja

Cardamine drymeja, commonly known as the drymeja watercress, is a small perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, rounded leaflets, and it produces small white or greenish flowers.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
