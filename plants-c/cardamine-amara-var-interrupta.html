Cardamine amara var. interrupta

Cardamine amara var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted bittercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The species is noted for its edible leaves, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
