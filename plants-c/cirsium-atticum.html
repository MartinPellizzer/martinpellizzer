Cirsium atticum

Cirsium atticum, commonly known as the Attic thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flowers and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cirsium atticum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
