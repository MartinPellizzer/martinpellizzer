Cola bruneelii

Cola bruneelii, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody pods containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
