Cyperus sanguinolentus

Cyperus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the red spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Cyperus sanguinolentus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
