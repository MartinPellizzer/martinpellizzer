Coix gigantea

Coix gigantea, commonly known as giant sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies.

Coix gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
