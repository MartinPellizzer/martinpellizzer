Cassia transversali-seminata

Cassia transversali-seminata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Cassia transversali-seminata is also valued for its role in agroforestry and soil stabilization in degraded lands.
