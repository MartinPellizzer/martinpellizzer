Clerodendrum trichotomum var. tomentosum

Clerodendrum trichotomum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a textured surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
