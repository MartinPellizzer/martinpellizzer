Chamaecyparis pisifera plumosa-argenteovariegata

Chamaecyparis pisifera plumosa-argenteovariegata, commonly known as the silver fronded Japanese cypress, is a cultivar of the Chamaecyparis genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its feathery, needle-like foliage that displays a striking silver-white variegation. The plant has a compact, conical shape with a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring minimal maintenance.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique coloration and year-round visual appeal.
