Crataegus orientalis var. sanguinea

Crataegus orientalis var. sanguinea, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
