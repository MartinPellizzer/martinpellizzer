Carex tenuissima

Carex tenuissima, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in water features and bog gardens.

Carex tenuissima is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
