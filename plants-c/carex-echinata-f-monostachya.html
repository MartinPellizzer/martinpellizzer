Carex echinata f. monostachya

Carex echinata f. monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a variant of the broad-leafed sedge Carex echinata.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a single spike.

This form is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
