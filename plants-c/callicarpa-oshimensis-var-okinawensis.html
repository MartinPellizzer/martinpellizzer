Callicarpa oshimensis var. okinawensis

Callicarpa oshimensis var. okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
