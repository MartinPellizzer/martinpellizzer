Cyamopsis psoraloides

Cyamopsis psoraloides, commonly known as the sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny, ball-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Cyamopsis psoraloides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for certain wildlife species.
