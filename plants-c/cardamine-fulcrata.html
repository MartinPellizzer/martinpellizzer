Cardamine fulcrata

Cardamine fulcrata, commonly known as the broad-leafed watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive midrib and a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

Cardamine fulcrata is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
