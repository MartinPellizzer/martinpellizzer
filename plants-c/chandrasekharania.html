Chandrasekharania

Chandrasekharania, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds. Chandrasekharania is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
