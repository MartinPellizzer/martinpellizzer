Crataegus monogyna var. maura

Crataegus monogyna var. maura, commonly known as the Maura hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
