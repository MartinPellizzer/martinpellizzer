Carthamus duvauxii

Carthamus duvauxii, commonly known as the Duvau's safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Carthamus duvauxii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
