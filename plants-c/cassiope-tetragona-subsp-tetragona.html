Cassiope tetragona subsp. tetragona

Cassiope tetragona subsp.

tetragona, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the rocky alpine regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette pattern, providing a compact and frost-resistant structure. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in subalpine and alpine meadows. Cassiope tetragona subsp.

tetragona is a hardy plant that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in harsh environments.
