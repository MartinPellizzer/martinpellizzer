Cydonia vulgaris silvestris

Cydonia vulgaris silvestris, commonly known as the wild pear, is a species of tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, which resembles a pear, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, sometimes tart flavor. The plant is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its fruit has been used historically for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
