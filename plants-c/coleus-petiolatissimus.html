Coleus petiolatissimus

Coleus petiolatissimus, commonly known as the "creeping coleus," is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from deep reds to bright oranges and yellows. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and are often variegated with contrasting patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
