Calanthe warpuri

Calanthe warpuri, commonly known as the Warburton's calanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom during the spring and early summer months.

Calanthe warpuri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
