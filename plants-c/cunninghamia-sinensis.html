Cunninghamia sinensis

Cunninghamia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
