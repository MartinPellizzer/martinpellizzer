Chusua nana

Chusua nana, commonly known as the dwarf chusquea, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered at the base of the plant, and it produces light, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chusua nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
