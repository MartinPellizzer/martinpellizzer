Clerodendrum johnstonii subsp. johnstonii

Clerodendrum johnstonii subsp.

johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that often resemble petals, which serve to attract pollinators.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish-white in color, surrounded by brightly colored bracts.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
