Coryphantha similis

Coryphantha similis, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Coryphantha similis thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
