Cryptocarya minutifolia

Cryptocarya minutifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often fragrant.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns dark when mature, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
