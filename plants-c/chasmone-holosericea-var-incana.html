Chasmone holosericea var. incana

Chasmone holosericea var. incana, commonly known as the woolly chasmone, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
