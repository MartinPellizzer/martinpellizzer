Callicarpa roigii

Callicarpa roigii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, which are edible for birds and small mammals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Callicarpa roigii is also valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
