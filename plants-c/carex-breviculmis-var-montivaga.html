Carex breviculmis var. montivaga

Carex breviculmis var. montivaga, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a spiky inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane or subalpine habitats.

Carex breviculmis var. montivaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in wetland and streamside environments.
