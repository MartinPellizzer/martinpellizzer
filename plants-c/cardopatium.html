Cardopatium

Cardopatium, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pale-colored flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and arranged in a unique opposite or whorled pattern. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Cardopatium is native to regions of South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
