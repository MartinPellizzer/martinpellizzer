Capparis alexandrae

Capparis alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra's caper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Capparis alexandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
