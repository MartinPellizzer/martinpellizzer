Correa lawrenciana var. rosea

Correa lawrenciana var. rosea, commonly known as the pink correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
