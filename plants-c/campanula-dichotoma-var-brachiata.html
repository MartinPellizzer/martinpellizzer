Campanula dichotoma var. brachiata

Campanula dichotoma var. brachiata, commonly known as the branched bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. This perennial species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
