Cassia frutescens

Cassia frutescens, commonly known as flame tree or torchwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red inflorescences, which resemble flames and bloom during the dry season. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a feathery appearance.

Cassia frutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
