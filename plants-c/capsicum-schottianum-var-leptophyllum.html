Capsicum schottianum var. leptophyllum

Capsicum schottianum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender-fruited chili pepper, is a variant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that typically range in color from yellow to red when mature. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, typical of many Capsicum species. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and are borne in the axils of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often used in traditional cuisines.
