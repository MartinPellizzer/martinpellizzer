Cymbopogon transvaalensis

Cymbopogon transvaalensis, commonly known as Transvaal grass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Cymbopogon transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
