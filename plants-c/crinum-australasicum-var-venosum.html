Crinum australasicum var. venosum

Crinum australasicum var. venosum, commonly known as the Vein-veined Crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that are often striped with contrasting colors, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in summer. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and resilience in tropical and subtropical climates.
