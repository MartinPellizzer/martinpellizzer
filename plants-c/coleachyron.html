Coleachyron

Coleachyron, commonly known as the giant water flea, is a type of aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant has a unique ability to absorb nutrients directly from the water, which contributes to its rapid growth. Coleachyron is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence can indicate poor water quality due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
