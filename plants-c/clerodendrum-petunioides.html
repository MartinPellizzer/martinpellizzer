Clerodendrum petunioides

Clerodendrum petunioides, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clerodendrum petunioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
