Carex curvula f. rodnensis

Carex curvula f. rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnian sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge native to eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
