Chorisia integrifolia

Chorisia integrifolia, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and large, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have smooth margins, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its showy blooms and unique foliage.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
