Carex × fulva var. intermedia

Carex × fulva var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in wet or damp soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
