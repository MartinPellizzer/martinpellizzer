Crocus clusii var. xanthostylus

Crocus clusii var. xanthostylus, commonly known as the yellow-styled crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, fragrant flowers with purple petals and a distinctive yellow stamen that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm and blooms in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
