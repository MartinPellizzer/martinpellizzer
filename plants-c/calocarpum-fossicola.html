Calocarpum fossicola

Calocarpum fossicola, commonly known as the rock calocarpum, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in rocky and limestone habitats.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth form and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to nutrient-poor, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for ecological and conservation studies.
