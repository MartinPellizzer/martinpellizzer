Ceratocarpus arenarius subsp. utriculosus

Ceratocarpus arenarius subsp.

utriculosus, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and other dry, open habitats.
