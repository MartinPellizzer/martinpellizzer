Calophysa testiculata

Calophysa testiculata, commonly known as the testicle cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, rounded, and often clustered stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Calophysa testiculata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
