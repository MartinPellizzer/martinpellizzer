Clematis mauritiana var. humilis

Clematis mauritiana var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf clematis, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, palmately divided leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a climbing plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
