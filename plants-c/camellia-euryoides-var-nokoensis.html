Camellia euryoides var. nokoensis

Camellia euryoides var. nokoensis, commonly known as the Noko camellia, is a species native to Japan, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Honshu.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, persistent leaves, and a preference for well-drained, acidic soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
