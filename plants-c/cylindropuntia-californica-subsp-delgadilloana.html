Cylindropuntia californica subsp. delgadilloana

Cylindropuntia californica subsp.

delgadilloana, commonly known as the Delgadillo prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to southern California and Baja California. It features cylindrical, spiny stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping.
