Carex paleacea var. transatlantica

Carex paleacea var. transatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow-green flowers. This variety thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of maritime ecosystems.
