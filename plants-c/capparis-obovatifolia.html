Capparis obovatifolia

Capparis obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and often thorny.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.
