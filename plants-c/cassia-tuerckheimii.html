Cassia tuerckheimii

Cassia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its bright golden flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Cassia tuerckheimii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in gardens and public landscapes.
