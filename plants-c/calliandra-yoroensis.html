Calliandra yoroensis

Calliandra yoroensis, commonly known as the Yoroensis calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, colorful flowers that range from pink to red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance.

Calliandra yoroensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
