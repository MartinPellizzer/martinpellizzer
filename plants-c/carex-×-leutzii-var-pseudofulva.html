Carex × leutzii var. pseudofulva

Carex × leutzii var. pseudofulva, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. This variety is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green foliage, which distinguishes it from other Carex species. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.
