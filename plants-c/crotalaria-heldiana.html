Crotalaria heldiana

Crotalaria heldiana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive rattle-like structure at the base of each bloom. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and palmate leaves, which are typically divided into three to five leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Crotalaria heldiana plays a role in ecological systems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity.
