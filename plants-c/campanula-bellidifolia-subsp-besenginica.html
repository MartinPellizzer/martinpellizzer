Campanula bellidifolia subsp. besenginica

Campanula bellidifolia subsp.

besenginica, commonly known as the Besengin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in subalpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
