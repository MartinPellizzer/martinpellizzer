Cissus wilmsii

Cissus wilmsii, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from red to black as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cissus wilmsii is also valued for its ability to grow on various surfaces, making it a versatile choice for green walls and trellises.
