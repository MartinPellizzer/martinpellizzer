Carex nitidiutriculata

Carex nitidiutriculata, commonly known as the glossy-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, glossy green leaves that are often arranged in tight, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically emerging from the base of the plant in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

Carex nitidiutriculata is valued in wetland restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
