Capsella perconcava

Capsella perconcava, commonly known as the concave weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases small seeds when mature.

Capsella perconcava is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various insects.
