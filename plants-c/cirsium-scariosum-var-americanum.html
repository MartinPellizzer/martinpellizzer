Cirsium scariosum var. americanum

Cirsium scariosum var. americanum, commonly known as the American thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is considered a noxious weed in many regions due to its aggressive growth and ability to invade various habitats.
