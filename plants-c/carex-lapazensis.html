Carex lapazensis

Carex lapazensis, commonly known as the Lapaz sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Carex lapazensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
