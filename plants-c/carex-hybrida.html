Carex hybrida

Carex hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It is often found in temperate regions and can thrive in partial to full shade.

Carex hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
