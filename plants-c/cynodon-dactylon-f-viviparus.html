Cynodon dactylon f. viviparus

Cynodon dactylon f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous Bermuda grass, is a variant of the common Bermuda grass characterized by its unique reproductive feature.

This form exhibits vivipary, where the seedlings develop while still attached to the parent plant, allowing for rapid propagation. Botanically, it is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a dense, mat-forming growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for erosion control and as a forage crop.
