Cirsium douglasii

Cirsium douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, prickly edges, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium douglasii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
