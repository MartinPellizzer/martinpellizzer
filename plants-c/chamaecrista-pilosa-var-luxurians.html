Chamaecrista pilosa var. luxurians

Chamaecrista pilosa var. luxurians, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
