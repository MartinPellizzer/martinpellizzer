Cryptocarya flavescens

Cryptocarya flavescens, commonly known as the golden ash or yellow cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and camphor trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

Cryptocarya flavescens is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
