Catasetum gardneri

Catasetum gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, featuring a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Catasetum gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
