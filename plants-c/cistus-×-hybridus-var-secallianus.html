Cistus × hybridus var. secallianus

Cistus × hybridus var. secallianus, commonly known as the Greek rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
