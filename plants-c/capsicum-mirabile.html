Capsicum mirabile

Capsicum mirabile, commonly known as the miracle pepper, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful fruits that range from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, white flowers that develop into the edible fruits.

Capsicum mirabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in certain regional cuisines.
