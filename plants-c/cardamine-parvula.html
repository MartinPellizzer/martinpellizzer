Cardamine parvula

Cardamine parvula, commonly known as smallleaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories. The plant has slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. Its tiny, white flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Cardamine parvula is often found in calcareous soils and is a member of the Brassicaceae family.
