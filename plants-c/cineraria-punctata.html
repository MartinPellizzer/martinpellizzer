Cineraria punctata

Cineraria punctata, commonly known as the dotted cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
