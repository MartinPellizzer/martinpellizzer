Clinopodium nepeta subsp. subisodontum

Clinopodium nepeta subsp.

subisodontum, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It is known for its strong aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its characteristic scent and effects on animals.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Nepeta genus and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
