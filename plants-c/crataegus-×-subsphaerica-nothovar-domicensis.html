Crataegus × subsphaerica nothovar. domicensis

Crataegus × subsphaerica nothovar. domicensis, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It typically features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant bears red to orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in different populations.
