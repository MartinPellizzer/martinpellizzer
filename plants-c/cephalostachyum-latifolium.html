Cephalostachyum latifolium

Cephalostachyum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pinnaclad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and therapeutic purposes.
