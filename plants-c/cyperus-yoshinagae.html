Cyperus yoshinagae

Cyperus yoshinagae, commonly known as Yoshinaga's flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or moist soil.

Cyperus yoshinagae is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
