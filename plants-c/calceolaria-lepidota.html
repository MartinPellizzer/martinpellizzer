Calceolaria lepidota

Calceolaria lepidota, commonly known as the velvet leaf calceolaria, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Calceolaria lepidota is known for its delicate appearance and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with partial shade.
