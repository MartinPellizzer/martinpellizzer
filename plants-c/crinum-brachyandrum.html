Crinum brachyandrum

Crinum brachyandrum, commonly known as the short-stalked crinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. It is typically found in wetlands or along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Crinum brachyandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in tropical and subtropical climates.
