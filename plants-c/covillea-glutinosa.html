Covillea glutinosa

Covillea glutinosa, commonly known as sticky covillea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Covillea glutinosa produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.
