Carex oligosperma var. tsuishikarensis

Carex oligosperma var. tsuishikarensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
