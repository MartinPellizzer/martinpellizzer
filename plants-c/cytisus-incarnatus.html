Cytisus incarnatus

Cytisus incarnatus, commonly known as the pink brier or scarlet broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus incarnatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
