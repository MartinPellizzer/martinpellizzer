Clematis aspleniifolia

Clematis aspleniifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound, with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
