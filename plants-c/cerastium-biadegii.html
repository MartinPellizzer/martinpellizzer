Cerastium biadegii

Cerastium biadegii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit, with small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
