Castanospermum cunninghamii

Castanospermum cunninghamii, commonly known as the Moreton Bay chestnut, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The tree produces distinctive yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several large, almond-shaped seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Indigenous Australian culture.
