Cinnamomum griffithii

Cinnamomum griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum griffithii, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped berries. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The tree prefers tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
