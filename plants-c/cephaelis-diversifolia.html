Cephaelis diversifolia

Cephaelis diversifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
