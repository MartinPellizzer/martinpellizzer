Crotalaria ochroleuca

Crotalaria ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a crown-like structure. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are pinnate, and the pods are elongated, containing several seeds.

Crotalaria ochroleuca is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
