Cassebeeria cordifolia

Cassebeeria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The seeds of Casssebeeria cordifolia are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.
