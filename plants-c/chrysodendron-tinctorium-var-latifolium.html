Chrysodendron tinctorium var. latifolium

Chrysodendron tinctorium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its rapid growth rate and adaptability make it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
