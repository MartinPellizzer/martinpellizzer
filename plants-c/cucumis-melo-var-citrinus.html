Cucumis melo var. citrinus

Cucumis melo var. citrinus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellow to orange rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is prized for its flavor and texture.
