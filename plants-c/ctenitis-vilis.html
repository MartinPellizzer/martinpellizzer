Ctenitis vilis

Ctenitis vilis, commonly known as the common rock fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and delicate fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a lanceolate shape and finely serrated margins.

Ctenitis vilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
