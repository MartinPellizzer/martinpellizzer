Croton glandulosus subsp. hirtus

Croton glandulosus subsp.

hirtus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in certain areas.
