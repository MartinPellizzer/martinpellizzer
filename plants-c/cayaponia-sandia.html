Cayaponia sandia

Cayaponia sandia, commonly known as the dragon fruit vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and distinctive, colorful flowers. The plant produces fruit that is typically red or yellow with a bright, scaly exterior. Its leaves are broad and paddle-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil.

Cayaponia sandia is cultivated for its ornamental value and the edible fruit it yields.
