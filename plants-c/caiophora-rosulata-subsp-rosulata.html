Caiophora rosulata subsp.

rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Caiophora, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
