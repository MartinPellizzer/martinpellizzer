Clinopodium villosum

Clinopodium villosum, commonly known as hairy woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, pale blue to violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium villosum is known for its aromatic leaves, which have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
