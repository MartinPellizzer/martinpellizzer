Convolvulus oxysepalus subsp. oxysepalus

Convolvulus oxysepalus subsp.

oxysepalus, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial with trailing stems that produce trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It blooms throughout the warmer months, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and erosion control.
