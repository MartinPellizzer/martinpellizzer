Crocion pubescens

Crocion pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, often violet or blue flowers. Native to southern Africa, this species thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, adding color to its natural habitat.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
