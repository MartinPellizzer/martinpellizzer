Canna latifolia

Canna latifolia, commonly known as the giant cane or Indian shot, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by vibrant, colorful bracts. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near water bodies. It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Canna latifolia is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
