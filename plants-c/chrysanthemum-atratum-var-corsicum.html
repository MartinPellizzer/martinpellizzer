Chrysanthemum atratum var. corsicum

Chrysanthemum atratum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, deeply lobed leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
