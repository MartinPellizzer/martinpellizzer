Crotalaria fenestrata

Crotalaria fenestrata, commonly known as the windowed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fenestrated (window-like) leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used as a food source in some parts of the world.

Crotalaria fenestrata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
