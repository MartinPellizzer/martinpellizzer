Chamaechaenactis scaposa

Chamaechaenactis scaposa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower that blooms in the spring, followed by a crown of white pappus-like bracts that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
