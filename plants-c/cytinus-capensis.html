Cytinus capensis

Cytinus capensis, commonly known as the Cape cress, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, flat, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is notable for its ability to form a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Cytinus capensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
