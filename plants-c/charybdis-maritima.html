Charybdis maritima

Charybdis maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and silvery-green, while its flowers range from blue to purple.

Charybdis maritima is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought-resistant properties.
