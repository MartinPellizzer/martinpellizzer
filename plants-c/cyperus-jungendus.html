Cyperus jungendus

Cyperus jungendus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Cyperus jungendus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist environments.
