Calamintha officinalis var. vulgaris

Calamintha officinalis var. vulgaris, commonly known as caraway, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval fruits called mericarps, which contain the essential oil responsible for its distinctive scent. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Calamintha officinalis var. vulgaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
