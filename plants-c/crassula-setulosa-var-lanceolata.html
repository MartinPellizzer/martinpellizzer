Crassula setulosa var. lanceolata

Crassula setulosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Lance-leaved Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal watering.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
