Cirsium × gonzaloi

Cirsium × gonzaloi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
