Capparis multiflora

Capparis multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has woody stems and hairy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
