Cordiera vaccinioides

Cordiera vaccinioides, commonly known as the red-flowered cordiera, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish hues, especially in winter. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscape design in suitable climates.
