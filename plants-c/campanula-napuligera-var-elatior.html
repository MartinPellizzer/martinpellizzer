Campanula napuligera var. elatior

Campanula napuligera var. elatior, commonly known as the greater bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sometimes branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
