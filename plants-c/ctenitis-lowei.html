Ctenitis lowei

Ctenitis lowei, commonly known as the lowei's brake, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments with a serrated edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Ctenitis lowei is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.
