Carex similis

Carex similis, commonly known as the similar sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in temperate regions.

Carex similis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
