Coryphantha radians var. sulcata

Coryphantha radians var. sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcatas or the "Cactus of the Sun," is a species of cactus native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow quite large, often reaching several feet in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its hardiness and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
