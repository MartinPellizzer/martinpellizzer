Ceropegia anceps

Ceropegia anceps, commonly known as the "Beaded String of Hearts," is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, bead-like tubercles along its stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia anceps is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
