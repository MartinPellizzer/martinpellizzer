Cleistocactus crassicaulis

Cleistocactus crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, a trait typical of many night-blooming cacti. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.

Cleistocactus crassicaulis is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
