Carduus thracicus

Carduus thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
