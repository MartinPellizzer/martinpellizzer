Copiapoa cuprea

Copiapoa cuprea, commonly known as the copper cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that turns reddish-brown with age, giving it its common name. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to extreme aridity, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Copiapoa cuprea is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
