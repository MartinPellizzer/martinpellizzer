Chaetostoma gardneri

Chaetostoma gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's plecostomus, is a type of armored catfish native to the rivers of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated body and the presence of bony plates covering its skin, which provide protection. The species is typically found in slow-moving or stagnant waters with muddy substrates. Its diet primarily consists of algae, plant matter, and detritus, making it an important herbivorous component of its ecosystem.

This fish is also known for its ability to cling to surfaces using its modified dorsal fin and barbels.
