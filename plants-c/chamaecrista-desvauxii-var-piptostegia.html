Chamaecrista desvauxii var. piptostegia

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. piptostegia, commonly known as the yellow coralbean, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, raceme-like clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and a distinctive, woody, branched stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
