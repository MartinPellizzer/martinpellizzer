Cleistes divaricata f. leucantha

Cleistes divaricata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered cleistes, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a subtle greenish tinge. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture in its natural environment.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
