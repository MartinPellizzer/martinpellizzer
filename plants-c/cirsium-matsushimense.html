Cirsium matsushimense

Cirsium matsushimense, commonly known as the Matsushima thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, gray-green leaves with rough textures.

Cirsium matsushimense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
