Caesalpinia millettii

Caesalpinia millettii, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that resemble small clouds, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. Its woody, thorned stems are often used in traditional medicine.

Caesalpinia millettii thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
