Citrus × bigaradia var. sylvestris

Citrus × bigaradia var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild citron.

It is a citrus hybrid native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, rough, green or yellowish fruit with a thick, aromatic rind. The plant is typically evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and a bushy growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses.

The fruit is used in culinary and medicinal applications, though it is less commonly consumed fresh compared to other citrus varieties.
