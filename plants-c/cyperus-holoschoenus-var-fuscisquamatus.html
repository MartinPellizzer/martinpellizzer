Cyperus holoschoenus var. fuscisquamatus

Cyperus holoschoenus var. fuscisquamatus, commonly known as the black-scaled flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, dark-colored scales. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, sunny environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
