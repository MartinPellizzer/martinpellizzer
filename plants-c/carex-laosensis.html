Carex laosensis

Carex laosensis, commonly known as the Laos sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
