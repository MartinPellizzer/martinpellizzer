Cephalophyllum pillansii var. grandiflorum

Cephalophyllum pillansii var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Cape Town orchid, is a unique and rare plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded structure that gives it its common name.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
