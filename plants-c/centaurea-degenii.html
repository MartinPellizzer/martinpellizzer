Centaurea degenii

Centaurea degenii, commonly known as the Degen's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, seed-like fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea degenii is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
