Camphusia glabra

Camphusia glabra, commonly known as the smooth camphor plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces a resinous substance that has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Camphusia glabra thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.
