Croton formosus

Croton formosus, commonly known as the Taiwan croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Taiwan, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that can vary in color from green to red, orange, and even purple, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

Croton formosus typically thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
