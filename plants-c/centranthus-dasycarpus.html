Centranthus dasycarpus

Centranthus dasycarpus, commonly known as the woolly centranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features hairy, often gray-green leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers in clustered inflorescences. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant's compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
