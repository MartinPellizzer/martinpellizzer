Chaetochloa oblongata

Chaetochloa oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.
