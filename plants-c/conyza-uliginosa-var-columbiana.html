Conyza uliginosa var. columbiana

Conyza uliginosa var. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia marshweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
