Carex langsdorffii

Carex langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
