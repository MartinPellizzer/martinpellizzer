Crepis comata

Crepis comata, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Crepis comata is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
