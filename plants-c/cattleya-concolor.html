Cattleya concolor

Cattleya concolor, commonly known as the green cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cattleya concolor is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
