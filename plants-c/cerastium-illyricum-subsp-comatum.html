Cerastium illyricum subsp. comatum

Cerastium illyricum subsp.

comatum, commonly known as the Balkan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with soft, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from May to July. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
