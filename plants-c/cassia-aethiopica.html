Cassia aethiopica

Cassia aethiopica, commonly known as the African candle bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spindle-shaped pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Cassia aethiopica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
