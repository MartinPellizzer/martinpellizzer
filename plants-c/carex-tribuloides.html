Carex tribuloides

Carex tribuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to regions of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil effectively.
