Cynoglossum ferganicum

Cynoglossum ferganicum, commonly known as the Persian hogweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is typically found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in full sun.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
