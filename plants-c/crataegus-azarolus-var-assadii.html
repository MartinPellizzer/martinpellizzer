Crataegus azarolus var. assadii

Crataegus azarolus var. assadii, commonly known as the Assad's hawthorn, is a rare and endemic species native to parts of the Middle East.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn.
