Conradina grandiflora var. grandiflora

Conradina grandiflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open woods and sandhill habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.
