Cyrtanthus brachyscyphus

Cyrtanthus brachyscyphus, commonly known as the short-stemmed bell-shaped iris, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and summer seasons. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cyrtanthus brachyscyphus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
