Campanula lusitanica

Campanula lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Campanula lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
