Clinopodium multicaule var. multicaule

Clinopodium multicaule var. multicaule, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Clinopodium multicaule var. multicaule is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
