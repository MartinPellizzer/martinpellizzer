Centaurea carduiformis subsp. carduiformis

Centaurea carduiformis subsp.

carduiformis, commonly known as the carduiform centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils.

The plant features spiny, leafy stems and produces vibrant yellow to orange composite flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
