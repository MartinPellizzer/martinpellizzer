Cyperus tosaensis

Cyperus tosaensis, commonly known as Tosa flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to Japan.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus tosaensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
