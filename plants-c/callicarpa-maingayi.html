Callicarpa maingayi

Callicarpa maingayi, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer and persist through winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Callicarpa maingayi is valued for its ornamental berries and is cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
