Crataegus Schuettei Var. Basilica

Basilica hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus schuettei var. basilica, is a rare and striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. This unique cultivar is prized for its showy, white flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a dramatic visual effect in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. The plant is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners. With its elegant appearance and ecological benefits, Basilica hawthorn is a valuable addition to any botanical collection.
