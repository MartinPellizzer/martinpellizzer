Calamagrostis patagonica

Calamagrostis patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian reed, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Calamagrostis patagonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
