Commiphora gillettii

Commiphora gillettii, commonly known as Gillett's myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes other resin-producing plants like frankincense and myrrh. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces a fragrant, aromatic resin from its bark. This resin has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
