Clytostoma campanulatum

Clytostoma campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Clytostoma campanulatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
