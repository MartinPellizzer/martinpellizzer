Cardamine × haussknechtiana

Cardamine × haussknechtiana, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a sterile hybrid, resulting from the cross between Cardamine bulbifera and Cardamine pratensis.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
