Crataegus × apiifolia

Crataegus × apiifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and light conditions.

Crataegus × apiifolia is often used in hedgerows and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
