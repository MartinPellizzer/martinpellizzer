Convolvulus cupanianus var. guttatus

Convolvulus cupanianus var. guttatus, commonly known as the spotted bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a sprawling perennial that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are typically hairy and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
