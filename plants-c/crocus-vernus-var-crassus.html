Crocus vernus var. crassus

Crocus vernus var. crassus, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, with a distinctive, rounded shape. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.
