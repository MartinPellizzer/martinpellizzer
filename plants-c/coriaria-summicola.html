Coriaria summicola

Coriaria summicola, commonly known as the tropical coriander, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Coriariaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
