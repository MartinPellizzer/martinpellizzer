Cheiranthus bocconei

Cheiranthus bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's wallflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, four-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs and slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed along the stems.
