Cirsium acarna

Cirsium acarna, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Cirsium acarna is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
