Clematis gentianoides

Clematis gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its stems are slender and twining, often covered with fine hairs.

Clematis gentianoides thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
