Calendula suffruticosa subsp. suffruticosa

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the shrub marigold, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk.

The species is valued for its ornamental use and has historical applications in traditional medicine.
