Cochleanthes picta

Cochleanthes picta, commonly known as the Painted Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking feature is its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

Cochleanthes picta is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
