Carex tomentosa f. globifera

Carex tomentosa f. globifera, commonly known as the globeflower sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spherical cluster. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and compact growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in wet soils and its unique floral structure.
