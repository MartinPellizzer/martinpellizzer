Cristaria mendocina

Cristaria mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by water.

Cristaria mendocina plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity and water filtration.
