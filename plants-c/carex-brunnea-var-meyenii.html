Carex brunnea var. meyenii

Carex brunnea var. meyenii, commonly known as the Meyer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain seeds.

Carex brunnea var. meyenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
