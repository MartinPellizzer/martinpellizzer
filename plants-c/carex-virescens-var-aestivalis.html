Carex virescens var. aestivalis

Carex virescens var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones.

Carex virescens var. aestivalis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
