Coreopsis delphiniifolia

Coreopsis delphiniifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a bluish hue that gives it its common name. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Coreopsis delphiniifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
