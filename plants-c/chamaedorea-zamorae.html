Chamaedorea zamorae

Chamaedorea zamorae, commonly known as the Zamora's bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Honduras and Nicaragua. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnate leaves that are often a deep green in color. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and requires well-draining, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
