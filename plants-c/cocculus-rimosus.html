Cocculus rimosus

Cocculus rimosus, commonly known as the creeping spurred cucumber, is a vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a fibrous, woody stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
