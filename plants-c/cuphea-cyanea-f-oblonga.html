Cuphea cyanea f. oblonga

Cuphea cyanea f. oblonga, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique flower structure.
