Cobaea lasseri

Cobaea lasseri, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that is a capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
