Coreopsis tinctoria f. atropurpurea

Coreopsis tinctoria f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a cultivar of the common coreopsis.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flower heads and feathery, greenish-blue foliage. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
