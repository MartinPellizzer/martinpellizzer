Croton grandifructus

Croton grandifructus, commonly known as the giant croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
