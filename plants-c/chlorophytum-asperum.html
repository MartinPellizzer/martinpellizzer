Chlorophytum asperum

Chlorophytum asperum, commonly known as the hardy spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that often have a rough or hairy texture. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by clusters of bright green berries. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Chlorophytum asperum is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to purify the air and its resilience to various environmental conditions.
