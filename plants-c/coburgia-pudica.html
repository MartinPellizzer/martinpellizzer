Coburgia pudica

Coburgia pudica, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name.
