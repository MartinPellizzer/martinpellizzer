Cayaponia ottoniana

Cayaponia ottoniana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, purple, and white. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain regions.
