Cyathea zambesiaca

Cyathea zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forests and along riverbanks. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea zambesiaca plays a role in local ecosystems by offering habitat and contributing to soil stability.
