Calamagrostis hirsuta

Calamagrostis hirsuta, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-green foliage that gives it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are often reddish-brown in color.

Calamagrostis hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
