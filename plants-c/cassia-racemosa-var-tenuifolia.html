Cassia racemosa var. tenuifolia

Cassia racemosa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and practical purposes.
