Cerastium mauritanicum

Cerastium mauritanicum, commonly known as the Moroccan chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cerastium mauritanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
