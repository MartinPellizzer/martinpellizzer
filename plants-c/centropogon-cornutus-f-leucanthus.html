Centropogon cornutus f. leucanthus

Centropogon cornutus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered centropogon, is a rare horticultural variant of the centropogon genus.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the species. This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

Its floral structure and growth habits make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
