Campanula cypria

Campanula cypria, commonly known as Cyprus bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the island of Cyprus.
