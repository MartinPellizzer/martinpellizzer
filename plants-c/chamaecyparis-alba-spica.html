Chamaecyparis alba-spica

Chamaecyparis alba-spica, commonly known as the white cedar or white cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand coastal conditions.
