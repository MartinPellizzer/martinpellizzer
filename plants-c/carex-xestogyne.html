Carex xestogyne

Carex xestogyne, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

Carex xestogyne plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
