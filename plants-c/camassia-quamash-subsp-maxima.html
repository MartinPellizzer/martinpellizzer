Camassia quamash subsp. maxima

Camassia quamash subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the large camas, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies maxima is distinguished by its larger flower size and more robust growth compared to other camas varieties.
