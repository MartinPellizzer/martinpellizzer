Cardamine × hydrophila

Cardamine × hydrophila, commonly known as the water cress.

It is a hybrid species within the buttercup family, Ranunculaceae, and is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit. The plant features alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist environments such as streams, ditches, and wet meadows.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, making it a popular leafy vegetable in various cuisines.
