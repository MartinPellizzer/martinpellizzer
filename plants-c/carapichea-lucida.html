Carapichea lucida

Carapichea lucida, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.

Carapichea lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
