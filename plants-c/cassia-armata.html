Cassia armata

Cassia armata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia armata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.
