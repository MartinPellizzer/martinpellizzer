Calluna vulgaris f. incana

Calluna vulgaris f. incana, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a variant of the common heather.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting diverse insect life.
