Centaurea debeauxii subsp. nemoralis

Centaurea debeauxii subsp.

nemoralis, commonly known as the wood centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in woodlands and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are surrounded by pale yellow to white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
