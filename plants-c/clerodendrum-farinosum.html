Clerodendrum farinosum

Clerodendrum farinosum, commonly known as the sticky clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a milky sap when damaged.
