Corynopuntia guccinii

Corynopuntia guccinii, commonly known as the Guachinii prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Corynopuntia guccinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to survive in harsh conditions.
