Crypsis alopecuroides subsp. brachystachys

Crypsis alopecuroides subsp.

brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked cryptic grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces compact inflorescences with tiny, pale flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its low profile and inconspicuous appearance, it is well adapted to avoid detection by herbivores.
