Cornus darvasica

Cornus darvasica, commonly known as the Darvasica dogwood, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Cornus darvasica typically grows in rocky or dry soil conditions, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for gardens in suitable climates.
