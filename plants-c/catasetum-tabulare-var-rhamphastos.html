Catasetum tabulare var. rhamphastos

Catasetum tabulare var. rhamphastos, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant red or orange hues with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several showy, lip-like petals.

Botanically, it is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect pollinators.
