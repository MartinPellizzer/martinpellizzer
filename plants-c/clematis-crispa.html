Clematis crispa

Clematis crispa, commonly known as the curled clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive crinkled appearance. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, blue-black berries in the fall. Clematis crispa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
