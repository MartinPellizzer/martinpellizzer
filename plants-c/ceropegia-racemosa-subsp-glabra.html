Ceropegia racemosa subsp. glabra

Ceropegia racemosa subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, often hairy stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor cultivation.
