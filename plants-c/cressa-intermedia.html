Cressa intermedia

Cressa intermedia, commonly known as slender cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are slender and often branched.
