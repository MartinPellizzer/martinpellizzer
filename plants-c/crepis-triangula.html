Crepis triangula

Crepis triangula, commonly known as the triangular hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread.

Its structure and growth habits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
