Cestrum dasyanthum

Cestrum dasyanthum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, shiny seeds.

Cestrum dasyanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its nocturnal fragrance.
