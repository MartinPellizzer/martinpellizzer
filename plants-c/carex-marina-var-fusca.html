Carex marina var. fusca

Carex marina var. fusca, commonly known as sea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand high salt exposure and fluctuating water levels. Its inflorescence consists of spikes of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex marina var. fusca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
