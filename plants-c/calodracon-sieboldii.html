Calodracon sieboldii

Calodracon sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Calodracon sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
