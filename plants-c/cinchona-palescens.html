Cinchona palescens

Cinchona palescens, commonly known as the pale quinine tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is notable for its bark, which contains alkaloids such as quinine, historically used in the treatment of malaria. Due to its medicinal significance, it is also cultivated in various tropical regions for pharmaceutical purposes.
