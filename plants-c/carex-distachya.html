Carex distachya

Carex distachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems. Carex distachya thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
