Croton erythroxyloides var. cneorifolius

Croton erythroxyloides var. cneorifolius, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant red or orange hues, especially during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
