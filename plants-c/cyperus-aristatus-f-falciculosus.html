Cyperus aristatus f. falciculosus

Cyperus aristatus f. falciculosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the common sedge that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showing a preference for moist soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
