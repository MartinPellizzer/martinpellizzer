Coussarea liliiflora

Coussarea liliiflora, commonly known as the Lily-flowered Coussarea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble lilies, hence its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
