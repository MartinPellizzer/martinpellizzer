Centaurea × teyberi

Centaurea × teyberi, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × teyberi is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
