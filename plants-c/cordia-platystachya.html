Cordia platystachya

Cordia platystachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked cottonwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or semi-deciduous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
