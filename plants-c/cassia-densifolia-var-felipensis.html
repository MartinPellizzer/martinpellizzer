Cassia densifolia var. felipensis

Cassia densifolia var. felipensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
