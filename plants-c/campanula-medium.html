Campanula medium

Campanula medium, commonly known as the bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is known for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
