Camaridium album

Camaridium album, commonly known as the white orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals that emit a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Camaridium album is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
