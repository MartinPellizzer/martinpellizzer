Crassula remota

Crassula remota, commonly known as the remote crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

Crassula remota is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
