Cynosurus splendens

Cynosurus splendens, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, golden-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silky texture.

Cynosurus splendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
