Cheirostylis cochinchinensis

Cheirostylis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically has strap-like leaves and blooms in the spring, often featuring a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
