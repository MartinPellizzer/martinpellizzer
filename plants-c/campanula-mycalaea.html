Campanula mycalaea

Campanula mycalaea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula mycalaea is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
