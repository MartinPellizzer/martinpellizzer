Campanula scheuchzeri subsp. rhaetica

Campanula scheuchzeri subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian bellflower, is a rare and alpine plant native to the Swiss Alps. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
