Cheilanthes squamosa

Cheilanthes squamosa, commonly known as the rock fern or stone fern, is a species of fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and drought-tolerant nature, making it well adapted to rocky and sandy soils. The fronds are usually short and leathery, with a pale green to grayish coloration. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is often found growing on limestone or in crevices of dry habitats.
