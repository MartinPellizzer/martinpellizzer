Calamagrostis transsylvanica

Calamagrostis transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian reed, is a perennial grass native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Calamagrostis transsylvanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
