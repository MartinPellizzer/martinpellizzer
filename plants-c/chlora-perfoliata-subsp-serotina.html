Chlora perfoliata subsp. serotina

Chlora perfoliata subsp.

serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering chlora, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically arranged in pairs and may have a slightly succulent texture.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
