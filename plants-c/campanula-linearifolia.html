Campanula linearifolia

Campanula linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms during the summer months and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Campanula linearifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
