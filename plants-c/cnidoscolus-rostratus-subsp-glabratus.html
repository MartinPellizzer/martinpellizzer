Cnidoscolus rostratus subsp. glabratus

Cnidoscolus rostratus subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the hairy rostratus. It is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
