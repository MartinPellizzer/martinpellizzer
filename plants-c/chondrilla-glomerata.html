Chondrilla glomerata

Chondrilla glomerata, commonly known as the clustered goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Chondrilla glomerata is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
