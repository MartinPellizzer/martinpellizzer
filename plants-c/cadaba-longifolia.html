Cadaba longifolia

Cadaba longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cadaba, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Cadaba longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
