Corycium rubiginosum

Corycium rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Corycium rubiginosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
