Corylus avellana var. porrecta

Corylus avellana var. porrecta, commonly known as the smooth hazel, is a variant of the hazel tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elongated, pendulous catkins that hang from the branches. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, often turning yellow in the fall. Its edible nuts are encased in a smooth, thin shell and are typically harvested in the autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
