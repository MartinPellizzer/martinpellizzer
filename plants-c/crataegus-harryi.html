Crataegus harryi

Crataegus harryi, commonly known as Harry's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly Arizona and New Mexico, where it thrives in rocky soils and dry climates. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Crataegus harryi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
