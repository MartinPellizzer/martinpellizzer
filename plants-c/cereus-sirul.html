Cereus sirul

Cereus sirul, commonly known as the night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a nocturnal flowering plant, producing large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has a tall, cylindrical stem with ribbed structure and spiny areoles, typical of cacti. Its flowers are short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by moths and bats.

Cereus sirul is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
