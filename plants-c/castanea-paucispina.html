Castanea paucispina

Castanea paucispina, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces spiny, spherical fruits that enclose one or two chestnut-like seeds. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
