Cheilanthopsis indusiosa

Cheilanthopsis indusiosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with numerous, densely packed, and often hairy leaf clusters. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the underside of the leaves.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
