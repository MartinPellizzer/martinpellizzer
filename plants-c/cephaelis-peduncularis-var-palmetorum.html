Cephaelis peduncularis var. palmetorum

Cephaelis peduncularis var. palmetorum, commonly known as the palmetum creeper, is a flowering plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
