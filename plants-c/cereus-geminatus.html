Cereus geminatus

Cereus geminatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth is typically slow, and it can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom only once a year.
