Chrysopogon gryllus var. pallidus

Chrysopogon gryllus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale galletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

Due to its low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
