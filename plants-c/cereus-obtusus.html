Cereus obtusus

Cereus obtusus, commonly known as the blunt cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, white flowers that open at night. The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Cereus obtusus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
