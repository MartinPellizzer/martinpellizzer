Coronilla varia var. pauciflora

Coronilla varia var. pauciflora, commonly known as the lesser variable crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
