Cucumis arabicus

Cucumis arabicus, commonly known as the Syrian cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, green, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The botanical features of Cucumis arabicus make it a unique species within the cucumber genus.
