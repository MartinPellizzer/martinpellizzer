Cionura erecta

Cionura erecta, commonly known as the upright cion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Cionura erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
