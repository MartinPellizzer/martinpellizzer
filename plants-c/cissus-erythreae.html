Cissus erythreae

Cissus erythreae, commonly known as the rock fig or African snowball, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus erythreae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
