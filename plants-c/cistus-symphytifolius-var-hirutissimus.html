Cistus symphytifolius var. hirutissimus

Cistus symphytifolius var. hirutissimus, commonly known as the silky rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora.

Its essential oils, derived from its aromatic leaves, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
