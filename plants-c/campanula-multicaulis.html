Campanula multicaulis

Campanula multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base and along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
