Carex gracilis var. tricostata

Carex gracilis var. tricostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a distinct, ribbed texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex gracilis var. tricostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
