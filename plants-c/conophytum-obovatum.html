Conophytum obovatum

Conophytum obovatum, commonly known as the "Cushion Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy, and often rosette-shaped leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Conophytum obovatum is prized for its compact form and vibrant floral display.
