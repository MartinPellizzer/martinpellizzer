Conyza elegantula

Conyza elegantula, commonly known as the elegant blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Conyza elegantula is often considered a weed but has ecological value by supporting pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
