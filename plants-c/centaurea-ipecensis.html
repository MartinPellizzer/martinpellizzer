Centaurea ipecensis

Centaurea ipecensis, commonly known as the Iberian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds in a distinctive, bristly pappus.

Centaurea ipecensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
