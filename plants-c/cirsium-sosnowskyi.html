Cirsium sosnowskyi

Cirsium sosnowskyi, commonly known as the Sosnowsky's thistle, is a tall, perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust growth in rocky and arid environments. Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Cirsium sosnowskyi is also of interest to botanists due to its genetic diversity and potential for hybridization with other thistle species.
