Calamagrostis fibrovaginata

Calamagrostis fibrovaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis fibrovaginata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
