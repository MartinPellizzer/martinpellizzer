Campomanesia sessiliflora var. sessiliflora

Campomanesia sessiliflora var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, apple-like berries with a sweet, tangy flavor. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
