Campanula castellana

Campanula castellana, commonly known as the Castilian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Campanula castellana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
