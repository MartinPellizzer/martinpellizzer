Carex phyllomanica

Carex phyllomanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex phyllomanica plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
