Croton chrysodaphne var. meridionalis

Croton chrysodaphne var. meridionalis, commonly known as the golden flame croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage.

The leaves are typically a mix of yellow, orange, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its bold leaf patterns and ability to add color to indoor or outdoor spaces.
