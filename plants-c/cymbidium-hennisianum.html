Cymbidium hennisianum

Cymbidium hennisianum, commonly known as the Hennisian orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its elegant, long-lasting blooms.
