Carum hispidum

Carum hispidum, commonly known as hairy caraway, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender stems and small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it its common name.

The seeds, which are small and oval, are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
