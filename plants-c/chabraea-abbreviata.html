Chabraea abbreviata

Chabraea abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf chabraea, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Chabraea abbreviata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
