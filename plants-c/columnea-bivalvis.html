Columnea bivalvis

Columnea bivalvis, commonly known as the two-lobed flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Columnea bivalvis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
