Calea lemmatioides

Calea lemmatioides, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, nocturnal blooms that open at night. The plant has a compact, herbaceous structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea lemmatioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
