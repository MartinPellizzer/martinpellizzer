Cytisus radiatus

Cytisus radiatus, commonly known as the golden broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Cytisus radiatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
