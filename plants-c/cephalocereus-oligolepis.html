Cephalocereus oligolepis

Cephalocereus oligolepis, commonly known as the night bloomer cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its growth habit is typically solitary, with a straight trunk and few branches.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with a slow growth rate and drought tolerance.
