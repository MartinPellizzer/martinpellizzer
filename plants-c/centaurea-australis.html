Centaurea australis

Centaurea australis, commonly known as the southern centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Centaurea australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
