Colubrina spinosa var. spinosa

Colubrina spinosa var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
