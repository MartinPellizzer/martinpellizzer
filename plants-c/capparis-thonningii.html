Capparis thonningii

Capparis thonningii, commonly known as the African gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers with a strong, often unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies and beetles. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Capparis thonningii is valued for its ornamental flowers and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
