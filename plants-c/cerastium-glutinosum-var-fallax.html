Cerastium glutinosum var. fallax

Cerastium glutinosum var. fallax, commonly known as sticky willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate stems that produce small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a sticky, glandular coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant's morphology and growth habit make it well adapted to wet, temperate climates.
