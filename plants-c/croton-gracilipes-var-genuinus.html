Croton gracilipes var. genuinus

Croton gracilipes var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
