Carex pisiformis var. mayebarana

Carex pisiformis var. mayebarana, commonly known as the Mayebarana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It thrives in shaded, damp conditions and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
