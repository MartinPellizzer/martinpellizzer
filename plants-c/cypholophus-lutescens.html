Cypholophus lutescens

Cypholophus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tubular, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
