Cassiope lycopodioides var. cristapilosa

Cassiope lycopodioides var. cristapilosa, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly curved. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in coastal gardens and natural habitats.
