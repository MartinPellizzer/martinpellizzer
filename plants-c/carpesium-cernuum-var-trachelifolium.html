Carpesium cernuum var. trachelifolium

Carpesium cernuum var. trachelifolium, commonly known as the curlycup goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features wiry, branching stems that often grow in clumps, and its leaves are characterized by their finely toothed, curled margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
