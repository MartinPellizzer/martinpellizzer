Calorophus gracilis

Calorophus gracilis, commonly known as the slender flameflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red and orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

Calorophus gracilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
