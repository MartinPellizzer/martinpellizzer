Carex nigritella

Carex nigritella, commonly known as blackstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Carex nigritella is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
