Clematis viorna subsp. coccinea

Clematis viorna subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its striking red to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
