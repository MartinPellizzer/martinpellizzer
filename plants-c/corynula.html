Corynula

Corynula, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to saline environments. They have distinctive, spiky leaves and showy, blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators. Corynula species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance.
