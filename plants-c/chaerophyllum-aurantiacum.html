Chaerophyllum aurantiacum

Chaerophyllum aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden chaerophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
