Cynanchum auriculatum var. amamianum

Cynanchum auriculatum var. amamianum, commonly known as the Amami bellflower, is a variant of the genus Cynanchum, which belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its milky sap and hairy stems. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinct auriculate base, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

This plant is native to the Ryukyu Islands and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
