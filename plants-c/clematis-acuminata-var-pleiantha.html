Clematis acuminata var. pleiantha

Clematis acuminata var. pleiantha, commonly known as the Japanese vine or pleiantha clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
