Commiphora merkeri

Commiphora merkeri, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the savannas of eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces yellowish flowers and is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
