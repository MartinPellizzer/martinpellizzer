Calamagrostis spiciformis

Calamagrostis spiciformis, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis spiciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
