Chenopodium baccatum

Chenopodium baccatum, commonly known as goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Chenopodium baccatum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
