Cerastium pumilum subsp. geniculatum

Cerastium pumilum subsp.

geniculatum, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grassy areas or along paths. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical name reflects its close relationship to other species in the Caryophyllaceae family.
