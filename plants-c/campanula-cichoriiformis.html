Campanula cichoriiformis

Campanula cichoriiformis, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Campanula cichoriiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
