Carex striata var. brevis

Carex striata var. brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, stream banks, or garden settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
