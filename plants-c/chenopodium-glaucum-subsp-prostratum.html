Chenopodium glaucum subsp. prostratum

Chenopodium glaucum subsp.

prostratum, commonly known as bluish goosefoot, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It features succulent, prostrate stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are usually bluish-green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, round seeds. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This species is sometimes used as a forage plant for livestock in dry environments.
