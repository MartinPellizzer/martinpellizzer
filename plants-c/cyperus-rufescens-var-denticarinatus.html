Cyperus rufescens var. denticarinatus

Cyperus rufescens var. denticarinatus, commonly known as the toothed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
