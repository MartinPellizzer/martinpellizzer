Corchorus asplenifolius

Corchorus asplenifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. Its fibrous roots are known for their historical use in textile production.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
