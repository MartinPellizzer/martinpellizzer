Cardamine amara var. macrophylla

Cardamine amara var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved watercress, is a perennial herb native to moist, calcareous habitats in Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, lanceolate leaflets and small, white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in wet meadows, streambanks, and other damp environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
