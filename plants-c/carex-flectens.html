Carex flectens

Carex flectens, commonly known as the bent-stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided berries.

Carex flectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
