Chascanum hederaceum

Chascanum hederaceum, commonly known as the nightshade or common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces bright red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is a perennial vine that can spread aggressively, often becoming a weed in agricultural and garden settings.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
