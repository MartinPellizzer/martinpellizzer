Citronella paraguariensis

Citronella paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan citronella, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has rhizomatous growth, which allows it to spread easily in suitable conditions. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural insect repellents.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
