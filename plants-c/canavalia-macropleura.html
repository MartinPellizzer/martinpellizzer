Canavalia macropleura

Canavalia macropleura, commonly known as the broad-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures or other plants. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
