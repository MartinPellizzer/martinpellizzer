Cnicus quercetorum

Cnicus quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance, and its flowers bloom in the spring.

Cnicus quercetorum is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
