Caraguata candelabrum

Caraguata candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive candelabra-like branching structure, with multiple stems emerging from a single point. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
