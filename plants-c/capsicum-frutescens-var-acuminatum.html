Capsicum frutescens var. acuminatum

Capsicum frutescens var. acuminatum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its small, pointed, and often fiery red or orange fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruits are widely used in culinary applications for their intense heat and flavor.
