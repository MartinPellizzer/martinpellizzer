Cyperus alternifolius subsp. textilis

Cyperus alternifolius subsp.

textilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf paper plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in wetland environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a fibrous texture that has been historically used for making paper and textiles.

The plant thrives in moist soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.
