Cheilanthes aurantiaca

Cheilanthes aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-stemmed rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and distinctive orange-tinged stems. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in dry, well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its fronds are usually short and dense, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.

Cheilanthes aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
