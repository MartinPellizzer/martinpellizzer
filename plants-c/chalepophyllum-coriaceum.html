Chalepophyllum coriaceum

Chalepophyllum coriaceum, commonly known as the leatherleaf plant, is a member of the Berberidaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Native to the temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in shady, moist environments.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
