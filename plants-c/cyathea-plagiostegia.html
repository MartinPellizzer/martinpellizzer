Cyathea plagiostegia

Cyathea plagiostegia, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to grow in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea plagiostegia plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
