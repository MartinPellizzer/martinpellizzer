Carex pycnostachya

Carex pycnostachya, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges.

Carex pycnostachya is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
