Centaurea arnoldi

Centaurea arnoldi, commonly known as Arnold's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea arnoldi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
