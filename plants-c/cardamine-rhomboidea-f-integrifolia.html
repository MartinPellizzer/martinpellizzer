Cardamine rhomboidea f. integrifolia

Cardamine rhomboidea f. integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed watercress, is a variant of the watercress species characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are often submerged or partially submerged. It produces small white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular plant in culinary and medicinal uses.
