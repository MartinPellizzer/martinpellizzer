Cissus rhomboidea

Cissus rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhombic cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, rhombus-shaped stems. The plant is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas, where it can anchor itself with its tendrils. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Cissus rhomboidea is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
