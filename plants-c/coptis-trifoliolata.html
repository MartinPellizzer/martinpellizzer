Coptis trifoliolata

Coptis trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant contains berberine, a powerful alkaloid with medicinal properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
