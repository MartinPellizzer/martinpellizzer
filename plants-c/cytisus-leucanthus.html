Cytisus leucanthus

Cytisus leucanthus, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Cytisus leucanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
