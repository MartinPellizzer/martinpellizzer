Convolvulus valentinus subsp. suffruticosus

Convolvulus valentinus subsp.

suffruticosus, commonly known as Valentine's convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial subshrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
