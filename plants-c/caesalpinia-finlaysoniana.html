Caesalpinia finlaysoniana

Caesalpinia finlaysoniana, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, feathery flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets, giving it a fern-like appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.
