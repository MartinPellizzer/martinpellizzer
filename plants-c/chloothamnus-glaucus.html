Chloothamnus glaucus

Chloothamnus glaucus, commonly known as the bluish sage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
