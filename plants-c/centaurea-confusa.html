Centaurea confusa

Centaurea confusa, commonly known as the confused cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
