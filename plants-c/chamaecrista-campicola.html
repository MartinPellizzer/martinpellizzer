Chamaecrista campicola

Chamaecrista campicola, commonly known as the hairy ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Chamaecrista campicola is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
