Casearia multiflora

Casearia multiflora, commonly known as the coastal casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Casearia multiflora is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
