Chamaecytisus supinus var. gallicus

Chamaecytisus supinus var. gallicus, commonly known as the French broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is often used in hedges or as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in gardens.
