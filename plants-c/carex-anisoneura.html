Carex anisoneura

Carex anisoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in terminal spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Carex anisoneura is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
