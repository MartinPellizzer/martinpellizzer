Calamagrostis stricta f. exserta

Calamagrostis stricta f. exserta, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a cultivar of the common reed grass.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pale green to golden in color. It is a clump-forming grass that thrives in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland gardens or pond edges.

This ornamental grass is valued for its elegant, feathery foliage and its ability to attract wildlife such as birds and insects.
