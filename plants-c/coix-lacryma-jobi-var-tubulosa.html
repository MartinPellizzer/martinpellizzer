Coix lacryma-jobi var. tubulosa

Coix lacryma-jobi var. tubulosa, commonly known as the tuberous Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, tuberous stems that store nutrients, making it a unique structure in the plant kingdom. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

This variety is cultivated for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
