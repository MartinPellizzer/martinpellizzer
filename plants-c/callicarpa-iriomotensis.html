Callicarpa iriomotensis

Callicarpa iriomotensis, commonly known as the Iriomote beautyberry, is a small shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter, providing a striking visual contrast against the evergreen foliage. The plant belongs to the Hibiscus family (Malvaceae) and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant fruit color. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a slight pubescence, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.
