Carex grypos

Carex grypos, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks, contributing to ecological diversity in these habitats.
