Clematis friesiorum

Clematis friesiorum, commonly known as the Dutch clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis friesiorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
