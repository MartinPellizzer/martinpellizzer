Capparis stuhlmannii

Capparis stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
