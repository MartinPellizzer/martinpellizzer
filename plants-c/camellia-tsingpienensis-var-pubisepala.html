Camellia tsingpienensis var. pubisepala

Camellia tsingpienensis var. pubisepala, commonly known as the pubescent-sepal camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Its sepals are hairy, giving it the varietal name "pubisepala," and it is highly valued for its horticultural appeal.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience in cooler climates.
