Convolvulus caracasanus

Convolvulus caracasanus, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
