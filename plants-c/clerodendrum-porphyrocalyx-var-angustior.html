Clerodendrum porphyrocalyx var. angustior

Clerodendrum porphyrocalyx var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame bush, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that gives the plant a vibrant appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in landscape design.
