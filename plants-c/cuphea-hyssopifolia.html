Cuphea hyssopifolia

Cuphea hyssopifolia, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a striking member of the Lythraceae family native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, providing a striking contrast to its fiery blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea hyssopifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
