Crataegus oxyacantha var. supravillosa

Crataegus oxyacantha var. supravillosa, commonly known as the silky hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, silken-haired foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and hedgerows.
