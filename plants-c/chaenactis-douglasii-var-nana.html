Chaenactis douglasii var. nana

Chaenactis douglasii var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a compact rosette of blue-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
