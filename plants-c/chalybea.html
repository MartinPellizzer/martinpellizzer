Chalybea

Chalybea, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and hard, heavy wood that is resistant to decay. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Chalybea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
