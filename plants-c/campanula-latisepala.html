Campanula latisepala

Campanula latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula latisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
