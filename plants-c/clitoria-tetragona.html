Clitoria tetragona

Clitoria tetragona, commonly known as the winged bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple or violet flowers that resemble small butterflies. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds and a starchy, tuberous root known as the "devil's bean.". It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its nutritional value.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it a valuable plant in sustainable agriculture.
