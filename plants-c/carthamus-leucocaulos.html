Carthamus leucocaulos

Carthamus leucocaulos, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems and striking white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flowers. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a distinctive, spiny bract beneath the flower head.

Carthamus leucocaulos is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
