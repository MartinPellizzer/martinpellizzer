Chasmopodium

Chasmopodium, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Chasmopodium species are characterized by their slender, needle-like leaves and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in their native habitats.

Many species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and are used in landscaping.
