Cardamine thermarum

Cardamine thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring watercress, is a rare and specialized plant species.

It is native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in mineral-rich, calcium-saturated springs. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate white or pale yellow flowers. Its unique habitat requirements make it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Brassicaceae, sharing characteristics with other members of the mustard family.
