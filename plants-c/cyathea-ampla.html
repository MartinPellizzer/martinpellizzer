Cyathea ampla

Cyathea ampla, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance in natural landscapes.

Cyathea ampla plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
