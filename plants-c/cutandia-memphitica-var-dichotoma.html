Cutandia memphitica var. dichotoma

Cutandia memphitica var. dichotoma, commonly known as the Egyptian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Valley.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient Egyptian culture.
