Crataegus songarica

Crataegus songarica, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Crataegus songarica thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.
