Caroxylon implicatum

Caroxylon implicatum, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep roots and waxy leaf surfaces to conserve water.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
