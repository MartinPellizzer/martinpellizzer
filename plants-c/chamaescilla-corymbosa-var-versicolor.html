Chamaescilla corymbosa var. versicolor

Chamaescilla corymbosa var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable chamaescilla, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage cover. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its variability in flower color makes it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
