Carex halleriana f. corsica

Carex halleriana f. corsica, commonly known as Corsican sedge, is a variant of the species Carex halleriana, belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
