Cereus pleiogonus

Cereus pleiogonus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its blooming period is usually during the warmer months, and the flowers emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Cereus pleiogonus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
