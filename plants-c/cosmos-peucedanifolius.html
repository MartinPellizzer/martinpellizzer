Cosmos peucedanifolius

Cosmos peucedanifolius, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible tubers resembling small potatoes. These tubers are rich in inulin, a prebiotic fiber, and are valued for their nutritional and culinary uses.

The plant is also known for its resilience to poor soil conditions and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
