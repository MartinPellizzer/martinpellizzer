Colutea persica var. buhsei

Colutea persica var. buhsei, commonly known as Persian coltsfoot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its basal leaves with heart-shaped lobes and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
