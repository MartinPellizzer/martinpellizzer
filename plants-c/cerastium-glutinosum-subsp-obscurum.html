Cerastium glutinosum subsp. obscurum

Cerastium glutinosum subsp.

obscurum, commonly known as sticky willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in sticky, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
