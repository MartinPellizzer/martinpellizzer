Carex dichroa subsp. pamirensis

Carex dichroa subsp.

pamirensis, commonly known as the Pamir sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.
