Carex breviscapa

Carex breviscapa, commonly known as the short-capped sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain both male and female flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.

Carex breviscapa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
