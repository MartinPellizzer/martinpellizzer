Cordia myxa var. ixiocarpa

Cordia myxa var. ixiocarpa, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree or the red-fruit cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
