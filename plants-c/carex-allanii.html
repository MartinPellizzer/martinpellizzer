Carex allanii

Carex allanii, commonly known as Allan's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often hidden among its foliage, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Carex allanii is valued for its ecological importance and can be found in various regions of the United States and Canada.
