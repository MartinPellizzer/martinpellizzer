Centaurea virgata subsp. aggregata

Centaurea virgata subsp.

aggregata, commonly known as the clustered cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescence make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native range.
