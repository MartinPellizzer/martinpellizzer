Cypripedium × manchuricum

Cypripedium × manchuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is adapted for pollination by specific insects. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves, and it produces a single flower per stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
