Centaurea argentea

Centaurea argentea, commonly known as silver centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with yellow centers, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Silver centaury is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
