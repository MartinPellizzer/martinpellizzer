Clinopodium carolinianum

Clinopodium carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads and bloom in late summer.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
