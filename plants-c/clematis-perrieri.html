Clematis perrieri

Clematis perrieri, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features attractive, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Clematis perrieri is also valued for its ability to climb and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
