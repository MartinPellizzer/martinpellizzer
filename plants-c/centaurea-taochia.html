Centaurea taochia

Centaurea taochia, commonly known as the Tauchia knapweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. Its flowers are surrounded by pale blue or white bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Centaurea taochia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking color and drought tolerance.
