Convolvulus sibthorpii

Convolvulus sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorpius convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Convolvulus sibthorpii is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
