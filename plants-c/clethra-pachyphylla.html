Clethra pachyphylla

Clethra pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved clethra, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, woody stems and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Clethra pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insect species.
