Columnea lariensis

Columnea lariensis, commonly known as the flame violet, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Columnea lariensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
