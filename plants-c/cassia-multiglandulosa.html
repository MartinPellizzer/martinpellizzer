Cassia multiglandulosa

Cassia multiglandulosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia multiglandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
