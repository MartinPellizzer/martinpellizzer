Carex urbani

Carex urbani, commonly known as the urban sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in urban environments. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex urbani is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
