Citrus × bigaradia var. sulcata

Citrus × bigaradia var. sulcata, commonly known as the sour orange.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, sour-tasting fruits. It has thick, peeling skin and is often used in the production of marmalade and as a rootstock for other citrus trees. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
