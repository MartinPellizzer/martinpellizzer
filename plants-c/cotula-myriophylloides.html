Cotula myriophylloides

Cotula myriophylloides, commonly known as the many-leaved daisy, is a small herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and ragworts. The plant features a low-growing rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellow-centered composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cotula myriophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
