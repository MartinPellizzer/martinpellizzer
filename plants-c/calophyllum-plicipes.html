Calophyllum plicipes

Calophyllum plicipes, commonly known as the fan-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
