Cephalocarpus obovoideus

Cephalocarpus obovoideus, commonly known as the broad-ovate headed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its unique seed structure, known as a cephalocarp, is a defining botanical feature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
