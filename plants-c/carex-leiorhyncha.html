Carex leiorhyncha

Carex leiorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often greenish or brownish in color.

Carex leiorhyncha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
