Chamaecrista frondosa

Chamaecrista frondosa, commonly known as the fronded burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding visual interest to its ecological role.
