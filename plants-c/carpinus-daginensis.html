Carpinus daginensis

Carpinus daginensis, commonly known as the Dagestan hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged samaras that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests at moderate elevations.
