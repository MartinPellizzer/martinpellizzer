Centaurea ossica

Centaurea ossica, commonly known as the Balkan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Centaurea ossica is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
