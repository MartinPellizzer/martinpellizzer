Cardamine viscosa

Cardamine viscosa, commonly known as sticky buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are covered with a sticky substance, giving it its common name.

The flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.
