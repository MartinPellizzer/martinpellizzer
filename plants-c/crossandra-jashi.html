Crossandra jashi

Crossandra jashi, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has smooth, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Crossandra jashi prefers full sun to partial shade and requires regular watering during its growing season.
