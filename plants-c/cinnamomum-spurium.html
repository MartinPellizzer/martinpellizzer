Cinnamomum spurium

Cinnamomum spurium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or smallleaf cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong cinnamon scent. The plant has simple, alternate, and often lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries.

The wood of Cinnamomum spurium is valued for its durability and is used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.
