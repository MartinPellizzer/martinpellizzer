Carex laxiflora var. leptonervia

Carex laxiflora var. leptonervia, commonly known as the slender-veined sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, upright leaves with a distinctively veined structure, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

This sedge is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
