Cochlearia officinalis subsp. oblongifolia

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scurvy grass, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and slightly lobed, while its flowers are pale yellow with four petals.

It is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
