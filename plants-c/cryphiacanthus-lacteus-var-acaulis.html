Cryphiacanthus lacteus var. acaulis

Cryphiacanthus lacteus var. acaulis, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its milky sap and climbing growth habit.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

However, its sap can be irritant to the skin, requiring careful handling.
