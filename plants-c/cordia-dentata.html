Cordia dentata

Cordia dentata, commonly known as the roughleaf cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, toothed leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange in color, containing several seeds. Cordia dentata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
