Cerastium semidecandrum subsp. siculum

Cerastium semidecandrum subsp.

siculum, commonly known as Sicilian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of tiny white flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.
