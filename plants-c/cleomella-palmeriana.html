Cleomella palmeriana

Cleomella palmeriana, commonly known as the Palmer's cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cleomella palmeriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
