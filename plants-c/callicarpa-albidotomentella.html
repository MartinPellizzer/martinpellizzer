Callicarpa albidotomentella

Callicarpa albidotomentella, commonly known as the white-tomentose beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces striking, violet-purple berries that are both ornamental and edible. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, white pubescence.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
