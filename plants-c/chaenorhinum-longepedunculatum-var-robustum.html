Chaenorhinum longepedunculatum var. robustum

Chaenorhinum longepedunculatum var. robustum, commonly known as the robust common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
