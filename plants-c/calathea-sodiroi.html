Calathea sodiroi

Calathea sodiroi, commonly known as the saddleback calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that often display a unique saddle-shaped pattern, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically a deep green with contrasting white or silver markings, creating a visually appealing appearance. This plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance houseplant.
