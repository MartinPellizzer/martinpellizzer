Chrysosplenium alternifolium subsp. alternifolium

Chrysosplenium alternifolium subsp.

alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate-leaved chrysosplenium, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Saxifragaceae family. It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
