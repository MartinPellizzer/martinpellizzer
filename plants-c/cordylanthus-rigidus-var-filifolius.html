Cordylanthus rigidus var. filifolius

Cordylanthus rigidus var. filifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf cordylanthus, is a perennial shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is noted for its dense growth habit and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
