Caragana caragana lusus pendula

Caragana caragana lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping barbed bush, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand harsh climates, contributing to its popularity in landscaping.
