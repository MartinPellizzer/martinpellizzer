Chromolaena picta

Chromolaena picta, commonly known as the painted false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Chromolaena picta is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to maintain in warm climates.
