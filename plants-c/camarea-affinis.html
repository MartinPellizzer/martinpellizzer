Camarea affinis

Camarea affinis, commonly known as the red-tipped palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Camarea affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking red-tipped leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
