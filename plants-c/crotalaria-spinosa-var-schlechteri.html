Crotalaria spinosa var. schlechteri

Crotalaria spinosa var. schlechteri, commonly known as the spiked crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny pods and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a hairy surface, and it produces seed pods that split open when mature to release its seeds.

This species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
