Carex pilosa

Carex pilosa, commonly known as silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
