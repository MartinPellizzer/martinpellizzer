Campulosus aciculatus

Campulosus aciculatus, commonly known as the needleleaf campulosus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a yellow or white coloration.

Campulosus aciculatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
