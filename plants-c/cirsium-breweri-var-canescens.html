Cirsium breweri var. canescens

Cirsium breweri var. canescens, commonly known as the Whiteleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive whitish coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
