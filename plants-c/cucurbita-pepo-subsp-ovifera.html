Cucurbita pepo subsp. ovifera

Cucurbita pepo subsp.

ovifera, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated, ribbed fruits. The plant is an annual vine that produces yellow or orange-colored fruits with a distinctive acorn-like shape.

Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and trailing. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruit develops from the female flowers.

This subspecies is cultivated for its edible, nutrient-rich fruits, which are commonly used in culinary applications.
