Camellia miyagii

Camellia miyagii, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to southern Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, double-layered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Camellia miyagii thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
