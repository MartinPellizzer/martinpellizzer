Chenopodium robertianum

Chenopodium robertianum, commonly known as redroot goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
