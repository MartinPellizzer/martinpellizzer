Centaurea dominii

Centaurea dominii, commonly known as the Sicilian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea dominii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
