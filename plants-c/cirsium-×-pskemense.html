Cirsium × pskemense

Cirsium × pskemense, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × pskemense is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
