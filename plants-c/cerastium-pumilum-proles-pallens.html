Cerastium pumilum proles pallens

Cerastium pumilum proles pallens, commonly known as the pale prostrate mouse-ear, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit, with stems that lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. Its leaves are rounded and hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in moist conditions.
