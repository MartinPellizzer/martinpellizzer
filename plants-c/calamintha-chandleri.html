Calamintha chandleri

Calamintha chandleri, commonly known as Chandler's germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that are often blue or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
