Chrysojasminum goetzeanum

Chrysojasminum goetzeanum, commonly known as the golden jasmine, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
