Croton adenophorus

Croton adenophorus, commonly known as the white-veined croton, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with white veins and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
