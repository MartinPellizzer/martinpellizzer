Combretum glandulosum

Combretum glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
