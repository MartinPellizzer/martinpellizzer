Chlamydanthus tarton-raira var. angustifolia

Chlamydanthus tarton-raira var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf chlamydanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight greenish tinge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
