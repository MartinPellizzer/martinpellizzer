Carex albolutescens var. meridionalis

Carex albolutescens var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various water conditions and its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.
