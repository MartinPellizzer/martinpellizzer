Chlorocyperus fendlerianus

Chlorocyperus fendlerianus, commonly known as the silver flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
