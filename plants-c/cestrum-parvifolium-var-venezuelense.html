Cestrum parvifolium var. venezuelense

Cestrum parvifolium var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong scent at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive flowers and aromatic properties.
