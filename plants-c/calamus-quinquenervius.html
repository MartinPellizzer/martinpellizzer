Calamus quinquenervius

Calamus quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Southeast Asia.

The plant is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves with five prominent veins. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
