Carthamus divaricatus

Carthamus divaricatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
