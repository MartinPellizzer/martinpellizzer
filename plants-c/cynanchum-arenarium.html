Cynanchum arenarium

Cynanchum arenarium, commonly known as sand wheatear, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces showy, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Cynanchum arenarium is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its resilience in harsh climates.
