Cyclopia teretifolia

Cyclopia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cyclopia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.

The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, which contributes to its common name.
