Cassia hildebrandtii var. crispata

Cassia hildebrandtii var. crispata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use.
