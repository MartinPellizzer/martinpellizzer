Chasea

Chasea, commonly known as the blazing star, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Chasea species typically grow in prairies, open woods, and rocky areas, preferring full sun and well-drained soils. Their foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and they produce cylindrical flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Chasea plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also important nectar sources for pollinators.
