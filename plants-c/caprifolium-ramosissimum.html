Caprifolium ramosissimum

Caprifolium ramosissimum, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, red, and edible, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Caprifolium ramosissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
