Chironia virgularis

Chironia virgularis, commonly known as the common marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing whorled leaves and spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and colonize moist environments effectively. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity.

Chironia virgularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation sites.
