Chiococca sambucina

Chiococca sambucina, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a mix of purple, red, and white hues. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Chiococca sambucina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
