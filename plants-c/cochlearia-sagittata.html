Cochlearia sagittata

Cochlearia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, arrow-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky and coastal habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
