Coix ovata

Coix ovata, commonly known as the Chinese bulrush, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and broad, flat leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Its seeds, which are encased in a hard, fibrous husk, are edible and have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Coix ovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
