Clethra laevigata

Clethra laevigata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually hairy beneath. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Clethra laevigata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
