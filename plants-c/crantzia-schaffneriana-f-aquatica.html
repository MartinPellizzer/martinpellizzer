Crantzia schaffneriana f. aquatica

Crantzia schaffneriana f. aquatica, commonly known as water crantzia, is a variant of the species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its semi-aquatic growth habit, with leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant typically has small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
