Carex austromexicana

Carex austromexicana, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Carex austromexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
