Cotoneaster davidianus

Cotoneaster davidianus, commonly known as David's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

Cotoneaster davidianus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
