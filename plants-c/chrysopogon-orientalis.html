Chrysopogon orientalis

Chrysopogon orientalis, commonly known as the oriental silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-haired leaves and produces small, pale green or brownish flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
