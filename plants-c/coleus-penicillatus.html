Coleus penicillatus

Coleus penicillatus, commonly known as the spiral ginger or creeping coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
