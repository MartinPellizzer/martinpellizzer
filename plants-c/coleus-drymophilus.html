Coleus drymophilus

Coleus drymophilus, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus drymophilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments.
