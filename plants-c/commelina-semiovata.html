Commelina semiovata

Commelina semiovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white midrib and produces small, blue-violet flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
