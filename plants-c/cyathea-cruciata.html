Cyathea cruciata

Cyathea cruciata, commonly known as the Cross-veined tree fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive cross-veining pattern in its leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyathea cruciata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
