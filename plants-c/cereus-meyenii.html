Cereus meyenii

Cereus meyenii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. These flowers are short-lived, typically lasting only a few hours.

Cereus meyenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
