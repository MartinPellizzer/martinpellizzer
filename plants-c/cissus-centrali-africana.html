Cissus centrali-africana

Cissus centrali-africana, commonly known as the African devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in small, translucent dots, which are specialized structures called trichomes.

Cissus centrali-africana is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
