Carex ripariiformis

Carex ripariiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks, marshes, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex ripariiformis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
