Caladium acuminatum

Caladium acuminatum, commonly known as the spurred caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other plants like philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in various shades of green, red, and white. Caladium acuminatum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage rather than its flowers.
