Ceraia peculiaris

Ceraia peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar snail, is a species of air-breathing land snail.

It belongs to the family Helicidae and is characterized by its spiral-shaped shell. The shell is typically light to dark brown in color and has a smooth, glossy surface. This snail is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
