Castanopsis stipitata

Castanopsis stipitata, commonly known as the hairy tailed chestnut, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mixed forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
