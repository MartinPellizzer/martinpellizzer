Campanula subramulosa

Campanula subramulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula subramulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
