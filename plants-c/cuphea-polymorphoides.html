Cuphea polymorphoides

Cuphea polymorphoides, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cuphea polymorphoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
