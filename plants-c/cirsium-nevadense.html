Cirsium nevadense

Cirsium nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cirsium nevadense plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in alpine meadows.
