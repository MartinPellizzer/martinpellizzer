Calochortus monophyllus

Calochortus monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf globemallow, is a striking perennial wildflower native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its single, large, waxy leaf and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a stem that bears the flower cluster at the top.

Calochortus monophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
