Colea cauliflora

Colea cauliflora, commonly known as the cauliflower plant, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, white or green flower heads that resemble cauliflower, which are actually clusters of small, tightly packed flower buds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and has dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Colea cauliflora is often cultivated for its edible flower heads, which are used in various culinary dishes.
