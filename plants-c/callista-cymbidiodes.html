Callista cymbidiodes

Callista cymbidiodes, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom during the winter season, hence its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
