Chrysolaena obovata

Chrysolaena obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features oval, waxy leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces showy yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
