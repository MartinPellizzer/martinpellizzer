Camellia heterophylla

Camellia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from elliptical to ovate. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its evergreen foliage provides ornamental value throughout the year, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Camellia heterophylla is also valued for its resilience to cold temperatures, which enhances its adaptability in temperate climates.
