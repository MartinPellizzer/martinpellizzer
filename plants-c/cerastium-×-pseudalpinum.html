Cerastium × pseudalpinum

Cerastium × pseudalpinum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or rounded, with a velvety texture. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Cerastium × pseudalpinum is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
