Corysanthes rivularis

Corysanthes rivularis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Corysanthes rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
