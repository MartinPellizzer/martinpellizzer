Cardamine ballidifolia

Cardamine ballidifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved watercress, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a raceme, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a traditional ingredient in some culinary and medicinal uses.
