Cassia chapmanii

Cassia chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's cassia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces elongated pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cassia chapmanii is often found in sandy soils and is a hardy species suitable for arid landscapes.
