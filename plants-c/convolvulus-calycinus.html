Convolvulus calycinus

Convolvulus calycinus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important plant for pollinators in its native habitat.
