Convolvulus rhyniospermus var. laevis

Convolvulus rhyniospermus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bladder-like fruits that contain seeds, a feature that gives it its scientific name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
