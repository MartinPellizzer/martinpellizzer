Caralluma gracilipes

Caralluma gracilipes, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of India and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its thick, water-storing stems and ability to photosynthesize at night. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, minimizing water loss, while its flowers are typically white or pale purple.

Caralluma gracilipes is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
