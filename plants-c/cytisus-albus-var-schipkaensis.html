Cytisus albus var. schipkaensis

Cytisus albus var. schipkaensis, commonly known as the White Broom or Schipka White Broom, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
