Calligonum × pseudomuravljanskyi

Calligonum × pseudomuravljanskyi, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Calligonum mongolicum and Calligonum pseudomuravljanskyi.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, typically featuring gray-green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles during the summer months, which are followed by dry, papery fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

This hybrid is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and thrive in challenging landscapes.
