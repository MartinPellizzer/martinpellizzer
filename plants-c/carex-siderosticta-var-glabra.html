Carex siderosticta var. glabra

Carex siderosticta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands.

Its smooth, glabrous leaves distinguish it from other varieties within the Carex genus.
