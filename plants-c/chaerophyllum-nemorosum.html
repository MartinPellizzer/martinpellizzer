Chaerophyllum nemorosum

Chaerophyllum nemorosum, commonly known as the woodruff, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
