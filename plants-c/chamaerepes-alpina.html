Chamaerepes alpina

Chamaerepes alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Chamaerepes alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
