Crucifera canescens

Crucifera canescens, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across North America. Its leaves are lobed and often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a dry, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
