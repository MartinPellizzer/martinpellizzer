Cinchona weddelliana var. rubrivenata

Cinchona weddelliana var. rubrivenata, commonly known as the red-veined quina, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red veins on its dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by oval, dark red berries. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for the alkaloids it contains, which are used in the production of quinine.

This variety is notable for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
