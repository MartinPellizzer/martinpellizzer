Crocus akdagensis

Crocus akdagensis, commonly known as the Akdage crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Crocus akdagensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
