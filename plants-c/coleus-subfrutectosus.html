Coleus subfrutectosus

Coleus subfrutectosus, commonly known as the fruiting coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
