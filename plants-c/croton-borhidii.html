Croton borhidii

Croton borhidii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that can vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathium structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton borhidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf patterns.
