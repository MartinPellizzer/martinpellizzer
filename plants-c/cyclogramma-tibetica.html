Cyclogramma tibetica

Cyclogramma tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan legume, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.

Cyclogramma tibetica is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
