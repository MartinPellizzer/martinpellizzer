Capsicum annuum var. microcarpum

Capsicum annuum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper or miniature bell pepper, is a compact variety of chili pepper native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often brightly colored fruits, which can range from red, yellow, orange, or even purple. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it ideal for container gardening and small spaces. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers before fruiting.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both culinary and decorative settings.
