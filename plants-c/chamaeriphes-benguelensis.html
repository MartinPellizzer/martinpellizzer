Chamaeriphes benguelensis

Chamaeriphes benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
