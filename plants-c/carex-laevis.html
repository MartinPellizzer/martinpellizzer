Carex laevis

Carex laevis, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and other similar plants. The plant has triangular, smooth, and often hairless stems that grow in dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a waxy coating.

Carex laevis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
