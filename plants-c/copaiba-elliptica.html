Copaiba elliptica

Copaiba elliptica, commonly known as Brazilian frankincense, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and aromatic resinous sap. The plant produces a valuable essential oil and balsam, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
