Cyperus spinulosus

Cyperus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams.

Cyperus spinulosus is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic environments due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
