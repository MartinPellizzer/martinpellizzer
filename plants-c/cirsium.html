Cirsium

Cirsium, commonly known as thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their spiny leaves and purple or yellow flowers. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Cirsium species vary in height and growth habit, with some being annuals and others perennials.

Many species are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine.
