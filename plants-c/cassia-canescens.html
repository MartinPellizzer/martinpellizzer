Cassia canescens

Cassia canescens, commonly known as silver cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces bright yellow, feathery inflorescences that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cassia canescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid landscapes.
