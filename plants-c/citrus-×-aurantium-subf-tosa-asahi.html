Citrus × aurantium subf. tosa-asahi

Citrus × aurantium subf.

tosa-asahi, commonly known as Tosa-Ashihara citrus, is a hybrid citrus species originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round, and thick-skinned fruits with a high oil content.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The fruit is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
