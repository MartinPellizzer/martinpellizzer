Carex leptogyna

Carex leptogyna, commonly known as slender spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex leptogyna plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
