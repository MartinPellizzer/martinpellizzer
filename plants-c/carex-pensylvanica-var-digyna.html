Carex pensylvanica var. digyna

Carex pensylvanica var. digyna, commonly known as the Pennsylvania sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth compared to other Carex species.
