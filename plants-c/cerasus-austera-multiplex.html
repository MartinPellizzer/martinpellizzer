Cerasus austera multiplex

Cerasus austera multiplex, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a species of flowering shrub belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

The fruit, though edible, is typically small and not widely cultivated for consumption.
