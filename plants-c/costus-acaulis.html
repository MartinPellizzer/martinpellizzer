Costus acaulis

Costus acaulis, commonly known as the stemless ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with leaves emerging directly from the base. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne in spike-like clusters.
