Carex novograblenovii

Carex novograblenovii, commonly known as the Novograblenov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like structures, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the seeds.

Carex novograblenovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
