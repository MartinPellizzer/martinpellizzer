Cerastium taschkendicum

Cerastium taschkendicum, commonly known as the Taschkent mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
