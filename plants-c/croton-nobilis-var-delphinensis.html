Croton nobilis var. delphinensis

Croton nobilis var. delphinensis, commonly known as the Blue Croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, blue-green foliage.

The leaves are typically thick and leathery, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and vibrant leaf color.
