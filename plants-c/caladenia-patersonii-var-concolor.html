Caladenia patersonii var. concolor

Caladenia patersonii var. concolor, commonly known as the Paterson's Spider Orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a single, slender, pale pink or white flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance, which is a hallmark of the Caladenia genus. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, often in heathlands or woodland areas. Its flowering period occurs during late winter to early spring, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
