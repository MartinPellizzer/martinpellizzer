Cheirolophus sventenii

Cheirolophus sventenii, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
