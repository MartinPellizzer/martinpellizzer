Corchorus quadrangularis

Corchorus quadrangularis, commonly known as the African nightshade or Egyptian spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and calcium.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
