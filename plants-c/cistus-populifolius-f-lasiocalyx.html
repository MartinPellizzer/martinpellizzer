Cistus populifolius f. lasiocalyx

Cistus populifolius f. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The subspecies lasiocalyx is distinguished by its thick, woolly leaves and is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
