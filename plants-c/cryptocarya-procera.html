Cryptocarya procera

Cryptocarya procera, commonly known as the giant cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
