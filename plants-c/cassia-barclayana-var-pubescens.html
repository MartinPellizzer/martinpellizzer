Cassia barclayana var. pubescens

Cassia barclayana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow, fluffy flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
