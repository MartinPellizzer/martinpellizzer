Chenopodina glauca

Chenopodina glauca, commonly known as blue goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, glaucous leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Chenopodina glauca is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, semi-arid conditions.
