Coleus mirus

Coleus mirus, commonly known as the silver leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of silver, white, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its decorative leaves.

Coleus mirus prefers partial to full sunlight and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance foliage.
