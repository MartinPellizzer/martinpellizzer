Chenopodium × solitarium

Chenopodium × solitarium, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found growing in disturbed soils such as fields and roadsides. Lambsquarters is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also a common weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature and prolific seed production.
