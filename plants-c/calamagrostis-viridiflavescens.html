Calamagrostis viridiflavescens

Calamagrostis viridiflavescens, commonly known as the green-yellow reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wet to moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, arching blades that are usually bright green, turning a yellowish-green as they mature. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of pale green to golden florets.

Calamagrostis viridiflavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
