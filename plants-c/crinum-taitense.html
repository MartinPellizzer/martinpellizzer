Crinum taitense

Crinum taitense, commonly known as the Taiti lily, is a bulbous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often emerging from a clustered bulb. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing in coastal areas or on rocky soils.

Crinum taitense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
