Calamagrostis sylvatica var. abietina

Calamagrostis sylvatica var. abietina, commonly known as the silvergrass or mountain reed, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
