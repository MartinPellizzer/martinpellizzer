Cardamine bellidifolia var. laxa

Cardamine bellidifolia var. laxa, commonly known as the loose-leaf watercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
