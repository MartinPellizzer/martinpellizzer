Calathea velutina

Calathea velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, waxy leaves that display intricate patterns and color variations. The leaves are typically dark green with contrasting veins and sometimes a reddish or purple tint. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
