Clematis chinensis var. tatushanensis

Clematis chinensis var. tatushanensis, commonly known as the Tatushanensis Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
