Convolvulus hochstetteri

Convolvulus hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, while its stems are slender and twining.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
