Centaurea paniculata f. straminea

Centaurea paniculata f. straminea, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bracts that are often more showy than the actual florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as gravelly soils and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, airy look.
