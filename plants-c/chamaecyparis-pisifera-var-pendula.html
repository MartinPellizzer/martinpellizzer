Chamaecyparis pisifera var. pendula

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading foliage that gives it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color and are arranged in flattened sprays. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green cones, typical of the cypress family.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
