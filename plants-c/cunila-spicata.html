Cunila spicata

Cunila spicata, commonly known as creeping caraway, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly bitter taste, making it useful in culinary applications.

Cunila spicata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
