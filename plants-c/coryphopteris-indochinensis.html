Coryphopteris indochinensis

Coryphopteris indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds with a serrated edge. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Coryphopteris indochinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
