Cephalophora gracilis

Cephalophora gracilis, commonly known as the slender-headed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Cephalophora gracilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
