Calamus orientalis

Calamus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a tall, slender plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by edible, black, spherical fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in various traditional applications.

The botanical features of Calamus orientalis make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
