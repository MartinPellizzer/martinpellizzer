Cabucala madagascariensis var. angustifolia

Cabucala madagascariensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cabucala, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
