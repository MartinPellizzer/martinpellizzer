Combretum capitatum

Combretum capitatum, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorny growth and clusters of small, red or yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is noted for its adaptability to dry conditions and its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
