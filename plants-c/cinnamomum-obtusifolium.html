Cinnamomum obtusifolium

Cinnamomum obtusifolium, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to dark purple berries containing winged seeds. Its bark, which is aromatic and cinnamon-like, has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its economic importance in the spice trade.
