Cassia boyanii

Cassia boyanii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall, and its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain numerous flat, brown seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia boyanii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
