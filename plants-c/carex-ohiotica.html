Carex ohiotica

Carex ohiotica, commonly known as Ohiotica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex ohiotica thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
