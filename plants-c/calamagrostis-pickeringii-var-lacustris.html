Calamagrostis pickeringii var. lacustris

Calamagrostis pickeringii var. lacustris, commonly known as the lakecarex reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, pale green to brown flowers.

Calamagrostis pickeringii var. lacustris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
