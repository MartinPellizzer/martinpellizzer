Calea lucidivenia var. punctata

Calea lucidivenia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted angel's trumpet, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are velvety and dark green, often with a waxy texture. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
