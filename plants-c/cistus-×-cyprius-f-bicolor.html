Cistus × cyprius f. bicolor

Cistus × cyprius f. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicolor flowers, which typically feature a white base with a pink or reddish hue at the edges. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
