Crocus vernus var. plumbeus

Crocus vernus var. plumbeus, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is a member of the Iridaceae family.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
