Croton cycloideus

Croton cycloideus, commonly known as the spiral croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also popular as ornamental plants due to their unique foliage.
