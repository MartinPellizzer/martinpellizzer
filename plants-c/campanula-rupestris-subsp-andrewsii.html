Campanula rupestris subsp. andrewsii

Campanula rupestris subsp.

andrewsii, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
