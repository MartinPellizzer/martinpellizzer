Convolvulus stenophyllus

Convolvulus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along rocky outcrops or in dry habitats.

Convolvulus stenophyllus is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
