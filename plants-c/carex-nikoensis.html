Carex nikoensis

Carex nikoensis, commonly known as Niko's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the genus Carex.

Niko's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
