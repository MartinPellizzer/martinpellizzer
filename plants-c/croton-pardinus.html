Croton pardinus

Croton pardinus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Croton pardinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
