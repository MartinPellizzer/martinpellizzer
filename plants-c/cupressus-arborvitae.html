Cupressus arborvitae

Cupressus arborvitae, commonly known as the white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramid-shaped crown and scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it has a shallow, fibrous root system adapted to rocky soils. Its foliage provides year-round cover, making it a valuable plant in landscaping and ecological restoration.

The tree is also known for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its longevity in natural environments.
