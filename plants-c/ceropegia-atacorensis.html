Ceropegia atacorensis

Ceropegia atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis string of hearts, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that attract specific pollinators, such as moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ceropegia atacorensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
