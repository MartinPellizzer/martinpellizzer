Clematis neomexicana

Clematis neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, blue to purple flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is typically found in rocky soils and open habitats. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and fragrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
