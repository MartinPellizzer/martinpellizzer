Catasetum franchinianum

Catasetum franchinianum, commonly known as the Franchinian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are unique for their specialized structure, including a long, tubular column that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Catasetum franchinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
