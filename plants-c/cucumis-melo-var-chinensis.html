Cucumis melo var. chinensis

Cucumis melo var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese melon, is a variant of the melon species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruits with a smooth, pale green skin and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and is used in various culinary traditions across Asia.
