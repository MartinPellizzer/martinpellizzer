Cyperus laevissimus

Cyperus laevissimus, commonly known as the flatleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are linear and have a smooth, waxy surface, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster.

Cyperus laevissimus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
