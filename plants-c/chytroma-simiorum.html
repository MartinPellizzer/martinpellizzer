Chytroma simiorum

Chytroma simiorum, commonly known as the banana leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically grows epiphytically on trees or rocks, using its roots to anchor itself while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
