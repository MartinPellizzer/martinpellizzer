Cyclamen parviflorum var. subalpinum

Cyclamen parviflorum var. subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers with spurred petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, calcareous soils. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Cyclamen parviflorum var. subalpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and flowers.
