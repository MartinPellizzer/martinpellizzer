Chrysodendron tinctorium var. longifolium

Chrysodendron tinctorium var. longifolium, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a glossy surface and a reddish underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and dyeing practices.
