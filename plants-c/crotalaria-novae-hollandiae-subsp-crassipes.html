Crotalaria novae-hollandiae subsp. crassipes

Crotalaria novae-hollandiae subsp.

crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and heart-shaped, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
