Cereus cinnabarinus

Cereus cinnabarinus, commonly known as the red torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in a reddish or orange bloom, giving it its striking appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which open at night. These flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

Cereus cinnabarinus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
