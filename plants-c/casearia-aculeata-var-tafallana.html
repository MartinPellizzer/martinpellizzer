Casearia aculeata var. tafallana

Casearia aculeata var. tafallana, commonly known as the Tafallana casearia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when ripe, containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas within humid forests.
