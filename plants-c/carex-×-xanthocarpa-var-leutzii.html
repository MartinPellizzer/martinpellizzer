Carex × xanthocarpa var. leutzii

Carex × xanthocarpa var. leutzii, commonly known as the yellowflower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and pond edges.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
