Cruciata armeniaca

Cruciata armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian crosswort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is native to regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
