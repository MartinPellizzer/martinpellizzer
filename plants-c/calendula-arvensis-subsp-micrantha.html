Calendula arvensis subsp. micrantha

Calendula arvensis subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the lesser pot marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the more common Calendula officinalis.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
