Carex leiogona

Carex leiogona, commonly known as smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex leiogona is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
