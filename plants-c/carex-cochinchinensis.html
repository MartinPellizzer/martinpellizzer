Carex cochinchinensis

Carex cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded forest floors.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
