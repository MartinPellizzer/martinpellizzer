Coleus madagascariensis

Coleus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar coleus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors, often mistaken for flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in texture and coloration.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
