Chloris glauca

Chloris glauca, commonly known as blue grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Chloris glauca is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats in suitable conditions.
