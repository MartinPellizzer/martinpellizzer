Carex bolliana

Carex bolliana, commonly known as Bolliana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Carex bolliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
