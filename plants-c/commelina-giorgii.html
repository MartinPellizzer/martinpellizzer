Commelina giorgii

Commelina giorgii, commonly known as the giant dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking blue-violet flowers with three petals and two white sepals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering period typically occurs during the rainy season, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
