Centunculus

Centunculus, commonly known as the "spider plant" or "century plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Montiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are small, mat-forming plants with delicate, thread-like leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a distinctive appearance resembling a spider's web. These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are adapted to dry conditions, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration.
