Celastrus gracilipes

Celastrus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
