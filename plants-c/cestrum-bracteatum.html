Cestrum bracteatum

Cestrum bracteatum, commonly known as the night-flowering trumpet honeysuckle, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Cestrum bracteatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
