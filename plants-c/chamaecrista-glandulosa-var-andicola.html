Chamaecrista glandulosa var. andicola

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
