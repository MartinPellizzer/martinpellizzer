Caesalpinia caladenia

Caesalpinia caladenia, commonly known as the pink pagoda, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-colored flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features a woody structure with compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
