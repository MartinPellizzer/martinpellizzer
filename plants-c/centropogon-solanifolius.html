Centropogon solanifolius

Centropogon solanifolius, commonly known as the purple false potato, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually clustered in terminal inflorescences and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Despite its ornamental value, it is not widely cultivated for food or medicinal purposes.
