Clinopodium megalanthum var. lancifolium

Clinopodium megalanthum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in shades of white to pale blue. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and antiseptic properties.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
