Centaurea collicola

Centaurea collicola, commonly known as the rock centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea collicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
