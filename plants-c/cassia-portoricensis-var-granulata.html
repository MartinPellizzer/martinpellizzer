Cassia portoricensis var. granulata

Cassia portoricensis var. granulata, commonly known as the granulate cassia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils along the shoreline.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
