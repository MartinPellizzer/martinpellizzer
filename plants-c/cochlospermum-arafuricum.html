Cochlospermum arafuricum

Cochlospermum arafuricum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has a distinctive, palmate leaf structure with five to seven lobes. It produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cochlospermum arafuricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
