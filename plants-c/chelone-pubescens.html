Chelone pubescens

Chelone pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
