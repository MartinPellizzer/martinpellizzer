Cyperus pseudodiaphanus var. pseudodiaphanus

Cyperus pseudodiaphanus var. pseudodiaphanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and culms that are often slightly hairy.
