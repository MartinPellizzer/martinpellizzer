Clerodendrum discolor var. oppositifolium

Clerodendrum discolor var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, which gives it its botanical variant name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types when provided with adequate sunlight.
