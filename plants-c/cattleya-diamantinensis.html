Cattleya diamantinensis

Cattleya diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamantina orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy flowers. The plant produces striking, fragrant blooms with a complex pattern of colors, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with good drainage.

Cattleya diamantinensis is prized by orchid growers for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
