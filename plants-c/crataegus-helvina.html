Crataegus helvina

Crataegus helvina, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and thornless branches. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip.

Crataegus helvina is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
