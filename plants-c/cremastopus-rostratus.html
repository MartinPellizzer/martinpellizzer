Cremastopus rostratus

Cremastopus rostratus, commonly known as the rostrated holly, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a pointed tip. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
