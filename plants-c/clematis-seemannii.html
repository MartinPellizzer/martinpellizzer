Clematis seemannii

Clematis seemannii, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis seemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
