Carex danaensis

Carex danaensis, commonly known as Dana's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex danaensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
