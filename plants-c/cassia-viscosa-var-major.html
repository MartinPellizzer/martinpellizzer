Cassia viscosa var. major

Cassia viscosa var. major, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in various cultures.
