Cerastium gracile

Cerastium gracile, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its compact stature and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cerastium gracile is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
