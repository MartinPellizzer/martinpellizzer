Cassine vaccinioides

Cassine vaccinioides, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in coastal areas, often growing in brackish or saline soils, and is adapted to withstand periodic flooding.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
