Cyathula uncinulata var. ceylanica

Cyathula uncinulata var. ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
