Cestrum tenuiflorum

Cestrum tenuiflorum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
