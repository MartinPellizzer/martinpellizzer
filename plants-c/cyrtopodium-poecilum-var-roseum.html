Cyrtopodium poecilum var. roseum

Cyrtopodium poecilum var. roseum, commonly known as the roseate corydalis, is a compact, evergreen orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle rose-colored hue. The plant produces pendulous, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements make it a desirable addition to orchid collections.
