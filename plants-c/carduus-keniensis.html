Carduus keniensis

Carduus keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are spiny and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are yellow and attract various pollinators.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.
