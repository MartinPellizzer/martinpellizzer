Crambe juncea var. koktebelica

Crambe juncea var. koktebelica, commonly known as the Koktebelica turnip, is a biennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, edible roots that are harvested in the second year of growth. The plant produces yellow flowers in the first year, followed by a rosette of leaves in the second. Its roots are rich in oil and are used in both culinary and industrial applications.

The variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
