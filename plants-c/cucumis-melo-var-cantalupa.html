Cucumis melo var. cantalupa

Cucumis melo var. cantalupa, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, green rind with a distinctive warty texture and a sweet, fragrant, pale yellow to orange flesh. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a sprawling growth habit, often requiring support for its vines. It thrives in warm climates and is typically harvested when the fruit reaches full size and the rind turns a deep green.

The fruit is prized for its flavor and is commonly used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
