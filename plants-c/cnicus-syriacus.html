Cnicus syriacus

Cnicus syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its ability to spread through seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
