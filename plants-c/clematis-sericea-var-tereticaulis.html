Clematis sericea var. tereticaulis

Clematis sericea var. tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender silky clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are adapted for climbing, allowing it to ascend trellises or other supports.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
