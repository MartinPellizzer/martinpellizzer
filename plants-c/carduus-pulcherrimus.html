Carduus pulcherrimus

Carduus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the showy cardoon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
