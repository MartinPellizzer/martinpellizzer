Catasetum sanguineum var. viride

Catasetum sanguineum var. viride, commonly known as the green catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and showy, often red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are unique in structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect pollinators.

The varietal name 'viride' refers to its distinctive green coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of Catasetum.
