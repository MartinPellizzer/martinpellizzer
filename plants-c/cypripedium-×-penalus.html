Cypripedium × penalus

Cypripedium × penalus, commonly known as the hybrid white lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the stem.

Cypripedium × penalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
