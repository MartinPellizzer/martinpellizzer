Crypsinus hastatus f. longisquamatus

Crypsinus hastatus f. longisquamatus, commonly known as the longscale crypsinus, is a unique variant of the base plant Crypsinus hastatus.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy scales on its leaves, which give it its specific epithet "longisquamatus.". It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The foliage is typically evergreen, with a dense, compact growth habit that makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation and adaptation to its native habitat.
