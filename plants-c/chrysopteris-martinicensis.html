Chrysopteris martinicensis

Chrysopteris martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.
