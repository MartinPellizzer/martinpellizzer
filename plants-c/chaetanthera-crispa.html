Chaetanthera crispa

Chaetanthera crispa, commonly known as the fragrant moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique flowering pattern and aromatic properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
