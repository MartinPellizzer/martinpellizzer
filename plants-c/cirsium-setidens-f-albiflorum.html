Cirsium setidens f. albiflorum

Cirsium setidens f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a hemicryptophyte with a rosette of basal leaves and a tall flowering stem. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically spiny and have a rough, hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique appearance.
