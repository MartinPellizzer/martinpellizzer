Clerodendrum baronianum

Clerodendrum baronianum, commonly known as the Baronia Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
