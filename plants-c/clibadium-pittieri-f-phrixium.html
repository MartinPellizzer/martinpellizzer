Clibadium pittieri f. phrixium

Clibadium pittieri f. phrixium, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a variant of the tamarind tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-brown bark and large, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces elongated, pendulous fruits that contain numerous seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
