Crataegus orientalis var. obtusata

Crataegus orientalis var. obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked stems and ovate to elliptical leaves with rounded or obtuse tips. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its seasonal interest.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
