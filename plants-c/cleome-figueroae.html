Cleome figueroae

Cleome figueroae, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated edge.

Cleome figueroae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and ease of growth.
