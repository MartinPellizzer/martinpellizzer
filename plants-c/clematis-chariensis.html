Clematis chariensis

Clematis chariensis, commonly known as the New Zealand clematis, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a climbing shrub that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with woody stems and deciduous leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Clematis chariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
