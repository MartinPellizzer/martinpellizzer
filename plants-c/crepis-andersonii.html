Crepis andersonii

Crepis andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. Crepis andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
