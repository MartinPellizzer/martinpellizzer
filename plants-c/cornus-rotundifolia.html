Cornus rotundifolia

Cornus rotundifolia, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems that become prominent in the winter. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that attract birds.

Cornus rotundifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
