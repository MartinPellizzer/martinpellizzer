Coniogramme caudata var. salwinensis

Coniogramme caudata var. salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin's climbing fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its pinnate leaves and spore-bearing structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
