Cymbopogon hirtus

Cymbopogon hirtus, commonly known as hairy lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Cymbopogon hirtus thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
