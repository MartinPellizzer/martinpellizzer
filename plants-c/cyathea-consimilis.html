Cyathea consimilis

Cyathea consimilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor deeply into the soil and absorb nutrients. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Cyathea consimilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
