Cayratia emarginata

Cayratia emarginata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, blue or purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant.
