Clematis pterantha var. grossidentata

Clematis pterantha var. grossidentata, commonly known as the giant-flowered clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, broad, recurved petal at the base. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its dramatic floral display.
