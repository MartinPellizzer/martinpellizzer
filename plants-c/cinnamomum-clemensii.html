Cinnamomum clemensii

Cinnamomum clemensii, commonly known as the Madagascan cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark, which is similar to true cinnamon. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries containing shiny, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy aroma.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
