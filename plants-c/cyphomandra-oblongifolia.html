Cyphomandra oblongifolia

Cyphomandra oblongifolia, commonly known as the longleaf tomato or wild tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into oval, green fruits resembling tomatoes but with a more acidic flavor. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a sprawling growth habit.

Cyphomandra oblongifolia is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has historical use in traditional medicine.
