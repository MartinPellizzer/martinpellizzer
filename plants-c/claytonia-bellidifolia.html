Claytonia bellidifolia

Claytonia bellidifolia, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are succulent and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.
