Cassia dumalis

Cassia dumalis, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which are clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are typically pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia dumalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
