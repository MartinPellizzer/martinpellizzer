Crataegus viridis var. interior

Crataegus viridis var. interior, commonly known as the interior green hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
