Cassia cowanii var. guianensis

Cassia cowanii var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
