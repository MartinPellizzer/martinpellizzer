Cyperus truncatus

Cyperus truncatus, commonly known as flatleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a bluish-green color. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

Cyperus truncatus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and disturbed soils, making it an adaptable and common wetland plant.
