Codonopsis mairei

Codonopsis mairei, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or Maire's bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Codonopsis mairei is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for traditional herbal use.
