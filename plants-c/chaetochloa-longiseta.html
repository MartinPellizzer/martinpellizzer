Chaetochloa longiseta

Chaetochloa longiseta, commonly known as the long-hairs grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
