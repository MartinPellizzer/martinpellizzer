Curcuma reclinata

Curcuma reclinata, commonly known as the reclining turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and ornamental leaves that are often used in horticulture. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in clusters and are followed by small, red berries.

Curcuma reclinata is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
