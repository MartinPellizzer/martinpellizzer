Cheilanthes moritziana

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white to brownish hairs. Its spores are released from the underside of the fronds, which are usually clustered in a tight, cushion-like formation.

This fern is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
