Carex vesicaria f. monstrosa

Carex vesicaria f. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a striking variant of the marsh sedge species.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, giving it a dramatic, almost fern-like appearance. This plant thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate moist conditions.
