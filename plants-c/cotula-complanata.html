Cotula complanata

Cotula complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant has flat, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cotula complanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
