Cassia plumosa var. erecta

Cassia plumosa var. erecta, commonly known as the upright cassia, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its showy flowers and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in warm climates.
