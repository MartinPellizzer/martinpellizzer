Ceratospermum papposum

Ceratospermum papposum, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is notable for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting specific pollinators such as moths.
