Calamus viminalis var. reinwardtii

Calamus viminalis var. reinwardtii, commonly known as the bamboo reed or reed grass, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, and often bamboo-like stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically green and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
