Chenopodiastrum murale

Chenopodiastrum murale, commonly known as wall goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, broadly ovate to deltate leaves with a rough, sometimes pubescent texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces dry, three-angled fruits.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.
