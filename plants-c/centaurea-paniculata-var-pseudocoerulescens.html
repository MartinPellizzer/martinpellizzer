Centaurea paniculata var. pseudocoerulescens

Centaurea paniculata var. pseudocoerulescens, commonly known as the bluehead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its blue to purple flower heads are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, and it produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly bluish hue compared to other centaury species.
