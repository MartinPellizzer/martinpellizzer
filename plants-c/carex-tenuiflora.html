Carex tenuiflora

Carex tenuiflora, commonly known as slender-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex tenuiflora plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
