Cubeba arcuata

Cubeba arcuata, commonly known as cubeb or Java pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark purple to black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its roots and seeds contain essential oils rich in cubebene, a compound with various pharmacological properties.

Cubeba arcuata thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic value.
