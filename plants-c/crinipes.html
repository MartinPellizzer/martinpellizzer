Crinipes

Crinipes, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. Crinipes thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in certain regions.
