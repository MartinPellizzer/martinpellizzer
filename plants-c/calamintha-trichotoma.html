Calamintha trichotoma

Calamintha trichotoma, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Calamintha trichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
