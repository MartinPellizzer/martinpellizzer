Chamaecytisus ratisbonensis subsp. ruthenicus

Chamaecytisus ratisbonensis subsp.

ruthenicus, commonly known as the Ruthenian broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal or rocky garden settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
