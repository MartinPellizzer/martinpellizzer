Cassia × luerssenii

Cassia × luerssenii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a hybrid species within the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Cassia × luerssenii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities in soil.
