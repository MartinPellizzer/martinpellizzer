Campanopsis huillana

Campanopsis huillana, commonly known as the Huillan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or forested habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Campanopsis huillana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
