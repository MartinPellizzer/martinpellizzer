Carex arizonensis

Carex arizonensis, commonly known as Arizona sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Arizona sedge plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
