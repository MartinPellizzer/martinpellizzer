Carex mannii subsp. thomasii

Carex mannii subsp.

thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions of Tanzania and Kenya.

The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to yellowish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands.

Thomas's sedge is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
