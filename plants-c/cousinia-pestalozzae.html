Cousinia pestalozzae

Cousinia pestalozzae, commonly known as the Kashmiri cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cousinia pestalozzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
