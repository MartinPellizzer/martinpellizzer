Cuphea dipetala

Cuphea dipetala, commonly known as the two-petaled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Cuphea dipetala is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
