Cattleya × mesquitae

Cattleya × mesquitae, commonly known as the Mesquitae Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments.

The species is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
