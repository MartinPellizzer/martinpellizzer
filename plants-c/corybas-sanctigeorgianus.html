Corybas sanctigeorgianus

Corybas sanctigeorgianus, commonly known as the Georgean hooded orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that encloses the reproductive organs, a feature typical of the Corybas genus. The plant produces small, dark red to black flowers that are often hidden beneath the hood, which serves to protect the reproductive parts from environmental stress. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it forms dense clumps of dark green leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its ecological niche.
