Cupania oppositifolia

Cupania oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf cupania, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as stream banks and forest understories. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a reddish color, attracting various bird species.
