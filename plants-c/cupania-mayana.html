Cupania mayana

Cupania mayana, commonly known as the wild coffee or Amazonian coffee, is a woody shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.

Cupania mayana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
