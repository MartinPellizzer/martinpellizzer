Conyza bryoides

Conyza bryoides, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters. Conyza bryoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

It is also characterized by its distinctive seed dispersal mechanism, which involves the release of small, wind-borne seeds.
