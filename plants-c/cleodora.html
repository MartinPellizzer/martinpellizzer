Cleodora

Cleodora, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It consists of only one species, Cleodora corymbosa, which is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. It has opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Cleodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
