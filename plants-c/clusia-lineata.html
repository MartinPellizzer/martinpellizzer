Clusia lineata

Clusia lineata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It is highly tolerant of shade and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ability to retain its foliage year-round makes it a valuable ornamental plant in warm climates.
