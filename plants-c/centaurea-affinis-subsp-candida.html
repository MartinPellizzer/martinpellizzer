Centaurea affinis subsp. candida

Centaurea affinis subsp.

candida, commonly known as the white centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, composite flower heads consisting of blue to purple ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
