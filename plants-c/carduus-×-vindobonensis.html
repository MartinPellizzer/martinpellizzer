Carduus × vindobonensis

Carduus × vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus carnipes and Carduus acanthoides.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas. It has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
