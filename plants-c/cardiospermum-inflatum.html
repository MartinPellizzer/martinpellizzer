Cardiospermum inflatum

Cardiospermum inflatum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated, balloon-like seed pods that develop from the flowers. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
