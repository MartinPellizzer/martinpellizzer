Clistanthocereus hertlingianus

Clistanthocereus hertlingianus, commonly known as the Hertling's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique flowering behavior and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
