Cardamine amara subsp. amara

Cardamine amara subsp.

amara, commonly known as bitter celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically palmately divided and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often appearing in early spring. Its roots are tuberous and contain various alkaloids, contributing to its bitter taste and medicinal uses.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
