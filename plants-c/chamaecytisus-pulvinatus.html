Chamaecytisus pulvinatus

Chamaecytisus pulvinatus, commonly known as the sweet pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that often have a reddish or brownish hue, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
