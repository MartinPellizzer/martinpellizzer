Croton boliviensis

Croton boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian croton, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, often featuring shades of green, yellow, and red. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits.

Croton boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
