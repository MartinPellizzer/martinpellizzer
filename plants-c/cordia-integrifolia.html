Cordia integrifolia

Cordia integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cordia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually entire or slightly lobed. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
