Carex andalusica

Carex andalusica, commonly known as the Andalusian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex andalusica produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
