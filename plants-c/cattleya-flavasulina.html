Cattleya flavasulina

Cattleya flavasulina, commonly known as the yellow-lipped cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers with a distinctive lip that has a yellowish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in tropical rainforests. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya flavasulina is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and unique floral structure.
