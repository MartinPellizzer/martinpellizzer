Carduus caulescens

Carduus caulescens, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, prickly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Carduus caulescens is considered a hardy species with some ornamental value in gardens.
