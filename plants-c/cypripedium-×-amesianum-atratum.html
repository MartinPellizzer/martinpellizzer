Cypripedium × amesianum atratum

Cypripedium × amesianum atratum, commonly known as the black-lace lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid with distinctive botanical features.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and a unique, intricate slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are often a deep purple or black, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
