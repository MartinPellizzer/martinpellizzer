Circaea alpina var. minor

Circaea alpina var. minor, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
