Capparis blanchetii

Capparis blanchetii, commonly known as the Madagascar caper, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
