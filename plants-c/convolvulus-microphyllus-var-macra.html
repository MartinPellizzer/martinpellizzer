Convolvulus microphyllus var. macra

Convolvulus microphyllus var. macra, commonly known as the small-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
