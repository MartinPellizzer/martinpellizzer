Cinnamomum camphora var. lanatum

Cinnamomum camphora var. lanatum, commonly known as the hairy camphor laurel, is a variant of the camphor tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to other varieties. The plant is evergreen, with lance-shaped foliage that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine and essential oils.
