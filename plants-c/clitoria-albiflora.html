Clitoria albiflora

Clitoria albiflora, commonly known as the white orchid bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a climbing legume with compound leaves and produces showy, butterfly-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clitoria albiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
