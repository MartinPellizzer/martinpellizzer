Chamaenerion crassifolium

Chamaenerion crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and large, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Chamaenerion crassifolium is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
