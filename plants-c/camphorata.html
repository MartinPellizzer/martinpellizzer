Camphorata

Camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic wood. The plant contains camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent. Camphorata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
