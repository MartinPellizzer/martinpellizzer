Cestrum jacaltenanginum var. jacaltenanginum

Cestrum jacaltenanginum var. jacaltenanginum, commonly known as the Jacaltenango nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It produces small, red berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds.
