Cornus oblonga

Cornus oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by bright red berries. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant yellow or red in autumn. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
