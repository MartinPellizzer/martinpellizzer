Comanthera chrysolepis

Comanthera chrysolepis, commonly known as the goldenleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange thallus, which gives it its common name. The lichen consists of a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to photosynthesize and absorb nutrients from the substrate. It is typically found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for ecosystem health.
