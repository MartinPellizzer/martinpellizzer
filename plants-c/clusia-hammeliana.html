Clusia hammeliana

Clusia hammeliana, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It is well adapted to humid, shady environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Clusia hammeliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate partial shade.
