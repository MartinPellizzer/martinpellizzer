Copiapoa schulziana

Copiapoa schulziana, commonly known as the Schulz's copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and a gray-green coloration. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to arid conditions with a thick, water-storing stem and spines for protection.

Copiapoa schulziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
