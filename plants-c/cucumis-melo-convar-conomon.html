Cucumis melo convar. conomon

Cucumis melo convar. conomon, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It is a vine that produces large, orange-colored fruits with a distinctive honeycomb pattern on the skin. The fruit develops from the female flower and is typically harvested when it reaches full maturity and turns a deep orange color.
