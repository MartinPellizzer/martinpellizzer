Cecropia sciadophylla var. subsessilis

Cecropia sciadophylla var. subsessilis, commonly known as the short-stalked ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common species in secondary forests. Its hollow, buttressed trunk provides habitat for various animals, and it plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems.

The varietal form subsessilis is distinguished by its shorter petioles compared to other varieties of Cecropia sciadophylla.
