Ceanothus guadalupae

Ceanothus guadalupae, commonly known as the Guadalupe ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Southern California.

It features aromatic, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that provide dense cover. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
