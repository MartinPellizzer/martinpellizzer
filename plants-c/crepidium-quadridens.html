Crepidium quadridens

Crepidium quadridens, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are small and usually greenish-white, with four prominent teeth on the lip.

Crepidium quadridens has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces numerous small, oval-shaped leaves.
