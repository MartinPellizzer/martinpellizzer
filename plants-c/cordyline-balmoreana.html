Cordyline balmoreana

Cordyline balmoreana, commonly known as the mountain cordyline, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish, followed by ornamental berries.

Cordyline balmoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add vertical interest to garden landscapes.
