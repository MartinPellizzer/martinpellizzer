Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. nemorensis

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

nemorensis, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are encased in a waxy coating.

The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ecological importance as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
