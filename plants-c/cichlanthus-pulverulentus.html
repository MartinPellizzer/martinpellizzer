Cichlanthus pulverulentus, commonly known as the dusty cichlanthus, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a dusty appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture due to the dense pubescence.

Cichlanthus pulverulentus is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
