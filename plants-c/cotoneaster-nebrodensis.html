Cotoneaster nebrodensis

Cotoneaster nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, orange-red berries that appear in the autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of white flowers in the spring.

Cotoneaster nebrodensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
