Cistus hirsutus var. nigrescens

Cistus hirsutus var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-hairy rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It features wiry, glandular-stemmed foliage that is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a greyish appearance. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and often have a resinous scent.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
