Cochemiea theresae

Cochemiea theresae, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, mound-forming plant with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Cochemiea theresae is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
