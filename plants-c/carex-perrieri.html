Carex perrieri

Carex perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and is characterized by its dense, rhizomatous growth habit.

Carex perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
