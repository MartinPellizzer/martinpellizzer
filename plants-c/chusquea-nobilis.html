Chusquea nobilis

Chusquea nobilis, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its structural strength and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes used in traditional construction and ornamental horticulture.
