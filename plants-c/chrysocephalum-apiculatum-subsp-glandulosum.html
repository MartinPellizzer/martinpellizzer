Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. glandulosum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular everlasting or sticky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of yellow to golden-orange flowers.

The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
