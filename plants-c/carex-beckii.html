Carex beckii

Carex beckii, commonly known as the Beck's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex beckii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
