Cyrtostachys lakka var. singaporensis

Cyrtostachys lakka var. singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical cities.
