Chamaefistula fagifolia

Chamaefistula fagifolia, commonly known as the Turkish lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blossoms and compact growth habit.
