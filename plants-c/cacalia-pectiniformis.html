Cacalia pectiniformis

Cacalia pectiniformis, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cacalia pectiniformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
