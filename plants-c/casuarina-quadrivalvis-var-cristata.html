Casuarina quadrivalvis var. cristata

Casuarina quadrivalvis var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate she-oak, is a unique variant of the she-oak genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, typical of the Casuarinaceae family. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune ecosystems.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
