Carex mossii

Carex mossii, commonly known as Moss's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Carex mossii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in challenging wetland conditions.
