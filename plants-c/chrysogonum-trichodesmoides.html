Chrysogonum trichodesmoides

Chrysogonum trichodesmoides, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
