Comastoma cyananthiflorum var. cyananthiflorum

Comastoma cyananthiflorum var. cyananthiflorum, commonly known as the blueflower comastoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland habitats.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by bees and other small insects.
