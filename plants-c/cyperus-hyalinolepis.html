Cyperus hyalinolepis

Cyperus hyalinolepis, commonly known as the glassy flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

The plant's ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels contributes to its ecological significance in wetland habitats.
