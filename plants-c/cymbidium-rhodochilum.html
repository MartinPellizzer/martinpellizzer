Cymbidium rhodochilum

Cymbidium rhodochilum, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in winter. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. Its flowers feature a striking red lip, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Cymbidium rhodochilum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
