Clematis zollingeri

Clematis zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
