Carludovica fanshawei

Carludovica fanshawei, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, spiny stems. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and can be quite large, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
