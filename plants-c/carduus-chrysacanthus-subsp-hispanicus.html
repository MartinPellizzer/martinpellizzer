Carduus chrysacanthus subsp. hispanicus

Carduus chrysacanthus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish golden thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
