Carex halei

Carex halei, commonly known as Hale's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Carex halei is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
