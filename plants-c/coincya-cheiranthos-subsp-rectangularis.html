Coincya cheiranthos subsp. rectangularis

Coincya cheiranthos subsp.

rectangularis, commonly known as the rectangular water clover, is a aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette formation, with each leaf blade being broad and rectangular in shape, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. It possesses a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in the substrate and facilitates nutrient absorption.

The leaves are usually a vibrant green, and the plant is known for its ability to provide habitat and oxygenation in aquatic ecosystems.
