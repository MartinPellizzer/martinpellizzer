Cereus serpentinus

Cereus serpentinus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, a trait known as nyctinasty. Its stems are succulent, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming habits.
