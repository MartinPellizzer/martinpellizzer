Combretum oxygonium

Combretum oxygonium, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to flake off in small pieces.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
