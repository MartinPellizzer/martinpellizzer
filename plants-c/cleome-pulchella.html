Cleome pulchella

Cleome pulchella, commonly known as the pretty cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance ornamental plant.

Cleome pulchella is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
