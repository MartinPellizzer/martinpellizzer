Cypholophus radicans

Cypholophus radicans, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, often with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cypholophus radicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
