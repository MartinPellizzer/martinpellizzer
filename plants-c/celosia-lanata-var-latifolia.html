Celosia lanata var. latifolia

Celosia lanata var. latifolia, commonly known as the woolly cockscomb, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fluffy, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, which are often vibrant in red, orange, or pink hues. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its soft, textured appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Celosia lanata var. latifolia is also known for its long blooming period, typically from summer to fall.
