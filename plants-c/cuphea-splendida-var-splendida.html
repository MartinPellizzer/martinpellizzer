Cuphea splendida var. splendida

Cuphea splendida var. splendida, commonly known as the flame beak plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
