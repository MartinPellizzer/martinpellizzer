Cyperus hemisphaericus var. gregorii

Cyperus hemisphaericus var. gregorii, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spherical clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
