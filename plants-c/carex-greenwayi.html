Carex greenwayi

Carex greenwayi, commonly known as Greenway's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in wetland and marsh habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Carex greenwayi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
