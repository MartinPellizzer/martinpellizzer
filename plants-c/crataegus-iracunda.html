Crataegus iracunda

Crataegus iracunda, commonly known as the thornapple or devil's-bit scabious, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus iracunda is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
