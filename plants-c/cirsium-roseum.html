Cirsium roseum

Cirsium roseum, commonly known as the rose thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium roseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its prickly foliage.
