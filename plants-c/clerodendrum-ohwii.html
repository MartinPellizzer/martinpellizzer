Clerodendrum ohwii

Clerodendrum ohwii, commonly known as the Chinese flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
