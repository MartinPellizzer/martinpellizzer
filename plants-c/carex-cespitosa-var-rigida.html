Carex cespitosa var. rigida

Carex cespitosa var. rigida, commonly known as rigid sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, upright leaves that are usually dark green and slightly rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
