Crepis newii subsp. oliveriana

Crepis newii subsp.

oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
