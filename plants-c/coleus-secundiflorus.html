Coleus secundiflorus

Coleus secundiflorus, commonly known as the red-leaved coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Coleus secundiflorus is primarily cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf coloration.
