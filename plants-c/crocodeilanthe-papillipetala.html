Crocodeilanthe papillipetala

Crocodeilanthe papillipetala, commonly known as the papillipetal orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy-like petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
