Cucumis parviflorus

Cucumis parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cucumbers but are typically less commonly cultivated. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes grown in gardens and greenhouses.
