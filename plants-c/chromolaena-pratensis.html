Chromolaena pratensis

Chromolaena pratensis, commonly known as black curlleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features dark green, waxy leaves with a distinctive curling edge, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread aggressively through rhizomes.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
