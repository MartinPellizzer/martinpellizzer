Cistanche rosea

Cistanche rosea, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nourishment.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive, branching structure with thin, thread-like stems. It is often found growing on the roots of various shrubs and grasses.
