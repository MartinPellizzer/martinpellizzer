Caesalpinia violacea

Caesalpinia violacea, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in long, pendulous clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The pods that follow the flowers are elongated and contain several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
