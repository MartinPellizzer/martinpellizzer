Cyclostemon falcatus

Cyclostemon falcatus, commonly known as the sickle cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a short, stout trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are often described as sickle-shaped, giving it its common name. Its cones are distinctive, with male cones being elongated and female cones being more compact.

Cyclostemon falcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
