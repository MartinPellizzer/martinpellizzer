Chamaedorea bracteata

Chamaedorea bracteata, commonly known as the Mexican feather plant, is a tropical evergreen that belongs to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its elegant appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
