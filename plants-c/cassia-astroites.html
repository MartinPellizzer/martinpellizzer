Cassia astroites

Cassia astroites, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia astroites thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
