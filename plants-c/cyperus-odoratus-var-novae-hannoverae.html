Cyperus odoratus var. novae-hannoverae

Cyperus odoratus var. novae-hannoverae, commonly known as the New Hanover sweetgrass, is a variant of the sweetgrass plant native to wetland habitats.

It is a perennial grass with slender, cylindrical stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal make it a valued plant in horticulture and traditional crafts.
