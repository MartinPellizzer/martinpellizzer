Cornopteris oosora

Cornopteris oosora, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow fronds that contrast with its dark green stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cornopteris oosora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
