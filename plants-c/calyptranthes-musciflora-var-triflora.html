Calyptranthes musciflora var. triflora

Calyptranthes musciflora var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host trees, while also performing photosynthesis. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.
