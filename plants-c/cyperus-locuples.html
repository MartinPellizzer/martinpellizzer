Cyperus locuples

Cyperus locuples, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cyperus locuples is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
