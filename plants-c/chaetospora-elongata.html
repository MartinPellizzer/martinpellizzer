Chaetospora elongata

Chaetospora elongata, commonly known as the elongated haircap moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and dense, cushion-like growth form. The leaves are typically elongated and gradually tapering, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered capsules that are often covered in a protective peristome.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or on rocky substrates.
