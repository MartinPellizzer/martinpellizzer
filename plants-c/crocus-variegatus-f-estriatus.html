Crocus variegatus f. estriatus

Crocus variegatus f. estriatus, commonly known as the striated crocus, is a cultivar of the crocus plant characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

This variety features leaves with striking green and white stripes, which set it apart from other crocus species. It is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The flowers are typically purple or violet, with a unique, rounded shape and a central tuft of stamens.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative element in floral arrangements.
