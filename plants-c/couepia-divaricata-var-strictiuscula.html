Couepia divaricata var. strictiuscula

Couepia divaricata var. strictiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved couepia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by small, round berries. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in dry to semi-arid habitats, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
