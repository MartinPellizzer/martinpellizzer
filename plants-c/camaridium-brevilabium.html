Camaridium brevilabium

Camaridium brevilabium, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns.

Camaridium brevilabium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
