Cyperus lucidus var. grandiusculus

Cyperus lucidus var. grandiusculus, commonly known as the grandiose flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant.
