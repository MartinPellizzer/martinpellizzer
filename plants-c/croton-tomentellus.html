Croton tomentellus

Croton tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
