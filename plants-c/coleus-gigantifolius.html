Coleus gigantifolius

Coleus gigantifolius, commonly known as the giant-leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with striking red, purple, or white veins, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often grown for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
