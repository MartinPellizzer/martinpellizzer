Chloris divaricata

Chloris divaricata, commonly known as variable grass or slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often spreading stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Chloris divaricata is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration.
