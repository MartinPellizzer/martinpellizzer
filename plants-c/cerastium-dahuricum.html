Cerastium dahuricum

Cerastium dahuricum, commonly known as the Siberian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium dahuricum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
