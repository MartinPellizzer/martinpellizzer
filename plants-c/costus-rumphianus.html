Costus rumphianus

Costus rumphianus, commonly known as the zigzag ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a zigzag pattern along the edges, giving it its common name.

Costus rumphianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, spicy aroma similar to ginger.
