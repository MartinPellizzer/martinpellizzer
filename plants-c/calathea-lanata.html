Calathea lanata

Calathea lanata, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique and textured appearance. The leaves are typically a deep green with intricate patterns of silver or white veins, which become more prominent in low light conditions. This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect sunlight to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
