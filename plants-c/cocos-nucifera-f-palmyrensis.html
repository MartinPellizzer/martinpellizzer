Cocos nucifera f. palmyrensis

Cocos nucifera f. palmyrensis, commonly known as the ancient palm, is a botanical variant of the coconut palm characterized by its unique morphological features.

It typically exhibits a shorter stature compared to the standard coconut palm, with a more compact trunk and broader leaves. This form is notable for its historical significance, having been cultivated in ancient civilizations such as those in Mesopotamia and the Levant. The plant's leaves are often broader and more robust, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional architecture and crafts.
