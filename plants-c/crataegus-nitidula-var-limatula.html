Crataegus nitidula var. limatula

Crataegus nitidula var. limatula, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist to dry soils in woodlands and open areas.

Its leaves are usually oval with serrated edges, and it is often found in the eastern and central United States.
