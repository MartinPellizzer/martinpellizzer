Cyperus macrophyllus

Cyperus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish, which develop into three-lobed, triangular seed capsules. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus macrophyllus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
