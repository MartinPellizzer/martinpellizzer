Cinnamomum leptophyllum

Cinnamomum leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and commercial cultivation.
