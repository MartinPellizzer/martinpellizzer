Calamus axillaris

Calamus axillaris, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Calamus axillaris is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
