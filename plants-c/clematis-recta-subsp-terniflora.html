Clematis recta subsp. terniflora

Clematis recta subsp.

terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and its ability to produce clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, which are opposite or alternate in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Clematis recta subsp.

terniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
