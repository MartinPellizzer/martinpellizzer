Coleus elatus

Coleus elatus, commonly known as the spiked coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or white, surrounded by colorful bracts.

Coleus elatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
