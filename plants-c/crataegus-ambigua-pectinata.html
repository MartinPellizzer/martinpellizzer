Crataegus ambigua pectinata

Crataegus ambigua pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pectinate (toothed) leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sun.

Crataegus ambigua pectinata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
