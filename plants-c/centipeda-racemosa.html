Centipeda racemosa

Centipeda racemosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers along its stems. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Centipeda racemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
