Condalia seleri

Condalia seleri, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used as food by local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid regions.
