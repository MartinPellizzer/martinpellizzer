Convolvulus althaeoides var. hirsutus

Convolvulus althaeoides var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
