Capsella bursa-pastoris druceana

Capsella bursa-pastoris druceana, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.

It is characterized by its ability to produce numerous small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, facilitating its wide dispersal.
