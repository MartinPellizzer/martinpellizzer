Comarostaphylis ledifolia

Comarostaphylis ledifolia, commonly known as the rock rose or ledifolia rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in a compact, bushy form.

Comarostaphylis ledifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
