Croton corallicola

Croton corallicola, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble coral, which give the plant its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Croton corallicola thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
