Calamagrostis mandoniana

Calamagrostis mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically golden to brown in color, appearing in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis mandoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
