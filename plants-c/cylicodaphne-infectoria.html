Cylicodaphne infectoria

Cylicodaphne infectoria, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
