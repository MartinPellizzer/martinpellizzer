Caladenia fulva

Caladenia fulva, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southern Australia.

It features a single, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is typically golden-yellow with a dark red or purple lip. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms during the late winter to early spring season. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats.

The species is noted for its intricate floral structure and is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
