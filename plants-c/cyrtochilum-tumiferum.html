Cyrtochilum tumiferum

Cyrtochilum tumiferum, commonly known as the tumefied orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Cyrtochilum tumiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
