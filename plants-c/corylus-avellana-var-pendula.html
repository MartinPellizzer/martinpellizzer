Corylus avellana var. pendula

Corylus avellana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping hazel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that hang gracefully, creating a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by edible hazelnuts in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, oval, and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
