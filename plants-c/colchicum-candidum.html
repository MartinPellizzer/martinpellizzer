Colchicum candidum

Colchicum candidum, commonly known as the meadow colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces striking white to pale pink flowers in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has distinctive, waxy, and often reflexed petals, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The bulbs of Colchicum candidum contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans.
