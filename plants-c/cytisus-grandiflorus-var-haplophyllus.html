Cytisus grandiflorus var. haplophyllus

Cytisus grandiflorus var. haplophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, woody stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive grayish-green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
