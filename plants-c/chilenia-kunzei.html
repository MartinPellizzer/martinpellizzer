Chilenia kunzei

Chilenia kunzei, commonly known as the Chilean tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant features a tall, unbranched trunk with a crown of feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its large, pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Chilenia kunzei plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
