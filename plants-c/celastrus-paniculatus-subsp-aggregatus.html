Celastrus paniculatus subsp. aggregatus

Celastrus paniculatus subsp.

aggregatus, commonly known as the Indian star jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but contain toxic seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
