Cerasus chinensis

Cerasus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Prunus pseudocerasus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Cerasus chinensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
