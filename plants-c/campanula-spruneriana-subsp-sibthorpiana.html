Campanula spruneriana subsp. sibthorpiana

Campanula spruneriana subsp.

sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Sibthorpian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
