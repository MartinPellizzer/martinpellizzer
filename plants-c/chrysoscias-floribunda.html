Chrysoscias floribunda

Chrysoscias floribunda, commonly known as the flowering chrysoscia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely cultivated outside its native range.
