Campomanesia maschalantha

Campomanesia maschalantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
