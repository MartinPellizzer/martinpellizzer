Crocus archibaldiorum

Crocus archibaldiorum, commonly known as the Archibald's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in areas of Spain and Portugal. This perennial plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early bloom and unique appearance.
