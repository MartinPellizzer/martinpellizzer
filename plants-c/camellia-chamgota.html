Camellia chamgota

Camellia chamgota, commonly known as the Kashmiri camellia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, often with a waxy coating.

Camellia chamgota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
