Carex bermudiana

Carex bermudiana, commonly known as Bermuda sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Bermuda and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and salt marshes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
