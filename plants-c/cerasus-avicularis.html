Cerasus avicularis

Cerasus avicularis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a species of flowering plant in the subfamily Prunoideae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, turning a vibrant green during the growing season.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a deep red or black color, often used in culinary and ornamental applications.
