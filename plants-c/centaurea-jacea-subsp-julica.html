Centaurea jacea subsp. julica

Centaurea jacea subsp.

julica, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with purple or reddish markings at the base, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
