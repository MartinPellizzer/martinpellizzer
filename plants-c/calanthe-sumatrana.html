Calanthe sumatrana

Calanthe sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves and bears intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed tropical habitats.
