Cassia glauca var. koenigii

Cassia glauca var. koenigii, commonly known as Indian cinnamon or cinnamon tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic bark and ornamental value.

The leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cinnamon.
