Cirsium wakasugianum

Cirsium wakasugianum, commonly known as the Wakasuga thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Cirsium wakasugianum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
