Conospermum linifolium

Conospermum linifolium, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, pink or red flower spikes. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The silky hakea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
