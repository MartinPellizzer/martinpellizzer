Croomia japonica var. heterosepala

Croomia japonica var. heterosepala, commonly known as the Japanese croomia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
