Clusia fragrans

Clusia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both drought and salt spray, making it suitable for coastal landscapes. Clusia fragrans is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
