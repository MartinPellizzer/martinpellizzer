Chionanthus virginicus var. latifolius

Chionanthus virginicus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, raceme-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. This variety is often found in moist, woodland habitats and can grow up to 6 feet tall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
