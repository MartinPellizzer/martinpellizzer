Cocos flexuosa var. cataphracta

Cocos flexuosa var. cataphracta, commonly known as the cataphracta coconut, is a variant of the flexuosa coconut species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruit with a thin, fibrous husk. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and arid conditions. Its leaves are long and pinnate, providing shade and contributing to its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.

This variant is of interest for its potential in horticulture and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
