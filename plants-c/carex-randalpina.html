Carex randalpina

Carex randalpina, commonly known as Randal's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, triangular leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex randalpina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
