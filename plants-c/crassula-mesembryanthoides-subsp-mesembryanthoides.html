Crassula mesembryanthoides subsp. mesembryanthoides

Crassula mesembryanthoides subsp.

mesembryanthoides, commonly known as the rock fig or stone plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
