Cyathea okiana

Cyathea okiana, commonly known as the Okinawa tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of its expansive fronds.

Cyathea okiana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
