Chionanthus pauciflorus

Chionanthus pauciflorus, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
