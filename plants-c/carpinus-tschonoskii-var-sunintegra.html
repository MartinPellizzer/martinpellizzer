Carpinus tschonoskii var. sunintegra

Carpinus tschonoskii var. sunintegra, commonly known as the Sun Integra hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for urban landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
