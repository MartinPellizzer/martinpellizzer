Chionochloa vireta

Chionochloa vireta, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, silky hairs on its leaves. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows and mountainous regions. Its flowering stems are slender and bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chionochloa vireta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
