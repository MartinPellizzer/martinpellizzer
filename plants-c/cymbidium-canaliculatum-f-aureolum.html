Cymbidium canaliculatum f. aureolum

Cymbidium canaliculatum f. aureolum, commonly known as the golden cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and the production of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinct golden hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in winter or early spring, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens and greenhouses.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique coloration and fragrance.
