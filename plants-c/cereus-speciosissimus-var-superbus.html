Cereus speciosissimus var. superbus

Cereus speciosissimus var. superbus, commonly known as the giant night blooming cereus, is a striking cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. These flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Its slow growth rate and dramatic nighttime blooming make it a popular and unique addition to desert gardens.
