Chloris mensensis

Chloris mensensis, commonly known as the Menzies' grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris mensensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
