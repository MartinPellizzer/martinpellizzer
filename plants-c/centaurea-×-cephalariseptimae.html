Centaurea × cephalariseptimae

Centaurea × cephalariseptimae, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its composite flower structure, consisting of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea × cephalariseptimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
