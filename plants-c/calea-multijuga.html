Calea multijuga

Calea multijuga, commonly known as the "Mexican ghost flower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble ghostly faces, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Calea multijuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
