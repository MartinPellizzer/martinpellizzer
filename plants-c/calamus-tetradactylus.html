Calamus tetradactylus

Calamus tetradactylus, commonly known as the four-fingered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Calamus tetradactylus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
