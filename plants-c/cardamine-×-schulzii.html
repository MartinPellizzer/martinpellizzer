Cardamine × schulzii

Cardamine × schulzii, commonly known as the Schultze's buttercup, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
