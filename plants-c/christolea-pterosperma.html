Christolea pterosperma

Christolea pterosperma, commonly known as the winged bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive winged pods that enclose the edible seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its high protein content.

Christolea pterosperma is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
