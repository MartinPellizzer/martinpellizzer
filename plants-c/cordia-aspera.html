Cordia aspera

Cordia aspera, commonly known as rough cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cordia aspera is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
