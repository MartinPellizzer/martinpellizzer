Chromolaena subscandens

Chromolaena subscandens, commonly known as the false chaff tree, is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological studies.
