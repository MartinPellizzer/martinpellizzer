Cristaria bipinnatifida

Cristaria bipinnatifida, commonly known as the featherleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a bipinnate pattern, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
