Citrus medica f. sudachi

Citrus medica f. sudachi, commonly known as sudachi, is a citrus fruit native to Japan.

It is a small, round citrus fruit with a thin, smooth skin that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow when mature. The fruit has a high concentration of citric acid, giving it a sharp, tart flavor. It is often used in Japanese cuisine for its intense flavor and aromatic qualities.

Botanically, it is a hybrid of the citron and mandarin, belonging to the Rutaceae family.
