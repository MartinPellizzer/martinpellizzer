Columella japonica var. dentata

Columella japonica var. dentata, commonly known as the Japanese columnar fern, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that grow in a columnar form, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a finely toothed margin that contributes to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its elegant, architectural form and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
