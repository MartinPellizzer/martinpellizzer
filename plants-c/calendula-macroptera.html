Calendula macroptera

Calendula macroptera, commonly known as the giant marigold, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers with prominent, elongated petals. Its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in full sun.

Calendula macroptera is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
