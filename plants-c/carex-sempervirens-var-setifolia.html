Carex sempervirens var. setifolia

Carex sempervirens var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or damp soil and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
