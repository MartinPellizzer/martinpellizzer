Campanula teucrioides

Campanula teucrioides, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula teucrioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
