Capparis brevisiliqua

Capparis brevisiliqua, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, thornless stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, edible, green berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
