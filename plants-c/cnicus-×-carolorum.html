Cnicus × carolorum

Cnicus × carolorum, commonly known as the hybrid sowthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cnicus benedictus and Cnicus arvensis, characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has finely toothed leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions.
