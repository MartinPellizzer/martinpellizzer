Cinchona dissimiliflora

Cinchona dissimiliflora, commonly known as the yellow quina, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates. It is closely related to other cinchona species, some of which are sources of quinine, a compound used in medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
