Coreopsis diffusa

Coreopsis diffusa, commonly known as the hairy coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features wiry, branching stems and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Coreopsis diffusa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
