Cuphonotus australis

Cuphonotus australis, commonly known as the Australian cup fern, is a small, delicate fern native to southeastern Australia.

It has a unique, compact growth habit with closely clustered fronds that resemble a cup or shield. The plant features a rhizome that produces both roots and short, branched shoots. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, curled appearance.

Cuphonotus australis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
