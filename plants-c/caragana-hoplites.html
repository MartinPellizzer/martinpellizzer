Caragana hoplites

Caragana hoplites, commonly known as the thorny locust, is a shrub belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Caragana hoplites is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a common species in steppe and semi-desert habitats.
