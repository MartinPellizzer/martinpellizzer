Ceiba rosea

Ceiba rosea, commonly known as the rosewood ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its density and beauty, making it a significant resource in local economies.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
