Chamaefistula contorta

Chamaefistula contorta, commonly known as the twisted boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Chamaefistula contorta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
