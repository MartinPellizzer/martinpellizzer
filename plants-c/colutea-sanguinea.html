Colutea sanguinea

Colutea sanguinea, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
