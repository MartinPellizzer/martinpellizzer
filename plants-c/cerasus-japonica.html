Cerasus japonica

Cerasus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are highly valued in culinary uses. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Cerasus japonica is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
