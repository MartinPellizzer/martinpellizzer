Clematis violacea var. rubromarginata

Clematis violacea var. rubromarginata, commonly known as the red-marginated clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-blue flowers with distinctive red margins on the petals. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can grow up to 15 feet in height.

Clematis violacea var. rubromarginata is relatively hardy and requires support for its climbing habit.
