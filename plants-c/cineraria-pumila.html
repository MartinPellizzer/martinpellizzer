Cineraria pumila

Cineraria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cineraria, is a low-growing succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
