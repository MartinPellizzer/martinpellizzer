Cucumis melo var. azmirli

Cucumis melo var. azmirli, commonly known as the Azmirli melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruit with a thick, waxy rind and sweet, juicy flesh. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. It typically grows on a vigorous vine that requires support for optimal growth.

The Azmirli melon is prized for its flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and home garden use.
