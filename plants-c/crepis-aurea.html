Crepis aurea

Crepis aurea, commonly known as the golden hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Crepis aurea is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
