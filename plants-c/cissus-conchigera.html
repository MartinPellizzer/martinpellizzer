Cissus conchigera

Cissus conchigera, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy bumps. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits resembling coral, hence its common name. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus conchigera is also valued for its ability to climb and its attractive foliage.
