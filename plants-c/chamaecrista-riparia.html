Chamaecrista riparia

Chamaecrista riparia, commonly known as the river cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and is adapted to wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
