Celtis rendleana

Celtis rendleana, commonly known as the Philippine hackberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.
