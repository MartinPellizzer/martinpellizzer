Carex sambiranensis

Carex sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
