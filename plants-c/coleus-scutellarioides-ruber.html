Coleus scutellarioides ruber

Coleus scutellarioides ruber, commonly known as the red-veined coleus, is a vibrant tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep green leaves with prominent red veins that create a bold visual contrast. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for containers, borders, or as a trailing plant.
