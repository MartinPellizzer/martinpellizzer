Centaurea alba var. latronum

Centaurea alba var. latronum, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces slender, bristly seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
