Cotula globifera

Cotula globifera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it its common name. Cotula globifera is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
