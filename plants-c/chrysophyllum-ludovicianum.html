Chrysophyllum ludovicianum

Chrysophyllum ludovicianum, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and yellow-orange star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruits are edible when mature and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
