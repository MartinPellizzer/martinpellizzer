Catasetum × splendens var. flavescens

Catasetum × splendens var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are often adorned with contrasting purple or red markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, spurred flowers that attract specific pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its complex pollination mechanism involving specialized insect symbiosis.
