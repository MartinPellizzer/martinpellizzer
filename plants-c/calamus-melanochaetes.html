Calamus melanochaetes

Calamus melanochaetes, commonly known as the black-haired rattan, is a climbing plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by triangular, winged fruits. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Calamus melanochaetes is valued for its durable and lightweight rattan-like material, making it an important resource in local economies.
