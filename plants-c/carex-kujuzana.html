Carex kujuzana

Carex kujuzana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, boat-shaped fruits.

Carex kujuzana is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
