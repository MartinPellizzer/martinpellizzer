Carex druceana

Carex druceana, commonly known as Druce's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often found in shaded environments.

Carex druceana is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
