Crataegus monogyna f. subpinnatifida

Crataegus monogyna f. subpinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, subpinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
