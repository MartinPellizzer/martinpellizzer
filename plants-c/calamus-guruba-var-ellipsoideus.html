Calamus guruba var. ellipsoideus

Calamus guruba var. ellipsoideus, commonly known as the elliptic calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, fibrous fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
