Chrysocyathus davidi

Chrysocyathus davidi, commonly known as the golden cup coral.

This marine invertebrate belongs to the order Scleractinia and is characterized by its calcareous skeleton. It forms branching, cup-shaped colonies that provide habitat for various marine organisms. The polyps are typically small and have a distinctive golden coloration.

It is found in shallow tropical waters, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region.
