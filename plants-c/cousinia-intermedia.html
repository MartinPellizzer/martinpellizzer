Cousinia intermedia

Cousinia intermedia, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and can thrive in harsh, low-nutrient conditions.

Cousinia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
