Cirsium mongolicum

Cirsium mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the steppes of Mongolia and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across arid and semi-arid areas.

Cirsium mongolicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
