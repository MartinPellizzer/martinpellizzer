Convolvulus aggregatus

Convolvulus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are heart-shaped with a rough texture. Its tendrils allow it to climb and twine around other plants or structures.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
