Cynoglossum pictum

Cynoglossum pictum, commonly known as the painted tongue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable addition to gardens.

Cynoglossum pictum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
