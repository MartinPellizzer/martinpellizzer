Cassia portoricensis

Cassia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican cassia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Cassia portoricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
