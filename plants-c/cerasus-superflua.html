Cerasus superflua

Cerasus superflua, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and is renowned for its showy, pink or white blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin, while its fruits are small, round drupes that ripen to a dark red or black color.

Cerasus superflua is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in many Asian countries.
