Carex pedunculata var. erythrobasis

Carex pedunculata var. erythrobasis, commonly known as the red-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistently moist soil conditions.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
