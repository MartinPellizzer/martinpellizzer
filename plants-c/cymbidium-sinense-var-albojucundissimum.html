Cymbidium sinense var. albojucundissimum

Cymbidium sinense var. albojucundissimum, commonly known as the White Joy Cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to the delicate blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
