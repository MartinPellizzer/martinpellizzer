Calliandra yucatanensis

Calliandra yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of small, bright red or pink flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Calliandra yucatanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
