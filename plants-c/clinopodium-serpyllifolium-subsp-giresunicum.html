Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp. giresunicum

Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp.

giresunicum, commonly known as alpine woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
