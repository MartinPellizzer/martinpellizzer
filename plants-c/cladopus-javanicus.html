Cladopus javanicus

Cladopus javanicus, commonly known as the Java cliff fern, is a climbing fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on rocky surfaces or in dense forests. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped pinnae that provide a lush, green appearance.

Cladopus javanicus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
