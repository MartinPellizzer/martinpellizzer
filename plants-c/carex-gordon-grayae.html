Carex gordon-grayae

Carex gordon-grayae, commonly known as Gordon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in texture. Carex gordon-grayae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
