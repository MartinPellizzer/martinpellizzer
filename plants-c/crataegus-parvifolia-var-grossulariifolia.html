Crataegus parvifolia var. grossulariifolia

Crataegus parvifolia var. grossulariifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits called pomes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
