Cnicus flavispinus

Cnicus flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The yellow centaury is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
