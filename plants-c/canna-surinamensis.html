Canna surinamensis

Canna surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam canna, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Canna surinamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
