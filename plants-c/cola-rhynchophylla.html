Cola rhynchophylla

Cola rhynchophylla, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces hard, woody pods that contain seeds surrounded by a stimulant-rich pulp. It is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
