Convolvulus microphyllus var. boissieri

Convolvulus microphyllus var. boissieri, commonly known as the small-leaved bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The leaves are small, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically pale purple or pink, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure at the base.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient addition to dry gardens.
