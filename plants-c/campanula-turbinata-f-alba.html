Campanula turbinata f. alba

Campanula turbinata f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in summer, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.
