Cordia tomentosa

Cordia tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cordia tomentosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
