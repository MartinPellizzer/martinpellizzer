Cineraria lyratipartita

Cineraria lyratipartita, commonly known as the lyrate cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
