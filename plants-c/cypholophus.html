Cypholophus

Cypholophus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically velvety and have a distinctive rosette shape, often with a waxy coating. Cypholophus species are popular as ornamental plants due to their compact size and vibrant blooms.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
