Ceanothus latifolius

Ceanothus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
