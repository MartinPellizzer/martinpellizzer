Cercocarpus mexicanus

Cercocarpus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican curlleaf, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that curl at the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
