Chamaecrista benthamiana

Chamaecrista benthamiana, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, twisted pods. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Chamaecrista benthamiana is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
