Carex atrata subsp. magellanica

Carex atrata subsp.

magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
