Crataegus oxyacantha var. cossonii

Crataegus oxyacantha var. cossonii, commonly known as the Cosson's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in late summer. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
