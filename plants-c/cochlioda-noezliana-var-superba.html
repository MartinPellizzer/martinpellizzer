Cochlioda noezliana var. superba

Cochlioda noezliana var. superba, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers resembling stars. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water and thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
