Croton meridionalis var. latifolius

Croton meridionalis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
