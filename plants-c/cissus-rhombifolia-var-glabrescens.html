Cissus rhombifolia var. glabrescens

Cissus rhombifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, and slightly waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its ability to climb makes it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets in indoor and outdoor settings.
