Callitris glauca

Callitris glauca, commonly known as the white cypress-pine, is a species of conifer native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with a conical shape, and its leaves are scale-like, arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, inland regions.

Callitris glauca plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
