Coleus ruckeri

Coleus ruckeri, commonly known as the Rucker's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Coleus ruckeri is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
