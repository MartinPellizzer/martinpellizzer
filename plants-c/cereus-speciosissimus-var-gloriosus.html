Cereus speciosissimus var. gloriosus

Cereus speciosissimus var. gloriosus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance and can remain open for several days.

The species is well adapted to arid environments, with thick stems that store water and reduce transpiration.
