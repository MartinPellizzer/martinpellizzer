Cuphea enneanthera

Cuphea enneanthera, commonly known as the nine-angled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a bushy growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cuphea enneanthera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
