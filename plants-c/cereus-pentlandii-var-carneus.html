Cereus pentlandii var. carneus

Cereus pentlandii var. carneus, commonly known as the red cereus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that are often covered in white or pale-colored spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, blooming at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.
