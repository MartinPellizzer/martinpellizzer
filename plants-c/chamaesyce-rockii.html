Chamaesyce rockii

Chamaesyce rockii, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, blooming from spring to early summer.

Chamaesyce rockii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
