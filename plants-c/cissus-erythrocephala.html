Cissus erythrocephala

Cissus erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded areas.

Cissus erythrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
