Ceratanthera pendula

Ceratanthera pendula, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination.

Ceratanthera pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
