Cytisopsis pseudocytisus

Cytisopsis pseudocytisus, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cytisopsis pseudocytisus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.
