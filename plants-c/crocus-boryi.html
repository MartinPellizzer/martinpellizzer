Crocus boryi

Crocus boryi, commonly known as the Bory's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Siberia. The plant produces delicate, early spring blooms with purple to violet flowers that are often adorned with a yellow or white center. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Crocus boryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early flowering habit.
