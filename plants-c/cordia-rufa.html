Cordia rufa

Cordia rufa, commonly known as the red cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cordia rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
