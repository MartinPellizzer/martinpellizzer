Carpha arundinacea

Carpha arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it thrives in wet or moist environments.

Giant reed is often used in landscaping and has been introduced to other regions for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
