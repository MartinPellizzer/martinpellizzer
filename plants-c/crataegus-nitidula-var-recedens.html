Crataegus nitidula var. recedens

Crataegus nitidula var. recedens, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
