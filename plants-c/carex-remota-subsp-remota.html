Carex remota subsp. remota

Carex remota subsp.

remota, commonly known as the remote sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
