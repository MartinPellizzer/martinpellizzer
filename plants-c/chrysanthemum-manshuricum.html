Chrysanthemum manshuricum

Chrysanthemum manshuricum, commonly known as the Far Eastern chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
