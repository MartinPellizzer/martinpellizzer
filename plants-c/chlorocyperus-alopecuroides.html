Chlorocyperus alopecuroides

Chlorocyperus alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chlorocyperus alopecuroides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various wetland environments.
