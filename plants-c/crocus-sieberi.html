Crocus sieberi

Crocus sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms with purple or violet petals and a yellow center. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, allowing it to emerge quickly in spring.

The flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
