Capparis brachybotrya f. brachybotrya

Capparis brachybotrya f. brachybotrya, commonly known as the narrow-fruited caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, and it is known for its strong, pungent aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
