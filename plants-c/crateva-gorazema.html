Crateva gorazema

Crateva gorazema, commonly known as the Indian balsam or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
