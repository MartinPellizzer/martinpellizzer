Carex aspericaulis

Carex aspericaulis, commonly known as roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, rough-textured leaves that are typically dark green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex aspericaulis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
