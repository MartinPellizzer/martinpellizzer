Carex schrenkiana

Carex schrenkiana, commonly known as Schrenk's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. The plant produces triangular, three-sided stems that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
