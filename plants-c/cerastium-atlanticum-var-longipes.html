Cerastium atlanticum var. longipes

Cerastium atlanticum var. longipes, commonly known as the Atlantic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It features small, hairy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are white, star-shaped, and typically appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of salt marshes.

This plant is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
