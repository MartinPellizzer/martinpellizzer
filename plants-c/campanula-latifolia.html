Campanula latifolia

Campanula latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanula latifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
