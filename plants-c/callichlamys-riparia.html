Callichlamys riparia

Callichlamys riparia, commonly known as the river water-plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along rivers, streams, and lakeshores. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually greenish-white in color.

Callichlamys riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing aquatic environments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
