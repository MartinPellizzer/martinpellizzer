Cytisus capitatus subsp. vulgaris

Cytisus capitatus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in sunny, well-drained soils. It has a long flowering period, typically from spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a hedge.
