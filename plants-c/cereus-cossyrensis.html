Cereus cossyrensis

Cereus cossyrensis, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a striking epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features tall, spindly stems that branch into clusters of fleshy, paddle-shaped segments. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, which help it conserve water in its natural habitat.

Cereus cossyrensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation, requiring bright light and well-draining soil.
