Crotonogyne thonneri

Crotonogyne thonneri, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its foliage and resilience.
