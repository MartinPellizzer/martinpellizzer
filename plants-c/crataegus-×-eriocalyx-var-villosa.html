Crataegus × eriocalyx var. villosa

Crataegus × eriocalyx var. villosa, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, often reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters. Its flowers are generally white or pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a red or orange pome that ripens in the fall.

This species is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.
