Clerodendrum verticillatum

Clerodendrum verticillatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered within the bracts, and it is typically cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Clerodendrum verticillatum is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
