Carex miyabei var. maopengensis

Carex miyabei var. maopengensis, commonly known as the Maopeng sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
