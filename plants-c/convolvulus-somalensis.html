Convolvulus somalensis

Convolvulus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Convolvulus somalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
