Cenchrus tripsaceus

Cenchrus tripsaceus, commonly known as the grassland dropseed, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion.

Cenchrus tripsaceus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
