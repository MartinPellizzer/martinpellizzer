Chaenostoma revolutum

Chaenostoma revolutum, commonly known as the curled water-mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive revolute (curled under) margin. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple and appear in whorled clusters along the stem.

Chaenostoma revolutum is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp environments.
