Carolus renidens

Carolus renidens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed carolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds, due to their rich nectar content.

Carolus renidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
