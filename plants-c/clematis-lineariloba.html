Clematis lineariloba

Clematis lineariloba, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, utilizing its twining stems to ascend supports. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Clematis lineariloba prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
