Cassia botteriana

Cassia botteriana, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia botteriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
