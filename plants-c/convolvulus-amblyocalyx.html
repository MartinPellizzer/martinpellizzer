Convolvulus amblyocalyx

Convolvulus amblyocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus amblyocalyx is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners.
