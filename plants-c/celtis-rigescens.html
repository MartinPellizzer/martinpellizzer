Celtis rigescens

Celtis rigescens, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical, dark blue berries. Celtis rigescens is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
