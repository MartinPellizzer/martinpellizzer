Clusia perscariosa

Clusia perscariosa, commonly known as the waxleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and often variegated. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. It is evergreen and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Clusia perscariosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
