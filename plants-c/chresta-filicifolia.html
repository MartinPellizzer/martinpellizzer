Chresta filicifolia

Chresta filicifolia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of a fern, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

Chresta filicifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
