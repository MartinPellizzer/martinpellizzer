Carex × boenninghausiana

Carex × boenninghausiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex morrowii and Carex appalachica, showcasing a blend of traits from both parents. The plant typically forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves. It produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by cylindrical, spiky seed heads.

Carex × boenninghausiana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive texture and ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions.
