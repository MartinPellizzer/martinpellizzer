Cotula laxa

Cotula laxa, commonly known as the common daisy or yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Cotula laxa is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

It is a low-growing herb that spreads by stolons, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
