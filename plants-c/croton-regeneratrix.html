Croton regeneratrix

Croton regeneratrix, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is notable for its ability to survive extreme dehydration by entering a state of dormancy. When rehydrated, the plant can "resurrect" itself, rapidly regaining its turgid structure and green coloration. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss.

This remarkable adaptability makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural practices.
