Callicarpa cavaleriei

Callicarpa cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
