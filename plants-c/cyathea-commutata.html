Cyathea commutata

Cyathea commutata, commonly known as the Commutate Cyathea, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.

The fronds are typically bright green and can be used as ornamental plants in tropical gardens.
