Convolvulus cephalopodus subsp. abhansis

Convolvulus cephalopodus subsp.

abhansis, commonly known as the Kashmir convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
