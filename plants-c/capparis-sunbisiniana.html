Capparis sunbisiniana

Capparis sunbisiniana, commonly known as the Philippine crossberry, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody shrub form. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and are accompanied by stamens that are often brightly colored.

The plant is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, shaded environments.
