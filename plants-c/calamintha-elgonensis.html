Calamintha elgonensis

Calamintha elgonensis, commonly known as East African calamint, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported cooling and antiseptic properties.

Calamintha elgonensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
