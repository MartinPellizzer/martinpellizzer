Carex sylvatica var. laxula

Carex sylvatica var. laxula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
