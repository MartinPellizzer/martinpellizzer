Campanula alliariifolia subsp. letschchumensis

Campanula alliariifolia subsp.

letschchumensis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Eastern Europe and the Caucasus. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in challenging mountainous conditions.
