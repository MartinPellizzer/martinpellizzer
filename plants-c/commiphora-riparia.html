Commiphora riparia

Commiphora riparia, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, leathery leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and incense purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
