Capparis speciosa f. malmeana

Capparis speciosa f. malmeana, commonly known as the Malmeana Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in coastal and rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
