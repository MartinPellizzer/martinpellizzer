Caryota monostachya

Caryota monostachya, commonly known as the single-stem areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clustered, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Caryota monostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
