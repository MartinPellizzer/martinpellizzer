Cyrtochilum ramosissimum f. albomaculatum

Cyrtochilum ramosissimum f. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with distinctive white spots. The plant typically features linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This orchid is popular among growers for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
