Clematis hexapetala var. longiloba

Clematis hexapetala var. longiloba, commonly known as the long petal clematis, is a rare and ornamental vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven ovate leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, moist soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique floral structure, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
