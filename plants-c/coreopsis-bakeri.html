Coreopsis bakeri

Coreopsis bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Coreopsis bakeri is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
