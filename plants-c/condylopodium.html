Condylopodium

Condylopodium, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Tamaricaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for bark. Condylopodium species are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.
