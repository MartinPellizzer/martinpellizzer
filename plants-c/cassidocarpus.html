Cassidocarpus

Cassidocarpus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble showers of golden beads, hence its common name. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Cassidocarpus is native to tropical regions of Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
