Capparidastrum dugandii

Capparidastrum dugandii, commonly known as the Dugand's caper, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae, which includes various species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
