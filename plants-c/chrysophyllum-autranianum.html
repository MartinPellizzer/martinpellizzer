Chrysophyllum autranianum

Chrysophyllum autranianum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical regions.

The tree also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
