Carex meadii

Carex meadii, commonly known as Mead's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. Mead's sedge typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
