Croton nitrariifolius var. genuinus

Croton nitrariifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
