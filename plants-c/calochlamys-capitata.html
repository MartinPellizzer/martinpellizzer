Calochlamys capitata

Calochlamys capitata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
