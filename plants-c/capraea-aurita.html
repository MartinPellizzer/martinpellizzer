Capraea aurita

Capraea aurita, commonly known as the golden twayblade, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grassy areas. Capraea aurita has a compact, rosette-like growth form with narrow, grass-like leaves.
