Campanula longifolia var. bicaulis

Campanula longifolia var. bicaulis, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a greyish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
