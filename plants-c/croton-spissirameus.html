Croton spissirameus

Croton spissirameus, commonly known as the thick-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
