Clematis viticella subsp. scandens

Clematis viticella subsp.

scandens, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe. It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets and produces small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and the dramatic, feathery appearance of its foliage.
