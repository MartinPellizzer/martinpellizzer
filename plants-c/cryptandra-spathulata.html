Cryptandra spathulata

Cryptandra spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cryptandra, is a small shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often glandular, leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open woodlands.

Cryptandra spathulata is a hardy species that is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat.
