Cedrela cedro

Cedrela cedro, commonly known as Spanish cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, durable wood that is highly valued in furniture and construction. The tree has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Cedrela cedro thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
