Crataegus pedicellata var. robesoniana

Crataegus pedicellata var. robesoniana, commonly known as the Robeson's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
