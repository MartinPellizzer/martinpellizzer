Cynometra lenticellata var. villosa

Cynometra lenticellata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and construction.
