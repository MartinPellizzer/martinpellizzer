Chondrorhyncha flaveola

Chondrorhyncha flaveola, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Chondrorhyncha flaveola is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
