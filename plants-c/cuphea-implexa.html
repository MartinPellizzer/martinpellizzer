Cuphea implexa

Cuphea implexa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cuphea implexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
