Carex anhuiensis

Carex anhuiensis, commonly known as the Anhui sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Carex anhuiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
