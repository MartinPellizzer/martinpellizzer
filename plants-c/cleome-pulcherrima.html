Cleome pulcherrima

Cleome pulcherrima, commonly known as the spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, spider-like flowers that range in color from pink to red and yellow. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces elongated, slender seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cleome pulcherrima is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
