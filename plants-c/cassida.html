Cassida

Cassida, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small cups, which attract a variety of pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny areas and is native to North America. Cassida has lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in small, rounded achenes.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
