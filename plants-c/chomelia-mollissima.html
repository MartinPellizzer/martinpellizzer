Chomelia mollissima

Chomelia mollissima, commonly known as the silky chomelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chomelia mollissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
