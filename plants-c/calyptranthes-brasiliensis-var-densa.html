Calyptranthes brasiliensis var. densa

Calyptranthes brasiliensis var. densa, commonly known as the dense calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
