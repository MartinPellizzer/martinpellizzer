Calamus ramulosus

Calamus ramulosus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps. The plant has long, slender rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of materials for crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a distinct aroma.

Calamus ramulosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
