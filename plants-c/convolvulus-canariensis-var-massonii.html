Convolvulus canariensis var. massonii

Convolvulus canariensis var. massonii, commonly known as the Canary Island bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a trailing or climbing herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
