Cactus heinei

Cactus heinei, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a type of cacti belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its cylindrical shape and spiny exterior. The plant features a dense covering of white bristles and has a globular form that tapers towards the top. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits.

Cactus heinei is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
