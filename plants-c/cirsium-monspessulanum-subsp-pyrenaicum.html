Cirsium monspessulanum subsp. pyrenaicum

Cirsium monspessulanum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It features robust, spiny stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a spherical shape. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
