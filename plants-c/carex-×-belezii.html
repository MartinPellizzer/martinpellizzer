Carex × belezii

Carex × belezii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Carex × belezii is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
