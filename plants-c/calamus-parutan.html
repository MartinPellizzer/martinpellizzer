Calamus parutan

Calamus parutan, commonly known as the golden bamboo or parutan reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Calamus parutan is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in wet, tropical environments.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
