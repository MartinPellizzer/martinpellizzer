Cinnamomum heyneanum

Cinnamomum heyneanum, commonly known as wild cinnamon or Indian cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark purple berries. The plant produces a fragrant bark that contains essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, while the tree's ornamental value makes it a popular choice in horticulture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ecological and economic significance.
