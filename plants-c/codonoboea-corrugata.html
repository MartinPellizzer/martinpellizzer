Codonoboea corrugata

Codonoboea corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag bignonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendant-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive corrugated texture, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Codonoboea corrugata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
