Cheiranthus contortuplicatus

Cheiranthus contortuplicatus, commonly known as the twisted-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and twisted, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
