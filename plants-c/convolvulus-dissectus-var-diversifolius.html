Convolvulus dissectus var. diversifolius

Convolvulus dissectus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the diverse-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant in the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its varietal name "diversifolius.". The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
