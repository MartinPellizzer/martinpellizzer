Clematis hancockiana

Clematis hancockiana, commonly known as the Hancock's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is valued for its ornamental value and can be trained on trellises or fences.

Clematis hancockiana is relatively low maintenance but benefits from regular pruning to promote flowering and prevent disease.
