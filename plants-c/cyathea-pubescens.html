Cyathea pubescens

Cyathea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a fibrous, corky trunk covered in a layer of brown, scaly tissue. Its fronds are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a soft, hairy texture.

Cyathea pubescens is often found in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in tropical ecosystems.
