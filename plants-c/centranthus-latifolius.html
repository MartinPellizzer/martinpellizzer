Centranthus latifolius

Centranthus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf redflower.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Centranthus latifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
