Cirsium pindicola

Cirsium pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its growth habit is usually upright, with a height ranging from 30 to 100 centimeters.

Cirsium pindicola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
