Commelina communis f. caeruleopurpurascens

Commelina communis f. caeruleopurpurascens, commonly known as the blue-purple commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-purple flower bracts that contrast with its greenish-white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adventitious.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its vibrant coloration.
