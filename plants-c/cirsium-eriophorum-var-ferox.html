Cirsium eriophorum var. ferox

Cirsium eriophorum var. ferox, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
