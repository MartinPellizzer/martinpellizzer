Campomanesia bullata

Campomanesia bullata, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow to orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and commercial purposes in tropical horticulture.
