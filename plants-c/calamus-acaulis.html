Calamus acaulis

Calamus acaulis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Arales.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. Calamus acaulis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its morphology and ecological adaptations.
