Cassia zygophylloides var. caribaea

Cassia zygophylloides var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribaea cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
