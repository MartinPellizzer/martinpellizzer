Carica aurantiaca

Carica aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant yellow-orange fruit. The fruit is typically oval-shaped and contains a soft, juicy interior with a sweet, tropical flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
