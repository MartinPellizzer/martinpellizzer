Centaurea scabiosa var. vulgaris

Centaurea scabiosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the knapweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiky, cone-shaped flower heads and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
