Chrysopogon aucheri var. quinqueplumis

Chrysopogon aucheri var. quinqueplumis, commonly known as the five-bracted grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces inflorescences with five bracts, giving it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
