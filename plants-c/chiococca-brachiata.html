Chiococca brachiata

Chiococca brachiata, commonly known as the Andean beauty or mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of white, pink, or red. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed and can be quite large, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chiococca brachiata is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
