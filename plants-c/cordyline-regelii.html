Cordyline regelii

Cordyline regelii, commonly known as the Red Cordyline, is a striking evergreen plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features tall, arching leaves that are typically bright red or orange, with a waxy texture and a pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the leaves, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.

Its unique foliage makes it a focal point in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
