Cathartocarpus nodosus

Cathartocarpus nodosus, commonly known as the nodding cat's-tail, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive inflorescence. The plant features a dense cluster of spikelets that dangle from a central stem, giving it a cat's-tail appearance. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Cathartocarpus nodosus thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
