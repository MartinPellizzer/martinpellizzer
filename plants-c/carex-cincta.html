Carex cincta

Carex cincta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex cincta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
