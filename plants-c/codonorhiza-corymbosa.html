Codonorhiza corymbosa

Codonorhiza corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Codonorhiza corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
