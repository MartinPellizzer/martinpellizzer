Carex uralensis

Carex uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Carex uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
