Catasetum taquariense

Catasetum taquariense, commonly known as the Taquari orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its flowers are notable for their intricate morphology, including specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum taquariense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
