Conyza collina

Conyza collina, commonly known as common white cockle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant has slender stems that are usually hairless, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Conyza collina is known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
