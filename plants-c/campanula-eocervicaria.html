Campanula eocervicaria

Campanula eocervicaria, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are often hairy.

Campanula eocervicaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
