Cousinia gaharensis

Cousinia gaharensis, commonly known as the Gharan hillflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing structure with dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cousinia gaharensis plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations.
