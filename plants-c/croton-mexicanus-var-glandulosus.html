Croton mexicanus var. glandulosus

Croton mexicanus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from green to red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often glandular, giving it a slightly sticky texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
