Cistus involucratus

Cistus involucratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds.

Cistus involucratus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
