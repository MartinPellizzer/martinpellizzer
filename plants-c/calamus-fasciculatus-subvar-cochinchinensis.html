Calamus fasciculatus subvar. cochinchinensis

Calamus fasciculatus subvar. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Aroid family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
