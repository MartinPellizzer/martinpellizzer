Callistemon linearifolius

Callistemon linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Callistemon linearifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds and insects.
