Caryota plumosa

Caryota plumosa, commonly known as the fishtail palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that grows upright, supporting the expansive foliage. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a soft, silky texture that adds to its ornamental value.

Caryota plumosa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
