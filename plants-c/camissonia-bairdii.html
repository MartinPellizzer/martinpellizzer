Camissonia bairdii

Camissonia bairdii, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Camissonia bairdii is adapted to arid environments and is an important species for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
