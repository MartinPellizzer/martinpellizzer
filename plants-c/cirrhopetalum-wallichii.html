Cirrhopetalum wallichii

Cirrhopetalum wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are often pale green or yellow with a distinctive, wavy petal structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
