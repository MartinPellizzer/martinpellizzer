Chamaecladon metallicum

Chamaecladon metallicum, commonly known as the metallic flax.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its common name, metallic flax, refers to the silvery appearance of its foliage.
