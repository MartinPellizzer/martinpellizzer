Convolvulus maritimus

Convolvulus maritimus, commonly known as sea bindweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that thrives in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines. The plant features trailing stems with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are small, funnel-shaped, and typically pink or purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
