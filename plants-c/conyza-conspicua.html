Conyza conspicua

Conyza conspicua, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, including sandy or clay soils.

Conyza conspicua is often found in fields, roadsides, and along the edges of forests.
