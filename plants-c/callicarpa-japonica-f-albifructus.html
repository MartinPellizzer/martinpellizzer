Callicarpa japonica f. albifructus

Callicarpa japonica f. albifructus, commonly known as the white-fruited beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is a cultivar of the common beautyberry, characterized by its pale white to cream-colored berries, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by the distinctive, showy fruit clusters in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The white fruits are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
