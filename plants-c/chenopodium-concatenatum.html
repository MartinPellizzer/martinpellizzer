Chenopodium concatenatum

Chenopodium concatenatum, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Chenopodium concatenatum is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, making it a characteristic species of desert and coastal ecosystems.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
