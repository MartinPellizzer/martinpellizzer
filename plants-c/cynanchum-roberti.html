Cynanchum roberti

Cynanchum roberti, commonly known as the Robert's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing compounds that deter herbivores.

Cynanchum roberti is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.
