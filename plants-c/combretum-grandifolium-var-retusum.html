Combretum grandifolium var. retusum

Combretum grandifolium var. retusum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
