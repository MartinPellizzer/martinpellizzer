Calamagrostis conferta

Calamagrostis conferta, commonly known as the silvergrass or bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis conferta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
