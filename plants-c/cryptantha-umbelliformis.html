Cryptantha umbelliformis

Cryptantha umbelliformis, commonly known as the clustered bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Cryptantha umbelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
