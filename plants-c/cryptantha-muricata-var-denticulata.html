Cryptantha muricata var. denticulata

Cryptantha muricata var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed cryptantha, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and often have a serrated or toothed edge, which gives the variety its name. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its flowers, which are usually blue or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
