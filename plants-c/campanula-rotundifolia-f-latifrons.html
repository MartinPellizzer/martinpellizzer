Campanula rotundifolia f. latifrons

Campanula rotundifolia f. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and broad, rounded leaves that are often lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and naturalized landscapes.
