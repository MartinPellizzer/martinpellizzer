Cerasus glandulosa var. trichostyla

Cerasus glandulosa var. trichostyla, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
