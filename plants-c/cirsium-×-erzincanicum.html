Cirsium × erzincanicum

Cirsium × erzincanicum, commonly known as the Erzincan thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, serrated leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
