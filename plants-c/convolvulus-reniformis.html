Convolvulus reniformis

Convolvulus reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in certain regions.
