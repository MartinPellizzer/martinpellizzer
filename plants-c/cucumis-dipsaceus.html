Cucumis dipsaceus

Cucumis dipsaceus, commonly known as the slender cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, trailing vines and small, pale green fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, cucumber-like flavor, making it a potential crop in suitable growing conditions.
