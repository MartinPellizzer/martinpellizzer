Canariopsis decumana

Canariopsis decumana, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Canariopsis decumana plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
