Campanula mairei var. flahaultiana

Campanula mairei var. flahaultiana, commonly known as the Flahault's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
