Coffea namorokensis

Coffea namorokensis, commonly known as the Namorokensis coffee, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high altitudes and consistent rainfall. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of Coffea namorokensis make it unique among coffee species, contributing to its distinct flavor profile.
