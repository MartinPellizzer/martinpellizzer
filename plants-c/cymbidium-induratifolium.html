Cymbidium induratifolium

Cymbidium induratifolium, commonly known as the hard-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces tall flowering spikes with numerous showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
