Cardamine impatiens proles dasycarpa

Cardamine impatiens proles dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its proles dasycarpa variant is distinguished by its densely hairy fruits, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
