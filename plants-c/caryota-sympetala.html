Caryota sympetala

Caryota sympetala, commonly known as the clustered palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fleshy fruits. Its unique growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.

Caryota sympetala thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
