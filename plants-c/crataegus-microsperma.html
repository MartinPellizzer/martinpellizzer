Crataegus microsperma

Crataegus microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features thorny branches with oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
