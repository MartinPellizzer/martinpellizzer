Carex canescens var. macilenta

Carex canescens var. macilenta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
