Cordyline calocoma f. nutans

Cordyline calocoma f. nutans, commonly known as the hanging cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers, giving it a unique visual appearance. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its decorative value in gardens and indoor spaces.

The hanging flowers make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and as a focal point in tropical landscapes.
