Cyperus zikae

Cyperus zikae, commonly known as the Zikae flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Cyperus zikae plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
