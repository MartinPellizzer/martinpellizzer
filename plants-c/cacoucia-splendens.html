Cacoucia splendens

Cacoucia splendens, commonly known as the splendid cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications, particularly in African and Caribbean cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
