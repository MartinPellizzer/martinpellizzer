Calamagrostis villosa var. hypacratera

Calamagrostis villosa var. hypacratera, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and slender, arching clumps that create a graceful appearance in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, feathery plumes that add visual interest throughout the growing season.
