Cyperus catharinensis

Cyperus catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus catharinensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
