Croton goudenhoughtii

Croton goudenhoughtii, commonly known as the Goudenhout's croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored, leaf patterns and its ability to produce small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, adding to its ornamental value.

Croton goudenhoughtii is also noted for its resilience to drought and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
