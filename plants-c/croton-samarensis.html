Croton samarensis

Croton samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow, followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules.

Croton samarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
