Cirsium cardo-leonis

Cirsium cardo-leonis, commonly known as the greater cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust growth habit. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the flowers attract various pollinators.

Cirsium cardo-leonis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
