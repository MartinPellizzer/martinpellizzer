Carex depressa subsp. dimorpha

Carex depressa subsp.

dimorpha, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish-brown spikelets.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
