Coleochloa rehmanniana

Coleochloa rehmanniana, commonly known as the mountain grass or Rehmann's grass, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine ecosystems.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
