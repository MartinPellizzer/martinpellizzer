Croton caboensis

Croton caboensis, commonly known as the Cape croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
