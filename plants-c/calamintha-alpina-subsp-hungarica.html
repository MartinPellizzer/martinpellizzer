Calamintha alpina subsp. hungarica

Calamintha alpina subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
