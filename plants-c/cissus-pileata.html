Cissus pileata

Cissus pileata, commonly known as the boneleaf creeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a pale underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus pileata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
