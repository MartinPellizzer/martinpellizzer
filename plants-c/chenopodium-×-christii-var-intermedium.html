Chenopodium × christii var. intermedium

Chenopodium × christii var. intermedium, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with reddish stems that often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy coating. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various ecological and agricultural settings.
