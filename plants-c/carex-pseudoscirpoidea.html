Carex pseudoscirpoidea

Carex pseudoscirpoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
