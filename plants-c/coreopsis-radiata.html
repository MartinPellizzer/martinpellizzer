Coreopsis radiata

Coreopsis radiata, commonly known as the radiate coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a radiate pattern with a central disk. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis radiata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
