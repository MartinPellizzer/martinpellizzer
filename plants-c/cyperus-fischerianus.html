Cyperus fischerianus

Cyperus fischerianus, commonly known as Fischer's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.

Cyperus fischerianus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
