Centropogon brumalis

Centropogon brumalis, commonly known as the winter grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

Centropogon brumalis is adapted to cooler climates and is typically in bloom during the late winter and early spring.
