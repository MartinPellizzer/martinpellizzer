Chloris rhachitricha

Chloris rhachitricha, commonly known as spear grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris rhachitricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
