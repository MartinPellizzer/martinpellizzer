Calamus johanis

Calamus johanis, commonly known as the noble canes or the royal reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Calamus johanis is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
