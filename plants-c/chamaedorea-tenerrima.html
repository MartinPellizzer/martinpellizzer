Chamaedorea tenerrima

Chamaedorea tenerrima, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the parlor palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity. Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The leaves are dark green and remain evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value.
