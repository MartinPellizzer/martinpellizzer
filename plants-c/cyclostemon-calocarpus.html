Cyclostemon calocarpus

Cyclostemon calocarpus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

Cyclostemon calocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
