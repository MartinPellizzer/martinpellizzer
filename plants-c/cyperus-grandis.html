Cyperus grandis

Cyperus grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus grandis is characterized by its robust growth and ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
