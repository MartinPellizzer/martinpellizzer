Centaurea seridis subsp. jacobi

Centaurea seridis subsp.

jacobi, commonly known as the Sinai centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky and arid habitats. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads.
