Cissus endresii

Cissus endresii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to thrive in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscaping in warm climates.
