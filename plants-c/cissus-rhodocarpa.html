Cissus rhodocarpa

Cissus rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are orange-red in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cissus rhodocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
