Commiphora montana

Commiphora montana, commonly known as the mountain myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and small, leathery leaves. It produces yellowish-white flowers and small, dark berries that are dispersed by birds.
