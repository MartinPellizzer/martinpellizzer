Carex taprobanensis

Carex taprobanensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex taprobanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
