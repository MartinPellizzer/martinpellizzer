Cistus hirsutus f. pumilus

Cistus hirsutus f. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf hairy rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glandular, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both wild and cultivated landscapes.
