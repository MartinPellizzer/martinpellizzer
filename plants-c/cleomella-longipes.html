Cleomella longipes

Cleomella longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed cleom, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Cleomella longipes is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
