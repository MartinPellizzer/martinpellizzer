Campomanesia hassleri

Campomanesia hassleri, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple or pitanga-preta, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a smooth, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red when ripe, containing a single large seed. It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, tropical flavor, and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
