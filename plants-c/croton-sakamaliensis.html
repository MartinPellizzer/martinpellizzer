Croton sakamaliensis

Croton sakamaliensis, commonly known as the Sakamali croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that vary in color from green to red, depending on the season and light exposure. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage.
