Clusia burchellii

Clusia burchellii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Clusia burchellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its tolerance to drought.
