Caroxylon verdoorniae

Caroxylon verdoorniae, commonly known as the Verdoorn's kaffir lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also a subject of conservation interest in its native habitat.
