Carex muricata proles leersiana

Carex muricata proles leersiana, commonly known as the Leers' rough sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves that are often covered in small, sharp teeth. The plant produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
