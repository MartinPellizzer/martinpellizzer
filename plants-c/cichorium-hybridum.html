Cichorium hybridum

Cichorium hybridum, commonly known as chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a blue or purple flower head that resembles a dandelion. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its leaves, roots, and flowers. Chicory contains compounds such as inulin, which is used as a sweetener and prebiotic.

Its botanical features make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
