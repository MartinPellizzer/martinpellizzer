Canavalia picta var. picta

Canavalia picta var. picta, commonly known as the painted bean or variegated cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it a valuable plant in sustainable farming practices.
