Centaurea cineraria subsp. cineraria

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

cineraria, commonly known as the cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
