Chrysanthemum hypargyreum

Chrysanthemum hypargyreum, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
