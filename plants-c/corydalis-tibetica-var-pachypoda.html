Corydalis tibetica var. pachypoda

Corydalis tibetica var. pachypoda, commonly known as the Tibetan Corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent areas in China. The plant features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
