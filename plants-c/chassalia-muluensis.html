Chassalia muluensis

Chassalia muluensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian chassalia, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Chassalia muluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
