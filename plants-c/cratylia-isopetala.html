Cratylia isopetala

Cratylia isopetala, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a climbing or spreading form depending on the environment.

Cratylia isopetala is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
