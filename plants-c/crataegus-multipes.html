Crataegus multipes

Crataegus multipes, commonly known as the many-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
