Chrysanthemum pseudanthemis

Chrysanthemum pseudanthemis, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flower heads. The plant typically produces yellow to orange blooms, though some cultivars may exhibit other colors. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for both ornamental horticulture and scientific study.
