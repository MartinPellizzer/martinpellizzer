Capparis grandis

Capparis grandis, commonly known as the large caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
