Calyptranthes grandifolia var. rufa

Calyptranthes grandifolia var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, red-orange flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a distinct reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its striking floral display.
