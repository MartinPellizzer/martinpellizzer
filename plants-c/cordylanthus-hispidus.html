Cordylanthus hispidus

Cordylanthus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy cordylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its hairy leaves and showy purple or pink flowers make it visually striking.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
