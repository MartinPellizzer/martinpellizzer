Carex trichosperma

Carex trichosperma, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in marshy areas, stream banks, and other damp habitats, often forming dense colonies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for insects and contributing to soil stabilization in wetlands.
