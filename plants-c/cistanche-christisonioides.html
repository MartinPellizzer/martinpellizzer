Cistanche christisonioides

Cistanche christisonioides, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to members of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers and is native to arid regions of China and the Tibetan Plateau. It is known for its ability to survive in extreme desert conditions due to its specialized adaptations.

Cistanche christisonioides has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
