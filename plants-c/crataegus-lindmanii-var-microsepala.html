Crataegus lindmanii var. microsepala

Crataegus lindmanii var. microsepala, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorned stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil in partial shade.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
