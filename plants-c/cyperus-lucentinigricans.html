Cyperus lucentinigricans

Cyperus lucentinigricans, commonly known as the silver-haired flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered with fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Cyperus lucentinigricans thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
