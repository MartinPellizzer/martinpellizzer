Crataegus mollis var. viburnifolia

Crataegus mollis var. viburnifolia, commonly known as the silky hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom of hairs, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its growth habit is typically compact, with multiple stems forming a dense, rounded shape.

The species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.
