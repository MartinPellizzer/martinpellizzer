Carex × joussetii

Carex × joussetii, commonly known as the silver grass or silver sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex × joussetii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
