Cyperus chrysokerkos

Cyperus chrysokerkos, commonly known as the golden club, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cyperus chrysokerkos is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water in wetland ecosystems.
