Cochlearia sempervivum

Cochlearia sempervivum, commonly known as the mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green, spoon-shaped leaves that help it conserve water. It produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Cochlearia sempervivum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environments.
