Crataegus aceroides

Crataegus aceroides, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
