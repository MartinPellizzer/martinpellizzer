Cardamine multicaulis

Cardamine multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered watercress, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are often hairy and have a peppery taste, similar to other members of the mustard family.

Cardamine multicaulis is a short-lived perennial that spreads through rhizomes, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
