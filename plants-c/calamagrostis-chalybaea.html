Calamagrostis chalybaea

Calamagrostis chalybaea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and its distinctive silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in autumn. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Calamagrostis chalybaea is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
