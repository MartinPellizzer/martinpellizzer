Centaurea paniculata var. lusitana

Centaurea paniculata var. lusitana, commonly known as the Portuguese centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces striking, spiky flower heads composed of bright yellow to orange-red bracts and small blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
