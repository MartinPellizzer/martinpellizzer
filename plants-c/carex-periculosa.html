Carex periculosa

Carex periculosa, commonly known as the dangerous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered hazardous to both humans and animals.
