Crassula montana

Crassula montana, commonly known as the mountain crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, usually during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula montana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
