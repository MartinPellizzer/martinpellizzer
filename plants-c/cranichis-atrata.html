Cranichis atrata

Cranichis atrata, commonly known as the black orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of white and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
