Crepis foetida var. vulgaris

Crepis foetida var. vulgaris, commonly known as stinkcup, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces a distinctive, pungent odor, which is a defense mechanism against herbivores. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia.

Its leaves are hairy and often have a reddish or purplish hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
