Cirsium lappaceum var. ferox

Cirsium lappaceum var. ferox, commonly known as the feral thistle, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright growth habit and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, contributing to its defensive structure.

The species is known for its robust nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
