Ceterach marantae

Ceterach marantae, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and lanceolate, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ceterach marantae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
