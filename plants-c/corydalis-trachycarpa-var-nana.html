Corydalis trachycarpa var. nana

Corydalis trachycarpa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf roughstem corydalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, spurred flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
