Carex × caesariensis

Carex × caesariensis, commonly known as the blue sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by cylindrical seed heads.

Carex × caesariensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
