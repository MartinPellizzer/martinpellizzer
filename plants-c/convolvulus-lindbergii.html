Convolvulus lindbergii

Convolvulus lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to cover ground make it both aesthetically pleasing and sometimes invasive in certain environments.
