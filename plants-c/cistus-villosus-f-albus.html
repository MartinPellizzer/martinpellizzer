Cistus villosus f. albus

Cistus villosus f. albus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
