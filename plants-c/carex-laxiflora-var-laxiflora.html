Carex laxiflora var. laxiflora

Carex laxiflora var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

This sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
