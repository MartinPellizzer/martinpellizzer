Cassia filicifolia

Cassia filicifolia, commonly known as the fern cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that resemble ferns, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cassia filicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
