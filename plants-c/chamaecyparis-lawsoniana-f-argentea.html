Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. argentea

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. argentea, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress variegated form, is a cultivar of the Western red cedar.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes on its needle-like leaves. This plant is a slow-growing evergreen with a conical shape, often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The variegation enhances its visual interest, though it may be less cold-hardy than the standard species.
