Crocus pygmaeus

Crocus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a small, bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
