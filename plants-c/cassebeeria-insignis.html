Cassebeeria insignis

Cassebeeria insignis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Cassebeeria insignis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
