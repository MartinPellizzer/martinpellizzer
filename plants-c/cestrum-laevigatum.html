Cestrum laevigatum

Cestrum laevigatum, commonly known as night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Cestrum laevigatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
