Cirsium suzukaense

Cirsium suzukaense, commonly known as Suzuka thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, violet-blue flower heads. The plant produces both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
