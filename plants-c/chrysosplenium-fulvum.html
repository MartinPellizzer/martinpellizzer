Chrysosplenium fulvum

Chrysosplenium fulvum, commonly known as the golden spleenwort, is a species of flowering plant in the family Spleenworts.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow in color, and appear in clusters.

Chrysosplenium fulvum is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
