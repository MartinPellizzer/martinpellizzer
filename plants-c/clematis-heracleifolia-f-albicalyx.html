Clematis heracleifolia f. albicalyx

Clematis heracleifolia f. albicalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered clematis, is a cultivar of the clematis genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscaping.
