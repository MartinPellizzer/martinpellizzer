Cryptocarya nothofagetorum

Cryptocarya nothofagetorum, commonly known as the New Zealand myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for native birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
