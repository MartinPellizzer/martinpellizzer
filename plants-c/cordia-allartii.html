Cordia allartii

Cordia allartii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought.
