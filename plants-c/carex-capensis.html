Carex capensis

Carex capensis, commonly known as Cape sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Carex capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
