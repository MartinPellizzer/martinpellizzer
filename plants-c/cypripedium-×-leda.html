Cypripedium × leda

Cypripedium × leda, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and green pattern resembling a leopard's spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single, erect flower spike.

Cypripedium × leda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
