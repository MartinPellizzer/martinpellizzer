Carex × aestivaliformis

Carex × aestivaliformis, commonly known as the summer sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Carex × aestivaliformis is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is relatively low maintenance once established.
