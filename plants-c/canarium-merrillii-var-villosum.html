Canarium merrillii var. villosum

Canarium merrillii var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy canarium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Canarium genus.
