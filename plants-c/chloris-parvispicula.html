Chloris parvispicula

Chloris parvispicula, commonly known as the small-eyed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Chloris parvispicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
