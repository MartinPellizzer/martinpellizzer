Cyperus fernandezianus

Cyperus fernandezianus, commonly known as the Fernandez's flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow leaves. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the sedge family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
