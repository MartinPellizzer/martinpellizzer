Cliffortia ilicifolia var. incisa

Cliffortia ilicifolia var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cliffortia, is a shrub native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along coastal cliffs and slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
