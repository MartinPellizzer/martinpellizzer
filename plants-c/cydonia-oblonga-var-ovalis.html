Cydonia oblonga var. ovalis

Cydonia oblonga var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-shaped quince, is a variety of the common quince that exhibits distinct oval-shaped fruits.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically harvested in late autumn and is known for its aromatic, sweet aroma and high pectin content. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit, which is used in jams, jellies, and other culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosaceae family.
