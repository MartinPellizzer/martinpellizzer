Cimicifuga foetida

Cimicifuga foetida, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are followed by red berries. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga foetida is valued for its potential hormonal and anti-inflammatory properties.
