Combretum wightianum

Combretum wightianum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement and turn red or orange before falling in autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
