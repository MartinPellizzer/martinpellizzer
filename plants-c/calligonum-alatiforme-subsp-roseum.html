Calligonum alatiforme subsp. roseum

Calligonum alatiforme subsp.

roseum, commonly known as the pink sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems covered in soft, pinkish-gray hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
