Curcuma heyneana

Curcuma heyneana, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The rhizomes of Curcuma heyneana are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to turmeric.
