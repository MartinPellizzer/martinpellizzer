Colutea aeschinomenoides

Colutea aeschinomenoides, commonly known as the red clover.

This plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

It is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it valuable in soil enrichment.
