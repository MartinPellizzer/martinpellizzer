Crataegus monogyna var. rubra

Crataegus monogyna var. rubra, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer, attracting various bird species. This plant is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
