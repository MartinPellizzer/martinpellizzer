Carex kitaibeliana var. kitaibeliana

Carex kitaibeliana var. kitaibeliana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly rough to the touch. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically green or brown in color.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
