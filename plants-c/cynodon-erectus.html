Cynodon erectus

Cynodon erectus, commonly known as African grass or dog grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and coarse, stiff blades that are often used for erosion control and livestock forage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cynodon erectus thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Its rapid growth and resilience make it a common weed in both agricultural and natural settings.
