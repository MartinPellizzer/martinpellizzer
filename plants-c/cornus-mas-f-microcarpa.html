Cornus mas f. microcarpa

Cornus mas f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cornus or small-fruited cornus, is a compact cultivar of the cornus species.

It is characterized by its small, red drupes that mature in late summer, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of around 1 to 2 meters, with dense, dark green foliage that provides year-round interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
