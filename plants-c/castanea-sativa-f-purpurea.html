Castanea sativa f. purpurea

Castanea sativa f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which turns red in the autumn, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the typical horse chestnut. It produces large, showy white flowers in spring, followed by spiny, woody fruits.

This variety is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
