Cheirostylis rubrifolius

Cheirostylis rubrifolius, commonly known as the red-leafed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a reddish hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Cheirostylis rubrifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique coloration.
