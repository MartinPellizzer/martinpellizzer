Condgiea

Condgiea, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including livestock. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Condgiea thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
