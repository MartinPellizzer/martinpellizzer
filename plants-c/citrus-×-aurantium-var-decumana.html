Citrus × aurantium var. decumana

Citrus × aurantium var. decumana, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thorny branches and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are typically bitter and not suitable for eating. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing before the leaves.

This variety is widely used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a rootstock for citrus cultivation.
