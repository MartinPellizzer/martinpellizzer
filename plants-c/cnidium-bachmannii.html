Cnidium bachmannii

Cnidium bachmannii, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
