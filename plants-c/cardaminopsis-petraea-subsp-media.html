Cardaminopsis petraea subsp. media

Cardaminopsis petraea subsp.

media, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to alpine regions of Europe. It features small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal leaves with deeply lobed or dissected margins, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas where it contributes to the alpine flora diversity.
