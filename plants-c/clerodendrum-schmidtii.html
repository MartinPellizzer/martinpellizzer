Clerodendrum schmidtii

Clerodendrum schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and diverse floral structures.
