Centaurea jacea subsp. schrankii

Centaurea jacea subsp.

schrankii, commonly known as Schrank's common centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracteate flower heads that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
