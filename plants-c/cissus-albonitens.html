Cissus albonitens

Cissus albonitens, commonly known as the white-veined cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive white veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
