Cardamine palenae

Cardamine palenae, commonly known as the pale watercress, is a small perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a peppery, slightly bitter taste.

It produces small white or yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.
