Chloraea calopogon

Chloraea calopogon, commonly known as the grass pink, is a striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear whorls of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with bright orange petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in bogs and marshes.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a notable species in botanical studies.
