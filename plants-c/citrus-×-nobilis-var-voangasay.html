Citrus × nobilis var. voangasay

Citrus × nobilis var. voangasay, commonly known as the Voangasay mandarin, is a citrus hybrid originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with a thin, smooth peel. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is prized for its sweet, juicy flesh and aromatic zest, making it popular in both culinary and ornamental contexts.

The variety is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and moderate watering.
