Cyanocephalus peduncularis

Cyanocephalus peduncularis, commonly known as the blue-headed grass.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive blue flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Cyanocephalus peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
