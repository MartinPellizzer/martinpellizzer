Capsella bursa-pastoris f. serrata

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated shepherd's purse, is a variant of the common shepherd's purse.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges, which give it its specific epithet. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions.

The plant is an annual herb that produces small white or purple flowers.
