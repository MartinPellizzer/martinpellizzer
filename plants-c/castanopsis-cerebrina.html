Castanopsis cerebrina

Castanopsis cerebrina, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree or Franklin tree, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its glossy, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiny husk. Castanopsis cerebrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
