Campanula jacobaea var. hispida

Campanula jacobaea var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its varietal name, and they are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
