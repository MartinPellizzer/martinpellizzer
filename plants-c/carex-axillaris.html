Carex axillaris

Carex axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex axillaris is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
