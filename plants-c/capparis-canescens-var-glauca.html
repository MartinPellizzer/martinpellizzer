Capparis canescens var. glauca

Capparis canescens var. glauca, commonly known as the blue caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and has small, spiny leaves with a distinctive whitish or bluish waxy coating. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
