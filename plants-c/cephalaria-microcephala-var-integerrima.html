Cephalaria microcephala var. integerrima

Cephalaria microcephala var. integerrima, commonly known as the small-headed cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in garden settings.
