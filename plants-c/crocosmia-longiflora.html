Crocosmia longiflora

Crocosmia longiflora, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant orange-red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Crocosmia longiflora is known for its long blooming period, typically from late summer to early autumn.
