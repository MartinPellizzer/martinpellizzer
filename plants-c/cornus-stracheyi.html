Cornus stracheyi

Cornus stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface.

Cornus stracheyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
