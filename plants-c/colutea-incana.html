Colutea incana

Colutea incana, commonly known as hoary balsam, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seed pods are typically slender and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Colutea incana is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
