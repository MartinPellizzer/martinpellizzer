Centaurea irritans var. longispinosa

Centaurea irritans var. longispinosa, commonly known as the long-spined bluemink, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces bristly, spiny seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species within the Asteraceae family.
