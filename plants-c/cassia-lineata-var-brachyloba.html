Cassia lineata var. brachyloba

Cassia lineata var. brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stalked cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, fluffy pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
