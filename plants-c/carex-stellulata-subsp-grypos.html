Carex stellulata subsp. grypos

Carex stellulata subsp.

grypos, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of small, three-sided flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
