Cynanchum roraimense

Cynanchum roraimense, commonly known as Roraima devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
