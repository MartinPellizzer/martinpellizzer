Cistus annuus

Cistus annuus, commonly known as the annual rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Cistus annuus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
