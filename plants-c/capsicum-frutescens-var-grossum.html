Capsicum frutescens var. grossum

Capsicum frutescens var. grossum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, pointed, and fiery red fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and narrow, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by the distinctive, pungent fruits.

The variety is valued for its heat and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
