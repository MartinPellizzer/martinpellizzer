Clematis linearifolia

Clematis linearifolia, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with finely divided, linear leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis linearifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
