Cryptocoryne wendtii

Cryptocoryne wendtii, commonly known as the Wendt's cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant thrives in soft, slightly acidic water and is often found in slow-moving rivers and ponds. It is popular in aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to oxygenate the water.

Cryptocoryne wendtii is also known for its resilience to poor water conditions, making it a favored choice for beginner aquarists.
