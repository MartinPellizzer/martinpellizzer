Cybianthus granulosus

Cybianthus granulosus, commonly known as the rough-hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
