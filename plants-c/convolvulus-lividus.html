Convolvulus lividus

Convolvulus lividus, commonly known as the bluish morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Convolvulus lividus is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
