Crataegus cherokeensis

Crataegus cherokeensis, commonly known as Cherokee thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

Crataegus cherokeensis is often found in woodland edges and open areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
