Crawfurdia bulleyana

Crawfurdia bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyana kigelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Kigelia, which includes several species of woody plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
