Cheiranthus × cheiri var. serratus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are serrated, giving the plant its specific variety name, and they are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
