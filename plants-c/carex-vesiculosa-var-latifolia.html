Carex vesiculosa var. latifolia

Carex vesiculosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are broad and flat, with a distinctive bulbous base that stores nutrients. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
