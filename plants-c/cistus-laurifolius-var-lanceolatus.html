Cistus laurifolius var. lanceolatus

Cistus laurifolius var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and Mediterranean-style landscapes.
