Calyptranthes pulchella var. parviflora

Calyptranthes pulchella var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered calyptranthes, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
