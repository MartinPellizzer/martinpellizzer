Citrus × oto

Citrus × oto, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, with a thin, edible peel. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit's unique feature is its segmented interior, which contains sweet, segmented segments surrounded by a juicy, edible rind.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit, which can be used in culinary and medicinal applications.
