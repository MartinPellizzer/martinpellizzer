Citrus × nobilis var. sunki

Citrus × nobilis var. sunki, commonly known as kumquat, is a citrus fruit-bearing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to orange when mature. The plant has evergreen leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. It produces fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is edible, with a thin, sweet skin and a segmented, juicy pulp inside.
