Convolvulus acicularis

Convolvulus acicularis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, bean-like seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

Convolvulus acicularis is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
