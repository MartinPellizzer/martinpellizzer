Cyperus serotinus var. serotinus

Cyperus serotinus var. serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of water quality.
