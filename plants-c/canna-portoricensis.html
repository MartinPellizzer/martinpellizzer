Canna portoricensis

Canna portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican canna, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, large inflorescences with vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna portoricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
