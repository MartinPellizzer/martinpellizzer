Collania dulcis var. parvifolia

Collania dulcis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf sweetleaf, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it its varietal name "parvifolia.". The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tasting berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
