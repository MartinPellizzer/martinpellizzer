Chaptalia flavicans

Chaptalia flavicans, commonly known as the yellow chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are usually covered in fine pubescence.

Chaptalia flavicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
