Clathrotropis grandiflora

Clathrotropis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered clathrotropis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
