Callista stellifera

Callista stellifera, commonly known as the starry callista, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain seeds, typical of its family. It thrives in moist, forested environments and is often found in the understorey of native forests.

Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.
