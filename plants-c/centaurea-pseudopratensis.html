Centaurea pseudopratensis

Centaurea pseudopratensis, commonly known as the false meadow centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.
