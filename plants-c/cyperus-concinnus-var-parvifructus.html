Cyperus concinnus var. parvifructus

Cyperus concinnus var. parvifructus, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella sedge, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often borne on short, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
