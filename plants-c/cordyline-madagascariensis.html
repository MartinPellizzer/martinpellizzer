Cordyline madagascariensis

Cordyline madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and rosette of large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant, pendulous clusters of white or pink flowers, followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical landscapes.
