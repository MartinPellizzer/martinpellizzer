Cassia paraunana

Cassia paraunana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of ovate leaflets, and it produces bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types. It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cassia paraunana thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
