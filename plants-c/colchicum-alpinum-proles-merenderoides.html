Colchicum alpinum proles merenderoides

Colchicum alpinum proles merenderoides, commonly known as the Alpine meadow colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and adjacent mountain ranges.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and has a corm-like bulbous structure for storing nutrients. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
