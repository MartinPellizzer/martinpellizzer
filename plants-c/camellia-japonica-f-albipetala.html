Camellia japonica f. albipetala

Camellia japonica f. albipetala, commonly known as the white-petaled Japanese camellia, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese camellia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive pinkish blush at the base. The plant is an evergreen shrub native to Japan, typically growing to a height of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy, dark green, and elliptical in shape.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a prized addition to gardens and botanical collections.
