Chimarrhis ekmanii

Chimarrhis ekmanii, commonly known as the red-flowered waratah, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of New South Wales, Australia.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, red bracts that encase small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thick, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
