Crassula argyrophylla var. ramosa

Crassula argyrophylla var. ramosa, commonly known as the silverleaf crassula or the coral pine, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with dense, fleshy foliage that is often tinged with silver or blue hues. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in dry, well-drained soil.
