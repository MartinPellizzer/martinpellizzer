Cotoneaster mongolicus

Cotoneaster mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant typically grows in forest understories and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

Cotoneaster mongolicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
