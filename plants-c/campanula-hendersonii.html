Campanula hendersonii

Campanula hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence.

Campanula hendersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
