Crataegus gratiosa

Crataegus gratiosa, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow, which attract birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus gratiosa is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
