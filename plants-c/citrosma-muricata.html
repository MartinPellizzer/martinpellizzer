Citrosma muricata

Citrosma muricata, commonly known as the rough citrus or wild lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, thorny bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles a citrus but is more elongated and has a distinct, aromatic flavor.

Citrosma muricata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
