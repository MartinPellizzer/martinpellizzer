Calothamnus planifolius var. latifolius

Calothamnus planifolius var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved goldenbush, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It features dense, cylindrical spikes of bright yellow flowers that bloom from late autumn to early spring. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a grey-green color and a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
