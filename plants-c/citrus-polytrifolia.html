Citrus polytrifolia

Citrus polytrifolia, commonly known as the sour orange.

This small evergreen shrub or tree is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant is known for its high concentration of citric acid, which gives it its tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The leaves and flowers also have aromatic properties and are used in various herbal preparations.
