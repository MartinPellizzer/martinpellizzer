Callicarpa rubella f. villosa

Callicarpa rubella f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive clusters of bright red berries that appear in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its specific epithet "villosa.". It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the showy fruit.

This shrub is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts birds and other wildlife.
