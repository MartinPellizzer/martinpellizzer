Circaea alpina f. pacifica

Circaea alpina f. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific whitebeak, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native habitat.
