Castanea vesca var. garelia

Castanea vesca var. garelia, commonly known as the Garelia horse chestnut, is a botanical variant of the horse chestnut tree.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its distinctive, large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to certain pests.
