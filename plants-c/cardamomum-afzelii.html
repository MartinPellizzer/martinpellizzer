Cardamomum afzelii

Cardamomum afzelii, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale yellow flowers, followed by dark blue berries containing tiny seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy fragrance, which is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
