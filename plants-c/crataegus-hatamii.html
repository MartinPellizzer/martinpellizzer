Crataegus hatamii

Crataegus hatamii, commonly known as the Hatam's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and attract various bird species. Crataegus hatamii is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
