Capsella alpina

Capsella alpina, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. Its small white or pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

Capsella alpina is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in mountainous areas where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
