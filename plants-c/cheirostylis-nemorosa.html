Cheirostylis nemorosa

Cheirostylis nemorosa, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Cheirostylis nemorosa has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
