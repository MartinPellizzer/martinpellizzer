Chaptalia maxima

Chaptalia maxima, commonly known as the giant chaptalia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
