Coronopus lepidioides var. garamas

Coronopus lepidioides var. garamas, commonly known as the Garamas dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, aiding in wind pollination.

Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, slightly bitter taste.
