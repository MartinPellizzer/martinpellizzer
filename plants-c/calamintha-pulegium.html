Calamintha pulegium

Calamintha pulegium, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves contain high levels of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its distinctive effects on cats and humans.

Calamintha pulegium is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and as a source of essential oils.
