Catharanthus scitulus

Catharanthus scitulus, commonly known as the Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, fragrant, purple or pink flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It is notable for containing alkaloids such as vincristine and vinblastine, which have medicinal uses in cancer treatments.

Catharanthus scitulus thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
