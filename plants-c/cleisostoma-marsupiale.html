Cleisostoma marsupiale

Cleisostoma marsupiale, commonly known as the cup-lid orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive cup-shaped lip that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Cleisostoma marsupiale is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
