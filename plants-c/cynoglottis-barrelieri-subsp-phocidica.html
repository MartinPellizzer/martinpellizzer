Cynoglottis barrelieri subsp. phocidica

Cynoglottis barrelieri subsp.

phocidica, commonly known as the Madagascar milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Madagascar. It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.

Its latex sap is milky and can cause skin irritation, a characteristic shared with other members of the milkweed family.
