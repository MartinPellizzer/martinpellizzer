Cordyline calocoma f. veitchii

Cordyline calocoma f. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture and often exhibit a reddish or purple hue at the base. This cultivar is prized for its dramatic appearance and is often used in landscaping for its visual impact.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
