Cymbidium sanguineum

Cymbidium sanguineum, commonly known as the Chinese orchid or the blood orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium sanguineum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
