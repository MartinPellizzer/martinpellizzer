Calophyllum rufigemmatum

Calophyllum rufigemmatum, commonly known as the red-stemmed candlewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
