Chamaeiris collina

Chamaeiris collina, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Chamaeiris collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
