Cynoglossum lithospermifolium subsp. cariense

Cynoglossum lithospermifolium subsp.

cariense, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features lanceolate to ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in loose racemes. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
