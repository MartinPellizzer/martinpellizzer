Cucurbita pepo var. chrysothela

Cucurbita pepo var. chrysothela, commonly known as the golden gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, yellow-orange rind and smooth, edible flesh.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit. It is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use, often used in soups and stews.

The fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and is valued for its unique flavor and texture.
