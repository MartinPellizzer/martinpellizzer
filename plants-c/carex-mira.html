Carex mira

Carex mira, commonly known as the elegant sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. Carex mira typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early to mid-summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
