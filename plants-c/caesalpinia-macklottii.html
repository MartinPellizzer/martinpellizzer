Caesalpinia macklottii

Caesalpinia macklottii, commonly known as the Macklott's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright orange to red bracts that surround its small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Caesalpinia macklottii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
