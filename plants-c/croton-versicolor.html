Croton versicolor

Croton versicolor, commonly known as the variable croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly variable leaf colors, ranging from bright green to yellow, red, and even purple. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape and often have a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
