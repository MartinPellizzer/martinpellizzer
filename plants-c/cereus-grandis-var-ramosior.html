Cereus grandis var. ramosior

Cereus grandis var. ramosior, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear vibrant flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the holiday season. Its leaves are paddle-shaped and fleshy, adapted to store water.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
