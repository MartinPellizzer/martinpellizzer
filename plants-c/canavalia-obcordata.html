Canavalia obcordata

Canavalia obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
