Clethra purpurea var. microcarpa

Clethra purpurea var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pepperbush, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
