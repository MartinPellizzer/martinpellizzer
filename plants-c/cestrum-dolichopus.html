Cestrum dolichopus

Cestrum dolichopus, commonly known as the long-stalked nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
