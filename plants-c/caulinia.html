Caulinia

Caulinia, commonly known as the "Cauliflower of the desert," is a unique member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plant features a compact, rosette-like growth pattern with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers are small and clustered, resembling cauliflower, hence its common name.

Caulinia is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
