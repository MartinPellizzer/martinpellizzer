Carex hubbardii

Carex hubbardii, commonly known as Hubbard's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikes of flowers.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
