Cashalia cuscatlanica

Cashalia cuscatlanica, commonly known as the El Salvador cashew.

It is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly El Salvador. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other economically important species like the cashew and mango. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
