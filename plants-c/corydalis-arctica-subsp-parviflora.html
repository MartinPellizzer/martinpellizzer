Corydalis arctica subsp. parviflora

Corydalis arctica subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the Arctic corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic regions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

The plant features slender stems and small, spurred flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are basal and arranged in a rosette, which helps to conserve heat and moisture. Corydalis arctica subsp.

parviflora is often found in tundra and alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
