Chenopodium riparium

Chenopodium riparium, commonly known as water spinach or river amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in terminal clusters, followed by small, triangular seeds. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Chenopodium riparium is a fast-growing species that can spread aggressively in suitable conditions.
