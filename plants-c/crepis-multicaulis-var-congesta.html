Crepis multicaulis var. congesta

Crepis multicaulis var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
