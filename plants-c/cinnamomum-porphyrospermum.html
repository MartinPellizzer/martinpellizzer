Cinnamomum porphyrospermum

Cinnamomum porphyrospermum, commonly known as the purple-spleenwort cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique purple veins in its leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
