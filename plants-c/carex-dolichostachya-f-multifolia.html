Carex dolichostachya f. multifolia

Carex dolichostachya f. multifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a sedge species characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower spikes.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in wetland or moist habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, three-parted flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
