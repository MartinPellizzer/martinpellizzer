Cassuvium subpeltatum

Cassuvium subpeltatum, commonly known as the silver-leafed bellflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and bell-shaped, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant prefers well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Cassuvium subpeltatum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
