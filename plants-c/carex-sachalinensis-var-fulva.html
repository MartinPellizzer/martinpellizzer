Carex sachalinensis var. fulva

Carex sachalinensis var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often yellowish-green in color, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
