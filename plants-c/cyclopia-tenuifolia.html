Cyclopia tenuifolia

Cyclopia tenuifolia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
