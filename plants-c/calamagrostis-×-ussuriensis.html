Calamagrostis × ussuriensis

Calamagrostis × ussuriensis, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, pale green to golden flowers in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Calamagrostis × ussuriensis is often used in landscaping for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
