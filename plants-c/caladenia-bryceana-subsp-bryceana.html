Caladenia bryceana subsp. bryceana

Caladenia bryceana subsp.

bryceana, commonly known as the Bryce's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southern regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces a single, strikingly colored flower with a prominent, hooded labellum that resembles a spider, giving it its common name. It typically blooms in spring, with flowers ranging in color from yellow to orange.

This subspecies is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in open habitats with good drainage.
