Camellia rostrata

Camellia rostrata, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, double flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in winter and early spring, come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white.

Camellia rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
