Crataegus cuneata

Crataegus cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus cuneata is often found in moist to wet habitats, including swamps, stream banks, and forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
