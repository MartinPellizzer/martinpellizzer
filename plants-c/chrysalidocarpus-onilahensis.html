Chrysalidocarpus onilahensis

Chrysalidocarpus onilahensis, commonly known as the Onilahensis palm, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, fibrous fruits containing edible seeds.

Due to its restricted habitat and low population numbers, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
