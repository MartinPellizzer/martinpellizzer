Croton catatii var. setosus

Croton catatii var. setosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, multicolored foliage, with leaves that often display shades of green, red, and yellow. Its leaves are hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.
