Campanopsis capillacea

Campanopsis capillacea, commonly known as the thread-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and produces small, thread-like leaves that give it its common name.

Campanopsis capillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
