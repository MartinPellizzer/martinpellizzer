Caragana tragacanthoides var. bongardiana

Caragana tragacanthoides var. bongardiana, commonly known as the Bongardiana shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.
