Chrysothamnus glaucus

Chrysothamnus glaucus, commonly known as the blue curlweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a distinctive blue-green appearance.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive plant.
