Centaurea acutifolia

Centaurea acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
