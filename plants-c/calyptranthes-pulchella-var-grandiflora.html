Calyptranthes pulchella var. grandiflora

Calyptranthes pulchella var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora calyptranthes, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
