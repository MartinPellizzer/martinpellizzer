Cyperus flavomariscus

Cyperus flavomariscus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus flavomariscus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
