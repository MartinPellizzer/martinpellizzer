Clerodendrum populneum

Clerodendrum populneum, commonly known as the white-flowered clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it has a moderate growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
