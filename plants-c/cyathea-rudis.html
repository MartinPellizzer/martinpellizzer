Cyathea rudis

Cyathea rudis, commonly known as the rough tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive rough, scaly trunk that becomes more pronounced with age. Its fronds are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Cyathea rudis is often found in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory.
