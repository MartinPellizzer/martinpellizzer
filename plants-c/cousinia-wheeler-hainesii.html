Cousinia wheeler-hainesii

Cousinia wheeler-hainesii, commonly known as Wheeler-Haines' cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.

Cousinia wheeler-hainesii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
