Campanula alpini var. hungarica

Campanula alpini var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
