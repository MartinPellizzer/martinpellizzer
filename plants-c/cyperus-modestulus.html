Cyperus modestulus

Cyperus modestulus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus modestulus thrives in moist soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for certain bird species.
