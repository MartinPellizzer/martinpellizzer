Chaetochloa villosissima

Chaetochloa villosissima, commonly known as the silky meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
