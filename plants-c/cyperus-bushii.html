Cyperus bushii

Cyperus bushii, commonly known as the Bush's flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically inconspicuous, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
