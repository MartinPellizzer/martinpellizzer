Cymbidium falcatum

Cymbidium falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
