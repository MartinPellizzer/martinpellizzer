Ceratostanthus ajacis

Ceratostanthus ajacis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Ceratostanthus ajacis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
