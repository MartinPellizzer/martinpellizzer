Chionanthus elaeocarpus

Chionanthus elaeocarpus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
