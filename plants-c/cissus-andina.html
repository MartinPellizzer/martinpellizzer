Cissus andina

Cissus andina, commonly known as the Andean grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus andina is also valued for its ability to purify air and its use in traditional medicine.
