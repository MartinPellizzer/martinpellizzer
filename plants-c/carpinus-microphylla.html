Carpinus microphylla

Carpinus microphylla, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged fruits called samaras. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
