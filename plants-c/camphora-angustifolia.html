Camphora angustifolia

Camphora angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy dark green leaves and fragrant, resinous wood that contains camphor. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
