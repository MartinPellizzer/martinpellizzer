Ceropegia dentata

Ceropegia dentata, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia dentata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
