Commelina arenicola

Commelina arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Commelina arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
