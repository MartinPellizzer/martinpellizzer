Calydermos atriplicifolius

Calydermos atriplicifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its roots are fibrous and can become invasive in certain environments.

Calydermos atriplicifolius is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
