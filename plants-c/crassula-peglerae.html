Crassula peglerae

Crassula peglerae, commonly known as the Peggy's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.
