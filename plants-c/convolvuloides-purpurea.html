Convolvuloides purpurea

Convolvuloides purpurea, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, shady areas of tropical and subtropical regions. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ornamental value in gardens.
