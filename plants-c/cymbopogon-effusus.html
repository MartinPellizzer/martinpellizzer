Cymbopogon effusus

Cymbopogon effusus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences. The plant is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cymbopogon effusus is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
