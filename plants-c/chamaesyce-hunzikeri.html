Chamaesyce hunzikeri

Chamaesyce hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its succulent leaves are adapted for storing water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Chamaesyce hunzikeri is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
