Crataegus macracantha var. neofluvialis

Crataegus macracantha var. neofluvialis, commonly known as the Neofluvial hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats along streams and rivers.
