Chrysogonum arnottianum

Chrysogonum arnottianum, commonly known as the Arnott's goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and greenish-yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Chrysogonum arnottianum is known for its showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
