Centaurea corbariensis

Centaurea corbariensis, commonly known as the Corsican thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea corbariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
