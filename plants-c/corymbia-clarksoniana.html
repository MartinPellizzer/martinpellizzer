Corymbia clarksoniana

Corymbia clarksoniana, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces distinctive bottle-shaped fruit that contains woody seeds, and it flowers with small, white or cream-colored blossoms. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
