Cassia tortuensis

Cassia tortuensis, commonly known as the zigzag cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is recognized for its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia tortuensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
