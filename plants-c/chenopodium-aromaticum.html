Chenopodium aromaticum

Chenopodium aromaticum, commonly known as garden spinach or lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a distinctive aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, triangular seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, gardens, and agricultural fields.

Its leaves are used in cooking and have a mild, spinach-like flavor.
