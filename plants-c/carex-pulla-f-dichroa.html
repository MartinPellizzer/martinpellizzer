Carex pulla f. dichroa

Carex pulla f. dichroa, commonly known as the dark-leaved sedge, is a variant of the Carex pulla species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. The plant is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves that are often darker on the underside, giving it a distinctive dichroic appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
