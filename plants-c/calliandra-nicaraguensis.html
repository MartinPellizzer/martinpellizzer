Calliandra nicaraguensis

Calliandra nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua calliandra, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

Calliandra nicaraguensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
