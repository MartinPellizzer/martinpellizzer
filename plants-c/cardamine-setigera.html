Cardamine setigera

Cardamine setigera, commonly known as the forked watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
