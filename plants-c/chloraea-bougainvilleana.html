Chloraea bougainvilleana

Chloraea bougainvilleana, commonly known as the Bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that display shades of red, pink, orange, and purple. The true flowers are small and inconspicuous, located inside the colorful bracts. This plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.
