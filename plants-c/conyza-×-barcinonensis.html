Conyza × barcinonensis

Conyza × barcinonensis, commonly known as the hybrid fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming a composite inflorescence.

Conyza × barcinonensis is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
