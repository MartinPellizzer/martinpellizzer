Ceratophyllum haynaldianum

Ceratophyllum haynaldianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It has whorled leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically found in clusters, and it is often found in still or slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
