Calendula lusitanica var. transtagana

Calendula lusitanica var. transtagana, commonly known as the Transylvania calendula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This variety is often found in meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions.
