Coelodiscus speciosus

Coelodiscus speciosus, commonly known as the ornate orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

This plant is characterized by its intricate, colorful flowers that display a unique combination of purple, yellow, and green hues. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
