Convolvulus

Convolvulus, commonly known as morning glory, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Convolvulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. They produce vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. Many species are annual or perennial herbs with twining stems that climb or trail along the ground.

The genus includes both ornamental and some invasive species, depending on the region and species.
