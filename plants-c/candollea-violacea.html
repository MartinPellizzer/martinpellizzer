Candollea violacea

Candollea violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet and purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapted to its mountainous habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, Candollea violacea is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
