Clinopodium nepeta subsp. bellantianum

Clinopodium nepeta subsp.

bellantianum, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty fragrance.

It is also a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and easy cultivation.
