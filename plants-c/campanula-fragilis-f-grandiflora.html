Campanula fragilis f. grandiflora

Campanula fragilis f. grandiflora, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of large, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The flowers are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens and borders.
