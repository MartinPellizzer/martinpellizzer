Cytisus hirsutus var. purpurascens

Cytisus hirsutus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, woody stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy on both sides.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
