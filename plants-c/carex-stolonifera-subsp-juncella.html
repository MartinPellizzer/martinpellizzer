Carex stolonifera subsp. juncella

Carex stolonifera subsp.

juncella, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows.

The plant produces triangular, grass-like leaves and bears small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. It spreads via stolons, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
