Cajanus inodorus

Cajanus inodorus, commonly known as the Indian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale violet to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Cajanus inodorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
