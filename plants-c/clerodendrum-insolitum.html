Clerodendrum insolitum

Clerodendrum insolitum, commonly known as the strange clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clerodendrum insolitum is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
