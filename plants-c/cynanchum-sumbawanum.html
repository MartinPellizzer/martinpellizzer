Cynanchum sumbawanum

Cynanchum sumbawanum, commonly known as Sumbawan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive seed pods. It is known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.

Cynanchum sumbawanum thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
