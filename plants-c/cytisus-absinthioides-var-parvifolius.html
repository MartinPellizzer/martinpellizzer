Cytisus absinthioides var. parvifolius

Cytisus absinthioides var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and disturbed habitats.
