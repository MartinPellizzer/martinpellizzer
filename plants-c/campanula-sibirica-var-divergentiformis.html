Campanula sibirica var. divergentiformis

Campanula sibirica var. divergentiformis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This variety is notable for its striking floral display and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
