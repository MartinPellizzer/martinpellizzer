Cyperus × hybridus

Cyperus × hybridus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purple tint, especially along the edges.

Cyperus × hybridus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
