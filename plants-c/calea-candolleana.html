Calea candolleana

Calea candolleana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, violet-blue petals that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Calea candolleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
