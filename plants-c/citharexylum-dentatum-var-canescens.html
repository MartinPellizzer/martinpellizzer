Citharexylum dentatum var. canescens

Citharexylum dentatum var. canescens, commonly known as the silky cithara, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
