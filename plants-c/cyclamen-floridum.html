Cyclamen floridum

Cyclamen floridum, commonly known as the Florida cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Cyclamen floridum has a short growing season, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
