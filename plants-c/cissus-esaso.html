Cissus esaso

Cissus esaso, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
