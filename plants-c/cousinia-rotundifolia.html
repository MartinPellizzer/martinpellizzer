Cousinia rotundifolia

Cousinia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf cousinia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are yellow to reddish in color and attract various pollinators.
