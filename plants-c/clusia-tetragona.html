Clusia tetragona

Clusia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with a reddish or purplish underside. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Clusia tetragona is adapted to coastal and tropical environments, often thriving in sandy or limestone soils.
