Ceropegia ballyi

Ceropegia ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
