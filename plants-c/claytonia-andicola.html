Claytonia andicola

Claytonia andicola, commonly known as the Andean spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs.

Claytonia andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, moist conditions.
