Cynorkis clarae

Cynorkis clarae, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a fragrant, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
