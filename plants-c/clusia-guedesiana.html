Clusia guedesiana

Clusia guedesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian shield tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
