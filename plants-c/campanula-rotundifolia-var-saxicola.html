Campanula rotundifolia var. saxicola

Campanula rotundifolia var. saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
