Carex stricta var. gracilis

Carex stricta var. gracilis, commonly known as slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense tussocks. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
