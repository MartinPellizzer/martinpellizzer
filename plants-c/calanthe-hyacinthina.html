Calanthe hyacinthina

Calanthe hyacinthina, commonly known as the hyacinth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. It has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients.

Calanthe hyacinthina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
