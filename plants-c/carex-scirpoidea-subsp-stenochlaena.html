Carex scirpoidea subsp. stenochlaena

Carex scirpoidea subsp.

stenochlaena, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
