Coleus maculosus subsp. lanatus

Coleus maculosus subsp.

lanatus, commonly known as the woolly coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that often display vibrant red, orange, or purple hues.

The plant features a fibrous root system and grows from a rhizomatous base, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.
