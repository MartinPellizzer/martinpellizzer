Cordiera hadrantha

Cordiera hadrantha, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
