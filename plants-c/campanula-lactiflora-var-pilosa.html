Campanula lactiflora var. pilosa

Campanula lactiflora var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features hairy, upright stems that support its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically basal, with a woolly texture due to dense pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
