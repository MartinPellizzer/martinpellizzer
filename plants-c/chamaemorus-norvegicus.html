Chamaemorus norvegicus

Chamaemorus norvegicus, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in harsh environments, often growing on rocky or barren ground. It is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate.

Chamaemorus norvegicus plays an important role in Arctic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various small animals and insects.
