Cavanilla

Cavanilla, commonly known as the "Cavanilla tree," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
