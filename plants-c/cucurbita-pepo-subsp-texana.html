Cucurbita pepo subsp. texana

Cucurbita pepo subsp.

texana, commonly known as the Texas gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the southern United States. It is a trailing vine that produces yellow or orange fruits with a rough, spiny exterior.

The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for drought-tolerant gardening.
