Cadaba barbigera

Cadaba barbigera, commonly known as the woolly spurge or desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
