Camellia japonica var. venosa

Camellia japonica var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated Japanese camellia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale yellow veins, creating a striking variegation pattern. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and elegant flowers make it a prized addition to both landscape design and indoor collections.
