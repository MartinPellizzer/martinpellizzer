Calamagrostis cusickii

Calamagrostis cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces feathery, silver-green plumes that are both ornamental and attractively structured. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereals.
