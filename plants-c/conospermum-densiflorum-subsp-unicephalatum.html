Conospermum densiflorum subsp. unicephalatum

Conospermum densiflorum subsp.

unicephalatum, commonly known as the single-headed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, erect growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
