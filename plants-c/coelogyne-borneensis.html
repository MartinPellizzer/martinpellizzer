Coelogyne borneensis

Coelogyne borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to the forests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne borneensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
