Cirsium echinatum

Cirsium echinatum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and tall, purple-pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium echinatum is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
