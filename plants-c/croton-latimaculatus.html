Croton latimaculatus

Croton latimaculatus, commonly known as the spotted croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Croton latimaculatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant and varied leaf patterns.
