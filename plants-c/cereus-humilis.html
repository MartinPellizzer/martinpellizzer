Cereus humilis

Cereus humilis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is often found in arid environments. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a single night.

Cereus humilis is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
