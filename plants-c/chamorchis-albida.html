Chamorchis albida

Chamorchis albida, commonly known as the white chamorchis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Chamorchis albida is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
