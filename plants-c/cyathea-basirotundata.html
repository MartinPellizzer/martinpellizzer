Cyathea basirotundata

Cyathea basirotundata, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is covered with persistent, leaf-like structures called rachises. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea basirotundata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
