Cucumis gracilis

Cucumis gracilis, commonly known as the slender cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thin, elongated fruit. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is usually harvested when immature, resembling a small cucumber in appearance.

Cucumis gracilis is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
