Cicuta nipponica

Cicuta nipponica, commonly known as Japanese water hemlock, is a toxic member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters. It is highly poisonous, containing toxic alkaloids that can be fatal to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
