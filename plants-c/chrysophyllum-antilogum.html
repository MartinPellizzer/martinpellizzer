Chrysophyllum antilogum

Chrysophyllum antilogum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
