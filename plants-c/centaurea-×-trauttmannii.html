Centaurea × trauttmannii

Centaurea × trauttmannii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea solstitialis (yellow star-thistle). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and bright blue or purple flowers with a distinctive ray floret. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
