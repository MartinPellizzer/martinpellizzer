Crocus kotschyanus subsp. suworowianus

Crocus kotschyanus subsp.

suworowianus, commonly known as the Suworowian crocus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with six petals and a distinctive, rounded shape. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
