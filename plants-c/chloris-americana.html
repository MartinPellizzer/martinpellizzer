Chloris americana

Chloris americana, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a native perennial grass species found throughout much of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. Its seed heads are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value.

Chloris americana plays an important ecological role by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in open areas.
