Cassia medica

Cassia medica, commonly known as the sickle pod or sickle tree, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain small, dark seeds. Cassia medica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of fiber.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
