Citrus × papillaris

Citrus × papillaris, commonly known as the sour orange.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. The leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. The tree is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and fragrant white flowers.

It is valued for its high vitamin C content and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
