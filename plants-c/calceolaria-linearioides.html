Calceolaria linearioides

Calceolaria linearioides, commonly known as the slender slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria linearioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, exotic appearance.
