Cymbidium virescens

Cymbidium virescens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that produces long, narrow leaves and is characterized by its striking greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its elegant, drooping inflorescences.

Cymbidium virescens is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
