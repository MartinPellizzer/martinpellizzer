Calceolaria cerasifolia

Calceolaria cerasifolia, commonly known as the cherry blossom calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble tiny cups, with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats and prefers cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Calceolaria cerasifolia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
