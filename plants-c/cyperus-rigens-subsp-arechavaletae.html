Cyperus rigens subsp. arechavaletae

Cyperus rigens subsp.

arechavaletae, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikelets that typically appear in the summer months.

This subspecies is native to parts of Africa and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. It thrives in moist soils and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its morphology and habitat make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
