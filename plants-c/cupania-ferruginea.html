Cupania ferruginea

Cupania ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cupania, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe that turns red when mature.

Cupania ferruginea is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
