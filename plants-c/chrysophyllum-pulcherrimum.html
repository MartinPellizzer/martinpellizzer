Chrysophyllum pulcherrimum

Chrysophyllum pulcherrimum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some uses in traditional medicine.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
