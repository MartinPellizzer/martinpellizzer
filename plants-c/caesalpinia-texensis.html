Caesalpinia texensis

Caesalpinia texensis, commonly known as the Texas wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a distinctive reddish or bronze coloration. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in late spring. Its pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds.

Caesalpinia texensis is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in limestone soils.
