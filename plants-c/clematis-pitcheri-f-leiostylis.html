Clematis pitcheri f. leiostylis

Clematis pitcheri f. leiostylis, commonly known as the western clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are typically woody and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly areas.

This variety is notable for its distinct floral morphology and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
