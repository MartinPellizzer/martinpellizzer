Conyza feae

Conyza feae, commonly known as the rough blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small blue to purple flowers.

Conyza feae is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in its native range.
