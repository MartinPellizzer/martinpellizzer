Cereus speciosissimus var. lothii

Cereus speciosissimus var. lothii, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a hybrid species native to southern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its branching, cylindrical stems that bear clusters of bright, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink, red, or white blooms during the winter months, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Despite its name, it is not closely related to true Christmas cacti, which belong to the Schlumbergera genus.
