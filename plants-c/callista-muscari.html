Callista muscari

Callista muscari, commonly known as the musk lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that resemble lilies. The plant produces a cluster of bell-shaped blooms that hang from a stem, often with a subtle musky scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Callista muscari is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and pleasant fragrance.
