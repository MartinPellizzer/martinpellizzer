Clematis barahonensis

Clematis barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahon clematis, is a flowering vine native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and climbing growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Clematis barahonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
