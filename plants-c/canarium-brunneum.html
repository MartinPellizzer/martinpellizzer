Canarium brunneum

Canarium brunneum, commonly known as the black oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fruits that contain a valuable oil. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Canarium brunneum plays an important role in both ecological and economic systems in its native habitats.
