Culcitium albifolium

Culcitium albifolium, commonly known as the white-leafed culcitium, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, aiding in its survival in harsh, sunny environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pale yellow and white, typically blooming during the spring and early summer months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from underground sources in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Culcitium albifolium is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
