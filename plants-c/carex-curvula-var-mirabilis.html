Carex curvula var. mirabilis

Carex curvula var. mirabilis, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that often has a silvery or grayish hue due to the presence of fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
