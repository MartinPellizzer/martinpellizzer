Cochlearia flagrans

Cochlearia flagrans, commonly known as the flowering scurvygrass, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and its solitary, erect flowering stalk. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in a raceme. It is native to coastal areas of Europe and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Cochlearia flagrans is adapted to maritime environments and is often used in coastal restoration projects.
