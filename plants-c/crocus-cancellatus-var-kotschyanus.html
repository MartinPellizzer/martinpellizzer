Crocus cancellatus var. kotschyanus

Crocus cancellatus var. kotschyanus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers with a distinctive pattern of darker markings on the petals. It typically blooms in early spring and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The bulbs of this species are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
