Croton menabeensis

Croton menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various chemical compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.
