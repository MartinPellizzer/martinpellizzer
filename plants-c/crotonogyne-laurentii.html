Crotonogyne laurentii

Crotonogyne laurentii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers and dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
