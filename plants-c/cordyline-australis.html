Cordyline australis

Cordyline australis, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is closely related to other flowering plants in the Cordyline genus.
