Copiapoa humilis subsp. variispinata

Copiapoa humilis subsp.

variispinata, commonly known as the Variegated Copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellow spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
