Carex bengalensis

Carex bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or pale yellow, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Carex bengalensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
