Capsella bursa-pastoris tenuissima

Capsella bursa-pastoris tenuissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
