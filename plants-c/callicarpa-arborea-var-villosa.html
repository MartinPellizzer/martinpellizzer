Callicarpa arborea var. villosa

Callicarpa arborea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are attractive to birds and other wildlife. The plant has hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental berries and autumn foliage.
