Cyathea stipitulata

Cyathea stipitulata, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and a thick, fibrous trunk that resembles a tree. The plant has large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea stipitulata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
