Chabertia agrestis

Chabertia agrestis, commonly known as the field chabertia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Chabertia agrestis is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
