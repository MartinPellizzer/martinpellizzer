Cancrinia paropamisica

Cancrinia paropamisica, commonly known as the Paropamisic rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
