Crossolepis eriocephala

Crossolepis eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly crossolepis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Crossolepis eriocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique texture.
