Castanea inermis

Castanea inermis, commonly known as the smooth chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain one or two edible nuts. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in catkins, and the plant is typically dioecious, with separate male and female trees.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
