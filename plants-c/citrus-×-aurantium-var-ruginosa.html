Citrus × aurantium var. ruginosa

Citrus × aurantium var. ruginosa, commonly known as the rough lemon, is a citrus hybrid native to Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, and often thorny rind, which is a distinguishing feature from other citrus varieties. The plant produces small, yellow, sour fruits that are typically used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.

The rough lemon is also valued for its aromatic leaves and its role in citrus breeding programs.
