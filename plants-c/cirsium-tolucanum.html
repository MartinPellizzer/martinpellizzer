Cirsium tolucanum

Cirsium tolucanum, commonly known as Tolucan thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in scrublands and grasslands. Its foliage is covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its native ecosystem.
