Catasetum × louiseae

Catasetum × louiseae, commonly known as the orchid hybrid Louiseae, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid that typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a unique structure that includes a specialized column for pollination. Its botanical features include pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and long, strap-like leaves.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care in suitable conditions.
