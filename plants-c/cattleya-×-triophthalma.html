Cattleya × triophthalma

Cattleya × triophthalma, commonly known as the triophthalma orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from Cattleya and Laelia genera.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with vibrant colors, often in shades of pink, orange, and yellow, and it has a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its evergreen foliage, and it produces multiple spikes with clustered blooms.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
