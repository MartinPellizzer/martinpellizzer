Calophyllum sundaicum

Calophyllum sundaicum, commonly known as the Sunda calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
