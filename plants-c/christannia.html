Christannia

Christannia, commonly known as the common thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their spiny leaves and showy composite flowers that typically bloom in the summer. Christannia species are native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. They are often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

The plant's structure includes a central stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in stiff, prickly hairs.
