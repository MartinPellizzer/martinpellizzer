Ceraia sanguinea

Ceraia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-veined beauty or the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitats.
