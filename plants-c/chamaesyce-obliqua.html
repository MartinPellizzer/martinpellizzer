Chamaesyce obliqua

Chamaesyce obliqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its leaves are narrow and often recurved, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Chamaesyce obliqua produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color.
