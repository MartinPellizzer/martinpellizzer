Cymboglossum grande

Cymboglossum grande, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, with thick, fleshy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cymboglossum grande is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries due to its striking floral display.
