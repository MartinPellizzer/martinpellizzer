Cymbidiella perrieri

Cymbidiella perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and small, delicate blossoms that bloom in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
