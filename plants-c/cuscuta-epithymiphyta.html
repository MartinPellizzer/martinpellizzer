Cuscuta epithymiphyta

Cuscuta epithymiphyta, commonly known as the dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrition.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure covered in tiny, adhesive tendrils that attach to host plants. The plant's seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, allowing it to colonize a wide range of hosts. It is often found on agricultural crops, weeds, and shrubs, making it a common weed in many regions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly reduce the growth and yield of its host plants.
