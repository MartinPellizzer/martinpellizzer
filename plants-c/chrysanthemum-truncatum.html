Chrysanthemum truncatum

Chrysanthemum truncatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Chrysanthemum truncatum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
