Citrus × limonum var. caly

Citrus × limonum var. caly, commonly known as the lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellow rind and a high concentration of citric acid. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and opposite, while the flowers are fragrant and white. The plant is evergreen, typically growing as a shrub or small tree, and thrives in warm, sunny climates.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.
