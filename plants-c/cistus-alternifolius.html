Cistus alternifolius

Cistus alternifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its leaves are typically alternate and narrow, giving it its scientific name.

Cistus alternifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
