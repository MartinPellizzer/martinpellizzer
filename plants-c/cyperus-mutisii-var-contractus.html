Cyperus mutisii var. contractus

Cyperus mutisii var. contractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
