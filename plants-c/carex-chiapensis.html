Carex chiapensis

Carex chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Carex chiapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
