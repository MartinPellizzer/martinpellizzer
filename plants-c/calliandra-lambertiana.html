Calliandra lambertiana

Calliandra lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Calliandra lambertiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
