Cordia elaeagnoides

Cordia elaeagnoides, commonly known as silverleaf cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It has alternate, simple, and usually elliptic or ovate leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and ornamental contexts.
