Carex × abitibiana

Carex × abitibiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex scoparia and Carexusta, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms clumps of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are followed by cylindrical, three-sided seed heads.

Carex × abitibiana is often found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.
