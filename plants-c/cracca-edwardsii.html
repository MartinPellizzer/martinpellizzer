Cracca edwardsii

Cracca edwardsii, commonly known as Edwards' cracca, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often triangular leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, adding a splash of color to its habitat. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can be beneficial in stabilizing soil.

Cracca edwardsii is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine regions.
