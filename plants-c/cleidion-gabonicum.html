Cleidion gabonicum

Cleidion gabonicum, commonly known as the Gabon ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, lowland forests and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
