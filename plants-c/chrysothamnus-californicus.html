Chrysothamnus californicus

Chrysothamnus californicus, commonly known as golden sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally by Native American communities.

Chrysothamnus californicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local pollinators.
