Ceropegia pachypodium

Ceropegia pachypodium, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
