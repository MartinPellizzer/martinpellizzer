Centaurea jacea var. vinyalsii

Centaurea jacea var. vinyalsii, commonly known as the Pyrenean strangleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region.

It features striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with rough textures. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native environment.
