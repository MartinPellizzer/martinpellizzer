Cinnamomum doederleinii var. raoanensis

Cinnamomum doederleinii var. raoanensis, commonly known as the Raoan cinnamon, is a rare and lesser-known species of the Cinnamomum genus.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, specifically found in the state of Kerala. This plant is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-like bark and small, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within dense forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species with conservation concerns.
