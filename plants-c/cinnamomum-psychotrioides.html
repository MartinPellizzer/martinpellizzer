Cinnamomum psychotrioides

Cinnamomum psychotrioides, commonly known as the psychotrioides cinnamon tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

The tree also bears small, fragrant flowers followed by egg-shaped berries that are typically red when mature.
