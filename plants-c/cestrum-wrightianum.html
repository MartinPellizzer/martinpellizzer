Cestrum wrightianum

Cestrum wrightianum, commonly known as the Wright's cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the evening. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy texture.

Cestrum wrightianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
