Cereus kroenleinii

Cereus kroenleinii, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically fragrant. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

Cereus kroenleinii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering habits.
