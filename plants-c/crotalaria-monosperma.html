Crotalaria monosperma

Crotalaria monosperma, commonly known as the one-seeded cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain a single seed, giving it its specific epithet "monosperma.". It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions due to its deep root system.

This species is also noted for its potential in soil nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in agricultural contexts.
