Cavendishia punctata

Cavendishia punctata, commonly known as the spotted cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its unique appearance, featuring white and green spines, makes it a striking addition to desert landscapes.

Cavendishia punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
