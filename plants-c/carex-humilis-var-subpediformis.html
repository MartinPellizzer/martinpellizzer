Carex humilis var. subpediformis

Carex humilis var. subpediformis, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with poor drainage.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
