Cichorium intybus var. khatoonae

Cichorium intybus var. khatoonae, commonly known as Persian chicory, is a variant of the common chicory plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically curly and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which can be used in salads or cooked like spinach.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
