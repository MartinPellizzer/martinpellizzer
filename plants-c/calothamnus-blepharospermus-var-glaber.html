Calothamnus blepharospermus var. glaber

Calothamnus blepharospermus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved golden bush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
