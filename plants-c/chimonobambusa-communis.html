Chimonobambusa communis

Chimonobambusa communis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of many grass species. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types.

Chimonobambusa communis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
