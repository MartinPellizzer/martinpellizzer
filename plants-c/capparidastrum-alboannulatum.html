Capparidastrum alboannulatum

Capparidastrum alboannulatum, commonly known as the white-annulated caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, edible capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
