Centella pilosa

Centella pilosa, commonly known as hairy centella, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Centella pilosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
