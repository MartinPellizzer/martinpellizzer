Caladenia aurulenta

Caladenia aurulenta, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, spider-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly wasps, which are attracted by the flower's unique shape and scent.

Caladenia aurulenta is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
