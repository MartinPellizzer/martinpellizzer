Conamomum flavidulum

Conamomum flavidulum, commonly known as the yellow star ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces yellow-orange berries that are edible.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
