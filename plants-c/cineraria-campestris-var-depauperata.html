Cineraria campestris var. depauperata

Cineraria campestris var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower cineraria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silvery-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in loose clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
