Cofer

Cofer, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Cofer thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
