Capsella bursa-pastoris f. coronopifolia

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown-leaf weed, is a variant of the common chickweed.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a taproot and hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive crown-like arrangement at the base. Its small white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by spherical, hairy seed pods.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and agricultural fields.
