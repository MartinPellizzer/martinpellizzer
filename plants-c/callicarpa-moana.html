Callicarpa moana

Callicarpa moana, commonly known as the Pacific beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered, with showy pink or white petals, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
