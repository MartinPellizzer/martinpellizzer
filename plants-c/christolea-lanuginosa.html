Christolea lanuginosa

Christolea lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly christolea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lanuginous leaves that give it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunlit habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
