Crypsinus induratus

Crypsinus induratus, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
