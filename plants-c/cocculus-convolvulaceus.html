Cocculus convolvulaceus

Cocculus convolvulaceus, commonly known as the morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to twine around supports. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and traditional medicinal uses.
