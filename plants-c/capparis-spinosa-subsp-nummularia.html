Capparis spinosa subsp. nummularia

Capparis spinosa subsp.

nummularia, commonly known as the Mediterranean sour fig, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fleshy, yellowish-orange berries that are edible when ripe.

The plant has prickly stems and oval-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a sour ingredient in local cuisines.
