Cirsium domonii

Cirsium domonii, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust stem covered in prickly bracts. Its leaves are usually alternate, with serrated edges and a grayish-green color.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
