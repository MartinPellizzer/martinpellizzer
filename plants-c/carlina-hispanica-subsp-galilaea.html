Carlina hispanica subsp. galilaea

Carlina hispanica subsp.

galilaea, commonly known as the Galilaea ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy composite flowers that typically bloom in late summer.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed or toothed appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
