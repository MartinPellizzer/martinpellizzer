Cordia lanata

Cordia lanata, commonly known as the woolly cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Cordia lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
