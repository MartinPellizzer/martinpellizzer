Cosmos pinnatus

Cosmos pinnatus, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery plumes that resemble soft, flowing hair, which bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has delicate, thread-like leaves that are opposite and linear, contributing to its airy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cosmos pinnatus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
