Cyperus distachyos

Cyperus distachyos, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, spike-like clusters.

Cyperus distachyos is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
