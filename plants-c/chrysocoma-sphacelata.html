Chrysocoma sphacelata

Chrysocoma sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture, aiding in water conservation.

Chrysocoma sphacelata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
