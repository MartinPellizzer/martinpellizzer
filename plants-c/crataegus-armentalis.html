Crataegus armentalis

Crataegus armentalis, commonly known as the silverthorn or winter thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

Crataegus armentalis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
