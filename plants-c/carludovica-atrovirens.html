Carludovica atrovirens

Carludovica atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing, leafless cactus that features dense clusters of spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its dark green foliage. Its structure allows it to thrive in arid environments by minimizing water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
