Crataegus hamata

Crataegus hamata, commonly known as the prickly hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
