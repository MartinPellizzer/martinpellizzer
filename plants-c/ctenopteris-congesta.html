Ctenopteris congesta

Ctenopteris congesta, commonly known as the clustered cliff fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that form a compact, bushy appearance. The plant has feather-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Ctenopteris congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
