Chionanthus polygamus

Chionanthus polygamus, commonly known as the common white fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Chionanthus polygamus is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.
