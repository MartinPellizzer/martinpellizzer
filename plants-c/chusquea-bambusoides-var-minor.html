Chusquea bambusoides var. minor

Chusquea bambusoides var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and exhibits a unique combination of bamboo-like growth and grass characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are covered in a fine layer of hair. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its potential use in horticultural landscapes.
