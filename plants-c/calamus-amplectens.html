Calamus amplectens

Calamus amplectens, commonly known as the clasping calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and broad, sword-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus amplectens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
