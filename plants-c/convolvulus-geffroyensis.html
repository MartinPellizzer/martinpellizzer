Convolvulus geffroyensis

Convolvulus geffroyensis, commonly known as the Geoffroy's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a ornamental and a potentially invasive species in certain regions.
