Carex incurva var. chartacea

Carex incurva var. chartacea, commonly known as the parchment sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly folded, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
