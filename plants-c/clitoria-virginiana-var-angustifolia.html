Clitoria virginiana var. angustifolia

Clitoria virginiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration. The plant produces small, butterfly-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
