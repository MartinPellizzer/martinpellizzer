Cymbidium crispatum

Cymbidium crispatum, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive crested lip that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium crispatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
