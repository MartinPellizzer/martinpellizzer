Cirsium charkeviczii

Cirsium charkeviczii, commonly known as the Charkevicz thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. It is known for its robust structure and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
