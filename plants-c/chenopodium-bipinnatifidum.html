Chenopodium bipinnatifidum

Chenopodium bipinnatifidum, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium bipinnatifidum is a nutritious plant, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals, and has been used in traditional cuisines.
