Croton elbertii

Croton elbertii, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the genus Croton in the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with diverse morphological features. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a range of hues, including red, yellow, and green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton elbertii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
