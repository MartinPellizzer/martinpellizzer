Chamaesyce rockii var. grandifolia

Chamaesyce rockii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, arid environments, often found in desert regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry climate.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
