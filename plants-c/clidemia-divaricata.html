Clidemia divaricata

Clidemia divaricata, commonly known as the creeping waxplant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Clidemia divaricata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential invasive species in certain areas.
