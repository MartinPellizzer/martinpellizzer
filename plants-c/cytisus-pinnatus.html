Cytisus pinnatus

Cytisus pinnatus, commonly known as the red broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
