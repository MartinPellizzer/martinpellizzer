Cirsium creticum subsp. creticum

Cirsium creticum subsp.

creticum, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various insect species.
