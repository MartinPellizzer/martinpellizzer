Clerodendrum wildii

Clerodendrum wildii, commonly known as the wild betony, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Clerodendrum wildii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
