Centaurea fenzlii

Centaurea fenzlii, commonly known as Fenzli's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, cylindrical achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea fenzlii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
