Crotalaria dasyclada

Crotalaria dasyclada, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several oblong seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Crotalaria dasyclada is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
