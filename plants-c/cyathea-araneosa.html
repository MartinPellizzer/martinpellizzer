Cyathea araneosa

Cyathea araneosa, commonly known as the spider's web tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that resemble a spider's web. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizome system supports the plant's structure and helps in nutrient absorption from the soil.

Cyathea araneosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
