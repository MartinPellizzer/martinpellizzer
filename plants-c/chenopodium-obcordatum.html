Chenopodium obcordatum

Chenopodium obcordatum, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into edible, seed-containing capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Purslane is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of omega-3 fatty acids and vitamins.
