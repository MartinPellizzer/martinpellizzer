Cirsium × treuinfelsianum

Cirsium × treuinfelsianum, commonly known as the Treuinfels thistle, is a naturally occurring hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, coarse leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy purple to pinkish composite flowers, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in mountainous regions of Central Europe.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
