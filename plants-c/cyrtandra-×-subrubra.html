Cyrtandra × subrubra

Cyrtandra × subrubra, commonly known as the red-flowered cyrtandra, is a hybrid species within the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including Hawaii and parts of Southeast Asia.

Cyrtandra × subrubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
