Cnestis pinnata

Cnestis pinnata, commonly known as the African horse bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a legume for soil enrichment.
