Cissampelos pareira subvar. hirta

Cissampelos pareira subvar. hirta, commonly known as the hairy devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated, leaf-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
