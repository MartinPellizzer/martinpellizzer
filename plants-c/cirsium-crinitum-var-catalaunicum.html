Cirsium crinitum var. catalaunicum

Cirsium crinitum var. catalaunicum, commonly known as the Catalaunic thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
