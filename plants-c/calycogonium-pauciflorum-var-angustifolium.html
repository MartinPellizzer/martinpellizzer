Calycogonium pauciflorum var. angustifolium

Calycogonium pauciflorum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calycogonium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the dry season. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
