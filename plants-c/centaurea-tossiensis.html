Centaurea tossiensis

Centaurea tossiensis, commonly known as the Tossi's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea tossiensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
