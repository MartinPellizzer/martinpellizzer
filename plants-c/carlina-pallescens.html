Carlina pallescens

Carlina pallescens, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and distinctive blue to violet flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant produces ray florets that are typically blue or purple, surrounded by a dense, yellow disk of tubular flowers. Carlina pallescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family, with potential ecological and medicinal significance.
