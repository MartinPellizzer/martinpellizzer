Celmisia hieracioides

Celmisia hieracioides, commonly known as the tufted flaxflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Celmisia hieracioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
