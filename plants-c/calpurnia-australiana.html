Calpurnia australiana

Calpurnia australiana, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a species native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Calpurnia australiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
