Cucumis melo var. pancherianus

Cucumis melo var. pancherianus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variant of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellowish-green fruit with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its high water content and nutritional profile.
