Cajanus albicans

Cajanus albicans, commonly known as the white gram or white pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, white to yellowish seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Cajanus albicans is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
