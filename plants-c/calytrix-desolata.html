Calytrix desolata

Calytrix desolata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and salt-laden environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Calytrix desolata is a hardy species that plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
