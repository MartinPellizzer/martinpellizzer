Crotalaria recta

Crotalaria recta, commonly known as straight cowpea or straight spurred cowpea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
