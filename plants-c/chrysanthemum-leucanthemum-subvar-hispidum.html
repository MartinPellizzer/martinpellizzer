Chrysanthemum leucanthemum subvar. hispidum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum subvar. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow ray florets and dark brown disk flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
