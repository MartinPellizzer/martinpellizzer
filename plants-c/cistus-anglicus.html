Cistus anglicus

Cistus anglicus, commonly known as the English rock rose, is a shrub native to southern England and western Europe.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cistus anglicus is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
