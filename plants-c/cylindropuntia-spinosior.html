Cylindropuntia spinosior

Cylindropuntia spinosior, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
