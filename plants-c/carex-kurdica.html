Carex kurdica

Carex kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and Iraq. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along streams. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Carex kurdica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
