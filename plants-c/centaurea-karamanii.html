Centaurea karamanii

Centaurea karamanii, commonly known as Karaman's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits known as cypsela.

Centaurea karamanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
