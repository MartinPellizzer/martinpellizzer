Cousinia rigidissima

Cousinia rigidissima, commonly known as the rigid globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cousinia rigidissima is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.
