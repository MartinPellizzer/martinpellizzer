Carex caesia

Carex caesia, commonly known as blue stem sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex caesia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
