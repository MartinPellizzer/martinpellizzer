Crotalaria persica

Crotalaria persica, commonly known as Persian crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including Iran, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria persica is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, some species in the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
