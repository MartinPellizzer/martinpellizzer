Cyrtopodium paludicola var. reginae

Cyrtopodium paludicola var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires a humid environment to flourish. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it is often found in peat bogs or marshy areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
