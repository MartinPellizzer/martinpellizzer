Chrysopogon caeruleus

Chrysopogon caeruleus, commonly known as blue grass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Chrysopogon caeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
