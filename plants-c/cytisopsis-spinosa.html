Cytisopsis spinosa

Cytisopsis spinosa, commonly known as the spinous broom, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, narrow leaflets.

Cytisopsis spinosa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
