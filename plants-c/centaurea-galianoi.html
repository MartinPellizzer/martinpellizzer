Centaurea galianoi

Centaurea galianoi, commonly known as the Galiano's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea galianoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
