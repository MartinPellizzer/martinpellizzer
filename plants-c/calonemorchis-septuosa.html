Calonemorchis septuosa

Calonemorchis septuosa, commonly known as the seven-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern, often with seven leaves emerging from the base.

The species is noted for its compact size and unique floral structure, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
