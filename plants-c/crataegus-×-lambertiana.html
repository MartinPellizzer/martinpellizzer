Crataegus × lambertiana

Crataegus × lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate urban environments.
