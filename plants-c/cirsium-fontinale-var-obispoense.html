Cirsium fontinale var. obispoense

Cirsium fontinale var. obispoense, commonly known as the Obispo thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to local pollinators.
