Clinopodium megalanthum var. intermedium

Clinopodium megalanthum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate meadow betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and marshes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
