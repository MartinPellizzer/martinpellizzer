Carex gokwanensis

Carex gokwanensis, commonly known as the Gokwan sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and usually have a bluish-green color, while its flowering spikes are small and inconspicuous.

Carex gokwanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
