Croton fishlockii

Croton fishlockii, commonly known as the Fishlock's croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton fishlockii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.
