Calophyllum thwaitesii

Calophyllum thwaitesii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a white, edible aril.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
