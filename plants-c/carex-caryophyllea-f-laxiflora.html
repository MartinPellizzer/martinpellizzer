Carex caryophyllea f. laxiflora

Carex caryophyllea f. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower sedge, is a variant of the broad-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
