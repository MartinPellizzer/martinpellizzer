Croton lapiazicola var. longibracteatus

Croton lapiazicola var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that are often brightly colored, serving as an attractant for pollinators. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and simple, with a waxy texture and prominent venation.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
