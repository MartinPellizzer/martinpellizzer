Chenopodium album var. reticulatum

Chenopodium album var. reticulatum, commonly known as wild spinach or fat hen, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, branched clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
