Cracca kuntzei

Cracca kuntzei, commonly known as the Kuntze's cracca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright red bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive structure that aids in wind pollination.

Cracca kuntzei is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and compact size.
