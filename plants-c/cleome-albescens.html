Cleome albescens

Cleome albescens, commonly known as white cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods that split open when mature.

Cleome albescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowers.
