Cytisus leucanthus subsp. pallidus

Cytisus leucanthus subsp.

pallidus, commonly known as the pale broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
