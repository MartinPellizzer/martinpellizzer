Chrysalidocarpus ruber

Chrysalidocarpus ruber, commonly known as the red fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are a vibrant red or orange in color, which distinguish it from other palm species. The plant grows as a solitary tree with a short trunk and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
