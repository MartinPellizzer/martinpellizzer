Convolvulus tujuntauensis

Convolvulus tujuntauensis, commonly known as the Tujuntau morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or blue blooms that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky slopes and in sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
