Cynanchum caespitulosum

Cynanchum caespitulosum, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and has small, usually blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cynanchum caespitulosum is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock.
