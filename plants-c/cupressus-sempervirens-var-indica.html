Cupressus sempervirens var. indica

Cupressus sempervirens var. indica, commonly known as the Indian cypress, is a variant of the Mediterranean cypress.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color. This evergreen conifer is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody cones.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
