Cynanchum albiflorum

Cynanchum albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Cynanchum albiflorum is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory and skin conditions.
