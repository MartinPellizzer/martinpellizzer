Chelonistele sulphurea var. crassifolia

Chelonistele sulphurea var. crassifolia, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular stem with a yellowish-green hue, covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically arranged in a star-like pattern. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
