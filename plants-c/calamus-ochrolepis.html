Calamus ochrolepis

Calamus ochrolepis, commonly known as the yellow-edged calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often yellow-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus ochrolepis is often found in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
