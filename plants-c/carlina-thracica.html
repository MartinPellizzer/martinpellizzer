Carlina thracica

Carlina thracica, commonly known as the Thrace carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by ray florets.

Carlina thracica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
