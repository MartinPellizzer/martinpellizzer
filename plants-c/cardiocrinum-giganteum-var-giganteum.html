Cardiocrinum giganteum var. giganteum

Cardiocrinum giganteum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It grows to a height of up to 6 feet, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in the lily family.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in traditional Chinese medicine.
