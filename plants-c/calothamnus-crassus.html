Calothamnus crassus

Calothamnus crassus, commonly known as the broad-leaved candlebush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Calothamnus crassus is often found in sandy or rocky soils along coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
