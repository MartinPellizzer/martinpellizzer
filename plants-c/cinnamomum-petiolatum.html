Cinnamomum petiolatum

Cinnamomum petiolatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-like bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces berries that contain aromatic seeds.

The essential oils from its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
