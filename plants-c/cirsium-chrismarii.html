Cirsium chrismarii

Cirsium chrismarii, commonly known as the Christmaria thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, prickly hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Cirsium chrismarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
