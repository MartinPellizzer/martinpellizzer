Cucumis odoratissimus

Cucumis odoratissimus, commonly known as the fragrant cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers, melons, and squashes. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that have a distinctively sweet and fragrant aroma. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued not only for its unique scent but also for its potential medicinal properties.
