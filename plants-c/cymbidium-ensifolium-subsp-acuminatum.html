Cymbidium ensifolium subsp. acuminatum

Cymbidium ensifolium subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and linear, narrow leaves.

The flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are often pale green or yellowish with a faintly colored lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
