Croton klotzschii var. intermedius

Croton klotzschii var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
