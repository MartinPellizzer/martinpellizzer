Calamus longisetus

Calamus longisetus, commonly known as the long-haired calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and long, narrow leaves with hairy margins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arums.

Calamus longisetus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
