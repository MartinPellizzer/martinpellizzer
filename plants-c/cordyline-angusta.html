Cordyline angusta

Cordyline angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that support narrow, strap-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The leaves are typically a deep green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside, adding visual interest. It produces small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in panicles during the warmer months.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor settings.
