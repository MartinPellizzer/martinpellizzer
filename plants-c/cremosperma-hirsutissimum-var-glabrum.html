Cremosperma hirsutissimum var. glabrum

Cremosperma hirsutissimum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth cremosperma, is a variant of the cremosperma species characterized by its reduced hair coverage compared to the typical form.

It is a perennial shrub native to specific regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and its role in local ecosystems.

The smooth cremosperma is also of interest to botanists due to its potential adaptations to arid environments.
