Convolvulus erubescens

Convolvulus erubescens, commonly known as the scarlet bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
