Commiphora lughensis

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. It produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
