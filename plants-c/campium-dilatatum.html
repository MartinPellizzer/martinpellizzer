Campium dilatatum

Campium dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Campium dilatatum is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil.
