Cymbopogon nardus subsp. nilgiricus

Cymbopogon nardus subsp.

nilgiricus, commonly known as the Nilgiri grass, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne at the nodes of the stems. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
