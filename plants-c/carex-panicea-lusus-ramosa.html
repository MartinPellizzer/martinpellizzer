Carex panicea lusus ramosa

Carex panicea lusus ramosa, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
