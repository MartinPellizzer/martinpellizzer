Cistus ladanifer immaculatus

Cistus ladanifer immaculatus, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that release a distinctive scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Cistus ladanifer immaculatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
