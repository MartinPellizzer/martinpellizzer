Crataegus michiganensis

Crataegus michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its simple, ovate leaves and persistent, woody fruits.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
