Carex sartwelliana

Carex sartwelliana, commonly known as Sartwell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Carex sartwelliana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
