Chaetochloa composita

Chaetochloa composita, commonly known as the composite hairgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hair-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
