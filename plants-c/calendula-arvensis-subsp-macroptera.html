Calendula arvensis subsp. macroptera

Calendula arvensis subsp.

macroptera, commonly known as the large-rayed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

It is often found in meadows and along the edges of woods, where it thrives in full sun.
