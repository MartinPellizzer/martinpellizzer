Carex leptopoda

Carex leptopoda, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to various regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Carex leptopoda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
