Clematis smilacifolia var. angustifolia

Clematis smilacifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
