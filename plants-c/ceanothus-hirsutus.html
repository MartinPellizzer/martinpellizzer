Ceanothus hirsutus

Ceanothus hirsutus, commonly known as the woolly ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus hirsutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
