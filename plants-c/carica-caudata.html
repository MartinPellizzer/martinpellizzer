Carica caudata

Carica caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, long-stemmed fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and juicy flesh that is often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
