Celtis chekiangensis

Celtis chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Celtis chekiangensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
