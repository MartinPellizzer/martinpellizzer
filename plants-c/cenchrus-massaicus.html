Cenchrus massaicus

Cenchrus massaicus, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets with bristly hairs, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cenchrus massaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
