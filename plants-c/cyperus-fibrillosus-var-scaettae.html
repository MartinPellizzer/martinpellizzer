Cyperus fibrillosus var. scaettae

Cyperus fibrillosus var. scaettae, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes make it a hardy species capable of spreading through vegetative propagation.
