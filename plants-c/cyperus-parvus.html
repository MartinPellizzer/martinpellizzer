Cyperus parvus

Cyperus parvus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than 10 cm in length. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus parvus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
