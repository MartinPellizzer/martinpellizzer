Clusia nutans

Clusia nutans, commonly known as the drooping glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Clusia nutans is well adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
