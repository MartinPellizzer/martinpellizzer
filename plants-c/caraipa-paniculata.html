Caraipa paniculata

Caraipa paniculata, commonly known as the yellow panic grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces panicles of small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in a branching pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open, disturbed areas.
