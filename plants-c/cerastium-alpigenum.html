Cerastium alpigenum

Cerastium alpigenum, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers with five petals and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Cerastium alpigenum plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
