Creochiton Furfuracea

Creochiton furfuracea is a unique and fascinating species of sea snail found in the rocky intertidal zones of the Pacific coast. This small, oval-shaped mollusk has a rough, leathery shell that helps it cling to surfaces in harsh marine environments. Its name comes from the Latin words for "skin" and "furry," reflecting its textured appearance and habitat. Despite its unassuming size, it plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling. Studying Creochiton furfuracea offers insights into the adaptations of marine invertebrates to extreme conditions.
