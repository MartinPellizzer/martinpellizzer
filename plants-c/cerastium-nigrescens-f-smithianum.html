Cerastium nigrescens f. smithianum

Cerastium nigrescens f. smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, appearing in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
