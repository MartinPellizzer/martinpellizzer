Calonema melanemum

Calonema melanemum, commonly known as the black-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent black veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Calonema melanemum requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
