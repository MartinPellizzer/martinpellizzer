Crepis foeniculacea

Crepis foeniculacea, commonly known as the celery thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of celery, hence its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
