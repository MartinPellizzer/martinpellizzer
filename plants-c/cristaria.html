Cristaria

Cristaria, commonly known as the starwort, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, star-shaped flowers and are often found in moist, temperate regions. Cristaria species typically have basal leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and their stems are often hairy. The flowers are small and clustered, producing a showy appearance that attracts pollinators.

They play an important role in their ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
