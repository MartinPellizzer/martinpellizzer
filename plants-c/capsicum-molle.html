Capsicum molle

Capsicum molle, commonly known as the wooly capsicum, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is classified within the Solanaceae family, sharing relatives with tomatoes and peppers.
