Cissus betiformis

Cissus betiformis, commonly known as the African devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cissus betiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb various structures.
