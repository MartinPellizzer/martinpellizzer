Circaea × decipiens

Circaea × decipiens, commonly known as the false white campion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Circaea × decipiens is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.
