Cajanus scarabaeoides

Cajanus scarabaeoides, commonly known as the scarab beetle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to dry environments and can thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Cajanus scarabaeoides plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural ecosystems.
