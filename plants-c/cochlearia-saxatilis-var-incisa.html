Cochlearia saxatilis var. incisa

Cochlearia saxatilis var. incisa, commonly known as the coastal scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to rocky coastal areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in exposed maritime environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins, which contribute to its specific epithet "incisa.".
