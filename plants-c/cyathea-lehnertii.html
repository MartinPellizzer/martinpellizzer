Cyathea lehnertii

Cyathea lehnertii, commonly known as the Lehnert's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, with sporangia developing on the undersides of its fronds. Cyathea lehnertii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable component of tropical ecosystems.
