Carum gairdneri var. latifolium

Carum gairdneri var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf caraway, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, narrow to broad leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, pungent scent. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, ribbed capsule containing tiny, aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
