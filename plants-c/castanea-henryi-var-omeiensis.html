Castanea henryi var. omeiensis

Castanea henryi var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are typically harvested in the fall. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
