Campanula cephalotes

Campanula cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula cephalotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
