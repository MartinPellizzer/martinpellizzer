Canavalia makahaensis

Canavalia makahaensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian burr bean, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a climbing legume that produces distinctive, spiny pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in disturbed areas and along the shoreline. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

Canavalia makahaensis is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and plays a role in local ecosystems.
