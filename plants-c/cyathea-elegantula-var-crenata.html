Cyathea elegantula var. crenata

Cyathea elegantula var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting pollinators.

This unique adaptation makes it a striking and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
