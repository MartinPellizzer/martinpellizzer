Crypsinopsis canaliculata

Crypsinopsis canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant possesses specialized leaves with internal bladders that function as digestive chambers. It thrives in nutrient-poor water, supplementing its diet with captured prey.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it a fascinating subject in the study of plant evolution and adaptation.
