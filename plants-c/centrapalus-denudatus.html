Centrapalus denudatus

Centrapalus denudatus, commonly known as the naked centrapalus, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and its fruits are capsule-like structures containing tiny seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Centrapalus denudatus is considered a botanically significant species for ecological studies.
