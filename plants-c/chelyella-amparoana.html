Chelyella amparoana

Chelyella amparoana, commonly known as the Amparoan shield fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped fronds that are tightly clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are well-developed, with sporangia arranged in clusters on the undersides of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Chelyella amparoana is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
