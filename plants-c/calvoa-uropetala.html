Calvoa uropetala

Calvoa uropetala, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's arid climate.

Calvoa uropetala is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
