Callista lilacina

Callista lilacina, commonly known as the lilac bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and distinctive appearance.
