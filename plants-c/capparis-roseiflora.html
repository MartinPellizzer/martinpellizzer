Capparis roseiflora

Capparis roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features woody stems and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, edible seeds.

Capparis roseiflora is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
