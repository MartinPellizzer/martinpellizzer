Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp. armata

Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp.

armata, commonly known as the Armenian centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and bright yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant produces small, bract-like leaves and has a distinctive, spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
