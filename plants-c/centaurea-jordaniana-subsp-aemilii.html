Centaurea jordaniana subsp. aemilii

Centaurea jordaniana subsp.

aemilii, commonly known as Jordanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers.

The subspecies aemilii is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and shorter stature compared to other Centaurea species. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
