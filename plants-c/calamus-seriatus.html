Calamus seriatus

Calamus seriatus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
