Carex nenningiana

Carex nenningiana, commonly known as the Nenning sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
