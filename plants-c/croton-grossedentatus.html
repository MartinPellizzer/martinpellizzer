Croton grossedentatus

Croton grossedentatus, commonly known as the giant croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its striking foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
