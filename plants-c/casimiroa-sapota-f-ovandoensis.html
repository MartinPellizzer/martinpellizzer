Casimiroa sapota f. ovandoensis

Casimiroa sapota f. ovandoensis, commonly known as the Ovando's sapote, is a botanical variant of the sapote tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and produces a fruit with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. It is valued for its edible fruit, which is often used in culinary applications, and for its ornamental value in landscaping.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and potential for hybridization with other Casimiroa species.
