Calamagrostis mcvaughii

Calamagrostis mcvaughii, commonly known as McVaugh's reedgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, upright flower stalks that produce fluffy, silver-gray plumes. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for wetland restoration and ecological gardening.
