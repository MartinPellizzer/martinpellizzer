Coleus kasomenensis

Coleus kasomenensis, commonly known as the Kasomenensis coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, often displaying striking shades of red, purple, and green. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
