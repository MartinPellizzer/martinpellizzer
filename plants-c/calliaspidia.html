Calliaspidia

Calliaspidia, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small cups. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Calliaspidia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
