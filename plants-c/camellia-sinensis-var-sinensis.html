Camellia sinensis var. sinensis

Camellia sinensis var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese green tea, is a variant of the tea plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, dark red flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant thrives in subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are harvested and processed to produce various green tea varieties.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for cultivation in regions with mild winters and warm summers.
