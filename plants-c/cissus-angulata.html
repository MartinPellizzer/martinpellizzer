Cissus angulata

Cissus angulata, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, diamond-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries that are consumed by both humans and animals. Cissus angulata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Its ability to climb and its unique leaf morphology make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor plant displays.
