Cirsium wheeleri

Cirsium wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with stiff, bristly hairs.

Cirsium wheeleri plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, disturbed areas.
