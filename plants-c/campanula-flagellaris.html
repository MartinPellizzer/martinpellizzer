Campanula flagellaris

Campanula flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Campanula flagellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
