Campsiandra macrocarpa var. alveolata

Campsiandra macrocarpa var. alveolata, commonly known as the large-fruited campsiandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by large, orange-red berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
