Cassia granitica

Cassia granitica, commonly known as the granite cassia, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Cassia granitica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
