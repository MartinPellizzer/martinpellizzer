Crocus athous

Crocus athous, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers that are often adorned with a distinctive yellow or orange center. Crocus athous thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable in horticultural cultivation.
