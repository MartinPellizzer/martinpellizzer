Convolvulus tricolor subsp. meonanthus

Convolvulus tricolor subsp.

meonanthus, commonly known as the meadow bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically blue or purple, with three petals, and bloom in early spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and meadows.
