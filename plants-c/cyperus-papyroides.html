Cyperus papyroides

Cyperus papyroides, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both an invasive species in some regions and a useful plant for erosion control.

Cyperus papyroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
