Callicarpa pilosissima

Callicarpa pilosissima, commonly known as the fuzzy beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red berries that appear in the fall, which are highly attractive to birds. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn color.
