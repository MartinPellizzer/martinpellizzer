Croton penaeaceus

Croton penaeaceus, commonly known as the Hawaiian croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant typically has smooth, waxy leaves that can range in color from green to red, orange, and yellow. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant and varied leaf colors.

Croton penaeaceus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
