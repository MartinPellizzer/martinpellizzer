Chamaecrista itambana

Chamaecrista itambana, commonly known as the Itambana shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are pinnately compound with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in nitrogen cycling.
