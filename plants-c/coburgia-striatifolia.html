Coburgia striatifolia

Coburgia striatifolia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that are light green with a subtle, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. Coburgia striatifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
