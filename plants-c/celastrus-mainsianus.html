Celastrus mainsianus

Celastrus mainsianus, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a woody climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shady areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
