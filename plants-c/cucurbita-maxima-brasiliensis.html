Cucurbita maxima brasiliensis

Cucurbita maxima brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, round, and often ribbed fruit. The plant produces sprawling vines with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. Its fruit can weigh several kilograms and is used for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
