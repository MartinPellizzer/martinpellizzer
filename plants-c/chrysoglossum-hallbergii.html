Chrysoglossum hallbergii

Chrysoglossum hallbergii, commonly known as the Hallberg's chrysoglossum, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial plant native to South Africa, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
