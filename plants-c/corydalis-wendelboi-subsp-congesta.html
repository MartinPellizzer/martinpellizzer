Corydalis wendelboi subsp. congesta

Corydalis wendelboi subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the Alpine Corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and violet. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Corydalis wendelboi subsp.

congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
