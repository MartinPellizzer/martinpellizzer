Campanopsis montana

Campanopsis montana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanopsis montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
