Campomanesia guaviroba var. insulae

Campomanesia guaviroba var. insulae, commonly known as the insular campomanesia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries. The berries are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
