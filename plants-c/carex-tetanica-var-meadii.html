Carex tetanica var. meadii

Carex tetanica var. meadii, commonly known as Mead's sedges, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters, typical of the Carex genus. This variant is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green, and it produces small, brownish flowers in early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
