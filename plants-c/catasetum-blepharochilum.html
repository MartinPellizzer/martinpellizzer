Catasetum blepharochilum

Catasetum blepharochilum, commonly known as the orchid with the lip-like structure, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a specialized lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with contrasting purple or red markings.

The orchid exhibits a unique pollination mechanism involving specific insect pollinators.
