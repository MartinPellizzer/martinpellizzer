Cyperus procerus

Cyperus procerus, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Cyperus procerus typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
