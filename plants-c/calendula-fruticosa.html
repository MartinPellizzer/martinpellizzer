Calendula fruticosa

Calendula fruticosa, commonly known as the perennial pot marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually woody at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also used in herbal remedies and natural dyes.
