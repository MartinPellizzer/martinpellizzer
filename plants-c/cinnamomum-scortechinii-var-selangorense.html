Cinnamomum scortechinii var. selangorense

Cinnamomum scortechinii var. selangorense, commonly known as the Selangor cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The varietal form is distinguished by its specific geographical distribution and unique aromatic properties.
