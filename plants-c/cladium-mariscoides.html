Cladium mariscoides

Cladium mariscoides, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soils, making it a key component of marsh and swamp ecosystems. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize the soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cladium mariscoides plays an important role in the ecological balance of wetlands by contributing to nutrient cycling and supporting biodiversity.
