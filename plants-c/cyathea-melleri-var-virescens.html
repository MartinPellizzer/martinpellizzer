Cyathea melleri var. virescens

Cyathea melleri var. virescens, commonly known as the Emerald Tree Fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that give it a lush, green appearance. The plant is typically evergreen and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens or tropical landscapes. Its fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique feature that aids in nighttime photosynthesis.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
