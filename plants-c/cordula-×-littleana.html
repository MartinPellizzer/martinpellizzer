Cordula × littleana

Cordula × littleana, commonly known as the hybrid sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a cross between *Cordula dichotoma* and *Cordula lanceolata*, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and ornamental value. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy green surface that turns red or orange in the fall. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spiky, spherical fruit clusters that persist through winter.

This tree is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
