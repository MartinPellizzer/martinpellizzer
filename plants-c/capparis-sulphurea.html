Capparis sulphurea

Capparis sulphurea, commonly known as the golden caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its yellow, fragrant flowers and thorny stems, which are typical of the Capparaceae family. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the plant thrives in coastal and rocky habitats.

Capparis sulphurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
