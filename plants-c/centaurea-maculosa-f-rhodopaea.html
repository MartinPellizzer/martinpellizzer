Centaurea maculosa f. rhodopaea

Centaurea maculosa f. rhodopaea, commonly known as the Rhodope centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking red-purple flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of clustered flowers with ray florets that are usually red or purple in color.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in the Rhodope Mountains, hence its name.
