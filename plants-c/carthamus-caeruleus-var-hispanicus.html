Carthamus caeruleus var. hispanicus

Carthamus caeruleus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish flossflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features showy, saucer-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white seed heads that are both ornamental and attractive to pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
