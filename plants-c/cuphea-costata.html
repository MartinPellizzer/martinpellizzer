Cuphea costata

Cuphea costata, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Cuphea costata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
