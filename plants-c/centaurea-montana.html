Centaurea montana

Centaurea montana, commonly known as the mountain bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by a ring of white to pink bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often branched.

Centaurea montana is known for its hardy nature and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value.
