Clematis canaliculata

Clematis canaliculata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features climbing stems with distinctive, furrowed bark and heart-shaped, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have five petals with a prominent central stamen.

Clematis canaliculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
