Cosmos caudatus

Cosmos caudatus, commonly known as the "Mexican coffee plant" or "plume poppy," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, feathery flower heads that range in color from white to deep red. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, bi- or tri-lobed flowers that bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cosmos caudatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
