Coelogyne simulacra

Coelogyne simulacra, commonly known as the false orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple with yellow markings. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Coelogyne simulacra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
