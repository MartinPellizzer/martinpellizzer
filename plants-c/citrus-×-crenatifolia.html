Citrus × crenatifolia

Citrus × crenatifolia, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctively wrinkled, greenish-yellow leaves and fragrant, citrusy aroma. The plant produces small, round, green fruits with a thick, bumpy rind. Its leaves and fruit are widely used in cooking for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
