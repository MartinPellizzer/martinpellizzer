Cornus bretschneideri var. crispa

Cornus bretschneideri var. crispa, commonly known as the crested dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems that often form a bushy shape. Its leaves are oval to elliptic, with a smooth texture and dark green coloration during the growing season.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
