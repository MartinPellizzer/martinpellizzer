Cyperus globosus f. fuscoater

Cyperus globosus f. fuscoater, commonly known as the dark-leaved umbrella sedge, is a botanical variant of the Cyperus globosus species.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, typically borne on tall, slender stems. The leaves of this plant are dark green to almost black, giving it a striking appearance. It is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
