Crepis angustata

Crepis angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, basal leaves that are narrow and hairy. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Crepis angustata is an important plant for pollinators and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
