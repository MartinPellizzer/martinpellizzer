Ctenosperma

Ctenosperma, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ctenospermataceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. They are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that often feature bright colors and unique structures. The leaves are typically alternate and can be either simple or lobed, depending on the species.

Ctenosperma species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
