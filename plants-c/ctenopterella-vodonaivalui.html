Ctenopterella vodonaivalui

Ctenopterella vodonaivalui, commonly known as the Vodonaivalui's feather fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
