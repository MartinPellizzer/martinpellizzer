Canarium stachyanthum

Canarium stachyanthum, commonly known as the yellow sterculia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
