Cabralea canjerana

Cabralea canjerana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or the cabralea, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

It is known for its aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in the production of musical instruments and furniture.
