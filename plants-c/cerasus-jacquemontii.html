Cerasus jacquemontii

Cerasus jacquemontii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its ornamental pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.
