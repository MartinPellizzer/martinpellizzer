Cyperus kerstenii var. irregularis

Cyperus kerstenii var. irregularis, commonly known as the irregular spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often irregularly arranged, giving it its scientific name.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
