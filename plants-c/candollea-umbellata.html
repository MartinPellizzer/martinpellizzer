Candollea umbellata

Candollea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of leaves. The plant features a rhizomatous stem that produces aerial roots and supports the lush foliage. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically emerging from a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Candollea umbellata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, feathery appearance.
