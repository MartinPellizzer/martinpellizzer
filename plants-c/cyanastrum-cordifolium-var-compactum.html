Cyanastrum cordifolium var. compactum

Cyanastrum cordifolium var. compactum, commonly known as the compact bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
