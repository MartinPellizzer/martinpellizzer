Cordia cumingiana

Cordia cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, often forming dense thickets along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
