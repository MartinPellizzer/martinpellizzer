Carex leptocladus

Carex leptocladus, commonly known as slender club sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its reproductive cycle.

Carex leptocladus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
