Coprosma montana var. crassa

Coprosma montana var. crassa, commonly known as the rough coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
