Calligonum orthotrichum

Calligonum orthotrichum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Calligonum orthotrichum is often found in sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
