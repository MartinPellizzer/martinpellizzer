Caryophyllus xanthostemifolius

Caryophyllus xanthostemifolius, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with some varieties exhibiting yellow or white hues. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments.

Native to parts of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
