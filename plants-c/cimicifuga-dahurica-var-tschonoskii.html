Cimicifuga dahurica var. tschonoskii

Cimicifuga dahurica var. tschonoskii, commonly known as the Siberian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It features tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late summer, which are arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
