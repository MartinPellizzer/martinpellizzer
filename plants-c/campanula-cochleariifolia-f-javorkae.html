Campanula cochleariifolia f. javorkae

Campanula cochleariifolia f. javorkae, commonly known as the Javorka bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features clustered, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
