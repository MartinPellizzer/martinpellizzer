Croton antisyphiliticus var. nitidulus

Croton antisyphiliticus var. nitidulus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
