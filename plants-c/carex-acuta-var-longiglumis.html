Carex acuta var. longiglumis

Carex acuta var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
