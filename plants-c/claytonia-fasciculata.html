Claytonia fasciculata

Claytonia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered springbeauty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by prominent stamens.

Claytonia fasciculata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
