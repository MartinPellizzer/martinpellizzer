Carex × clausa

Carex × clausa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers in the summer.

Carex × clausa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
