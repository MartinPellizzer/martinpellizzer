Caladenia citrina

Caladenia citrina, commonly known as the Yellow Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy yellow flower that has intricate patterns resembling spider webs. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It blooms during the late spring to early summer season, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Caladenia citrina is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
