Colchicum bertolonii var. hirtum

Colchicum bertolonii var. hirtum, commonly known as the Hirt's autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking purple to violet flowers with yellow stamens, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that emerge from a corm, and it is adapted to grow in rocky or dry soils. It is part of the Colchicaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which sets it apart from other members of the species.
