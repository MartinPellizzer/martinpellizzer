Campanula collina var. dasycarpa

Campanula collina var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive floral display.
