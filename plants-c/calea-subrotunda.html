Calea subrotunda

Calea subrotunda, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Calea subrotunda is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in treating ailments such as inflammation and respiratory issues.
