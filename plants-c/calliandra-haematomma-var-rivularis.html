Calliandra haematomma var. rivularis

Calliandra haematomma var. rivularis, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red and yellow flower clusters, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branching. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and erosion control.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
