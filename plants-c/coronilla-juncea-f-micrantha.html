Coronilla juncea f. micrantha

Coronilla juncea f. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coronilla, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, which bloom during the summer months. The leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, serrated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
