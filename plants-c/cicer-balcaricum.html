Cicer balcaricum

Cicer balcaricum, commonly known as the Balkan vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are typically small and oblong. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Cicer balcaricum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
