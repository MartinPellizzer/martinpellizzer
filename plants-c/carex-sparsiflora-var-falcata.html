Carex sparsiflora var. falcata

Carex sparsiflora var. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically borne on terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its narrowly elongated leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
