Crocus tauri

Crocus tauri, commonly known as the Taurian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The Taurian crocus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
