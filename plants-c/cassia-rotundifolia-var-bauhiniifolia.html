Cassia rotundifolia var. bauhiniifolia

Cassia rotundifolia var. bauhiniifolia, commonly known as the Bauhiniifolia Cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in long, drooping clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
