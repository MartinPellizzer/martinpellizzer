Carex speciosa subsp. pinetorum

Carex speciosa subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and bright yellow to orange flower heads that appear in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wet to mesic habitats such as swamps, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
