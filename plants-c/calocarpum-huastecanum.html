Calocarpum huastecanum

Calocarpum huastecanum, commonly known as the Huastec rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and valuable wood, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
