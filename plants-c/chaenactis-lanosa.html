Chaenactis lanosa

Chaenactis lanosa, commonly known as the woolly paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its structure consists of a short stem with numerous branches, and its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
