Cheilanthes hirta var. hyaloglandulosa

Cheilanthes hirta var. hyaloglandulosa, commonly known as the silver fringed rock fern, is a perennial fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The fronds are typically gray-green with a dense covering of fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found growing on limestone or sandstone outcrops.
