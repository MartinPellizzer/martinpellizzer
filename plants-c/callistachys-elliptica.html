Callistachys elliptica

Callistachys elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are five-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
