Combretum dilembense

Combretum dilembense, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides.
