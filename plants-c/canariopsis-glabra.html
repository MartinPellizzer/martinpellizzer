Canariopsis glabra

Canariopsis glabra, commonly known as the smooth canarygrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Canariopsis glabra is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
