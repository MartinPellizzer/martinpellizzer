Crataegus flavescens

Crataegus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-red berries that are edible but often sour in taste. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Crataegus flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for birds and wildlife.
