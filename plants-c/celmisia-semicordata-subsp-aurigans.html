Celmisia semicordata subsp. aurigans

Celmisia semicordata subsp.

aurigans, commonly known as the golden celmisia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock faces. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.
