Cinnamomum neesii var. khasianum

Cinnamomum neesii var. khasianum, commonly known as Khasi cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,000 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a distinct cinnamon-like fragrance when crushed.

The bark, which is the primary source of its aromatic properties, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
