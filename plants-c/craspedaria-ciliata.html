Craspedaria ciliata

Craspedaria ciliata, commonly known as the hairy craspeda, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
