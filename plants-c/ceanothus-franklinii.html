Ceanothus franklinii

Ceanothus franklinii, commonly known as Franklin's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Ceanothus franklinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
