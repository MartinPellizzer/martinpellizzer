Clusia microphylla

Clusia microphylla, commonly known as the little-leaf glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance make it a popular ornamental plant.

Clusia microphylla is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal and urban environments.
