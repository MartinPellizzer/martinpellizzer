Ceratochloa willdenowii

Ceratochloa willdenowii, commonly known as the African grass or willdenow's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
