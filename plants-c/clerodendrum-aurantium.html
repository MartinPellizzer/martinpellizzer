Clerodendrum aurantium

Clerodendrum aurantium, commonly known as the golden flame or Chinese flame bush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and landscapes.
