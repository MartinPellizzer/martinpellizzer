Chimonobambusa unifolia

Chimonobambusa unifolia, commonly known as the single-leaf bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its rapid growth and resilience in various environmental conditions.

Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
