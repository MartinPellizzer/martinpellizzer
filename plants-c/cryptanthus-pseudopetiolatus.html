Cryptanthus pseudopetiolatus

Cryptanthus pseudopetiolatus, commonly known as the red-tipped cryptanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with bright red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cryptanthus pseudopetiolatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
