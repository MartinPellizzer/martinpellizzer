Cytisus caucasicus

Cytisus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, wiry hairs.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
