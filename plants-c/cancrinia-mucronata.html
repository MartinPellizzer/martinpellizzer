Cancrinia mucronata

Cancrinia mucronata, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Cancrinia mucronata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
