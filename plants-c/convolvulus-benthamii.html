Convolvulus benthamii

Convolvulus benthamii, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Convolvulus benthamii is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of morning glories.
