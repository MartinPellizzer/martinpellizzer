Carex filicina

Carex filicina, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex filicina plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
