Chionolaena wittigiana

Chionolaena wittigiana, commonly known as the silverleaf chionolaena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
