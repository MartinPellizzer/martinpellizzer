Cornulaca korshinskyi

Cornulaca korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cornulaca korshinskyi is often found in rocky soils and is a common species in steppe and desert ecosystems.
