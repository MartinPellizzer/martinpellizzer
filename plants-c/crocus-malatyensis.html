Crocus malatyensis

Crocus malatyensis, commonly known as the Malatya crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming period. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which are often accompanied by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
