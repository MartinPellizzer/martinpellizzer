Carex medwedewii

Carex medwedewii, commonly known as the Medwedew's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tundra and alpine habitats. Carex medwedewii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small arctic fauna.
