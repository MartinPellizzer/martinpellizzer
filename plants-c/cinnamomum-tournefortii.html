Cinnamomum tournefortii

Cinnamomum tournefortii, commonly known as the Cyprus cedar or sweet cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It has aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant, spicy scent, and its bark is used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique fragrance and ornamental value contribute to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
