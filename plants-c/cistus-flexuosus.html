Cistus flexuosus

Cistus flexuosus, commonly known as the creeping rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems that often grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs.

Cistus flexuosus is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, well-drained soils.
