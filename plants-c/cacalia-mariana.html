Cacalia mariana

Cacalia mariana, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
