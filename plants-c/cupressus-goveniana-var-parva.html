Cupressus goveniana var. parva

Cupressus goveniana var. parva, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a small evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It has a compact, pyramidal shape with scale-like leaves that are bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in rocky soils.

Its slow growth rate and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
