Campanula cephalantha

Campanula cephalantha, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Campanula cephalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
