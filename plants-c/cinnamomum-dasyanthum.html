Cinnamomum dasyanthum

Cinnamomum dasyanthum, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that contain a single seed. It is valued for its aromatic wood and leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
