Castanea vesca var. corticata

Castanea vesca var. corticata, commonly known as the European horse chestnut.

This variant is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white or cream-colored flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain one or two chestnuts.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is closely related to the sweet chestnut.
