Croton thouarsianus var. macrocalyx

Croton thouarsianus var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
