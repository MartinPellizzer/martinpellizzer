Cleome stocksiana

Cleome stocksiana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome stocksiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
