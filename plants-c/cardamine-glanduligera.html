Cardamine glanduligera

Cardamine glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters.

The species is notable for its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which secrete a sticky substance.
