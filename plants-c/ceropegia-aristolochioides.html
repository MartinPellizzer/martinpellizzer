Ceropegia aristolochioides

Ceropegia aristolochioides, commonly known as the snake plant or mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

The plant is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through its offsets or seeds.
