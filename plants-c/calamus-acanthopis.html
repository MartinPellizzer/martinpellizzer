Calamus acanthopis

Calamus acanthopis, commonly known as the spiny calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that are often covered in small spines. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems.

Calamus acanthopis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
