Carex verna

Carex verna, commonly known as early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, slender, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are arranged in spikelets.

Carex verna is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
