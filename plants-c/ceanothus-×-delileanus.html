Ceanothus × delileanus

Ceanothus × delileanus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ceanothus × delileanus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in native plant gardens.
