Clerodendrum emarginatum

Clerodendrum emarginatum, commonly known as the African balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in terminal spikes. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it often has a mild aromatic scent.

Clerodendrum emarginatum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
