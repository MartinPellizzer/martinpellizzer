Camellia crassisepala

Camellia crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features thick, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Camellia crassisepala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
