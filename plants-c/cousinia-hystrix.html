Cousinia hystrix

Cousinia hystrix, commonly known as the spiked tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Cousinia hystrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging climates.
