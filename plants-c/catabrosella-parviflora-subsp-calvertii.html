Catabrosella parviflora subsp. calvertii

Catabrosella parviflora subsp.

calvertii, commonly known as the Calvert's sand verbena, is a small, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It has narrow, linear leaves and a low-growing habit, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
