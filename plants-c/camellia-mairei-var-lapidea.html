Camellia mairei var. lapidea

Camellia mairei var. lapidea, commonly known as the Maire's camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in temperate climates.
