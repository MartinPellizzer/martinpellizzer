Coilonox stuetzelianum

Coilonox stuetzelianum, commonly known as the silver-leafed bellflower, is a unique plant species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Coilonox stuetzelianum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
