Carex michauxiana var. asiatica

Carex michauxiana var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
