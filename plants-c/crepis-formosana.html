Crepis formosana

Crepis formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of yellow ray florets surrounding disc florets, typical of the genus Crepis.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
