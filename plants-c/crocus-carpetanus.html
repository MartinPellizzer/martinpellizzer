Crocus carpetanus

Crocus carpetanus, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces delicate, early spring blooms with violet-blue petals and a central stigma that resembles saffron threads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.

The flowers typically appear before the leaves, making it a notable early bloom in the spring flora.
