Convolvulus mascatensis

Convolvulus mascatensis, commonly known as the Mascate bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Convolvulus mascatensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological features.
