Carex chapmanii

Carex chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers in the summer.

Carex chapmanii is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
