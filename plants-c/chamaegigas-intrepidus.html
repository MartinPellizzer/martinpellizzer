Chamaegigas intrepidus

Chamaegigas intrepidus, commonly known as the bold mountain cactus, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, spiny stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are pollinated by insects. Its thick, waxy cuticle helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is well-suited for rocky and well-drained soils.
