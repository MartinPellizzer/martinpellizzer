Clematis rosea

Clematis rosea, commonly known as the rose clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Clematis rosea thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
