Cynanchum ipecacuanha

Cynanchum ipecacuanha, commonly known as the American ipecac, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have been historically used in medicine. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been the subject of both traditional and modern botanical study.
