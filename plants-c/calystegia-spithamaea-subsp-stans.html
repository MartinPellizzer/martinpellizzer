Calystegia spithamaea subsp. stans

Calystegia spithamaea subsp.

stans, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence the common name.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
