Carminatia papagayana

Carminatia papagayana, commonly known as the Papagayo orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns, which are often adorned with a red or orange lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
