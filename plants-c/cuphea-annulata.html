Cuphea annulata

Cuphea annulata, commonly known as the wheelplant or coral cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its showy flowers and compact growth habit.

Cuphea annulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
