Carex crinita var. simulans

Carex crinita var. simulans, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that often has a silvery sheen due to fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in the summer, which are followed by soft, mat-forming seeds. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This versatile plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
