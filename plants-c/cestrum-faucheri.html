Cestrum faucheri

Cestrum faucheri, commonly known as the golden cestrum, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Cestrum faucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
