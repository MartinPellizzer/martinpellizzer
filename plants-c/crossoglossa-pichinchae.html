Crossoglossa pichinchae

Crossoglossa pichinchae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in the understory of humid, tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a blend of white and violet hues. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
