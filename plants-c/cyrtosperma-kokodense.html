Cyrtosperma kokodense

Cyrtosperma kokodense, commonly known as the Bird's Nest Palm, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling bird's nests, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
