Cassia ramiflora var. trijuga

Cassia ramiflora var. trijuga, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Cassia ramiflora var. trijuga is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
