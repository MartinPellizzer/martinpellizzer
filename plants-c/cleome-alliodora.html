Cleome alliodora

Cleome alliodora, commonly known as the sweet cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome alliodora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
