Crataegus glariosa

Crataegus glariosa, commonly known as the red chokecherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and mammals but contain toxins for humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
