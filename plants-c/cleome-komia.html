Cleome komia

Cleome komia, commonly known as Mexican spider flower, is an annual flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.

Cleome komia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
