Cola acuminata var. grandiflora

Cola acuminata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, capsule-like fruits that contain stimulant-rich seeds used in traditional medicine and beverages. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in its native habitats.
