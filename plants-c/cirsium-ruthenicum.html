Cirsium ruthenicum

Cirsium ruthenicum, commonly known as the Russian thistle or tarragon weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its purple to pink flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in some regions.

Cirsium ruthenicum has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.
