Cissus anisophylla

Cissus anisophylla, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a hardy and resilient species.
