Calamintha officinalis var. villosissima

Calamintha officinalis var. villosissima, commonly known as hairy caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds, similar in appearance to those of caraway, are often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, including antispasmodic and carminative effects.
