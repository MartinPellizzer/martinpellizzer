Clidemia sparsiflora

Clidemia sparsiflora, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant relies on host trees for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis. Due to its parasitic nature, it can sometimes cause stress to its host plants if left unchecked.
