Cayaponia longifolia

Cayaponia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved cayaponia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Cayaponia longifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
