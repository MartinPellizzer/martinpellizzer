Chrysopsis camphorata

Chrysopsis camphorata, commonly known as the camphor aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chrysopsis camphorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
