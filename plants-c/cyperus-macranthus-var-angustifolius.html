Cyperus macranthus var. angustifolius

Cyperus macranthus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or pale in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
