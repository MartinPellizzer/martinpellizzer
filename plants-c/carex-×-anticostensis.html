Carex × anticostensis

Carex × anticostensis, commonly known as the Anticostis sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern North America, particularly along the Atlantic coast. This plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Carex × anticostensis plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
