Cereus saxicola var. anguiniformis

Cereus saxicola var. anguiniformis, commonly known as the red-bellied cereus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically pale pink to red, with a strong, sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and flowering habits, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
