Cissus degraeri

Cissus degraeri, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on its leaves, which give it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus degraeri is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
