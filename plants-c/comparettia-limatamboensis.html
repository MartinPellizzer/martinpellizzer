Comparettia limatamboensis

Comparettia limatamboensis, commonly known as the Peruvian buttercup, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high Andes of Peru.

It features showy, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip that resembles a buttercup. The plant has thick, fleshy roots adapted to its cold, high-altitude environment. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
