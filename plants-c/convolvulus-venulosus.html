Convolvulus venulosus

Convolvulus venulosus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.
