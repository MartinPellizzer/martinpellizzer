Cousinia parjumanensis

Cousinia parjumanensis, commonly known as the Persian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia parjumanensis is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
