Calophyllum tomentosum

Calophyllum tomentosum, commonly known as the silky maple or white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
