Carlina × acauloides

Carlina × acauloides, commonly known as the garden carline thistle, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that form a dense, compact base. The plant produces showy, purple-blue composite flowers in late summer, resembling daisies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
