Cereus speciosissimus var. colmariensis

Cereus speciosissimus var. colmariensis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This popular houseplant is well-suited for indoor cultivation and is prized for its ornamental value.
