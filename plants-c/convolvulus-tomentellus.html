Convolvulus tomentellus

Convolvulus tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Convolvulaceae family.
