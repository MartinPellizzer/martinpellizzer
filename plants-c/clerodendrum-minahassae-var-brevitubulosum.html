Clerodendrum minahassae var. brevitubulosum

Clerodendrum minahassae var. brevitubulosum, commonly known as the Brevitubular Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
