Chirita polycephala

Chirita polycephala, commonly known as the clustered leaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and greenhouse cultivation.
