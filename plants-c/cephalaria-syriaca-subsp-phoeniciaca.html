Cephalaria syriaca subsp. phoeniciaca

Cephalaria syriaca subsp.

phoeniciaca, commonly known as the Syrian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
