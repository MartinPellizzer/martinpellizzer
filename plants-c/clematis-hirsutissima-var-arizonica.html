Clematis hirsutissima var. arizonica

Clematis hirsutissima var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often with a subtle greenish tinge. The plant has sturdy, twining stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid climates.
