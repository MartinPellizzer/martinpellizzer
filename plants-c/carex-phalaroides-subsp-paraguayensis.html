Carex phalaroides subsp. paraguayensis

Carex phalaroides subsp.

paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
