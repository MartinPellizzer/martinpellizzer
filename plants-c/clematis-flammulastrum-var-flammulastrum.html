Clematis flammulastrum var. flammulastrum

Clematis flammulastrum var. flammulastrum, commonly known as the flameleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle red or purple tinge. The plant has compound, palmately lobed leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial to local wildlife.
