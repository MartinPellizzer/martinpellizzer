Carex tenella var. misera

Carex tenella var. misera, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green color and a glossy surface.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer, followed by three-sided, spike-like seed heads.
