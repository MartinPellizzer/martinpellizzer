Capsella bursa-pastoris biformis

Capsella bursa-pastoris biformis, commonly known as the common groundsel, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
