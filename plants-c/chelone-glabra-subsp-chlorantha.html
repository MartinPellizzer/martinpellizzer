Chelone glabra subsp. chlorantha

Chelone glabra subsp.

chlorantha, commonly known as bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its striking clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Chelone glabra subsp.

chlorantha is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
