Campanula balcanica var. velebetica

Campanula balcanica var. velebetica, commonly known as the Velebetica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
