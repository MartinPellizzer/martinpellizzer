Cerastium fischerianum f. schmidtianum

Cerastium fischerianum f. schmidtianum, commonly known as the Schmidt's mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. The subspecies f. schmidtianum is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to the typical form.

It is a hardy plant that is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry, well-drained areas.
