Calamus oxycoccus

Calamus oxycoccus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, tufted manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, a common feature among grasses. It is native to various regions in Asia and is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests or wetlands.

Calamus oxycoccus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
