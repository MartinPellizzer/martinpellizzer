Coryanthes maculata

Coryanthes maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and contrasting colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its flowers are highly specialized, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Coryanthes maculata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinator diversity.
