Crataegus submollis

Crataegus submollis, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and mammals but generally considered unpalatable to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Crataegus submollis plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
