Corydalis liana

Corydalis liana, commonly known as the climbing corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant has a climbing habit, supported by its wiry stems that twine around other vegetation. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.
