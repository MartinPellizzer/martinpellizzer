Celtis harperi

Celtis harperi, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Celtis harperi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments.
