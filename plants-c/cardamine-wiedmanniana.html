Cardamine wiedmanniana

Cardamine wiedmanniana, commonly known as the Wiedmann's watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in a cluster and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
