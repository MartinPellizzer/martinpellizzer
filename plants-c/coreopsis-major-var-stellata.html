Coreopsis major var. stellata

Coreopsis major var. stellata, commonly known as the starry coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
