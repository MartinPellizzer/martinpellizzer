Carex oligocarpa var. minor

Carex oligocarpa var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
