Caladenia montana

Caladenia montana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination.

Caladenia montana is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, making it a notable species in native flora gardens.
