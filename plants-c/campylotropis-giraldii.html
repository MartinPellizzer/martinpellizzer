Campylotropis giraldii

Campylotropis giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii climbing bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique growth habit includes a climbing or spreading form, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
