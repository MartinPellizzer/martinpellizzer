Cajanus obcordifolia

Cajanus obcordifolia, commonly known as the obcordate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
