Cereus hoppenstedtii

Cereus hoppenstedtii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented structure. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Cereus hoppenstedtii is often cultivated for its striking floral display and unique blooming habit.
