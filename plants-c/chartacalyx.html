Chartacalyx

Chartacalyx, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that provides a textured appearance. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

The Chartacalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its vibrant floral display.
