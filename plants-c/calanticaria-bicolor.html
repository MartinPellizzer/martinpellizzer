Calanticaria bicolor

Calanticaria bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow-orange central disk. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Calanticaria bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive, delicate appearance.
