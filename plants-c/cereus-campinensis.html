Cereus campinensis

Cereus campinensis, commonly known as the Campinas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spines that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Cereus campinensis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
