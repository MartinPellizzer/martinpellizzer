Cardamine ovata proles thamnophila

Cardamine ovata proles thamnophila, commonly known as the thamnophila water shield, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, shield-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating on water surfaces. The plant produces delicate, yellowish flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
