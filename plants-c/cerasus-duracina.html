Cerasus duracina

Cerasus duracina, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamazakura, is a species of flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its ornamental value and seasonal blooms. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Cerasus duracina is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese gardens.
