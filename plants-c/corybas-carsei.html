Corybas carsei

Corybas carsei, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flower that resembles a Christmas bell, hence its common name. The plant produces a single, showy bloom that is typically greenish-yellow with purple or red markings. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Corybas carsei is noted for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
