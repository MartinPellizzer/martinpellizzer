Carex ornithopoda subsp. bulgarica

Carex ornithopoda subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
