Capparis brevis

Capparis brevis, commonly known as the short caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Capparis brevis is typically found in rocky or coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
