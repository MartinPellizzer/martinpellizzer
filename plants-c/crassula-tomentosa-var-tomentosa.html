Crassula tomentosa var. tomentosa

Crassula tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring and summer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor succulent collections.
