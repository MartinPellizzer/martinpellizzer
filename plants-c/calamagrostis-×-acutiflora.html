Calamagrostis × acutiflora

Calamagrostis × acutiflora, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, arching stems and striking purple or pink flower plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a silver-green hue that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.

Calamagrostis × acutiflora is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
