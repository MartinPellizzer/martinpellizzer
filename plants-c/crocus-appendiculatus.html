Crocus appendiculatus

Crocus appendiculatus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive purple or violet tinge at the base. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are relatively easy to cultivate and can be divided to propagate the plant.
