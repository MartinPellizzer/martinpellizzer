Cynometra marginata var. laevis

Cynometra marginata var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its timber, which is valued for its durability.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
