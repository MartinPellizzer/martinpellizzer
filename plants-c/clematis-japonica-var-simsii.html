Clematis japonica var. simsii

Clematis japonica var. simsii, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis or the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis japonica var. simsii is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
