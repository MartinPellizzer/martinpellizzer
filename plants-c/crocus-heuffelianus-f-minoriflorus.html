Crocus heuffelianus f. minoriflorus

Crocus heuffelianus f. minoriflorus, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to white petals. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in gardens.
