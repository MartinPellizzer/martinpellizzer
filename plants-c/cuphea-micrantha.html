Cuphea micrantha

Cuphea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cuphea micrantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
