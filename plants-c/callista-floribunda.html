Callista floribunda

Callista floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Callista floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
