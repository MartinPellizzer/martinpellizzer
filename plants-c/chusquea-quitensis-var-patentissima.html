Chusquea quitensis var. patentissima

Chusquea quitensis var. patentissima, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 5 meters in height. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
