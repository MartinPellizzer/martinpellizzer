Campanula macrantha var. polyantha

Campanula macrantha var. polyantha, commonly known as the polyantha bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clusters of large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, moist soil in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves, and it is known for its long blooming period, often from late spring through early autumn.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
