Crocus flavus subsp. sarichinarensis

Crocus flavus subsp.

sarichinarensis, commonly known as the Sarichinarensis Crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It produces showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
