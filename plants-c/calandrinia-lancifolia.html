Calandrinia lancifolia

Calandrinia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved purslane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Calandrinia lancifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
