Carex pairae subsp. leersii

Carex pairae subsp.

leersii, commonly known as the Leers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce wind-pollinated flowers, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in diverse wetland conditions.
