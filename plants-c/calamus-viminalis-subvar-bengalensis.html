Calamus viminalis subvar. bengalensis

Calamus viminalis subvar. bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal reed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical rhizomes and long, flexible leaves that often grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as marshes and riverbanks. Its rhizomes are frequently harvested for use in basket weaving and as a source of aromatic oils.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
