Calamagrostis littorea var. elongata

Calamagrostis littorea var. elongata, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-blue in color. This variety thrives in moist, saline environments such as salt marshes and dunes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often adorned with silky, hairy bracts.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
