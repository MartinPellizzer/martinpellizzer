Cota talyschensis

Cota talyschensis, commonly known as the Talysch mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, yellowish-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Cota talyschensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
