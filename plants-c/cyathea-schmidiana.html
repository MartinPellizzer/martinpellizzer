Cyathea schmidiana

Cyathea schmidiana, commonly known as the Schmidian tree fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant features a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a layer of persistent, fibrous roots. Its fronds are large, pinnately compound, and typically arch outward from the trunk.

Cyathea schmidiana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
