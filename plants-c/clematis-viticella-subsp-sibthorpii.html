Clematis viticella subsp. sibthorpii

Clematis viticella subsp.

sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp Clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to southern Europe. It features compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in early summer.

The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
