Campanula peshmenii

Campanula peshmenii, commonly known as the Peshmenii bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Campanula peshmenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
