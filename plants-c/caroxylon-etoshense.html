Caroxylon etoshense

Caroxylon etoshense, commonly known as the Etoshe date palm, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Etoshe Pan region of Namibia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into date-like fruits, though it is not closely related to the common date palm.

Due to its restricted habitat and environmental threats, Caroxylon etoshense is considered critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
