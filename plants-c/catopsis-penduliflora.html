Catopsis penduliflora

Catopsis penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging catopsis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
