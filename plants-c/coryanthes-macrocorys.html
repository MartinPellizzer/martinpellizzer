Coryanthes macrocorys

Coryanthes macrocorys, commonly known as the giant coryanthe, is a striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, with intricate, often colorful patterns on the labellum. The plant produces a unique structure called a "corymbe," which is a specialized inflorescence that supports its numerous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of light for optimal growth.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.
