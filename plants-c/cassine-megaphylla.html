Cassine megaphylla

Cassine megaphylla, commonly known as the giant cassine, is a large, evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing a distinctive texture.

Cassine megaphylla is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
