Chamaecrista adenosperma

Chamaecrista adenosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
