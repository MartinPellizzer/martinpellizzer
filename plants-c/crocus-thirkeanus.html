Crocus thirkeanus

Crocus thirkeanus, commonly known as the Thirkean crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in countries like Italy and Spain. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms with petals that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
