Cleome ternata

Cleome ternata, commonly known as false mallow, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Cleome ternata is often found in gardens and along roadsides, where it can become invasive in some regions.
