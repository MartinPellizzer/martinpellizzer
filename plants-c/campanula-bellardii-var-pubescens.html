Campanula bellardii var. pubescens

Campanula bellardii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its varietal name "pubescens," which means "hairy" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
