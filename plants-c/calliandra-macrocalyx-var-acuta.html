Calliandra macrocalyx var. acuta

Calliandra macrocalyx var. acuta, commonly known as the acute calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is also valued in some regions for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
