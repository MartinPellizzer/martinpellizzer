Chenopodium berlandieri var. opulifoliforme

Chenopodium berlandieri var. opulifoliforme, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a perennial shrub native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, pastures, and in dry, open areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
