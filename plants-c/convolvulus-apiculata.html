Convolvulus apiculata

Convolvulus apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides, where it can spread rapidly.
