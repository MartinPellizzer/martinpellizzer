Citrus × aurantium var. dulcis

Citrus × aurantium var. dulcis, commonly known as sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, peeling rind and segmented, juicy white flesh that is rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, and it produces fruit that ripens from green to orange in color.

The tree is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, beverages, and essential oils.
