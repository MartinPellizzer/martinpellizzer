Crataegus balkwillii

Crataegus balkwillii, commonly known as Balkwill’s hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Balkwill’s hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
