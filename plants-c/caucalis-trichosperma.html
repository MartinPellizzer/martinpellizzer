Caucalis trichosperma

Caucalis trichosperma, commonly known as the hairy caucalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Caucalis trichosperma is a perennial herb that is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
