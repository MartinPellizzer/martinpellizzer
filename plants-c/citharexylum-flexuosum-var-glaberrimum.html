Citharexylum flexuosum var. glaberrimum

Citharexylum flexuosum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the silky cithara, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, aromatic leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
