Cirsium tuberosum subsp. bulbosum

Cirsium tuberosum subsp.

bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, spiny leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy purple to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. Its root system is tuberous, giving it the subspecies name *bulbosum*, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
