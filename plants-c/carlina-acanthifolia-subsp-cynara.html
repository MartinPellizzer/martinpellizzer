Carlina acanthifolia subsp. cynara

Carlina acanthifolia subsp.

cynara, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features a robust stem with spiny leaves that are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
