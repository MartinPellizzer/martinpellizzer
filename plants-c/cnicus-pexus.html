Cnicus pexus

Cnicus pexus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Cnicus pexus is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
