Cardamine virginiana

Cardamine virginiana, commonly known as Appalachian water shield, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as stream banks, marshes, and shaded woodlands. The plant features a rosette of kidney-shaped, hairy leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small, white or greenish flowers are borne on slender stems and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial insect populations.
