Cnidoscolus eglandulatus

Cnidoscolus eglandulatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

While it is cultivated for ornamental purposes, it is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
