Carex aphyllopus var. paludicola

Carex aphyllopus var. paludicola, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are often surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland habitats.

This species plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland ecosystems.
