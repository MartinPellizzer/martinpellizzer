Ceanothus rigidus var. fresnensis

Ceanothus rigidus var. fresnensis, commonly known as the San Francisco bluecurls, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine, curling hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
