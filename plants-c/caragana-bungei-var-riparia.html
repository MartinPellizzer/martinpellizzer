Caragana bungei var. riparia

Caragana bungei var. riparia, commonly known as the riparian caragana, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to small, needle-like structures, and its yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in harsh, sandy soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and preventing soil erosion.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for reclamation projects in degraded landscapes.
