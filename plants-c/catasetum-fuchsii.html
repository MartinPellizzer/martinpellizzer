Catasetum fuchsii

Catasetum fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers with a distinctive, often colorful lip that is specialized for pollination by specific insects. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, featuring a unique column that aids in the transfer of pollen.

Catasetum fuchsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
