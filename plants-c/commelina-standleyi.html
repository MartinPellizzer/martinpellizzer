Commelina standleyi

Commelina standleyi, commonly known as the red spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with three prominent petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are slender and branching.

Commelina standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
