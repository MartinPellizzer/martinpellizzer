Carex lyngbyei subsp. cryptocarpa

Carex lyngbyei subsp.

cryptocarpa, commonly known as the cryptic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
