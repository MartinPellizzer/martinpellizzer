Cotoneaster anatolii

Cotoneaster anatolii, commonly known as Anatolian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster anatolii is also valued for its role in ecological habitats and as a source of food for wildlife.
