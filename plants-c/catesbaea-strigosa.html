Catesbaea strigosa

Catesbaea strigosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its rough, gray bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Catesbaea strigosa is also known for its adaptability and can become invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
