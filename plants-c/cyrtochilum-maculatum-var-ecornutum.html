Cyrtochilum maculatum var. ecornutum

The plant grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rock faces or in forest understories, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.
