Coelogyne remota

Coelogyne remota, commonly known as the remote orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne remota is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
