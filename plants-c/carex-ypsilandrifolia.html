Carex ypsilandrifolia

Carex ypsilandrifolia, commonly known as the slender flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows.

Carex ypsilandrifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
