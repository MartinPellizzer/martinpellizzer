Chenopodium ambrosioides f. genuinum

Chenopodium ambrosioides f. genuinum, commonly known as wormseed, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
