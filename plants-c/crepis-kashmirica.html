Crepis kashmirica

Crepis kashmirica, commonly known as Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are often basal, with a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in shades of yellow and orange.

Crepis kashmirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
