Cyanotis cristata

Cyanotis cristata, commonly known as the crested bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Cyanotis cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
