Cordia caracasana

Cordia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas gloryflower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Cordia caracasana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
