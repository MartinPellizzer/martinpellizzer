Caesalpinia oblongifolia

Caesalpinia oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that resemble a peacock's tail, typically displaying vibrant red and yellow colors. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Caesalpinia oblongifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
