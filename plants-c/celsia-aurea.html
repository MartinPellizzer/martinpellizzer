Celsia aurea

Celsia aurea, commonly known as the golden celsia, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Celsia aurea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
