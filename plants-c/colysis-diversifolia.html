Colysis diversifolia

Colysis diversifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed colysis, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
