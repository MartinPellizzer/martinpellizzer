Centaurea transcaucasica subsp. georgica

Centaurea transcaucasica subsp.

georgica, commonly known as the Georgian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a single, showy flower per stem.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of alpine and steppe flora.
