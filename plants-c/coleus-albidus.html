Coleus albidus

Coleus albidus, commonly known as white coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Coleus albidus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
