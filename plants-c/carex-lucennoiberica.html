Carex lucennoiberica

Carex lucennoiberica, commonly known as the Iberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-parted flowers.

Carex lucennoiberica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
