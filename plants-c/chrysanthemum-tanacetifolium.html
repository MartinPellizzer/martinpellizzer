Chrysanthemum tanacetifolium

Chrysanthemum tanacetifolium, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that resemble those of dill, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal properties. Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal gardens.
