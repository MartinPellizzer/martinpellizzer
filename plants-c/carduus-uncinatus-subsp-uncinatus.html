Carduus uncinatus subsp. uncinatus

Carduus uncinatus subsp.

uncinatus, commonly known as the goat's horn thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive, spiky flower head that resembles a goat's horn.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing purple to pink flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
