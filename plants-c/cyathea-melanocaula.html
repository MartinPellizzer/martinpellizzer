Cyathea melanocaula

Cyathea melanocaula, commonly known as the black-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The trunk is covered in a dense, dark brown to blackish layer of old leaf bases, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or lowland forests.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
