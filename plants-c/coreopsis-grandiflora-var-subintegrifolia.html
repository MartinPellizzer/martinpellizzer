Coreopsis grandiflora var. subintegrifolia

Coreopsis grandiflora var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall, adorned with bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other coreopsis varieties. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
