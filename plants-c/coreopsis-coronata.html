Coreopsis coronata

Coreopsis coronata, commonly known as the crownbeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange ray flowers that surround a central disk, creating a crown-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to sandy soils.

Coreopsis coronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
