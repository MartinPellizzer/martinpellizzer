Cynanchum tamifolium var. major

Cynanchum tamifolium var. major, commonly known as the greater milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
