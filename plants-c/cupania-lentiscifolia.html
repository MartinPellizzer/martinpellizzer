Cupania lentiscifolia

Cupania lentiscifolia, commonly known as the creeping elder, is a woody shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, a common trait among members of its family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
