Chrysophyllum bancanum

Chrysophyllum bancanum, commonly known as the African cherry or black sapote, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, creamy pulp containing black seeds.

Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
