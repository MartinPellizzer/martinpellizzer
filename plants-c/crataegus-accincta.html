Crataegus accincta

Crataegus accincta, commonly known as the sandcherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with finely serrated margins.

Crataegus accincta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
