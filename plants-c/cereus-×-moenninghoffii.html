Cereus × moenninghoffii

Cereus × moenninghoffii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure, typical of the Cereus genus. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
