Cylindrolepis rehmanniana

Cylindrolepis rehmanniana, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a species of conifer native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
