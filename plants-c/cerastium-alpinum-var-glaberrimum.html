Cerastium alpinum var. glaberrimum

Cerastium alpinum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the glabrous alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.

Its compact growth form and frost-resistant nature make it a resilient species in harsh climates.
