Camellia magniflora

Camellia magniflora, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting eye. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade.

Camellia magniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
