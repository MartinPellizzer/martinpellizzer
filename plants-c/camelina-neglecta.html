Camelina neglecta

Camelina neglecta, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along streams. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive elongated seed pods.

Camelina neglecta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
