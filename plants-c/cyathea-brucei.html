Cyathea brucei

Cyathea brucei, commonly known as the Bruce's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizome system supports the development of its sturdy trunk and contributes to its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Cyathea brucei plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
