Convolvulus engleriana

Convolvulus engleriana, commonly known as Engler's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
