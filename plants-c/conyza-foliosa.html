Conyza foliosa

Conyza foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Conyza foliosa produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
