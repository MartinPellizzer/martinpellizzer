Crataegus rosiformis var. carpatica

Crataegus rosiformis var. carpatica, commonly known as the Czech hedgehog or Carpathian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense hedgerows.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and adaptability to various garden conditions.
