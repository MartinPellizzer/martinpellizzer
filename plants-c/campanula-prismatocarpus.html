Campanula prismatocarpus

Campanula prismatocarpus, commonly known as the prism leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are distinctive for their wavy, prismatic appearance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
