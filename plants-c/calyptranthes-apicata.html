Calyptranthes apicata

Calyptranthes apicata, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found at high elevations.

Calyptranthes apicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
