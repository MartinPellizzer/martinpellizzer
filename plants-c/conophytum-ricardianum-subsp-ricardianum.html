Conophytum ricardianum subsp. ricardianum

Conophytum ricardianum subsp.

ricardianum, commonly known as the "Ricardian Coneplant," is a succulent native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Botswana. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical or conical stems that store water.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
