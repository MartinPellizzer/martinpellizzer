Chlorophytum umbraticola

Chlorophytum umbraticola, commonly known as the shadow ivy, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, trailing growth habit and dark green, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. This plant thrives in low-light conditions, making it ideal for shaded indoor environments. Its ability to propagate through rhizomes allows it to spread easily, forming dense mats over time.

Native to South Africa, it is a popular choice for hanging baskets and terrariums due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
