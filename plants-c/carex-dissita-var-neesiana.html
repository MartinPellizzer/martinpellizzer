Carex dissita var. neesiana

Carex dissita var. neesiana, commonly known as the Neesian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
