Chenopodium album subsp. ficifolium

Chenopodium album subsp.

ficifolium, commonly known as broadleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by kidney-shaped, edible seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
