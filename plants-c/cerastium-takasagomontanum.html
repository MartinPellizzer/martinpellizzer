Cerastium takasagomontanum

Cerastium takasagomontanum, commonly known as the Japanese snowberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from April to May. The plant has prostrate stems covered with fine, hairy foliage, and its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense mats in garden settings.
