Cytisus multiflorus f. incarnatus

Cytisus multiflorus f. incarnatus, commonly known as the pink-flowered broom, is a variant of the common broom plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or pale red flowers, which contrast with the typical yellow blooms of the species. The plant is a shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of small, trifoliate leaves, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
