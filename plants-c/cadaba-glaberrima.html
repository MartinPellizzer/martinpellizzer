Cadaba glaberrima

Cadaba glaberrima, commonly known as the African mallow, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Cadaba glaberrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
