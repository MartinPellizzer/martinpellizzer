Carthamus turkestanicus

Carthamus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan safflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seeds are used for oil extraction, and the plant has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.

The leaves and flowers are also used in local culinary practices.
