Capsella bursa-pastoris lata

Capsella bursa-pastoris lata, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white flowers with four petals and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in gardens. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. Its fruit is a short, hairy pod that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is a common weed in many regions and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
