Claytonia polycarpoides

Claytonia polycarpoides, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and forest floors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its flowers are usually borne on short stems, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Claytonia polycarpoides is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
