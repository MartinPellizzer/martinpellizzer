Chrozophora parvifolia

Chrozophora parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy stems and small, narrow leaves to reduce water loss. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
