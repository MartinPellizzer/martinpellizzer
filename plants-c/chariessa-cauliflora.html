Chariessa cauliflora

Chariessa cauliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered chariessa, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems, a feature known as cauliflory. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
