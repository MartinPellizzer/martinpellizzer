Calochortus umpquaensis subsp. confertus

Calochortus umpquaensis subsp.

confertus, commonly known as the Umpqua mariposa lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
