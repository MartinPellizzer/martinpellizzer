Cotoneaster cinnabarinus

Cotoneaster cinnabarinus, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The berries persist through winter, adding visual interest to the landscape.
