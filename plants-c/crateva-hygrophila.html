Crateva hygrophila

Crateva hygrophila, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are usually light green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, fertile soil and is often found near water bodies or in wetlands. Its stems are edible and commonly used in Asian cuisine.

Crateva hygrophila is also valued for its ability to purify water and support aquatic ecosystems.
