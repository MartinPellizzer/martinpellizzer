Cissampelopsis walkeri var. floccosa

Cissampelopsis walkeri var. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly cissampelopsis, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
