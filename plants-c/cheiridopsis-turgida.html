Cheiridopsis turgida

Cheiridopsis turgida, commonly known as the fat pad plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
