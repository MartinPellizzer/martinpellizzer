Crepidium megalanthum subsp. novoguineense

Crepidium megalanthum subsp.

novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of New Guinea. It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its root system is fibrous, and it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
