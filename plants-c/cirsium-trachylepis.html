Cirsium trachylepis

Cirsium trachylepis, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, pale blue to purple flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with rough, sandpapery textures along their surfaces. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
