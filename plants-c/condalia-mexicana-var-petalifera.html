Condalia mexicana var. petalifera

Condalia mexicana var. petalifera, commonly known as the Mexican petalleaf condalia, is a shrub native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves that are often adorned with small, petal-like structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
