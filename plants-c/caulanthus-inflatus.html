Caulanthus inflatus

Caulanthus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its inflated, pod-like seed capsules that give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Caulanthus inflatus produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
