Camellia tegmentosa

Camellia tegmentosa, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces striking, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes. Camellia tegmentosa prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding color to the landscape during the colder months.
