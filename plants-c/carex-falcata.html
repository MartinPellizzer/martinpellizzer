Carex falcata

Carex falcata, commonly known as the hooked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex falcata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
