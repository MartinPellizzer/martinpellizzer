Centrosema oblongum

Centrosema oblongum, commonly known as the common centrosema, is a perennial legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in forage and soil improvement. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Centrosema oblongum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
