Crepidomanes pseudoblepharistomum

Crepidomanes pseudoblepharistomum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It typically features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained sandy soils.

This species is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
