Cyperus cardosoi

Cyperus cardosoi, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Cyperus cardosoi produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
