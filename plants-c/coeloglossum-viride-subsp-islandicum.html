Coeloglossum viride subsp. islandicum

Coeloglossum viride subsp.

islandicum, commonly known as the Island green hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in elongated racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
