Chassalia violacea

Chassalia violacea, commonly known as the violet chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chassalia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
