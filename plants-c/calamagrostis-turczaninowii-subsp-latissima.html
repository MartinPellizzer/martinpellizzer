Calamagrostis turczaninowii subsp. latissima

Calamagrostis turczaninowii subsp.

latissima, commonly known as the broadleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and wide, grass-like leaves that are often a striking blue-green color.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
