Crataegus pyriformis

Crataegus pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus pyriformis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
