Caralluma commutata subsp. hesperidum

Caralluma commutata subsp.

hesperidum, commonly known as the Indian date cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan. It features fleshy, spineless stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, and it has a branching growth habit.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
