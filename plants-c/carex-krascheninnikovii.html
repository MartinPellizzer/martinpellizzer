Carex krascheninnikovii

Carex krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Carex krascheninnikovii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments and supports local wildlife.
