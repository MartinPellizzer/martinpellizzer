Chamaecrista hildebrandtii

Chamaecrista hildebrandtii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
