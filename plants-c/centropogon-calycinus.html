Centropogon calycinus

Centropogon calycinus, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

Centropogon calycinus is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.
