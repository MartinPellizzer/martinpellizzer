Ceropegia arabensis

Ceropegia arabensis, commonly known as the Arabian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a succulent perennial with fleshy, tuberous roots and thin, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color, adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Ceropegia arabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
