Convolvulus malacophyllus

Convolvulus malacophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Convolvulus malacophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
