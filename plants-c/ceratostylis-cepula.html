Ceratostylis cepula

Ceratostylis cepula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ceratostylis cepula is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate and ephemeral floral display.
