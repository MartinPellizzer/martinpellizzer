Celastrus tonkinensis

Celastrus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese staff vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
