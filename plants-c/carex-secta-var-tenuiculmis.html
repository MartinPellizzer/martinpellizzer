Carex secta var. tenuiculmis

Carex secta var. tenuiculmis, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
