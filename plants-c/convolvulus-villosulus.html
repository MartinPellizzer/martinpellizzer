Convolvulus villosulus

Convolvulus villosulus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus villosulus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
