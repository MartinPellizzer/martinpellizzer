Carex teretiuscula f. simplicior

Carex teretiuscula f. simplicior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a variant of the narrowleaf sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like foliage, characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like structure bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
