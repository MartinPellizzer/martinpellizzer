Coelogyne cumingii

Coelogyne cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of 5 to 10 on a branched inflorescence. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Coelogyne cumingii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
