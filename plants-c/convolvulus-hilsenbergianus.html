Convolvulus hilsenbergianus

Convolvulus hilsenbergianus, commonly known as the Hilsenberg's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
