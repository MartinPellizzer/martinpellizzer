Canhamo

Canhamo, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum novogranatense*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Canhamo thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
