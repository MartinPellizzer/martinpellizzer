Chamaesyce burmanica

Chamaesyce burmanica, commonly known as Burman's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its flowering stems bear clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers.

Chamaesyce burmanica is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in challenging conditions.
