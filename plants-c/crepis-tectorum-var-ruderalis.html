Crepis tectorum var. ruderalis

Crepis tectorum var. ruderalis, commonly known as the common wallweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, waste areas, and urban environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bristly, cylindrical seed heads.

The species is known for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common sight in human-altered landscapes.
