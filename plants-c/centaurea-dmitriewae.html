Centaurea dmitriewae

Centaurea dmitriewae, commonly known as the Dmitriew's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, showy head. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Centaurea dmitriewae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
