Cereus albispinus var. major

Cereus albispinus var. major, commonly known as the giant night blooming cereus, is a striking cactus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

The plant is well-suited for cultivation in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.
