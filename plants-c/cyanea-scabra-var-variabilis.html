Cyanea scabra var. variabilis

Cyanea scabra var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable rough coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its woody, branching structure and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a distinctive texture that gives the species its common name. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
