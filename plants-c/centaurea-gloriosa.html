Centaurea gloriosa

Centaurea gloriosa, commonly known as the gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea gloriosa is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
