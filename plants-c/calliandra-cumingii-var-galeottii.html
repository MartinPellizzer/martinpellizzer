Calliandra cumingii var. galeottii

Calliandra cumingii var. galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
