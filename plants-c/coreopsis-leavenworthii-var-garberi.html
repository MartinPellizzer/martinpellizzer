Coreopsis leavenworthii var. garberi

Coreopsis leavenworthii var. garberi, commonly known as the Garber's tickseed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange-yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in native plant gardens.
