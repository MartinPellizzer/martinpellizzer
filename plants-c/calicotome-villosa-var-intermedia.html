Calicotome villosa var. intermedia

Calicotome villosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grey-green, silky-haired leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
