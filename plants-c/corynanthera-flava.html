Corynanthera flava

Corynanthera flava, commonly known as the yellow sand orchid, is a species of orchid native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas. It has a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces small, clustered flowers.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
