Callicoma ternata

Callicoma ternata, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Callicoma ternata is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and workability.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
