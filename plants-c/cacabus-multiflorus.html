Cacabus multiflorus

Cacabus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered burr cucumber, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a spiny, elongated capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
