Campanula crassa

Campanula crassa, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula crassa is known for its long blooming period, from late spring through early autumn.
