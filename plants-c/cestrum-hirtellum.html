Cestrum hirtellum

Cestrum hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy cestrum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of white, yellow, or pink, which bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cestrum hirtellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
