Carica erythrocarpa

Carica erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fleshed papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinct, fleshy fruit. The fruit is typically orange to red in color, with a sweet and aromatic pulp that contains numerous black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.
