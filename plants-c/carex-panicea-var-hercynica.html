Carex panicea var. hercynica

Carex panicea var. hercynica, commonly known as the Hercynian panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
