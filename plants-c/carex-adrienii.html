Carex adrienii

Carex adrienii, commonly known as Adrian's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands or along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
