Camarea linearifolia

Camarea linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features elongated, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spindle-shaped fruits containing numerous seeds. Camarea linearifolia is valued for its edible tubers, which are similar in texture to traditional arrowroot but less commonly cultivated.

Its adaptability to moist, shaded environments makes it a notable species in tropical botany.
