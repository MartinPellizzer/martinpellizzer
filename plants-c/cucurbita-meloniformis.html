Cucurbita meloniformis

Cucurbita meloniformis, commonly known as the horned melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, greenish-yellow fruit with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy, edible pulp surrounding numerous black, seed-like structures. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
