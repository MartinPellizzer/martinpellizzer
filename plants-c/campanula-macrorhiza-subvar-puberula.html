Campanula macrorhiza subvar. puberula

Campanula macrorhiza subvar. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy pubescence, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands, where it contributes to biodiversity and ecological stability.
