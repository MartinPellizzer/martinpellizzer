Chenopodium glaucophyllum

Chenopodium glaucophyllum, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, beak-like seed capsules. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Chenopodium glaucophyllum is edible, with both the leaves and young stems being used in traditional cuisines.
