Cyathea abrapatriciana

Cyathea abrapatriciana, commonly known as the Philippine tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Cyathea abrapatriciana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
