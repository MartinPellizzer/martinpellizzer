Canistrum binotii

Canistrum binotii, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker plant, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often armed with spines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Canistrum binotii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
