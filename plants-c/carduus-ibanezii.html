Carduus ibanezii

Carduus ibanezii, commonly known as Ibanez's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets.

Carduus ibanezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
