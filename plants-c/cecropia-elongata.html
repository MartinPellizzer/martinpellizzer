Cecropia elongata

Cecropia elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and elongated, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody pods containing elongated seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to high light conditions.

The tree plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
