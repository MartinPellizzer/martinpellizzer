Crepinia surculosa

Crepinia surculosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its botanical structure is characterized by a rhizomatous root system, which helps it spread and establish in various environments.

Crepinia surculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
