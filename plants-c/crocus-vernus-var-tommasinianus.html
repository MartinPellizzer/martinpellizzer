Crocus vernus var. tommasinianus

Crocus vernus var. tommasinianus, commonly known as the Tuscan crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.
