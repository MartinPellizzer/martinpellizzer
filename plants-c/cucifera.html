Cucifera

Cucifera, commonly known as the cucumber plant, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, edible fruits. Its botanical name, Cucurbita pepo, reflects its classification within the genus Cucurbita.

The cucumber plant is cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in water and nutrients.
