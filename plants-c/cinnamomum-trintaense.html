Cinnamomum trintaense

Cinnamomum trintaense, commonly known as the Tinta cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
