Caragana digitata

Caragana digitata, commonly known as the slender peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5-9 pairs of leaflets, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often used for erosion control due to its deep root system.

Caragana digitata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
