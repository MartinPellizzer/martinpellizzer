Cirsium × pustariacum

Cirsium × pustariacum, commonly known as the spiked centaury, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
