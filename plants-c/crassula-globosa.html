Crassula globosa

Crassula globosa, commonly known as the "Globular Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
