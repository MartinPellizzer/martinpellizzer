Crotalaria multicolor

Crotalaria multicolor, commonly known as the multicolored cowpea or rainbow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, spurred flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers come in a variety of hues, including yellow, orange, and red. It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Crotalaria multicolor is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
