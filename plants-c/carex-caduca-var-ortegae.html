Carex caduca var. ortegae

Carex caduca var. ortegae, commonly known as the Ortega's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often dark green in color.

It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
