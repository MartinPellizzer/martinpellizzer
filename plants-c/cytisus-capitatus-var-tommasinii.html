Cytisus capitatus var. tommasinii

Cytisus capitatus var. tommasinii, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas due to its drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species within the Fabaceae family.
