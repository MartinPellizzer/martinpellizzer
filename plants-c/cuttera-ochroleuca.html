Cuttera ochroleuca

Cuttera ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaf cuttera, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
