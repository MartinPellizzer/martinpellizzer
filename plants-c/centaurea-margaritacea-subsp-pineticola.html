Centaurea margaritacea subsp. pineticola

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

pineticola, commonly known as the alpine cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
