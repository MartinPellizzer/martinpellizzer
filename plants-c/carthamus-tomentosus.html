Carthamus tomentosus

Carthamus tomentosus, commonly known as the fuzzy poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal remedies, while the plant itself is valued in gardens for its distinctive appearance.

Carthamus tomentosus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
