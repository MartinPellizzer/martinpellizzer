Convolvulus pseudosiculus

Convolvulus pseudosiculus, commonly known as the Sicilian morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Convolvulus pseudosiculus is often found growing along coastal areas and in rocky habitats.
