Cerastium lanigerum subsp. stojanovii

Cerastium lanigerum subsp.

stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically hairy and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
