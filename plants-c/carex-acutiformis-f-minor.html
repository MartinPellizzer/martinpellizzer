Carex acutiformis f. minor

Carex acutiformis f. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly recurved. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This sedges is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
