Capparis singalensis

Capparis singalensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan caper, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Capparis singalensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas and along coastal regions.
