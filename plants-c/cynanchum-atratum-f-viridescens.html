Cynanchum atratum f. viridescens

Cynanchum atratum f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-leafed milkweed, is a variant of the common milkweed species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered with a fine layer of white hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive bluish-green hue, which gives it its common name.

The flowers are typically pale green or yellowish, arranged in clusters, and are followed by capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds.
