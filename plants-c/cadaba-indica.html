Cadaba indica

Cadaba indica, commonly known as the Indian mallow or wild hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
