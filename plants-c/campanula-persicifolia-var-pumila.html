Campanula persicifolia var. pumila

Campanula persicifolia var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to alpine garden settings.
