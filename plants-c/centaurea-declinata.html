Centaurea declinata

Centaurea declinata, commonly known as the leaning cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often spindly and somewhat inclined.

Centaurea declinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
