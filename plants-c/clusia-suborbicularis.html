Clusia suborbicularis

Clusia suborbicularis, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight yellowish or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Clusia suborbicularis is evergreen, tolerates a wide range of light conditions, and is relatively drought-resistant.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
