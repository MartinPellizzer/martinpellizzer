Carex hornschuchiana f. subdistans

Carex hornschuchiana f. subdistans, commonly known as the slender bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks.

Its subdistans form is distinguished by its slender, elongated leaves and compact, clustered flowering structures.
