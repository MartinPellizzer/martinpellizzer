Croton caudatus var. oblongifolius

Croton caudatus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated, foliage with broad, lance-shaped leaves that can range from green to yellow or red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
