Chamaecytisus absinthioides subsp. rhodopeus

Chamaecytisus absinthioides subsp.

rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria. It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
