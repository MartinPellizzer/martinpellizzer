Celastrus rothianus

Celastrus rothianus, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Celastrus rothianus is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
