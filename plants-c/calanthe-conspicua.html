Calanthe conspicua

Calanthe conspicua, commonly known as the showy calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring or early summer. The plant has rhizomatous growth and features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns.

Calanthe conspicua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.
