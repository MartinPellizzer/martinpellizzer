Calyptrocarya fragifera var. angustifolia

Calyptrocarya fragifera var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calyptrocarya, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
