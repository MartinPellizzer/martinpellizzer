Camissonia integrifolia

Camissonia integrifolia, commonly known as the Coast Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale yellow.

Camissonia integrifolia is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in maritime scrub habitats.
