Cirsium lineare f. albidum

Cirsium lineare f. albidum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, pale to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and serrated edges. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
