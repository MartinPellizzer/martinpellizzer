Croton boiteaui

Croton boiteaui, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often milky sap-containing species. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
