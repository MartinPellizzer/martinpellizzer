Chrozophora obliqua

Chrozophora obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems, which give it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chrozophora obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
