Caucalis japonica

Caucalis japonica, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Caucalis japonica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
