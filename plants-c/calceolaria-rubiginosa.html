Calceolaria rubiginosa

Calceolaria rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from red to orange.

Calceolaria rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
