Chenopodium pueblense

Chenopodium pueblense, commonly known as Mexican goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and often displays a reddish or purple hue. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as utricles.

Chenopodium pueblense is often used in traditional medicine and is also considered a potential forage plant for livestock.
