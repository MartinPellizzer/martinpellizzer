Carex pararadicalis

Carex pararadicalis, commonly known as the swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-stamened flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex pararadicalis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
