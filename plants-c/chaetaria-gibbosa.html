Chaetaria gibbosa

Chaetaria gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy bristled orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and bristled stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
