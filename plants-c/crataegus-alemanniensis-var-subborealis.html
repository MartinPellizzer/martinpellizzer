Crataegus alemanniensis var. subborealis

Crataegus alemanniensis var. subborealis, commonly known as the northern hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorned branches and dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface.

This species is well adapted to cooler climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
