Cyathula prostrata var. grandiflora

Cyathula prostrata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a prostrate growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
