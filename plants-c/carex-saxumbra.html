Carex saxumbra

Carex saxumbra, commonly known as the saxum bracken sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat.

Carex saxumbra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
