Canthium siamense

Canthium siamense, commonly known as the Siam spurge, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
