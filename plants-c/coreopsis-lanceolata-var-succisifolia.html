Coreopsis lanceolata var. succisifolia

Coreopsis lanceolata var. succisifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent yellow centers and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early fall. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
