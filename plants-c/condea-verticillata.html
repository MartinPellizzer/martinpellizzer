Condea verticillata

Condea verticillata, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around the stem, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas.

Condea verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
