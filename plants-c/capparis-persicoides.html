Capparis persicoides

Capparis persicoides, commonly known as Persian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
