Cineraria vestita

Cineraria vestita, commonly known as the white cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its woolly, silver-green foliage and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal among horticultural enthusiasts.
