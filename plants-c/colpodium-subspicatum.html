Colpodium subspicatum

Colpodium subspicatum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Colpodium subspicatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
