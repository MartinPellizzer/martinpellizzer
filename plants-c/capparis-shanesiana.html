Capparis shanesiana

Capparis shanesiana, commonly known as the Shanes' caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant's leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
