Cupania spectabilis

Cupania spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cupania, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Cupania spectabilis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
