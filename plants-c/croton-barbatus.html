Croton barbatus

Croton barbatus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking, palmate leaf structure. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by capsule fruits.

Croton barbatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
