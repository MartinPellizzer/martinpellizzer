Crocus reinwardtii

Crocus reinwardtii, commonly known as the Reinwardt's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. It typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its corms are used for propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
