Carex acutiformis subsp. kochiana

Carex acutiformis subsp.

kochiana, commonly known as the sharp-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shady, damp environments.
