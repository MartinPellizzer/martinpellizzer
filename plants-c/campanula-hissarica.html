Campanula hissarica

Campanula hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula hissarica is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
