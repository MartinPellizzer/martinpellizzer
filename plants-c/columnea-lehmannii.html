Columnea lehmannii

Columnea lehmannii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a trailing or shrub-like species, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a houseplant.

Columnea lehmannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
