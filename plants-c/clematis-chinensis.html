Clematis chinensis

Clematis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Clematis chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
