Cryptogramma crispa var. chilensis

Cryptogramma crispa var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean cryptogramma, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Pterobryaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and have a distinctive, crinkled appearance.
