Cestrum paucinervium

Cestrum paucinervium, commonly known as the lesser trumpetflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, including countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Cestrum paucinervium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
