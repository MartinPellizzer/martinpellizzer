Chrysanthellum indicum subsp. mexicanum

Chrysanthellum indicum subsp.

mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican chrysanthellum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, herbaceous stems.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
