Commelina petersii subsp. loandensis

Commelina petersii subsp.

loandensis, commonly known as the Loango commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Africa. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and distinctive blue-violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. It produces small, three-lobed bracts that surround its inflorescence, which consists of numerous tiny, white to blue flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
