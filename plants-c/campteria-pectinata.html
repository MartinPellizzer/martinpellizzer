Campteria pectinata

Campteria pectinata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Campteria pectinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
