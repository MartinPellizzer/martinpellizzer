Cattleya bicolor subsp. canastrensis

Cattleya bicolor subsp.

canastrensis, commonly known as the Canastrense Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, typically featuring a white or pale pink lip and a contrasting purple or red dorsal sepal and petals.

The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with good air circulation and partial shade. It produces fragrant blooms during the late spring to early summer season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
