Crataegus acanthacolonensis

Crataegus acanthacolonensis, commonly known as the Acanthacolonensis hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.
