Casearia lasiophylla

Casearia lasiophylla, commonly known as the glossy casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical gardens.
