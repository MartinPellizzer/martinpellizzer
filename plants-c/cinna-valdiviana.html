Cinna valdiviana

Cinna valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivia cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown bark that is used in traditional medicine. Cinna valdiviana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native region.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
