Carex breviculmis f. filiculmis

Carex breviculmis f. filiculmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in width, giving it a delicate, refined look. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream edges.

This sedge is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
