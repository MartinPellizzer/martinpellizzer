Cyathea fallax

Cyathea fallax, commonly known as the Hawaiian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered in a layer of old fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea fallax thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
