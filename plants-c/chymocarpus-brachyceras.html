Chymocarpus brachyceras

Chymocarpus brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-headed chymocarp, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are typically arranged in compact clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Chymocarpus brachyceras is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
