Cereus longisetus

Cereus longisetus, commonly known as the long-hair cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features dense, white to pale blue spines that cover its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are large, funnel-shaped, and usually white or pale yellow, blooming at night.

Cereus longisetus is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and requires minimal water once established.
