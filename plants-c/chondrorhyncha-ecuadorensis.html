Chondrorhyncha ecuadorensis

Chondrorhyncha ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian chondrorhynch, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive labellum that plays a crucial role in pollination. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, adapted to attract specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species within its native range.
