Chassalia chrysoclada

Chassalia chrysoclada, commonly known as the golden chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray florets and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chassalia chrysoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
