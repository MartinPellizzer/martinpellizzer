Collinsia pauciflora

Collinsia pauciflora, commonly known as the slender fairybell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

Collinsia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
