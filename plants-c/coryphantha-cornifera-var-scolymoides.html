Coryphantha cornifera var. scolymoides

Coryphantha cornifera var. scolymoides, commonly known as the scolymoides coryphantha, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with thick, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
