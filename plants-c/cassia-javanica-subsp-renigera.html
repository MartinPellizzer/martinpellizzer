Cassia javanica subsp. renigera

Cassia javanica subsp.

renigera, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
