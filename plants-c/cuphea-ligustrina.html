Cuphea ligustrina

Cuphea ligustrina, commonly known as the "whispering bells" or "cuphea," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It produces striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.

Cuphea ligustrina is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
