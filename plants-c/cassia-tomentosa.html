Cassia tomentosa

Cassia tomentosa, commonly known as the white cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia tomentosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
