Caesia parviflora var. vittata

Caesia parviflora var. vittata, commonly known as the spotted caesia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters. Its varietal name, *vittata*, refers to the distinctive white or yellow streaks on its leaves.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
