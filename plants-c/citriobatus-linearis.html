Citriobatus linearis

Citriobatus linearis, commonly known as the lemon cucumber, is a vine-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces elongated, thin fruits that resemble small lemons, hence its common name. The plant has palmately divided leaves and prefers warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, slightly tart flavor.

Citriobatus linearis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
