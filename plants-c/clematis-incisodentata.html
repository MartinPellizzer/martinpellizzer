Clematis incisodentata

Clematis incisodentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue. The plant has compound leaves with finely toothed margins, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clematis incisodentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
