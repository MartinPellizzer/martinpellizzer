Croton urceolatus

Croton urceolatus, commonly known as the "cup-shaped croton," is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, orb-shaped seed capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton urceolatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
