Callicarpa cumingiana var. dentata

Callicarpa cumingiana var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
