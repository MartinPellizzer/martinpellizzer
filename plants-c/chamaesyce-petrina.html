Chamaesyce petrina

Chamaesyce petrina, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant often used in xeriscaping.
