Coreopsis patula

Coreopsis patula, commonly known as the dwarf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 inches and features bright yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Coreopsis patula is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
