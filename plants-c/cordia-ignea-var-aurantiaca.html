Cordia ignea var. aurantiaca

Cordia ignea var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vivid display during the flowering season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often heart-shaped or elliptical in form. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
