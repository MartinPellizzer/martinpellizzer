Cyperus vaginatus var. gymnocaulos

Cyperus vaginatus var. gymnocaulos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, glabrous surface, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus vaginatus var. gymnocaulos is often found in disturbed wetlands and is considered an indicator species for certain aquatic environments.
