Croton ivohibensis

Croton ivohibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and diverse leaf shapes. The plant features alternate, often palmately compound leaves that are typically green with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
