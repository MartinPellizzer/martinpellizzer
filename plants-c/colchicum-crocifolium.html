Colchicum crocifolium

Colchicum crocifolium, commonly known as the creeping colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
