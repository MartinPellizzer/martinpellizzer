Canarium venosum

Canarium venosum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a bright red rind and segmented white flesh, which is rich in antioxidants. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and the edible fruit, which has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological systems.
