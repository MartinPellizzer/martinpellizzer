Cassia rotundifolia var. longipedicellata

Cassia rotundifolia var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-panicle cassia, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its rounded, palmate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The fruit is a elongated, cylindrical pod containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
