Carex acuta subsp. rufa

Carex acuta subsp.

rufa, commonly known as the red-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spikelets that often have a reddish hue, especially in the fruiting stage. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
