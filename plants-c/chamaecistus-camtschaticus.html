Chamaecistus camtschaticus

Chamaecistus camtschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine.

It is also used as a source of essential oils due to its chemical composition.
