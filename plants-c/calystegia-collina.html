Calystegia collina

Calystegia collina, commonly known as the prairie morning glory, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a trailing vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in prairies and open fields. Calystegia collina is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or spread along the ground.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
