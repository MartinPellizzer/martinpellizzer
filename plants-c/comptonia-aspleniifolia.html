Comptonia aspleniifolia

Comptonia aspleniifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a fragrant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Comptonia aspleniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
