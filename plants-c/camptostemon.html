Camptostemon

Camptostemon, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plants are typically herbaceous, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Camptostemon species are often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
