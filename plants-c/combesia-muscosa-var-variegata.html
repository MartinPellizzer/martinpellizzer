Combesia muscosa var. variegata

Combesia muscosa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated mossy combesia, is a variant of the combesia genus characterized by its unique leaf patterns.

This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its leaves are often variegated with contrasting colors, which may serve as a form of photoprotection. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with a dense, cushion-like appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
