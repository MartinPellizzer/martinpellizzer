Cocculus thunbergii

Cocculus thunbergii, commonly known as the Chinese devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a spiny, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Cocculus thunbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
