Centranthus angustifolius proles lecoqii

Centranthus angustifolius proles lecoqii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
