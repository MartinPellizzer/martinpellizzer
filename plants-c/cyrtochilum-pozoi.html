Cyrtochilum pozoi

Cyrtochilum pozoi, commonly known as the Pozo's Cymbidium, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with dark purple to brown petals. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its roots are adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Cyrtochilum pozoi is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

It requires well-drained soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
