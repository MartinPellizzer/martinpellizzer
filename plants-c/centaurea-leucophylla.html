Centaurea leucophylla

Centaurea leucophylla, commonly known as the whiteleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea leucophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
