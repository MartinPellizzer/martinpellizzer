Calycothrix cuspidata

Calycothrix cuspidata, commonly known as the slender calycanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Calycanthaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces red or orange-red berries that are edible for birds. Calycothrix cuspidata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
