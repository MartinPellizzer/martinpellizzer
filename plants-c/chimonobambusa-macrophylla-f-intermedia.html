Chimonobambusa macrophylla f. intermedia

Chimonobambusa macrophylla f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers a humid, temperate climate. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as ornamental plantings in gardens.

This bamboo is also valued for its strong, flexible culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
