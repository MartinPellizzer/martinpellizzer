Cassia mucronulosa

Cassia mucronulosa, commonly known as the narrow-point cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Cassia mucronulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
