Citrus grandis var. natsudaidai

Citrus grandis var. natsudaidai, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or natsudaidai, is a variant of the loquat tree.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
