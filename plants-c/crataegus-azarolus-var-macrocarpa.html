Crataegus azarolus var. macrocarpa

Crataegus azarolus var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, a pome, is typically large, yellowish, and edible, often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
