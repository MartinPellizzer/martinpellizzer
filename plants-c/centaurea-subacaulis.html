Centaurea subacaulis

Centaurea subacaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea subacaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
