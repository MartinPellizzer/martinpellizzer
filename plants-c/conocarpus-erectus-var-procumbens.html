Conocarpus erectus var. procumbens

Conocarpus erectus var. procumbens, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, grey bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This variant is known for its adaptability to tidal zones and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
