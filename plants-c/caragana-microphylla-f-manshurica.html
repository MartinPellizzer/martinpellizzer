Caragana microphylla f. manshurica

Caragana microphylla f. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian bushclover, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Russia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils and tolerating drought conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
