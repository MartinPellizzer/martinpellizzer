Carpinus turczaninovii

Carpinus turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its wood is dense and hard, making it valuable for construction and carving.
