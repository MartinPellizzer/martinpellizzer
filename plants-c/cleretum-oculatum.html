Cleretum oculatum

Cleretum oculatum, commonly known as the eye-plant or the eye of the peacock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble eyes, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Cleretum oculatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
