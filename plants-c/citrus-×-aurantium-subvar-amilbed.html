Citrus × aurantium subvar. amilbed

Citrus × aurantium subvar. amilbed, commonly known as the Amilbed kumquat, is a citrus hybrid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to orange as they ripen. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has glossy, dark green leaves. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
