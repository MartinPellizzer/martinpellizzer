Croton laccifer

Croton laccifer, commonly known as the lac tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant is notable for producing a resin called lac, which is harvested from its bark. This resin has been used historically for varnishing and as a natural dye.

Croton laccifer thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
