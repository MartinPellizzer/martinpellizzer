Cassia langsdorffii var. parvifolia

Cassia langsdorffii var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia langsdorffii var. parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
