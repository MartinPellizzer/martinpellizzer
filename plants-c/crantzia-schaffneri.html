Crantzia schaffneri

Crantzia schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's crantzia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Crantzia schaffneri is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique ecosystem in the Western Cape region of South Africa.
