Clerodendrum myricoides var. chartaceum

Clerodendrum myricoides var. chartaceum, commonly known as paperleaf clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, papery leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by ornamental, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
