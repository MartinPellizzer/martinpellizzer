Combretum trichanthum

Combretum trichanthum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
