Carduus carpetanus subsp. braun-blanquetii

Carduus carpetanus subsp.

braun-blanquetii, commonly known as Braun-Blanquet's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and a distinctive purple to pink flower head. It is characterized by its tightly packed, yellow-centered flower heads surrounded by pale blue or white ray florets.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or marginal environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
