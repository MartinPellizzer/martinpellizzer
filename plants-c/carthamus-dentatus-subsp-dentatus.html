Carthamus dentatus subsp. dentatus

Carthamus dentatus subsp.

dentatus, commonly known as the serrated sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
