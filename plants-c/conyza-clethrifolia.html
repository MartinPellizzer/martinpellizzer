Conyza clethrifolia

Conyza clethrifolia, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as fields and roadsides, and it is known for its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers.

Conyza clethrifolia is also notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
