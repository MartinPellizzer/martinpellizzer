Calamus flagellum var. furvifurfuraceus

Calamus flagellum var. furvifurfuraceus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo reed, is a variant of the calamus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments, often growing in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in aquatic and bog gardens.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
