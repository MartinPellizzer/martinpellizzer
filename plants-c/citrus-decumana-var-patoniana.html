Citrus decumana var. patoniana

Citrus decumana var. patoniana, commonly known as the Paton's mandarin, is a citrus variant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is often used for its edible pulp and has a mild, tangy flavor similar to other mandarin varieties.
