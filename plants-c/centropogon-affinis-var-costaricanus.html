Centropogon affinis var. costaricanus

Centropogon affinis var. costaricanus, commonly known as the Costa Rican flameflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, with a prominent midrib.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
