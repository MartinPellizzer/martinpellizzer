Cyperus refractus var. antillanus

Cyperus refractus var. antillanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences are typical of the genus Cyperus.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
