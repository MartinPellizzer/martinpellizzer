Crinum odoratissimum

Crinum odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet-scented crinum, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, white or pale pink blossoms that emit a strong, sweet aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Crinum odoratissimum is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be used for propagation.
