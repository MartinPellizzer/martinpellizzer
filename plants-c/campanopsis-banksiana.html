Campanopsis banksiana

Campanopsis banksiana, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to lavender. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. It produces showy blooms in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
