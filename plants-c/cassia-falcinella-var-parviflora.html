Cassia falcinella var. parviflora

Cassia falcinella var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The seeds of this species are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
