Cerastium alpinum lusus kamtschaticum

Cerastium alpinum lusus kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. This plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It is a hardy species that can tolerate cold temperatures and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and ecological restoration projects.
