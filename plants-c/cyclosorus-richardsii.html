Cyclosorus richardsii

Cyclosorus richardsii, commonly known as the Richards' cyclosorus, is a small, perennial fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a compact, rosette-like growth pattern with delicate, lance-shaped fronds. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyclosorus richardsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
