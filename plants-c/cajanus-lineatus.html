Cajanus lineatus

Cajanus lineatus, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Cajanus lineatus is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility.

It is also cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
