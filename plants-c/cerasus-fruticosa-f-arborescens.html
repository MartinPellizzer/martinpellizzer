Cerasus fruticosa f. arborescens

Cerasus fruticosa f. arborescens, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with branches drooping gracefully from the central stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white during spring. Its foliage is dark green and provides a striking contrast to the cascading blossoms.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
