Cyanotis longifolia var. rupicola

Cyanotis longifolia var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock cyanotis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
