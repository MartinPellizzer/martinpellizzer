Crataegus crus-galli var. barrettiana

Crataegus crus-galli var. barrettiana, commonly known as the Barrett's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
