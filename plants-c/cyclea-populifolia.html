Cyclea populifolia

Cyclea populifolia, commonly known as the "Lemon Tree" or "Lemon Bush," is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red fruits that contain several seeds. Cyclea populifolia is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
