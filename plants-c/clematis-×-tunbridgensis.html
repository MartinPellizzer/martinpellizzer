Clematis × tunbridgensis

Clematis × tunbridgensis, commonly known as the Tunbridge Wells clematis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Clematis viticella and Clematis integrifolia.

It is a vigorous climbing vine characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces white to pale blue flowers with five petals and a distinctive, recurved form. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its ability to climb trellises and structures.
