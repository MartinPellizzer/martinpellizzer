Chrysocoma undulata

Chrysocoma undulata, commonly known as the undulating chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
