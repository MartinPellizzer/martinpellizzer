Clerodendrum trichotomum f. albicarpum

Clerodendrum trichotomum f. albicarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, clustered in panicles, and the fruit is a follicle that is white or pale in color, giving it its common name.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
