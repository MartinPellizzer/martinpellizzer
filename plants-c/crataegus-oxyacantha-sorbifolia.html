Crataegus oxyacantha sorbifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha sorbifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed sites, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
