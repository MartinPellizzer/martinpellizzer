Cardamine rariflora

Cardamine rariflora, commonly known as the rare fernbloom, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely divided lobes, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with five sepals and numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
