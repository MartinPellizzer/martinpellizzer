Crataegus alemanniensis f. microalemanniensis

Crataegus alemanniensis f. microalemanniensis, commonly known as the dwarf Alemanni cherry, is a unique form of the Alemanni cherry.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller leaves compared to the typical species. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
