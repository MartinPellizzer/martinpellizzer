Cytisus balansae subsp. europaeus

Cytisus balansae subsp.

europaeus, commonly known as the European broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
