Cichorium noeanum

Cichorium noeanum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and curly, leafy foliage. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its edible leaves and roots. Its growth habit includes a basal rosette of leaves with a tall flowering stem.

Cichorium noeanum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
