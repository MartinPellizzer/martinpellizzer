Campylotropis teretiracemosa

Campylotropis teretiracemosa, commonly known as the narrow-branch climbing clover, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
