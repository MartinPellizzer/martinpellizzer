Chloris arundinacea

Chloris arundinacea, commonly known as silky grass or soft brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and tufted growth habit, with leaves that are narrow and slightly rolled. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
