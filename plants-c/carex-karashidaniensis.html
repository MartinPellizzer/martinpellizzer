Carex karashidaniensis

Carex karashidaniensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or marshy areas.

Carex karashidaniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
