Carex bulbostylis

Carex bulbostylis, commonly known as the bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.
