Cytisus glomeratus

Cytisus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus glomeratus is characterized by its fast growth rate and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
