Chrysophyllum omumu

Chrysophyllum omumu, commonly known as the African cherry or omumu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of timber and fruit in its native region.
