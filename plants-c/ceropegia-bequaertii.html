Ceropegia bequaertii

Ceropegia bequaertii, commonly known as the silver bush or silver leaf, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trailing stems that produce small, white, tubular flowers. The plant has fleshy, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Ceropegia bequaertii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
