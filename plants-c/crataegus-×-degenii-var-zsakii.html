Crataegus × degenii var. zsakii

Crataegus × degenii var. zsakii, commonly known as the Zsáki hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is native to Hungary and is often found in woodland edges and dry soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
