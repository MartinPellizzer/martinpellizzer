Croton tessmannii

Croton tessmannii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves, which can vary in shape and hue depending on the variety. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
