Chionanthus greenii

Chionanthus greenii, commonly known as the greenflower chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Chionanthus greenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
