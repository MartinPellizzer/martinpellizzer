Cylindropuntia ganderi subsp. ganderi

Cylindropuntia ganderi subsp.

ganderi, commonly known as the Gander's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

The spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.
