Canarium intermedium

Canarium intermedium, commonly known as the middle candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its latex, similar to that of other Canarium species, has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.
