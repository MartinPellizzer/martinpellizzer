Croton tetradenius

Croton tetradenius, commonly known as the four-veined croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Croton tetradenius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
