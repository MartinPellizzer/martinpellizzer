Cerasus laurocerasus var. myrtifolia

Cerasus laurocerasus var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel or myrtle-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which give way to red, cherry-like berries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it suitable for various garden settings.
