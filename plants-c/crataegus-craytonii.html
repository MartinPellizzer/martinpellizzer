Crataegus craytonii

Crataegus craytonii, commonly known as the Crayton hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptical leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red to purple berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in arid and semi-arid regions.
