Cunila menthiformis

Cunila menthiformis, commonly known as marsh mints, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It typically grows in moist soils, often near streams, ponds, or marshes, and has a distinctive aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a strong mint-like scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in late summer.

Cunila menthiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
