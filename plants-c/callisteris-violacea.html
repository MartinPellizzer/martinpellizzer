Callisteris violacea

Callisteris violacea, commonly known as the violet bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers, which are clustered along the central axis of the inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Callisteris violacea is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
