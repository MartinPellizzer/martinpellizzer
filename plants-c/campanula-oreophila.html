Campanula oreophila

Campanula oreophila, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
