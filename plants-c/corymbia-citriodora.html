Corymbia citriodora

Corymbia citriodora, commonly known as the lemon eucalyptus, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lemon-scented leaves and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its essential oils, particularly citronella, are widely used in natural repellents and aromatherapy.

Due to its aromatic properties and ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
