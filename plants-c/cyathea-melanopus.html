Cyathea melanopus

Cyathea melanopus, commonly known as the black tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas. Its fronds are typically dark green to black in color, giving it its common name.

Cyathea melanopus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
