Crotalaria brevidens var. parviflora

Crotalaria brevidens var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seeds are enclosed in pods. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it contains alkaloids that can be toxic to animals if ingested in large quantities.
