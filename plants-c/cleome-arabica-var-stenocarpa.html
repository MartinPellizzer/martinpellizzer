Cleome arabica var. stenocarpa

Cleome arabica var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and showy, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
