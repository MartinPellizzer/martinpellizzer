Cordia scabrida

Cordia scabrida, commonly known as rough cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Cordia scabrida is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
