Calathea violacea

Calathea violacea, commonly known as the violet calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display intricate patterns in shades of purple, pink, and green. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.
