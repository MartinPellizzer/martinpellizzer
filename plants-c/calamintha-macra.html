Calamintha macra

Calamintha macra, commonly known as large-leaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters and bloom in late summer.

Calamintha macra is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
