Clitoria multiflora

Clitoria multiflora, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that resemble butterflies, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye source.
