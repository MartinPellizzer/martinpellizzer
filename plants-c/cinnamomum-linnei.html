Cinnamomum linnei

Cinnamomum linnei, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly in the Guangdong and Guangxi provinces. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark, which is the source of its name, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and prefers a warm, humid climate with well-drained soil.
