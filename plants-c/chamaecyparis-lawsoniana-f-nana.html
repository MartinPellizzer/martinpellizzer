Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. nana

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Lawson cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a dense, bushy habit.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant in landscaping.
