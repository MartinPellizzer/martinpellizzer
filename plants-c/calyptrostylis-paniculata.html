Calyptrostylis paniculata

Calyptrostylis paniculata, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
