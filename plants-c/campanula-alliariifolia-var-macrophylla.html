Campanula alliariifolia var. macrophylla

Campanula alliariifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a garlic-like scent, which is characteristic of the Campanula genus. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
