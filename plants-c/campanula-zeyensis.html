Campanula zeyensis

Campanula zeyensis, commonly known as the Zey's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many species of bellflowers. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Campanula zeyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
