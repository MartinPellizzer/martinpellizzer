Cordia dichotoma

Cordia dichotoma, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and its showy, white, spherical flower clusters that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
