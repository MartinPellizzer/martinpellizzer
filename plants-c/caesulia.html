Caesulia

Caesulia, commonly known as the blueweed or sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Caesulia species are herbaceous perennials, often with silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest to gardens.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
