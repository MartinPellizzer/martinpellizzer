Centaurea bertolonii

Centaurea bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea bertolonii is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
