Coussapoa duquei

Coussapoa duquei, commonly known as the Duque's orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Coussapoa duquei is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
