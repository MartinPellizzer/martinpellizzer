Cassia fasciculata f. mutata

Cassia fasciculata f. mutata, commonly known as the golden shower tree or yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the species Cassia fasciculata, characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its striking floral display. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.
