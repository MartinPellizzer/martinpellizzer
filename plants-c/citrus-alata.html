Citrus alata

Citrus alata, commonly known as the golden lemon or variegated lemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that lead to the development of round, yellow-orange fruits. These fruits are typically harvested when they are fully mature, offering a tart and aromatic flavor.

Citrus alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
