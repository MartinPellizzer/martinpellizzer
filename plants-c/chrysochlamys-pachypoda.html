Chrysochlamys pachypoda

Chrysochlamys pachypoda, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a smooth, greyish-brown trunk and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

The golden bell tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
