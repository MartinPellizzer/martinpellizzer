Campanula elatines subsp. glaberrima

Campanula elatines subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, smooth texture, giving the plant its subspecies name "glaberrima.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
