Caragana zahlbruckneri var. pekinensis

Caragana zahlbruckneri var. pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking barbed wire bush, is a shrub native to northern China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by legume pods containing small, edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it useful in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
