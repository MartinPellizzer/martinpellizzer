Cynanchum grandidieri

Cynanchum grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar moonseed, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, hooded structure. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
