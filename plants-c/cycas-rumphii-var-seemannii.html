Cycas rumphii var. seemannii

Cycas rumphii var. seemannii, commonly known as the dwarf cycas, is a cycad species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing plant with a short trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing cones rather than flowers. Its leaves are typically a deep green and can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in tropical and subtropical climates.
