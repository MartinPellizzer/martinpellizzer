Ceropegia bourneae

Ceropegia bourneae, commonly known as the string of turtles, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, waxy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia bourneae is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
