Croton glutinosus

Croton glutinosus, commonly known as the sticky croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a sticky texture due to the presence of glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves and roots contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and chemical composition contribute to its ecological and pharmacological significance.
