Carex peucophila

Carex peucophila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Carex peucophila is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
