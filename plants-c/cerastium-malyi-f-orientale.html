Cerastium malyi f. orientale

Cerastium malyi f. orientale, commonly known as the oriental mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
