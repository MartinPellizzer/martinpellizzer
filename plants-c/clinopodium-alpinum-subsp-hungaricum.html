Clinopodium alpinum subsp. hungaricum

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

hungaricum, commonly known as Hungarian mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of purple or pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
