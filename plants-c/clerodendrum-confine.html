Clerodendrum confine

Clerodendrum confine, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Clerodendrum confine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
