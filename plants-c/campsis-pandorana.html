Campsis pandorana

Campsis pandorana, commonly known as the pandora flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that are alternate and often have a velvety texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Campsis pandorana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
