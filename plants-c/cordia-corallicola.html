Cordia corallicola

Cordia corallicola, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking coral-pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cordia corallicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
