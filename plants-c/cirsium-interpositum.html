Cirsium interpositum

Cirsium interpositum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters. Cirsium interpositum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
