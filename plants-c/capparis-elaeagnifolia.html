Capparis elaeagnifolia

Capparis elaeagnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, green berries. These berries are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
