Chrysanthemum hirtum

Chrysanthemum hirtum, commonly known as the roughleaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its blooms appear in the fall and are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic value.

Chrysanthemum hirtum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
