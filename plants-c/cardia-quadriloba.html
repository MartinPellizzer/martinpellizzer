Cardia quadriloba

Cardia quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed cardia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
