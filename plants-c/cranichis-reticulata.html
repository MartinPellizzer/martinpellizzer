Cranichis reticulata

Cranichis reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and have a distinctive reticulated pattern on the petals.

Cranichis reticulata is a rare species, with limited populations, making it a subject of conservation interest.
