Chascanum glanduliferum

Chascanum glanduliferum, commonly known as the Chilean fireweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Chascanum glanduliferum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
