Chenopodium ambrosioides var. graveolens

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. graveolens, commonly known as wormseed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and a mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a valuable plant in some culinary and medicinal traditions.
