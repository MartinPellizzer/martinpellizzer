Ceanothus grandis

Ceanothus grandis, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and foothill habitats. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, contributing to its ecological significance as a food source for native wildlife.

Ceanothus grandis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
