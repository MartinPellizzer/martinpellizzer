Celosia chiapensis

Celosia chiapensis, commonly known as the flame flower or Mexican firecracker, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually red, orange, or yellow, and they bloom in dense, brush-like clusters.

Celosia chiapensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
