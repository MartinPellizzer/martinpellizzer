Calathea noctiflora

Calathea noctiflora, commonly known as the night-blooming calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display intricate patterns in shades of green, silver, and purple. The plant is known for its unique flowering habit, where it produces small, fragrant white flowers at night. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Calathea noctiflora is a popular ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
