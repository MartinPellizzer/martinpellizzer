Cleome aspera

Cleome aspera, commonly known as the spiked cleome, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome aspera is often found in disturbed soils and is cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate, lace-like foliage.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
