Cladium riparium var. crassum

Cladium riparium var. crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, often forming dense mats in shallow water. The plant thrives in marshy areas and is adapted to fluctuating water levels, making it a key component of wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil, preventing erosion.

Cladium riparium var. crassum plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
