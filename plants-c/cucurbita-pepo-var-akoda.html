Cucurbita pepo var. akoda

Cucurbita pepo var. akoda, commonly known as the Akoda gourd, is a variant of the common gourd species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruits that can range in color from pale green to yellowish-orange. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible parts, though it is less commonly consumed than other Cucurbita species.
