Calea coridifolia

Calea coridifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has a distinctive rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Calea coridifolia is also noted for its aromatic fragrance, which becomes more pronounced during the evening.
