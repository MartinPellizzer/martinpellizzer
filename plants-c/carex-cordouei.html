Carex cordouei

Carex cordouei, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are usually flat and cylindrical, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex cordouei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
