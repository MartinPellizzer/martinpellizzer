Ceropegia armandii

Ceropegia armandii, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a succulent native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often trailing or climbing. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive tubular structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves to survive dry conditions.
