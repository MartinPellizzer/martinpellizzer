Conostephium pungens

Conostephium pungens, commonly known as the spiny cactus or needlepoint cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Conostephium pungens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
