Cardamine infausta

Cardamine infausta, commonly known as the meadow corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that bear alternate, trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped lobes. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in a loose cluster, producing a delicate, bell-shaped appearance.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
