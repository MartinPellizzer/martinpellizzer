Ceanothus × humboldtensis

Ceanothus × humboldtensis, commonly known as the Humboldt ceanothus, is a hybrid shrub native to coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
