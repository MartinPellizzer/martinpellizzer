Cajanus niveus

Cajanus niveus, commonly known as the snowflax or white cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Cajanus niveus is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
