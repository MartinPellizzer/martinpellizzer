Citropsis articulata

Citropsis articulata, commonly known as the Articulated Cabbage, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that resemble a chain of small, rounded leaves.

It is native to freshwater environments in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in slow-moving or still water. The plant's leaves are typically green and succulent, adapted to store water and nutrients. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.

Citropsis articulata is often used in water gardens and artificial ponds due to its ornamental appearance and ease of maintenance.
