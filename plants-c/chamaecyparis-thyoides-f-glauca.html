Chamaecyparis thyoides f. glauca

Chamaecyparis thyoides f. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a cultivar of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking blue-gray foliage. The leaves are scale-like and tightly pressed against the branches, giving the plant a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This evergreen is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and hardiness.
