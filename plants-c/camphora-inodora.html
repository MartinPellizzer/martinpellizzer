Camphora inodora

Camphora inodora, commonly known as the odorless camphor tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its camphor-rich wood, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
