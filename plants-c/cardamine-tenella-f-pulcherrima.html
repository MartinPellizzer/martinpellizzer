Cardamine tenella f. pulcherrima

Cardamine tenella f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are delicate and fern-like, often with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
