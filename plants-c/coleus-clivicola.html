Coleus clivicola

Coleus clivicola, commonly known as the cliffcoleus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. It typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Coleus clivicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
