Cavendishia veraguensis

Cavendishia veraguensis, commonly known as the banana plant, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and a central stem that produces clusters of bananas. The plant's pseudostem is formed by tightly packed leaf sheaths, which provide structural support. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires abundant sunlight.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is a staple food in many countries and is rich in potassium and other nutrients.
