Cranichis cucullata

Cranichis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

Cranichis cucullata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
