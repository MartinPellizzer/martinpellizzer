Cyathea portoricensis

Cyathea portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Cyathea portoricensis plays an important ecological role in the humid tropical forests of Puerto Rico.
