Cleome formosa

Cleome formosa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features palmate leaves and cylindrical, branching stems that often grow along the ground. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Cleome formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
