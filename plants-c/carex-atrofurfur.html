Carex atrofurfur

Carex atrofurfur, commonly known as black tufted sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green to blackish in color. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stems, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex atrofurfur thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.
