Cerasus nipponica var. kurilensis

Cerasus nipponica var. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Kuril Islands and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically white or pale pink, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
