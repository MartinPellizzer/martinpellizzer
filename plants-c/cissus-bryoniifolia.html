Cissus bryoniifolia

Cissus bryoniifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid environments with indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
