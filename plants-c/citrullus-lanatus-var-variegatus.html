Citrullus lanatus var. variegatus

Citrullus lanatus var. variegatus, commonly known as the striped watermelon, is a variant of the watermelon species characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns and striped rind.

This plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The fruit typically has a sweet, juicy flesh with a high water content and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural displays.
