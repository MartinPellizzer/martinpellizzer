Campomanesia houlletii

Campomanesia houlletii, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they reach full size and change color, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
