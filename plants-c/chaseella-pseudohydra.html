Chaseella pseudohydra

Chaseella pseudohydra, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
