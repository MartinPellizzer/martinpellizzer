Chassalia hiernii var. glandulosa

Chassalia hiernii var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular chassalia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with glandular dots, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
