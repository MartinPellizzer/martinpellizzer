Canarium euryphyllum var. euryphyllum

Canarium euryphyllum var. euryphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing oily seeds. It is valued for its timber and oil, which are used in traditional crafts and cosmetics.

The species is also important in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in degraded soils.
