Croton corumbensis

Croton corumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in many regions.
