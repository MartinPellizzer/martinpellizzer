Cyperus impolitus

Cyperus impolitus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with sharp spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus impolitus is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
