Coronilla emera

Coronilla emera, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of the Mediterranean. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its pods contain small, round seeds that are often used as a food source for livestock.

Coronilla emera is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
