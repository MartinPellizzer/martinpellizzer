Cordia buddleoides

Cordia buddleoides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fluffy flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a snowball-like appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Cordia buddleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
