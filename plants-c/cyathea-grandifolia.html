Cyathea grandifolia

Cyathea grandifolia, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent at night, a unique feature that aids in attracting pollinators.

Cyathea grandifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
