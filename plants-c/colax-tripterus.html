Colax tripterus

Colax tripterus, commonly known as the three-lobed colax, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or purple in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Colax tripterus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
