Citharexylum poeppigii

Citharexylum poeppigii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom during the warmer months.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by the fragrant scent of its flowers and the attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
