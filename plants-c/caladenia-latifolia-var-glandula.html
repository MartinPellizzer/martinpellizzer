Caladenia latifolia var. glandula

Caladenia latifolia var. glandula, commonly known as the broad-leafed spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive, spider-like flowers with a vivid pink to purple hue. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens due to its unique appearance.
