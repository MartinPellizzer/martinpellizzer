Carex ternaria var. pallida

Carex ternaria var. pallida, commonly known as the pale flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
