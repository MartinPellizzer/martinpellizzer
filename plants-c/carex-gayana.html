Carex gayana

Carex gayana, commonly known as the Gayana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence.

Carex gayana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
