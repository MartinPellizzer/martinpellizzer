Crataegus pechiana

Crataegus pechiana, commonly known as the Pechian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in native habitats.
