Campanula macrorhiza var. thyrsoides

Campanula macrorhiza var. thyrsoides, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear whorled leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
