Chrysophyllum carolinense

Chrysophyllum carolinense, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has glossy, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically consumed when fully ripe. Its flowers are fragrant and white, with five petals, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The starfruit is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
