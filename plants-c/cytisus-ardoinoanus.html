Cytisus ardoinoanus

Cytisus ardoinoanus, commonly known as the Madagascar broom, is a flowering shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it requires careful cultivation due to its specific environmental needs.
