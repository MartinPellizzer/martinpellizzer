Cyperus infucatus

Cyperus infucatus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered purple flowers.

Cyperus infucatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
