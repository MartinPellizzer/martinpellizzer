Castanea crenata f. pendula

Castanea crenata f. pendula, commonly known as the Japanese horse chestnut or weeping Japanese chestnut, is a unique variant of the chestnut genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and elongated, drooping leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that enclose edible nuts, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptability to various soil types.
