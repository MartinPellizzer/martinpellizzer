Chenopodium sanctae-clarae

Chenopodium sanctae-clarae, commonly known as Santa Clara goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Chenopodium sanctae-clarae is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
