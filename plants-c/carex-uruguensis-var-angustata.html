Carex uruguensis var. angustata

Carex uruguensis var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its reproductive success.

The narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
