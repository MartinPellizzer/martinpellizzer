Carex glauca var. typica

Carex glauca var. typica, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wetland habitats.
