Combretum viscosum

Combretum viscosum, commonly known as sticky combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, often sticky bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Combretum viscosum is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
