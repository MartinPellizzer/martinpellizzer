Coaxana purpurea

Coaxana purpurea, commonly known as the purple wax plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Coaxana purpurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and drought tolerance.
