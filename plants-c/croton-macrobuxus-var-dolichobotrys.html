Croton macrobuxus var. dolichobotrys

Croton macrobuxus var. dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-stalked croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
