Calytrix sagei

Calytrix sagei, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Calytrix sagei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
