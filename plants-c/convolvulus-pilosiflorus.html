Convolvulus pilosiflorus

Convolvulus pilosiflorus, commonly known as the hairyflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, hairy petals that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
