Cathartocarpus erubescens

Cathartocarpus erubescens, commonly known as the redseed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright red seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value comes primarily from its vibrant seed color, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
