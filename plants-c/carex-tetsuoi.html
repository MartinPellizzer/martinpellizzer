Carex tetsuoi

Carex tetsuoi, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex tetsuoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shady, wet environments.
