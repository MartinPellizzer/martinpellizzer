Cassia mystacicarpa

Cassia mystacicarpa, commonly known as the Indian cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Cassia mystacicarpa is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments and skin conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
