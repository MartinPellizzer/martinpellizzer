Capparis kanehirae

Capparis kanehirae, commonly known as the Japanese caper, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
