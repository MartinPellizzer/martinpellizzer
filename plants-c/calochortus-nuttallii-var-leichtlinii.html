Calochortus nuttallii var. leichtlinii

Calochortus nuttallii var. leichtlinii, commonly known as the Leichtlin's mariposa lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

This variety is noted for its delicate beauty and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
