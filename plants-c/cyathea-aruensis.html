Cyathea aruensis

Cyathea aruensis, commonly known as the Aru tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cyathea aruensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various tropical organisms.
