Crepis kotschyana

Crepis kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.

Crepis kotschyana is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
