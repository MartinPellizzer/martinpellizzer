Cyanus novakii

Cyanus novakii, commonly known as the Novak's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cyanus novakii typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
