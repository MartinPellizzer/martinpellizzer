Canthium subaureum

Canthium subaureum, commonly known as golden-seeded spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small yellowish flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.
