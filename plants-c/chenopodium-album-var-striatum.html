Chenopodium album var. striatum

Chenopodium album var. striatum, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive narrow, ribbed appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in various environments.
