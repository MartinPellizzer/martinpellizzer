Cirsium fruticosum

Cirsium fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its stems are often woody and creeping.

Cirsium fruticosum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
