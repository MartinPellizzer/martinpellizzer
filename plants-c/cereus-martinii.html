Cereus martinii

Cereus martinii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are known for their brief but spectacular display, often lasting only a few hours.

Cereus martinii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
