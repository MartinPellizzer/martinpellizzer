Caloseris rupestris

Caloseris rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of blue-gray leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent yellow centers. It is often found in rocky outcrops and is well-suited to drought conditions.

Caloseris rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in xerophytic gardens.
