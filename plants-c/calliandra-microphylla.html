Calliandra microphylla

Calliandra microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous tiny leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Calliandra microphylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
