Cucubalus baccatus

Cucubalus baccatus, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that give way to black, dark red, or white berries. The plant is part of the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties. Its berries contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
