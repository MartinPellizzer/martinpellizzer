Caesalpinia wrightiana

Caesalpinia wrightiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
