Cassia macdougaliana

Cassia macdougaliana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Cassia macdougaliana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is a symbol of Australian national identity.
