Citrus × kinokuni

Citrus × kinokuni, commonly known as the Kishu mandarin.

This hybrid citrus fruit is a cross between the mandarin orange and the kumquat, resulting in a small, oval-shaped fruit with a thin, loose rind. The fruit typically weighs between 30 to 50 grams and has a sweet, tangy flavor with a high juice content. It is native to Japan and thrives in subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is evergreen, featuring glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
