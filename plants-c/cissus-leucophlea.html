Cissus leucophlea

Cissus leucophlea, commonly known as the silver-leafed creeper, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly silver or white in color, with a green margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus leucophlea is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
