Cocos flexuosa var. densiflora

Cocos flexuosa var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered coconut palm, is a variant of the coconut palm native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, tall stature and clustered inflorescences that produce an abundance of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically broad and segmented, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are often densely packed, giving it the "densiflora" designation, which refers to its dense flowering habit.

This variant is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its potential in horticultural and ecological applications.
