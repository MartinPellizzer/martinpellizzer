Crinum australe var. exaltatum

Crinum australe var. exaltatum, commonly known as the tall rainforest lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Crinum australe var. exaltatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the biodiversity of Australian rainforests.
