Croton argenteus

Croton argenteus, commonly known as silver croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
