Camaridium amabile

Camaridium amabile, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their subtle, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and moths.
