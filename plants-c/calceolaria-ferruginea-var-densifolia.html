Calceolaria ferruginea var. densifolia

Calceolaria ferruginea var. densifolia, commonly known as the rusty calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy leaves and showy, hooded flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
