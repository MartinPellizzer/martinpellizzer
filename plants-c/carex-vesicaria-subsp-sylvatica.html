Carex vesicaria subsp. sylvatica

Carex vesicaria subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.
