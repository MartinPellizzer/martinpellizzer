Cyperus michelianus subsp. michelianus

Cyperus michelianus subsp.

michelianus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
