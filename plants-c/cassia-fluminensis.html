Cassia fluminensis

Cassia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Cassia fluminensis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
