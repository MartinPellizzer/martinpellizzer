Campanula cochleariifolia f. tubulosa

Campanula cochleariifolia f. tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, tubular-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
