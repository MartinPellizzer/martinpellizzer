Cynoctonum crassifolium

Cynoctonum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom during the summer months and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates.
