Calliandra viscidula var. leucantha

Calliandra viscidula var. leucantha, commonly known as the white calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that produce clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
