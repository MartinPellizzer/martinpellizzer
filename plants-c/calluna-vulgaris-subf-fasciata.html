Calluna vulgaris subf. fasciata

Calluna vulgaris subf.

fasciata, commonly known as the variegated heather, is a botanical variant of the common heather. It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow stripes on its leaves.

This subform is native to parts of Europe and is often found in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in sunny, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
