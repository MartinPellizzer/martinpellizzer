Carex cernua

Carex cernua, commonly known as the nodding sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that droop downward, giving it its common name. Carex cernua thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
