Commelina communis f. minor

Commelina communis f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy sheen and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological landscapes.
