Calluna vulgaris subf. extensa

Calluna vulgaris subf.

extensa, commonly known as the extended heather, is a subshrub native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

This plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
