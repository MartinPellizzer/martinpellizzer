Crepidomanes pyxidiferum var. australe

Crepidomanes pyxidiferum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a shield shape. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This species is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a common sight in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
