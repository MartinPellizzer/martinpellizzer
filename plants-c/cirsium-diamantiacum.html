Cirsium diamantiacum

Cirsium diamantiacum, commonly known as the diamondweed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cirsium diamantiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
