Cryptandra polyclada subsp. aequabilis

Cryptandra polyclada subsp.

aequabilis, commonly known as the red-flowered cryptandra, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
