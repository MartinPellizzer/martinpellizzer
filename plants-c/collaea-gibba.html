Collaea gibba

Collaea gibba, commonly known as the knobbed rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Collaea gibba is adapted to withstand periods of drought, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
