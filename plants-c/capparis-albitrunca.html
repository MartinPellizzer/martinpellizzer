Capparis albitrunca

Capparis albitrunca, commonly known as the white-stemmed caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green to white stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Capparaceae family.
