Crocus chrysanthus var. citrinus

Crocus chrysanthus var. citrinus, commonly known as the citron crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Iridaceae family.

The citron crocus is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of saffron-like threads.
