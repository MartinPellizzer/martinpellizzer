Centaurea aegusae

Centaurea aegusae, commonly known as the Alaskan goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in cold climates with short growing seasons. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea aegusae is also known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
