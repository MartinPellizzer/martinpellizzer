Carex pediformis subsp. macroura

Carex pediformis subsp.

macroura, commonly known as the long-stemmed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their cylindrical, often three-ranked leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
