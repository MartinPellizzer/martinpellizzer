Crocus moesiacus f. brunnescens

Crocus moesiacus f. brunnescens, commonly known as the Brunneous Crocus, is a distinctive variety of the Moesiacus crocus.

It is characterized by its deep brownish-red flowers, which contrast with its dark green, strap-like foliage. This plant is native to the Balkan region and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. It typically blooms in late autumn to early winter, making it a notable addition to autumn gardens.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique coloration.
