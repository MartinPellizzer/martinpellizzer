Cordia trichotoma

Cordia trichotoma, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cordia trichotoma is often found in dry, open woodlands and is adapted to arid conditions.
