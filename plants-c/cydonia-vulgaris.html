Cydonia vulgaris

Cydonia vulgaris, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces a fragrant, yellowish fruit that ripens in late autumn. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is often used in cooking and has a distinctive, aromatic flavor when cooked.
