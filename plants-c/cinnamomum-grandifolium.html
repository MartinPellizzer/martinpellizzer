Cinnamomum grandifolium

Cinnamomum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by ellipsoid berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy scent.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
