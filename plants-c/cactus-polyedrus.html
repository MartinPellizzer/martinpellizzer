Cactus polyedrus

Cactus polyedrus, commonly known as the star cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped spines and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment. Its adaptations to harsh desert conditions include a slow growth rate and the ability to survive long periods without rain.

Cactus polyedrus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
