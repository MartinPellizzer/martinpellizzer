Cardiospermum truncatum

Cardiospermum truncatum, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, typically blue or purple blooms that open in the morning and close at night. Its stems are trailing or climbing, often forming dense mats or vines.
