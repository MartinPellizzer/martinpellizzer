Citrullus anguria

Citrullus anguria, commonly known as watermelon, is a vigorous vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, lobed leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that are both male and female, with the female flowers developing into the familiar bright red or yellow fruits. The fruit is renowned for its high water content and sweet, juicy flesh, which contains numerous seeds.

Botanically, Citrullus anguria is an important species in both agriculture and horticulture due to its nutritional value and adaptability to various climates.
