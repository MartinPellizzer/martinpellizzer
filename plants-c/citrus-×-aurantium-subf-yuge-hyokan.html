Citrus × aurantium subf. yuge-hyokan

Citrus × aurantium subf.

yuge-hyokan, commonly known as the Yuge-hyokan mandarin, is a citrus hybrid originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round, and aromatic fruits with a thin, smooth rind.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is prized for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The subsp.

yuge-hyokan is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
