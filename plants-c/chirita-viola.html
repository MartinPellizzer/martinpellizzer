Chirita viola

Chirita viola, commonly known as the violet viola, is a member of the Violaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves and small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chirita viola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
