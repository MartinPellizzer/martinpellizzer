Chrysothamnus parryi var. asper

Chrysothamnus parryi var. asper, commonly known as the rough goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species adapted to harsh desert conditions.
