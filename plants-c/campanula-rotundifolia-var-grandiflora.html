Campanula rotundifolia var. grandiflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rounded, lobed leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas, stream banks, and moist meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
