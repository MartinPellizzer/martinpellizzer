Calodracon terminalis

Calodracon terminalis, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has strap-like leaves that are long and narrow, often with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Calodracon terminalis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
