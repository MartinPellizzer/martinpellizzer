Cyphostemma keniense

Cyphostemma keniense, commonly known as the Kenyan moonvine, is a climbing plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It is often found in rocky or arid habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cyphostemma keniense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
