Callisia silvatica

Callisia silvatica, commonly known as the forest spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Callisia silvatica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
