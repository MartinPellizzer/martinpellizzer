Canella obtusifolia

Canella obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed canella, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Canella obtusifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
