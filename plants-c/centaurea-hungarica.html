Centaurea hungarica

Centaurea hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Centaurea genus.
