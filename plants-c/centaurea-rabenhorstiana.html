Centaurea rabenhorstiana

Centaurea rabenhorstiana, commonly known as the Rabenhorst's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its bright blue to purple flowers. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea rabenhorstiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
