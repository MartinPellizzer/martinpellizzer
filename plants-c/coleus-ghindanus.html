Coleus ghindanus

Coleus ghindanus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and striking shades of red, purple, and green. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
