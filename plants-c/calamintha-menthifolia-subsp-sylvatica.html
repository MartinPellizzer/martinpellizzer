Calamintha menthifolia subsp. sylvatica

Calamintha menthifolia subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as woodland calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often along stream banks or in forest understories.

The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves with a mint-like scent, and its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
