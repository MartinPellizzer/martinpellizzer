Clematis laxistrigosa

Clematis laxistrigosa, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, often climbing supports with its tendrils. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Clematis laxistrigosa thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
