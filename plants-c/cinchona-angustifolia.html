Cinchona angustifolia

Cinchona angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds, and it is well-known for its medicinal properties, particularly the alkaloid quinine.
