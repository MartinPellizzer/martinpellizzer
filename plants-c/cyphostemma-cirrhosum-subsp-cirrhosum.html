Cyphostemma cirrhosum subsp. cirrhosum

Cyphostemma cirrhosum subsp.

cirrhosum, commonly known as the Desert Trumpet, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The plant produces long, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are small and often reduced to spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
