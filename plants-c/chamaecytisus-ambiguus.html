Chamaecytisus ambiguus

Chamaecytisus ambiguus, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its woody stems are often twisted and covered in small, scale-like structures, giving it a unique appearance.

Sweet shrub is valued in landscaping for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
