Campanopsis linarioides var. longifolia

Campanopsis linarioides var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
