Ceanothus milleri

Ceanothus milleri, commonly known as the Santa Barbara ceanothus, is a shrub native to California and Baja California.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue to violet flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Ceanothus milleri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
