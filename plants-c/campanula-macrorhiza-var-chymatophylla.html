Campanula macrorhiza var. chymatophylla

Campanula macrorhiza var. chymatophylla, commonly known as the broadroot bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
