Commiphora mombassensis

Commiphora mombassensis, commonly known as the Mombassa myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a yellowish resin, which has been historically used for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
