Corylus sieboldiana var. mandshurica

Corylus sieboldiana var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkins in early spring, followed by edible nuts encased in a spiny husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
