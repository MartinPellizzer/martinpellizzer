Cousinia fascicularis

Cousinia fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are basal and linear, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

Cousinia fascicularis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
