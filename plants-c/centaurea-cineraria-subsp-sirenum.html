Centaurea cineraria subsp. sirenum

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

sirenum, commonly known as the Siren's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its structure includes upright stems with spiny leaves, and it produces small, bract-like leaves at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
