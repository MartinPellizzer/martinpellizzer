Cyperus aschenbornianus

Cyperus aschenbornianus, commonly known as the aschenbornian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Cyperus aschenbornianus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.
