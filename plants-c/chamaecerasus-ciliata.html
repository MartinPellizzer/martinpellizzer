Chamaecerasus ciliata

Chamaecerasus ciliata, commonly known as the downy cherry or the hairy cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, often arching branches covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn reddish in the autumn.

The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.
