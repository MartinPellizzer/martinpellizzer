Caladium bicolor f. poecile

Caladium bicolor f. poecile, commonly known as the "Peacock Caladium," is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, mottled foliage.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with intricate patterns of red, pink, and white, creating a visually appealing display. It is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant prefers well-draining soil and partial to full shade, making it ideal for garden borders or container planting.

Its foliage remains decorative throughout the growing season, adding color and texture to landscapes.
