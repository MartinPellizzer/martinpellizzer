Cirsium variabile

Cirsium variabile, commonly known as the variable thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium variabile is often found in the eastern and central United States.
