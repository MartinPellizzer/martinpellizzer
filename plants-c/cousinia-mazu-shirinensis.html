Cousinia mazu-shirinensis

Cousinia mazu-shirinensis, commonly known as the Persian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, featuring a deep root system and silver-green foliage to reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppes and deserts.

Cousinia mazu-shirinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
