Callicarpa candicans var. integrifolia

Callicarpa candicans var. integrifolia, commonly known as the red-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with smooth edges, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
