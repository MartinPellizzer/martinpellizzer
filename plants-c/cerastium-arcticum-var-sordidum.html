Cerastium arcticum var. sordidum

Cerastium arcticum var. sordidum, commonly known as the sordid mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its compact growth form helps it withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
