Carex peliosanthifolia

Carex peliosanthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are usually found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex peliosanthifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
