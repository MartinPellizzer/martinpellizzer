Chaetocalyx fissus

Chaetocalyx fissus, commonly known as the fissured calyx tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fissured calyx that remains attached to the fruit even after maturity. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.
