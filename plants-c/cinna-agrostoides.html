Cinna agrostoides

Cinna agrostoides, commonly known as the grass-like fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for true grasses, but it possesses the characteristic fronds of a fern. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cinna agrostoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
