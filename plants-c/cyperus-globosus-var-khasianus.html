Cyperus globosus var. khasianus

Cyperus globosus var. khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi sedge, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
