Cochlearia conwayi

Cochlearia conwayi, commonly known as Conway's cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are typically lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cochlearia conwayi is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
