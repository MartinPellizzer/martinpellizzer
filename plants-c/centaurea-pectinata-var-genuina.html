Centaurea pectinata var. genuina

Centaurea pectinata var. genuina, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
