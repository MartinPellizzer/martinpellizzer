Carex costata

Carex costata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex costata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
