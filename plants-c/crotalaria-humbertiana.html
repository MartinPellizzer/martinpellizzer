Crotalaria humbertiana

Crotalaria humbertiana, commonly known as Humbert's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moist conditions.

Crotalaria humbertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
