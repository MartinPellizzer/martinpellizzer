Conophytum leucanthum

Conophytum leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, segmented leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
