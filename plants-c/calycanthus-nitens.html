Calycanthus nitens

Calycanthus nitens, commonly known as the white sand calycanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Calycanthus nitens thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for garden landscapes.
