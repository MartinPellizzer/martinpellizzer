Centaurea tenuifolia f. pinae

Centaurea tenuifolia f. pinae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. This subspecies is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
