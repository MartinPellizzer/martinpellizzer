Cryptocarya polyneura

Cryptocarya polyneura, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Cryptocarya polyneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
