Cyanus lanigerus

Cyanus lanigerus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has spiny leaves and a robust stem, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Cyanus lanigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
