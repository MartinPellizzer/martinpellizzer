Carex stenostachys var. stenostachys

Carex stenostachys var. stenostachys, commonly known as narrow-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The leaves are usually narrow and have a distinctive, grass-like appearance, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
