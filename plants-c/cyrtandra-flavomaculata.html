Cyrtandra flavomaculata

Cyrtandra flavomaculata, commonly known as the yellow-spotted heliconia, is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a spiral of flowers, known as a spadix, enclosed within a leaf-like bract. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with distinctive spots. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
