Cryptomeria japonica var. dacrydioides

Cryptomeria japonica var. dacrydioides, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or sometimes referred to as the dwarf cryptomeria, is a variant of the Japanese cedar native to Japan.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and small, scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

This variety is adapted to a range of soil types and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
