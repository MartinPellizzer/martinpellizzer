Chaetaria gigantea

Chaetaria gigantea, commonly known as the giant hairgrass, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of long, feathery leaves that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires specific care to flourish.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and related plants.
