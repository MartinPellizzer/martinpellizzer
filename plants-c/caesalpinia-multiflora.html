Caesalpinia multiflora

Caesalpinia multiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange feathery flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia multiflora make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
