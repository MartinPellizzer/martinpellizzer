Cardamine borbonica

Cardamine borbonica, commonly known as the Alpine watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small white or greenish flowers.

Cardamine borbonica is valued for its delicate flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisines or as a decorative plant in gardens.
