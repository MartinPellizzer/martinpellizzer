Canavalia obtusifolia

Canavalia obtusifolia, commonly known as the horse gram or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Canavalia obtusifolia also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
