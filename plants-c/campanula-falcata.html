Campanula falcata

Campanula falcata, commonly known as the sickle bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and bloom in late summer.

Campanula falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal garden conditions.
