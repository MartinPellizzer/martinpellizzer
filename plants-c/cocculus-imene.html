Cocculus imene

Cocculus imene, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue or black berries. Its fruits contain a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp.

Cocculus imene is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
