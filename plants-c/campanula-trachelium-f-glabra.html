Campanula trachelium f. glabra

Campanula trachelium f. glabra, commonly known as the naked bellflower, is a variant of the common campanula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The flowers are bell-shaped, typically ranging in color from blue to violet, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This subspecies is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland areas and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
