Cadaba virgata

Cadaba virgata, commonly known as the African rose or red bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or red flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
