Capsella bursa-pastoris lingulata

Capsella bursa-pastoris lingulata, commonly known as the heartweed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions worldwide. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small white or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, contributing to its role in ecological and agricultural contexts.
