Cantua sinuata

Cantua sinuata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers feature a distinctive fringed appearance around the corolla.

Cantua sinuata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
