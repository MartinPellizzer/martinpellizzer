Calamagrostis pilosa

Calamagrostis pilosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
