Centaurea verutum

Centaurea verutum, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Centaurea verutum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
