Cariniana multiflora

Cariniana multiflora, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing multiple seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These nuts are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and protein.

Cariniana multiflora plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
