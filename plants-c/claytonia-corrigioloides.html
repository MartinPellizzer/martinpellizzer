Claytonia corrigioloides

Claytonia corrigioloides, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, showy pink or white flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Claytonia corrigioloides is adapted to arid environments and is often found in alpine or desert regions.
