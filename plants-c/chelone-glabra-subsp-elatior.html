Chelone glabra subsp. elatior

Chelone glabra subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the white hound's tongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features distinctive, tongue-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and attractive flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
