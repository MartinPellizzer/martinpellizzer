Corylus hasibani

Corylus hasibani, commonly known as the Mongolian hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Corylaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clustered, edible nuts. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold climates. Its nuts are encased in a thick, woody husk and are an important food source for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
