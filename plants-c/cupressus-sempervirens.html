Cupressus sempervirens

Cupressus sempervirens, commonly known as the Mediterranean cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features scale-like leaves that are densely packed on its branches, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its visual character.

Cupressus sempervirens is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
