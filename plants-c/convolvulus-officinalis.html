Convolvulus officinalis

Convolvulus officinalis, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth or hairy surface, depending on the variety.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated areas.
