Carex michelii var. ultra

Carex michelii var. ultra, commonly known as the Micheli's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its small, clustered flowers appear in summer, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Carex michelii var. ultra is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
