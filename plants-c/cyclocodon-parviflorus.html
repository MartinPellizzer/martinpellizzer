Cyclocodon parviflorus

Cyclocodon parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered cyclocodon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Cyclocodon parviflorus prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
