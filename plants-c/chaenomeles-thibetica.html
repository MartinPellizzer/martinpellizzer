Chaenomeles thibetica

Chaenomeles thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces showy, orange-red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for temperate climates.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
