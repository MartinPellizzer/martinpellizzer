Cicerbita borealis

Cicerbita borealis, commonly known as the northern bittercress, is a herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Cicerbita borealis is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants.
