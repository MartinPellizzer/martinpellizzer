Carex yangii

Carex yangii, commonly known as Yang's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive three-angled berries.

Carex yangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
