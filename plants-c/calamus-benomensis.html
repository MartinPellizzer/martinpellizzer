Calamus benomensis

Calamus benomensis, commonly known as the sacred reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the stem.

The plant has cultural significance in traditional medicine and is sometimes used in ritualistic practices.
