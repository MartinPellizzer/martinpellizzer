Carex agglomerata var. rhizomata

Carex agglomerata var. rhizomata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. It has rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical structure includes narrow, strap-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.
