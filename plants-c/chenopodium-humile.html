Chenopodium humile

Chenopodium humile, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into clusters of dry, triangular seeds. It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient weed and a nutritious edible plant.
