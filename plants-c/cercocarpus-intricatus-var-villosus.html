Cercocarpus intricatus var. villosus

Cercocarpus intricatus var. villosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
