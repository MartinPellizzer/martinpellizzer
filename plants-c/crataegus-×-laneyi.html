Crataegus × laneyi

Crataegus × laneyi, commonly known as the Laney hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant features thorned branches, oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red or orange pome that ripens in the autumn, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The Laney hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
