Calamus nagbettai

Calamus nagbettai, commonly known as the Nagbettai reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and robust rhizomes. The plant thrives in moist, swampy habitats and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.

The botanical features of Calamus nagbettai make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
