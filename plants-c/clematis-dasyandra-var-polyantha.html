Clematis dasyandra var. polyantha

Clematis dasyandra var. polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, showy form. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible when ripe but are often consumed by birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
