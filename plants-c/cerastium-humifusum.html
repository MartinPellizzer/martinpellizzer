Cerastium humifusum

Cerastium humifusum, commonly known as the prostrate mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with small, rounded leaves and white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
