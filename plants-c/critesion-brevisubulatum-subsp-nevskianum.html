Critesion brevisubulatum subsp. nevskianum

Critesion brevisubulatum subsp.

nevskianum, commonly known as the Nevskean dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It is often found at high altitudes, where it tolerates harsh climatic conditions.
