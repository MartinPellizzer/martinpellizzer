Capparis fusifera

Capparis fusifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and has small, often spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Capparis fusifera is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
