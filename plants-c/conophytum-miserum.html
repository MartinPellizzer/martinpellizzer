Conophytum miserum

Conophytum miserum, commonly known as the "miserable conophytum," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
