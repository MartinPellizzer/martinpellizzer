Cissus pisocarpa

Cissus pisocarpa, commonly known as the African snowball vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that are often mistaken for vegetables due to their appearance. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Cissus pisocarpa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.
