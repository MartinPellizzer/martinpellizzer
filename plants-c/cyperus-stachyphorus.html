Cyperus stachyphorus

Cyperus stachyphorus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features tall, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump. Its inflorescence consists of a single, umbrella-shaped cluster of spikelets, giving it its common name.

Cyperus stachyphorus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet or moist conditions.
