Chenopodium papulosum

Chenopodium papulosum, commonly known as rough goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered green flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Chenopodium papulosum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
