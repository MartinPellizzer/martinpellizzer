Ceropegia luzhiensis

Ceropegia luzhiensis, commonly known as the Luzhi's arrowroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its succulent, tuberous stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and rock gardens.
