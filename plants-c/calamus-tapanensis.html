Calamus tapanensis

Calamus tapanensis, commonly known as Tapan reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Calamus tapanensis thrives in wet, marshy environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
