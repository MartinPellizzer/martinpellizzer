Chiogenes hispidula var. japonica

Chiogenes hispidula var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
