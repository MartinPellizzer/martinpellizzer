Creusa tetragona subsp. lignescens

Creusa tetragona subsp.

lignescens, commonly known as the woody creusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and edible plants.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust, woody stems that become more pronounced with age. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive silvery underside that reflects light.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and is often found in dry, calcareous soils.
