Cladium flexuosum var. polyanthemum

Cladium flexuosum var. polyanthemum, commonly known as the purple sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, violet to purple flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in inflorescences that resemble spikes. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in water-saturated environments.

This species is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
