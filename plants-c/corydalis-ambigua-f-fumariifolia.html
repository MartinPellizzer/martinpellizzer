Corydalis ambigua f. fumariifolia

Corydalis ambigua f. fumariifolia, commonly known as the smokeleaf corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae, which is characterized by its delicate, often blue or purple flowers. The foliage of this plant is distinctive, with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a smoky or bluish hue, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
