Campanula pseudolanceolata subsp. semiamplexicaulis

Campanula pseudolanceolata subsp.

semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the semi-amplexicaulis bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features lanceolate leaves that are semi-amplexicaul, meaning they partially clasping the stem.

The plant produces blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
