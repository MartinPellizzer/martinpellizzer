Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp.

szepesfalvyi, commonly known as the Szepesfalvy hawthorn, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its rounded, thornless shoots and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces red berries that are typically edible and attract various bird species. It is native to Hungary and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
