Clematis flammulastroides

Clematis flammulastroides, commonly known as the flame-leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 20 feet in length, supporting its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are often adorned with a distinctive red or orange center. Its foliage is characterized by heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a striking, flame-like appearance.

Clematis flammulastroides thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
