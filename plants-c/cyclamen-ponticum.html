Cyclamen ponticum

Cyclamen ponticum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms. Cyclamen ponticum has a tuberous root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticultural settings.
