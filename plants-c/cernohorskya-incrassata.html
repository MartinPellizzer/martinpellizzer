Cernohorskya incrassata

Cernohorskya incrassata, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Cernohorskya incrassata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
