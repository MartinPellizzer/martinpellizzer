Caladenia patersonii var. suaveolens

Caladenia patersonii var. suaveolens, commonly known as the Sweet-scented Spider-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spider-like appearance with a single, elongated flower that often displays a vivid pink or red hue. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant scent, which plays a crucial role in attracting its primary pollinators, such as thynnid wasps. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats, preferring full sun exposure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
