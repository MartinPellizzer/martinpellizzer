Carex lagopodioides var. glomerata

Carex lagopodioides var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
