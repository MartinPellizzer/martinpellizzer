Caesalpinia calycina

Caesalpinia calycina, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Caesalpinia calycina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
