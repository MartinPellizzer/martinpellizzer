Calystegia malacophylla var. berryi

Calystegia malacophylla var. berryi, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and intense sunlight.
