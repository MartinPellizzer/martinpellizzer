Chenopodium conardii

Chenopodium conardii, commonly known as Navajo spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used by Native American communities for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Chenopodium conardii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions.
