Cassia conjugata

Cassia conjugata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa, the Indian subcontinent, and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The flowers are followed by long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia conjugata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
