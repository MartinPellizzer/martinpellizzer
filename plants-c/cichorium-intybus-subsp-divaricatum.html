Cichorium intybus subsp. divaricatum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in various culinary applications.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.
