Centaurea toletana var. cavanillesiana

Centaurea toletana var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Toletan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, violet-blue flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is closely related to other Centaurea species and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
