Ceterach canariensis

Ceterach canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock samphire, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant has narrow, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months.

Ceterach canariensis is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a characteristic species of coastal vegetation.
