Ceropegia sulcata

Ceropegia sulcata, commonly known as the string of pearls, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thin, twisting stems that bear small, waxy leaves resembling beads or pearls. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
