Crataegus fulleriana var. miranda

Crataegus fulleriana var. miranda, commonly known as the Miranda hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

The Miranda hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
