Cactus leucodasys

Cactus leucodasys, commonly known as the white-tipped cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted for quick water absorption.

The cactus leucodasys is often used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
