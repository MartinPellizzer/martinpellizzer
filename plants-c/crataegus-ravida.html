Crataegus ravida

Crataegus ravida, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, swamps, and stream banks. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Crataegus ravida is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
