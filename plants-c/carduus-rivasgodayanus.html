Carduus rivasgodayanus

Carduus rivasgodayanus, commonly known as the Rivasgodayan thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly Spain, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a tall, flowering stem with several flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
