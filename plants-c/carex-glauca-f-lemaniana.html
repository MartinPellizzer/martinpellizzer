Carex glauca f. lemaniana

Carex glauca f. lemaniana, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a variant of the common sedge, Carex glauca, characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, acidic soils in woodland and wetland habitats. The plant forms dense clumps of grass-like leaves, with slender, cylindrical stems that produce small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. It is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
