Calamaria lechleri

Calamaria lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and is known for its latex-producing properties. The plant's bark contains a milky latex that has been historically used for making rubber. Calamaria lechleri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a source of natural rubber and its contribution to biodiversity in the rainforest.
