Crataegus oxyacantha var. gumpperi-bicolor

Crataegus oxyacantha var. gumpperi-bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored leaves, which typically display a striking contrast between green and white or silver. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
