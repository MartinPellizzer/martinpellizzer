Crepis bulbosa f. willkommii

Crepis bulbosa f. willkommii, commonly known as the bulbous hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, typical of the genus Crepis. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

This form is distinguished by its bulbous base and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
