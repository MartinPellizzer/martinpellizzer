Campanula saxifragoides var. latifolia

Campanula saxifragoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
