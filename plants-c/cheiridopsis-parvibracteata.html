Cheiridopsis parvibracteata

Cheiridopsis parvibracteata, commonly known as the small-bract carpetweed, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
