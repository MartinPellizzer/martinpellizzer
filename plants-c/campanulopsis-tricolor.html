Campanulopsis tricolor

Campanulopsis tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, three-colored flowers that range from white to pink and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Campanulopsis tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
