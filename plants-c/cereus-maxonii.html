Cereus maxonii

Cereus maxonii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique appearance, with a vividly colored upper portion and a green, stem-like base. The plant produces night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements and striking visual contrast.

The base of the plant, known as the "pseudostem," is actually a grafted segment that allows the plant to thrive in various conditions.
