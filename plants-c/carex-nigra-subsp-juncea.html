Carex nigra subsp. juncea

Carex nigra subsp.

juncea, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers, which are often hidden among the foliage.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
