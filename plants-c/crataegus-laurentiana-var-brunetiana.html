Crataegus laurentiana var. brunetiana

Crataegus laurentiana var. brunetiana, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small dark green leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries known as sloes, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
