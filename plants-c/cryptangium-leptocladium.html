Cryptangium leptocladium

Cryptangium leptocladium, commonly known as the slender-lobed cryptangium, is a member of the Proteaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often ribbed fruiting structures. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to the dry conditions of its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological adaptations.
