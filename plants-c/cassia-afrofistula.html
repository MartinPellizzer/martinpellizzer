Cassia afrofistula

Cassia afrofistula, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
