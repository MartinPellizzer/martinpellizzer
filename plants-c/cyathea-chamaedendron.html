Cyathea chamaedendron

Cyathea chamaedendron, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

Cyathea chamaedendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
