Calamus calapparius

Calamus calapparius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often arched or curved. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

Calamus calapparius is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic rhizomes.
