Cymbidium latifolium

Cymbidium latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the winter months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Cymbidium latifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
