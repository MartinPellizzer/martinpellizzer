Cayaponia reticulata

Cayaponia reticulata, commonly known as the spider plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Cayaponia reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
