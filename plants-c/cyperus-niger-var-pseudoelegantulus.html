Cyperus niger var. pseudoelegantulus

Cyperus niger var. pseudoelegantulus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its root system is fibrous and often forms dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate contaminated soils.
