Commelina appendiculata

Commelina appendiculata, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, three-lobed bracts that enclose its inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
