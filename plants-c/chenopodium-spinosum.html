Chenopodium spinosum

Chenopodium spinosum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-angled capsule that contains several seeds.

Chenopodium spinosum is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
