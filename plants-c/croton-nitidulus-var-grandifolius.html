Croton nitidulus var. grandifolius

Croton nitidulus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the grandifolius croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
