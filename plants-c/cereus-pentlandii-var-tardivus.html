Cereus pentlandii var. tardivus

Cereus pentlandii var. tardivus, commonly known as the late bloomer cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. Its stems are covered in sparse, white spines, and it is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions.

This variety is known for its slow growth and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
