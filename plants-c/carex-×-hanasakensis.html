Carex × hanasakensis

Carex × hanasakensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant species within the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often a bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
