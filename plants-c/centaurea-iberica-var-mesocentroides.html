Centaurea iberica var. mesocentroides

Centaurea iberica var. mesocentroides, commonly known as the Iberian centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

This variety is distinguished by its central disc florets and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
