Cynara pusilla

Cynara pusilla, commonly known as the small globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually spiny and have a rosette growth pattern, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Cynara pusilla is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
