Cleistochloa sclerachne

Cleistochloa sclerachne, commonly known as the rigid hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically enclosed within leaf sheaths, a feature common in many grasses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Cleistochloa sclerachne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
