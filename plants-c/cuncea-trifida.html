Cuncea trifida

Cuncea trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often tinged with purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Cuncea trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
