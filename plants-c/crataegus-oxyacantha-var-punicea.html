Crataegus oxyacantha var. punicea

Crataegus oxyacantha var. punicea, commonly known as the red-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.

The variety is noted for its vibrant floral display and ornamental value in gardens.
