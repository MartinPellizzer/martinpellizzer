Carex × palezieuxii

Carex × palezieuxii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex appropria and Carex vernifica, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
