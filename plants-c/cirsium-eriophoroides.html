Cirsium eriophoroides

Cirsium eriophoroides, commonly known as the prairie thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, showy purple or blue composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a waxy coating.

Cirsium eriophoroides plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
