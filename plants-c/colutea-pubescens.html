Colutea pubescens

Colutea pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Colutea pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its distinctive foliage and delicate blooms.
