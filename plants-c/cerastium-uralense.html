Cerastium uralense

Cerastium uralense, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium uralense thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes.
