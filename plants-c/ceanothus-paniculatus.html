Ceanothus paniculatus

Ceanothus paniculatus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Ceanothus paniculatus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
