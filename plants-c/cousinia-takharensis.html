Cousinia takharensis

Cousinia takharensis, commonly known as the Takharan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in dry climates.

Cousinia takharensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
