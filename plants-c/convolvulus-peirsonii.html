Convolvulus peirsonii

Convolvulus peirsonii, commonly known as the pearson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various arid environments.

Convolvulus peirsonii is often found in rocky areas and along coastal regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
