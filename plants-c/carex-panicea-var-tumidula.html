Carex panicea var. tumidula

Carex panicea var. tumidula, commonly known as the inflated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that are initially pale green and later turn brown, containing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
