Crepidium micholitzianum

Crepidium micholitzianum, commonly known as the Micholitz's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mossy habitats, thriving in cool and humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine zones.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
