Chenopodium graveolens f. rubellum

Chenopodium graveolens f. rubellum, commonly known as red amaranth, is a cultivated variant of the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is native to the Americas and is often grown for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

The species thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
