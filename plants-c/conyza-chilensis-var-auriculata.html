Conyza chilensis var. auriculata

Conyza chilensis var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces and often have auriculate (ear-shaped) lobes at the base. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
