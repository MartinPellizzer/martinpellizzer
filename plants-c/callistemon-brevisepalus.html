Callistemon brevisepalus

Callistemon brevisepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-blue pubescence. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Callistemon brevisepalus is well-suited to coastal and dry environments due to its drought tolerance and salt resistance.
