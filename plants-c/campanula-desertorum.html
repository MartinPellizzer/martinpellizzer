Campanula desertorum

Campanula desertorum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it thrives with minimal water.

Campanula desertorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
