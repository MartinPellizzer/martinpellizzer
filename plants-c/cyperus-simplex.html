Cyperus simplex

Cyperus simplex, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes.

Cyperus simplex is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
