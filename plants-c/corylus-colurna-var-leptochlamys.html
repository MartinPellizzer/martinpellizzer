Corylus colurna var. leptochlamys

Corylus colurna var. leptochlamys, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a variant of the European hazelnut.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in ecological habitats.
