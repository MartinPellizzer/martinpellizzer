Cestrum laevigatum var. collinum

Cestrum laevigatum var. collinum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. These flowers are followed by orange-red berries that contain the seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
