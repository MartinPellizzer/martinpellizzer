Cistus albereensis

Cistus albereensis, commonly known as the Albera rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora.

Cistus albereensis is also valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
