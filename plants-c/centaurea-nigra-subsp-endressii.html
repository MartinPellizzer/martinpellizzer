Centaurea nigra subsp. endressii

Centaurea nigra subsp.

endressii, commonly known as the Blackweed or Purplehead Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracts that resemble petals.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
