Cryptotaenia japonica var. atropurpurea

Cryptotaenia japonica var. atropurpurea, commonly known as Japanese cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive purple hue on the underside, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
