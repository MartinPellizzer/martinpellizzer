Clematis pogonandra var. alata

Clematis pogonandra var. alata, commonly known as the winged clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like bracts that adorn the stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
