Carex tyttholepis

Carex tyttholepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, typically in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
