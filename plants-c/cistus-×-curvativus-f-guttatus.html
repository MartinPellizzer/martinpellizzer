Cistus × curvativus f. guttatus

Cistus × curvativus f. guttatus, commonly known as the glandular rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local flora.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and horticulture.
