Carex woroschilovii

Carex woroschilovii, commonly known as the Woroschilov's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex woroschilovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
