Camellia fansipanensis

Camellia fansipanensis, commonly known as the Fan Mountain camellia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is typically found in subtropical and tropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. Camellia fansipanensis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
