Centropogon cornutus var. angustifolius

Centropogon cornutus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved screw-tongue lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
