Catalpa × hybrida var. purpurea

Catalpa × hybrida var. purpurea, commonly known as the Purple Catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to purple, appearing in late spring. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. It produces long, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
