Carthamus tenuis

Carthamus tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright yellow ray flowers that surround a central disk of tubular florets. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
