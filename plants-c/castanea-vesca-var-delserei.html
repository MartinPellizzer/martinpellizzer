Castanea vesca var. delserei

Castanea vesca var. delserei, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain one or two edible nuts. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
