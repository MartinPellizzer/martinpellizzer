Centaurea debeauxii subsp. nevadensis

Centaurea debeauxii subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada starflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, often in alpine meadows. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
