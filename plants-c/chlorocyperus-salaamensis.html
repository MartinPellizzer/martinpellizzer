Chlorocyperus salaamensis

Chlorocyperus salaamensis, commonly known as the Salamensis flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
