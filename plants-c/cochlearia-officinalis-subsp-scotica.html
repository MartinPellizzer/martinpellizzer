Cochlearia officinalis subsp. scotica

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

scotica, commonly known as Scottish wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles. It produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming from May to July.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and maritime habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
