Cannabis sativa

Cannabis sativa, commonly known as hemp, is a tall, annual plant native to Central Asia.

It features narrow leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in height, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that contain cannabinoid compounds such as THC and CBD. Its fibrous stalks are used for industrial purposes, while its leaves and flowers are utilized in medicinal and recreational contexts.

The botanical characteristics of Cannabis sativa make it a versatile species with a wide range of applications.
