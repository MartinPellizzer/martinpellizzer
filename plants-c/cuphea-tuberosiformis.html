Cuphea tuberosiformis

Cuphea tuberosiformis, commonly known as the tuberous cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tuberous roots and showy, red-orange bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cuphea tuberosiformis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
