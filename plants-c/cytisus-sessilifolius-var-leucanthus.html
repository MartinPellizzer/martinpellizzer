Cytisus sessilifolius var. leucanthus

Cytisus sessilifolius var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, sessile leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
