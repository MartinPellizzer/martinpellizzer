Cardamine spathulata

Cardamine spathulata, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to spatulate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
