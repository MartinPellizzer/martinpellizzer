Cryptanthus warren-loosei

Cryptanthus warren-loosei, commonly known as the pink snowflake, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with shades of pink or red. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to its foliage. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and compact growth make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
