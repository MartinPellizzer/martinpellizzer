Coussapoa ficina

Coussapoa ficina, commonly known as the Brazilian fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, smooth bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
