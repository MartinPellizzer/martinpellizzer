Commelina ehrenbergiana

Commelina ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, erect stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and vibrant addition to their landscape.
