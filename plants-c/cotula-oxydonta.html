Cotula oxydonta

Cotula oxydonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming a daisy-like appearance.

Cotula oxydonta is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a ornamental plant.
