Chassalia pyriformis

Chassalia pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pear-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Chassalia pyriformis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and resilience.
