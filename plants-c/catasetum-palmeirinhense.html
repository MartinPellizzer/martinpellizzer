Catasetum palmeirinhense

Catasetum palmeirinhense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in yellow or orange hues. The plant produces a unique structure called a "catasetum," which is a specialized organ used in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
