Chamaezelum margaritaceum

Chamaezelum margaritaceum, commonly known as the margarita lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and native plant gardens.
