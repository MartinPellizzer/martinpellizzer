Cynanchum rioparanense

Cynanchum rioparanense, commonly known as the Parana milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Parana region.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in wetland or marshy areas. It features showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, and its leaves are opposite and often heart-shaped.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
