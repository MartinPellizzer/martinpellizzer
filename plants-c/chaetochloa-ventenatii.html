Chaetochloa ventenatii

Chaetochloa ventenatii, commonly known as the grasshopper grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in swampy or marshy areas. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with two to three spikelets.

Chaetochloa ventenatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
