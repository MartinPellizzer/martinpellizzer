Cissus crassifolia

Cissus crassifolia, commonly known as the rock fig or elephant foot plant, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale green leaves that are opposite and often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it bears greenish-white fruits that are edible for some animals.

Cissus crassifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
