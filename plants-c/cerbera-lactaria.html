Cerbera lactaria

Cerbera lactaria, commonly known as the milk tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. Its sap is milky and can be toxic, which is why it is sometimes called the poison tree.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
