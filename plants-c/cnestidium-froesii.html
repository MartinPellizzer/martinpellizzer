Cnestidium froesii

Cnestidium froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and savannas. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, alternate leaves, and a composite flower head that attracts various pollinators.

Cnestidium froesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
