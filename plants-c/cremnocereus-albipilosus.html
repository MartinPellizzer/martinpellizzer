Cremnocereus albipilosus

Cremnocereus albipilosus, commonly known as the white-hair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Cremnocereus albipilosus is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
