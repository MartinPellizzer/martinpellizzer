Carex hebecarpa var. ligulata

Carex hebecarpa var. ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikes.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
