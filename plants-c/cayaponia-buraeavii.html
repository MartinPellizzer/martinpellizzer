Cayaponia buraeavii

Cayaponia buraeavii, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cayaponia buraeavii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
