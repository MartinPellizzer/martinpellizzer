Cypripedium × nicholsonianum

Cypripedium × nicholsonianum, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip, which is a hallmark feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in rich, humus-filled soil. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
