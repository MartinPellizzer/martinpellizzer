Cunonia pterophylla

Cunonia pterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cunonia, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cunonia pterophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
