Cyperus eleusinoides var. dinklageanus

Cyperus eleusinoides var. dinklageanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. This variety is native to parts of Africa and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. It typically grows in dense stands and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
