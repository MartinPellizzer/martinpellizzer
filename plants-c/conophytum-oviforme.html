Conophytum oviforme

Conophytum oviforme, commonly known as the oval-shaped rockrose, is a member of the Aizoaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
