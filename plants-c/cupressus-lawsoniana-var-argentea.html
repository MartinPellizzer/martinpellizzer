Cupressus lawsoniana var. argentea

Cupressus lawsoniana var. argentea, commonly known as the silver lace cypress, is a variety of the Lawson cypress native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a narrow, columnar tree with a tall, upright form. Its leaves are scale-like and covered in a silvery-white coating, which contributes to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
