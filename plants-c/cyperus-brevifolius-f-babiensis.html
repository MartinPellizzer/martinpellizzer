Cyperus brevifolius f. babiensis

Cyperus brevifolius f. babiensis, commonly known as the Babian flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to parts of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its botanical features include rhizomes and stolons, which help in spreading and colonizing new areas.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
