Chlorocyperus oostachyus

Chlorocyperus oostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often stand upright, with small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct midrib, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Chlorocyperus oostachyus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
