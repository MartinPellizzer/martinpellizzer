Codanthera glabra

Codanthera glabra, commonly known as the plains larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and a basal rosette growth habit.
