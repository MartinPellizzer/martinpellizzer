Cheiranthus syriacus

Cheiranthus syriacus, commonly known as Syrian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
