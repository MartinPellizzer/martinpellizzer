Copaiba conjugata

Copaiba conjugata, commonly known as yellow copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The tree is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a source of fragrances.
