Carex × alberti

Carex × alberti, commonly known as the Albert's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks, and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to brown in color, and it is often found in temperate regions of North America. Carex × alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to its ecological significance.
