Cerastium kablikianum

Cerastium kablikianum, commonly known as the Kablik's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Cerastium kablikianum is adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
