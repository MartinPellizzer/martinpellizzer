Calophyllum havilandii

Calophyllum havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's torch tree, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and furniture-making.
