Calamagrostis huttoniae

Calamagrostis huttoniae, commonly known as Hutton's reed, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Calamagrostis huttoniae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
