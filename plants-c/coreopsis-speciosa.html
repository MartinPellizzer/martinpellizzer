Coreopsis speciosa

Coreopsis speciosa, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features bright yellow to orange flower heads with surrounding white to light yellow ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis speciosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
