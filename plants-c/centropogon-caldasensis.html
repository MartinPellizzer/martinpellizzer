Centropogon caldasensis

Centropogon caldasensis, commonly known as the Caldasensis flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Centropogon caldasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its showy blooms.
