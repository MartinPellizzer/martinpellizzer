Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. humilis

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. humilis, commonly known as the sticky flower goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and sticky, glandular leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of ecological study.
