Caesalpinia clementis

Caesalpinia clementis, commonly known as the Clement's ticktree, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. Its flowers are bright yellow and arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, often with a distinctive red or orange calyx.

The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.
