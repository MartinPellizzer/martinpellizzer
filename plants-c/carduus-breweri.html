Carduus breweri

Carduus breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's thistle, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The plant's resilience to drought and heat makes it well adapted to its native habitat.
