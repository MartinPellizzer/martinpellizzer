Calycanthus occidentalis

Calycanthus occidentalis, commonly known as the western sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

Calycanthus occidentalis prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens in suitable climates.
