Centaurea acaulis subsp. boissieri

Centaurea acaulis subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows on rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
