Ceratostanthus consolida

Ceratostanthus consolida, commonly known as the purpleheart or bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
