Cyperus orgadophilus

Cyperus orgadophilus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and produce small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.
