Crassocephalum lemuricum

Crassocephalum lemuricum, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, glandular surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Crassocephalum lemuricum is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
