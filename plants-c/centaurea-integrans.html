Centaurea integrans

Centaurea integrans, commonly known as the blueweed or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, grass-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Centaurea integrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
