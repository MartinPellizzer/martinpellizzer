Calamus reinwardtii var. ruptilis

Calamus reinwardtii var. ruptilis, commonly known as the fragrant calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic, with a distinct, pleasant scent. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish-green flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrance and aesthetic qualities.
