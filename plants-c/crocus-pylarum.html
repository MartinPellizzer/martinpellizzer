Crocus pylarum

Crocus pylarum, commonly known as the Pylarum Crocus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, early spring flowers with six to eight petals. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive, prominent central stigma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
