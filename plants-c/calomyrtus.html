Calomyrtus

Calomyrtus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, where it thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and coastal areas. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often pink or white flowers. Calomyrtus species are typically shrubs or small trees, with smooth, sometimes greyish bark.

They play an important role in local ecosystems and are valued for their ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
