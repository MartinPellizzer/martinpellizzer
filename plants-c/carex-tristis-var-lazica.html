Carex tristis var. lazica

Carex tristis var. lazica, commonly known as the Lazica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The Lazica sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
