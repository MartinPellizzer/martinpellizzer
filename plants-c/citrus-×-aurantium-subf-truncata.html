Citrus × aurantium subf.

truncata, commonly known as the citron. This plant is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, thick, and often lobed leaves.

It produces large, yellowish-green fruits that are typically used in culinary and medicinal applications. The fruit has a thick rind and a segmented pulp, and it is native to Southeast Asia.
