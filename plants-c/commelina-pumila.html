Commelina pumila

Commelina pumila, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive spatulate bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina pumila is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
