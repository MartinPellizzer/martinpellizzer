Cynoglossum parvifolium

Cynoglossum parvifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is a perennial species that blooms from late summer to early autumn.

The plant has a distinctive scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
