Cyperus conicus var. ramosus

Cyperus conicus var. ramosus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The variety ramosus is distinguished by its branched stems, which contribute to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
