Camaridium hoehnei var. carinatum

Camaridium hoehnei var. carinatum, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
