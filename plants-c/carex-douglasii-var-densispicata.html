Carex douglasii var. densispicata

Carex douglasii var. densispicata, commonly known as Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
