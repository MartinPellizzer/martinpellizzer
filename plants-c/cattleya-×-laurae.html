Cattleya × laurae

Cattleya × laurae, commonly known as the Laura's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The species is prized for its fragrant blooms and is frequently used in orchid breeding programs.
