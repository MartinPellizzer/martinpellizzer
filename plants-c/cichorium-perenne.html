Cichorium perenne

Cichorium perenne, commonly known as chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its leaves, roots, and flowers. Chicory contains inulin, a prebiotic fiber, and is often used in salads, coffee substitutes, and herbal teas.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient and versatile plant in both agricultural and ornamental settings.
