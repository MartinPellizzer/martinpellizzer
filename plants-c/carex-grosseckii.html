Carex grosseckii

Carex grosseckii, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

Carex grosseckii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
