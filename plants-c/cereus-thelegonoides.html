Cereus thelegonoides

Cereus thelegonoides, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and xeriscaping.
