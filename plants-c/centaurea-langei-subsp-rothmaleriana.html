Centaurea langei subsp. rothmaleriana

Centaurea langei subsp.

rothmaleriana, commonly known as the Rothmaler's cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive ray floret and a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
