Chloris paspaloides

Chloris paspaloides, commonly known as false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It has fine, hairless leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

Chloris paspaloides is also used in some regions for forage and as a ground cover.
