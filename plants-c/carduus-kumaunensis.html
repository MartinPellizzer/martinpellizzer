Carduus kumaunensis

Carduus kumaunensis, commonly known as the Kumaun thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its robust stems and hairy foliage help it withstand the harsh climatic conditions of its native environment.

The flowers are primarily visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
