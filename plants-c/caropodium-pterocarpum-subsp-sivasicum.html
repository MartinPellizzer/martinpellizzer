Caropodium pterocarpum subsp. sivasicum

Caropodium pterocarpum subsp.

sivasicum, commonly known as the Sivasikum carob tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are small, clustered, and often pale yellow or greenish-white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf and fruit morphology compared to other members of the Caropodium genus.
