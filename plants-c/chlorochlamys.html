Chlorochlamys

Chlorochlamys, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking blue or purple flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean, they thrive in sandy or rocky soils. The leaves are typically narrow and sometimes covered in fine hairs.
