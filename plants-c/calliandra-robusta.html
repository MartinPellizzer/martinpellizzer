Calliandra robusta

Calliandra robusta, commonly known as the robust calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Calliandra robusta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

It also has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
