Carex tristachya var. pocilliformis

Carex tristachya var. pocilliformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that are typically arranged in two to three clusters. This plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.

The varietal form, pocilliformis, is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical variety.
