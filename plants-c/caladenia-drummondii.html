Caladenia drummondii

Caladenia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, strikingly colored flower resembling a spider in appearance. Its floral structure is complex, with a labellum that is distinctly shaped and colored to attract specific pollinators.

The species is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate adaptations to its native environment.
