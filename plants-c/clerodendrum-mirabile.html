Clerodendrum mirabile

Clerodendrum mirabile, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its dramatic common name. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
