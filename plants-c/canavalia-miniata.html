Canavalia miniata

Canavalia miniata, commonly known as the dwarf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, bean-like pods containing seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
