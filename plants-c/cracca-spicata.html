Cracca spicata

Cracca spicata, commonly known as the spiked cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are characteristic of the genus Cracca. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cracca spicata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
