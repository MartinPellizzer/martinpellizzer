Chrysodium repandum

Chrysodium repandum, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
