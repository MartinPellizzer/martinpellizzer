Colysis longipes

Colysis longipes, commonly known as the long-footed colysis, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its unique morphology, including its long, slender stems, contributes to its ecological niche.

Colysis longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
