Cyrtochilum incarum

Cyrtochilum incarum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal habitats. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, acidic soils in shaded environments.

Cyrtochilum incarum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
