Capsicum indicum cerasocarpon

Capsicum indicum cerasocarpon, commonly known as the red bell pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant is native to the Americas and has been cultivated for centuries for its culinary and medicinal uses. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are typically green, and it produces small, white flowers that develop into the edible fruits.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
