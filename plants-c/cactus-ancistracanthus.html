Cactus ancistracanthus

Cactus ancistracanthus, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Overall, Cactus ancistracanthus is well adapted to desert conditions with its unique morphological features.
