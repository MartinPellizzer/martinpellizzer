Calophyllum pentapetalum var. pulgarense

Calophyllum pentapetalum var. pulgarense, commonly known as the Philippine calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
