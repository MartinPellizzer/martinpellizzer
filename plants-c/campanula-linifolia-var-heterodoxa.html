Campanula linifolia var. heterodoxa

Campanula linifolia var. heterodoxa, commonly known as the heterodox bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
