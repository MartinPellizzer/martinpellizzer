Chironia verdickii

Chironia verdickii, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
