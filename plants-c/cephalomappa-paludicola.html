Cephalomappa paludicola

Cephalomappa paludicola, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in dense clusters near water bodies. Its roots help stabilize soil in marshy environments, contributing to ecological balance.

Cephalomappa paludicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
