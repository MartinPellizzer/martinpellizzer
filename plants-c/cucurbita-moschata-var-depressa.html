Cucurbita moschata var. depressa

Cucurbita moschata var. depressa, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin or casaba pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its round, slightly flattened shape and thick, warty skin that ranges in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and typically bears yellow flowers. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
