Carex disticha subsp. grossheimii

Carex disticha subsp.

grossheimii, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive floral structures.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in a panicle-like structure.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
