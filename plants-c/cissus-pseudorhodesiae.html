Cissus pseudorhodesiae

Cissus pseudorhodesiae, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cissus pseudorhodesiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in harsh environmental conditions.
