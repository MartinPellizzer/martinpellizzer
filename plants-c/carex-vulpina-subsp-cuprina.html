Carex vulpina subsp. cuprina

Carex vulpina subsp.

cuprina, commonly known as the fox sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
