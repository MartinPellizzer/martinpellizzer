Cheiranthus sulphureus

Cheiranthus sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur-yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as walls, roadsides, and waste ground. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful, long-lasting blooms.
