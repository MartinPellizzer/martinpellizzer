Clerodendrum aucubifolium var. longiflorum

Clerodendrum aucubifolium var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking floral display.
