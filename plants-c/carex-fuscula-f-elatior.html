Carex fuscula f. elatior

Carex fuscula f. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence.

The foliage is often a deep green, and the plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
