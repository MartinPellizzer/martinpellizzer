Commelina aequinoctialis

Commelina aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
