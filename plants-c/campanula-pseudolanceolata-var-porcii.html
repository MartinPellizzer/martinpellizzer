Campanula pseudolanceolata var. porcii

Campanula pseudolanceolata var. porcii, commonly known as the Porcine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
