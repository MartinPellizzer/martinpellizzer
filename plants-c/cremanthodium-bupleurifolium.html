Cremanthodium bupleurifolium

Cremanthodium bupleurifolium, commonly known as the bupleurum globe flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
