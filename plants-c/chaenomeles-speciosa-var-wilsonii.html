Chaenomeles speciosa var. wilsonii

Chaenomeles speciosa var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
