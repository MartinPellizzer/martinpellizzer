Catasetum lanxiforme

Catasetum lanxiforme, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, pendulous flowers with vivid colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with specialized pollination mechanisms, often involving specific insect pollinators.

Catasetum lanxiforme is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
