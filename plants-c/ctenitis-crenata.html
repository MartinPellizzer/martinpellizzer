Ctenitis crenata

Ctenitis crenata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated edge, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived component of its ecosystem.
