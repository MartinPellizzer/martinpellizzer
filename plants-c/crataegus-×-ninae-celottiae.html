Crataegus × ninae-celottiae

Crataegus × ninae-celottiae, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
