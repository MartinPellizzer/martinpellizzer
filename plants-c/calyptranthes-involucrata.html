Calyptranthes involucrata

Calyptranthes involucrata, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray layer of fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
