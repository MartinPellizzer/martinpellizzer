Clerodendrum lutambense

Clerodendrum lutambense, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The genus Clerodendrum is known for its diverse species, many of which are valued for their ornamental and medicinal properties.
