Carex buennemeijeri

Carex buennemeijeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and the Philippines. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex buennemeijeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
