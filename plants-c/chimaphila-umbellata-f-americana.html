Chimaphila umbellata f. americana

Chimaphila umbellata f. americana, commonly known as the American wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in forest understories.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to provide early seasonal interest in gardens.
