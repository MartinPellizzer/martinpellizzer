Charpentiera tomentosa var. tomentosa

Charpentiera tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly charpentiera, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a soft, hairy texture.

It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural habitat.
