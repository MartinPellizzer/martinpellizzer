Calliandra tamarindifolia

Calliandra tamarindifolia, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or red inflorescences that attract pollinators. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds encased in a sweet, sticky pulp. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the plant has medicinal uses and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
