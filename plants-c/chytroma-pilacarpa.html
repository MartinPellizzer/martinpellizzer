Chytroma pilacarpa

Chytroma pilacarpa, commonly known as the red-spotted flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Chytroma pilacarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
