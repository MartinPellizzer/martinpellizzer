Callerya unijuga

Callerya unijuga, commonly known as the single-flower callerya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, solitary flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Callerya unijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
