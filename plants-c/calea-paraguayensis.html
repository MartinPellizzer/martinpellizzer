Calea paraguayensis

Calea paraguayensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, opening its bracts at night. Calea paraguayensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and blooming pattern make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
