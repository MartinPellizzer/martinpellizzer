Crataegus jackii

Crataegus jackii, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crataegus jackii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
