Chrysophyllum rhodoneurum

Chrysophyllum rhodoneurum, commonly known as the red-veined satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, smooth fruits containing a single large seed. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture and cabinet making.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
