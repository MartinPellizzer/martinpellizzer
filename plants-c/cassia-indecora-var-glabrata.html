Cassia indecora var. glabrata

Cassia indecora var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in whorls and have a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oblong seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
