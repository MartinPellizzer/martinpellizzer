Cistus × ultraviolaceus

Cistus × ultraviolaceus, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's aromatic oils have historically been used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
