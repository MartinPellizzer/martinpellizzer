Carex chalcodeta

Carex chalcodeta, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a bluish-green color, which gives it its common name. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex chalcodeta plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
