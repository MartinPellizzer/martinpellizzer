Chaetanthera magellanica

Chaetanthera magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths and bats during the night.
