Cymbalaria fragilis

Cymbalaria fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Cymbalaria fragilis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
