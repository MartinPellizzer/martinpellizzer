Crambe tetuanensis

Crambe tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan mustard, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Crambe tetuanensis is also noted for its potential as a source of oilseed.
