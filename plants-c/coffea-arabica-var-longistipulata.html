Coffea arabica var. longistipulata

Coffea arabica var. longistipulata, commonly known as the long-stipuled coffee, is a variant of the Arabica coffee species.

It is characterized by its elongated leaf stipules, which are distinctive features in its morphology. This variety typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, humid climates. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the coffee beans used for brewing.

Its botanical traits make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and genetic studies.
