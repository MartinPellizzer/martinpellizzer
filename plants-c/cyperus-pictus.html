Cyperus pictus

Cyperus pictus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with distinctive variegated leaves that display shades of green and white. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically inconspicuous.

Cyperus pictus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
