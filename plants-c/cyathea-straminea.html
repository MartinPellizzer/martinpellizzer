Cyathea straminea

Cyathea straminea, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically covered in a silvery or pale-colored coating, giving it its common name.

Cyathea straminea plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
