Crepis bhotanica

Crepis bhotanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese mouse-ear.

It is a perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to the Himalayan region, including Bhutan, Nepal, and parts of India.

Crepis bhotanica is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
