Convolvulus falckia

Convolvulus falckia, commonly known as the red sandalwood vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
