Claytonia spathulata var. exigua

Claytonia spathulata var. exigua, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with rosettes of spoon-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its compact size and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
