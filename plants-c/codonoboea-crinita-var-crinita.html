Codonoboea crinita var. crinita

Codonoboea crinita var. crinita, commonly known as the curly hair vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.
