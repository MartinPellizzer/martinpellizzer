Conyza scabiosifolia

Conyza scabiosifolia, commonly known as the silky weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Conyza scabiosifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
