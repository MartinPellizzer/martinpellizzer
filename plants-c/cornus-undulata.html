Cornus undulata

Cornus undulata, commonly known as the zigzag dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Cornus undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
