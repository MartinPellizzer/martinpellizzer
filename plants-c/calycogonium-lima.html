Calycogonium lima

Calycogonium lima, commonly known as the Lima bean vine, is a climbing plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical structure supports its role as a legume, capable of nitrogen fixation through symbiotic bacteria.

The plant is valued for its nutritional seeds and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
