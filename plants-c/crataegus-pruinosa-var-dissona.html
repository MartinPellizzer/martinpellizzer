Crataegus pruinosa var. dissona

Crataegus pruinosa var. dissona, commonly known as the dissonous hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a silvery-white underside. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
