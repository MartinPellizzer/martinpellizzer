Capparis sikkimensis subsp. yunnanensis

Capparis sikkimensis subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan caper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and medicinal properties traditionally used in folk medicine.
