Cytisus capitatus var. paniculatus

Cytisus capitatus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are small, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
