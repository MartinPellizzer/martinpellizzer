Chuquiraga leptacantha

Chuquiraga leptacantha, commonly known as the Andean bur plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
