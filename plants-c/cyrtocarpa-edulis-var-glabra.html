Cyrtocarpa edulis var. glabra

Cyrtocarpa edulis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-fruited fig tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or processed into jams and syrups. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruiting season typically occurs during the rainy months, making it a valuable crop in tropical agriculture.
