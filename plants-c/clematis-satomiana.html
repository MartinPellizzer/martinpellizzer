Clematis satomiana

Clematis satomiana, commonly known as the Japanese vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Japan.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lilac, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Clematis satomiana thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
