Cucubalus saponariifolius

Cucubalus saponariifolius, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange berries that contain a toxic seed. Its fruit is the source of saponins, which give it its name and contribute to its use in natural detergents.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid climates.
