Crataegus sabineana

Crataegus sabineana, commonly known as Sabine hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall.
