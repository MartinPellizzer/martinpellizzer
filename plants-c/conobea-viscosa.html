Conobea viscosa

Conobea viscosa, commonly known as the sticky conobea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky substance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months.
