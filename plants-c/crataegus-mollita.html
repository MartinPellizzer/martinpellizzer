Crataegus mollita

Crataegus mollita, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Crataegus mollita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
