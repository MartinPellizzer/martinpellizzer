Centaurea suaveolens

Centaurea suaveolens, commonly known as sweet centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Centaurea suaveolens is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
