Chavica exasperata

Chavica exasperata, commonly known as the scarlet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant as well as a valuable component of local ecosystems.
