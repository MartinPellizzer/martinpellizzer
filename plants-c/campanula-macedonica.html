Campanula macedonica

Campanula macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan region.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Campanula macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
