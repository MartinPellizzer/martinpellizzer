Casearia mollipila

Casearia mollipila, commonly known as the silky casearia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Casearia mollipila is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
