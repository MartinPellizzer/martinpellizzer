Capparis spinosa var. nummularia

Capparis spinosa var. nummularia, commonly known as the Spanish fly or sea holly, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, waxy fruits resembling small coins. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky areas.

Its unique appearance and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable botanical species.
