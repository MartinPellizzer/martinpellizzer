Curio avasimontanus

Curio avasimontanus, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Curio avasimontanus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
