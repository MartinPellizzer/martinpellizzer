Carex melaena

Carex melaena, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Carex melaena is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
