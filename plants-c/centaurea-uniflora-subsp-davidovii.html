Centaurea uniflora subsp. davidovii

Centaurea uniflora subsp.

davidovii, commonly known as the Davidovii Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms during the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
