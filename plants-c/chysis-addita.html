Chysis addita

Chysis addita, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading raindrops, which bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Chysis addita is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
