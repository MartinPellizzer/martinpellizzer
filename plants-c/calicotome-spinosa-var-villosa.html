Calicotome spinosa var. villosa

Calicotome spinosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy calicotome, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its woody structure and adaptability to arid conditions make it a hardy, drought-resistant species.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in local landscaping for its ornamental and protective qualities.
