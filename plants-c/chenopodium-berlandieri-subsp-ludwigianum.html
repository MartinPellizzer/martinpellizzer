Chenopodium berlandieri subsp. ludwigianum

Chenopodium berlandieri subsp.

ludwigianum, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Pigweed is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
