Cestrum mucronatum

Cestrum mucronatum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking nocturnal blooms and ornamental value.
