Caladium argyrospilum

Caladium argyrospilum, commonly known as the silver-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white veining against a deep green background. The plant thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Caladium argyrospilum typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and prefers partial to full shade in garden settings.
