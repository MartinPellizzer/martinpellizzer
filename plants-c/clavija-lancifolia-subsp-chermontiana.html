Clavija lancifolia subsp. chermontiana

Clavija lancifolia subsp.

chermontiana, commonly known as the Chermontian clavija, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
