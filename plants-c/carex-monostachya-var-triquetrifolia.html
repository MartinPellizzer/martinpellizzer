Carex monostachya var. triquetrifolia

Carex monostachya var. triquetrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, compact cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which is a common feature among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.
