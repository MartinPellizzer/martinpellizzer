Croton pachyclados

Croton pachyclados, commonly known as the Woolly Croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some regions for their purported health benefits.
