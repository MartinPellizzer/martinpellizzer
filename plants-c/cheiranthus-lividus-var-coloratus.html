Cheiranthus lividus var. coloratus

Cheiranthus lividus var. coloratus, commonly known as the showy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its vibrant flowers, which range from yellow to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
