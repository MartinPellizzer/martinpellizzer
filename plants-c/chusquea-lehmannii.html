Chusquea lehmannii

Chusquea lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations, often forming thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Chusquea lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
