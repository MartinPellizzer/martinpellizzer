Crinissa

Crinissa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Crinissa is often grown as a climbing plant in gardens or as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
