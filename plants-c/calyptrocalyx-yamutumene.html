Calyptrocalyx yamutumene

Calyptrocalyx yamutumene, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
