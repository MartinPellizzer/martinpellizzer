Canarium quadrangulare

Canarium quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-sided candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.
