Carex austroussuriensis

Carex austroussuriensis, commonly known as the Southern Ussuri sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
