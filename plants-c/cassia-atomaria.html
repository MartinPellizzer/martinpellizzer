Cassia atomaria

Cassia atomaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Cassia atomaria is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
