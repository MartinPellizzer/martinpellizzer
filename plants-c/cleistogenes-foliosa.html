Cleistogenes foliosa

Cleistogenes foliosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth habit.

Cleistogenes foliosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
