Crataegus callidens

Crataegus callidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus callidens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
