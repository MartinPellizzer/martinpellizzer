Carex oederi var. pulchella

Carex oederi var. pulchella, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

The showy sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
