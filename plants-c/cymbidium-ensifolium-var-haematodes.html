Cymbidium ensifolium var. haematodes

Cymbidium ensifolium var. haematodes, commonly known as the blood-leaf orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often exhibit a reddish or purplish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
