Cissus hochstetteri

Cissus hochstetteri, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a climbing succulent native to South Africa.

It features thin, wiry stems and small, opposite, waxy leaves that are often tinged with silver or pale green. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its trailing habit, it is often used as a hanging plant or to cover trellises in gardens.
