Clematis sinensis

Clematis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Clematis sinensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
