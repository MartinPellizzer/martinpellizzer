Corylus avellana var. laciniata

Corylus avellana var. laciniata, commonly known as the Japanese hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
