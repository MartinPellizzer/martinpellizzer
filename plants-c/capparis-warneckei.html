Capparis warneckei

Capparis warneckei, commonly known as the Warneck's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright yellow berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Capparis warneckei thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
