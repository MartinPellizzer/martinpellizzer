Calathea hieroglyphica

Calathea hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Egyptian sword leaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, patterned leaves that resemble ancient hieroglyphics, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its vibrant coloration, including shades of green, silver, and red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea hieroglyphica is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
