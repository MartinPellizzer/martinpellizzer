Clematis schinziana

Clematis schinziana, commonly known as the Schinz's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls with its strong, flexible stems.
