Catopsis pendula

Catopsis pendula, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, drooping fronds that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a thick, spongy sheath.

It thrives in high humidity and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
