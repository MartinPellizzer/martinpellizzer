Crataegus laevigata subsp. walokochiana

Crataegus laevigata subsp.

walokochiana, commonly known as the smooth thorned hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
