Campanula rotundifolia var. hispanica

Campanula rotundifolia var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are often described as heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies enhances its ecological value in garden settings.
