Cyathea poeppigii

Cyathea poeppigii, commonly known as the Poeppig's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, sturdy trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are responsible for sexual reproduction through spores. Cyathea poeppigii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
