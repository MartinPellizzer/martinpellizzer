Cleome bungei

Cleome bungei, commonly known as the spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome bungei thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to many landscapes.
