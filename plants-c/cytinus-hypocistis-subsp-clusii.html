Cytinus hypocistis subsp. clusii

Cytinus hypocistis subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the "broad-lobed broomrape," is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family. It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and reliance on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of legumes.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it is often found in rocky or arid environments.
