Capparis spinosa var. canescens

Capparis spinosa var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, greenish-white fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant with a long history of cultivation.
