Calendula suffruticosa subsp. lusitanica

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese marigold, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe. It features hairy, branched stems and produces yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
