Cissus pendula

Cissus pendula, commonly known as the weeping cutleaf goosefoot, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a waxy, slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.
