Cynanchum maximoviczii

Cynanchum maximoviczii, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the milk vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive seed pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cynanchum maximoviczii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.
