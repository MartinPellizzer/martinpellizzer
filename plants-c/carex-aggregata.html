Carex aggregata

Carex aggregata, commonly known as clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex aggregata thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in swampy or pond edges.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
