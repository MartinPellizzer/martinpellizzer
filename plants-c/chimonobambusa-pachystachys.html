Chimonobambusa pachystachys

Chimonobambusa pachystachys, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. Its flowering occurs infrequently, producing clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its elegant appearance.
