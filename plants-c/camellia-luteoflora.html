Camellia luteoflora

Camellia luteoflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered camellia, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking yellow flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and can grow up to 3 meters tall under optimal conditions. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia luteoflora is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
