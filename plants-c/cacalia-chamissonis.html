Cacalia chamissonis

Cacalia chamissonis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines, thriving in saline and well-drained environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple markings.

Cacalia chamissonis is often found in maritime climates and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
