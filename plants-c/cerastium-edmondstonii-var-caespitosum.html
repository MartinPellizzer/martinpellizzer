Cerastium edmondstonii var. caespitosum

Cerastium edmondstonii var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, often forming a compact cluster at the base.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
