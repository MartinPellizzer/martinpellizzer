Calea diffusa

Calea diffusa, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety blue or purple flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Calea diffusa is a short-lived perennial that prefers full sun to partial shade.
