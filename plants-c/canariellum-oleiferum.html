Canariellum oleiferum

Canariellum oleiferum, commonly known as the Canary Island olive.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, olive-like fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, often growing in rocky soils. Its fruit contains a high oil content, making it valuable for both culinary and industrial uses.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.
