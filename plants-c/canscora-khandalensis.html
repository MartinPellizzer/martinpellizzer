Canscora khandalensis

Canscora khandalensis, commonly known as the Khandala mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a slightly rough texture.

Canscora khandalensis is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas.
