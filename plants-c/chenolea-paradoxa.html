Chenolea paradoxa

Chenolea paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical chenolea, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lanceolate leaves with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming during the wet season. Its growth habit is low and creeping, often forming dense mats on moist, shaded forest floors.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Chenolea paradoxa is considered a botanically significant but understudied species.
