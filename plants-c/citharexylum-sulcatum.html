Citharexylum sulcatum

Citharexylum sulcatum, commonly known as the smoothleaf citharexylum, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Citharexylum sulcatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
