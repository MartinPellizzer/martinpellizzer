Corryocactus brevistylus var. puquiensis

Corryocactus brevistylus var. puquiensis, commonly known as the Puquian cactus, is a rare and distinctive species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.
