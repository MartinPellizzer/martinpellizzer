Carex obovatosquamata

Carex obovatosquamata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curved and have a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Carex obovatosquamata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.
