Centaurea × pseudopannonica

Centaurea × pseudopannonica, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Pannonian region of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
