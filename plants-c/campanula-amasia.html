Campanula amasia

Campanula amasia, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Campanula amasia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
