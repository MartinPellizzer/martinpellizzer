Chironia gerardii

Chironia gerardii, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, often prickly texture.

Chironia gerardii is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit.
