Caesalpinia palmeri

Caesalpinia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bleeding heart, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces distinctive red or orange flowers arranged in clusters resembling a heart shape. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is known for its showy, pendulous blooms that appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The genus Caesalpinia is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structures and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
