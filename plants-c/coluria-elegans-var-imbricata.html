Coluria elegans var. imbricata

Coluria elegans var. imbricata, commonly known as the silverleaf groundcherry, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and edible fruits.
