Carex humilis f. callitrichos

Carex humilis f. callitrichos, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
