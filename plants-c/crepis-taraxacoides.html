Crepis taraxacoides

Crepis taraxacoides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis taraxacoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
