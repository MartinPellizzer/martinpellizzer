Cistus riphienensis

Cistus riphienensis, commonly known as the Rif mountain rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Rif Mountains of northern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Cistus riphienensis plays an important role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
