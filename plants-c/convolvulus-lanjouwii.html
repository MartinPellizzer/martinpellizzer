Convolvulus lanjouwii

Convolvulus lanjouwii, commonly known as the Lanjouw morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are wiry and twining.

Convolvulus lanjouwii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
