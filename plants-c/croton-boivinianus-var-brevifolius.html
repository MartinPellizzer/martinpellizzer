Croton boivinianus var. brevifolius

Croton boivinianus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically short and broad, often with a distinctive coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
