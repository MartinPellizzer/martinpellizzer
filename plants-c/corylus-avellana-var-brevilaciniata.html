Corylus avellana var. brevilaciniata

Corylus avellana var. brevilaciniata, commonly known as the European hazel with narrow leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are shorter and narrower than those of the typical hazel. The plant produces catkins and edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of wildlife food.
