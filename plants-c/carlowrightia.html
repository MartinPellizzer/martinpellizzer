Carlowrightia

Carlowrightia, commonly known as the western redcedar, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is primarily valued for its durable, aromatic wood. Carlowrightia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
