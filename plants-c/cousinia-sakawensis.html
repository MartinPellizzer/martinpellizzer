Cousinia sakawensis

Cousinia sakawensis, commonly known as the Sakawensis cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

Cousinia sakawensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
