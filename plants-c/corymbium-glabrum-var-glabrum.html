Corymbium glabrum var. glabrum

Corymbium glabrum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth corymbia, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, pale bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
