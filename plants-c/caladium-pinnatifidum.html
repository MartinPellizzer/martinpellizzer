Caladium pinnatifidum

Caladium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the split-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and split into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its foliage displays a variety of colors, including shades of green, red, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
