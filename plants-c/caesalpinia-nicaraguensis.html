Caesalpinia nicaraguensis

Caesalpinia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

The seeds are enclosed in long, pod-like fruits that mature to a dark brown color.
