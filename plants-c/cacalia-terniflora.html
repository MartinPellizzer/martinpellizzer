Cacalia terniflora

Cacalia terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, which is a distinguishing feature.
