Crataegus monticola

Crataegus monticola, commonly known as the mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and streambanks. The plant features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
