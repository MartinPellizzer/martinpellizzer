Cyperus serotinus var. inundatus

Cyperus serotinus var. inundatus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in other waterlogged soils, showcasing its adaptability to saturated conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in clusters, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its ability to thrive in flooded environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
