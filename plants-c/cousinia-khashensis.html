Cousinia khashensis

Cousinia khashensis, commonly known as the Khansari thistle, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Cousinia khashensis is often found in steppe and semi-desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
