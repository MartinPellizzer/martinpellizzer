Capsella bursa-pastoris subalgida

Capsella bursa-pastoris subalgida, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb with prostrate or ascending stems.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a distinctive seed pod that resembles a heart shape. It is often found in disturbed soils, along pathways, and in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in temperate regions.
