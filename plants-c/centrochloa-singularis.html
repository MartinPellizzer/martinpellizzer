Centrochloa singularis

Centrochloa singularis, commonly known as the singular clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Its growth habit makes it a common weed in disturbed soils, and it is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil health.
