Clematis commutata

Clematis commutata, commonly known as the smooth Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Clematis commutata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
