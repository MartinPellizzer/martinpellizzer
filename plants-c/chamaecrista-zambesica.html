Chamaecrista zambesica

Chamaecrista zambesica, commonly known as the Zambesi burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, bean-like seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months.

Chamaecrista zambesica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
