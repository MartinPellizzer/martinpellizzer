Cocos amadelpha

Cocos amadelpha, commonly known as the African date palm, is a species of palm native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible date-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional settings.
