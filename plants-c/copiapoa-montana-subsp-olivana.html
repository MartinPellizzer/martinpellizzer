Copiapoa montana subsp. olivana

Copiapoa montana subsp.

olivana, commonly known as the Olivana cactus, is a species native to northern Chile. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive greenish-gray color and produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring.

The plant has a dense, spiny exterior that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It is adapted to grow in rocky soils and can survive in extreme desert conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
