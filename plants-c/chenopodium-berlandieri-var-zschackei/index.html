Chenopodium Berlandieri Var. Zschackei

Chenopodium berlandieri var. zschackei is a unique plant species native to the central and western regions of the United States. It is commonly known as the prickly goosefoot and is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and edible, nutrient-rich stems. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its historical significance includes use by indigenous peoples for food and medicine. Today, it remains an important subject of botanical study due to its adaptability and ecological role.
