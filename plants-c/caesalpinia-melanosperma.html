Caesalpinia melanosperma

Caesalpinia melanosperma, commonly known as black-seed Caesalpinia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets and distinctive, bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, dark brown pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
