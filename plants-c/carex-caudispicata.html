Carex caudispicata

Carex caudispicata, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of the stem.

Carex caudispicata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
