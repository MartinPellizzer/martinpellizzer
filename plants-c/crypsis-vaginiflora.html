Crypsis vaginiflora

Crypsis vaginiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive violet or purple flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is notable for its velvety, hairy texture on both the leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
