Campanula trachelium var. athoa

Campanula trachelium var. athoa, commonly known as the Scottish bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

The variety 'athoa' is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to the typical form.
