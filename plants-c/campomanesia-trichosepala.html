Campomanesia trichosepala

Campomanesia trichosepala, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, orange to red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, slightly astringent flavor when ripe.
