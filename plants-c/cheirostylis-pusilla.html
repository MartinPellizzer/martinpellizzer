Cheirostylis pusilla

Cheirostylis pusilla, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as under forest canopies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Cheirostylis pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
