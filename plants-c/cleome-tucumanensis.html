Cleome tucumanensis

Cleome tucumanensis, commonly known as the tucumanensis spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and often colorful inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Cleome tucumanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and ease of growth.
