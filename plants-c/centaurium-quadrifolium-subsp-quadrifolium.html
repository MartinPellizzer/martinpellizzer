Centaurium quadrifolium subsp. quadrifolium

Centaurium quadrifolium subsp.

quadrifolium, commonly known as four-leaf centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that often form a rosette.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.
