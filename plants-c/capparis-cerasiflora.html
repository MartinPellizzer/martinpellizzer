Capparis cerasiflora

Capparis cerasiflora, commonly known as the sour orange or sour plum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form, with thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a high acidity and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in coastal and rocky environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.
