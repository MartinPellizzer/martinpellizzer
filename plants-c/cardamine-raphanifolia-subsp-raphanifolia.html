Cardamine raphanifolia subsp. raphanifolia

Cardamine raphanifolia subsp.

raphanifolia, commonly known as the radishleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and often resemble those of a radish, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its edible leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a leafy green in gardens.
