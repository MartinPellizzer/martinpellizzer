Cacuvallum altissimus

Cacuvallum altissimus, commonly known as the skyreach vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can extend several meters in search of sunlight. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are large and showy, typically appearing in clusters and ranging in color from deep purple to bright yellow.

The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.
