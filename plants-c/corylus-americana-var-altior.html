Corylus americana var. altior

Corylus americana var. altior, commonly known as the American hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Corylaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The plant produces catkins in early spring, with male and female flowers on separate plants. Its edible nuts, enclosed in a spiny husk, mature in late summer to early fall.

The shrub thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
