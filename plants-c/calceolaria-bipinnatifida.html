Calceolaria bipinnatifida

Calceolaria bipinnatifida, commonly known as the bi-pinnatifid slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Calceolaria bipinnatifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
