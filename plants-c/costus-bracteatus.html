Costus bracteatus

Costus bracteatus, commonly known as the golden costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, yellow-orange inflorescence, which resembles a spike of small flowers.
