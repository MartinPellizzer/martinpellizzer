Cissus arachnoidea

Cissus arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that can extend several meters in length. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a distinctive veining pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.
