Carex × lambertiana

Carex × lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, part of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. It has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing both male and female parts, which facilitate wind pollination.
