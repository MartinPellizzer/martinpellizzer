Cacalia kamtschatica

Cacalia kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It features a basal rosette of leaves and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Cacalia kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
