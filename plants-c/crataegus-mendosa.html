Crataegus mendosa

Crataegus mendosa, commonly known as the Mendocino hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various environments.

Crataegus mendosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
