Campanula collina var. fondervisii

Campanula collina var. fondervisii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
