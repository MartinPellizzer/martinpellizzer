Cousinia massalskyi

Cousinia massalskyi, commonly known as the Massalskyi's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features hairy, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Cousinia massalskyi is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
