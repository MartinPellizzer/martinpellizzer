Convolvulus orinoccensis

Convolvulus orinoccensis, commonly known as the Orinoco morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Convolvulus orinoccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
