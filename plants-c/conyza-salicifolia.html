Conyza salicifolia

Conyza salicifolia, commonly known as willowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and roadsides, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Conyza salicifolia is known for its resilience and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
