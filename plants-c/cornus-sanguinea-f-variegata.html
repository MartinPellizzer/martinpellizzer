Cornus sanguinea f. variegata

Cornus sanguinea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

The leaves are typically a mix of green, white, and red, creating a vibrant visual display throughout the growing season. It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of small, red berries in the autumn. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
