Cyphostemma pseudomanikense

Cyphostemma pseudomanikense, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
