Cassia rotundifolia var. grandiflora

Cassia rotundifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cassia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its round or ovate leaves and bright yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
