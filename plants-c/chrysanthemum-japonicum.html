Chrysanthemum japonicum

Chrysanthemum japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces both ray and disc florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it an important species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
