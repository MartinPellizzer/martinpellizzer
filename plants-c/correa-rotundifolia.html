Correa rotundifolia

Correa rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pink or red, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Correa rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
