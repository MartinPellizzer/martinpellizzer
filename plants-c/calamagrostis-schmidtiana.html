Calamagrostis schmidtiana

Calamagrostis schmidtiana, commonly known as Schmidt's reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or pink at the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
