Campylotropis bonii var. angusticarpa

Campylotropis bonii var. angusticarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited bottlebrush clover, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces distinctive cylindrical flower clusters that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its unique fruiting structures, which are elongated and cylindrical, giving it the common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
