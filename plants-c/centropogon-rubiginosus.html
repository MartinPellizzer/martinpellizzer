Centropogon rubiginosus

Centropogon rubiginosus, commonly known as the red-stemmed panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in wetland and coastal environments, often growing in marshy or brackish areas.

Centropogon rubiginosus is adapted to saline conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
