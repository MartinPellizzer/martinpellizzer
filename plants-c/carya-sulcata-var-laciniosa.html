Carya sulcata var. laciniosa

Carya sulcata var. laciniosa, commonly known as the slender hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form. The leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, with a serrated margin. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single edible nut enclosed in a thick, spiny husk.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
