Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp. chilense

Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp.

chilense, commonly known as wild spinach or lamb's quarters, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a leafy green vegetable in various cuisines.
