Cinchona peruviana var. nitida

Cinchona peruviana var. nitida, commonly known as the Peruvian quinine tree, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It is particularly noted for its high concentration of quinine, a compound historically used in the treatment of malaria.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical climates.
