Cephalotrophis javanica

Cephalotrophis javanica, commonly known as the Javanese head-plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves that form a dense, floating structure. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental cultivation.
