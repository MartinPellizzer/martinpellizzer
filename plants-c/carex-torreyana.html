Carex torreyana

Carex torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its cylindrical, grass-like leaves are usually dark green and stiff, with a triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive three-sided seed capsules.
