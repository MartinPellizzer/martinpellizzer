Cardamine uliginosa var. amethystina

Cardamine uliginosa var. amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet, acidic habitats in temperate regions.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, purple-tinged flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in bogs, marshes, and other moist environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in a single umbel, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.

It is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
