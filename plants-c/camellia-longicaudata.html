Camellia longicaudata

Camellia longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed camellia, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with prominent, elongated stamens that give it its common name. Camellia longicaudata thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
