Convolvulus anceps

Convolvulus anceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habits.
