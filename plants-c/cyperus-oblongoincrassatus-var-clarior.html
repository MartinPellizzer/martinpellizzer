Cyperus oblongoincrassatus var. clarior

Cyperus oblongoincrassatus var. clarior, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common species in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its robust, grass-like appearance and preference for moist habitats.
