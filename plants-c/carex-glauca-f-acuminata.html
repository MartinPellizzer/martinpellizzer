Carex glauca f. acuminata

Carex glauca f. acuminata, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a variant of the common blue sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually inconspicuous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This form is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
