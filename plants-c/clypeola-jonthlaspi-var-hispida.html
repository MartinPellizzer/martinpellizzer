Clypeola jonthlaspi var. hispida

Clypeola jonthlaspi var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy clypeola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clustered heads, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
