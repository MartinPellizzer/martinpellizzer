Chenopodium murale var. acutidentatum

Chenopodium murale var. acutidentatum, commonly known as sharp-toothed goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered greenish flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same inflorescence. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
