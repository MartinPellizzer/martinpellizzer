Capparis sandwichiana

Capparis sandwichiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
