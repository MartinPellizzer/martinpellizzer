Cousinia hypochionea

Cousinia hypochionea, commonly known as the Kashmiri hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers in spring. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Cousinia hypochionea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
