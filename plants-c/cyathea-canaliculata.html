Cyathea canaliculata

Cyathea canaliculata, commonly known as the channel-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer of old fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, with a distinctive channel-like pattern on the undersides of the leaves. Cyathea canaliculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
