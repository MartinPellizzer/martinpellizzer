Cleome niamniamensis

Cleome niamniamensis, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescences that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals.

Cleome niamniamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
