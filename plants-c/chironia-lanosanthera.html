Chironia lanosanthera

Chironia lanosanthera, commonly known as the woolly sandalwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody scent and is valued for its essential oils. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and attract various pollinators.
