Calathiana pontica

Calathiana pontica, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features attractive blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calathiana pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
