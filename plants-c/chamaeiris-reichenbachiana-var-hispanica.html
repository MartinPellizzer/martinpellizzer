Chamaeiris reichenbachiana var. hispanica

Chamaeiris reichenbachiana var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
