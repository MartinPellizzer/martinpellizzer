Carludovica ecuadoriensis

Carludovica ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Carludovica ecuadoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
