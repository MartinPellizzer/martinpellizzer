Cattleya labiata var. semialba

Cattleya labiata var. semialba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip, which sets it apart from other varieties of Cattleya. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.

This variety is prized for its elegance and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
