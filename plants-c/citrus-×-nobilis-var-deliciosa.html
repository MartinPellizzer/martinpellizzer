Citrus × nobilis var. deliciosa

Citrus × nobilis var. deliciosa, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, peeling rind and segmented, juicy pulp that is rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, followed by the development of the characteristic orange fruit.

The fruit is widely cultivated for consumption and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
