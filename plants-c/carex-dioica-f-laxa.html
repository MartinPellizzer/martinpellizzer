Carex dioica f. laxa

Carex dioica f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf waterplantain, is a variant of the common waterplantain.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often loosely arranged, giving it its characteristic appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Carex genus.

This form is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
