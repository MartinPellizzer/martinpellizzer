Cenchrus compressus

Cenchrus compressus, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Cenchrus compressus is valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
