Cynoglossum coeruleum subsp. kenyense

Cynoglossum coeruleum subsp.

kenyense, commonly known as the Kenyan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Africa. It features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with five lobes.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its subspecific distinction highlights its unique adaptations to the Kenyan ecosystem.
