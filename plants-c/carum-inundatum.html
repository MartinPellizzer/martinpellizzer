Carum inundatum

Carum inundatum, commonly known as water caraway, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, spicy scent.

Carum inundatum thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.
