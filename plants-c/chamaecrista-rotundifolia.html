Chamaecrista rotundifolia

Chamaecrista rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ticktree, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive round or oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
