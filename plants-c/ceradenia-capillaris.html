Ceradenia capillaris

Ceradenia capillaris, commonly known as the slender ceradenia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are long and narrow, often covered in fine, hair-like structures.
