Cousinia shebliensis

Cousinia shebliensis, commonly known as the Afghan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia shebliensis produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
