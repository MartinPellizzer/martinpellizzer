Cerastium banaticum var. stenophyllum

Cerastium banaticum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions with suitable humidity and soil conditions.
