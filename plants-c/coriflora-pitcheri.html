Coriflora pitcheri

Coriflora pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's coriflora, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
