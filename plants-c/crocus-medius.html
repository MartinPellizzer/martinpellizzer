Crocus medius

Crocus medius, commonly known as the meadow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, early spring blooms with three to six petals in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Crocus medius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
