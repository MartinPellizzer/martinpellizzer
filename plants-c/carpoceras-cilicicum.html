Carpoceras cilicicum

Carpoceras cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in rocky or dry soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
