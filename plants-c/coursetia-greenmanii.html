Coursetia greenmanii

Coursetia greenmanii, commonly known as the greenman's coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils. Its growth habit is typically compact, with multiple stems forming a low, spreading shrub.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
