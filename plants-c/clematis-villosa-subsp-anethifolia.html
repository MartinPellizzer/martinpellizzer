Clematis villosa subsp. anethifolia

Clematis villosa subsp.

anethifolia, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It features slender, twining stems covered in soft, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The compound leaves are trifoliate, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, appearing in late spring to early summer, and are borne in clusters along the stems.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
