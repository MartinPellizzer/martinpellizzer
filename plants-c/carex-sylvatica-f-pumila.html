Carex sylvatica f. pumila

Carex sylvatica f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms a mat of narrow, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy along the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by distinctive three-sided seed heads.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice in horticultural settings.
