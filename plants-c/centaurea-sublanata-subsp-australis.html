Centaurea sublanata subsp. australis

Centaurea sublanata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and bristly, glandular stems.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
