Clematis tamurae

Clematis tamurae, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a flowering vine native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, typically blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
