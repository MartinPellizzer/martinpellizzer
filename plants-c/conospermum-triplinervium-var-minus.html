Conospermum triplinervium var. minus

Conospermum triplinervium var. minus, commonly known as the small-leaf conospermum, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
