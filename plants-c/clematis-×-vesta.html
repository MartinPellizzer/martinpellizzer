Clematis × vesta

Clematis × vesta, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces feathery, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis × vesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and showy blooms.
