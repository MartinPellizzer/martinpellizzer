Carduus andersonii

Carduus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
