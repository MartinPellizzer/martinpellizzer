Carex vesicaria var. inalpina

Carex vesicaria var. inalpina, commonly known as the Alpine marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in mountain meadows or wetlands. Its small, greenish-brown flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, inflated seed capsules.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
