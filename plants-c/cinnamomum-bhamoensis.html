Cinnamomum bhamoensis

Cinnamomum bhamoensis, commonly known as Bhamo cinnamon, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of northern Myanmar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark contains essential oils and has been used traditionally in local medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
