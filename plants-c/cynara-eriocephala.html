Cynara eriocephala

Cynara eriocephala, commonly known as the Spanish artichoke, is a thistle-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged stems and large, silvery-green, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woolly fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible stems and leaves.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adaptable to various climates and is used in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
