Carex eburnea

Carex eburnea, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in moist or wet environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from silver to gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in clusters, forming low, compact clumps that can thrive in partial to full shade.

Carex eburnea is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
