Coleus caninus subsp. flavovirens

Coleus caninus subsp.

flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage featuring shades of green, yellow, and white.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This subspecies is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
