Caladenia filifera

Caladenia filifera, commonly known as the thread-leaved spider orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and a single, intricate flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between late winter and early spring.

Caladenia filifera is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique and delicate beauty.
