Campanula wiedmannii

Campanula wiedmannii, commonly known as the Wiedmann's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Campanula wiedmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
