Calamus koribanus

Calamus koribanus, commonly known as the Japanese calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Calamus koribanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its rhizomes are also utilized in the production of aromatic oils and perfumes.
