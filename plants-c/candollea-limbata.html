Candollea limbata

Candollea limbata, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and a dense, spiral arrangement of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Candollea limbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
