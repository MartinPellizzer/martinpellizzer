Cerastium purpurascens f. ensifolium

Cerastium purpurascens f. ensifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf mouse-ear, is a hybrid form of the common mouse-ear.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive, elongated, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
