Calycophyllum papillosum

Calycophyllum papillosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a velvety texture and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Calycophyllum papillosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
