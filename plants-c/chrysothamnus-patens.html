Chrysothamnus patens

Chrysothamnus patens, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Chrysothamnus patens is a drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
