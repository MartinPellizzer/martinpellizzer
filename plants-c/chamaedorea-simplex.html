Chamaedorea simplex

Chamaedorea simplex, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or feather palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and thrives in humid, well-drained soil. It is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Chamaedorea simplex is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in tropical landscaping.
