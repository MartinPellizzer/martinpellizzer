Cumetea montana

Cumetea montana, commonly known as the mountain cumaru, is a tropical tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rough, dark-brown bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Cumetea montana is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
