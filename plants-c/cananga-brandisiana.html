Cananga brandisiana

Cananga brandisiana, commonly known as the white champak tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The tree has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
