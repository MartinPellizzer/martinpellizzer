Commelina vogelii

Commelina vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, which are often accompanied by prominent white or yellow bracts. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Commelina vogelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
