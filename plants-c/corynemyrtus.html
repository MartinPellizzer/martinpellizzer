Corynemyrtus

Corynemyrtus, commonly known as the waxflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plants typically have narrow, lance-shaped leaves and are often shrubs or small trees. Corynemyrtus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are cultivated in gardens for their long-lasting blooms.

They thrive in well-drained soils and are drought-tolerant once established.
