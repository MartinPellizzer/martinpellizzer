Cochlearia sphaerocarpa

Cochlearia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Cochlearia sphaerocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
