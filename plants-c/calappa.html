Calappa

Calappa, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. They typically grow as compact shrubs with upright stems and are often cultivated as ornamental plants. Calappa species are popular in horticulture due to their ability to thrive in indoor environments.

The flowers are usually borne on long, slender stalks and are known for their vibrant hues and longevity.
