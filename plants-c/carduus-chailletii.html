Carduus chailletii

Carduus chailletii, commonly known as the Chaillet's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across different habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
