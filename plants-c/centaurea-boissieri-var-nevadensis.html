Centaurea boissieri var. nevadensis

Centaurea boissieri var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. It has a low, spreading growth habit with slender, branched stems.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
