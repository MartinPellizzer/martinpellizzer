Citrus yamabuki

Citrus yamabuki, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or yamabuki orange, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thin, yellow-orange peel and a sweet, tangy pulp. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and oval-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

The citrus yamabuki is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its edible fruit.
