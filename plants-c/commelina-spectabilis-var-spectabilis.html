Commelina spectabilis var. spectabilis

Commelina spectabilis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by white or pinkish bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It blooms from spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
