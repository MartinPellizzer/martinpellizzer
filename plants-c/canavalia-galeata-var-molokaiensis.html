Canavalia galeata var. molokaiensis

Canavalia galeata var. molokaiensis, commonly known as Molokai morning glory, is a climbing legume native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
