Calanthe nana

Calanthe nana, commonly known as the dwarf calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a small plant, typically growing to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with a rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often during the spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The genus Calanthe is characterized by its pseudobulbs and is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
