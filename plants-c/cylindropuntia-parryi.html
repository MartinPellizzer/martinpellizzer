Cylindropuntia parryi

Cylindropuntia parryi, commonly known as the Parry's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaves and the ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Cylindropuntia parryi plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for desert wildlife.
