Ceropegia lujae

Ceropegia lujae, commonly known as the Lujas's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia lujae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
