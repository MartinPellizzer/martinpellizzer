Cereus strigosus var. rufispinus

Cereus strigosus var. rufispinus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a spiny stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its areoles bear both white and red spines, which are a distinguishing feature of this variety. The plant produces night-blooming flowers that are typically pale yellow or white.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
