Chamaedorea verschaffeltii

Chamaedorea verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Verschaffelt's bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, compact plant with slender, upright stems and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moderate humidity and well-draining soil. It is often used as a decorative indoor plant due to its air-purifying qualities and ease of care.

Its foliage remains evergreen, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor spaces.
