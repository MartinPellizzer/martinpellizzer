Calophyllum spurium

Calophyllum spurium, commonly known as the Spurpole tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
