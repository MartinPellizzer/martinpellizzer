Cyperus aureostramineus var. decolorans

Cyperus aureostramineus var. decolorans, commonly known as the yellow-striped flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a yellowish stripe running along the midrib, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swamps, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some areas where it can outcompete native vegetation.
