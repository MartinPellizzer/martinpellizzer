Crassula perforata subsp. kougaensis

Crassula perforata subsp.

kougaensis, commonly known as the Koiha or Koe ha plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves with small, round holes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
