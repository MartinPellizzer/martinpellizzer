Carex anthericoides

Carex anthericoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are often surrounded by bracts.

Carex anthericoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
