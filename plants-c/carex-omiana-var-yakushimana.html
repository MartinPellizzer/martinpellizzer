Carex omiana var. yakushimana

Carex omiana var. yakushimana, commonly known as the Yakushimana sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
