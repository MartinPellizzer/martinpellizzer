Cissus acida

Cissus acida, commonly known as sour fig or ox blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its succulent, often prickly stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, sour-tasting berries. Cissus acida is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
