Clematis bigelovii var. arizonica

Clematis bigelovii var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
