Carex stenophylla f. elatior

Carex stenophylla f. elatior, commonly known as the slender-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex stenophylla species, which belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. This sedge typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along streambanks. The plant is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
