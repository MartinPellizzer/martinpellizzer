Chytraculia axillaris

Chytraculia axillaris, commonly known as the axillary chytracul, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a herbaceous plant with small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
