Chlidanthus boliviensis

Chlidanthus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its structure includes a woody stem with alternate, lanceolate leaves.

Chlidanthus boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
