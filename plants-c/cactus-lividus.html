Cactus lividus

Cactus lividus, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale blue-gray stems, which are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Despite its delicate flowers, the ghost cactus is highly drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
