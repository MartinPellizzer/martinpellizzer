Cucumis sativus f. hardwickii

Cucumis sativus f. hardwickii, commonly known as the hardwickii cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more elongated fruits compared to typical cucumber varieties. The plant typically produces dark green, spiny leaves and has a shorter vine compared to standard cucumbers. It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and use in ornamental gardening.

This form is less common in commercial agriculture but appreciated for its aesthetic value.
