Chrysophyllum guyanense

Chrysophyllum guyanense, commonly known as the Guyana cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, with a sweet, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
