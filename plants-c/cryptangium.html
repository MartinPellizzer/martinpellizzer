Cryptangium

Cryptangium, commonly known as the "crown vetch," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. Cryptangium species are often used for erosion control due to their extensive root systems and ability to fix nitrogen.

They are also popular in ornamental gardening for their attractive foliage and blooms.
