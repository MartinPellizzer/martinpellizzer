Calceolaria purpurea

Calceolaria purpurea, commonly known as the purple hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like petals that resemble small cups. The plant produces vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calceolaria purpurea prefers partial shade and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
