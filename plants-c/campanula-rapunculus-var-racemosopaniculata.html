Campanula rapunculus var. racemosopaniculata

Campanula rapunculus var. racemosopaniculata, commonly known as the racemose bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
