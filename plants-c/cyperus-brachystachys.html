Cyperus brachystachys

Cyperus brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Cyperus brachystachys is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its aesthetic appeal.
