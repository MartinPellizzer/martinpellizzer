Carpinus henryana var. chuana

Carpinus henryana var. chuana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits that disperse by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
