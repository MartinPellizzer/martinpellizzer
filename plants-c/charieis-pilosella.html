Charieis pilosella

Charieis pilosella, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. This plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Charieis pilosella is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
