Crataegus pruinosa var. magnifolia

Crataegus pruinosa var. magnifolia, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
