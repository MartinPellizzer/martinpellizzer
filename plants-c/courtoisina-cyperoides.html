Courtoisina cyperoides

Courtoisina cyperoides, commonly known as the African grass or wild panicgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its flowering stems are usually erect, bearing small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Courtoisina cyperoides is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
