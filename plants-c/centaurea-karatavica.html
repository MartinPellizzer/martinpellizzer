Centaurea karatavica

Centaurea karatavica, commonly known as Karatavica thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea karatavica is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
