Cleonia

Cleonia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that often display vibrant yellow or orange hues. Cleonia species are typically found in temperate regions, growing in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. They have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
