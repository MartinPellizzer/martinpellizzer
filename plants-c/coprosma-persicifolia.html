Coprosma persicifolia

Coprosma persicifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf coprosma, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth, waxy texture and a distinctive pinkish or reddish hue on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and compact growth habit.
