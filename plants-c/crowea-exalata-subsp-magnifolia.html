Crowea exalata subsp. magnifolia

Crowea exalata subsp.

magnifolia, commonly known as the showy crowea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The leaves are lance-shaped and evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year.
