Cerastium furcatum var. ibukiense

Cerastium furcatum var. ibukiense, commonly known as the Ibuki cushion plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
