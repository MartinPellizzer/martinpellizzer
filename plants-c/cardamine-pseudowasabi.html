Cardamine pseudowasabi

Cardamine pseudowasabi, commonly known as Japanese watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate white or greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Asian cuisine, similar to true wasabi.

Botanically, it is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
