Clerodendrum schmidtii var. glanduliferum

Clerodendrum schmidtii var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, which serve to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
