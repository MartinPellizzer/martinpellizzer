Cerasus nikaii

Cerasus nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet, and tart fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are pale pink to white and are highly valued for their ornamental beauty.

The fruits, known as sakura, are edible but are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and beverages.
