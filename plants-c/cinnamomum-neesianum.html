Cinnamomum neesianum

Cinnamomum neesianum, commonly known as the Sunda cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark brown to black berries that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
