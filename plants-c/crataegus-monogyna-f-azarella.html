Crataegus monogyna f. azarella

Crataegus monogyna f. azarella, commonly known as the Azarella hawthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, rounded form and thorny branches.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in late summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a glossy green upper surface. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, showing good tolerance to urban environments.
