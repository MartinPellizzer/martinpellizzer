Caralluma rivae

Caralluma rivae, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its succulent stems and small, showy flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems. Its flowers typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Caralluma rivae is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in treating ailments like diabetes and obesity.
