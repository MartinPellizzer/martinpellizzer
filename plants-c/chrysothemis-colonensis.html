Chrysothemis colonensis

Chrysothemis colonensis, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its columnar stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
