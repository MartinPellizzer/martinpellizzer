Capparis cuneiformis

Capparis cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, thorny stems and pinnate leaves with lance-shaped lobes. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by edible, tangy fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.

Capparis cuneiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional cuisine.
