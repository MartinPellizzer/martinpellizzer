Carex paludosa var. brachystachys

Carex paludosa var. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
