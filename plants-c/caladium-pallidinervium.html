Caladium pallidinervium

Caladium pallidinervium, commonly known as the pale-veined arrowleaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive pale veins that contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as houseplants.

Caladium pallidinervium typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.
