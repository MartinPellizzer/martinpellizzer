Carex melinacra

Carex melinacra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Carex melinacra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
