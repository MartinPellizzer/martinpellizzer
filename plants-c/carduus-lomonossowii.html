Carduus lomonossowii

Carduus lomonossowii, commonly known as the Russian thistle or goatweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Central Asia, this plant has become an invasive species in many parts of the world, including the United States. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

The plant's seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its rapid spread and ecological impact.
