Commelina eckloniana subsp. thikaensis

Commelina eckloniana subsp.

thikaensis, commonly known as the Thika commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, clustered flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
