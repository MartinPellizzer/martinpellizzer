Croton pauciflorus

Croton pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flower croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly East Africa. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or yellow. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and are not showy.

Croton pauciflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
