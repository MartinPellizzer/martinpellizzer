Cirsium chikushiense

Cirsium chikushiense, commonly known as the Chikushi thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
