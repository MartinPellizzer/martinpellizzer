Cyphostemma rowlandii

Cyphostemma rowlandii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, horn-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage and historical uses in traditional medicine.
