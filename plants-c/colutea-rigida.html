Colutea rigida

Colutea rigida, commonly known as rigid cowpea or goat's rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Colutea rigida is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
