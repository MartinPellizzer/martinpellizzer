Cupania polyzyga

Cupania polyzyga, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Cupania polyzyga is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
