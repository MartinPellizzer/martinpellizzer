Cytisus austriacus var. cinereus

Cytisus austriacus var. cinereus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to central Europe.

It features slender, arching stems covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
