Cotula spilanthes

Cotula spilanthes, commonly known as the African daisy or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, while the flowers are arranged in a flat, composite head.

Cotula spilanthes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
