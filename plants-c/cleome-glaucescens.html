Cleome glaucescens

Cleome glaucescens, commonly known as the blue cleome, is an annual flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
