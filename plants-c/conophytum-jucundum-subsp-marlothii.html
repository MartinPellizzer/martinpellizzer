Conophytum jucundum subsp. marlothii

Conophytum jucundum subsp.

marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's conophytum, is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, often with a bluish or grayish hue.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
