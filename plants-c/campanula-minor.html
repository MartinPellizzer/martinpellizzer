Campanula minor

Campanula minor, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula minor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
