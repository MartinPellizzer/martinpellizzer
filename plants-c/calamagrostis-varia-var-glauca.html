Calamagrostis varia var. glauca

Calamagrostis varia var. glauca, commonly known as blue bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and silver-gray inflorescence, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland restoration and water garden settings. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish in color, and it produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer.

Calamagrostis varia var. glauca is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
