Crassula cathcartensis

Crassula cathcartensis, commonly known as the Cathcart's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring or summer.

Crassula cathcartensis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
