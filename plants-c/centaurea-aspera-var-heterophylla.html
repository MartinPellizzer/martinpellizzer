Centaurea aspera var. heterophylla

Centaurea aspera var. heterophylla, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features spiny, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
