Chromolaena ortegae

Chromolaena ortegae, commonly known as the orange tree or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
