Croton manampetsae var. angustifolius

Croton manampetsae var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many plants with milky sap.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
