Campanula excisa f. annalaurae

Campanula excisa f. annalaurae, commonly known as the Annalaura bellflower, is a compact, ornamental perennial belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It features distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

This cultivar is valued for its elegant appearance and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
