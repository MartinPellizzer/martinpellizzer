Cytisus hirsutus f. falcatus

Cytisus hirsutus f. falcatus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, hairy leaflets that give the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
