Cryptomeria araucarioides

Cryptomeria araucarioides, commonly known as the Australian hoop pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody, spiky structures that release seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
