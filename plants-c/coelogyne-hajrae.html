Coelogyne hajrae

Coelogyne hajrae, commonly known as the Kashmiri orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayas.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, leathery leaf and produces a raceme of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip and are typically 5-7 cm in diameter. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
