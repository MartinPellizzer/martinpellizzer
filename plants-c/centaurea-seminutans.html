Centaurea seminutans

Centaurea seminutans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its foliage is narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers.

Centaurea seminutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
