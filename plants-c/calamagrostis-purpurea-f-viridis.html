Calamagrostis purpurea f. viridis

Calamagrostis purpurea f. viridis, commonly known as the green fountain grass, is a variant of the purple moor grass.

It is a perennial grass that features narrow, upright blades with a distinctive green coloration. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It is native to Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This ornamental grass is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to add texture to landscapes.
