Ceropegia acicularis

Ceropegia acicularis, commonly known as the needle leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its fleshy, spine-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, adapted for pollination by specific insects. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in rocky soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
