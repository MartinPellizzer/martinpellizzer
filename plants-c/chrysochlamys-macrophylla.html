Chrysochlamys macrophylla

Chrysochlamys macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its golden-yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Chrysochlamys macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
