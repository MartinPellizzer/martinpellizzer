Chaetaria setacea

Chaetaria setacea, commonly known as the hairy hairgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and long, slender leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often curved or twisted.

Chaetaria setacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native environments.
