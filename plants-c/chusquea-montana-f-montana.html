Chusquea montana f. montana

Chusquea montana f. montana, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance and hardiness.
