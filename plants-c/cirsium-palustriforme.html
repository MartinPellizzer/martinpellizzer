Cirsium palustriforme

Cirsium palustriforme, commonly known as the marsh thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and have a distinctive basal rosette structure.

Cirsium palustriforme plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various insects and wildlife.
