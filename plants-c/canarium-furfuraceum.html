Canarium furfuraceum

Canarium furfuraceum, commonly known as the rough canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
