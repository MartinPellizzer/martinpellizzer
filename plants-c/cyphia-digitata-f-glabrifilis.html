Cyphia digitata f. glabrifilis

Cyphia digitata f. glabrifilis, commonly known as the glabrous-leaved cyphia, is a botanical variant of the Cyphia digitata species.

It is a small shrub native to specific regions of South Africa, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and habitat specificity.
