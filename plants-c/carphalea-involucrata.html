Carphalea involucrata

Carphalea involucrata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange flowers that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-care-for blooms.

Carphalea involucrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
