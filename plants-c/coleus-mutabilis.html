Coleus mutabilis

Coleus mutabilis, commonly known as the "changing color coleus," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that changes with the seasons, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coleus mutabilis is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
