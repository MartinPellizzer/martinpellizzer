Croton choristadenius

Croton choristadenius, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that range from yellow to red and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
