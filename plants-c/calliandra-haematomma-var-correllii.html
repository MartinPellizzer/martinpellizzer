Calliandra haematomma var. correllii

Calliandra haematomma var. correllii, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange inflorescences, which resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and can exude a reddish sap when wounded.
