Cleistes romeroana

Cleistes romeroana, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features showy, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or clay soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
