Celastrus myrtifolius

Celastrus myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leafed staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in short inflorescences. Its fruits are woody, fleshy capsules that split open to release seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
