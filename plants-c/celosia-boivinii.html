Celosia boivinii

Celosia boivinii, commonly known as the flame flower or red dwarf celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are clustered in dense, spiky formations.

Celosia boivinii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.
