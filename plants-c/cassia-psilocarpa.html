Cassia psilocarpa

Cassia psilocarpa, commonly known as the sacred flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

However, it is important to note that some parts of the plant may contain psychoactive compounds, requiring caution in handling.
