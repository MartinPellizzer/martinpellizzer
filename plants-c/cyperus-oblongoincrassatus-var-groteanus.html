Cyperus oblongoincrassatus var. groteanus

Cyperus oblongoincrassatus var. groteanus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various moist habitats, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
