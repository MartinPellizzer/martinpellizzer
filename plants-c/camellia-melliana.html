Camellia melliana

Camellia melliana, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Camellia melliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
