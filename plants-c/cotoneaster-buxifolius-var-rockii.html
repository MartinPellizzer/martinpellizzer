Cotoneaster buxifolius var. rockii

Cotoneaster buxifolius var. rockii, commonly known as the Rock's boxleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is particularly valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
