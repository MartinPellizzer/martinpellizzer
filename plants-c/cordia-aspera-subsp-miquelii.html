Cordia aspera subsp. miquelii

Cordia aspera subsp.

miquelii, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
