Commelina gerrardii

Commelina gerrardii, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Commelina genus and is part of the Commelinales order. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Commelina gerrardii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
