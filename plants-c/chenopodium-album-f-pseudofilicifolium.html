Chenopodium album f. pseudofilicifolium

Chenopodium album f. pseudofilicifolium, commonly known as goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This subspecies exhibits elongated, filiform leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to the typical form. It is an annual herbaceous plant that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it both a weed and a potential forage plant in certain ecological contexts.
