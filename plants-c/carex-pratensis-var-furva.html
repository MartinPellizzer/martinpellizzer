Carex pratensis var. furva

Carex pratensis var. furva, commonly known as the dark-fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Carex pratensis var. furva plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and supporting local biodiversity.
