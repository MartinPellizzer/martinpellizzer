Capsella subalgida

Capsella subalgida, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear.

It is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are hairy and lobed. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in fields.

The species is part of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its unique seed pods that split open when mature.
