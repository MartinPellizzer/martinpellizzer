Crotalaria longipes

Crotalaria longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and pairs of opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive seed pods that are elongated and often curved. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
