Calamus fissus

Calamus fissus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spadix, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Calamus fissus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
