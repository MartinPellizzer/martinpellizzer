Cirsium americanum

Cirsium americanum, commonly known as American thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, prickly edges, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

American thistle is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
