Crepis arenaria subsp. arenaria

Crepis arenaria subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand couch grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in sandy and gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

It is often found in coastal dunes, steppes, and other disturbed soils, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
