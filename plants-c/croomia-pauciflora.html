Croomia pauciflora

Croomia pauciflora, commonly known as the dwarf lily or the Carolina lily, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
