Carex jepsonii

Carex jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and along stream banks. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex jepsonii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
