Conospermum acinacifolium

Conospermum acinacifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Conospermum acinacifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
