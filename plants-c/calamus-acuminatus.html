Calamus acuminatus

Calamus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tailed arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of Calamus acuminatus make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
