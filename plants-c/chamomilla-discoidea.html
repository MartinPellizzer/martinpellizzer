Chamomilla discoidea

Chamomilla discoidea, commonly known as the disc chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
