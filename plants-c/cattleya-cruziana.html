Cattleya cruziana

Cattleya cruziana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya cruziana is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
