Chenopodium album var. spicatum

Chenopodium album var. spicatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and features narrow, lobed leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
