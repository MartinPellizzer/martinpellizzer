Circaea × intermedia

Circaea × intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wood sorrel, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both *Circaea lutetiana* and *Circaea americana*.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

The leaves and flowers are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
