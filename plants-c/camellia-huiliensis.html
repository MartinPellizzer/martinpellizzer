Camellia huiliensis

Camellia huiliensis, commonly known as the Huili camellia, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and woody stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia huiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
