Caroxylon ericoides

Caroxylon ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath or dwarf Erica, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is characteristic of the Cape Floristic Region.

Caroxylon ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
