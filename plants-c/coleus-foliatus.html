Coleus foliatus

Coleus foliatus, commonly known as the ornamental coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, pink, and yellow. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking foliage rather than its flowers.

Coleus foliatus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
