Carex dieckii

Carex dieckii, commonly known as the Dieck's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex dieckii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
