Cucumis melo adana

Cucumis melo adana, commonly known as the Adana melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruit with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic, yellow-orange flesh. The plant is a trailing vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. It requires a long growing season and is often cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, ensuring maximum flavor and sweetness.
