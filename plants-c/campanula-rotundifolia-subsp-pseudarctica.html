Campanula rotundifolia subsp. pseudarctica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

pseudarctica, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, rounded leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
