Calligonum klementzii

Calligonum klementzii, commonly known as the desert sandspurry, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Calligonum klementzii is well adapted to drought and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
