Carex fritschii

Carex fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to certain regions of Asia, this plant typically grows in moist or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable environments.
