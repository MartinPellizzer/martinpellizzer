Convolvulus pilosella

Convolvulus pilosella, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Convolvulus pilosella is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.
