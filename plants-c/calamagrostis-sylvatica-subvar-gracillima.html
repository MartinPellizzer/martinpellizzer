Calamagrostis sylvatica subvar. gracillima

Calamagrostis sylvatica subvar. gracillima, commonly known as the slender woodland reed, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, arching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. Its inflorescence consists of loose, panicle-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in wetland habitats.
