Coreocarpus arizonicus var. arizonicus

Coreocarpus arizonicus var. arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona coreocarpus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive fruit capsules that split open to release seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
