Chrysothamnus latifolius

Chrysothamnus latifolius, commonly known as golden aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silver-gray foliage and a bushy growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its scientific name reflects its golden color and broad leaves, with "latifolius" meaning broad-leaved in Latin.
