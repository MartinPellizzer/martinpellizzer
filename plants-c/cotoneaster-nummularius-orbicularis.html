Cotoneaster nummularius orbicularis

Cotoneaster nummularius orbicularis, commonly known as the round-leaf cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded, glossy leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Cotoneaster nummularius orbicularis is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of food for wildlife.
