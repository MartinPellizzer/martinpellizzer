Cissus stenopoda

Cissus stenopoda, commonly known as the rock fig or stone cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
