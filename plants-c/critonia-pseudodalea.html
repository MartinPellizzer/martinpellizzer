Critonia pseudodalea

Critonia pseudodalea, commonly known as the false dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
