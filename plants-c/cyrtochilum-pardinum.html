Cyrtochilum pardinum

Cyrtochilum pardinum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. The plant typically produces small, dark green leaves and bears fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive lip. Cyrtochilum pardinum thrives in rocky or shaded environments, often growing on cliffs or in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
