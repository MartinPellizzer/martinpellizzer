Camphora sumatrana

Camphora sumatrana, commonly known as Indonesian camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. Its bark and leaves contain camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

Camphora sumatrana is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in tropical forests.
