Cassia dispar

Cassia dispar, commonly known as the sicklepod cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in terminal clusters.

Cassia dispar produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
