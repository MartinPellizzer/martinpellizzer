Cattleya trianae f. alba

Cattleya trianae f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered Cattleya trianae, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and fragrant blooms.
