Centaurea achaia var. submutica

Centaurea achaia var. submutica, commonly known as the Greek knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with purple markings, typical of the Centaurea genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
