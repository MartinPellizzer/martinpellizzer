Calycobolus campanulatus

Calycobolus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped calycobolus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its unique floral structure and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
