Canna barbadica

Canna barbadica, commonly known as the giant cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three feet in length, often with a reddish or purple tint along the edges. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by vibrant, colorful bracts that range from yellow to red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Canna barbadica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
