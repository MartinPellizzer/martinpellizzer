Coronilla orientalis

Coronilla orientalis, commonly known as the oriental crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has been introduced to other parts of the world as a forage crop and for soil stabilization.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for improving soil fertility.
