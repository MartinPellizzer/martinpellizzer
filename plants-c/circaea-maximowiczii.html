Circaea maximowiczii

Circaea maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian white bead, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold climates of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Circaea maximowiczii is often found in alpine and subarctic regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
