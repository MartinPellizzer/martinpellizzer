Crepis athoa, commonly known as the Atho's mouse-ear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal areas or on disturbed ground.
