Chamaecrista pumila

Chamaecrista pumila, commonly known as the small burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny pod that contains several seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
