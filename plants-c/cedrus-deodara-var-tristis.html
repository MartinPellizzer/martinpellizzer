Cedrus deodara var. tristis

Cedrus deodara var. tristis, commonly known as the Kashmir cedar, is a variant of the deodar cedar native to the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal crown and scale-like leaves. The cones are typically pendulous and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions at high altitudes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine and woodworking.
