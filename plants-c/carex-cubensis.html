Carex cubensis

Carex cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Carex cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
