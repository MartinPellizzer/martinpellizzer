Canarium clementis var. perumbrinum

Canarium clementis var. perumbrinum, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It produces a distinctive fruit with a smooth, black skin and a creamy, sweet pulp that is often compared to chocolate. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit and potential medicinal properties.
