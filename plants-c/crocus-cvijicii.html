Crocus cvijicii

Crocus cvijicii, commonly known as the Balkan crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
