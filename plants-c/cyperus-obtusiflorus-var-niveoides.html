Cyperus obtusiflorus var. niveoides

Cyperus obtusiflorus var. niveoides, commonly known as the snowdrop sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
