Croton sumatranus

Croton sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Croton sumatranus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
