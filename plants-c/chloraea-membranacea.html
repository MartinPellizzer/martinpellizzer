Chloraea membranacea

Chloraea membranacea, commonly known as the Paperbark Pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, peeling bark that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Chloraea membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
