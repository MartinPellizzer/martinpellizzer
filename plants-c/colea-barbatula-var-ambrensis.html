Colea barbatula var. ambrensis

Colea barbatula var. ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrense Woolly Cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and geographic distribution compared to other members of the Colea genus.
