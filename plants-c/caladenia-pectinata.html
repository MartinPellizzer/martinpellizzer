Caladenia pectinata

Caladenia pectinata, commonly known as the Pectinata Spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy, spider-like flower with a distinctive, narrow labellum. The plant typically blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant pink to purple flowers. Caladenia pectinata is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.
