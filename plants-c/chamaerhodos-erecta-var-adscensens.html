Chamaerhodos erecta var. adscensens

Chamaerhodos erecta var. adscensens, commonly known as the upright chamaerhodos, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features upright, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, while its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and often pink or red in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
