Crotalaria capitata

Crotalaria capitata, commonly known as the clustered crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Crotalaria capitata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
