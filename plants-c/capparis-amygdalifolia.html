Capparis amygdalifolia

Capparis amygdalifolia, commonly known as the almond-flowered caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Capparis amygdalifolia is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
