Crataegus × pyriformis

Crataegus × pyriformis, commonly known as the oval hawthorn, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped fruits and thorny branches, which are typical features of the hawthorn genus. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, red or yellow berry that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
