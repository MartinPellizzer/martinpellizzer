Crossoglossa aurantiilineata

Crossoglossa aurantiilineata, commonly known as the red-line orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange and red stripes on its petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small but numerous, arranged in a dense inflorescence.

Crossoglossa aurantiilineata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
