Commelina barbata

Commelina barbata, commonly known as the barbed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive central spur. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with heart-shaped leaves and slender stems. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in the early morning, hence its common name.

Commelina barbata is adapted to moist environments and is often found in disturbed soils or near water sources.
