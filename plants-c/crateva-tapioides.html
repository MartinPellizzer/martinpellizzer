Crateva tapioides

Crateva tapioides, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble an arrowhead. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a ground cover.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
