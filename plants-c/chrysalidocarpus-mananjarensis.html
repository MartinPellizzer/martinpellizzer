Chrysalidocarpus mananjarensis

Chrysalidocarpus mananjarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, spiny trunk and rosette of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

It is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a resilient species in tropical climates.
