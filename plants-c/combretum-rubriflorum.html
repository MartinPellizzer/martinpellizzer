Combretum rubriflorum

Combretum rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in panicles, which bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
