Carex bordzilowskii

Carex bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Karakachan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are usually inconspicuous.

Carex bordzilowskii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
