Cyperus piptolepis

Cyperus piptolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or pale in color. It is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus piptolepis is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
