Cestrum acuminatum

Cestrum acuminatum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.
