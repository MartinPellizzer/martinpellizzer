Clethra nutantiflora

Clethra nutantiflora, commonly known as the weeping clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
