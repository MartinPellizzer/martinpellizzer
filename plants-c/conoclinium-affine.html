Conoclinium affine

Conoclinium affine, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, clustered blooms and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Conoclinium affine is also a host plant for several species of butterflies, making it ecologically significant.
