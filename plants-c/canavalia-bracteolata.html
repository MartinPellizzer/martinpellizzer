Canavalia bracteolata

Canavalia bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines, while the plant itself is valued for its ornamental and agricultural significance.

Canavalia bracteolata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.
