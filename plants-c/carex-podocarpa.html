Carex podocarpa

Carex podocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, bogs, and stream sides. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often dark green in color.

Carex podocarpa produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
