Capsicum indicum var. pyramidale

Capsicum indicum var. pyramidale, commonly known as the bird pepper or Indian pepper, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, often colorful fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is usually oblong or pyramidal, hence the variety name "pyramidale," and is used in traditional cuisines for its mild to spicy flavor.
