Calceolaria humilis

Calceolaria humilis, commonly known as the humble slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant is herbaceous, with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
