Cerastium gibraltaricum var. viride

Cerastium gibraltaricum var. viride, commonly known as the Gibraltar green mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright green leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on slender stems.
