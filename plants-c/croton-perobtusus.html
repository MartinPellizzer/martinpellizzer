Croton perobtusus

Croton perobtusus, commonly known as the broad-leafed croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a dense canopy of foliage.

Croton perobtusus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
