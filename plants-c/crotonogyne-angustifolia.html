Crotonogyne angustifolia

Crotonogyne angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem and palmate venation in its leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
