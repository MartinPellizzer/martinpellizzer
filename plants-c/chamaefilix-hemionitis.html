Chamaefilix hemionitis

Chamaefilix hemionitis, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
