Closia anthemoides

Closia anthemoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Closia anthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
