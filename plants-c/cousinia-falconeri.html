Cousinia falconeri

Cousinia falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, pinkish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia falconeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.
