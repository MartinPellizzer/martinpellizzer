Cullen obtusifolium

Cullen obtusifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved cullen, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cullen obtusifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
