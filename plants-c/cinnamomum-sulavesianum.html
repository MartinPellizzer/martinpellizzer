Cinnamomum sulavesianum

Cinnamomum sulavesianum, commonly known as the Sulawesi cinnamon or yellow cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other cinnamon and laurel species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, slightly oily texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, oval berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic bark and leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
