Caladium crassipes

Caladium crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Caladium crassipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly in shaded garden settings.

Its leaves display vibrant colors ranging from green to red, depending on environmental conditions and genetic factors.
