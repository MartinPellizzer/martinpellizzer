Cirsium eriophorum subsp. giraudiasii

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

giraudiasii, commonly known as the Giraud's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish-blue flower heads.

The subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Spain and France. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, showy inflorescence.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
