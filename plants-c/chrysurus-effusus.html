Chrysurus effusus

Chrysurus effusus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Chrysurus effusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
