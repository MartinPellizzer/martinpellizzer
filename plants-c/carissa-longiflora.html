Carissa longiflora

Carissa longiflora, commonly known as the yellow bells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring dense, thorny branches and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, yellow, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Carissa longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
