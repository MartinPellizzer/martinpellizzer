Carum tortuosum

Carum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, thread-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas. Its seeds, similar in appearance to those of caraway, are used in some traditional herbal remedies.

Carum tortuosum is valued for its fragrance and has been used in perfumery and culinary applications.
