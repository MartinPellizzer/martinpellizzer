Calamagrostis youngii

Calamagrostis youngii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.

Calamagrostis youngii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
