Cordia insignis var. glabrifolia

Cordia insignis var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved glorybower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
