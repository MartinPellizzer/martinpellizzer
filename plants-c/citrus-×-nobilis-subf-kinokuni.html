Citrus × nobilis subf. kinokuni

Citrus × nobilis subf.

kinokuni, commonly known as the kumquat, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn orange when mature.

The fruit has a thin, edible peel and a segmented, sweet flesh. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant in subtropical climates.
