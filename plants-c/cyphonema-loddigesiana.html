Cyphonema loddigesiana

Cyphonema loddigesiana, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of slow-moving or still water bodies. Its flowers, which are typically white or pink, bloom above the water surface and attract pollinators such as beetles.

Cyphonema loddigesiana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and contributing to water quality through oxygenation.
