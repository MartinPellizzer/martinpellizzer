Celtis caucasica

Celtis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are borne in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Celtis caucasica is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
