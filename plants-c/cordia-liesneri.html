Cordia liesneri

Cordia liesneri, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with thick, rough bark and large, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
