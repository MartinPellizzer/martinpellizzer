Cumarinia

Cumarinia, commonly known as the sweet clover.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant contains coumarin, a compound responsible for its distinctive sweet scent and potential medicinal properties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Cumarinia has been used historically in herbal medicine for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects.
