Cerasus × lannesiana

Cerasus × lannesiana, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are widely used in cooking and baking. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it blooms in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit ripens in late summer, turning from green to a deep red or black depending on the variety.
