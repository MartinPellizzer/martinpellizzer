Cirsium spathulatum var. parnassi

Cirsium spathulatum var. parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its distinctive appearance and habitat make it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
