Carex musartiana

Carex musartiana, commonly known as the muskgrass, is a species of sedge native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Carex musartiana plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
