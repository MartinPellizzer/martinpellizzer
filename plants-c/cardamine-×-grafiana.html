Cardamine × grafiana

Cardamine × grafiana, commonly known as the graphic buttercup, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cardamine bulbifera and Cardamine hirsuta.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is characterized by a distinctive pattern of leaflets and a hairy texture.

Cardamine × grafiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
