Cineraria aspasia

Cineraria aspasia, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cineraria aspasia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
