Cornus canadensis subsp. pristina

Cornus canadensis subsp.

pristina, commonly known as the slender dogwood, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its fruit is a small, red drupe.

This subspecies is often found in the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.
