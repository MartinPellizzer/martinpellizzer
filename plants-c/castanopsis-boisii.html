Castanopsis boisii

Castanopsis boisii, commonly known as the African chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Castanopsis boisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
