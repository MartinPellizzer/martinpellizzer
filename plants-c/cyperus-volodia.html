Cyperus volodia

Cyperus volodia, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Cyperus volodia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed aquatic habitats.
