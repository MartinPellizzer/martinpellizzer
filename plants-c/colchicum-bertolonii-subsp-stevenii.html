Colchicum bertolonii subsp. stevenii

Colchicum bertolonii subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as Steven's autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces striking violet-blue to purple flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm-like structure for storage. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique bloom.
