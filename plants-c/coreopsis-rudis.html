Coreopsis rudis

Coreopsis rudis, commonly known as the sea coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it withstand salty air and wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shorelines.

Coreopsis rudis is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
