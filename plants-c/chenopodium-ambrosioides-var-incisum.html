Chenopodium ambrosioides var. incisum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. incisum, commonly known as wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
