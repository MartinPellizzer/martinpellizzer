Cytisus purpureus f. erectus

Cytisus purpureus f. erectus, commonly known as the purple broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, thornless stems that are often upright, giving it a compact and bushy appearance. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
