Crotalaria lineata

Crotalaria lineata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are edible and can be used in various culinary applications.
