Clematis simplicifolia

Clematis simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis simplicifolia is relatively low maintenance and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
