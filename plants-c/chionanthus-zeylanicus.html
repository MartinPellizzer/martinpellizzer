Chionanthus zeylanicus

Chionanthus zeylanicus, commonly known as the whiteflower tree or Indian snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Chionanthus zeylanicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
