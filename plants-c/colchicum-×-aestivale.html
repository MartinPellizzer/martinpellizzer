Colchicum × aestivale

Colchicum × aestivale, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a hybrid plant in the Colchicaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm, which stores nutrients for the flowering season. Its flowers typically have six petals and are borne on tall, slender stalks.

Colchicum × aestivale is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
