Crucifera thaliana

Crucifera thaliana, commonly known as Arabidopsis thaliana, is a small flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is widely recognized as a model organism in plant biology due to its short life cycle, small genome, and ease of genetic manipulation. Arabidopsis thaliana has simple morphology, with rosette-shaped leaves and small white flowers. It is native to Eurasia and thrives in a variety of soil types and climates.

The plant's genetic and molecular research has significantly advanced our understanding of plant development, genetics, and responses to environmental stress.
