Carex contigua f. pallida

Carex contigua f. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are generally lighter in color compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with partial to full sun.

This sedges are important for wildlife, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
