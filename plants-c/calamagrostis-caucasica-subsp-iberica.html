Calamagrostis caucasica subsp. iberica

Calamagrostis caucasica subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Caucasian sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often arranged in panicles. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
