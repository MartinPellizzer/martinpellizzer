Crucifera graminifolia

Crucifera graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and racemose inflorescence with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a type of dry, dehiscent fruit typical of the crucifer family.

The plant has been studied for its potential role in ecological succession and its allelopathic properties.
