Crataegus cruenta

Crataegus cruenta, commonly known as the bloody crassness, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and swamps, and it is often found in the southeastern United States.

The species is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
