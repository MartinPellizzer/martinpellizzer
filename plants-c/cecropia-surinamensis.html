Cecropia surinamensis

Cecropia surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam ceiba, is a fast-growing, tree-like plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and a tall, straight trunk that can reach heights of up to 40 meters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. It produces woody pods containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
