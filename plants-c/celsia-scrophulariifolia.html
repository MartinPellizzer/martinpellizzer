Celsia scrophulariifolia

Celsia scrophulariifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved celsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically tubular and come in shades of pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Celsia scrophulariifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
