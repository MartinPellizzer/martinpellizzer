Centaurea macedonica

Centaurea macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea macedonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
