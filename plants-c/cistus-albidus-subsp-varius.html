Cistus albidus subsp. varius

Cistus albidus subsp.

varius, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in arid conditions.
