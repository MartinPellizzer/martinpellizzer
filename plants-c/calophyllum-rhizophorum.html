Calophyllum rhizophorum

Calophyllum rhizophorum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is also valued for its durable, heavy timber.
