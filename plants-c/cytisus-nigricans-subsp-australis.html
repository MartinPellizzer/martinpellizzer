Cytisus nigricans subsp. australis

Cytisus nigricans subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian broom, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features slender, often thorned stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several leaflets, and it produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
