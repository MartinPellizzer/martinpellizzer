Cattleya storeyi

Cattleya storeyi, commonly known as the Storey's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Cattleya storeyi is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
