Croton piptocalyx

Croton piptocalyx, commonly known as the white croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit, it is a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
