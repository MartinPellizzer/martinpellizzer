Centaurea microlepis

Centaurea microlepis, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often hairy. Its flowers are small, typically blue or purple, and are arranged in compact clusters.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
