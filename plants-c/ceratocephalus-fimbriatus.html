Ceratocephalus fimbriatus

Ceratocephalus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed sandbur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny seed pods that release seeds when disturbed. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Ceratocephalus fimbriatus is adapted to dry conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
