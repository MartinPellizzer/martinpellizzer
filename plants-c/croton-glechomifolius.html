Croton glechomifolius

Croton glechomifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, ranging from ovate to lanceolate. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its bark and leaves contain latex, which can be toxic and has been used in traditional medicine.

Croton glechomifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
