Cryosophila mocinoi

Cryosophila mocinoi, commonly known as the snow lotus, is a rare alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, white to pink flowers during the brief summer months. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, Cryosophila mocinoi is considered a vulnerable species.
