Colona hospita

Colona hospita, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray florets and yellow disk florets, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Colona hospita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
