Cereus pentlandii var. pulchellus

Cereus pentlandii var. pulchellus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a fascinating cactus species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique symbiotic relationship with a specific type of photosynthetic fungus, which provides it with additional nutrients. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Despite its small size, it is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
