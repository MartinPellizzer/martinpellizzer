Cephalaria angustifolia

Cephalaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its slender, angular stems are often adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.
