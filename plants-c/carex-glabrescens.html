Carex glabrescens

Carex glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous, with a distinctive triangular stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne in a single terminal spike.

Carex glabrescens plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
