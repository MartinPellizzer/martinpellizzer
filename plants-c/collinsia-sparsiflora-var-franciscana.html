Collinsia sparsiflora var. franciscana

Collinsia sparsiflora var. franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
