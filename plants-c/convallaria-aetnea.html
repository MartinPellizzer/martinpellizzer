Convallaria aetnea

Convallaria aetnea, commonly known as the silver bell lily, is a rare and elusive species of flowering plant belonging to the Convallariaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a silvery sheen, contributing to its common name.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Convallaria aetnea is considered a delicate and endangered species in many regions.
