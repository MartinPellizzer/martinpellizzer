Clinopodium glabrum

Clinopodium glabrum, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Clinopodium glabrum is often used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and antispasmodic properties.
