Cracca pisiformis

Cracca pisiformis, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in clusters and are often seen in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive shape that helps in identification.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in various habitats.
