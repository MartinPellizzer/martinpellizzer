Cuphea glauca

Cuphea glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
