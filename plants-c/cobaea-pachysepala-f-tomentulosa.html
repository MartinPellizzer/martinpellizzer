Cobaea pachysepala f. tomentulosa

Cobaea pachysepala f. tomentulosa, commonly known as the woolly giant cup, is a striking plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and velvety foliage.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is native to South America. The plant features thick, hairy stems and leaves that are covered in a dense, white tomentum, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive tubular shape that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and botanical collections.
