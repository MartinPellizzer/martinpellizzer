Commidendrum burchellii

Commidendrum burchellii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spike-like flower clusters that are typically bright red or orange, giving it its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces showy flowers throughout the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens in regions with warm climates.
