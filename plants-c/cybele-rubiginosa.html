Cybele rubiginosa

Cybele rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty cybele, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
