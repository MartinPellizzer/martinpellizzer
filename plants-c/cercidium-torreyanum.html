Cercidium torreyanum

Cercidium torreyanum, commonly known as the Torrey's palo verde, is a small, deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, green bark and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically shed during the dry season. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and rocky soils.

Cercidium torreyanum plays an important role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
