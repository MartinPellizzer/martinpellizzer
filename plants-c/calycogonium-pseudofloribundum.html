Calycogonium pseudofloribundum

Calycogonium pseudofloribundum, commonly known as the false flower cucumber, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble those of true cucurbits, though it rarely produces edible fruit. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has a vigorous climbing habit.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in ecological interactions within its native habitat.
