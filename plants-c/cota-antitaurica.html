Cota antitaurica

Cota antitaurica, commonly known as the Antitauric thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Cota antitaurica is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands.
