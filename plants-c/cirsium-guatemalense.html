Cirsium guatemalense

Cirsium guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
