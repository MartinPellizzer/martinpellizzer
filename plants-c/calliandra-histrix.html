Calliandra histrix

Calliandra histrix, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant is known for its dense, thorny branches and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration. Its flowers, which are typically pink or red, are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators.

Calliandra histrix is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
