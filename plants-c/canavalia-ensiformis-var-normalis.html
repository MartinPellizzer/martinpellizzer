Canavalia ensiformis var. normalis

Canavalia ensiformis var. normalis, commonly known as sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural systems.
