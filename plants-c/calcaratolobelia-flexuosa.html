Calcaratolobelia flexuosa

Calcaratolobelia flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Calcaratolobelia flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
