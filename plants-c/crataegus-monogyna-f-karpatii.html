Crataegus monogyna f. karpatii

Crataegus monogyna f. karpatii, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features, including smaller, more clustered flowers and narrower leaves. Native to the Carpathian Mountains, this form exhibits adaptations to the region's temperate climate and rocky soils. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
