Clavimyrtus claviflora

Clavimyrtus claviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Clavimyrtus claviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
