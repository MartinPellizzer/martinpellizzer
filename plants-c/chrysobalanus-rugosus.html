Chrysobalanus rugosus

Chrysobalanus rugosus, commonly known as the rough-shelled chrysobalanus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit covering, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
