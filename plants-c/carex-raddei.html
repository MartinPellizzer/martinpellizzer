Carex raddei

Carex raddei, commonly known as the Radde's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex raddei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
