Consolida arenaria

Consolida arenaria, commonly known as the sand consolida, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is a hardy annual that thrives in full sun and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
