Carex × ketonensis

Carex × ketonensis, commonly known as the Ketonensian sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually brown or purplish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex × ketonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
