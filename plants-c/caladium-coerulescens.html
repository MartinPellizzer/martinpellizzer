Caladium coerulescens

Caladium coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-veined caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of green, pink, and white, with prominent blue veins. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used as a ornamental plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Caladium coerulescens is typically grown from tubers and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.
