Cissus nymphaeifolia

Cissus nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water grape vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of round, fleshy fruits resembling grapes. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

Cissus nymphaeifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
