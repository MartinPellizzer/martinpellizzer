Carex honglinii

Carex honglinii, commonly known as Honglin sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex honglinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
