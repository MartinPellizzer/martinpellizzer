Cotulina

Cotulina, commonly known as the common cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks. Cotulina has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
