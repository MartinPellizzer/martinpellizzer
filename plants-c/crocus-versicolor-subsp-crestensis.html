Crocus versicolor subsp. crestensis

Crocus versicolor subsp.

crestensis, commonly known as the Crested Crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its striking violet and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne on a tall stem and have a distinct crested appearance, giving it its common name.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
