Carex sachalinensis var. tenuinervis

Carex sachalinensis var. tenuinervis, commonly known as the slender-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green or brown in color.

The leaves are generally narrow and have a distinct, parallel venation pattern, which is a key identifying feature of this variety.
