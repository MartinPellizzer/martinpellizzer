Camissonia crassifolia

Camissonia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf camissonia, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Camissonia crassifolia is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is considered a rare and endangered species.
