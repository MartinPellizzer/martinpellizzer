Cymbopogon nardus var. confertiflorus

Cymbopogon nardus var. confertiflorus, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Its essential oils, particularly citral, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.
