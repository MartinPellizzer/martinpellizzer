Cyclostemon microphyllus

Cyclostemon microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved cyclostemon, is a member of the Arecaceae family, commonly referred to as the palm family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible, coconut-like fruits that are used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation efforts.
