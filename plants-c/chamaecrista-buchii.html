Chamaecrista buchii

Chamaecrista buchii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its fruit is a spiny pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Chamaecrista buchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
