Cyathea pubens

Cyathea pubens, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a mass of soft, hairy tissue, which gives it its common name. Cyathea pubens is a slow-growing plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
