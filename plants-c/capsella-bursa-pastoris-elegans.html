Capsella bursa-pastoris elegans

Capsella bursa-pastoris elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in gardens and along roadsides, where it can become a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
