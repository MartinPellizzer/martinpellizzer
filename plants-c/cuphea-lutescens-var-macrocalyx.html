Cuphea lutescens var. macrocalyx

Cuphea lutescens var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered cuphea, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
