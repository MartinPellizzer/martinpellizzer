Cajanus

Cajanus, commonly known as pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. Cajanus species, such as Cajanus cajan, are widely cultivated for their edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

It also plays an important role in traditional agriculture and is used in various cuisines around the world.
