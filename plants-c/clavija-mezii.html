Clavija mezii

Clavija mezii, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Clavija mezii is often used in traditional medicine and landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
