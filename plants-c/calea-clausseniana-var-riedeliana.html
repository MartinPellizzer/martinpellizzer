Calea clausseniana var. riedeliana

Calea clausseniana var. riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's candleplant, is a striking herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which consists of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, elongated cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
