Cirsium × erucagineum var. hallerianum

Cirsium × erucagineum var. hallerianum, commonly known as the Haller's thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with coarse teeth and a rough texture. This variety is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It serves as a food source for certain butterfly species and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
