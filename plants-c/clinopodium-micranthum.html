Clinopodium micranthum

Clinopodium micranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like aroma when crushed.
