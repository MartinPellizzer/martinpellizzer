Clerodendrum aurantiacum

Clerodendrum aurantiacum, commonly known as the Orange Flame Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clerodendrum aurantiacum is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil.
