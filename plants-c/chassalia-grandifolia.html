Chassalia grandifolia

Chassalia grandifolia, commonly known as the giant chassalia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters in height, with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in the late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chassalia grandifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
