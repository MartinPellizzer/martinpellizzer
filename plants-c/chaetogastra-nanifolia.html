Chaetogastra nanifolia

Chaetogastra nanifolia, commonly known as the dwarf featherleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are finely divided and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecosystem.
