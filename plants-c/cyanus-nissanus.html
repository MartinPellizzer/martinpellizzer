Cyanus nissanus

Cyanus nissanus, commonly known as the Nissan's Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant produces showy blue or purple ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cyanus nissanus typically grows in open, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
