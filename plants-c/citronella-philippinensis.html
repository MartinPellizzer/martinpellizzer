Citronella philippinensis

Citronella philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine citronella, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils are also utilized in natural insect repellents due to their strong citronella scent.
