Cleomella obtusifolia

Cleomella obtusifolia, commonly known as the bluntleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically ovate with rounded tips. Cleomella obtusifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
