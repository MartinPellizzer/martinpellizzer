Cassiope mertensiana subsp. gracilis

Cassiope mertensiana subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the alpine regions of western North America. It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, woolly hair. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky, mountainous terrain where it forms dense, mat-like growths.
