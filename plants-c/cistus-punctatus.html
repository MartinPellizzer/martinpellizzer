Cistus punctatus

Cistus punctatus, commonly known as the dotted rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves and is often found growing on rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus punctatus plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
