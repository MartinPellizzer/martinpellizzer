Chrysanthemum platylepis

Chrysanthemum platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
