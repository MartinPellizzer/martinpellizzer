Chaetochloa corrugata

Chaetochloa corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag or undulating leaf patterns, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dense mats, forming a low-growing vegetation that thrives in waterlogged or swampy conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in its often saturated habitat.

Chaetochloa corrugata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
