Crataegus philippii

Crataegus philippii, commonly known as the Philippi hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Crataegus philippii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
