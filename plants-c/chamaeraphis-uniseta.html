Chamaeraphis uniseta

Chamaeraphis uniseta, commonly known as the single-set glasswort, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in saline and brackish environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Chamaeraphis uniseta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize shorelines and filter water.
