Callistemon speciosus var. glaucus

Callistemon speciosus var. glaucus, commonly known as the silver bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive glaucous appearance. Its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters and are highly attractive to birds and insects.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
