Cephalaria armerioides

Cephalaria armerioides, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its basal leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the local flora diversity.
