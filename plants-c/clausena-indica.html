Clausena indica

Clausena indica, commonly known as the Indian sour plum or kathal, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are highly acidic and used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Clausena indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
