Chamaemelum reuterianum

Chamaemelum reuterianum, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and emit a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
