Callicarpa quaternifolia

Callicarpa quaternifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
