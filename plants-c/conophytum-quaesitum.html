Conophytum quaesitum

Conophytum quaesitum, commonly known as the "Desert Rose" or "Corky Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow during the warmer months. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Conophytum quaesitum requires minimal water and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
