Centaurea epapposa

Centaurea epapposa, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Centaurea epapposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
