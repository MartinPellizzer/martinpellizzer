Camara canescens

Camara canescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.

Camara canescens is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
