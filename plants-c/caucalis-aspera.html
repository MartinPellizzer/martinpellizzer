Caucalis aspera

Caucalis aspera, commonly known as the rough caucalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas. Its small, white or greenish flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name.
