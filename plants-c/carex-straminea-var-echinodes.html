Carex straminea var. echinodes

Carex straminea var. echinodes, commonly known as the spiky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
