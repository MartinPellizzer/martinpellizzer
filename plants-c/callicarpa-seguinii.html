Callicarpa seguinii

Callicarpa seguinii, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through the winter, providing ornamental value. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
