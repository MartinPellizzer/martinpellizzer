Centaurea kandavanensis

Centaurea kandavanensis, commonly known as the Kandavan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually spiny and gray-green, while its flowers range from pink to purple, adding visual interest to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
