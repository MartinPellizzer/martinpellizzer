Calamagrostis canadensis var. dubia

Calamagrostis canadensis var. dubia, commonly known as Canadian bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. This variety is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including erosion control and habitat provision for wildlife.
