Carex frigida var. brevirostris

Carex frigida var. brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cold climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitats.
