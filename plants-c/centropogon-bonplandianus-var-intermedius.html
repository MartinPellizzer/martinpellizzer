Centropogon bonplandianus var. intermedius

Centropogon bonplandianus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate bonplandian grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
