Croton guaraniticus f. latifolius

Croton guaraniticus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a variant of the Croton genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
