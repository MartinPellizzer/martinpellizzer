Cicendia fastigiata

Cicendia fastigiata, commonly known as the upright cicendia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that grow in a vertical manner, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged oppositely along the stem.

Cicendia fastigiata is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
