Crataegus monogyna var. vineticola

Crataegus monogyna var. vineticola, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
