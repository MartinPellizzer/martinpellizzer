Cucumis costatus

Cucumis costatus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and ridged fruit, which resembles a horn or a spiky ball. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces yellow flowers that are typically unisexual.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, though it is less common than other cucumber varieties.
