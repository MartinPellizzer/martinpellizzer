Calanthe trifida

Calanthe trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe trifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
