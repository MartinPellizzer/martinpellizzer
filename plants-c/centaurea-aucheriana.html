Centaurea aucheriana

Centaurea aucheriana, commonly known as the Aucher's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
