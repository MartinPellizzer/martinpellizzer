Cuphea alatosperma

Cuphea alatosperma, commonly known as the hairy cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea alatosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
