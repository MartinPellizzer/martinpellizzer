Croton rectangularis

Croton rectangularis, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features vibrant, palmate leaves with deep green veins and striking red or orange hues on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
