Centropogon costaricae

Centropogon costaricae, commonly known as the Costa Rican flame flower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Costa Rica. The plant features striking red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Centropogon costaricae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded habitats.
