Carpha rodwayi

Carpha rodwayi, commonly known as the slender reed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often bending over water surfaces. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carpha rodwayi plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
