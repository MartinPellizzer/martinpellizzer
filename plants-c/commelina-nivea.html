Commelina nivea

Commelina nivea, commonly known as the silver commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are often visited by various pollinators like bees and flies.
