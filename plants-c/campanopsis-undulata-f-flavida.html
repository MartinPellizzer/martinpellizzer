Campanopsis undulata f. flavida

Campanopsis undulata f. flavida, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a unique variant of the campanopsis genus.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. This plant is native to specific regions in China and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
