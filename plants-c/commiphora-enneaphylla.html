Commiphora enneaphylla

Commiphora enneaphylla, commonly known as the Indian myrrh or wild myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudate, which has been used traditionally in medicinal and religious contexts. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.
