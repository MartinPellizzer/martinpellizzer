Chenopodium platanoides

Chenopodium platanoides, commonly known as tree spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in small, dry fruits. It is often grown as a leafy vegetable, valued for its nutritional content and mild flavor.

Chenopodium platanoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.
