Chloris guaranitica

Chloris guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Chloris guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
