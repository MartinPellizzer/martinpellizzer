Cocculus moorei

Cocculus moorei, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, kidney-shaped fruits. Its roots and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and along stream banks.
