Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. variabile

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. variabile, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant bark, which is used to produce the spice cinnamon. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its bark is typically light brown and has a distinctive, layered structure.

The botanical features of this variety make it valuable for both commercial and traditional uses.
