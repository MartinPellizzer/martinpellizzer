Capparis liangii

Capparis liangii, commonly known as the Chinese caper, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in dry and sunny conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
