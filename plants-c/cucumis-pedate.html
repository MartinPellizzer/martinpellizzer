Cucumis pedate

Cucumis pedate, commonly known as the leafy gourd or pedate cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits that are typically green when mature. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines for its mild, slightly sweet flavor and nutritional value.
