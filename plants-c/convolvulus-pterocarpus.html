Convolvulus pterocarpus

Convolvulus pterocarpus, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinct dark blotch at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and woodland edges.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
