Cupressus disticha

Cupressus disticha, commonly known as the spreading or decumbent cypress, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage that forms a compact, rounded shape. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and remain throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cupressus disticha is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
