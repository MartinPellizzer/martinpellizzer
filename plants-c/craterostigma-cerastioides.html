Craterostigma cerastioides

Craterostigma cerastioides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Craterostigma cerastioides is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
