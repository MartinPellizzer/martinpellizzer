Catha vitiensis

Catha vitiensis, commonly known as Khat, is a flowering plant native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its stimulant properties, primarily due to the alkaloid cathinone found in its leaves. It is often cultivated for traditional use in social and cultural settings.
