Cymbopogon lepidus

Cymbopogon lepidus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Cymbopogon lepidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
