Carex luteobrunnea

Carex luteobrunnea, commonly known as the yellow-brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with the inflorescence often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Carex luteobrunnea is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
