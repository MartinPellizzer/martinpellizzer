Chlamydacanthus euphorbioides

Chlamydacanthus euphorbioides, commonly known as the red-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
