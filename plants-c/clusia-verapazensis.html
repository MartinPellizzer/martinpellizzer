Clusia verapazensis

Clusia verapazensis, commonly known as the Verapaz glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, also known as the genus of the genus Clusia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Clusia verapazensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
