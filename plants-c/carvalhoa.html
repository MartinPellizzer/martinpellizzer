Carvalhoa

Carvalhoa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is characteristic of the fynbos vegetation type. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, serrated edge.

Carvalhoa is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
