Cyperus wawrae

Cyperus wawrae, commonly known as the Wawra's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically inconspicuous and lack showy petals. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus wawrae is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
