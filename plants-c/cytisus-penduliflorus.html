Cytisus penduliflorus

Cytisus penduliflorus, commonly known as the hanging flower broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
