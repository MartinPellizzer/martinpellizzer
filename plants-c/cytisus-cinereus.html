Cytisus cinereus

Cytisus cinereus, commonly known as the silver bush broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky habitats.

Cytisus cinereus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils.
