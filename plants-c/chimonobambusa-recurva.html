Chimonobambusa recurva

Chimonobambusa recurva, commonly known as the recurved bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully curved culms that often exhibit a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Chimonobambusa recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
