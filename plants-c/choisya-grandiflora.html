Choisya grandiflora

Choisya grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican orange or golden dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features bright green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Choisya grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
