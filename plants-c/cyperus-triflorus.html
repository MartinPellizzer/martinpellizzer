Cyperus triflorus

Cyperus triflorus, commonly known as the tri-flowered flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Cyperus triflorus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
