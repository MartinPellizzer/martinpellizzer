Crataegus straminea

Crataegus straminea, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
