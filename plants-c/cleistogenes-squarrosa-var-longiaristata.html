Cleistogenes squarrosa var. longiaristata

Cleistogenes squarrosa var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-awned bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, with elongated awns that distinguish the variety.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and providing forage for wildlife.
