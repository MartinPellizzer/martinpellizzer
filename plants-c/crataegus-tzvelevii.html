Crataegus tzvelevii

Crataegus tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev’s hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Crataegus tzvelevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native habitats.
