Cheiranthus asperrimus

Cheiranthus asperrimus, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Its rugged appearance and adaptability to harsh environments make it a resilient and distinctive species.
