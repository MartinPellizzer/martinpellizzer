Cyperus manimae var. pseudospectabilis

Cyperus manimae var. pseudospectabilis, commonly known as the Manimae sedge, is a flowering plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetland or marshy environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
