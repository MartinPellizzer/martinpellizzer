Calamagrostis varia var. corsica

Calamagrostis varia var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue-green hues. The plant produces small, clustered panicles of pale green to reddish flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ornamental grass in landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is appreciated by gardeners and conservationists alike.
