Cybianthus guyanensis

Cybianthus guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana candlewood, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
