Callistachys alpestris

Callistachys alpestris, commonly known as the alpine honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Callistachys alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
