Convolvulus hederaceus

Convolvulus hederaceus, commonly known as creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a trailing growth habit and can spread rapidly, often forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
