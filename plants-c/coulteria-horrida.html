Coulteria horrida

Coulteria horrida, commonly known as the rough coulteria, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that remain on the plant for an extended period. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Coulteria horrida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
