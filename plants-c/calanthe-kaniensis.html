Calanthe kaniensis

Calanthe kaniensis, commonly known as the Kaniensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the spring and early summer. Calanthe kaniensis is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
