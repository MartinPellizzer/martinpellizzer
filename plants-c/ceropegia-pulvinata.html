Ceropegia pulvinata

Ceropegia pulvinata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Ceropegia pulvinata is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient choice for dry gardens or indoor cultivation.
