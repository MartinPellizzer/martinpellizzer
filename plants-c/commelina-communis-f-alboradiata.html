Commelina communis f. alboradiata

Commelina communis f. alboradiata, commonly known as the white-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a distinctive flowering structure with a spathaceous bract that encloses a cluster of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread easily in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
