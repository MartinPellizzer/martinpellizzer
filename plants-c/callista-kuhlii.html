Callista kuhlii

Callista kuhlii, commonly known as the Kuhlii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Callista kuhlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
