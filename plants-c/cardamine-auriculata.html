Cardamine auriculata

Cardamine auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate watercress, is a small perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender flowering stems. Cardamine auriculata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
