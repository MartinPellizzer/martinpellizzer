Conyza absinthifolia

Conyza absinthifolia, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features finely divided, silver-haired leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellow flower heads arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks. It is characterized by its erect stem and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Conyza absinthifolia is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
