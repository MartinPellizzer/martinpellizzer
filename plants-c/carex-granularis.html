Carex granularis

Carex granularis, commonly known as the granular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex granularis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
