Cynanchum insigne

Cynanchum insigne, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweed species known for their unique floral structures. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release silky, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cynanchum insigne is often found in open woods and along roadsides, thriving in moist to mesic soil conditions.
