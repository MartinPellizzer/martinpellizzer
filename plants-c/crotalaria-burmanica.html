Crotalaria burmanica

Crotalaria burmanica, commonly known as Burmese crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a short stem with upright branches and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
