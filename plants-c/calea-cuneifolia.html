Calea cuneifolia

Calea cuneifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
