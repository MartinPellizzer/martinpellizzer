Cordyline diffusa

Cordyline diffusa, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, strap-like leaves that are often used for weaving. The plant has a fibrous stem that is rich in cellulose, making it valuable for traditional crafts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cordyline diffusa is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and ability to add a tropical feel to gardens.
