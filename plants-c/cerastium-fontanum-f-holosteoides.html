Cerastium fontanum f. holosteoides

Cerastium fontanum f. holosteoides, commonly known as the Holosteoides mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are small, white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread readily in suitable conditions.
