Crepis aculeata

Crepis aculeata, commonly known as the prickly hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features spiny bracts and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Crepis aculeata is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
