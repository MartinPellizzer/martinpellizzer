Carex sinclairii

Carex sinclairii, commonly known as Sinclair's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and other similar plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered brownish flowers in the summer.

Sinclair's sedge is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
