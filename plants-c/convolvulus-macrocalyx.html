Convolvulus macrocalyx

Convolvulus macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
