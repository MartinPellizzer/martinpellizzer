Convolvulus hermanniae var. elongatus

Convolvulus hermanniae var. elongatus, commonly known as the Hermann's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often twining. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
