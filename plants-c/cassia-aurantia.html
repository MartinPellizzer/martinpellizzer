Cassia aurantia

Cassia aurantia, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia aurantia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
