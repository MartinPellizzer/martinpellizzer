Chlorophytum polyphyllum

Chlorophytum polyphyllum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil.

Chlorophytum polyphyllum is also noted for its air-purifying properties, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
