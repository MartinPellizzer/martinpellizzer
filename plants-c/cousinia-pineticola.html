Cousinia pineticola

Cousinia pineticola, commonly known as the mountain cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny and arid conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cousinia pineticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
