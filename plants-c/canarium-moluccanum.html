Canarium moluccanum

Canarium moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
