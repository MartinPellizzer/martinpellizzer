Croton dispar

Croton dispar, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems and vibrant, often colorful leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and can vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by three-winged, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
