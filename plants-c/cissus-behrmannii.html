Cissus behrmannii

Cissus behrmannii, commonly known as the African arrowroot or the climbing fig-leaf.

It is a vigorous trailing plant native to tropical Africa, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
