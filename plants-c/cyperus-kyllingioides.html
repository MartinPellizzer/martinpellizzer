Cyperus kyllingioides

Cyperus kyllingioides, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus kyllingioides thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
