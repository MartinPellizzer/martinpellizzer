Cremopyrum

Cremopyrum, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with long, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that grow in clusters. The plant is native to wetland and marshy habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cremopyrum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting diverse wildlife.
