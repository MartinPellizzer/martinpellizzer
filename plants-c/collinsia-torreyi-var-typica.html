Collinsia torreyi var. typica

Collinsia torreyi var. typica, commonly known as the Torrey's bluecup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
