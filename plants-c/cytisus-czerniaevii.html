Cytisus czerniaevii

Cytisus czerniaevii, commonly known as the Czerniaev's broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
