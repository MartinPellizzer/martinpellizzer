Chytroma glossiniformis

Chytroma glossiniformis, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It possesses a specialized root system that attaches to the host's vascular system, allowing it to absorb water and nutrients. The plant produces small, green leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its berries are edible for some animals but toxic to others.

Chytroma glossiniformis plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and influencing the growth of its host trees.
