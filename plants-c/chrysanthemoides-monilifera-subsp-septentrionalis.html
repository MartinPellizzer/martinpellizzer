Chrysanthemoides monilifera subsp. septentrionalis

Chrysanthemoides monilifera subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
