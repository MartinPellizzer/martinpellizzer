Campomanesia reticulata

Campomanesia reticulata, commonly known as the pitanga or Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its potential ecological benefits.
