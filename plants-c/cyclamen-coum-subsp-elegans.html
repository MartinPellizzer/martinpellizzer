Cyclamen coum subsp. elegans

Cyclamen coum subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, and it produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, and red.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers. Cyclamen coum subsp.

elegans is also valued for its ability to bloom in winter and early spring.
