Carex stenophylla subsp. stenophylloides

Carex stenophylla subsp.

stenophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikelets that produce seeds.

Native to regions of North America, this plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
