Cissus asperifolia

Cissus asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its succulent, somewhat fleshy stems and leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough, textured surface that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
