Crotalaria lanata

Crotalaria lanata, commonly known as hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a bushy growth habit with compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some areas for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
