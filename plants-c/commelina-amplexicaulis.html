Commelina amplexicaulis

Commelina amplexicaulis, commonly known as the heartleaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
