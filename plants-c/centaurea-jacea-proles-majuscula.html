Centaurea jacea proles majuscula

Centaurea jacea proles majuscula, commonly known as the Greater Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bright yellow to orange flowers, which are surrounded by purple bracts. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
