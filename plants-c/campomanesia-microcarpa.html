Campomanesia microcarpa

Campomanesia microcarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree or caramboleira, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
