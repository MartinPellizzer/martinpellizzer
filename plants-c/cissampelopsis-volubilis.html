Cissampelopsis volubilis

Cissampelopsis volubilis, commonly known as the climbing fig or climbing soursop, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Cissampelopsis volubilis thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
