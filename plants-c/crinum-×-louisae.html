Crinum × louisae

Crinum × louisae, commonly known as the hybrid lily or Louisiana crinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Crinum asiaticum and Crinum bulbosum, resulting in a plant with striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features large, strap-like leaves and produces bulbous roots that store nutrients. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Crinum × louisae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.
