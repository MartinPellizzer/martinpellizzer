Citrus inermis

Citrus inermis, commonly known as the desert citron, is a small evergreen shrub native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It produces small, yellow, inedible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates. Its flowers are fragrant and white, attracting pollinators like bees.

Citrus inermis is valued for its resilience and has been cultivated for centuries for its medicinal and ornamental properties.
