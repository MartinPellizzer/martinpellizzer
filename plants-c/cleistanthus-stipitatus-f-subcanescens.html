Cleistanthus stipitatus f. subcanescens

Cleistanthus stipitatus f. subcanescens, commonly known as the pale-haired cleistanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
