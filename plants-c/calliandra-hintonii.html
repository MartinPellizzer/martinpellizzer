Calliandra hintonii

Calliandra hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Calliandra hintonii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
