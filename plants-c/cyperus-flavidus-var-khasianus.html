Cyperus flavidus var. khasianus

Cyperus flavidus var. khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This variant is distinguished by its yellowish coloration and is often found in the mountainous regions of the Khasi Hills.
