Carex pseudomitrata

Carex pseudomitrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Carex pseudomitrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
