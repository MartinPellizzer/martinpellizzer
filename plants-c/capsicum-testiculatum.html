Capsicum testiculatum

Capsicum testiculatum, commonly known as the testicle pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often tinged with red.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
