Cnicus niveus

Cnicus niveus, commonly known as the white cockle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cnicus niveus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sandy or gravelly habitats.
