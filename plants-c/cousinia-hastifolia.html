Cousinia hastifolia

Cousinia hastifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cottonthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia hastifolia produces showy yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
