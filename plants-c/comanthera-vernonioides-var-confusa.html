Comanthera vernonioides var. confusa

Comanthera vernonioides var. confusa, commonly known as the small-flowered false buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks.
