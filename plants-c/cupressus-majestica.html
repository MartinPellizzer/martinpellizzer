Cupressus majestica

Cupressus majestica, commonly known as the Queen's cypress, is a species of coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, pyramidal shape and dark blue-green foliage. The tree has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or mountainous regions.

Cupressus majestica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its formal appearance.
