Cotula barbata

Cotula barbata, commonly known as the hairy daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cotula barbata is used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
