Crepidium latum

Crepidium latum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale green, and are clustered in a raceme, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
