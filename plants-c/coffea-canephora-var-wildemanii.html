Coffea canephora var. wildemanii

Coffea canephora var. wildemanii, commonly known as the Western African coffee.

This variety is native to the humid lowland regions of West Africa, where it thrives in tropical climates with high rainfall. It is a shrub or small tree that produces red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces white flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is valued for its robust flavor and is often used in the production of Arabica-like coffee blends.
