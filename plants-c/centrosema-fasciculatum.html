Centrosema fasciculatum

Centrosema fasciculatum, commonly known as creeping cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often used as a forage crop or in soil conservation efforts.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in agricultural systems.
