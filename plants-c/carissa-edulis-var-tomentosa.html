Carissa edulis var. tomentosa

Carissa edulis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. The berries are sweet and juicy, often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.
