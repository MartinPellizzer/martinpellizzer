Carex laeta

Carex laeta, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typical of the sedge family. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex laeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
