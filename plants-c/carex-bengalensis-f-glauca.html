Carex bengalensis f. glauca

Carex bengalensis f. glauca, commonly known as the Bengal sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This ornamental and ecological plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
