Colubrina vellozoi var. latifolia

Colubrina vellozoi var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an attractive addition to native gardens.
