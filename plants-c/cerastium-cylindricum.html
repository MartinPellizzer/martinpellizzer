Cerastium cylindricum

Cerastium cylindricum, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium cylindricum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
