Calochortus venustus var. purpurascens

Calochortus venustus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the showy glory-of-the-snow, is a striking bulbous perennial native to California.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stocky stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
