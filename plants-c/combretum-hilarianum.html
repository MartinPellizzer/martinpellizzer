Combretum hilarianum

Combretum hilarianum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
