Cerastium carinthiacum

Cerastium carinthiacum, commonly known as Carpathian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers with five petals, typically appearing in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cerastium carinthiacum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
