Crataegus gattingeri var. rigida

Crataegus gattingeri var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting.
