Cayaponia dubia

Cayaponia dubia, commonly known as the rare ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often bioluminescent petals that emit a soft glow in the dark. The plant's leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Cayaponia dubia is known for its unusual reproductive cycle, which involves a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its natural habitat.
