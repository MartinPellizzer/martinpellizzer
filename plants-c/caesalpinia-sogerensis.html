Caesalpinia sogerensis

Caesalpinia sogerensis, commonly known as the Soger's candlewood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, tropical environments. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, often resembling candles, hence its common name. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and the pods are long and slender.

Caesalpinia sogerensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
