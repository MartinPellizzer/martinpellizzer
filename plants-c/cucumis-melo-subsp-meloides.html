Cucumis melo subsp. meloides

Cucumis melo subsp.

meloides, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often spiny fruit, which can vary in color from pale green to yellow.

The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its crisp, mild-flavored cucumbers, often used in fresh consumption or pickling.
