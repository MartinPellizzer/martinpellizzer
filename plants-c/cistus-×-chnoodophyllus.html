Cistus × chnoodophyllus

Cistus × chnoodophyllus, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

The species is a natural hybrid between Cistus ladaniferus and Cistus monspeliacus.
