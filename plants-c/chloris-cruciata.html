Chloris cruciata

Chloris cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaf sheaths, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chloris cruciata is an important component of native grassland ecosystems and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
