Calboa globosa

Calboa globosa, commonly known as the globular calboa, is a unique succulent plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of fleshy, green leaves that form a compact, globe-like structure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its distinctive appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
