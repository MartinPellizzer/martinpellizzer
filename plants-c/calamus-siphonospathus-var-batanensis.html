Calamus siphonospathus var. batanensis

Calamus siphonospathus var. batanensis, commonly known as the Batanese calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the Batan Islands, and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, which are enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
