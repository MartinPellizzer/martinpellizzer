Colobanthus muscoides

Colobanthus muscoides, commonly known as Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

It is an important component of the vegetation in Antarctica and sub-Antarctic regions.
