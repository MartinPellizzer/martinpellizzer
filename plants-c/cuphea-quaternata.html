Cuphea quaternata

Cuphea quaternata, commonly known as the four-angled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
