Cleidion vieillardii var. macrophyllum

Cleidion vieillardii var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leafed cleidion, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
