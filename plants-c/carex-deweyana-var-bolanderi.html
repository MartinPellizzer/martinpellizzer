Carex deweyana var. bolanderi

Carex deweyana var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or riparian habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
