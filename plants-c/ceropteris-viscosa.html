Ceropteris viscosa

Ceropteris viscosa, commonly known as the sticky spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually crowded and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive sticky surface.

Ceropteris viscosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in controlled environments.
