Chamaesyce glyptosperma var. pubescens

Chamaesyce glyptosperma var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fleshy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species well adapted to arid environments.
