Cassia glandulosa var. flavicoma

Cassia glandulosa var. flavicoma, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with glandular hairs and yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
