Crusea rubra

Crusea rubra, commonly known as the red crusea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a shrub-like structure. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crusea rubra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
