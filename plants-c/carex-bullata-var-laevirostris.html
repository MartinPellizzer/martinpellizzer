Carex bullata var. laevirostris

Carex bullata var. laevirostris, commonly known as the smooth-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers on spikes, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in temperate regions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
