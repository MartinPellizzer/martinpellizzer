Centranthus angustifolius var. longecalcaratus

Centranthus angustifolius var. longecalcaratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centranthus, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
