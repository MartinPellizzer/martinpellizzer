Cirsium cephalotes

Cirsium cephalotes, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces achenes as its seeds.

Cirsium cephalotes is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
