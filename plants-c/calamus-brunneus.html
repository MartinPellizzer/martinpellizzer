Calamus brunneus

Calamus brunneus, commonly known as the brown reed or brown calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a distinct yellowish or brownish hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits.
