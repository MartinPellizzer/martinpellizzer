Campium christensenii

Campium christensenii, commonly known as the Christensen's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemes, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Campium christensenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
