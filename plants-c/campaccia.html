Campaccia

Campaccia, commonly known as the Italian wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, woody stems and hairy, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in Mediterranean regions.

Campaccia is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
