Crataegus habilis

Crataegus habilis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits called pomes, which are typically eaten by birds and small mammals.

Crataegus habilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
