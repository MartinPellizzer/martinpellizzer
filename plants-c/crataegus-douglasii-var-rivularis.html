Crataegus douglasii var. rivularis

Crataegus douglasii var. rivularis, commonly known as the rivular serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, attracting various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy spring blossoms.
