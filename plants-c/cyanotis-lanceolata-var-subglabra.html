Cyanotis lanceolata var. subglabra

Cyanotis lanceolata var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
