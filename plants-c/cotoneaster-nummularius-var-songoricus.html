Cotoneaster nummularius var. songoricus

Cotoneaster nummularius var. songoricus, commonly known as the Songari cotoneaster, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is particularly noted for its compact form and adaptability to various garden conditions.
