Cyathea batesii

Cyathea batesii, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the structural support of the plant.

Cyathea batesii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
