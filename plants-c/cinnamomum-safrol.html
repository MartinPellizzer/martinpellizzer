Cinnamomum safrol

Cinnamomum safrol, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain high concentrations of essential oils. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive cinnamon-like fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
