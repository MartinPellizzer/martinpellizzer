Cassia petensis

Cassia petensis, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Cassia petensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
