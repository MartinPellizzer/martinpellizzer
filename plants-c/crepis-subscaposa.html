Crepis subscaposa

Crepis subscaposa, commonly known as the scapose hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and often have a scapose (sheath-like) structure, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a single head, often surrounded by bracts.
