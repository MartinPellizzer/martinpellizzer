Christolea incana

Christolea incana, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Christolea incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
