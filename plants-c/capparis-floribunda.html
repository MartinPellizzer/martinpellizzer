Capparis floribunda

Capparis floribunda, commonly known as the sweetbriar or redcurrant shrub, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
