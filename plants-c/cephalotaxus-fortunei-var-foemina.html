Cephalotaxus fortunei var. foemina

Cephalotaxus fortunei var. foemina, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to China.

It features a compact, pyramidal shape with dense, scale-like foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive form.
