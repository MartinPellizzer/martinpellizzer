Cousinia lachnobasis

Cousinia lachnobasis, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Cousinia lachnobasis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
