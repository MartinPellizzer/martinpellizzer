Clethra alnifolia var. michauxii

Clethra alnifolia var. michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny, dry seeds.
