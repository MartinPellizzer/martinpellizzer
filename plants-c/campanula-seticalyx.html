Campanula seticalyx

Campanula seticalyx, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and gray-green, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
