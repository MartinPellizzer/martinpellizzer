Chenopodium petiolare f. scutatum

Chenopodium petiolare f. scutatum, commonly known as the broadleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant characterized by its broad, rounded leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually pubescent, with a distinctive scutate (shield-like) base, giving it its specific epithet.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
