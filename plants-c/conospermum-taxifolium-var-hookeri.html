Conospermum taxifolium var. hookeri

Conospermum taxifolium var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences that produce vibrant red or orange flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating, and the plant is evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
