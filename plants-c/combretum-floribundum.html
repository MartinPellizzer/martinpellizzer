Combretum floribundum

Combretum floribundum, commonly known as the red flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a slightly rough texture. The plant is part of the Combretaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
