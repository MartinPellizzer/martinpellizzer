Centaurea napulifera subsp. pseudoaxillaris

Centaurea napulifera subsp.

pseudoaxillaris, commonly known as the Sicilian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
