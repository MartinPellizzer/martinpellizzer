Crataegus lemingtonensis

Crataegus lemingtonensis, commonly known as the Lemington hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitat.
