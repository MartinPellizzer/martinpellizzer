Clematis grandiflora var. mutabilis

Clematis grandiflora var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink to red, often changing hue as they age. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
