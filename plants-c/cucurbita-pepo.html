Cucurbita pepo

Cucurbita pepo, commonly known as the common squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Americas.

It is a vine that produces a variety of edible fruits, including zucchini, acorn squash, and pumpkins. The plant has large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female.

Its fruits vary in shape, size, and color, depending on the cultivar. The plant is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
