Crataegus oxyacantha var. oliveriana

Crataegus oxyacantha var. oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
