Copiapoa dealbata var. carrizalensis

Copiapoa dealbata var. carrizalensis, commonly known as the Carrizalensis cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its light green to pale blue stems, which are often covered with a whitish bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

This variety is relatively rare and is often found in rocky soils at higher elevations.
