Capparis saxatilis

Capparis saxatilis, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are small, oval, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and succulent.

Capparis saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
