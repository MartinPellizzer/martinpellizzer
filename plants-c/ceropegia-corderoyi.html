Ceropegia corderoyi

Ceropegia corderoyi, commonly known as the Cape loopleaf, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia corderoyi is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
