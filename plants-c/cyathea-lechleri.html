Cyathea lechleri

Cyathea lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is known for its latex-producing properties, which have been traditionally used by indigenous communities. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk with a crown of broad leaves, making it a distinctive feature in the rainforest canopy.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates with high rainfall and well-drained soil.
