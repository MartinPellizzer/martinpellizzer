Carex infuscata

Carex infuscata, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet environments such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its foliage and flowering structure make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
