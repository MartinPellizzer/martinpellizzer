Capparis carvalhoana

Capparis carvalhoana, commonly known as the Cape Harebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Capparis carvalhoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
