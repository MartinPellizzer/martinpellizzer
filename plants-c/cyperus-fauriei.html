Cyperus fauriei

Cyperus fauriei, commonly known as the yellow papyrus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Cyperus fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
