Carthamus × doumerguei

Carthamus × doumerguei, commonly known as the hybrid sunflower, is a cross between Carthamus tinctorius and Carthamus balsamiflorus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces large, showy blooms with a distinctive central disk and radiating ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

The botanical features of Carthamus × doumerguei make it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
