Carmelita formosa

Carmelita formosa, commonly known as the beautiful Carmel plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Carmelita formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
