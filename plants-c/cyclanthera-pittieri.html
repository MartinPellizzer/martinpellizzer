Cyclanthera pittieri

Cyclanthera pittieri, commonly known as the red cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces distinctive, fleshy, red or orange fruits that are edible when ripe. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. Cyclanthera pittieri is often grown for its ornamental value and unique fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.
