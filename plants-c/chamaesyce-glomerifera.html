Chamaesyce glomerifera

Chamaesyce glomerifera, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, smooth, and often blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
