Cyclosorus acuminatus f. cristatus

Cyclosorus acuminatus f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate cyclosorus, is a rare and striking variant of the cyclosorus genus.

It is characterized by its unique, crest-like growth pattern, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This fern is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of delicate, lance-shaped fronds that form a rosette-like structure.

The plant's botanical interest lies in its unusual morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of study in horticulture and botany.
