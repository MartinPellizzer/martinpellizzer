Couma guatemalensis

Couma guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan couma, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
