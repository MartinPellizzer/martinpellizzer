Cyathea brittoniana

Cyathea brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's structure and facilitating the storage of nutrients.

Cyathea brittoniana plays an important ecological role in its native forests by providing habitat and contributing to the local biodiversity.
