Canavalia mexicana

Canavalia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are used as a food source. Canavalia mexicana is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in agricultural systems.
