Clerodendrum ervatamioides

Clerodendrum ervatamioides, commonly known as the tropical glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or orange corolla. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
