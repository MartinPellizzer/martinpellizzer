Croton ripensis

Croton ripensis, commonly known as the river croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often striking foliage. The plant typically has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule.

Croton ripensis is often found near water sources, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
