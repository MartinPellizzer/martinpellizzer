Calea urticifolia var. yucatanensis

Calea urticifolia var. yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs, similar to those found on nettle plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
