Calamintha caucasica

Calamintha caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Calamintha caucasica is valued for its fragrant foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
