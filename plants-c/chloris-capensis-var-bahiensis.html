Chloris capensis var. bahiensis

Chloris capensis var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia chloris, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions as a landscape plant.
