Chenopodium album subsp. virgatum

Chenopodium album subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as wild spinach or fat-hen, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by egg-shaped, yellowish-brown seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used as a leaf vegetable in various cuisines.
