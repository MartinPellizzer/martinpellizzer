Canna leucocarpa

Canna leucocarpa, commonly known as the white canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a glossy green surface. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna leucocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
