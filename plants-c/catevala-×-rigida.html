Catevala × rigida

Catevala × rigida, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two orchid species.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and the production of showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers can vary in color, ranging from white to pink and purple, depending on the parent species.

Catevala × rigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
