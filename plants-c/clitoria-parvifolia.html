Clitoria parvifolia

Clitoria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bean or the lesser butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing legume with slender stems and trifoliate leaves, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Clitoria parvifolia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

It is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate floral display.
