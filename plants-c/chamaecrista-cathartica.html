Chamaecrista cathartica

Chamaecrista cathartica, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the use of its seeds in traditional remedies. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
