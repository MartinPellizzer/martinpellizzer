Centaurea paniculata subsp. cosana

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

cosana, commonly known as the Cosine Centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, grayish-green leaves with toothed margins.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
