Comparettia stenochila

Comparettia stenochila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color, bloom in clusters and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Comparettia stenochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
