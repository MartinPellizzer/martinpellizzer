Cassia robiniifolia

Cassia robiniifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia robiniifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
