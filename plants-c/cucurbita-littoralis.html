Cucurbita littoralis

Cucurbita littoralis, commonly known as the coastal gourd, is a vine-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange. The plant has palmate leaves and tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which can be used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
