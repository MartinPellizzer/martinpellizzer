Calceolaria ascendens subsp. pristiphylla

Calceolaria ascendens subsp.

pristiphylla, commonly known as the mountain calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather. Calceolaria ascendens subsp.

pristiphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
