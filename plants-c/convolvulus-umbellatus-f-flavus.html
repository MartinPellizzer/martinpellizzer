Convolvulus umbellatus f. flavus

Convolvulus umbellatus f. flavus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant yellow blooms.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals and a prominent central spur.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
