Chamaefilix lunulata var. pteropus

Chamaefilix lunulata var. pteropus, commonly known as the moonflower saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features silver-gray foliage with a dense, mat-forming habit, and its flowers are small, white, and bell-shaped, opening at night. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a compact growth pattern, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to specialized gardens.
