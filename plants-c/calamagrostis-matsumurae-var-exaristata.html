Calamagrostis matsumurae var. exaristata

Calamagrostis matsumurae var. exaristata, commonly known as the Japanese feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It features slender, arching blades that are typically gray-green in color, with a fine, silky texture. The plant produces delicate, feathery panicles that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist to wet soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
