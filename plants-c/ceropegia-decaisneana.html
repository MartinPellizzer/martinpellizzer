Ceropegia decaisneana

Ceropegia decaisneana, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, fuchsia-colored flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that resembles a capsule. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia decaisneana is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
