Cacalia cordiifolia

Cacalia cordiifolia, commonly known as heartleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Cacalia cordiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
