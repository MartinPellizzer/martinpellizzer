Coreopsis grandiflora var. pilosa

Coreopsis grandiflora var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy coreopsis, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it its varietal name, "pilosa," meaning hairy in Latin. The plant produces showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its upright growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
