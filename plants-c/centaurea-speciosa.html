Centaurea speciosa

Centaurea speciosa, commonly known as the showy centaury, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow to orange petals with reddish-brown centers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Centaurea speciosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
