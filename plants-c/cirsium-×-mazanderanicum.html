Cirsium × mazanderanicum

Cirsium × mazanderanicum, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. It has been introduced to other parts of the world, including Europe and North America, where it sometimes becomes invasive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
