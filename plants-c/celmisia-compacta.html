Celmisia compacta

Celmisia compacta, commonly known as the silver tufted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Celmisia compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
