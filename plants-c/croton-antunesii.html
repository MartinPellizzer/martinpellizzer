Croton antunesii

Croton antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes' croton, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that range from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by unique, spiky fruit capsules. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
