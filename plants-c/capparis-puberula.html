Capparis puberula

Capparis puberula, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, green berries. Capparis puberula is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
