Colutea arborescens var. affinis

Colutea arborescens var. affinis, commonly known as the red bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, bright red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
