Croton salviformis var. rufopunctatus

Croton salviformis var. rufopunctatus, commonly known as the red-spotted croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that often display shades of green, red, and yellow, with distinctive red or orange spots. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its colorful leaf patterns.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
