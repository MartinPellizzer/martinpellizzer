Chamaecrista ensiformis var. plurifoliolata

Chamaecrista ensiformis var. plurifoliolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple small leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a common species in xeric habitats.
