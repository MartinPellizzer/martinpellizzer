Carex polygama var. brevisquamosa

Carex polygama var. brevisquamosa, commonly known as the short-squamate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, short, scaly leaves. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
