Cassia versicolor

Cassia versicolor, commonly known as the variable senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is often rough and fissured.

Cassia versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
