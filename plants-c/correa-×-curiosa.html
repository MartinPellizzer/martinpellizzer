Correa × curiosa

Correa × curiosa, commonly known as the hybrid correa, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
