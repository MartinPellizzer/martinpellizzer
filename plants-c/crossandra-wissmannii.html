Crossandra wissmannii

Crossandra wissmannii, commonly known as the Wissmann's flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Crossandra wissmannii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
