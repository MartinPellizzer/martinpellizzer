Cyperus melanospermus subsp. elatus

Cyperus melanospermus subsp.

elatus, commonly known as the blackseed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive dark seeds.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

The subspecies elatus is distinguished by its elongated, slender stems and dark, hard seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.
