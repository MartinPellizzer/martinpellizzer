Chlorophytum silvaticum

Chlorophytum silvaticum, commonly known as the forest spider plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
