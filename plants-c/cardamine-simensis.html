Cardamine simensis

Cardamine simensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes.

Cardamine simensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks.
