Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. drupacea

Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. drupacea, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to China.

It features a compact, dense form with horizontal branches that create a rounded or conical shape. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones, and its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a range of soil types, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
