Cephalophilon runcinatum

Cephalophilon runcinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various gardens across temperate regions.
