Corydalis × zahlbruckneri

Corydalis × zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the hybrid corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, slender stems and produces clusters of small, typically pink or purple flowers with two upper petals and two lower ones. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Corydalis × zahlbruckneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
