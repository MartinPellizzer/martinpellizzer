Carex obscuriceps

Carex obscuriceps, commonly known as the dark-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of dark brown to black flowers, which gives it its common name.

Carex obscuriceps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
