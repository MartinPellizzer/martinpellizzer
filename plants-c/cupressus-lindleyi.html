Cupressus lindleyi

Cupressus lindleyi, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
