Cucumis baladensis

Cucumis baladensis, commonly known as the Balad cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature and tender. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it has a vine-like growth habit.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has been historically cultivated for its edible, crisp texture.
