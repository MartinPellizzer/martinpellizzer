Cleome longistyla

Cleome longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces clusters of small, elongated flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Cleome longistyla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
