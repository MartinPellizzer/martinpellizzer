Chelone

Chelone, commonly known as honeysuckle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caprifoliaceae.

It includes both herbaceous perennials and shrubs, often characterized by their fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically opposite and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value and edible flowers.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of honey.
