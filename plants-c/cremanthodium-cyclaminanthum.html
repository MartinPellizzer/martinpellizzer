Cremanthodium cyclaminanthum

Cremanthodium cyclaminanthum, commonly known as the cyclamen lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of the cyclamen genus, with petals that are often pink or purple in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
