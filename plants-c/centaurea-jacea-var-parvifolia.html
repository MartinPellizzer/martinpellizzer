Centaurea jacea var. parvifolia

Centaurea jacea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from summer to autumn. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, and the plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful blooms.
