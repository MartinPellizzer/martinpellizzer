Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. bonplandii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. bonplandii, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or cinnamon bark tree, is a species native to Sri Lanka and parts of southern India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The bark is harvested and dried to produce the familiar cinnamon spice used worldwide.
