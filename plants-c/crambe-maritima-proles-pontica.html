Crambe maritima proles pontica

Crambe maritima proles pontica, commonly known as the Pontic sea kale, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Black Sea.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune ecosystems and coastal marshes. The plant features robust, fleshy stems and large, lobed leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. Its flowering occurs in late summer, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine.
