Calamagrostis violacea

Calamagrostis violacea, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Calamagrostis violacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
