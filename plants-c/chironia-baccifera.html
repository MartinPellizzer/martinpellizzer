Chironia baccifera

Chironia baccifera, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Chironia baccifera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies in its natural habitat.
