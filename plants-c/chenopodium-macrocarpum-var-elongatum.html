Chenopodium macrocarpum var. elongatum

Chenopodium macrocarpum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-seeded goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, inflated fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
