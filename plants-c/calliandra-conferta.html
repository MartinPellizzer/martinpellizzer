Calliandra conferta

Calliandra conferta, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its showy flowers.

Calliandra conferta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
