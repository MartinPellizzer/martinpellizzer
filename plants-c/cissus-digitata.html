Cissus digitata

Cissus digitata, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Cissus digitata thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and ornamental purposes.
