Cestrum bolivianum

Cestrum bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian jessamine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
