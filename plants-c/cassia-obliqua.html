Cassia obliqua

Cassia obliqua, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
