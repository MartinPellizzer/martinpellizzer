Clinopodium cordatum

Clinopodium cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a minty fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Clinopodium cordatum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
