Cardamomum arboreum

Cardamomum arboreum, commonly known as the tree cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes contain essential oils rich in compounds like cineole and limonene, contributing to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

Cardamomum arboreum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
