Chrysophyllum oliviforme subsp. angustifolium

Chrysophyllum oliviforme subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, edible fruits with a creamy, sweet flesh. The fruit has a unique, pear-like shape and is often referred to as the "custard apple.".

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native habitat.
