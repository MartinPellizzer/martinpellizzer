Chaenomeles lagenaria var. cathayensis

Chaenomeles lagenaria var. cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, which appear in early spring before the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that mature into a distinctive, inflated, orange or red color in autumn. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
