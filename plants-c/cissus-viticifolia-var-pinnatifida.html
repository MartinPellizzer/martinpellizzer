Cissus viticifolia var. pinnatifida

Cissus viticifolia var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive pinnatifid (broadly lobed) leaf shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for vertical gardening and green walls.
