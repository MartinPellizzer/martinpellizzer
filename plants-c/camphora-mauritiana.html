Camphora mauritiana

Camphora mauritiana, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain hard, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils with medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of camphor.
