Cordyline hedychioides

Cordyline hedychioides, commonly known as the white cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems with long, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with pink or red at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, adding an elegant touch to gardens. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring moderate watering to maintain its health and appearance.
