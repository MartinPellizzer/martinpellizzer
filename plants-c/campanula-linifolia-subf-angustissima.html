Campanula linifolia subf. angustissima

Campanula linifolia subf.

angustissima, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
