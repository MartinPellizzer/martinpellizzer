Clethra fabri

Clethra fabri, commonly known as the Chinese sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Clethra fabri is valued for both its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
