Carex congdonii

Carex congdonii, commonly known as Congdon's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex congdonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for wildlife.
