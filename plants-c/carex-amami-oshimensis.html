Carex amami-oshimensis

Carex amami-oshimensis, commonly known as the Amami reed or Amami sedge, is a rare sedge species native to the Amami Islands in southern Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Carex amami-oshimensis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
