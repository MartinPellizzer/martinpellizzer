Chamaedrys cretica

Chamaedrys cretica, commonly known as the Crete stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
