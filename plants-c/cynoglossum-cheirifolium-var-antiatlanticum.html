Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. antiatlanticum

Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
