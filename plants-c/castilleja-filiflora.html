Castilleja filiflora

Castilleja filiflora, commonly known as the threadleaf Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
