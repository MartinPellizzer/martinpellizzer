Campanula rapunculus f. hirsutissima

Campanula rapunculus f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white throat. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and meadows.

Campanula rapunculus f. hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
