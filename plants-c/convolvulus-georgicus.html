Convolvulus georgicus

Convolvulus georgicus, commonly known as the Georgian morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Native to the Caucasus region, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
