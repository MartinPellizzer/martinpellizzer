Connarus maingayi

Connarus maingayi, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Connarus maingayi is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
