Crocus zanjanensis

Crocus zanjanensis, commonly known as the Zanjan crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which appear before the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is also of interest to botanists and conservationists.
