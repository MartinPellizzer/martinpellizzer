Ceratocephalus javanicus

Ceratocephalus javanicus, commonly known as the Java burr, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its spiny, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
