Copernicia australis

Copernicia australis, commonly known as the Australian copernicia palm, is a flowering plant species native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional Aboriginal crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by ornamental, palm-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
