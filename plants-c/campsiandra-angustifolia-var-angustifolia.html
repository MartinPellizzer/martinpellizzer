Campsiandra angustifolia var. angustifolia

Campsiandra angustifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf campsiandra, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
