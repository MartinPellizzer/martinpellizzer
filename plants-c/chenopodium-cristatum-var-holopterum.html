Chenopodium cristatum var. holopterum

Chenopodium cristatum var. holopterum, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
