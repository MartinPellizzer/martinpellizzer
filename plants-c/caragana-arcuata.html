Caragana arcuata

Caragana arcuata, commonly known as the Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species for reclamation projects.

Its root system is extensive, helping to prevent soil erosion in arid environments.
