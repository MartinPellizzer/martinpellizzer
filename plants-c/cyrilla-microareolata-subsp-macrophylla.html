Cyrilla microareolata subsp. macrophylla

Cyrilla microareolata subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed cyrilla, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptic or ovate leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical subspecies.

The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This subspecies is often found in moist, sandy soils in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
