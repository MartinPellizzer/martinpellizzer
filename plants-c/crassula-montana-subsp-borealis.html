Crassula montana subsp. borealis

Crassula montana subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the cooler months. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
