Cytisus skrobiszewskii

Cytisus skrobiszewskii, commonly known as the Skrobiszewski broom, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
