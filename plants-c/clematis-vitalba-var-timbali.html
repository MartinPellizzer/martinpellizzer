Clematis vitalba var. timbali

Clematis vitalba var. timbali, commonly known as the Timbali Clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in some areas.
