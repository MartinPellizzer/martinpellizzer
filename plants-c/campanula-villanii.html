Campanula villanii

Campanula villanii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine down, while its stems are often hairy.

Campanula villanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
