Campanula linifolia f. umbrosa

Campanula linifolia f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pale blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
