Carduus modestii

Carduus modestii, commonly known as the modest thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carduus modestii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun.
