Camarotis proboscidea

Camarotis proboscidea, commonly known as the proboscidean orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and striking, fragrant flowers that often resemble the shape of a proboscis. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, primarily bees and wasps, which are attracted by its nectar-rich spurs.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
