Cyperus tabina

Cyperus tabina, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes, pond margins, and wetlands, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, three-lobed flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are often borne in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Cyperus tabina is an important plant in wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
