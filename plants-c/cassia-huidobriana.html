Cassia huidobriana

Cassia huidobriana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically fragrant. Cassia huidobriana thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
