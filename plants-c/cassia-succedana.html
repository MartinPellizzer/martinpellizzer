Cassia succedana

Cassia succedana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a striking appearance during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with small, ovate leaflets. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in a hairy, woody capsule that splits open when mature.
