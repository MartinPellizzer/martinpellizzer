Cynanchum Barbigerum

Cynanchum barbigerum is a striking member of the Asclepiadaceae family known for its unique and often showy flowers. Native to regions of Asia, this plant thrives in diverse habitats ranging from forests to rocky slopes. Its distinctive features include long, hooked spines and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped blooms. Often cultivated for its ornamental value, it also holds significance in traditional medicine. Despite its beauty, it can be toxic, requiring caution when handling or consuming.
