Calamintha confinis

Calamintha confinis, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

Calamintha confinis is often used in herbal medicine for its mild antiseptic and digestive properties.
