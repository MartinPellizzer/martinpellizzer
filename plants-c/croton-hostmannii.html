Croton hostmannii

Croton hostmannii, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant yellow, orange, or red hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical and subtropical climates.
