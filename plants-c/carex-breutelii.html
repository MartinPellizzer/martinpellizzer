Carex breutelii

Carex breutelii, commonly known as the Breutel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Carex breutelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
