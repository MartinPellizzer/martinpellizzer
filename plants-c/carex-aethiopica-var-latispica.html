Carex aethiopica var. latispica

Carex aethiopica var. latispica, commonly known as the broad-spiked sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variant is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
