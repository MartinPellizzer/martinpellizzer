Calliandra physocalyx

Calliandra physocalyx, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, fluffy, pink to reddish flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

Calliandra physocalyx is also valued in some regions for its nitrogen-fixing properties and as a source of fodder for livestock.
