Carex verecunda

Carex verecunda, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. Native to parts of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
