Centaurea pseudouniflora

Centaurea pseudouniflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
