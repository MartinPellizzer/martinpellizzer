Cyphostemma grandistipulatum

Cyphostemma grandistipulatum, commonly known as the giant-leaved cyphostemma, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
