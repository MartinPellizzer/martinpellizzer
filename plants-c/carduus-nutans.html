Carduus nutans

Carduus nutans, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a tall, spiny stem with lobed, hairy leaves and large, purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal head and are visited by various pollinators.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
