Centropogon chamissonianus

Centropogon chamissonianus, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, salmon-pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
