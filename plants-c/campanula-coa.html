Campanula coa

Campanula coa, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Campanula coa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
