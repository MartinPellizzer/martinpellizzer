Cremastra bifolia

Cremastra bifolia, commonly known as the twinleaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are divided into two distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Cremastra bifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
