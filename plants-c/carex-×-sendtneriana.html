Carex × sendtneriana

Carex × sendtneriana, commonly known as the silver grass or white sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that often displays a striking silvery or white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Carex × sendtneriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
