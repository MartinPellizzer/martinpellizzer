Convolvulus thonningii

Convolvulus thonningii, commonly known as the Thonning's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Convolvulus thonningii is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.
