Crataegus monogyna f. variegata

Crataegus monogyna f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
