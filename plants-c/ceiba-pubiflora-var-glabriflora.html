Ceiba pubiflora var. glabriflora

Ceiba pubiflora var. glabriflora, commonly known as the woolly ceiba or white ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and towering height, often reaching over 30 meters. The tree has smooth, pale bark and produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is often found in lowland rainforests.
