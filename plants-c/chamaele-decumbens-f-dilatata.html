Chamaele decumbens f. dilatata

Chamaele decumbens f. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf chamaele, is a variant of the chamaele plant characterized by its broad, spreading leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant features a low-growing, decumbent stem that lies flat on the ground, often with the tips curling upward. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
