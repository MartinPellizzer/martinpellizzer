Celtis pubescens

Celtis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, green berries that turn dark purple when mature.

Celtis pubescens is typically found in moist bottomlands and is valued for its adaptability and ecological benefits.
