Cirsium nelsonii

Cirsium nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bristly leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a tall, branching stem with numerous flowering heads.

Cirsium nelsonii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and animals.
