Chrysophyllum pomiferum

Chrysophyllum pomiferum, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-colored fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
