Cuphea watsonii

Cuphea watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's firecracker, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble firecrackers, which bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.

Cuphea watsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
