Calathea leopardina

Calathea leopardina, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a tropical perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled foliage with deep green veins and light green to white spots, resembling a leopard's coat. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Calathea leopardina is often grown as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
