Chondrilla secunda

Chondrilla secunda, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, gray-green stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Chondrilla secunda is adapted to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
