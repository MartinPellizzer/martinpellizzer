Cyclobalanopsis semiserrata

Cyclobalanopsis semiserrata, commonly known as the serrated evergreen ironwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Fagaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. The tree is characterized by its dense, hard wood and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. It produces small, edible acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine.
