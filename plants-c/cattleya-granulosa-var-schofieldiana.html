Cattleya granulosa var. schofieldiana

Cattleya granulosa var. schofieldiana, commonly known as the Schofield's cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral development. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it produces fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
