Cymbidium madidum

Cymbidium madidum, commonly known as the rainforest orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, often blooming during the rainy season.

Cymbidium madidum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
