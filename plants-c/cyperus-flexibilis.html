Cyperus flexibilis

Cyperus flexibilis, commonly known as the flexible flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its flexible, arching stems that allow it to withstand water currents and flooding.

Cyperus flexibilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
