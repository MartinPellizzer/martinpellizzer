Codonopsis nervosa

Codonopsis nervosa, commonly known as the veined ginseng, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with distinct veining. Its root system is similar to that of true ginseng, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine.

The flowers are usually white or pale blue, and the plant blooms in late summer.
