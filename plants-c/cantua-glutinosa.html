Cantua glutinosa

Cantua glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds.

Cantua glutinosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas.
