Cymbidium nipponicum

Cymbidium nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a species native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with red or purple markings. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium nipponicum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and seasonal blooming.
