Cuphea hyssopifolia f. subrevoluta

Cuphea hyssopifolia f. subrevoluta, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, velvety silver-green foliage that is finely textured and densely packed. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a standout in ornamental horticulture.
