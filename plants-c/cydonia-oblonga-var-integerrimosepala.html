Cydonia oblonga var. integerrimosepala

Cydonia oblonga var. integerrimosepala, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its rough, pale bark and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces large, yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested in late autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
