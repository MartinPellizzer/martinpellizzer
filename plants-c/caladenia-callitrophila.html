Caladenia callitrophila

Caladenia callitrophila, commonly known as the pink-stemmed spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pinkish stems and small, intricate flowers that resemble spider-like structures. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forest habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white with a distinctive red or purple lip, and it blooms during the autumn season.

Caladenia callitrophila is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native environment.
