Croton myrsinites

Croton myrsinites, commonly known as the silver croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton myrsinites is relatively hardy and can be grown in warm climates as a houseplant or outdoors in suitable conditions.
