Capparis loranthifolia

Capparis loranthifolia, commonly known as the Indian crossberry, is a flowering plant native to parts of South Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are small, berry-like structures that ripen to a dark red color. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the stems are woody and often thorny.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
