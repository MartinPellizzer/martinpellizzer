Centropogon ciliatus

Centropogon ciliatus, commonly known as the pink trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Centropogon ciliatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
