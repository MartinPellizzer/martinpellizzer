Croton almadinensis

Croton almadinensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically palmate, with lobes that vary in color from green to reddish or yellowish.
