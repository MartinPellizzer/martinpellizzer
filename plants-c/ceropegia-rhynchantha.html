Ceropegia rhynchantha

Ceropegia rhynchantha, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous root system and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are usually thick and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Ceropegia rhynchantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
