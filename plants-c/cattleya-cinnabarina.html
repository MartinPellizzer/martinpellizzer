Cattleya cinnabarina

Cattleya cinnabarina, commonly known as the cinnamon orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bright orange flowers with red and yellow streaks, which are highly sought after in the orchid trade. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Cattleya cinnabarina is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
