Crinum angustifolium var. blandum

Crinum angustifolium var. blandum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
