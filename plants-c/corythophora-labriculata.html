Corythophora labriculata

Corythophora labriculata, commonly known as the rock corythophora, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand arid and rocky environments.

Corythophora labriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
