Carex fusca var. chlorostachya

Carex fusca var. chlorostachya, commonly known as the green-stem sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

The variety chlorostachya is distinguished by its distinctly green stems and foliage, setting it apart from other variants of Carex fusca.
