Cephalophyllum vanheerdei

Cephalophyllum vanheerdei, commonly known as the Cape mountain beauty, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces striking, lantern-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
