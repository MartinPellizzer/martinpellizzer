Clematis heracleifolia var. kousabotan

Clematis heracleifolia var. kousabotan, commonly known as Japanese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
