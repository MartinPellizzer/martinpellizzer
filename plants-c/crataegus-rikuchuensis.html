Crataegus rikuchuensis

Crataegus rikuchuensis, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its ornamental value.

Crataegus rikuchuensis thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
