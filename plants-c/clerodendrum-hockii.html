Clerodendrum hockii

Clerodendrum hockii, commonly known as the Hockii flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
