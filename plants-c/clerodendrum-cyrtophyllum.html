Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum

Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum, commonly known as the white clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The genus Clerodendrum is part of the Rubiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
