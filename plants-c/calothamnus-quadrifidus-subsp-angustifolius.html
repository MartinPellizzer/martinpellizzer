Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp. angustifolius

Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a small shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating, and the plant is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is well adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
