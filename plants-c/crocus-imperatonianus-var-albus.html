Crocus imperatonianus var. albus

Crocus imperatonianus var. albus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant produces a bulbous rhizome and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The white crocus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
