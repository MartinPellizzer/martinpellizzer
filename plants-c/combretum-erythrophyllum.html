Combretum erythrophyllum

Combretum erythrophyllum, commonly known as the red-leaf combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red to orange-red leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
