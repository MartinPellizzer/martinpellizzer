Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. variabile

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

variabile, commonly known as the variable golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange floral heads and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands or open woodlands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies variabile is distinguished by its variable leaf morphology and flower coloration compared to other subspecies of Chrysocephalum apiculatum.
