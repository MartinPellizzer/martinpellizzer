Cordia peteri

Cordia peteri, commonly known as the African star jelly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Cordia peteri is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
