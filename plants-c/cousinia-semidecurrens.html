Cousinia semidecurrens

Cousinia semidecurrens, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting a low, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Cousinia semidecurrens is known for its resilience to drought and extreme temperatures, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
