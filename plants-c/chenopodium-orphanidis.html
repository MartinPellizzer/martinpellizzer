Chenopodium orphanidis

Chenopodium orphanidis, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Chenopodium orphanidis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in extreme desert conditions.
