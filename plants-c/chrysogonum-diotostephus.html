Chrysogonum diotostephus

Chrysogonum diotostephus, commonly known as the yellow sandroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and dunes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
