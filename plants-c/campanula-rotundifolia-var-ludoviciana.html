Campanula rotundifolia var. ludoviciana

Campanula rotundifolia var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants that support local wildlife.
