Cynosurus floccifolius

Cynosurus floccifolius, commonly known as the fluffy feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, soft, fluffy appearance due to its silky, white-haired inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Cynosurus floccifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
