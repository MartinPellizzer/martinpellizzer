Crataegus compta

Crataegus compta, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Crataegus compta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
