Crataegus edita

Crataegus edita, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody structure typical of the Rosaceae family.

It is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
