Campanula aureliana

Campanula aureliana, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, golden-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft down. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
