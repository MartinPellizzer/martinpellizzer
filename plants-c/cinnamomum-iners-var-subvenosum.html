Cinnamomum iners var. subvenosum

Cinnamomum iners var. subvenosum, commonly known as the narrow-veined cinnamonwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle spicy flavor to dishes.
