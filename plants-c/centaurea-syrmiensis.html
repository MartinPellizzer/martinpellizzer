Centaurea syrmiensis

Centaurea syrmiensis, commonly known as the Sirmium centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea syrmiensis is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.
