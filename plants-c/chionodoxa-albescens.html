Chionodoxa albescens

Chionodoxa albescens, commonly known as the white glory of the snow.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and appear after the flowers have faded.

Chionodoxa albescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
