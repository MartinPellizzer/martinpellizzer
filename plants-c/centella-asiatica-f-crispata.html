Centella asiatica f. crispata

Centella asiatica f. crispata, commonly known as the crested centella or crisp centella, is a variant of the Indian pennywort plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled, waxy leaves that give it a unique appearance. This plant is native to Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported benefits in promoting skin health and wound healing.
