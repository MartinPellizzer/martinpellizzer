Crotalaria senegalensis

Crotalaria senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegal crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is an erect annual herb that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like fruit, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Crotalaria senegalensis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
