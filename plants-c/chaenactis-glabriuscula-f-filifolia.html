Chaenactis glabriuscula f. filifolia

Chaenactis glabriuscula f. filifolia, commonly known as the filifoliate plainspeedwell, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, featuring wiry, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
