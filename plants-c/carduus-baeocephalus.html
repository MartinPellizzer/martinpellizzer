Carduus baeocephalus

Carduus baeocephalus, commonly known as the bluehead thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and a striking blue or purple flower head that resembles a small crown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
