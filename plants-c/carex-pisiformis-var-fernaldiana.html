Carex pisiformis var. fernaldiana

Carex pisiformis var. fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the top of the plant.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
