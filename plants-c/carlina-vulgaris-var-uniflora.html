Carlina vulgaris var. uniflora

Carlina vulgaris var. uniflora, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower head in its second year. Its flowers are surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
