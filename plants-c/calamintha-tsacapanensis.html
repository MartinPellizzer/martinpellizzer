Calamintha tsacapanensis

Calamintha tsacapanensis, commonly known as the Peruvian pennyroyal, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a strong, pungent scent, often described as similar to mint or camphor.

Calamintha tsacapanensis is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
