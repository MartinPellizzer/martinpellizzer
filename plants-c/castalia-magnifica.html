Castalia magnifica

Castalia magnifica, commonly known as the royal castalia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often displaying vibrant shades of green with intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
