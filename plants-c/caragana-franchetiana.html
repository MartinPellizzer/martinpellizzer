Caragana franchetiana

Caragana franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese bush clover, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

Caragana franchetiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
