Cordia macrocephala

Cordia macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest ecosystems.

Cordia macrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
