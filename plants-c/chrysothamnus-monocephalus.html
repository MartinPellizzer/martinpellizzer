Chrysothamnus monocephalus

Chrysothamnus monocephalus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the fall. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and poor soil.
