Chrozophora tinctoria

Chrozophora tinctoria, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
