Crataegus chantcha

Crataegus chantcha, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Crataegus chantcha is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
