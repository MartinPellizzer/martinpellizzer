Cistus monspeliensis subsp. canariensis

Cistus monspeliensis subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary rockrose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the warm, dry climate of its native habitat. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the unique flora of the region.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
