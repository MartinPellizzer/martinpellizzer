Colubrina alluaudii

Colubrina alluaudii, commonly known as the Madagascar trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or scrub habitats.

Colubrina alluaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
