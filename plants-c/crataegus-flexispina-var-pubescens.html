Crataegus flexispina var. pubescens

Crataegus flexispina var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, downy hairs. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
