Crepis erythia

Crepis erythia, commonly known as red hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flower heads that resemble those of other dandelion relatives. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its life cycle is primarily annual, with seeds dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
