Coelogyne speciosa var. fimbriata

Coelogyne speciosa var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often fringed petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
