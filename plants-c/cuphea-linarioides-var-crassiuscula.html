Cuphea linarioides var. crassiuscula

Cuphea linarioides var. crassiuscula, commonly known as the waxplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well-suited for container gardening and is often used as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
