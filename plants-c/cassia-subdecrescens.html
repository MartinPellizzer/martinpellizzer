Cassia subdecrescens

Cassia subdecrescens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia subdecrescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
