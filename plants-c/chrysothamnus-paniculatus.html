Chrysothamnus paniculatus

Chrysothamnus paniculatus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert areas.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and is an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
