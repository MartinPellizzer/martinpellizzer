Cepa fistulosa

Cepa fistulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that serve as storage organs for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Cepa fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
