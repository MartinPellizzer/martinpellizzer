Cynanchica naufraga

Cynanchica naufraga, commonly known as the wandering cucumber, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinct hairy texture.

Cynanchica naufraga is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types.
