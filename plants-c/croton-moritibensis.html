Croton moritibensis

Croton moritibensis, commonly known as the Moritibense Croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features striking, colorful leaves that range from green to shades of red and purple, making it visually distinctive. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathium structures, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton moritibensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
