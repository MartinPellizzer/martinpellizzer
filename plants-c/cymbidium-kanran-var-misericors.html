Cymbidium kanran var. misericors

Cymbidium kanran var. misericors, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, pale pink or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
