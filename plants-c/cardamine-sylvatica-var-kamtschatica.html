Cardamine sylvatica var. kamtschatica

Cardamine sylvatica var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and edible leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical Cardamine sylvatica.
