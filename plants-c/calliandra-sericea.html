Calliandra sericea

Calliandra sericea, commonly known as silky calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful in soil stabilization and agroforestry.

Calliandra sericea is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a source of fodder for livestock.
