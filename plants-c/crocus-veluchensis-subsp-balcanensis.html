Crocus veluchensis subsp. balcanensis

Crocus veluchensis subsp.

balcanensis, commonly known as the Balkan crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter or early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
