Crataegus wilsonii

Crataegus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
