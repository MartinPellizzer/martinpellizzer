Carex extensa subsp. viestina

Carex extensa subsp.

viestina, commonly known as the Viestina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Siberia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, fens, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

The subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting diverse insect and bird life.
