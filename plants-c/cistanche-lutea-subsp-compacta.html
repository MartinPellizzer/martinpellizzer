Cistanche lutea subsp. compacta

Cistanche lutea subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers and has a highly branched, vine-like structure. It is often found growing on the roots of various shrubs and grasses.

Due to its unique ecological role and medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
