Capsella bursa-pastoris f. coronopus

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. coronopus, commonly known as the crown-leaf shepherd's purse, is a variant of the common shepherd's purse.

It is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods. The leaves are lobed and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, typical of the family. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in temperate regions.
