Chrysophyllum argenteum

Chrysophyllum argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf starapple, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a smooth, yellowish-green berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single large seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
