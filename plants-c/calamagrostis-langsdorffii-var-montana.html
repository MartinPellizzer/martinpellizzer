Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. montana

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. montana, commonly known as the mountain blue grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically blue to purple in hue, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a backdrop in landscaped areas.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for temperate climates.
