Cavendishia awa

Cavendishia awa, commonly known as the Awa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous flowers arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cavendishia awa is often cultivated for its beauty and is adapted to grow in well-drained, humus-rich soils.
