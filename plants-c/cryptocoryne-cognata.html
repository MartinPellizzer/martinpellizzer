Cryptocoryne cognata

Cryptocoryne cognata, commonly known as the Java cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, broad leaves that are often ribbed and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in soft, acidic water with moderate to high light conditions. It is commonly cultivated in freshwater aquariums due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

Cryptocoryne cognata is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of water parameters, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced aquarists.
