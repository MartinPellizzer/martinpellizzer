Calanthe lepida

Calanthe lepida, commonly known as the pink calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
