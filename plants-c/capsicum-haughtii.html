Capsicum haughtii

Capsicum haughtii, commonly known as the Mexican chili or the Huautla chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit develops from a single ovary and is used in traditional Mexican cuisine for its mild to medium heat level.
