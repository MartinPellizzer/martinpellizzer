Campanula glomerata f. farinosa

Campanula glomerata f. farinosa, commonly known as the farinose bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, and the plant is often found in rocky or dry habitats. This variety is notable for its distinctive powdery coating on the leaves, which gives it its name "farinosa," meaning flour-like.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
