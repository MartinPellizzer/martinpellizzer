Carex goodenowii var. olmucensis

Carex goodenowii var. olmucensis, commonly known as the Olmec sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to saturated soils. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section typical of sedges, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
