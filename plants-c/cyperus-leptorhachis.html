Cyperus leptorhachis

Cyperus leptorhachis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, forming dense stands that can dominate aquatic habitats.

Cyperus leptorhachis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
