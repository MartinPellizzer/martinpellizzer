Coleus barbatus var. grandis

Coleus barbatus var. grandis, commonly known as the giant flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with broad, lance-shaped leaves that can be red, purple, or green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
