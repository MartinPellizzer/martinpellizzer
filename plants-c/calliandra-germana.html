Calliandra germana

Calliandra germana, commonly known as the yellow calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow into a shrub or small tree.

Calliandra germana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
