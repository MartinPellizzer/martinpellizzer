Cynoglossum alatum

Cynoglossum alatum, commonly known as the heartwound plant, is a flowering plant in the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
