Calochortus venustus var. superbus

Calochortus venustus var. superbus, commonly known as the showy gold crocus, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to California.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red or purple throat, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
