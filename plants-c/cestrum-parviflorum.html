Cestrum parviflorum

Cestrum parviflorum, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cestrum parviflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
