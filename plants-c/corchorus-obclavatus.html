Corchorus obclavatus

Corchorus obclavatus, commonly known as the common kochia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It features prostrate or erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with soft, silky hairs. The leaves are broadly ovate to rhombic in shape, often with a waxy coating and a bluish-green color. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called heads.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
