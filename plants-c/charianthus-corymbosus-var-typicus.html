Charianthus corymbosus var. typicus

Charianthus corymbosus var. typicus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
