Cyperus poeppigii

Cyperus poeppigii, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures.

Cyperus poeppigii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
