Coleus aquaticus

Coleus aquaticus, commonly known as water coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Coleus aquaticus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
