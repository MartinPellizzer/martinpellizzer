Clintonia uniflora

Clintonia uniflora, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy white flower that blooms in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and moths.

Clintonia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
