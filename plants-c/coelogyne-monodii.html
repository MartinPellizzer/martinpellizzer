Coelogyne monodii

Coelogyne monodii, commonly known as the Monod's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, narrow leaf and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant blooms once every few years, typically in the summer or early autumn. Its flowers are borne on a raceme and are known for their intricate patterns and long-lasting fragrance.

Coelogyne monodii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
