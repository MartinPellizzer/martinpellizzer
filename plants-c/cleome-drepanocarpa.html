Cleome drepanocarpa

Cleome drepanocarpa, commonly known as the spreading cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that attract pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cleome drepanocarpa is also known for its ability to self-seed and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
