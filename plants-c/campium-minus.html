Campium minus

Campium minus, commonly known as lesser campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Campium minus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
