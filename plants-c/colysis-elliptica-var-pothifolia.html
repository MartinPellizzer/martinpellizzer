Colysis elliptica var. pothifolia

Colysis elliptica var. pothifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf colysis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
