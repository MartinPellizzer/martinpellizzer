Chamaemelum uniglandulosum

Chamaemelum uniglandulosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and it has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in disturbed areas.
