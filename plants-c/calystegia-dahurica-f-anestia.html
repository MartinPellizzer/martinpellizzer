Calystegia dahurica f. anestia

Calystegia dahurica f. anestia, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a perennial vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful management.
