Cassia magnifolia

Cassia magnifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia magnifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
