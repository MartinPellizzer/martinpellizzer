Cleistogenes thoroldii

Cleistogenes thoroldii, commonly known as the Thorold's bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Cleistogenes thoroldii is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
