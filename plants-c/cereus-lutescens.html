Cereus lutescens

Cereus lutescens, commonly known as the Yellow Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
