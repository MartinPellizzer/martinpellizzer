Cucubalus royenii

Cucubalus royenii, commonly known as the royal cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, edible fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are typically harvested when they reach a mature green color, though they can be left to ripen to a yellow or orange hue.

The botanical characteristics of Cucubalus royenii make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
