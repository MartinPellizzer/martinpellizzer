Centaurea rhenana subsp. tartarea

Centaurea rhenana subsp.

tartarea, commonly known as the Carpathian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry grasslands, open woodlands, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features slender stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces bright red or purple flowers in composite flower heads. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem, alternate leaves, and a basal rosette of foliage.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
