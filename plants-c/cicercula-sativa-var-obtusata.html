Cicercula sativa var. obtusata

Cicercula sativa var. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf salsify, is a variant of the salsify plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is a biennial herb characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and lobed, serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are often harvested for culinary use, offering a slightly sweet and nutty flavor.

This variety is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
