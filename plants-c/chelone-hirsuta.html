Chelone hirsuta

Chelone hirsuta, commonly known as hairy jugger, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.
