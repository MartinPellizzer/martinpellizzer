Caladium auritum

Caladium auritum, commonly known as the earleaf caladium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant's unique ear-like leaves, which give it its common name, are a notable feature in its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Caladium auritum is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces due to its vibrant foliage.
