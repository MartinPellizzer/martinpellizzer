Clematis ochotensis subsp. coerulescens

Clematis ochotensis subsp.

coerulescens, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
