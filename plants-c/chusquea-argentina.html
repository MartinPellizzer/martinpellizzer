Chusquea argentina

Chusquea argentina, commonly known as the Argentine bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to South America.

It is a clump-forming plant that typically grows in humid, temperate regions, often in mountainous areas. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, tufted appearance.

Chusquea argentina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
