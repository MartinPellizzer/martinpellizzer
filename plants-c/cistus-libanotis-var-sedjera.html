Cistus libanotis var. sedjera

Cistus libanotis var. sedjera, commonly known as the Lebanese rock rose, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, while its resilience makes it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and its ability to support local pollinators.
