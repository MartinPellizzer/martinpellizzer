Convolvulus microphyllus var. orreanus

Convolvulus microphyllus var. orreanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
