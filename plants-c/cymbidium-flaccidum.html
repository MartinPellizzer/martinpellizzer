Cymbidium flaccidum

Cymbidium flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Thailand and Vietnam.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a short, stout stem that supports its showy, fragrant blooms.

Cymbidium flaccidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
