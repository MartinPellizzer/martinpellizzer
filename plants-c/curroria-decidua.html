Curroria decidua

Curroria decidua, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. Its ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

The tree also plays a role in traditional medicine and local agriculture.
