Carduus foliosus

Carduus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
