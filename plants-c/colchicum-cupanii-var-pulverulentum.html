Colchicum cupanii var. pulverulentum

Colchicum cupanii var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the powdered colchicum, is a rare and striking member of the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Iberian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variant is distinguished by its finely powdered appearance on the petals, giving it a unique and delicate aesthetic.
