Calanthe bicolor var. treutleri

Calanthe bicolor var. treutleri, commonly known as the Treutler's calanthe, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a glossy sheen. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
