Crocodilodes kuntzei

Crocodilodes kuntzei, commonly known as the Kuntze's crocodile plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky, red bracts that resemble the scales of a crocodile. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
