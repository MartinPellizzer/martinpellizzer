Chrysothamnus turbinata

Chrysothamnus turbinata, commonly known as Turbinated goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
