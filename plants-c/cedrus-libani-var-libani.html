Cedrus libani var. libani

Cedrus libani var. libani, commonly known as the Lebanese cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, and it produces large, globose cones. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

This species is notable for its historical significance and is considered a symbol of strength and endurance.
