Crataegus neofaxonii

Crataegus neofaxonii, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and they serve as a food source for various birds and mammals.

Crataegus neofaxonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
