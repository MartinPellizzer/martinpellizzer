Castanopsis ceratacantha var. semiserrata

Castanopsis ceratacantha var. semiserrata, commonly known as the serrate talaud.

This variant is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, inedible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are dispersed by animals.
