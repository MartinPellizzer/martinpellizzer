Celtis gardneri

Celtis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

Celtis gardneri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in rocky soils and along canyons.
