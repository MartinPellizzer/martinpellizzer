Convolvulus stachydifolius var. stachydifolius

Convolvulus stachydifolius var. stachydifolius, commonly known as the stachydifolius convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
