Cinchona carabayensis

Cinchona carabayensis, commonly known as the Peruvian bark, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is most notable for its bark, which contains alkaloids such as quinine, historically used to treat malaria. It typically grows at high altitudes in humid, shaded environments.
