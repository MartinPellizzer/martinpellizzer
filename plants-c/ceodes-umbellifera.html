Ceodes umbellifera

Ceodes umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat, spreading cluster. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.
