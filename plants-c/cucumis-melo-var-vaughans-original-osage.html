Cucumis melo var. vaughans-original-osage

Cucumis melo var. vaughans-original-osage, commonly known as the Osage orange, is a variant of the cucumber family plant.

It is native to the southern United States and is characterized by its thick, yellow-orange fruit with a rough, bumpy skin. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences or in hedgerows. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit, while not typically eaten due to its bitter taste, has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a natural barrier due to its thorny exterior.
