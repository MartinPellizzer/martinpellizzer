Crassula andegavensis

Crassula andegavensis, commonly known as the Andes crassula, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often at high altitudes where temperatures can be quite cool. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
