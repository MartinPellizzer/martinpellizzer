Cereus siedelii

Cereus siedelii, commonly known as the Siedel's candleplant, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xerophytic gardens and rockeries.
