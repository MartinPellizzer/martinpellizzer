Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. congestum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

congestum, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting or silver everlasting, is a perennial herb native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers.

The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The subspecies congestum is distinguished by its more compact form and dense flowering compared to other subspecies of Chrysocephalum.
