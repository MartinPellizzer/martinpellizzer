Cissus aegirophylla

Cissus aegirophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf creeper, is a vigorous climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus aegirophylla is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
