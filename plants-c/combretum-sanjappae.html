Combretum sanjappae

Combretum sanjappae, commonly known as the Sanjappa combretum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
