Campanula saxifraga var. transcaucasica

Campanula saxifraga var. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
