Cacalia gnaphalioides

Cacalia gnaphalioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
