Cylindrokelupha havilandii

Cylindrokelupha havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's cylindrical pod plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical pods that encase its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of yellow or green, adding to its ornamental value.
