Campanula macrorhiza subsp. angustiflora

Campanula macrorhiza subsp.

angustiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
