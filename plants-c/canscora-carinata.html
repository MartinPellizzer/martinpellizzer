Canscora carinata

Canscora carinata, commonly known as the hairy root orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is known for its intricate floral structure. Its roots are covered in fine, hairy structures, which may aid in water retention and nutrient absorption.

Canscora carinata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
