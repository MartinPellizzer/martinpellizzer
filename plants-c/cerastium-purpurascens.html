Cerastium purpurascens

Cerastium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Cerastium purpurascens is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
