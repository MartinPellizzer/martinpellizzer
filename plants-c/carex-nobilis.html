Carex nobilis

Carex nobilis, commonly known as the noble sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
