Cenchrus thulinii

Cenchrus thulinii, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Cenchrus thulinii is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
