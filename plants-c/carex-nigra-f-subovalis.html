Carex nigra f. subovalis

Carex nigra f. subovalis, commonly known as black sedge.

It is a variant of Carex nigra, characterized by its dark, almost black foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a glossy appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish or brownish hues.
