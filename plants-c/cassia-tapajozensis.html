Cassia tapajozensis

Cassia tapajozensis, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is adapted to the dense canopy of the rainforest.

Cassia tapajozensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and animals.
