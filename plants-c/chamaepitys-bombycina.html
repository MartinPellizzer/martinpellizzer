Chamaepitys bombycina

Chamaepitys bombycina, commonly known as the silky fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and dense, soft needles that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The tree produces small, spherical cones that are often covered in a fine, silky layer of hairs. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically found at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
