Celastrus vitiensis

Celastrus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Vanuatu.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical regions.
