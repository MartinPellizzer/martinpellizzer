Caesalpinia falcaria var. capitata

Caesalpinia falcaria var. capitata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of clustered, pod-like fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The golden wattle is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
