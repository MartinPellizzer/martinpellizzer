Caesalpinia resupinata

Caesalpinia resupinata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange feathery flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and urban landscapes.
