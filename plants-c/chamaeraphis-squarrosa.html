Chamaeraphis squarrosa

Chamaeraphis squarrosa, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically forms dense mats on sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually linear and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.
