Carex magnursina

Carex magnursina, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream edges. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-stamened flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex magnursina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
