Caltha dentata

Caltha dentata, commonly known as the toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
