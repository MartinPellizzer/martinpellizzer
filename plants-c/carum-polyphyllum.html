Carum polyphyllum

Carum polyphyllum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their carminative and digestive properties.

The plant is also valued in some cuisines for its pungent, spicy flavor.
