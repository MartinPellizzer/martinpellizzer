Cornus alba subsp. tatarica

Cornus alba subsp.

tatarica, commonly known as the Siberian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark that peels in thin layers, often revealing a reddish inner bark.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental bark and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
