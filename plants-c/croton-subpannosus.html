Croton subpannosus

Croton subpannosus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
