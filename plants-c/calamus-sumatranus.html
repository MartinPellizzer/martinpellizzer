Calamus sumatranus

Calamus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a distinct, aromatic fragrance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically emerging from the base of the plant.

Calamus sumatranus is often cultivated for its rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
