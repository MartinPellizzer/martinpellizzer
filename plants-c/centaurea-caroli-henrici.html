Centaurea caroli-henrici

Centaurea caroli-henrici, commonly known as Caroli-Henrici's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
