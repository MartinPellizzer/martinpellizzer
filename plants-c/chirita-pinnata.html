Chirita pinnata

Chirita pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in spring and summer. Chirita pinnata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.
