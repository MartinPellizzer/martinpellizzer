Carex paniculata var. caucasica

Carex paniculata var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
