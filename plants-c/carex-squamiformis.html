Carex squamiformis

Carex squamiformis, commonly known as the squamose sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive scaly appearance on the lower stem.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.
