Canscora roxburghii

Canscora roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or Indian sarsaparilla, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and wetlands.

The rhizomes of Canscora roxburghii are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
