Convolvulus sepium var. typicus

Convolvulus sepium var. typicus, commonly known as field bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its twining stems that can climb or spread along the ground. It has a deep root system, making it highly invasive and difficult to eradicate.

Its botanical features make it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
