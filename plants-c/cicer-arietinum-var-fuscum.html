Cicer arietinum var. fuscum

Cicer arietinum var. fuscum, commonly known as the dark chickpea, is a variety of the chickpea plant characterized by its darker seed coloration.

This variety belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is an annual herb that produces trifoliate leaves and cylindrical pods containing several seeds. The plant is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and fiber.

Due to its dark color, it is often used in specialty culinary applications and traditional medicine.
