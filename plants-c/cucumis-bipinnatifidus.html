Cucumis bipinnatifidus

Cucumis bipinnatifidus, commonly known as the prickly cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits resembling small cucumbers. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a food source in certain regions.
