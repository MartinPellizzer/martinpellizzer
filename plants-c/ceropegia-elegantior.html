Ceropegia elegantior

Ceropegia elegantior, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing perennial vine characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery-gray underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia elegantior is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
