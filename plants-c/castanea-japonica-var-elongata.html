Castanea japonica var. elongata

Castanea japonica var. elongata, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut elongata, is a variant of the Japanese chestnut native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, spiny fruits that mature in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
