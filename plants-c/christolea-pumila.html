Christolea pumila

Christolea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Christolea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
