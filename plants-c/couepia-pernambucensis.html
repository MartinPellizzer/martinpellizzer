Couepia pernambucensis

Couepia pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco couepia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Couepia pernambucensis is valued for its ornamental potential and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
