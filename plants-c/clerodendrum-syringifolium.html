Clerodendrum syringifolium

Clerodendrum syringifolium, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Clerodendrum syringifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
