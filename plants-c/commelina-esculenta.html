Commelina esculenta

Commelina esculenta, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
