Calamintha kilimandschari

Calamintha kilimandschari, commonly known as Kilimanjaro mint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of East Africa.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Calamintha kilimandschari is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive ailments and as a natural remedy for headaches.
