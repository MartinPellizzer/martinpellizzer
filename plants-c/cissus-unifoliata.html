Cissus unifoliata

Cissus unifoliata, commonly known as the single-leaf fig or glasshouse ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thin, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
