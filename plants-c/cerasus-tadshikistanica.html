Cerasus tadshikistanica

Cerasus tadshikistanica, commonly known as the Tadshikistan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, is edible but not widely cultivated.

Cerasus tadshikistanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
