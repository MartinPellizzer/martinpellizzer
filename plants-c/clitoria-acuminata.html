Clitoria acuminata

Clitoria acuminata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds and a starchy, tuberous root. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Clitoria acuminata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
