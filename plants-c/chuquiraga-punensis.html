Chuquiraga punensis

Chuquiraga punensis, commonly known as the Andean burr plant, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, which can resemble small leaves. It thrives in cool, dry climates and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments.

Chuquiraga punensis is also valued for its traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of fiber.
