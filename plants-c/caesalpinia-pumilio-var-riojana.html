Caesalpinia pumilio var. riojana

Caesalpinia pumilio var. riojana, commonly known as the Riojana coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has compound leaves with leaflets that are usually oblong and slightly serrated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
