Conyza burmeana

Conyza burmeana, commonly known as the Burmese fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as fields and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Conyza burmeana is often considered a weed but has some traditional medicinal uses in certain regions.
