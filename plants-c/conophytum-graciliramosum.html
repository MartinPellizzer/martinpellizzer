Conophytum graciliramosum

Conophytum graciliramosum, commonly known as the slender-branched conophytum, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
