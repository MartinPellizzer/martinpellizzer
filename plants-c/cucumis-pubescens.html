Cucumis pubescens

Cucumis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, green fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms.

Cucumis pubescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
