Cynanchum yesoense

Cynanchum yesoense, commonly known as the Yesso milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers with a distinctive horn-shaped structure, typical of the milkweed family. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Cynanchum yesoense thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.
