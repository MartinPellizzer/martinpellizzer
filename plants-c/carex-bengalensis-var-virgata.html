Carex bengalensis var. virgata

Carex bengalensis var. virgata, commonly known as the Bengal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex bengalensis var. virgata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
