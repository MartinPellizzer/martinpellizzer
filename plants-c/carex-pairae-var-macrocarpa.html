Carex pairae var. macrocarpa

Carex pairae var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
