Corydalis stricta subsp. holosepala

Corydalis stricta subsp.

holosepala, commonly known as the narrowleaf lousewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, clustered in loose racemes, and have spurred petals that aid in pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
