Cerastium caesareosylvaticum f. pseudouniflorum

Cerastium caesareosylvaticum f. pseudouniflorum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its prostrate habit and small, hairy leaves.

It produces clusters of white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. The plant is native to mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
