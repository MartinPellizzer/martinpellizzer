Calamus grandis

Calamus grandis, commonly known as the giant sweetflag, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Aroidae.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Calamus grandis is native to wetlands and marshy areas in Southeast Asia and is valued for its aromatic rhizomes.
