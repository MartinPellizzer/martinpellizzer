Cyathea carolinae

Cyathea carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests and coastal areas. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea carolinae plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
