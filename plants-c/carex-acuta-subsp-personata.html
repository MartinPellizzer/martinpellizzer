Carex acuta subsp. personata

Carex acuta subsp.

personata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
