Cassia glutinosa

Cassia glutinosa, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia glutinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
