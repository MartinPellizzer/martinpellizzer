Cyperus jacquinii

Cyperus jacquinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the stem. Cyperus jacquinii is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
