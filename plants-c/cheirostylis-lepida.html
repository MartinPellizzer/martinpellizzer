Cheirostylis lepida

Cheirostylis lepida, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.

Cheirostylis lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
