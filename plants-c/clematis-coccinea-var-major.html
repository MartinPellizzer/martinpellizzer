Clematis coccinea var. major

Clematis coccinea var. major, commonly known as the red clematis, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with compound leaves and produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length, and its flowers are typically 5 to 7 inches in diameter.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
