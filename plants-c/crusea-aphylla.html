Crusea aphylla

Crusea aphylla, commonly known as the leafless crusea, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its absence of leaves.

It belongs to the family Cruseaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its most distinctive feature is its lack of foliage, which sets it apart from other members of its family.

Despite its lack of leaves, it is capable of photosynthesis through its stems, adapting to its environment in a specialized way.
