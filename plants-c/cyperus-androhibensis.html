Cyperus androhibensis

Cyperus androhibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or brown.

Cyperus androhibensis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
