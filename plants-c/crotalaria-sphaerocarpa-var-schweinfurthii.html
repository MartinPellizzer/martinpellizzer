Crotalaria sphaerocarpa var. schweinfurthii

Crotalaria sphaerocarpa var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the globe cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed pods, which are typically green and covered in small, hooked spines. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
