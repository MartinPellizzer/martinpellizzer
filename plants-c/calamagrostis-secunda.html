Calamagrostis secunda

Calamagrostis secunda, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tan plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that gives it its common name.

Calamagrostis secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
