Cissus articulata

Cissus articulata, commonly known as the bone plant or devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that can break easily, giving it the common name "devil's backbone.". The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

Cissus articulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
