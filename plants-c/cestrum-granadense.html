Cestrum granadense

Cestrum granadense, commonly known as the granadense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Cestrum granadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
