Coffea nigerina

Coffea nigerina, commonly known as the Nigerian coffee plant, is a species native to West Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Coffea nigerina is less commonly cultivated than other coffee species but is valued for its unique flavor profile.
