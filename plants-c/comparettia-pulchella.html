Comparettia pulchella

Comparettia pulchella, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Comparettia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
