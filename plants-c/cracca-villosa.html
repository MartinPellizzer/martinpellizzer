Cracca villosa

Cracca villosa, commonly known as the hairy cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, upright stem covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cracca villosa produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
