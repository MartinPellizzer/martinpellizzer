Crepis rodigioides

Crepis rodigioides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant is typically found at high altitudes, where it adapts to cold and harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
