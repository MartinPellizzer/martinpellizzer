Croton insularis var. genuinus

Croton insularis var. genuinus, commonly known as the Island Croton, is a tropical shrub native to specific regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, yellow, and orange. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.
