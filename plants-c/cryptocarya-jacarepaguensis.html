Cryptocarya jacarepaguensis

Cryptocarya jacarepaguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in Atlantic Forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
