Cerastium arvense var. ruderale

Cerastium arvense var. ruderale, commonly known as field mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
