Calliandra nebulosa

Calliandra nebulosa, commonly known as the cloud flower, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble clouds, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra nebulosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
