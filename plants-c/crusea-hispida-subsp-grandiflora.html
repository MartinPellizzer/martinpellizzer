Crusea hispida subsp. grandiflora

Crusea hispida subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a rosette growth habit with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
