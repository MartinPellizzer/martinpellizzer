Centaurea pepoliana

Centaurea pepoliana, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has a compact growth habit and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea pepoliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
