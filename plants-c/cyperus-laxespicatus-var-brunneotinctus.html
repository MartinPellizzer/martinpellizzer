Cyperus laxespicatus var. brunneotinctus

Cyperus laxespicatus var. brunneotinctus, commonly known as the brown-stained flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, or in flooded fields.

Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it a common weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
