Cerasus × media

Cerasus × media, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild fragrance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and seasonal interest.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
