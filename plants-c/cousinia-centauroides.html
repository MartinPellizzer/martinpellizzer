Cousinia centauroides

Cousinia centauroides, commonly known as the centaur's crown, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cousinia centauroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
