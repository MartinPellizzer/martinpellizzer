Chrysolyga salicifolia

Chrysolyga salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
