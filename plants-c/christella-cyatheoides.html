Christella cyatheoides

Christella cyatheoides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a tall, evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a large, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, dark brown to blackish layer of dead fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that develop on the undersides of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
