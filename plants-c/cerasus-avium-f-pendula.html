Cerasus avium f. pendula

Cerasus avium f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a unique variation of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible cherries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
