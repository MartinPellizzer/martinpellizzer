Crataegus jozana

Crataegus jozana, commonly known as the Jozan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often sour, and they serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Crataegus jozana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscape design due to its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
