Carex elibates

Carex elibates, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to wet or moist environments, it thrives in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces spikelets that are typically green or brown, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
