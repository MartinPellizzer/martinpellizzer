Chassalia curviflora var. grandifolia

Chassalia curviflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered chassalia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is valued for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for warm climates.
