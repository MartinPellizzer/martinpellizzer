Cyathea decurrens var. vaupelii

Cyathea decurrens var. vaupelii, commonly known as the Vaupel's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer of old fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
