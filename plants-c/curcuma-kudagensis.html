Curcuma kudagensis

Curcuma kudagensis, commonly known as the Kudagani turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often reaching up to 30 cm in length.

The rhizomes are valued for their potential medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
