Cyathea dryopteroides

Cyathea dryopteroides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer of old fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive and dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-drained soil.

Cyathea dryopteroides plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and habitat for various organisms.
