Chamaecrista bahiae

Chamaecrista bahiae, commonly known as Bahian ticktree, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with upright stems and small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
