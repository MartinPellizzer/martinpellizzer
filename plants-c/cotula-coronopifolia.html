Cotula coronopifolia

Cotula coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns or white daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white flowers with yellow centers, often arranged in a circular pattern. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Cotula coronopifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
