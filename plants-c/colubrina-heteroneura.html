Colubrina heteroneura

Colubrina heteroneura, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.
