Carex limosa var. humilior

Carex limosa var. humilior, commonly known as the low sedgeland sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland environments.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This variety typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and small, three-ranked flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
