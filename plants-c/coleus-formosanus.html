Coleus formosanus

Coleus formosanus, commonly known as the Taiwan coleus, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 inches. Its leaves are often brightly colored, ranging from red, purple, and maroon to shades of green.

It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes.
