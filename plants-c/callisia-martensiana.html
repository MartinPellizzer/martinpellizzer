Callisia martensiana

Callisia martensiana, commonly known as the Mexican carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes plants like spiderworts and grasses. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.
