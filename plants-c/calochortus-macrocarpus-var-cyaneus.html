Calochortus macrocarpus var. cyaneus

Calochortus macrocarpus var. cyaneus, commonly known as the blue glacier lily, is a striking perennial wildflower native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, sky-blue to lavender-colored petals that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often arranged in a single stem, with each bloom featuring a prominent, recurved tepal that resembles a cup.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
