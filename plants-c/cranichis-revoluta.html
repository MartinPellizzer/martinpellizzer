Cranichis revoluta

Cranichis revoluta, commonly known as the rolled orchid, is a delicate terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive rolled lip. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
