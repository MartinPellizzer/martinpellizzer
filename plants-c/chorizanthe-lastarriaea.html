Chorizanthe lastarriaea

Chorizanthe lastarriaea, commonly known as the Lastarria's cholla, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, branched stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its extensive root system and ability to store water in its stems.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
