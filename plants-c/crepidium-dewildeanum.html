Crepidium dewildeanum

Crepidium dewildeanum, commonly known as the Dewilde's orchid, is a rare and unusual orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, often twisted or spiraled floral structure, which sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with intricate patterns of colors that range from white to pale pink.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
