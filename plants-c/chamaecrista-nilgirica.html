Chamaecrista nilgirica

Chamaecrista nilgirica, commonly known as the Nilgiri tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are encased in distinctive, twisted pods, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

Chamaecrista nilgirica is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
