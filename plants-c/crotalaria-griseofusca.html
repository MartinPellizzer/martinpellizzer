Crotalaria griseofusca

Crotalaria griseofusca, commonly known as the grayleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with dark veins and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
