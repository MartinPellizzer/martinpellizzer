Chrysanthemum disciforme

Chrysanthemum disciforme, commonly known as the flatleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chrysanthemum disciforme is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
