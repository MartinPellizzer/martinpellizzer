Chartacalyx accrescens

Chartacalyx accrescens, commonly known as the creeping calyx plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive calyx that enlarges as the fruit matures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
