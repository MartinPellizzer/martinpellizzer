Cassia sumatrana

Cassia sumatrana, commonly known as Java cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia sumatrana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
