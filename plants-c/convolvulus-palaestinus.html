Convolvulus palaestinus

Convolvulus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Convolvulus palaestinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
