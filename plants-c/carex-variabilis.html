Carex variabilis

Carex variabilis, commonly known as variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its reproductive success.

Carex variabilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
