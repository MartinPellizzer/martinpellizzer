Crataegus × poplavskae

Crataegus × poplavskae, commonly known as the Poplavska rosehip.

This hybrid plant is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, specifically originating from crosses between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus monspichosa. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
