Crataegus heldreichii

Crataegus heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
