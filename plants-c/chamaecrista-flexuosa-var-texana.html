Chamaecrista flexuosa var. texana

Chamaecrista flexuosa var. texana, commonly known as Texas burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, twisted pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
