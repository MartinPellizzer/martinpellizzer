Crocus aureus

Crocus aureus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland habitats or meadows. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

The golden crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens.
