Cinchona capuli

Cinchona capuli, commonly known as the Philippine quinine tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain the alkaloid quinine, which has historically been used in medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland to mid-elevation forests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical and pharmacological research.
