Cordyline amboynensis

Cordyline amboynensis, commonly known as the Ti plant or prayer plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Pacific Islands and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white, pink, or red streaks. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a favored plant for both ornamental and cultural purposes.
