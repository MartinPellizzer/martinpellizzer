Carex ebenea

Carex ebenea, commonly known as the velvet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex ebenea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
