Carex berteroana var. colchaguensis

Carex berteroana var. colchaguensis, commonly known as the Magellanic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the temperate regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in forest understories or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
