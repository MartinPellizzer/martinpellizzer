Commelina senegalensis

Commelina senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelina genus within the family Commelinaceae, which includes many species with striking blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences that resemble spikes.

Commelina senegalensis is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural areas.
