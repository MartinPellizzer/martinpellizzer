Crocus speciosus subsp. bolensis

Crocus speciosus subsp.

bolensis, commonly known as the Bolens' crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late autumn to early winter.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.

The subspecies is closely related to other crocus species but is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and geographic distribution.
