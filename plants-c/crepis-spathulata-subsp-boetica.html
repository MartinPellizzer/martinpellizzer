Crepis spathulata subsp. boetica

Crepis spathulata subsp.

boetica, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.
