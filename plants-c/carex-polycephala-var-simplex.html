Carex polycephala var. simplex

Carex polycephala var. simplex, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized root systems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swamps.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
