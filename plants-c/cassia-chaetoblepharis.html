Cassia chaetoblepharis

Cassia chaetoblepharis, commonly known as the hairy-leaved cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, hairy leaflets, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds.

Cassia chaetoblepharis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of biomass due to its hardy nature.
