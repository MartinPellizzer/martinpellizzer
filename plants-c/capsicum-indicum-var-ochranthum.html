Capsicum indicum var. ochranthum

Capsicum indicum var. ochranthum, commonly known as the red bird pepper, is a tropical plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that turn bright red when mature. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and relatively small, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
