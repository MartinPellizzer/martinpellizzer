Calceolaria landbeckii

Calceolaria landbeckii, commonly known as the landbeck's slipper orchid, is a member of the Calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
