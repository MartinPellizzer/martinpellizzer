Clinopodium floridum

Clinopodium floridum, commonly known as Florida mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported soothing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
