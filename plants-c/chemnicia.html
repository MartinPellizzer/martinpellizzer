Chemnicia

Chemnicia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Chemnicia has a distinctive root system that allows it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.
