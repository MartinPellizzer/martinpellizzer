Cissus repanda var. repanda

Cissus repanda var. repanda, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and restoration projects.
