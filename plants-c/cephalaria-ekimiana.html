Cephalaria ekimiana

Cephalaria ekimiana, commonly known as the Turkish thistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought resistance.

Cephalaria ekimiana is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
