Calandrinia trifida

Calandrinia trifida, commonly known as the common velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Calandrinia trifida is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in various habitats.
