Calamus palembanicus

Calamus palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often used for making rattan and other handicrafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a distinct, aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.
