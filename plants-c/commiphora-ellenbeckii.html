Commiphora ellenbeckii

Commiphora ellenbeckii, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
