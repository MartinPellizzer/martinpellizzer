Crepis × oenipontana

Crepis × oenipontana, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Crepis × oenipontana is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
