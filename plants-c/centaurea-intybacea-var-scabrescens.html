Centaurea intybacea var. scabrescens

Centaurea intybacea var. scabrescens, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, rough hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
