Carex henryi

Carex henryi, commonly known as Henry's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and forest understories. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are usually a bluish-green color.

Carex henryi produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
