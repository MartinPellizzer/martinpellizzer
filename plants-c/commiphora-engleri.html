Commiphora engleri

Commiphora engleri, commonly known as African frankincense, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces a resinous sap that is harvested as frankincense, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and religious rituals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
