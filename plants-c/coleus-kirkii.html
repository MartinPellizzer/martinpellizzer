Coleus kirkii

Coleus kirkii, commonly known as the African shield leaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or purple.

Coleus kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
