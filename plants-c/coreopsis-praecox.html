Coreopsis praecox

Coreopsis praecox, commonly known as early coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow to orange flowers in the early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
