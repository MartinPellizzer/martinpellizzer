Chondrosea virgata

Chondrosea virgata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Chondrosea virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
