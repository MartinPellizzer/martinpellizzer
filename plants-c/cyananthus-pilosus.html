Cyananthus pilosus

Cyananthus pilosus, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

Cyananthus pilosus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
