Crepis nemorosa

Crepis nemorosa, commonly known as the wood mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a single head.

Crepis nemorosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
