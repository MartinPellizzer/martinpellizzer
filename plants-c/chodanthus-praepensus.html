Chodanthus praepensus

Chodanthus praepensus, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal conditions.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
