Castela erecta

Castela erecta, commonly known as the Natal plum or red-hot cat's tail, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features striking, red bracts that resemble cat tails, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Castela erecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
