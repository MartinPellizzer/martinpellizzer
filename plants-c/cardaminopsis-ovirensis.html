Cardaminopsis ovirensis

Cardaminopsis ovirensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
