Cinnamomum rhynchophyllum var. lampongum

Cinnamomum rhynchophyllum var. lampongum, commonly known as the Lampong cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate. Its bark is used to produce a spice similar to cinnamon, though it is less common than Cinnamomum verum.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
