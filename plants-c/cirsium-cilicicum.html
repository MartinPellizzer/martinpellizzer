Cirsium cilicicum

Cirsium cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are usually purple or pink and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
