Clematis orientalis f. glaucescens

Clematis orientalis f. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-gray clematis, is a compact shrub-like plant native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy, grayish-green appearance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens, particularly in late spring.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for trellises or as a container plant.
