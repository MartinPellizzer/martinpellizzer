Chaetogastra fraterna

Chaetogastra fraterna, commonly known as the fraternally clustered grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
