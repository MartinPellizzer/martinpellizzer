Cymbocarpum amanum

Cymbocarpum amanum, commonly known as the desert thornapple, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in harsh, dry environments, often found in the Andes and Patagonia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruiting bodies that resemble a cluster of small, spiky balls. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, windy conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it well-suited for survival in extreme climates.
