Convolvulus erubescens var. dilatatus

Convolvulus erubescens var. dilatatus, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and relatively easy cultivation.
