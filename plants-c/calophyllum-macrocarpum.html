Calophyllum macrocarpum

Calophyllum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is enclosed in a thick, fleshy aril. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
