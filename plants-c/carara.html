Carara

Carara, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to reddish as they mature. Carara is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Its leaves are typically opposite and simple, with a glossy surface that adds to its visual appeal.
