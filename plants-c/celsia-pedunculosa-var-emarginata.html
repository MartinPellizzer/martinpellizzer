Celsia pedunculosa var. emarginata

Celsia pedunculosa var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate celsia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive notched or emarginate base, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
