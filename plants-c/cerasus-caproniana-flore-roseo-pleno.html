Cerasus caproniana flore-roseo-pleno

Cerasus caproniana flore-roseo-pleno, commonly known as the rose-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its ornamental, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, edible fruits in the summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological habitats.
