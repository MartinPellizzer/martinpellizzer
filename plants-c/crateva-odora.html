Crateva odora

Crateva odora, commonly known as the sweet-scented quince, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its fragrance is particularly strong when the fruit is ripe, and it is also used in ornamental gardening.
