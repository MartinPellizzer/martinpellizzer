Convolvulus ruizii

Convolvulus ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Convolvulus ruizii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
