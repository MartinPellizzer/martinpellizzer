Cytisus Pendulinus

Yellow broom, scientifically known as Cytisus pendulinus, is a strikingly beautiful shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance addition to their landscapes. Despite its ornamental appeal, yellow broom is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for bees and other pollinators. However, it is important to note that this plant can be invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
