Chloris ramosissima

Chloris ramosissima, commonly known as blue grass or slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, creeping growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Chloris ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
