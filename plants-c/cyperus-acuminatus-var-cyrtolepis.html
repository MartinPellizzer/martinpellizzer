Cyperus acuminatus var. cyrtolepis

Cyperus acuminatus var. cyrtolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and linear, with a waxy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This plant is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
