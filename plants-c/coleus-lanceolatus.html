Coleus lanceolatus

Coleus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep reds to purples and yellows. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color.

Coleus lanceolatus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
