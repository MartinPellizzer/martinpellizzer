Cardamine jamesonii var. speciosa

Cardamine jamesonii var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy watercress, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
