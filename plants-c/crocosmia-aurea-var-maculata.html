Crocosmia aurea var. maculata

Crocosmia aurea var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted flame grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Iris family (Iridaceae) and features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with distinctive black spots, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Crocosmia aurea var. maculata is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
