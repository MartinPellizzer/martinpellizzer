It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spiral pattern.

Costus laevis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
