Carex vulpina var. crassinervis

Carex vulpina var. crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
