Campanula ludoviciana

Campanula ludoviciana, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
