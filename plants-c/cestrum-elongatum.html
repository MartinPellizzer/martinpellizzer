Cestrum elongatum

Cestrum elongatum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its flowers are followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds.

Cestrum elongatum is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
