Clematis thalictrifolia

Clematis thalictrifolia, commonly known as the meadow clematis, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, typically white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Clematis thalictrifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
