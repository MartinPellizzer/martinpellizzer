Chrysophyllum contumacense

Chrysophyllum contumacense, commonly known as the West African cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
