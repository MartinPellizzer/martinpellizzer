Crepis × druceana

Crepis × druceana, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves and small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Crepis × druceana is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
