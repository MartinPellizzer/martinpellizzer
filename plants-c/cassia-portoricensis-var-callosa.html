Cassia portoricensis var. callosa

Cassia portoricensis var. callosa, commonly known as the Puerto Rican cassia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
