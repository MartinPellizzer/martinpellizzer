Carex foliosissima var. pallidivaginata

Carex foliosissima var. pallidivaginata, commonly known as the pale bract sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a loose panicle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
