Crucifera arabis

Crucifera arabis, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically four-petaled, with a distinctive cross-shaped appearance typical of the crucifer family.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
