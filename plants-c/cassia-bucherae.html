Cassia bucherae

Cassia bucherae, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets that provide a lush, green canopy.

Cassia bucherae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
