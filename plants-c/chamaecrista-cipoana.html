Chamaecrista cipoana

Chamaecrista cipoana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, which aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.
