Cattleya reginae

Cattleya reginae, commonly known as the Imperial Cattleya, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya reginae requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
