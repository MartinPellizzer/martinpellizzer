Carex vulgaris var. juncella

Carex vulgaris var. juncella, commonly known as the slender sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist soils in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy, with a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are usually brown or greenish in color.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
