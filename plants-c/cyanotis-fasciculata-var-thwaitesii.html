Cyanotis fasciculata var. thwaitesii

Cyanotis fasciculata var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
