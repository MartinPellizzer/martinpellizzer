Cormus foliolosa

Cormus foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy corm, is a unique plant characterized by its corm-like structure, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients.

It typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features a tall, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a mild, sweet fragrance.

Cormus foliolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
