Carex pseudolanceolata

Carex pseudolanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually brown or reddish in color, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Carex pseudolanceolata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
