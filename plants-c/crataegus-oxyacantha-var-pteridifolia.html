Crataegus oxyacantha var. pteridifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha var. pteridifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble those of ferns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
