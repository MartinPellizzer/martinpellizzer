Crambe strigosa var. arborea

Crambe strigosa var. arborea, commonly known as the tree crambe, is a unique variant of the crambe genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
