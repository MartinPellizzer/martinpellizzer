Carex trachycarpos

Carex trachycarpos, commonly known as rough-skinned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream sides.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
