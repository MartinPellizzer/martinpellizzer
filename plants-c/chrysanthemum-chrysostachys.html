Chrysanthemum chrysostachys

Chrysanthemum chrysostachys, commonly known as the yellow chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features upright, branching stems that bear numerous small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
