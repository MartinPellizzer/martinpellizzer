Cryptocarya septentrionalis

Cryptocarya septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya septentrionalis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
