Clematis umbellifera

Clematis umbellifera, commonly known as the umbelliferous clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a vigorous climber with compound leaves and showy, umbel-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark red or black color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis umbellifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in garden settings to add visual interest.
