Cardamine hirsuta subsp. puberula

Cardamine hirsuta subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shady habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.
