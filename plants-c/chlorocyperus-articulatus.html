Chlorocyperus articulatus

Chlorocyperus articulatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chlorocyperus articulatus is often found in wetland ecosystems and can serve as an indicator species for water quality and habitat conditions.
