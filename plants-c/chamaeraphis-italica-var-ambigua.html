Chamaeraphis italica var. ambigua

Chamaeraphis italica var. ambigua, commonly known as the Italian sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens.
