Catasetum × apertum

Catasetum × apertum, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a specialized lip, which plays a role in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence can produce multiple blooms, with each flower having a distinct, elongated column that aids in the transfer of pollen.

Catasetum × apertum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
