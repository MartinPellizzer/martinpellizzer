Croton doratophylloides

Croton doratophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
