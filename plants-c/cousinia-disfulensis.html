Cousinia disfulensis

Cousinia disfulensis, commonly known as the Kashmir copperflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking copper-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, daisy-like inflorescences.

Cousinia disfulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.
