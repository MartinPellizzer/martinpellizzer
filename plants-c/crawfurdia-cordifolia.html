Crawfurdia cordifolia

Crawfurdia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved crawfurdia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The leaves are heart-shaped, hence its common name, and are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
