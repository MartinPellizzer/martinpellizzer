Camelina incana

Camelina incana, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Camelina incana is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

It is also valued for its potential as a source of oilseed for biodiesel production.
