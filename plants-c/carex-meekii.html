Carex meekii

Carex meekii, commonly known as Meek's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region, it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
