Corchorus olitorius var. grandifolius

Corchorus olitorius var. grandifolius, commonly known as Ethiopian spinach, is a leafy green vegetable native to Ethiopia and cultivated in various parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are tender and edible, with a mild, slightly nutty flavor. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often grown as a leafy vegetable, with the leaves harvested before the plant flowers.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins A and C, iron, and calcium.
