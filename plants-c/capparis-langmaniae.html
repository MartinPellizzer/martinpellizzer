Capparis langmaniae

Capparis langmaniae, commonly known as the Philippine yellow caper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its yellowish flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while its fruits are small, capsule-like structures.

Capparis langmaniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
