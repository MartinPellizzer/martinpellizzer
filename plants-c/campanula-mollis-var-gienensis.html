Campanula mollis var. gienensis

Campanula mollis var. gienensis, commonly known as the Gienens Galloway bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
