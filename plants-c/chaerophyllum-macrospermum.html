Chaerophyllum macrospermum

Chaerophyllum macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded hogweed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands and streambanks. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces distinctive, large, dry fruits.

The species is known for its potential to spread aggressively, making it a concern in certain ecological contexts.
