Carex riparia var. chilensis

Carex riparia var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean ribbon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that are often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
