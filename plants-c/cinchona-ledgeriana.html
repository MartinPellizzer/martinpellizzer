Cinchona ledgeriana

Cinchona ledgeriana, commonly known as the Peruvian bark or wild quinine tree, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, featuring glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Its historical significance lies in its use as a source of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.
