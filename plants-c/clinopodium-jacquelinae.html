Clinopodium jacquelinae

Clinopodium jacquelinae, commonly known as Jacquelin's woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense whorls along the upper stems.

Clinopodium jacquelinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
