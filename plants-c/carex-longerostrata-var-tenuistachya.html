Carex longerostrata var. tenuistachya

Carex longerostrata var. tenuistachya, commonly known as the slender-stamined sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense cluster of flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a spike that emerges from the foliage.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
