Carex dielsiana

Carex dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex dielsiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
