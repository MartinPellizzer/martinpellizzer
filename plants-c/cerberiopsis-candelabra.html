Cerberiopsis candelabra

Cerberiopsis candelabra, commonly known as the candlestick hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that resembles a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a yellow or orange center. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cerberiopsis candelabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
