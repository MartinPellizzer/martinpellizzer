Cephalaria uralensis subsp. uralensis

Cephalaria uralensis subsp.

uralensis, commonly known as the Ural goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
