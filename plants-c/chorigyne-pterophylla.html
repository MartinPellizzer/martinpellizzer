Chorigyne pterophylla

Chorigyne pterophylla, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its unique, orchid-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, making it a subtle yet striking addition to botanical gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often protected in conservation programs.
