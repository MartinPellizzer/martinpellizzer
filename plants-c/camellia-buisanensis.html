Camellia buisanensis

Camellia buisanensis, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking red or pink flowers in the winter months. The plant is closely related to the tea plant, Camellia sinensis, and shares similar botanical features. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Camellia buisanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
