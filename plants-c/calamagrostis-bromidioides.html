Calamagrostis bromidioides

Calamagrostis bromidioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are loosely arranged and usually tinged with purple or blue.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
