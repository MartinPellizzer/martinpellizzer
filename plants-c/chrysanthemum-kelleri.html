Chrysanthemum kelleri

Chrysanthemum kelleri, commonly known as the Kellers chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
