Capparis chrysomeia

Capparis chrysomeia, commonly known as the yellow caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sour berries. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Capparis chrysomeia is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
