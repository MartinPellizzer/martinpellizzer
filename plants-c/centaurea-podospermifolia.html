Centaurea podospermifolia

Centaurea podospermifolia, commonly known as the Armenian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, star-shaped flowers arranged in a head.

Centaurea podospermifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
