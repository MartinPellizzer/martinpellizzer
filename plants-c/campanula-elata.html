Campanula elata

Campanula elata, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Campanula elata is well-suited for moist soils and partial to full sun, thriving in a variety of temperate climates.
