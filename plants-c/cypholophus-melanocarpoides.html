Cypholophus melanocarpoides

Cypholophus melanocarpoides, commonly known as the black-fruited mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces dark, fleshy berries that are toxic to most animals. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes to a limited extent. It is native to parts of southern Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in the study of parasitic plant interactions.
