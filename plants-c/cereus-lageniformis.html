Cereus lageniformis

Cereus lageniformis, commonly known as the bottle cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that resemble bottles, which store water to survive in arid environments. The plant has spiny areoles and produces showy, nocturnal flowers that open at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptation to harsh climates make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
