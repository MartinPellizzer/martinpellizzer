Carex lineolata

Carex lineolata, commonly known as the stripe grass or line-leaf sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex lineolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
