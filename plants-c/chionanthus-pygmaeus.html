Chionanthus pygmaeus

Chionanthus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf whiteflower plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Chionanthus pygmaeus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
