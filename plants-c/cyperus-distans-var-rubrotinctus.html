Cyperus distans var. rubrotinctus

Cyperus distans var. rubrotinctus, commonly known as the red-stained flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle.

The variety rubrotinctus is distinguished by its reddish tinge on the stems and leaves, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions.
