Crataegus monogyna var. pteridifolia

Crataegus monogyna var. pteridifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn or simply hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called hips in the autumn, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and open scrublands.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
