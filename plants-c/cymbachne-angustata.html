Cymbachne angustata

Cymbachne angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf cymbachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, lowland habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cymbachne angustata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
