Correa alba var. rotundifolia

Correa alba var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the white correa or round-leaf correa, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its attractive white or pale pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are oval and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species is well-suited to coastal gardens due to its drought tolerance and salt resistance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
