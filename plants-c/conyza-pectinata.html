Conyza pectinata

Conyza pectinata, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Conyza pectinata is known for its adaptability and can be found in a variety of climates across its range.
