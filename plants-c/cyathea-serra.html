Cyathea serra

Cyathea serra, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is a member of the order Cyatheales. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated edge, which is a key identifying feature.

Cyathea serra thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
