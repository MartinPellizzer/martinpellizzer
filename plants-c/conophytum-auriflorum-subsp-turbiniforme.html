Conophytum auriflorum subsp. turbiniforme

Conophytum auriflorum subsp.

turbiniforme, commonly known as the golden flower conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
