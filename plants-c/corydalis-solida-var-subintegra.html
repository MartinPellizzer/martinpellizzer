Corydalis solida var. subintegra

Corydalis solida var. subintegra, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
