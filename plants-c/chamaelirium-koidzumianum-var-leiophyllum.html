Chamaelirium koidzumianum var. leiophyllum

Chamaelirium koidzumianum var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
