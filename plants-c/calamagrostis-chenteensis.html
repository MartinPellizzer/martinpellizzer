Calamagrostis chenteensis

Calamagrostis chenteensis, commonly known as the Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Calamagrostis chenteensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its aesthetic appeal in wetland landscapes.
