Capparis spinosa var. kruegeriana

Capparis spinosa var. kruegeriana, commonly known as the Krueger's caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by green, round fruits, which mature into the edible capers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The variant is notable for its distinct morphology and is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
