Cyathea hunsteiniana

Cyathea hunsteiniana, commonly known as the Hunstein's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive trunk covered with remnants of old leaf bases, giving it a rugged appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea hunsteiniana plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
