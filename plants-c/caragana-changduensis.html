Caragana changduensis

Caragana changduensis, commonly known as the Changdu shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.

Caragana changduensis plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
