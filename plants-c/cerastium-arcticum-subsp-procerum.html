Cerastium arcticum subsp. procerum

Cerastium arcticum subsp.

procerum, commonly known as Arctic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It features small, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers.

The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
