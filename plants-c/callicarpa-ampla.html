Callicarpa ampla

Callicarpa ampla, commonly known as the red-berried beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces vibrant purple to red berries in the fall, which are edible for humans and birds. Callicarpa ampla thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
