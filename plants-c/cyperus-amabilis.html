Cyperus amabilis

Cyperus amabilis, commonly known as the sweet grass or marsh grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Cyperus amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
