Chamaecrista longistyla

Chamaecrista longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Chamaecrista longistyla is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
