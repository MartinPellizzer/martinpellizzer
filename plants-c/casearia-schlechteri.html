Casearia schlechteri

Casearia schlechteri, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by spherical, white to pale blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
