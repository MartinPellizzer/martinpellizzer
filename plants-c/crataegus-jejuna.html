Crataegus jejuna

Crataegus jejuna, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus jejuna is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is adapted to partial shade.
