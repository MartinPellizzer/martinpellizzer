Cryptocarya ovalifolia

Cryptocarya ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leafed cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Cryptocarya ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
