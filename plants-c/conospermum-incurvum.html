Conospermum incurvum

Conospermum incurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waratah, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, red-orange flower spikes. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.
