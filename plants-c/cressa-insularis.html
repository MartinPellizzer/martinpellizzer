Cressa insularis

Cressa insularis, commonly known as the island cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a peppery taste when crushed.

Cressa insularis is often found in rocky or sandy soils near coastal areas.
