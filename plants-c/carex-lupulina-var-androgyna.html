Carex lupulina var. androgyna

Carex lupulina var. androgyna, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in heathlands and bogs, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes, a feature known as dioecious reproduction.

This variation contributes to its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
