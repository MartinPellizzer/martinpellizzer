Colutea arborescens var. atrocalyx

Colutea arborescens var. atrocalyx, commonly known as the black-berried broom, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 7 to 13 pairs of ovate leaflets, which are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed pod containing several black, elliptical seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its dark purple calyx and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.
