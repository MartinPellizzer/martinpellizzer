Coelogyne whitmeei

Coelogyne whitmeei, commonly known as the White's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a prominent lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Coelogyne whitmeei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
