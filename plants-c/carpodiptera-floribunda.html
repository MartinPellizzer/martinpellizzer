Carpodiptera floribunda

Carpodiptera floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
