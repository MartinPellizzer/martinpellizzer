Carpinus tsaiana

Carpinus tsaiana, commonly known as the Caucasian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Carpinus tsaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
