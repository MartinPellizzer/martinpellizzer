Carpesium velutinum

Carpesium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.
