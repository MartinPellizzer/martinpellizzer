Calamus javensis var. firmus

Calamus javensis var. firmus, commonly known as the firm calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind or insects.
