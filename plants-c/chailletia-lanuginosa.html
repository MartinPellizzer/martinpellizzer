Chailletia lanuginosa

Chailletia lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly chailletia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
