Crataegus radina

Crataegus radina, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Crataegus radina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
