Cotoneaster frigidus var. aldenhamensis

Cotoneaster frigidus var. aldenhamensis, commonly known as the Aldenham cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in garden settings.
