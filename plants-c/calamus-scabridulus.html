Calamus scabridulus

Calamus scabridulus, commonly known as the rough horsetail, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a dense, compact form.

Calamus scabridulus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.
