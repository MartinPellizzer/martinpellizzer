Coburgia chachapoyensis

Coburgia chachapoyensis, commonly known as the Chachapoya orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
