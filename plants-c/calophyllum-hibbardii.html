Calophyllum hibbardii

Calophyllum hibbardii, commonly known as Hibbard's flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.
