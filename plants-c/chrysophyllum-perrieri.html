Chrysophyllum perrieri

Chrysophyllum perrieri, commonly known as the African cherry or Perrier's cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
