Campanula cochleariifolia subf. foliosa

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This sub-species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
