Colobanthus acicularis

Colobanthus acicularis, commonly known as the slender pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in cold, harsh environments. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures.

Colobanthus acicularis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying Arctic flora.
