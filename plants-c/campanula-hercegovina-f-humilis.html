Campanula hercegovina f. humilis

Campanula hercegovina f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Hercegovina bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It features slender stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
