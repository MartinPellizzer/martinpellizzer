Crassula expansa var. longifolia

Crassula expansa var. longifolia, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, giving it its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted for storing water. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it both drought-tolerant and visually appealing.
