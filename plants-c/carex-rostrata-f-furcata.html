Carex rostrata f. furcata

Carex rostrata f. furcata, commonly known as the forked beak sedge, is a variant of the beak sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges.

This subspecies is distinguished by its forked or branched inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet "furcata.".
