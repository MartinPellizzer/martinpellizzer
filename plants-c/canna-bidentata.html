Canna bidentata

Canna bidentata, commonly known as the broadleaf canna, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy leaves and striking inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated. Its flowers are usually orange or red, arranged in a spike with three to five bracts.

Canna bidentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
