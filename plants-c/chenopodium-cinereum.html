Chenopodium cinereum

Chenopodium cinereum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically tiny and wind-dispersed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Chenopodium cinereum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
