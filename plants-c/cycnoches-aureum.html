Cycnoches aureum

Cycnoches aureum, commonly known as the golden cycnoches, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns that resemble the wings of butterflies. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or green markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Cycnoches aureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
