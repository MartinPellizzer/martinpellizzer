Chaerophyllum hirsutum subsp. umbrosum

Chaerophyllum hirsutum subsp.

umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a robust root system and a tall, hollow stem covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a fuzzy texture, while the flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
