Cinnamomum septentrionale

Cinnamomum septentrionale, commonly known as the northern cinnamon or American cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
