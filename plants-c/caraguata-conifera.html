Caraguata conifera

Caraguata conifera, commonly known as the Andean pine, is a coniferous tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of conifers. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
