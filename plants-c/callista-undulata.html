Callista undulata

Callista undulata, commonly known as the undulating bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Callista undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
