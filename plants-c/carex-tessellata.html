Carex tessellata

Carex tessellata, commonly known as the checkered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Carex tessellata thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Its distinctive leaf patterns and ecological adaptability make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
