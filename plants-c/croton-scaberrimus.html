Croton scaberrimus

Croton scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Croton scaberrimus thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
