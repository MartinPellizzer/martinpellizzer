Calophysa pilosa

Calophysa pilosa, commonly known as the woolly calophysa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pilose, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Calophysa pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
