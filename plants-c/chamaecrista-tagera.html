Chamaecrista tagera

Chamaecrista tagera, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
