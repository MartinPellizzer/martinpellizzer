Chamaemelum mixtum var. glabrescens

Chamaemelum mixtum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous golden marguerite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically hairy but become glabrous as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
