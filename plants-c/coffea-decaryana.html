Coffea decaryana

Coffea decaryana, commonly known as the Abyssinian coffee.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family, native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. It is typically grown in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
