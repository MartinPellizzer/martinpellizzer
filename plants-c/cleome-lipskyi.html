Cleome lipskyi

Cleome lipskyi, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spidery blooms that resemble spiders or small flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cleome lipskyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
