Cerasus lusitanica var. crassifolia

Cerasus lusitanica var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved cherry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart cherries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local use.
