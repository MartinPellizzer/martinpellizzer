Carex jisaburo-ohwiana

Carex jisaburo-ohwiana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.
