Crepis chrysantha

Crepis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a dense, spiky central disk, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces small, oblong achenes with pappus hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
