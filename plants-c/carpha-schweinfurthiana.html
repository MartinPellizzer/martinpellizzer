Carpha schweinfurthiana

Carpha schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schweinfurth reed, is a perennial grass native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
