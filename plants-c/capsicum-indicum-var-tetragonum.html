Capsicum indicum var. tetragonum

Capsicum indicum var. tetragonum, commonly known as the square bell pepper, is a variety of chili pepper characterized by its unique square or rectangular fruit shape.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lanceolate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. Its fruits are usually small, measuring around 2–4 cm in length, and are known for their mild to medium heat level.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and culinary use in various cuisines.
