Carex × barrei

Carex × barrei, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Carex × barrei is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
