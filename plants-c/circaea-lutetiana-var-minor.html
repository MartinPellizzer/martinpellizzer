Circaea lutetiana var. minor

Circaea lutetiana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser white bead, is a low-growing perennial herb native to moist, shaded environments in Europe.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in woodland soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
