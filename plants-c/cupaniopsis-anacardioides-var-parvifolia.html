Cupaniopsis anacardioides var. parvifolia

Cupaniopsis anacardioides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved satinash, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, often attracting birds and other wildlife.
