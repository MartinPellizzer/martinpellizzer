Campanula mollis var. tlemcenensis

Campanula mollis var. tlemcenensis, commonly known as the Algerian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Algeria.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
