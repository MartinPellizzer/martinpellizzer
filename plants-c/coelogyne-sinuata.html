Coelogyne sinuata

Coelogyne sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and sword-shaped, while its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne sinuata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral structure.
