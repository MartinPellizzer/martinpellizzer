Colax

Colax, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of several species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants are characterized by their large, showy, umbrella-shaped inflorescences known as spadices. They typically have waxy, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped.

Colax species are often grown as ornamental plants due to their striking foliage and unique floral structures.
