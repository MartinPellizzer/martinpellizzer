Carex kiangsuensis

Carex kiangsuensis, commonly known as the Kiangsu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its foliage is dense and often forms compact clumps, and it produces small, clustered flower heads during the summer months.

Carex kiangsuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
