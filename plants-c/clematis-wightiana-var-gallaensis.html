Clematis wightiana var. gallaensis

Clematis wightiana var. gallaensis, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
