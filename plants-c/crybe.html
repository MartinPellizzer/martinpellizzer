Crybe

Crybe, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that encase a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used for their aromatic properties and are sometimes employed in herbal remedies.

The Crybe tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
