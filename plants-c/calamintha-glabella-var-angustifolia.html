Calamintha glabella var. angustifolia

Calamintha glabella var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue or violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the upper stem.

It is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a ornamental plant.
