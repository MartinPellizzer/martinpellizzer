Calamagrostis × trinii

Calamagrostis × trinii, commonly known as the pink moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, pinkish-purple plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.

Calamagrostis × trinii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
