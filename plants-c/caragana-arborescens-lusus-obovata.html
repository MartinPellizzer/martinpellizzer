Caragana arborescens lusus obovata

Caragana arborescens lusus obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed shrub willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that often grow in a sprawling or ascending manner. The leaves are obovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for reclamation projects.
