Cynoglossum decurrens

Cynoglossum decurrens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with blue or purple. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Cynoglossum decurrens is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
