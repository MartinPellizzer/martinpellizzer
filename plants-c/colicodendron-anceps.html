Colicodendron anceps

Colicodendron anceps, commonly known as the yellow-veined elder, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with yellow veins and small, white to yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy.
