Claytonia lanceolata var. peirsonii

Claytonia lanceolata var. peirsonii, commonly known as the Peirson's spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to woodland gardens.
