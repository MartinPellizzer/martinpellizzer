Coccothrinax clarensis subsp. brevifolia

Coccothrinax clarensis subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved palm, is a compact, shrub-like palm native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It features a slender, unbranched stem with a smooth, grayish-brown trunk and small, clustered leaves that are short and stiff.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, orange fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
