Cimicifuga arizonica

Cimicifuga arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive, somewhat fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine, though its primary ecological role is as a nectar source for pollinators.
