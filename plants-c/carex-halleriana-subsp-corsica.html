Carex halleriana subsp. corsica

Carex halleriana subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically found in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
