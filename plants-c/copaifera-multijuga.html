Copaifera multijuga

Copaifera multijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces yellowish to reddish-brown resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes. The plant belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes.

Copaifera multijuga is valued for its aromatic resin, which is harvested for its potential therapeutic properties.
