Calytrix asperula var. gracilis

Calytrix asperula var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the warmer months, which are often visited by nectar-feeding insects. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
