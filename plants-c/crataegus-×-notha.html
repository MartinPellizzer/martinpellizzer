Crataegus × notha

Crataegus × notha, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit, a red or yellow berry, is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The leaves are oval with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
