Croton chloroleucus

Croton chloroleucus, commonly known as the white croton, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a waxy, white or pale underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton chloroleucus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
