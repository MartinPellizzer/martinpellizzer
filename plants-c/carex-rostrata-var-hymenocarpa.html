Carex rostrata var. hymenocarpa

Carex rostrata var. hymenocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female parts present on the same spike, a feature known as gynodioecy.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
