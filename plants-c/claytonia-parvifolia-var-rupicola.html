Claytonia parvifolia var. rupicola

Claytonia parvifolia var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock Claytonia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
