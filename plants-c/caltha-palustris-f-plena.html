Caltha palustris f. plena

Caltha palustris f. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered marsh marigold, is a botanical variant of the marsh marigold native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other wet environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens with suitable water conditions.
