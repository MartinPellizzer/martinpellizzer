Cordia cylindristachya

Cordia cylindristachya, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The tree has smooth, pale bark and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Cordia cylindristachya is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
