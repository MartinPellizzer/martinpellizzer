Casearia esculenta

Casearia esculenta, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine.

The fruit is also a rich source of vitamin C and is commonly used in culinary applications.
