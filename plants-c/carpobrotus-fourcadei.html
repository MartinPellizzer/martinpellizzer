Carpobrotus fourcadei

Carpobrotus fourcadei, commonly known as the yellow ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense mat on the ground. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically from spring to summer. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, demonstrating high tolerance to salt and drought.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
