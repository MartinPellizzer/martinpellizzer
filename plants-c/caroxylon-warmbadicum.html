Caroxylon warmbadicum

Caroxylon warmbadicum, commonly known as the Warmbadicum date palm, is a rare and ancient species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible dates, which are highly valued for their sweetness and nutritional content. Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Botanists study it to better understand its genetic diversity and potential for conservation efforts.
