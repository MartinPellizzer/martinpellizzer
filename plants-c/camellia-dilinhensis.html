Camellia dilinhensis

Camellia dilinhensis, commonly known as the Dali camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with a subtropical climate, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia dilinhensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
