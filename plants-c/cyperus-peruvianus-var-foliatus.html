Cyperus peruvianus var. foliatus

Cyperus peruvianus var. foliatus, commonly known as the Peruvian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
