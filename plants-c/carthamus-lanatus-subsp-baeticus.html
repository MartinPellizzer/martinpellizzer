Carthamus lanatus subsp. baeticus

Carthamus lanatus subsp.

baeticus, commonly known as the Iberian safflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
