Coleus suganda

Coleus suganda, commonly known as Java coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it popular as an ornamental plant. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in spikes.

Coleus suganda thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking appearance and ease of care.
