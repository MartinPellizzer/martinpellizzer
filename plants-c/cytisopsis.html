Cytisopsis

Cytisopsis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They produce yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often resembling those of the dandelion, hence their common name. Cytisopsis species are characterized by their narrow, often hairy leaves and small, pod-like fruits.

The genus includes several species, such as Cytisopsis saxicola, which is native to southern Africa.
