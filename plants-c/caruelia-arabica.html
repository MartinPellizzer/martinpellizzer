Caruelia arabica

Caruelia arabica, commonly known as coffee.

It is a flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant produces red berries containing two seeds, which are the primary source of coffee beans. These beans are roasted and ground to make coffee beverages.
