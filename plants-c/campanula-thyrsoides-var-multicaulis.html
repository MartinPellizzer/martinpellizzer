Campanula thyrsoides var. multicaulis

Campanula thyrsoides var. multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves and produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, adding color to the landscape during the warmer months.
