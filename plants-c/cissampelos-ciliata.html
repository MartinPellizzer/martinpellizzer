Cissampelos ciliata

Cissampelos ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
