Cheilanthes lentigera

Cheilanthes lentigera, commonly known as the spotted rock fern, is a perennial fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically less than 10 inches in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with dark, velvety, spore-bearing leaves that are often covered in small, dark spots. It thrives in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

The spores are released from the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in harsh environments.
