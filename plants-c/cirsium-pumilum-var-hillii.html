Cirsium pumilum var. hillii

Cirsium pumilum var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. The plant features a robust root system and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. It produces small, bracteate flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
