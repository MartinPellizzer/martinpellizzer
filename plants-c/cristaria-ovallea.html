Cristaria ovallea

Cristaria ovallea, commonly known as the ovalleaf cristaria, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette formation.

It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds with soft, nutrient-rich substrates. The plant's leaves are typically a deep green, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Cristaria ovallea plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
