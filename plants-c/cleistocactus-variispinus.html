Cleistocactus variispinus

Cleistocactus variispinus, commonly known as the variable spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of spines that vary in color and shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, a trait typical of many nocturnal flowering plants. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to harsh desert conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
