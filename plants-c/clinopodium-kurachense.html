Clinopodium kurachense

Clinopodium kurachense, commonly known as Kurachenskii mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Clinopodium kurachense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
