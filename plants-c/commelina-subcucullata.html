Commelina subcucullata

Commelina subcucullata, commonly known as the white spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and forest undergrowth.
