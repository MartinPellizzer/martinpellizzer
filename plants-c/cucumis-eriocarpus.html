Cucumis eriocarpus

Cucumis eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant produces small, hairy, and typically yellow or green fruits that are edible. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
