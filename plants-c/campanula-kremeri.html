Campanula kremeri

Campanula kremeri, commonly known as the Kremer's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula kremeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
