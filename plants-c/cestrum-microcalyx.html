Cestrum microcalyx

Cestrum microcalyx, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Cestrum microcalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its nocturnal fragrance.
