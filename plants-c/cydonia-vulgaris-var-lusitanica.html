Cydonia vulgaris var. lusitanica

Cydonia vulgaris var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese quince, is a cultivar native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit, which turns from green to yellow-orange as it matures. The fruit is typically harvested in late autumn and is used in cooking and traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
