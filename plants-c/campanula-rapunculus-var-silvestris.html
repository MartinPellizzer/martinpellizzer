Campanula rapunculus var. silvestris

Campanula rapunculus var. silvestris, commonly known as the wild bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Campanula rapunculus var. silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
