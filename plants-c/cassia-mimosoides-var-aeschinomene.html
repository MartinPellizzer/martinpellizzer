Cassia mimosoides var. aeschinomene

Cassia mimosoides var. aeschinomene, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that resemble those of the mimosoid legumes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in Australian flora.
