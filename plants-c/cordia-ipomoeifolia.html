Cordia ipomoeifolia

Cordia ipomoeifolia, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
