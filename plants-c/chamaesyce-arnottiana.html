Chamaesyce arnottiana

Chamaesyce arnottiana, commonly known as the Arnott's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed, with a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the Asteraceae family.
