Cousinia oxiana

Cousinia oxiana, commonly known as the Afghan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Cousinia oxiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
