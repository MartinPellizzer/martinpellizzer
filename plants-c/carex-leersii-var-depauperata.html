Carex leersii var. depauperata

Carex leersii var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
