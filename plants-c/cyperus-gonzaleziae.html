Cyperus gonzaleziae

Cyperus gonzaleziae, commonly known as the Gonzalez's flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
