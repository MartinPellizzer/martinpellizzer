Cyrtorchis crenata

Cyrtorchis crenata, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its habitat specificity, Cyrtorchis crenata is considered a rare and endangered species.
