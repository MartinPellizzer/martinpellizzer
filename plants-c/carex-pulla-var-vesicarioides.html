Carex pulla var. vesicarioides

Carex pulla var. vesicarioides, commonly known as the purple sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spikes of small, often reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the inflorescence consists of a single, compact spike.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
