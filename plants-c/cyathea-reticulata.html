Cyathea reticulata

Cyathea reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive network of veins on its fronds, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea reticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
