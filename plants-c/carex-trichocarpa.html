Carex trichocarpa

Carex trichocarpa, commonly known as slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, triangular leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
