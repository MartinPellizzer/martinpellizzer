Callicarpa acuminata

Callicarpa acuminata, commonly known as the pointed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are a food source for birds and are also used in traditional medicine.
