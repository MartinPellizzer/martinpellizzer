Crataegus laurifolia

Crataegus laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including clay and loam. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance, and they turn yellow or red in the autumn.

Crataegus laurifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
