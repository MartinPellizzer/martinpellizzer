Cardamomum hemisphaericum

Cardamomum hemisphaericum, commonly known as the mountain cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow flowers that develop into dark brown, three-lobed capsules containing aromatic seeds.

The seeds are prized for their strong, spicy flavor and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
