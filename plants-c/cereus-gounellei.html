Cereus gounellei

Cereus gounellei, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. Its flowers are known for their striking appearance and emit a mild fragrance.

Cereus gounellei is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
