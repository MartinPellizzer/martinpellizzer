Clematis acutangula subsp. ranunculoides

Clematis acutangula subsp.

ranunculoides, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to other clematis species.
