Clitoria erecta

Clitoria erecta, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, with five petals arranged in a distinctive pattern. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its flowers are rich in anthocyanins, giving them their vibrant color and making them popular in herbal teas and natural dyes.

Clitoria erecta is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
