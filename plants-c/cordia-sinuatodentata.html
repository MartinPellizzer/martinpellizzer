Cordia sinuatodentata

Cordia sinuatodentata, commonly known as the narrow-toothed cordia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cordia sinuatodentata is often found in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
