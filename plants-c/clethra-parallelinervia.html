Clethra parallelinervia

Clethra parallelinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent parallel veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clustered racemes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Clethra parallelinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
