Cicer nuristanicum

Cicer nuristanicum, commonly known as the Nuristan chickpea, is a leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its seeds are rich in protein and have been traditionally used as a food source in local communities.

Due to its resilience and nutritional value, it holds potential for agricultural development in marginal lands.
