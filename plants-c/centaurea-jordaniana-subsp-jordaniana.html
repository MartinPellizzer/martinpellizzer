Centaurea jordaniana subsp. jordaniana

Centaurea jordaniana subsp.

jordaniana, commonly known as Jordanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bright yellow to reddish purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
