Calamagrostis hakonensis var. glauca

Calamagrostis hakonensis var. glauca, commonly known as blue bluestem, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of Europe and western Siberia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces feathery panicles of pale blue to silver flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
