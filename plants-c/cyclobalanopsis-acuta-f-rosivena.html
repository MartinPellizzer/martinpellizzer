Cyclobalanopsis acuta f. rosivena

Cyclobalanopsis acuta f. rosivena, commonly known as the rose-veined ironwood, is a rare and distinctive variant of the cyclobalanopsis genus.

It is characterized by its unique rose-colored veins that run through the leaves, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland and hill forests.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
