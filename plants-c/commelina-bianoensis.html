Commelina bianoensis

Commelina bianoensis, commonly known as the white commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking white petals and bright blue-violet sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The flowers are small but attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
