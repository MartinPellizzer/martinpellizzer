Clerodendrum swynnertonii

Clerodendrum swynnertonii, commonly known as the Swynnerton's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value is due to its showy blooms and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
