Calendula anemoniflora

Calendula anemoniflora, commonly known as the Alpine calendula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant features showy, yellow to orange petals that are typically arranged in a single row, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often hairy and lobed, adapted to thrive in cooler, alpine environments.

Calendula anemoniflora is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
