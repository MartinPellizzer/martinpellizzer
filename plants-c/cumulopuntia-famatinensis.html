Cumulopuntia famatinensis

Cumulopuntia famatinensis, commonly known as the Famatinian clustered cactus, is a species native to northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often form dense clusters. The plant features prominent areoles with spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its adaptations include thick, waxy stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Cumulopuntia famatinensis is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
