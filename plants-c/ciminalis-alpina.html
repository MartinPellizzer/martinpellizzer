Ciminalis alpina

Ciminalis alpina, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive at high altitudes.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
