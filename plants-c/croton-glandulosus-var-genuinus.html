Croton glandulosus var. genuinus

Croton glandulosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
