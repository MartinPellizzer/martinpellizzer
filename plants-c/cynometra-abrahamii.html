Cynometra abrahamii

Cynometra abrahamii, commonly known as African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
