Carex rhizina subsp. rhizina

Carex rhizina subsp.

rhizina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces spikelets that are often arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
