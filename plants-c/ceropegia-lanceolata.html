Ceropegia lanceolata

Ceropegia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often hairy, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
