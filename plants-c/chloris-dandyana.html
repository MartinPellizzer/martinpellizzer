Chloris dandyana

Chloris dandyana, commonly known as Dandy's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by slender, spike-like seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
