Cupressus Lusitanica Var. Tristis

Cupressus lusitanica var. tristis is a rare and ancient conifer native to the Iberian Peninsula. This variant of the Portuguese cypress is known for its distinctive, gnarled appearance and slow growth rate. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in local ecosystems. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces increasing threats to its survival.
