Chrysanthemum brachyanthum

Chrysanthemum brachyanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
