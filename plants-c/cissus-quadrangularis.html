Cissus quadrangularis

Cissus quadrangularis, commonly known as the devil's ivy or Indian rubber plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its four-sided, waxy stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a popular houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
