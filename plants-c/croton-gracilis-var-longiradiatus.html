Croton gracilis var. longiradiatus

Croton gracilis var. longiradiatus, commonly known as the slender croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, branching structure and strikingly colorful foliage.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a vibrant array of colors ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
