Convolvulus argyi

Convolvulus argyi, commonly known as the Chinese bindweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its vines can spread aggressively, making it both a weed and a decorative plant in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other species of flowering plants with similar characteristics.
