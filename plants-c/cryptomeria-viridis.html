Cryptomeria viridis

Cryptomeria viridis, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically green on top and white or blue underneath, providing a distinctive visual appearance. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and reforestation purposes.
