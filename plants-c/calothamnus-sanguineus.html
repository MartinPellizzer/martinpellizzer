Calothamnus sanguineus

Calothamnus sanguineus, commonly known as the red-flowered devil's bit, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Calothamnus sanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
