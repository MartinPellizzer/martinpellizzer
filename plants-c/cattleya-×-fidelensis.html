Cattleya × fidelensis

Cattleya × fidelensis, commonly known as the Fidelensis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shady environments. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and it requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

Cattleya × fidelensis is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
