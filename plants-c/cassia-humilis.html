Cassia humilis

Cassia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf senna, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the use of its seeds in traditional medicine. Cassia humilis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
