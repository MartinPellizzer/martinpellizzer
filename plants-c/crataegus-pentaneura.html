Crataegus pentaneura

Crataegus pentaneura, commonly known as the five-veined hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The leaves are typically ovate with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.
