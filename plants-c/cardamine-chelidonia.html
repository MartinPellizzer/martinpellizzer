Cardamine chelidonia

Cardamine chelidonia, commonly known as greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
