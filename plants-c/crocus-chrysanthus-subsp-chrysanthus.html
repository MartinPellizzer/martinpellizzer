Crocus chrysanthus subsp. chrysanthus

Crocus chrysanthus subsp.

chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vibrant color.
