Cardamine acris subsp. vardousiae

Cardamine acris subsp.

vardousiae, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
