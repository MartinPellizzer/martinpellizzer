Calliandra houstoniana var. anomala

Calliandra houstoniana var. anomala, commonly known as the Houstonia calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
