Cynanchum orientense

Cynanchum orientense, commonly known as the oriental dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spindle-shaped fruits containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
