Clusia quapoya

Clusia quapoya, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

Clusia quapoya is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
