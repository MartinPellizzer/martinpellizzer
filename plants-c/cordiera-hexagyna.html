Cordiera hexagyna

Cordiera hexagyna, commonly known as the six-angled cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical name reflects the six-sided structure of its leaf bases.

Cordiera hexagyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
