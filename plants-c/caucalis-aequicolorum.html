Caucalis aequicolorum

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, grassy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
