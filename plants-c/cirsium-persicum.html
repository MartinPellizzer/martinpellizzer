Cirsium persicum

Cirsium persicum, commonly known as Persian thistle, is a perennial plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and can be invasive in some areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though care must be taken due to its prickly nature.
