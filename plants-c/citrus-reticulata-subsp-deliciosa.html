Citrus reticulata subsp. deliciosa

Citrus reticulata subsp.

deliciosa, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a small citrus tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits with a thin, easy-to-peel rind.

The fruit contains segmented, juicy pulp that is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This subspecies is closely related to other citrus species and is widely used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
