Capparis klossii

Capparis klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Capparis klossii is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
