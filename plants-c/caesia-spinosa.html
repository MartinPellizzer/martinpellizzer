Caesia spinosa

Caesia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny caesia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Caesia spinosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
