Carex japonica var. gracilis

Carex japonica var. gracilis, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or shaded forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add texture and interest to water features or shaded landscapes.
