Cerasus longipes

Cerasus longipes, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or longipes cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet red berries. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface.

Cerasus longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in traditional Chinese horticulture.
