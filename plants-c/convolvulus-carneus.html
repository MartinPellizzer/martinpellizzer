Convolvulus carneus

Convolvulus carneus, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus carneus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
