Chamaemelum leucanthemum

Chamaemelum leucanthemum, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
