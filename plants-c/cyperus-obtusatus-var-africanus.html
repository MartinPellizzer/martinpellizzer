Cyperus obtusatus var. africanus

Cyperus obtusatus var. africanus, commonly known as African umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and dense, spiky inflorescences that resemble an umbrella. It typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in humid environments.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
