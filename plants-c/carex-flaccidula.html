Carex flaccidula

Carex flaccidula, commonly known as the soft sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Carex flaccidula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
