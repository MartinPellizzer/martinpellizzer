Carex salina var. subspathacea

Carex salina var. subspathacea, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand high salt concentrations. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
