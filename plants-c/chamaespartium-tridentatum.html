Chamaespartium tridentatum

Chamaespartium tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has slender stems and trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
