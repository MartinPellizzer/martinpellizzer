Calamus powlingii

Calamus powlingii, commonly known as the Chinese calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Calamus powlingii is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
