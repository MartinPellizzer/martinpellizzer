Cornus stolonifera var. occidentalis

Cornus stolonifera var. occidentalis, commonly known as western ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that root at the nodes, forming dense, sprawling colonies. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with toothed margins, and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
