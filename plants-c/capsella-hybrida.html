Capsella hybrida

Capsella hybrida, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, often along roadsides and in fields. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Capsella hybrida is an annual herb that is native to Europe but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
