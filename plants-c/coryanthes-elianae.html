Coryanthes elianae

Coryanthes elianae, commonly known as the Elia's coryanthe, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, with vibrant red and yellow hues. The plant has a unique structure, featuring a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
