Campanula versicolor subvar. lancifolia

Campanula versicolor subvar. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lanceolate, hence the subspecies name, and are typically hairy and slightly toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
