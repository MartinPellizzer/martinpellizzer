Citharexylum polystachyum

Citharexylum polystachyum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the winter season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
