Celtis subdentata

Celtis subdentata, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by yellowish drupes that mature in late summer.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
