Campylocentrum robustum

Campylocentrum robustum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
