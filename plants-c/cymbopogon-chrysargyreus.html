Cymbopogon chrysargyreus

Cymbopogon chrysargyreus, commonly known as golden grass or sweet grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, feathery leaves that emit a pleasant, sweet aroma. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural fragrance in rituals and crafts.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
