Clinopodium vulgare subsp. vulgare

Clinopodium vulgare subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, mint-like fragrance.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
