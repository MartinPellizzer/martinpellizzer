Clitoria ternatea var. albiflora

Clitoria ternatea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bean, is a variant of the common butterfly pea.

It is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, contrasting with its green foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye source.
