Cymbalaria muelleri subsp. muelleri

Cymbalaria muelleri subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the narrowleaf figwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in loose spikes, often in shades of purple or pink.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in damp conditions and its role in supporting local insect populations.
