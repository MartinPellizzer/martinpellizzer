Combretum herbaceum

Combretum herbaceum, commonly known as the velvetwood or African broom.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
