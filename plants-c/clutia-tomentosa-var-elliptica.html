Clutia tomentosa var. elliptica

Clutia tomentosa var. elliptica, commonly known as the woolly clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a waxy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Clutia tomentosa var. elliptica is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
