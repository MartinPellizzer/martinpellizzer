Calamaria histrix

Calamaria histrix, commonly known as the spurred caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins and a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Calamaria histrix is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and is popular in tropical gardens.
