Canna indica var. coccinea

Canna indica var. coccinea, commonly known as the red flame cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, and its showy, fiery red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It is a member of the Cannaceae family and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
