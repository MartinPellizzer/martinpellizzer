Carex leporina var. virescens

Carex leporina var. virescens, commonly known as the green-tongued sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and lighter in color underneath. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils and its ecological importance as a component of wetland ecosystems.
