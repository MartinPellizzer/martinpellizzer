Clerodendrum myricoides subsp. ussukumae

Clerodendrum myricoides subsp.

ussukumae, commonly known as the Nigerian flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
