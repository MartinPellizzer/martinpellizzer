Cynoglossum leptostachyum

Cynoglossum leptostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
