Canarium manongarivum

Canarium manongarivum, commonly known as the Manongarivum nut or Manongarivum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single large seed. Its seeds are encased in a hard shell and are used for various purposes, including as a source of oil and in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical forests.
