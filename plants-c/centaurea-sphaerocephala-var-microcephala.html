Centaurea sphaerocephala var. microcephala

Centaurea sphaerocephala var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical flower heads that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bracteate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
