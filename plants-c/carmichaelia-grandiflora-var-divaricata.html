Carmichaelia grandiflora var. divaricata

Carmichaelia grandiflora var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a distinctive species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
