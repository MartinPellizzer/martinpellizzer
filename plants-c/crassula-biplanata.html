Crassula biplanata

Crassula biplanata, commonly known as the two-leaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and pairs of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula biplanata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
