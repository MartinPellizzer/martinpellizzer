Carex araucana

Carex araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Carex araucana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
