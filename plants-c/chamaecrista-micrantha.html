Chamaecrista micrantha

Chamaecrista micrantha, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in small, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
