Cactus tectus

Cactus tectus, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. Its roots are shallow and widespread, enabling it to quickly absorb rainfall.

Despite its tough appearance, the cactus plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife, providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
