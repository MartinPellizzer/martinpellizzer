Chenopodium ambrosioides var. anthelminticum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. anthelminticum, commonly known as wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant contains compounds such as absinthin and thujone, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is often used in traditional medicine for its anthelmintic effects, helping to expel parasitic worms.

However, it is toxic in high doses and requires careful handling.
