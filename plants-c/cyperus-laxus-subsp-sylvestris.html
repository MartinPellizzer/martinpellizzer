Cyperus laxus subsp. sylvestris

Cyperus laxus subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wetlands. Its flowering structure consists of small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Cyperus laxus subsp.

sylvestris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
