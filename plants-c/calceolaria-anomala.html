Calceolaria anomala

Calceolaria anomala, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small ghosts, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers cool temperatures. Its foliage consists of elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive blooms.
