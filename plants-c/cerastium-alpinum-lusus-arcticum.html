Cerastium alpinum lusus arcticum

Cerastium alpinum lusus arcticum, commonly known as the arctic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme cold and strong winds. Its growth habit is cushion-like, which helps it conserve heat and moisture.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and provides food for various herbivorous insects.
