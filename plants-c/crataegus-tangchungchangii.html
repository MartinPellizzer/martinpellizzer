Crataegus tangchungchangii

Crataegus tangchungchangii, commonly known as the Tangchungchang hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
