Capparis artensis var. angusta

Capparis artensis var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and narrow, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is adapted to grow in arid, rocky environments.
