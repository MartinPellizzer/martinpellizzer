Cinnamomum xanthophyllum

Cinnamomum xanthophyllum, commonly known as the yellow cinnamon or yellow camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries containing winged seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also cultivated for its timber and ornamental qualities.
