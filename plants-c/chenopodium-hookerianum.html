Chenopodium hookerianum

Chenopodium hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by local communities for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
