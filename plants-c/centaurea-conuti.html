Centaurea conuti

Centaurea conuti, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with prominent central disks, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Centaurea conuti is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
