Cousinia pentacantha

Cousinia pentacantha, commonly known as the five-armed goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Cousinia pentacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
