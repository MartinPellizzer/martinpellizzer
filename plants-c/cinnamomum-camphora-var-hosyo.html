Cinnamomum camphora var. hosyo

Cinnamomum camphora var. hosyo, commonly known as the Hosyo camphor tree, is a variant of the camphor laurel native to Japan.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain compounds like camphor and cineole. The plant is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinct waxy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The Hosyo variety is particularly noted for its compact growth habit and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
