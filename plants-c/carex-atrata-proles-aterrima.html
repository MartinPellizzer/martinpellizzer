Carex atrata proles aterrima

Carex atrata proles aterrima, commonly known as the blackstem sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, clustered flowers.

Carex atrata proles aterrima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
