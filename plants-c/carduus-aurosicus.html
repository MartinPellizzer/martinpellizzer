Carduus aurosicus

Carduus aurosicus, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, broadly lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The golden thistle is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
