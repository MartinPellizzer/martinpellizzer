Carex ramosa var. crinigera

Carex ramosa var. crinigera, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of its flowering stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
