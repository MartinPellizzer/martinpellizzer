Cleistocactus brookeae subsp. vulpis-cauda

Cleistocactus brookeae subsp.

vulpis-cauda, commonly known as the fox-tail cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that resemble a fox's tail, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists.
