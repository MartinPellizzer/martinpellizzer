Cardamomum daniellii

Cardamomum daniellii, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is sometimes used as a substitute for true cardamom in certain cuisines.
