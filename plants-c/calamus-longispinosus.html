Calamus longispinosus

Calamus longispinosus, commonly known as the long-spined reed, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family, and has long, narrow leaves with prominent veins. Calamus longispinosus is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique spiny structures on the stems give it its common name and contribute to its ecological role in its habitat.
