Commiphora hartmannii

Commiphora hartmannii, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to the savannas of eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 meters and features elliptical, leathery leaves with a rough texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
