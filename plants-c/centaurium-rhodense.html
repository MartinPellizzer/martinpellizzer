Centaurium rhodense

Centaurium rhodense, commonly known as the pink centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact, upright growth habit.

Centaurium rhodense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
