Camellia rosiflora

Camellia rosiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that resemble roses, with pink or red petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Camellia rosiflora is also valued for its ability to bloom in late winter or early spring, adding color to gardens during the colder months.
