Clethra talamancana

Clethra talamancana, commonly known as the Talamancan sweet pepper, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Clethra talamancana is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
