Cylindropuntia fosbergii

Cylindropuntia fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems, which are segmented and covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by bright red fruits. Fosberg's cholla is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for desert habitats.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
