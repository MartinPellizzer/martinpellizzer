Ceratostema pubescens

Ceratostema pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a soft pubescence, contributing to its overall textured look.

Ceratostema pubescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
