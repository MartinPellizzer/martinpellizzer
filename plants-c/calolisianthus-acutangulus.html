Calolisianthus acutangulus

Calolisianthus acutangulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Calolisianthus acutangulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
