Crataegus agrestina

Crataegus agrestina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a strong, woody structure that supports its growth in various soil types.

Crataegus agrestina is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed habitats.
