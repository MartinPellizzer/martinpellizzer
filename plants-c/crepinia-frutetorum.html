Crepinia frutetorum

Crepinia frutetorum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Crepinia frutetorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
