Cleome digitata

Cleome digitata, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences that consist of small, white or pink flowers clustered on long, slender stems. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome digitata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique and colorful blooms.
