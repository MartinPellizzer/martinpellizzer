Cyperus macrosciadion

Cyperus macrosciadion, commonly known as the broad-leaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, branching stems.

Cyperus macrosciadion is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
