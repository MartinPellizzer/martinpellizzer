Comolia leptophylla

Comolia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf comolia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in width. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
