Cirsium iowense

Cirsium iowense, commonly known as Iowa thistle, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, disturbed soils, and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, prickly surfaces, while its flowers are clustered in a composite head.

The plant plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
