Clematis virginiana f. paucidentata

Clematis virginiana f. paucidentata, commonly known as the few-toothed virgin's bower, is a compact, deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with small, trifoliate leaves that have fewer leaflets compared to the typical species. The flowers are showy, blue to purple, and appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
