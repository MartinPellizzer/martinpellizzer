Cola verticillata

Cola verticillata, commonly known as African cola or wild cola, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their stimulant properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
