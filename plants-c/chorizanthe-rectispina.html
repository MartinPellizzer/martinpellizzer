Chorizanthe rectispina

Chorizanthe rectispina, commonly known as the spikeless choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Chorizanthe rectispina is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.
