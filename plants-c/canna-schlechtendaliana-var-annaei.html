Canna schlechtendaliana var. annaei

Canna schlechtendaliana var. annaei, commonly known as the pink dwarf canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge, and its flowers are showy, with vibrant pink petals that bloom in summer. The plant is compact, typically growing to a height of around 1 to 2 feet, making it ideal for smaller garden spaces. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in tropical and subtropical climates.
