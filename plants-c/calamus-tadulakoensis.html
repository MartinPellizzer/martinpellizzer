Calamus tadulakoensis

Calamus tadulakoensis, commonly known as the Tadulako cane, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves. It produces inflorescences with spadices and female flowers, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus tadulakoensis is often used in traditional crafts for making rattan and other woven goods.
