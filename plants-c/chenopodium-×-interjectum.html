Chenopodium × interjectum

Chenopodium × interjectum, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a species that results from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berteroianum.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and branching stems. The leaves are typically alternate, with a distinctive shape that varies depending on environmental conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
