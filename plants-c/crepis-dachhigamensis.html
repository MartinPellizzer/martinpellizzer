Crepis dachhigamensis

Crepis dachhigamensis, commonly known as the Dachhigam crested grass, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
