Cinnamomum camphora var. rotundifolia

Cinnamomum camphora var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf camphor laurel, is a variant of the camphor tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often rounded at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. It is valued for its aromatic oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
