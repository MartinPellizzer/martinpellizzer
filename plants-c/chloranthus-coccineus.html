Chloranthus coccineus

Chloranthus coccineus, commonly known as the redflowered chloranthus, is a member of the Chloranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to yellow. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and are surrounded by bract-like structures.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
