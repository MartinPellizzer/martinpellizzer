Celtis sellowiana

Celtis sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, green berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It is often found in riverine forests and is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Celtis sellowiana also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
