Carex cranaocarpa

Carex cranaocarpa, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, often nutrient-poor soils.

Carex cranaocarpa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
