Commelina tinctoria

Commelina tinctoria, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in moist environments.

Commelina tinctoria is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in dye production.
