Cyphostemma johannis

Cyphostemma johannis, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, orange-red berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
