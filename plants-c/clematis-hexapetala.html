Clematis hexapetala

Clematis hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers with six petals, typically in shades of white and pink, that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis hexapetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
