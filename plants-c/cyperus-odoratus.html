Cyperus odoratus

Cyperus odoratus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Sweetgrass is valued for its pleasant fragrance and is used in traditional crafts and rituals in various cultures.
