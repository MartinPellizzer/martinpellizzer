Cucurbita succedo

Cucurbita succedo, commonly known as the creeping pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing vine that produces large, lobed leaves and orange-yellow fruits. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, revealing a fibrous interior with edible seeds.
