Castela erecta subsp. galapageia

Castela erecta subsp.

galapageia, commonly known as the Galápagos castela, is a shrub native to the Galápagos Islands. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the Galápagos.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
