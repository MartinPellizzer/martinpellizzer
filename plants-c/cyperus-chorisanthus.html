Cyperus chorisanthus

Cyperus chorisanthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus chorisanthus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
