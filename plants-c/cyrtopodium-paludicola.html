Cyrtopodium paludicola

Cyrtopodium paludicola, commonly known as the marsh cyrtopodium, is a rare orchid species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soils, which is essential for its survival.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
