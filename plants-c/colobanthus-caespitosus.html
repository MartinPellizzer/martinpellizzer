Colobanthus caespitosus

Colobanthus caespitosus, commonly known as Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It thrives in harsh environments such as Antarctica, sub-Antarctic islands, and parts of South America. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in extreme conditions. It produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom close to the ground.

Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, allowing it to spread across barren landscapes.
