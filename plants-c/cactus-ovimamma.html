Cactus ovimamma

Cactus ovimamma, commonly known as the egg-bearing cactus, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, egg-shaped structure that contains seeds, contributing to its common name.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
