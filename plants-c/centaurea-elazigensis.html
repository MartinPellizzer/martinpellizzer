Centaurea elazigensis

Centaurea elazigensis, commonly known as the Elazığ centaury, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
