Cousinia kasachstanica

Cousinia kasachstanica, commonly known as the Kazakhstan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand drought and has a compact growth habit.

Cousinia kasachstanica is often found in steppes and along riverbanks in its native habitat.
