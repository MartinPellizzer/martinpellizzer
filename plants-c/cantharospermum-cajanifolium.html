Cantharospermum cajanifolium

Cantharospermum cajanifolium, commonly known as the velvet bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used as a source of protein in animal feed. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine due to its bioactive compounds.
