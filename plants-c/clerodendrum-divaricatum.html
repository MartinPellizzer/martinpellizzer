Clerodendrum divaricatum

Clerodendrum divaricatum, commonly known as the Variegated Flame Vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that have striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
