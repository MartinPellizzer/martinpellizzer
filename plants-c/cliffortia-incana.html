Cliffortia incana

Cliffortia incana, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cliffortia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
