Convolvulus riparius

Convolvulus riparius, commonly known as the river bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a climbing or trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other genera such as Ipomoea and Calystegia.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing riverbanks through its extensive root system.
