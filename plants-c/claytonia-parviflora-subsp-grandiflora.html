Claytonia parviflora subsp. grandiflora

Claytonia parviflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered spring beauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features rosettes of fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
