Calycogonium heterophyllum subsp. maculatum

Calycogonium heterophyllum subsp.

maculatum, commonly known as the spotted calycogonium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
