Coelogyne vinhii

Coelogyne vinhii, commonly known as the Vanhii orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces showy, bell-shaped blooms during the late winter to early spring.

Coelogyne vinhii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
