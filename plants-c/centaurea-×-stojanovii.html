Centaurea × stojanovii

Centaurea × stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanovii knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny bracts that surround its flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
