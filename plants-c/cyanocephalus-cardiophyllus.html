Cyanocephalus cardiophyllus

Cyanocephalus cardiophyllus, commonly known as the blue-headed heart-leaf plant, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique blue-violet flower heads and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, this species is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
