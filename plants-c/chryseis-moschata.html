Chryseis moschata

Chryseis moschata, commonly known as the golden squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its botanical name reflects its golden color and its association with musk, likely due to its aromatic properties.

Chryseis moschata is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
