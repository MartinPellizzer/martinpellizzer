Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp. pumilus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are densely arranged along its stems.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.
