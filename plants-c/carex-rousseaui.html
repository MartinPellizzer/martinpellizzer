Carex rousseaui

Carex rousseaui, commonly known as the Rousseaui sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Carex rousseaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
