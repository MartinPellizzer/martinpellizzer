Camellia polypetala

Camellia polypetala, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. Camellia polypetala typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
