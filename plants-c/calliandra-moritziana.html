Calliandra moritziana

Calliandra moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
