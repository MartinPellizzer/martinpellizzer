Coluteocarpus vesicaria subsp. boissieri

Coluteocarpus vesicaria subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Syrian bladder nut, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces distinctive bladder-like fruits that are inflated and contain a single seed. These fruits are typically green when immature and turn brown as they mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
