Collaea scarlatina

Collaea scarlatina, commonly known as the red-flowered rock heath.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes Mountains. The plant is characterized by its bright red petals and is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas. It has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
