Cyperus sylvestris

Cyperus sylvestris, commonly known as the swamp sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus sylvestris thrives in moist, swampy soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is adapted to waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
