Calendula persica

Calendula persica, commonly known as Persian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, daisy-like blooms. Calendula persica is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
