Cissus usambarensis

Cissus usambarensis, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a climbing perennial vine native to eastern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, waxy, translucent berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing on trees or rocky surfaces.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
