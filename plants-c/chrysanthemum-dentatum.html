Chrysanthemum dentatum

Chrysanthemum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow to orange flower heads composed of tiny tubular florets. The leaves are typically lobed and toothed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
