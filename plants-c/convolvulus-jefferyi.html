Convolvulus jefferyi

Convolvulus jefferyi, commonly known as Jeffery's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Convolvulus jefferyi is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
