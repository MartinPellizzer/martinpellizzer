Celosia sanguinea

Celosia sanguinea, commonly known as the blood grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, erect stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, feathery clusters.

Celosia sanguinea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
