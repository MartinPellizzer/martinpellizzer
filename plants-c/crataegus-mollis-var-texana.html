Crataegus mollis var. texana

Crataegus mollis var. texana, commonly known as the Texas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green on the upper surface and whitish underneath. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The Texas hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
