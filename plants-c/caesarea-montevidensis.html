Caesarea montevidensis

Caesarea montevidensis, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers are followed by a distinctive seed head.

Caesarea montevidensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
