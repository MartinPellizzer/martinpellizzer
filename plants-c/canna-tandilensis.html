Canna tandilensis

Canna tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna tandilensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features due to its dramatic foliage and colorful blooms.
