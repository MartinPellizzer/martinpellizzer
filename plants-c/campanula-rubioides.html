Campanula rubioides

Campanula rubioides, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in rocky or grassy areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Campanula rubioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
