Calanthe lutescens

Calanthe lutescens, commonly known as the golden calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a broad, lance-shaped leaf. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Calanthe lutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
