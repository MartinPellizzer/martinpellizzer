Coccothrinax × angelae

Coccothrinax × angelae, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Coccothrinax argentata and Coccothrinax mirabilis.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, dark blue-black fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in the Caribbean and Florida.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation programs.
