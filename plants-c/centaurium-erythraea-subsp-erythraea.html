Centaurium erythraea subsp. erythraea

Centaurium erythraea subsp.

erythraea, commonly known as red centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Centaurium erythraea subsp.

erythraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
