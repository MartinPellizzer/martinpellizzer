Cinnamomum burmanni

Cinnamomum burmanni, commonly known as Burmann's cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
