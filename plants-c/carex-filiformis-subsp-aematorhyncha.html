Carex filiformis subsp. aematorhyncha

Carex filiformis subsp.

aematorhyncha, commonly known as the redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish tinge on the stems. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
