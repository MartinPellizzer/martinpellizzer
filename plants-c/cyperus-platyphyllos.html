Cyperus platyphyllos

Cyperus platyphyllos, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green color.

Cyperus platyphyllos produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or brown, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
