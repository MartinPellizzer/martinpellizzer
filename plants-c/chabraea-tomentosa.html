Chabraea tomentosa

Chabraea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly chabraea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
