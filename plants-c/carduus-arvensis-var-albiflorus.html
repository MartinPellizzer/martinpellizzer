Carduus arvensis var. albiflorus

Carduus arvensis var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered sow thistle, is a variant of the common sow thistle.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that are slightly smaller than those of the typical variety. Its white-flowered form is a distinguishing characteristic, setting it apart from other Carduus species.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
