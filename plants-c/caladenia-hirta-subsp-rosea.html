Caladenia hirta subsp. rosea

Caladenia hirta subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose-hair orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia. It features a single, slender stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive pinkish-red coloration.

The flower has a narrow, hairy labellum that is adorned with intricate patterns, enhancing its visual appeal. This subspecies thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, typically in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

Its blooming period occurs during the spring, adding a vibrant touch to the local flora.
