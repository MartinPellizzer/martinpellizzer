Carica cundinamarcensis

Carica cundinamarcensis, commonly known as the Cundinamarca papaya, is a tropical plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is closely related to the common papaya. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has potential for cultivation in warm climates.
