Calamagrostis irazuensis

Calamagrostis irazuensis, commonly known as the Irazu reed, is a grass species native to the highlands of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually brown or tan in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Calamagrostis irazuensis plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
