Campanula rotundifolia var. latifolia

Campanula rotundifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
