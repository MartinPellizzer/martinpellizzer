Cheiranthus × cheiri var. canescens

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
