Coix lacryma

Coix lacryma, commonly known as Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tear-shaped caryopsis, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in spikelets.

The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been cultivated for thousands of years in China and other parts of Asia.
