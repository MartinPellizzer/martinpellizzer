Cyrtanthus pallidus

Cyrtanthus pallidus, commonly known as the pale cyrtanthus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in spring, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
