Carex obtusobracteata

Carex obtusobracteata, commonly known as the blunt-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of male and female flowers, with the female flowers often enclosed in bracts that are shorter than the spike.

Carex obtusobracteata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
