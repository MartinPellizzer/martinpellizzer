Clinopodium laxiflorum

Clinopodium laxiflorum, commonly known as slenderflower betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of both horticultural and pharmacological interest.
