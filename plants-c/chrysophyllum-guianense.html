Chrysophyllum guianense

Chrysophyllum guianense, commonly known as the Guianan star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
