Ceropegia flavida

Ceropegia flavida, commonly known as the yellow ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
