Cardamine amara f. grandiflora

Cardamine amara f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bittercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a slightly bitter taste.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
