Carpha aubertii

Carpha aubertii, commonly known as the Aubert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes that help it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Carpha aubertii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
