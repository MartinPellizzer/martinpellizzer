Croton mahafaliensis

Croton mahafaliensis, commonly known as the Mahafali croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Croton. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, with a variety of shapes and colors.

The Mahafali croton is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
