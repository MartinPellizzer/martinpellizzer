Clerodendrum porphyrocalyx var. dentatum

Clerodendrum porphyrocalyx var. dentatum, commonly known as the red-leaved clerodendrum, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its vibrant, deep red leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and seasonal floral display.
