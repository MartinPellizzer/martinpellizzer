Cymbidium serrulatum

Cymbidium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during dry periods. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium serrulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
