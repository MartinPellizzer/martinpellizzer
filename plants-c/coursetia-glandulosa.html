Coursetia glandulosa

Coursetia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several feet in height.

Coursetia glandulosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert habitats.
