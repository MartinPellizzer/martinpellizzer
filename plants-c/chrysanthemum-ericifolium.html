Chrysanthemum ericifolium

Chrysanthemum ericifolium, commonly known as the heath chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and its evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. It is often found in heathland and open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
