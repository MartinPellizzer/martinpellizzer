Chimonocalamus armatus

Chimonocalamus armatus, commonly known as the armed bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features stiff, hollow stems that are often covered with short, sharp spines, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
