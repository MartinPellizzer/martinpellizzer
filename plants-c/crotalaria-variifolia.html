Crotalaria variifolia

Crotalaria variifolia, commonly known as the variable cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive black or purple pattern on the petals.

Crotalaria variifolia is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
