Clarionea carthamoides var. crispa

Clarionea carthamoides var. crispa, commonly known as the crisped thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant features a tall, upright stem with silver-green, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
