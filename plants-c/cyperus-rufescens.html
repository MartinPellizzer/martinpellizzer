Cyperus rufescens

Cyperus rufescens, commonly known as the red rush, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus rufescens is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water filter in aquatic environments.
