Calamagrostis pubescens

Calamagrostis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calamagrostis, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silver-blue foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Calamagrostis pubescens is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
