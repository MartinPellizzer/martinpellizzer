Cuphea epilobiifolia var. caquetae

Cuphea epilobiifolia var. caquetae, commonly known as the Caqueta cuphea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
