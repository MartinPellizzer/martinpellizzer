Crataegus succulenta var. michiganensis

Crataegus succulenta var. michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central and northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are usually elliptical with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
