Coleocephalocereus fluminensis subsp. paulensis

Coleocephalocereus fluminensis subsp.

paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's night bloomer, is a cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and is typically sparsely branched.

The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
