Cylindrolobus aurantius

Cylindrolobus aurantius, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has a slender, cylindrical trunk and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

The leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.
