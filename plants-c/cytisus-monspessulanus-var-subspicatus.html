Cytisus monspessulanus var. subspicatus

Cytisus monspessulanus var. subspicatus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate or pinnate. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful management.
