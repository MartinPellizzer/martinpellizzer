Chrysactinia acerosa

Chrysactinia acerosa, commonly known as the hardstem sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant is known for its upright, spiny stems and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and sagebrush habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species adapted to arid environments.
