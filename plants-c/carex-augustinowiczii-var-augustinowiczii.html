Carex augustinowiczii var. augustinowiczii

Carex augustinowiczii var. augustinowiczii, commonly known as the Augustinowicz sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a spiky cluster of small, three-sided flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
