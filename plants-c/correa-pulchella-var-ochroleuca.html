Correa pulchella var. ochroleuca

Correa pulchella var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the White Correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
