Crataegus monogyna var. quercifolia

Crataegus monogyna var. quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of oak trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
