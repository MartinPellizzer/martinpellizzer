Carex jacens var. pubescens

Carex jacens var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters called spikes, typically in a two-tiered structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
