Crepidium xanthochilum

Crepidium xanthochilum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive yellow-orange flowers with a lip that resembles a lip-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
