Cerasus juliana var. nigra

Cerasus juliana var. nigra, commonly known as the black cherry or black chokecherry, is a variety of the Prunus species native to eastern North America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it has a distinctive, fragrant blossom in the spring.

The plant is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
