Chamaecyparis obtusa var. alba

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. alba, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar, is a variety of the cedar tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its white-variegated foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens and landscapes. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat sprays, and its cones are small and cylindrical. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

This variety is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical significance in traditional Japanese culture.
