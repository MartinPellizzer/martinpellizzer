Cryphiacanthus acaulis

Cryphiacanthus acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
