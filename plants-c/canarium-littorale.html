Canarium littorale

Canarium littorale, commonly known as the coastal canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in saline and humid environments, often found in mangrove forests and tidal mudflats.

Canarium littorale plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
