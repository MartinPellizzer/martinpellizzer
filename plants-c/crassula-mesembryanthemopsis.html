Crassula mesembryanthemopsis

Crassula mesembryanthemopsis, commonly known as the "Lipstick Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that often branch out, covered in small, rounded leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it both ornamental and relatively easy to care for.
