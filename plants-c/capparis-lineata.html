Capparis lineata

Capparis lineata, commonly known as the sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has aromatic, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Capparis lineata is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
