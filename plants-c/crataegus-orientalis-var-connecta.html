Crataegus orientalis var. connecta

Crataegus orientalis var. connecta, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
