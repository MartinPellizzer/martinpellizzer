Cirsium heterophyllum

Cirsium heterophyllum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish and hairy.

The yellow thistle is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
