Campanula schimperi var. sarmentosa

Campanula schimperi var. sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It features upright stems that often produce trailing shoots, giving it a vine-like appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
