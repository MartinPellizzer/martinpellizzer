Campanula filicaulis var. elata

Campanula filicaulis var. elata, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
