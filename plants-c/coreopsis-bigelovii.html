Coreopsis bigelovii

Coreopsis bigelovii, commonly known as the prairie coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Coreopsis bigelovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
