Cymbopogon schimperi

Cymbopogon schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its essential oils, rich in citral, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
