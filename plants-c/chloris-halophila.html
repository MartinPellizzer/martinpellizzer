Chloris halophila

Chloris halophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces small, greenish flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and salt-affected soils, making it an important species for ecological restoration.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it valuable for soil stabilization and revegetation projects.
