Croton lepidotus

Croton lepidotus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to shades of red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Croton lepidotus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
