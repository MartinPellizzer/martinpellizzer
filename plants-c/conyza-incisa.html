Conyza incisa

Conyza incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Conyza incisa is known for its resilience and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
