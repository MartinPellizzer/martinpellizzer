Calamus impressus

Calamus impressus, commonly known as the impressed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus impressus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
