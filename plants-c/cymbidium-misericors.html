Cymbidium misericors

Cymbidium misericors, commonly known as the weeping cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, providing a lush, evergreen appearance.

Cymbidium misericors is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
