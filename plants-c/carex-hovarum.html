Carex hovarum

Carex hovarum, commonly known as the Hovar's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
