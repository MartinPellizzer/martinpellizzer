Conchium cornutum

Conchium cornutum, commonly known as the horned conch, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling, horn-like structure that forms a protective casing around its reproductive organs. The plant exhibits a fascinating adaptation to its environment, with its conch-like structure providing both structural support and defense against herbivores. Botanically, it belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family.

Its morphology and ecological role make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
