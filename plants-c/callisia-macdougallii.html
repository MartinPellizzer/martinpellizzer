Callisia macdougallii

Callisia macdougallii, commonly known as the Macdougall's spider plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink blooms that are typically borne on slender, hairy stalks. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Callisia macdougallii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers or hanging baskets.
