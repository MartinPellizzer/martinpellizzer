Chirita pungentisepala

Chirita pungentisepala, commonly known as the spiny-sepaled chirita, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny sepals that give it its common name. It produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a contrasting yellow throat.

Chirita pungentisepala is typically grown as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
