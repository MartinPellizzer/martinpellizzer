Cucurbita tuberculosa

Cucurbita tuberculosa, commonly known as the prickly gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical fruits with a rough, spiny surface. Its fruit is edible when mature, though it is often used as a vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
