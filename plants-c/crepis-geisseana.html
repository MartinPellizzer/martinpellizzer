Crepis geisseana

Crepis geisseana, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Crepis geisseana produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
