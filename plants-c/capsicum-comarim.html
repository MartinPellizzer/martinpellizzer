Capsicum comarim

Capsicum comarim, commonly known as the wild pepper or mountain pepper, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, green or red berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Capsicum comarim is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
