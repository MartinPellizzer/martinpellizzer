Cissus oreophila

Cissus oreophila, commonly known as the rock fig or mountain fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and adaptable species within its native habitat.
