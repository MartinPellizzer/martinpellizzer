Cyperus mollipes var. paolii

Cyperus mollipes var. paolii, commonly known as the pink flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that often display a pinkish hue. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
