Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. vulgare

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. vulgare, commonly known as cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
