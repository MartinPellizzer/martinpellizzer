Crataegus transcaspica

Crataegus transcaspica, commonly known as the Transcaspian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

Crataegus transcaspica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
