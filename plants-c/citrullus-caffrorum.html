Citrullus caffrorum

Citrullus caffrorum, commonly known as the Caffrorum cucumber, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a vine that produces small, edible fruits with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant has hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are typical features of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and can be cultivated as a ornamental plant.
