Cerastium illyricum subsp. illyricum

Cerastium illyricum subsp.

illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are hairy and often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
