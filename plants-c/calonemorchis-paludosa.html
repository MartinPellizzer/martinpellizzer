Calonemorchis paludosa

Calonemorchis paludosa, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Calonemorchis paludosa thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.
