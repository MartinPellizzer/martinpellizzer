Campanula stylidioides

Campanula stylidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Campanula stylidioides thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
