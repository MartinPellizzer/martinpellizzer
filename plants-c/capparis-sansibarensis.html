Capparis sansibarensis

Capparis sansibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, green, cucumber-like fruits. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in sandy or coastal soils.

Capparis sansibarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
