Cavendishia punctatifolia

Cavendishia punctatifolia, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with distinctive white spots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
