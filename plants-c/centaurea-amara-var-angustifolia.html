Centaurea amara var. angustifolia

Centaurea amara var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bitterweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical heads. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Centaurea amara var. angustifolia is known for its bitter-tasting foliage, which has historically been used in traditional herbal medicine.
