Cestrum schlechtendalii

Cestrum schlechtendalii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral displays.
