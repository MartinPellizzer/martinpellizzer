Cucubalus behen var. antelopum

Cucubalus behen var. antelopum, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins.

It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient in various cultures.
