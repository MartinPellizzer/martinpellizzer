Crocus thracicus

Crocus thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

The bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky or dry soils, and the plant is often found in mountainous areas.
