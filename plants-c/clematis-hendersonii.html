Clematis hendersonii

Clematis hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's clematis, is a flowering vine native to China.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, twining stems that climb by means of small tendrils, and its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
