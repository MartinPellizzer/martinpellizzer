Conophallus commutatus

Conophallus commutatus, commonly known as the variable conophallus, is a rare and specialized fungal plant that exhibits unique morphological features.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruiting bodies that often display a range of colors depending on environmental conditions. The species is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often associated with decaying organic matter. Its spore-producing structures, known as sporocarps, are crucial for its reproductive cycle.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Conophallus commutatus is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
