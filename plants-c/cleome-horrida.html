Cleome horrida

Cleome horrida, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Cleome horrida is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
