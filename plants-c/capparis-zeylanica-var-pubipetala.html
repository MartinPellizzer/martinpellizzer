Capparis zeylanica var. pubipetala

Capparis zeylanica var. pubipetala, commonly known as the hairy capparis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
