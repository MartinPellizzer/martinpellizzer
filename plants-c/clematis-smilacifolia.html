Clematis smilacifolia

Clematis smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis smilacifolia is often used in landscaping for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or walls.
