Carex pisiformis var. pallescens

Carex pisiformis var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale carex, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually light green to pale blue-gray in color. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
