Citrus × tachibana subf. depressa

Citrus × tachibana subf.

depressa, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or tachibana, is a citrus hybrid native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant, and often seedless fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree with thick, glossy leaves and a compact, rounded shape.

Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters, and it is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruit. The subsp.

depressa is particularly noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience to cold temperatures.
