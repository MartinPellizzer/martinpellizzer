Christolea kashgarica

Christolea kashgarica, commonly known as the Kashgar velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and thick leaves that reduce water loss. It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is often found in riverbanks and saline soils.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species in semi-arid and desert ecosystems.
