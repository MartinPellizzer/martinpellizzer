Cereus del-moralii

Cereus del-moralii, commonly known as the Del Moral Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
