Capitularina involucrata

Capitularina involucrata, commonly known as the clustered cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, yellowish flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
