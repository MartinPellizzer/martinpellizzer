Cassia leptocarpa

Cassia leptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia leptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
