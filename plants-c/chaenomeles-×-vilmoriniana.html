Chaenomeles × vilmoriniana

Chaenomeles × vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces ornamental fruit that turns from green to orange-red as it matures. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
