Chamaecrista basifolia

Chamaecrista basifolia, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in small, oblong pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Chamaecrista basifolia is also valued as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
