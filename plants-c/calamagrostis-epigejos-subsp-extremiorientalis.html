Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. extremiorientalis

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp.

extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
