Cirsium hellenicum

Cirsium hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium hellenicum is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
