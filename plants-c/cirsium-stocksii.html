Cirsium stocksii

Cirsium stocksii, commonly known as the Stock's thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Cirsium stocksii is often found in moist to dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
