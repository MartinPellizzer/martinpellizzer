Crataegus curtisii

Crataegus curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that matures in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
