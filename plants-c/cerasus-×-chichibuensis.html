Cerasus × chichibuensis

Cerasus × chichibuensis, commonly known as the Chichibu cherry, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to white, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible cherries that are smaller than those of other cherry species.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
