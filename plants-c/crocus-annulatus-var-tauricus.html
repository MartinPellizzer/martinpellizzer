Crocus annulatus var. tauricus

Crocus annulatus var. tauricus, commonly known as the Taurian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its distinctive, often speckled flower petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
