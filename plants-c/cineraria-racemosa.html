Cineraria racemosa

Cineraria racemosa, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cineraria racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
