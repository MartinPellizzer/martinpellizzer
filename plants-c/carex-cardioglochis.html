Carex cardioglochis

Carex cardioglochis, commonly known as the heart-shaped sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically found in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex cardioglochis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
