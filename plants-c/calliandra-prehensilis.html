Calliandra prehensilis

Calliandra prehensilis, commonly known as the hanging calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and dense clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and its stems are often used as living fences or support structures in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
