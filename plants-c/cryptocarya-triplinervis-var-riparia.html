Cryptocarya triplinervis var. riparia

Cryptocarya triplinervis var. riparia, commonly known as river cryptocarya, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves, which are often glossy and have a distinct three-veined structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
