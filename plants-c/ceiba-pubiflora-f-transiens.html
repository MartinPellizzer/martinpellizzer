Ceiba pubiflora f. transiens

Ceiba pubiflora f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Ceiba, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and elongated, slender trunk that can grow up to 30 meters tall. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture with a dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to coastal and mangrove environments, making it an important component of tropical coastal ecosystems.
