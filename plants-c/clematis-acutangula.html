Clematis acutangula

Clematis acutangula, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces blue to purple flowers with five petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and support for its vigorous growth.

Clematis acutangula is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
