Copiapoa rubriflora

Copiapoa rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and bright red flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a dense, woolly coating that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
