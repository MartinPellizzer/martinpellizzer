Chamaedorea verapazensis

Chamaedorea verapazensis, commonly known as the Verapaz bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central America, including parts of Honduras and Guatemala. The plant features slender, upright stems with clustered, feather-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in indoor gardening due to its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.
