Chascolytrum barbatum

Chascolytrum barbatum, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is sometimes grown in botanical gardens.
