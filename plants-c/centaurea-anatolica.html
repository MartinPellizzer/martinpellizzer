Centaurea anatolica

Centaurea anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and parts of southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped. Centaurea anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and visually appealing species in both natural and cultivated settings.
