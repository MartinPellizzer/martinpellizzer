Combretum caudatisepalum

Combretum caudatisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
