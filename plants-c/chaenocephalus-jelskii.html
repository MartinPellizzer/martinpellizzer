Chaenocephalus jelskii

Chaenocephalus jelskii, commonly known as the Jelskii's cypress, is a species of coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that form a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain the seeds necessary for reproduction. It thrives in cool, dry climates with well-drained soils, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
