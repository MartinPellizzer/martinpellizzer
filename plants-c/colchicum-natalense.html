Colchicum natalense

Colchicum natalense, commonly known as Natal meadow saffron, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, saffron-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The bulbs of Colchicum natalense contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
