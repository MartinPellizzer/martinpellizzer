Cleistocactus jugatiflorus

Cleistocactus jugatiflorus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the warm season, typically in the summer months. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cleistocactus jugatiflorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
