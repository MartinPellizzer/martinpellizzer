Cineraria scirpina

Cineraria scirpina, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The flowers are usually blue to violet in color and bloom in the spring and summer months.
