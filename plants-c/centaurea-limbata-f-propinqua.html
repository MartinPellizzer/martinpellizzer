Centaurea limbata f. propinqua

Centaurea limbata f. propinqua, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
