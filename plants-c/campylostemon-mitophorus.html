Campylostemon mitophorus

Campylostemon mitophorus, commonly known as the white-flowered corymbiferous plant, is a species native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its adaptability in moist environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
