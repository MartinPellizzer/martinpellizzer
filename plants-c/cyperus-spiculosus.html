Cyperus spiculosus

Cyperus spiculosus, commonly known as spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Cyperus spiculosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
