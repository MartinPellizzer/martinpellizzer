Coelogyne sarawakensis

Coelogyne sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Coelogyne sarawakensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
