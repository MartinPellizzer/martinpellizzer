Carex cespitosa f. polystachya

Carex cespitosa f. polystachya, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets arranged in a loose, branched structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
