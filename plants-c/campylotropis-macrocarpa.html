Campylotropis macrocarpa

Campylotropis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are woody, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Campylotropis macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
