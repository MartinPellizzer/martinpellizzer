Cylindrophyllum obsubulatum

Cylindrophyllum obsubulatum, commonly known as the slender-leafed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged in a dense, feather-like pattern. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
