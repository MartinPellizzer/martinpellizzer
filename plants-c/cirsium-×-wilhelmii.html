Cirsium × wilhelmii

Cirsium × wilhelmii, commonly known as the German thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
