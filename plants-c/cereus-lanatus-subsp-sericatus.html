Cereus lanatus subsp. sericatus

Cereus lanatus subsp.

sericatus, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
