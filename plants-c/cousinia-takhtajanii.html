Cousinia takhtajanii

Cousinia takhtajanii, commonly known as the Afghan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia takhtajanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
