Clutia pterogona var. angustifolia

Clutia pterogona var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 4 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are narrow and elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
