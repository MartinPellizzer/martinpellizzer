Cucumis melo subsp. chandalak

Cucumis melo subsp.

chandalak, commonly known as the Indian cucumber melon, is a subspecies of the melon genus native to South Asia. It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
