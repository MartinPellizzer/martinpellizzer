Clinopodium canescens

Clinopodium canescens, commonly known as white wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Clinopodium canescens has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
