Cassia pittieri

Cassia pittieri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South and Central America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces flat, elliptical pods that contain numerous flat, winged seeds. Cassia pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
