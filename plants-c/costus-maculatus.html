Costus maculatus

Costus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves with a distinctive spotted or streaked pattern on the upper surface. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, yellowish-white flowers surrounded by bracts.

Costus maculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
