Calamus rhabdocladus var. globulosus

Calamus rhabdocladus var. globulosus, commonly known as the globular calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, swollen rhizomes and large, showy spadices. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is a common feature in the Araceae family.
