Carex hystericina var. dudleyi

Carex hystericina var. dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
