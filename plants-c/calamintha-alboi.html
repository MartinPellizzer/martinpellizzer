Calamintha alboi

Calamintha alboi, commonly known as white thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, slightly hairy, and emit a mint-like scent when crushed.

Calamintha alboi is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming and digestive properties.
