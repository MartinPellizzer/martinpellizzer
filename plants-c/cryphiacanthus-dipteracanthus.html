Cryphiacanthus dipteracanthus

Cryphiacanthus dipteracanthus, commonly known as the African giant hyacinth, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its bulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
