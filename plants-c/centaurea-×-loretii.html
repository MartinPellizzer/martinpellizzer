Centaurea × loretii

Centaurea × loretii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and gardens.

Centaurea × loretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
