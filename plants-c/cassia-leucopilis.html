Cassia leucopilis

Cassia leucopilis, commonly known as the white-winged cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, delicate leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Cassia leucopilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
