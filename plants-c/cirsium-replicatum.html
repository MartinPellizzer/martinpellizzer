Cirsium replicatum

Cirsium replicatum, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cirsium replicatum is an annual plant that produces seeds in late autumn, ensuring its survival through seasonal changes.
