Cleome perplexa

Cleome perplexa, commonly known as the swamp cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Cleome perplexa is characterized by its unique, branching inflorescences and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.
