Cyathea tsilotsilensis

Cyathea tsilotsilensis, commonly known as the Tasilotzil fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which release spores for propagation. Its rhizome system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea tsilotsilensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
