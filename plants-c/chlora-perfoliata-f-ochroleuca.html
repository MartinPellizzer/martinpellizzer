Chlora perfoliata f. ochroleuca

Chlora perfoliata f. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-stemmed chlora, is a variant of the chlora perfoliata species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive white or pale green stems. The leaves are typically opposite and have a perfoliate arrangement, meaning they pass through the stem. This form is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
