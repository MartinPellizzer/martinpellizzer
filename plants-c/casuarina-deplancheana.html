Casuarina deplancheana

Casuarina deplancheana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

Casuarina deplancheana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
