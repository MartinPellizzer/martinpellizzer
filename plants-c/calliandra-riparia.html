Calliandra riparia

Calliandra riparia, commonly known as the river calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water sources, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Calliandra riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil along riverbanks.
