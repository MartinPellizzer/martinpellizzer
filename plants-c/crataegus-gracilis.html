Crataegus gracilis

Crataegus gracilis, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched form and its clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus gracilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
