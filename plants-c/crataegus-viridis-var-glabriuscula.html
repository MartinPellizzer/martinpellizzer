Crataegus viridis var. glabriuscula

Crataegus viridis var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves that are often glossy green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
