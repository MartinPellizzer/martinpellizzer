Commiphora kraeuseliana

Commiphora kraeuseliana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
