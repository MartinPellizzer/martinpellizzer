Calophylica gnidioides

Calophylica gnidioides, commonly known as the African bluebush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
