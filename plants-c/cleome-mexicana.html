Cleome mexicana

Cleome mexicana, commonly known as Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble small spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Cleome mexicana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
