Cladium insulare

Cladium insulare, commonly known as the island spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense clusters with tall, slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikes of small, three-ranked flowers.

Cladium insulare plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
