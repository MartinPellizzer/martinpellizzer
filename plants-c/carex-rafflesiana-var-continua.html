Carex rafflesiana var. continua

Carex rafflesiana var. continua, commonly known as the continuous sedge, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-green, with a smooth texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
