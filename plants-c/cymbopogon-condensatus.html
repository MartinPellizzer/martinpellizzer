Cymbopogon condensatus

Cymbopogon condensatus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often cultivated for its essential oils. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in cooking.

The botanical features of Cymbopogon condensatus make it a valuable species in both horticulture and aromatherapy.
