Cornus aspera

Cornus aspera, commonly known as rough cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on its stems. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

Cornus aspera is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
