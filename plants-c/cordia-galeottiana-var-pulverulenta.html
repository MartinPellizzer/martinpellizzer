Cordia galeottiana var. pulverulenta

Cordia galeottiana var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the powderpuff tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fluffy white inflorescences that resemble clouds, which appear in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive waxy texture with a pale green upper surface and a whitish underside. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
