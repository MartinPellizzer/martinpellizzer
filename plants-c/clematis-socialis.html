Clematis socialis

Clematis socialis, commonly known as the clustered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis socialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
