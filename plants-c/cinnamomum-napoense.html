Cinnamomum napoense

Cinnamomum napoense, commonly known as the Amazonian cinnamon or *canela de Amazonas*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinct cinnamon-like aroma when crushed.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional remedies and as a spice.
