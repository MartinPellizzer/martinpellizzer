Carduncellus pinnatus var. caulescens

Carduncellus pinnatus var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stalks and large, palmate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its robust growth, it is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
