Chenopodium petiolare f. hookeri

Chenopodium petiolare f. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's spinach, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both a weed and a potential forage plant in certain regions.
