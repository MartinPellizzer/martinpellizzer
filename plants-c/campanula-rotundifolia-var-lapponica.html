Campanula rotundifolia var. lapponica

Campanula rotundifolia var. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, with a rosette growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
