Cissus subramanyamii

Cissus subramanyamii, commonly known as the Indian snow vine, is a climbing plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
