Canavalia boliviana

Canavalia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian bean or wild cowpea, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, similar to other members of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Canavalia boliviana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
