Corion purpureum var. longipes

Corion purpureum var. longipes, commonly known as the purple corion with long stems, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems that can reach several feet in height, often branching profusely. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
