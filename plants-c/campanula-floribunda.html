Campanula floribunda

Campanula floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
