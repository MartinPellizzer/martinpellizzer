Carex lepidocarpa f. laeviculmis

Carex lepidocarpa f. laeviculmis, commonly known as the smooth-ribbed sedge, is a variant of the broader Carex lepidocarpa species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly curved. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, three-sided seed capsules.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
