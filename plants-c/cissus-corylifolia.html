Cissus corylifolia

Cissus corylifolia, commonly known as the Indian money plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
