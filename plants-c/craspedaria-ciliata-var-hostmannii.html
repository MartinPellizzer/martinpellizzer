Craspedaria ciliata var. hostmannii

Craspedaria ciliata var. hostmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive hairy texture, and the flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
