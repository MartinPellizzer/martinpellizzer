Cineraria

Cineraria, commonly known as the "blueweed" or "rock carnation," is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. Cineraria thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its name is derived from the Latin word "cinerarius," meaning ash-colored, referring to the color of its foliage.
