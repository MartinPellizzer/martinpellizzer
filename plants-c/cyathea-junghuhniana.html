Cyathea junghuhniana

Cyathea junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant features large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Cyathea junghuhniana thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland rainforests.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a valuable specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
