Cakile maritima subsp. aegyptiaca

Cakile maritima subsp.

aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in salty, well-drained soils along shorelines.

The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by kidney-shaped pods containing seeds. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh coastal environments and plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
