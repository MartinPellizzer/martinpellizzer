Cestichis pendula

Cestichis pendula, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

Cestichis pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, hanging blooms.
