Crataegus iracunda var. populnea

Crataegus iracunda var. populnea, commonly known as the Poplar Thornapple, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
