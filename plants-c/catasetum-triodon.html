Catasetum triodon

Catasetum triodon, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette.

Catasetum triodon is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its intricate and colorful blooms.
