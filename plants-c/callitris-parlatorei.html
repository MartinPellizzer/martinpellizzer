Callitris parlatorei

Callitris parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of conifers. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Callitris parlatorei is considered a vulnerable species.
