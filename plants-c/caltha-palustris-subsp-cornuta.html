Caltha palustris subsp. cornuta

Caltha palustris subsp.

cornuta, commonly known as the horned buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas of northern Europe. It features bright yellow flowers with distinctive horn-shaped petals that give it its common name.

The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and along the edges of ponds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
