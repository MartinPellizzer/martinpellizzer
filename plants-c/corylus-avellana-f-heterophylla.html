Corylus avellana f. heterophylla

Corylus avellana f. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hazel, is a cultivar of the European hazel that exhibits notable variation in leaf morphology.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, ranging from broad to narrow, which can vary depending on environmental conditions and genetic factors. The plant produces small, edible nuts that are often used in culinary and ornamental contexts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.

This form is valued in horticulture for its adaptability and visual interest in landscapes.
