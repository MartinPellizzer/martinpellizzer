Conoclinium betonicifolium var. integrifolium

Conoclinium betonicifolium var. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with smooth edges, giving it its varietal name "integrifolium.". It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
