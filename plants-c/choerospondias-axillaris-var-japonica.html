Choerospondias axillaris var. japonica

Choerospondias axillaris var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces edible, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiny husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
