Cirsium arvense var. argenteum

Cirsium arvense var. argenteum, commonly known as silver cockle, is a variant of the common cocklebur.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features silver-white hairs on its leaves and bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It has spiny, lobed leaves and produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered invasive in some regions.
