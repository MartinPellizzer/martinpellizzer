Carthamus magellanicus

Carthamus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and animals.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience in harsh environments.
