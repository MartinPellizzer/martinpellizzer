Cryptomeria variegata

Cryptomeria variegata, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive, scale-like leaves that are often variegated in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.
