Cylicodaphne costata

Cylicodaphne costata, commonly known as the ridge-leaf dracaena, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that have prominent parallel veins. The leaves are usually a deep green color and often have a slightly waxy texture. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.
