Cerastium alpinum lusus leiopetalum

Cerastium alpinum lusus leiopetalum, commonly known as the smooth-petaled snowberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas where it contributes to alpine vegetation.
