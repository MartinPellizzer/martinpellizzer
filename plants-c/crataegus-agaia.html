Crataegus agaia

Crataegus agaia, commonly known as the Armenian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Crataegus agaia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cardiovascular benefits.
