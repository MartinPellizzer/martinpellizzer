Ceriops boviniana

Ceriops boviniana, commonly known as the mangrove apple or red mangrove, is a tropical tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Rhizophoraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red bark and aerial roots. The plant thrives in brackish water environments, playing a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting marine ecosystems. Its small, pinkish flowers are followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are important for local wildlife and human consumption.

Ceriops boviniana is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
