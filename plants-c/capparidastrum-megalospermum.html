Capparidastrum megalospermum

Capparidastrum megalospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are typically succulent and branching.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
