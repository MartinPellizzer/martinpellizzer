Commelina avenifolia

Commelina avenifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with three petals and three sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina avenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
