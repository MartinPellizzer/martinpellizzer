Croton serratoideus

Croton serratoideus, commonly known as the serrated croton, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage.
