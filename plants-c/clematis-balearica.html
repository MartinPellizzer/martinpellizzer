Clematis balearica

Clematis balearica, commonly known as the Balearic Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for climbing structures or trellises.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
