Campanula rotundifolia subsp. montana

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
