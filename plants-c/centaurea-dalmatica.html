Centaurea dalmatica

Centaurea dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea dalmatica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
