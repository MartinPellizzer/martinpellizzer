Clinopodium dentatum

Clinopodium dentatum, commonly known as narrowleaf mint or narrow-leaved catnip, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Clinopodium dentatum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
