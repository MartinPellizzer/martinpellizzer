Cirsium collivagum

Cirsium collivagum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Cirsium collivagum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
