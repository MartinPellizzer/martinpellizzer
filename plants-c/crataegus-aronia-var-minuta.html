Crataegus aronia var. minuta

Crataegus aronia var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf black hawthorn, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and thorny branches. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the characteristic berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
