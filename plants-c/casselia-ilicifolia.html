Casselia ilicifolia

Casselia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf casselia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Casselia ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
