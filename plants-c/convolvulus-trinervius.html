Convolvulus trinervius

Convolvulus trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a creeping perennial that produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
