Cosmanthus fimbriatus

Cosmanthus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringe-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its delicate appearance.
