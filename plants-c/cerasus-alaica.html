Cerasus alaica

Cerasus alaica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Cerasus alaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
