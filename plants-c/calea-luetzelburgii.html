Calea luetzelburgii

Calea luetzelburgii, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral behavior, which is triggered by changes in light and temperature. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calea luetzelburgii is often cultivated for its beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
