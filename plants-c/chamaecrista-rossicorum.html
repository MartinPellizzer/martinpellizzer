Chamaecrista rossicorum

Chamaecrista rossicorum, commonly known as the Russian crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Chamaecrista rossicorum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
