Cardamine plumieri

Cardamine plumieri, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and its small white flowers are borne in clusters. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Cardamine plumieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
