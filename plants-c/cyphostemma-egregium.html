Cyphostemma egregium

Cyphostemma egregium, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
