Crepis maritima

Crepis maritima, commonly known as sea hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that help it withstand salty sea air and strong winds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is often found in maritime habitats.

Crepis maritima is an important plant for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
