Cenchrus petiolaris

Cenchrus petiolaris, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cenchrus petiolaris is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun.
