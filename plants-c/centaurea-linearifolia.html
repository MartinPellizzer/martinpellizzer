Centaurea linearifolia

Centaurea linearifolia, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea linearifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
