Centella hederifolia

Centella hederifolia, commonly known as Asiatic pennywort, is a creeping perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams. Its stems can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Centella hederifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
