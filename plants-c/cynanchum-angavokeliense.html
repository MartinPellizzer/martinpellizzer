Cynanchum angavokeliense

Cynanchum angavokeliense, commonly known as the Angavo kela vine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and milky sap. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically opposite and ovate. Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
