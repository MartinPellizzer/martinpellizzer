Corynopuntia grahamii

Corynopuntia grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white, woolly flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Corynopuntia grahamii is a slow-growing plant that is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
