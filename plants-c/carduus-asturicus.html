Carduus asturicus

Carduus asturicus, commonly known as the Asturian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its historical use in traditional medicine.
