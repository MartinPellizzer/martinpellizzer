Coleus hallii

Coleus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorled clusters.

Coleus hallii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and partial shade.
