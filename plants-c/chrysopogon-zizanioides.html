Chrysopogon zizanioides

Chrysopogon zizanioides, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and marshes.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become invasive in certain regions.
