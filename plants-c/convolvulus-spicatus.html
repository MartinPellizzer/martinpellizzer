Convolvulus spicatus

Convolvulus spicatus, commonly known as the spiny morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Convolvulus spicatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
