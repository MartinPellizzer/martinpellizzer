Commiphora flaviflora

Commiphora flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
