Cotoneaster garhwalensis

Cotoneaster garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan region of India.

It typically grows in subalpine and alpine habitats, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter.

Cotoneaster garhwalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountainous ecosystems.
