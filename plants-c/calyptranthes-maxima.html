Calyptranthes maxima

Calyptranthes maxima, commonly known as the giant calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its striking appearance and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical climates.
