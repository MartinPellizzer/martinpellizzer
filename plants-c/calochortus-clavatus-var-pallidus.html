Calochortus clavatus var. pallidus

Calochortus clavatus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to cream, with a distinctive red or orange center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
