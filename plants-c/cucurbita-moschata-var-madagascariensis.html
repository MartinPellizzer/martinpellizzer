Cucurbita moschata var. madagascariensis

Cucurbita moschata var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the African pumpkin or Malabar cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar.

It produces large, elongated fruits with a pale yellow to orange skin and a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its edible fruits and ornamental qualities.
