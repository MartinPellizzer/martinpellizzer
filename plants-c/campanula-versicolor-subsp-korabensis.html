Campanula versicolor subsp. korabensis

Campanula versicolor subsp.

korabensis, commonly known as the Korab bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
