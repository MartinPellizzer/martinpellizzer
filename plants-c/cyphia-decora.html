Cyphia decora

Cyphia decora, commonly known as the showy cyphia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Cyphia decora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
