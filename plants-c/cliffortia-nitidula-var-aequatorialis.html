Cliffortia nitidula var. aequatorialis

Cliffortia nitidula var. aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
