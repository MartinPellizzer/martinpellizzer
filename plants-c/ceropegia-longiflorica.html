Ceropegia longiflorica

Ceropegia longiflorica, commonly known as the long-flowered arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and distinctive, spurred flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine, silky hair.
