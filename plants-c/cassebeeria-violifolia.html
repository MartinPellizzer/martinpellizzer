Cassebeeria violifolia

Cassebeeria violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with a violet or purple hue, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Cassebeeria violifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
