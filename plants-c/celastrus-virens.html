Celastrus virens

Celastrus virens, commonly known as the greenbark creeper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and persistent green bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Celastrus virens is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
