Caltha malvacea

Caltha malvacea, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are arranged in a dense, cup-shaped inflorescence.

Caltha malvacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
