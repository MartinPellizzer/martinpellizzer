Cola marsupium

Cola marsupium, commonly known as the African cola nut, is a tropical shrub native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits. The plant produces seeds enclosed in a unique, swollen calyx that resembles a pouch, giving it its scientific name "marsupium.". The fruits are often harvested for their seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts within its native habitat.
