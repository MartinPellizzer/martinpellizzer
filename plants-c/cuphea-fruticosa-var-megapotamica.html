Cuphea fruticosa var. megapotamica

Cuphea fruticosa var. megapotamica, commonly known as the Mexican heather or flameflower, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy, bracted inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
