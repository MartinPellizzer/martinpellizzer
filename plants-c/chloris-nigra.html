Chloris nigra

Chloris nigra, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is known for its ability to regrow from its rhizomes after being grazed.

Chloris nigra is an important component of native ecosystems and has been studied for its ecological and agricultural significance.
