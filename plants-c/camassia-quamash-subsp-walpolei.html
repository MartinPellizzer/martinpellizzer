Camassia quamash subsp. walpolei

Camassia quamash subsp.

walpolei, commonly known as the yellow camas, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
