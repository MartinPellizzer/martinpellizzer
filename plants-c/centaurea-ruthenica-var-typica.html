Centaurea ruthenica var. typica

Centaurea ruthenica var. typica, commonly known as the typical cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
