Camellia limonia

Camellia limonia, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces elliptical, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia limonia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
