Cineraria subdentata

Cineraria subdentata, commonly known as the subdentate cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
