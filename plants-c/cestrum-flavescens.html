Cestrum flavescens

Cestrum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
