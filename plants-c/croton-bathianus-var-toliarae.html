Croton bathianus var. toliarae

Croton bathianus var. toliarae, commonly known as the Toliara croton, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
