Casearia brevipes

Casearia brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
