Carpinus polyneura

Carpinus polyneura, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and smooth, dark-gray bark. The tree produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped nuts. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, turning yellow in autumn.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and tolerance to urban conditions.
