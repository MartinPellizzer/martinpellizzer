Carex divergens

Carex divergens, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Carex divergens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
