Campylia verbasciflora

Campylia verbasciflora, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
