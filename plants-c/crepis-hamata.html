Crepis hamata

Crepis hamata, commonly known as the bent grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Crepis hamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
