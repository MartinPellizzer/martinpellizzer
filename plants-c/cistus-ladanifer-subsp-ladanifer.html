Cistus ladanifer subsp. ladanifer

Cistus ladanifer subsp.

ladanifer, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces a resinous gum called ladanum, which has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
