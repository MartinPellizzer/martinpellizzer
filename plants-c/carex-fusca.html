Carex fusca

Carex fusca, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.

Carex fusca plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
