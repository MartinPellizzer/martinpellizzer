Crataegus × subsphaerica nothovar. csapodyae

Crataegus × subsphaerica nothovar. csapodyae, commonly known as the Csapodya hawthorn, is a hybrid plant characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to white flowers.

It typically produces small, red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
