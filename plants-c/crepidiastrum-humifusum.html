Crepidiastrum humifusum

Crepidiastrum humifusum, commonly known as the groundsel or yellow groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by pale green to reddish seed capsules. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a distinct central vein.
