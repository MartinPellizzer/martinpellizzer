Carex cirrhosa

Carex cirrhosa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, contributing to its success in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
