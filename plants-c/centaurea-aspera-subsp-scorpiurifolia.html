Centaurea aspera subsp. scorpiurifolia

Centaurea aspera subsp.

scorpiurifolia, commonly known as the Scorpion's Tail Centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often ornamental species in its native range.
