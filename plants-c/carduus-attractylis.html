Carduus attractylis

Carduus attractylis, commonly known as yellow cocklebur.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, but has been introduced to various parts of the world. The plant features a tall, branched stem with spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
