Carex echinata var. elata

Carex echinata var. elata, commonly known as the tall spiny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its tall, upright stems and dense, spiky inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
