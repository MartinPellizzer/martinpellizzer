Cyanotis concanensis

Cyanotis concanensis, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyanotis concanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
