Chuquiraga oppositifolia

Chuquiraga oppositifolia, commonly known as the Andean burr or velvet burr, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features dense, woolly foliage and produces small, spiky fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
