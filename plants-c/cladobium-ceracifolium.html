Cladobium ceracifolium

Cladobium ceracifolium, commonly known as the golden leaf plant, is a unique species native to certain arid regions.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or gold in color. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters or mats. Its foliage is adapted to reduce water loss, making it well-suited for dry environments.

The leaves are also known for their ability to reflect sunlight, which helps protect the plant from excessive heat.
