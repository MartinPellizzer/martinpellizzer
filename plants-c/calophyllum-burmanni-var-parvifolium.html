Calophyllum burmanni var. parvifolium

Calophyllum burmanni var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
