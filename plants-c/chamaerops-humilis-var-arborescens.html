Chamaerops humilis var. arborescens

Chamaerops humilis var. arborescens, commonly known as the desert palm or tree chamaerops, is a variant of the dwarf date palm.

It is a cold-hardy plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, tree-like form and clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates under favorable conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
