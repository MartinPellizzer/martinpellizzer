Celastrus rugosus

Celastrus rugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf staffvetch, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
