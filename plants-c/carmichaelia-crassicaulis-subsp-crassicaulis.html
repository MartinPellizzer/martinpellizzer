Carmichaelia crassicaulis subsp. crassicaulis

Carmichaelia crassicaulis subsp.

crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved Carmichaelia, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
