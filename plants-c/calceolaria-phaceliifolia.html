Calceolaria phaceliifolia, commonly known as the "cup-shaped flower" or "butterfly flower," is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is known for its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small cups. Calceolaria phaceliifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
