Calliandra orientalis

Calliandra orientalis, commonly known as the oriental calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with compound leaves that are often trifoliate. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calliandra orientalis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticultural settings.
