Cereus nyctago

Cereus nyctago, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is a member of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers typically bloom only once a year, making it a rare and striking sight.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
