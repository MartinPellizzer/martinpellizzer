Calanthe lyroglossa

Calanthe lyroglossa, commonly known as the lyre-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its tropical and subtropical habitats. Its flowers have a unique lyre-shaped lip, which is a distinguishing feature in the orchid family.

Calanthe lyroglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
