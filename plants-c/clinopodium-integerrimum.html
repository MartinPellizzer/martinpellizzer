Clinopodium integerrimum

Clinopodium integerrimum, commonly known as mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong mint-like fragrance. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink to purple in color.

Clinopodium integerrimum is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
