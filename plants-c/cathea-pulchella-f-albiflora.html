Cathea pulchella f. albiflora

Cathea pulchella f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cathea, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to hold water. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, enhancing its ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its unique appearance and is often used in tropical gardens or as a houseplant.
