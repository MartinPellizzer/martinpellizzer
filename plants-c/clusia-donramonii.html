Clusia donramonii

Clusia donramonii, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Clusia donramonii is also known for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
