Crucifera contracta

Crucifera contracta, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

This species is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
