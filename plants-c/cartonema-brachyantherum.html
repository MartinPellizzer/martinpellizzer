Cartonema brachyantherum

Cartonema brachyantherum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

Cartonema brachyantherum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
