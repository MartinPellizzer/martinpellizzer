Corymbia hamersleyana

Corymbia hamersleyana, commonly known as the Hamersley red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months.

The plant is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it an important component of the local flora.
