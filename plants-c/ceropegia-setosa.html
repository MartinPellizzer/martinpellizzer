Ceropegia setosa

Ceropegia setosa, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and it thrives in well-draining soil with moderate sunlight.

Ceropegia setosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
