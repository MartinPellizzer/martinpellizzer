Centaurea biebersteinii subsp. radoslavoffii

Centaurea biebersteinii subsp.

radoslavoffii, commonly known as the Radoslavoff's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
