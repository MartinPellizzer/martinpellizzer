Crepis runcinata var. andersonii

Crepis runcinata var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pale yellow to white in color.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
