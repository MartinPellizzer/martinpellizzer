Comanthosphace formosana

Comanthosphace formosana, commonly known as the Formosan ghost shrimp plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble the translucent bodies of ghost shrimp, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate light conditions. Its growth is often associated with specific substrate types, such as sandy or muddy bottoms.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for freshwater ecosystem studies.
