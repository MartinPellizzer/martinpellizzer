Combretum glomeruliflorum var. riparium

Combretum glomeruliflorum var. riparium, commonly known as the river combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are often borne along the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies, making it a common sight in riparian zones.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
