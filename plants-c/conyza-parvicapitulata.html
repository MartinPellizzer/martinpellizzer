Conyza parvicapitulata

Conyza parvicapitulata, commonly known as the small-headed groundsel, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale purple ray florets. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Conyza parvicapitulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
