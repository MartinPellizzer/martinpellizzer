Chamaecladon purpurascens

Chamaecladon purpurascens, commonly known as the red-stemmed chamaecladon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
