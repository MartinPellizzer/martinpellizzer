Cyrilla viscosa

Cyrilla viscosa, commonly known as rough cyrilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, sticky resin, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a small, dark purple berry that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
