Cyathea rubella

Cyathea rubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk that is often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea rubella thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
