Copiapoa paposoensis

Copiapoa paposoensis, commonly known as the Paposo cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick stem tissue.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
