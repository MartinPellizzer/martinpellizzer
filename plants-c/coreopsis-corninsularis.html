Coreopsis corninsularis

Coreopsis corninsularis, commonly known as the island coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
