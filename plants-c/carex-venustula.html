Carex venustula

Carex venustula, commonly known as the beautiful sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green, with a slightly rough texture. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to brown in color.

Carex venustula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
