Carex oederi f. argillacea

Carex oederi f. argillacea, commonly known as the clay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
