Chilita viridiflora

Chilita viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered chilita, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, with a distinctive dark green coloration.

Chilita viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
