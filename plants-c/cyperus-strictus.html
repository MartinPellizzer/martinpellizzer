Cyperus strictus

Cyperus strictus, commonly known as hardy flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Cyperus strictus is known for its ability to tolerate flooding and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
