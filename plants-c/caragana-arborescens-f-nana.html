Caragana arborescens f. nana

Caragana arborescens f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf shrub barony, is a low-growing, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, compact growth with slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming a mat-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and erosion control in arid landscapes.
