Calea sabazioides

Calea sabazioides, commonly known as the sacred candleplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are often white or pale yellow. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Calea sabazioides is also known for its traditional use in folk medicine.
