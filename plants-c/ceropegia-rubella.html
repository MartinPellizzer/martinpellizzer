Ceropegia rubella

Ceropegia rubella, commonly known as the red-veined string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive red or pinkish vein pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a unique, tubular shape. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is popular as a houseplant and in hanging baskets.
