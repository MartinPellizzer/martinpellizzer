Cassia roncadorensis

Cassia roncadorensis, commonly known as the Roncador Cassia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, angular seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Cassia roncadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
