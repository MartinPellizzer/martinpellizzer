Campomanesia crassifolia

Campomanesia crassifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
