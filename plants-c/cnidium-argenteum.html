Cnidium argenteum

Cnidium argenteum, commonly known as silver cnidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its rhizomes and roots are the primary parts used in traditional medicine.

Cnidium argenteum contains various bioactive compounds, including essential oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
