Cupressus lusitanica var. typica

Cupressus lusitanica var. typica, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its historical significance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
