Carex muricata var. chabertii

Carex muricata var. chabertii, commonly known as the Chabert's rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its dense growth habit makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
