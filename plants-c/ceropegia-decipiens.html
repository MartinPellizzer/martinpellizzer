Ceropegia decipiens

Ceropegia decipiens, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and often hairy stems. The plant produces unique flowers with a complex structure, featuring a corona that resembles a lion's mane, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Ceropegia decipiens is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
