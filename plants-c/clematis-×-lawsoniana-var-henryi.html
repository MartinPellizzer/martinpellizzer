Clematis × lawsoniana var. henryi

Clematis × lawsoniana var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
