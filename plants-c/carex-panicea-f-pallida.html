Carex panicea f. pallida

Carex panicea f. pallida, commonly known as the pale form of panicled sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale brown spikes of flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
