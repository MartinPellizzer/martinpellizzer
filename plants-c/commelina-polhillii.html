Commelina polhillii

Commelina polhillii, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers with white markings, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Commelina polhillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
