Calliandra bullata

Calliandra bullata, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
