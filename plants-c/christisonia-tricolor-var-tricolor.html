Christisonia tricolor var. tricolor

Christisonia tricolor var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful bracts that display three distinct hues, typically red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its showy, papery petals that resemble those of a hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
