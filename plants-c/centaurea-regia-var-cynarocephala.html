Centaurea regia var. cynarocephala

Centaurea regia var. cynarocephala, commonly known as the crown daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets, which resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
