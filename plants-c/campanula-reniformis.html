Campanula reniformis

Campanula reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It produces small, oval fruits called capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Campanula reniformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
