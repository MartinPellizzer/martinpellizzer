Cyrtochloa puser

Cyrtochloa puser, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and ecological landscaping. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Cyrtochloa puser thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
