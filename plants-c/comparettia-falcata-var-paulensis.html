Comparettia falcata var. paulensis

Comparettia falcata var. paulensis, commonly known as the Pauline's arrowleaf, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or orange. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vivid orange or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
