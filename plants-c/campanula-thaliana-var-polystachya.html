Campanula thaliana var. polystachya

Campanula thaliana var. polystachya, commonly known as the polystachya bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
