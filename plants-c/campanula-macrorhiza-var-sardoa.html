Campanula macrorhiza var. sardoa

Campanula macrorhiza var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to purple in color. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
