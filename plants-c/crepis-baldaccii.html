Crepis baldaccii

Crepis baldaccii, commonly known as the Baldaccii's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant has small, bract-like leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain alpine and coastal habitats.
