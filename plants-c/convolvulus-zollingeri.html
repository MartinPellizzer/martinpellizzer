Convolvulus zollingeri

Convolvulus zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
