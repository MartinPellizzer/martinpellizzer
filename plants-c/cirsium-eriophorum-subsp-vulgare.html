Cirsium eriophorum subsp. vulgare

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
