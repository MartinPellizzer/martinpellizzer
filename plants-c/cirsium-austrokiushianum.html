Cirsium austrokiushianum

Cirsium austrokiushianum, commonly known as the southern Korean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its structure includes a robust stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Cirsium austrokiushianum is often found in natural habitats such as forest edges and meadows, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
