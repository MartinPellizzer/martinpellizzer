Cinnamomum turfosum

Cinnamomum turfosum, commonly known as the swamp cinnamon or turfy cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum turfosum plays a role in local biodiversity and is valued for its unique ecological and cultural significance.
