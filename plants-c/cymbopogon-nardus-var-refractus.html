Cymbopogon nardus var. refractus

Cymbopogon nardus var. refractus, commonly known as patchouli, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers, which are clustered in spike-like formations. Its essential oil, extracted from the leaves, is widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy due to its distinctive earthy and spicy fragrance.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
