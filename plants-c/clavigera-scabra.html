Clavigera scabra

Clavigera scabra, commonly known as the rough-stemmed clavigera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rugged, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are elongated and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Clavigera scabra is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
