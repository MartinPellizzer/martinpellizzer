Coccinia sessilifolia

Coccinia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers, followed by edible, round fruits that are typically about 2-3 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

Coccinia sessilifolia is valued not only for its edible fruits but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
