Chionographis japonica var. yakusimensis

Chionographis japonica var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima silver pine, is a rare and ornamental evergreen conifer native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, frost-like appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, forming a broad, rounded canopy. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its unique aesthetic and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
