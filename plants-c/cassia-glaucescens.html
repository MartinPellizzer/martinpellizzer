Cassia glaucescens

Cassia glaucescens, commonly known as golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with numerous narrow, lance-shaped leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 10 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Cassia glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
