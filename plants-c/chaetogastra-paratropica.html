Chaetogastra paratropica

Chaetogastra paratropica, commonly known as the tropical thorned grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and sharp, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively in suitable environments.

Due to its robust nature, it is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain areas.
