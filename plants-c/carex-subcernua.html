Carex subcernua

Carex subcernua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are grouped in spikelets.

Carex subcernua plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
