Cirsium linkii

Cirsium linkii, commonly known as the Link's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium linkii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
