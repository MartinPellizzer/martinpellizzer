Crossandra mucronata

Crossandra mucronata, commonly known as the spiked flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Crossandra mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
