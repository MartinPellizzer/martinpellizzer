Cirsium × tirolense

Cirsium × tirolense, commonly known as the Alpine thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
