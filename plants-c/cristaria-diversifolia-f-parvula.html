Cristaria diversifolia f. parvula

Cristaria diversifolia f. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf cristaria, is a compact, low-growing succulent with distinctive, fleshy, crinkled leaves.

It originates from arid regions and is characterized by its ability to store water in its thick, waxy foliage. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
