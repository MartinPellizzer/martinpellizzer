Chrysophyllum carvalhoi

Chrysophyllum carvalhoi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single hard seed.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
