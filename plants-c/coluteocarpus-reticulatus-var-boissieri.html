Coluteocarpus reticulatus var. boissieri

Coluteocarpus reticulatus var. boissieri, commonly known as the Kashmiri cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing small, oval seeds. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
