Cryptocoryne annamica

Cryptocoryne annamica, commonly known as the Annam cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking reddish or brownish coloration, especially under low light conditions. This plant is typically found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cryptocoryne annamica is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
