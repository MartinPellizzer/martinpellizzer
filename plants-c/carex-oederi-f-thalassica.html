Carex oederi f. thalassica

Carex oederi f. thalassica, commonly known as the coastal sedge, is a variant of the species Carex oederi, characterized by its adaptation to coastal environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences containing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
