Cirsium bodinieri

Cirsium bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to sunny environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
