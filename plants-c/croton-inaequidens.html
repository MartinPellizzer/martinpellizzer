Croton inaequidens

Croton inaequidens, commonly known as the unequal-leaf croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that exhibit a wide variation in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The leaves often display vibrant colors, ranging from deep green to reddish hues, which enhance its ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative species in tropical gardens.
