Capparis heydeana

Capparis heydeana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds. The plant features small, white flowers with four petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are succulent, fleshy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The fruit develops from the flower and turns from green to orange-red as it matures.
