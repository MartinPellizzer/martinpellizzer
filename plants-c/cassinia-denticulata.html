Cassinia denticulata

Cassinia denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, giving it its scientific name.
