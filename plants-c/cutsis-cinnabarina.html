Cutsis cinnabarina

Cutsis cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
