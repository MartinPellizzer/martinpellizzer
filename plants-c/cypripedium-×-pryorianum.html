Cypripedium × pryorianum

Cypripedium × pryorianum, commonly known as the Siberian lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is typically pink or white in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
