Cupania mollis

Cupania mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible, sticky fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

Cupania mollis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
