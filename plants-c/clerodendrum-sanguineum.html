Clerodendrum sanguineum

Clerodendrum sanguineum, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clerodendrum sanguineum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
