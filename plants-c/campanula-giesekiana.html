Campanula giesekiana

Campanula giesekiana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Campanula giesekiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
