Calamagrostis silvatica var. pyramidalis

Calamagrostis silvatica var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the slender woodland reed, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, and upright growth habit, forming dense clumps that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish to brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
