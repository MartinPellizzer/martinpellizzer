Cyperus houghtonii var. uberior

Cyperus houghtonii var. uberior, commonly known as the Houghton's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has slender, triangular stems with small, scale-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale brown to yellowish flowers in spikes.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for various wildlife.
