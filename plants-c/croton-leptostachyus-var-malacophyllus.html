Croton leptostachyus var. malacophyllus

Croton leptostachyus var. malacophyllus, commonly known as the Malacophyllous Croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
