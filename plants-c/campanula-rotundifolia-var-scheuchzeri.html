Campanula rotundifolia var. scheuchzeri

Campanula rotundifolia var. scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of rounded, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
