Corymbia eremaea subsp. oligocarpa

Corymbia eremaea subsp.

oligocarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited ghost gum, is a distinctive Australian tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, round, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is typically found in the southern regions of Western Australia and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
