Carex schliebenii

Carex schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex schliebenii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
