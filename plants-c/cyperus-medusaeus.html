Cyperus medusaeus

Cyperus medusaeus, commonly known as the crown rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus medusaeus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
