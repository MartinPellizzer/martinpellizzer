Cucubalus quadrifidus

Cucubalus quadrifidus, commonly known as the four-lobed cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, edible fruits. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and small, lobed leaves.

It is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
