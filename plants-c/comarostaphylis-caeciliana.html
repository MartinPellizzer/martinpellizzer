Comarostaphylis caeciliana

Comarostaphylis caeciliana, commonly known as the Ceylon starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
