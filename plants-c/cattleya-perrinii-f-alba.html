Cattleya perrinii f. alba

Cattleya perrinii f. alba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid variety that originated from crosses between Cattleya perrinii and other Cattleya species.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a slight pinkish tinge at the base. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light. It has strap-like, dark green leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.
