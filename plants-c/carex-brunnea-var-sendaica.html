Carex brunnea var. sendaica

Carex brunnea var. sendaica, commonly known as the Sendaica sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
