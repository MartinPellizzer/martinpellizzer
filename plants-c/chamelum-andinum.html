Chamelum andinum

Chamelum andinum, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that open in the morning and close in the evening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Chamelum andinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
