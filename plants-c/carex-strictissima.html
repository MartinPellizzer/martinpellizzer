Carex strictissima

Carex strictissima, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow. The plant produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along water edges.

Carex strictissima is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
