Camellia japonica var. decumbens

Camellia japonica var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide year-round interest.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hardy plant suitable for shaded environments.
