Cyanococcus cuthbertii

Cyanococcus cuthbertii, commonly known as the Cuthbert's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its habitat.

Cyanococcus cuthbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal areas.
