Ceiba thonningii

Ceiba thonningii, commonly known as the African ceiba, is a towering tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
