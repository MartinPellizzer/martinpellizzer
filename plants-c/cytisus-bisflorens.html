Cytisus bisflorens

Cytisus bisflorens, commonly known as the two-flowered broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, typical of its Mediterranean climate. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
