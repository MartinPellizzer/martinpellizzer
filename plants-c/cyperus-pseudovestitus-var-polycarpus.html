Cyperus pseudovestitus var. polycarpus

Cyperus pseudovestitus var. polycarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and seasonally flooded areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
