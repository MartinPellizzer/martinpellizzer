Carex gayana var. rufa

Carex gayana var. rufa, commonly known as the red-fruited sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive reddish-brown to black spikelets that are often used in horticultural arrangements. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and landscape design.
