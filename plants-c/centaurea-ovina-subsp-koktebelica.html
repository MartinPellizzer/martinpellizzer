Centaurea ovina subsp. koktebelica

Centaurea ovina subsp.

koktebelica, commonly known as the Koktebel thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, with a somewhat succulent texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
