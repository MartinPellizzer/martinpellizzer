Cissus glandulosissima

Cissus glandulosissima, commonly known as the sticky cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a sticky texture. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species within the Cissus genus.
