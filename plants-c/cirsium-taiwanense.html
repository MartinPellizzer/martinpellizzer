Cirsium taiwanense

Cirsium taiwanense, commonly known as the Taiwan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for insects and birds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
