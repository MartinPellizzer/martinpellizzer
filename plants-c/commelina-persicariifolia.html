Commelina persicariifolia

Commelina persicariifolia, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Commelina persicariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
