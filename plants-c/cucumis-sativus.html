Cucumis sativus

Cucumis sativus, commonly known as the cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, palmately lobed leaves, and yellow flowers that develop into the familiar green, edible fruit. The fruit is typically harvested before it fully matures, when it is still tender and juicy. Cucumbers are rich in water, vitamins, and minerals, making them a popular vegetable in various cuisines.

They are widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and processing into products like pickles.
