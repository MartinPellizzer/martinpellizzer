Cyperus echinatus var. multiflorus

Cyperus echinatus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, ditches, and along waterways. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
