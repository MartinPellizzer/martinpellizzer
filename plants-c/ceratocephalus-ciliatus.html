Ceratocephalus ciliatus

Ceratocephalus ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped seed heads that release seeds when disturbed. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Ceratocephalus ciliatus is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.
