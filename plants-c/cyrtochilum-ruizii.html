Cyrtochilum ruizii

Cyrtochilum ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a spike. Cyrtochilum ruizii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
