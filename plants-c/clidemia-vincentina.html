Clidemia vincentina

Clidemia vincentina, commonly known as Vincent's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and typically grows on the branches of trees, drawing water and nutrients from its host. The plant has small, ovate leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact the health of its host plants if left unchecked.
