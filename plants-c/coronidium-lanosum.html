Coronidium lanosum

Coronidium lanosum, commonly known as the woolly crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a crown-like cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Coronidium lanosum is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
