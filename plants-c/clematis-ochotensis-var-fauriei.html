Clematis ochotensis var. fauriei

Clematis ochotensis var. fauriei, commonly known as the Siberian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Far East of Russia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a support structure for its vigorous growth.
