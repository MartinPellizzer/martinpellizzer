Canarium chevalieri

Canarium chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's candlenut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
