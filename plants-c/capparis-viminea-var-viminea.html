Capparis viminea var. viminea

Capparis viminea var. viminea, commonly known as the creeping cucumber or sweet flag, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes, producing a mild, sweet fragrance.

The plant is notable for its edible, fleshy fruits that contain a single, large, inedible seed.
