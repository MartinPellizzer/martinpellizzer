Colladonia angustifolia

Colladonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved colladonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
