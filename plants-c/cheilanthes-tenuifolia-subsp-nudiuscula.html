Cheilanthes tenuifolia subsp. nudiuscula

Cheilanthes tenuifolia subsp.

nudiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family. It is a perennial, evergreen fern that typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of hair. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a common sight in rocky landscapes across its native range.
