Clethra secundiflora

Clethra secundiflora, commonly known as the southern sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Clethra secundiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
