Centaurea pallescens var. iberica

Centaurea pallescens var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian bluemink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.
