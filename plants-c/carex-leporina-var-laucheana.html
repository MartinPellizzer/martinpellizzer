Carex leporina var. laucheana

Carex leporina var. laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, often shaded habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pale green to silver-white coloration.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in a loose, cylindrical shape, typical of many sedges.
