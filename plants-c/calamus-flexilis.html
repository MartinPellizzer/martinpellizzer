Calamus flexilis

Calamus flexilis, commonly known as the flexible reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by the development of hard, fibrous seeds. Its rhizomes are rich in aromatic compounds and have been historically used in perfumery and medicine.

The botanical features of Calamus flexilis make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in various cultural contexts.
