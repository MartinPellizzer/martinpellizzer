Corymbia latifolia

Corymbia latifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ghost gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and a spreading crown. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green color with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and clustered in umbels, producing a striking display during the warmer months.

The tree is known for its hard, durable wood and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
