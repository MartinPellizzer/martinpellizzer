Cannabis Sativa Var. Chinensis

Chinese hemp, scientifically known as Cannabis sativa var. chinensis, is a versatile plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine and agriculture. Native to East Asia, it has been cultivated for thousands of years for its fibers, seeds, and medicinal properties. The plant contains various compounds, including cannabinoids, which have sparked both scientific interest and legal debate. Despite its potential benefits, Chinese hemp remains a subject of controversy due to its association with cannabis. Its cultural and economic significance continues to shape its role in modern society.
