Caladenia longiclavata

Caladenia longiclavata, commonly known as the long-clawed spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are striking, with deep violet petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a spider's leg.

Caladenia longiclavata is known for its intricate pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
