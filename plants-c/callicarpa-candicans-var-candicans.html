Callicarpa candicans var. candicans

Callicarpa candicans var. candicans, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by vibrant red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly the striking color of its fruits. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract wildlife, such as birds, due to their high nutritional value.
