Calamagrostis minor

Calamagrostis minor, commonly known as narrowleaf graygrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with brown or purple.

Calamagrostis minor plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
