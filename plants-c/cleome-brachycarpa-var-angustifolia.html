Cleome brachycarpa var. angustifolia

Cleome brachycarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is part of the Cleome genus, which belongs to the Malvaceae family, known for its showy flowers and cotton-like seeds.
