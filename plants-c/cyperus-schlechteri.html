Cyperus schlechteri

Cyperus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. Its slender, triangular stems are covered in fine, scale-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
