Cynara cardunculus proles corsica

Cynara cardunculus proles corsica, commonly known as the Corsican artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a dense, edible bud that is harvested before it fully opens, similar to the common artichoke. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a temperate climate with moderate rainfall.

This variety is particularly valued for its unique flavor and culinary versatility.
