Conyza membranifolia

Conyza membranifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and finely divided leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Conyza membranifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
