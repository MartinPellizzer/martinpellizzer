Catananche arenaria var. dimorpha

Catananche arenaria var. dimorpha, commonly known as the sand catananche, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is often found in coastal and inland areas where it thrives in full sun.
