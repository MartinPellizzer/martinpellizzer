Cardamomum medium

Cardamomum medium, commonly known as the medium cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its fruit, a three-lobed capsule, contains aromatic seeds used extensively in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for its valuable spice.
