Centaurea alba subsp. sterilis

Centaurea alba subsp.

sterilis, commonly known as the white centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
