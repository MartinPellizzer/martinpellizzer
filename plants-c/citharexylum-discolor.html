Citharexylum discolor

Citharexylum discolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf or purpleleaf citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct purple or reddish tinge on the underside. Its fragrant flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
