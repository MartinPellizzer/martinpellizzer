Conoclinium grandiflorum

Conoclinium grandiflorum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Conoclinium grandiflorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
