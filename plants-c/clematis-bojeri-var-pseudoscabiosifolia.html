Clematis bojeri var. pseudoscabiosifolia

Clematis bojeri var. pseudoscabiosifolia, commonly known as the Bojer's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental value and can be grown as a climbing plant or in containers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to horticultural collections.
