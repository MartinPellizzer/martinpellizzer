Camellia marginata

Camellia marginata, commonly known as the white camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its elegant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. Camellia marginata is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for shaded garden environments and indoor cultivation.
