Cotoneaster Salicifolius Var. Henryanus

Henryan's cotoneaster, scientifically known as Cotoneaster salicifolius var. henryanus, is a unique variant of the cotoneaster genus native to the mountainous regions of China. This plant is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with partial shade. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it has gained attention among horticulturists and conservationists. However, its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it a rare and vulnerable species in the wild.
