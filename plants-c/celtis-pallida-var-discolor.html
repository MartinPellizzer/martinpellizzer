Celtis pallida var. discolor

Celtis pallida var. discolor, commonly known as the white ash or variegated ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
