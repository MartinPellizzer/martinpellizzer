Campanula ochroleuca var. silvatica

Campanula ochroleuca var. silvatica, commonly known as the silvery bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
