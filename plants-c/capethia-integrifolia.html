Capethia integrifolia

Capethia integrifolia, commonly known as the Cape Thornveld.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

The plant is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
