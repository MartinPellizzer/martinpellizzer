Chondrorhyncha inedita

Chondrorhyncha inedita, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, making it highly dependent on its environment. Its delicate structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered in many regions.
