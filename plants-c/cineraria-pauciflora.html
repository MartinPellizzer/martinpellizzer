Cineraria pauciflora

Cineraria pauciflora, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white and purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
