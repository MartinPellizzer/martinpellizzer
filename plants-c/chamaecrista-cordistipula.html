Chamaecrista cordistipula

Chamaecrista cordistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets that are often narrow and pointed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
