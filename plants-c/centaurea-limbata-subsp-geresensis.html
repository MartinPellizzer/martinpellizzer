Centaurea limbata subsp. geresensis

Centaurea limbata subsp.

geresensis, commonly known as the Geres mountain star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features erect stems with spiny leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in summer. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a distinctive capitulum with ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
