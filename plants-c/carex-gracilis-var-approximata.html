Carex gracilis var. approximata

Carex gracilis var. approximata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
