Cerastium lanigerum f. semiglabrum

Cerastium lanigerum f. semiglabrum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are white, five-petaled, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
