Cassia filipendula

Cassia filipendula, commonly known as the sicklepod cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
