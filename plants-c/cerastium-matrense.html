Cerastium matrense

Cerastium matrense, commonly known as the Matrenskaya chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs.

Cerastium matrense is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
