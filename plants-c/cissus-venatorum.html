Cissus venatorum

Cissus venatorum, commonly known as the climbing fig or rattan fig, is a vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in greenhouses and indoor gardens.
