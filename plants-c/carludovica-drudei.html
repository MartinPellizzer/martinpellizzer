Carludovica drudei

Carludovica drudei, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires caution.
