Corryocactus squarrosus

Corryocactus squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrosus cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines, providing insulation and protection from intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
