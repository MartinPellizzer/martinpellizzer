Crotalaria lukomae

Crotalaria lukomae, commonly known as the African cowpea or wild velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
