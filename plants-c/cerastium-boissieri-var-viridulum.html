Cerastium boissieri var. viridulum

Cerastium boissieri var. viridulum, commonly known as the green sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy plant for rock gardens and alpine displays.
