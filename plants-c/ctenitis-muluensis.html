Ctenitis muluensis

Ctenitis muluensis, commonly known as the African tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in favorable conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can remain on the plant for several years.
