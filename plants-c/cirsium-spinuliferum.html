Cirsium spinuliferum

Cirsium spinuliferum, commonly known as the spiked thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its fuzzy, silver-green leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are also armed with sharp spines.

Cirsium spinuliferum is often found in the wild and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
