Cerastium pallens var. ucrainicum

Cerastium pallens var. ucrainicum, commonly known as the Ukrainian mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Eastern Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in horticultural and ecological studies.
