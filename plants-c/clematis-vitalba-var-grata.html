Clematis vitalba var. grata

Clematis vitalba var. grata, commonly known as the sweet Clematis, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
