Capsicum frutescens var. baccatum

Capsicum frutescens var. baccatum, commonly known as the aji amarillo, is a species of chili pepper native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on maturity. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its spicy fruits used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The botanical features of this variety include its glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aroma.
