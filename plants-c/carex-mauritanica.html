Carex mauritanica

Carex mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex mauritanica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
