Carex pluvia var. koolauensis

Carex pluvia var. koolauensis, commonly known as the Koolauensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-like leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
