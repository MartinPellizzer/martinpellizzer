Chamaecrista lavradiiflora

Chamaecrista lavradiiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual element to its native landscape.
