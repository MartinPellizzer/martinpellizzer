Calamus nanus

Calamus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf calamus, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and North America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it reproduces both sexually and through rhizome propagation. Calamus nanus thrives in shallow water with moderate sunlight and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Its compact size and adaptability make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
