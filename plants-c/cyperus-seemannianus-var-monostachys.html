Cyperus seemannianus var. monostachys

Cyperus seemannianus var. monostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
