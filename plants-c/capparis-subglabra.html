Capparis subglabra

Capparis subglabra, commonly known as the smooth caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by edible, round, green berries. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Capparis subglabra is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
