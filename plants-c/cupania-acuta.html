Cupania acuta

Cupania acuta, commonly known as the sharpcup hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet, disturbed soils along stream banks and in swamps. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red, with a distinctive, sharp, spiny calyx. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Cupania acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
