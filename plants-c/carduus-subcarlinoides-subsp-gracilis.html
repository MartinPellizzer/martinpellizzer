Carduus subcarlinoides subsp. gracilis

Carduus subcarlinoides subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers.

The plant features spiny leaves and a distinctive, elongated fruit that contains seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a weed in agricultural settings.
