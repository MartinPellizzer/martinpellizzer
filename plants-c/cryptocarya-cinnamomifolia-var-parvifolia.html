Cryptocarya cinnamomifolia var. parvifolia

Cryptocarya cinnamomifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cinnamonwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries.

This variant is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
