Chamaeraphis minuta

Chamaeraphis minuta, commonly known as the dwarf glassworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in saline or coastal environments. The plant features tiny, green, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or reddish and bloom in the summer months.

Chamaeraphis minuta is adapted to thrive in harsh, salty soils and is often found in marshes, estuaries, and along shorelines.
