Cladium falcatum var. celebicum

Cladium falcatum var. celebicum, commonly known as the Celebes sawgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and robust rhizomes, which allow it to thrive in wetland and marsh environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are often used for thatching and basket weaving in local communities.

Cladium falcatum var. celebicum plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
