Cowellocassia domingensis

Cowellocassia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican Cassia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
