Carex setifolia var. setifolia

Carex setifolia var. setifolia, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, and it often forms dense colonies in its native habitat.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
