Centaurea boissieri var. beltranii

Centaurea boissieri var. beltranii, commonly known as the Beltran's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
