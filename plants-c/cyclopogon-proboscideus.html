Cyclopogon proboscideus

Cyclopogon proboscideus, commonly known as the elephant tusk orchid, is a striking bromeliad native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its elongated, tusk-like inflorescence that can grow up to 3 meters in length, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
