Coutarea hexandra f. albiflora

Coutarea hexandra f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and is known for its rapid growth rate. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The bark and leaves contain alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in pharmacological research.
