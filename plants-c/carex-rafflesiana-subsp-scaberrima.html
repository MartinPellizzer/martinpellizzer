Carex rafflesiana subsp. scaberrima

Carex rafflesiana subsp.

scaberrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, while its small, spike-like inflorescences contain tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
