Carlina corymbosa

Carlina corymbosa, commonly known as the meadow carline thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Carlina corymbosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
