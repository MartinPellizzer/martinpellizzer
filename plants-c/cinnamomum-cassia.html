Cinnamomum cassia

Cinnamomum cassia, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used to produce the spice cinnamon. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely cultivated for its bark, which is harvested and dried for use in cooking and traditional medicine.
