Cardiaca stachys

Cardiaca stachys, commonly known as heartworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their square stems and opposite leaves, often with a hairy texture. They typically produce small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. Cardiaca stachys species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
