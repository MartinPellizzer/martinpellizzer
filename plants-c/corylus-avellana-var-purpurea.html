Corylus avellana var. purpurea

Corylus avellana var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-husk hazelnut, is a cultivar of the hazel tree characterized by its distinctive purple bracts that encase the nuts.

This variety is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique nut husks. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The nuts are small, edible, and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
