Cenchrus perinvolucratus

Cenchrus perinvolucratus, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are often covered with silky hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cenchrus perinvolucratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
