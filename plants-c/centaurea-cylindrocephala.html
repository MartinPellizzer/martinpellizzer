Centaurea cylindrocephala

Centaurea cylindrocephala, commonly known as the cylindrical-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features erect, branching stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
