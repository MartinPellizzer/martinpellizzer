Centaurea torreana

Centaurea torreana, commonly known as the Torrean guinea flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright red to orange flowers, which are often surrounded by a ring of bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its compact, upright growth habit. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Centaurea torreana is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
