Clematis × diversifolia

Clematis × diversifolia, commonly known as the evergreen clematis, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various clematis species.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is evergreen, with dark green, leathery leaves that provide visual interest throughout the year.

Clematis × diversifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to different growing conditions.
