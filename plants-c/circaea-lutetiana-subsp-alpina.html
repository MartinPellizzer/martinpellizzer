Circaea lutetiana subsp. alpina

Circaea lutetiana subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine woodruff, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
