Camellia rosacea

Camellia rosacea, commonly known as the rose camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia rosacea is known for its winter blooming habit, adding color to gardens during the colder months.
