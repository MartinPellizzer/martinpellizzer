Chenopodium leptophyllum var. oblongifolium

Chenopodium leptophyllum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly salty, spinach-like flavor, making it a valuable food source in desert regions.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and high temperatures, which contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
