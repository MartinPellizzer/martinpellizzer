Chrozophora brocchiana var. hartmannii

Chrozophora brocchiana var. hartmannii, commonly known as Hartmann's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are followed by fluffy, disk-like seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and coastal habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
