Caladenia glossodia

Caladenia glossodia, commonly known as the Glossy Sun-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its single, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive glossy, greenish-yellow dorsal sepal. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, usually from December to February.

The species is notable for its intricate floral structure and is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
