Caylusea hexagyna

Caylusea hexagyna, commonly known as the six-sided cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Caylusea hexagyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
