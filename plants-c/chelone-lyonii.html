Chelone lyonii

Chelone lyonii, commonly known as the pink turtlehead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological importance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
