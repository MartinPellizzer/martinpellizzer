Coryanthes toulemondiana

Coryanthes toulemondiana, commonly known as the Touloumundo's coryanthe, is a striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, intricate flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, with a unique structure that includes a central column and modified leaves. The plant's flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a distinctive spur that extends from the base of the flower. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
