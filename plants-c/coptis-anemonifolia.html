Coptis anemonifolia

Coptis anemonifolia, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, resembling those of the buttercup. The plant has palmately divided, bright green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Coptis anemonifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains bioactive compounds such as berberine.
