Clematis tomentella

Clematis tomentella, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
