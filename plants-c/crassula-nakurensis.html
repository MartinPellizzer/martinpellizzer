Crassula nakurensis

Crassula nakurensis, commonly known as the Nakurensis Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
