Cytisus spinescens subsp. ala-venti

Cytisus spinescens subsp.

ala-venti, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a shrub native to the western coast of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
