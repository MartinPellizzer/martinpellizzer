Ceanothus azureus var. parvifolius

Ceanothus azureus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the Bluecurls ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
