Campanula phyctidocalyx

Campanula phyctidocalyx, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
