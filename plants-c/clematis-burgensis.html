Clematis burgensis

Clematis burgensis, commonly known as the Burgundy clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbers.

Its historical use in traditional medicine adds to its botanical significance.
