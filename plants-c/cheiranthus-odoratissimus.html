Cheiranthus odoratissimus

Cheiranthus odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent, especially noticeable when touched. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
