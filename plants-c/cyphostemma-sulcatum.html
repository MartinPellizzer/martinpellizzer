Cyphostemma sulcatum

Cyphostemma sulcatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and its distinctive, waxy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Cyphostemma sulcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
