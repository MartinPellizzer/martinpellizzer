Crataegus pedicellata var. sertata

Crataegus pedicellata var. sertata, commonly known as the thorny hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, which are often used for hedging or ornamental purposes. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.
