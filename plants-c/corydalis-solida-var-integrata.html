Corydalis solida var. integrata

Corydalis solida var. integrata, commonly known as the purple corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces distinctive, two-lobed seed pods that mature in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a basal rosette.

Corydalis solida var. integrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in woodland or shady garden settings.
