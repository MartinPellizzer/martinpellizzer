Cystopteris fragilis subsp. canariensis

Cystopteris fragilis subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands brittle fern, is a fern species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically bipinnate, with a finely divided appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are usually light green and have a fragile texture, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
