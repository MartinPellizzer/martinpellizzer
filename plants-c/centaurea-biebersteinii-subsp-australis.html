Centaurea biebersteinii subsp. australis

Centaurea biebersteinii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
