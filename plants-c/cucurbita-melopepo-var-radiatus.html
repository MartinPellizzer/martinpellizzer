Cucurbita melopepo var. radiatus

Cucurbita melopepo var. radiatus, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the Americas, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits called "tunas.". Its leaves are typically spine-tipped and adapted for reducing water loss.

The plant has been used for food, medicine, and as a source of natural dyes by indigenous peoples for centuries.
