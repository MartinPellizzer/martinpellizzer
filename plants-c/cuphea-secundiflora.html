Cuphea secundiflora

Cuphea secundiflora, commonly known as the red hot poker or firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Cuphea secundiflora is also valued for its ability to add a bold visual impact to garden designs.
