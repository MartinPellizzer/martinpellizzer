Coleus shirensis

Coleus shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus shirensis prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
