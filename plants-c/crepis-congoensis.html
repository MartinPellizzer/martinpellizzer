Crepis congoensis

Crepis congoensis, commonly known as the Congo mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by achene fruits.

Crepis congoensis is known for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
