Chironia centaurium var. ramosissima

Chironia centaurium var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
