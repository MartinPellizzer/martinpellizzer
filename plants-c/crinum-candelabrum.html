Crinum candelabrum

Crinum candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces tall, unbranched stems topped with clusters of large, showy white or pale purple flowers. The leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its strong fragrance, which is especially noticeable in the evening.
