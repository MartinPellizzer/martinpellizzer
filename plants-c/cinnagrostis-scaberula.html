Cinnagrostis scaberula

Cinnagrostis scaberula, commonly known as rough bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to North America.

It features narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Cinnagrostis scaberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
