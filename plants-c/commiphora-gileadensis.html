Commiphora gileadensis

Commiphora gileadensis, commonly known as the Gilead myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.
