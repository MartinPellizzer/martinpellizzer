Calliandra virgata

Calliandra virgata, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
