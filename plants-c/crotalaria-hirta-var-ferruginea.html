Crotalaria hirta var. ferruginea

Crotalaria hirta var. ferruginea, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria or rusty crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the pods are elongated and contain several seeds.

This species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a potential plant for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
