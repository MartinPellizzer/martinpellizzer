Carduncellus gracilis

Carduncellus gracilis, commonly known as the slender cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a tall, sturdy stem and deeply lobed foliage. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
