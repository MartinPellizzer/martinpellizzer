Conophytum pellucidum subsp. cupreatum

Conophytum pellucidum subsp.

cupreatum, commonly known as the "Cupreata" or "Cupreous Cactus," is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
