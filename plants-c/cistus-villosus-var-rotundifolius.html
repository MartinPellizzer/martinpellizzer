Cistus villosus var. rotundifolius

Cistus villosus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and showy pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and coastal environments. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
