Catasetum × violascens

Catasetum × violascens, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific bee species, showcasing a unique mutualistic relationship.

Botanically, it belongs to the Catasetum genus, known for its specialized pollination mechanisms and ornamental value.
