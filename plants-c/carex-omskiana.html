Carex omskiana

Carex omskiana, commonly known as the Omsk sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Carex omskiana is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
