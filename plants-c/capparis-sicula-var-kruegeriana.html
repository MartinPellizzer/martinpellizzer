Capparis sicula var. kruegeriana

Capparis sicula var. kruegeriana, commonly known as the Krueger's caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round, greenish fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
