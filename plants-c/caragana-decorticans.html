Caragana decorticans

Caragana decorticans, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, including sandy soils and arid climates.

Caragana decorticans is often used for erosion control due to its deep root system and hardy nature.
