Convolvulus × turcicus nothosubsp.

turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish bindweed, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species. It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
