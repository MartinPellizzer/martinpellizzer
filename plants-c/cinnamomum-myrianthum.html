Cinnamomum myrianthum

Cinnamomum myrianthum, commonly known as the African cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, green berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a fragrant, slightly citrusy scent.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
