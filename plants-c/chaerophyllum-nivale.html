Chaerophyllum nivale

Chaerophyllum nivale, commonly known as snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Chaerophyllum nivale is known for its early blooming habit, making it a symbol of the arrival of spring.
