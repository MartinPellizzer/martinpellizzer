Chenopodium foetidum subvar. resediforme

Chenopodium foetidum subvar. resediforme, commonly known as skunkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its reddish, hairy stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in wetland areas or along roadsides. It has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is sometimes considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
