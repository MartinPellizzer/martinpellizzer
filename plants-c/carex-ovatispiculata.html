Carex ovatispiculata

Carex ovatispiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex ovatispiculata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
