Crotalaria globifera var. congolensis

Crotalaria globifera var. congolensis, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow with prominent red or orange spurs, making it visually striking and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
