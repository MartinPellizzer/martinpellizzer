Copaiba multijuga

Copaiba multijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its thick, aromatic resinous bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its latex, often referred to as "copaiba oil," is valued for its medicinal and industrial uses.

The tree plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
