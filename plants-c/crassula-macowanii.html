Crassula macowanii

Crassula macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
