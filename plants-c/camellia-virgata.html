Camellia virgata

Camellia virgata, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting red or yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Camellia virgata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its edible seeds, which can be pressed into oil.
