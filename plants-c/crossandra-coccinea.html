Crossandra coccinea

Crossandra coccinea, commonly known as the red flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Crossandra coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
