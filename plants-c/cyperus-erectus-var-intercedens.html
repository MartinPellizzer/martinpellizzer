Cyperus erectus var. intercedens

Cyperus erectus var. intercedens, commonly known as the upright flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus erectus var. intercedens is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
