Chenopodium candolleanum subsp. argenteum

Chenopodium candolleanum subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silver goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine, dense hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
