Chenopodium diversifolium

Chenopodium diversifolium, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes and serrated edges. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Chenopodium diversifolium is often considered a weed but has been used historically for its edible leaves and seeds.
