Cocculus erectus

Cocculus erectus, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spiny, orange berries. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
