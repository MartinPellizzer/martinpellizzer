Cassia micrantha

Cassia micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, slender pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia micrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it an important species in horticulture and herbal practices.
