Crepis sorocephala

Crepis sorocephala, commonly known as the clustered hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Crepis sorocephala is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, daisy-like appearance.
