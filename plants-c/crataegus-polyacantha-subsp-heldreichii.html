Crataegus polyacantha subsp. heldreichii

Crataegus polyacantha subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
