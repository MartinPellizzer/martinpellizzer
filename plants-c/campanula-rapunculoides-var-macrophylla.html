Campanula rapunculoides var. macrophylla

Campanula rapunculoides var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often tinged with purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
