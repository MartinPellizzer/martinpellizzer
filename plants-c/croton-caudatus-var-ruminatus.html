Croton caudatus var. ruminatus

Croton caudatus var. ruminatus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and branching structure. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough, textured surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
