Crotalaria longifolia

Crotalaria longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a protein-rich food source. Crotalaria longifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
