Campanula macrorhiza subsp. aitanica

Campanula macrorhiza subsp.

aitanica, commonly known as the large-rooted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
