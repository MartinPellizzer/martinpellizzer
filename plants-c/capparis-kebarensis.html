Capparis kebarensis

Capparis kebarensis, commonly known as the Kebane caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to the dry, coastal environments of its native habitat.

Capparis kebarensis is notable for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
