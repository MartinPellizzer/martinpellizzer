Cynoglossum montanum subvar. natolicum

Cynoglossum montanum subvar. natolicum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
