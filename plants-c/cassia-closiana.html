Cassia closiana

Cassia closiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble shower-like cascades. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Cassia closiana is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
