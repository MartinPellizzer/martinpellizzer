Chusquea barbata

Chusquea barbata, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Chusquea barbata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
