Cnidoscolus tepiquensis

Cnidoscolus tepiquensis, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible parts, it is both cultivated and studied for its botanical and agricultural significance.
