Carex jovis

Carex jovis, commonly known as the Jovis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges, rushes, and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

Carex jovis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
