Cardamomum latifolium

Cardamomum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces aromatic, reddish-brown pods containing aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valued species in both traditional medicine and the spice trade.
