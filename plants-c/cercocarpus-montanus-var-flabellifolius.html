Cercocarpus montanus var. flabellifolius

Cercocarpus montanus var. flabellifolius, commonly known as the flabellifolius curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are often curled or twisted. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its distinctive foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in arid landscapes.
