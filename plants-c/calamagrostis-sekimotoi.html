Calamagrostis sekimotoi

Calamagrostis sekimotoi, commonly known as Sekimoto's reed, is a grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams. It produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that add ornamental value to gardens.

Calamagrostis sekimotoi is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
