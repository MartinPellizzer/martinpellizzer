Cestrum lambertii

Cestrum lambertii, commonly known as the Lambert's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum lambertii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
