Canarium engleri

Canarium engleri, commonly known as the Philippine canarium, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic uses.
