Cucurbita hispida

Cucurbita hispida, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy, ribbed stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to fruits with a tough, spiny rind and a sweet, edible flesh. Cucurbita hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional cooking.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
