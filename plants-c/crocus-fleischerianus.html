Crocus fleischerianus

Crocus fleischerianus, commonly known as the Fleischer's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with six petals and a distinct, narrow style. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
