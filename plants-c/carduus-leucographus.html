Carduus leucographus

Carduus leucographus, commonly known as the white-stemmed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive white or pale-colored vein pattern.

The flowers are composed of ray florets and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
