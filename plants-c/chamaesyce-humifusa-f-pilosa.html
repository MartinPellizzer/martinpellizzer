Chamaesyce humifusa f. pilosa

Chamaesyce humifusa f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy prostrate spurge, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is notable for its dense foliage and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
