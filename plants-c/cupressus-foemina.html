Cupressus foemina

Cupressus foemina, commonly known as the Mexican lacebark cypress, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and distinctive, flaky, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The plant has scale-like leaves that are densely packed along the branches, giving it a dense, compact appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cupressus foemina prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant in suitable climates.
