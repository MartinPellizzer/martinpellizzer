Chlorophytum brachystachyum

Chlorophytum brachystachyum, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or white midrib. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, grass-like flowers on long, slender stems, which are usually white or pale blue in color.

This plant is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
