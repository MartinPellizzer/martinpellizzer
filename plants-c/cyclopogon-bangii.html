Cyclopogon bangii

Cyclopogon bangii, commonly known as the Bangi grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyclopogon bangii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
