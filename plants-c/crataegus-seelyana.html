Crataegus seelyana

Crataegus seelyana, commonly known as the Seely's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Crataegus seelyana is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
