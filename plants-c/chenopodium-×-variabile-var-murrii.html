Chenopodium × variabile var. murrii

Chenopodium × variabile var. murrii, commonly known as Buffalo bur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, prickly leaves and distinctive, spiny seed heads that resemble small burs. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fence rows. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers.

The species is known for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in many regions.
