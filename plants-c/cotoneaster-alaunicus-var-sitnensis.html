Cotoneaster alaunicus var. sitnensis

Cotoneaster alaunicus var. sitnensis, commonly known as the Sitnian cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a slightly serrated margin, and it blooms with clusters of white or pink flowers in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
