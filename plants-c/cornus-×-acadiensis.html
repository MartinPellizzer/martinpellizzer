Cornus × acadiensis

Cornus × acadiensis, commonly known as the Canadian dogwood, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy white or pink bracts that appear in early spring, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Cornus × acadiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
