Carex smalliana

Carex smalliana, commonly known as the small-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact inflorescences. Native to parts of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
