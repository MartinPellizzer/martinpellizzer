Cistanche tubulosa var. tomentosa

Cistanche tubulosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose苁蓉, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of various shrubs and trees. The plant is characterized by its yellowish or reddish stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces small, pale flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, where it is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
