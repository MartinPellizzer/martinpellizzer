Columnea lanata

Columnea lanata, commonly known as the velvety mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Columnea lanata is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking floral display and unique foliage.
