Calystegia hederacea var. pentapetala

Calystegia hederacea var. pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in late summer. This variety is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.

It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
