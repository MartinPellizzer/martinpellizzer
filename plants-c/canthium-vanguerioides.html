Canthium vanguerioides

Canthium vanguerioides, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
