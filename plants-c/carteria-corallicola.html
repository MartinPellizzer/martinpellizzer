Carteria corallicola

Carteria corallicola, commonly known as the coral-like cartilage plant, is a unique succulent native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, branching stems that resemble coral in appearance, hence its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
