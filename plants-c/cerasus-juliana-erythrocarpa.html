Cerasus juliana erythrocarpa

Cerasus juliana erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive red fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
