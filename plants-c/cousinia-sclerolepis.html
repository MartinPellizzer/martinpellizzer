Cousinia sclerolepis

Cousinia sclerolepis, commonly known as the woolly cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia sclerolepis is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
