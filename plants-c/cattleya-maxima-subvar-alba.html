Cattleya maxima subvar. alba

Cattleya maxima subvar. alba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements and ornamental purposes.
