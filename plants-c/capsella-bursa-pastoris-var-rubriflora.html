Capsella bursa-pastoris var. rubriflora

Capsella bursa-pastoris var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered common chickweed, is a variant of the common weed Capsella bursa-pastoris.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which contrast with the typical white or pink blooms of the standard variety. The plant is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and easily identifiable member of the Brassicaceae family.
