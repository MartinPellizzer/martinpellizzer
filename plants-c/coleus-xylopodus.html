Coleus xylopodus

Coleus xylopodus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Coleus xylopodus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
