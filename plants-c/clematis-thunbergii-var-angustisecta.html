Clematis thunbergii var. angustisecta

Clematis thunbergii var. angustisecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, making it an excellent choice for trellises or support structures. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
