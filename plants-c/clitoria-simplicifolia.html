Clitoria simplicifolia

Clitoria simplicifolia, commonly known as the winged bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers, which are typically violet or blue in color. The plant produces small, edible beans that are rich in protein and nutrients. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

Clitoria simplicifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes grown as a shade plant in gardens.
