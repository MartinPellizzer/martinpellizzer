Clusia amaraliana

Clusia amaraliana, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Clusia amaraliana is also known for its resilience and adaptability to various soil types.
