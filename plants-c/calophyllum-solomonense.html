Calophyllum solomonense

Calophyllum solomonense, commonly known as Solomon's candlewood, is a tropical tree native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
