Crotalaria orientalis

Crotalaria orientalis, commonly known as the oriental spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent spurred lobes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Crotalaria orientalis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
