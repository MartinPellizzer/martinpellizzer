Calamus binnendijkii

Calamus binnendijkii, commonly known as the Binnendijk's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by hard, spiny fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some local communities.
