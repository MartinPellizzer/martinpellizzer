Carex × petrae-fu

Carex × petrae-fu, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant is typically found in moist, rocky habitats, such as stream banks and mountainous regions. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
