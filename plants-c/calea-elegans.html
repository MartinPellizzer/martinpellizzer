Calea elegans

Calea elegans, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features striking, velvety purple flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Calea elegans is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
