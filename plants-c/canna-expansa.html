Canna expansa

Canna expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces showy, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Canna expansa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
