Cornus × slavinii

Cornus × slavinii, commonly known as the Slavinii dogwood, is a hybrid shrub that results from the cross between Cornus alba and Cornus sanguinea.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that remain colorful throughout the winter, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color.

Cornus × slavinii is hardy in USDA zones 3-7 and is relatively disease-resistant.
