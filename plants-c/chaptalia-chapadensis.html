Chaptalia chapadensis

Chaptalia chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Chaptalia chapadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
