Cordyline calocoma f. lineata

Cordyline calocoma f. lineata, commonly known as the Variegated Cabbage Tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, sword-shaped leaves that display a distinctive variegation of green, white, and pink. The foliage often has a waxy texture and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a dramatic addition to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add vertical interest to landscapes.
