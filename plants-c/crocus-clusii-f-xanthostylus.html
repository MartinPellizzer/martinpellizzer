Crocus clusii f. xanthostylus

Crocus clusii f. xanthostylus, commonly known as the yellow-styled crocus, is a botanical variant of the Crocus clusii species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow stigmas that stand out against the typical purple or white petals. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape during the colder months.
