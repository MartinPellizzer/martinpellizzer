Callista convexa

Callista convexa, commonly known as the warty lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Its bulbs are large and store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Callista convexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens and containers.
