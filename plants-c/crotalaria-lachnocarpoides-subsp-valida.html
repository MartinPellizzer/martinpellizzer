Crotalaria lachnocarpoides subsp. valida

Crotalaria lachnocarpoides subsp.

valida, commonly known as the golden crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
