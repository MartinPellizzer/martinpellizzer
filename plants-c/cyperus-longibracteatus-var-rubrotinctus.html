Cyperus longibracteatus var. rubrotinctus

Cyperus longibracteatus var. rubrotinctus, commonly known as the red-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to reddish in color. The plant is characterized by its reddish tinge in the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
