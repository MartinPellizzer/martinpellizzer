Cleistes triflora

Cleistes triflora, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Americas, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, smooth, and broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cleistes triflora is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as swamps and woodlands.
