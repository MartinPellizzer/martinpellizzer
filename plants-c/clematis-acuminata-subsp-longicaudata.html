Clematis acuminata subsp. longicaudata

Clematis acuminata subsp.

longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated margin.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a climbing plant in gardens.
