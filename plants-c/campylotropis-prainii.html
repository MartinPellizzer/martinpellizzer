Campylotropis prainii

Campylotropis prainii, commonly known as the Prain's false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
