Cousinia elata

Cousinia elata, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often woolly or hairy, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Cousinia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
