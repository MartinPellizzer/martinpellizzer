Chenopodium album subf. cymigerum

Chenopodium album subf.

cymigerum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters called cymes, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its seeds are small, smooth, and yellowish, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
