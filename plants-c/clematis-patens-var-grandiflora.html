Clematis patens var. grandiflora

Clematis patens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and can grow up to 15 feet in length, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
