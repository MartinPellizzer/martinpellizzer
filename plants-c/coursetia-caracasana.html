Coursetia caracasana

Coursetia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas coursetia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Coursetia caracasana is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
