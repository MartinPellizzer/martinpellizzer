Cerastium mutabile var. lanatum

Cerastium mutabile var. lanatum, commonly known as the downy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features prostrate stems covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
