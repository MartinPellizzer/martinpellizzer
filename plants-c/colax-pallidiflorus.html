Colax pallidiflorus

Colax pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered colax, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four to five petals, and are arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
