Camphora sieboldii

Camphora sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 ( Lauraceae ) family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Its essential oil, camphor, is extracted from the tree's heartwood and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
