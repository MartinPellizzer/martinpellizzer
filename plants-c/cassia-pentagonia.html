Cassia pentagonia

Cassia pentagonia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically five to seven leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Cassia pentagonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its vibrant floral display.
