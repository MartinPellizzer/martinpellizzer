Cheirodendron dominii

Cheirodendron dominii, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which has historically been used in furniture and construction.
