Cydista diversifolia

Cydista diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse-leaved cydista, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves that exhibit a wide range of variegation, often with white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
