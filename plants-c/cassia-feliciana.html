Cassia feliciana

Cassia feliciana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery inflorescences that produce clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia feliciana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
