Cereus hyalacanthus

Cereus hyalacanthus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a striking cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habits make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
