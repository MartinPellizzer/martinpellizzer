Centaurea garganica

Centaurea garganica, commonly known as the Garganica thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its inflorescence is a dense cluster of small, showy florets.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
