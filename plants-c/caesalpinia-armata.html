Caesalpinia armata

Caesalpinia armata, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Caesalpinia armata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
