Cassia abbreviata var. glabrifructifera

Cassia abbreviata var. glabrifructifera, commonly known as the smooth-fruited cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces smooth, cylindrical pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
