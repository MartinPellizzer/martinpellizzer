Columbia javanica

Columbia javanica, commonly known as the Javanese orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features strap-like leaves and a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Columbia javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
