Carex viridula subsp. pulchella

Carex viridula subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
