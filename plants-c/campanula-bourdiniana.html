Campanula bourdiniana

Campanula bourdiniana, commonly known as the Bourdin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows in rocky or dry soils. It is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Campanula bourdiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
