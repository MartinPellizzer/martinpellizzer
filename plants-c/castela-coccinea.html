Castela coccinea

Castela coccinea, commonly known as the red castella or red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Castela coccinea is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking color and drought tolerance.
