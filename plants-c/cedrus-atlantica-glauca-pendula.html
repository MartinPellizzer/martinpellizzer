Cedrus atlantica glauca-pendula

Cedrus atlantica glauca-pendula, commonly known as the blue pendulum cedar, is a rare and ornamental variety of the Atlas cedar.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and pendulous branches that create a cascading effect. Native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun. Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for small gardens or as a specimen plant.

The foliage retains its color throughout the year, offering year-round visual interest.
