Cakile maritima var. aegyptica

Cakile maritima var. aegyptica, commonly known as the Egyptian sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

It is an important part of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
