Clematis wangiana

Clematis wangiana, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
