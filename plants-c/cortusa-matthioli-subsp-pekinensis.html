Cortusa matthioli subsp. pekinensis

Cortusa matthioli subsp.

pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly foliage.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
