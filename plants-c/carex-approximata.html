Carex approximata

Carex approximata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex approximata produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are followed by triangular, three-sided berries.
