Clematis viornoides

Clematis viornoides, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive, sweetly scented fragrance. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis viornoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vigorous climbing habit.
