Calycopteris joan

Calycopteris joan, commonly known as the Joan's feather grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Calycopteris joan is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
