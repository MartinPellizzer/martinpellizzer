Calamagrostis albescens

Calamagrostis albescens, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive silvery-white panicles that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to landscapes.

Silver bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
