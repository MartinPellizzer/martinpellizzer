Cyrtochilum crispatissimum

Cyrtochilum crispatissimum, commonly known as the crisped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a compact, rhizomatous growth habit with a single, upright stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. Its flowers have a unique structure with prominent, crisped sepals and petals, giving it its common name.

Cyrtochilum crispatissimum thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
