Calamagrostis caucasica var. kubanensis

Calamagrostis caucasica var. kubanensis, commonly known as Kuban reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
