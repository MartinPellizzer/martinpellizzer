Centaurea preissmannii

Centaurea preissmannii, commonly known as the Preissmann's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea preissmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
