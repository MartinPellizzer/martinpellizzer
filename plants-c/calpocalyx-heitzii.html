Calpocalyx heitzii

Calpocalyx heitzii, commonly known as the Zambesi bean, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with stipules and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in savanna habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it useful for both ornamental purposes and as a source of leguminous protein.
