Campanula cephalotes var. canescens

Campanula cephalotes var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine, silvery-white down, giving the plant its "woolly" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
