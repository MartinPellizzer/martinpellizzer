Carex platyrhyncha

Carex platyrhyncha, commonly known as the broad-beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces three-sided, triangular seed capsules.

Carex platyrhyncha is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
