Chaetostoma debile

Chaetostoma debile, commonly known as the slender-leaf water plantain, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to its aquatic environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the muddy substrates of its habitat.

Chaetostoma debile plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
