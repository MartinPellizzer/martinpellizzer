Cymbidium × cooperi

Cymbidium × cooperi, commonly known as the Orchid Tree, is a hybrid orchid species that combines the characteristics of Cymbidium and Dendrobium.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is known for its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The Orchid Tree is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
