Ceranthera dentata

Ceranthera dentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, toothed leaves and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique structure that resembles a bird's beak.

Ceranthera dentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
