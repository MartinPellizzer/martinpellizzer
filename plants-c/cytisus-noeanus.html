Cytisus noeanus

Cytisus noeanus, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus noeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils in full sun.
