Campanula pusilla var. bellardii

Campanula pusilla var. bellardii, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its low-growing habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens with limited space.
