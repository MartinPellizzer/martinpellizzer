Calopogon tuberosus var. latifolius

Calopogon tuberosus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bog onion, is a rare and delicate wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils of pine flatwoods and wetlands, often in shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, tuberous roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many areas and is protected in several states.
