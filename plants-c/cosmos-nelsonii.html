Cosmos nelsonii

Cosmos nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy, pink to red blooms that appear in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cosmos nelsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
