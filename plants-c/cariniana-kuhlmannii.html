Cariniana kuhlmannii

Cariniana kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or *Cerrado* chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits that are important for both wildlife and local communities. It plays a vital role in the ecological balance of the Cerrado biome, providing habitat and food for various species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
