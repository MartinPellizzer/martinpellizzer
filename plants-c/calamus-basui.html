Calamus basui

Calamus basui, commonly known as the broadleaf calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spadices, typical of the palm family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local cultures.
