Cochlearia sinuata var. qianwuensis

Cochlearia sinuata var. qianwuensis, commonly known as the Qianwu coastal carnation, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of China.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, tolerating salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are lobed and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.
