Cleomella multicaulis

Cleomella multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered cleom, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cleomella multicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
