Curcuma stolonifera

Curcuma stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers and has fleshy, aromatic rhizomes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture.

The rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.
