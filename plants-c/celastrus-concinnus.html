Celastrus concinnus

Celastrus concinnus, commonly known as the red spiderling, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright orange berries that are attractive to birds. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs.

The species is valued for its ornamental berries and is often used in native plant gardens.
