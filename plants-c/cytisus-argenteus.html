Cytisus argenteus

Cytisus argenteus, commonly known as silver bush broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus argenteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
