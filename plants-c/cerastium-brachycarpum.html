Cerastium brachycarpum

Cerastium brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-fruited mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features small, hairy, oval leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
