Cyrtochilum aciculatum

Cyrtochilum aciculatum, commonly known as the needle orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Cyrtochilum aciculatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
