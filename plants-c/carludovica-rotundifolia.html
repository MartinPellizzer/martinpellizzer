Carludovica rotundifolia

Carludovica rotundifolia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports and structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical settings.
