Clematis puberula

Clematis puberula, commonly known as the downy clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Clematis puberula thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.
