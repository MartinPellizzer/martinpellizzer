Cistus × dansereaui f. substellatus

Cistus × dansereaui f. substellatus, commonly known as the starry rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, often with a distinctive star-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
