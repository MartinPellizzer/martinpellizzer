Centaurea arbutifolia

Centaurea arbutifolia, commonly known as the strawberry tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its fruit is a small, round capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Centaurea arbutifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
