Corallodendron

Corallodendron, commonly known as the coral tree, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Bignoniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, and the genus includes several species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

Corallodendron species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
