Cinnamomum scortechinii

Cinnamomum scortechinii, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon or Japanese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a fragrant, slightly waxy texture.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
