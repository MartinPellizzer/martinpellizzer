Calamagrostis littorea var. annulata

Calamagrostis littorea var. annulata, commonly known as the coastal silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and slender, tufted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in moist, saline soils and is often found in salt marshes and tidal areas. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and wetland restoration projects.

The plant's ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
