Cirsium halacsyi

Cirsium halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Cirsium halacsyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
