Campomanesia guazumifolia var. grisea

Campomanesia guazumifolia var. grisea, commonly known as the gray-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive grayish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
