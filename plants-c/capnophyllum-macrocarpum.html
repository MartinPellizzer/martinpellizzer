Capnophyllum macrocarpum

Capnophyllum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited capno plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Capnoidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy and contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions.
