Canistrum aurantiacum

Canistrum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange fig.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
