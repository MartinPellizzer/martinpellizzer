Cynanchum odoratissimum

Cynanchum odoratissimum, commonly known as the fragrant swallowworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
