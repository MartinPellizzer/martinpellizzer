Cissus modesta

Cissus modesta, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
