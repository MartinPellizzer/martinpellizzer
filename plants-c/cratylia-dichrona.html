Cratylia dichrona

Cratylia dichrona, commonly known as the twin-fruit cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive pods that contain two seeds each, giving it its common name. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and forage value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
