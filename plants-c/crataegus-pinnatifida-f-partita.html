Crataegus pinnatifida f. partita

Crataegus pinnatifida f. partita, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnately compound leaves, which give it its specific epithet "pinnatifida.". The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
