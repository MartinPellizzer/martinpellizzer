Cotoneaster chulingensis

Cotoneaster chulingensis, commonly known as the Chulung cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Cotoneaster chulingensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.
