Cytinus sanguineus

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients through its specialized roots. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Its unique appearance and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
