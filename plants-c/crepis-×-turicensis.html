Crepis × turicensis

Crepis × turicensis, commonly known as the Turiciens crested wheatgrass, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and meadows, and is native to parts of Europe. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Crepis × turicensis is often cultivated for erosion control due to its robust growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
