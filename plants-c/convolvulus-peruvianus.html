Convolvulus peruvianus

Convolvulus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as trellises or fences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a visually striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
