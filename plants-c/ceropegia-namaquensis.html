Ceropegia namaquensis

Ceropegia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Ceropegia namaquensis is well adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
