Cnemidostachys serrulata

Cnemidostachys serrulata, commonly known as the serrated bamboo or slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its common name.

Cnemidostachys serrulata is typically found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
