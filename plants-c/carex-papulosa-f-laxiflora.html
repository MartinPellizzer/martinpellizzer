Carex papulosa f. laxiflora

Carex papulosa f. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower sedge, is a variant of the papulosa sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
