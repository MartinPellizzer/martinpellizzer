Chrysopogon perlaxus

Chrysopogon perlaxus, commonly known as the slender silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Chrysopogon perlaxus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
