Cyphostemma allophylloides subsp. allophylloides

Cyphostemma allophylloides subsp.

allophylloides, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, white, fragrant flowers.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, clustered fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
