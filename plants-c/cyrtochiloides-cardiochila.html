Cyrtochiloides cardiochila

Cyrtochiloides cardiochila, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique pinkish-white coloration. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.

Its botanical study contributes to the understanding of orchid diversity and conservation in tropical ecosystems.
