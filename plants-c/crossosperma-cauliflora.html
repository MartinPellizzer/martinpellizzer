Crossosperma cauliflora

Crossosperma cauliflora, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Crossosperma cauliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
