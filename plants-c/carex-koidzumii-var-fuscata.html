Carex koidzumii var. fuscata

Carex koidzumii var. fuscata, commonly known as the dark-fuscous sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to regions of East Asia, including Japan and Korea, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
