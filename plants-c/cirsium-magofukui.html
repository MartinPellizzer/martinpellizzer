Cirsium magofukui

Cirsium magofukui, commonly known as the Magofukui thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spherical seed heads that disperse via wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
