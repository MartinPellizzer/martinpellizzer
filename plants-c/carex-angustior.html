Carex angustior

Carex angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
