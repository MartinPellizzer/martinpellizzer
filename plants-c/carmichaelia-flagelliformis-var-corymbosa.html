Carmichaelia flagelliformis var. corymbosa

Carmichaelia flagelliformis var. corymbosa, commonly known as the tufted kowhai, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and lowland areas.

The flowers attract a variety of native pollinators, including bees and birds.
