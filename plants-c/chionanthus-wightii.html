Chionanthus wightii

Chionanthus wightii, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chionanthus wightii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and air-purifying properties.
