Corytholoma caracasanum

Corytholoma caracasanum, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in specialized clusters called sori.

The golden cup fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
