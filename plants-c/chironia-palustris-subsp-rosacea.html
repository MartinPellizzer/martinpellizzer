Chironia palustris subsp. rosacea

Chironia palustris subsp.

rosacea, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and bogs, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.

The subspecies rosacea is distinguished by its more vibrant floral coloration compared to other subspecies of Chironia palustris.
