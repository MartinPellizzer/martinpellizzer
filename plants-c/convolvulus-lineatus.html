Convolvulus lineatus

Convolvulus lineatus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, twining stems that often climb or trail along the ground. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
