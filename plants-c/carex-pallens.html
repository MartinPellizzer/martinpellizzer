Carex pallens

Carex pallens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. Native to North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
