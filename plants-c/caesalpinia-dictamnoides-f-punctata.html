Caesalpinia dictamnoides f. punctata

Caesalpinia dictamnoides f. punctata, commonly known as the spotted caesalpinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves with a unique spotted or variegated pattern on the foliage. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
