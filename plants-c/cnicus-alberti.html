Cnicus alberti

Cnicus alberti, commonly known as Albert's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive prickly texture.

Cnicus alberti is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
