Calamintha arundana

Calamintha arundana, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces a distinctive, licorice-scented seed pod that contains tiny, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Calamintha arundana is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
