Centaurea uniflora proles ferdinandi

Centaurea uniflora proles ferdinandi, commonly known as Ferdinand's singleflower centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, this species thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm and produces a single flower per stem.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
