Cucumis sativus var. serotinus

Cucumis sativus var. serotinus, commonly known as the late cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its delayed fruiting cycle, often maturing later than standard cucumber varieties. The plant typically produces elongated, slender fruits with a mild flavor and a slightly bitter taste. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in specialty markets and for its unique harvesting timing.
