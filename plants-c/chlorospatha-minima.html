Chlorospatha minima

Chlorospatha minima, commonly known as the dwarf sunstar, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
