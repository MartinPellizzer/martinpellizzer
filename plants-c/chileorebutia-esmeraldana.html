Chileorebutia esmeraldana

Chileorebutia esmeraldana, commonly known as the Emerald Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
