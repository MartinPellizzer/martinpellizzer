Cansjera manillana

Cansjera manillana, commonly known as the Manila hemp or Abaca, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, banana-like leaves and tall, slender stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers at the top of its stem. Its fibrous leaves are highly valued for their strength and durability, making them useful in textile and industrial applications.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
