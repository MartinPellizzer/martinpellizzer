Cerastium arvense var. japonicum

Cerastium arvense var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features small, hairy, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often hairy foliage.
