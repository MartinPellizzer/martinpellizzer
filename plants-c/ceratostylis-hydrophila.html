Ceratostylis hydrophila

Ceratostylis hydrophila, commonly known as the water hound's tongue, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred labellum and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in marshy areas. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Ceratostylis hydrophila is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, humid conditions.
