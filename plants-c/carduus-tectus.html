Carduus tectus

Carduus tectus, commonly known as the common cocklebur.

It is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae, native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and spherical, spiky fruit heads that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind and can persist in the soil for several years.
