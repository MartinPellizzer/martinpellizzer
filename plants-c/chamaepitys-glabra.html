Chamaepitys glabra

Chamaepitys glabra, commonly known as the smooth fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. It produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
