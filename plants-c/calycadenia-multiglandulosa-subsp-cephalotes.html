Calycadenia multiglandulosa subsp. cephalotes

Calycadenia multiglandulosa subsp.

cephalotes, commonly known as the Sierra Madre globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its globular shape and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
