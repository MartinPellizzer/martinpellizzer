Calliandra carrascana

Calliandra carrascana, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in dense, feathery clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Calliandra carrascana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
