Centaurea amplexicaulis

Centaurea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Centaurea amplexicaulis is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
