Croton laniflorus

Croton laniflorus, commonly known as the woolly-flowered croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
