Calamagrostis silvatica subsp. contracta

Calamagrostis silvatica subsp.

contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf woodland grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
