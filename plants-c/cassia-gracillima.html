Cassia gracillima

Cassia gracillima, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Cassia gracillima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
