Cyphomandra subhastata

Cyphomandra subhastata, commonly known as the purple husk eggplant, is a tropical and subtropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its deep purple, egg-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm and unripe. The plant is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit's skin is thick and smooth, with a mild, slightly sweet flavor when ripe.
