Cephalaria tenella

Cephalaria tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves are adapted to arid environments.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late spring and are followed by cylindrical, spiky seed heads.
