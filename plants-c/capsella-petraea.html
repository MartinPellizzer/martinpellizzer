Capsella petraea

Capsella petraea, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by triangular, seed-like fruits.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
