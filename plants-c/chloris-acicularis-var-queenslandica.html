Chloris acicularis var. queenslandica

Chloris acicularis var. queenslandica, commonly known as Queensland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
