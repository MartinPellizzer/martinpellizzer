Cereus martianus

Cereus martianus, commonly known as the Martian candle, is a cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and flowering habits, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
