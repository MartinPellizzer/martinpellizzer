Cistus oelandicus var. canescens

Cistus oelandicus var. canescens, commonly known as the grey-haired rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
