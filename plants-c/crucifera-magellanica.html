Crucifera magellanica

Crucifera magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its yellow flowers and lobed leaves. It has a short growing season and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
