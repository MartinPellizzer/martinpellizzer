Calamus lamprolepis

Calamus lamprolepis, commonly known as the silver-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, glabrous, and often silver-tinged stems, which give it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Calamus lamprolepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
