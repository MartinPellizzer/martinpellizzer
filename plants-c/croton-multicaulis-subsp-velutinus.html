Croton multicaulis subsp. velutinus

Croton multicaulis subsp.

velutinus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
