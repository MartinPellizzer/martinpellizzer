Carania

Carania, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically evergreen. Carania is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
