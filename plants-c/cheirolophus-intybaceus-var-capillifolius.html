Cheirolophus intybaceus var. capillifolius

Cheirolophus intybaceus var. capillifolius, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
