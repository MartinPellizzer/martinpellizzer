Carex simulata

Carex simulata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces spikes of tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Carex simulata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
