Crucifera capsella

Crucifera capsella, commonly known as mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing easily by wind.

Mouse-ear cress is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
