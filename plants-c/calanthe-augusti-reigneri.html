Calanthe augusti-reigneri

Calanthe augusti-reigneri, commonly known as the Chinese helleborine, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip and are often fragrant.

Calanthe augusti-reigneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
