Cardamomum mannii

Cardamomum mannii, commonly known as black cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It produces aromatic, dark brown pods containing small, dark seeds that are highly valued in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil and a cool climate. Its leaves are broad and leathery, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The essential oils extracted from its seeds contain compounds like cineole and terpinene, contributing to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.
