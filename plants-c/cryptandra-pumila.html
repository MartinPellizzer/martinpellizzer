Cryptandra pumila

Cryptandra pumila, commonly known as the smooth cryptandra, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cryptandra pumila is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
