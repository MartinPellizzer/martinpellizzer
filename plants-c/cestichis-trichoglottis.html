Cestichis trichoglottis

Cestichis trichoglottis, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often covered in a fine, silky texture.

Cestichis trichoglottis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
