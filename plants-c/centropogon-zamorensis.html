Centropogon zamorensis

Centropogon zamorensis, commonly known as the Zamora grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
