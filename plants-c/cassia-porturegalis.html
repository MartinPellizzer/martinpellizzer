Cassia porturegalis

Cassia porturegalis, commonly known as golden cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated pods containing numerous flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia porturegalis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
