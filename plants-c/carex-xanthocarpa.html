Carex xanthocarpa

Carex xanthocarpa, commonly known as yellow spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to reddish spikelets that form dense, cylindrical clusters. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex xanthocarpa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
