Clematis viticaulis

Clematis viticaulis, commonly known as the purple Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from purple to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, reddish hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis viticaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
