Conchophyllum copelandii

Conchophyllum copelandii, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of bright red, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Conchophyllum copelandii is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is popular in horticultural settings.
