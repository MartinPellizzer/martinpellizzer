Calamintha montana

Calamintha montana, commonly known as mountain calamintha, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its fragrant, mint-scented leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Calamintha montana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
