Cerinthe glabra var. tenuifolia

Cerinthe glabra var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
