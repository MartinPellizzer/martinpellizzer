Cirsium lappaceum subsp. kandavanicum

Cirsium lappaceum subsp.

kandavanicum, commonly known as the Kandavan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or arid environments. Its flowering stems are usually solitary and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
