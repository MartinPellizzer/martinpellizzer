Cleome microaustralica

Cleome microaustralica, commonly known as the dwarf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of around 30 to 60 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with five petals and a distinctive central structure that resembles a spider.

Cleome microaustralica is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and ease of care.
