Chlorophora mollis

Chlorophora mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Chlorophora mollis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
