Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. elongatum

Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed pods and vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
