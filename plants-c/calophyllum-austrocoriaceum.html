Calophyllum austrocoriaceum

Calophyllum austrocoriaceum, commonly known as the Australian white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing oily seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
