Cirsium bitchuense

Cirsium bitchuense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with coarse teeth and often have a woolly texture.

Cirsium bitchuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
