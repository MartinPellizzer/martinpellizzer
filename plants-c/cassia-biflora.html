Cassia biflora

Cassia biflora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
