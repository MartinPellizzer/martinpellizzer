Cynanchica tephrocarpa subsp. petraea

Cynanchica tephrocarpa subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the rock milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a hairy, dehiscent pod that releases seeds when mature.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
