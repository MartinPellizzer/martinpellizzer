Cycas gracilis

Cycas gracilis, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a slow-growing evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a short, stout trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and feather-like. The plant produces cones, with male cones typically located at the base of the plant and female cones at the top. Cycas gracilis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and historical significance.
