Calamus quinquesetinervius

Calamus quinquesetinervius, commonly known as the five-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by edible, starchy rhizomes used in traditional cuisine. Its rhizomes are rich in carbohydrates and have been utilized for their nutritional value in various cultures.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in its native regions.
