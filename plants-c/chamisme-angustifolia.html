Chamisme angustifolia

Chamisme angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its essential oil, extracted from the flowers, is widely used in aromatherapy and skincare products.
