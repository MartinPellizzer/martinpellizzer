Ceropegia inornata subsp. inornata

Ceropegia inornata subsp.

inornata, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, branching stems that often trail or climb, and its leaves are typically thick and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually greenish-white with a faint scent. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant.
