Cochlospermum wrightii

Cochlospermum wrightii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
