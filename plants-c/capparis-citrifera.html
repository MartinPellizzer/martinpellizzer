Capparis citrifera

Capparis citrifera, commonly known as the sour orange, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a green citrus-like berry, is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The leaves are pinnately compound and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.
