Cucumis melo var. susianuus

Cucumis melo var. susianuus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a trailing vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It requires well-drained soil and a long growing season to develop its full flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for its high water content and distinct, honey-like taste.
