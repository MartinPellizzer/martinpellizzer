Chasechloa madagascariensis

Chasechloa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and upright growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
