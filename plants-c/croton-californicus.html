Croton californicus

Croton californicus, commonly known as the California croton, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive yellow-green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
