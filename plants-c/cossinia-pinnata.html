Cossinia pinnata

Cossinia pinnata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional craftsmanship.

Cossinia pinnata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
