Calliandra rhodocephala

Calliandra rhodocephala, commonly known as the red-headed calliandra, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking red or pink inflorescences. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Additionally, it has potential uses in soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
