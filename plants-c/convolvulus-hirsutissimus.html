Convolvulus hirsutissimus

Convolvulus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
