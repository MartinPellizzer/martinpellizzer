Convolvulus cavanillesii

Convolvulus cavanillesii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite and ovate, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its fibrous roots allow it to thrive in well-drained soils, and it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
