Ceratostema ventricosum

Ceratostema ventricosum, commonly known as the bladderpod or globular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders, which give the plant its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ceratostema ventricosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
