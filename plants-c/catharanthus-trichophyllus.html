Catharanthus trichophyllus

Catharanthus trichophyllus, commonly known as the velvet flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and attractive blooms, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
