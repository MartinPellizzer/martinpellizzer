Crataegus microphylla var. dolichocarpa

Crataegus microphylla var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-seed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, containing several seeds. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny branches, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
