Coryphantha laredoi

Coryphantha laredoi, commonly known as the Laredo coryphantha, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless plant with a globular stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by cylindrical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
