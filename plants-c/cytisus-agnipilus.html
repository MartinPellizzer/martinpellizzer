Cytisus agnipilus

Cytisus agnipilus, commonly known as the firethorn acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
