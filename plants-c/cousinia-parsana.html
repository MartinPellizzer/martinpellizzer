Cousinia parsana

Cousinia parsana, commonly known as the Kashmiri feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and cool temperatures.

Cousinia parsana is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
