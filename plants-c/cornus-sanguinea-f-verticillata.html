Cornus sanguinea f. verticillata

Cornus sanguinea f. verticillata, commonly known as the red dogwood or redtwig dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that remain visible throughout the winter, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental features make it a popular choice for landscape design.
