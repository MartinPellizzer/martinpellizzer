Colocasia antiquorum var. typica

Colocasia antiquorum var. typica, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil. Its leaves are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.

It produces small, yellow flowers that are typically hidden beneath the foliage.
