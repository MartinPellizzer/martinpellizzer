Chrysodium lomarioides var. hastatum

Chrysodium lomarioides var. hastatum, commonly known as the hairy hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow ray florets that contrast with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
