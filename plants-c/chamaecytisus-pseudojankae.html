Chamaecytisus pseudojankae

Chamaecytisus pseudojankae, commonly known as the Jank's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in mild climates.
