Centaurea ghomerytha

Centaurea ghomerytha, commonly known as the Persian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. Its growth habit includes a basal rosette of leaves with a tall, branching stem that bears the flowers.

Centaurea ghomerytha is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.
