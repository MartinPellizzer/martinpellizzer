Commelina hirsuta

Commelina hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Commelina hirsuta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate moisture.
