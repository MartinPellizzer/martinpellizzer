Chenopodium pumilio f. glandulosum

Chenopodium pumilio f. glandulosum, commonly known as the dwarf goosefoot, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clustered, greenish-white flowers. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in reducing water loss.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
