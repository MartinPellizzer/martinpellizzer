Cymbidium × florinda

Cymbidium × florinda, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid between Cymbidium goeringii and Cymbidium lowianum, resulting in a plant that exhibits traits from both parent species. This orchid typically produces tall, slender stems with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lip being a distinctive, often contrasting color.

It is native to regions in China and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
