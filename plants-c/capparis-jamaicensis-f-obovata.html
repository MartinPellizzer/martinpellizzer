Capparis jamaicensis f. obovata

Capparis jamaicensis f. obovata, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its obovate leaves are broad and rounded, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This variety is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
