Cardamine dentariifolia

Cardamine dentariifolia, commonly known as the Japanese watercress, is a delicate flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny white or green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for cultivation in cool, humid climates.
