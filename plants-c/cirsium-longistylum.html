Cirsium longistylum

Cirsium longistylum, commonly known as the longstem thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and prominent spines.

Cirsium longistylum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
