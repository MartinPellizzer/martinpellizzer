Celtis leveillei

Celtis leveillei, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some animals.

Celtis leveillei is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil types.
