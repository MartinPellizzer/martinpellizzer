Cleistogenes chinensis

Cleistogenes chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Cleistogenes chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
