Carex tsaratananensis var. laevis

Carex tsaratananensis var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
