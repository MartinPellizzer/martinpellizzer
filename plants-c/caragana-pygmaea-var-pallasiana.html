Caragana pygmaea var. pallasiana

Caragana pygmaea var. pallasiana, commonly known as the Pallas's shrub pea, is a low-growing shrub native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It has a spreading habit and can form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
