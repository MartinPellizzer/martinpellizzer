Crocus speciosus var. caucasicus

Crocus speciosus var. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive golden-yellow center, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store energy for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
