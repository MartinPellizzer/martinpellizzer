Concocidium dasytyle

Concocidium dasytyle, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Concocidium dasytyle is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
