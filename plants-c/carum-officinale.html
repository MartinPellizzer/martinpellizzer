Carum officinale

Carum officinale, commonly known as parsley, is an annual herb native to southern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like dill and fennel. Its leaves are used as a culinary herb, while the seeds are valued for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Carum officinale thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often growing in gardens and wild areas.
