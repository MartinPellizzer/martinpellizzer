Clusia macropoda

Clusia macropoda, commonly known as the broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. These flowers are followed by yellowish-orange berries that contain several seeds.

Clusia macropoda is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.
