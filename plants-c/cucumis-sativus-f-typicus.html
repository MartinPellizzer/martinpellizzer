Cucumis sativus f. typicus

Cucumis sativus f. typicus, commonly known as the garden cucumber, is a cultivated form of the cucumber plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruit that typically measures 15 to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a shallow root system that thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it is typically grown as an annual crop.

The fruit develops from the female flowers and is harvested when immature for optimal flavor and texture.
