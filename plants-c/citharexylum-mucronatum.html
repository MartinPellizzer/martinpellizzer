Citharexylum mucronatum

Citharexylum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cithara, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually white or pale yellow in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fragrant blooms.
