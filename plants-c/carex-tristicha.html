Carex tristicha

Carex tristicha, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and other similar plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex tristicha is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
