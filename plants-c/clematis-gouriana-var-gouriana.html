Clematis gouriana var. gouriana

Clematis gouriana var. gouriana, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem. The plant produces showy, blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade with well-drained soil.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
