Cavendishia complectens subsp. striata

Cavendishia complectens subsp.

striata, commonly known as the striped orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with distinct longitudinal stripes.

The plant produces tall inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers in various shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
