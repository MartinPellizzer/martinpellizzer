Coccoloba itzana

Coccoloba itzana, commonly known as Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, producing a showy display of white or pale pink blooms.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
