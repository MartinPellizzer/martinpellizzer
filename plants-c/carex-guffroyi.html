Carex guffroyi

Carex guffroyi, commonly known as the Guffroy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green or reddish in color.

Carex guffroyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
