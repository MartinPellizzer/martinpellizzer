Carex feta var. multa

Carex feta var. multa, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually brown or reddish in color, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
