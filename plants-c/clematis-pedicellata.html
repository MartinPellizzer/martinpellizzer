Clematis pedicellata

Clematis pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Clematis pedicellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be a rewarding addition to gardens with proper support.
