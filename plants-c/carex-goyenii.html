Carex goyenii

Carex goyenii, commonly known as the Goyen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Carex goyenii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
