Chamomilla pubescens

Chamomilla pubescens, commonly known as wild chamomile, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a creeping stem that produces opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant bears yellow flowers with white petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Chamomilla pubescens is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
