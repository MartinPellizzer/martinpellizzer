Chapelieria melleri

Chapelieria melleri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often striking floral structures. The plant typically features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with complex petal arrangements that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Chapelieria melleri is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
