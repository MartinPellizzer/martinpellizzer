Carex huehueteca

Carex huehueteca, commonly known as the sacred sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and cultural significance in traditional Mesoamerican practices.
