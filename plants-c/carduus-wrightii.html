Carduus wrightii

Carduus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, bristly textures, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Wright's thistle is often found in the central and western United States, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
