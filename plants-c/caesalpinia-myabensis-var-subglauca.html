Caesalpinia myabensis var. subglauca

Caesalpinia myabensis var. subglauca, commonly known as the white glaucous burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with prominent red or orange stamens, and it produces a unique, globular fruit that splits open when mature.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
