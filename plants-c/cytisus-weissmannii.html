Cytisus weissmannii

Cytisus weissmannii, commonly known as the Weissmann's broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to parts of southern Europe. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.
