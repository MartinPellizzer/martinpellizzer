Calamintha hungarica var. villicaulis

Calamintha hungarica var. villicaulis, commonly known as Hungarian caraway, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Its seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a spice.
