Calyptranthes jambolifera

Calyptranthes jambolifera, commonly known as the Jambolifera or Jambolifera tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
