Carlina longifolia var. spinosa

Carlina longifolia var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny carline thorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive spiny bracts that surround its flower heads. The plant produces composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
