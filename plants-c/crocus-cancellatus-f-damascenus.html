Crocus cancellatus f. damascenus

Crocus cancellatus f. damascenus, commonly known as the Damascus saffron crocus, is a cultivar of the genus Crocus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers with distinctive purple markings and a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant produces a fine, golden stigmas that are harvested for culinary and medicinal use, similar to true saffron. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typically blooming in late autumn or early winter.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in traditional medicine and cuisine.
