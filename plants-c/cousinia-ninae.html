Cousinia ninae

Cousinia ninae, commonly known as the Kashmiri cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia ninae is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native region.
