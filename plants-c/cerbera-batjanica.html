Cerbera batjanica

Cerbera batjanica, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
