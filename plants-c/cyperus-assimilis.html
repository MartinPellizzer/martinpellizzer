Cyperus assimilis

Cyperus assimilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively.

Cyperus assimilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
