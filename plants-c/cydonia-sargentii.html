Cydonia sargentii

Cydonia sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a yellow or orange fruit, similar to a quince, which is typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Cydonia sargentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
