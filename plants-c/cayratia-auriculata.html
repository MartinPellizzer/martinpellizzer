Cayratia auriculata

Cayratia auriculata, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Cayratia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
