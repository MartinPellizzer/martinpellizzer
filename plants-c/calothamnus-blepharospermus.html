Calothamnus blepharospermus

Calothamnus blepharospermus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a perennial shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to orange, which are typical of the Myrtaceae family. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Calothamnus blepharospermus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
