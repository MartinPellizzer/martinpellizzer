Centaurea × gros-miquelii

Centaurea × gros-miquelii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, forming a composite structure typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea × gros-miquelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
