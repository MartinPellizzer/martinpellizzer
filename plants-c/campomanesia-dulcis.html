Campomanesia dulcis

Campomanesia dulcis, commonly known as pitanga-doce, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
