Cymbopogon nardus

Cymbopogon nardus, commonly known as citron grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Citron grass is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource in both horticulture and aromatherapy.
