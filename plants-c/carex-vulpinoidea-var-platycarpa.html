Carex vulpinoidea var. platycarpa

Carex vulpinoidea var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that mature into distinctive, broad, berry-like fruits. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
