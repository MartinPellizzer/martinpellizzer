Campanula elatines proles garganica

Campanula elatines proles garganica, commonly known as the Garganica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
