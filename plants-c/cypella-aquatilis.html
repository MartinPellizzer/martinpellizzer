Cypella aquatilis

Cypella aquatilis, commonly known as the water cypella, is a unique aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in tropical regions.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Cypella aquatilis plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
