Crinum sesse-mocinoi

Crinum sesse-mocinoi, commonly known as the Mocino's crinum, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow blooms that are often fragrant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Crinum sesse-mocinoi is also known for its bulbous root system, which stores nutrients and water for periods of drought.
