Commiphora playfairii

Commiphora playfairii, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap that exudes from its bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by reddish-brown fruits containing three elliptical seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
