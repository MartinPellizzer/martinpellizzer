Catasetum × splendens var. albopurpureum

Catasetum × splendens var. albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that display a mix of white and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, lance-shaped leaves and a complex inflorescence with multiple blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
