Chloris sagrana

Chloris sagrana, commonly known as the sacred grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its historical and cultural significance, it has been used in traditional rituals and herbal medicine.
