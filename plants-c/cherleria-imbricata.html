Cherleria imbricata

Cherleria imbricata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, oblong leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or arctic regions.

Cherleria imbricata is adapted to cold climates and is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
