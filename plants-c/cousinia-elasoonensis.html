Cousinia elasoonensis

Cousinia elasoonensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. It thrives in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments and is often found at high elevations.

Cousinia elasoonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
