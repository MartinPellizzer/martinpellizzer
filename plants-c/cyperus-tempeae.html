Cyperus tempeae

Cyperus tempeae, commonly known as the Tempeae flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Cyperus tempeae has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often inconspicuous.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
