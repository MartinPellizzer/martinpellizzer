Campanula tridens var. crenatoserrata

Campanula tridens var. crenatoserrata, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated margins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
