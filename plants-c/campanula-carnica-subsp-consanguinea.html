Campanula carnica subsp. consanguinea

Campanula carnica subsp.

consanguinea, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of the Alps and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
