Cerastium banaticum f. kellereri

Cerastium banaticum f. kellereri, commonly known as the Kellerer's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to cool, humid climates and poor soils.
