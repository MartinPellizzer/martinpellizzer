Crupina vulgaris proles brachypappa

Crupina vulgaris proles brachypappa, commonly known as the broadleaf crupina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with rough, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiny, triangular fruits. It is adapted to coastal and saline environments, showing tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a characteristic species in Mediterranean flora.
