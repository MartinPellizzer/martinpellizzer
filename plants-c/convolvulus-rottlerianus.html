Convolvulus rottlerianus

Convolvulus rottlerianus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in its native and introduced habitats.
