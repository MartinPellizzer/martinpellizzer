Campanula dichotoma f. alba

Campanula dichotoma f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
