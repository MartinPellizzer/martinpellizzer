Clematis integrifolia subsp. ochroleuca

Clematis integrifolia subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the white Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America. It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The plant thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale flower color and slightly different leaf structure compared to other Clematis species.
