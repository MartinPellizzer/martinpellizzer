Crucifera grandiflora

Crucifera grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and distinctive pod fruits.

It is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces bright yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a peppery taste, similar to other members of the mustard family.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
