Clinopodium palmeri

Clinopodium palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Clinopodium palmeri is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
