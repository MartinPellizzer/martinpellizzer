Crataegus × petiolata

Crataegus × petiolata, commonly known as the common hawthorn or petioled hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are typically harvested in the fall. Crataegus × petiolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
