Cnidium tilingia

Cnidium tilingia, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, pipe-shaped flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including aristolochic acids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. However, it is also known to be toxic and has been associated with severe health issues.

Due to its ecological and medicinal significance, it is often found in moist, shaded environments in forests and gardens.
