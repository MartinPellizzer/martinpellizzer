Cistus aegypticus

Cistus aegypticus, commonly known as Egyptian rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its resinous leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soils in arid environments.
