Calamagrostis lanceolata f. viridis

Calamagrostis lanceolata f. viridis, commonly known as the green panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that remain a consistent green throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in wetland or moist soil environments, thriving in areas with high humidity. Its compact growth habit and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The foliage provides visual interest year-round, making it a versatile plant for ecological and aesthetic purposes.
