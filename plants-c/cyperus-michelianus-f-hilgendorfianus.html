Cyperus michelianus f. hilgendorfianus

Cyperus michelianus f. hilgendorfianus, commonly known as the Hilgendorf's flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus Cyperus.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grass-like cluster.

The inflorescence is a spike-like structure with small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
