Caprifolium flavum

Caprifolium flavum, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Caprifolium flavum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
