Cephalaria isaurica

Cephalaria isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian candleflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

Cephalaria isaurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
