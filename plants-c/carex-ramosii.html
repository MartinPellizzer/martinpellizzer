Carex ramosii

Carex ramosii, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. The plant produces wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomes contribute to its ability to form dense colonies.

Carex ramosii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
