Cranzia bilocularis

Cranzia bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered cranza, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cranzia bilocularis is known for its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in the summer months.
