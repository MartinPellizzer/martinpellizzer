Centaurea × losana

Centaurea × losana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular florets, typical of the daisy family.

Centaurea × losana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.
