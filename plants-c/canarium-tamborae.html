Canarium tamborae

Canarium tamborae, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by yellowish fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
