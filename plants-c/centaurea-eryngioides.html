Centaurea eryngioides

Centaurea eryngioides, commonly known as the thistleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and is often found in rocky or dry soils. The plant has a tall, erect stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Centaurea eryngioides is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
