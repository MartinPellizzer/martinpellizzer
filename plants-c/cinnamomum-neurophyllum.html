Cinnamomum neurophyllum

Cinnamomum neurophyllum, commonly known as the yellow cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, dark red berries that contain seeds. The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are often used in cooking, imparting a distinct spicy flavor.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
