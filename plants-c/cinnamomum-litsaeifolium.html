Cinnamomum litsaeifolium

Cinnamomum litsaeifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, cinnamon-scented bark. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
