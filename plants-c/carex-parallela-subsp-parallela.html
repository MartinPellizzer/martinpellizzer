Carex parallela subsp. parallela

Carex parallela subsp.

parallela, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

This species typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
