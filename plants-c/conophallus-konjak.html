Conophallus konjak

Conophallus konjak, commonly known as the konjak plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a fibrous, starchy texture, which is the primary part used for culinary purposes. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy surface.

The plant also produces an attractive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.
