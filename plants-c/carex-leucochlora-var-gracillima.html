Carex leucochlora var. gracillima

Carex leucochlora var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender white-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system and forms dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
