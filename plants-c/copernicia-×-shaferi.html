Copernicia × shaferi

Copernicia × shaferi, commonly known as the hybrid copernicia, is a species resulting from the cross between Copernicia cerifera and Copernicia melolabonum.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, commonly referred to as the palm family. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to produce a type of wax, similar to that of its parent species.
