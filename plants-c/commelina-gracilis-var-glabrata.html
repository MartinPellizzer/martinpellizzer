Commelina gracilis var. glabrata

Commelina gracilis var. glabrata, commonly known as slender commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or violet flowers, which are typically surrounded by white bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
