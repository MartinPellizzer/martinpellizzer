Cistus salviifolius f. biflorus

Cistus salviifolius f. biflorus, commonly known as the twin-flowered rock rose, is a rare and distinctive variant of the rock rose genus.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that often appear in pairs, giving it its specific epithet "biflorus.". The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
