Chadsia andravinensis

Chadsia andravinensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in extreme desert conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces striking white or pale purple flowers, which bloom during brief periods of rainfall. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, minimizing water loss and protecting the plant from herbivores.

Chadsia andravinensis plays a vital ecological role in its habitat, supporting local biodiversity and serving as an indicator of rare desert ecosystems.
