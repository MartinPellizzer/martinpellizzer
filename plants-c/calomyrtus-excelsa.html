Calomyrtus excelsa

Calomyrtus excelsa, commonly known as the tall myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of eastern Australia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, often with a slight aromatic scent.

Calomyrtus excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
