Cupressus goveniana var. huberiana

Cupressus goveniana var. huberiana, commonly known as the Torrey cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
