Crepis simulatrix

Crepis simulatrix, commonly known as the mimic ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.
