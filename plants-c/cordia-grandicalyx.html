Cordia grandicalyx

Cordia grandicalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential healing properties.
