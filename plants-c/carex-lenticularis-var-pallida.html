Carex lenticularis var. pallida

Carex lenticularis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its pale coloration and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.
