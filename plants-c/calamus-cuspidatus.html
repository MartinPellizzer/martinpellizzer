Calamus cuspidatus

Calamus cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-tailed reed, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and basketry. Its flowering occurs in clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by spiny fruits.

Calamus cuspidatus is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in wet, swampy environments.
