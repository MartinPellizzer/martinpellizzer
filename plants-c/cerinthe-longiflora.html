Cerinthe longiflora

Cerinthe longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
