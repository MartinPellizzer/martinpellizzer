Carex alboatra

Carex alboatra, commonly known as the white-tongued sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex alboatra is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
