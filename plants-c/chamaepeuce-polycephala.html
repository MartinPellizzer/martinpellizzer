Chamaepeuce polycephala

Chamaepeuce polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features multiple stems that arise from a single root system, giving it its distinctive "many-headed" appearance. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in arid environments.
