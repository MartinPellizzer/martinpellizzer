Campanula chamissonis

Campanula chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in areas with rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanula chamissonis is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
