Cystium agninum

Cystium agninum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
