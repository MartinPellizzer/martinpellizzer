Centaurea argyrocephala

Centaurea argyrocephala, commonly known as silverhead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea argyrocephala is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
