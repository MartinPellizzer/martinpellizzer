Cirsium candelabrum

Cirsium candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure that resembles a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant features large, spiny leaves with a blue to purple coloration, and its flowers are typically purple or pink. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium candelabrum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
