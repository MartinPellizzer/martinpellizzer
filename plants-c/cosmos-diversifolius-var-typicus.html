Cosmos diversifolius var. typicus

Cosmos diversifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the Texas blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features delicate, feathery blue-violet flowers that bloom from midsummer to early fall. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cosmos diversifolius var. typicus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
