Chamaecrista polytricha var. pulchella

Chamaecrista polytricha var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
