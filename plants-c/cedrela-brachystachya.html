Cedrela brachystachya

Cedrela brachystachya, commonly known as the yellow cedar or Mexican cedar, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, lightweight wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

Cedrela brachystachya thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
