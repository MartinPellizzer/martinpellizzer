Casearia arborea

Casearia arborea, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. Casearia arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and versatile plant in tropical landscapes.
