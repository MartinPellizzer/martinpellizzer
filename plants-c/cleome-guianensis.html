Cleome guianensis

Cleome guianensis, commonly known as spider flower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Cleome guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
