Cassia latifolia var. falcistipula

Cassia latifolia var. falcistipula, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a variant of the cassia genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and showy inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
