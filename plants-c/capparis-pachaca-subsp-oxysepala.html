Capparis pachaca subsp. oxysepala

Capparis pachaca subsp.

oxysepala, commonly known as the Andean coral tree, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
