Calceolaria tenera

Calceolaria tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Calceolaria tenera is a member of the orchid family, although it is not closely related to true orchids.
