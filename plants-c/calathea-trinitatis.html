Calathea trinitatis

Calathea trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America, specifically Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and display a range of hues including green, pink, and red, which intensify in low light conditions. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
