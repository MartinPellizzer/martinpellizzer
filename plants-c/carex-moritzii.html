Carex moritzii

Carex moritzii, commonly known as the Variegated Sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant features striking variegated foliage with white and green stripes, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Carex moritzii is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
