Crataegus longipes

Crataegus longipes, commonly known as the longpedal hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types across its native range.
