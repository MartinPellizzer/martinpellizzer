Cyathea brunonis

Cyathea brunonis, commonly known as the Brunon's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, forested areas where it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and biodiversity.
