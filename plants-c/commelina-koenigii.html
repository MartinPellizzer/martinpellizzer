Commelina koenigii

Commelina koenigii, commonly known as Indian nettle or false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has rhizomatous roots that help in its spread and survival in various soil types.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
