Crepis foetida f. glandulosa

Crepis foetida f. glandulosa, commonly known as the stinking sow-thistle, is a variant of the common sow-thistle.

It is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive, sometimes unpleasant odor.

This form is notable for its horticultural interest and adaptability to various growing conditions.
