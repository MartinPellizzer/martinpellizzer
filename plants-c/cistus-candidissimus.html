Cistus candidissimus

Cistus candidissimus, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Cistus candidissimus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
