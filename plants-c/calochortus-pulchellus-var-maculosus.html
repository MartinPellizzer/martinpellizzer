Calochortus pulchellus var. maculosus

Calochortus pulchellus var. maculosus, commonly known as the spotted glory-of-the-snow, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

This perennial herb features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, erect stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
