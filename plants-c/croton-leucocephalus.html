Croton leucocephalus

Croton leucocephalus, commonly known as the white-headed croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a contrasting white or yellow margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton leucocephalus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
