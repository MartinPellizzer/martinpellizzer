Cyperus longistolon subsp. atrofuscus

Cyperus longistolon subsp.

atrofuscus, commonly known as black-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and floodplains. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet with small, three-lobed flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental appearance.
