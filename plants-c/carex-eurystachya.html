Carex eurystachya

Carex eurystachya, commonly known as the broad-awned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex eurystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
