Cucumis melo var. zaami

Cucumis melo var. zaami, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruits with a smooth, pale green skin and a sweet, crisp flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
