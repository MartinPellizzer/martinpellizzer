Capparis spinosa subsp. orientalis

Capparis spinosa subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The oriental caper is valued for its edible flowers and leaves, which are used in culinary applications.
