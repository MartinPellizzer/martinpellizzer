Calanthe ovalifolia

Calanthe ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf calanthe, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Calanthe ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
