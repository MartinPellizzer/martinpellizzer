Centaurea fraylensis

Centaurea fraylensis, commonly known as the Frayl's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea fraylensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
