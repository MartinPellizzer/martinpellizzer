Carduus afromontanus

Carduus afromontanus, commonly known as the East African thistle, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia and neighboring regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its spiny foliage and yellow flower heads serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal properties.
