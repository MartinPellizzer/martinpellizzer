Carex hakonemontana

Carex hakonemontana, commonly known as Hakon's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Norway.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form compact clumps, making it a useful ornamental plant in gardens.

Carex hakonemontana is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in its native habitat.
