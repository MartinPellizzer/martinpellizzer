Centaurea achaia subsp. corinthiaca

Centaurea achaia subsp.

corinthiaca, commonly known as Corinthian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single flower head per stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
