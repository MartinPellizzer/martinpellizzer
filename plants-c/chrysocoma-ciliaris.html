Chrysocoma ciliaris

Chrysocoma ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, round fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry conditions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
