Cotula hombronii

Cotula hombronii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Cotula hombronii has hairy foliage and produces numerous small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
