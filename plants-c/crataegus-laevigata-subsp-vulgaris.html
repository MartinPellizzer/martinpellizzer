Crataegus laevigata subsp. vulgaris

Crataegus laevigata subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
