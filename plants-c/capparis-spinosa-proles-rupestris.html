Capparis spinosa proles rupestris

Capparis spinosa proles rupestris, commonly known as the rock caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, citrus-like berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

This resilient species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
