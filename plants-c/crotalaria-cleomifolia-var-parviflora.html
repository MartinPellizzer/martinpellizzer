Crotalaria cleomifolia var. parviflora

Crotalaria cleomifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seed pods contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source in some parts of the world.

The plant is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
