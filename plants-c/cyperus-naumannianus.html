Cyperus naumannianus

Cyperus naumannianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperflower, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Cyperus naumannianus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
