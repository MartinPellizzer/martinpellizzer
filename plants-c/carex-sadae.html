Carex sadae

Carex sadae, commonly known as the Sadae sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly China and Korea. This plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Carex sadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
