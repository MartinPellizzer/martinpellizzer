Casuarina chamaecyparis

Casuarina chamaecyparis, commonly known as the coastal she-oak, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, consisting of needle-like leaves arranged in spiraled clusters. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
