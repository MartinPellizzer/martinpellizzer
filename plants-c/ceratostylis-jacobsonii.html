Ceratostylis jacobsonii

Ceratostylis jacobsonii, commonly known as the Jacobson's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with dark markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Ceratostylis jacobsonii is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
