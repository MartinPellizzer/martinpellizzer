Carex rafflesiana var. macrithyrsa

Carex rafflesiana var. macrithyrsa, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
