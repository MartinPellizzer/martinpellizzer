Cynanchum paramorum

Cynanchum paramorum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the arrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which is characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually yellowish-green to white, with a distinctive corona structure.
