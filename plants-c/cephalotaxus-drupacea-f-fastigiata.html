Cephalotaxus drupacea f. fastigiata

Cephalotaxus drupacea f. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yew, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It features upright, columnar growth with dense, dark green foliage that provides year-round visual interest. The leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive glossy appearance. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in formal garden settings.
