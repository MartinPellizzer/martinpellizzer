Coppensia ourobranquensis

Coppensia ourobranquensis, commonly known as the Ouro Branco orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white and green floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
