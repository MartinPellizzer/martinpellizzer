Carex × vimariensis

Carex × vimariensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex × vimariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
