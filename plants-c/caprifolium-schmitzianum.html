Caprifolium schmitzianum

Caprifolium schmitzianum, commonly known as the Schmitz's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
