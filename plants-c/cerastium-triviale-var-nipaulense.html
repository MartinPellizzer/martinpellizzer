Cerastium triviale var. nipaulense

Cerastium triviale var. nipaulense, commonly known as the Himalayan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its compact growth habit and ability to form dense mats make it well-suited for rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is also noted for its frost tolerance and is often found in mountainous regions of India and Nepal.
