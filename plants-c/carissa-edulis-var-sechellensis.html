Carissa edulis var. sechellensis

Carissa edulis var. sechellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles moonfruit, is a tropical shrub native to the Seychelles.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. The plant features thick, waxy leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep yellow, indicating maturity.
