Carduus personatoformis

Carduus personatoformis, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and its flowers are arranged in a rounded head.

The hairy thistle is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for several insect species.
