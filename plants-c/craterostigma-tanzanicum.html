Craterostigma tanzanicum

Craterostigma tanzanicum, commonly known as the Tanzanian rock cress, is a small, perennial herb native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with a fine layer of hairs, aiding in water retention.

Craterostigma tanzanicum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
