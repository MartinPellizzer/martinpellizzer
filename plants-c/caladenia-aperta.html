Caladenia aperta

Caladenia aperta, commonly known as the Broad-leafed Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking, spider-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with several small, lance-shaped petals and sepals. Caladenia aperta prefers sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

It is a short-lived perennial, with a flowering period usually occurring in spring.
