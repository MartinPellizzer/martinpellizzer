Cylindrolobus aporoides

Cylindrolobus aporoides, commonly known as the smooth globular liverwort, is a member of the family Porellaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming liverwort that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive globular or cylindrical thallus with a smooth surface. Its reproductive structures, called gemmae, are produced in clusters on the upper surface of the thallus.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention.
