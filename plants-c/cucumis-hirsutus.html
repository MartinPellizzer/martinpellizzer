Cucumis hirsutus

Cucumis hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, hairy, green fruits that are typically harvested when immature for consumption. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

The hairy cucumber is often grown for its edible fruit, which has a mild, cucumber-like flavor.
