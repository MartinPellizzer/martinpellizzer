Carex nigra subsp. dacica

Carex nigra subsp.

dacica, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or black in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streams. Carex nigra subsp.

dacica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
