Campanula microcodon

Campanula microcodon, commonly known as the small bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. Campanula microcodon is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it forms dense mats of foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
