Calamintha nervosa

Calamintha nervosa, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic leaves with a distinctive vein pattern, and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Calamintha nervosa is valued in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
