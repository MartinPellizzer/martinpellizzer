Calophaca tomentosa

Calophaca tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly calophaca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
