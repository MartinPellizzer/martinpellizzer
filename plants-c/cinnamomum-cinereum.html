Cinnamomum cinereum

Cinnamomum cinereum, commonly known as the silver cinnamon or black cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that exudes a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
