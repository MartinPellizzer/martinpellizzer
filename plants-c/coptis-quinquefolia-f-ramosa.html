Coptis quinquefolia f. ramosa

Coptis quinquefolia f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched goldthread, is a variant of the American goldthread plant.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
