Calamagrostis acrathera

Calamagrostis acrathera, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis acrathera plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
