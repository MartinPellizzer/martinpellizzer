Calliandra scottiana

Calliandra scottiana, commonly known as the scarlet calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces bright red, pink, or yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Calliandra scottiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
