Carex heudesii

Carex heudesii, commonly known as Heude's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Carex heudesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
