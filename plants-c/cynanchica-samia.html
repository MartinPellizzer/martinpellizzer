Cynanchica samia

Cynanchica samia, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to most herbivores. Its flowering period typically occurs in summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cynanchica samia is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
