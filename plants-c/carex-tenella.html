Carex tenella

Carex tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Carex tenella has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
