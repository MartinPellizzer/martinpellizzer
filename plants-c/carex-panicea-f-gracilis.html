Carex panicea f. gracilis

Carex panicea f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The inflorescence consists of multiple cylindrical spikes, with the lower spike often bearing female flowers and the upper spike bearing male flowers.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
