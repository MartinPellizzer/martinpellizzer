Cheiranthus × cheiri var. fruticulosus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. fruticulosus, commonly known as the fruticose wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense foliage, which gives it a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
