Clematis aristata var. occidentalis

Clematis aristata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
