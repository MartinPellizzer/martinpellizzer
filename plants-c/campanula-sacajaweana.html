Campanula sacajaweana

Campanula sacajaweana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cold, arid conditions of its habitat.

Campanula sacajaweana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
