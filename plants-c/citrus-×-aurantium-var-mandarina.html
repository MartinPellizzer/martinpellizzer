Citrus × aurantium var. mandarina

Citrus × aurantium var. mandarina, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a hybrid citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and segmented fruit with a thin, loose rind. The plant is a shrub or small tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and juice production.

The peel, or zest, is also used in cooking and aromatherapy due to its aromatic compounds.
