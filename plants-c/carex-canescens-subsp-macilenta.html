Carex canescens subsp. macilenta

Carex canescens subsp.

macilenta, commonly known as the slender bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
