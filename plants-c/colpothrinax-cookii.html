Colpothrinax cookii

Colpothrinax cookii, commonly known as the Cook's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the island of Hispaniola, specifically found in the Dominican Republic. This palm is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.
