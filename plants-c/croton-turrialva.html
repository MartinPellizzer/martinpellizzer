Croton turrialva

Croton turrialva, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
