Centaurea stevenii subsp. stevenii

Centaurea stevenii subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as Steven's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers in composite inflorescences. Its flowers are surrounded by pale blue to purple bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
