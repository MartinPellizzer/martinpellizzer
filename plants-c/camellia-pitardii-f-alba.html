Camellia pitardii f. alba

Camellia pitardii f. alba, commonly known as the white camellia, is a unique cultivar of the camellia plant characterized by its striking white flowers.

It is a hybrid form that exhibits a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The flowers are typically large, with a smooth, waxy texture and a subtle fragrance, blooming in late winter to early spring. This variety is well-suited to USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a prized addition to both traditional and modern horticultural collections.
