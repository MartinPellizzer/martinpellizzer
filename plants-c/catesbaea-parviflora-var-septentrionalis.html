Catesbaea parviflora var. septentrionalis

Catesbaea parviflora var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a slow growth rate and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
