Crocus chiaicus

Crocus chiaicus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Crocus chiaicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Its bulbs are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
