Cynanchum luteifluens var. longicoronae

Cynanchum luteifluens var. longicoronae, commonly known as the yellow-flowered dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is part of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and contains latex, which can be toxic to livestock.
