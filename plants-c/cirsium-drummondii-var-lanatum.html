Cirsium drummondii var. lanatum

Cirsium drummondii var. lanatum, commonly known as Drummond's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The variety lanatum is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
