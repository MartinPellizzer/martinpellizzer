Caldesia grandis

Caldesia grandis, commonly known as the giant caldesia, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters at the water's surface.

Caldesia grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can play a role in aquatic ecosystem restoration.
