Calliandra clavellina

Calliandra clavellina, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red bract-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Calliandra clavellina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
