Catha alaternifolia

Catha alaternifolia, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its stimulant leaves.

The plant has a long history of traditional use, particularly for its psychoactive properties.
