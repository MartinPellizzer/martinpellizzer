Centaurea princeps

Centaurea princeps, commonly known as the Prince's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny environments. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Centaurea princeps is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
