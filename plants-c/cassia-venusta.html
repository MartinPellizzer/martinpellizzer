Cassia venusta

Cassia venusta, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. Cassia venusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
