Cyanotris angustifolia

Cyanotris angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Cyanotris angustifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
