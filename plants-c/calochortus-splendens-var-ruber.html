Calochortus splendens var. ruber

Calochortus splendens var. ruber, commonly known as the red glory-of-the-snow, is a striking bulbous perennial native to California.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas of California.
