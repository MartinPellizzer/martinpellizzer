Colchicum pusillum

Colchicum pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf meadow saffron, is a member of the Colchicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, nodding flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

Colchicum pusillum is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.
