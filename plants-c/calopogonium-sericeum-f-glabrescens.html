Calopogonium sericeum f. glabrescens

Calopogonium sericeum f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky calopogonium, is a variant of the species Calopogonium sericeum, which is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

This particular form is characterized by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical hairy form. It is a low-growing annual herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
