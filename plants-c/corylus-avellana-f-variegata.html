Corylus avellana f. variegata

Corylus avellana f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated hazel, is a unique variant of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow variegation on its leaves. This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, edible nuts similar to those of the common hazel, though the fruit is less abundant.
