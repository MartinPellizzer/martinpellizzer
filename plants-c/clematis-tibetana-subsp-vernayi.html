Clematis tibetana subsp. vernayi

Clematis tibetana subsp.

vernayi, commonly known as the Tibetan Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet. It features slender, twining stems that can climb to considerable heights, often reaching up to 5 meters.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
