Cyrtochilum midas

Cyrtochilum midas, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically produces small, fragrant blooms that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Cyrtochilum midas thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
