Celastrus peruvianus

Celastrus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian creeper, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Celastrus peruvianus is often found in humid, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
