Chamaemelum karjaginii

Chamaemelum karjaginii, commonly known as Karjagin's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
