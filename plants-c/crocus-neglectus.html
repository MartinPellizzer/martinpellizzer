Crocus neglectus

Crocus neglectus, commonly known as the common crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus neglectus is often found growing in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens, where it adds vibrant color to the landscape.
