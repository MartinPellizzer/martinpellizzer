Catasetum × mesquitae

Catasetum × mesquitae, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers with a distinct lip that is often modified for pollination by specific insects. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring humid and shaded environments.

Catasetum × mesquitae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
