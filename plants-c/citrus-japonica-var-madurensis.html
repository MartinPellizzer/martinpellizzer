Citrus japonica var. madurensis

Citrus japonica var. madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese kumquat, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to orange. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
