Corybas calophyllus

Corybas calophyllus, commonly known as the golden helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing a single flower. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette at the base.

Corybas calophyllus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest or heathland habitats.
