Cassia pulcherrima

Cassia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, orange, or yellow bract clusters that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia pulcherrima is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
