Cirsium buschianum

Cirsium buschianum, commonly known as the Busch's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Cirsium buschianum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
