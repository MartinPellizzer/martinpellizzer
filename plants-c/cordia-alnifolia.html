Cordia alnifolia

Cordia alnifolia, commonly known as the white cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

Cordia alnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
