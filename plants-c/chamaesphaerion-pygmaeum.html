Chamaesphaerion pygmaeum

Chamaesphaerion pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. The plant has compact, cushion-like growth habits, with small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and appearing in early spring.
