Carex petricosa var. petricosa

Carex petricosa var. petricosa, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

This variety is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in specialized horticultural settings.
