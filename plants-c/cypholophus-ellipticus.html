Cypholophus ellipticus

Cypholophus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
