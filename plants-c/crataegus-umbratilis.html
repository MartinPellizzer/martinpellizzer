Crataegus umbratilis

Crataegus umbratilis, commonly known as the shade crabapple, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
