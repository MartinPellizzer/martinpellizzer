Carex mackenziei

Carex mackenziei, commonly known as Mackenzie's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers.

Carex mackenziei plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
