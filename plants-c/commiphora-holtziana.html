Commiphora holtziana

Commiphora holtziana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in warm, dry climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The plant is valued for its unique fragrance and has been utilized in traditional medicine and incense-making.
