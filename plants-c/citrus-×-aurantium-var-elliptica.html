Citrus × aurantium var. elliptica

Citrus × aurantium var. elliptica, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a citrus hybrid native to southern Europe and northern Africa, characterized by its elliptical, thornless leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, bitter, and used primarily for its zest and essential oils rather than consumption. It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and role in the cultivation of other citrus varieties.

The plant is also known for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
