Chaenactis glabriuscula var. tenuifolia

Chaenactis glabriuscula var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
