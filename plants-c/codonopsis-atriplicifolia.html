Codonopsis atriplicifolia

Codonopsis atriplicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
