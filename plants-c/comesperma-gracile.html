Comesperma gracile

Comesperma gracile, commonly known as the slender comesperma, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Comesperma gracile is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
