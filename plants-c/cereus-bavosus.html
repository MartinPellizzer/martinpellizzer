Cereus bavosus

Cereus bavosus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, opening at night to attract specific pollinators.

Despite its small size, the Moon Cactus is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
