Cinnamomum wiliamsii

Cinnamomum wiliamsii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and other parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by egg-shaped berries that contain aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a distinct scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its bark and used in perfumery and pharmaceutical applications.
