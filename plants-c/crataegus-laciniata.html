Crataegus laciniata

Crataegus laciniata, commonly known as the fragrant crataegus or the cutleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, dark green leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Crataegus laciniata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
