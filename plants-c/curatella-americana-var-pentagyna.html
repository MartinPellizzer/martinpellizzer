Curatella americana var. pentagyna

Curatella americana var. pentagyna, commonly known as the five-angled curatella, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, five-angled berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
