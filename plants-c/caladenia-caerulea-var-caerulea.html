Caladenia caerulea var. caerulea

Caladenia caerulea var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-tinged spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. The flowers have a distinctive spider-like appearance due to their elongated, narrow petals and sepals. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

The plant is known for its delicate beauty and is often found in coastal regions of New South Wales and Victoria.
