Centaurea korthiaca

Centaurea korthiaca, commonly known as the Korth's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.
