Cirsium nipponense

Cirsium nipponense, commonly known as Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and tall, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Cirsium nipponense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience.
