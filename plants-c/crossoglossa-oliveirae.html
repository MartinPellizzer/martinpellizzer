Crossoglossa oliveirae

Crossoglossa oliveirae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Crossoglossa oliveirae is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution.
