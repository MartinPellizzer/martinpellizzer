Casearia leucolepis

Casearia leucolepis, commonly known as the white-leaved casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often white- or grayish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a slightly aromatic scent, while its growth habit is compact with a dense canopy.

Casearia leucolepis is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
