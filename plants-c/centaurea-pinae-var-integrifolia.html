Centaurea pinae var. integrifolia

Centaurea pinae var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Pina's knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces strikingly purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
