Campsiandra macrocarpa var. macrocarpa

Campsiandra macrocarpa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
