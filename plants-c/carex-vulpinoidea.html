Carex vulpinoidea

Carex vulpinoidea, commonly known as fox sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular, often hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex vulpinoidea is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
