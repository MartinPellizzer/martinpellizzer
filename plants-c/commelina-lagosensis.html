Commelina lagosensis

Commelina lagosensis, commonly known as the Lagos spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Nigeria.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branching.

Commelina lagosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
