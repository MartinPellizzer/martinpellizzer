Callicarpa × shirasawana

Callicarpa × shirasawana, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Callicarpa cathayana and Callicarpa japonica. The plant is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning red in the autumn.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
