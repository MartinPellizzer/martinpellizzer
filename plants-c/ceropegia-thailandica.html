Ceropegia thailandica

Ceropegia thailandica, commonly known as the Thai milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ceropegia thailandica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
