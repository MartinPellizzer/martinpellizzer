Carex cruciata var. rafflesiana

Carex cruciata var. rafflesiana, commonly known as the Raffles' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their unique morphology and ecological adaptability. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for small organisms.
