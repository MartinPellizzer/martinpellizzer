Cardamine hirsuta var. major

Cardamine hirsuta var. major, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the hairy bittercress.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
