Cucumis melo var. beida

Cucumis melo var. beida, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variety of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruit with a thin, edible peel and a crisp, juicy interior. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, producing yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
