Corynopuntia bulbispina

Corynopuntia bulbispina, commonly known as the apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Corynopuntia bulbispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
