Chorizema rhombeum

Chorizema rhombeum, commonly known as the diamondleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, diamond-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique leaf structure and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
