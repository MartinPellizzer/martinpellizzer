Clusia maxima

Clusia maxima, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, often with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Clusia maxima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
