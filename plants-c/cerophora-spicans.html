Cerophora spicans

Cerophora spicans, commonly known as the feathered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, often with a white or pale blue hue. The plant produces a unique, feathery bract that resembles a plume, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Cerophora spicans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
