Carex sachalinensis var. duvaliana

Carex sachalinensis var. duvaliana, commonly known as the Duval's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth or slightly rough texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.
