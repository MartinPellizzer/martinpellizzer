Carduus gouanii

Carduus gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in grasslands and scrub habitats. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, tall head.

Carduus gouanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
