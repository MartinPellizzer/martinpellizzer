Campanula divergens var. cernua

Campanula divergens var. cernua, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
