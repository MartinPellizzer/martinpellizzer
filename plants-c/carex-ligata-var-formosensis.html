Carex ligata var. formosensis

Carex ligata var. formosensis, commonly known as the Formosan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
