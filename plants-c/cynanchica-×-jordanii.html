Cynanchica × jordanii

Cynanchica × jordanii, commonly known as the Jordanian milkvetch, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
