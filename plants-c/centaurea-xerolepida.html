Centaurea xerolepida

Centaurea xerolepida, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or dry environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
