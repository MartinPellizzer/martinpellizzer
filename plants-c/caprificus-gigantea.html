Caprificus gigantea

Caprificus gigantea, commonly known as the giant caprifig, is a large, evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and large, clustered fruiting structures called syconia. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for landscaping and windbreaks.

The tree's unique floral structure supports specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
