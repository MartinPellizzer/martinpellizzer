Cyperus tabularis var. ecklonii

Cyperus tabularis var. ecklonii, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically flat and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
