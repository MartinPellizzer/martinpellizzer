Clusia aripoensis

Clusia aripoensis, commonly known as the Aripo clusterleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which includes species known for their showy flowers and often ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is thick and waxy, providing a glossy appearance. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences.

Clusia aripoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
