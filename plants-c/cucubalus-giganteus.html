Cucubalus giganteus

Cucubalus giganteus, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny fruit that can weigh up to 10 kilograms. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is known for its unique flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
