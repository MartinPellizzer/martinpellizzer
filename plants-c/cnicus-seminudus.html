Cnicus seminudus

Cnicus seminudus, commonly known as the naked thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Cnicus seminudus is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
