Cleisostoma melanorachis

Cleisostoma melanorachis, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers with dark purple petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their unique, intricate structure.

Cleisostoma melanorachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
