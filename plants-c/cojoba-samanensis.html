Cojoba samanensis

Cojoba samanensis, commonly known as the Caribbean umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its trunk is often swollen and can store water, making it well adapted to tropical and semi-arid environments.

Cojoba samanensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
