Carex paniculata f. canaliculata

Carex paniculata f. canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that form panicles. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wetland environments.

The inflorescence is usually slender and may vary in color from green to reddish depending on the season.
