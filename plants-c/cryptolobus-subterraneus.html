Cryptolobus subterraneus

Cryptolobus subterraneus, commonly known as the underground lichen, is a unique species that thrives in subterranean environments.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal component and a cyanobacterial partner, forming a composite structure that can survive in low-light, nutrient-poor conditions. The plant's morphology is adapted for anchoring in soil and absorbing moisture and minerals from the surrounding substrate. Its reproductive structures are typically hidden beneath the surface, contributing to its elusive nature.

Due to its specialized habitat, it plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of underground ecosystems.
