Cassia tephrosiifolia

Cassia tephrosiifolia, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous mesh. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically and horticulturally significant in its native habitats.
