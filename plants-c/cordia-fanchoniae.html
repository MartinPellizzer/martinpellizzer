Cordia fanchoniae

Cordia fanchoniae, commonly known as the red cedar or white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Cordia fanchoniae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
