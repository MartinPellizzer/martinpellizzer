Carex halleriana var. thymicola

Carex halleriana var. thymicola, commonly known as the thyme-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant features dense, compact growth with narrow, linear leaves that have a slightly rough, textured surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.
