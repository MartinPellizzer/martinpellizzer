Carex maximowiczii var. suifunensis

Carex maximowiczii var. suifunensis, commonly known as the Suifun sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to East Asia, particularly Korea and northeastern China, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The flowers are typically small and greenish, with a distinctive three-parted perianth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
