Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. grandiflorus

Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the sweet willie or sweet scented broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its pleasant scent and ability to attract pollinators.
