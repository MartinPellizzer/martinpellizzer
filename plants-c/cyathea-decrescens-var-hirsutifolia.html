Cyathea decrescens var. hirsutifolia

Cyathea decrescens var. hirsutifolia, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The fronds are typically arching and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to the plant's dramatic appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
