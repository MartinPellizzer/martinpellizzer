Coreopsis simplicifolia

Coreopsis simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis simplicifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
