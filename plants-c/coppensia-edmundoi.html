Coppensia edmundoi

It is characterized by its striking pale green and white flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a small, terrestrial herb with a single, slender stem and a few leaves. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as the broad-tailed honeyeater.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Coppensia edmundoi is considered critically endangered and is protected under Australian conservation laws.
