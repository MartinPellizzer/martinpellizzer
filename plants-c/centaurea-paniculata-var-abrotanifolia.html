Centaurea paniculata var. abrotanifolia

Centaurea paniculata var. abrotanifolia, commonly known as sweet centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and dry grasslands.

This variety is notable for its aromatic scent and historical use in herbal medicine.
