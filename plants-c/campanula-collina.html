Campanula collina

Campanula collina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Campanula collina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
