Centaurea × villaris

Centaurea × villaris, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea jacea (brown cock's-comb). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and vibrant blue to purple flower heads. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × villaris is often found in disturbed soils and meadows, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions.
