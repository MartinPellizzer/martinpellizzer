Corchorus flexuosus

Corchorus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
