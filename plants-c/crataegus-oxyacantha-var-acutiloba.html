Crataegus oxyacantha var. acutiloba

Crataegus oxyacantha var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharplobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and gardens.
