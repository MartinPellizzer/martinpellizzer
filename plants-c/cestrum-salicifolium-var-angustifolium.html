Cestrum salicifolium var. angustifolium

Cestrum salicifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its poisonous properties.
