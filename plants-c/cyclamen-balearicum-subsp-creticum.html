Cyclamen balearicum subsp. creticum

Cyclamen balearicum subsp.

creticum, commonly known as the Cretan cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Crete. It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, limestone-rich environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to alpine and rock garden collections.
