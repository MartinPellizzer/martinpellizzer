Codonoprasum fuscum

Codonoprasum fuscum, commonly known as the brown carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or brownish hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Codonoprasum fuscum is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
