Crataegus lassa var. integra

Crataegus lassa var. integra, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological systems and a subject of interest in horticulture.
