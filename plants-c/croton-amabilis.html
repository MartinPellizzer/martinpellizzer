Croton amabilis

Croton amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from green to red, orange, and yellow, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton amabilis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
