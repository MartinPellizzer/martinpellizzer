Chrysanthemum × superbum

Chrysanthemum × superbum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors and forms. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and disk florets, and bloom in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
