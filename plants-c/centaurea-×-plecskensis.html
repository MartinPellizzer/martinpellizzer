Centaurea × plecskensis

Centaurea × plecskensis, commonly known as the Hungarian crocus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea solstitialis.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, blue to purple flowers surrounded by bracts.

Centaurea × plecskensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
