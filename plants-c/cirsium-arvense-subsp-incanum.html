Cirsium arvense subsp. incanum

Cirsium arvense subsp.

incanum, commonly known as field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, rough, and often hairy stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers arranged in a distinctive cylindrical shape, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges and a waxy coating.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
