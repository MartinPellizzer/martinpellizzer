Cotula pubescens

Cotula pubescens, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Cotula pubescens is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
