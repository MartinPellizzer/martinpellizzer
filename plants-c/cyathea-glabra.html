Cyathea glabra

Cyathea glabra, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name.

Cyathea glabra is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
