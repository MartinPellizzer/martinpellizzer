Croton redolens var. parvifolius

Croton redolens var. parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
