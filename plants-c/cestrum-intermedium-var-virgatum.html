Cestrum intermedium var. virgatum

Cestrum intermedium var. virgatum, commonly known as the Virgin's Bells, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. These flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
