Cyrtophlebium

Cyrtophlebium, commonly known as the noble fern, is a genus of evergreen shrubs native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

These plants are characterized by their dense, leathery foliage and are often cultivated for their ornamental value. Cyrtophlebium species typically have a compact growth habit and produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules. They thrive in humid, shaded environments and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus is notable for its ability to form dense thickets, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
