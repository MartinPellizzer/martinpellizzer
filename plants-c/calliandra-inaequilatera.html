Calliandra inaequilatera

Calliandra inaequilatera, commonly known as the showy calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Calliandra inaequilatera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
