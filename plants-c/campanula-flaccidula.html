Campanula flaccidula

Campanula flaccidula, commonly known as the flaccid bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapting to its often rocky or sandy habitats. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
