Capparis djurica

Capparis djurica, commonly known as the Ethiopian crossberry, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Capparis djurica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
