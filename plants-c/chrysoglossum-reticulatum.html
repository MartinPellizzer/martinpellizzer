Chrysoglossum reticulatum

Chrysoglossum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined chrysoglossum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered blooms during the spring and summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
