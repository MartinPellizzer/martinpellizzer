Carex gravida var. laxifolia

Carex gravida var. laxifolia, commonly known as the slender bulblet sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. This variant of Carex gravida is distinguished by its loose, spreading foliage and smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
