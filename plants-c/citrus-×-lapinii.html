Citrus × lapinii

Citrus × lapinii, commonly known as the grapefruit.

It is a hybrid citrus species that originated from the cross between sweet orange and pomelo. The plant is characterized by its thornless branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white and fragrant, and it produces large, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, segmented rind.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its juice and is known for its tart flavor and high vitamin C content.
