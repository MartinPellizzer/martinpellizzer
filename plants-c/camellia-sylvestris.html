Camellia sylvestris

Camellia sylvestris, commonly known as the wild camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Camellia sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.

It also has historical significance in traditional medicine and tea production.
