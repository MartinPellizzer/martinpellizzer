Cassia marginata

Cassia marginata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia marginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
