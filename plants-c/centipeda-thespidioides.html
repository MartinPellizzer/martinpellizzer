Centipeda thespidioides

Centipeda thespidioides, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Centipeda thespidioides is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
