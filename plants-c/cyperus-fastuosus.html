Cyperus fastuosus

Cyperus fastuosus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, purple to pink flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
