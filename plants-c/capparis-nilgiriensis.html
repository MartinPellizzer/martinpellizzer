Capparis nilgiriensis

Capparis nilgiriensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, capsaicin-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a waxy texture, while its stems are often covered in thorns.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
