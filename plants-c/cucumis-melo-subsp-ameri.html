Cucumis melo subsp. ameri

Cucumis melo subsp.

ameri, commonly known as the Casaba melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spherical, yellow-orange rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow-petaled flowers, typical of the Cucumis genus. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is often used in fresh consumption or as a dessert melon.
