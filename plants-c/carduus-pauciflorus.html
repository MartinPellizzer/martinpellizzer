Carduus pauciflorus

Carduus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The few-flowered thistle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
