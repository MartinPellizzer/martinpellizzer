Carex kelloggii var. impressa

Carex kelloggii var. impressa, commonly known as the impressed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are generally narrow and have a distinct, slightly compressed appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
