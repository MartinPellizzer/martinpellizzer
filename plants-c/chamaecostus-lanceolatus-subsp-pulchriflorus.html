Chamaecostus lanceolatus subsp. pulchriflorus

Chamaecostus lanceolatus subsp.

pulchriflorus, commonly known as the beautiful-flowered costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
