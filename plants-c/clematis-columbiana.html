Clematis columbiana

Clematis columbiana, commonly known as the Vancouver clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in cooler climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
