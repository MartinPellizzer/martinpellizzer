Chelone glabra var. typica

Chelone glabra var. typica, commonly known as white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, smooth, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
