Colchicum hierrense subsp. macrospermum

Colchicum hierrense subsp.

macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded autumn crocus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often persist through winter, while its stem is erect and bears the showy inflorescence. Its distinctive feature is the large, elongated seeds it produces, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
