Crotalaria crenata

Crotalaria crenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Crotalaria crenata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
