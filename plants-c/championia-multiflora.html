Championia multiflora

Championia multiflora, commonly known as the multibract fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it is often found in cool, moist forest environments.

Championia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
