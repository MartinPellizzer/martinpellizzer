Campanula creutzburgii

Campanula creutzburgii, commonly known as the Creutzburg bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Campanula creutzburgii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
