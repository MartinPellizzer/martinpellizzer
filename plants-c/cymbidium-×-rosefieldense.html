Cymbidium × rosefieldense

Cymbidium × rosefieldense, commonly known as the rosefield orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often growing in a fan shape.

Cymbidium × rosefieldense prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in cool, humid environments.
