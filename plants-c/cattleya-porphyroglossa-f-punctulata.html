Cattleya porphyroglossa f. punctulata

Cattleya porphyroglossa f. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive purple and white coloration, and its petals often display intricate spotting. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and fragrance.
