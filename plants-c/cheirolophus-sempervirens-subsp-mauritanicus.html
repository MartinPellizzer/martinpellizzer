Cheirolophus sempervirens subsp. mauritanicus

Cheirolophus sempervirens subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in Mediterranean climates.
