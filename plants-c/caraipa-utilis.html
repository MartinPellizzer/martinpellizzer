Caraipa utilis

Caraipa utilis, commonly known as the caraipa plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela.

It is a woody shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Caraipa utilis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
