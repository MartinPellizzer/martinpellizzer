Chlamydia

Chlamydia, commonly known as a genus of bacteria, includes several species that are significant in both plant and human pathology.

In botanical contexts, certain Chlamydia species can act as endosymbionts or pathogens in various plant species, influencing growth and development. These bacteria often reside within specialized structures called vacuoles within host cells. They are obligate intracellular parasites, meaning they cannot survive outside of a host cell.

The study of Chlamydia in plants contributes to understanding microbial interactions and potential agricultural impacts.
