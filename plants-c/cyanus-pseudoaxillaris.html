Cyanus pseudoaxillaris

Cyanus pseudoaxillaris, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it often grows in dry, rocky soils.

Cyanus pseudoaxillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
