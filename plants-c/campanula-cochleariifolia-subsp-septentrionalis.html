Campanula cochleariifolia subsp. septentrionalis

Campanula cochleariifolia subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern bellflower, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
