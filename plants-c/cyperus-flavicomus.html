Cyperus flavicomus

Cyperus flavicomus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus flavicomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
