Carex macra

Carex macra, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, greenish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex macra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
