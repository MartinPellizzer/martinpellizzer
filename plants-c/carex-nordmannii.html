Carex nordmannii

Carex nordmannii, commonly known as Nordmann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly in China, Korea, and Japan. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a dense, compact growth habit. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Carex nordmannii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
