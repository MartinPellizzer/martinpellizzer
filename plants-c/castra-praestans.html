Castra praestans

Castra praestans, commonly known as the "showy tower" or "towering fortress," is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of yellow and orange, often with a prominent central disk.

Castra praestans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
