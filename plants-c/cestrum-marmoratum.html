Cestrum marmoratum

Cestrum marmoratum, commonly known as the marble vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, marbled-patterned leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.

Cestrum marmoratum is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-drained soil and regular watering during the growing season.
