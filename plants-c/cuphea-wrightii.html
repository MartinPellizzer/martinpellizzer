Cuphea wrightii

Cuphea wrightii, commonly known as the scarlet cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea wrightii is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
