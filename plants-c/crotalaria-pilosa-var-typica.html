Crotalaria pilosa var. typica

Crotalaria pilosa var. typica, commonly known as the hairy crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, often with a distinctive hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
