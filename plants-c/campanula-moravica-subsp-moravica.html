Campanula moravica subsp. moravica

Campanula moravica subsp.

moravica, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
