Coptis trifolia var. groenlandica

Coptis trifolia var. groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland goldenthread, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments. The plant features a creeping rhizome and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids similar to those found in other members of the Ranunculaceae family.
