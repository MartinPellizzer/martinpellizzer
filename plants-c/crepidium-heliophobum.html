Crepidium heliophobum

Crepidium heliophobum, commonly known as the sun-avoiding orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves. The plant produces delicate, elongated flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
