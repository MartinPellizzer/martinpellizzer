Celastrus tenuispinus

Celastrus tenuispinus, commonly known as the slenderleaf staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in small umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
