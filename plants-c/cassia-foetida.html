Cassia foetida

Cassia foetida, commonly known as the stinking cassia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia foetida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Its name derives from the strong, unpleasant odor of its flowers and leaves.
