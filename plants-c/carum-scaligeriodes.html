Carum scaligeriodes

Carum scaligeriodes, commonly known as the slender caraway, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender stems and small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a subtle, spicy aroma.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and mild culinary uses.
