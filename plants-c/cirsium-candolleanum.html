Cirsium candolleanum

Cirsium candolleanum, commonly known as the Candolle's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
