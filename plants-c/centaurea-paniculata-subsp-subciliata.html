Centaurea paniculata subsp. subciliata

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

subciliata, commonly known as the subciliate cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, paniculate inflorescences.

The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
