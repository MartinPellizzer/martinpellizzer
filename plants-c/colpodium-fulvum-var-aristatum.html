Colpodium fulvum var. aristatum

Colpodium fulvum var. aristatum, commonly known as the hairy colpodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
