Carex inornata

Carex inornata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Carex inornata has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
