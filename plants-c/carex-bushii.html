Carex bushii

Carex bushii, commonly known as Bush's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually green to reddish in color.

Carex bushii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
