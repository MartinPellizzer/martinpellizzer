Calyptrion excelsum

Calyptrion excelsum, commonly known as the skyward lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy white flowers with a distinctive purple hue at the base. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white coating that helps reflect excess sunlight.

Calyptrion excelsum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
