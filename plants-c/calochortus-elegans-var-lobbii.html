Calochortus elegans var. lobbii

Calochortus elegans var. lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's fairygarden, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the coastal regions of California.

This perennial herbaceous plant features delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure, often displaying shades of pink, lavender, and white. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
