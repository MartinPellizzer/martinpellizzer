Calepina irregularis

Calepina irregularis, commonly known as the irregular vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Calepina irregularis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
