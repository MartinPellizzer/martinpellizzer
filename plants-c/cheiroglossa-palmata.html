Cheiroglossa palmata

Cheiroglossa palmata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It has a short stem with a single, fleshy leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of hair.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.
