Capsella bursa-pastoris rubiginosa

Capsella bursa-pastoris rubiginosa, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. The plant typically has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions worldwide.
