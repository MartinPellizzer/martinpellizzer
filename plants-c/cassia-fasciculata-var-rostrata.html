Cassia fasciculata var. rostrata

Cassia fasciculata var. rostrata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
