Cheilanthes gigantea

Cheilanthes gigantea, commonly known as the giant rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen fronds that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

This hardy fern is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
