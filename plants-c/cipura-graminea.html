Cipura graminea

Cipura graminea, commonly known as the grassy cipura, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in areas with moist, tropical climates. Cipura graminea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to colonize disturbed soils effectively.
