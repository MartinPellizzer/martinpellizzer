Commelina africana var. boehmiana

Commelina africana var. boehmiana, commonly known as the African spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by a prominent white or yellow spatulate bract. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
