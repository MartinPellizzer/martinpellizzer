Ceropegia hirsuta var. ophiocephala

Ceropegia hirsuta var. ophiocephala, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, tuberous roots. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
