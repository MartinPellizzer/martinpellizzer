Calpidochlamys

Calpidochlamys, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of clustered inflorescences. The plants typically have fleshy, strap-like leaves and produce showy, often fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar, Calpidochlamys species are valued for their ornamental appeal.

Due to their specific habitat requirements, they are often cultivated in controlled environments.
