Carex longipedicellata

Carex longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
