Carlina rhopalachyron

Carlina rhopalachyron, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and conical, spiky flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, dry fruits called achenes.

Carlina rhopalachyron is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
