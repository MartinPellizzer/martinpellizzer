Cissus subdiaphana

Cissus subdiaphana, commonly known as the translucent cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thin, waxy leaves that often appear translucent or semi-transparent, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are flexible and can climb using tendrils, making it well adapted to growing in shaded or humid environments.

Cissus subdiaphana is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and air-purifying qualities.
