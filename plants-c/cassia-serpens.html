Cassia serpens

Cassia serpens, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in the dry season. Its flowers are followed by long, bean-like pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds.

Cassia serpens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
