Combretum chionanthoides

Combretum chionanthoides, commonly known as the white-flowered combretum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
