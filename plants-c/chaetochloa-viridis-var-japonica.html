Chaetochloa viridis var. japonica

Chaetochloa viridis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese green threadgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and riparian ecosystems.
