Chlorophytum blepharophyllum var. amplexicaule

Chlorophytum blepharophyllum var. amplexicaule, commonly known as the Cape Ivy or African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This varietal is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
