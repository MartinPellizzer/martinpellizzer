Campanula bellidifolia subsp. saxifraga

Campanula bellidifolia subsp.

saxifraga, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
