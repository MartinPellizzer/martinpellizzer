Cassia occidentalis var. scabriuscula

Cassia occidentalis var. scabriuscula, commonly known as rough cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, feathery spikes, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark contains compounds used in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
