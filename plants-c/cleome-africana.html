Cleome africana

Cleome africana, commonly known as the African spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and often colorful inflorescences that resemble spider webs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branched.

Cleome africana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
