Croton semivestitus

Croton semivestitus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
