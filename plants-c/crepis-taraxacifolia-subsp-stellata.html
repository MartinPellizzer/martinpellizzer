Crepis taraxacifolia subsp. stellata

Crepis taraxacifolia subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the starry hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Its subspecific name, *stellata*, refers to the star-like appearance of its flower heads.
