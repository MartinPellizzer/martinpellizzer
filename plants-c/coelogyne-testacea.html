Coelogyne testacea

Coelogyne testacea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and erect, hairy pseudobulbs. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and dark green, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the air.

Coelogyne testacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated for its beauty.
