Carlina nebrodensis var. longifolia

Carlina nebrodensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are elongated and covered with stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
