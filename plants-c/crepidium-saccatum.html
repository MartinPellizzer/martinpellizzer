Crepidium saccatum

Crepidium saccatum, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure on the stem, which is believed to aid in water retention. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a delicate fragrance.

Crepidium saccatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
