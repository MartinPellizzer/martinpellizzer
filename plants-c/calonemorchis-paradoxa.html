Calonemorchis paradoxa

Calonemorchis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, elongated flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the forest floor or among moss-covered rocks. Its botanical features include slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, adapted to its specific ecological niche.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Calonemorchis paradoxa is considered a conservation concern.
