Chrysaea aurea

Chrysaea aurea, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Chrysaea aurea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking color and delicate appearance.
