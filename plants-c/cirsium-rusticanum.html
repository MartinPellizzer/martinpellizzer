Cirsium rusticanum

Cirsium rusticanum, commonly known as the rusty thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can be found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides. The plant features spiny, gray-green leaves with a distinctive rusty-colored underside, and its flowers are purple to pink in color, appearing in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Cirsium rusticanum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
