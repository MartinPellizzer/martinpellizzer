Cassia sophera var. pubescens

Cassia sophera var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
