Capparis coccolobifolia

Capparis coccolobifolia, commonly known as the Caribbean coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong scent.

Capparis coccolobifolia is often found in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
