Campylotropis macrocarpa var. alba

Campylotropis macrocarpa var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea vine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of small, white, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks. Its fruit is a hairy, legume pod that matures to a yellowish-brown color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
