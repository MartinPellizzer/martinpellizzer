Calanthe villosagastris

Calanthe villosagastris, commonly known as the hairy-stomach calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Calanthe villosagastris is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
