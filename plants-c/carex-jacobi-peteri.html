Carex jacobi-peteri

Carex jacobi-peteri, commonly known as the Jacob-Peter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex jacobi-peteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
