Camaridium dendrobioides

Camaridium dendrobioides, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, airy substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
