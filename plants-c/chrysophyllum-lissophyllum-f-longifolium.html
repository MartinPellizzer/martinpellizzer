Chrysophyllum lissophyllum f. longifolium

Chrysophyllum lissophyllum f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved sweet cheese tree, is a variant of the sweet cheese tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, yellow fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native habitat.
