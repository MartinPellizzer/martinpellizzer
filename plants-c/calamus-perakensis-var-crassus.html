Calamus perakensis var. crassus

Calamus perakensis var. crassus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its robust, thick rhizomes and long, slender leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swampy forests and wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
