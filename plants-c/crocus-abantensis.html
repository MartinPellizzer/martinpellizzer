Crocus abantensis

Crocus abantensis, commonly known as the Abantian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive, narrow shape.
