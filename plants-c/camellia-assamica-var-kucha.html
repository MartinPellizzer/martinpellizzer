Camellia assamica var. kucha

Camellia assamica var. kucha, commonly known as the Kuchan camellia, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and showy, double flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits containing two to three seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in tea production.
