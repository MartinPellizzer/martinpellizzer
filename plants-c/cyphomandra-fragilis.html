Cyphomandra fragilis

Cyphomandra fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant potato vine, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers and edible, tuberous roots that resemble potatoes. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, while its fruits are small, yellow berries that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and edible parts, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for its potential culinary uses.
