Calamagrostis subulata

Calamagrostis subulata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color.

Calamagrostis subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
