Clematis pitcheri var. pitcheri

Clematis pitcheri var. pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the central and eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
