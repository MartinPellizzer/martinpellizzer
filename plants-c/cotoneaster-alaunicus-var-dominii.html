Cotoneaster alaunicus var. dominii

Cotoneaster alaunicus var. dominii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
