Cyperus hooperae

Cyperus hooperae, commonly known as the Hoopera flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus hooperae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for aquatic life.
