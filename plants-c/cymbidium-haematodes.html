Cymbidium haematodes

Cymbidium haematodes, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as mountain forests. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in less fertile soils.

Cymbidium haematodes is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
