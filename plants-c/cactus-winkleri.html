Cactus winkleri

Cactus winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water efficiently. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to extreme heat and drought conditions. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
