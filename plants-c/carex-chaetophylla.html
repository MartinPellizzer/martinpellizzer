Carex chaetophylla

Carex chaetophylla, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex chaetophylla is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
