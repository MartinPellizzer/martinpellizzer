Cuphea dibrachiata

Cuphea dibrachiata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea dibrachiata is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 feet, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.
