Callicarpa anomala

Callicarpa anomala, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its striking red fruits in the fall. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Callicarpa anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
