Croton bractifer

Croton bractifer, commonly known as the bract-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in clusters. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
