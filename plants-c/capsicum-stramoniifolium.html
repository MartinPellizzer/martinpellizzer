Capsicum stramoniifolium

Capsicum stramoniifolium, commonly known as the wild bell pepper, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, while the fruit is typically a small, green, bell-shaped capsule. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

This species is often grown as a wild relative of cultivated peppers, providing genetic diversity for breeding programs.
