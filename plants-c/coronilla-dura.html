Coronilla dura

Coronilla dura, commonly known as hard crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Coronilla dura is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
