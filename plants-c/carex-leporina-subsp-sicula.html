Carex leporina subsp. sicula

Carex leporina subsp.

sicula, commonly known as Sicilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Sicilian sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
