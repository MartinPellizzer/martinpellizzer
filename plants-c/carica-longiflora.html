Carica longiflora

Carica longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, aromatic fruit that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Carica longiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic importance in traditional medicine.
