Calamus penibukanensis

Calamus penibukanensis, commonly known as the Penibukan reed, is a species of arum native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in lowland forests. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its ecological role within its native habitat.

The species is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to humid environments.
