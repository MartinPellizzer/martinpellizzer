Cinnamomum siltepecanum

Cinnamomum siltepecanum, commonly known as the Mexican cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique fragrance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
