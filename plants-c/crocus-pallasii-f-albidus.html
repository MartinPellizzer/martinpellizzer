Crocus pallasii f. albidus

Crocus pallasii f. albidus, commonly known as the white-flowered Pallas' crocus, is a botanical variant of the Pallas' crocus, characterized by its pale to white petals.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of the Caucasus and Central Asia. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy, cup-shaped flowers that are often fragrant. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and cold hardiness.
