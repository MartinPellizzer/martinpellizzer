Cuphea cyanea f. strigillosa

Cuphea cyanea f. strigillosa, commonly known as the velvet cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
