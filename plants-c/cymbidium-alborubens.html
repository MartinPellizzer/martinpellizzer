Cymbidium alborubens

Cymbidium alborubens, commonly known as the red-lip orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear multiple racemes of fragrant, showy flowers. Its flowers are typically white with a distinctive red or pink lip, which is a key identifying feature.

Cymbidium alborubens thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
