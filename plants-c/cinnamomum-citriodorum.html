Cinnamomum citriodorum

Cinnamomum citriodorum, commonly known as lemon grass, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a strong lemony aroma. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oils, particularly citral, are widely used in perfumes, aromatherapy, and as a natural insect repellent.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
