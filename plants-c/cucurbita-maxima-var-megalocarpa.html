Cucurbita maxima var. megalocarpa

Cucurbita maxima var. megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often orange-colored fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The fruit's thick, smooth rind and high sugar content make it popular for both culinary and competitive purposes.
