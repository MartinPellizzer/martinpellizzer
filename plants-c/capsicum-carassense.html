Capsicum carassense

Capsicum carassense, commonly known as the Carpathian pepper or Carpathian chili, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a rough texture and a serrated margin. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, followed by bright red, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its spicy flavor and is often used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
