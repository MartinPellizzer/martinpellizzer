Cassia gaumeri

Cassia gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Cassia gaumeri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
