Cerasus vulgaris var. albidus

Cerasus vulgaris var. albidus, commonly known as the white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The white cherry is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and as a shade tree.
