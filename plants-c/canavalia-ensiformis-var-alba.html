Canavalia ensiformis var. alba

Canavalia ensiformis var. alba, commonly known as white jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
