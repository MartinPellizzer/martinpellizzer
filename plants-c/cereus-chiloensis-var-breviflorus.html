Cereus chiloensis var. breviflorus

Cereus chiloensis var. breviflorus, commonly known as the Chilean night-blooming cereus, is a cactus native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in clusters, and it is well adapted to arid and coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with minimal water.
