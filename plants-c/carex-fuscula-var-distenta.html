Carex fuscula var. distenta

Carex fuscula var. distenta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and usually have a distinct midrib. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
