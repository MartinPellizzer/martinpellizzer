Calamagrostis drummondii

Calamagrostis drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer to early fall.

Calamagrostis drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
