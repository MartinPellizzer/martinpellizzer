Caulinia pectinata

Caulinia pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Caulinia pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
