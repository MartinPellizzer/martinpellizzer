Collea

Collea, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Collea has a slender, branching stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.
