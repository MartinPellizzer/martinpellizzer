Ceropegia aurea

Ceropegia aurea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, greenish-white leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia aurea is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental flowers and ease of care.
