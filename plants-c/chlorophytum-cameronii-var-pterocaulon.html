Chlorophytum cameronii var. pterocaulon

Chlorophytum cameronii var. pterocaulon, commonly known as the Cameron's spider plant, is a unique variant of the spider plant family.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble the legs of a spider, giving it its common name. The plant is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate to bright indirect light. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by delicate, grass-like seed heads.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
