Cyperus gubanii

Cyperus gubanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant typically thrives in wet or marshy environments. Cyperus gubanii is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
