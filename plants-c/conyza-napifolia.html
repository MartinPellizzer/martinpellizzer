Conyza napifolia

Conyza napifolia, commonly known as the common ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
