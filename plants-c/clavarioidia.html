Clavarioidia

Clavarioidia, commonly known as the club fungi, are a group of fungi characterized by their unique, club-shaped or branched fruiting bodies.

These fungi belong to the order Clavarioidales and are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. They are often mistaken for mushrooms due to their shape but lack the typical cap and stem structure. Clavarioidia play an important role in decomposing organic matter and recycling nutrients in ecosystems.

Their diverse forms and colors make them a fascinating subject for mycological study.
