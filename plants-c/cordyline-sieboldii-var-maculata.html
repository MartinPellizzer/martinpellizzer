Cordyline sieboldii var. maculata

Cordyline sieboldii var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in containers or as part of a garden landscape.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
