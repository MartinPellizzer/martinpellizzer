Calamus usitatus var. major

Calamus usitatus var. major, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly in countries like India and Indonesia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes.

Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
