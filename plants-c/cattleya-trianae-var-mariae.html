Cattleya trianae var. mariae

Cattleya trianae var. mariae, commonly known as the Marie's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with intricate patterns and a fragrant scent. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structure. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
