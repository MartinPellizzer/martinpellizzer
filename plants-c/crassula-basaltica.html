Crassula basaltica

Crassula basaltica, commonly known as the Basalt Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Crassula basaltica is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
