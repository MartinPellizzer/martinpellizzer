Cyrtochilum suarezianum

Cyrtochilum suarezianum, commonly known as the Suarez's cyclochilum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help retain moisture.

Cyrtochilum suarezianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
