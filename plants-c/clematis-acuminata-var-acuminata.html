Clematis acuminata var. acuminata

Clematis acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can climb trellises or walls with its strong, twisting stems.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
