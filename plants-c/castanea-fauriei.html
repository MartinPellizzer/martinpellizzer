Castanea fauriei

Castanea fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers that develop into spiny, oval nuts enclosed in a spiky husk.

The nuts are edible when fully mature and are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
