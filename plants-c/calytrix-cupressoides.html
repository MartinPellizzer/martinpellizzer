Calytrix cupressoides

Calytrix cupressoides, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along its branches, giving it a compact, shrub-like appearance. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers in dense, brush-like clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal areas.
