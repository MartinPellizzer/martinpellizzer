Calamintha alpina subsp. granatensis

Calamintha alpina subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their aromatic and mild medicinal properties.
