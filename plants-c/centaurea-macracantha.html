Centaurea macracantha

Centaurea macracantha, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically arranged in a composite head. Centaurea macracantha is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
