Capparis spinosa var. mariana

Capparis spinosa var. mariana, commonly known as the Canary Island caper, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.
