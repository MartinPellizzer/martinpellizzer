Chorizanthe pungens var. pungens

Chorizanthe pungens var. pungens, commonly known as the spiked choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
