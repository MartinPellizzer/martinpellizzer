Convolvulus aitchisonii

Convolvulus aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its stems are hairy and twining, allowing it to climb or trail along the ground.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to retain moisture in harsh environments.
