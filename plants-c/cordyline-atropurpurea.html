Cordyline atropurpurea

Cordyline atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep purple or red, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Cordyline atropurpurea is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
