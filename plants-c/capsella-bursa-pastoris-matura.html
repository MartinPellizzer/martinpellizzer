Capsella bursa-pastoris matura

Capsella bursa-pastoris matura, commonly known as the common weed or shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as fields, roadsides, and gardens. It produces small white or yellowish flowers and has a fibrous root system.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
