Cassia albida

Cassia albida, commonly known as white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its showy, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Cassia albida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
