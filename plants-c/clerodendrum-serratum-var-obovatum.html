Clerodendrum serratum var. obovatum

Clerodendrum serratum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved champa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, obovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
