Castalia pulchella

Castalia pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful castalia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Castalia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
