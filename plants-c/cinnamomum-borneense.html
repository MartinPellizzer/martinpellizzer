Cinnamomum borneense

Cinnamomum borneense, commonly known as Bornean cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by egg-shaped berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a pleasant, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has ecological and economic significance in its native habitat.
