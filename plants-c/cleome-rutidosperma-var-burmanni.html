Cleome rutidosperma var. burmanni

Cleome rutidosperma var. burmanni, commonly known as Burmann's cleome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome rutidosperma var. burmanni is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
