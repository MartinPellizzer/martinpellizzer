Cyathodes tahitensis

Cyathodes tahitensis, commonly known as Tahitian cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being elongated and the female cones more compact. It is native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia and thrives in tropical climates.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for conservation efforts.
