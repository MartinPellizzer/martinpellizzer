Crassula coronata

Crassula coronata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and attractive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments with minimal care.
