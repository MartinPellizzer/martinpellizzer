Cardamine hirsuta var. pterocarpa

Cardamine hirsuta var. pterocarpa, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the common watercress species.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. Its small white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
