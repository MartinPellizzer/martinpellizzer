Celastrus japonicus

Celastrus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese staff vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens.
