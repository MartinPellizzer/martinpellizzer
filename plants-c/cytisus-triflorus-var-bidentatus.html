Cytisus triflorus var. bidentatus

Cytisus triflorus var. bidentatus, commonly known as the two-toothed broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems with small, compound leaves that have a distinctive two-toothed appearance on the margins. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
