Chamaepitys chasmophila

Chamaepitys chasmophila, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a hardy coniferous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to cold, dry environments and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species often found in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
