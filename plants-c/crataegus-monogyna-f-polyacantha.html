Crataegus monogyna f. polyacantha

Crataegus monogyna f. polyacantha, commonly known as the bristly hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
