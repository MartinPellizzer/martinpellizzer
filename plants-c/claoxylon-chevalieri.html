Claoxylon chevalieri

Claoxylon chevalieri, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming at night and attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its roots anchor it in shallow waters, while its leaves support a diverse ecosystem on the water's surface.

Claoxylon chevalieri plays an important role in maintaining water quality and habitat diversity in its native wetland environments.
