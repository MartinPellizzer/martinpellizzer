Cynanchum extensum

Cynanchum extensum, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which is known for its milkweed-like characteristics. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to several feet in height.

Cynanchum extensum is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
