Carex × uechtritziana

Carex × uechtritziana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This species is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and swampy areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
