Centella villosa var. latifolia

Centella villosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf centella, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, hairy leaves and creeping growth habit. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is closely related to other centella species, such as Centella asiatica, and shares similar botanical features.
