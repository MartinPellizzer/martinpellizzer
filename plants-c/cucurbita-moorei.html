Cucurbita moorei

Cucurbita moorei, commonly known as the Moore's pumpkin, is a species of squash native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, round, pale green fruits with a rough, warty surface. The plant is a vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many members of the Cucurbitaceae family.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, nutty flavor, and is often used in traditional Mexican cuisine.
