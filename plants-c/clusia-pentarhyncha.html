Clusia pentarhyncha

Clusia pentarhyncha, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. These flowers are followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds.

Clusia pentarhyncha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
