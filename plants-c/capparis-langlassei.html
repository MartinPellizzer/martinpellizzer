Capparis langlassei

Capparis langlassei, commonly known as the langlasse's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

Capparis langlassei is adapted to grow in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
