Chrysanthemum × marginatum

Chrysanthemum × marginatum, commonly known as the marguerite chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species originating from crosses between *Chrysanthemum indicum* and *Chrysanthemum morifolium*.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and an abundance of daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant, long-lasting flowers that are popular in gardens and floral arrangements. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both container and in-ground planting.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
