Chrysocoma strigosa

Chrysocoma strigosa, commonly known as the African hairless thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Chrysocoma strigosa is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
