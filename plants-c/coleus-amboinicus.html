Coleus amboinicus

Coleus amboinicus, commonly known as Java mint, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Coleus amboinicus is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
