Cassia striata

Cassia striata, commonly known as the flame tree or golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia striata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
