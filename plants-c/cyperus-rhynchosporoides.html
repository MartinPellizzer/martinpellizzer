Cyperus rhynchosporoides

Cyperus rhynchosporoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus rhynchosporoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
