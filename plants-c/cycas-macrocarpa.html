Cycas macrocarpa

Cycas macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a striking member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to New Caledonia and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the female cones being particularly massive and ornate. Cycas macrocarpa is a slow-growing, ancient plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique morphology and evolutionary history make it a significant species in botanical studies.
