Coleus gomphophyllus

Coleus gomphophyllus, commonly known as the flameleaf coleus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting green veins. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, while the small, tubular flowers are typically less showy.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
