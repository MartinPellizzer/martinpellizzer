Chilochloa collina

Chilochloa collina, commonly known as the mountain grass or New Zealand mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in high-altitude regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of New Zealand's native flora.
