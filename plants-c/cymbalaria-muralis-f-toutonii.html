Cymbalaria muralis f. toutonii

Cymbalaria muralis f. toutonii, commonly known as the wall geometer or rock snail violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well. This plant thrives in rocky or wall environments, often growing in cracks and crevices.

It is considered a rare and often overlooked species within its native range.
