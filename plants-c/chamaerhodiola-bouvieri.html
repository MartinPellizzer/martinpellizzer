Chamaerhodiola bouvieri

Chamaerhodiola bouvieri, commonly known as the Tibetan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia, particularly Tibet and the Himalayas.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in cold, arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in its dry environment.

Chamaerhodiola bouvieri is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
