Centaurea argentea subsp. chionantha

Centaurea argentea subsp.

chionantha, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, pale blue to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically forms dense mats and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its subspecies name, *chionantha*, refers to its snowy or white appearance, particularly in the foliage.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative appeal and hardy nature.
