Camellia suaveolens

Camellia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.
