Carex aquatilis var. minor

Carex aquatilis var. minor, commonly known as the small water sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along stream edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
