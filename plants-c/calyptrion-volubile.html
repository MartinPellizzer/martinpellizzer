Calyptrion volubile

Calyptrion volubile, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
