Cirsium praealtum

Cirsium praealtum, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and a distinctive prickly texture.

Cirsium praealtum plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
