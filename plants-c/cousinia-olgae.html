Cousinia olgae

Cousinia olgae, commonly known as the Kashmiri globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Cousinia olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
