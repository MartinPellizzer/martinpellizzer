Centaurea kestanica

Centaurea kestanica, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers range in color from pale pink to deep purple.

Centaurea kestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
