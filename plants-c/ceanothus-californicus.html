Ceanothus californicus

Ceanothus californicus, commonly known as California ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often aromatic and can vary in shape depending on the habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Ceanothus californicus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
