Cyathea andicola

Cyathea andicola, commonly known as the Andean tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments at high elevations. Its rhizome system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or soil substrates.

Cyathea andicola plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
