Coix stigmatosa

Coix stigmatosa, commonly known as the stigmatose coix, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or yellowish in color. Coix stigmatosa is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
