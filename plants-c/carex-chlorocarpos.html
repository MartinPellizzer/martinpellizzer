Carex chlorocarpos

Carex chlorocarpos, commonly known as the green-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex chlorocarpos plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
