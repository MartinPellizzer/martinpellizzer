Coffea costatifructa

Coffea costatifructa, commonly known as the costate-fruited coffee.

It is a species native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Ethiopia and Kenya. The plant produces small, red berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. Its leaves are elliptical with a waxy texture and a serrated margin.

The plant is characterized by its unique fruit structure, with a ridged or costate surface on the outer layer of the fruit.
