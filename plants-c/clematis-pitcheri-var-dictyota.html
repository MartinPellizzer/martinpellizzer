Clematis pitcheri var. dictyota

Clematis pitcheri var. dictyota, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh mountain conditions.
