Cyathea carrii

Cyathea carrii, commonly known as the Carri's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural support, while the fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea carrii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
