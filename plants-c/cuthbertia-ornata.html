Cuthbertia ornata

Cuthbertia ornata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive dark purple or reddish hue at the base.

Cuthbertia ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
