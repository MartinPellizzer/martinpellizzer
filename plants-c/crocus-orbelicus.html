Crocus orbelicus

Crocus orbelicus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which appear before the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
