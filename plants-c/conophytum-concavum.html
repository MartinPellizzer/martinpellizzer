Conophytum concavum

Conophytum concavum, commonly known as the "Cushion Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum concavum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
