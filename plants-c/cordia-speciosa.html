Cordia speciosa

Cordia speciosa, commonly known as the showy cordia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring thick, dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Cordia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
