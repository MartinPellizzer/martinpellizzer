Cynanchum pleianthum

Cynanchum pleianthum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the white milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which is toxic. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cynanchum pleianthum is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
