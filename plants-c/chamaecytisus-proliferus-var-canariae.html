Chamaecytisus proliferus var. canariae

Chamaecytisus proliferus var. canariae, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Its vigorous growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
