Cypripedium concolor var. sulphurinum

Cypripedium concolor var. sulphurinum, commonly known as the sulphur yellow lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

This variant is characterized by its vivid yellow petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that serves as a pollinator attractant. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. The plant has a short, fleshy rhizome and produces a single, showy flower with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
