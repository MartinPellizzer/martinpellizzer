Cornus × dubia

Cornus × dubia, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become prominent in winter, and its showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Cornus × dubia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
