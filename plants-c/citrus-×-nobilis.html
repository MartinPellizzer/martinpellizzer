Citrus × nobilis

Citrus × nobilis, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a natural cross between the mandarin and the pummelo.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and easy-to-peel fruits, which are typically orange in color and have a thin, segmented rind. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and produces flowers that are white and fragrant.

Its fruit is widely cultivated for consumption both fresh and processed, and it is valued for its high vitamin C content and aromatic properties.
