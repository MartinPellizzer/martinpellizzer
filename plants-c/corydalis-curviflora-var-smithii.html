Corydalis curviflora var. smithii

Corydalis curviflora var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's curlflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, slender stems that often curl as they grow, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Corydalis curviflora var. smithii is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
