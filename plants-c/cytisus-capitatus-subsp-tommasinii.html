Cytisus capitatus subsp. tommasinii

Cytisus capitatus subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky environments.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various climates.
