Cirsium × woodwardii

Cirsium × woodwardii, commonly known as the woodland thistle, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Cirsium × woodwardii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
