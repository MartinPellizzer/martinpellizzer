Clidemia plumosa

Clidemia plumosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Clidemia plumosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some areas.
