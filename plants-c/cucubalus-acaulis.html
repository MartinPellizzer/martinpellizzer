Cucubalus acaulis

Cucubalus acaulis, commonly known as the stemless cucumber or false cucumber, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant is characterized by its lack of a visible stem, with leaves directly emerging from the root system. Its flowers are typically yellow and are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that resemble cucumbers in appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which can be used in various culinary applications.
