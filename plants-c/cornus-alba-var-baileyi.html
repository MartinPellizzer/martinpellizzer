Cornus alba var. baileyi

Cornus alba var. baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, multi-stemmed growth with opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its bark is smooth and often displays a distinctive pinkish or reddish hue, especially in winter.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
