Caruelina pubescens

Caruelina pubescens, commonly known as the hairy caruelina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Caruelina pubescens is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
