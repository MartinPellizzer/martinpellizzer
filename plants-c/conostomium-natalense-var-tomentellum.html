Conostomium natalense var. tomentellum

Conostomium natalense var. tomentellum, commonly known as the Natal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also covered in a fine layer of soft hair.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
