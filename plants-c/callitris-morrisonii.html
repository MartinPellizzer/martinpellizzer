Callitris morrisonii

Callitris morrisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's white cypress, is a species of conifer native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, inland regions.

Due to its slow growth and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
