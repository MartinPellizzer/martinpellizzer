Castanea vesca var. variegata

Castanea vesca var. variegata, commonly known as the variable horse chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which often display striking variegation of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two chestnuts, which are edible when ripe.
