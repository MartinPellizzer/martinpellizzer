Campanula baicalensis

Campanula baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Campanula baicalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
