Ceropegia campanuliformis

Ceropegia campanuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.
