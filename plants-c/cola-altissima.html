Cola altissima

Cola altissima, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces bright red, woody pods that contain stimulant-rich seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
