Calystegia silvatica subsp. pulchra

Calystegia silvatica subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with five petals and a prominent central spur. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
