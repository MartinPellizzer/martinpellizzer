Cerasus mahaleb

Cerasus mahaleb, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cerasus mahaleb is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
