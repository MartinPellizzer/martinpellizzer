Camaridium graminifolium

Camaridium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leafed camaridium, is a rare and delicate plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator species for ecosystem health.
