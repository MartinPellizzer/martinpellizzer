Cynanchum sieboldii

Cynanchum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
