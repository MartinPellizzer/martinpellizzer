Carex extensa var. tenuifolia

Carex extensa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
