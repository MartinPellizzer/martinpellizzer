Cerasus parvifolia

Cerasus parvifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
