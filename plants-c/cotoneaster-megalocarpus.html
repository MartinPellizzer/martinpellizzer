Cotoneaster megalocarpus

Cotoneaster megalocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant seasonal display and adaptability to various soil types.

Cotoneaster megalocarpus is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and small mammals.
