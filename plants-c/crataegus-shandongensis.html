Crataegus shandongensis

Crataegus shandongensis, commonly known as the Shandong hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is typically found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Crataegus shandongensis is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
