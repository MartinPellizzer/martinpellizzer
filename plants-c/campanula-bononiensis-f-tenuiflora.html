Campanula bononiensis f. tenuiflora

Campanula bononiensis f. tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and borders.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
