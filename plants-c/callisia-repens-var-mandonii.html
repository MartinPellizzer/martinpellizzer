Callisia repens var. mandonii

Callisia repens var. mandonii, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, rhizomatous perennial that forms dense mats of foliage. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
