Carduus pycnocephalus var. albidus

Carduus pycnocephalus var. albidus, commonly known as the white musk thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its floral structure includes both ray and disk flowers, with the latter being tubular and arranged in a central cluster.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
