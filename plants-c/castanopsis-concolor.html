Castanopsis concolor

Castanopsis concolor, commonly known as the Chinese tallow or Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood. The tree produces small, oval-shaped nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule, which typically ripens in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
