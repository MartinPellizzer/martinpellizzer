Cochlospermum gillivraei

Cochlospermum gillivraei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a trumpet-shaped structure. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, seed-containing structures called arils. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically alternate and palmately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
