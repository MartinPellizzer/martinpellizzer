Chlorophytum namorokense

Chlorophytum namorokense, commonly known as the Namoroko spider plant, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces long, arching stems that bear small white or pale green flowers, followed by clusters of bright green, bead-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a distinctive addition to any plant collection.
