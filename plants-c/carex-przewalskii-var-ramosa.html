Carex przewalskii var. ramosa

Carex przewalskii var. ramosa, commonly known as the Przewalski's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into three-lobed, three-angled berries.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
