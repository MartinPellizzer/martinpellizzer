Cyperus malabaricus

Cyperus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Cyperus malabaricus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
