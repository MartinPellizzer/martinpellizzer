Carex atherodes

Carex atherodes, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Carex atherodes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.
