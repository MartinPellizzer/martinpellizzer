Cyathea suprapilosa

Cyathea suprapilosa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can span several meters in width.

Cyathea suprapilosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
