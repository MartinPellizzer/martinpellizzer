Crataegus chungtienensis

Crataegus chungtienensis, commonly known as the Chungtien hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

The species is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health properties.
