Comocladia ilicifolia var. glabra

Comocladia ilicifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, white or pale green berries. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its stems that are embedded in the host. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various insect and bird species.
