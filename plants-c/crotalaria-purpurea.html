Crotalaria purpurea

Crotalaria purpurea, commonly known as the purple crownflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
