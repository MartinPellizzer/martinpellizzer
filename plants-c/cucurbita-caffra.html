Cucurbita caffra

Cucurbita caffra, commonly known as the African pumpkin or kheskesh, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and gourds. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, waxy skin that can range in color from green to yellow or orange. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is edible, often used in traditional dishes, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
