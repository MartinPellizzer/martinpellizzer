Crocus hadriaticus var. chrysobelonicus

Crocus hadriaticus var. chrysobelonicus, commonly known as the Adriatic crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and limestone-rich soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
