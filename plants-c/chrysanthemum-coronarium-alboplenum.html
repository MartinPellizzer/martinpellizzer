Chrysanthemum coronarium alboplenum

Chrysanthemum coronarium alboplenum, commonly known as the white-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green to white leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling growth habit that can reach up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
