Chloraea prodigiosa

Chloraea prodigiosa, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and sturdy.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Chloraea prodigiosa is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
