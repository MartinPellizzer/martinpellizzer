Commiphora habessinica var. crenulata

Commiphora habessinica var. crenulata, commonly known as the Habeshan myrrh tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or crenate, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
