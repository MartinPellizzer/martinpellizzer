Caltha kamtschatica

Caltha kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatka buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic flowering plants.
