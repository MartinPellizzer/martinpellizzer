Croton paludosus

Croton paludosus, commonly known as the swamp croton, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, swampy environments. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, unpleasant odor that attracts insects.

Croton paludosus is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
