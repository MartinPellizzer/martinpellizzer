Capparis beneolens

Capparis beneolens, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, round, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red when ripe.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
