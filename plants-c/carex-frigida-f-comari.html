Carex frigida f. comari

Carex frigida f. comari, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to cold and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Due to its cold tolerance, it plays a significant role in Arctic and subarctic ecosystems.
