Carex wallichiana var. miyabei

Carex wallichiana var. miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and water features.
