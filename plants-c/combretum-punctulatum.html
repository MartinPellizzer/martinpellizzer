Combretum punctulatum

Combretum punctulatum, commonly known as the dotted combretum, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often dotted with small, raised glands. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
