Chymocarpus speciosus

Chymocarpus speciosus, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, three-lobed pod that splits open when mature.

Chymocarpus speciosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
