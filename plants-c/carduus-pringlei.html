Carduus pringlei

Carduus pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive lobed shape.

Carduus pringlei is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
