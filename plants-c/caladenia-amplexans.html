Caladenia amplexans

Caladenia amplexans, commonly known as the emu lily, is an orchid native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It features a single, erect stem that bears a single, showy orchid flower with a distinctive greenish-blue hue. The plant has a fleshy, tuberous root system that helps it survive in its arid environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the base.

Caladenia amplexans is a member of the orchid family, which is characterized by its specialized floral structures and complex pollination mechanisms.
