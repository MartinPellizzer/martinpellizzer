Crataegus monogyna var. biserrata

Crataegus monogyna var. biserrata, commonly known as the hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called haws in the autumn, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
