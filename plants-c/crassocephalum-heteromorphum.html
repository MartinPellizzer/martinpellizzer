Crassocephalum heteromorphum

Crassocephalum heteromorphum, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can vary in size. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in disturbed areas.

Crassocephalum heteromorphum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
