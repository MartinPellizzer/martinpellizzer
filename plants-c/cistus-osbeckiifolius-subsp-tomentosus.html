Cistus osbeckiifolius subsp. tomentosus

Cistus osbeckiifolius subsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
