Cornus officinalis

Cornus officinalis, commonly known as Chinese cornel or Shan Zhu Yu, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, berry-like fruits that ripen in autumn. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the dried fruits used in traditional Chinese medicine to nourish the kidneys and replenish vital energy. Its leaves and bark also contain bioactive compounds that contribute to its therapeutic uses.

Cornus officinalis thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and pharmaceutical purposes.
