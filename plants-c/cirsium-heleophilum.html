Cirsium heleophilum

Cirsium heleophilum, commonly known as the sun-loving thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Cirsium heleophilum is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils.
