Combretum perakense

Combretum perakense, commonly known as the Perak combretum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae, which is characterized by its small, often deciduous leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland areas with high humidity and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, with four or five petals, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.
