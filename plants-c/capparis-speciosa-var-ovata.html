Capparis speciosa var. ovata

Capparis speciosa var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf sweet pepper tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The berries are edible when ripe and have a sweet, peppery flavor.
