Cinnamomum puberulum

Cinnamomum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy cinnamon or mountain cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
