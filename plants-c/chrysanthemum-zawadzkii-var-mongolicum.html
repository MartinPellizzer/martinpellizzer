Chrysanthemum zawadzkii var. mongolicum

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii var. mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates. Its foliage is usually gray-green with hairy surfaces, aiding in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
