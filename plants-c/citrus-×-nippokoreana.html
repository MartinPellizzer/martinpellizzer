Citrus × nippokoreana

Citrus × nippokoreana, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or nippokorena, is a hybrid citrus species originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This species is also valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving citrus resistance to diseases and environmental stress.
