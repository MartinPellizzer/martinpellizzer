Chamaecrista calycioides

Chamaecrista calycioides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
