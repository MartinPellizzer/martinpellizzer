Cryptocarya perlucida

Cryptocarya perlucida, commonly known as the glossy ash or white ash, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that have a waxy, glossy appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
