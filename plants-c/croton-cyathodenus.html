Croton cyathodenus

Croton cyathodenus, commonly known as the red croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and orange. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.

Croton cyathodenus prefers warm, humid climates and is commonly grown as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
