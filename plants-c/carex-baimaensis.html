Carex baimaensis

Carex baimaensis, commonly known as the Baima sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountain meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex baimaensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
