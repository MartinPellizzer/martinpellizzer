Convolvulus wheeleri

Convolvulus wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers and twining stems. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a notable species in its native habitat.
