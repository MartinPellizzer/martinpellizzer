Capsicum diversifolium

Capsicum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf pepper, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its diverse leaf shapes and sizes, which vary significantly across different populations. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with varying degrees of lobing.

This species is notable for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its potential in crop improvement programs.
