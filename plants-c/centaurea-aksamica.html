Centaurea aksamica

Centaurea aksamica, commonly known as the Aksam thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Centaurea aksamica is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
