Canthium glaucum subsp. glaucum

Canthium glaucum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are usually covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The fruit is a follicle that contains a single, elongated seed with a tuft of silky hairs.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
