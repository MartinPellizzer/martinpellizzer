Calpurnia aurea

Calpurnia aurea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Calpurnia aurea is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
