Cyperus fuscus subsp. virescens

Cyperus fuscus subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the greenish flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color, and its flowering spikes are clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker green foliage compared to other Cyperus species, making it visually distinct in its native environment.
