Crataegus mackenzii

Crataegus mackenzii, commonly known as the Mackenzie hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Crataegus mackenzii plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
