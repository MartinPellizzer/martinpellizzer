Calliandra amazonica

Calliandra amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian calliandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It also has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion, making it useful in reforestation efforts.

Additionally, its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
