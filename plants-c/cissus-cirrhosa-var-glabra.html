Cissus cirrhosa var. glabra

Cissus cirrhosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on supports.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
