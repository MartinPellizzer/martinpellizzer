Carduus phyllolepis

Carduus phyllolepis, commonly known as the leafy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster, forming a globular inflorescence.

Carduus phyllolepis is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in disturbed or arid environments.
