Chrysopsis floridana var. highlandsensis

Chrysopsis floridana var. highlandsensis, commonly known as the Highlands coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
