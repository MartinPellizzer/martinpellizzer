Calamagrostis sesquiflora

Calamagrostis sesquiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis sesquiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
