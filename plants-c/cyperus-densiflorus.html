Cyperus densiflorus

Cyperus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense spikes, which contribute to its common name.

Cyperus densiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
