Crataegus monogyna f. macrocarpa

Crataegus monogyna f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its notably larger berries compared to the typical species, which are usually red or orange when mature. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows across Europe. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces a fragrant, edible fruit.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
