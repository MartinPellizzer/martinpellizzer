Cytisus aethiopicus

Cytisus aethiopicus, commonly known as the Ethiopian broom, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cytisus aethiopicus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
