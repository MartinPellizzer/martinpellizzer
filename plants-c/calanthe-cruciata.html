Calanthe cruciata

Calanthe cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rhizomatous growth habit and its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals, which gives it its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
