Centaurea scabiosa subsp. spinulosa

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked cudweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia. It features upright, branched stems covered with fine, white hairs.

The plant produces purple to pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which are characteristic of the genus Centaurea. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with small, stiff bristles.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in meadows and rocky soils.
