Calcearia striata

Calcearia striata, commonly known as the striped calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the spring.

Calcearia striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
