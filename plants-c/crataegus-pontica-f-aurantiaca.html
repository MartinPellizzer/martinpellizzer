Crataegus pontica f. aurantiaca

Crataegus pontica f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn species native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-red flowers that appear in early spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, red-pink berry that matures in late summer, providing food for various wildlife species.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
