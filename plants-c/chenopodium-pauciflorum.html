Chenopodium pauciflorum

Chenopodium pauciflorum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture, and the plant produces small, clustered fruits.

Chenopodium pauciflorum is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
