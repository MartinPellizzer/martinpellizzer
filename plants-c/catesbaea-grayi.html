Catesbaea grayi

Catesbaea grayi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria glabrata*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves that are alternately arranged. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible drupes with a sweet, red flesh. Its fruit is widely consumed and used in local cuisines, while its wood is valued for its strength and durability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
