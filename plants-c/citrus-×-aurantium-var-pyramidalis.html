Citrus × aurantium var. pyramidalis

Citrus × aurantium var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Mexican lime or Persian lime, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its elongated, narrow fruit shape.

The plant is a small evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are typically harvested when they reach a mature green color, though they can be left to ripen to a yellowish hue. The peel contains essential oils and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is well-suited to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
