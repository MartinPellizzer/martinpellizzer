Calamus fimbriatus

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often have a fringed appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

Calamus fimbriatus is often found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, tropical conditions.
