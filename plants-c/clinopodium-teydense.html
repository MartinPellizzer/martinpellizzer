Clinopodium teydense

Clinopodium teydense, commonly known as the Teyden savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the small, pinkish flowers appear in whorls along the stem.

Clinopodium teydense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
