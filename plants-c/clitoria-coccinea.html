Clitoria coccinea

Clitoria coccinea, commonly known as the flame bean or the butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright red, butterfly-shaped flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and as natural dyes. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Clitoria coccinea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
