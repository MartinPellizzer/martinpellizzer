Campanula punctata var. litoralis

Campanula punctata var. litoralis, commonly known as the sea hare bellflower, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water in its saline environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky shores or sandy beaches.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it a hardy and resilient species.
