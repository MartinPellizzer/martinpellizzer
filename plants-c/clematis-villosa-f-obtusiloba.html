Clematis villosa f. obtusiloba

Clematis villosa f. obtusiloba, commonly known as the woolly Clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "villosa," meaning "hairy.". The flowers are typically white or pale blue and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is a compact form of the species, with rounded, obtuse leaf lobes, as indicated by "obtusiloba.".

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.
