Crataegus crus-galli f. spinulosa

Crataegus crus-galli f. spinulosa, commonly known as the bristly hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red berries in the fall. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, bristly growth habit and is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
