Cephaelis hemicephaelis

Cephaelis hemicephaelis, commonly known as the two-headed cephaleis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
