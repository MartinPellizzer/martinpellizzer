Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp. boranensis

Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp.

boranensis, commonly known as the Boran flame flower, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
