Cyperus nutans

Cyperus nutans, commonly known as the nodding flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Cyperus nutans is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
