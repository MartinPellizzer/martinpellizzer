Chloris pendula

Chloris pendula, commonly known as the hanging grass or pendulous grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, drooping stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
