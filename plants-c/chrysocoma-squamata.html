Chrysocoma squamata

Chrysocoma squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysocoma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

Chrysocoma squamata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
