Corydalis heterocentra

Corydalis heterocentra, commonly known as the heterocentra lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has slender, ascending stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Corydalis heterocentra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
