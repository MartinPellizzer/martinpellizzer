Cyperus diffusus var. pubisquama

Cyperus diffusus var. pubisquama, commonly known as swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, typically reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

This plant thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, making it a common sight in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.
