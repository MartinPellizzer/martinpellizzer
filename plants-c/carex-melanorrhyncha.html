Carex melanorrhyncha

Carex melanorrhyncha, commonly known as black-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex melanorrhyncha produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence.
