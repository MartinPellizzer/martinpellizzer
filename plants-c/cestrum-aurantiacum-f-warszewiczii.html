Cestrum aurantiacum f. warszewiczii

Cestrum aurantiacum f. warszewiczii, commonly known as the orange-flowered cestrum, is a cultivar of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.
