Coluria potentilloides

Coluria potentilloides, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and it produces small, clustered fruits known as achenes.

Coluria potentilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
