Cassia ripleyana

Cassia ripleyana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Cassia ripleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.
