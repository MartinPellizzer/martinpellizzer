Crataegus cocksii

Crataegus cocksii, commonly known as the Cocks' hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly Arizona and New Mexico, where it thrives in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins.

Crataegus cocksii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
