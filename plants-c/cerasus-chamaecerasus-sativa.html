Cerasus chamaecerasus sativa

Cerasus chamaecerasus sativa, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red or black berries that are edible and widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
