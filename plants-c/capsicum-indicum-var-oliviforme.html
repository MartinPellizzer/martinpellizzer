Capsicum indicum var. oliviforme

Capsicum indicum var. oliviforme, commonly known as the olive-shaped chili pepper, is a variety native to India.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, oval-shaped fruits that range in color from green to red when mature. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit develops from a single ovary and is used both as a spice and in traditional culinary practices.
