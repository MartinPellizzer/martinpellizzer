Cerastium arvense subsp. pallasii

Cerastium arvense subsp.

pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine pubescence.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.
