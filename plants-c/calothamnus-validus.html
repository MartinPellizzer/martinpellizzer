Calothamnus validus

Calothamnus validus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating, contributing to its unique appearance.

Calothamnus validus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
