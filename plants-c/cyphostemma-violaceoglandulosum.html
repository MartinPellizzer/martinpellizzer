Cyphostemma violaceoglandulosum

Cyphostemma violaceoglandulosum, commonly known as the violet glandular cyphostemma, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with violet. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
