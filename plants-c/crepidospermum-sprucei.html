Crepidospermum sprucei

Crepidospermum sprucei, commonly known as the silverleaf creeper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls effectively.
