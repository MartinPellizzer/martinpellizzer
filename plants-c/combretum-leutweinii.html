Combretum leutweinii

Combretum leutweinii, commonly known as the red-stemmed bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Combretum leutweinii is adapted to seasonal rainfall and can withstand periods of drought.
