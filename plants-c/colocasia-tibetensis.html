Colocasia tibetensis

Colocasia tibetensis, commonly known as the Tibetan arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, with a deep green color and a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
