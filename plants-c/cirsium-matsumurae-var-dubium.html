Cirsium matsumurae var. dubium

Cirsium matsumurae var. dubium, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
