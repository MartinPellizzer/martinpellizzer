Convolvulus cantabrica subsp. radicosus

Convolvulus cantabrica subsp.

radicosus, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient species in its native habitat.
