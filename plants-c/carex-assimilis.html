Carex assimilis

Carex assimilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex assimilis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
