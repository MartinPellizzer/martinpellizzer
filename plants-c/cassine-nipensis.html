Cassine nipensis

Cassine nipensis, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of swamps. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy yellow flowers with red centers. It is often found in moist, acidic soils and is adapted to tidal environments.

Cassine nipensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
