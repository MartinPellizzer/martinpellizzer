Cacoucia exannulata

Cacoucia exannulata, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The species is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and biodiversity support.
