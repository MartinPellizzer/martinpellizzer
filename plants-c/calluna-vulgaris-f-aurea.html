Calluna vulgaris f. aurea

Calluna vulgaris f. aurea, commonly known as the golden heather, is a variant of the common heather.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with the typical green of the species. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and moorlands.

The golden heather is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
