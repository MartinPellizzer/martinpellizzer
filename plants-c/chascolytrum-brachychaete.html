Chascolytrum brachychaete

Chascolytrum brachychaete, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chascolytrum, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in grasslands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
