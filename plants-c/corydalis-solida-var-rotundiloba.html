Corydalis solida var. rotundiloba

Corydalis solida var. rotundiloba, commonly known as the rounded leaf corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its flowers have spurred petals, a characteristic feature of the Corydalis genus.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.
