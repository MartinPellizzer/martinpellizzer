Cerasus laurocerasus colchica

Cerasus laurocerasus colchica, commonly known as the Persian cherry or Prunus colchica, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
