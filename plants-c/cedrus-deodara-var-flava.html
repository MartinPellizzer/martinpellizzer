Cedrus deodara var. flava

Cedrus deodara var. flava, commonly known as the golden cedar, is a variant of the deodar cedar characterized by its striking yellowish foliage.

This plant is native to the western Himalayas and thrives in temperate mountainous regions. It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is a coniferous tree with a conical shape and dense, feathery branches. The leaves are scale-like, and the cones are typically woody and elongated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique color and hardiness.
