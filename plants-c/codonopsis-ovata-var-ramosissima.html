Codonopsis ovata var. ramosissima

Codonopsis ovata var. ramosissima, commonly known as the branched codonopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
