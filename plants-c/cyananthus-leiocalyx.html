Cyananthus leiocalyx

Cyananthus leiocalyx, commonly known as the blue-flowered leatheryleaf, is a member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Yunnan and Sichuan provinces. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
