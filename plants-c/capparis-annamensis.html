Capparis annamensis

Capparis annamensis, commonly known as the Annam caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, capsaicin-like fruits. Its botanical features include a woody structure, glandular hairs on the leaves, and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

Capparis annamensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
