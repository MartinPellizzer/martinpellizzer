Cerastium × richardsonii

Cerastium × richardsonii, commonly known as the hairy chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cerastium × richardsonii is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields across the northern United States and southern Canada.
