Ceanothus divergens var. confusus

Ceanothus divergens var. confusus, commonly known as the bluecurls or California lilac, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
