Carex curaica var. coacta

Carex curaica var. coacta, commonly known as the Curaica sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy or swampy habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy.

Carex curaica var. coacta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
