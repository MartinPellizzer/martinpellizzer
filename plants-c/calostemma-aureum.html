Calostemma aureum

Calostemma aureum, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tubular, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Calostemma aureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
