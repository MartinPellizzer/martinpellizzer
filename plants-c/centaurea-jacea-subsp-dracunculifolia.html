Centaurea jacea subsp. dracunculifolia

Centaurea jacea subsp.

dracunculifolia, commonly known as the dragon's tail centaurea, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its striking red to orange flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a rough or hairy texture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, drooping flower heads that resemble a dragon's tail, giving it its common name.
