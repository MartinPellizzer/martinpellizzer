Croton stanneus

Croton stanneus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
