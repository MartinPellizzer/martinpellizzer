Cephaelis furcata

Cephaelis furcata, commonly known as the pink-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, often at high altitudes. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Cephaelis furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
