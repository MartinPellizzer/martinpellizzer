Chassalia tsaratanensis var. angustifolia

Chassalia tsaratanensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chassalia, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
