Cytisus ratisbonensis var. cinereus

Cytisus ratisbonensis var. cinereus, commonly known as the silver flame acacia, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feathery leaflets. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its silver-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant.
