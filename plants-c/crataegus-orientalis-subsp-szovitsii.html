Crataegus orientalis subsp. szovitsii

Crataegus orientalis subsp.

szovitsii, commonly known as the Hungarian hawthorn, is a subspecies native to the Carpathian Basin. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers in spring.

The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
