Chamaemelum corymbosum

Chamaemelum corymbosum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties, though it is toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
