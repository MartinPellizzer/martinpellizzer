Cerastium macrocarpum subsp. lucorum

Cerastium macrocarpum subsp.

lucorum, commonly known as white alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features small, clustered leaves that are typically hairy and have a rounded shape.

The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Its compact growth habit and delicate appearance make it a notable species in alpine botany.
