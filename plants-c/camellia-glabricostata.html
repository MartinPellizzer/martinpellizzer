Camellia glabricostata

Camellia glabricostata, commonly known as the smooth-costate camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are smooth along the edges. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers in late winter to early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Camellia glabricostata thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
