Chelone caerulea

Chelone caerulea, commonly known as blue cardinalflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in mid to late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Chelone caerulea is a valuable plant for pollinators, particularly hummingbirds and butterflies.
