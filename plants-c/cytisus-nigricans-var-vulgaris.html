Cytisus nigricans var. vulgaris

Cytisus nigricans var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common broom, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often thorned stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
