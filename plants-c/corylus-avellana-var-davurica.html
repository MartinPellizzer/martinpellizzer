Corylus avellana var. davurica

Corylus avellana var. davurica, commonly known as the dwarf hazel, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, upright growth with dark green, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, spiny husks that enclose edible hazelnuts, which are a valuable food source for wildlife and humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive form.
