Crinum binghamii

Crinum binghamii, commonly known as the Bingham's crinum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. Crinum binghamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
