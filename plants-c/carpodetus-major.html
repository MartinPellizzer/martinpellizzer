Carpodetus major

Carpodetus major, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarack, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped red flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
