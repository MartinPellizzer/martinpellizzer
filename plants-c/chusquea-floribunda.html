Chusquea floribunda

Chusquea floribunda, commonly known as the flowering chusquea, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, showy inflorescences that are often brightly colored, making it visually striking. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
