Convolvulus javanicus

Convolvulus javanicus, commonly known as the Java morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, spiral-shaped capsule that splits open when mature to release the seeds.
