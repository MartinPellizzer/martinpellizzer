Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. latisquameus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. latisquameus, commonly known as the broad-scale goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and cylindrical, spiny stems covered in broad, overlapping scales. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
