Crataegus sanguinea altaica

Crataegus sanguinea altaica, commonly known as the Altai hawthorn, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
