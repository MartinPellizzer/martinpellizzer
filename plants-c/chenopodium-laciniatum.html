Chenopodium laciniatum

Chenopodium laciniatum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in cooking. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Chenopodium laciniatum is also valued for its ability to tolerate frost and its use in traditional Māori cuisine.
