Chimborazoa lachnocarpa

Chimborazoa lachnocarpa, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It typically grows in rocky soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive greenish-blue color and a faintly fragrant scent. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

The species is valued for its unique morphology and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.
