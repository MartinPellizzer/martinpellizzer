Cypripedium × watsonianum

Cypripedium × watsonianum, commonly known as the Watson's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a defining feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a velvety texture and a strong, sweet fragrance.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
