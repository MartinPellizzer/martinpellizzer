Carex bolanderi var. elongata

Carex bolanderi var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bulrush, is a perennial sedge native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
