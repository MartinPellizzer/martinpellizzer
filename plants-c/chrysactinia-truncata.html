Chrysactinia truncata

Chrysactinia truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often lobed and truncate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
