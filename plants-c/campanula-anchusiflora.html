Campanula anchusiflora

Campanula anchusiflora, commonly known as the anchusiflora bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
