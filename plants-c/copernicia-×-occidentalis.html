Copernicia × occidentalis

Copernicia × occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian wax palm, is a hybrid species native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces a unique type of wax, which is harvested for commercial use in products such as candles and cosmetics. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
