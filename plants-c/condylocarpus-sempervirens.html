Condylocarpus sempervirens

Condylocarpus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and can grow to significant heights, often reaching over 30 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used in construction and furniture-making.
