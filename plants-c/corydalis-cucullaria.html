Corydalis cucullaria

Corydalis cucullaria, commonly known as the hooded corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early spring. Its flowers have a distinctive hooded structure, giving it its common name.

Corydalis cucullaria is typically found in rocky or woodland soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
