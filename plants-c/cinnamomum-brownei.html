Cinnamomum brownei

Cinnamomum brownei, commonly known as the Bornean cinnamon or Bornean camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the islands of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum brownei plays an important role in the biodiversity of tropical rainforests and is also used in traditional medicine.
