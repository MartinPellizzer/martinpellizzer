Caltha palustris var. borbasii

Caltha palustris var. borbasii, commonly known as the Borbasii marsh marigold, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water, adapting to its aquatic environment. It thrives in muddy or sandy soils along the edges of ponds, streams, and marshes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular plant in water garden settings.
