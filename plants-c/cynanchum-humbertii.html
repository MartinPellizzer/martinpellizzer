Cynanchum humbertii

Cynanchum humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a cylindrical capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Cynanchum humbertii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
