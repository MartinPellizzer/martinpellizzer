Chaetocyperus limnocharis

Chaetocyperus limnocharis, commonly known as the watergrass or swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, thriving in waterlogged or muddy soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often submerged or partially submerged, with small, grass-like leaves arranged in dense clusters. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chaetocyperus limnocharis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
