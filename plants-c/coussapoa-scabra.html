Coussapoa scabra

Coussapoa scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
