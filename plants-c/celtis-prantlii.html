Celtis prantlii

Celtis prantlii, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, green berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Celtis prantlii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
