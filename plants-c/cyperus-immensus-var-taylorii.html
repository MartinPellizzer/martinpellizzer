Cyperus immensus var. taylorii

Cyperus immensus var. taylorii, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with standing water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.
