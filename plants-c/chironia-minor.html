Chironia minor

Chironia minor, commonly known as the lesser chironia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
