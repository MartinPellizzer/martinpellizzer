Crotalaria juncea var. bengalensis

Crotalaria juncea var. bengalensis, commonly known as Indian hemp, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
