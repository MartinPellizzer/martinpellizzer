Carex douglasii f. brunnea

Carex douglasii f. brunnea, commonly known as the Douglas sedge subspecies brunnea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that distinguishes this form from other Carex species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
