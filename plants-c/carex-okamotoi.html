Carex okamotoi

Carex okamotoi, commonly known as Okamoto's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually wind-pollinated, typical of many sedges.
