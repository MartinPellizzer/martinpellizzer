Convolvulus gonatodes

Convolvulus gonatodes, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
