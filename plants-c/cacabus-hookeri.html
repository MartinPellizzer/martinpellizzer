Cacabus hookeri

Cacabus hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's Cacabus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Cacabus hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
