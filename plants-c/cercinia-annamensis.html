Cercinia annamensis

Cercinia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, non-showy flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
