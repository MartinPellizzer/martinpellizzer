Centaurea ceratophylla

Centaurea ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
