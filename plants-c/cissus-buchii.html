Cissus buchii

Cissus buchii, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Cissus buchii is also valued for its ability to grow on rocky surfaces, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
