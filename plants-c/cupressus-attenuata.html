Cupressus attenuata

Cupressus attenuata, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, pyramid-shaped crown and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and historical significance, it is also valued in horticulture and traditional medicine.
