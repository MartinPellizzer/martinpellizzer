Caladenia tessellata

Caladenia tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open forests and woodlands. The plant produces a single, slender stem with a single, showy orchid flower that has a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its flower features intricate patterns of pink, white, and purple, often with a darkened central stripe.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.
