Cerastium mutabile var. brachypetalum

Cerastium mutabile var. brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate floral display to its habitat.
