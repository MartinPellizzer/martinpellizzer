Cactus tuna

Cactus tuna, commonly known as Opuntia ficus-indica, is a species of cactus native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, known as cladodes, which are often used as leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, called a tuna, is a large, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
