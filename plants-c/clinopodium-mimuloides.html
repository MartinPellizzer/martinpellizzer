Clinopodium mimuloides

Clinopodium mimuloides, commonly known as the mimic clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a minty scent when crushed.

Clinopodium mimuloides is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
