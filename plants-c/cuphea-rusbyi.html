Cuphea rusbyi

Cuphea rusbyi, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and branched. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea rusbyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
