Crataegus bisulcata

Crataegus bisulcata, commonly known as the two-ridged hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its distinctive two-ridged fruiting bodies. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

Crataegus bisulcata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
