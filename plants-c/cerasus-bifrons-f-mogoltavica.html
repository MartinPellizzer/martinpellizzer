Cerasus bifrons f. mogoltavica

Cerasus bifrons f. mogoltavica, commonly known as the Mogoltava cherry, is a rare and ornamental variation of the cherry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often double or semi-double flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red fruits in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
