Carduus uncinatus var. transcaspicus

Carduus uncinatus var. transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid regions.
