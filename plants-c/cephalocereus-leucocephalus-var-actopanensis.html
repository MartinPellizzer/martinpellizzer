Cephalocereus leucocephalus var. actopanensis

Cephalocereus leucocephalus var. actopanensis, commonly known as the Actopan candle, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white, candle-like flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and blooming habits make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
