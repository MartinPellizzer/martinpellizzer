Calorophus pubescens

Calorophus pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent sunflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
