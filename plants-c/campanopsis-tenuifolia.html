Campanopsis tenuifolia

Campanopsis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanopsis tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
