Clerodendrum bernieri

Clerodendrum bernieri, commonly known as the African bishop's candlestick, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces striking clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
