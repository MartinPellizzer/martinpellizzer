Cordia floridana

Cordia floridana, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy surface, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cordia floridana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
