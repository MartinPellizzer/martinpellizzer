Cnicus pinnatisectus

Cnicus pinnatisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as coastal dunes or grasslands. Its leaves are finely divided and pinnatisected, giving it a feathery appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
