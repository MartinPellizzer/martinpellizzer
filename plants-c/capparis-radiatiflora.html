Capparis radiatiflora

Capparis radiatiflora, commonly known as the radiate caper, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and shrub-like growth habit, often found in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces showy, radially symmetrical flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

Capparis radiatiflora is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their ornamental and sometimes culinary uses.
