Commelina albescens var. hirsutissima

Commelina albescens var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
