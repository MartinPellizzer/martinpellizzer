Calendula suffruticosa subsp. tlemcensis

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

tlemcensis, commonly known as the Moroccan marigold, is a shrub-like plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
