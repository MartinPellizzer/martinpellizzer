Capparis magnifica

Capparis magnifica, commonly known as the giant caper, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a distinctive, often thorny structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
