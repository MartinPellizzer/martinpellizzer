Crataegus mollis

Crataegus mollis, commonly known as the soft hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus mollis is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
