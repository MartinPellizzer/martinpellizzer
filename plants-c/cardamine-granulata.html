Cardamine granulata

Cardamine granulata, commonly known as the granulate watercress, is a small perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its small white flowers appear in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
