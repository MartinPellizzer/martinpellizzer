Calytrix sullivanii

Calytrix sullivanii, commonly known as the Sullivan's honeysuckle, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually pink to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Calytrix sullivanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
