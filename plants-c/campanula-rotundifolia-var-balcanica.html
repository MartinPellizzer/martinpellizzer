Campanula rotundifolia var. balcanica

Campanula rotundifolia var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features rosettes of rounded, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
