Campanula hesperidifolia

Campanula hesperidifolia, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Campanula hesperidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
