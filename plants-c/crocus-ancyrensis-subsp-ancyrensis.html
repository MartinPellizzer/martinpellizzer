Crocus ancyrensis subsp. ancyrensis

Crocus ancyrensis subsp.

ancyrensis, commonly known as the Anatolian crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.
