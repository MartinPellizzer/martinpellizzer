Cynara humilis

Cynara humilis, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant features silver-gray, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, globe-shaped fruits. Cynara humilis thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
