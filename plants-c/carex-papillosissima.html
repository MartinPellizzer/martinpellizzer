Carex papillosissima

Carex papillosissima, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex papillosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
