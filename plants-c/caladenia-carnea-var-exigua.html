Caladenia carnea var. exigua

Caladenia carnea var. exigua, commonly known as the pink-stemmed spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid endemic to Western Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are typically pink or purplish in color. The flowers have a distinctive, intricate structure with a labellum that is often marked with contrasting patterns. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
