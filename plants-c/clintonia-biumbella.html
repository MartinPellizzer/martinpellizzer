Clintonia biumbella

Clintonia biumbella, commonly known as the twin-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often found in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features striking white or pale green bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are oval and have a distinct two-lobed base, giving it its common name.

Clintonia biumbella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
