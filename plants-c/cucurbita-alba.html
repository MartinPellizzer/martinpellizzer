Cucurbita alba

Cucurbita alba, commonly known as the white pumpkin or Italian pumpkin, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squashes. The plant produces pale yellow flowers and develops round, smooth, white to pale green fruits. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the vines can spread extensively.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines.
