Crataegus kulingensis

Crataegus kulingensis, commonly known as the Kuling hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in forest margins or rocky areas.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
