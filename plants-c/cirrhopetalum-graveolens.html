Cirrhopetalum graveolens

Cirrhopetalum graveolens, commonly known as the stinking laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, often unpleasant odor. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that develops from the flower.
