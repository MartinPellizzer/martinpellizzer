Crataegus lecta

Crataegus lecta, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible but generally not consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval to ovate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus lecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
