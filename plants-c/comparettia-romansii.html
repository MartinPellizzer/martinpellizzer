Comparettia romansii

Comparettia romansii, commonly known as the Roman's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower structure that resembles a slipper, a feature common in many orchid genera. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Comparettia romansii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
