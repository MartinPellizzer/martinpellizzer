Carex asa-grayi var. hispidula

Carex asa-grayi var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispidula sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, often hairy, and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
