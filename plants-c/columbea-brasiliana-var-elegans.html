Columbea brasiliana var. elegans

Columbea brasiliana var. elegans, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
