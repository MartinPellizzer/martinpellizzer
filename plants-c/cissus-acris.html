Cissus acris

Cissus acris, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus acris is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils and its adaptability to various soil types.
