Combretum sulcatum

Combretum sulcatum, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and distinctive, ridged, elongated fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, sandpaper-like texture, while its branches are often thorned.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
