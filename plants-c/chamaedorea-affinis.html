Chamaedorea affinis

Chamaedorea affinis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Chamaedorea affinis is valued for its ability to purify air and adapt well to low-light conditions.
