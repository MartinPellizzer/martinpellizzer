Cirsium variegatum

Cirsium variegatum, commonly known as the variable thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's hairy stems and leaves provide habitat for small invertebrates.
