Cassia berteroana

Cassia berteroana, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like leaflets.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
