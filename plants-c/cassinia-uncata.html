Cassinia uncata

Cassinia uncata, commonly known as the slender cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spherical heads, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cassinia uncata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
