Carex cyprica

Carex cyprica, commonly known as the Cypric sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a dense, compact cluster. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Carex cyprica plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
