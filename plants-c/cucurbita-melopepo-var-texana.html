Cucurbita melopepo var. texana

Cucurbita melopepo var. texana, commonly known as the Texas prickly gooseberry, is a wild gourd native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and orange to yellow-orange, elongated fruits. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and pumpkins. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it is used in traditional Native American cuisine and medicine.
