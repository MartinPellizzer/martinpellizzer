Cyanus austriacus

Cyanus austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, with the central florets being tubular.

Cyanus austriacus is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
