Cerastium alpinum lusus lanatum

Cerastium alpinum lusus lanatum, commonly known as the downy snowberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are typically oval or rounded, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often red or black in color.

Cerastium alpinum lusus lanatum is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes.
