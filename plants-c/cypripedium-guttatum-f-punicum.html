Cypripedium guttatum f. punicum

Cypripedium guttatum f. punicum, commonly known as the scarlet lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive red or pink lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it forms a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the stems are usually unbranched.

This rare and ornamental species is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
