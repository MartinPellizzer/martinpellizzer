Campanula scheuchzeri f. humilis

Campanula scheuchzeri f. humilis, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
