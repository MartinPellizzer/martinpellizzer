Carex × calderi

Carex × calderi, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex lyngbyana and Carex acutata. This plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex × calderi is often found in northern regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
