Camellia terminalis

Camellia terminalis, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, often with a contrasting white base. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia terminalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and winter blooming habit.
