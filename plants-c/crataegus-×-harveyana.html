Crataegus × harveyana

Crataegus × harveyana, commonly known as the Harverson hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
