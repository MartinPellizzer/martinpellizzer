Chorispora insignis

Chorispora insignis, commonly known as the Indian wintercress, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowers are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Chorispora insignis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various insects and wildlife.
