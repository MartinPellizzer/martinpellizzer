Cyperus microumbellatus

Cyperus microumbellatus, commonly known as the narrow-umbel sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads known as umbels, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus microumbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
