Cinna robusta

Cinna robusta, commonly known as the robust cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a reddish-brown bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its growth habit includes a shrub or small tree form, often with smooth, dark bark.

Cinna robusta thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
