Cnicus hybridus

Cnicus hybridus, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along coastal regions.

Cnicus hybridus is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
