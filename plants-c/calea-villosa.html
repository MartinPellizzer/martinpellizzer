Calea villosa

Calea villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea villosa is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
