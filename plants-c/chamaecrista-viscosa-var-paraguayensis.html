Chamaecrista viscosa var. paraguayensis

Chamaecrista viscosa var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay ticktree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a hairy stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
