Centaurea homoeosceros

Centaurea homoeosceros, commonly known as the mountain centaury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flower heads that are surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
