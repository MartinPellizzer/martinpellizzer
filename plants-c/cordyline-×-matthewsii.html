Cordyline × matthewsii

Cordyline × matthewsii, commonly known as the pink-leafed cordyline, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cordyline australis and Cordyline fruticosa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-purple variegated foliage, which contrasts with its deep green central leaves. The plant is native to New Zealand and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light conditions.

Cordyline × matthewsii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
