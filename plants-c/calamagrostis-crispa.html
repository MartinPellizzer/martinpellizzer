Calamagrostis crispa

Calamagrostis crispa, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces feathery panicles of pale green to golden flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along waterways.

Calamagrostis crispa is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
