Campanula rotundifolia subsp. hispanica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
