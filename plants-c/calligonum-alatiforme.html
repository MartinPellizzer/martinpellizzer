Calligonum alatiforme

Calligonum alatiforme, commonly known as the winged saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that further aid in minimizing transpiration. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calligonum alatiforme is well adapted to saline soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
