Corydalis verticillaris var. boissieri

Corydalis verticillaris var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's clustered lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The leaves are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving the plant a distinctive, compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in moist, shady environments.
