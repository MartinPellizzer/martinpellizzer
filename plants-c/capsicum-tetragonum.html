Capsicum tetragonum

Capsicum tetragonum, commonly known as the square bell pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its distinctive square or rectangular-shaped fruits, which are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. The plant produces small white flowers and has hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and culinary uses.
