Cissus ledermannii

Cissus ledermannii, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by translucent, waxy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
