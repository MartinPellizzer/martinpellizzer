Camellia salicifolia

Camellia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, willow-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are borne on pedicels and are often clustered in terminal panicles.

Camellia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms and attractive foliage.
