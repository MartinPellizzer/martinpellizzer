Cnidium sylvestre

Cnidium sylvestre, commonly known as wild cowbane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound umbel-shaped inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers and has finely divided, feather-like leaves. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Cnidium sylvestre contains various bioactive compounds, including essential oils and alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
