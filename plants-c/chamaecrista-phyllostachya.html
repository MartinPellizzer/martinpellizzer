Chamaecrista phyllostachya

Chamaecrista phyllostachya, commonly known as the featherleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with feather-like foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage or as a cover crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
