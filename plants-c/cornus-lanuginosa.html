Cornus lanuginosa

Cornus lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy cornel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Cornus lanuginosa is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
