Campylospermum glomeratum

Campylospermum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
