Carex brevispicula

Carex brevispicula, commonly known as the short-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters called spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex brevispicula plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
