Coleus heynei

Coleus heynei, commonly known as the purple glory balm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range from deep purple to red, often mistaken for flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
