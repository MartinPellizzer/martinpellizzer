Cucumella engleri

Cucumella engleri, commonly known as the Engler's cucumber gourd, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are trailing and vine-like.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common feature in the Cucurbitaceae family.
