Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. simpliciusculum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

simpliciusculum, commonly known as the slender everlasting daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia. It features small, bright yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, white pappus hairs that aid in seed dispersal. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in coastal and inland regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and landscaping.
