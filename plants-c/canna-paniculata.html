Canna paniculata

Canna paniculata, commonly known as the giant cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.

Canna paniculata is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and flowers, which add dramatic visual impact to gardens and landscapes.
