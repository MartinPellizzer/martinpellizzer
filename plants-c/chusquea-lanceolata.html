Chusquea lanceolata

Chusquea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Chusquea lanceolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
