Cypripedium × castleanum

Cypripedium × castleanum, commonly known as the castleanum lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a large, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats and has thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a yellowish throat, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
