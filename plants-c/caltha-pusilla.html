Caltha pusilla

Caltha pusilla, commonly known as lesser marsh marigold, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist meadows in temperate regions.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.

Caltha pusilla is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable species like buttercups and anemones.
