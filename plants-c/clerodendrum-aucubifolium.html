Clerodendrum aucubifolium

Clerodendrum aucubifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
