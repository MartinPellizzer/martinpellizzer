Cissus macrocarpa

Cissus macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruit curling vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that curl when dry, and its distinctive, large, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
