Cryptocoryne grabowskii

Cryptocoryne grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowski's cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light conditions. It is often cultivated in aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to form dense, attractive foliage.

Cryptocoryne grabowskii is also valued for its role in biological filtration and as a habitat for small aquatic organisms.
