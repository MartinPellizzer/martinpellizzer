Coronilla vaginalis subsp. hercegovinica

Coronilla vaginalis subsp.

hercegovinica, commonly known as the Hercegovina vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
