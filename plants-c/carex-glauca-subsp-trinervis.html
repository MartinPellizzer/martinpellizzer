Carex glauca subsp. trinervis

Carex glauca subsp.

trinervis, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often borne on short, upright stems.

Blue sedge thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.
