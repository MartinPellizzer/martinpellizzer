Coussarea megalocarpa

Coussarea megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit coussarea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
