Calamus hoplites

Calamus hoplites, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Aroidae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among aroid plants. Calamus hoplites is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in perfumery and herbal remedies.
