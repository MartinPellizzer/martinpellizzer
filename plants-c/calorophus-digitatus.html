Calorophus digitatus

Calorophus digitatus, commonly known as the fingerleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of deeply lobed, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem topped with a cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
