Chamissomneia luzulifolia

Chamissomneia luzulifolia, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, which gives it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Chamissomneia luzulifolia plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
