Camellia waldenae

Camellia waldenae, commonly known as the Walden's camellia, is a species native to China.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Camellia waldenae thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of tea.
