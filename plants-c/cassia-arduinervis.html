Cassia arduinervis

Cassia arduinervis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a hairy coating.

Cassia arduinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
