Caldasia eriopus

Caldasia eriopus, commonly known as the velvetleaf caldasia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Caldasia eriopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
