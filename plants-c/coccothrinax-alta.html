Coccothrinax alta

Coccothrinax alta, commonly known as the tall cabbage palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. It thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing in sandy or limestone soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
