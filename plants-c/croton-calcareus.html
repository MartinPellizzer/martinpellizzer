Croton calcareus

Croton calcareus, commonly known as the limestone croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and requires bright, indirect light.

Croton calcareus is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with specific soil conditions.
