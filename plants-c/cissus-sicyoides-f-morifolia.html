Cissus sicyoides f. morifolia

Cissus sicyoides f. morifolia, commonly known as the variegated rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, ovate leaves that often display striking variegation of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and decorative plantings.
