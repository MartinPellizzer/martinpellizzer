Chuquiraga ruscifolia

Chuquiraga ruscifolia, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and fuzzy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Chuquiraga ruscifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
