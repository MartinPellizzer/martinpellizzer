Chascanum pinnatifidum var. racemosum

Chascanum pinnatifidum var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose chascanum, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, racemose inflorescences, which are typical of the Solanaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
