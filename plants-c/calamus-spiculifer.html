Calamus spiculifer

Calamus spiculifer, commonly known as the spiny calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered, spiny fruits. The plant's rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses. Calamus spiculifer thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in lowland forests.

Its growth pattern and unique spiny fruit structure make it distinctive among its genus.
