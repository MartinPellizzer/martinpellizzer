Campanula colorata var. tibetica

Campanula colorata var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with white or pink markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
