Culcitium canescens

Culcitium canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-gray coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spike-like clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Culcitium canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
