Centaurea procumbens var. aemilii

Centaurea procumbens var. aemilii, commonly known as the Emili's prostrate cornflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers with reddish-brown bracts, typical of the genus Centaurea. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and wildflower displays.
