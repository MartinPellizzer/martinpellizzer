Carex amblyorhyncha var. fusca

Carex amblyorhyncha var. fusca, commonly known as the dark-stemmed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
