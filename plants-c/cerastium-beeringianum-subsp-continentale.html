Cerastium beeringianum subsp. continentale

Cerastium beeringianum subsp.

continentale, commonly known as the European mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, white to pale green hairs.

The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to round, and often covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
