Cirsium × boulayi

Cirsium × boulayi, commonly known as the Boulay's thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or disturbed areas, where it can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
