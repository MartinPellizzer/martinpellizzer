Cassia leptadenia var. jaliscensis

Cassia leptadenia var. jaliscensis, commonly known as Mexican senna, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its small, elongated pods contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
