Capparis spinosa subsp. sicula

Capparis spinosa subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, greenish fruits that mature into a peppery, edible capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The subspecies is notable for its unique flavor and is used in Mediterranean cuisine.
