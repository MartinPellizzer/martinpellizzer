Capparis scheffleri

Capparis scheffleri, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often grown as an ornamental or for its fruit.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
