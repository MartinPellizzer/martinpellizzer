Carex spicata f. fumosa

Carex spicata f. fumosa, commonly known as the smoky sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish-green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically arranged in a single or double spike. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique coloration and is often used in water garden settings.
