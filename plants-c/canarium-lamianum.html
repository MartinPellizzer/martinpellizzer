Canarium lamianum

Canarium lamianum, commonly known as the Malayan candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces drupes that are rich in oils, making it economically important for its seed oil. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its timber and traditional uses in various cultures.
