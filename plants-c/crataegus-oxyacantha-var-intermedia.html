Crataegus oxyacantha var. intermedia

Crataegus oxyacantha var. intermedia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny branches and is characterized by its white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
