Coccinia aurantiaca

Coccinia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, spherical fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are hairy and twining.

Coccinia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
