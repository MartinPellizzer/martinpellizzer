Cyperus kerstingii

Cyperus kerstingii, commonly known as the narrowleaf paper plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically borne on tall, slender stems. Cyperus kerstingii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to wet environments makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in many regions.
