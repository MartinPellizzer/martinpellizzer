Cedrela toona var. sublaxiflora

Cedrela toona var. sublaxiflora, commonly known as the Mexican cedar or white cedar, is a member of the Meliaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to Central America and northern South America, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is valued for its durable, lightweight wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species also plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems.
