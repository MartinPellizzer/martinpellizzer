Cornus mas f. conica

Cornus mas f. conica, commonly known as the spindle-shaped cornel.

It is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its narrow, spindle-like form and clusters of white, bract-like flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its unique shape and early spring blooms, making it a popular choice in small gardens.
