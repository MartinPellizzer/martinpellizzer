Carex multinervis

Carex multinervis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex multinervis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
