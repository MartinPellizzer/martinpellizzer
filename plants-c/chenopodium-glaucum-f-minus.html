Chenopodium glaucum f. minus

Chenopodium glaucum f. minus, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, glaucous leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal practices, though it is not widely cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.

The species is notable for its resilience and ability to survive in arid conditions.
