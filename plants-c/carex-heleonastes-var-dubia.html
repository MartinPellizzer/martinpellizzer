Carex heleonastes var. dubia

Carex heleonastes var. dubia, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to regions of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, dry fruits.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
