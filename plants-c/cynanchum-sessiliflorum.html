Cynanchum sessiliflorum

Cynanchum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessileflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cynanchum sessiliflorum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
