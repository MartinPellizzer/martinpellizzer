Cereus stramineus

Cereus stramineus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a type of cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its stems are often brightly colored, and it is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Cereus stramineus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
