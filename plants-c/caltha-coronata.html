Caltha coronata

Caltha coronata, commonly known as the crown flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Caltha coronata thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
