Chlamydia tenacissima

Chlamydia tenacissima, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.
