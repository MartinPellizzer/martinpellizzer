Chaenostoma caeruleum

Chaenostoma caeruleum, commonly known as the blue star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, star-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, wavy-edged leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Chaenostoma caeruleum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
