Cladopus chinensis

Cladopus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.
