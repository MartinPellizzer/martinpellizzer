Conyza vernonioides

Conyza vernonioides, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza vernonioides is adaptable to various climates and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
