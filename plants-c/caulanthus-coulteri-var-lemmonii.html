Caulanthus coulteri var. lemmonii

Caulanthus coulteri var. lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's popcornflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of linear leaves, and it produces fluffy, seed-filled pods that disperse seeds by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
