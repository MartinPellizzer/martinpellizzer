Caprifolium acuminatum var. loureiroi

Caprifolium acuminatum var. loureiroi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Caprifoliaceae family.
