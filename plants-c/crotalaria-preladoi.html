Crotalaria preladoi

Crotalaria preladoi, commonly known as the Prelado's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, pod-like structures and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria preladoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural applications.
