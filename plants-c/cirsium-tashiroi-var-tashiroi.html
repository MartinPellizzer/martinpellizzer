Cirsium tashiroi var. tashiroi

Cirsium tashiroi var. tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is distinguished by its compact growth habit and relatively small size compared to other thistle species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
