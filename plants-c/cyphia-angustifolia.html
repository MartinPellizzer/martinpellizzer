Cyphia angustifolia

Cyphia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cyphia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Cyphia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
