Crepis simulans

Crepis simulans, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Crepis simulans is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
