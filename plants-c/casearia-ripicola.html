Casearia ripicola

Casearia ripicola, commonly known as the creeping casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
