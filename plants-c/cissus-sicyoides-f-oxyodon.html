Cissus sicyoides f. oxyodon

Cissus sicyoides f. oxyodon, commonly known as the spiny cissus, is a varietal form of the genus Cissus, characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a climbing plant native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is distinguished by its thick, waxy leaves and the presence of spines along the leaf margins and stems. Its stems are often armed with small, sharp spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb various structures, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
