Cerasus laurocerasus var. intermedia

Cerasus laurocerasus var. intermedia, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a hybrid shrub or small tree that exhibits characteristics of both Prunus laurocerasus and Prunus avium.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, edible drupes. It is native to the Caucasus region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

The plant is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
