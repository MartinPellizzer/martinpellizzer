Carex granularioides

Carex granularioides, commonly known as the granular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by three-sided, spiky fruits.

Carex granularioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
