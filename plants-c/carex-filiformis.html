Carex filiformis

Carex filiformis, commonly known as slender sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with separate male and female parts, typical of many sedges.

Carex filiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
