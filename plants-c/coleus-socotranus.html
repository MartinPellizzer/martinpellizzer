Coleus socotranus

Coleus socotranus, commonly known as Socotra coleus, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are often brightly colored with red, yellow, and green patterns, making it a visually appealing species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

The plant is also notable for its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.
