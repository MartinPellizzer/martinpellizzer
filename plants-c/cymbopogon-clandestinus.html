Cymbopogon clandestinus

Cymbopogon clandestinus, commonly known as the secret grass, is a rare and lesser-known member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale-green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
