Ceanothus thyrsiflorus var. griseus

Ceanothus thyrsiflorus var. griseus, commonly known as the gray ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native landscaping.
