Cassia robinioides

Cassia robinioides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Cassia robinioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
