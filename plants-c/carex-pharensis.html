Carex pharensis

Carex pharensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is often found in wetlands, stream sides, and other damp habitats.

Carex pharensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
