Chrysanthemum macrocephalum

Chrysanthemum macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of yellow to orange flowers.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
