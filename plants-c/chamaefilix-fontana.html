Chamaefilix fontana

Chamaefilix fontana, commonly known as the Alpine thyme or mountain thyme, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.
