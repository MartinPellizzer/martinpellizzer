Carex walkeri

Carex walkeri, commonly known as the Walker's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a dense, compact form. Its flowering spikes consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in three rows.

Carex walkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
