Convolvulus valentinus subsp. euvalentinus

Convolvulus valentinus subsp.

euvalentinus, commonly known as the Valentine convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring bloom and delicate appearance.
