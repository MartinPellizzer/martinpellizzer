Carex remota var. remotispicula

Carex remota var. remotispicula, commonly known as the slender bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, cylindrical inflorescence and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
