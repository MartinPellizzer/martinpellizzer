Chrysanthemum flosculosum

Chrysanthemum flosculosum, commonly known as the woolly chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish-white fuzzy foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking ornamental and low-maintenance plants.

Chrysanthemum flosculosum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
