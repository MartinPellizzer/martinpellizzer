Crataegus croomiana

Crataegus croomiana, commonly known as Croom's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that matures in the fall.

Crataegus croomiana is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
