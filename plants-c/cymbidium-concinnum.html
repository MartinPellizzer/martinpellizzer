Cymbidium concinnum

Cymbidium concinnum, commonly known as the concinnum orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid characterized by its tall, branched stems and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically during the winter months. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Cymbidium concinnum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
