Carex anningensis

Carex anningensis, commonly known as Anning's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
