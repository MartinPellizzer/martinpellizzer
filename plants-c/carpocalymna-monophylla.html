Carpocalymna monophylla

Carpocalymna monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf burr, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its single, large, leathery leaf that persists for several years. The plant produces a distinctive, spiny fruit that encloses a single seed, which is adapted for dispersal by animals. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and evolutionary contexts.

It thrives in arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
