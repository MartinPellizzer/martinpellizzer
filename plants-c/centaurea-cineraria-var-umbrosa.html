Centaurea cineraria var. umbrosa

Centaurea cineraria var. umbrosa, commonly known as the dark blue thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which provide a striking contrast. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bracts that surround the flower head, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
