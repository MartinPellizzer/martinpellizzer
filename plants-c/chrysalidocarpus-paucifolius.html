Chrysalidocarpus paucifolius

Chrysalidocarpus paucifolius, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near mangrove forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
