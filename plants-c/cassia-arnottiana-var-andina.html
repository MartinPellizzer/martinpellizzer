Cassia arnottiana var. andina

Cassia arnottiana var. andina, commonly known as Andean senna, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Cassia arnottiana var. andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
