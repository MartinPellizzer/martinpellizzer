Carex malaccensis

Carex malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. It has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curved, with small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex malaccensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
