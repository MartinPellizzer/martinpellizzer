Chaenomeles japonica f. alba

Chaenomeles japonica f. alba, commonly known as the white Japanese quince, is a cultivar of the Japanese quince plant.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thick, rugged stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The fruit, which develops in late summer, is typically yellow or orange and has a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its winter interest and fragrance.
