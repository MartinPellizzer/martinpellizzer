Carum ammi

Carum ammi, commonly known as bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. Its seeds are small, black, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

Carum ammi is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
