Carex diluta var. karelinii

Carex diluta var. karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to regions of Siberia and northern Asia, where it thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
