Carduncellus maroccanus

Carduncellus maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa, particularly Morocco.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry climates. Its leaves are spiny and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Carduncellus maroccanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
