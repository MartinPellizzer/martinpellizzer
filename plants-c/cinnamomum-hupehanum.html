Cinnamomum hupehanum

Cinnamomum hupehanum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum camphora, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils such as camphor. The plant is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are also used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive flavor to various dishes.
