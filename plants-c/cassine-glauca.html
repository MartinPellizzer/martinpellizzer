Cassine glauca

Cassine glauca, commonly known as the waxleaf cassine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with a waxy, bluish-green surface that gives it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cassine glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
