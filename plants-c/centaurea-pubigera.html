Centaurea pubigera

Centaurea pubigera, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with reddish-brown centers.

Centaurea pubigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
