Capsicum annuum var. milleri

Capsicum annuum var. milleri, commonly known as the Mexican chili or Poblano pepper, is a variety of Capsicum annuum native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lantern-shaped fruits that typically range from 4 to 6 inches in length. The plant produces bright red or orange fruits when mature, which are used both fresh and dried in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The variety is valued for its mild to medium heat level and rich, smoky flavor.
