Cotoneaster turcomanicus

Cotoneaster turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract birds.

Cotoneaster turcomanicus is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
