Columnea filipendula

Columnea filipendula, commonly known as the thread-leaved flame flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with contrasting yellow or orange throats. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Columnea filipendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
