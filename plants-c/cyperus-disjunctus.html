Cyperus disjunctus

Cyperus disjunctus, commonly known as the Disjunct flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including South Africa, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, and it is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus disjunctus is adapted to moist conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
