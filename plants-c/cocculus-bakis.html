Cocculus bakis

Cocculus bakis, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
