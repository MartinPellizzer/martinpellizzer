Croton garckeanus f. latifolius

Croton garckeanus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a variant of the Croton genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
