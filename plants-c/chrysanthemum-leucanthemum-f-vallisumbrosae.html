Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. vallisumbrosae

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. vallisumbrosae, commonly known as the shade-loving chrysanthemum, is a hybrid form of the common chrysanthemum.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for shady garden environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in cooler climates.

This cultivar is often used in woodland gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
