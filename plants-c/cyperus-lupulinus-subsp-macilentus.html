Cyperus lupulinus subsp. macilentus

Cyperus lupulinus subsp.

macilentus, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various aquatic environments and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
