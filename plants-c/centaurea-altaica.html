Centaurea altaica

Centaurea altaica, commonly known as the Altai knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly the Altai Mountains.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow or orange-red flowers in composite flower heads. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and a basal rosette of leaves.

Centaurea altaica is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in harsh, high-altitude environments.
