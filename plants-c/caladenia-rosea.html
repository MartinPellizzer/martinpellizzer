Caladenia rosea

Caladenia rosea, commonly known as the pink spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces a single, slender flower spike that bears one or two flowers, each with a distinctive, intricate labellum. It blooms during the autumn season, usually between August and October.

Caladenia rosea is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate and ephemeral beauty.
