Cajanum

Cajanum, commonly known as pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant produces small, green pods that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Cajanum is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various cuisines around the world.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important crop for sustainable agriculture.
