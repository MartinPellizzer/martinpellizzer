Centaurea subconica

Centaurea subconica, commonly known as the narrow conehead, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with narrow, cone-shaped involucres.

Centaurea subconica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
