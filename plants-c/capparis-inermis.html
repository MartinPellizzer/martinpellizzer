Capparis inermis

Capparis inermis, commonly known as the smooth caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring thornless stems and aromatic, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by green, spherical fruits. These fruits mature into a pale yellow or orange color and are edible, though they have a tart, sharp flavor.

Capparis inermis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruits in culinary applications.
