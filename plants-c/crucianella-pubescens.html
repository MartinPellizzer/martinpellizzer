Crucianella pubescens

Crucianella pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crucianella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
