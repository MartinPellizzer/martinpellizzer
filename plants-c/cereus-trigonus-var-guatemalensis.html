Cereus trigonus var. guatemalensis

Cereus trigonus var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan torch cactus, is a species native to Central America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and full sun exposure. Its stems are often ribbed and can grow up to several meters in height.

This cactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
