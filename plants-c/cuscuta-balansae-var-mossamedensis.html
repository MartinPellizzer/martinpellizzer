Cuscuta balansae var. mossamedensis

Cuscuta balansae var. mossamedensis, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host plants. The species is native to arid regions of North America and is often found growing on shrubs and grasses. Its life cycle involves germinating from seeds and establishing a parasitic relationship with host plants within days.

Due to its ecological role and unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
