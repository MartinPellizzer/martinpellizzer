Cladium mariscus subsp. intermedium

Cladium mariscus subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense tussocks, typically growing in shallow water or moist soil.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, spathate fruits. It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its morphology and growth habits make it well adapted to fluctuating water levels and nutrient-poor environments.
