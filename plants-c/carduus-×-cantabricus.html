Carduus × cantabricus

Carduus × cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian thistle, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a perennial growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
