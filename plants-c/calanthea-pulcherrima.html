Calanthea pulcherrima

Calanthea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Calanthea pulcherrima thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
