Columnea scandens-hirsuta

Columnea scandens-hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy trailing columnea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vine-like species that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or greenhouses.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
