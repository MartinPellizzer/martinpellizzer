Chionogentias filipes

Chionogentias filipes, commonly known as the silver feather plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among botanists and gardeners alike.

Chionogentias filipes is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates, which contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
