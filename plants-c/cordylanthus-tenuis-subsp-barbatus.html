Cordylanthus tenuis subsp. barbatus

Cordylanthus tenuis subsp.

barbatus, commonly known as the slenderleaf scurfpea, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
