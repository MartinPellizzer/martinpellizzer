Campsis × intermedia

Campsis × intermedia, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Campsis radicans (trumpetvine) and Campsis × wilsoniana (wilsoniana). The plant is characterized by its showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. These flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees.

Its foliage is dark green and provides attractive fall color, making it a popular ornamental plant.
