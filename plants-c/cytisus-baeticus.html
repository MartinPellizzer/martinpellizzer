Cytisus baeticus

Cytisus baeticus, commonly known as the Baeticus broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
