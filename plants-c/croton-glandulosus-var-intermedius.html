Croton glandulosus var. intermedius

Croton glandulosus var. intermedius, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
