Ceropegia cimiciodora

Ceropegia cimiciodora, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, tuberous roots and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
