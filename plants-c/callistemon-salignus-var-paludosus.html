Callistemon salignus var. paludosus

Callistemon salignus var. paludosus, commonly known as the swamp bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
