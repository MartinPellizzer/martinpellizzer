Carex panicea f. conferta

Carex panicea f. conferta, commonly known as the dense panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, panicled inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
