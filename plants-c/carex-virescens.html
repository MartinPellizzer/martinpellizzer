Carex virescens

Carex virescens, commonly known as green sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex virescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
