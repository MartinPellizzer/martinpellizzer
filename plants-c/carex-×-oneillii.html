Carex × oneillii

Carex × oneillii, commonly known as the O’Neill sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex × oneillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
