Carelia matricarioides

Carelia matricarioides, commonly known as the false ground ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Carelia matricarioides is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
