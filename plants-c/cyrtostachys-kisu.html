Cyrtostachys kisu

Cyrtostachys kisu, commonly known as the Kisu bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Cyrtostachys kisu is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
