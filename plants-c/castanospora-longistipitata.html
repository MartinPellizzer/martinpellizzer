Castanospora longistipitata

Castanospora longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed castanospora, is a species of fungus found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Phomaceae and is characterized by its distinct morphology, including a long, slender stipe and a broad, irregular cap. The fruiting bodies typically develop on decaying wood, contributing to the decomposition process in forest ecosystems. This fungus plays a role in nutrient cycling and is of interest in mycological research.
