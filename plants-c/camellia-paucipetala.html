Camellia paucipetala

Camellia paucipetala, commonly known as the few-flowered camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers with prominent stamens, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Camellia paucipetala prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture make it a prized plant among gardeners and botanists.
