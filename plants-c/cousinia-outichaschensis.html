Cousinia outichaschensis

Cousinia outichaschensis, commonly known as the Armenian mountain carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cousinia outichaschensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
