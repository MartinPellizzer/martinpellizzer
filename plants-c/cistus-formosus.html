Cistus formosus

Cistus formosus, commonly known as the beautiful rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring and summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often releasing a strong scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
