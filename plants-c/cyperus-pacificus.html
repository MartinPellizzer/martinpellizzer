Cyperus pacificus

Cyperus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus pacificus is known for its ability to tolerate varying water levels, making it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
