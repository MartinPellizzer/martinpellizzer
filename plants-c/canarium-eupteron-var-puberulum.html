Canarium eupteron var. puberulum

Canarium eupteron var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy candlenut.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
