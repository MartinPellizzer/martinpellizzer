Cyperus superatus

Cyperus superatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus superatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
