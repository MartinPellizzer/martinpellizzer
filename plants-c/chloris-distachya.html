Chloris distachya

Chloris distachya, commonly known as slender grass or silver grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris distachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
