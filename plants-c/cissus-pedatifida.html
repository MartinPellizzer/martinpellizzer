Cissus pedatifida

Cissus pedatifida, commonly known as the climbing snow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a snowflake, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cissus pedatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
