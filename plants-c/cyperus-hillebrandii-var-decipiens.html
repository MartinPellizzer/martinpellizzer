Cyperus hillebrandii var. decipiens

Cyperus hillebrandii var. decipiens, commonly known as the false umbrella sedge, is a perennial wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles an umbrella. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
