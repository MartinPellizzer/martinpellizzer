Cousinia turcomanica

Cousinia turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cousinia turcomanica is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
