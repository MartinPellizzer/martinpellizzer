Coreopsis macrocarpa

Coreopsis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-flowered coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce vibrant yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
