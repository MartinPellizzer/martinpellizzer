Centaurea pichleri subsp. extrarosularis

Centaurea pichleri subsp.

extrarosularis, commonly known as the Alpine centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
