Cordylanthus palmatus subsp. carnulosus

Cordylanthus palmatus subsp.

carnulosus, commonly known as the woolly bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and harsh climates.
