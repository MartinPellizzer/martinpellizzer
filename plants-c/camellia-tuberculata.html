Camellia tuberculata

Camellia tuberculata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive waxy texture and a prominent center. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.

Camellia tuberculata prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
