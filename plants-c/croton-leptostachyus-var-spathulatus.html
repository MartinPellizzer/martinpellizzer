Croton leptostachyus var. spathulatus

Croton leptostachyus var. spathulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
