Cattleya luteola var. multiflora

Cattleya luteola var. multiflora, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
