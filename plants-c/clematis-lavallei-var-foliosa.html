Clematis lavallei var. foliosa

Clematis lavallei var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive, fragrant floral display. The plant produces showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
