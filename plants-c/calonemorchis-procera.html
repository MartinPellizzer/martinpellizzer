Calonemorchis procera

Calonemorchis procera, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It blooms during the spring and summer months, producing clusters of pale green to yellow flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
