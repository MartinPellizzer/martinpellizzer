Carex peruvida

Carex peruvida, commonly known as the Peruvian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex peruvida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
