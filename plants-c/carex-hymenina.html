Carex hymenina

Carex hymenina, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex hymenina prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its silver-gray foliage makes it a visually appealing ornamental plant in garden settings.
