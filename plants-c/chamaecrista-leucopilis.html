Chamaecrista leucopilis

Chamaecrista leucopilis, commonly known as the white-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive leaf structure and its role in local ecosystems.
