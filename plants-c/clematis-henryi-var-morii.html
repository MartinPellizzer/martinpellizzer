Clematis henryi var. morii

Clematis henryi var. morii, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
