Chamaedryfolia candida

Chamaedryfolia candida, commonly known as the white sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
