Coleochloa virgata

Coleochloa virgata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Coleochloa virgata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
