Convolvulus laciniatus f. subglabra

Convolvulus laciniatus f. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf morning glory, is a variant of the species characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is often grown for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
