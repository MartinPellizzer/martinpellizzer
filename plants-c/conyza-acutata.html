Conyza acutata

Conyza acutata, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Conyza acutata is also known for its potential as a weed in agricultural settings.
