Carica boissieri

Carica boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's papaya, is a species of flowering plant in the Caricaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit that is edible and has a sweet, musky flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
