Centaurea hyalolepis subsp. negevensis

Centaurea hyalolepis subsp.

negevensis, commonly known as the Negev centaury, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny bracts around its flower heads. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in steppes and semi-desert areas.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
