Cassia tettensis

Cassia tettensis, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia tettensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
