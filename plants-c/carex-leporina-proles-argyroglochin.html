Carex leporina proles argyroglochin

Carex leporina proles argyroglochin, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective ground cover in wetland restoration projects.
