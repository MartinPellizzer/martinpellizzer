Crataegus succulenta var. succulenta

Crataegus succulenta var. succulenta, commonly known as the succulent hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature adapted to its native habitat in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it blooms in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
