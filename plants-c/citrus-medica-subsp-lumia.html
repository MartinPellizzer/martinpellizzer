Citrus medica subsp. lumia

Citrus medica subsp.

lumia, commonly known as the Persian lime, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, smooth, green fruit with a thin, easy-to-peel rind.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a bright yellow color, indicating maturity. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub with pinnate leaves and fragrant white flowers.

It thrives in warm climates and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
