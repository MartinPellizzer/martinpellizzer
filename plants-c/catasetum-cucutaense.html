Catasetum cucutaense

Catasetum cucutaense, commonly known as the Cucuta orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique structure, featuring a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific bees. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with red markings, and it blooms during the dry season.

Catasetum cucutaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
