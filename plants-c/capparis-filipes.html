Capparis filipes

Capparis filipes, commonly known as the Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with woody stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a fleshy, red or yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Capparis filipes is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and arid conditions.
