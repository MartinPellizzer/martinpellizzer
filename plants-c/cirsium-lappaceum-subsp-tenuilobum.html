Cirsium lappaceum subsp. tenuilobum

Cirsium lappaceum subsp.

tenuilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a robust taproot and spiny, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple and are borne in dense, terminal clusters.

This subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaf lobes compared to the typical form of Cirsium lappaceum.
