Callerya dielsiana

Callerya dielsiana, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, elongated seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it requires careful management in gardens and natural habitats.
