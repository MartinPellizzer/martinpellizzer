Combretum gueinzii var. holosericeum

Combretum gueinzii var. holosericeum, commonly known as the velvetwood or silverwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
