Chelonanthus chelonoides

Chelonanthus chelonoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
