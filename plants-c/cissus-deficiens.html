Cissus deficiens

Cissus deficiens, commonly known as the silver leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus deficiens is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant due to its elegant appearance and easy care requirements.
