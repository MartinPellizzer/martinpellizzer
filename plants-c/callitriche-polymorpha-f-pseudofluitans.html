Callitriche polymorpha f. pseudofluitans

Callitriche polymorpha f. pseudofluitans, commonly known as floating moss or water starwort, is a aquatic plant characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and marshes. The plant exhibits a unique ability to remain buoyant due to air pockets within its leaves, which aid in its floating habit. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This species is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to water quality and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
