Cirsium chabertii

Cirsium chabertii, commonly known as the Chabert's thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium chabertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
