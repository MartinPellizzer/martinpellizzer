Cyperus cyrtostachys

Cyperus cyrtostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often yellow or brown in color.

Cyperus cyrtostachys is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
