Croton crocodilorum var. platyaster

Croton crocodilorum var. platyaster, commonly known as the broad-leaf croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant colors ranging from green to red. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
