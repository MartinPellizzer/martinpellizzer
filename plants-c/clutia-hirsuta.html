Clutia hirsuta

Clutia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy clutia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in cymes. Its fruits are three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Clutia hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
