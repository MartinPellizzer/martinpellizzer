Cleianthus

Cleianthus, commonly known as the white-flowered cleianthus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plants typically have showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. They are shrubs or small trees with palmate leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cleianthus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their attractive blooms.
