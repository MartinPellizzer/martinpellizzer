Cyphocarpa trichinioides

Cyphocarpa trichinioides, commonly known as the desert tamarack, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it is often found in rocky soils or along dry streambeds.

The species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
