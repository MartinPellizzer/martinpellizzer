Coleus trichophorus

Coleus trichophorus, commonly known as the hairy coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and oval-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Coleus trichophorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
