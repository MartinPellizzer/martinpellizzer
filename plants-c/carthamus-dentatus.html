Carthamus dentatus

Carthamus dentatus, commonly known as the serrated safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and serrated leaf margins. The plant produces distinctive seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate.

Carthamus dentatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in herbal remedies.
