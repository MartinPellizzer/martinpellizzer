Commelina reniformis

Commelina reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina reniformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
