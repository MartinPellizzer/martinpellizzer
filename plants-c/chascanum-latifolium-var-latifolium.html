Chascanum latifolium var. latifolium

Chascanum latifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chascanum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
