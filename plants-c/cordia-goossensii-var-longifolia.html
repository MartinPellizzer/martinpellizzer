Cordia goossensii var. longifolia

Cordia goossensii var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and adaptability to harsh environments.
