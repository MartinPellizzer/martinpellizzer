Conothamnus divaricatus

Conothamnus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable thornbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Conothamnus divaricatus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
