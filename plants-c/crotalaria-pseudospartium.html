Crotalaria pseudospartium

Crotalaria pseudospartium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
