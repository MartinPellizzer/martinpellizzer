Calyptranthes pileata

Calyptranthes pileata, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

Calyptranthes pileata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
