Canthium henriettiae

Canthium henriettiae, commonly known as Henrietta's spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
