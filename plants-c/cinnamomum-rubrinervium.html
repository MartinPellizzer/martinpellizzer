Cinnamomum rubrinervium

Cinnamomum rubrinervium, commonly known as the red-veined cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for flavoring.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
