Cnicus parryi

Cnicus parryi, commonly known as Parry's burroweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Cnicus parryi produces burrs that aid in seed dispersal, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
