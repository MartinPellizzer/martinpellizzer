Campanula velutina var. gibraltarica

Campanula velutina var. gibraltarica, commonly known as the Gibraltar bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
