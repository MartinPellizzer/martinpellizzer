Chenopodium fremontii f. farinosum

Chenopodium fremontii f. farinosum, commonly known as the farinose goosefoot, is a variant of the genus Chenopodium, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating known as farina, which gives it its common name. It is a perennial herb that is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

The farina on its leaves may serve as a protective mechanism against water loss and herbivory.
