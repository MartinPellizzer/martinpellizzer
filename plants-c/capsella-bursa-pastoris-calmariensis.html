Capsella bursa-pastoris calmariensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris calmariensis, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant species native to temperate regions.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, oval leaves and white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, bladder-like seed pods, which are a distinguishing feature. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed with ecological and historical significance.
