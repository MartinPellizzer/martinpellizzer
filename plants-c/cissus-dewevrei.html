Cissus dewevrei

Cissus dewevrei, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Cissus dewevrei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
