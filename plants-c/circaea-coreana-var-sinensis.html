Circaea coreana var. sinensis

Circaea coreana var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese whiteflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and often reddish in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
