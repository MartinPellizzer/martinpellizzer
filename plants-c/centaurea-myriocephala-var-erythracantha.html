Centaurea myriocephala var. erythracantha

Centaurea myriocephala var. erythracantha, commonly known as the red-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
