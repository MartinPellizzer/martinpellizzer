Carduus spachianus subsp. atlantis

Carduus spachianus subsp.

atlantis, commonly known as the Atlantic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its adaptations to coastal environments include salt tolerance and drought resistance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
