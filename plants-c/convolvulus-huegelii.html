Convolvulus huegelii

Convolvulus huegelii, commonly known as the Hugel's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a sprawling annual vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving the plant its common name.
