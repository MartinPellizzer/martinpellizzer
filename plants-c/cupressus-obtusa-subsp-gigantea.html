Cupressus obtusa subsp. gigantea

Cupressus obtusa subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or giant Japanese cedar, is a tall coniferous tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
