Carex praecox var. reflexa

Carex praecox var. reflexa, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often arranged in a loose, cylindrical shape.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
