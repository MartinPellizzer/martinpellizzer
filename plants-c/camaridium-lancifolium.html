Camaridium lancifolium

Camaridium lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camaridium, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
