Cyathea paucifolia

Cyathea paucifolia, commonly known as the few-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its rhizomes produce roots that anchor it to the substrate, while its fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea paucifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to add a tropical feel to gardens.
