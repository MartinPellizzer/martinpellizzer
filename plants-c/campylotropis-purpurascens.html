Campylotropis purpurascens

Campylotropis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered burr clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
