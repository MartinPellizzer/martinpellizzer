Calea cymosa var. trichophylla

Calea cymosa var. trichophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf candleplant, is a striking perennial herb native to Central America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage and its unique, candle-like inflorescence that resembles a cluster of candles. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are borne on tall, erect stems.

Calea cymosa var. trichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
