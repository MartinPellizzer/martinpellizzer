Cordia corymbosa f. hirsuta

Cordia corymbosa f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cordia, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
