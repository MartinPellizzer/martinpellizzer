Chamaecrista marianensis

Chamaecrista marianensis, commonly known as the Marian's ticktree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
