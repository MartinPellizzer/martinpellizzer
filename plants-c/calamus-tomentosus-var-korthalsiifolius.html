Calamus tomentosus var. korthalsiifolius

Calamus tomentosus var. korthalsiifolius, commonly known as the Korthals' betel palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous leaves that form a rosette, providing a unique structural appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas with rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
