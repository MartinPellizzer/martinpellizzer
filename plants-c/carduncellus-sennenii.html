Carduncellus sennenii

Carduncellus sennenii, commonly known as the African cardoon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, spindle-shaped root and tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

Carduncellus sennenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
