Crassula tetragona subsp. lignescens

Crassula tetragona subsp.

lignescens, commonly known as the Forest Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that often develop a reddish tinge when exposed to sunlight.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
