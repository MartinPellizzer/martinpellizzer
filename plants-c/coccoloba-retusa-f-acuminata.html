Coccoloba retusa f. acuminata

Coccoloba retusa f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leafed coccoloba, is a variant of the coastal coccoloba native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy texture. The leaves are usually a deep green, and the plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils, showing some tolerance to salt spray.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
