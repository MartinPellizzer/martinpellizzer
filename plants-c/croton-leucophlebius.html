Croton leucophlebius

Croton leucophlebius, commonly known as the silver-leaved croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a waxy texture that gives them a distinctive appearance.

Croton leucophlebius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and eye-catching foliage.
