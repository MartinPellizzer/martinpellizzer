Chamaecrista brevipes

Chamaecrista brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, paired leaflets. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chamaecrista brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
