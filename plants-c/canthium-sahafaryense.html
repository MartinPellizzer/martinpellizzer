Canthium sahafaryense

Canthium sahafaryense, commonly known as Sahafary's spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Somalia and Ethiopia. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in complex inflorescences called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium sahafaryense is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.
