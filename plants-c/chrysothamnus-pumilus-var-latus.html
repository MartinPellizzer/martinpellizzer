Chrysothamnus pumilus var. latus

Chrysothamnus pumilus var. latus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
