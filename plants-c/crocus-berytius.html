Crocus berytius

Crocus berytius, commonly known as the Cyprus crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is also cultivated for its edible corms and medicinal properties.
