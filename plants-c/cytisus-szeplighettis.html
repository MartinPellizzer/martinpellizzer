Cytisus szeplighettis

Cytisus szeplighettis, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a shrub native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, often thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus szeplighettis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open landscapes and along roadsides.
