Conophytum klaverense

Conophytum klaverense, commonly known as the "Rainbow Stone" or "Rainbow Cactus," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance with thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Conophytum klaverense is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent leaves to survive drought conditions.
