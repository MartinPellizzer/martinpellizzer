Cestrum suaveolens

Cestrum suaveolens, commonly known as sweet Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

The plant is known for its strong, sweet scent, which is most noticeable in the evening.
