Cerastium pensylvanicum

Cerastium pensylvanicum, commonly known as Pennsylvania snowberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
