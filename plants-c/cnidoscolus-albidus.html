Cnidoscolus albidus

Cnidoscolus albidus, commonly known as the white prickly pear, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, palmate leaves that are covered in sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits similar to those of the prickly pear cactus. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
