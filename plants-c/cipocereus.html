Cipocereus

Cipocereus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a genus of cacti native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and the ability to produce large, showy flowers at night. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Cipocereus species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and unique flowering habits.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh desert environments.
