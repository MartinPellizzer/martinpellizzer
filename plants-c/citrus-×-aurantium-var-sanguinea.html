Citrus × aurantium var. sanguinea

Citrus × aurantium var. sanguinea, commonly known as blood orange.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive deep red or purple pigmentation in the flesh and peel, which is due to the presence of anthocyanins. It is a hybrid citrus plant, originating from the cross between Citrus sinensis and Citrus aurantium. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit.
