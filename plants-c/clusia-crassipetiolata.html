Clusia crassipetiolata

Clusia crassipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-petioled clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, dark green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb and store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Clusia crassipetiolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.
