Calamintha aegyptiaca

Calamintha aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Calamintha aegyptiaca is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
