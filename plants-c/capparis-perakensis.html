Capparis perakensis

Capparis perakensis, commonly known as the Perak caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that contain edible seeds, though the fruit is often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a preference for tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Capparis perakensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in local habitats.
