Carex limosa var. irrigata

Carex limosa var. irrigata, commonly known as the marsh sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is adapted to moist or water-saturated soils and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
