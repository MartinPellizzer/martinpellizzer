Carex lasiocarpa f. meduaensis

Carex lasiocarpa f. meduaensis, commonly known as the Meduenas sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, greenish flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
