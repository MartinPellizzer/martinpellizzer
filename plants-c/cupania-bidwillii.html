Cupania bidwillii

Cupania bidwillii, commonly known as the Australian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Cupania bidwillii thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in coastal regions and rainforests.
