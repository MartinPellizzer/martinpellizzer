Chamaesyce anthonyi

Chamaesyce anthonyi, commonly known as Anthony's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with numerous small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Chamaesyce anthonyi is often found in rocky soils and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
