Carex microdonta

Carex microdonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marsh habitats, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, typically green or brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
