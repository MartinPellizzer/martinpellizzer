Croton subferrugineus

Croton subferrugineus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which ranges from deep red to orange and yellow hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Croton subferrugineus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
