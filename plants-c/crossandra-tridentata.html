Crossandra tridentata

Crossandra tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and cylindrical. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
