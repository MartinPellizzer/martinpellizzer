Cacalia tomentosa var. hybrida

Cacalia tomentosa var. hybrida, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
