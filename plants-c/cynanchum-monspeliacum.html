Cynanchum monspeliacum

Cynanchum monspeliacum, commonly known as the European dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
