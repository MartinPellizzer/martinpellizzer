Cyrtandra × chartiformis

Cyrtandra × chartiformis, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra uniflora and Cyrtandra chartiformis, characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth surface, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

The hybrid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated settings.
