Cephaloscirpus macrocephalus

Cephaloscirpus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed scirpus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance with tall, cylindrical stems that often exceed 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stems, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
