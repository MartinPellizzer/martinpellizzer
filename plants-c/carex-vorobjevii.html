Carex vorobjevii

Carex vorobjevii, commonly known as Vorobjev's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets of small, three-lobed flowers. Native to eastern Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
