Cyperus erectus var. schlechteri

Cyperus erectus var. schlechteri, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in disturbed or aquatic habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
