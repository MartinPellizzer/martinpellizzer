Cucumis melo var. thessalonicus

Cucumis melo var. thessalonicus, commonly known as the Cretan melon, is a variety of the cantaloupe melon native to the Greek region of Crete.

It is characterized by its rough, green rind with a distinctive netted pattern and a sweet, aromatic, honey-colored flesh. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by bees, leading to the development of its edible fruit.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
