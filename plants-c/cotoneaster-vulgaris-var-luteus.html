Cotoneaster vulgaris var. luteus

Cotoneaster vulgaris var. luteus, commonly known as the golden cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The golden cotoneaster is also valued for its adaptability and ability to attract wildlife.
