Carex kagoshimensis

Carex kagoshimensis, commonly known as the Kagoshima sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a deep green color.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color.
