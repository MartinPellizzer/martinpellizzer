Centaurea triumfettii

Centaurea triumfettii, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flower heads and a robust, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Centaurea triumfettii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
