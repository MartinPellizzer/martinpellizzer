Carex leucochlora

Carex leucochlora, commonly known as the white-capped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters. Native to parts of North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green with a white or pale band near the base.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
