Carex laevigata

Carex laevigata, commonly known as smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex laevigata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
