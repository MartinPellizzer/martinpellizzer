Chrysocoma foetida

Chrysocoma foetida, commonly known as the stinking acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in various indigenous communities.
