Coffea arabica f. abyssinica

Coffea arabica f. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian coffee plant, is a wild form of Arabica coffee native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries containing two seeds, which are used to make coffee beans. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and high rainfall.

This subspecies is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often used in breeding programs to improve coffee varieties.
