Cattleya forbesii f. coerulea

Cattleya forbesii f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, vivid blue petals and sepals.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant typically produces large, showy blooms with a distinctive white lip that contrasts with the deep blue coloration. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

This cultivar is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
