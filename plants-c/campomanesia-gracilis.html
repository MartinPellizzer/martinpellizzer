Campomanesia gracilis

Campomanesia gracilis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries.

Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible pulp, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
