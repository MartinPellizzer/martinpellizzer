Colpodium trichopodum

Colpodium trichopodum, commonly known as the hairy colpodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The hairy colpodium is often found in clusters and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
