Costus chrysocephalus

Costus chrysocephalus, commonly known as the golden head costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescence. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
