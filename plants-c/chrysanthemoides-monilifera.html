Chrysanthemoides monilifera

Chrysanthemoides monilifera, commonly known as the coastal chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or rocky soils with high salt tolerance. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Chrysanthemoides monilifera is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
