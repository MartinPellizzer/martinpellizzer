Crassula connata var. muscoides

Crassula connata var. muscoides, commonly known as the musk crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
