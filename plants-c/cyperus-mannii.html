Cyperus mannii

Cyperus mannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

Cyperus mannii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
