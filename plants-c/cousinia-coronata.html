Cousinia coronata

Cousinia coronata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cousinia coronata is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
