Cryptocarya minima

Cryptocarya minima, commonly known as the small-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cryptocarya minima is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
