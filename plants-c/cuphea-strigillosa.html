Cuphea strigillosa

Cuphea strigillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract hummingbirds and bees, making it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
