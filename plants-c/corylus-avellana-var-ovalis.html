Corylus avellana var. ovalis

Corylus avellana var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval hazelnut, is a variety of the common hazelnut.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped nuts and dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves that are serrated along the edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible nuts.

The flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers on separate plants, typical of the hazelnut genus.
