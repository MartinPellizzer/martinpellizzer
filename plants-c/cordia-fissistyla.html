Cordia fissistyla

Cordia fissistyla, commonly known as the fissured cordia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, smooth bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
