Cheiranthus laciniatus

Cheiranthus laciniatus, commonly known as the fringed poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers with frayed petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
