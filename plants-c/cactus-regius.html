Cactus regius

Cactus regius, commonly known as the imperial cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

Native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Peru, it is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features spines that provide protection against herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, bloom in the early morning and last only a single day.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Cactus regius is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
