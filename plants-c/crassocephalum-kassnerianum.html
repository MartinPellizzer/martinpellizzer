Crassocephalum kassnerianum

Crassocephalum kassnerianum, commonly known as the African blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by a dense layer of white to pale blue bracts. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value.
