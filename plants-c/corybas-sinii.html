Corybas sinii

Corybas sinii, commonly known as the Chinese hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to China.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that resembles a skull, giving it a striking and somewhat eerie appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually a deep purple or maroon color, with a prominent central spur.

Corybas sinii is known for its delicate structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
