Crataegus × silesiaca

Crataegus × silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Crataegus × silesiaca is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
