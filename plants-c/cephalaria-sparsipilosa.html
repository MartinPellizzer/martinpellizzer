Cephalaria sparsipilosa

Cephalaria sparsipilosa, commonly known as the slender cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, exhibiting a herbaceous structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cephalaria sparsipilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
