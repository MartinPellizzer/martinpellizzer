Caralluma sarkariae var. longipedicellata

Caralluma sarkariae var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the Longipedicellate Candelabra Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
