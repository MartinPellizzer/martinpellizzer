Copiapoa cinerascens

Copiapoa cinerascens, commonly known as the gray copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a grayish-green stem that becomes more reddish with age. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Copiapoa cinerascens is also known for its slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
