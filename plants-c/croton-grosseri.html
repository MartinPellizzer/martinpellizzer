Croton grosseri

Croton grosseri, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a distinctive white or yellowish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Croton grosseri is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and resilience to drought.
