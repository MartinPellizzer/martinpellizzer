Corydalis bulleyana subsp. bulleyana

Corydalis bulleyana subsp.

bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyan fumariola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit and distinctive floral structure.
