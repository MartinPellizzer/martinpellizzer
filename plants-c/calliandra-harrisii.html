Calliandra harrisii

Calliandra harrisii, commonly known as the coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often resembling coral. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy flowers.

Calliandra harrisii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
