Chlorospatha pubescens

Chlorospatha pubescens, commonly known as the hairy green fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Chlorospatha pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
