Cratylia floribunda var. nuda

Cratylia floribunda var. nuda, commonly known as the naked flowering cratylia, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
