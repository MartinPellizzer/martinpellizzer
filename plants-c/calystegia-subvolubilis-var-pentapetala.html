Calystegia subvolubilis var. pentapetala

Calystegia subvolubilis var. pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled morning glory, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and have five distinct petals, giving the species its varietal name. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
