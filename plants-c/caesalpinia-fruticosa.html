Caesalpinia fruticosa

Caesalpinia fruticosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feather-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish hue. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The seeds are enclosed in long, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.
