Canscora melastomacea

Canscora melastomacea, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom in various colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Canscora melastomacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
