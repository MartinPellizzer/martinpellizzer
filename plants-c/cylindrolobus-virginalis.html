Cylindrolobus virginalis

Cylindrolobus virginalis, commonly known as the slender leaved lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, elongated thallus structure.

It typically grows on bark, stones, or soil in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant's thallus is composed of a dense network of fungal hyphae intertwined with algae or cyanobacteria, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. Its color ranges from pale green to yellowish, depending on the species of photobiont it hosts.

Cylindrolobus virginalis plays a role in ecological succession and serves as an indicator of environmental conditions due to its sensitivity to air pollution.
