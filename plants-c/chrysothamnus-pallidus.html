Chrysothamnus pallidus

Chrysothamnus pallidus, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
