Calamus dumetorum

Calamus dumetorum, commonly known as the marsh reed or sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Calamus dumetorum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
