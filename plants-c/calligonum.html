Calligonum

Calligonum, commonly known as the saltbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These shrubs are well adapted to arid and saline environments, often found in coastal areas and inland deserts. They have small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss and are typically covered in a waxy coating. The flowers are usually greenish or yellowish and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Calligonum species are valued for their ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock in harsh climates.
