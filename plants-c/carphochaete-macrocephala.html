Carphochaete macrocephala

Carphochaete macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed false aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Carphochaete macrocephala is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
