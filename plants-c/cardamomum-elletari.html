Cardamomum elletari

Cardamomum elletari, commonly known as the black cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit, which is a dark, aromatic pod, is the primary part used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
