Cactus amabilis

Cactus amabilis, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to withstand periods of drought.
