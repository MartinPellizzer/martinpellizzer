Calceolaria chelidonioides subsp. gibbiformis

Calceolaria chelidonioides subsp.

gibbiformis, commonly known as the warty calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy blooms in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
