Candollea setacea

Candollea setacea, commonly known as the hairy cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. Candollea setacea is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
