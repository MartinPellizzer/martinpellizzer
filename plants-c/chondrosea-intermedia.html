Chondrosea intermedia

Chondrosea intermedia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue or purple flowers that resemble small holly berries. The plant is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and grows in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Chondrosea intermedia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
