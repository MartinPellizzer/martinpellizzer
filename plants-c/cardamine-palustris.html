Cardamine palustris

Cardamine palustris, commonly known as marsh watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbages and mustards. The plant has small, alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Cardamine palustris thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats, often growing in partial to full shade.
