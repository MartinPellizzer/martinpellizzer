Crocus chrysanthus subsp. punctatus

Crocus chrysanthus subsp.

punctatus, commonly known as the spotted crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, violet-blue flowers with distinctive yellow markings and a central green stigma.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful blooms.
