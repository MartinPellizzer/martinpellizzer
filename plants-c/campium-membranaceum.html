Campium membranaceum

Campium membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous campion, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are ovate and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Campium membranaceum is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
