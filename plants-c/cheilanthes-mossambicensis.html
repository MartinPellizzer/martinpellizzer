Cheilanthes mossambicensis

Cheilanthes mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mossambique lip fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, arid environments. The plant has fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into lanceolate, serrated leaves. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The fronds are typically green, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.
