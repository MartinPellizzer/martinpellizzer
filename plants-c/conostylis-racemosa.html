Conostylis racemosa

Conostylis racemosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that remain on the plant for an extended period, giving it its common name. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and inland regions.

Conostylis racemosa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
