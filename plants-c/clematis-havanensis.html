Clematis havanensis

Clematis havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually blue or purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, fluffy fruits known as achenes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
