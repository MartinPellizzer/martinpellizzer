Cephaelis ombrophila

Cephaelis ombrophila, commonly known as the rainforest bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of dense rainforests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often covered in fine hairs.

Cephaelis ombrophila is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting diverse insect and bird life.
