Coronilla charadzeae

Coronilla charadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian crown vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and serrated. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Caucasus region and is adapted to rocky and arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
