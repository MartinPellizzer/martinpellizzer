Cyperus schaffneri

Cyperus schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus schaffneri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
