Calonemorchis arenaria

Calonemorchis arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Calonemorchis arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
