Canarium eupteron

Canarium eupteron, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its most notable botanical feature is the aromatic, oil-rich seed kernel, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental value and potential economic importance in sustainable forestry.
