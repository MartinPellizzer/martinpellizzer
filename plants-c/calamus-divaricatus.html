Calamus divaricatus

Calamus divaricatus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant has slender, triangular stems that often exhibit a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically found at the ends of the stems.

Calamus divaricatus is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in aquatic environments.
