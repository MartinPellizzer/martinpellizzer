Cheesemania enysii

Cheesemania enysii, commonly known as the cheese plant, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, white, cheese-like flowers that emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unusual appearance and fragrance make it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.

Despite its name, it is not related to true cheese but is named for the texture and scent of its flowers.
