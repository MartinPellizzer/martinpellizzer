Carex subphacota var. glauca

Carex subphacota var. glauca, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish or grayish hue, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
