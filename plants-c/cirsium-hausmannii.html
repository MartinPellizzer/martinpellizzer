Cirsium hausmannii

Cirsium hausmannii, commonly known as the Hausmann's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, violet-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium hausmannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking visual appeal.
