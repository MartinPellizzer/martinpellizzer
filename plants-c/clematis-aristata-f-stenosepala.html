Clematis aristata f. stenosepala

Clematis aristata f. stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled Clematis, is a variant of the Clematis aristata species.

It is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, twining stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and floral display.
