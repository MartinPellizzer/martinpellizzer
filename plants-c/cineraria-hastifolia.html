Cineraria hastifolia

Cineraria hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cineraria hastifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
