Cyrtochilum aemulum

Cyrtochilum aemulum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Cyrtochilum aemulum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
