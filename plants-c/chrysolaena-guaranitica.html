Chrysolaena guaranitica

Chrysolaena guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani goldenbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Chrysolaena guaranitica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
