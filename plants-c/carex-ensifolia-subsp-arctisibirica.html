Carex ensifolia subsp. arctisibirica

Carex ensifolia subsp.

arctisibirica, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and sword-shaped, with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the top of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
