Cyperus musarius

Cyperus musarius, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus musarius is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
