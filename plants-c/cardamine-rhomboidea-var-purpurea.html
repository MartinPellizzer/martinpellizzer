Cardamine rhomboidea var. purpurea

Cardamine rhomboidea var. purpurea, commonly known as purple watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being rhomboid in shape, hence the species name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams and in wetlands. Its flowers are small and white or pale purple, arranged in a raceme, and it produces small, round seeds.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
