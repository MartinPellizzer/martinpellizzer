Citrus × aurantium f. intermedia

Citrus × aurantium f. intermedia, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used for their high acidity and aromatic properties. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and is also used in the production of essential oils.

However, it is not typically consumed as food because of its strong, tart flavor.
