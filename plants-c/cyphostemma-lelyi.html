Cyphostemma lelyi

Cyphostemma lelyi, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing black seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
