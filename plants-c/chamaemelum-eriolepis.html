Chamaemelum eriolepis

Chamaemelum eriolepis, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
