Centropogon mandonis

Centropogon mandonis, commonly known as the Mandon's centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
