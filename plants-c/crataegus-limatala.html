Crataegus limatala

Crataegus limatala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often reddish when young. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus limatala is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
