Crudia brevipes

Crudia brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

It is a low-growing perennial that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
