Calophyllum subsessile

Calophyllum subsessile, commonly known as the slender calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
