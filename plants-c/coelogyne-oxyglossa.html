Coelogyne oxyglossa

Coelogyne oxyglossa, commonly known as the silver-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

Coelogyne oxyglossa is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its role in local horticultural practices.
