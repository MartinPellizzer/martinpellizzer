Calligonum pseudohumile

Calligonum pseudohumile, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in sandy and saline soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Calligonum pseudohumile plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
