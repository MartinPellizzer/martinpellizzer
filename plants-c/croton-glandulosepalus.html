Croton glandulosepalus

Croton glandulosepalus, commonly known as the glandular-sepal croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that can vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
