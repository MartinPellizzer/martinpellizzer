Costus pictus

Costus pictus, commonly known as the painted costus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
