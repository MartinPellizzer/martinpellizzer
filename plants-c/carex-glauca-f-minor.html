Carex glauca f. minor

Carex glauca f. minor, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that range from blue-green to gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in the summer months. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is often used in wetland restoration and natural landscaping projects.
