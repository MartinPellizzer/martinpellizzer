Cratylia

Cratylia, commonly known as the burr legume, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of South America and are characterized by their distinctive, spiny seed pods that enclose the seeds. The leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the flowers are small and often clustered in racemes. Cratylia species are often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as forage for livestock.

Some species are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their unique appearance and adaptability to different soil conditions.
