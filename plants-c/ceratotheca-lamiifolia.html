Ceratotheca lamiifolia

Ceratotheca lamiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases seeds when mature.
