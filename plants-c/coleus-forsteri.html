Coleus forsteri

Coleus forsteri, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and display vibrant shades of red, purple, and green. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
