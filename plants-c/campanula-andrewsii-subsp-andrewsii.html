Campanula andrewsii subsp. andrewsii

Campanula andrewsii subsp.

andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to rocky or alpine environments.
