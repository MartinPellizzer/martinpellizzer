Cerastium yazawanum

Cerastium yazawanum, commonly known as the Yozan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, rounded, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Cerastium yazawanum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
