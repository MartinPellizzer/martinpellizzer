Commelina hexandra

Commelina hexandra, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It is a vigorous climber, often found growing along the ground or climbing supports in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
