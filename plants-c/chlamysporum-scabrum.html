Chlamysporum scabrum

Chlamysporum scabrum, commonly known as the rough scabrum, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface.

It is noted for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well adapted to harsh coastal conditions.
