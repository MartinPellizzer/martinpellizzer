Cylindropuntia munzii

Cylindropuntia munzii, commonly known as the Munz's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems, which are covered in spines and often bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are typically orange in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to rocky soils and full sun exposure.

The species is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
