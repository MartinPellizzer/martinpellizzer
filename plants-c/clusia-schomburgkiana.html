Clusia schomburgkiana

Clusia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia schomburgkiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
