Chrysothamnus speciosus var. plattensis

Chrysothamnus speciosus var. plattensis, commonly known as Platt's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its yellow to orange flowers and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Platt's golden aster is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
