Carex pseudobuxbaumii

Carex pseudobuxbaumii, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form compact clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Carex pseudobuxbaumii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
