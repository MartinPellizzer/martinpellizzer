Crataegus stipulosa

Crataegus stipulosa, commonly known as the stipulate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and often have stipules at the base.

Crataegus stipulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
