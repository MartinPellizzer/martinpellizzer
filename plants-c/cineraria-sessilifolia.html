Cineraria sessilifolia

Cineraria sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
