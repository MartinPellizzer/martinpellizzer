Cnicus alpinus

Cnicus alpinus, commonly known as the Alpine sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months.

Cnicus alpinus is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
