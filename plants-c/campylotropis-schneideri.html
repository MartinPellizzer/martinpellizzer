Campylotropis schneideri

Campylotropis schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, dense clusters of small, pink to red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial to full sun.
