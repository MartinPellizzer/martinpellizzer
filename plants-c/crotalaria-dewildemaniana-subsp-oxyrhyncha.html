Crotalaria dewildemaniana subsp. oxyrhyncha

Crotalaria dewildemaniana subsp.

oxyrhyncha, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its adaptability to diverse soil types.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
