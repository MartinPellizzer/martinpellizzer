Cerasus bifrons f. media

Cerasus bifrons f. media, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or sweet cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and early blooming, producing showy pink to white flowers in spring. The plant typically bears small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and fruit-bearing gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly during the flowering season.
