Cytisus purgans

Cytisus purgans, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Cytisus purgans is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
