Cocculus japonicus

Cocculus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese cocklebur, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and spiny fruits. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit, a woody capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cocculus japonicus is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
