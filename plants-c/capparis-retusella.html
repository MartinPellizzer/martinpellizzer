Capparis retusella

Capparis retusella, commonly known as the rough caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its unique foliage and flowers.
