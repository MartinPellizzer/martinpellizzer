Chaydaia

Chaydaia, commonly known as the "wild lettuce" or "chayda," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Chaydaia has hairy stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
