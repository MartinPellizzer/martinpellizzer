Cracca longifolia

Cracca longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and finely divided, pinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland or grassy areas.

Cracca longifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
