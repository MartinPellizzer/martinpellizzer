Cucumis melo var. schraderianus

Cucumis melo var. schraderianus, commonly known as the Schraderian melon, is a botanical variant of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
