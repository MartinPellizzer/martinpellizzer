Carex backiana

Carex backiana, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Carex backiana is often found in coastal dunes and other xerophytic habitats.
