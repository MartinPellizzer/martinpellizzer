Croton draconoideus

Croton draconoideus, commonly known as the dragon's blood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and a distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its most notable feature is the reddish-brown latex it exudes, which has been historically used for medicinal and dye purposes.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
