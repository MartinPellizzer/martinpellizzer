Curcuma officinalis

Curcuma officinalis, commonly known as turmeric, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The rhizomes are the primary part used, containing the bioactive compound curcumin.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary applications.
