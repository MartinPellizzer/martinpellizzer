Cacalia birmanica

Cacalia birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a bell-shaped appearance, and bloom during the summer months.

Cacalia birmanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
