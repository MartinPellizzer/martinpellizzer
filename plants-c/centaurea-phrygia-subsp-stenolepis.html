Centaurea phrygia subsp. stenolepis

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other forms of Centaurea phrygia.
