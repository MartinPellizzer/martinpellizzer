Carex rachillis

Carex rachillis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often surrounded by bracts.

Carex rachillis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
