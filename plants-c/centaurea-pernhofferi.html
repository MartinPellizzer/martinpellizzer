Centaurea pernhofferi

Centaurea pernhofferi, commonly known as the Pernhoffer's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its slender, upright stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight hairy texture.

Centaurea pernhofferi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in poor soils.
