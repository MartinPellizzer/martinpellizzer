Colchicum circinatum subsp. circinatum

Colchicum circinatum subsp.

circinatum, commonly known as the circling colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces a single, erect stem with large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
