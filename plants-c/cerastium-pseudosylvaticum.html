Cerastium pseudosylvaticum

Cerastium pseudosylvaticum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions.

Cerastium pseudosylvaticum thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
