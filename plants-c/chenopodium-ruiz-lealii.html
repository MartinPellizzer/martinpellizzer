Chenopodium ruiz-lealii

Chenopodium ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the Ruiz-Leal's goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
