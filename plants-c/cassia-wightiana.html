Cassia wightiana

Cassia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia wightiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
