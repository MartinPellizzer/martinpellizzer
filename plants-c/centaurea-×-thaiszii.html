Centaurea × thaiszii

Centaurea × thaiszii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Centaurea × thaiszii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
