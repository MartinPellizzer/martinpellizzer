Campanulopsis cyanea

Campanulopsis cyanea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanulopsis cyanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
