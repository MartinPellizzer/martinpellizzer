Clematis craibiana

Clematis craibiana, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often with a distinctive purple hue. Its leaves are compound, typically with three leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs.

Clematis craibiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
