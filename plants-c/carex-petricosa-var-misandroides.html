Carex petricosa var. misandroides

Carex petricosa var. misandroides, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological value.
