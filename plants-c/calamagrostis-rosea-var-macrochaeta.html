Calamagrostis rosea var. macrochaeta

Calamagrostis rosea var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the pink moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple inflorescence, which adds ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
