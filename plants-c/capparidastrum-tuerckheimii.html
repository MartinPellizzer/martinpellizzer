Capparidastrum tuerckheimii

Capparidastrum tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique floral display.
