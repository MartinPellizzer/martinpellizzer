Crotalaria agatiflora subsp. vaginifera

Crotalaria agatiflora subsp.

vaginifera, commonly known as the hooded cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a prominent hood-like structure covering the reproductive organs.

The plant produces slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod that contains several seeds.

This species is often grown as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.
