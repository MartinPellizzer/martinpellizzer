Cyperus × amuricoides

Cyperus × amuricoides, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
