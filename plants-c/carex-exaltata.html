Carex exaltata

Carex exaltata, commonly known as tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex exaltata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
