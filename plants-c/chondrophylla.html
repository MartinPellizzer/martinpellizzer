Chondrophylla

Chondrophylla, commonly known as the glassweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with low temperatures and strong winds. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Chondrophylla is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
