Cistus lilacinus

Cistus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, lilac-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus lilacinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
