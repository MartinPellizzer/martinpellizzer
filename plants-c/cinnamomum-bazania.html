Cinnamomum bazania

Cinnamomum bazania, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and emit a strong cinnamon scent when crushed.

The bark is used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.
