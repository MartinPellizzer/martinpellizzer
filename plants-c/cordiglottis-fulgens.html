Cordiglottis fulgens

Cordiglottis fulgens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Cordiglottis fulgens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
