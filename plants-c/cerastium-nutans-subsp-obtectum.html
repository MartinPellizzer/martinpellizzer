Cerastium nutans subsp. obtectum

Cerastium nutans subsp.

obtectum, commonly known as the bent-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.
