Cassia aphylla var. trichosepala

Cassia aphylla var. trichosepala, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by elongated, narrow pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive foliage and floral display.
