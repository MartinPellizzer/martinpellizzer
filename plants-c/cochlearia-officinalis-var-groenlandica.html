Cochlearia officinalis var. groenlandica

Cochlearia officinalis var. groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is often found in coastal areas and tundra habitats across Greenland and northern Scandinavia.
