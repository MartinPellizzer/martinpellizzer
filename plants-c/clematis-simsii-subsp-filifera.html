Clematis simsii subsp. filifera

Clematis simsii subsp.

filifera, commonly known as the Chinese climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China. It features fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in late spring.

The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, woody berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and showy blooms.
