Coronilla coronata var. rossica

Coronilla coronata var. rossica, commonly known as the Siberian crownvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in horticulture and restoration projects.
