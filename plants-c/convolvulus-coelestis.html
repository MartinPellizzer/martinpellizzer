Convolvulus coelestis

Convolvulus coelestis, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
