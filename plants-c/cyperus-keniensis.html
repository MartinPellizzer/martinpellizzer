Cyperus keniensis

Cyperus keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedge species characterized by their triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Cyperus keniensis is often found in shallow aquatic environments and can form dense vegetation mats.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
