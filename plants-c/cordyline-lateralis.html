Cordyline lateralis

Cordyline lateralis, commonly known as the silver cordyline, is a striking evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It features tall, slender stems with striking silver-blue leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish, followed by distinctive, three-lobed berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a standout plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
