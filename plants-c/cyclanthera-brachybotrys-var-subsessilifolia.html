Cyclanthera brachybotrys var. subsessilifolia

Cyclanthera brachybotrys var. subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow, edible fruits that are typically bitter in taste. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use in certain regions.
