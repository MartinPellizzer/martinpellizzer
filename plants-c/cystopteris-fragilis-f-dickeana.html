Cystopteris fragilis f. dickeana

Cystopteris fragilis f. dickeana, commonly known as the brittle fern or fragrant water fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The fronds are typically light green, with a soft, leathery texture and a distinctive, fragrant aroma when crushed. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in humid, tropical gardens.
