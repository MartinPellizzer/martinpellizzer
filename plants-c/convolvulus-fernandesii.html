Convolvulus fernandesii

Convolvulus fernandesii, commonly known as the Portuguese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native environment.
