Carex × pieperiana

Carex × pieperiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Carex pansa and Carex echinata, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and its inflorescence consists of several spikelets.

Carex × pieperiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
