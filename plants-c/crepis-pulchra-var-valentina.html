Crepis pulchra var. valentina

Crepis pulchra var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to southern Europe, this species thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can be seen in meadows and along pathways.
