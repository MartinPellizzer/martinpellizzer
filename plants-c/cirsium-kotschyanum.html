Cirsium kotschyanum

Cirsium kotschyanum, commonly known as the Kotschyan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust, upright stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cirsium kotschyanum is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture due to its striking appearance.
