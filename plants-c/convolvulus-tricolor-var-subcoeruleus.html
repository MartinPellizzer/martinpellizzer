Convolvulus tricolor var. subcoeruleus

Convolvulus tricolor var. subcoeruleus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems that twine around supports. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often blooming from spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
