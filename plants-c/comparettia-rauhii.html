Comparettia rauhii

Comparettia rauhii, commonly known as the Rauh's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower structure that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Comparettia rauhii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
