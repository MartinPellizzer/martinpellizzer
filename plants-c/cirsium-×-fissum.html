Cirsium × fissum

Cirsium × fissum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, thriving in temperate climates. Its foliage is covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Cirsium × fissum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
