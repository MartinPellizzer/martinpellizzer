Combretum anisopterum

Combretum anisopterum, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting birds and insects. The leaves are simple, alternate, and usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant has a rough, fissured bark that becomes more prominent with age.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical and tropical regions.
