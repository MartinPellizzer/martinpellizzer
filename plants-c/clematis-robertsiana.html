Clematis robertsiana

Clematis robertsiana, commonly known as the Robertsiana Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with palmately compound leaves that are typically three to five lobes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five to nine sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

Clematis robertsiana thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
