Cissus davidiana

Cissus davidiana, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus davidiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
