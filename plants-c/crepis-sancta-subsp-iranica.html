Crepis sancta subsp. iranica

Crepis sancta subsp.

iranica, commonly known as the Iranian crepis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
