Conophora maxima

Conophora maxima, commonly known as the giant conophora, is a striking member of the Conophoraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the most visually prominent plants in its native habitat. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires a significant amount of sunlight and moisture.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in the study of botanical adaptation and biodiversity.
