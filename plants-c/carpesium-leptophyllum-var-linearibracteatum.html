Carpesium leptophyllum var. linearibracteatum

Carpesium leptophyllum var. linearibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow bract horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

The variety linearibracteatum is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the flower heads.
