Cirsium lanceolatum var. virens

Cirsium lanceolatum var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spiny, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
