Chamaesyce Canescens Subsp. Glabra

Chamaesyce canescens subsp. glabra is a hardy flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is known for its distinctive gray-green foliage and delicate yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. This succulent species is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in low-water environments. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Despite its small size, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
