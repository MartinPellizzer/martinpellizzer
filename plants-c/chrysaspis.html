Chrysaspis

Chrysaspis, commonly known as the golden mossy orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, moss-like appearance and vibrant golden coloration, which is particularly striking in its inflorescence. These plants are typically epiphytic, growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions, and they rely on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The leaves are often dense and clustered, providing a habitat for small invertebrates.

Chrysaspis species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
