Convolvulus involucratus

Convolvulus involucratus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces trumpet-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus involucratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
