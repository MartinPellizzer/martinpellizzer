Cissus lutea

Cissus lutea, commonly known as the yellow fig or creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance make it a common ornamental plant.

Cissus lutea is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
