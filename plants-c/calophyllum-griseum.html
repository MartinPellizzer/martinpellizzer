Calophyllum griseum

Calophyllum griseum, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing seeds. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt spray make it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
