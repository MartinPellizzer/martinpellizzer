Cyphomandra calycina

Cyphomandra calycina, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces small, yellow, oval-shaped fruits that are highly aromatic and contain a sweet, tangy pulp. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically pale yellow with five petals and are essential for fruit development.
