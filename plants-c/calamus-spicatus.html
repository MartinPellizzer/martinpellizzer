Calamus spicatus

Calamus spicatus, commonly known as the spicate calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has long, slender rhizomes that can reach several meters in length, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Calamus spicatus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
