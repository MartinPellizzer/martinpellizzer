Cirsium fimbriatum

Cirsium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a tall flowering stem with bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
