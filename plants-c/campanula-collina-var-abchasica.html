Campanula collina var. abchasica

Campanula collina var. abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
