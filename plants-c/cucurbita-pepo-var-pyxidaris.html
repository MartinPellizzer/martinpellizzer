Cucurbita pepo var. pyxidaris

Cucurbita pepo var. pyxidaris, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinct, elongated, acorn-shaped fruits.

The plant produces large, hairy leaves and yellow flowers, typical of many cucurbit species. Its fruit is typically orange to yellow in color, with a firm, edible flesh that is rich in nutrients. The plant is commonly cultivated for its edible squash, which is used in various culinary preparations.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
