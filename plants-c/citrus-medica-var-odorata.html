Citrus medica var. odorata

Citrus medica var. odorata, commonly known as the citron.

This variety of citrus is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that are typically yellow to green in color. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its fragrant rind and edible pulp. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The leaves are evergreen, and the plant can grow as a tree or shrub, depending on the climate and care provided.
