Corynophallus angolensis

Corynophallus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola devil's tongue, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Angola, and thrives in rocky, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its unique morphology includes a fleshy, reddish stem and waxy, heart-shaped leaves.

Corynophallus angolensis is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and visually distinctive plant.
