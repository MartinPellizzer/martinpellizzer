Carduus tweedyi

Carduus tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces spiny, prickly leaves and a showy purple to pink composite flower that blooms in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
