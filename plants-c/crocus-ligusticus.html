Crocus ligusticus

Crocus ligusticus, commonly known as the Ligurian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinct purple or brown stripe. The plant typically blooms in early spring, with its foliage appearing shortly after flowering. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
