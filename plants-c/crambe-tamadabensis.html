Crambe tamadabensis

Crambe tamadabensis, commonly known as the Tamada bean, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has pinnate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Crambe tamadabensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
