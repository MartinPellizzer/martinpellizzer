Cuphea ericoides var. oxycedrina

Cuphea ericoides var. oxycedrina, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative shrub. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
