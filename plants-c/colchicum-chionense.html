Colchicum chionense

Colchicum chionense, commonly known as the snow colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces a bulbous corm, which serves as its storage organ and is crucial for its survival during harsh winters. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Colchicum chionense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
