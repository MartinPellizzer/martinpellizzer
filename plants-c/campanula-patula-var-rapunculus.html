Campanula patula var. rapunculus

Campanula patula var. rapunculus, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
