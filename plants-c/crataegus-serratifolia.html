Crataegus serratifolia

Crataegus serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
