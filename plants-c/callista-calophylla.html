Callista calophylla

Callista calophylla, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callista calophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
