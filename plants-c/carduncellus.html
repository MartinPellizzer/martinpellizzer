Carduncellus

Carduncellus, commonly known as the cardoon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, spoon-shaped leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Its roots and leaves are edible, though they require thorough preparation to remove their natural bitterness.
