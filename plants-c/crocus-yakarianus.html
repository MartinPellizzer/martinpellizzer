Crocus yakarianus

Crocus yakarianus, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are borne on short, sturdy stems. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and the plant is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Crocus yakarianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in the regions where it is native.
