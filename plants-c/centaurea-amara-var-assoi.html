Centaurea amara var. assoi

Centaurea amara var. assoi, commonly known as the bitter centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant is known for its bitter-tasting leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
