Cracca linearis

Cracca linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its linear, grass-like leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Cracca linearis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
