Conophytum gratum

Conophytum gratum, commonly known as the "Cape snowflake," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
