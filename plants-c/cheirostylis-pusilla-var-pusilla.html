Cheirostylis pusilla var. pusilla

Cheirostylis pusilla var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with a few narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are delicate, with white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a spider's leg.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
