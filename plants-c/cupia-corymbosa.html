Cupia corymbosa

Cupia corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
