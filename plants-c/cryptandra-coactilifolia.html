Cryptandra coactilifolia

Cryptandra coactilifolia, commonly known as the slender cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
