Chionachne wallichiana

Chionachne wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan snowgrass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white to pale blue florets that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its alpine habitat. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.
