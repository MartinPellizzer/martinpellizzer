Carex oederi subsp. pulchella

Carex oederi subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Carex oederi subsp.

pulchella is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
