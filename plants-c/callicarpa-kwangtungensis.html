Callicarpa kwangtungensis

Callicarpa kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, bright purple to violet berries that are clustered in groups of four, making it visually striking. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are not typically eaten by humans but are popular with birds and other wildlife.
