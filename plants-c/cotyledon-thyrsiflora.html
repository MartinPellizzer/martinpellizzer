Cotyledon thyrsiflora

Cotyledon thyrsiflora, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
