Carex abscondita var. rostellata

Carex abscondita var. rostellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
