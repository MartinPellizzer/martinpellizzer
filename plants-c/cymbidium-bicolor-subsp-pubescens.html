Cymbidium bicolor subsp. pubescens

Cymbidium bicolor subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers.

The flowers typically bloom in winter or early spring, displaying a combination of white and green hues. The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy texture on the leaves and flower stems.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
