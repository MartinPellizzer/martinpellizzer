Calamagrostis rubella

Calamagrostis rubella, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
