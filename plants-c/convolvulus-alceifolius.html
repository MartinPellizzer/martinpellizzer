Convolvulus alceifolius

Convolvulus alceifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
