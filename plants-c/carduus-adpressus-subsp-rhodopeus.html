Carduus adpressus subsp. rhodopeus

Carduus adpressus subsp.

rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant features a robust taproot system and produces spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects.
