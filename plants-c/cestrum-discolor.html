Cestrum discolor

Cestrum discolor, commonly known as the white trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Cestrum discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
