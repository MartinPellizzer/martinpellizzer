Cyperus fenzelianus var. badiiformis

Cyperus fenzelianus var. badiiformis, commonly known as the banded flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The varietal form, badiiformis, is distinguished by its distinctive banded or striped appearance on the stems.
