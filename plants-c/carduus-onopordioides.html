Carduus onopordioides

Carduus onopordioides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
