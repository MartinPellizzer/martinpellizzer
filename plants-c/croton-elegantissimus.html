Croton elegantissimus

Croton elegantissimus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
