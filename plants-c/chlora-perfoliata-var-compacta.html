Chlora perfoliata var. compacta

Chlora perfoliata var. compacta, commonly known as the compact bluetail, is a unique variant of the bluetail plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of North America, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often clustered closely together, giving it a lush, compact appearance. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, and they bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
