Carex nardina

Carex nardina, commonly known as slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Carex nardina plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
