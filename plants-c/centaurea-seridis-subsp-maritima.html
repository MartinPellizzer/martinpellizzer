Centaurea seridis subsp. maritima

Centaurea seridis subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky coastal areas. It is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh maritime environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
