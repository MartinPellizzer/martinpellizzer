Cleistogenes mucronata

Cleistogenes mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and prairies. It has a C4 photosynthetic pathway, which allows it to efficiently utilize water and thrive in warm, dry environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
