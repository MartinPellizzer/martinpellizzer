Chrysophyllum dioicum

Chrysophyllum dioicum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is edible but not widely cultivated.
