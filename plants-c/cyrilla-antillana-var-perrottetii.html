Cyrilla antillana var. perrottetii

Cyrilla antillana var. perrottetii, commonly known as the Florida cyrilla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife species.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas along the coasts of Florida and the Gulf Coast.
