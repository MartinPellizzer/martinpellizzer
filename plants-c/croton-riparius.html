Croton riparius

Croton riparius, commonly known as the river croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that can vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
