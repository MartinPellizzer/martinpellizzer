Carex foraminatiformis

Carex foraminatiformis, commonly known as the porous-surfaced sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex foraminatiformis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
