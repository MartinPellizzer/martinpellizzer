Cirsium × schefferi

Cirsium × schefferi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium horridulum, resulting in a plant with unique morphological characteristics. The species is notable for its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and large, showy yellow to orange flower heads. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
