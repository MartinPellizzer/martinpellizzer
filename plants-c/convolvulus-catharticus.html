Convolvulus catharticus

Convolvulus catharticus, commonly known as the Spanish needle or beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
