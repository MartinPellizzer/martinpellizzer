Cyperus pustulatus var. djalonis

Cyperus pustulatus var. djalonis, commonly known as the African bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to West Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its swollen, bulbous nodes along its stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
