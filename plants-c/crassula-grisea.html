Crassula grisea

Crassula grisea, commonly known as the Grey Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and fleshy, greyish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring or summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Crassula grisea prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
