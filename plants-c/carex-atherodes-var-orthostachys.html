Carex atherodes var. orthostachys

Carex atherodes var. orthostachys, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
