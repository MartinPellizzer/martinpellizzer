Caroxylon kalaharicum

Caroxylon kalaharicum, commonly known as the Kalahari grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, particularly the Kalahari Desert region. This plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Caroxylon kalaharicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals in its native ecosystem.
