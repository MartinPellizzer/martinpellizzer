Celtis discolor

Celtis discolor, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Celtis discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
