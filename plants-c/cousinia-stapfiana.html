Cousinia stapfiana

Cousinia stapfiana, commonly known as the Kashmir thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia stapfiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
