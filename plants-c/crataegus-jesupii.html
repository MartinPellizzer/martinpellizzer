Crataegus jesupii

Crataegus jesupii, commonly known as Jesup's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall and are an important food source for various wildlife.

The botanical features of Crataegus jesupii make it a valuable component of native ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
