Callitris fruticosa

Callitris fruticosa, commonly known as the coastal white cypress, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often found on sandy soils and in dune ecosystems. It is well adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal regions.
