Croton sanctae-crucis

Croton sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the Cross-vein Croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's unique leaf patterns and adaptability to various soil types make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
