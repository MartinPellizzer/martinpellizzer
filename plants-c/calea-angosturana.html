Calea angosturana

Calea angosturana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent flowers that glow faintly in the dark. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular florets that open at night. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.
