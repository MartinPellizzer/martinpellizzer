Centromadia

Centromadia, commonly known as the "Mexican moonflower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. Centromadia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique blooming pattern and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
