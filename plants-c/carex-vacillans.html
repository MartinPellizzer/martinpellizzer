Carex vacillans

Carex vacillans, commonly known as the wavering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex vacillans prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
