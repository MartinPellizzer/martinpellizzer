Cucurbita pepo var. turonensis

Cucurbita pepo var. turonensis, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its elongated, ridged, and often pale green or yellowish fruit. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers typically bearing the fruit. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
