Cyphostemma ukerewense var. gabonicum

Cyphostemma ukerewense var. gabonicum, commonly known as the Gabon moonvine, is a climbing plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas in forests or along riverbanks.
