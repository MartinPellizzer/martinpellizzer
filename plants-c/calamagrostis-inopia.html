Calamagrostis inopia

Calamagrostis inopia, commonly known as the slender reed, is a grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Calamagrostis inopia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
