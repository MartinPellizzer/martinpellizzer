Cotyledon tanquana

Cotyledon tanquana, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in arid climates.
