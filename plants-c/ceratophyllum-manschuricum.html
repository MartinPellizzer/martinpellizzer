Ceratophyllum manschuricum

Ceratophyllum manschuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian hornwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds and lakes. Ceratophyllum manschuricum is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it useful in water purification.

Its reproductive system includes both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its adaptability in various aquatic environments.
