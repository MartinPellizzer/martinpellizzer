Carex dayuongensis

Carex dayuongensis, commonly known as the Dayuong sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptation to moist habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-parted flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
