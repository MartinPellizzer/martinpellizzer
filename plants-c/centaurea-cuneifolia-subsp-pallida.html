Centaurea cuneifolia subsp. pallida

Centaurea cuneifolia subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or white flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
