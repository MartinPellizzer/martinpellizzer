Conophytum reconditum

Conophytum reconditum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
