Cerastium ligusticum

Cerastium ligusticum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically hairy and rounded. Cerastium ligusticum is adapted to cold climates and is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value.
