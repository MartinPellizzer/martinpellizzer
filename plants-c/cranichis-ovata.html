Cranichis ovata

Cranichis ovata, commonly known as the ovate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
