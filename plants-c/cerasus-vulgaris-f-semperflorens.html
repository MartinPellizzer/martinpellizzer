Cerasus vulgaris f. semperflorens

Cerasus vulgaris f. semperflorens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom profusely in early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn green in summer and yellow in autumn. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic beauty.

The species is also valued for its cultural significance in Japanese traditions and festivals.
