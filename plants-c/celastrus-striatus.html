Celastrus striatus

Celastrus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Celastrus striatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
