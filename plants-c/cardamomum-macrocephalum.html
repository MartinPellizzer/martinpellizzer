Cardamomum macrocephalum

Cardamomum macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and striking, showy orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds enclosed in a three-lobed, three-chambered fruit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.
