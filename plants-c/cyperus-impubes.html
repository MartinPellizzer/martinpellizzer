Cyperus impubes

Cyperus impubes, commonly known as spiny flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the plant. Cyperus impubes thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
