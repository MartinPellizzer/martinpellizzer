Cyperus tibialis

Cyperus tibialis, commonly known as the Tibetan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Nepal.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.

Cyperus tibialis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
