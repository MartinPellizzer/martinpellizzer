Crataegus punctata var. pausiaca

Crataegus punctata var. pausiaca, commonly known as the Korean hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and showy white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
