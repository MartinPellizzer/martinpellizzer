Cirsium aciculare

Cirsium aciculare, commonly known as the needleleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's hardiness and ability to thrive in various environments make it a common sight in many regions.
