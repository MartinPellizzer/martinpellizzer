Cynanchum crispum

Cynanchum crispum, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by orange to red fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Cynanchum crispum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
