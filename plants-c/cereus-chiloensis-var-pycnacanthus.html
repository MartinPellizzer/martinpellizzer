Cereus chiloensis var. pycnacanthus

Cereus chiloensis var. pycnacanthus, commonly known as the Chilean night-blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is often cultivated for its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a columnar growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet scent.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
