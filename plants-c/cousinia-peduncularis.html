Cousinia peduncularis

Cousinia peduncularis, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing at high altitudes. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Cousinia peduncularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals in its native range.
