Coris monspeliensis subsp. rivasiana

Coris monspeliensis subsp.

rivasiana, commonly known as the Rivasian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal teas.
