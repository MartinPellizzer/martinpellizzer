Celastrus mossambicensis

Celastrus mossambicensis, commonly known as the African staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in its native range.
