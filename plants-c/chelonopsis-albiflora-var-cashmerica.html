Chelonopsis albiflora var. cashmerica

Chelonopsis albiflora var. cashmerica, commonly known as the Cashmere Chelonopsis, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white-pink flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle elegance to its foliage. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, which helps reflect sunlight in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
