Cucurbita maxima var. chlorophthalma

Cucurbita maxima var. chlorophthalma, commonly known as the golden hubbard squash, is a variant of the winter squash family characterized by its vibrant, golden-orange skin and dense, sweet flesh.

It is native to North America and is cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in both culinary and decorative applications due to its striking appearance and versatile uses.
