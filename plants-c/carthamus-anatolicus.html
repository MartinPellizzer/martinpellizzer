Carthamus anatolicus

Carthamus anatolicus, commonly known as Turkish safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow blooms. The plant produces seed heads that contain oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. Its botanical features include serrated leaves and a robust root system adapted to arid conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
