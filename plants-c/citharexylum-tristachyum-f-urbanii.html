Citharexylum tristachyum f. urbanii

Citharexylum tristachyum f. urbanii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
