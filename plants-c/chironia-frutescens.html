Chironia frutescens

Chironia frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf waxplant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, waxy, yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in warm climates.
