Carex cespitosa var. vulgaris

Carex cespitosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves and a dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other wet or damp habitats.

Carex cespitosa var. vulgaris plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
