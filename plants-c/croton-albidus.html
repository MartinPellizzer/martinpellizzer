Croton albidus

Croton albidus, commonly known as white croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton albidus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
