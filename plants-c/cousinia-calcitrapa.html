Cousinia calcitrapa

Cousinia calcitrapa, commonly known as the stonebreaker or desert hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia calcitrapa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
