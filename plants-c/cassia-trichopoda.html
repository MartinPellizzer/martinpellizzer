Cassia trichopoda

Cassia trichopoda, commonly known as African candle bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Cassia trichopoda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
