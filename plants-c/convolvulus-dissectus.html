Convolvulus dissectus

Convolvulus dissectus, commonly known as the dissected morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a trailing growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive, intricate appearance.

Convolvulus dissectus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
