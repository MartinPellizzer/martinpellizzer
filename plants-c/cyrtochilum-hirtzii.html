Cyrtochilum hirtzii

Cyrtochilum hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy structures. Cyrtochilum hirtzii thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
