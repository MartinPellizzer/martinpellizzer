Chamaeraphis glauca var. penicillata

Chamaeraphis glauca var. penicillata, commonly known as sea wheat, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with small, blue-green leaves. The plant forms compact clumps and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize coastal soils. It thrives in intertidal zones and is often found in salt marshes and sandy beaches.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important species for coastal ecosystem stability.
