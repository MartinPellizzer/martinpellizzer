Clutia rubricaulis

Clutia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, reddish stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with five petals, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Clutia rubricaulis is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
