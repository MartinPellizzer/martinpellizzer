Chionanthus ligustrinus

Chionanthus ligustrinus, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
