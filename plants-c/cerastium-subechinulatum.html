Cerastium subechinulatum

Cerastium subechinulatum, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats on the ground, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in early spring, producing small, inconspicuous blooms.

It is a hardy species that thrives in cool climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
