Caesalpinia pluviosa var. maraniona

Caesalpinia pluviosa var. maraniona, commonly known as the Maranona tree, is a tropical species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and the flowers are bright yellow with red streaks, forming dense inflorescences. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

It is often found in humid lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
