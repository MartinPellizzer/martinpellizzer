Chenopodium anthelminticum var. hastatum

Chenopodium anthelminticum var. hastatum, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It is native to temperate regions of the Americas but has been introduced to other parts of the world.

This species is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in vitamins and minerals.
