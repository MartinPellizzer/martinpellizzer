Croton hildebrandtii

Croton hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
