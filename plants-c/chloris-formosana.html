Chloris formosana

Chloris formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that help it spread and establish itself in various environments.

Chloris formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
