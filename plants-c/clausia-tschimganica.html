Clausia tschimganica

Clausia tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimganica cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers bloom during the warm season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clausia tschimganica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
