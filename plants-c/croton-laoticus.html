Croton laoticus

Croton laoticus, commonly known as the Laotian croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds.

Croton laoticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
