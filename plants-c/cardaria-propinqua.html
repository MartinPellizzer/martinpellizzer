Cardaria propinqua

Cardaria propinqua, commonly known as the lesser caraway.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage.
