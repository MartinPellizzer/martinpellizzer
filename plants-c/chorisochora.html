Chorisochora

Chorisochora, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. Chorisochora thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
