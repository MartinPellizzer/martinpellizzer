Carex buckleyi

Carex buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Buckley's sedge typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
