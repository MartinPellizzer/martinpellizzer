Campanula rotundifolia var. sudetica

Campanula rotundifolia var. sudetica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems with opposite, rounded or heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens and rockeries.
