Carludovica plumeri

Carludovica plumeri, commonly known as the Mexican giant nightshade, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming at night. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Carludovica plumeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
