Cardamine × degeniana

Cardamine × degeniana, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a natural hybrid between Cardamine bulbifera and Cardamine pratensis, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
