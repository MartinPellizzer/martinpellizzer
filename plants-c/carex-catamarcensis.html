Carex catamarcensis

Carex catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and riparian zones. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex catamarcensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
