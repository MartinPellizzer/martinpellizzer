Cinnamomum javanicum var. neglectum

Cinnamomum javanicum var. neglectum, commonly known as wild cinnamon or lesser cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is similar to true cinnamon but is less commonly used in commercial products. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
