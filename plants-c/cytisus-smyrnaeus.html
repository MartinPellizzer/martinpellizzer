Cytisus smyrnaeus

Cytisus smyrnaeus, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and hardy nature.
