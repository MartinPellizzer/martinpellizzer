Carex ischnostachya var. fastigiata

Carex ischnostachya var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes that emerge in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and stiff, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
