Centaurium bianoris var. sulfureum

Centaurium bianoris var. sulfureum, commonly known as the sulfur-yellow centaury, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its bright, eye-catching flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
