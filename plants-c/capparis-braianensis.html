Capparis braianensis

Capparis braianensis, commonly known as the Brazilian caper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its often arid habitat. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical regions.

Capparis braianensis is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their edible flowers and ornamental value.
