Cassia foederalis

Cassia foederalis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are bright green and often have a reddish or bronze tint on the underside. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual display. Cassia foederalis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its showy flowers and rapid growth.

It is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support for various wildlife species.
