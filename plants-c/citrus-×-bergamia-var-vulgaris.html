Citrus × bergamia var. vulgaris

Citrus × bergamia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the bergamot orange, is a hybrid citrus plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit's peel, or zest, is widely used in perfumery and flavoring due to its strong, aromatic oils. The plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires regular pruning for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and commercial orchards.
