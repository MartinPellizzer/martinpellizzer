Carex dunnii

Carex dunnii, commonly known as the Dunns sedge, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and streambanks, and is often found in coastal areas.

Carex dunnii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
