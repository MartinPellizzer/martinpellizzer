Cerastium decalvans f. macedonicum

Cerastium decalvans f. macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are oblanceolate, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and disturbed soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
