Clinopodium breviflorum

Clinopodium breviflorum, commonly known as narrowflower betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Clinopodium breviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
