Chenopodina tortuosa

Chenopodina tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted goosefoot, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a bottlebrush. Chenopodina tortuosa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
