Cotoneaster virgatus

Cotoneaster virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's thorn or Virgin's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster virgatus is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and small mammals.
