Crotalaria leptoclada

Crotalaria leptoclada, commonly known as the slender cowpea or narrowleaf false flax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with a hairy texture.

Crotalaria leptoclada is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
