Carlina acanthifolia subsp. baetica

Carlina acanthifolia subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetican cudweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
