Cavanilla acerifolia

Cavanilla acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved cavanilla, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, maple-like leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a striking addition to any landscape.
