Cyperus marginatus

Cyperus marginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the species and stage of development. Cyperus marginatus is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
