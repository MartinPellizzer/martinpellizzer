Carphobolus sellovii

Carphobolus sellovii, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny burrs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Carphobolus sellovii is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
