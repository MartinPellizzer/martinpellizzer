Campanula patula var. pilosa

Campanula patula var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Campanula patula var. pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
