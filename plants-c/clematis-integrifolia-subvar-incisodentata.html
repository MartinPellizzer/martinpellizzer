Clematis integrifolia subvar. incisodentata

Clematis integrifolia subvar. incisodentata, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, which are characteristic of its subspecies. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb and support itself on structures or trees.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
