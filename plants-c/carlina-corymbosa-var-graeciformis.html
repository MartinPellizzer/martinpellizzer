Carlina corymbosa var. graeciformis

Carlina corymbosa var. graeciformis, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust stem with grayish-green, woolly leaves that are lance-shaped and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces showy, spherical flower heads composed of blue or purple ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
