Cirrhopetalum candelabra

Cirrhopetalum candelabra, commonly known as the candlestick orchid, is a unique and striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, with clusters of small, colorful flowers emerging from the top. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens or greenhouses.
