Centaurea boissieri subvar. pinae

Centaurea boissieri subvar. pinae, commonly known as the Pina centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
