Cirsium buchingeri

Cirsium buchingeri, commonly known as the Buchinger's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, rocky soils, and open woodlands. It has a robust root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Cirsium buchingeri is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a valuable plant for biodiversity in its native range.
