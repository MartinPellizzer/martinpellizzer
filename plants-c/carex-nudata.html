Carex nudata

Carex nudata, commonly known as the naked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex nudata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
