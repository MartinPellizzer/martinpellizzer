Capparis pendula

Capparis pendula, commonly known as the hanging or trailing caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, pungent fragrance. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Capparis pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
