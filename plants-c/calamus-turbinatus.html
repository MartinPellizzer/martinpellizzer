Calamus turbinatus

Calamus turbinatus, commonly known as the turbinated arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves that often curl at the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of clustered, finger-like fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy, edible roots, which have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common species in tropical rainforests.
