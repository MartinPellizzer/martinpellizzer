Cyperus pseudostrigosus

Cyperus pseudostrigosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering structures are small and inconspicuous, consisting of female and male inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage.

Cyperus pseudostrigosus plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
