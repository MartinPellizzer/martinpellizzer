Cymbispatha plusiantha

Cymbispatha plusiantha, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.

Cymbispatha plusiantha is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
