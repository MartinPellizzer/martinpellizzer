Cirrhopetalum cornutum

Cirrhopetalum cornutum, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its unique appearance. Native to the Andes Mountains, it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
