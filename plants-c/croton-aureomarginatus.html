Croton aureomarginatus

Croton aureomarginatus, commonly known as the golden-margin croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant yellow margins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
