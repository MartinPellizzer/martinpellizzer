Capparis trifoliata

Capparis trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, green berries. Capparis trifoliata is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
