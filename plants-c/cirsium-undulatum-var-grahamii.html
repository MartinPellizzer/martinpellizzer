Cirsium undulatum var. grahamii

Cirsium undulatum var. grahamii, commonly known as Graham's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
