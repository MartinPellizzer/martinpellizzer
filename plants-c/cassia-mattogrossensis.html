Cassia mattogrossensis

Cassia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazonian golden shower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia mattogrossensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
