Canthium pseudoverticillatum subsp. somaliense

Canthium pseudoverticillatum subsp.

somaliense, commonly known as the Somali spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Somalia and neighboring areas.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it is often found in dry savannas and rocky habitats.
