Carlina pola

Carlina pola, commonly known as the pola thistle, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often prickly foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Carlina pola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
