Cirsium callilepis var. oregonense

Cirsium callilepis var. oregonense, commonly known as the Oregon thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy with spines.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
