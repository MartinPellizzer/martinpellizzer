Cerastium sugawarae

Cerastium sugawarae, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit, with small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
