Cyrilla perrottetii

Cyrilla perrottetii, commonly known as the Florida cyrilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in acidic, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant blooms and red, edible berries that attract wildlife.

Cyrilla perrottetii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to moist environments.
