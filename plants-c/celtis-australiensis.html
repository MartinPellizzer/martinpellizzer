Celtis australiensis

Celtis australiensis, commonly known as the Australian box-leaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is typically found in open forests and woodlands, thriving in a variety of soil types.

Celtis australiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
