Coreopsis dentifolia

Coreopsis dentifolia, commonly known as the toothleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy, upright stems and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
