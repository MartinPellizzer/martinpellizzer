Chassalia catatii

Chassalia catatii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Chassalia catatii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
