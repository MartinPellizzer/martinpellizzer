Calantica sphaerocephala

Calantica sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed calantica, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, rounded yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a dense, upright cluster. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
