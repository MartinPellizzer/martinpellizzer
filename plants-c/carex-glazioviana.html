Carex glazioviana

Carex glazioviana, commonly known as Glaziov's sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences. Carex glazioviana prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
