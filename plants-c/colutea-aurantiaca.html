Colutea aurantiaca

Colutea aurantiaca, commonly known as the red boston ivy, is a climbing legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Colutea aurantiaca is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
