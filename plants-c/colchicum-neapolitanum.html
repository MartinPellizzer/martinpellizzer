Colchicum neapolitanum

Colchicum neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
