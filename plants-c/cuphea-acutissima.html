Cuphea acutissima

Cuphea acutissima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Cuphea acutissima is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
