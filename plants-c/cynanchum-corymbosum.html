Cynanchum corymbosum

Cynanchum corymbosum, commonly known as Canadian snake vine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial vine that belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its distinctive milkweed-like appearance. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in corymbs, which are flat-topped or rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to a variety of habitats, including woodland edges and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
