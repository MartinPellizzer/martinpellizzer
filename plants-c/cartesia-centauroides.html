Cartesia centauroides

Cartesia centauroides, commonly known as the centaur's shield, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, shield-shaped leaves that resemble armor, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant's fleshy stems and leaves are adapted to thrive in arid environments, showcasing its resilience and beauty.
