Chrysanthemum hissaricum

Chrysanthemum hissaricum, commonly known as the Hissar chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
