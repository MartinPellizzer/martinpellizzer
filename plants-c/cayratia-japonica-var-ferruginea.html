Cayratia japonica var. ferruginea

Cayratia japonica var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cayratia, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive rust-colored underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
