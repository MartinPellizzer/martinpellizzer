Carex × sharensis

Carex × sharensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions. Carex × sharensis is a sterile hybrid, resulting from the cross between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
