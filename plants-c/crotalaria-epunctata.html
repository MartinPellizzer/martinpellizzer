Crotalaria epunctata

Crotalaria epunctata, commonly known as the punctate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with dark veins, and its trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a notable ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Crotalaria epunctata typically grows in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
