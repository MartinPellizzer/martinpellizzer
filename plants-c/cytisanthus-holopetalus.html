Cytisanthus holopetalus

Cytisanthus holopetalus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
