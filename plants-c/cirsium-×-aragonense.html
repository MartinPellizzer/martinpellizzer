Cirsium × aragonense

Cirsium × aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it forms dense stands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
