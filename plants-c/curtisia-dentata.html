Curtisia dentata

Curtisia dentata, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It is often found in riverine forests and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Curtisia dentata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
