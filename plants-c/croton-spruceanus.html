Croton spruceanus

Croton spruceanus, commonly known as the spruceleaf croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a bluish tint. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
