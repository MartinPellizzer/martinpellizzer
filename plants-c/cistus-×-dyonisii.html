Cistus × dyonisii

Cistus × dyonisii, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cistus monspeliensis and Cistus albidus. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Cistus × dyonisii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
