Coriandrum latifolium

Coriandrum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf coriander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its seeds, often used in cooking, have a distinctively sharp and citrusy flavor.

The plant is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
