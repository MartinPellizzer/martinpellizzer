Coreopsis glaucescens

Coreopsis glaucescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
