Cyperus prescottianus

Cyperus prescottianus, commonly known as the Prescott sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown or white flowers. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.

Cyperus prescottianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
