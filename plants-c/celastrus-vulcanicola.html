Celastrus vulcanicola

Celastrus vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic bindweed, is a rare flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, acidic soils typical of volcanic landscapes. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered in fine hairs.

Despite its limited distribution, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its unique habitat.
