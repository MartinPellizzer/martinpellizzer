Carthamus calvus

Carthamus calvus, commonly known as the naked thorn.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its spiny stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Carthamus calvus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
