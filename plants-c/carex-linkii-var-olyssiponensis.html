Carex linkii var. olyssiponensis

Carex linkii var. olyssiponensis, commonly known as the Olyssiponensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
