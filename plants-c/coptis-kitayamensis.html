Coptis kitayamensis

Coptis kitayamensis, commonly known as Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its yellow rhizomes, which contain the alkaloid berberine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

Coptis kitayamensis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
