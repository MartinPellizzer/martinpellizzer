Cremosperma ecuadoranum

Cremosperma ecuadoranum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian climbing shrub, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
