Cinnamomum perrottetii

Cinnamomum perrottetii, commonly known as the Bornean cinnamon or Cinnamomum perrottetii, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils similar to those found in true cinnamon. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also used in traditional perfumery.
