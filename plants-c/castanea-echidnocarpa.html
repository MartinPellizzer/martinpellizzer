Castanea echidnocarpa

Castanea echidnocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in panicle inflorescences, followed by spiny, cup-like fruits that enclose smooth, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
