Cyanus tchihatcheffii

Cyanus tchihatcheffii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features striking blue or violet petals that surround a central yellow disk, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Cyanus tchihatcheffii typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
