Cerastium vulgatum f. gracile

Cerastium vulgatum f. gracile, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover in horticulture.
