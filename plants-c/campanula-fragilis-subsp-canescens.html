Campanula fragilis subsp. canescens

Campanula fragilis subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hoary bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features hairy, grayish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Campanula fragilis subsp.

canescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
