Cardamine oxycarpa

Cardamine oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-fruited watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, elongated silique that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
