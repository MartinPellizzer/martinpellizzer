Coryphantha octacantha

Coryphantha octacantha, commonly known as the Desert Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale spines. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
