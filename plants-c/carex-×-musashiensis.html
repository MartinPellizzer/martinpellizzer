Carex × musashiensis

Carex × musashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy.

Carex × musashiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
