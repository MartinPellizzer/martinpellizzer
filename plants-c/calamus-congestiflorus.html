Calamus congestiflorus

Calamus congestiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are densely arranged along the inflorescence. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
