Celtis tetrandra subsp. sinensis

Celtis tetrandra subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
