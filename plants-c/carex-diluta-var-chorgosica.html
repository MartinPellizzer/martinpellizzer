Carex diluta var. chorgosica

Carex diluta var. chorgosica, commonly known as the Chorgosik sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
