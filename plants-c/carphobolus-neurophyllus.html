Carphobolus neurophyllus

Carphobolus neurophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandburs, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spiny fruits that mature into burrs. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.
