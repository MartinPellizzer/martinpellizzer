Cistus ladanifer proles angustifolius

Cistus ladanifer proles angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves and flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant produces resinous secretions, which have been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
