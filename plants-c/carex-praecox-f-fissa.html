Carex praecox f. fissa

Carex praecox f. fissa, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures consist of small, three-parted flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex praecox f. fissa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
