Cyrtopodium brandonianum

Cyrtopodium brandonianum, commonly known as the Brazilian hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single, lance-shaped leaf and produces pendulous, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. It is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in warm climates.

The species is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
