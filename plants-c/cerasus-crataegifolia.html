Cerasus crataegifolia

Cerasus crataegifolia, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Cerasus crataegifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
