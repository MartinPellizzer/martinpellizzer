Collomia linearis f. congesta

Collomia linearis f. congesta, commonly known as the clustered flaxviolet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. Its leaves are linear and often have a slight bluish tinge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
