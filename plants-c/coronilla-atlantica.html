Coronilla atlantica

Coronilla atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
