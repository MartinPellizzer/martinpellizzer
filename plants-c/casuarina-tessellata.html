Casuarina tessellata

Casuarina tessellata, commonly known as the she-oak, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a hardy, evergreen tree or shrub that can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and saline soils. The plant has distinctive needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches, giving it a unique appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds.

Casuarina tessellata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil rehabilitation and erosion control.
