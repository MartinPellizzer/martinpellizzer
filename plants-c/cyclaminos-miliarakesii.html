Cyclaminos miliarakesii

Cyclaminos miliarakesii, commonly known as the Greek cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features tuberous roots and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cyclaminos miliarakesii is well adapted to rocky soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
