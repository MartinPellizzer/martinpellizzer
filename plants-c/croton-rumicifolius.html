Croton rumicifolius

Croton rumicifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in small, glandular hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its striking foliage, it is popular as an ornamental plant in horticulture.
