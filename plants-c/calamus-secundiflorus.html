Calamus secundiflorus

Calamus secundiflorus, commonly known as the secondary-flowered arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and tall, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calamus secundiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
