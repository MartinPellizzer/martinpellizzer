Carum bretschneideri

Carum bretschneideri, commonly known as Chinese parsley or wild chervil, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. Its aromatic, slightly spicy scent is due to the presence of volatile oils.

Carum bretschneideri is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary herb.
