Citrus × tamurana

Citrus × tamurana, commonly known as the sudanese lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originates from a cross between the citron (Citrus medica) and the pomelo (Citrus maxima). This species is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruit with a thick, aromatic peel. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated edge.

It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils and citric acid.
