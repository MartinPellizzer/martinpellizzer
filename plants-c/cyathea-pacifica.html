Cyathea pacifica

Cyathea pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along coastal areas. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the plant's vertical growth.

Cyathea pacifica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
