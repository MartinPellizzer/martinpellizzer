Conopodium elatum

Conopodium elatum, commonly known as the tall bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed capsules that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Conopodium elatum is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for certain insects.
