Carex dioica var. gaudiniana

Carex dioica var. gaudiniana, commonly known as the Gaudin's sedges, is a variant of the common sedges, characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
