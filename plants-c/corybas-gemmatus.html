Corybas gemmatus

Corybas gemmatus, commonly known as the gemmated rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, spurred flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike per stem.

Corybas gemmatus is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is often used in horticultural displays due to its unique appearance.
