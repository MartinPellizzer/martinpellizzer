Conyza cupressifomis

Conyza cupressifomis, commonly known as the "false cypress" or "cupressus weed," is an annual flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of cypress trees, giving it its common name. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters.

Conyza cupressifomis is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
