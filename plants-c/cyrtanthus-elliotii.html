Cyrtanthus elliotii

Cyrtanthus elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's crocus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features strap-like, grassy leaves. Elliot's crocus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming period and vibrant colors.
