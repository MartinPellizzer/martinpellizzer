Calophyllum eputamen

Calophyllum eputamen, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
