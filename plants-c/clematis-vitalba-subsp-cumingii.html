Clematis vitalba subsp. cumingii

Clematis vitalba subsp.

cumingii, commonly known as the Chinese traveler's joy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
