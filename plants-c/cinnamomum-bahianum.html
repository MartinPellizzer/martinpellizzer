Cinnamomum bahianum

Cinnamomum bahianum, commonly known as Brazilian cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their essential oils, which are valued for their aromatic properties.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fragrance.
