Calamus myriacanthus

Calamus myriacanthus, commonly known as the many-thorned calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, spiky fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
