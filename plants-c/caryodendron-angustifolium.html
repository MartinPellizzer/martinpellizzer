Caryodendron angustifolium

Caryodendron angustifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is valued for its timber and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a distinctive, aromatic wood texture.

It plays an important role in both ecological and economic contexts in its native habitats.
