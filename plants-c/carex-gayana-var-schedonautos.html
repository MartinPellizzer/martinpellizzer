Carex gayana var. schedonautos

Carex gayana var. schedonautos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
