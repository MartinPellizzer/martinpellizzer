Chamaecrista lentiscifolia

Chamaecrista lentiscifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system, which helps prevent soil erosion and makes it useful in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for improving soil fertility.
