Capparis rosanowiana

Capparis rosanowiana, commonly known as the Rosanow's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
