Cestrum verbascifolium

Cestrum verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Its fruits are berries that change from green to red as they mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
