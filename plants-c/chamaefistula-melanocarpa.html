Chamaefistula melanocarpa

Chamaefistula melanocarpa, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces white or pale green flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries that are rich in sugars and acids. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

The species is often used in hedgerows and as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
