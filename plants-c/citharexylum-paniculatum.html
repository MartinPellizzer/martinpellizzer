Citharexylum paniculatum

Citharexylum paniculatum, commonly known as the sweet thorn or silver bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, often producing a sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
