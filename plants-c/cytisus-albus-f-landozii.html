Cytisus albus f. landozii

Cytisus albus f. landozii, commonly known as the white broom or white firethorn, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a botanical variant of the common broom, characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
