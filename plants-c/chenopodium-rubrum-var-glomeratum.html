Chenopodium rubrum var. glomeratum

Chenopodium rubrum var. glomeratum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically red-tinged. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in green to reddish clusters, which are followed by triangular, beak-like fruits. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
