Chrysanthemum makinoi var. wakasaense

Chrysanthemum makinoi var. wakasaense, commonly known as the Wakasa chrysanthemum, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
