Coelospermum barbatum

Coelospermum barbatum, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Coelospermum barbatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
