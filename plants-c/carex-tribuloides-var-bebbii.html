Carex tribuloides var. bebbii

Carex tribuloides var. bebbii, commonly known as Bebb's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Bebb's sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
