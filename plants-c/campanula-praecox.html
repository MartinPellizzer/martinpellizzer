Campanula praecox

Campanula praecox, commonly known as the early bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically blooms in early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

Campanula praecox is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
