Carex josselynii

Carex josselynii, commonly known as the Josselyn sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America, particularly found in the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along streams. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers in the summer.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
