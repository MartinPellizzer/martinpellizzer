Crepidium brachycaulos

Crepidium brachycaulos, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and short, sturdy stems. The plant produces delicate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Crepidium brachycaulos thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
