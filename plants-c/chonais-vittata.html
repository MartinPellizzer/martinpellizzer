Chonais vittata

Chonais vittata, commonly known as the spotted chonais, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental leaves that feature distinct yellow or white spots. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of pink or white.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
