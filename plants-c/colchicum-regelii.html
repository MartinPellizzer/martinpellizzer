Colchicum regelii

Colchicum regelii, commonly known as the Siberian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has a short, stocky stem and lance-shaped leaves that are basal and evergreen. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Colchicum regelii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
