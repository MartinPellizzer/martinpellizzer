Calothamnus plumosus

Calothamnus plumosus, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, papery bracts that give it a soft, feathery appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Calothamnus plumosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and long-lasting floral display.
