Chasmanthe fucata

Chasmanthe fucata, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers that resemble torches, typically blooming in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Chasmanthe fucata is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes, creating dense, colorful displays.
