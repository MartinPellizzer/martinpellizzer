Carduus kilimandscharicus

Carduus kilimandscharicus, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro thistle, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine habitats. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain insects and animals.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
