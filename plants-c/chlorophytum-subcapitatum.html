Chlorophytum subcapitatum

Chlorophytum subcapitatum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive silver or white markings. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear white or greenish flowers, which are followed by small, round berries. It is a hardy plant that thrives in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ability to purify the air and tolerate neglect has made it a popular houseplant worldwide.
