Calophyllum acutiputamen

Calophyllum acutiputamen, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with five petals, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
