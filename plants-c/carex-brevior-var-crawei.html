Carex brevior var. crawei

Carex brevior var. crawei, commonly known as the Crawe's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
