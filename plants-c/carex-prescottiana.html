Carex prescottiana

Carex prescottiana, commonly known as Prescott's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.
