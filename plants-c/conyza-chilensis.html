Conyza chilensis

Conyza chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Conyza chilensis is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
