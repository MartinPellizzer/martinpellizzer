Commelina guineensis

Commelina guineensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Commelina family (Commelinaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in groups of three, with two small white petals and one larger blue or purple petal.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
