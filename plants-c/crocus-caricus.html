Crocus caricus

Crocus caricus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, cold climates, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The Persian crocus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
