Cinnamodendron tenuifolium

Cinnamodendron tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed cinnamodendron, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
