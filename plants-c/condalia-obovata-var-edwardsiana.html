Condalia obovata var. edwardsiana

Condalia obovata var. edwardsiana, commonly known as the Edwards' condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, obovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
