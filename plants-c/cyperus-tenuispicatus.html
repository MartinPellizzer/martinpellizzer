Cyperus tenuispicatus

Cyperus tenuispicatus, commonly known as the narrow-spined flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus tenuispicatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
