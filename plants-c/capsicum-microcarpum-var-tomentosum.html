Capsicum microcarpum var. tomentosum

Capsicum microcarpum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly pepper.

This variant is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically covered in a dense, white, woolly pubescence. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.

The leaves are broad and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive botanical profile.
