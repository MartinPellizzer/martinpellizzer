Carex willdenowii var. pauciflora

Carex willdenowii var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
