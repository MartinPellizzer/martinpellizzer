Calea megacephala

Calea megacephala, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral behavior. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, almost waxy texture.

Calea megacephala is also noted for its aromatic scent, which is most intense during the evening hours.
