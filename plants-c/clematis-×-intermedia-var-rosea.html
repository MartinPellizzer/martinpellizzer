Clematis × intermedia var. rosea

Clematis × intermedia var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered Clematis, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, arching stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces showy, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is compound, with three to five oval-shaped leaflets that are dark green and glossy.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
