Cyperus rubiginosus var. cornutus

Cyperus rubiginosus var. cornutus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in aquatic and riparian habitats.
