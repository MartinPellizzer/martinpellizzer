Cunila fragrans

Cunila fragrans, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and compound leaves with a delicate, parsley-like scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its roots are often used in herbal medicine for their carminative and digestive properties.

Cunila fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
