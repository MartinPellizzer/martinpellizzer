Chrysothamnus graveolens var. glabratus

Chrysothamnus graveolens var. glabratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive yellow blooms. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.
