Clidemia marginata

Clidemia marginata, commonly known as silverleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in coastal areas or near streams. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, appearing in spring to summer.

Clidemia marginata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
