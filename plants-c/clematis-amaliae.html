Clematis amaliae

Clematis amaliae, commonly known as Amalia's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Chile.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with palmate lobes and has distinctive, finely divided foliage. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, displaying shades of white, pink, and purple, and are pollinated by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
