Cyathea amazonica

Cyathea amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant features a large, crown of feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea amazonica thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of the rainforest.

Its unique morphology and ecological importance make it an important component of the Amazonian flora.
