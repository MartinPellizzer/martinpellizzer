Crepinella umbraculifera

Crepinella umbraculifera, commonly known as the shade-loving fern, is a species native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green fronds that form a compact, umbrella-like shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and indirect light to flourish. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

The fronds are typically divided into small, serrated segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
