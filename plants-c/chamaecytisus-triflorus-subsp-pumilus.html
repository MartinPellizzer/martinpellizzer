Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp. pumilus

Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf flowering broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
