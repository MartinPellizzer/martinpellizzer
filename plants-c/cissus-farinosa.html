Cissus farinosa

Cissus farinosa, commonly known as the stonecrop fig or silver vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery, whitish coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in various light conditions.

Cissus farinosa is also valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
