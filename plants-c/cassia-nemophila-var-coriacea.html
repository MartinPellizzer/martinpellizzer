Cassia nemophila var. coriacea

Cassia nemophila var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with rough, textured surfaces and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value is notable, though it may be invasive in certain regions.
