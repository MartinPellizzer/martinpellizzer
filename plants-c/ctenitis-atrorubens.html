Ctenitis atrorubens

Ctenitis atrorubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a reddish hue on the underside, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Ctenitis atrorubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
