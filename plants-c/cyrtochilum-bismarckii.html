Cyrtochilum bismarckii

Cyrtochilum bismarckii, commonly known as the Bismarck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple or red markings. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cyrtochilum bismarckii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
