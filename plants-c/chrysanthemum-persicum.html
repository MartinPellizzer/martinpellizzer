Chrysanthemum persicum

Chrysanthemum persicum, commonly known as Persian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant color and compact growth habit.
