Conyza spiculosa

Conyza spiculosa, commonly known as roughleaf weed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves that are often glandular, giving it a rough texture. It is native to Africa but has been introduced to other regions, including parts of the United States.

Conyza spiculosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
