Convolvulus fruticetorum

Convolvulus fruticetorum, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are wiry and can root at the nodes.

Convolvulus fruticetorum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry, sunny habitats.
