Chamomilla tzvelevii

Chamomilla tzvelevii, commonly known as the Balkan chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Chamomilla tzvelevii is also valued for its potential medicinal uses, including anti-inflammatory and sedative effects.
