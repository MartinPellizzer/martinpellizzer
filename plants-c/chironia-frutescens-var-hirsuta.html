Chironia frutescens var. hirsuta

Chironia frutescens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy chironia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with hairy stems and leaves that give it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The hairy chironia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
