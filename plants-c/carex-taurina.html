Carex taurina

Carex taurina, commonly known as the Taurine Sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It is typically herbaceous, with a rhizomatous growth habit that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex taurina is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
