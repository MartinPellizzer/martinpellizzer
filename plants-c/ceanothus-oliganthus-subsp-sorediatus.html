Ceanothus oliganthus subsp. sorediatus

Ceanothus oliganthus subsp.

sorediatus, commonly known as the red-flowered ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy red or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
