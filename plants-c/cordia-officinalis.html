Cordia officinalis

Cordia officinalis, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant's fruit is a small, round, and typically red or yellow berry with a thick, leathery skin. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
