Centaurea paniculata var. cosana

Centaurea paniculata var. cosana, commonly known as the Cosina knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads consisting of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
