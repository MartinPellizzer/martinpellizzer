Carica

Carica, commonly known as the papaya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America but is now cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with large, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which is the most valuable part of the plant, is rich in vitamins and enzymes like papain.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
