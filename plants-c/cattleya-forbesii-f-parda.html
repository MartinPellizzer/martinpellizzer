Cattleya forbesii f. parda

Cattleya forbesii f. parda, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a hybrid orchid species prized for its unique coloration.

It features dark, velvety petals that range from deep brown to near-black, with contrasting white or cream edges. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
