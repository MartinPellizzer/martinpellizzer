Castanea japonica var. stricta

Castanea japonica var. stricta, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces oval, spiny fruits that contain one or two edible nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant in suitable climates.
