Carex sempervirens subsp. pseudotristis

Carex sempervirens subsp.

pseudotristis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and stiff, with a dark green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
