Cyperus prionodes

Cyperus prionodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus prionodes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
