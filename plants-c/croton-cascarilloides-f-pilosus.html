Croton cascarilloides f. pilosus

Croton cascarilloides f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a unique variant of the croton genus characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are typically large, elliptical, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts worldwide.
