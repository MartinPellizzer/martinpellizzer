Clematis clemensiae

Clematis clemensiae, commonly known as the dwarf clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
