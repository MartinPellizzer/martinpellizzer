Cyananthus microphyllus subsp. williamsonii

Cyananthus microphyllus subsp.

williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
