Cyclophorus gralla

Cyclophorus gralla, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique leaf structure, which is circular and thick. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
