Cynara integrifolia

Cynara integrifolia, commonly known as the Spanish artichoke, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, fibrous stems. The plant features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves with smooth, wavy edges, and it produces purple or pink flower heads that develop into edible buds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils, while its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
