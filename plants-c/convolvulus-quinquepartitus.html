Convolvulus quinquepartitus

Convolvulus quinquepartitus, commonly known as the five-parted bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
