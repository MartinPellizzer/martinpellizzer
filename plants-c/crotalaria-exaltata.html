Crotalaria exaltata

Crotalaria exaltata, commonly known as the tall cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals, and its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves. Crotalaria exaltata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
