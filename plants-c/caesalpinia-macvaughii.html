Caesalpinia macvaughii

Caesalpinia macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bright yellow or orange-red flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Caesalpinia macvaughii is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
