Cosmia montana

Cosmia montana, commonly known as the mountain cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange central disks surrounded by white to pink petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cosmia montana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
