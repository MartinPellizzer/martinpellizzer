Cuminum minutum

Cuminum minutum, commonly known as dwarf cumin, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma similar to cumin.

Cuminum minutum is often grown as a decorative plant due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
