Calea urticifolia var. axillaris

Calea urticifolia var. axillaris, commonly known as the "Mexican candle bush," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and branched.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
