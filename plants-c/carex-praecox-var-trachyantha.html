Carex praecox var. trachyantha

Carex praecox var. trachyantha, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats.
