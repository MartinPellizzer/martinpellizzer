Conyza cichoriifolia

Conyza cichoriifolia, commonly known as mouse-ear or common mouse-ear, is an annual flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, composite flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza cichoriifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
