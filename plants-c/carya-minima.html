Carya minima

Carya minima, commonly known as the little walnut, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, round nuts enclosed in a fleshy, amber-colored husk. The tree produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, with the female flowers developing into the edible nuts. Carya minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
