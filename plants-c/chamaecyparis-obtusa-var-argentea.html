Chamaecyparis obtusa var. argentea

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. argentea, commonly known as the silver hinoki cypress, is a variant of the hinoki cypress native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales that give the plant a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The leaves are scale-like and retain their color throughout the year, making it an attractive evergreen for ornamental purposes. It typically grows as a conical or columnar tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
