Carex fluviatilis var. unisexualis

Carex fluviatilis var. unisexualis, commonly known as the unisexual sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This variant is notable for its unisexual flowers, with separate male and female inflorescences, a feature that distinguishes it from other Carex species. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
