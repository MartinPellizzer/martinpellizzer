Conophytum pellucidum subsp. pellucidum

Conophytum pellucidum subsp.

pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent conophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often translucent or have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
