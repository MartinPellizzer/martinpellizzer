Cyperus tenuiculmis var. schweinfurthianus

Cyperus tenuiculmis var. schweinfurthianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
