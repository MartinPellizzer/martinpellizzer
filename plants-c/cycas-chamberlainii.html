Cycas chamberlainii

Cycas chamberlainii, commonly known as the Chamberlain's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a crown of large, feathery leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. Its cones are dioecious, with separate male and female plants, and it produces both pollen and seed cones.

Due to its slow growth and habitat specificity, Cycas chamberlainii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
