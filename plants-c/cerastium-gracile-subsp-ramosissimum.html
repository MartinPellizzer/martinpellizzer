Cerastium gracile subsp. ramosissimum

Cerastium gracile subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to damp, nutrient-rich soils.
