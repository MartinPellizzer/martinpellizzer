Crataegus vaga

Crataegus vaga, commonly known as the wandering hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus vaga typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found along stream banks and in thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
