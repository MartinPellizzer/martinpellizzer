Celtis fragifera

Celtis fragifera, commonly known as the Indian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it often grows in dry, disturbed soils.

Celtis fragifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
