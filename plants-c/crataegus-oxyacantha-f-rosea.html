Crataegus oxyacantha f. rosea

Crataegus oxyacantha f. rosea, commonly known as the rosehip dogwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and showy, rose-like red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries known as rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The f. rosea form is distinguished by its more elaborate and showy floral structure compared to the typical species.
