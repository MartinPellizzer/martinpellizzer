Corothamnus agnipilus

Corothamnus agnipilus, commonly known as the flamebrush or firebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Corothamnus agnipilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
