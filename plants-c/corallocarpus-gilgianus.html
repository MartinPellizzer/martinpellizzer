Corallocarpus gilgianus

Corallocarpus gilgianus, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble coral, which give it its common name. Its leaves are opposite and simple, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
