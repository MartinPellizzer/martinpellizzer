Callicarpa pentandra var. cumingiana

Callicarpa pentandra var. cumingiana, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to red berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
