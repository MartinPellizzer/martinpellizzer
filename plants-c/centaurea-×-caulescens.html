Centaurea × caulescens

Centaurea × caulescens, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
