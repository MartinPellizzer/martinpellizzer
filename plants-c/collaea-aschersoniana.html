Collaea aschersoniana

Collaea aschersoniana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Collaea aschersoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
