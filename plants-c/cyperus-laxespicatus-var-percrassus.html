Cyperus laxespicatus var. percrassus

Cyperus laxespicatus var. percrassus, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) but is often classified in the sedge family (Cyperaceae) due to its morphological similarities. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
