Crepis runcinata var. hispidulosa

Crepis runcinata var. hispidulosa, commonly known as the hispidulose mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.
