Chenopodium hoggarense

Chenopodium hoggarense, commonly known as Hogg's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, saline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. It produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds, typical of the Chenopodium genus.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
