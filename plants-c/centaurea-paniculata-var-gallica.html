Centaurea paniculata var. gallica

Centaurea paniculata var. gallica, commonly known as the French centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, branched inflorescences of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
