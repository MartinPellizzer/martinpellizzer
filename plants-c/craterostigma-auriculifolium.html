Craterostigma auriculifolium

Craterostigma auriculifolium, commonly known as the ear-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique ear-shaped leaves and drought tolerance make it a notable species in Mediterranean and subtropical botany.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
