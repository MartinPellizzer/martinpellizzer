Clematis recta var. mandshurica

Clematis recta var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can climb trellises or fences with its strong stems.

This variety is particularly hardy, capable of surviving in colder climates.
