Coriandrum didymum

Coriandrum didymum, commonly known as the slender coriander, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds, which are similar to those of common coriander, are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
