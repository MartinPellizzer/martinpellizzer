Centropogon coccineus var. leucostoma

Centropogon coccineus var. leucostoma, commonly known as the white-flowered red sage, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that contrast with its white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It blooms from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
