Canthium monstrosum

Canthium monstrosum, commonly known as the giant spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it a potential hazard to handle.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
