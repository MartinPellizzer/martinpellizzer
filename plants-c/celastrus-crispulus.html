Celastrus crispulus

Celastrus crispulus, commonly known as the creeping staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Its twining stems allow it to climb trees and shrubs, often forming dense thickets.

The plant is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological role.
