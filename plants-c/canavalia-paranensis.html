Canavalia paranensis

Canavalia paranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves and produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil.

Canavalia paranensis is often used in agroforestry systems due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
