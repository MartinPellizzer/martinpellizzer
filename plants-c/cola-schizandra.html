Cola schizandra

Cola schizandra, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West and Central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing hard, brown seeds, which are the primary part used for medicinal and commercial purposes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its stimulant and antispasmodic properties.
