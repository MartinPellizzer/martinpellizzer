Croton abutiloides

Croton abutiloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
