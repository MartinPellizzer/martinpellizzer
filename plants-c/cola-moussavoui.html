Cola moussavoui

Cola moussavoui, commonly known as African cola, is a species of the Cola genus native to West Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces bright red, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its caffeine content and traditional use in cultural and medicinal practices.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in tropical forest ecosystems.
