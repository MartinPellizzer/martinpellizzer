Crepis nana subsp. clivicola

Crepis nana subsp.

clivicola, commonly known as the rock crepis, is a small, annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on slopes or along paths.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptation to harsh, sunny environments.
