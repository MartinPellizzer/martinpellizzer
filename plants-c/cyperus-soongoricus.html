Cyperus soongoricus

Cyperus soongoricus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense rosette. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus soongoricus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
