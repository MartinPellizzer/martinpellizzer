Cirsium lutescens

Cirsium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, preferring full sun. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium lutescens is often found in temperate regions and is known for its hardy nature.
