Cerastium decalvans f. stevanovicii

Cerastium decalvans f. stevanovicii, commonly known as the Stevanovicii mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. This variety is native to specific regions in Europe and is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
