Cerbera muricata

Cerbera muricata, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces bright red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and is not recommended for indoor cultivation without proper precautions.
