Corylus avellana var. glomerata

Corylus avellana var. glomerata, commonly known as the European hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the autumn. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and catkins that appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to form dense thickets.
