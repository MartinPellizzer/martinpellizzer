Cogswellia leibergii

Cogswellia leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, spiny areoles and is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance.

Cogswellia leibergii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
