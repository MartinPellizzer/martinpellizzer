Cleistanthus myrianthus subsp. cupreus

Cleistanthus myrianthus subsp.

cupreus, commonly known as the copper flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that display a range of colors from yellow to red.

The plant has pinnate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
