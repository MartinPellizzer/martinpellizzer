Culcitium magellanicum f. angustifolium

Culcitium magellanicum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved culcitium, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
