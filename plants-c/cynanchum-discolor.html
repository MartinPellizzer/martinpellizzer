Cynanchum discolor

Cynanchum discolor, commonly known as the variable milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. Cynanchum discolor is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
