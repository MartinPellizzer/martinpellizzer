Cistus × pagei

Cistus × pagei, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is a natural hybrid between Cistus monspicensis and Cistus clusii, showcasing genetic diversity within the Cistus genus.
