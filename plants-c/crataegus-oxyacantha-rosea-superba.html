Crataegus oxyacantha rosea-superba

Crataegus oxyacantha rosea-superba, commonly known as the superba hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The superba hawthorn is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
