Cucumis melo var. gerger

Cucumis melo var. gerger, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, crisp flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and use in culinary applications.
