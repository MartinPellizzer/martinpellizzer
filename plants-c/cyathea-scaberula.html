Cyathea scaberula

Cyathea scaberula, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds are typically dark green with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name.

Cyathea scaberula plays a significant role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
