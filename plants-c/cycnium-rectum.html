Cycnium rectum

Cycnium rectum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and spiny, spherical fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with a robust stem that is often branched. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces a distinctive, burr-like fruit that aids in seed dispersal.

Cycnium rectum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
