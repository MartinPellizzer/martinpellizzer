Cassia pleurocarpa var. longifolia

Cassia pleurocarpa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cassia pleurocarpa var. longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
