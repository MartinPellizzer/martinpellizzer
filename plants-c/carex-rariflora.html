Carex rariflora

Carex rariflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets. Native to wetland and marshy habitats, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
