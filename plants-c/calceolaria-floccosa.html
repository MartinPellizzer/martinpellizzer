Calceolaria floccosa

Calceolaria floccosa, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and distinctive slipper-shaped flowers that resemble small, colorful cups. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Calceolaria floccosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and cool climates.
