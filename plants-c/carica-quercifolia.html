Carica quercifolia

Carica quercifolia, commonly known as the oakleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, melon-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Carica quercifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
