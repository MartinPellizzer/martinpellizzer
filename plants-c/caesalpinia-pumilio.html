Caesalpinia pumilio

Caesalpinia pumilio, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia pumilio is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
