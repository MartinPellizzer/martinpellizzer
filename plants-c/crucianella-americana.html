Crucianella americana

Crucianella americana, commonly known as the American crucianella, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.
