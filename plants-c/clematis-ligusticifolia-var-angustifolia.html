Clematis ligusticifolia var. angustifolia

Clematis ligusticifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, with palmately divided, ovate leaves that are typically 3 to 5 inches wide. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, with five petals that are usually blue to purple in color. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
