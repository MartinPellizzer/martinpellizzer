Crataegus oresbia

Crataegus oresbia, commonly known as the Prickly Ash or Red Osier Dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Crataegus oresbia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
