Chlorocyperus patuliflorus

Chlorocyperus patuliflorus, commonly known as the broadflower umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, with numerous small white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.
