Campanula pamphylica subsp. pamphylica

Campanula pamphylica subsp.

pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
