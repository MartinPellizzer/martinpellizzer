Cirsium × moravicum

Cirsium × moravicum, commonly known as the Moravian thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, showy inflorescence.

Cirsium × moravicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
