Crudia lanceolata

Crudia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf cudweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
