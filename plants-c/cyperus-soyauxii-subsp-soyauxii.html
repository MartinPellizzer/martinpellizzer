Cyperus soyauxii subsp. soyauxii

Cyperus soyauxii subsp.

soyauxii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and habitat provision for wetland organisms.
