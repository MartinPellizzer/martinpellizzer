Cynanchum leucophellum

Cynanchum leucophellum, commonly known as white milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Cynanchum leucophellum is often found in rocky or arid environments where it thrives in well-drained soils.
