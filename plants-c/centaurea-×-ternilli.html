Centaurea × ternilli

Centaurea × ternilli, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea solstitialis (yellow starthistle). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm and features striking blue to purple flower heads with bright yellow centers. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Centaurea × ternilli is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
