Chamaecrista fasciculata var. depressa

Chamaecrista fasciculata var. depressa, commonly known as yellow false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides.
