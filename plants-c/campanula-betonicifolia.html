Campanula betonicifolia

Campanula betonicifolia, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
