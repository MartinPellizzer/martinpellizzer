Clethra canescens var. novoguineensis

Clethra canescens var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea clethra, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. The plant has hairy, grayish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
