Calligonum babakianum

Calligonum babakianum, commonly known as the Babakian's tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and compact, often multi-branched growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it resilient to drought.
