Cyclocheilon eriantherum var. decurrens

Cyclocheilon eriantherum var. decurrens, commonly known as the creeping cyclocheilon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, decumbent stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
