Cyphostemma crassiusculum

Cyphostemma crassiusculum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with persistent, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native environment.
