Campanula rapunculoides var. speciosa

Campanula rapunculoides var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
