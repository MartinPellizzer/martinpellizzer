Cochlospermum intermedium

Cochlospermum intermedium, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in the spring. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy with compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds, contributing to its natural spread.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
