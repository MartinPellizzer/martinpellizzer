Coreopsis leucanthema

Coreopsis leucanthema, commonly known as white coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with white centers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a bushy, compact form. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

White coreopsis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
