Chrysanthemum pratense

Chrysanthemum pratense, commonly known as field chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow to brown centers and white to pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It blooms from late summer into autumn, producing numerous small flowers that attract pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
