Cylicodiscus

Cylicodiscus, commonly known as the African violets, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their attractive, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Cylicodiscus species are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their ornamental value. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and prefer indirect sunlight.

The genus includes several species, with *Cylicodiscus multifidus* being one of the most popular for its vibrant purple blooms.
