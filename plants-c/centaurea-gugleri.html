Centaurea gugleri

Centaurea gugleri, commonly known as the Gugler's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Centaurea gugleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
