Carduus × nuriae

Carduus × nuriae, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Carduus benedictus (blessed thistle) and Carduus marianus (milk thistle), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant features robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and dark green. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both yellow disk flowers and pale blue to purple ray flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Carduus × nuriae is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
