Chisocheton cumingianus subsp. cumingianus

Chisocheton cumingianus subsp.

cumingianus, commonly known as the Cuming's chisocheton, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and parts of Indonesia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a yellowish color, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
