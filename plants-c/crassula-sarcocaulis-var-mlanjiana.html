Crassula sarcocaulis var. mlanjiana

Crassula sarcocaulis var. mlanjiana, commonly known as the Mlanjiana crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
