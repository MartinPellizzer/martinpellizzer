Calendula forskohleana

Calendula forskohleana, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive crown-like shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Calendula forskohleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
