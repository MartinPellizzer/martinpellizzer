Clianthus carneus

Clianthus carneus, commonly known as the red kowhai, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage consists of bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets.

Clianthus carneus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the ecological and cultural landscapes of New Zealand.
