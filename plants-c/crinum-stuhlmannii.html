Crinum stuhlmannii

Crinum stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Crinum stuhlmannii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
