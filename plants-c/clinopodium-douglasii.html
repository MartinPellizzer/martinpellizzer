Clinopodium douglasii

Clinopodium douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong mint-like scent when crushed.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
