Calligonum setosum var. majus

Calligonum setosum var. majus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features grey-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it valuable for desert reclamation projects.

It is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, showcasing remarkable resilience in challenging climates.
