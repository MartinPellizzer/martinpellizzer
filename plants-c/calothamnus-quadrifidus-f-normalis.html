Calothamnus quadrifidus f. normalis

Calothamnus quadrifidus f. normalis, commonly known as the common golden glory.

It is a flowering plant native to Australia, characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
