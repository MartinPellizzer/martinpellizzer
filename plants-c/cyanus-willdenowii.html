Cyanus willdenowii

Cyanus willdenowii, commonly known as the blueweed or Willdenow's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in a single terminal head. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves, which are often gray-green in color. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Cyanus willdenowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
