Clematis wyethii

Clematis wyethii, commonly known as Wyeth's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Clematis wyethii is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
