Carex lanceolata var. laxa

Carex lanceolata var. laxa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
