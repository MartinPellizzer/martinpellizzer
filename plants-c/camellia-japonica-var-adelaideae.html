Camellia japonica var. adelaideae

Camellia japonica var. adelaideae, commonly known as the Adelaide camellia, is a cultivar native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant is an evergreen shrub with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is prized for its winter blooming habit and its ability to add year-round interest to the landscape.
