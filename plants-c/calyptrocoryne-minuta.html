Calyptrocoryne minuta

Calyptrocoryne minuta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Calyptrocoryne minuta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
