Carissa edulis var. septentrionalis

Carissa edulis var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern yellow bell.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean. It produces yellow-orange flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. The fruit is edible, though typically consumed in small quantities due to its tart flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
