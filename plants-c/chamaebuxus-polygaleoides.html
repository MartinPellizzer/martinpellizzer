Chamaebuxus polygaleoides

Chamaebuxus polygaleoides, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is often wax-coated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
