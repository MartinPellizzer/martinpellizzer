Corylus avellana f. glomerata

Corylus avellana f. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered hazelnut, is a horticultural form of the common hazelnut.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the production of tightly clustered female flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender, twisted branches and dark green, ovate leaves. It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the fall.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts in small-scale agriculture.
