Carex filipendula var. concolor

Carex filipendula var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that bear small, three-lobed flowers, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
