Corynophallus fontanesii

Corynophallus fontanesii, commonly known as the giant pteridium, is a rare and striking fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are clustered on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
