Condalia repens

Condalia repens, commonly known as the creeping condalia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Condalia repens is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
