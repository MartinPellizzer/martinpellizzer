Carex tenera var. richii

Carex tenera var. richii, commonly known as the richii sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex tenera var. richii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
