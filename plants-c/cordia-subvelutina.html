Cordia subvelutina

Cordia subvelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Cordia subvelutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
