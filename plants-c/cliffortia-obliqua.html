Cliffortia obliqua

Cliffortia obliqua, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with narrow, grey-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes and cliffs.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens across regions with a Mediterranean climate.
