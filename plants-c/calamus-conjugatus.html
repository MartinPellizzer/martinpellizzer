Calamus conjugatus

Calamus conjugatus, commonly known as the common calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a feature common in many monocot plants. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Calamus conjugatus is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
