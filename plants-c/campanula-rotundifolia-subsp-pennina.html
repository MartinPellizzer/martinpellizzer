Campanula rotundifolia subsp. pennina

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

pennina, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features distinctive bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
