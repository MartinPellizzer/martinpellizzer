Commelina kirkii

Commelina kirkii, commonly known as Kirk's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically features slender, branching stems and produces small, blue or violet flowers that are often surrounded by white bracts. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Commelina kirkii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
