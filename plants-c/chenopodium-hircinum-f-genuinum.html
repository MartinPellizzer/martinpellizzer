Chenopodium hircinum f. genuinum

Chenopodium hircinum f. genuinum, commonly known as the true goosefoot, is a frost-tender perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
