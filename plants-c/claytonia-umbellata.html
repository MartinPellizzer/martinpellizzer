Claytonia umbellata

Claytonia umbellata, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a rosette of oval, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in an umbel, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Claytonia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
