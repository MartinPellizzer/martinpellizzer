Carex pilulifera var. vaginata

Carex pilulifera var. vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream edges.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
