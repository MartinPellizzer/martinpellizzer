Cyathea philippinensis

Cyathea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the trunk and roots.

Cyathea philippinensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
