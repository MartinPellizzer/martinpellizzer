Coreopsis remyi

Coreopsis remyi, commonly known as the Mexican coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis remyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
