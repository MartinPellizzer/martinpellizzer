Calceolaria bicolor

Calceolaria bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers display a striking contrast of colors, often featuring a purple upper lip and a white or yellow lower lip.

Calceolaria bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
