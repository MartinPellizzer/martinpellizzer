Clinopodium plicatulum

Clinopodium plicatulum, commonly known as the wrinkled mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a wrinkled texture, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink in color. Clinopodium plicatulum thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
