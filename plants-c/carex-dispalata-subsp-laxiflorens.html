Carex dispalata subsp. laxiflorens

Carex dispalata subsp.

laxiflorens, commonly known as the looseflower sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence is composed of a dense, cylindrical spike that is often loosely arranged.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
