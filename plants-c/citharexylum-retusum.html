Citharexylum retusum

Citharexylum retusum, commonly known as the velvetleaf cithara or sweetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Citharexylum retusum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
