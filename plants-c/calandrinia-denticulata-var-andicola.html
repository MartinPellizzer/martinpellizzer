Calandrinia denticulata var. andicola

Calandrinia denticulata var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, often arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
