Conophytum luteum

Conophytum luteum, commonly known as the yellow conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
