Campanula collina var. leiocalyx

Campanula collina var. leiocalyx, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
