Crotalaria filipes var. filipes

Crotalaria filipes var. filipes, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a tropical annual legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
