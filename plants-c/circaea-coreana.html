Circaea coreana

Circaea coreana, commonly known as the Korean white geranium, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Circaea coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
