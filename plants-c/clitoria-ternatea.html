Clitoria ternatea

Clitoria ternatea, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or purple flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as natural food coloring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. Its roots are capable of fixing nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

Clitoria ternatea is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
