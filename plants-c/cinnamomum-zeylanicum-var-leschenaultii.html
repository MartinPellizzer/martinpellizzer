Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. leschenaultii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. leschenaultii, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is harvested and used as a spice. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for both its culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
