Cerastium vulgatum var. brachypetalum

Cerastium vulgatum var. brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and streambanks.
