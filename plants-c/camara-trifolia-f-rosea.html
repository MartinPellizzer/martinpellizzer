Camara trifolia f. rosea

Camara trifolia f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along the edges of forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
