Convolvulus contortus

Convolvulus contortus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, spiral-shaped capsule.

This species is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
