Cactus rhaphidacanthus

Cactus rhaphidacanthus, commonly known as the beaded cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in short, spiny bristles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Despite its tough exterior, it is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
