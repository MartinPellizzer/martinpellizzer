Cephalaria flava subsp. flava

Cephalaria flava subsp.

flava, commonly known as the yellow cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, producing bright yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
