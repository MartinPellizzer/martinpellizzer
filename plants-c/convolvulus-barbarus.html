Convolvulus barbarus

Convolvulus barbarus, commonly known as the wild sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink, purple, or white, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
