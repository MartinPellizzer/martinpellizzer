Calamus endauensis

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Calamus endauensis thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
