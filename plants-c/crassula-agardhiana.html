Crassula agardhiana

Crassula agardhiana, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, crinkled leaves that are often tinged with red or pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Crassula agardhiana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
