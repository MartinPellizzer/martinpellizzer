Capparis punctata

Capparis punctata, commonly known as the punctate caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

Capparis punctata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
