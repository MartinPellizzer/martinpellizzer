Conyzanthus squamatus

Conyzanthus squamatus, commonly known as the silverleaf conyzanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
