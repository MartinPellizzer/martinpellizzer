Catalpa × galleana

Catalpa × galleana, commonly known as the Southern catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, bean-like pods that contain sticky seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate urban conditions further enhance its value in landscaping.
