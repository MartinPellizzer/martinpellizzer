Chamaefistula densiflora

Chamaefistula densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that forms compact, rounded clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and as a hedge plant.
