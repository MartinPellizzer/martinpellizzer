Carpha bracteosa

Carpha bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and triangular stems.

Carpha bracteosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
