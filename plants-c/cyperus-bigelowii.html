Cyperus bigelowii

Cyperus bigelowii, commonly known as the Bigelow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a prominent midrib. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus bigelowii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
