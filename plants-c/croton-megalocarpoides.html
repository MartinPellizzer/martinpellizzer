Croton megalocarpoides

Croton megalocarpoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that vary in shape from ovate to elliptical. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruit.
