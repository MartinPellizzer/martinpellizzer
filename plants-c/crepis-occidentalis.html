Crepis occidentalis

Crepis occidentalis, commonly known as the western mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It features basal leaves that are hairy and heart-shaped, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Crepis occidentalis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
