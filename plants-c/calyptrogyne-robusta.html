Calyptrogyne robusta

Calyptrogyne robusta, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, brush-like inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has woody stems and is often found growing in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Calyptrogyne robusta is adapted to saline environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
