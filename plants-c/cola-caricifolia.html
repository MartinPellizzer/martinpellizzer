Cola caricifolia

Cola caricifolia, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, lowland forests. Cola caricifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
