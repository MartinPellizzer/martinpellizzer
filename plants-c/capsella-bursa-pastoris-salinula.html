Capsella bursa-pastoris salinula

Capsella bursa-pastoris salinula, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and hairy, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides. Its fruit is a distinctive three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate saline conditions, which is reflected in its subspecies name "salinula.".
