Chamaecyparis lawsoniana gracilis-pendula

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana gracilis-pendula, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress weeping form, is a cultivar of the Lawson's cypress, characterized by its cascading, drooping foliage.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a narrow, columnar shape and long, slender, bluish-green needles. The plant exhibits a unique pendulous growth habit, with branches hanging in a weeping manner from the central trunk. It is native to the western United States and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its elegant appearance.

This cultivar prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
