Crinum australasicum var. blandum

Crinum australasicum var. blandum, commonly known as the broad-leaved crinum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring or early summer.

The variety 'blandum' is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other crinum species.
