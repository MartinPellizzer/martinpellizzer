Celtis polycarpa

Celtis polycarpa, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, spherical berries that turn red when mature.

Celtis polycarpa is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common ornamental and shade tree in urban landscapes.
