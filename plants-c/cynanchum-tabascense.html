Cynanchum tabascense

Cynanchum tabascense, commonly known as Mexican milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cynanchum tabascense is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
