Crataegus dallasiana

Crataegus dallasiana, commonly known as Dallas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny branches, elliptical leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally not favored by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
