Cucumis melo var. duripulposus

Cucumis melo var. duripulposus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon genus characterized by its distinctive, warty skin and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and large, lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is usually oval or oblong, with a thick rind and a high water content, making it a popular summer fruit.
