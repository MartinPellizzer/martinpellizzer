Clerodendrum capitatum var. butayei

Clerodendrum capitatum var. butayei, commonly known as the Butayev's Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy bracts that often resemble flowers, which are used to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
