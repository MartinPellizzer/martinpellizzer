Centaurea troitzkyi

Centaurea troitzkyi, commonly known as the Troitzky's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environments. Its growth habit is usually upright, with multiple flowering stems emerging from a central base.

Centaurea troitzkyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
