Cannabis sativa var. spontanea

Cannabis sativa var. spontanea, commonly known as wild cannabis, is a naturally occurring variant of the cannabis plant.

It typically grows in wild or semi-wild environments and is characterized by its tall, slender stature and loosely branched structure. This variety is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in open, sunny conditions. Its leaves are usually narrower and less dense compared to cultivated cannabis strains.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is notable for its lower THC content compared to other cannabis varieties.
