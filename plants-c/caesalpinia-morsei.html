Caesalpinia morsei

Caesalpinia morsei, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright red bracteate inflorescences that resemble small flames, giving it its common name. Its compound leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia morsei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
