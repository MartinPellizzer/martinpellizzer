Cerasus cucullata

Cerasus cucullata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamabuki, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red or black fruits that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with serrated edges.

Cerasus cucullata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
