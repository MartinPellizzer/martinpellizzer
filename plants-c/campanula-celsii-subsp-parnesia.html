Campanula celsii subsp. parnesia

Campanula celsii subsp.

parnesia, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, aiding in water retention. Campanula celsii subsp.

parnesia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardiness.
