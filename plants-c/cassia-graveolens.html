Cassia graveolens

Cassia graveolens, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cassia graveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
