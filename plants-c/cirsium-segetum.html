Cirsium segetum

Cirsium segetum, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Cirsium segetum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
