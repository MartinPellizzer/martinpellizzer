Cistus villosus var. genuinus

Cistus villosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
