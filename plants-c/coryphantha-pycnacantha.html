Coryphantha pycnacantha

Coryphantha pycnacantha, commonly known as the dense-fleshed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with a shallow root system and a protective covering of fine hairs.
