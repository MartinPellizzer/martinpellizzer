Clathrotropis nitida

Clathrotropis nitida, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in yellow or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 5 meters in height under optimal conditions.

Clathrotropis nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
