Chrysanthemum floridum

Chrysanthemum floridum, commonly known as the Florida chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and thrives in moist, sandy soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Chrysanthemum floridum is often found in swamps and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
