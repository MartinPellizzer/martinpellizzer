Camphorina cassia

Camphorina cassia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
