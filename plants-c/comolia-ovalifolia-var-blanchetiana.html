Comolia ovalifolia var. blanchetiana

Comolia ovalifolia var. blanchetiana, commonly known as the Blanche's ovalleaf comolia, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and potential ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
