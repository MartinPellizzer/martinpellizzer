Centaurea ucrainica

Centaurea ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are bright yellow with red or purple centers.

Centaurea ucrainica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
