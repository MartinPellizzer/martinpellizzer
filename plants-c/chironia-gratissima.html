Chironia gratissima

Chironia gratissima, commonly known as the sweetgrass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its aromatic leaves have been used traditionally by various Indigenous cultures for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.
