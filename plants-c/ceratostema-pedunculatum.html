Ceratostema pedunculatum

Ceratostema pedunculatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features a short, hairy stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is appreciated by gardeners and botanists alike.
