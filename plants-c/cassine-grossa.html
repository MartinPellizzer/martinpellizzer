Cassine grossa

Cassine grossa, commonly known as the broadleaf cassine, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Following flowering, it develops bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Cassine grossa is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
