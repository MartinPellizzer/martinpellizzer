Chrysanthemum indicum var. edule

Chrysanthemum indicum var. edule, commonly known as the edible chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow or white flowers. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its growth habit includes a bushy structure with alternate, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
