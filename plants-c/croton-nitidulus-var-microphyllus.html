Croton nitidulus var. microphyllus

Croton nitidulus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
