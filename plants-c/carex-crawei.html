Carex crawei

Carex crawei, commonly known as the Craw's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

Carex crawei produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
