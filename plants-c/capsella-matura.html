Capsella matura

Capsella matura, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has small, green, spoon-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. Its fruit is a dry, segmented pod that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Capsella matura is often found in Europe, Asia, and North America, and it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
