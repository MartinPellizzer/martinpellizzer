Cyathea dealbata

Cyathea dealbata, commonly known as the white-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a distinct trunk that is covered in remnants of old leaf bases. Its fronds are typically light green with a pale, almost white, midrib, giving it its common name.

Cyathea dealbata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
