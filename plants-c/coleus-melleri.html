Coleus melleri

Coleus melleri, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Coleus melleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful foliage and blooms.
