Cyperus cearaensis

Cyperus cearaensis, commonly known as the Ceara flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its morphology features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus cearaensis is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
