Cotoneaster obovatus

Cotoneaster obovatus, commonly known as the broadleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster obovatus is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of food for wildlife.
