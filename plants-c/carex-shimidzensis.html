Carex shimidzensis

Carex shimidzensis, commonly known as the Shimidzense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
