Croton malvavisciifolius

Croton malvavisciifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
