Cassia semifalcata

Cassia semifalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia semifalcata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
