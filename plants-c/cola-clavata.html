Cola clavata

Cola clavata, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant is notable for its caffeine-containing seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with thorn-like projections and opposite leaf arrangement.

Cola clavata thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.
