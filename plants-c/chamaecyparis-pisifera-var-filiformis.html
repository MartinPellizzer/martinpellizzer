Chamaecyparis pisifera var. filiformis

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. filiformis, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or hairpin cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive, narrow, thread-like appearance.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that forms a compact, cylindrical shape, giving it a unique, sculptural form. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated as a ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and has a shallow root system that makes it suitable for rock gardens or small spaces.

Its foliage remains green throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest.
