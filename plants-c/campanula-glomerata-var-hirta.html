Campanula glomerata var. hirta

Campanula glomerata var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the "hirta" designation, which means hairy in Latin.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
