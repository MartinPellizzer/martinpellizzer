Carex vesicaria var. distenta

Carex vesicaria var. distenta, commonly known as the inflated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the plant plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats.
