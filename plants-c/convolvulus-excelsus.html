Convolvulus excelsus

Convolvulus excelsus, commonly known as the tall morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along fences or in disturbed areas.

Convolvulus excelsus is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
