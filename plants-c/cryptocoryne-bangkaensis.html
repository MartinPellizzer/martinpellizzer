Cryptocoryne bangkaensis

Cryptocoryne bangkaensis, commonly known as the Bangka cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater stands. It is a popular choice among aquarium enthusiasts due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

Cryptocoryne bangkaensis is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing excess nutrients.
