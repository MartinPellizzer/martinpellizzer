Crassula caffra

Crassula caffra, commonly known as the Cape rose or African snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Crassula caffra is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
