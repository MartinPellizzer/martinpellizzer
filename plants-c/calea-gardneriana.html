Calea gardneriana

Calea gardneriana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Calea gardneriana is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
