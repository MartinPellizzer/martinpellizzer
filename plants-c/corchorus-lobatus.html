Corchorus lobatus

Corchorus lobatus, commonly known as common sandmat, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. Its fibrous roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Corchorus lobatus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in dry, temperate climates.
