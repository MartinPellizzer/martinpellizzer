Cistus lavandulifolius var. syriacus

Cistus lavandulifolius var. syriacus, commonly known as Syrian rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Syria and Turkey.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces vibrant pink or purple flowers in the spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
