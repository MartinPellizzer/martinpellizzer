Callistemon gnidioides var. microphyllus

Callistemon gnidioides var. microphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that appear in winter and spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
