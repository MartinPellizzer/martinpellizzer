Chardinia macrocarpa

Chardinia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited chardinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking floral display.
