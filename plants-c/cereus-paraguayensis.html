Cereus paraguayensis

Cereus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay candleplant, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are often fragrant. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to desert gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
