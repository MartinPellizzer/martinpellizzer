Cyrtochilum rigidum

Cyrtochilum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in a spike. Cyrtochilum rigidum thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
