Casearia mexicana

Casearia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican casearia, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and studied for its ecological benefits.
