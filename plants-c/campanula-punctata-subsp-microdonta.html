Campanula punctata subsp. microdonta

Campanula punctata subsp.

microdonta, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained to moderately wet soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Campanula punctata subsp.

microdonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
