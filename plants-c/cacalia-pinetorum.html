Cacalia pinetorum

Cacalia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine woods bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the pine woodlands of the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in clumps, often reaching heights of 12 to 18 inches. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun.

Cacalia pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
