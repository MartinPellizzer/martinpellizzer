Chenopodium hircinum

Chenopodium hircinum, commonly known as wild spinach or lamb'squarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by dry, three-angled seed capsules. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium hircinum is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine.
