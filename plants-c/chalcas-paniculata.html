Chalcas paniculata

Chalcas paniculata, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically red or purple in color. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the leaves often displaying a bluish-gray hue.

Chalcas paniculata is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, which contributes to its widespread use in landscaping.
