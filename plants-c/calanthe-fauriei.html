Calanthe fauriei

Calanthe fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, often fragrant blossoms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe fauriei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
