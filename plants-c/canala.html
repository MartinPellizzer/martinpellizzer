Canala

Canala, commonly known as the canella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Canala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
