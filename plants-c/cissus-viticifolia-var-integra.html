Cissus viticifolia var. integra

Cissus viticifolia var. integra, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries that are typically consumed fresh. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil with regular watering.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor spaces.
