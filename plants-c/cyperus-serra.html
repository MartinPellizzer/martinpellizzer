Cyperus serra

Cyperus serra, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas. Cyperus serra produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
