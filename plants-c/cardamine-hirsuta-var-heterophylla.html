Cardamine hirsuta var. heterophylla

Cardamine hirsuta var. heterophylla, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the broad-leaved watercress.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are often lobed or divided, and it produces siliques that contain small, round seeds.

The plant is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
