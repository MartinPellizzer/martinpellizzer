Cirsium chikushiense var. yakusimense

Cirsium chikushiense var. yakusimense, commonly known as the Yakusim Island thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
