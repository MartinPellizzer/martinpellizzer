Cercocarpus mojadensis var. pringlei

Cercocarpus mojadensis var. pringlei, commonly known as the Mojave curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native range.
