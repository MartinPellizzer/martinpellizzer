Clematoclethra sichuanensis

Clematoclethra sichuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
