Cyperus procerus var. lasiorrhachis

Cyperus procerus var. lasiorrhachis, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
