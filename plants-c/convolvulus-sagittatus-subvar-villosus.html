Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. villosus

Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. villosus, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
