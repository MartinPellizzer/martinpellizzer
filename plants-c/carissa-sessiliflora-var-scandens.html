Carissa sessiliflora var. scandens

Carissa sessiliflora var. scandens, commonly known as the silver moonvine, is a tropical climbing shrub characterized by its woody stems and trailing growth habit.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces fragrant, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several small seeds.

This species is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
