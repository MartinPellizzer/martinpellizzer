Carex bordabensis

Carex bordabensis, commonly known as the border sedge, is a species of sedge native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

Carex bordabensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
