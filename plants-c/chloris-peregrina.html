Chloris peregrina

Chloris peregrina, commonly known as the wandering grass or slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce minute seeds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, exhibiting drought tolerance and rapid growth during favorable periods.

Chloris peregrina plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
