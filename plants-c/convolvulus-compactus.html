Convolvulus compactus

Convolvulus compactus, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, trailing stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Convolvulus compactus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
