Carex haematosaccus

Carex haematosaccus, commonly known as the blood sac sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows and woodlands.

Its unique name refers to the reddish or blood-like coloration observed in certain parts of the plant, particularly the inflorescence.
