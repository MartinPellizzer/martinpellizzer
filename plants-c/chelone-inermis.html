Chelone inermis

Chelone inermis, commonly known as the white turtlehead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in dense, spire-like clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Chelone inermis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
