Clematis grata var. argentilucida

Clematis grata var. argentilucida, commonly known as the silver leaf clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance.
