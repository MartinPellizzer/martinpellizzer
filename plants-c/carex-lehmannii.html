Carex lehmannii

Carex lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate climates.

Carex lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
