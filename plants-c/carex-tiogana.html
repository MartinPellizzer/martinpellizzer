Carex tiogana

Carex tiogana, commonly known as the Tioga sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps.

Carex tiogana produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often pollinated by wind.
