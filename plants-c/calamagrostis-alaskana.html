Calamagrostis alaskana

Calamagrostis alaskana, commonly known as Alaska giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it forms dense stands. Its flowering spikes are tall and cylindrical, producing small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Calamagrostis alaskana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in moist areas.
