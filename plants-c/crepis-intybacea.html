Crepis intybacea

Crepis intybacea, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Crepis intybacea produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, contributing to its ecological role in pollinator support.
