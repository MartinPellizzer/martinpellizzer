Crassula badspoortensis

Crassula badspoortensis, commonly known as the Badspoort Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and has a compact, rosette-like structure. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
