Cissus ernstii

Cissus ernstii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to grow on rocky surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy fig that turns red when mature.

Cissus ernstii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
