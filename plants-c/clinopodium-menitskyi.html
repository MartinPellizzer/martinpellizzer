Clinopodium menitskyi

Clinopodium menitskyi, commonly known as the Menitsky's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Clinopodium menitskyi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic effects.
