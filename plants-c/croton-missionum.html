Croton missionum

Croton missionum, commonly known as the European croton, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Croton missionum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.
