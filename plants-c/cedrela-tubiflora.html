Cedrela tubiflora

Cedrela tubiflora, commonly known as the yellow cedar or red cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and aromatic wood. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its durable, lightweight wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and construction.
