Cucubalus angustissimus

Cucubalus angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cucumber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, elongated fruits resembling cucumbers. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and horticulture.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
