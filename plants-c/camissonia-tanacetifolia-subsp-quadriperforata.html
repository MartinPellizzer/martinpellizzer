Camissonia tanacetifolia subsp. quadriperforata

Camissonia tanacetifolia subsp.

quadriperforata, commonly known as the four-perforate camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Camissoniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, herbaceous growth habit.

The leaves are typically linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive four-perforate pattern along the midrib, giving it its subspecific name. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in early spring.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
