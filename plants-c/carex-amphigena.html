Carex amphigena

Carex amphigena, commonly known as the western sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Carex amphigena produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
