Coombea riparia

Coombea riparia, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers and broad, waxy leaves. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Coombea riparia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
