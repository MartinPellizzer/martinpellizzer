Chrysopsis nervosa var. stenolepis

Chrysopsis nervosa var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
