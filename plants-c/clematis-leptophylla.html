Clematis leptophylla

Clematis leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Clematis leptophylla thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
