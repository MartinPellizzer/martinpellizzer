Commelina spectabilis var. ramosa

Commelina spectabilis var. ramosa, commonly known as the showy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are borne on slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its ornamental value in horticulture.
