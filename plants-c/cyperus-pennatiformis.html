Cyperus pennatiformis

Cyperus pennatiformis, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus pennatiformis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
