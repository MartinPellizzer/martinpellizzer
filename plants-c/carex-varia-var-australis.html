Carex varia var. australis

Carex varia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex varia var. australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
