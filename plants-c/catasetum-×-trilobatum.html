Catasetum × trilobatum

Catasetum × trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a key feature in its pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, and are known for their intricate structure and fragrance.

Catasetum × trilobatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
