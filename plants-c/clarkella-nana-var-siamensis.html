Clarkella nana var. siamensis

Clarkella nana var. siamensis, commonly known as the dwarf clarkella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native habitat.
