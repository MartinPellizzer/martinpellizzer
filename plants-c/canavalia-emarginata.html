Canavalia emarginata

Canavalia emarginata, commonly known as the coastal bean or sea bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is often found growing along coastal areas and in sandy soils due to its tolerance for saline conditions. Its pods contain edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Canavalia emarginata also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
