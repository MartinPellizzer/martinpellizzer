Coelogyne incurvibrachia

Coelogyne incurvibrachia, commonly known as the curved-armed coelogyne, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, erect leaf and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns and are borne on slender stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
