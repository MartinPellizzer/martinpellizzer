Crocus fauseri

Crocus fauseri, commonly known as the Fauser's crocus, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are small and underground, storing nutrients for the next growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
