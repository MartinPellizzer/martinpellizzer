Cestrum aronioideum

Cestrum aronioideum, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a vigorous climber with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cestrum aronioideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
