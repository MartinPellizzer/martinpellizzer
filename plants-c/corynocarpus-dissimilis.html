Corynocarpus dissimilis

Corynocarpus dissimilis, commonly known as the kahikatea, is a large evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and its ability to thrive in swampy or wetland environments. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, and it produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's native forests and wetlands.
