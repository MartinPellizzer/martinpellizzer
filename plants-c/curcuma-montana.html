Curcuma montana

Curcuma montana, commonly known as the mountain turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous stems and produces large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The rhizomes of Curcuma montana are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.
