Cystorchis luzonensis

Cystorchis luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in montane forests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat destruction, Cystorchis luzonensis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
