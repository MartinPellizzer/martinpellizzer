Colchicum ancyrense

Colchicum ancyrense, commonly known as the Persian meadow saffron, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It produces striking, nodding flowers with bright orange petals and a distinctive yellow stigma, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is closely related to other members of the Colchicaceae family, which includes several species with medicinal and toxic properties.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
