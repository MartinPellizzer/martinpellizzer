Cynara cardunculus var. typica

Cynara cardunculus var. typica, commonly known as the common cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stalks and large, dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a large, spherical cluster, which are followed by woody, spiky seed heads. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been historically used for its edible leaves and stems.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
