Campanula sibirica f. elatior

Campanula sibirica f. elatior, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
