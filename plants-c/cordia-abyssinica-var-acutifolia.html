Cordia abyssinica var. acutifolia

Cordia abyssinica var. acutifolia, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with acute tips and its showy, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
