Conicosia brevicaulis

Conicosia brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed conicosia, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial with a compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Conicosia brevicaulis is adapted to rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.
