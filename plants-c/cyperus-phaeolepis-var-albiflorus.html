Cyperus phaeolepis var. albiflorus

Cyperus phaeolepis var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower sedgeland, is a variant of the Cyperus phaeolepis species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
