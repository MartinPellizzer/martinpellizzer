Carex brunnea var. stipitinux

Carex brunnea var. stipitinux, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often dark green and have a distinct, fibrous texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are typically wind-pollinated.
