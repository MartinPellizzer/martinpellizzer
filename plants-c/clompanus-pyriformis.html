Clompanus pyriformis

Clompanus pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, pear-shaped stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant typically grows low to the ground, with a single stem that may branch out slightly. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Clompanus pyriformis produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance.
