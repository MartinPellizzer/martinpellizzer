Commelina phalangoides

Commelina phalangoides, commonly known as the zigzag commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers with white markings. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina phalangoides is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
