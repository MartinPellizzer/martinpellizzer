Camellia ilicifolia var. ilicifolia

Camellia ilicifolia var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia ilicifolia var. ilicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
