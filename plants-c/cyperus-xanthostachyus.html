Cyperus xanthostachyus

Cyperus xanthostachyus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of yellowish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
