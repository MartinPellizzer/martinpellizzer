Caladium colocasia

Caladium colocasia, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often displaying vibrant shades of green, red, and white. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with rich, well-draining soil. It produces clusters of small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spiky berries.

Caladium colocasia is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and is often used in garden landscapes or as a houseplant.
