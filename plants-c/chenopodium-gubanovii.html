Chenopodium gubanovii

Chenopodium gubanovii, commonly known as Gubanov's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often used as a food source by local wildlife, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
