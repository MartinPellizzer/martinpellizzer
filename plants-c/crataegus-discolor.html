Crataegus discolor

Crataegus discolor, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has thorny branches and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus discolor is often found in open woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
