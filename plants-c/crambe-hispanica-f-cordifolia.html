Crambe hispanica f. cordifolia

Crambe hispanica f. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf crambe, is a variant of the common crambe plant.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant is a biennial or short-lived perennial, typically growing in cool climates. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
