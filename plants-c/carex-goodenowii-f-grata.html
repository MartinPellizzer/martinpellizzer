Carex goodenowii f. grata

Carex goodenowii f. grata, commonly known as the graceful sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower spikes. Native to the eastern United States, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The inflorescence consists of male and female flowers, with the female flowers typically positioned below the male ones.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
