Clematis bojeri

Clematis bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive violet throat. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis bojeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
