Centaurea drabifolia subsp. libanotica

Centaurea drabifolia subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other centaurea species. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
