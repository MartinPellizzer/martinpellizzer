Calamagrostis arundinacea var. inaequata

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. inaequata, commonly known as the unequal reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It produces fluffy, seed-bearing heads that are attractive to birds and can be used in floral arrangements.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat enhancement.
