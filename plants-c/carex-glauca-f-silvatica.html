Carex glauca f. silvatica

Carex glauca f. silvatica, commonly known as the silvery woodland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers in early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This sedgy plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
