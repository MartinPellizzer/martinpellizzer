Cyperus boreochrysocephalus

Cyperus boreochrysocephalus, commonly known as the golden-headed flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive yellowish to golden inflorescences that give it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in moist soils.

The plant's structure and growth patterns make it an important species in aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
