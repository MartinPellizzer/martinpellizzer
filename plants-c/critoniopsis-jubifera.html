Critoniopsis jubifera

Critoniopsis jubifera, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a striking contrast between the yellow petals and green sepals.
