Cerastium stevenii

Cerastium stevenii, commonly known as Steven's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, rounded, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
