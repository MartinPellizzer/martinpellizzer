Carex × binderi

Carex × binderi, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex breviculmis, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Carex × binderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
