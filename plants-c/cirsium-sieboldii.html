Cirsium sieboldii

Cirsium sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, prickly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
