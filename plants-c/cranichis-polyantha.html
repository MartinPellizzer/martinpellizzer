Cranichis polyantha

Cranichis polyantha, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a compact growth habit with a single, erect stem bearing several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are delicate, often pink or white, and are arranged in a loose spike, giving it a graceful appearance.

Cranichis polyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
