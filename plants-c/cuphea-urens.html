Cuphea urens

Cuphea urens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically green and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea urens is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
