Cytisus triangularis

Cytisus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems with narrow, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
