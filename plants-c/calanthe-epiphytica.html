Calanthe epiphytica

Calanthe epiphytica, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on the trunks or branches of trees. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its roots are specialized to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
