Cleyera japonica var. tricolor

Cleyera japonica var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color cleyera, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which display a striking combination of green, white, and red hues. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
