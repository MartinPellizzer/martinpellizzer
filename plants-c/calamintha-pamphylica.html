Calamintha pamphylica

Calamintha pamphylica, commonly known as Greek pennyroyal, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a strong, minty aroma.

Calamintha pamphylica is valued in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and carminative properties.
