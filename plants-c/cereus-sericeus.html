Cereus sericeus

Cereus sericeus, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Cereus sericeus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
