Caryota rumphiana var. moluccana

Caryota rumphiana var. moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that develops a fibrous sheath as it matures. Its leaves are typically a deep green, with a slightly waxy texture and prominent veins.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
