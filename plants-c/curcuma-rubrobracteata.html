Curcuma rubrobracteata

Curcuma rubrobracteata, commonly known as the red-bract ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that enclose the inflorescence. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers and has rhizomatous growth, typical of many ginger species. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for use in gardens and as a decorative foliage plant.
