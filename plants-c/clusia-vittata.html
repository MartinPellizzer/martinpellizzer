Clusia vittata

Clusia vittata, commonly known as the wax plant or silverleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Clusia vittata is evergreen, with dense foliage that makes it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light environments and its resistance to pests and diseases.
