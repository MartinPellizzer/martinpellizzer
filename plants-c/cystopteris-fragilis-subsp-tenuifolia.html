Cystopteris fragilis subsp. tenuifolia

Cystopteris fragilis subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender fern, is a delicate fern species native to eastern North America. It features finely divided, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing sori on the underside of its leaves, which are covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fragile, papery fronds give it a distinctive and ephemeral appearance.
