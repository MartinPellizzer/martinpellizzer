Caryocar brasiliense subsp. intermedium

Caryocar brasiliense subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian pecan or *pata de vaca*, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin. It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves.

The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The subspecies is notable for its potential economic value due to its edible nuts and use in traditional medicine.
