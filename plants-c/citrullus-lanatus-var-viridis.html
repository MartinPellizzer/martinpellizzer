Citrullus lanatus var. viridis

Citrullus lanatus var. viridis, commonly known as the green melon, is a variant of the watermelon species characterized by its vibrant green rind and sweet, juicy flesh.

This plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash, and is native to Africa. It typically grows on trailing vines with lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic melon fruits.

The green melon is valued for its unique color and flavor, making it a popular choice in both culinary and ornamental settings.
