Cnicus gnaphaloides

Cnicus gnaphaloides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing herb with a dense mat of silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dandelion-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
