Carex trifida

Carex trifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex trifida plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
