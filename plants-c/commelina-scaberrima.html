Commelina scaberrima

Commelina scaberrima, commonly known as the rough commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowering stems often bear two to three bracts, which are often mistaken for petals.

Commelina scaberrima is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
