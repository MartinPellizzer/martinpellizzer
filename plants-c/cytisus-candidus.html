Cytisus candidus

Cytisus candidus, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Cytisus candidus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
