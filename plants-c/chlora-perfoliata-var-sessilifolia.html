Chlora perfoliata var. sessilifolia

Chlora perfoliata var. sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved chlora, is a variant of the chlora plant characterized by its sessile leaves that are directly attached to the stem.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in humid conditions. The flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and flies.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
