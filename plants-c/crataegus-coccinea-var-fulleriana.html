Crataegus coccinea var. fulleriana

Crataegus coccinea var. fulleriana, commonly known as the Fuller's red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
