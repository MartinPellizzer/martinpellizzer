Carpodinus landolphioides

Carpodinus landolphioides, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
