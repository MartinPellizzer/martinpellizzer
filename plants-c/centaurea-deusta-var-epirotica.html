Centaurea deusta var. epirotica

Centaurea deusta var. epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple ray flowers and upright, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, cylindrical flower heads that attract pollinators.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
