Carex muricata subsp. leersii

Carex muricata subsp.

leersii, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective pioneer species in wetland restoration projects.
