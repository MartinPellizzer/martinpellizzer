Cryptotaeniopsis asplenioides

Cryptotaeniopsis asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The fronds are usually divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving it a distinctive, airy appearance.
