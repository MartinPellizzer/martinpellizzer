Cryptocoryne parva

Cryptocoryne parva, commonly known as the dwarf cryptocoryne, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and dark green, waxy leaves that are often folded. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is well-suited for freshwater aquariums. It has a rhizome that anchors it to the substrate and produces numerous small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cryptocoryne parva is valued for its ease of care and aesthetic appeal in planted tanks.
