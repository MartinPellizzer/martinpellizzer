Chloris sesquiflora

Chloris sesquiflora, commonly known as the slender grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris sesquiflora is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a minor component of various grassland ecosystems.
