Chomelia malaneoides

Chomelia malaneoides, commonly known as the Malaneoides flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
