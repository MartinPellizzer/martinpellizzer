Carex glauca var. bulbosa

Carex glauca var. bulbosa, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from blue-green to gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wetland ecosystems.
