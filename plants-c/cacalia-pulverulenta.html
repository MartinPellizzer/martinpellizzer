Cacalia pulverulenta

Cacalia pulverulenta, commonly known as the dustflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, powdery white hairs.

Cacalia pulverulenta is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
