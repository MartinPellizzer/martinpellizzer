Castanea japonica var. ookasi

Castanea japonica var. ookasi, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by spiny, cup-like fruits that mature to a chestnut brown color. The nuts are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor when roasted.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.
