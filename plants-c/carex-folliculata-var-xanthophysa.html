Carex folliculata var. xanthophysa

Carex folliculata var. xanthophysa, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

The yellowish coloration of its inflorescence is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
