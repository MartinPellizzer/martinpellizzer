Calytrix flavescens var. curtophylla

Calytrix flavescens var. curtophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically during the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
