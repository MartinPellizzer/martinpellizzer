Crocanthemum carolinianum

Crocanthemum carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina crocanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinct botanical appearance.
