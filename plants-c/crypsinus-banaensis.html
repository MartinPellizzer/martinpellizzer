Crypsinus banaensis

Crypsinus banaensis, commonly known as the Banaensis crypts, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the dense rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-arranged leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils by forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, very little is known about its ecological role and reproductive biology.
