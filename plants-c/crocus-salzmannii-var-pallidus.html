Crocus salzmannii var. pallidus

Crocus salzmannii var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, with a distinctive, often purple-tinted base. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its early spring blooming habit, often appearing before many other spring flowers.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
