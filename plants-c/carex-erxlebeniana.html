Carex erxlebeniana

Carex erxlebeniana, commonly known as the Erxleben sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex erxlebeniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
