Chlorospatha castula

Chlorospatha castula, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a shield-like structure, providing protection for the plant's spore-producing structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are rich in chlorophyll, contributing to its vibrant green color.

The fern's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in botanical studies.
