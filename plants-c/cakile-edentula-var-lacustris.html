Cakile edentula var. lacustris

Cakile edentula var. lacustris, commonly known as lake sandspurr, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils, often found along lake shores and in open fields.

Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a rough, sandpaper-like texture.
