Centaurea tenella

Centaurea tenella, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea tenella is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
