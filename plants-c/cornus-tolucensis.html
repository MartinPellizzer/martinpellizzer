Cornus tolucensis

Cornus tolucensis, commonly known as the Mexican dogwood, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its showy white or pink bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside.

Cornus tolucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
