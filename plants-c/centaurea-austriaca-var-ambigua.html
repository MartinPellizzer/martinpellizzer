Centaurea austriaca var. ambigua

Centaurea austriaca var. ambigua, commonly known as the Austrian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in conservation areas due to its ecological significance.
