Capparis brevispina var. rheedei

Capparis brevispina var. rheedei, commonly known as the Rheede's dwarf caper, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, thorned stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.
