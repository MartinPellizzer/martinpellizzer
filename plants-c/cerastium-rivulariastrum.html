Cerastium rivulariastrum

Cerastium rivulariastrum, commonly known as the water chickweed, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and rocky areas.

Cerastium rivulariastrum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
