Carex vesicaria var. monstrosa

Carex vesicaria var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant swamp sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive, inflated spikelets that are often brightly colored. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its unique morphology, including its swollen, bladder-like structures, gives it a striking appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
