Clematis sumatrana

Clematis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran Clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five elliptical leaflets that are glossy and dark green.

Clematis sumatrana thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and support for its climbing habit.
