Calamus setulosus

Calamus setulosus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers. Calamus setulosus is often found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
