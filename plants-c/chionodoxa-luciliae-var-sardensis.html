Chionodoxa luciliae var. sardensis

Chionodoxa luciliae var. sardensis, commonly known as the Sardinian glory of the snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This variety is prized for its delicate beauty and early blooming habit.
