Clematis gentianoides var. tasmanica

Clematis gentianoides var. tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian clematis, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
