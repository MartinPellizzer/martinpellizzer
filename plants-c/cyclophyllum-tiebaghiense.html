Cyclophyllum tiebaghiense

Cyclophyllum tiebaghiense, commonly known as the Madagascar cycad, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with a stout trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species.

Its botanical features include a thick, fibrous root system and a cycad-like structure that resembles a tree with a woody stem and a cluster of pinnate leaves.
