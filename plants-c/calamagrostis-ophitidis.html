Calamagrostis ophitidis

Calamagrostis ophitidis, commonly known as the mountain reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to golden in color, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Calamagrostis ophitidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
