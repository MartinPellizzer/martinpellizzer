Carex teretiuscula

Carex teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
