Corylus avellana var. aurea

Corylus avellana var. aurea, commonly known as the golden hazel, is a decorative variety of the hazelnut tree.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns bronze in the fall. The plant produces small, edible nuts similar to those of the common hazel, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
