Catasetum mentosum

Catasetum mentosum, commonly known as the Mentos orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a unique coloration that ranges from yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving wasps. Its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves are typical of many orchid species, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Catasetum mentosum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
