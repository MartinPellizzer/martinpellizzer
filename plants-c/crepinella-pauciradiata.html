Crepinella pauciradiata

Crepinella pauciradiata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette, allowing it to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its fibrous root system helps it anchor in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Crepinella pauciradiata is an important component of xerophytic communities and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
