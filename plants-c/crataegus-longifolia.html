Crataegus longifolia

Crataegus longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Crataegus longifolia typically grows in open woods, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
