Carex micheliana

Carex micheliana, commonly known as Michel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and narrow, with a distinctive triangular cross-section.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often followed by three-sided, three-rowed seed heads.
