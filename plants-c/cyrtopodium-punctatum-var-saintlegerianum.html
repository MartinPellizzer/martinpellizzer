Cyrtopodium punctatum var. saintlegerianum

Cyrtopodium punctatum var. saintlegerianum, commonly known as the Saint-Leger's cymbidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for greenhouse or indoor cultivation. It blooms once a year, typically in the spring or early summer.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
