Calanthe albolongicalcarata

Calanthe albolongicalcarata, commonly known as the white-veined calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers have prominent white veins and are often borne on tall, slender stalks.

Calanthe albolongicalcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
