Capparis winitii

Capparis winitii, commonly known as the Chinese caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

Capparis winitii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
