Chloraea × flavovirens

Chloraea × flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to greenish-yellow in color. Native to New Zealand, this species thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
