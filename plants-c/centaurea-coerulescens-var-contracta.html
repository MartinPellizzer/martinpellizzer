Centaurea coerulescens var. contracta

Centaurea coerulescens var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
