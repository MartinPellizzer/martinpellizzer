Cirsium virginianum

Cirsium virginianum, commonly known as the Virgin's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish-red flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The species is part of the thistle family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
