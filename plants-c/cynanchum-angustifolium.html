Cynanchum angustifolium

Cynanchum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant has slender, ascending stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in racemes, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

It is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
