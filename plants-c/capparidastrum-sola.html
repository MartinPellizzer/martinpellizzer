Capparidastrum sola

Capparidastrum sola, commonly known as the golden caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various species known for their distinctive leaf and flower structures. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
