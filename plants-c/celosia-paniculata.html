Celosia paniculata

Celosia paniculata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, branched clusters.

Celosia paniculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
