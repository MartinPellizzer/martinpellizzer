Cnicus pringlei

Cnicus pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Cnicus pringlei is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
