Cousinia austrojordanica

Cousinia austrojordanica, commonly known as the Jordanian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Cousinia austrojordanica is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
