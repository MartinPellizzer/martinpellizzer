Cyathea ligulata

Cyathea ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea ligulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture and landscaping.
