Crocus chrysanthus subsp. sipyleus

Crocus chrysanthus subsp.

sipyleus, commonly known as the Sipylean crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
