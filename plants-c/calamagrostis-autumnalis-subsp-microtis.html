Calamagrostis autumnalis subsp. microtis

Calamagrostis autumnalis subsp.

microtis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
