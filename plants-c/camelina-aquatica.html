Camelina aquatica

Camelina aquatica, commonly known as water camelina, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as mustard and broccoli. Camelina aquatica plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
