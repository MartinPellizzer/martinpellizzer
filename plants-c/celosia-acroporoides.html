Celosia acroporoides

Celosia acroporoides, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a striking flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bold, colorful inflorescences. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Celosia acroporoides is also known for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
