Cereus candicans var. courantii

Cereus candicans var. courantii, commonly known as the Easter Cactus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of bright red flowers during the Easter season. The plant has thick, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, fleshy structures, and it produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
