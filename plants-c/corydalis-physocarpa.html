Corydalis physocarpa

Corydalis physocarpa, commonly known as the balloon corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink or purple flowers in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Corydalis physocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and unique seed structures.
