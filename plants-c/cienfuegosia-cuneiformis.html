Cienfuegosia cuneiformis

Cienfuegosia cuneiformis, commonly known as the Cuban ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its hard wood and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
