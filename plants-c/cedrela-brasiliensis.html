Cedrela brasiliensis

Cedrela brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its straight, durable wood. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cedrela brasiliensis is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ecological and economic importance.
