Centaurea valesiaca

Centaurea valesiaca, commonly known as the Valais centaury, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spiny bracts around its flowers.

Centaurea valesiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
