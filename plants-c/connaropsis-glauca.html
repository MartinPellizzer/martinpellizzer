Connaropsis glauca

Connaropsis glauca, commonly known as the blue connar, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine blue waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Connaropsis glauca is typically found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
