Commiphora incisa

Commiphora incisa, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which is harvested from its bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Commiphora incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
