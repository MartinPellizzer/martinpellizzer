Cyathea subinermis

Cyathea subinermis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in maintaining forest ecosystems.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing both aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
