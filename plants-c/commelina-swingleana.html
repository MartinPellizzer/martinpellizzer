Commelina swingleana

Commelina swingleana, commonly known as the Swingle's dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes a variety of flowering plants known for their ornamental and ecological significance. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers with white markings, typically blooming in the early morning and closing by midday. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, contributing to its visual appeal in natural or cultivated settings.
