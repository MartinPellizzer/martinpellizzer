Convolvulus fruticulosus

Convolvulus fruticulosus, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a sprawling annual vine characterized by its hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
