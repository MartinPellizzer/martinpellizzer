Cucumis melo var. atossae

Cucumis melo var. atossae, commonly known as the Afghan melon, is a botanical variety of the melon species characterized by its unique fruit morphology and growth habits.

This variety typically produces small, elongated fruits with a thin, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is a herbaceous vine that climbs using tendrils, often requiring support for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other melon varieties but distinguished by its specific morphological and ecological adaptations.
