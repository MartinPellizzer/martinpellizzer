Calanthe × dominyi

Calanthe × dominyi, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a rare and unique species resulting from the cross between Calanthe discolor and Calanthe triplicata.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple-colored flowers with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees in the understory of tropical forests. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
