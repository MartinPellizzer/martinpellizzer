Carex leptophylla

Carex leptophylla, commonly known as slender-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
