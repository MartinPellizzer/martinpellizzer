Campanula balcanica f. divaricata

Campanula balcanica f. divaricata, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to landscapes.
