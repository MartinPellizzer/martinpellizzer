Cryptocarya reticulata

Cryptocarya reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined cryptocarya, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by egg-shaped berries.

Cryptocarya reticulata is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
