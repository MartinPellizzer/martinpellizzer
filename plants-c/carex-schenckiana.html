Carex schenckiana

Carex schenckiana, commonly known as Schenck's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

Carex schenckiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
