Cremolobus pubescens

Cremolobus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cremolobus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
