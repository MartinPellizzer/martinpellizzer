Cephaelis justiciifolia

Cephaelis justiciifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the spring season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique floral display makes it a notable species in horticulture and botanical gardens.
