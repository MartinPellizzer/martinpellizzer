Ceratochloa boryana

Ceratochloa boryana, commonly known as the Boryan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along streams or in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical formations.

The botanical features of Ceratochloa boryana make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
