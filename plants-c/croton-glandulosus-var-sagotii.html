Croton glandulosus var. sagotii

Croton glandulosus var. sagotii, commonly known as the Philippine croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
