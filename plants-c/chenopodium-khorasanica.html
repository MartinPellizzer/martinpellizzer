Chenopodium khorasanica

Chenopodium khorasanica, commonly known as the Afghan goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and branching stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
