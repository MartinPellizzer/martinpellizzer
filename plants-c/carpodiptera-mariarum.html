Carpodiptera mariarum

Carpodiptera mariarum, commonly known as the sea feather, is a unique aquatic plant found in coastal regions.

It has delicate, feather-like leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it to the seabed and absorbing nutrients from the water. It plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its structure is adapted to withstand strong ocean currents and saltwater conditions.
