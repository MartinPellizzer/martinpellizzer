Campanula graminifolia var. sordidifolia

Campanula graminifolia var. sordidifolia, commonly known as the sordid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
