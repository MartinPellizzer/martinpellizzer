Cyperus corymbosus var. macrostachyus

Cyperus corymbosus var. macrostachyus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
