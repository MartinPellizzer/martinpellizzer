Chionolaena cryptocephala

Chionolaena cryptocephala, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
