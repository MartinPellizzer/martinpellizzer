Cerastium radians

Cerastium radians, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
