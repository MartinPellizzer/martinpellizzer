Cyathula sanguinolenta

Cyathula sanguinolenta, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive red-orange root that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a short stem with a single, palmately lobed leaf and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, followed by the development of bright red berries.

The plant is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
