Cistus lusitanicus

Cistus lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus lusitanicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
