Cardamine sinuatifolia

Cardamine sinuatifolia, commonly known as the sinuous watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often sinuously curled, giving it its common name. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
