Crocus algeriensis

Crocus algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both garden use and botanical study.
