Cautleya spicata

Cautleya spicata, commonly known as the spicate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Cautleya spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
