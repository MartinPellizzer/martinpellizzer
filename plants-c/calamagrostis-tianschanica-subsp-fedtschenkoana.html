Calamagrostis tianschanica subsp. fedtschenkoana

Calamagrostis tianschanica subsp.

fedtschenkoana, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and river valleys. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
