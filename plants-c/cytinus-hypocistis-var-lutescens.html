Cytinus hypocistis var. lutescens

Cytinus hypocistis var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow broomcup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and unique growth habit. The plant produces a swollen, fleshy stem called a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
