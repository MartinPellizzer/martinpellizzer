Ceanothus elongatus

Ceanothus elongatus, commonly known as the longleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Ceanothus elongatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
