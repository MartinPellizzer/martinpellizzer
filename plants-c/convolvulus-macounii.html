Convolvulus macounii

Convolvulus macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Convolvulus macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
