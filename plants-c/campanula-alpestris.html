Campanula alpestris

Campanula alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Campanula alpestris is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
