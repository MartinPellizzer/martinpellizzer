Cistus populifolius var. major

Cistus populifolius var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
