Corymbis batjanica

Corymbis batjanica, commonly known as the batjanica corymb, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
