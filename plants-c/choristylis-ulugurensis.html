Choristylis ulugurensis

Choristylis ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Uluguru Mountains of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and relies on host trees for water and nutrients while conducting photosynthesis. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange, attracting birds that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
