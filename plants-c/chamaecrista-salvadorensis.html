Chamaecrista salvadorensis

Chamaecrista salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its small, yellow flowers add ornamental value, making it a subject of botanical interest.
