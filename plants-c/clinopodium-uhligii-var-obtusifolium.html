Clinopodium uhligii var. obtusifolium

Clinopodium uhligii var. obtusifolium, commonly known as broad-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a minty scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in dense terminal clusters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
