Colombiana tetroxys

Colombiana tetroxys, commonly known as the Andean flame orchid, is a strikingly colorful flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
