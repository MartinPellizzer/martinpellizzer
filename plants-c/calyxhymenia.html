Calyxhymenia

Calyxhymenia, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small cups. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its calyx, which is modified into a cup-like structure, persists throughout the flowering period.

Calyxhymenia thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
