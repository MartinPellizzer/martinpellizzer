Cyperus retrorsus

Cyperus retrorsus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus retrorsus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
