Calanthe ecarinata

Calanthe ecarinata, commonly known as the hooked calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and strikingly veined, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with distinctive, elongated petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Calanthe ecarinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
