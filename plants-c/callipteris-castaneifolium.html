Callipteris castaneifolium

Callipteris castaneifolium, commonly known as the Florida hickory or candle bush, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, nut-like fruits that are important for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
