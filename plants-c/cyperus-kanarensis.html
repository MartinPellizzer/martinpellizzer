Cyperus kanarensis

Cyperus kanarensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a triangular cross-section. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, which are usually found at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus kanarensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist soils.
