Cucumis melo subvar. geyeidy

Cucumis melo subvar. geyeidy, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, slender fruit.

Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in cucurbit plants. The fruit is usually pale green, with a thin, crisp rind and a sweet, juicy interior.
