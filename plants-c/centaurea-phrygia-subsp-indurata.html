Centaurea phrygia subsp. indurata

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

indurata, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey. It features upright stems covered in dense, grayish-blue hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers surrounded by bracts that are often red or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and usually have a rough texture.

It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
