Cariniana rubra

Cariniana rubra, commonly known as the red tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
