Cacabus miersii

Cacabus miersii, commonly known as the Miers' cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in parts of Argentina and Chile. This plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
