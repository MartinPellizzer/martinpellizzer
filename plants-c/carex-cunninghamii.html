Carex cunninghamii

Carex cunninghamii, commonly known as Cunningham's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swampy areas and creek margins. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Carex cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
