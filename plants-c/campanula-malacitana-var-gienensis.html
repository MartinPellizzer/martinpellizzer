Campanula malacitana var. gienensis

Campanula malacitana var. gienensis, commonly known as the Gienensian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
