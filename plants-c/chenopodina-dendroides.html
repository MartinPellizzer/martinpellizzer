Chenopodina dendroides

Chenopodina dendroides, commonly known as the tree spinach, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branching structure and small, fleshy leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional diets for their nutritional value.

Chenopodina dendroides also plays a role in preventing soil erosion due to its extensive root system.
