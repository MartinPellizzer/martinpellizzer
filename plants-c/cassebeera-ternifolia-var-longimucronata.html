Cassebeera ternifolia var. longimucronata

Cassebeera ternifolia var. longimucronata, commonly known as the long-pointed cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves and robust, fibrous roots that are used for food. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a woody stem and alternate leaves with a serrated margin.

The variety is notable for its high starch content, making it an important crop in certain regions.
