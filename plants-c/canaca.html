Canaca

Canaca, commonly known as the "hemp plant," is a member of the Cannabaceae family and is native to various parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a tall, herbaceous annual plant that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with serrated leaves and small greenish-white flowers. The plant contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including cannabinoids such as CBD and THC, which contribute to its medicinal and industrial significance. Canaca thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fibrous stalks are used in the production of textiles, paper, and building materials, while its leaves and flowers are utilized in pharmaceutical and recreational applications.
