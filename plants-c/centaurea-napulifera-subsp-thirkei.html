Centaurea napulifera subsp. thirkei

Centaurea napulifera subsp.

thirkei, commonly known as the Thirkei knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and bristly foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and coastal areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
