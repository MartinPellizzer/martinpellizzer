Cinnamomum malabathricum

Cinnamomum malabathricum, commonly known as Indian cinnamon or malabathrum, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that exudes a strong, spicy scent when cut. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries containing elliptical seeds. Its leaves are glossy, lance-shaped, and have a pleasant, camphor-like fragrance.

The tree is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties, with its bark and leaves used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
