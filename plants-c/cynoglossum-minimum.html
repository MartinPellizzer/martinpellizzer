Cynoglossum minimum

Cynoglossum minimum, commonly known as lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cynoglossum minimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
