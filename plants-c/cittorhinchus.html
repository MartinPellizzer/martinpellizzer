Cittorhinchus

Cittorhinchus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate in shape with a smooth margin. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
