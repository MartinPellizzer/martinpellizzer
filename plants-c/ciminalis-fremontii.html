Ciminalis fremontii

Ciminalis fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's cimicifuga, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
