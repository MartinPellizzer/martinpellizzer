Carduncellus cryptocephalus

Carduncellus cryptocephalus, commonly known as the broad-leafed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
