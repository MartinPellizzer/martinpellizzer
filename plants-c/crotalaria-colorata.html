Crotalaria colorata

Crotalaria colorata, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria colorata is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in agriculture.
