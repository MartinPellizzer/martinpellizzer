Carex lepidocarpa var. laxior

Carex lepidocarpa var. laxior, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually broader than those of other sedge species, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
