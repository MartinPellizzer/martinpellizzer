Carex obtusifolia

Carex obtusifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically broad at the base and tapering towards the tip. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex obtusifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
