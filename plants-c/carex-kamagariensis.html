Carex kamagariensis

Carex kamagariensis, commonly known as the Kamagari sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section typical of sedges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
