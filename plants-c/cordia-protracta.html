Cordia protracta

Cordia protracta, commonly known as the longleaf cordia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-white color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
