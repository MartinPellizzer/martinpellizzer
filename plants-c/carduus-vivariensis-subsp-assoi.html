Carduus vivariensis subsp. assoi

Carduus vivariensis subsp.

assoi, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive purple flower head that resembles a thistle.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. It has a robust root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.

The species is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
