Chrysopsis shastensis

Chrysopsis shastensis, commonly known as the Shasta goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Chrysopsis shastensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
