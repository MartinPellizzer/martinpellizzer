Centaurea amplifolia

Centaurea amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea amplifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
