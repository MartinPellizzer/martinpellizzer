Carex arcatica

Carex arcatica, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and hardy species in extreme conditions.
