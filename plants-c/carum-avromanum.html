Carum avromanum

Carum avromanum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds, which are harvested when mature, are valued for their distinct flavor and essential oils.

Carum avromanum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.
