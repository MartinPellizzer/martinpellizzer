Crucifera aubrieta

Crucifera aubrieta, commonly known as the Persian speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves and is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and hardy nature.
