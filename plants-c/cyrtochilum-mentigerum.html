Cyrtochilum mentigerum

Cyrtochilum mentigerum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and a single, showy flower that resembles a ghostly face, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Cyrtochilum mentigerum is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
