Carex simensis var. nemorum

Carex simensis var. nemorum, commonly known as the mossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
