Cichorium intybus var. glabratum

Cichorium intybus var. glabratum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bluish-green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often used in salads or as a bitter tonic. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible cabbages or leaves that can be cooked or raw. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and a robust stem that supports its upright growth habit.
