Cyperus reflexus f. major

Cyperus reflexus f. major, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a variant of the Cyperus reflexus species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
