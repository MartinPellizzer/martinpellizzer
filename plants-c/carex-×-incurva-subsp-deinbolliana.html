Carex × incurva subsp. deinbolliana

Carex × incurva subsp.

deinbolliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
