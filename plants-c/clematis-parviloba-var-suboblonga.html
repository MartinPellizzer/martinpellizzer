Clematis parviloba var. suboblonga

Clematis parviloba var. suboblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to heights of several meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils.
