Crambe hispanica subvar. glabrata

Crambe hispanica subvar. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth crambe, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in dry, temperate climates.

This subspecies is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of oil-rich seeds.
