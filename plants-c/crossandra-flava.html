Crossandra flava

Crossandra flava, commonly known as the yellow flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Crossandra flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
