Calopyxis subumbellata

Calopyxis subumbellata, commonly known as the small umbellate orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in a loose umbel. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
