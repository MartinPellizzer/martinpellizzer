Cariniana brasiliensis

Cariniana brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize the soil and supports the ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its ecological importance and economic value as a source of Brazil nuts.
