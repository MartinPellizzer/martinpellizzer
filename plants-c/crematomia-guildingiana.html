Crematomia guildingiana

Crematomia guildingiana, commonly known as the Guianese star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
