Conyza longispina

Conyza longispina, commonly known as the long-spined fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces numerous small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Conyza longispina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
