Conosilene conica subsp. macrodonta

Conosilene conica subsp.

macrodonta, commonly known as the giant conosilene, is a striking member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often colorful stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces unique, showy inflorescences known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate and can vary in shape from ovate to elliptical.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
