Casearia grandiflora var. pauciflora

Casearia grandiflora var. pauciflora, commonly known as the small-flowered casearia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
