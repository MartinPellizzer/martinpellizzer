Calothamnus quadrifidus

Calothamnus quadrifidus, commonly known as the four-lobed bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Calothamnus quadrifidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
