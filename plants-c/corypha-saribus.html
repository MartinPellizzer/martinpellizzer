Corypha saribus

Corypha saribus, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the plant.

Corypha saribus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
