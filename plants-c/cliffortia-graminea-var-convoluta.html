Cliffortia graminea var. convoluta

Cliffortia graminea var. convoluta, commonly known as the rolling grass or mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats or cushions on rocky outcrops and slopes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
