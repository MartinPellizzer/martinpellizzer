Campomanesia stictopetala

Campomanesia stictopetala, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a sweet, slightly astringent flavor.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
