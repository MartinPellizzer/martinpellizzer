Convolvulus trabutianus

Convolvulus trabutianus, commonly known as the Trabut's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Convolvulus trabutianus is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
