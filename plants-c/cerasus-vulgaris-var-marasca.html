Cerasus vulgaris var. marasca

Cerasus vulgaris var. marasca, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces small, yellowish fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color, known for their intense tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The fruit is used in jams, preserves, and as a flavoring agent in various food products.
