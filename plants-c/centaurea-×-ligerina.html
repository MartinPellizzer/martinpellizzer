Centaurea × ligerina

Centaurea × ligerina, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × ligerina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
