Cucumis melo var. senani

Cucumis melo var. senani, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variety of melon native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow to orange skin and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces small, yellow flowers that are essential for fruit development.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary use and is valued for its high water content and flavor.
