Croton arenicola f. pubescens

Croton arenicola f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient choice for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular specimen for rockeries and dryland landscapes.
