Chrysanthemum leucanthemum subsp. montanum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the alpine white daisy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central disc.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often considered a specialist species.
