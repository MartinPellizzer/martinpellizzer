Catasetum calceolatum

Catasetum calceolatum, commonly known as the orchid with the "foot-shaped" flower, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals and a distinctive, often brightly colored lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Catasetum calceolatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
