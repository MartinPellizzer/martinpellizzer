Cassia ciliolata var. pulchella

Cassia ciliolata var. pulchella, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
