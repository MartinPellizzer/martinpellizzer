Combesia muscosa var. fulva

Combesia muscosa var. fulva, commonly known as the red moss cushion, is a unique member of the Bryophyta division, characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form.

It is a type of moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant's foliage is typically reddish in color, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It has a low-growing structure with closely packed, leaf-like structures that contribute to its ability to retain moisture.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitats.
