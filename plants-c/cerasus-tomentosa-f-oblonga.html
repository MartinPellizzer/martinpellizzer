Cerasus tomentosa f. oblonga

Cerasus tomentosa f. oblonga, commonly known as the woolly cherry or Japanese yew, is a unique variant of the Prunus tomentosa species.

This plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong in shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

While it is not typically cultivated for fruit, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
