Carex lazica var. clavata

Carex lazica var. clavata, commonly known as the Clavate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters that typically bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
