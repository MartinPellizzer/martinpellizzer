Coccoloba paraguariensis var. genuina

Coccoloba paraguariensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Paraguayan beautyberry, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered purple berries that appear in late summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
