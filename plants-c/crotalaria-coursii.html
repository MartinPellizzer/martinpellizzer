Crotalaria coursii

Crotalaria coursii, commonly known as the African capsicum, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Crotalaria coursii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
