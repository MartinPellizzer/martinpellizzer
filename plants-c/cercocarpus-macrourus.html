Cercocarpus macrourus

Cercocarpus macrourus, commonly known as the bigleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
