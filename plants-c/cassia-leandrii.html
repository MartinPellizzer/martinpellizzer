Cassia leandrii

Cassia leandrii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often covering the plant in a vibrant display. The leaves are pinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
