Campanula rapunculus var. lambertiana

Campanula rapunculus var. lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
