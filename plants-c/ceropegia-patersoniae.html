Ceropegia patersoniae

Ceropegia patersoniae, commonly known as the Paterson's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
