Claoxylon indicum var. spathulatum

Claoxylon indicum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical or spatulate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
