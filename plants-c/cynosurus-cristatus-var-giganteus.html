Cynosurus cristatus var. giganteus

Cynosurus cristatus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, often tufted, inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowering stems can reach considerable heights.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
