Carex macrorhyncha

Carex macrorhyncha, commonly known as the large-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in early summer. Carex macrorhyncha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
