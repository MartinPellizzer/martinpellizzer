Cicerbita brevirostris

Cicerbita brevirostris, commonly known as the short-nosed cicerbit, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.

The species is known for its mild, peppery flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use in certain regions.
