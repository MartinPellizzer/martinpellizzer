Carex acuta var. elegans

Carex acuta var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
