Chamaecyparis obtusa var. gracilis

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. gracilis, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or slender Japanese cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar form and dark green, scale-like foliage that provides a dense, soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and has a unique ability to develop a secondary trunk if damaged, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact size and elegant form make it suitable for rock gardens and as a specimen plant in gardens.
