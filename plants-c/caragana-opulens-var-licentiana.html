Caragana opulens var. licentiana

Caragana opulens var. licentiana, commonly known as the licentian thorned locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm long and consist of 10–22 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green pea-like flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful for soil stabilization and reclamation projects.
