Coleus mechowianus

Coleus mechowianus, commonly known as the Mechow's coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing similarities with other members like mint and sage. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
