Cyperus pustulatus

Cyperus pustulatus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowering spikes. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense mats.

Cyperus pustulatus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
