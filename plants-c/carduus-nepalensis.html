Carduus nepalensis

Carduus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Carduus nepalensis plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food for certain pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its habitat.
