Cinnamomum macrocarpum

Cinnamomum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited cinnamon or *Cinnamomum macrocarpum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by large, edible, purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and thrives in tropical rainforest environments.
