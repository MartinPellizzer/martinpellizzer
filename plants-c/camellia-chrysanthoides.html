Camellia chrysanthoides

Camellia chrysanthoides, commonly known as the golden camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are notable for their bright color and long-lasting bloom.

Camellia chrysanthoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
