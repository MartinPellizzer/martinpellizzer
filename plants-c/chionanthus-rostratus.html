Chionanthus rostratus

Chionanthus rostratus, commonly known as the rostratus fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chionanthus rostratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
