Carex hirta subsp. hirtiformis

Carex hirta subsp.

hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, erect leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex hirtiformis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
