Carum elegans

Carum elegans, commonly known as Persian cumin or Persian coriander, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are used as a spice and have a distinct, aromatic flavor.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
