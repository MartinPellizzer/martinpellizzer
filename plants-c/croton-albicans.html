Croton albicans

Croton albicans, commonly known as white croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a white or pale underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
