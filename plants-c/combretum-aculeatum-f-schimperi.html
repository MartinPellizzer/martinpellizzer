Combretum aculeatum f. schimperi

Combretum aculeatum f. schimperi, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble flames, which are often more showy than the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand dry conditions.
