Cyathea lewisii

Cyathea lewisii, commonly known as the Lewis's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and allows for the development of new shoots.

Cyathea lewisii plays an important ecological role in tropical forests by providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy structure.
