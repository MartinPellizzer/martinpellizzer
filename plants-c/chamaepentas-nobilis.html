Chamaepentas nobilis

Chamaepentas nobilis, commonly known as the Indian laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The Indian laurel is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
