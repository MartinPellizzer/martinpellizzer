Crataegus alleghaniensis var. ignava

Crataegus alleghaniensis var. ignava, commonly known as the Allegheny crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
