Calliandra coccinea

Calliandra coccinea, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bract-like flowers that are often mistaken for petals. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Calliandra coccinea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
