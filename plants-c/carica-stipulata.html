Carica stipulata

Carica stipulata, commonly known as the stipule passionfruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow with red throats.

The fruit develops from the flowering stems and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its nutritional value.
