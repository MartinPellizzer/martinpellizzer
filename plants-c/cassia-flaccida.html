Cassia flaccida

Cassia flaccida, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia flaccida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
