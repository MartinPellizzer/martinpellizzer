Ceropegia spathulata

Ceropegia spathulata, commonly known as the arrowroot vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Ceropegia spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
