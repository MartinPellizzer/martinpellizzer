Crocosmia mathewsiana

Crocosmia mathewsiana, commonly known as the Mathews' crocus, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds within a dry, three-angled capsule, which is typical of the genus Crocosmia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
