Campylocentrum amazonicum

Campylocentrum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, star-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
