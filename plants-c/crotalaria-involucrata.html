Crotalaria involucrata

Crotalaria involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmately compound leaves. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a legume crop. Crotalaria involucrata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

However, it can become a weed in certain environments due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
