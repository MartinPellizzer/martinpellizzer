Calliandra colimae

Calliandra colimae, commonly known as the Mexican orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra colimae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
