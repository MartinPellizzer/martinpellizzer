Calea submembranacea

Calea submembranacea, commonly known as the Mexican ghost flower, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, showy white flowers that resemble ghostly faces, hence its common name. The plant has a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Calea submembranacea is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
