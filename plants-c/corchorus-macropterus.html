Corchorus macropterus

Corchorus macropterus, commonly known as Chinese brooklime, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, often in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
