Capparis subtomentosa

Capparis subtomentosa, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and distinctive, hairy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis subtomentosa is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
