Crotalaria arcuata

Crotalaria arcuata, commonly known as the curlleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants capable of nitrogen fixation. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds.

Crotalaria arcuata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
