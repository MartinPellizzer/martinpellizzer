Canthium sessilifolium

Canthium sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

Its leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a petiole, and are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
