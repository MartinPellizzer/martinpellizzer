Caryota javanica

Caryota javanica, commonly known as the Javan palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is covered with a fibrous, spongy layer that provides structural support.

Caryota javanica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
