Cannabis sativa var. pedemontana

Cannabis sativa var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Piedmont cannabis, is a variety native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and broad, serrated leaves. This plant typically flowers in late summer, producing small, greenish-white flowers that are rich in cannabinoids. It is often cultivated for its fiber and historical use in textile production.

Due to its genetic distinctiveness, it holds significance in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
