Chondrilla coronifera

Chondrilla coronifera, commonly known as the crownflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of blue-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in a crown-like cluster at the top of the stem, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Chondrilla coronifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
