Cassebeeria impatiens

Cassebeeria impatiens, commonly known as the impatient cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in some environments.
