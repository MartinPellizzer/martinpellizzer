Convolvulus angulatus

Convolvulus angulatus, commonly known as the angular convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
