Citrus medica var. proper

Citrus medica var. proper, commonly known as the citron.

It is a small, fragrant citrus fruit with a thick, rough rind and a yellowish or greenish pulp. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking, traditional medicine, and religious rituals. The leaves are evergreen, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches full size, and it can be used to make jams, marmalades, and essential oils.
