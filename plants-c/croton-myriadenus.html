Croton myriadenus

Croton myriadenus, commonly known as the many-leaved croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in complex inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Croton myriadenus is valued for its ornamental foliage and is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
