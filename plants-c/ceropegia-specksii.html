Ceropegia specksii

Ceropegia specksii, commonly known as the Speckled Ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically green or yellowish with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
