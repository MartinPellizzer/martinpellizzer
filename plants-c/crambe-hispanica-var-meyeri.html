Crambe hispanica var. meyeri

Crambe hispanica var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's crambe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring upright stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody capsule, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Meyer's crambe is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
