Croton multiramineus

Croton multiramineus, commonly known as the multibranched croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its highly branched structure and striking, often colorful foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and vary in shape from ovate to elliptical. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
