Carex sachapata

Carex sachapata, commonly known as the Andean sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Carex sachapata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
