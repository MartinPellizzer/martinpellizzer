Campanula urticifolia var. multiflora

Campanula urticifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the stinging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, sometimes stinging leaves that can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This perennial plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
