Crepinia brachyacantha

Crepinia brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stalked crepe flower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, crepe-paper-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
