Carex liddonii var. incerta

Carex liddonii var. incerta, commonly known as the uncertain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
