Cryptantha inaequata

Cryptantha inaequata, commonly known as the unequal bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

Cryptantha inaequata is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
