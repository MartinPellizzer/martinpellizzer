Calophyllum calaboides

Calophyllum calaboides, commonly known as the African sandpaper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark. The tree produces yellowish-orange flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
