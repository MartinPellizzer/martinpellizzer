Caryocar brasiliense subsp. brasiliense

Caryocar brasiliense subsp.

brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest. It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its large, woody fruit that resembles a chestnut.

The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, spiny capsule, and the tree can grow up to 25 meters tall.

The species is valued for its timber and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
