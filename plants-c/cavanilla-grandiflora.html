Cavanilla grandiflora

Cavanilla grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora cavanilla, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
