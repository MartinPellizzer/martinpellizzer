Chrysogonum madagascariense

Chrysogonum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
