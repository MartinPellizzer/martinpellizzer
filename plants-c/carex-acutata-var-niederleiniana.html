Carex acutata var. niederleiniana

Carex acutata var. niederleiniana, commonly known as the Niederlein's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
