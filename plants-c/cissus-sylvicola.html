Cissus sylvicola

Cissus sylvicola, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cissus sylvicola is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
