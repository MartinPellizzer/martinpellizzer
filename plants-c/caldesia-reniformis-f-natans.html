Caldesia reniformis f. natans

Caldesia reniformis f. natans, commonly known as the floating water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the water surface.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in buoyancy. This fern thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the water and germinate into new plants.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
