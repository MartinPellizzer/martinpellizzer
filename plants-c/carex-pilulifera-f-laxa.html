Carex pilulifera f. laxa

Carex pilulifera f. laxa, commonly known as the loose-stemmed sedge, is a variant of the species Carex pilulifera, characterized by its more loosely arranged inflorescences.

It is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

This form is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
