Calligonum uzunachmatense

Calligonum uzunachmatense, commonly known as the long-stemmed saltbush, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species in harsh climates. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.

Calligonum uzunachmatense plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert and semi-arid regions.
