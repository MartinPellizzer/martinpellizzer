Citrus × aurantium subsp. japonica

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits with thin, peely skins and sweet, segmented pulp.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This subspecies is closely related to other citrus species and is valued for its cold tolerance compared to other mandarins.
