Cassia mutisiana

Cassia mutisiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of light. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and has compound leaves with multiple leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cassia mutisiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
