Calamagrostis leersii

Calamagrostis leersii, commonly known as the blue stemmed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and tall, slender inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.

Calamagrostis leersii is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
