Canavalia pubescens

Canavalia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a climbing habit with compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers. The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Canavalia pubescens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
