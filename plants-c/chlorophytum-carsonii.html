Chlorophytum carsonii

Chlorophytum carsonii, commonly known as the Carson's spider plant, is a popular ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces long, arching stems that bear small white flowers, followed by clusters of green, golf ball-sized offsets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it an easy-to-care-for indoor plant.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a favorite among plant enthusiasts for its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
