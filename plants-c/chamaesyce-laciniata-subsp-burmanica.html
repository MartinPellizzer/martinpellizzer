Chamaesyce laciniata subsp. burmanica

Chamaesyce laciniata subsp.

burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese cutleaf spurge, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground.

The leaves are deeply lobed and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
