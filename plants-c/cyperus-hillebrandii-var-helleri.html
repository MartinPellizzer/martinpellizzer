Cyperus hillebrandii var. helleri

Cyperus hillebrandii var. helleri, commonly known as the Heller's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus hillebrandii var. helleri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
