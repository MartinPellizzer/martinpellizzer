Cornus sibirica var. aurea-spaethii

Cornus sibirica var. aurea-spaethii, commonly known as the golden spice dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that provide vibrant autumn color. The plant produces small white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
