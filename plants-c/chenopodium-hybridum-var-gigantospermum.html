Chenopodium hybridum var. gigantospermum

Chenopodium hybridum var. gigantospermum, commonly known as giant goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, succulent leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are edible, often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
