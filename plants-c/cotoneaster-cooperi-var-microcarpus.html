Cotoneaster cooperi var. microcarpus

Cotoneaster cooperi var. microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf cotoneaster, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a foundation plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
