Calamagrostis holciformis

Calamagrostis holciformis, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing stems.

Calamagrostis holciformis is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
