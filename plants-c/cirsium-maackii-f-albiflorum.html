Cirsium maackii f. albiflorum

Cirsium maackii f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered thistle, is a variant of the common thistle characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia, specifically China, and is part of the Asteraceae family. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces a showy inflorescence composed of both ray and disk flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
