Cerastium alpinum var. subtriflorum

Cerastium alpinum var. subtriflorum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
