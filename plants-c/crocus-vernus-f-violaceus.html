Crocus vernus f. violaceus

Crocus vernus f. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered crocus, is a cultivar of the common crocus.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive, pointed style. It is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers have faded.
