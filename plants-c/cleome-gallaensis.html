Cleome gallaensis

Cleome gallaensis, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome gallaensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine in some African regions.
