Crassula elegans subsp. namibensis

Crassula elegans subsp.

namibensis, commonly known as the Namibian snowflake, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
