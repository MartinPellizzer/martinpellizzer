Cotoneaster cashmiriensis

Cotoneaster cashmiriensis, commonly known as Kashmir cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It typically grows in mountainous areas at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces bright red berries that are edible for birds.

Cotoneaster cashmiriensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
