Camptandra parvula var. angustifolia

Camptandra parvula var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camptandra, is a small herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
