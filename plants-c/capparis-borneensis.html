Capparis borneensis

Capparis borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean caper, is a flowering plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
