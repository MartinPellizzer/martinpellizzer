Cistus × incanus var. reichenbachii

Cistus × incanus var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
