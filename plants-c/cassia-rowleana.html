Cassia rowleana

Cassia rowleana, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, feathery inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine. Cassia rowleana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.
