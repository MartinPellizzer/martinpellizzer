Carthamus atractyloides

Carthamus atractyloides, commonly known as the Egyptian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its oil and medicinal properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
