Carex nigricans f. feminea

Carex nigricans f. feminea, commonly known as the feminine sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
