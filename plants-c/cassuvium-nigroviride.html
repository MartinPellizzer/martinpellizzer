Cassuvium nigroviride

Cassuvium nigroviride, commonly known as the black-green velvet fern, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its striking bi-colored foliage.

Its leaves are a combination of deep green and black, giving it a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other fern species. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle touch of color to its foliage. Native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
