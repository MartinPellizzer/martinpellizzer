Cleistochloa

Cleistochloa, commonly known as the spike grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, spike-like inflorescence and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. Cleistochloa species are known for their small, inconspicuous flowers that are often enclosed within the spike, a feature that gives the genus its scientific name.
