Clarisia ilicifolia

Clarisia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree or silverleaf clarisia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Clarisia ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
