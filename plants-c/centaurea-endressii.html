Centaurea endressii

Centaurea endressii, commonly known as the Endriss's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea endressii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
