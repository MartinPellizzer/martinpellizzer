Crocus tahtaliensis

Crocus tahtaliensis, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, early-flowering blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically appearing in late autumn to early winter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus tahtaliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, seasonal display.
