Coleus viridis

Coleus viridis, commonly known as the green nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Coleus viridis is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
