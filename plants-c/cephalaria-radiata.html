Cephalaria radiata

Cephalaria radiata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and can grow up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
