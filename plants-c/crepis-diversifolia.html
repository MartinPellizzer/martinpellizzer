Crepis diversifolia

Crepis diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing a robust root system and hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, often with a rough texture and serrated edges, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Crepis diversifolia is known for its adaptability and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
