Cephaelis krukovii

Cephaelis krukovii, commonly known as the golden flame or red-eyed dahlia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

The flowers are known for their long blooming period, adding continuous color to gardens from spring through summer.
