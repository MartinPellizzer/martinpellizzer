Calanthe kazuoi

Calanthe kazuoi, commonly known as the Kazuoi Calanthe, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the forest floor or on rocky substrates. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Calanthe kazuoi is considered a conservation concern.
