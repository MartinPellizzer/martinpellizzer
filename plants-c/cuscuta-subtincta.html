Cuscuta subtincta

Cuscuta subtincta, commonly known as the redstem dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique appearance with thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to North America and can be found growing on a variety of plants, including grasses and shrubs. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through contact with the host plant.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause damage to agricultural crops and natural vegetation.
