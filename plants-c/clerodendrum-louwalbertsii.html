Clerodendrum louwalbertsii

Clerodendrum louwalbertsii, commonly known as the Madagascar glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Clerodendrum louwalbertsii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
