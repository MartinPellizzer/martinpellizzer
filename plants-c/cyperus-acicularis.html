Cyperus acicularis

Cyperus acicularis, commonly known as the spike rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Cyperus acicularis is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
