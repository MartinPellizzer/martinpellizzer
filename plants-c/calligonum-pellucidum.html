Calligonum pellucidum

Calligonum pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often glaucous leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its ability to tolerate extreme conditions makes it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
