Cattleya marcaliana

Cattleya marcaliana, commonly known as the Marcaliana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant pink or red color with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has pseudobulbs and produces long, arching inflorescences with multiple blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
