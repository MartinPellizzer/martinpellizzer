Cyathea leptolepia

Cyathea leptolepia, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along stream banks. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its robust, fibrous roots, which help anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Cyathea leptolepia plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
