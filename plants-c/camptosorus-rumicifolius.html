Camptosorus rumicifolius

Camptosorus rumicifolius, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's distinctive appearance and early blooming habit make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
