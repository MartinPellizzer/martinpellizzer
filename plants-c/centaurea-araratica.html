Centaurea araratica

Centaurea araratica, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Centaurea araratica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
