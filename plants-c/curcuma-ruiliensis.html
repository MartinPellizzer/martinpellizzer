Curcuma ruiliensis

Curcuma ruiliensis, commonly known as the Ruili turmeric, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically measuring up to 30 cm in length.

The rhizomes of Curcuma ruiliensis are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
