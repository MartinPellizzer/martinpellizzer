Citharexylum bahamense

Citharexylum bahamense, commonly known as Bahamian rose or Bahamian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
