Calamagrostis lucida

Calamagrostis lucida, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to yellowish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Calamagrostis lucida is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
