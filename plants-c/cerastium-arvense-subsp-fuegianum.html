Cerastium arvense subsp. fuegianum

Cerastium arvense subsp.

fuegianum, commonly known as the Fuegian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
