Calamagrostis plinii

Calamagrostis plinii, commonly known as the Plinii reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows. The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Calamagrostis plinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
