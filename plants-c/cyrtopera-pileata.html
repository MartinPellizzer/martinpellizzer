Cyrtopera pileata

Cyrtopera pileata, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Cyrtopera pileata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
