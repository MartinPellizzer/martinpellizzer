Cyperus flavens

Cyperus flavens, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus flavens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
