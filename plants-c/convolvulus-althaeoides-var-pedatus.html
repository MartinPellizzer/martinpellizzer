Convolvulus althaeoides var. pedatus

Convolvulus althaeoides var. pedatus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is known for its resilience and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
