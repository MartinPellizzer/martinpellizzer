Crucifera thlaspi

Crucifera thlaspi, commonly known as whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas, fields, and roadsides. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces long, slender stems that bear clusters of yellow or white flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod called a silique, a typical feature of the crucifer family.

Whitlow grass is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in ecological succession.
