Capsicum annuum var. grossum

Capsicum annuum var. grossum, commonly known as the horn pepper, is a variant of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its elongated, horn-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically has a bushy growth habit with dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruits are usually harvested when they turn red, orange, or yellow, depending on the variety.
