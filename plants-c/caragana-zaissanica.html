Caragana zaissanica

Caragana zaissanica, commonly known as the desert peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Caragana zaissanica plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
