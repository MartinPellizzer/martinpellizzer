Coreopsis triloba

Coreopsis triloba, commonly known as the tickseed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with dark central disks, which bloom from late spring to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial that grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Coreopsis triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
