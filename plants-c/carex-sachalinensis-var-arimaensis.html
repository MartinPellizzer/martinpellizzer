Carex sachalinensis var. arimaensis

Carex sachalinensis var. arimaensis, commonly known as Arima sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
