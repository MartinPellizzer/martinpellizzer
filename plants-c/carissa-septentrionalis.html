Carissa septentrionalis

Carissa septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern carissa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
