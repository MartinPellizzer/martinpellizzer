Cacalia mucronifolia

Cacalia mucronifolia, commonly known as the sharp-pointed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia mucronifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
