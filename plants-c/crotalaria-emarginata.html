Crotalaria emarginata

Crotalaria emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces oblong, ridged pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a legume crop.

Crotalaria emarginata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
