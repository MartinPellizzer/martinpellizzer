Coleus puberulus

Coleus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink, red, or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Coleus puberulus is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
