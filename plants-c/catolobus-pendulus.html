Catolobus pendulus

Catolobus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging catclaw, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its long, pendulous branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are usually small and alternate, and it produces small, hard pods containing seeds.
