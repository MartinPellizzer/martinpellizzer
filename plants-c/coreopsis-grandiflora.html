Coreopsis grandiflora

Coreopsis grandiflora, commonly known as the tickseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Coreopsis grandiflora is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
