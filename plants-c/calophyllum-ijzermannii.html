Calophyllum ijzermannii

Calophyllum ijzermannii, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ecological contributions and potential in sustainable agriculture.
