Cuphea spicata var. tropica

Cuphea spicata var. tropica, commonly known as the tropical flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to container gardening.
