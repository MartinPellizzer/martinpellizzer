Centratherum punctatum subsp. camporum

Centratherum punctatum subsp.

camporum, commonly known as the Camp's centipede grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight blue-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
