Citrus spinosa

Citrus spinosa, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, round citrus fruits that are typically green when mature, with a thick, leathery rind. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slight waxy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
