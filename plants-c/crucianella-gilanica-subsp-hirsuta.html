Crucianella gilanica subsp. hirsuta

Crucianella gilanica subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy crucianella, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae. It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to regions of Asia, particularly in countries like Iran and Afghanistan.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
