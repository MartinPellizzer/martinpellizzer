Carex perplexa

Carex perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Carex perplexa has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
