Crotalaria lunaris

Crotalaria lunaris, commonly known as the moonflower crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
