Cinnamomum caudatifolium

Cinnamomum caudatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved cinnamon or narrow-leaved cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain the seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
