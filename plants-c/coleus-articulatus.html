Coleus articulatus

Coleus articulatus, commonly known as the creeping zingiber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in spikes, which are often borne on long, slender stems. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Coleus articulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
