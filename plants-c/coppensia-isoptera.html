Coppensia isoptera

Coppensia isoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It is typically found in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Coppensia isoptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
