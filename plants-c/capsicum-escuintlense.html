Capsicum escuintlense

Capsicum escuintlense, commonly known as the Mexican chili or huaycan, is a species of Capsicum native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that typically range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is an annual herb with hairy, branched stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Capsicum escuintlense is valued for its spicy flavor and is used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
