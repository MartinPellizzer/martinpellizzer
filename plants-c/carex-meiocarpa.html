Carex meiocarpa

Carex meiocarpa, commonly known as the small-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Carex meiocarpa is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
