Cyne capitulifera

Cyne capitulifera, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Cyne capitulifera is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
