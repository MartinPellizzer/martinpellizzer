Cotula cotuloides

Cotula cotuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its flowering stems are hairy and often bear a single flower at the top.

Cotula cotuloides is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
