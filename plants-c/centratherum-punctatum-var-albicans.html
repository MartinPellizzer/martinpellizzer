Centratherum punctatum var. albicans

Centratherum punctatum var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered centatherum, is a variant of the centatherum plant characterized by its striking white flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are tubular with a yellow throat, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

This variant is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
