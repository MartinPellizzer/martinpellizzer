Carlowrightia huicholiana

Carlowrightia huicholiana, commonly known as the Huichol sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is sturdy and upright.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance among the Huichol people.
