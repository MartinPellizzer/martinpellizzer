Croton racemosus

Croton racemosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescences and striking, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and sometimes lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

Croton racemosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
