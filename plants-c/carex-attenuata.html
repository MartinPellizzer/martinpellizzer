Carex attenuata

Carex attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and tapering, with a distinct sheathing base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex attenuata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
