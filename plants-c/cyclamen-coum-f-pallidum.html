Cyclamen coum f. pallidum

Cyclamen coum f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive harsh winter conditions.

Cyclamen coum f. pallidum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
