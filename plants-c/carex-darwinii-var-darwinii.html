Carex darwinii var. darwinii

Carex darwinii var. darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Darwin's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
