Combesia lanceolata subsp. transvaalensis

Combesia lanceolata subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal combesia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal spikes, and can range in color from white to pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
