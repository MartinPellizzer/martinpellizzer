Catasetum bergoldianum

Catasetum bergoldianum, commonly known as the Berggold orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and are often pollinated by specific insects.

Catasetum bergoldianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique blooming habits.
