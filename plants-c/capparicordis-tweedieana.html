Capparicordis tweedieana

Capparicordis tweedieana, commonly known as the Tweedie's heartleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the eastern region of Australia.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae, which includes various species of capers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
