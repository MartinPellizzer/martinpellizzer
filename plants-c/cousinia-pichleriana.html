Cousinia pichleriana

Cousinia pichleriana, commonly known as the Pichler's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a cylindrical shape.

Cousinia pichleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
