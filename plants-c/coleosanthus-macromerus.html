Coleosanthus macromerus

Coleosanthus macromerus, commonly known as the giant sea holly, is a unique flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray from the ocean. Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in coastal botany.

It is often found growing in dunes and rocky shores, where it plays a role in stabilizing these dynamic environments.
