Carex montana subsp. fritschii

Carex montana subsp.

fritschii, commonly known as Fritsch's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
