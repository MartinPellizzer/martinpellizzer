Chlorophytum petiolatum

Chlorophytum petiolatum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a hardy, evergreen perennial that belongs to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal indoor plant. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stems, though it is more popular for its foliage than its blooms.

Chlorophytum petiolatum is also known for its air-purifying qualities, enhancing its appeal as a houseplant.
