Citrus ichangensis subsp. latipes

Citrus ichangensis subsp.

latipes, commonly known as the Guangxi lemon, is a citrus species native to southern China. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

The plant produces fragrant, glossy leaves and has a compact growth habit. It is valued for its aromatic fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

The subspecies is closely related to other citrus species but is distinguished by its unique fruit morphology and aroma.
