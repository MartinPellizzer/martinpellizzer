Calophyllum cucullatum

Calophyllum cucullatum, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
