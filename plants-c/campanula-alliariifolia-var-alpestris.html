Campanula alliariifolia var. alpestris

Campanula alliariifolia var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a garlic-like aroma, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically growing in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine displays.
