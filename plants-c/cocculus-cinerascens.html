Cocculus cinerascens

Cocculus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored cocculus, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
