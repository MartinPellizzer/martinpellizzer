Cola vera

Cola vera, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant is renowned for its caffeine-containing seeds, which are used in various traditional and commercial products. Cola vera thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
