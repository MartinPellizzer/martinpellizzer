Chenopodium blitum

Chenopodium blitum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, branching clusters called inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium blitum is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
