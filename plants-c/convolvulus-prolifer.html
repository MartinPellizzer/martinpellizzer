Convolvulus prolifer

Convolvulus prolifer, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial vine characterized by its rapid growth and twining stems. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, blooming in the morning and closing in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Convolvulus prolifer is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
