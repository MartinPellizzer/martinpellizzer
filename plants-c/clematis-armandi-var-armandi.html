Clematis armandi var. armandi

Clematis armandi var. armandi, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
