Cyathea cyatheoides

Cyathea cyatheoides, commonly known as the tree fern, is a large evergreen fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Cyathea cyatheoides plays an important ecological role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
