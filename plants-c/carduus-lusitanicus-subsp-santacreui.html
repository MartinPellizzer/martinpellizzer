Carduus lusitanicus subsp. santacreui

Carduus lusitanicus subsp.

santacreui, commonly known as the Santacreu thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, prickly seed heads. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
