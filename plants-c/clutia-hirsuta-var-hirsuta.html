Clutia hirsuta var. hirsuta

Clutia hirsuta var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are often red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
