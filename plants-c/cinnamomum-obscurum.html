Cinnamomum obscurum

Cinnamomum obscurum, commonly known as the dark cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is highly valued in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fragrant, slightly spicy aroma.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests with high humidity.
