Cirsium eriophorum subsp. degenii

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the Degen's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flower heads that are composed of numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
