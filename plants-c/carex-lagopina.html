Carex lagopina

Carex lagopina, commonly known as the moorland sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and typically grows in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
