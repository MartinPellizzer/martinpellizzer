Cuphea saxatilis

Cuphea saxatilis, commonly known as the rock cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, bracted inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Cuphea saxatilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
