Cordula spiceriana

Cordula spiceriana, commonly known as the yellow cordula, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Cordula spiceriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
