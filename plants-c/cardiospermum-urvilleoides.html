Cardiospermum urvilleoides

Cardiospermum urvilleoides, commonly known as the heartleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for study in botany and ecology.
