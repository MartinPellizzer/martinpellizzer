Croton sarcopetalus

Croton sarcopetalus, commonly known as the red-flowered croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Croton sarcopetalus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
