Ceropegia subterranea

Ceropegia subterranea, commonly known as the underground ceropegia, is a unique succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous root system, which allows it to store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Ceropegia subterranea is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unusual appearance and ability to thrive in low-light environments.
