Cnicus pinnatifidus

Cnicus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is an annual or biennial herb that can spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
