Claytonia parviflora subsp. parviflora

Claytonia parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the common spring beauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features rosettes of glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators.

It is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
