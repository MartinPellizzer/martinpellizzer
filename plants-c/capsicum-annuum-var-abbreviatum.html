Capsicum annuum var. abbreviatum

Capsicum annuum var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf chili pepper, is a compact variety of the common bell pepper species.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that typically range in color from red to yellow or orange. This variety is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, as well as fleshy, fivewinged fruits.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a spicy condiment in various cuisines.
