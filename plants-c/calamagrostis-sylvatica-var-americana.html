Calamagrostis sylvatica var. americana

Calamagrostis sylvatica var. americana, commonly known as American wood reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
