Claytonia obovata

Claytonia obovata, commonly known as the "peacock flower," is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of broad, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Claytonia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
