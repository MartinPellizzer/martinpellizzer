Calyptranthes grandifolia var. macrantha

Calyptranthes grandifolia var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the wet season.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
