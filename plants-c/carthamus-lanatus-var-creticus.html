Carthamus lanatus var. creticus

Carthamus lanatus var. creticus, commonly known as the Cretan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces elongated, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant has been historically used for its oil-rich seeds and as a traditional medicinal herb.
