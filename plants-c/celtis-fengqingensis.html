Celtis fengqingensis

Celtis fengqingensis, commonly known as the Fengqing hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas, contributing to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
