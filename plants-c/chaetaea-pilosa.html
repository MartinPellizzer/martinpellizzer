Chaetaea pilosa

Chaetaea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy chaetaea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley.
