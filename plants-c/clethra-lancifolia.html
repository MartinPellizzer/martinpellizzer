Clethra lancifolia

Clethra lancifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, red, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Clethra lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
