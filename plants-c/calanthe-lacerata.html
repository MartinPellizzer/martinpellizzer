Calanthe lacerata

Calanthe lacerata, commonly known as the fragrant calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the species is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Calanthe lacerata is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
