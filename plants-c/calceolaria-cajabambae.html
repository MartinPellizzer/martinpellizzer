Calceolaria cajabambae

Calceolaria cajabambae, commonly known as the Cajabamba slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and delicate flower structure.

Calceolaria cajabambae is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
