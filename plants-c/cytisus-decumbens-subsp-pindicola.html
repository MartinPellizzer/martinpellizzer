Cytisus decumbens subsp. pindicola

Cytisus decumbens subsp.

pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans. It is characterized by its slender, prostrate or creeping growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
