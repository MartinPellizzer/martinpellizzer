Commiphora coriacea

Commiphora coriacea, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a species native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
