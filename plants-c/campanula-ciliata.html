Campanula ciliata

Campanula ciliata, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, oval leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula ciliata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
