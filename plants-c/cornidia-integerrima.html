Cornidia integerrima

Cornidia integerrima, commonly known as the white sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or open woodlands.

Cornidia integerrima is an important species for ecological studies due to its unique adaptations to coastal environments.
