Centropogon saltuum

Centropogon saltuum, commonly known as the salt marsh eryngo, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands. The plant features a tall, branching stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its root system is adapted to tolerate high salinity and fluctuating water levels.

Centropogon saltuum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
