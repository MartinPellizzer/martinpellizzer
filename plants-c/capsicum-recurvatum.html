Capsicum recurvatum

Capsicum recurvatum, commonly known as the curly capsicum or wild chili, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that range in color from green to red, depending on maturity. Its botanical features include hairy stems and glandular leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild chili flavor.
