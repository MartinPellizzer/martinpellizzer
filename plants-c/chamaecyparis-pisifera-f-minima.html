Chamaecyparis pisifera f. minima

Chamaecyparis pisifera f. minima, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress or dwarf Japanese cedar, is a compact evergreen conifer with a slow growth rate.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically yellow to reddish in color. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens and as a specimen plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
