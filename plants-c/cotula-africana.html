Cotula africana

Cotula africana, commonly known as African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often covered in fine white or grayish hairs.

Cotula africana is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
