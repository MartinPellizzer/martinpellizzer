Cucumis heptadactylus

Cucumis heptadactylus, commonly known as the seven-finger cucumber, is a rare and unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of western India and is characterized by its distinctive, branched tendrils that resemble fingers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits with a pale green skin and a mild, slightly sweet flavor. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
