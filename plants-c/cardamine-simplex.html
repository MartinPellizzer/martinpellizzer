Cardamine simplex

Cardamine simplex, commonly known as the lesser watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually rounded or heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, green flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
