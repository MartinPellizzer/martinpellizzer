Citrus gordonii

Citrus gordonii, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel, which is thick and edible. The plant thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit's high vitamin C content makes it both nutritious and flavorful.
