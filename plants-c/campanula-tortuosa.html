Campanula tortuosa

Campanula tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, often covered with fine hairs, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value contribute to its horticultural appeal.
