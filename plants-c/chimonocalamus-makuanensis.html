Chimonocalamus makuanensis

Chimonocalamus makuanensis, commonly known as the Makuan bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province, where it thrives in moist, mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
