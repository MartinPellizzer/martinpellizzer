Chelone glabra subsp. ochroleuca

Chelone glabra subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It prefers moist, shady habitats and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
