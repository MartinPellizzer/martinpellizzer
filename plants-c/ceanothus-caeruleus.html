Ceanothus caeruleus

Ceanothus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue curl buckeye, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are gray-green in color and often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Ceanothus caeruleus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
