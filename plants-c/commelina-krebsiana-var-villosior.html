Commelina krebsiana var. villosior

Commelina krebsiana var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, hairy structures, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
