Cerastium brachypetalum var. tauricum

Cerastium brachypetalum var. tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals that are short and broad, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
