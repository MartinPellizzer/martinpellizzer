Cerastium banaticum f. adenotrichum

Cerastium banaticum f. adenotrichum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, which give it a distinctive hairy appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
