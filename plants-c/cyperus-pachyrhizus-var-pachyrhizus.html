Cyperus pachyrhizus var. pachyrhizus

Cyperus pachyrhizus var. pachyrhizus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its morphology includes grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
