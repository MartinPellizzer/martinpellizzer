Campanula speciosa var. bicaulis

Campanula speciosa var. bicaulis, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
