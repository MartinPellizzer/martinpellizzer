Caesalpinia celendiniana

Caesalpinia celendiniana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom profusely during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its pods are elongated, woody, and often covered in a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
