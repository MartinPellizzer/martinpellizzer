Cytisus hirsutus

Cytisus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years, contributing to its persistence in the wild.

Cytisus hirsutus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
