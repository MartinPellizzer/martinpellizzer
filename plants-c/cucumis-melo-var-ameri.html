Cucumis melo var. ameri

Cucumis melo var. ameri, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a variant of the melon plant characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit.

The plant is typically vine-like, with hairy stems and lobed leaves that are alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms, a common trait in the Cucurbitaceae family. The fruit develops from the female flower and matures to a pale green or yellowish color, often with a slightly ribbed surface.

This variety is valued for its crisp, mild flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
