Clutia pulchella var. obtusata

Clutia pulchella var. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often hairy leaves that are broad and rounded at the tip. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
