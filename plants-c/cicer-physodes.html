Cicer physodes

Cicer physodes, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Cicer physodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
