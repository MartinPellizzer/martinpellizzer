Carex maidenii

Carex maidenii, commonly known as the Maidenhair Sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded woodlands.

Carex maidenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
