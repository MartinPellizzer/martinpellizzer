Curcuma amada

Curcuma amada, commonly known as the red ginger or red turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red-colored rhizomes. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Curcuma amada is also valued for its medicinal properties, similar to other members of the Curcuma genus.
