Calceolaria longiinternodia

Calceolaria longiinternodia, commonly known as the long internode calceolaria, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, while the stems exhibit long internodes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
