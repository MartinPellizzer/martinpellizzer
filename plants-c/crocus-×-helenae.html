Crocus × helenae

Crocus × helenae, commonly known as the Helen's crocus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crocus cartwrightianus and Crocus speciosus.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early color.
