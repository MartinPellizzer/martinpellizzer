Cardaminopsis halleri subsp. ovirensis

Cardaminopsis halleri subsp.

ovirensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of Ecuador. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as cloud forests and is often found in rocky or clayey soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
