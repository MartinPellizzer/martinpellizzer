Chenopodium hybridum

Chenopodium hybridum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, greenish flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its seeds are easily dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Chenopodium hybridum is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in vitamins and minerals.
