Cananga candolleana

Cananga candolleana, commonly known as the white champaca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements and traditional perfumery.

Cananga candolleana thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
