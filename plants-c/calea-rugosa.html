Calea rugosa

Calea rugosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sacred plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and striking, showy flowers that range from white to violet in color. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, tactile texture.

Calea rugosa is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in folk remedies for various health purposes.
