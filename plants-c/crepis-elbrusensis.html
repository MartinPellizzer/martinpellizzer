Crepis elbrusensis

Crepis elbrusensis, commonly known as the Elbrus hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh climate.

Crepis elbrusensis is a rare species, reflecting the unique ecological conditions of its mountainous habitat.
