Croton berlandieri

Croton berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated foliage, which can range from green to yellow and red. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical and subtropical climates.
