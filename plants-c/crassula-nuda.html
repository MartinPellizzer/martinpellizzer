Crassula nuda

Crassula nuda, commonly known as the naked crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Crassula nuda is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
