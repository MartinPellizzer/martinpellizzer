Costus asplundii

Costus asplundii, commonly known as the asplundii costus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the warmer months.

Costus asplundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
