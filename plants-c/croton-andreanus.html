Croton andreanus

Croton andreanus, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and green, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
