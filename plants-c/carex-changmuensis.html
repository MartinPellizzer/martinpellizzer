Carex changmuensis

Carex changmuensis, commonly known as the Changmu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Carex changmuensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native environment.
