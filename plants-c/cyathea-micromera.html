Cyathea micromera

Cyathea micromera, commonly known as the small-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyathea micromera plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests as both a habitat provider and a component of the forest understory.
