Cyathea capensis

Cyathea capensis, commonly known as the Cape tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
