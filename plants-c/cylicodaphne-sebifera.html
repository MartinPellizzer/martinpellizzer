Cylicodaphne sebifera

Cylicodaphne sebifera, commonly known as the sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed, three-chambered capsule that contains seeds.

Cylicodaphne sebifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
