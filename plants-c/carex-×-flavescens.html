Carex × flavescens

Carex × flavescens, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex × flavescens is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
