Cerasus fruticosa f. umbelliflora

Cerasus fruticosa f. umbelliflora, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in clusters during late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant floral display and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
