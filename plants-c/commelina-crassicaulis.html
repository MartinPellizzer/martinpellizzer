Commelina crassicaulis

Commelina crassicaulis, commonly known as broadleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically arranged in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
