Cyathea phalerata

Cyathea phalerata, commonly known as the Phalerata tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the spore-bearing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyathea phalerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture and landscaping.
