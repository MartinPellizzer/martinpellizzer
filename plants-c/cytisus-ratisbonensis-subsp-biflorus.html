Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp. biflorus

Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp.

biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
