Chascanum cernuum

Chascanum cernuum, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chascanum cernuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
