Crepis willemetioides

Crepis willemetioides, commonly known as the Willemet's mouse-ear, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant has narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces numerous tiny florets arranged in a compact cluster.

It is typically found in open, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
