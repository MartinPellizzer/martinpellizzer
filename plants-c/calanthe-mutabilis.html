Calanthe mutabilis

Calanthe mutabilis, commonly known as the variable calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can change hue as they mature. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, favoring rocky or forested habitats. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Calanthe mutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
