Cerastium holosteoides f. glutinosum

Cerastium holosteoides f. glutinosum, commonly known as sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and hairy, often sticky stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance. Its small white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
