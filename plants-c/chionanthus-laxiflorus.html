Chionanthus laxiflorus

Chionanthus laxiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The Chinese fringeflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
