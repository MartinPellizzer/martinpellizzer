Crassula lambertiana

Crassula lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically forms a compact, rosette-shaped structure with a smooth, bluish-green foliage. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Crassula lambertiana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
