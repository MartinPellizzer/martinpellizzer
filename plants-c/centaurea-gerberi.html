Centaurea gerberi

Centaurea gerberi, commonly known as the Gerber's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Centaurea gerberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
