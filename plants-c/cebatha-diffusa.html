Cebatha diffusa

Cebatha diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf cebatha, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
