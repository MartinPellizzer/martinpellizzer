Capparis spinosa var. rupestris

Capparis spinosa var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, waxy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by edible, green berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
