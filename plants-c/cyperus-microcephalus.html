Cyperus microcephalus

Cyperus microcephalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus microcephalus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
