Caryodaphne laevigata var. angustifolia

Caryodaphne laevigata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by hard, woody fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
