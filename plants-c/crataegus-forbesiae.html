Crataegus forbesiae

Crataegus forbesiae, commonly known as Forbes' hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
