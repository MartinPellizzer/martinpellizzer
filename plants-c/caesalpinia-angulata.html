Caesalpinia angulata

Caesalpinia angulata, commonly known as the angular crown fern, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its seed pods are long and angular, which gives it its common name.

Caesalpinia angulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
