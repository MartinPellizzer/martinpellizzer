Centaurea × eversiana

Centaurea × eversiana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Centaurea × eversiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its colorful blooms.
