Commiphora anacardiifolia

Commiphora anacardiifolia, commonly known as the Indian frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by unique, resinous capsules containing aromatic gum resins. These resins have been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
