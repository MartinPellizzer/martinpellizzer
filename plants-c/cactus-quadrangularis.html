Cactus quadrangularis

Cactus quadrangularis, commonly known as the jointed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, angular, and segmented stems that are typically green and covered in small spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique shape and resilience.
