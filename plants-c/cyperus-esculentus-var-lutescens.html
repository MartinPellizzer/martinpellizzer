Cyperus esculentus var. lutescens

Cyperus esculentus var. lutescens, commonly known as yellow nutsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its triangular, tuberous roots and its distinctive yellowish to brownish stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. Yellow nutsedge thrives in wet, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and marshy areas.

It is an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative reproduction.
