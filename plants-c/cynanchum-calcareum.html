Cynanchum calcareum

Cynanchum calcareum, commonly known as the limestone milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late summer.

The species is notable for its milky sap and its role in supporting local pollinators.
