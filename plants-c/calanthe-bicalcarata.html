Calanthe bicalcarata

Calanthe bicalcarata, commonly known as the two-horned calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces erect spikes bearing numerous small, hooded flowers with prominent spurred petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe bicalcarata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
