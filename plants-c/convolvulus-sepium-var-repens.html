Convolvulus sepium var. repens

Convolvulus sepium var. repens, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and kidney-shaped.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
