Ceropteris serrata

Ceropteris serrata, commonly known as the serrated huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically narrow and serrated along the edges. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ceropteris serrata thrives in humid, shaded environments, making it a common epiphyte in tropical and subtropical forests.
