Cerastium semidecandrum subsp. glutinosum

Cerastium semidecandrum subsp.

glutinosum, commonly known as sticky chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in a sticky, glandular coating.

The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.
