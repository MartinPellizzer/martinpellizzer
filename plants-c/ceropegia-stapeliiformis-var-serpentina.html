Ceropegia stapeliiformis var. serpentina

Ceropegia stapeliiformis var. serpentina, commonly known as the snake plant or viper's bugloss, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, somewhat cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The leaves are typically green, sometimes with a bluish tint, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellow, and it is adapted to arid environments.

This plant is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
