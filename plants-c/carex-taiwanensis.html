Carex taiwanensis

Carex taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
