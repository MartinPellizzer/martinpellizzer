Cirsium megacephalum

Cirsium megacephalum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, spherical flower heads that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with coarse teeth and a thick, hairy stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Cirsium megacephalum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
