Cicer flexuosum subsp. baldshuanicum

Cicer flexuosum subsp.

baldshuanicum, commonly known as the Baldshuan vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, hairy pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often used for forage and soil stabilization in its native habitats.
