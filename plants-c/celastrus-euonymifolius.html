Celastrus euonymifolius

Celastrus euonymifolius, commonly known as the European bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant bears orange-red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, and it has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in length.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
