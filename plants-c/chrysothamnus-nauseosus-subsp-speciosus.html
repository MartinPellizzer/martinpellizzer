Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. speciosus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

speciosus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which are adaptations to arid environments.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
