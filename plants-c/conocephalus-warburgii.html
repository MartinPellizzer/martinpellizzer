Conocephalus warburgii

Conocephalus warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's conocephalus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Aroidae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence. The plant produces a large, white spathe with a central spadix, which is typical of many aroid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Conocephalus warburgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
