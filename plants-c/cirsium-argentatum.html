Cirsium argentatum

Cirsium argentatum, commonly known as the silver centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. Its leaves are silver-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cirsium argentatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.
