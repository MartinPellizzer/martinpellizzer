Coptis occidentalis var. japonica

Coptis occidentalis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes are rich in berberine, a compound with medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
