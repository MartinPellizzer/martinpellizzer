Crocus vernus var. puniceus

Crocus vernus var. puniceus, commonly known as the red crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, showy flowers with deep red petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The variety is closely related to other crocus species but is distinguished by its vivid red coloration.
