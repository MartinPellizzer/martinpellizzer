Cestrum undulatum

Cestrum undulatum, commonly known as night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which open at night. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
