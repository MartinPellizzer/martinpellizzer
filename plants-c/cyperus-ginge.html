Cyperus ginge

Cyperus ginge, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its triangular stems and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Cyperus ginge has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
