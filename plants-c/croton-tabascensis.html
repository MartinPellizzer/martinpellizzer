Croton tabascensis

Croton tabascensis, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton tabascensis is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
