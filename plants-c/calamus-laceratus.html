Calamus laceratus

Calamus laceratus, commonly known as the broken reed, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and can be quite fibrous. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
