Cyanicula sericea

Cyanicula sericea, commonly known as the silky cyanicula, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its ornamental value comes from its soft, silvery foliage and striking flower color.

Cyanicula sericea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
