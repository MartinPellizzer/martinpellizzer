Cymbidium lushuiense

Cymbidium lushuiense, commonly known as the Lushui orchid, is a species of orchid native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province.

It is characterized by its tall, slender pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cymbidium lushuiense is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
