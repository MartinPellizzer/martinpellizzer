Corytholoma curtiflorum

Corytholoma curtiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered bell-plant, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Corytholoma curtiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
