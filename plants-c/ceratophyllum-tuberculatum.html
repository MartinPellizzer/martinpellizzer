Ceratophyllum tuberculatum

Ceratophyllum tuberculatum, commonly known as the spiked watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in tiny bumps. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in lakes and ponds.

Ceratophyllum tuberculatum is known for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and help in water filtration.
