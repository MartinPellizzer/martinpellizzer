Calystegia sepium subsp. appalachiana

Calystegia sepium subsp.

appalachiana, commonly known as the Appalachian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
