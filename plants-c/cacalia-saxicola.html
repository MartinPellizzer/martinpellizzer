Cacalia saxicola

Cacalia saxicola, commonly known as the rock cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cacalia saxicola is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
