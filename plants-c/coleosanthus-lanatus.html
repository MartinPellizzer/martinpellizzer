Coleosanthus lanatus

Coleosanthus lanatus, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa, characterized by its strap-like, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor, making it a cultivated crop in some regions.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
