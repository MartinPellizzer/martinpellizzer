Clitoria ternatea var. pleniflora

Clitoria ternatea var. pleniflora, commonly known as the double-flowered butterfly pea, is a variant of the butterfly pea plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-petaled flowers that come in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
