Clerodendranthus spicatus

Clerodendranthus spicatus, commonly known as the spiculate clerodendron, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, pink to purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
