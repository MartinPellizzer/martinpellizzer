Cirsium grandirosuliferum

Cirsium grandirosuliferum, commonly known as the grandiose thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds via the wind. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cirsium grandirosuliferum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
