Cerastium rubescens f. tetraschistum

Cerastium rubescens f. tetraschistum, commonly known as the four-leafed mouse-ear chickweed, is a horticultural form of the species Cerastium rubescens.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its distinctive four-leafed rosette, which is a rare and ornamental feature. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

This cultivar is valued for its compact size and ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
