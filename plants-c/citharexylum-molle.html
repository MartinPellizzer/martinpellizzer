Citharexylum molle

Citharexylum molle, commonly known as velvetleaf or sweetleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often velvety leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Citharexylum molle is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
