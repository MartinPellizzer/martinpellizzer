Cutandia rohlfsiana

Cutandia rohlfsiana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cutandia rohlfsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
