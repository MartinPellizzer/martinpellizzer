Cucumis engleri

Cucumis engleri, commonly known as the Chinese ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, slender, ridged fruits that are typically green when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are used in various traditional cuisines, particularly in China and India, for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons.
