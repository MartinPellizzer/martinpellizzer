Chaetochloa liebmannii

Chaetochloa liebmannii, commonly known as the silvergrass or silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Chaetochloa liebmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
