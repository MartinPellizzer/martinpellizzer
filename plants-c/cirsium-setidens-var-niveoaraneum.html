Cirsium setidens var. niveoaraneum

Cirsium setidens var. niveoaraneum, commonly known as the snow spider thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, purplish-green stems and large, woolly leaves that are typically grayish-white in color. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
