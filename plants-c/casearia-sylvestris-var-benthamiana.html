Casearia sylvestris var. benthamiana

Casearia sylvestris var. benthamiana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed bush.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America, characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant foliage and colorful fruits, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.
