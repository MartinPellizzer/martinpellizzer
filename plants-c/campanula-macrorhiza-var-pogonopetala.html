Campanula macrorhiza var. pogonopetala

Campanula macrorhiza var. pogonopetala, commonly known as the long-bearded bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive long, hairy beard at the base of each petal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
