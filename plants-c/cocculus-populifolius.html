Cocculus populifolius

Cocculus populifolius, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant's fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky appendages. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
