Crataegus recurva

Crataegus recurva, commonly known as the bentthorn hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches with oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is edible but often tart, and it is sometimes used in jams or as a forage plant for wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
