Commelina pyrrhoblepharis

Commelina pyrrhoblepharis, commonly known as the red-eyed spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with prominent red markings around the throat, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
