Canna indica subsp. orientalis

Canna indica subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length.

The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
