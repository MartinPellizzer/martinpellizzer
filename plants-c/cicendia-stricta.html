Cicendia stricta

Cicendia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, ascending stems that often grow in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Cicendia stricta is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
