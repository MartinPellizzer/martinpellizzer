Campnosperma brassii

Campnosperma brassii, commonly known as the broad-leaved waratah, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and dry forest habitats. The plant features showy, red or pink floral spikes that bloom in spring, attracting birds and insects. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, and the bark is smooth and often fissured.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
