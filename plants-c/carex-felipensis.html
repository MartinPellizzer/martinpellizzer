Carex felipensis

Carex felipensis, commonly known as the Felip's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive mid-green color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

Carex felipensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
