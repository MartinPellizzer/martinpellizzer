Cactus cassythoides

Cactus cassythoides, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to the desert landscape. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Cactus cassythoides is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky soils and sandy plains.
