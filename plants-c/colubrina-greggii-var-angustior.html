Colubrina greggii var. angustior

Colubrina greggii var. angustior, commonly known as Gregg's colubrina, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in drought-prone regions.
