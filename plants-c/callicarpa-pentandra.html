Callicarpa pentandra

Callicarpa pentandra, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that mature in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pale green to pink and appear in clusters.

Callicarpa pentandra is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and autumn foliage.
