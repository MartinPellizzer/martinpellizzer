Cyperus pseudovegetus

Cyperus pseudovegetus, commonly known as the false umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its cylindrical, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus pseudovegetus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
