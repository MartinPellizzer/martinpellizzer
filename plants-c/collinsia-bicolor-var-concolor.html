Collinsia bicolor var. concolor

Collinsia bicolor var. concolor, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
