Clematis longistyla

Clematis longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil. Its long, slender stems are equipped with hooked tendrils that aid in climbing.

Clematis longistyla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring-blooming display.
