Cerasus × subhirtella f. ascendens

Cerasus × subhirtella f. ascendens, commonly known as the Weeping Cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits striking ornamental features.

This plant is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, with clusters of pinkish-white flowers appearing in early spring. The leaves are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The Weeping Cherry is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
