Cuphea sanguinea

Cuphea sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular red flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cuphea sanguinea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
